## Project Notes
Option Period Selected - Begins on April 14, 2026

The Phase I effort will demonstrate the technical feasibility of integrating magnetic anomaly detection (MAD) and passive acoustic sensing technologies into a SL UAS platform. This effort will encompass sensor integration, platform modification, system testing, and preliminary validation in laboratory environments. The work will be conducted at BST facilities, in collaboration with Cetacean Research Technology (CRT) and QuSpin, with field testing performed at BST’s designated test site in Longmont, Colorado. The final deliverables will include a functional prototype, comprehensive test results, and recommendations for Phase II development. The work plan also includes both Base and Option tasks where the Option tasks mainly involve preliminary work related to Phase II deliverables. The deliverable for each of these tasks will be a briefing deck outlining the results to be presented to the TPOC. Further deliverables will include a final report of the Base and Option period of Phase 1 at 6 months and 1 year.
