Since 2018, NOAA, OMAO, Uncrewed Systems Operations Center (UxSOC) collaborated with Black Swift under the Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) program awards SBIR Phase I (1305M218CNRMW0059), and 2019-2020 SBIR Phase II (1305M219CNRMW0030). Through the awards, NOAA and Black Swift have collaborated to develop a platform that can collect critical measurements from air-launched sUAS in places and times of TCs that other manned platforms are incapable of. Black Swift hardware has been developed and purchased to be used in the WP-3D which allows for control and data collection of the meteorological data sets collected by the Black Swift S0 as well as system control. This data is critically important to a variety of stakeholders including forecasters, researchers, emergency managers and operational modeling centers.

This effort will allow for the Black Swift S0 to obtain SBIR Phase III and provide continuous successful, measurable operations from the NOAA aircraft. NOAA has invested in the infrastructure required to routinely use this platform on the NOAA aircraft. Failure to acquire advanced technology such as the Black Swift S0 platform inhibits NOAA’s ability to meet research and operational requirements associated with collecting critical measurements from air-launched UAS in difficult to observe and dangerous to navigate TC low level, high wind environments .

Black Swift is required to provide: 
    150 S0 SUAS Platforms
    Mission Days Operational Support (including travel) 
    Operator and Observer Course Development and Training
    Instructor Training Session
    Technical Documentation of Software and Hardware development
