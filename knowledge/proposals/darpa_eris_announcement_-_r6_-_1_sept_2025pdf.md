## Executive Summary

This document is DARPA's master solicitation for the Expedited Research Innovation System (ERIS), a digital marketplace and rapid acquisition pathway for disruptive technologies. ERIS allows U.S.-based companies, non-traditional contractors, academia, and individuals to submit video pitches of breakthrough technologies that are assessed competitively and, if deemed "awardable," placed in a marketplace for rapid procurement by DoD activities through Other Transaction Authority. The system establishes a streamlined, low-barrier entry point for technology transition from research through prototype development and testing.

## Program Structure & Goals

**What ERIS Is:**
- A digital environment hosting post-competition video solutions
- Assessed through competitive procedures compliant with 10 USC 4021, 4022, and 4023
- Provides venue for government to search, view, review, and procure breakthrough technologies via rapid acquisition pathways
- Open call structure with monthly collection periods and continuous submissions

**Core Goals:**
1. Establish centralized location for DARPA to assess disruptive, cutting-edge technological solutions
2. Enable industry, academia, and developers to present beyond state-of-the-art technologies to government
3. Streamline procurement of available solutions, addressing major impediments for large/small businesses and nontraditional defense contractors
4. Provide technology transition pathway: idea → research → prototype → experimental test and evaluation and beyond

## Eligibility & Participation

**Who Can Participate:**
- All U.S.-based companies (traditional and nontraditional, large and small businesses)
- U.S. universities, non-profit organizations
- Individuals
- Foreign universities, research institutions, and entities (if compliant with international law and export regulations)

**Why Participate:**
- Direct exposure to DARPA program offices
- Fast-track assessment (notification within 30 calendar days of collection period end)
- "Post-competitive" awardable status eligible for award without further competition
- Fully negotiable award terms (payment, IP/data rights, schedule, milestones, pricing)
- Potential for multiple awards from single submission with no price ceilings or duration limits
- Access to feedback sessions and improvement guidance

## Topic Areas (Current Focus)

ERIS seeks video solution pitches in the following areas:

1. **Detection and Tracking:** Detecting and tracking elusive objects of interest across air, land, and space domains

2. **SWaP-C Advancement:** Overcoming limitations of current sensing systems (size, weight, power, cost, performance)

3. **Strategic Systems Resilience:** Advanced technologies for improved resilience, efficiency, and effectiveness of strategic systems; critical infrastructure and military command-and-control systems (networks and applications across strategic, command, operational, and tactical edges)

4. **Chemical and Biological Defense:** Technologies for defense against potential chemical and biological threats (human-made or naturally occurring); threat surveillance, detection, diagnosis, countermeasure design/deployment, and attribution for humans and bioeconomy-critical elements (agricultural crops, livestock, biomanufacturing)

5. **Operational Resilience & Human Performance:** Technologies for resilience throughout prepare, deployment, execute, and return cycle; human health and performance optimization; biotechnologies for production of supplies and in situ resource utilization in austere locations; AI/ML convergence with chemistry and biology

6. **Complex Systems Metrology:** Groundbreaking methods and metrology for complex, emergent, and adaptive systems; quantum properties at scale; material property engineering; adaptive and resilient human-AI ecosystems (AI-only, data-driven approaches out of scope)

## Submission Requirements & Process

### Timeline & Collection
- **Continuously open** with monthly collection periods
- Monthly cutoff: Final day of month at 12:00 PM (Noon) ET
- Assessment in following month with results within 30 days of collection period end

### Submission Form Requirements
Each submission must include:
1. Submitting entity/individual name (full legal name matching SAM.gov if available)
2. Website address
3. Unique Entity ID (UEI) or "NA"
4. Business size (per SBA 13 CFR Part 121)
5. Traditional/nontraditional contractor status (per 10 USC 3014)
6. Small business program designations (must match SAM.gov)
7. Country
8. Principal place of business address
9. **Topic area** (solution may fit multiple areas; selection affects categorization only, not assessment)
10. **Technology Readiness Level (TRL)** - single level, not range (see Appendix A for definitions)
11. At least five relevant keywords (comma-separated)
12. Primary and alternate point of contact with email and phone
13. Submission title (< 128 characters)
14. Abstract description (1,500 characters max) - may include clearance status, certifications, active contracts, GSA schedules, FEDRAMP status
15. Submission type: "New ERIS Submission" or "ERIS Resubmission"
16. **ERIS Supplemental Slides** (four slides per Appendix B format):
    - Slide 1: ERIS Submission Information (including CAGE code if available)
    - Slide 2: Company Introduction (succinct one-slide overview of company and why engaging DARPA/DoD)
    - Slide 3: ERIS Submission Criteria Quad Chart (summarizes four video content criteria)
    - Slide 4: Solution Overview & DARPA White Space Chart (clearly communicate what you're doing and how; use metrics to demonstrate impact)

### Video Format Requirements
- **Length:** Maximum 7:00 minutes (videos exceeding 7 minutes will not be assessed)
- **Resolution:** HD 1280x720 minimum
- **File format:** .mp4 encoded for streaming, under 1 GB
- **Color space:** Rec709 or sRGB
- **Markings:** No restrictive markings other than valid copyright notice
- **Non-proprietary:** Must be unclassified; no proprietary, export-controlled, or CUI information

### Video Content Requirements (Four Mandatory Elements)

Each video must address:

1. **Defining the Problem & Current State of the Art**
   - Clearly define scope of problem(s) the solution addresses
   - Explain current state of art and limits of current practice
   - Ensure problem falls within at least one ERIS topic area
   - Explain thoroughly but leave time for all four elements

2. **Advancing the State of the Art**
   - Detail why solution likely advances current state of art
   - Explain how this contributes to DARPA's mission of creating technological surprise
   - Describe how problem is being addressed today
   - Clearly articulate what is "new" in your approach vs. current practices
   - Describe unique new insight for advancing state of art
   - Identify barriers to advancement
   - Explain why you think your approach will succeed
   - Briefly state proposed plan/strategy if funded

3. **Team Capability (Key Personnel, Vision, Expertise, Experience)**
   - Demonstrate team (not just company) capability to drive project to successful outcome
   - Highlight team's unique vision, expertise, and experience
   - Discuss key personnel qualifications specific to proposed effort
   - Explain why this team is best and possesses the "it" factor for successful completion

4. **Defense and/or Commercial Market Use Case/Impact**
   - Clearly demonstrate solution has or will have impact on defense and/or commercial markets
   - Detail specific applications or use cases
   - Identify the "so what" of the solution/project

### Submission Portal
- All submissions through **DARPAConnect.us/ERIS**
- No hard copy, CD/DVD/Blu Ray submissions accepted
- Select "Create Submission" to begin

## Compliance Review Process

**Compliance Check Timeline:** Within 1 business day of submission (pending volume)

**Scope of Compliance Review:**
1. Video length ≤ 7:00
2. Video viewable and audible
3. Graphics/charts legible
4. No prohibited markings
5. Conforms to resolution, file type, color space requirements
6. Contains all required submission criteria
7. Submission form data complete and accurate
8. Business size and nontraditional status verified against SAM.gov
9. Effort fits within one ERIS topic area
10. Selected TRL consistent with proposed solution
11. Supplemental slides completed per Appendix B format

**Non-Compliant Submissions:** Notified via email with request to correct and resubmit as "New ERIS Submission"

**Best Practice:** Do not submit on final day of collection period to ensure compliance notice

## Assessment Process

**Assessment Criteria:** Per Appendix C of announcement

**Assessor Panel Composition:**
- Peer panel of government, industry, and academic subject matter experts (SMEs)
- Non-government SMEs operate under signed non-disclosure agreements (NDAs)
- Pool includes experts from industry, academia, non-profit research institutions with DARPA-relevant programs

**Assessment Output:**
- Written assessment of each submission applying established criteria, weighting, and rating
- All assessments rated as either **"awardable"** or **"non-awardable"**
- Minimum three-member peer panel (government, industry, academic representative)
- Government Selecting Official (GSO) makes final determination

**Monthly Cycle:**
- Peer panel develops recommendations
- Recommendations presented monthly to designated GSO
- GSO determines final awardable/non-awardable status

**Important Notes:**
- All submitters receive assessor comments and rating scorecard
- Assessment against published rubric for technical merit, not against specific requirement
- Non-proprietary submissions assessed on viability to support future needs
- Government monitors consistency of assessor ratings to identify biases

## ERIS Resubmissions

**Resubmission Eligibility:**
- Only for solutions previously submitted as "New ERIS Submission," completed peer assessment, and deemed non-awardable
- Can resubmit in any subsequent collection period while ERIS active
- Multiple resubmissions permitted

**Support for Resubmissions:**
- Each unsuccessful submitter receives rating scorecard with assessor comments and improvement suggestions
- ERIS Marketplace Manager (ARI) provides weekly feedback sessions to review assessor comments
- Feedback sessions help develop strategy to address weaknesses and prepare stronger resubmission
- **Highly recommended** that unsuccessful providers participate in feedback sessions
- Office hours information available at www.darpaconnect.us/ERIS

**Submission Type Designation:**
- Non-compliant submissions returned for technical deficiency remain "New ERIS Submission" when corrected
- Only peer-assessed, non-awardable solutions designated as "ERIS Resubmission"

## Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI) Mitigation

**OCI Identified:** SME assessors evaluating their own solutions or competitors' solutions

**Mitigation Measures:**

1. **Self-Assessment Conflict:**
   - Assessors prohibited from evaluating their own technology solutions
   - Assessors prohibited from evaluating solutions of competitors in which they hold financial interest
   - Assessors required to complete government ethics training
   - Assessors required to deliver statement certifying recognition of prohibition
   - Affirmative duty to identify financial interests
   - All assessors operate under NDAs

2. **Competitor Assessment Conflict (Lesser Risk):**
   - Non-proprietary submissions reduce OCI risk
   - Assessed against published rubric for technical merit
   - Minimum three-member peer panel with government, industry, academic representation
   - GSO responsible for final decisions
   - Assessments not evaluated against specific requirement
   - Government monitors assessor rating consistency to identify biases
   - All assessors required to complete government ethics training
   - All assessors operate under NDAs
