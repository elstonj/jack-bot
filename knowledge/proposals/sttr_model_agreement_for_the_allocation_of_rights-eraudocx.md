## Executive Summary
This is a formal STTR Program agreement between Black Swift Technologies (a Delaware LLC based in Boulder, Colorado) and Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University governing intellectual property rights and allocation for a Navy-funded STTR project focused on expendable air-sea profiling observations using small uncrewed aircraft systems in hazardous weather. The agreement allocates ownership of project intellectual property, commercialization rights, and licensing options between the parties and establishes confidentiality and publication procedures.

**Ownership Allocation:**

### Follow-On Work
- All subsequent licenses, contracts, subcontracts, and sublicenses must implement the Project IP rights provisions
- PARTIES and Government must obtain and retain granted rights in all future research, development, and commercialization work

### Confidentiality & Publication
- **Confidential Handling:** Proprietary information of each party must be held confidential by the receiving party; exceptions apply for publicly known information, independent development, or information from independent sources
- **Publication Rights:** Either party may publish results, subject to:
  - Right of refusal notice to the other party
  - 21-day review period for commenting
  - Upon request, additional 14-day deferral for patent application filing
  - Comments given "full consideration" before publication

### Liability & Indemnification
- **Warranty Disclaimers:** Both parties disclaim all warranties (expressed/implied), including merchantability, fitness for particular purpose, and freedom from infringement
- **Indemnification:**
  - BST indemnifies ERAU for claims arising from BST's commercialization activities
  - Both PARTIES indemnify the Government for commercialization-related claims

### Termination
- Either party may terminate with 60 days written notice
- Either party may terminate for non-compliance of the other party
- Each party responsible for its share of costs through termination date
- Confidentiality, use, and non-disclosure obligations survive termination

1. **Blank/Unfilled Terms:** The agreement contains multiple blank fields (initial option periods, extension periods, offset percentages) that were not completed at the time this version was drafted. This suggests the agreement was a template or that final terms were still under negotiation.
