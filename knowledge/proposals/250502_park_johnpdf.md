## Executive Summary
Old Dominion University Research Foundation proposes a subcontract under Black Swift Technologies to develop and validate the S0 expendable atmospheric profiling system for Navy operations in hazardous weather conditions. The work focuses on engineering enhancements, integration with Navy platforms (sonobuoys, P-8A Poseidon, CH-53E helicopters), calibration/validation activities, and onboard data processing capabilities.

## Technical Approach

### Task 2: Engineering Enhancements and Error Analysis
- Develop integration plans for the S0 with Navy-specific launch platforms:
  - Sonobuoy launch systems
  - External pod mounts on P-8A Poseidon aircraft
  - CH-53E helicopter mounts
- Conduct simulations and mechanical tests to validate platform compatibility
- Perform detailed error analysis of current S0 data, identifying sources of uncertainty in:
  - Wind measurements
  - Turbulence estimation
  - Height calculations

### Task 3: Calibration and Validation Activities
- Plan and execute calibration/validation activities to improve S0 data accuracy
- Wind tunnel tests
- Field deployments in controlled conditions
- Comparative studies using:
  - Instrumented towers
  - NCAR dropsondes
- Refine algorithms for:
  - Vertical velocity estimation
  - Turbulence estimation
- Ensure robust performance in remote oceanic environments

### Task 5: Onboard Data Processing Development
- Enhance onboard processing capabilities for:
  - Quality control (QC)
  - Derived metric computation (wind and wave spectra, edge energy)
- Develop computationally efficient algorithms for:
  - High-rate data processing onboard
  - Compressed data transmission to ground stations
  - Maximized bandwidth utility
- Investigate adaptable AI algorithms for:
  - Training surrogates of expensive physics models
  - Optimizing flight trajectories based on new observations

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 System
- **What it is**: Expendable air-sea profiling observation platform for deployment in hazardous weather conditions
- **Proposed use**: Navy maritime weather reconnaissance and profiling operations
- **Current limitations being addressed**: 
  - Data accuracy issues in wind measurements, turbulence, and height calculations
  - Limited integration with Navy launch platforms
  - Basic onboard data processing capabilities
- **Performance targets**: Robust oceanic environment operation; efficient onboard processing with bandwidth constraints

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary use case**: Hazardous weather profiling in oceanic environments
- **Deployment platforms**: 
  - Sonobuoy launch systems (traditional Navy air-deployed ASW/oceanographic tool)
  - P-8A Poseidon (Navy maritime patrol aircraft)
  - CH-53E Super Stallion (Navy transport/heavy-lift helicopter)
- **Mission context**: Air-sea profiling observations in conditions where traditional methods are impractical
