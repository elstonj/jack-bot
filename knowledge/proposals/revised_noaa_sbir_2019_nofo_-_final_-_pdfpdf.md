## Executive Summary
This is the official NOAA SBIR Program solicitation for fiscal year 2019, inviting small businesses to submit Phase I feasibility research proposals across multiple topic areas aligned with NOAA's mission in oceanography, atmospheric science, fisheries, and coastal management. The program provides up to $120,000 for Phase I projects with a 6-month performance period, with successful Phase I awardees eligible to advance to Phase II awards of up to $400,000 for R&D development over approximately 24 months.

## Technical Approach
This document outlines the three-phase SBIR program structure:
- **Phase I (Feasibility Research):** Up to $120,000, 6-month period to determine scientific, technical, and commercial merit and feasibility
- **Phase II (R&D Development):** Up to $400,000, typically 24 months plus 1 year for commercialization planning
- **Phase III (Commercialization):** Non-SBIR funded phase where companies obtain private sector or other government funding to commercialize prototype into viable product/service

Minimum 2/3 of Phase I research effort must be performed by awardee; maximum 1/3 can be subcontracted/consultant costs.

## Eligibility & Requirements

**Applicant Qualifications:**
- Must be a Small Business Concern (SBC) as defined in 13 C.F.R. § 121.702
- Principal Investigator must have primary employment (>50% time) with the SBC
- R&D work must be performed in the United States (exemptions rare)
- Company must be registered in SBA Company Registry (www.sbir.gov/registration)
- Companies with 21+ Phase I awards in past 5 years must maintain Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate of at least 0.25

**Priority Areas:**
- Manufacturing-related R&D (per Executive Order 13329)
- Energy efficiency and renewable energy systems R&D (per Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007)

**Certifications Required:**
- Fraud, Waste, and Abuse training completion certificate
- Performance Benchmark Ratings verification
- Human subjects research compliance (if applicable)
- Vertebrate animal research compliance (if applicable)
- Tax delinquency and felony conviction certifications

## Notable Details

1. **Application Submission:** Through Grants.gov; deadline January 8, 2019 at 5:00 p.m. ET

2. **Evaluation Criteria (Phase I):**
   - Scientific and technical merit
   - Feasibility of proposed research
   - Potential for commercial success
   - Quality of performance of the small business concern

3. **Competitive Fairness:** Strict prohibition on oral or written communication with NOAA regarding proposals, except for public Q&A on technical subtopics (deadline December 5, 2018)

4. **Phase II Eligibility:** Only Phase I awardees may apply for Phase II in subsequent NOFO

5. **No Cost Sharing Required:** Explicitly not a requirement for SBIR awards

6. **Intellectual Property:** SBIR awardees retain significant rights in data and inventions developed during the project, with Government receiving specified license rights

7. **Performance Benchmarks:** Companies with failing Transition Rates are barred from Phase I submission for one year from June 1st assessment date

8. **Typical Phase I Awards:** NOAA expects to make up to 30 Phase I awards in a typical fiscal year

9. **Fraud Definitions:** Application includes detailed definitions of fraud, waste, and abuse specific to SBIR program, with reporting mechanisms to Department of Commerce OIG

10. **Proprietary Information:** Applicants must identify confidential commercial information and mark as "Privileged, Confidential, Commercial or Financial Information" to protect from FOIA disclosure
