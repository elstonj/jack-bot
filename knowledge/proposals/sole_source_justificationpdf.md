# Sole Source Justification for Ultra Maritime Subcontractor Selection

## Document Metadata
- Type: Sole Source Justification (regulatory/procurement document)
- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Topic N251-016, Phase II (Phase I Contract: N6833525C0492)
- Date: 2026-03-23
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0-Acoustic airframe
- Key Personnel: Paul DeMond (Ultra Maritime representative), Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This document justifies the selection of Ultra Maritime (UM) as the sole-source subcontractor for developing acoustic and Electric-field (E-field) sensor payloads on BST's Phase II Sonobuoy-Launched Unmanned Aerial System (SL UAS) effort. Ultra Maritime is the only known source with the required combination of mature acoustic hardware, Navy-validated E-field technology, and Phase I-integrated form factors necessary to meet Navy Size, Weight, and Power (SWaP) and acoustic sensitivity requirements.

## Technical Approach
The Phase II effort involves integrating specialized sensor payloads into BST's S0-Acoustic airframe. Ultra Maritime provides:

**Acoustic Sensor Architecture:**
- Modified Calibrated Omnidirectional (CO) sensor derived from UM's production Q53H sonobuoy
- Textured ceramic material technology that reduces self-noise and improves acoustic performance by 6-8 dB (proven in Digital Vertical Line Array 2.0 sonobuoy development)

**E-Field Sensing Architecture:**
- Low-frequency E-Field sensing capability (0.01 Hz – 5 Hz)
- Dual-band approach: Extremely Low Frequency Electromagnetic (ELFE) and Ultra Low Frequency (ULF) bands

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0-Acoustic Airframe:**
- BST's custom-engineered airframe designed specifically around UM's sensor architecture
- Features elastically-loaded belly compartment with cable compliance assembly
- Includes gravity-assisted deployment mechanism
- Integrated during Phase I with validated CAD models and precise physical/electrical specifications

**Ultra Maritime Sensors:**
- **Q53H Sonobuoy:** Production-grade acoustic sensor platform
- **Digital Vertical Line Array (DVLA) 2.0:** Advanced sonobuoy with ceramic-enhanced acoustic performance
- **E-Field Sensors:** PMA-264 validated technology for submarine detection

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Mission:** Air Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW) operations via sonobuoy-launched UAS
- **Navy Program:** Air Anti-Submarine Warfare Systems Program (PMA-264)
- **Detection Capability:** Submarine detection via acoustic and electromagnetic signatures

## Notable Details

**Unique Qualification Factors:**
- UM has direct, recent past performance on Navy PMA-264 programs
- Successfully demonstrated E-field sensors under two previous SBIR efforts:
  - **N091-019:** Multi-Modal Sensor for Tactical Marine Surveillance / EA Buoy (partner with Information Systems Lab)
  - **N192-060:** Multi-Sensor Sonobuoy / MSS (exclusive large business partner)

**Sole-Source Justification Basis:**
- UM provided precise physical dimensions, weight profiles, and electrical interface requirements used to design the S0-Acoustic airframe's complex mechanical systems
- Airframe is now validated and integrated to UM's specifications
- Switching vendors would require discarding Phase I CAD models and completely re-engineering the S0-Acoustic airframe
- Replacement would cause unacceptable program schedule delays and massive engineering duplication
- UM is "the only known source" possessing the specific combination of flight-ready Q53H/DVLA-derived hardware, PMA-264-validated E-field technology, and pre-integrated Phase I form factors

**Key Performance Requirements Met:**
- Navy's stringent SWaP (Size, Weight, Power) constraints
- Acoustic sensitivity thresholds
- Integration compatibility with existing Phase I design
