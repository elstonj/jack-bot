- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (BST); Carlos Montalvo, Ph.D. (University of South Alabama)

## Executive Summary
Dr. Carlos Montalvo from the University of South Alabama writes in support of BST's proposed S0 atmospheric sounding UAS, confirming institutional interest in the system for atmospheric science research. The letter validates the market need for a purpose-built atmospheric sounding platform and endorses the S0's suitability for their sea-breeze and bay-breeze convection research.

## Technical Approach
The S0 UAS was proposed as a purpose-built vehicle incorporating:
- Latest developments in wind velocity measurements
- Sensor-reactive control capabilities enabling complex mission profiles
- Compact form factor for easy deployment
- Data products captured in standardized formats for model assimilation
- Capability to obtain unique datasets unavailable through current technology

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0 UAS**
- Purpose-built for atmospheric sounding missions
- Can provide majority of necessary measurements for accurate atmospheric modeling
- Features compact, readily deployable form factor
- Executes complex mission profiles with sensor-reactive control
- Outputs standardized data formats compatible with existing atmospheric models
- Provides advanced capability not available through other current technologies

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Sea-breeze and bay-breeze convection analysis** in the South Alabama region
- Atmospheric data collection including:
  - Pressure
  - Temperature
  - Humidity
  - Wind speed as a function of altitude
- Feeding real-world observational data into atmospheric simulation models
- Supporting research platform for increased model fidelity

## Notable Details
- University of South Alabama's Mechanical Engineering and Earth Sciences departments expressed specific interest
