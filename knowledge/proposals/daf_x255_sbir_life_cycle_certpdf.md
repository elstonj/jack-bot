## Executive Summary
This is a standard federal compliance certification form required by all SBIR/STTR Phase I and Phase II awardees. It certifies that the awardee meets specific program requirements regarding personnel commitment, work performance percentages, funding duplication, location of work, and facility usage. BST would need to complete and sign this form as part of any SBIR award from the Air Force.

## Certification Requirements
The form requires verification and checkbox confirmation of the following eight compliance points:

1. **Principal Investigator/Project Manager Time Commitment**: PI/PM must spend more than 50% of time (based on 40-hour workweek) as an employee of the awardee, or obtain written deviation from Funding Agreement Officer.

2. **Essentially Equivalent Work**: Work must not have been submitted for funding elsewhere, or if submitted, must not have been funded under another grant/contract/subcontract without approval.

3. **Work Performance Percentage (Upon Completion)**: 
   - SBIR Phase I: at least 66⅔% of research
   - SBIR Phase II: at least 50% of research
   - STTR Phase I or II: at least 40% of research

4. **Work Completion Verification**: Awardee must verify same percentages as item 3 upon completion, or mark N/A if work not yet completed.

5. **Management Control (STTR only)**: Small Business Concern must exercise management direction and control (not applicable to SBIR).

6. **U.S. Performance Location**: R&D must be performed in the United States unless waiver granted.

7. **Facility & Personnel**: R&D must be performed at awardee's facilities by awardee's employees, except as noted in application and approved in award agreement.

8. **Notification Requirement**: Awardee must immediately notify agency if any funded work is subsequently funded by another Federal Agency.

9. **Information Sharing Acknowledgment**: Awardee understands information may be shared with Federal, State, and local agencies for law enforcement purposes.

## Legal Acknowledgment
The signatory certifies under penalty of law that all information is true and correct, and acknowledges potential criminal, civil, and administrative sanctions for misrepresentation, including:
