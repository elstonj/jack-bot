## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies, a Boulder-based developer of flight management systems and custom sensors for small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS), sought $240,000 in Colorado state funding to accelerate commercialization of a Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS developed in collaboration with University of Colorado Boulder and NASA. Current funding supports only technical development; the grant would enable expanded business and sales efforts. The company identified that these state funds could be leveraged for partial matching from NASA through the SBIR Phase II-E program, with the entire Colorado-based supply chain positioned to benefit from accelerated go-to-market.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed to commercialize an integrated sUAS platform combining three core technologies:

1. **Soil Moisture Sensor**: A miniaturized passive L-band microwave radiometer developed through collaboration with CU Boulder's Center for Environmental Technology (CET). Measures volumetric soil moisture at depths as low as 20 cm with 15-meter resolution.

2. **SuperSwift Airframe**: A robust, field-serviceable aircraft designed for scalable manufacturing and safety-approved operations, informed by 30+ years of combined operational experience with sUAS. System weight approximately 5 kg, designed for autonomous operations at 15 meters above ground.

3. **SwiftCore Flight Management System**: A fully integrated autopilot, ground station, and tablet-based user interface providing next-generation command and control for sUAS. Key innovation: uses commercial sensors with advanced control and estimation algorithms (maintained as trade secrets) to deliver high autonomy and usability with minimal user training required.

The grant was intended to fund two flight deployments over instrumented test sites (prototype system, then commercial-ready system) plus development of sales and marketing materials targeting scientific, government, and commercial markets.

### Competitive Positioning
BST positioned the Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS against three primary alternatives:
1. **In situ soil sensors**: Provide greater depth but are fixed, require infrastructure/maintenance; SMM sUAS offers flexibility and broader coverage
2. **Manned aircraft**: Expensive to operate, severely limited by airspace and scheduling; SMM sUAS offers lower cost and higher responsiveness
3. **Satellite systems (SMAP)**: Complementary rather than competitive; SMAP provides global coverage at 3 km resolution; SMM sUAS provides 15 m resolution for areas of high variability
