## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed developing a Soil Moisture Mapping Unmanned Aerial System (SMUAS)—an easy-to-operate UAS equipped with a highly sensitive passive L-band microwave radiometer—to measure volumetric soil moisture content at high spatial resolution for agricultural irrigation management. The system would operate at very low altitudes to achieve resolution far superior to satellite systems (NASA SMAP, ESA SMOS) while providing timely, plot-scale moisture data to optimize water usage and crop management.

## Technical Approach
- Integration of passive L-band microwave radiometer on unmanned aircraft platform
- Measurement principle based on upwelling microwave thermal emission from soil, analogous to satellite soil moisture sensors but with enhanced spatial resolution via low-altitude flight
- Regular operational flights over controlled agricultural test plots to refine data application and interpretation
- Demonstration of practical irrigation management use cases

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Soil Moisture Mapping Unmanned Aerial System (SMUAS)
- **What it is:** A simple-to-operate UAS equipped with a passive L-band microwave radiometer
- **Primary capability:** Measures volumetric soil moisture content over agricultural-plot-sized areas
- **Key advantage:** Much higher spatial resolution than satellite-based systems (SMAP, SMOS) due to close proximity to ground
- **Measurement method:** Passive sensing of upwelling microwave thermal emission from soil
- **Operational advantage:** Low cost and reliability enabling regular use by small farms and commercial agricultural operators

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary application:** Agricultural irrigation management and optimization
- **Specific benefit areas:**
  - Optimizing water usage and reducing water consumption
  - Energy savings (reduced pumping/irrigation costs)
  - Optimized plant management across crop lifecycle (germination through growth to harvest)
  - Mapping spatial soil moisture variability across fields

## Notable Details
- Addresses spatial and temporal variability of soil moisture (varies daily due to insolation, temperature, humidity, wind, precipitation, runoff, transpiration; varies spatially by soil type, terrain grade, and depth)
- Positioned as timely development given widespread commercial UAS adoption in agriculture
- Proposed approach leverages established satellite radiometry principles (SMAP, SMOS) adapted to UAS platform
- Collaborative team includes university and research center partners (Colorado State University, Center for Environmental Tech)
