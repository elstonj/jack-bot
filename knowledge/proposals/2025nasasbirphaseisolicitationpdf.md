## Executive Summary
This document is NASA's official Phase I SBIR solicitation for FY 2025, establishing requirements and procedures for small businesses to submit innovation proposals. The program provides up to $150,000 in non-dilutive funding for 6-month Phase I projects to demonstrate technical feasibility and commercial potential. Notable changes for 2025 include reduced maximum submissions per firm (10→5), reduced awards per firm (5→3), shortened technical proposal length (19→15 pages), and updated evaluation criteria.

## Program Description & Key Parameters

**Three-Phase Structure:**
- **Phase I**: Maximum $150,000, 6 months - demonstrate technical feasibility and NASA/commercial potential
- **Phase II**: Maximum $850,000, 24 months - advance R&D toward use in NASA missions or commercial markets
- **Phase III**: Sole-source follow-on contracts post-Phase I, funded by NASA or other customers

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- Must qualify as Small Business Concern (SBC) per SBA definition
- Combined with affiliates: ≤500 employees
- Principal Investigator must be primarily employed (>50% time on 40-hr workweek basis) with SBC at time of award and throughout project
- Awardee must perform ≥67% of effort; subcontractors/consultants limited to ≤33%
- Cannot be majority-owned by venture capital, hedge funds, or private equity firms
- Must register with SAM under NAICS code 541713 or 541715 as small business at proposal submission
- Must register with SBA's Company Registry and maintain active registration throughout contracting process
- All work must be performed in United States (American-made equipment/products required unless justified through Foreign Vendor Form)

**Foreign Involvement Restrictions:**
- Must complete "Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries" form
- Failure to disclose may result in award denial
- Must disclose: foreign talent recruitment program membership, foreign subsidiaries, foreign contractual obligations, foreign ownership status, foreign venture capital/institutional investment %, IP licensing to foreign countries, offshore/foreign-related entities
- Cannot work with countries on NASA Designated Country list
- NASA conducts security risk assessment based on cybersecurity practices, patent analysis, employee analysis, foreign ownership, and financial ties/obligations

## Submission Requirements & Format

**Technical Proposal Structure (15-page maximum, 1-inch margins, 10pt+ font):**
1. Identification and Significance of Innovation (3.5 pages suggested)
2. Technical Objectives (1 page)
3. Work Plan (3 pages)
4. Related R&D/R&R&D (1 page)
5. Key Personnel and Bibliography (2.5 pages)
6. Market Opportunity (1 page)
7. Facilities/Equipment (1 page)
8. Subcontractors and Consultants (1 page)
9. Related, Essentially Equivalent, and Duplicate Proposals (1 page)

**Complete Proposal Package Must Include:**
- Proposal Contact Information Form
- Proposal Certifications Form (electronically endorsed)
- Proposal Summary Form (public information)
- Proposal Budget Form (maximum $150,000 base or $156,500 if requesting TABA)
- Technical Proposal (15 pages max)
- Briefing Chart (must not contain proprietary data)
- NASA Evaluation License Application (if using NASA Technology Available)
- TABA Request Form (optional - up to $6,500 supplement)
- I-Corps Interest Form
- SBC-Level Forms: Firm Information, Firm Certifications, Audit Information, Foreign Affiliations Disclosures, Prior Awards Addendum, Commercial Metrics Report
- Electronic endorsement by designated SBC representative and PI before deadline

**Submission Method:** ProSAMS (Proposal Submissions and Award Management System) - https://prosams.nasa.gov

**Deadline:** March 10, 2025, 5:00 p.m. Eastern Time

**Critical Restrictions:**
- Maximum 5 proposal submissions per firm (reduced from 10)
- Maximum 3 awards per firm (reduced from 5)
- No human/animal subjects research allowed in Phase I
- No classified information allowed
- Proposals exceeding page limits will be declined
- No letters of support, technical papers, product samples, videos, PowerPoint decks, or ancillary items will be considered

## Budget & Funding

- **Phase I Maximum Contract Value:** $150,000
- **Optional TABA Supplement:** Up to $6,500 (total not to exceed $156,500)
- **TABA Approved Uses:** Product sales assistance, IP protection, market research/validation, regulatory/manufacturing plan development, access to technical/business literature databases, access to non-NASA scientist/engineer networks
- **No Cost Sharing Required**
- **Profit/Fee:** Not specified in extracted text
- **Payment:** NASA uses Department of Treasury Invoice Processing Platform (IPP) for invoicing

## Technical & Compliance Standards

**Required Compliance Areas:**
- Software development projects subject to NASA Procedural Requirements (NPR) 7150.2D if applicable
- Flight safety requirements per NPR 8079.1 if delivering spacecraft
- Commercial Air Service (CAS) and NASA Airworthiness reviews required for flight activities (may preclude Phase I due to short timeline)
- Personal Identity Verification (PIV) credentials required if award involves 6+ consecutive months access to federally controlled facilities or federal information systems

**IP & Rights:**
- Rights in data developed under SBIR funding remain with government and awardee per 5.7
- Copyrights addressed in section 5.8
- Invention reporting and patent filing requirements per section 5.9
- Government-furnished property and contractor-acquired property addressed in section 5.10

## NASA Technology Available (TAV) for SBIR Use

- NASA offers >1,400 patents available for licensing (sensors, materials focus)
- >1,000 software applications/tools available through NASA Technology Transfer Portal (http://technology.nasa.gov)
- **Software Usage:** Nonexclusive, royalty-free Software Usage Agreement (SUA) required; clause 48 C.F.R. 1852.227-88 applies
- **Patents:** Nonexclusive, royalty-free evaluation license required prior to proposal submission via NASA Automated Licensing System (ATLAS)
- Inventor knowledge transfer available on non-interference basis (may require NDA or Space Act Agreement; reimbursement possible; not recommended for Phase I due to approval timeline)
- Use of NASA technology is not a selection factor

## Support Programs

**I-Corps™ (Innovation Corps):**
- Partnership with National Science Foundation (NSF)
- Optional program for Phase I awardees
- Enables customer discovery and business plan development
- Up to $10,000 funding for shortened I-Corps version
- Awards made separately via Phase I contract modification
- Interest form required with proposal (section 3.1.3.9)

**Technical and Business Assistance (TABA):**
- Optional supplement up to $6,500 Phase I (up to $50,000 Phase II)
- For contracting with external vendors for: technical decision-making, problem-solving, risk minimization, product development/commercialization, business services
- Approved vendors: commercialization assistance providers, business development services, public-private partnerships, entrepreneurial support organizations, IP/licensing attorneys
- TABA funds cannot be used for internal activities
- Awardees must deliver description of services and results at contract completion
- NASA does not guarantee approval

**SBA Support Resources:**
- Local SBA partnerships available for training and support
- Find assistance at https://www.sbir.gov/local-assistance
- SBA federal resources at https://www.sbir.gov/resources

## Registrations Required Before Submission

1. **SAM (System for Award Management):**
   - Must have active registration under NAICS 541713 or 541715 as small business at proposal submission
   - Must obtain Unique Entity Identifier (UEI)
   - Must obtain Commercial and Government Entity (CAGE) code
   - Must list "All Awards" as purpose of registration
   - Registration must remain active through contract performance
   - Information at https://sam.gov/
   - Phone: 866-606-8220

2. **SBA Company Registry:**
   - Must register and update commercialization status
   - Required SBC Control ID from this registration
   - Must upload PDF copy of registration with Firm Certification Form
   - Registration at https://www.sbir.gov/registration

3. **ProSAMS (Proposal Submissions and Award Management System):**
   - Required for all proposal submissions
   - Requires firm registration and login
   - Secure submission portal: https://prosams.nasa.gov/

## Notable Details & Notable Changes for 2025

**Program Maturity Benchmark:**
- Companies with 21+ prior Phase I awards must meet performance benchmarks to continue participation
- Benchmarks ensure progress toward commercialization
- SBA notifies companies failing benchmarks
- Reference: https://www.sbir.gov/performance-benchmarks

**Changes from Prior Years:**
1. Maximum submissions reduced from 10 to 5 per firm
2. Maximum awards reduced from 5 to 3 per firm
3. Technical proposal length reduced from 19 to 15 pages
4. Table of contents no longer required
5. Technical evaluation criteria and scoring/weighting updated (Appendix A)
6. NAICS code 541713 or 541715 registration required at proposal submission (not selection)

**Key Program Features:**
- Equity-free funding (no equity stake taken by NASA)
- Seed-stage R&D focus
- Non-dilutive capital source
- Path to Phase II, Phase III, and Post-Phase
