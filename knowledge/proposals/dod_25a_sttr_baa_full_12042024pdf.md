# DoD 25.A STTR BAA Full Announcement

## Document Metadata
- Type: Broad Agency Announcement (BAA)
- Client/Agency: Department of Defense (DoD)
- Program/Solicitation: STTR 25.A Annual Program (Small Business Technology Transfer)
- Date: December 2024 (Topics Pre-release; open January 8, 2025; close February 5, 2025)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None mentioned in this document
- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is the DoD's annual STTR Program BAA soliciting Phase I proposals from small business concerns in partnership with research institutions. The program aims to stimulate technological innovation, strengthen small business participation in DoD R&D, and encourage commercialization of research results. Proposals are due February 5, 2025, with evaluation based on scientific/technical merit and commercialization potential.

## Program Structure
**Three-Phase Program:**
- **Phase I**: 6-12 months; $150K-$250K typical range (not specified in this document); proves scientific/technical feasibility and commercial merit
- **Phase II**: 24 months; continues and develops Phase I results; made to SBCs based on Phase I results and Phase II proposal merit
- **Phase III**: Commercialization phase using non-STTR funding (private sector, non-STTR federal sources)

## Eligibility Requirements
- Small Business Concern (SBC) must qualify per 13 CFR §§ 701-705
- **Minimum work allocation**: SBC performs minimum 40% of project; single research institution performs minimum 30% (measured by direct and indirect costs)
- **Principal Investigator**: Primary employment (>50% time) must be with SBC or research institution at time of award and during conduct
- **Performance location**: All R&D work conducted in the United States
- **Joint Ventures/Limited Partnerships**: Permitted if entity qualifies as small business; must disclose foreign relationships
- **IP Rights**: Mandatory written agreement between SBC and research institution allocating intellectual property and follow-on research rights
- **Prior Awards**: Experienced firms must meet performance benchmarks (Phase I-to-Phase II transition rate and commercialization benchmark per SBIR/STTR Extension Act of 2022)

## Key Program Requirements

### Critical Technology Areas Aligned with OUSD(R&E)
Program topics align with DoD's critical technology areas including:
- FutureG, Trusted AI and Autonomy, Biotechnology
- Advanced Computing and Software, Integrated Sensing and Cyber
- Directed Energy, Hypersonics, Microelectronics
- Quantum Science, Space Technology, Renewable Energy
- Advanced Materials, Human-Machine Interfaces
- Plus mission-specific areas (Combat Casualty Care, Nuclear, Sustainment & Logistics, etc.)

### Proposal Structure (DSIP Submission)
- **Volume 1**: Proposal Cover Sheet (technical abstract and commercial benefits; max 3,000 characters each)
- **Volume 2**: Technical Volume (single PDF; specific page limits per Service/Component)
- **Volume 3**: Cost Volume (DSIP form; detailed labor, materials, equipment, travel)
- **Volume 4**: Company Commercialization Report (CCR from SBIR.gov if prior awards exist)
- **Volume 5**: Supporting Documents (letters of support, additional cost info, technical data rights, etc.)
- **Volume 6**: Fraud, Waste and Abuse Training
- **Volume 7**: Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries

### Technical Volume Content (Phase I)
1. Identification and Significance of Problem/Opportunity
2. Phase I Technical Objectives
3. Phase I Statement of Work
4. Related Work
5. Relationship with Future R&D
6. Commercialization Strategy
7. Key Personnel
8. Foreign Citizens
9. Facilities/Equipment
10. Subcontractors/Consultants
11. Prior, Current, or Pending Support
12. Restrictions on Government Use/Disclosure of Technical Data

### Cost Proposal Requirements
- Detail all key personnel with individual hours and labor rates
- Equipment/materials inclusion carefully reviewed for appropriateness
- Travel costs must be justified and project-related
- Cost sharing permitted but not required; not an evaluation factor
- Subcontractor/consultant costs detailed at same level as prime contractor
- If awarded, SBC must submit substantiation to contracting officer (cost estimates for equipment, materials, consultants)

## Certifications and Registrations Required
- **SAM Registration**: Generates Unique Entity ID (UEI) and CAGE code
- **SBA Company Registry**: Must register and provide SBC Control ID and Proof of Registration during proposal submission
- **DSIP Registration**: Individual user accounts authenticated by Login.gov; Firm Admin creates Firm PIN and controls access
- **Required Certifications**: 
  - Small Business status (size, ownership, control per 13 CFR § 121.702)
  - Certifications required at Phase I award, prior to Phase I final payment, at Phase II award, prior to 50% Phase II receipt, and prior to Phase II final payment
  - SBIR/STTR Extension Act of 2022 certifications

## Foreign Ownership, Control, and Influence (FOCI) & National Security Review
- DoD conducts risk-based due diligence per 15 U.S.C. §638(vv) assessing:
  - Cybersecurity practices
  - Patent analysis
  - Employee analysis and foreign ownership/financial ties/obligations
  - Ties to foreign countries/persons/entities
  - Open-source analysis to confirm accuracy and detect undisclosed information
- May request true copies of contractual/financial agreements with foreign state-owned or foreign-controlled enterprises from prior 5 years
- **Ineligibility**: Proposals posing unacceptable national security risk not eligible regardless of technical merit
- FOCI Training: Free online course available through Project Spectrum (projectspectrum.io)
- **VC/PE/Hedge Fund Restrictions**: Multiple VC/PE/hedge fund majority-owned SBCs ineligible unless Service/Component authorizes; if authorized, must disclose foreign nationals/entities holding >5% equity and ultimate parent owners

## Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI)
- FAR 9.5 compliance required; proposing SBC must identify and disclose all facts relevant to potential OCIs
- Must include OCI mitigation plan describing actions taken/intended to prevent conflicting roles and unfair competitive advantage
- **DoD Supplemental Policy**: Contractors cannot concurrently provide SETA (Scientific Engineering Technical Assistance), A&AS (Advisory and Assistance Services), or similar support AND be technical performer
- Proposing SBC must affirm whether providing SETA/A&AS to any DoD Service/Component under current award or past award ending within one calendar year of proposal submission
- If SETA/A&AS provided, proposal must include: DoD office name, prime contract number, team member identification, and OCI mitigation plan
- Government evaluates OCI mitigation plans only for selectable proposals; may require additional information
- Failed disclosure, failure to obtain waiver, or failure to reasonably provide requested information may result in proposal rejection

## Human Subjects and Animal Research Requirements
- **Human Subjects Research**: 
  - Must comply with federal/state laws and agency policy (32 CFR 219)
  - Institutions must provide federal assurance of compliance (e.g., HHS OHRP assurance)
  - Personnel must have completed appropriate training
  - Proposals meeting exemption criteria (32 CFR 219.101) not required to have federal assurance
  - IRB approval required (or exemption determination) prior to research conduct; average approval 1-3 months
  - DoD review (3-6 months) required after IRB approval
  - NO funding for human subject research until ALL approvals granted
  
- **Animal Subjects Research**:
  - Must comply with federal/agency animal acquisition, transport, care, handling, use rules
  - IACUC approval required
  - Secondary/headquarters-level approval from DoD veterinarian required
  - No animal research may be conducted using DoD funding until appropriate DoD office approvals granted

- **Recombinant DNA**: Institutional biosafety committee approval required

## Evaluation Criteria
- Scientific merit
- Technical merit
- Commercialization potential
- Risk-based due diligence security review
- Funds availability

### Intellectual Property & Data Rights
- SBC and research institution must negotiate written agreement allocating IP rights and follow-on research/commercialization rights
- Model Agreement available (referenced but not included)
- Technical data rights provisions apply (FAR clauses)
- Restrictive marking permitted for proprietary data; unmarked data assumed non-proprietary

### Performance Benchmarks for Experienced Firms
Per SBIR/STTR Extension Act of 2022, firms with multiple prior awards must meet:
- **Phase I-to-Phase II Transition Rate**: Extent SBC progresses Phase I projects to Phase II
- **Commercialization Benchmark**: Extent SBC moves Phase II work toward commercialization
- **Consequence of Failure**: Companies failing either benchmark limited to 80 total Phase I and Direct-to-Phase II awards across DoD:
  - Army: 20 awards
  - Navy: 20 awards
  - Air Force: 20 awards
  - All other DoD Components combined: 20 awards

### Special Programs
- **Phase II Enhancement Policy**: Available from certain DoD Services/Components; allows additional Phase II funding if SBC matches with non-SBIR/STTR DoD or private sector funds
- **Commercialization Readiness Program (CRP)**: Each Military Department (Army, Navy, Air Force) has CRP; check Service/Component instructions
- **OSD Transitions SBIR/STTR Technology (OTST) Pilot Program**: Interim Phase II technology maturity phase available; contact osd.ncr.ousd-r-e.mbx.sbir-sttr-tech-transition@mail.mil
- **Technical and Business Assistance (TABA)**: Discretionary; DoD has not mandated; check Service/Component-specific instructions for availability

### Open Topics
- Generalized mission requirements or specific technology areas for adapting commercial products to close capability gaps
- One proposal per open topic per SBC maximum; subsequent submissions will have prior versions marked nonresponsive
- Instructions may vary significantly by Service/Component

### State Support
Many states have SBIR/STTR support offices offering information, technical assistance, matching funds, and Phase III funding assistance

### Export Control & Classified Work
- **ITAR/EAR Topics**: Require certified DD Form 2345 (Militarily Critical Technical Data Agreement) or application evidence
- **Classified Proposals**: NOT accepted; Phase II classified work requires proposing SBC to have facility clearance (contact DCSA at https://www.dcsa.mil/mc/ctp/fc/)

### Due Diligence & Security
- All awards subject to risk-based due diligence security review
- DoD may assess using open-source analysis and analytical tools
- If OCI disclosure deemed insufficient or waiver not obtained, proposal may be rejected and withdrawn from consideration regardless of technical merit

### Fraud Warnings
- False/fraudulent statements punishable under 18 U.S.C. § 1001 (up to $10,000 fine and/or 5 years imprisonment)
- DoD Office of Inspector General Defense Hotline: (800) 424-9098 (8 a.m.-5 p.m. ET) or hotline@dodig.mil

### Contract Administration & Reporting
- DoD assumes no liability for disclosure of unmarked data
- Support contractors handle proposals for administrative purposes only; bound by non-disclosure agreements
- Final Technical Reports required for Phase I, II, and III
- Patent and invention reporting required
- Copyrights: Government retains certain rights
- Agency Recovery Authority: ongoing reporting requirements during award life cycle
