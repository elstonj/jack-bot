## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies requested $240,000 in grant funding to accelerate commercialization of a soil moisture mapping small unmanned aircraft system and enabling technologies. The company projected this investment would generate $5.6M in additional revenue over 3 years and create 6 new jobs in Colorado, building on existing NASA Phase II SBIR funding and a strong track record of advanced technology development and flight operations.

## Technical Approach
BST's strategy involved three phases:
1. **Develop**: Transition advanced technology from CU Boulder researchers; design modular aircraft enabling multiple missions
2. **Test**: Conduct flight testing of integrated system; collect key performance data
3. **Commercialize**: Hire key team members; fund collaboration with thought leaders; assemble and distribute documentation to early customers

The core technology stack combined consumer electronics, advanced algorithms, and the SwiftCore Flight Management System to create the Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS platform.
