## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to modify its existing S0 UAS platform into a purpose-built atmospheric sounding vehicle for Air Force weather modeling and forecasting. Phase I focuses on establishing Air Force customer needs for high-resolution atmospheric thermodynamic measurements, developing a detailed modification plan for the S0, and documenting integration pathways into Air Force weather operations. The system would provide in situ vertical atmospheric profiles with standardized data products and advanced sensor-reactive control algorithms.

## Technical Approach

**Core Concept:** Adapt the S0 UAS (originally designed for hurricane deployment) into a dedicated atmospheric sounding platform by:
- Modifying tablet control software to enable sampling mission command and live data display tailored to Air Force operations
- Upgrading wireless command/control and telemetry links (leveraging SwiftCore's modular avionics architecture for rapid radio integration)
- Integrating advanced atmospheric sensors, starting with existing meteorological package (pressure, temperature, humidity, 3D wind via 5-hole probe)
- Developing control algorithms for sensor-reactive missions that focus sampling on areas of interest
- Delivering data in standardized formats for direct assimilation into Air Force weather models

**Key Technical Advantage:** The S0 features wind velocity measurement capabilities (latest developments in this area) that enable comprehensive thermodynamic profiling while maintaining compact form factor.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS
- **What it is:** Fixed-wing small UAS originally designed for deployment into hurricanes; features advanced guidance algorithms for maintaining flight through high-data-value regions of storms
- **Specifications/Performance:**
  - 2-hour cruise time
  - Cost: $5,000 per aircraft
  - Reusable/recoverable platform
  - Capable of operating in high wind environments and hazardous atmospheric conditions
  - Integrates 3D wind measurement (5-hole probe), pressure, temperature, humidity sensors
  - All U.S.-made with BST-developed autopilot and firmware/software
- **Proposed Use:** Atmospheric profiling for Air Force weather operations; capable of vertical profile measurements and boundary layer sampling

### SwiftCore Flight Management System / Avionics
- **What it is:** BST's autopilot system serving as the avionics architecture foundation
- **Capabilities:** Modular design allowing rapid integration of new communication radios; supports tablet-based mission control interface; can enable real-time data feeds to Air Force systems
- **Proposed Enhancement:** Modified control software for Air Force-specific sampling mission profiles and live data display

## Use Cases & Applications

1. **Air Force Weather Operations:** High-resolution atmospheric boundary layer measurements to improve local-scale weather forecasting and modeling
2. **Nowcast/Forecast Optimization:** Sensor-reactive control allows vehicle to autonomously focus sampling on measurement-rich areas, improving data value for forecasters
3. **Severe Convective Storm Monitoring:** Building on S0's existing hurricane/severe weather capability
4. **Boundary Layer Profiling:** Simultaneous multi-vehicle deployment capability (mentioned in Elston's experience with vertically stacked formation flying)
5. **Alternative to Balloon Radiosondes:** Potential market for weather balloon-launched version; 800+ weather stations worldwide currently use non-recoverable balloon-borne radiosondes twice daily

## Phase I Technical Objectives

1. Establish specific Air Force customer needs for high-resolution atmospheric thermodynamic measurements and integration into existing weather models
2. Develop detailed modification plan for S0 platform addressing aircraft changes, instruments, CONOPS, and data structures
3. Perform detailed study on S0 integration into Air Force weather monitoring operations
4. Document all changes and corresponding Phase II development plan

   - Wireless command/control and telemetry upgrades

5. Deliver final report with Phase II continuation plan

## Commercialization Strategy

**Primary Market:** U.S. Air Force, initially targeting low-altitude atmospheric boundary layer measurement users

**Market Advantages:**
- Price point ($5,000 per aircraft) significantly below standalone 3D wind sensors (Aeroprobe) that must be integrated with UAS and thermodynamic sensors
- Reusability vs. expendable balloon radiosondes (cost savings over time for ~800 weather stations using twice-daily soundings)
- Capability to operate in difficult atmospheric conditions (hurricane-tested platform)
- 2-hour cruise time
- All U.S. manufacturing (airframe, autopilot, firmware, software)

**Secondary Markets:**
- NOAA and National Weather Service
- Meteorological community requiring boundary layer atmospheric soundings
