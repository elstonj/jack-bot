### SBIR Program Requirements Incorporated
The contract includes standard DoD SBIR Phase I requirements:
- Minimum 2/3 of R&D effort must be performed by BST (small business concern)
- All R&D work must be performed in the United States (unless specifically approved by Government)
- Primary employment requirement: Principal Investigator/Project Manager must have primary employment with BST
- American-made equipment preference for purchases
- SBA certification of firm size, ownership, and SBIR program compliance required with final payment
- Acknowledgment of Air Force Research Laboratory sponsorship required in any public releases

### Regulatory/Compliance Requirements
The contract incorporates extensive FAR and DFARS clauses addressing:
- Export control compliance (ITAR Category XI - Military Electronics)
- Cybersecurity safeguarding of covered defense information
- Prohibition on certain telecommunications equipment (Huawei, ZTE, etc.)
- Patent rights reporting and invention disclosure
- Security clearance requirements for facility/network access (U.S. citizens required for AFRL base/IT network access)
- Communications security (COMSEC) monitoring procedures
- Buy American provisions
- Whistleblower protections
- Equal opportunity and employment compliance

### Key Restriction
**Access to AFRL Facilities:** Any BST employee requiring access to USAF bases, AFRL facilities, or U.S. Government IT networks must be a U.S. citizen. Permanent resident status does not satisfy this requirement unless specifically waived in writing by the Contracting Officer.

### Reporting Requirements
Invention reports must be submitted to: **sbrk.sprints@us.af.mil**
- Include contract number and "Invention Reporting" in subject line
- Use DD Form 882 (Report of Invention and Subcontracts) recommended
- Timeline: Within 2 months of inventor disclosure for each subject invention
