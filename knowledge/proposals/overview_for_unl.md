## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed a UAS-based soil moisture measurement system for NASA's 2012 SBIR program. The system combines the Tempest airframe and SwiftPilot control system with a novel lightweight L-band radiometer (LDCR) to provide high-resolution soil moisture data suitable for calibration/validation of satellite sensors and operational deployment in areas where space-based sensors have footprint limitations.

## Technical Approach
- **Platform:** Tempest airframe (proven performance in diverse weather/atmospheric conditions) equipped with SwiftPilot flight control
- **Sensor:** Ultra-Lightweight L-Band Digital Lobe-Differencing Correlation Radiometer (LDCR) with:
  - Completely digital backend designed to operate in RFI-rich environments
  - Latest proven digital RFI mitigation techniques
  - Modest size and lightweight component requirements for UAS integration
- **Ground Control:** Android Tablet-based GUI with ruggedized ground station for intuitive operation
- **Data Processing:** Standard kriging methods to generate soil moisture maps; GIS layer visualization for end-user display

## Products & Capabilities Described

### LDCR (Ultra-Lightweight L-Band Digital Lobe-Differencing Correlation Radiometer)
- **What it is:** Lightweight, digital L-band radiometer designed for UAS deployment
- **Proposed use:** Soil moisture measurement at field scale
- **Key specifications:**
  - Achieves volumetric soil moisture accuracy within 5%-10%
  - ~15-meter diameter sensor footprints (geo-tagged)
  - High-resolution product for areas with space sensor footprint limitations
  - Inexpensive to manufacture and operate (no manned aircraft required)

### SwiftPilot
- Autopilot/flight control system enabling required mission control

### Tempest
- Airframe proven in diverse weather and atmospheric conditions

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary mission:** Soil moisture mapping and measurement
- **Satellite validation:** Data product suitable for calibration and validation of EOS Aqua Satellite
- **Operational deployment:** Long-term and operational soil moisture monitoring
- **Data utility:** High-resolution soil moisture mapping in areas where planned space sensors encounter footprint limitations

## Notable Details
- **Competitive advantages:** 
  - Lightweight, inexpensive sensor suitable for long-term operational deployment
  - Digital RFI mitigation enabling operation in interference-rich environments
  - Higher spatial resolution than satellite sensors in target areas
  - Simple, intuitive ground control interface
- **Data output:** Geo-tagged soil moisture measurements processed into GIS-compatible map layers
