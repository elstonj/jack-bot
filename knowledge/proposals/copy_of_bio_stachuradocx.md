## Executive Summary
Dr. Maciej Stachura is the Chief Technology Officer of Black Swift Technologies with extensive expertise in UAS operations, cooperative multi-aircraft systems, and autonomous sensing. He brings over 300 flight experiments, advanced FAA certifications, and NASA-level flight safety expertise to BST.

## Professional Background & Credentials
- **Education:** M.S. and Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering, University of Colorado Boulder
- **Current Role:** Chief Technology Officer, Black Swift Technologies
- **Co-founder:** Black Swift Technologies (pre-founding work detailed below)

## Technical Experience & Expertise

### UAS Flight Operations & Regulatory
- Conducted over 300 flight experiments
- Secured and maintained over 140 FAA Certificates of Authorization (COA) covering Colorado, Kansas, and Nebraska
- Led completion of NASA Airworthiness Flight Safety Review Board (AFSRB) and Flight Readiness Review Board processes for UAS operations in National Airspace System

### Notable Flight Campaigns & Research
- **VORTEX2 Field Campaign:** First-ever intercept of a tornadic supercell thunderstorm using UAS
- **Multi-aircraft cooperative flight experiments:** Extensive research in coordinated UAS operations
- **Soil Moisture Mapping UAS:** Principal Investigator for NASA Phase I and Phase II SBIR projects focused on development, testing, and commercialization

### Academic Research Focus
- **Doctoral Work:** Designing cooperative control algorithms for multi-UAS experiments combining sensing and communication into unified framework
- Successfully demonstrated and evaluated cooperative control algorithms through multi-UAS flight experiments
- Published research on communication-aware information gathering and cooperative planning for UAS sensor networks
