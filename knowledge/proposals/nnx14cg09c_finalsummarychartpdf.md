## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies completed Phase II-E development of a soil moisture mapping small unmanned aircraft system (sUAS) focused on mitigating electromagnetic interference (EMI) that affected radiometer payload performance. The team successfully designed EMI shielding into the SuperSwift airframe to replace ad-hoc Phase II solutions, while also securing FAA Certificates of Authorization and making structural improvements to reduce cost and weight.

## Technical Approach

### EMI Mitigation Challenge & Solution
- **Problem Identified**: Electric motor, speed controller, and battery in the propulsion system produced significant noise at L-band spectrum, creating unexpected EMI that degraded soil moisture measurements
- **Phase II Solution**: Ad-hoc modifications and mitigation measures (labor-intensive, difficult to maintain)
- **Phase II-E Objective**: Integrate EMI improvements permanently into aircraft design

### Key Technical Achievements
1. Characterized noise signature of the SuperSwift airframe
2. Designed, built, and tested payload EMI cases
3. Designed, built, and tested airframe EMI shielding
4. Upgraded structures and nose cone for increased robustness and cost reduction
5. Modified airframe to improve flight characteristics
6. Enhanced high-rate data logger to achieve 80 MS/s sampling rate (third Nyquist zone of sensor)
7. Redesigned tail for improved handling and tracking
8. Tested alternative launch methods
9. Refined manufacturing and structures to reduce weight and cost

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift Aircraft
- Purpose: Primary platform for SMM sUAS
- EMI characteristics: Propulsion components produce significant L-band noise
- Phase II-E improvements: 
  - EMI shielding design (payload and airframe)
  - New tail design for improved handling
  - New propeller for increased flight time
  - Structural refinements for reduced weight and cost
  - FAA Certificates of Authorization obtained through NASA

### Soil Moisture Mapping (SMM) sUAS
- Primary function: Soil moisture measurements via L-band radiometer payload
- Payload components: Radiometer antenna, high-rate data logger, RF sensors
- Capability status: Validated through flight testing with significantly reduced EMI noise compared to Phase II

## Use Cases & Applications

### Current/Planned Missions
1. **Drought Monitoring Campaign** (Summer 2017): Long-duration flight campaign over farmland in Eastern Colorado with University of Colorado partnership
2. **Precision Agriculture**: Commercial partnership to utilize SMM sUAS for water usage reduction on farms
3. **Ocean Salinity Mapping**: Potential application near coastlines and estuaries
4. **SMAP Calibration Activities**: Consideration for involvement with NASA's SMAP (Soil Moisture Active Passive) mission

## Key Results

### EMI Performance
- Demonstrated significant reduction in EMI noise in payload sensor through combined payload component shielding and airframe RF emitter shielding
- Validated designs through flight testing

### System Improvements
- Increased flight time (new propeller)
- Improved handling and tracking (new tail design)
- Reduced manufacturing cost and weight
- Robust, maintainable design (versus ad-hoc Phase II approach)

### Regulatory Status
- Successfully obtained FAA Certificates of Authorization through NASA for SuperSwift aircraft
