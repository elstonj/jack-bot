## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes ABLGrid, a networked tethersonde system for distributed atmospheric measurements within the atmospheric boundary layer (ABL). The system addresses the critical need for hyper-local, in-situ weather observations to support both recreational and commercial UAS operations, as well as broader weather forecasting and nowcasting applications.

## Technical Approach
The ABLGrid tethersonde is based on NASA-licensed AeroPod design, modified from BST's SwiftFlow probe. Key technical elements include:
- **Sensor Suite**: Temperature, relative humidity (RH), pressure, and 3D wind measurements
- **Active Control Design**: Completed during Phase I for stability and functionality
- **Radio/Wireless Network**: Custom radio link design enabling data dissemination between networked tethersondes and central location
- **Deployment Method**: Tether-suspended from balloon for atmospheric profiling
- **Data System**: Networked architecture supporting real-time data aggregation for nowcasts and forecasts

## Products & Capabilities Described

### ABLGrid Tethersonde System
- **What it is**: A networked atmospheric measurement platform combining a tethersonde probe with balloon-tether deployment and wireless data network
- **Proposed Use**: Distributed deployment for hyper-local atmospheric monitoring; addresses gap left by UAS weather limitations and lack of fine-scale forecasting
- **Key Specifications**: Full ABL sensor suite (temp, RH, pressure, 3D wind); active control; wireless network topology; ease of deployment with fewer operational restrictions than UAS
- **TRL Level**: Estimated advancement through Phase I development

### SwiftFlow Probe (as basis)
- Serves as foundation design for tethersonde adaptation
- Provides measurement capabilities

## Use Cases & Applications

### NASA Applications
- Planetary boundary layer (PBL) measurements, identified as key target in 2017 National Academies Earth Science Decadal Survey
- Enables low-cost, easily deployable alternative to UAS with fewer operational restrictions

### Non-NASA Applications
- **Weather Forecasting**: Local airports, citizen scientists, educational groups
- **Government**: Local, state, and federal entities requiring weather data
- **Industries**: Precision agriculture, transportation (applications relying on hyper-local weather products)
