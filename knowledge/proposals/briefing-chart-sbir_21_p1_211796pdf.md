## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to design and build a gondola-launched fixed-wing glider capable of operating in Venus's lower atmosphere beneath the cloud layers. The glider would complement balloon-based atmospheric measurements by providing high-speed (50-70 m/s) station-keeping capability and surface reconnaissance via near-infrared imaging, overcoming the mobility limitations of balloon platforms while remaining small and light enough to serve as a low-risk mission component.

## Technical Approach

**Core Concept:**
- Small, fixed-wing glider deployed from a high-altitude balloon gondola
- Designed for sustained operations in Venus lower atmosphere (beneath cloud layers)
- High-speed cruise (50-70 m/s) enables station keeping with upper atmosphere balloon
- Can carry scientific payload for in situ measurements and remote sensing

**Key Technical Tasks:**
1. Vehicle aerodynamic design optimized for Venus atmospheric conditions
2. Aircraft construction materials identification for high-temperature environment
3. Sensor and avionics selection for harsh Venus conditions
4. Relative navigation system design for station keeping with balloon
5. Candidate system simulation to demonstrate concept-of-operation
6. Commercial potential and product viability assessment

**Navigation & Control:**
- Development of sensors and control algorithms to maintain station-keeping position relative to the balloon
- Relative navigation system to enable coordinated operations with balloon platform

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Venus Lower Atmosphere Glider:**
- **What it is:** Small fixed-wing aircraft designed for deployment from balloon gondola
- **Proposed use:** Complement balloon measurements; image Venus surface through near-infrared wavelengths; conduct in situ experiments in lower atmosphere
- **Key specifications:**
  - Cruise speed: 50-70 m/s
  - Small enough to fit in aeroshell voids (enabling carriage on missions without dedicated balloon payload)
  - Weight and size constrained by payload integration requirements
  - Must operate in high-temperature environment with minimal power budget
- **Sensors:** Remote observing sensors (details to be selected) for surface imaging at near-infrared wavelengths; in situ atmospheric sensors (TBD)

## Use Cases & Applications

**NASA Applications:**
- Venus atmospheric science missions with balloon platforms
- Technology demonstrator providing low-risk, high-reward science component
- Carriage in aeroshell voids on Venus missions (even those without dedicated balloon payload)
- Aerial reconnaissance of Venus surface beneath clouds

**Non-NASA Applications:**
- NOAA hurricane observation and monitoring (balloon-borne platform with station-keeping glider)
- Severe convective storm research and monitoring
- Severe weather research platforms requiring persistent atmospheric presence

## Key Milestones
1. Complete initial material selection for aircraft construction
2. Complete aircraft aerodynamic design and scientific payload selection
3. Complete sensor selection and develop control algorithm for station keeping
4. Demonstrate desired concept-of-operation in simulation

## Notable Details

- **Innovation driver:** Venus surface imaging in near-infrared has never been achieved due to thick cloud layers; gliders overcome balloon mobility limitations while remaining small enough to integrate into existing mission architecture
- **Risk mitigation:** Small, light vehicle design allows deployment as secondary payload with minimal mission penalty
- **Dual-use potential:** Platform designed for Venus but explicitly noted for NOAA and severe weather applications on Earth
