# CO-WY Engine Translation Grant Program Solicitation

- Type: Grant solicitation / funding opportunity announcement
- Client/Agency: Colorado-Wyoming Climate Resilience Engine (CO-WY Engine), funded by National Science Foundation (NSF)
- Program/Solicitation: NSF Regional Innovation Engines - Translation Grant Program

- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None mentioned
- Key Personnel: Mark Gorham (Program Contact, mark@innosphere.org)

## Executive Summary
The Colorado-Wyoming Climate Resilience Engine is soliciting proposals from startup companies to commercialize innovations addressing climate resilience challenges in Colorado and Wyoming. The Translation Grant Program will provide $250K-$750K per project (1-3 awards anticipated) to support startups in advancing their technologies toward market deployment, with a focus on four core use-case areas: Wildfire Preparedness and Response, Soil Health, Water Quality and Availability, and Air Quality.

## Program Overview
The CO-WY Engine is one of ten inaugural NSF Regional Innovation Engines awardees, with $15M initial funding and potential for up to $160M over ten years. The Engine's organizing framework is "Advanced Sensing and Computation for Environmental Decision Making" (ASCEND), which emphasizes:
1. Leveraging environmental sensing assets in the CO-WY region
2. Transforming environmental sensing data through advanced analytics (including AI/ML)
3. Developing actionable decision-support tools

## Eligibility Requirements

**Applicant Type:**
- Must be led and submitted by startup companies
- Less than $20 million in cumulative grants and third-party investor funding since inception
- Annual revenue less than $2 million
- Substantial operations required in Colorado or Wyoming
- Technology may have been developed with regional institutions of higher education or Federal Laboratories (not required)

**Principal Investigator:**
- Must be founder, co-founder, or C-level operational executive at the startup company
- Appropriate qualifications/experience to lead federally funded project

**Eligible Collaborators:**
- Academic institutions: Colorado School of Mines, Colorado State University, Metro State University, University of Colorado Boulder, University of Colorado Denver, University of Northern Colorado, University of Wyoming
- Federal labs/agencies: NREL, USDA Agricultural Research Service, NCAR, NOAA (CIRES, CIRA), NEON, NIST
- Other cross-sector partners (industry, nonprofits, government agencies, community groups)

## Funding Details

**Award Amount:** $250,000-$750,000 per project over 12-month project period
**Number of Awards:** 1-3 per funding cycle
**Project Period:** 12 calendar months

**Matched/Leveraged Resources Required:**
- Standard requirement: 1:2 ratio ($1 Engine funding to $2 matching resources)
- Exception: Teams working with tribal or underserved communities may document less than 1:2
- Corporate partnership projects expected to provide more than 1:2

**Eligible Matching Resource Types:**
- Cash contributions and employee salaries/fringe
- In-kind contributions (volunteered time, donated equipment, office/lab space, data sets, goods/services)
- Partnership resources and shared expertise
- Integration with existing programs/initiatives
- Leveraged data and research assets
- Public-private partnerships
- Community assets utilization
- Award stacking from other federal, public, and private sources

## Allowable Costs
- Senior personnel
- Postdoctoral fellows
- Graduate and undergraduate students
- Equipment
- Domestic travel
- Materials and supplies
- Consultant services
- Other direct costs
- Indirect costs (federally-negotiated rates or 10% de minimis of MTDC if no negotiated rate)

**Mandatory Requirements:**
- Participation in incubator or accelerator program
- Participation in regular Engine convenings
- Progress updates and evaluations every six months

## Core Use-Case Areas (Required Focus)
Proposals must address at least one of the following:
1. **Wildfire Preparedness and Response**
2. **Soil Health**
3. **Water Quality and Availability**
4. **Air Quality**

Proposals failing to address these core use-case challenge areas will not be considered.

## Evaluation Criteria

**Reviewers:** CO-WY Engine team plus external technical, academic, and business experts

**Evaluation Areas:**
1. **Technical Viability:** Novelty, likelihood of success, competitive advantages, IP strategy strength
2. **Project Viability:** Appropriateness of scope, translation plan, milestones, budget
3. **Scalability:** Likelihood of product adoption and scaled deployment
4. **Market Analysis:** Market opportunity level for the product
5. **Funding Path and Business Model:** Strength of plans and likelihood to achieve market impact
6. **Team Readiness:** Strength of qualifications, management structure, execution record
7. **Engagement and Partnerships:** Partnership strength, breadth, and matched/leveraged resources
8. **Potential Impact:** Potential for broad economic and community impacts to climate resiliency

## Accountability & Reporting
- Six-month progress update intervals required
- Progress and final reports must include: activity summaries with impact evaluation, outcomes assessment with metrics/timelines, expenditure summary
- Unspent funds must be returned to CO-WY Engine
- Awardees considered for follow-on funding from other Engine programs based on demonstrated progress

## Notable Details

- Strong regional focus on Colorado and Wyoming innovation ecosystem
