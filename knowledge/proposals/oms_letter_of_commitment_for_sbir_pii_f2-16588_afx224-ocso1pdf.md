## Executive Summary
Orbital Micro Systems, Inc. (OMS) commits to serving as a subcontractor to Black Swift Technologies on a US Air Force SBIR Phase II program to develop runway soil integrity validation capability using L-band microwave radiometer technology. OMS will support sensor design refinement, manufacturing, testing, and data analysis over a 24-month period with a fixed cost of not to exceed $605,000.

## Technical Approach
- Refine L-band Lobe Differencing Correlation Radiometer (LDCR) design from RevC2 (current) to RevC3 (Phase II optimized version)
- Design and layout printed circuit boards for RevC3 with EMI mitigation features
- Manufacture 20 RevC3 sensors and 20 antenna modules for flight testing and laboratory use
- Conduct laboratory and field testing/validation of RevC3 sensor systems
- Support data analysis and soil integrity algorithm development

## Products & Capabilities Described

**L-band Microwave Radiometer (LDCR)**
- Lobe Differencing Correlation Radiometer design
- Current iteration: RevC2
- Phase II optimized iteration: RevC3
- Used for determining runway soil integrity measurements
- Part of multi-sensor technique for soil integrity validation
- Will be integrated with antenna modules

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Runway Integrity Validation**: Determining soil integrity of airfield runways via soil moisture measurements
- **Unimproved Runways**: Global US Air Force operations on unimproved/unprepared runways
- **National Defense Operations**: Critical national defense applications
