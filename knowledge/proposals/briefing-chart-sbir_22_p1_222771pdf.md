# A Common Operating Picture and Flight Demonstrations for UAS Integration With Active Wildfire Operations

## Document Metadata
- Type: SBIR Phase I Proposal / Briefing Chart
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR 2022, Topic A3.04-2771
- Date: March 9, 2022
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Small UAS platforms (unspecified model), UAS for wildfire characterization
- Key Personnel: Maciej Stachura (PI), Jack Elston (BST contact)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to develop a common operating picture (COP) and airspace management solution for unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) operating in active wildfire response environments. The solution will integrate existing software (ATAK) with NASA's UTM research and BST's experience in UAS operations to enable safe, coordinated flight of multiple manned and unmanned assets while providing enhanced situational awareness for fire characterization and management.

## Technical Approach
BST's approach leverages:
- **Existing software platforms** already deployed in wildland firefighting (specifically ATAK—Army Tactical Operations Center)
- **NASA UTM research** conducted for urban airspace integration
- **BST's prior experience** in atmospheric sampling, UAS operations in extreme environments, and wildfire model improvement
- Development of a **simulation and flight test-bed** to validate the integrated system
- Creation of standardized **interface definitions** to enable data dissemination beyond typical near real-time EO/IR imagery
- User interface design informed by BST's previous UAS development experience

**Key focus areas:**
- Airspace management and deconfliction between manned and unmanned assets
- Real-time data dissemination for situational awareness
- Safe standoff maintenance between airborne assets
- Integration of diverse sensor data for fire state assessment

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **BST Small UAS**: Described as "highly capable" platforms designed for wildfire response; capable of operations in hazardous environments with particulates and severe turbulence
- **UAS-based sensing systems**: Referenced but not detailed in this briefing chart; intended to provide data beyond standard EO/IR imagery
- **Software integration capability**: BST's experience developing user interfaces for UAS systems

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Application
- **Active wildfire monitoring and response**: Multi-asset coordination, real-time airspace management, fire characterization and model refinement during active fire suppression operations

### Secondary Applications (Non-NASA)
- **Volcano monitoring**: Leveraging capability to operate in hazardous environments with particulates and turbulence
- **Cooperative UTM applications**: Federal and commercial airspace integration efforts (unspecified)

### NASA Cross-Cutting Applications
- Potential users beyond NASA Ames include:
  - Tropospheric Chemistry Program (TCP)
  - Applied Sciences Air Quality Program
  - CALIPSO mission
  - Aura mission
  - CATS (Cloud-Aerosol Transport System)
  - OCO-3 (Orbiting Carbon Observatory)
  - Earth Ventures program for airborne field campaigns

## Phase I Milestones & Deliverables

1. **Summary document**: Currently used software for wildfire UTM and data dissemination
2. **Summary document**: Desired UAS-based sensing systems
3. **Interface Definition Document**: Specifications for system integration
4. **Flight authorization documentation**: Necessary approvals for Phase II flight demonstrations
5. **Functioning simulation**: Fully operational simulation environment with BST UAS models

## Notable Details
- **Software compatibility**: Proposal emphasizes building on existing widely-deployed tools (ATAK) rather than developing from scratch, ensuring field readiness and user familiarity
- **Informed by agency research**: Direct reference to leveraging NASA's UTM work to avoid duplication and ensure integration with broader national airspace initiatives
- **Multi-stakeholder approach**: Proposes working with "several of the leading current operators" in wildland firefighting to ensure operational relevance
- **Hazardous environment capability**: BST differentiates itself on ability to operate reliably in particulate-rich, high-turbulence environments typical of active fire zones
- **Expansion beyond imagery**: Proposal seeks to move beyond traditional EO/IR data to provide richer situational picture for fire state assessment

## Innovation Claim
The innovation is framed as addressing a specific gap: UAS cannot currently participate fully in wildfire operations due to lack of standardized data dissemination and airspace management solutions tailored to the wildfire domain, despite extensive UTM development for urban airspace.
