# Black Swift Technologies Business Plan 2015

## Document Metadata
- Type: Business Plan
- Client/Agency: State of Colorado Advanced Industries (investment/funding application)
- Program/Solicitation: Colorado Advanced Industries program
- Date: March 2, 2015
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: SwiftCore Flight Management System, SwiftPilot Autopilot, SwiftTab User Interface, SwiftStation Ground Station, SuperSwift Airframe, Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS
- Key Personnel: Not named individually

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is a Boulder, Colorado-based sUAS avionics startup (founded 2011) seeking investment to capitalize on the emerging commercial unmanned aircraft market. The company has established government and research customers and proposes to position itself as a leading supplier of flight management systems and integrated sUAS solutions as FAA regulations for commercial UAS operations are expected to open in 2016-2017.

## Technical Approach
BST's strategy centers on a fully integrated, end-to-end sUAS ecosystem built on proprietary algorithms and electronics:
- **Core philosophy**: Reduce operational complexity and training requirements compared to traditional UAS (which require crews of up to 12 personnel)
- **Key technical advantage**: Single operators can theoretically manage multiple aircraft simultaneously
- **Modular design**: Software features scale from simple/safe operations to advanced capabilities for skilled users
- **Integration model**: SwiftCore avionics paired with various airframes through manufacturer partnerships
- **Customization approach**: Offering discounts and free airframe compatibility customization to increase adoption

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SwiftCore Flight Management System
Complete suite consisting of three integrated components:
- **SwiftPilot Autopilot**: 50 grams, 6.7 x 4 x 1.8 cm; described as small, accurate, powerful, mission-focused
- **SwiftTab User Interface**: User-friendly, mobile, real-time ground control interface (tablet-based)
- **SwiftStation Ground Station**: Integrated, rugged, robust ground control station

### SuperSwift Airframe
- Payload capacity: 2 kg
- Flight time: 90 minutes
- Field-swappable payload capability
- Hand launch capable
- Autonomous operations capable

### Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS
Referenced as current product offering but no specifications provided.

## Competitive Advantages Claimed
1. **Technical team**: Three doctorates in Aerospace Engineering focused on sUAS automation, applications, and design
2. **System integration**: Proprietary algorithms combined in fully integrated end-to-end system
3. **Usability & reliability**: Superior to competing products for demanding, high-value applications
4. **Operational efficiency**: Significant reduction in training and crew requirements compared to military-grade systems
5. **Cost advantage**: Referenced quote from RECUV: "The Black Swift autopilot is cheaper, easier to use, and in many ways more capable than military systems costing 10 times as much"

## Intellectual Property
- **Protection method**: Primarily trade secret; patents pending for tablet-based user interface design
- **Rationale for limited patenting**: Rapid development pace makes formal patent protection economically impractical
- **Future plans**: Formal patent protection to be pursued if product set stabilizes
- **IP composition**: Software, electronics designs, and algorithms

## Current Customer Base
- University researchers
- NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration)
- Local government entities
- Research institutions with FAA Certificates of Authorization (COAs)
- Early commercial prospects in agriculture, mapping, and scientific applications have inquired directly

## Market Opportunity Assessment
- **Total UAS market (non-consumer)**: Projected to grow from $6.6 billion (2012) to $11.4 billion (2022) per Teal Group
- **Commercial market potential**: AUVSI estimates $13.6 billion economic impact in US during 2015-2017 period alone
- **Regulatory catalyst**: FAA expected to complete commercial UAS regulations by late 2016 or early 2017, opening legal commercial operations

## Business Model Details
- **Distribution**: End-to-end systems via existing channels familiar to customers plus new channels for emerging markets
- **Pricing strategy**: Variable, based on agreements and competitive positioning
- **Revenue drivers**: System sales and associated margins; manufacturer partnerships
- **Current limitation**: Restricted to public entities (universities, law enforcement, government) under FAA COA framework

## Sales & Marketing Strategy
- **Current approach**: Direct contact through trade shows and industry events (effective for limited market)
- **Lead management**: Web-based lead tracking system with ongoing customer communications
- **Key success factor**: Customer referrals from satisfied early adopters
- **Target customer profile**: Modernizing organizations with spending authority; value usability over price; seek intelligent data gathering to optimize operations and resource utilization
- **Future scaling**: Wider marketing campaign planned post-commercial regulation
- **Manufacturer partnerships**: Critical growth lever; manufacturers both motivated and effective at generating new sales

## Development Plan & Next Steps
- **Current focus**: Deep technical development and innovation (completed)
- **Pivot direction**: Business development, innovation leadership, marketing, and sales
- **Key gap**: Company seeking experienced management team members to build sales and marketing capabilities
- **Requested support**: Investment capital from State of Colorado to fund business-side expansion and strengthen management team
- **Strategic goal**: Position as top avionics option in UAS field

## Notable Details
- **Company founding**: October 2011; actively operating as of 2015
- **Early strategy**: 2012 partnering model with other startups for specific research projects; funded R&D but deemed unsustainable long-term
- **Current positioning**: Recognized as top-tier avionics supplier for high-impact government-funded projects
- **Regulatory strategy**: Leveraging existing government/research customer base to build credibility and case studies for future commercial market
- **Operational philosophy**: Emphasize reduced operational complexity and risk through simplified training and multi-aircraft capability
- **Leadership expertise**: Team has entrepreneurial training and commercialization experience in aerospace services and software
