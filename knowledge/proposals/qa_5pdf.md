## Technical Approach & Requirements

### Subtopic 8.2.7: Glider Water Sampling System

**Primary Objective:**
- Develop glider capability to collect reliable water samples at or within 1 meter of the bottom in shallow, highly stratified shelf environments
- Goal is to complement existing ship-based annual hypoxia survey cruises (depth range: 5.5m - 118m)

**Sensor Requirements:**
- **Required:** Dissolved oxygen (D.O.), salinity, temperature, depth
- **Desired (not required):** pH, chlorophyll
- Data collection capability: approximately 1-meter intervals throughout water column

**Operational Performance:**
- Minimum speed: 15-25 km per day (to match current ship-based equipment)
- Sampling frequency: Once every 1 km throughout entire water column (sufficient)
- Temporal requirement: At least monthly transects at 4 locations
- Transect duration: Completion in less than one week (ideally 5 days)
- Operational season: Summer hypoxia season

## Use Cases & Applications
- Complementing ship-based hypoxia monitoring programs
- Water column sampling in nearshore, shallow, highly stratified environments
- Multi-depth water quality assessment for hypoxia research

## Notable Details
- Depth capability is NOT the primary goal; existing gliders already reach necessary depths
- Focus is on **sampling reliability and accuracy** in challenging nearshore conditions
- Sample spacing of 1 km intervals specified as sufficient (not requiring higher-density sampling patterns like "once per square mile")
- Document emphasizes fair competition and routing all communications through the Contracting Officer (CO)
