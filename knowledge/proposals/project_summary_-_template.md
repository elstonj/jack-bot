## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies developed a low-cost, small Unmanned Aircraft System (sUAS) equipped with a passive L-band microwave radiometer capable of mapping volumetric soil moisture content at 15m resolution down to 5-20cm depth. Operating at 15-30m altitude over agricultural-plot sized areas, the Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS represents the first sUAS platform of its kind and was successfully demonstrated at an instrumented test site in Oklahoma, providing high-resolution data to augment satellite missions like SMAP.

## Technical Approach

**Core Design Philosophy:**
- Integration of passive L-band microwave radiometer with sUAS avionics and airframe
- Tight coupling for precise flight control at low altitudes (15-30m AGL)
- Novel antenna design and high-rate data acquisition to enable soil moisture mapping

**Key Technical Components:**
1. **L-band MiCo Antenna** - Custom-designed microwave antenna specifically engineered to fit within small UAS constraints
2. **LDCR Instrument** - Evolved from Phase I prototype to full flight-ready unit; passive L-band radiometer for soil moisture measurement
3. **High-Rate Data Logger & Onboard Processing Unit** - Unique system capable of logging at very high rates while maintaining small form factor compatible with hand-launched sUAS
4. **Onboard Digital Correlator** - Designed and built to support radiometric processing
5. **SuperSwift Airframe** - Entirely new airframe developed because no commercial platforms met all SMM requirements; robust design with modular, field-swappable payload bay

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Soil Moisture Mapping (SMM) sUAS
- **What it is:** Low-cost sUAS with integrated passive L-band microwave radiometer for Earth science research
- **Key Capability:** Only sUAS capable of mapping soil moisture content at critical depths in low-cost, easy-to-operate package
- **Performance Specifications:**
  - Operating altitude: 15-30m AGL
  - Soil moisture mapping resolution: ~15m
  - Soil depth penetration: ~5-20cm
  - Measurement type: Volumetric soil moisture content
- **Status:** Successfully fielded commercially; validated through Oklahoma test site deployment

### SuperSwift Airframe
- **What it is:** New aircraft platform developed under the project due to lack of suitable commercial options
- **Design Features:**
  - Robust construction for scientific missions
  - Modular, field-swappable payload bay
  - Hand-launchable capability
  - Designed for multi-payload flexibility
- **Applications:** SMM radiometer integration; commercial surveying with sensing nose cone; multiple science payloads

### Tempest sUAS
- **Role:** Platform used for initial integration and testing of sensor package

## Use Cases & Applications

### NASA Applications
- **SMAPVEX Field Campaign:** Calibration/validation support for SMAP (Soil Moisture Active Passive) satellite
- **Ocean Surface Salinity Mapping:** Follow-on capability for higher-resolution local-scale data to complement satellite missions like Aquarius
- **Airborne Microwave Observatory of Subcanopy and Subsurface (AMOSS) Program:** Inexpensive option for follow-on work
- **SuperSwift Multi-Payload Research:** Scientific missions leveraging modular payload bay

### Non-NASA Commercial Applications
- **Precision Agriculture:** Primary commercial application; reducing water usage in agriculture through soil moisture data
- **Flood Prediction:** Mapping water-soil saturation for flood risk assessment
- **Soil Integrity Assessment:** Combining technology with other sensing modalities
- **Commercial Surveying:** SuperSwift equipped with sensing nose cone

## Key Results

**Technical Accomplishments:**
1. Successfully completed and tested L-band radiometer and MiCo antenna
2. Developed and validated onboard digital correlator and high-rate data logger
3. Designed and built SuperSwift airframe
4. Integrated complete sensor package in Tempest sUAS platform
5. **Successful deployment at instrumented Oklahoma test site** - Flight data correlated well with in situ soil moisture sensors, validating design decisions and ground testing
6. Developed and validated soil moisture extraction algorithm based on flight data
7. Assessed system feasibility for ocean salinity measurement applications

**Key Innovation Achievement:**
- Created first-ever sUAS capable of measuring soil moisture at operationally significant depths with 15m spatial resolution
- Advanced state of science in microwave radiometry miniaturization for sUAS platforms
