# CCRPP Commitment Letter - NOAA (Hurricane Operations)

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Commitment Letter / Funding Agreement
- **Client/Agency:** National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA)
- **Program/Solicitation:** NASA SBIR/STTR Civilian Commercialization Readiness Pilot Program (CCRPP); Application #2022-1-1118; Related to Phase II Contract #NNX17CP13C
- **Date:** January 6, 2022
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** S0 (small UAS)
- **Key Personnel:** Dr. Jack Elston (BST); Dr. Joseph J. Cione (NOAA)

## Executive Summary
NOAA commits $500,987.14 in matching funds to develop the BST S0 small UAS for hurricane reconnaissance and tropical cyclone (TC) boundary layer data collection. The project aims to create a multi-hour duration, air-deployable UAS capable of continuous meteorological and oceanographic data gathering within severe storm environments, with command and control range up to 150 nm from a NOAA WP-3D aircraft, enabling routine operational data collection in conditions previously inaccessible.

## Technical Approach
BST's S0 system is being developed to:
1. **Air-deployment capability** from NOAA WP-3D aircraft with extended multi-hour endurance
2. **Extended communication range:** Continuous command and control up to 150 nm distance between WP-3D and S0
3. **Payload integration:** S0 equipped with laser altimeter and Multi-Hole Turbulence Probe (MHTP)
4. **Autonomous operations:** Full mission automation based on scientific measurement goals
5. **Real-time data management:** Integration with NOAA's KARMA (Keeping Accurate Real-time Meteorological Awareness) system and WMO HDOBS data standards
6. **VTOL variants:** Two VTOL configurations of S0 for ground-based meteorological observation stations
7. **Enhanced flight performance:** Specific design modifications for hurricane/severe storm environments

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0 (Small UAS)**
- Multi-hour duration aircraft designed for severe storm environments
- Air-deployable from manned research aircraft (NOAA WP-3D)
- Intended as unrecoverable/expendable technology
- Capable of sustained operation in severely turbulent tropical cyclone boundary layers
- Base configuration: S0 with laser altimeter and MHTP payload

**Multi-Hole Turbulence Probe (MHTP)**
- Developed in coordination with Area-I Altius 600
- Enhanced design to handle heavy precipitation in hurricane conditions
- Performance-critical for boundary layer wind/turbulence measurements in tropical cyclones

**Communication/Data System**
- Continuous range of operation to 150 nm
- Real-time data delivery meeting NOAA meteorological/oceanographic requirements
- Integration with KARMA system for situational awareness
- WMO HDOBS format compliance

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Application:** Hurricane/Tropical Cyclone Reconnaissance
- Boundary layer meteorological measurements within active tropical cyclones
- Oceanographic data collection in severe storm environments
- Extended-range observations from NOAA's WP-3D research aircraft
- Data collection from within TC center that is operationally hazardous for manned platforms

**Secondary Applications:**
- Ground-based meteorological observations using VTOL S0 variants from surface stations
- Support for Area-I Altius 600 calibration missions
- Real-time data delivery for operational forecasting and situational awareness

## Development Timeline & Deliverables

**2022 Schedule:**
- 31 Jan: S0 Preliminary Design Review (PDR)
- 28 Feb: Complete integration/performance/automation/data NRE tasks
- 31 Mar: S0 Critical Design Review (CDR)
- 30 Apr: Deliver 2 field-ready S0 sUAS with laser altimeter and MHTP
- 17-31 May: P-3/sUAS calibration and dummy missions
- 31 Jul: Deliver 6 additional field-ready S0 sUAS with payloads
- 26 Sept: Deliver 2 VTOL S0 variants for ground operations
- 1 Aug - 30 Nov: Hurricane field deployment operations

**2023 Schedule:**
- 28 Feb: Complete NRE improvements from 2022 field operations
- 31 Mar: Complete MHTP design and deliver 2 MHTPs to Area-I; possible CDR#2
- 30 Apr: Deliver 2 field-ready S0s with MHTP and video capability
- 31 May: Optional second calibration mission; possible Area-I Altius 600 MHTP calibration support
- 30 Jun: Deliver 8 additional MHTPs to Area-I
- 31 Jul: Deliver 6 additional field-ready S0s with MHTP and video capability
- 1 Aug - 30 Nov: Second year hurricane field deployment operations

**Deployment Goals:** Total of 14 S0 sUAS systems and 10 MHTPs developed/delivered across two field seasons.
