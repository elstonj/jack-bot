## SUBTOPIC 1: Advanced Air Traffic Management for Nontraditional Airspace Operations (A3.02)

### Subtopic Focus
Nontraditional Aviation Operations for **Advanced Air Mobility (AAM)**

### Executive Summary
NASA seeks technologies to enable safe and efficient integration of Advanced Air Mobility vehicles into the National Airspace System (NAS) through service-based architectures, dynamic routing and scheduling, and human-autonomy teaming approaches.

### Scope & Key Areas Sought
- Service-based architecture designs for scalable AAM operations
- Dynamic route planning accounting for environmental conditions, vehicle performance, endurance, airspace congestion, and traffic avoidance
- Dynamic scheduling for on-demand access to constrained resources with vehicles of different performance characteristics
- Integration of AAM with legacy users, commercial transport, and ultra-high altitude operations
- Operational concepts for fleet and network management
- Certification approaches for new vehicle types (e.g., electric vertical takeoff and landing - eVTOL)
- Human-autonomy teaming between operators and autonomous agents/technology
- Cyber-resiliency methods integrated into service-based architectures

### Critical Gaps Identified
- Integrating air transportation technologies across different domains and operators
- Providing comprehensive, strategic scheduling and traffic management technologies
- Enabling concepts that scale to meet demand and operational complexity

### Expected Deliverables
**Phase I:** Prototype/proof-of-concept decision-support tool, automation, and/or service; proof-of-concept demonstration of underlying architecture; validation of approach for specific use case

**Phase II:** Higher TRL (technology readiness level) tools/decision-support services demonstrating solution to R&D challenge

### Expected TRL Range at Completion
TRL 1-4

### Technology Taxonomy
- Level 1: TX 16 Air Traffic Management and Range Tracking Systems
- Level 2: TX 16.3 Traffic Management Concepts

### Relevant NASA Programs
- Airspace Operations and Safety Program (AOSP)
- Air Traffic Management-eXploration (ATM-X) Project

### Important Constraints
- Proposals must have **clear application to AAM airspace operations only**
- Proposals focusing on AAM vehicle capabilities or onboard vehicle technologies will be rejected
- Proposals specific to other nontraditional operations (space traffic management, automated air cargo, UTM, ultra-high altitude) without clear AAM application will be rejected
- Proposals focused exclusively on cybersecurity without addressing specific AAM services will be rejected

## SUBTOPIC 2: Nontraditional Aviation Operations for Wildfire Response

### Executive Summary
NASA seeks technologies to enable coordination of aerial assets for wildfire suppression and mitigation through advanced airspace management, persistent monitoring, strategic planning, communications, and decision-support tools—adapting UTM and AAM concepts to wildfire disaster operations.

### Context & Urgency
- U.S. wildfires are becoming increasingly severe in frequency and intensity
- 2020 fire season: 4+ million acres burned, 8,500+ structures destroyed, 30+ lives lost
- Annual fire suppression costs increased from ~$425M (1999) to $1.6B (2019)
- California and western states face persistent drought and higher temperatures
- Fire seasons now last 6-8 months; in some cases year-round

### Current Aerial Wildfire Management Applications
- Smoke-jumper deployment
- Transport of firefighters, equipment, supplies
- Fire retardant or water drops
- Fire reconnaissance and behavior monitoring
- Supervision of air tactical operations

### Current Challenges
- Existing airspace management is manual and cannot accommodate new aircraft types (unmanned)
- Aerial firefighting limited to acceptable visual conditions (no night operations)
- Monitoring and remote sensing are intermittent or satellite-dependent
- Lack of reliable, resilient, secure data communications
- Manual coordination across multiple entities and assets
- Aircraft awareness conducted via visual monitoring and radio only
- Unmanned systems not easily integrated into suppression operations
- Surveillance imagery captured/disseminated only every 4 hours
- Intermittent communication delays response
- Conditions change rapidly; decision makers overloaded with data
- Tools and data spread across numerous applications

### Technologies Sought
NASA seeks innovations to provide:

1. **Strategic planning capabilities** for persistent monitoring of wildland fire conditions from satellites, conventional aircraft, and UAS

2. **Strategic planning and tracking capabilities** for effective use of ground crews, equipment, and aircraft (single and multiple incidents)

3. **Multimission planning capabilities** for concurrent operations (air attack, search and rescue, etc.)

4. **Extended UTM network** adapted to unique wildfire needs (e.g., GPS-degraded, nonconnected environments)

5. **Enhanced communications** with increased throughput, reduced latency, interoperability with existing systems, reliable network for UAS and emergency responders

6. **Mobile positioning, navigation, and timing (PNT) solutions** for automated operations (precision water drops) in GPS-degraded environments (mountainous canyons)

7. **Integrated prediction and situational awareness** combining wildland fire prediction, airspace coordination, and resource tracking to support incident command structure (incident commander, air tactical group supervisor, aircraft dispatch, UAS pilot, etc.)

8. **Safety and efficiency assurance** for all operations

### Expected Deliverables
**Phase I:** Prototype/proof-of-concept decision-support tool, automation, and/or service; proof-of-concept architecture demonstration; validation for specific R&D challenge aspect or use case

**Phase II:** Higher TRL tools/decision-support services demonstrating convincing solution to R&D challenge

### Expected TRL Range at Completion
TRL 1-5

### Technology Taxonomy
- Level 1: TX 16 Air Traffic Management and Range Tracking Systems
- Level 2: TX 16.3 Traffic Management Concepts

### Relevant NASA Programs
- Airspace Operations and Safety Program (AOSP)

### Policy Context
- June 30, 2021: President Biden directed federal government actions (in coordination with state/local governments and private sector) to strengthen prevention, preparedness, mitigation, and response to wildfire threats

## Notable Details

1. **Two Complementary Subtopics:** The wildfire response subtopic builds on AAM/UTM concepts, adapting them to disaster response scenarios

2. **No Cyber-Security-Only Proposals:** Both subtopics emphasize cyber-resiliency as a component but reject proposals addressing cybersecurity in isolation

3. **TRL Expectations:** Wildfire response allows higher final TRL (up to 5) compared to AAM airspace operations (up to 4), reflecting relative maturity expectations

4. **Decision Support Focus:** Both subtopics emphasize decision-support tools, automation, and services rather than hardware or vehicle development

5. **Multi-stakeholder Integration:** Both seek technologies that integrate diverse users and assets (legacy, commercial, unmanned, emergency response)

6. **Human-Autonomy Balance:** AAM subtopic explicitly emphasizes need for human-autonomy teaming in increasingly complex operations
