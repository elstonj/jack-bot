- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes using its S2 fixed-wing UAS equipped with a Quanta3 methane spectrometer to detect and quantify methane emissions from orphaned oil and gas wells at a cost below $100 per well—a dramatic reduction from the current $750 per well using ground-based technicians. This approach combines high sensitivity methane detection with autonomous BVLOS flight capabilities to rapidly scan dozens of wells per flight while providing 3D wind field measurements for accurate emission characterization.

## Technical Approach

**Core System: S2 UAS with Methane Leak Detection (MLD)**
- Platform: 3-meter wingspan fixed-wing UAS
- Operational range: 100 km
- Flight capability: Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) autonomous operations
- Sensor: Custom-designed Quanta3 methane spectrometer
- Detection sensitivity: 3 parts per billion (ppb)
- Real-time autonomous flight path modification based on methane gradients and concentrations
- Simultaneous 3D wind field measurement capability at multiple altitudes
- Endurance/capacity: Can survey up to 50 well sites in one day

**Validation Approach:**
- Technology currently at TRL 5 (demonstrated through controlled releases at BST's Colorado test site)
- Field validation planned using flux towers for comparison and accuracy confirmation
- Mitigation of atmospheric variation risks through controlled comparison testing

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Application: Orphaned Oil & Gas Well Inspection**
- Challenge addressed: Millions of orphaned wells in the U.S. lack economically feasible inspection methods
- Current baseline: $750 per well using ground technician inspections (labor-intensive, not scalable)
- Proposed solution cost: <$100 per well
- Scale potential: Ability to inspect 50 wells per flight day dramatically increases monitoring efficiency
- Environmental impact: Creates economically viable path to monitoring millions of U.S. orphaned wells

## Competitive Advantages vs. Current State-of-the-Art

**Existing approaches and limitations:**
1. Ground crews with Optical Gas Imaging (OGI) cameras: High labor costs, not scalable
2. Short-range multi-copter UAS deployed at each site: High labor costs, limited range
3. Manned aircraft with gas mapping lidar: Insufficient sensitivity for lower emission range desired in RFP

**Proposed solution advantages:**
- Combines required high sensitivity (3 ppb) with operational scalability
- Large operational range (100 km) enables multi-site surveys per mission
- BVLOS autonomous capability reduces operational labor
- Cost structure ($<100/well vs. $750/well) enables economic viability for orphaned wells
- 3D wind measurement capability provides unique advantage for emission characterization in complex/remote environments

## Key Technical Challenges & Risk Mitigation

**Identified Challenges:**
- Ensuring consistent performance across varied atmospheric conditions
- Validation in diverse geographic areas

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Flux tower comparison and validation approach for real-world accuracy confirmation
- Controlled testing already completed at BST Colorado test site
