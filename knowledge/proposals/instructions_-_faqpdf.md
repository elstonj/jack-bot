## Executive Summary
This is NASA's official guidance document clarifying what types of investor relationships and funding arrangements qualify as "investment" under NASA's SBIR/STTR Phase II Enhancement program. The document provides detailed examples of qualifying and non-qualifying investments to help small businesses understand eligibility for matching fund requirements.

## Document Purpose & Context
This is an internal intelligence document that Black Swift Technologies would have consulted when considering Phase II Enhancement applications. It is not a BST proposal, product description, or technical document, but rather reference material for understanding NASA SBIR program rules.

## Key Guidance on Qualifying Investments

### General Requirements
- Outside investor must provide **cash** (not in-kind contributions)
- Funds must further development and/or commercialization of SBIR technology (R&D, manufacturing, marketing, etc.)
- Investment may take form of: equity, royalty share, technology rights, profit percentage, or advance purchase orders
- Outside validation of commercial potential is the core principle

### Definition of "Outside Investor"
- **Qualifies:** venture capital firms, angel investors, other companies (if not affiliated), non-SBIR/STTR government programs
- **Does NOT qualify:** company owners, family members, affiliates (as defined in 13 C.F.R. Section 121.103)
- Multiple entities can jointly invest
- Affiliation determination guided by CFR definition but NASA exercises discretion

### What Does NOT Qualify
- Company's own internal funds
- Loans (unless convertible to equity and actually converted before application)
- In-kind contributions
- Circular investments (mutual investments between two companies)
- Family member investments
- Affiliated company investments (with presumption rebuttable by showing lack of control/common interests)

### What DOES Qualify
- Cash from unaffiliated venture capital firms
- Cash from angel investors unrelated to company
- Advance purchase orders for products directly resulting from SBIR project (payment non-refundable, received within 45 days of selection)
- IPO funds earmarked for SBIR-related work
- Investment from venture capital firm owning <50% of company (not controlling investor)
- Investment during Phase I period (timing flexibility)

### Important Net-Out Rule
When outside investor also serves as subcontractor or has separate commercial contracts with the SBIR company, only the **net cash contribution** counts as matching funds:
- Example: $750K investment minus $250K subcontracting = $500K qualifies
- Exception: If separate contract pre-dates SBIR proposal submission, entire investment counts

### Special Situations
- **Spin-off companies:** Investment in spin-off can count for parent company if deemed "substantially the same entity"
- **University collaborations (STTR):** Investment must be in small company, not university; company can subcontract funds to university
- **Convertible loans from public entities:** Qualify as investment
- **Statement of work:** Investor-funded activities need not be included in Phase II SOW
