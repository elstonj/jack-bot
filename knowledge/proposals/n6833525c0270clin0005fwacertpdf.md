## Key Compliance Areas Emphasized

### Statutory & Regulatory Requirements
- 15 USC 638(k)(2): Fraud database for SBIR/STTR participant tracking
- 2019 SBA SBIR/STTR Policy Directive: Agencies must evaluate and prevent FWA risks

### Areas of Potential Fraud, Waste, and Abuse Outlined
1. **Eligibility violations** – ownership, size requirements, facility adequacy, PI employment
2. **Multiple agency payments** – undisclosed duplicate or overlapping funding
3. **Recordkeeping failures** – inadequate documentation of budgets, expenditures, timesheets
4. **Firm Fixed Price misuse** – treating award as unrestricted funding
5. **Unallowable costs** – personal expenses, vacations, home mortgages, etc.
6. **Indirect cost abuse** – charging indirect costs on unallowable direct costs
7. **Work requirement violations:**
   - SBIR: ≥2/3 work by SBC in Phase I, ≥1/2 in Phase II
   - STTR: ≥40% by SBC, ≥30% by single research institution in both phases
8. **PI employment violations** – PI must be ≥51% employed by SBC (SBIR); any PI change requires written Contracting Officer approval
9. **Technical proposal plagiarism** – presenting others' work without attribution
10. **Fabricated letters of support** – letters must be specific, dated, reachable, and unmodified

### Criminal Consequences
- **False Statements** (18 USC §1001): 5 years prison, $250K fine, forfeiture
- **Theft of Federal Property** (18 USC §641): 10 years prison, $250K fine, forfeiture
- **Wire Fraud** (18 USC §1343): 20 years prison, $250K fine, forfeiture

### Civil Liability
- Treble (3x) actual damages
- Up to $11,000 per false claim
- May be imposed in addition to criminal prosecution

### Administrative Remedies
- Contract termination
- Debarment from SBIR/STTR programs (typically 3 years)
- Prohibition from any federal contracting
- Prohibition from subcontracting on federal contracts
