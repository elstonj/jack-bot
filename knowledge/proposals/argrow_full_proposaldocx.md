# TTwistor: Advanced Small Unmanned Aircraft System

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Proof-of-Concept Proposal (TTO Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator Program)
- **Client/Agency:** Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator Program; NOAA Unmanned Aircraft Systems Program Office (first customer/investor)
- **Program/Solicitation:** Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator Program
- **Date:** March 3, 2014
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** SwiftPilot Pro autopilot; command/control/communications/data networking software
- **Key Personnel:** Brian Argrow (PI, University of Colorado RECUV); Jack Elston (BST founder, former RECUV PhD researcher); James Mack (RECUV Professional Research Assistant, chief technician)

## Executive Summary
This proposal describes the TTwistor, an advanced small unmanned aircraft system (UAS) derived from the successful Tempest UAS that was developed by RECUV researchers and deployed in the VORTEX2 tornado research campaign. The TTwistor improves upon the Tempest design through a twin-propeller configuration and integration of BST's SwiftPilot Pro autopilot, enabling better instrument payload placement and modular nosecone designs. The proposal positions TTwistor to address NOAA's vision of revolutionizing observing strategies while capitalizing on anticipated growth in the small electric UAS commercial market.

## Technical Approach

**Airframe Design:**
- Twin-propeller design (motor-propeller mounted on each wing) replacing the single fuselage-mounted motor of Tempest
- Enables instruments and probes to be mounted near the airframe centerline with forward-facing capability into undisturbed airflow
- Propeller airflow increases local circulation to improve takeoff lift
- Swappable modular "nosecones" custom-designed for specific instruments or payloads
- Manufactured by UASUSA, Inc.

**Autopilot & Control:**
- Integration of BST SwiftPilot Pro autopilot
- Custom command, control, communications, and data networking software developed by BST (proof-of-concept deployment software)

**Communications (Planned):**
- 900-MHz phased-array communications system with electronic beam steering (superior to mechanically-steered antennas used in Tempest)
- Prototype tested; second prototype in development
- Partnership with Boulder, CO antenna manufacturer First RF Corp. for development and commercialization
- Not included in current proof-of-concept but noted as future capability

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SwiftPilot Pro Autopilot
- **What it is:** Advanced autopilot system for small UAS
- **How proposed to be used:** Integrated into TTwistor airframes to replace/improve upon earlier Tempest autopilot systems
- **Performance context:** Enables reliable, robust operation with unmatched performance in small electric UAS class

### BST Command/Control/Communications/Data Networking Software
- **What it is:** Software architecture for UAS mission operations
- **How proposed to be used:** Custom development of proof-of-concept demonstration software for TTwistor deployment
- **Heritage:** Derived from Dr. Jack Elston's NetUAS communications architecture successfully deployed with Tempest in VORTEX2

### TTwistor Aircraft (Airframe + Autopilot Integration)
- **What it is:** Complete small electric UAS combining UASUSA airframe with BST SwiftPilot Pro
- **Specifications:** Small, electrically-powered, twin-propeller configuration with payload capacity and modular nosecone design
- **Performance claims:** Superior to Tempest design; expanded capabilities for scientific and commercial applications

## Use Cases & Applications

**Historical Context (Tempest):**
- Severe weather research (VORTEX2 tornado research campaign, spring 2009-2010)
- Atmospheric sensing and mobile communications experiments
- Demonstrated successful operation in supercell storms and tornado environments

**Proposed TTwistor Applications:**
- NOAA environmental monitoring and observing (addressing NOAA UASPO vision)
- Precision agriculture
- Emergency/first responder operations
- Surveying and mapping
- Education and science research
- Monitoring dangerous or remote areas (poles, oceans, wildlands, volcanic islands, wildfires)

**Market Targets:**
- Public entities (government agencies, state universities)
- Civil/commercial operators (once FAA regulatory framework established)
- Former Tempest UAS customers (University of Nebraska, MIT, US Navy Research Lab, Raytheon, Lockheed Martin, Airware)

## Market & Commercial Context

**Existing Market Validation:**
- More than 50 Tempest UAS units sold by UASUSA
- Proven customer base across government, defense, and academic sectors

**Growth Projections (from 2013 AUVSI Report):**
- Small electric UAS market identified as emerging high-growth sector
- Precision agriculture and public safety cited as most promising commercial and civil markets
- Projected $13.6 billion economic impact in first three years of NAS integration (2015-2017)
- Projected $82.1 billion cumulative impact 2015-2025
- Estimated 103,776 new jobs by 2025

**Regulatory Context:**
- FAA Modernization and Reauthorization Act of 2012 mandated FAA develop integration plan for civil UAS by September 30, 2015
- Currently "public" entities (government, state universities) have COA access; "civil" (commercial) market awaits regulatory framework (target November 2014)
- First responders and law enforcement already receiving expanded FAA flexibility

**First Customer/Investor:**
- NOAA Unmanned Aircraft Systems Program Office identified as first customer and investor
- TTwistor directly addresses NOAA UASPO goals: increase observing capacity, develop high science-return missions, transition cost-effective operationally feasible UAS into routine operations
