## Executive Summary
This milestone report documents BST's progress on developing an airborne soil moisture and integrity mapping system for the Air Force to assess runway integrity. Milestone #6 focuses on refining soil integrity algorithms based on field testing data collected at two locations (Sod Farm and Pawnee National Grasslands), comparing airborne L-band radiometer soil moisture measurements against ground truth data, and deriving empirical coefficients to calculate California Bearing Ratio (CBR) and Rating Cone Index (RCI) from remote-sensed data.

## Technical Approach

**System Components:**
- L-band Dielectric Constant Radiometer (LDCR) for soil moisture measurement
- Thermal sensor for surface temperature
- NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) sensor for vegetation mapping
- Cone penetrometer for ground truth soil integrity measurements
- Capacitance soil moisture probes for in situ validation

**Methodology:**
1. Airborne data collection using modified UAS with integrated L-band radiometer
2. Post-processing of airborne data to calculate soil moisture and soil integrity maps
3. Concurrent ground-based measurements at flight path locations for validation
4. Algorithm development to derive soil strength metrics (CBR, RCI) from L-band soil moisture data using empirically derived equations from published literature (Mason, "Predicting Soil Strength in Terms of Cone Index and California Bearing Ratio for Trafficability")
5. Iterative refinement based on correlation analysis between remote-sensed and in situ measurements

## Products & Capabilities Described

**L-Band Dielectric Constant Radiometer (LDCR):**
- Measures volumetric soil moisture (VSM)
- Operates from small UAS platform
- Provides spatial mapping capability
- Data post-processed to generate soil moisture maps

**Small UAS Platform:**
- Modified to carry multiple integrated sensors (radiometer, thermal, NDVI)
- Enables airborne data collection at test locations
- Integrated with auxiliary sensors for real-time calculations

**Soil Integrity Mapping Capability:**
- Derives California Bearing Ratio (CBR) maps from LDCR soil moisture data
- Generates Rating Cone Index (RCI) maps
- Maps cover multiple soil types and vegetation conditions
- Provides spatial resolution suitable for runway integrity assessment

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Application:** Runway Integrity Validation for Air Force operations
- Assesses soil strength and trafficability of potential airfield sites
- Determines bearing capacity for aircraft operations
- Supports deployment and contingency operations

**Test Locations:**
1. **Sod Farm:** Three mapping boxes covering 2 soil types (Sandy Loam-SM, Clay Loam-CL, Silty Clay Loam-CL)
2. **Pawnee National Grasslands (PNG):** Four mapping boxes covering 3 soil types (Silt Loam-ML, Loam-CL, Sandy Loam-SM)

## Key Results (Milestone #5-6 Findings)

**Data Collection Summary:**
- Soil moisture range collected: 10-30% VSM (15-25% at PNG; 20-40% at Sod Farm)
- 3 soil types tested (CL, ML, SM classifications)
- 2 vegetation types represented
- Data collection window: February 6-28, 2025 at Sod Farm; additional collections at PNG

**Sensor Validation - Soil Moisture (VSM):**
- LDCR VSM shows good agreement with in situ probe measurements
- In situ VSM variation: ~5% within measurement groups; up to 15-20% variation in some groups
- Correlation coefficients for PNG: r = 0.26 (all points), r = 0.37 (group averages)
- Spatial variability of VSM acknowledged as source of representational error

**Soil Integrity Validation - CBR Measurements:**

*Sod Farm Results:*
- LDCR-calculated CBR correlates with in situ measured CBR
- Both LDCR-based and in situ VSM-based CBR calculations show correlation with ground truth
- Correlation improved when combining datasets across locations

*Pawnee National Grasslands Results:*
- CBR data ranges 5-20
- LDCR VSM vs. In situ CBR: r = 0.20
- In situ VSM vs. In situ CBR: r = 0.20
- LDCR-calculated CBR vs. In situ CBR: r = 0.47

*Combined Dataset (Sod Farm + PNG):*
- **CL soil type:** r = 0.72 (LDCR VSM vs. measured CBR), r = 0.65 (in situ VSM vs. measured CBR)
- **ML soil type:** r = 0.85 (LDCR VSM vs. measured CBR), r = 0.42 (in situ VSM vs. measured CBR)
- Stronger correlations achieved with larger combined dataset

**Limitations & Data Gaps:**
- Time constraints limited cone penetrometer measurements to one reading per VSM measurement group
- SM (Sandy Loam) soil type lacks empirically derived CBR equations; requires additional data collection
- Current collected VSM data (15-25%) insufficient for deriving SM soil type coefficients; additional drier condition data needed

## Notable Details

**Technical Achievements:**
- Successfully integrated multiple sensor types (radiometer, thermal, NDVI) on small UAS
- Demonstrated capability to map soil moisture and derived soil strength metrics across multiple soil types
- Developed post-processing pipeline from raw radiometer data to operational soil integrity maps

**Validation Approach:**
- Multiple validation layers: direct sensor comparison, calculated metric vs. ground truth, correlation analysis
- Ground measurements conducted within 30 minutes of airborne collection for temporal consistency
- Grouped in situ measurements (maximum 4-meter spacing) to address spatial variability and representational error

**Correlation Analysis Insights:**
- Stronger correlations observed when combining data across multiple collection events and soil types
- ML soil type shows particularly strong correlation (r = 0.85) between LDCR VSM and CBR
- CL soil type shows moderate correlation (r = 0.72)
- SM soil type pending—no published empirical equations available; team plans to derive from collected data

**Environmental Factors:**
- Weather limitations documented (snow, rain events in February 2025 affected collection window)
- Seasonal conditions affected soil moisture ranges
