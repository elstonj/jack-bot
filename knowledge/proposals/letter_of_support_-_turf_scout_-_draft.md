# Letter of Support - Turf Scout

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: U.S. Air Force (AFWERX evaluation team)
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Phase II (Air Force)
- Date: May 3, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: L-band radiometer, UAS (soil moisture mapping system)
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
TurfScout provides a letter of support for Black Swift Technologies' SBIR Phase II proposal to develop an L-band radiometer and UAS system for mapping soil integrity and soil moisture. TurfScout emphasizes the commercial importance of the technology for golf course applications and references a 2022 pilot project conducted with BST and Toro.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed to improve and commercialize an L-band radiometer mounted on a UAS platform for soil moisture mapping. The improvements to both the radiometer and UAS platform are intended to enable wider commercial adoption of the core technology.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### L-Band Radiometer
- Purpose-built sensor for soil moisture measurement
- Core technology being developed and improved by BST
- Capable of accurate soil moisture mapping from UAS platform
- Improvements planned to enhance commercial viability

### UAS Platform
- Integrated soil moisture mapping system
- Designed for operational use in commercial applications
- Improvements planned as part of Phase II effort

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Golf Course Irrigation Management**: Primary application identified for reducing water usage on golf courses through accurate soil moisture mapping
- Pilot testing conducted on golf courses in partnership with Toro

## Key Partnership/Collaboration
- **Toro**: Equipment/systems partner for golf course application
- **TurfScout**: Remote sensing and data analysis support; conducted 2022 pilot project with BST

## Notable Details
- TurfScout's expertise areas: remote sensing, geographic information systems, data analysis, spectral data from cameras/drones and ground-based sensors
- 2022 pilot project involved day of mapping with BST's soil moisture sensor and comparison against ground-based sensor validation
- Letter emphasizes data integrity and superior product performance as priorities
- Supports technology development for commercial water conservation applications in turf management
