# BlackSwift Volcano Monitoring Nevado del Ruiz SSJ

## Document Metadata
- **Type**: Sole-source acquisition justification / contract approval document
- **Client/Agency**: United States Geological Survey (USGS), Department of the Interior, Volcano Science Center
- **Program/Solicitation**: FY22/23 USGS Volcano Monitoring Contract
- **Date**: Created 2023-05-17; Modified 2023-05-19; Certification date 5/31/2022
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced**: S2 UAS platform
- **Key Personnel**: Jack Elston (last editor), Angie Diefenbach (VSC UAS Project Lead/Geologist)

## Executive Summary
USGS seeks sole-source contract with Black Swift Technologies for $80,000 to conduct multiparametric volcano monitoring operations at Nevado del Ruiz using the S2 UAS platform. BST is justified as the only U.S. vendor capable of meeting the unique combination of flight performance requirements, sensor integration, and telemetry capabilities needed for high-altitude volcanic observation during crisis situations.

## Technical Approach
The S2 UAS platform will execute volcano monitoring missions with the following technical capabilities:

**Aircraft Performance Requirements:**
- Fixed-wing, class small (<55 lbs)
- Flight ceiling: 20,000 ft
- Endurance: 90 minutes with 5 lb payload
- Operating temperature range: down to -20°C
- Corrosive environment tolerance
- Runway-independent operations
- Intermediate range: >25 km
- Low-altitude terrain following capability (150 ft AGL)
- Dynamically adaptable in-flight flight pattern creation for atmospheric sampling

**Sensor & Monitoring Payload (>2 kg capacity, 8" x 12" payload section):**
- Gas sensors: CO2, SO2, H2S
- Upward-looking UV spectrometer with real-time spectral analysis capability
- 3D wind sensor
- Temperature, relative humidity, pressure sensors
- GPS
- RTK geotagging
- Nadir photogrammetry camera
- Gimbaled or forward-looking video camera

**Telemetry & Data System:**
- High-data-rate long-distance telemetry (>10 km range)
- Real-time sensor data streaming to base station
- Base-station display software with UV spectral data analysis
- Custom payload data telemetry (not limited to integrated camera systems)

**Operational Scope:**
- Complete mission flight execution at target location
- Flight permissions and regulatory compliance
- Equipment and operator deployment logistics
- Data acquisition and cataloging
- Final reports detailing flight activities

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS Platform
- **What it is**: Fixed-wing small UAS (<55 lbs) with advanced high-altitude capabilities
- **Proposed use**: Primary platform for multiparametric volcano monitoring at Nevado del Ruiz
- **Key specifications**: 20,000 ft ceiling, 90-min endurance, >2 kg payload capacity, runway-independent, cold-weather/corrosive-environment capable
- **Unique capability**: Interchangeable sensor nose cones allowing rapid sensor swaps without compromising data collection; integration of custom atmospheric sampling sensors and telemetry

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Use Case**: High-altitude volcano monitoring during volcanic crisis
- Safe observation of stratovolcano activity (Nevado del Ruiz)
- Multiparametric gas and atmospheric monitoring
- Real-time hazard assessment and forecasting
- Adaptive observation strategy based on live telemetry data

**Specific Mission Profile**:
- 10+ km flight to approach region of interest
- 10,000 ft altitude gain capability
- 15+ minutes on-station observation time
- Real-time data return enabling tactical decision-making
- Multiple sensor observations per mission via interchangeable nose cones

## Market Differentiation & Competitive Analysis

**Why sole-source is justified**: Extensive market research identified competing platforms and specific deficiencies:

| Vendor | Platform | Key Deficiency |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| C-Astral | Bramor/Atlas | Fully integrated camera systems only; limited datalink (<30 km); small payload; requires parachute recovery |
| UAV Factory | Penguin BE | <20 min electric endurance; icing-prone carburetor; large ground equipment; max 9,000 ft ceiling |
| Altavian | NOVA | 3.7 lb max payload; 10,000 ft ceiling; 15 km range; requires parachute recovery |
| Aurora | Orion | Requires runway; too large for remote deployment |
| AeroVironment | Puma/Raven/Wasp | 20 km range only; fully integrated systems; insufficient payload capacity |
| BirdsEyeView | FireFLY | 25 kt wind limit; 3 nm range; 1.5 lb payload only |

**BST Unique Advantages**:
- Only vendor with intermediate-range (>25 km), high-altitude (20,000 ft), mid-range (>2 kg) payload combination
- Proprietary expertise in custom sensor integration, software development, and telemetry solutions
- Runway-independent rapid deployment for remote areas
- Parachute-free recovery suitable for forested/windy conditions
- Real-time atmospheric sampling pattern execution and adaptation
- Custom payload data telemetry (not restricted to pre-integrated systems)

## Notable Details

- **Contract Value**: $79,269 (approved up to $80,000)
- **Statutory Authority**: FAR 13.106-1(b)(1) — sole source on basis that only one source reasonably available
- **Target Location**: Nevado del Ruiz volcano (Mexico/Colombia region)
- **USGS Endorsement**: Document signed by Angie Diefenbach, VSC UAS Project Lead, certifying unique requirement and accuracy of sole-source justification
- **Future Competition**: USGS notes that while sole-source is appropriate now, future requirements will undergo competition if market develops; acknowledges BST operations would "welcome and consider" future competitive options
- **Market Conditions**: USGS acknowledges intermediate-range UAS demand insufficient to attract competing manufacturers to develop alternative products
- **Deployment Advantage**: S2's small size and runway-independence specifically cited as advantage for getting equipment to remote volcano monitoring locations
