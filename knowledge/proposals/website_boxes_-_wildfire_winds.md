## Executive Summary
BST proposes to improve wildland firefighting safety by developing capabilities to collect targeted UAS observations of low-level winds and turbulence (lowest 5,000 ft of atmosphere) and assimilate this data into improved 3D wind and turbulence forecasts for integration into ATAK (common operational planning tool). Partnership with NCAR/RAL and Colorado's Center of Excellence for Advanced Technology Aerial Firefighting will use advanced data assimilation methods to demonstrate forecasting improvements over 3-12 hours post-sampling.

## Technical Approach

**Core Strategy:**
- Deploy S0 UAS equipped with meteorological sensors to make targeted observations near active wildfires
- Use Ensemble Kalman Filter data assimilation methods (developed by NCAR) to process observations
- Automatically generate UAS sampling patterns based on forecast models and observations
- Integrate improved wind/turbulence products into ATAK for operational use

**Phase I Specific Objectives:**
1. Develop algorithms for automated UAS sampling pattern generation based on current forecasts and observations
2. Create operational requirements document for S0 deployment in wildfire response (including risks and mitigation)
3. Conduct field data collection on 1-2 high-risk fire days with S0 UAS from locations identified through data denial experiments
4. Demonstrate and quantify forecasting improvements for low-level wind fields over 3-12 hours using sequential S0 flights for validation

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0 UAS**
- Small, purpose-built unmanned aircraft system
- Equipped (or planned to be equipped) with meteorological sensors
- Capable of targeted low-altitude wind and turbulence observations
- Designed for deployment in complex terrain near active wildfires
- Intended as demonstration platform; future concept includes outfitting other UAS and crewed aircraft with similar capabilities

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Use Case: Wildland Firefighting**
- Safety improvement for mixed crewed/uncrewed aircraft operations
- Support for nighttime air tanker operations (explicitly mentioned as currently dangerous/fatal risk)
- Enhanced operational planning for aerial firefighting assets
- Safe integration of UAS into aerial firefighting operations to build confidence among manned aircraft operators

**Secondary/Future Applications:**
- Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) in mountainous and urban corridor environments
- NASA Earth Science 3D-Winds mission support (per National Research Council decadal survey)

**Specific Trigger for Proposal:**
Fatal 2021 air tanker crash at night potentially caused by strong low-level turbulence, demonstrating critical need for accurate low-level wind forecasts.

## Key Technical Drivers

- **Data Gap Identified:** Observations of winds and turbulence in lowest 5,000 ft of atmosphere are severely limited, creating forecasting blind spot
- **UTM Integration Need:** Accurate wind/turbulence knowledge required for safe integration of manned and unmanned aircraft in complex airspace during firefighting operations
- **Operational Requirements:** Need for 3-12 hour wind/turbulence forecasts to enable safe operational planning
