# DAF X22.4 Phase II Follow On MOU Template

## Document Metadata
- Type: Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) Template
- Client/Agency: Department of the Air Force (DAF) / AFRL (Air Force Research Laboratory)
- Program/Solicitation: AFWERX SBIR Phase II, Topic AF22.4-CSO (Soil Moisture Mapping)
- Date: January 2023 (template; document created/modified October 23, 2023)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None explicitly named in this template document
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is a mandatory MOU template for DAF organizations to document their participation interest and support for SBIR Phase II Follow-On projects under AFWERX. The template establishes the framework for defining defense mission needs, funding commitments, stakeholder roles, milestones, security/cyber requirements, and Phase III transition strategies for small business innovations.

## Document Purpose & Scope
This is a **template document**, not a completed proposal. It provides the mandatory structure that DAF organizations must use when expressing interest in partnering with Phase I SBIR awardees for Phase II funding. Per the document: "The use of this template is MANDATORY; all elements MUST BE present and complete. Proposals without completed and signed memoranda accomplished IAW this template will be determined non-responsive to the solicitation and not considered for award."

## Key Sections & Requirements

### 1. Defense Mission Need (Section 2)
- Requires DAF organization to articulate specific national defense-related mission need
- Must include measurable goals and impact statements
- Documents how technology development will contribute to future mission fulfillment

### 2. Project Timeline (Section 3)
- Expected execution: October 2023 to June 2025
- Maximum Period of Performance (PoP): 21 months total
  - 18 months technical performance
  - 3 months for reports
  - Can be tailored shorter if appropriate

### 3. Funding (Section 4)
- SBIR Phase II funding: **Max $1,250,000**
- Government funding commitments NOT required for Phase II awards
- DAF Customer may supplement SBIR/STTR funds with own funds to increase scope
- Non-SBIR/STTR Federal matching and certain non-Federal funds eligible for Phase IIB enhancements
- Funds transfer to AFRL/RGF must occur within **30 calendar days after solicitation closing date**
- If funds don't arrive on time, Phase II award may not occur
- If Phase I company not selected, funds returned to providing DAF organization

### 4. Stakeholder Requirements (Section 8)
Required stakeholders with assigned responsibilities:

**Primary Roles:**
- End-User Organization (operationally benefits from solution)
- Customer Organization (acquires solution on behalf of End-User)

**Phase II Technical Points of Contact (TPOCs):**
- Primary: Must be DAF military or civilian personnel
- Alternate: May be from another organization with relevant expertise
- Support contractors/SETA contractors **cannot** be TPOCs
- Responsibilities include: receiving/accepting deliverables, providing feedback, overcoming execution barriers, driving Phase III transition

**Strategic Stakeholders:**
- MAJCOM or HQ function most directly impacted
- PEO or Center most directly impacted

**Staff Function Concurrences Required:**
- Security Office
- Information Assurance/Cybersecurity
- Finance (if Gov funding commitments)
- Contracting
- Strategy, Integration, & Requirements (A5)
- Small Business Office
- Commander
- Legal
- Engineering/SPO/Test
- Public Affairs
- Additional as necessary

### 5. Security & Access (Section 10)
Address whether SBIR company requires:
- Military installation/facility access
- Common Access Cards (CACs) for DoD network access
- Classified access via DD254

If required, DAF Customer/TPOC confirms they will:
- Sponsor individuals for base access
- Process CAC documentation
- Draft DD254 and facilitate classified access

### 6. Cybersecurity Requirements (Section 11)
If proposed solution involves software/IT connecting to DoD networks/systems or utilizing Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI):
- Likely requires Authorization to Operate (ATO) or Interim Authorization to Test (IATT)
- May utilize Platform One DevSecOps environments
- Must comply with NIST SP 800-171 Rev 1 and DFARS PGI 239.76
- Information Assurance/Cybersecurity Office coordinates with SBIR Company and Authorizing Official

### 7. UAS/Flight Testing (Section 12)
If Phase II work requires flight testing or UAS utilization:
- AFRL/DO has blanket waiver for procurement of COTS UAS for cyber analysis, signature analysis, system checkout, or profile data in benign/controlled RF environments
- Contractors need "use waiver" via Government TPOC
- Contract execution not dependent on use waiver completion, but early start recommended as it may impact milestone schedules

### 8. Phase III Transition (Section 6, 9)
- If Phase II successful, DAF Customer will consider transitioning innovations to Phase III contracts/subcontracts/other instruments
- Must address Phase III funding strategy, requirements personnel role, contract vehicle, and integration plan
- Subject to Government acquisition planning, funds availability, security assessments, and Small Business/Legal/Contracting reviews
- DAF Small Business Office advises on potential contracting/subcontracting opportunities

## Milestone Structure (Attachment 1a)

Maximum 15 milestones spanning 18 months technical work:
- Milestones tied to **measurable completion events** (not status reports)
- Examples provided:
  - Milestone 01: Kickoff + Analyze AF requirements (Award + 1 month, $50,000)
  - Milestone 02: Deliver 5 working prototypes (Award + 2 months, $100,000)
  - Milestone 03: Deliver 3 adapted commercial widgets (Award + 4 months, $250,000)
- Each milestone requires deliverable, acceptance criteria, and payment amount
- Acceptance criteria include demonstrated completion and customer acceptance

## Deliverables & Documentation Requirements

1. **Attachment 1a:** Schedule of Milestones (detailed above)
2. **Attachment 1b:** Non-proprietary Work Plan (to be reviewed before MOU signature, incorporated into Phase II award if selected)
3. **TPOC Designation Letter:** Required if proposal selected for potential award (template provided in attachments)

## Signature Requirements

Two required signatories:
1. **DAF Customer** (First MI Last, Rank/Grade, USAF, Duty Title)
2. **DAF End-User** (First MI Last, Rank/Grade, USAF, Duty Title)

Each signature requires brief justification of signatory's appropriateness for the effort.
