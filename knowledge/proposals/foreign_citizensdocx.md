## Executive Summary
This is a compliance/disclosure template document that instructs BST (and other SBIR/STTR offerors) on requirements for identifying and reporting foreign citizens, dual citizens, or individuals with work permits who will be involved in a DOD-funded SBIR/STTR project. The document emphasizes that failure to report such individuals can result in proposal rejection.

## Key Compliance Requirements
- **Mandatory Disclosure**: All foreign nationals (as defined in Section 3.5 of the CSO - Contracting Officer's Statement of Objectives) must be identified
- **Scope**: Applies to direct employees, subcontractors, and consultants
- **Required Information**: Country of origin, visa/work permit type, and anticipated level of involvement
- **Critical Note**: Dual citizenship and work permits do NOT automatically permit work on SBIR/STTR projects
- **Verification**: Offerors may be asked for additional information during negotiations to verify eligibility
- **Privacy Protection**: Supplemental information is protected under Privacy Act (5 U.S.C. 552a) and FOIA (5 U.S.C. 552(b)(6))

## Notable Details
- This is a frequently misunderstood requirement—offerors commonly assume work permits or dual citizenship provide automatic authorization
- Document emphasizes firms must report "any and all" foreign nationals to avoid proposal rejection
- A compliance table format is referenced for proposals with foreign national involvement
