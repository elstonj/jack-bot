## Executive Summary
This document provides instructions to NOAA TPO-funded grantees (including SBIR Phase I recipients) for completing Research Performance Progress Reports (RPPR). It clarifies federal reporting requirements specific to TPO projects, with key differences for SBIR Phase I grants: reporting frequency changes from semi-annual (6 months) to quarterly (3 months), and final reports follow grant-specific schedules rather than standard 90-day timelines.

## Document Purpose & Authority
- Derives from Department of Commerce (DOC) standard RPPR instructions
- Based on U.S. Office of Management and Budget (OMB) requirements (2 CFR 200.328)
- Provides TPO-specific clarifications and condensed guidance for brevity
- References the full DOC Instructions for additional details

## Key Reporting Requirements for SBIR Phase I Projects

### Reporting Frequency
- Standard multiyear projects: every 6 months
- SBIR Phase I projects (6-month duration): every 3 months
- Replace "6 months" with "3 months" throughout standard instructions for Phase I

### Interim Progress Reports (Clarification 1 - Section 1: Accomplishments)
- Report actual progress vs. proposed work plan during most recent reporting period
- Compare proposed vs. actual: tasks completed, milestones achieved, deliverables
- Use summary tables for clarity
- May include cumulative progress if current period is clearly distinguished

### Products Reporting (Clarification 2 - Section 2)
- Report all project products: publications, conference papers, presentations
- Include full references and Digital Object Identifiers (DOI)
- Either attach publications/presentations or provide web links
- Must credit NOAA/OAR/TPO and SBIR Program in all publications
- Required credit language: "This material is based upon work supported by the SBIR Program within the NOAA Technology Partnerships Office under Grant No. XXXXXXX."

### Changes/Problems (Clarification 3 - Section 5)
- Explain all deviations from original proposal (incomplete tasks, missed milestones, late deliverables)
- Detail mitigation plans and revised schedules
- All major task/schedule changes require pre-approval from TPO via formal request in Grants Online
- If no deviations, explicitly state that

### Budgetary Information (Clarification 5 - Section 7)
- Report whether project is on budget relative to proposed budget and schedule
- If not on budget, explain why and provide plan to get back on budget
- SF-425 form (separate) reports actual expenditures
- This section must be included in all progress reports despite omission from DOC PDF template

### Submission Requirements (Clarification 8)
- Submit only through NOAA's Grants Online system (not email)
- Use outline in Appendix A in place of DOC PDF fillable-form template
- Embed tables, figures, and graphical content within report document
- Convert final report to PDF format for upload to Grants Online
- Also cut-and-paste text portions into Grants Online web form text boxes
- Coordinate with institution's designated Grant Office Representative for submission
- Request they add lead PI email to receive automated deadline reminders

### NOAA Review (Clarification 9)
- Reviewers have 30 days to complete review
- If reviewer comments require revision, institution receives action through Grants Online
- Revisions must be submitted within 30 days of return
- Append all reviewer comments and PI responses in new "Responses to Reviewer Comments" section at end of revised report

### Final Reports (Clarification 10)
- Two reports due at grant completion:
  1. Semi-annual progress report (3-month for SBIR Phase I) covering most recent period—due 30 days after period end
  2. Final progress report covering entire grant performance period—due per schedule in official grant Specific Award Conditions (not standard 90-day timeline for Phase I)
- Both use identical RPPR format
- Final Report must be comprehensive, cumulative, and stand alone
- Final Report is the single most important deliverable; expected to be significantly longer than interim reports
- Must fully describe scientific approach, methods, results, and final conclusions

## RPPR Section Outline (Appendix A)

### Required Report Header Information
- Federal Agency: NOAA/OAR
- Federal Grant Award Number
- Project Title
- Project/Grant Period (Start and End Dates)
- Project Director/Principal Investigator names, titles, contact information
- Recipient Authorizing Official names, titles, contact information
- Recipient Organization (name, address, DUNS, EIN)
- Submission Date
- Current Reporting Period (start date, end date)
- Report Frequency (annual, semi-annual, quarterly)
- Final Annual Report? (yes or no)

### Section 1: Accomplishments
- State major project goals and objectives
- Describe accomplishments under each goal
- Compare planned vs. actual using summary tables
- Report on task completion status (percent complete, due dates)
- Compare proposed vs. actual milestones with dates
- Describe training and professional development opportunities provided
- Explain how results were disseminated to communities of interest
- Plan for next reporting period

### Section 2: Products
- Report completed products compared to proposed products in table format:
  - Publications, conference papers, presentations
  - Technologies or techniques
  - Inventions, patent applications, licenses
  - Other products (data, databases, software, models, curricula, instruments, new business creation, etc.)
- Include DOC required disclaimer language in all publications/presentations

### Section 3: Participants & Collaborating Organizations
- List individuals and organizations that worked on project
- Note any changes in PD/PI or senior/key personnel support
- Identify partner organizations
- Identify other collaborators or contacts

### Section 4: Impact
- Impact on principal discipline(s)
- Impact on other disciplines
- Impact on human resource development
- Impact on teaching and educational experiences
- Impact on physical, institutional, information resources/infrastructure
- Impact on technology transfer
- Impact on society beyond science and technology
- Percentage of award budget spent in foreign country(ies)

### Section 5: Changes/Problems
- Changes in approach and reasons
- Actual or anticipated problems/delays and mitigation plans
- Changes with significant budget impact
- Changes in human subjects, vertebrate animals, biohazards, or select agents
- Changes in primary performance site location

### Section 6: Special Reporting Requirements
- For TPO SBIR: Currently no special requirements (state if none apply)

### Section 7: Budgetary Information
- Confirm budget status
- Describe major budget anomalies or deviations
- Provide reasons and plan to return to budget

### Section 8: Project Outcomes
- Define project outcomes (broad public/societal benefits vs. concrete products)
- Report on performance measures defined in proposal
- Answer "So what?" and "Why should people care?" questions

### Section 9: Demographic Information
- Voluntary section for significant contributors (refer to DOC Instructions for details)

## Page Numbers & Format
- Include page numbers throughout all reports (Clarification 6)
- Joint progress reports not applicable to NOAA SBIR (Clarification 7)

## Notable Details
- Document acknowledges that DOC PDF fillable-form template and Grants Online web form have limitations (no embedded images/tables, restrictive character limits) as of April 2020
- TPO allows alternative approach using Appendix A outline until deficiencies resolved
- Instructions emphasize that Final Reports are the most critical deliverable and must be comprehensive, standing-alone documents
- Strict deadlines and pre-approval requirements for any project modifications
- Institutional Grant Office Representatives play key coordinating role in submissions and deadline management
