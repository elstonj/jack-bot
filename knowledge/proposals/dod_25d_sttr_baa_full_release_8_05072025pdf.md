## Executive Summary
This is the official Department of Defense Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) Program solicitation for fiscal year 2025. It establishes a three-phase funding program (Phase I, II, and III) for small business concerns partnering with research institutions to conduct R&D and commercialize results in defense-related areas. The BAA implements statutory requirements including security due diligence reviews, performance benchmarks for experienced firms, and alignment with OUSD(R&E) critical technology areas.

## Technical Approach
The STTR Program is structured as a three-phase effort:

**Phase I (6-12 months):** Establishes scientific, technical, and commercial merit and feasibility. Focus on research or R&D to prove concept viability and commercialization potential.

**Phase II (24 months):** Continues and further develops R&D from Phase I. Awards made based on Phase I results and Phase II proposal merit.

**Phase III:** Commercialization phase funded by non-STTR sources (private sector, non-STTR federal). SBCs develop prototype into viable product for military or private sector markets.

## Key Program Requirements

### Eligibility Requirements
- Proposing small business concerns (SBCs) must meet SBIR/STTR definitions per 13 CFR §§ 121-702 at time of award
- **Minimum 40%** of project work conducted by SBC; **minimum 30%** by single research institution (measured in direct and indirect costs)
- Principal investigator must have primary employment (>50% time) with SBC or research institution at award and during performance
- At least one SBC management employee not also employed by research institution
- All R&D work must be performed in the United States
- Joint ventures and limited partnerships permitted if qualifying as small business
- **Required:** Written agreement between SBC and research institution allocating intellectual property rights

### Performance Benchmarks (for experienced firms)
Per SBIR/STTR Extension Act of 2022, experienced SBCs with multiple prior awards must meet:
1. **Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate:** Addresses progression from Phase I to Phase II
2. **Commercialization Benchmark:** Measures progress toward commercialization post-Phase II

Consequences for failure to meet benchmarks:
- **Army:** Maximum 20 total Phase I and Direct-to-Phase II awards
- **Navy:** Maximum 20 total Phase I and Direct-to-Phase II awards
- **Air Force:** Maximum 20 total Phase I and Direct-to-Phase II awards
- **All other DoD Components combined:** Maximum 20 total Phase I and Direct-to-Phase II awards

### Critical Restrictions

**Ownership Restrictions:**
- SBCs with majority ownership by multiple venture capital operating companies (VCOCs), hedge funds, or private equity firms are generally ineligible (unless Service/Component otherwise authorizes)
- No single VCOC, hedge fund, or PE firm may own >50% unless that firm qualifies as small business and is >50% owned by U.S. citizens or permanent residents
- Foreign ownership/control/influence (FOCI) disclosure and training required

**Security Due Diligence:**
- DoD conducts risk-based due diligence review assessing:
  - Cybersecurity practices
  - Patent analysis
  - Employee analysis and foreign ownership
  - Financial ties and obligations
  - Employee ties to foreign countries/entities
- Unacceptable national security risks will result in proposal rejection regardless of technical merit
- Foreign affiliations/relationships must be disclosed in Volume 7 of proposal

**Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI):**
- Per FAR 9.5, proposers must identify and disclose all facts relevant to potential OCIs
- OCI mitigation plan required discussing conflicts in context of FAR 9.505-1 through 9.505-4
- Supplemental DoD policy prohibits concurrent provision of SETA, A&AS, or similar support and technical performance
- Current or recent (within 1 calendar year) support to DoD offices must be disclosed with mitigation plan

**Human Subject Research (HSR):**
- Must comply with applicable federal and state laws
- Federal assurance of compliance required (e.g., HHS OHRP federal-wide assurance)
- Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval required before human subject research begins
- IRB review: 1-3 months; DoD review: 3-6 months; no funding can be used until ALL approvals granted
- Personnel must provide documentation of completed training for human subject protection

**Animal Subject Research:**
- Must comply with applicable federal and agency rules on animal acquisition, care, handling, use
- Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC) approval required
- Secondary/HQ-level approval from DoD veterinarian required
- No animal research may be conducted until DoD office approves

**Research Involving Recombinant DNA:**
- Must comply with applicable federal, state law, regulation, and agency guidance
- Institutional biosafety committee approval required

### Proposal Submission & Certification Requirements

**Required Registrations:**
1. System for Award Management (SAM) – generates Unique Entity ID (UEI) and CAGE code
2. SBA Company Registry – requires SBC Control ID and Proof of Registration
3. Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP) – authenticated via Login.gov; firm admin creates firm PIN

**Timing:** Registration/DSIP access must be completed before proposal submission deadline (varies by Release; Release 8 closes June 25, 2025)

**Required Certifications (at proposal submission and award lifecycle):**
- Size, ownership, and STTR Program requirements compliance
- SBIR/STTR Extension Act of 2022 requirements
- Certifications required at: Phase I award, prior to Phase I final payment, prior to receiving 50% of Phase II award amount, prior to Phase II final payment

**Proposal Structure (7 Volumes via DSIP):**
1. Volume 1: Proposal Cover Sheet
2. Volume 2: Technical Volume
3. Volume 3: Cost Volume
4. Volume 4: Company Commercialization Report (CCR)
5. Volume 5: Supporting Documents
6. Volume 6: Fraud, Waste and Abuse Training
7. Volume 7: Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries

### Phase I Proposal Requirements

**Cover Sheet (Volume 1):**
- Brief technical abstract (max 3,000 characters)
- Anticipated benefits and potential commercial applications (max 3,000 characters)
- Percentage of work (POW) certification – must meet 40%/30% minimums; if not, proposal submission blocked
- POW automatically calculated from cost volume (Volume 3) entry

**Technical Volume (Volume 2):**
- Single PDF file, no encryption/password protection, no embedded videos/active media
- Page limit per Service/Component instructions
- 10-point font minimum, standard 8.5" x 11" with 1-inch margins
- Header on each page with: SBC name, topic number, DSIP-assigned proposal number
- **Required sections (in order):**
  1. Identification and Significance of Problem/Opportunity
  2. Phase I Technical Objectives
  3. Phase I Statement of Work
  4. Related Work
  5. Relationship with Future Research/R&D
  6. Commercialization Strategy
  7. Key Personnel
  8. Foreign Citizens
  9. Facilities/Equipment
  10. Subcontractors/Consultants
  11. Prior, Current, or Pending Support of Similar Proposals/Awards
  12. Identification and Assertion of Restrictions on Government's Use, Release, Disclosure of Technical Data/Computer Software
- Template provided in Appendix A

**Cost Volume (Volume 3):**
- Complete via DSIP cost form
- List all key personnel with individual hours as direct labor
- Equipment/material carefully reviewed for need and appropriateness
- Special tooling/test equipment must be advantageous to U.S. Government and directly relate to topic
- Travel costs must be justified and project-related
- Cost sharing permitted but not required; not an evaluation factor
- All subcontractor/consultant costs (labor, travel, equipment, materials) detailed at same level as prime costs
- Detailed cost substantiation required if selected for award negotiation

**Company Commercialization Report (Volume 4):**
- SBCs with prior DoD/non-DoD Phase I/II SBIR/STTR awards must download CCR PDF from SBIR.gov and upload to DSIP "Firm Forms"
- Firm admin manages CCR for all proposals
- SBA requires update upon final deliverable completion; voluntary annual updates for minimum 5 years
- **WARNING:** Uploading new CCR in "Firm Forms" reopens ALL proposals under any open BAAs/CSOs; affected proposals must be recertified and resubmitted

### Evaluation & Selection

**Evaluation Criteria (per Section 4.2):**
- Scientific/technical merit
- Commercialization potential
- Cost reasonableness
- Organizational capability
- Other criteria per Service/Component instructions

**Status & Feedback:** Post-award status updates available via DSIP; pre-award and post-award protests allowed

**Award Conditions:**
- DoD not obligated to make any Phase I, II, or III awards
- All awards subject to risk-based due diligence security review and funds availability
- DoD not responsible for monies spent before award issuance
- Multiple proposals on same topic may be funded; DoD may fund none or several approaches

### Post-Award Requirements

**Intellectual Property:**
- SBC must execute written agreement with research institution allocating IP rights and right to conduct follow-on R&D/commercialization
- Agreement must comply with SBA SBIR/STTR Policy Directive §8(d) and DFARS 252.227-7040
- Copyrights: Owned per contract terms
- Patents: Rights per DFARS 252.227-7039
- Technical Data Rights: Per DFARS 252.227-7013
