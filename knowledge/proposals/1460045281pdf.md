## Executive Summary
This disclosure documents modifications made to the Lobe Differencing Correlating Radiometer (LDCR) during Phase II development to prepare it for flight testing on an unmanned aircraft system. The work addressed three critical integration issues: electromagnetic noise contamination, insufficient output voltage range for analog-to-digital conversion, and temperature-dependent measurement drift. Solutions included power filtering, amplification stages, and thermistor-based temperature monitoring.

## Technical Approach

### Primary Problems Addressed
1. **Noise contamination** - Output noise was 6x higher than expected, traced to the analog correlator board
2. **Voltage range limitation** - Analog correlator output voltage was insufficient for data logger A/D converter resolution
3. **Temperature drift** - Radiometer gain varies with temperature, requiring compensation for accurate measurements

### Solutions Implemented

**Noise Mitigation:**
- Added LC filter to input power voltage
- Inserted IF gain amplifiers between LDCR output and analog correlator
- Built low-pass filter at analog correlator output (1.5ms time constant)
- Enhanced power supply isolation from RF interference:
  - Two parallel decoupling capacitors (0.1 µF and 22 µF) with 1 µH series inductor at DC feed-through
  - Shielded twisted triple wires for DC power supply lines
  - 0.1 µF capacitor pairs in parallel on TEC (thermoelectric cooler) heating/cooling pins

**Voltage Scaling:**
- Added video amplifier between low-pass filter output and A/D input of data logger

**Temperature Compensation:**
- Installed 5 thermistors on LDCR receiving board to monitor:
  - TEC on channel 1
  - TEC on channel 2
  - Isolator
  - Local oscillator
  - IF signal filter

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Lobe Differencing Correlating Radiometer (LDCR)
- **What it is:** L-band passive microwave radiometer instrument designed for soil moisture measurement
- **Original development:** NASA NESSF Award NNX12AO08H
- **Use case in this project:** Integrated into small UAS platform for soil moisture mapping; paired with MiCo antenna
- **Performance capability:** Enables soil moisture measurements at 15-meter resolution (vs. ~40 km from SMAP satellite)
- **Key specifications:** L-band receiver with analog correlator architecture; temperature-sensitive gain requiring monitoring and correction

### Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS
- **What it is:** Small unmanned aircraft system equipped with LDCR and MiCo antenna for measuring soil moisture
- **Flight test:** Conducted September 8, 2015, in Canton, Oklahoma
- **Ground testing:** Outdoor ground tests completed October 6, 2014

## Use Cases & Applications

1. **SMAP Satellite Validation and Enhancement**
   - Provides ground-truth validation data for NASA's Soil Moisture Active Passive (SMAP) satellite mission
   - Augments SMAP science data products with finer (15m vs. 40km) resolution in specific areas

2. **Watershed and Drought Monitoring**
   - High-resolution soil moisture data near watersheds
   - Local soil moisture information in drought-affected areas
   - Supports FEMA in understanding flash flood vulnerability

3. **Flood Prediction and Early Warning**
   - Historical application: 2007 NASA-funded research mapping soil moisture in North Texas/Oklahoma flood-affected regions
   - Data used to provide flood alerts and warnings to vulnerable areas
   - Cost-effective alternative to manned aircraft operations

## Key Development Timeline
- **May 4, 2014:** Local oscillator leakage identified; initial power circuit filter design work began

- **October 6, 2014:** Outdoor ground testing of updated LDCR

- **September 8, 2015:** First successful flight test (Canton, Oklahoma)
