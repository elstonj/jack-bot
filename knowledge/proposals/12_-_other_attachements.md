## Document Type & Purpose
This is a guidance document outlining requirements for Field 12 (Other Attachments) of a USDA NIFA SBIR Phase I application. It specifies what documentation BST needed to include with their soil moisture mapping UAS proposal.

## Key Requirements for Application Attachments

### 1. Facility/Equipment Use Letters
- Required if using university, private, or government lab facilities
- Must include letter from authorized representative confirming exclusive use and control

### 2. Outside Services Documentation
- Consultants, subcontractors, or CRADA partnerships permitted
- Maximum 1/3 of grant funds can be used for outside services
- Requires letters of commitment from all proposed outside parties

### 3. Letters of Support (Strongly Encouraged)
- Should demonstrate market validation and technical support
- Must show evidence of stakeholder dialogue (potential customers, partners, investors)
- Should include affiliation and contact information for signatories
- Recommended limit: 2 pages per letter
- If not included, applicant must provide 2-page justification addressing technical and commercial considerations
- Must be submitted in PDF format

### 4. Project Duration Justification
- Phase I projects normally limited to 8 months
- If exceeding 8 months, requires written justification
- Maximum allowable duration: 20 months

### 5. Subsidiary Documentation (if applicable)
- Parent company small business status must be demonstrated
- Parent company must reside in U.S. and cannot be nonprofit
- Subsidiary must document independent viable financial status

### 6. Federal Debt Delinquency Statement
- Required if applicant is delinquent on federal debts
- Must include detailed explanatory information in PDF format

### 7. Non-Domestic Performance Explanation
- Budget justification required for foreign travel
- Must specify: purpose, destination, method of travel, number of persons, duration, estimated cost
- All foreign travel requires prior written USDA NIFA approval
