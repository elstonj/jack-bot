- Date: July 5, 2013

- Key Personnel: Jack Elston, PhD (President, Black Swift Technologies)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies submits a proposal response to the WWF Wildlife Crime Technology Project, positioning itself as an experienced provider of affordable, reliable small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS) for conservation applications. The company highlights partnerships with Rocketship Systems Inc., Autonomous Systems Research, and Conservation Drones, as well as prior testing with Kenya Wildlife Services, to demonstrate capability in deploying sUAS to combat illegal wildlife poaching.

## Technical Approach
BST proposes leveraging existing partnerships and collaborative expertise in sUAS design and deployment. The approach emphasizes:
- Turn-key sUAS solutions requiring minimal training for end users
- Integration of proven platforms (SwiftSentinel) with operational experience in wildlife conservation contexts
- Deployment support through partner organizations already active in target regions
