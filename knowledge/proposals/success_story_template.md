# Success Story Template

## Document Metadata
- Type: Template/Form (for NASA SBIR success story documentation)
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research)
- Date: Created/Modified 2015-05-26
- Original Context: 2012 NASA SBIR Phase II - Soil Moisture project
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is a blank template/form used by NASA to document success stories from SBIR-funded companies. It guides companies in presenting innovations developed through SBIR funding, covering technical achievements, commercialization progress, and government/science applications.

## Document Structure & Required Information Sections

### Innovation Section
- Brief description of the innovation or new technology/application in clear, plain language
- Accompanying photo/graphic

### Accomplishments
**Technical:**
- Development stage (prototype, small production runs, ongoing commercial operations)
- Significant technical achievements to date
- Patents and patent applications

**Business:**
- Noteworthy business development
- Cumulative private capital investment by firm (if not commercially sensitive)
- Evidence of private sector commitment

### Commercialization
- Unique competitive advantage vs. existing solutions
- Product/service name and trademark
- Primary target market sectors (actual vs. potential)
- Current sales status
- Cumulative sales revenues (if not commercially sensitive)
- Associated revenues (licensing fees, royalties, technology sell-off)
- Customer interest demonstrated
- Remaining development needed for commercial application
- Venture structure (new profit center, spinoff, joint venture, strategic alliance)
- Partner/spinoff firm names (if not commercially sensitive)
- Full-time job equivalents associated with venture

### Government/Science Applications
- Product/service description for NASA and USG applications (actual vs. potential)
- Remaining development needed for NASA/USG applications
- Identify specific NASA and other USG missions utilizing the product/service
- Demonstrated NASA/USG customer interest
- NASA and other USG Phase III contracts with dollar amounts (if possible)
- Estimated NASA mission/project cost savings due to SBIR innovation

### Required Metadata
- Points of Contact (NASA and Company representatives with phone numbers)
- Program Year and SBIR Phase
- NASA Contract Number (NAS #)
- NASA Field Center
- Date of Update
- Success Story Number

## Notable Details
- This template emphasizes documentation of both technical AND business accomplishments
- Distinction between "actual" vs. "potential" applications/markets is important throughout
- Sensitivity to commercial confidentiality is noted multiple times (proprietary information can be omitted)
- Template is designed to demonstrate SBIR program ROI and effectiveness to government stakeholders

**Note:** This is a template document with no substantive content about Black Swift Technologies products or capabilities. It provides the structure that BST or other SBIR companies would use to report their success stories to NASA.
