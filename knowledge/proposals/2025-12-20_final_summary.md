## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies conducted flight testing and design work on the Mustang 2 aircraft (formerly called "Chilli"), including final validation flights with optimized propellers and new fuselage design. Concurrent work began on Mustang 3 wing design, which is projected to deliver 16-17% aerodynamic improvements over the current Chilli platform.

## Technical Approach

### Mustang 2 (Chilli) Flight Testing
- Conducted 4 final test flights on 12/10/2025 and 12/12/2025
- Tested with 17x13 propeller (identified as most efficient)
- Configurations tested: 35 lbs and 40 lbs total weight
- Old and new fuselage designs compared in same atmospheric conditions
- Measured power consumption, throttle settings, and efficiency across all flights

### Mustang 3 Wing Design
- New 4.5m wingspan with 25:1 aspect ratio (high aspect ratio design)
- Aerodynamic improvements achieved through refined design geometry
- Potential for 20% scaling while maintaining similar wing loading to Chilli

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Mustang 2 Aircraft ("Chilli")
- **Purpose**: Long-endurance demonstration platform
- **Configuration**: Modular design allowing weight/fuselage variations
- **Propulsion**: 17x13 propeller (optimal configuration)
- **Test weights**: 35-40 lbs total airframe weight
- **Performance data** (12/12/2025 new fuselage flight at 35 lbs):
  - Efficiency: 9.6
  - Power requirement at 30 m/s: 477 W
  - Represents significant improvement over old fuselage (7.7 efficiency, 589 W)

### Mustang 3 Aircraft (Planned)
- **Wing design**: 4.5m span, 25:1 aspect ratio
- **Aerodynamic gains**:
  - 17% improvement in L/D (lift-to-drag ratio)
  - 14% improvement in minimum sink rate
  - Achieved at approximately same velocity as Chilli wing
  - Performance gains with equal parasitic drag
- **Target weight**: 35 lbs (baseline) or 40 lbs+ for extended range
- **Projected range**: 400+ km at 35-36 lbs with better batteries; 400 km at 40 lbs with less expensive batteries
- **Scalability option**: 20% larger wing would reduce parasitic drag by 8-10% while maintaining similar wing loading

## Key Results

**Fuselage Optimization (Mustang 2)**:
| Date | Weight | Fuselage | Efficiency | Power @ 30 m/s | Notes |
|------|--------|----------|------------|----------------|-------|
| 12/10/2025 | 35 lbs | Old | 7.5 | 602 W | Propeller validation |
| 12/12/2025 | 40 lbs | Old | 8.5 | 605 W | Airframe at 40 lbs |
| 12/12/2025 | 35 lbs | New | 9.6 | 477 W | **New fuselage** |
| 12/12/2025 | 35 lbs | Old | 7.7 | 589 W | Atmospheric control |

**Key Finding**: New fuselage design delivers ~28% efficiency improvement (9.6 vs. 7.5) and ~19% power reduction (477 W vs. 602 W) at same weight, directly supporting range targets in planned battery analysis.

**Mustang 3 Wing Performance vs. Chilli**:
- L/D improvement: +17%
- Minimum sink rate improvement: +14%
- Velocity range: Comparable to Chilli (enabling equal parasitic drag)
