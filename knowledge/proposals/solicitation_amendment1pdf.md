## Executive Summary
Amendment 1 to the DAF SBIR CSO AFX24.7 Phase I clarifies applicant responsibilities regarding NIST SP 800-171 cybersecurity assessments and provides detailed instructions for completing self-assessments and submitting scores to the Supplier Performance Risk System (SPRS). The amendment emphasizes that failure to properly submit NIST 800-171 documentation may result in proposal disqualification. This is a broad, open-topic solicitation seeking innovative dual-purpose technologies with clear Air Force stakeholder needs.

## Solicitation Overview
**Topic Title:** Phase I Open Call for Innovative Defense Related Dual-Purpose Technologies/Solutions with a Clear Air Force Stakeholder Need

**Funding & Timeline:**

## Critical Technical Requirements (Amendment 1 Changes)

### NIST SP 800-171 Assessment Requirements
Amendment 1 adds mandatory cybersecurity compliance procedures:

**Self-Assessment Process:**
1. Complete NIST SP 800-171 self-assessment at https://www.projectspectrum.io/#/cyberreadiness-Check
2. Register/login and navigate to "NIST 800-171" section
3. Save completed assessment with score and submission date
4. Email results to WEBPTSMH.fct@navy.mil with required information per DFARS 252.204-7019(d)(1)
5. Confirm score is published in PIEE's SPRS (Supplier Performance Risk System) module in Procurement Integrated Enterprise Environment

**Consequences:**
- Failure to ensure NIST SP 800-171 score is published in PIEE's SPRS module → **proposal disqualification**
- Failure to provide additional information per DFARS 252.204-7019 → **proposal disqualification**

This requirement added to both Section 5(h)(i) and Section 3(j) of the solicitation.

## Evaluation Criteria (Equal Importance)

### a. Commercialization Potential
- Small business concern's (SBC) commercialization record (pre-sales, pilots, sales, revenue, active users, subscriptions, downloads, adoption metrics)
- Market size and revenue potential
- Clarity of business plan
- Evidence of Phase II follow-on commitments from Government (DAF Customer Memorandum) or private sources (letters of commitment)

### b. Defense Need
- Appropriateness, relevance, and specificity of identified Defense Need
- Letters of support from Government personnel (supporting but not required)
- Adequacy of proposed effort relative to Defense Need fulfillment
- Level of defense mission impact and urgency
- Breadth of applicability across DoD/Government

### c. Technical Approach
- Clarity of problem statement
- Soundness and technical merit of proposed approach
- Innovation and differentiation from current alternatives
- Qualifications of Principal Investigator/Project Manager, supporting staff, and consultants

**Cost Consideration:** All evaluation criteria combined are significantly more important than cost/price. Cost considered when technical evaluations are essentially equal.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Small Business Concern (SBC) Definition
- Maximum 500 employees (including affiliates)
- More than 50% directly owned and controlled by:
  - U.S. citizens or permanent resident aliens, OR
  - Other eligible small businesses, OR
  - Indian tribes, Alaska Native Corporations (ANC), or Native Hawaiian Organizations (NHO), OR
  - Multiple venture capital operating companies (VCOCs), hedge funds, or private equity firms

### Ownership Restrictions
- No single VCOC, hedge fund, or private equity firm may own >50% unless that entity itself qualifies as small business >50% owned by U.S. citizens
- SBCs with majority ownership by multiple VCOCs/hedge funds/private equity eligible but capped at 15% of Air Force SBIR budget

### Denial of Award Triggers
DAF will NOT award if applicant:
- Has owner/covered individual in malign foreign talent recruitment program
- Has business entity, parent company, or subsidiary in People's Republic of China or designated foreign country of concern
- Has owner/covered individual with foreign affiliation to foreign entity in PRC or country of concern
- And these relationships interfere with DoD activities, create duplication, present conflicts of interest, were not disclosed, violate Federal law, or pose national security risk

### Performance of Work Requirement
- Minimum 2/3 of Phase I R&D work must be performed by proposing firm (measured by direct + indirect costs, excluding profit)
- Deviations require prior written approval from DAF Contracting Officer (Daniel.Brewer.13@us.af.mil)

### Principal Investigator Requirements
- Only one PI/Project Manager per proposal
- Primary employment must be with small business firm (>50% time)
- Precludes full-time employment elsewhere
- May be replaced subject to contracting officer approval

### Performance Benchmarks for Repeat Applicants
Companies with multiple prior SBIR/STTR awards must meet minimum performance standards:
- Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate requirement
- Commercialization Benchmark requirement
- Firms failing benchmarks capped at 80 total awards from DoD

**Self-attestation required:** Applicants must confirm eligibility in DSIP; DAF will not consider proposals from ineligible applicants.

### Location of Work
- R&D work must be performed in United States (50 states, territories, DC, Puerto Rico, Marshall Islands, Micronesia, Palau)
- Rare exceptions may be approved for non-U.S. work if materials/supplies unavailable domestically; requests must be submitted with initial proposal

## Export Control & ITAR Compliance

**Requirements:**
- Applicants must review U.S. Munitions List and determine ITAR/Export Administration Regulations (EAR) applicability
- If ITAR-restricted, submit certified DD Form 2345 (Military Critical Technology Agreement)
- Non-U.S. citizens may only perform work if "Lawfully Admitted for Permanent Residence" or designated "Protected Individual" per 8 U.S.C. 1101(20) and 1324b(a)(3)
- Proposals with non-U.S. citizens not meeting above categories may be rejected

## Security & Due Diligence

**DoD Risk Assessment Program (SBIR and STTR Extension Act of 2022):**
- DoD will review all proposals for security risks
- Assessment includes: cybersecurity practices, patent analysis, employee analysis, foreign ownership
- Financial ties and obligations (surety, equity, debt) to foreign countries/entities assessed
- Open-source analysis and analytical tools used for nondisclosure detection
- If risks identified: DoD may create mitigation plan OR deny award based on totality of circumstances
