## Executive Summary
This year 1 report documents Black Swift Technologies' progress using Colorado Advanced Industries funding to commercialize soil moisture mapping small unmanned aircraft and enabling technologies. The grant funding accelerated commercialization efforts for the SuperSwift airframe and SwiftCore Flight Management System, resulting in follow-on NASA funding, commercial sales, and expansion of SwiftCore capabilities to support additional applications and partner companies.

## Grant Obligations & Progress

### Obligation 1: Commercialize Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS and Core Technologies
**Status:** Significant progress achieved

Key accomplishments:
- Secured follow-on NASA funding: $125,000 to support soil moisture system
- Secured deployment contract: $35,090
- Generated SuperSwift airframe sales revenues: $55,000
- Obtained formal export classification for key products, enabling overseas sales
- Funding accelerated commercialization timeline and enabled bootstrapping during FAA regulatory uncertainty

### Obligation 2: Expand SwiftCore Flight Management System Availability
**Status:** Two primary pathways executed

**Pathway 1 - Vertical Takeoff/Landing (VTOL) Support:**
- Customized SwiftCore capabilities for companies with aircraft requiring vertical takeoff/landing
- Expected to support at least three Colorado companies bringing systems to market
- Additional customers outside Colorado identified

**Pathway 2 - SwiftTrainer Development:**
- Created end-to-end entry-level mapping system based on SwiftCore FMS
- Targeted at surveyor market with majority of early customers in Colorado
- Generated revenues from both pathways: >$60,000 with additional sales pipeline

## Technical Difficulties & Challenges
- **FAA Regulatory Delay:** Significant impediment to market entry; regulatory framework for commercial UAS operations undefined during most of grant period. Report notes FAA was "days away" from introducing reasonable regulations at time of reporting.
- **Commercialization Complexity:** Noted as inherent difficulty in bringing cutting-edge technologies to market
- **Mitigation:** Grant funding enabled company to bootstrap, focus on customer-driven development, and work through regulatory uncertainty
