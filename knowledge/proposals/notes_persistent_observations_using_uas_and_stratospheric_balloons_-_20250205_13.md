## Executive Summary
NOAA, NASA, and Black Swift Technologies met to explore integrating BST's S0 drone with Aerostar high-altitude balloons for on-demand data collection in hurricane monitoring, wildfire surveillance, and maritime traffic monitoring. The team identified technical feasibility of stratospheric deployment and discussed funding pathways through NASA Flight Opportunities Program, AFRL, and Southcom.

## Technical Approach
- **Integration Method**: Deploy S0 drone from a gondola attached to Aerostar's stratospheric balloon
- **Launch Mechanism**: Vertical tube arrangement within gondola to enable automated launch without manual drone manipulation
- **Environmental Adaptation**: Address high-altitude stratospheric conditions for both drone and deployment mechanism
- **Communication Architecture**: Hybrid line-of-sight and satellite communication system for drone-to-gondola and data transmission
- **Design Support**: Potential collaboration with NASA Langley for dispenser system design and construction

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 Drone
- Operationally deployable UAS designed for high-altitude release from stratospheric platform
- Capable of on-demand data collection operations
- Can function in stratospheric deployment conditions with appropriate mechanical interface

## Use Cases & Applications
1. **Hurricane Monitoring** – Real-time observations and data collection during storm events
2. **Wildfire Surveillance** – Monitoring active fire progression and spread
3. **Maritime Traffic Monitoring** – Ship traffic observation and tracking
4. Additional government agency applications under discussion

## Key Results
This was a planning/alignment meeting with no experimental results. Outcome was agreement on technical feasibility and identification of next steps.

## Notable Details
- **Deployment Strategy**: Vertical tube configuration chosen to avoid drone manipulation in extreme high-altitude conditions (practical engineering solution)
- **Funding Model Discussion**: Explored per-drone usage fees or data-based sales models
- **NOAA Position**: Expressed interest but indicated unlikely to be primary development funder
- **Proposed Lead**: NASA civil servant to lead Flight Opportunities Program proposal with NOAA and Langley collaboration
- **Timeline**: White paper development underway for dissemination to AFRL, Southcom, and NASA FOP
- **Partner Organization**: Aerostar/Raven Systems for balloon platform; Stratospheric Balloon contact (Mark Ketchum) to be engaged
