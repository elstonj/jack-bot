# RUNWAY INTEGRITY VALIDATION THROUGH SOIL MOISTURE MEASUREMENTS

## Executive Summary
BST proposes to adapt its soil moisture mapping radiometer sensor payload for rapid, high-resolution (~15m) collection of soil integrity data to validate aircraft landing zones. Flown on small unmanned aircraft, the system will enable the USAF to remotely assess whether runways, airstrips, and roads can safely support cargo aircraft operations like C-130 landings without requiring boots-on-the-ground reconnaissance in denied areas.

## Technical Approach
- **Sensor Type**: Radiometer that directly measures soil moisture through plant canopy
- **Resolution**: ~15m spatial resolution
- **Platform**: Small unmanned aircraft
- **Key Capability**: Computes maximum pressure soil can support based on moisture measurements
- **Operational Concept**: Rapid collection and dissemination of soil condition mapping to forward air controllers

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Soil Moisture Mapping Sensing Payload
- **What it is**: Radiometer sensor for remote soil moisture measurement
- **Technical capability**: Direct moisture measurement through vegetation canopy; calculates soil bearing capacity
- **Proposed use**: Mounted on small UAS to rapidly map landing zone soil integrity
- **Performance claims**: ~15m resolution; provides "safe vs. unsafe ground conditions" classification for cargo aircraft

## Use Cases & Applications

### DOD Primary Mission
- Remote validation of airstrip and road soil integrity for USAF operations
- Eliminate need for special operations infiltration to assess denied areas
- Enable global reach by de-risking forward operating base landing zone assessments
- Support cargo aircraft operations (e.g., C-130) with landing zone fidelity

### Dual-Use Commercial Applications
- **Rail**: Monitor rail bed integrity post-precipitation to prevent derailment from ballast failure
- **Emergency Management**: Pre-event flash flood risk assessment; post-event landslide and rockfall prediction and mitigation

### Government (Non-DOD)
- Army Corps of Engineers use case for heavy vehicle tracking and aircraft landing operations assessment

## Key Technical Rationale
Soil moisture is a primary variable controlling soil integrity. As moisture increases, soil becomes more slippery and sticky while losing strength—directly affecting bearing capacity for aircraft and vehicles. Remote measurement eliminates operational risk and infiltration requirements.
