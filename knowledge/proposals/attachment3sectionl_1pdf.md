## Executive Summary
This is Section L of an AFRL AMAC IDIQ solicitation establishing procurement instructions and evaluation criteria for multiple-award Science & Technology (S&T) contracts. The solicitation seeks prime contractors with demonstrated S&T performance across four technical domains: Air, Space, Cyberspace, and Cross-Cutting Technologies. Offerors will be evaluated on technical experience and small business participation commitment, with no specific funding currently available.

## Notable Details

### Contract Structure
- **IDIQ Contract:** Multiple awards anticipated; specific pricing, contract types, delivery requirements, and performance periods determined at Delivery Order (DO)/Task Order (TO) level
- **Funding Status:** No funds currently available; solicitation may be canceled without obligation to reimburse offerors
- **Contract Validity:** 180 days from proposal submission

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Factor 1 (60% weight, implied):** Technical Experience across four domain subfactors (1.1–1.4)
  - Offerors submit self-scoring worksheets identifying past/active federal contracts demonstrating prime contractor performance
  - Single contract can support multiple areas of interest; multiple contracts for same area count only once
  - Supporting documentation: SF 30, 1449, or equivalent contract documents + requirements documentation
  
- **Factor 2 (40% weight, implied):** Small Business Participation Commitment Document (SBPCD)
  - Required from all offerors (including small businesses)
  - Must list participating small businesses and socioeconomic categories (SB, SDB, WOSB, HUBZone, SDVOSB)
  - Must include NAICS and PSC codes

### Small Business Pool
- Small businesses qualifying under NAICS codes 541713, 541714, 541715 will be designated to a small business pool
- Government may set aside individual task orders for competition within this pool
- Small Business Subcontracting Plan Goals (for non-SB prime contractors):
  - Small Business: 33% of subcontracted value
  - Small Disadvantaged Business: 12%
  - Women-Owned Small Business: 8%
  - Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned: 5%
  - HUBZone: 1%

### Submission Requirements
- **Format:** PDF and Excel (AMAC Proposal Response Guide template provided)
- **Volumes:**
  - Volume I: Company information, contract documentation (SF 33), Reps & Certs
  - Volume II: Technical Experience Factor (self-scoring worksheets + supporting contracts/requirements docs)
  - Volume III: Small Business Subcontracting Plan
- **Submission:** Electronic via Enterprise Portal; late submissions not accepted per FAR 15.208
- **Page Limits:** Specified in Table 2.3; excess pages removed by PCO

### Key Restrictions
- **Foreign Ownership:** Foreign firms or U.S. companies under Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence (FOCI) prohibited
- **No Consulting/A&AS:** Contract vehicle restricted to S&T; Consulting and Advisory & Assistance work excluded
- **Duplicate Proposals:** Identical proposals considered non-responsive
- **One Subsidiary per CAGE Code:** Offerors limited to one subsidiary/business unit; each must submit separate proposal
- **Joint Ventures Allowed:** Joint ventures permitted as single-party offerors; contracts from individual JV members count toward total score

### Points of Contact
- **Government PCO:** afrl.amac.mailbox@us.af.mil
- **Communications:** PCO is sole point of contact; all industry exchanges controlled by PCO
- **Encrypted email required** for source selection information
