## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes conducting field testing and methane detection studies across multiple years, targeting orphaned well sites with public land access in Colorado, South Dakota, Montana, and Arizona. The company has identified 50+ accessible well sites and is actively engaging with Indigenous tribes (Osage Nation, Sioux Nation, Crow Nation, Arizona Navajo tribe) to conduct studies on tribal lands while fostering economic development in oil and gas infrastructure regions.

## Technical Approach
- Uses S2 UAS for methane detection flights at altitudes lower than FAA 14 CFR Part 107 criteria
- All BST pilots and technicians are Part 107 certified to operate in all conditions and altitudes
- Initial strategy prioritizes orphaned well sites accessible via public roads and flying above public lands to avoid complex permitting
- Prospective expansion to tribal lands with formal partnership agreements

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- Unmanned aerial system capable of flying at low altitudes for methane detection
- Pilots and operators are FAA Part 107 certified
- Can conduct field detection studies in various operational contexts

## Use Cases & Applications

### Methane Detection and Monitoring
- **Orphaned Wells**: Detection studies at abandoned well sites in Colorado's San Luis Valley and Pawnee National Grasslands
- **Geographic Focus**: 
  - San Luis Valley, Colorado (50+ identified sites with public access)
  - Pawnee National Grasslands, northeastern Colorado
  - Potential expansion to South Dakota, Montana, and Arizona
- **Economic Development**: Tribal partnership focus aims to enhance methane detection capabilities while supporting economic development in regions with significant oil and gas infrastructure
