## Executive Summary

AREN is a NASA Science Activation initiative focused on training the next generation of scientists, engineers, and professionals through experiential learning with kite-borne instrumentation (AEROKATS) and instrumented mobile platforms (ROVERs). In Year 9 (2023-2024), the program reached participants across diverse settings including K-12 schools, universities, indigenous communities in Arctic Alaska, and public citizen science events. The project operates through 10 partner institutions and achieved significant milestones in technology development, educator training, and community engagement while advancing toward commercialization of key platforms.

## Technical Approach

### Education & Training Model
- **Two primary flagship events:** TerraROVER workshop (May) and Summer Institute (July-August)
- **Virtual training delivery** for geographically dispersed educators (35-46 participants in main events)
- **Tiered engagement:** Individual consultations, small group training, large public events, formal/informal education
- **Train-the-trainer model** emphasizing sustainable implementation in partner institutions

### Technology Development Approach
- **Iterative hardware maturation:** Progression from in-house manufacturing to commercial production readiness
- **Lightweight platform philosophy:** MiniCam <40g enables use on smaller, more manageable kites
- **Modular instrumentation:** UHIP (Universal Hardware Instrumentation Package) provides flexible sensor integration
- **Production optimization:** Transitioning from 3D printing to injection-molded components and domestic commercial manufacturing partners
- **Version control & documentation:** Comprehensive operational manuals, tutorial videos, and lesson plans

### Integration with Broader NASA Mission
- Alignment with 4 NASA SMD Science Activation Mid-Level Objectives (MLOs)
- Focus on diversity, STEM career development, and community science
- Cross-collaboration with other SciAct initiatives (GLOBE, NEBP, PLACES)

## Products & Capabilities Described

### MiniCam Aeropod
- **What it is:** Lightweight (<40g) kite aerial imaging platform with fixed camera system
- **Evolution:** Progressed from V1 to V4 with improvements in durability, functionality, image quality
- **Latest upgrades:** Color correction filter for high-quality imagery from low-cost camera
- **Production:** ~80 units provided for Summer Institute and partner use in 2024
- **Uses:** Remote sensing education, community science, citizen science applications
- **Advantages:** Simple, affordable, easily flies on smaller kites, accessible for inexperienced participants
- **Commercial status:** Licensed for external production; prototypes from Great Winds Kite Co. (GWK) also deployed

### MiniTwinCam
- **What it is:** Lightweight iteration of dual-camera concept with different optical filters
- **Purpose:** Basic spectral comparisons from aerial platform
- **Status:** In development; prototype tested with favorable results at ISARRA conference

### TerraROVER (Terrestrial Remote Observation Vehicle)
- **What it is:** Wheeled ground platform for close-range remote sensing and thermal imaging
- **Status:** Approaching mature configuration; 40 units produced in 2024
- **Design updates:** Simplified for reduced production time/costs; purchased wheels and outsourced polymer decks (moved from in-house 3D printing)
- **Instrumentation:** Integrates with UHIP V2 (Universal Hardware Instrumentation Package) with FLIR thermal camera
- **Primary mission:** Urban Heat Island effect mapping and surface temperature measurement
- **Production evolution:** Boston University engineering capstone project investigating scaling and kit-based solutions
- **Commercialization:** Production transitioning to domestic commercial partner

### UHIP (Universal Hardware Instrumentation Package)
- **What it is:** Modular sensor integration platform for TerraROVER
- **Current versions:** UHIP V1 and UHIP V2 (newer)
- **Core capability:** Surface temperature mapping and data visualization
- **Developer:** James Moon-Dupree (RESA) with support from BST team
- **Future development:** TRIP (Totally Remote Instrumentation Package) using Raspberry Pi 5 with web-based user interface and autonomous operation capability

### Aeropod Systems (Various Payloads)
- **Standard imaging:** MiniCam for basic aerial photography
- **Atmospheric profiling:** Kestrel Profiler AeroPod (flagship high-capability option)
- **Air quality monitoring:** AtmoTube particle sensors, AtmoTubePro
- **Weather measurement:** PocketLab Weather Profiler AeroPod (entry-level atmospheric profiler in development)
- **Spectral imaging:** MiniTwinCam (dual-camera with different filters)
- **Concept stage:** AeroAcq Zero (minimal sensor package with Aeropod, small/lightweight)

### AquaROVER
- **What it is:** Water-based platform for aquatic science observations
- **Recent milestone:** Sailing AquaROVER successfully fabricated and tested (2024, Boston University collaboration)
- **Uses:** Water quality monitoring, habitat assessment
- **Educational focus:** Integration into formal and informal education settings (e.g., Living Classrooms Foundation)

### Kite Systems
- **Frustrationless Flier:** Beginner-friendly kites for training
- **Delta kites:** Stable platform for aerial work
- **Tower Kite:** Purpose-built for AREN, developed by Ken and Suzanne Sadow; field tested with larger audiences in 2024
- **Aerovee:** Standard research kite with documented aerodynamic characteristics
- **Edo kite:** Reference platform for aerodynamic validation
