## Executive Summary
This FAQ document provides guidance on the PY25 STRATFI/TACFI program, an Air Force initiative offering supplemental SBIR funding for small businesses with completed Phase II efforts. It clarifies eligibility requirements, funding levels, customer/end-user requirements, matching fund ratios, signatory requirements, and submission procedures.

## Key Program Structure

### Funding Tracks
- **TACFI:** $375K to $2M over up to 24 months
- **STRATFI:** $3M to $15M over up to 48 months

### Eligibility
- Small Business Concern (SBC) must have active Phase II SBIR/STTR or completed Phase II within two years of Capability Package submission
- Two-year window calculated from final deliverable date on Phase II contract
- Phase III companies ineligible unless based on active/recent Phase II
- At least 90 days must pass since Phase II activation

## Customer & End-User Requirements

### Definitions
- **End-User:** Organization that operationally benefits from the solution
- **Customer:** Organization responsible for acquiring the solution on behalf of End-User

### Primary Requirements
- Primary End-User must be from the Department of Air Force (DAF)
- Customer can be from another DoD service (e.g., SOCOM, F-35 JPO) if supporting a DAF End-User
- Phase II from another service can transition to DAF STRATFI/TACFI if it has a DAF End-User
- DAF SBIR funds cannot be used for another service's project
- Same Phase II Customer/End-User/TPOC not required; companies may bring different/additional customers

## Signatory Requirements

### TACFI
- Agreement signatory must hold minimum rank O-6/GS-15
- No special rules on organization type, size, or nature

### STRATFI
- Customer organization must be at Strategic level (e.g., USAF PEO, MAJCOM, AFMC Center)
- Agreement signatory must be ranking member of organization (e.g., Program Executive Officer) or deputy
- Minimum rank O-6/GS-15
- A-Level signatories appropriate at MAJCOM level
- TD signatories appropriate for non-PEOs (e.g., AFRL)

### Staff Function Acceptance
- Staff Functions play vital role in contract execution
- Accurate points of contact required to prevent processing delays

## Matching Funds Requirements

### Matching Ratios
**TACFI:**
- 1:1 ratio (one part SBIR funds for every one part eligible third-party or non-SBIR/STTR Government funding)
- Defense-only or Dual-use eligible

**STRATFI:**
- Defense-only: 1:2 ratio (one part SBIR funds for every two parts eligible non-SBIR/STTR Government funding)
- Dual-use: 1:1:2 ratio (one part SBIR funds, one part eligible non-SBIR/STTR Government funding, two parts third-party funding)

### Eligible Matching Fund Sources
- Customer, End-User, other Government Agency, or third-party investor
- Government matching funds must be applied to SBIR/STTR development and used appropriately per budget designation (3600=R&D, 3400=O&M, 3020=Space/Missiles, etc.)

### Phase III as Matching
- Phase III award executed within 6 months preceding Capability Package submission can count as strategic matching if it logically extends, completes, or derives from the Phase II effort

### Dual-Use vs. Defense-Only
- **Dual-Use:** Matching funds from eligible third-party sources
- **Defense-Only:** Matching funds from non-SBIR/STTR Government funding
- Type must be determined before package submission (cannot be changed post-submission)

## Submission & Selection Process

### Submission Requirements
- Government POC responsible for submitting package via PY25 STRATFI/TACFI Submission App
- Only one Capability Package per qualified SBC for entire firm
- Total funds requested must be determined before submission (cannot be increased post-submission)
- Complete packages vital to prevent review delays

### Selection Process
- Multiple levels of review required before AFWERX final determination
- Letters of support not required or considered during selection

### Contracting
- Customer responsible for providing or identifying a DAF Contracting Office to award contract/agreement if effort selected

## Notable Details

### Program Distinctions
- **Phase II b:** Modification to current Phase II contract
- **Sequential Contract:** New Phase II contract issued to increase scope of original Phase II effort
- Only active Phase IIs or those completed within two years eligible; cannot have received "sequential" Phase II
