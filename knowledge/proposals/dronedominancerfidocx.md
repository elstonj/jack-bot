# Unleashing American Drone Dominance Request for Information

## Document Metadata
- Type: Request for Information (RFI)
- Client/Agency: Department of War (DoW), Secretary of War
- Program/Solicitation: Drone Dominance Program (DDP); 10 U.S.C. 4022 Other Transaction Authority (OTA)
- Date: December 2025 (RFI release date; document created/modified 2025-12-04)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None explicitly mentioned
- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
The Department of War is issuing an RFI to solicit industry feedback on the Drone Dominance Program, a $1 billion initiative to rapidly develop and procure low-cost, attritable small unmanned aerial systems (sUAS) capable of One-Way Attack (OWA) missions. The government seeks to understand industry concerns regarding large-scale production, supply chain challenges, and feasibility before releasing a formal Request for Solutions (RFS) on December 17, 2025.

## Program Overview & Structure

**DDP Objective:**
- Accelerate U.S. industrial base capacity for low-cost, supply-chain-secure sUAS manufacturing at scale
- Enable every warfighter access to affordable, attritable OWA systems
- Establish multiple U.S. vendors capable of production at scale for future commercial procurement models

**Funding & Timeline:**
- $1 billion in fixed-price orders over 4 phases within 2 years
- Fixed-price, delivery-based payment model (no progress payments or cost-reimbursement)
- Phase I Gauntlet challenge: February 16, 2026 start date
- Up to 25 vendors invited to Phase I
- Vendor selection: RFS to post December 17, 2025; final selections/notifications week of January 26, 2026

**Phase Structure:**
- Each phase begins with a Gauntlet challenge event and ends with production-quality sUAS delivery
- Progressive difficulty: Phases II-IV substantially increase in difficulty and operational realism
- Phases II-IV include significant Counter-UAS (C-UAS) challenges
- Not a down-select process; vendors not selected in earlier phases encouraged to participate later
- Up to 12 vendors to receive fixed-price orders at end of Phase I Gauntlet
- Minimum order: 1,000 units per vendor per phase
- Deliveries in two tranches: no later than 2.5 and 4.5 months after each Gauntlet

**Planned Awards Table (Phase I):**
- Pricing and unit quantities specified by phase, with price per unit decreasing after Phase II
- Vendor numbers decrease across phases as competition intensifies

## Mission Requirements (Phase I)

- 10.0 km strike capability across open territory
- 1.0 km strike capability in simulated urban territory
- Minimum 2 kg dummy payload
- Public scoring via leaderboard; feedback provided
- Experienced U.S. Army and U.S. Marine Corps sUAS operators will fly all Gauntlets
- Vendor training time allocated: approximately 2 hours

## Key Selection & Evaluation Criteria
- Overall system performance at cost
- Production scalability
- Ease of operation (simplicity of design critical)
- Supply chain security (non-covered countries beyond NDAA requirements)
- National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) compliance required

## Use Cases & Applications
- One-Way Attack (OWA) missions
- Augmentation of current warfighting capabilities across Services
- Integration into standard acquisition pathways post-program completion

## Industry Questions Posed in RFI

1. **Operator Training:** Is ~2 hours of vendor training sufficient for experienced operators to fly the system, or alternative methods recommended?

2. **Phase I Pricing:** Is the proposed Phase I price point appropriate for delivering a sUAS with kit to meet mission requirements?

3. **Design & Manufacturing Strategy:** At stated prices and quantities, what design/manufacturing approaches would vendors use? At what volumes/prices would third-party suppliers or assemblers be leveraged? When would additional U.S.-based capacity investment occur? Which components present procurement bottlenecks?

4. **Phase I Requirements & C-UAS:** What changes to Phase I requirements suggested? What C-UAS conditions appropriate for Phase II?

5. **Scaling & Cost Reduction:** What realistic capability improvements, price reductions, and volume increases achievable across phases? What concerns regarding monthly production scaling to tens of thousands of OWA drones? What concerns meeting Schedule and Unit targets in Table 1?

6. **Supply Chain Compliance:** Are NDAA/Blue List supply chain requirements reasonable? What additional components should require secure supply chain designation? Is your sUAS NDAA compliant? Would you use a Blue List accession process at Phase I Gauntlet?

7. **Payload Integration:** Are you already working with munition vendors for OWA payloads, or will you require partners/government solutions?

8. **Legal Authority:** Any concerns meeting requirements of 10 USC 4022(d) – Appropriate Use of Authority?

9. **Participation:** Will you participate? If not, what DDP provision requires change for your participation?

## Legal & Regulatory Framework
- Procurement Method: 10 U.S.C. 4022 Other Transaction Authority (OTA) via S2MARTS consortia
- Compliance Requirement: National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) compliance mandatory
- Blue List Accession: Potential process for supply chain verification at Phase I Gauntlet
- Classification: No classified material to be submitted; proprietary/corporate data fully protected from external release

## Notable Details

- **Commercial Procurement Model:** Government explicitly intends to buy "commercial technologies for novel applications supporting defense purposes"
- **Risk Allocation:** Vendors bear development and manufacturing risk; government pays fixed price only for delivered, inspected, and accepted units
- **No Down-Select Penalty:** Vendors not selected in earlier phases strongly encouraged to re-compete in later phases—program focuses on iterative improvement across vendor pool
- **Operational Realism:** Progression toward realistic battlefield conditions, including C-UAS threats
- **Response Mechanism:** All RFI responses must be submitted via standardized fillable form at https://nstxl.org/opportunity/drone-dominance (external submissions not accepted)
- **Information Source:** Additional details available at drone-dominance.io

## Document Assessment
This is a substantive RFI establishing parameters and seeking baseline industry feedback prior to formal RFS release. It provides significant detail on program intent, timeline, evaluation criteria, and specific technical/operational questions. The document is not specific to Black Swift Technologies but represents a government-wide solicitation to the broader sUAS industry.
