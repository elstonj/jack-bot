## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is building a Chilli Double Carbon airframe with dual motors and developing an improved fuselage design for the Mustang Project. Week 5 focused on assembling the Chilli vehicle and optimizing battery placement and fuselage design to meet a 400 km mission range while maintaining proper center of gravity (CG) balance with a dense munitions payload.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Chilli Airframe
- COTS (Commercial Off-The-Shelf) Double Carbon airframe
- Dual motor configuration
- Current configuration being flight-tested to establish baseline power consumption

### Battery System - Amprius SA112 21700 Cylindrical Cells
- Energy density: 315 Wh/kg
- Selected for reliability, durability, and high energy density
- Design configuration: 6 sections of 16 cylindrical cells = 96 total cells
- Estimated capacity: 2,112 Wh
- Estimated weight: ~7 kg
- Estimated length: ~18 inches
- Cylindrical design provides precise fuselage fit and reduced weight

### Fuselage Design
- Inner diameter (ID) scaled up from 4 inches to 4.25 inches to accommodate battery pack
- Battery placement strategy: 3-section distributed configuration (4 sections aft of wings, 1 forward, 1 between wing pylon bulkheads) to balance CG with munitions payload
- Target CG: 105 mm aft of leading edge
- Lattice structures attached to battery cell terminals for structural support

### Wing Attachment System
- Two M5 to 5/16-18" thread adapter standoffs (primary support)
- Hex threaded inserts into blue bulkhead sections
- 3D-printed wing pylon providing flush mount for aerodynamic efficiency
- Pylon includes optimal placement for BST GPS Module
- Wire routing through pylon for motor power and GPS module

### GPS Module
- BST-designed GPS module
- Mounted on wing pylon attachment

## Use Cases & Applications

## Key Results & Findings

### Battery Sizing Analysis
- Mission range of 400 km requires 1,600–2,250 Wh battery capacity
- Initial estimate: 2,112 Wh (96 SA112 cells in 6-section configuration)
- Refinement pending Week 6 flight test data
- Potential future optimization: reduction to 4-inch ID fuselage (13 SA112 cells per section) if smaller battery meets requirements; would save weight and manufacturing costs

### CG Optimization
- Original aft-only battery placement rejected due to CG imbalance and unstable flight characteristics
- Revised distributed battery configuration achieves balanced CG and "healthy" flight characteristics
