## Executive Summary
This document presents official clarifications from NOAA regarding Subtopic 8.1.1 of the 2018 SBIR solicitation, which focuses on an air balloon platform capable of atmospheric radio occultation measurements. The Q&A addresses technical requirements, altitude specifications, platform configuration options, and testing location flexibility.

## Technical Approach / Solicitation Requirements
Subtopic 8.1.1 calls for:
- **Platform Type**: Air-filled balloon (not hydrogen)
- **Altitude Requirement**: 2 km or higher (higher altitudes preferred)
- **Key Function**: Atmospheric radio occultation measurements
- **Recovery**: Balloons must be recoverable (tethered balloons noted as acceptable only for proof-of-concept, likely insufficient for final solution)
- **Testing**: Can be conducted over water or land; lower altitude balloons acceptable only for preliminary proof-of-concept demonstrations
- **Technical Note**: Lower altitudes problematic due to GPS signal interference near surface

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Atmospheric Radio Occultation**: Primary application—using GPS/radio signals to measure atmospheric properties through signal bending as the balloon moves into the signal's shadow
- Satellite mission heritage referenced (see Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radio_occultation)

## Notable Details
- Document emphasizes fair competition: all offerors receive identical information through the Contracting Officer (CO)
- Tethered balloon option acknowledged but with significant altitude limitations for the occultation measurement approach
- Test location flexibility (water or land) removes geographical constraints on proposal feasibility
