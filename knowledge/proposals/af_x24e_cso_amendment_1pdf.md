## Executive Summary
This amendment to the DAF's STTR Phase I Commercial Solutions Opening clarifies applicant responsibilities regarding NIST SP 800-171 cybersecurity assessments and updates compliance procedures for proposal submission. The solicitation seeks innovative dual-purpose defense technologies with commercial applications, offering up to $110,000 for Phase I feasibility studies with a maximum 90-day performance period.

## Document Content Overview

This is a technical solicitation amendment document, not a Black Swift Technologies proposal or report. It contains no information about BST's products, capabilities, or technical approaches. The document establishes requirements for small businesses submitting proposals to the Air Force STTR program.

## Key Solicitation Details (for context)

**Submission Timeline:**
- Proposal submission opens: September 11, 2024
- Full proposals due: October 10, 2024, 12:00 PM ET
- Maximum funding: $110,000 (Phase I)
- Maximum performance period: 90 calendar days

**Amendment Focus:**
Amendment 1 primarily addresses:
1. NIST SP 800-171 Self-Assessment procedures via SPRS (Supplier Performance Risk System)
2. Instructions for completing cybersecurity readiness assessments at https://www.projectspectrum.io/#/cyberreadiness-Check
3. Requirement to submit assessment scores to WEBPTSMH.fct@navy.mil
4. Mandatory publication of scores in PIEE's SPRS module
5. Warning that failure to comply may result in proposal disqualification

## Evaluation Criteria (Equal Importance)
The solicitation evaluates proposals on three equal factors:
- **Commercialization Potential**: Track record of commercializing SBIR/STTR research, market size, business plan clarity, follow-on funding commitments
- **Defense Need**: Appropriateness and specificity of identified Air Force need, proposal adequacy, defense mission impact, breadth of applicability
- **Technical Approach**: Problem clarity, technical merit, innovation, differentiation from alternatives, team qualifications

## Eligibility Requirements

**Size & Ownership:**
- Small Business Concerns (SBCs) with ≤500 employees
- >50% U.S. citizen or permanent resident ownership
- OR majority ownership by single venture capital/private equity entity (with restrictions)
- NOT eligible if majority-owned by multiple VCOCs/hedge funds/private equity firms

**Work Distribution Requirements:**
- ≥40% of R&D work performed by the SBC
- ≥30% performed by single partnering Research Institution
- Both measured in direct + indirect costs (excluding profit)

**Foreign Affiliation Restrictions:**
DAF will deny awards if applicant or covered individuals have:
- Participation in malign foreign talent recruitment programs
- Business entities/subsidiaries in People's Republic of China or other countries of concern
- Foreign affiliations that interfere with DoD activities, create duplication, present conflicts of interest, or pose national security risks

## Topic Objective
"Phase I Open Call for Innovative Defense Related Dual-Purpose Technologies/Solutions with a Clear Air Force Stakeholder Need"

The DAF seeks technologies applicable to both defense and commercial markets that:
- Address one of seven Air Force Operational Imperatives or (if applicable) represent strategic "BLUE SKY" capabilities
- Adapt non-defense commercial solutions to meet Air Force needs
- Demonstrate product-market fit with specific Air Force stakeholders
- Show potential for rapid Phase I feasibility validation and Phase II development

**Air Force Operational Imperatives:**
1. Space Order of Battle
2. Operationally Focused Air Battle Management Systems (ABMS)
3. Moving Target Engagement
4. Tactical Air Dominance
5. Resilient Basing
6. Global Strike
7. Readiness to Deploy and Fight

## Phase I Requirements
Feasibility studies must:
1. Identify potential stakeholders for the adapted solution
2. Describe pathway to integrating with Air Force operations, including core technology development, regulatory navigation, and systems integration
3. Explain how solution can serve other DoD/Government customers

## Phase II Requirements (if awarded)
Development, installation, integration, demonstration, and test/evaluation including:
1. Evaluation against proposed objectives and measurable key results
2. Detailed comparison of adapted solution vs. non-defense commercial offering
3. Clear transition path with input from end-users, engineering, sustainment, contracting, finance, legal, and cyber security
4. Integration specifications with current/future solutions
5. Sustainability and supportability assessment
6. Identification of additional DoD/Government customers

## Phase III Pathway
Solutions with verified product-market fit may proceed directly from Phase I to Phase III to transition adapted solutions for broader government and civilian applications.

## Disqualification Triggers
Common reasons proposals are disqualified:
- SAM.gov registration not current or not eligible for all awards
- Improper work allocation percentages
- Work not meeting R&D definition
- Submission after deadline
- Exceeding maximum funding or duration
- Not meeting STTR eligibility requirements
- Previous award for essentially equivalent work
- Failure to submit NIST SP 800-171 assessment documentation
- Non-compliance with NIST SP 800-171 score publication in PIEE SPRS module

## Notable Details

**Security & Due Diligence:**
- DoD will conduct risk-based due diligence review per 15 U.S.C. 638(vv)
- Assessment includes cybersecurity practices, patent analysis, employee analysis, foreign ownership, and foreign financial ties/obligations
- DoD may create mitigation plans or deny award based on security risks identified

**Portal & Registration:**
- All proposals must be submitted via DOD SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP)
- DSIP only accessible from within the U.S.
- Login.gov account required and must match DSIP email address
- UEI (Unique Entity ID) replaces DUNS number for entity identification

**Proposal Submission Limitations:**
- Small businesses may submit only ONE proposal under this solicitation
- If multiple proposals received, only the most recent certified/submitted proposal prior to deadline receives evaluation

**Principal Investigator Requirements:**
- Primary employment (>50% time) must be with SBC or single partnering Research Institution
- Cannot constitute full-time employment elsewhere

**Performance Benchmarks:**
- Companies with multiple SBIR/STTR awards must meet minimum performance requirements
- Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate and Commercialization Benchmark thresholds apply
- Companies failing benchmarks limited to 80 total DoD awards:
  - Army: 20 Phase I/Direct-to-Phase-II awards max
  - Navy: 20 Phase I/Direct-to-Phase-II awards max
  - Air Force: 20 Phase I/Direct-to-Phase-II awards max
  - Other DoD Components: 20 combined awards max

**Contracting Terms:**
- DAF Phase I awards are FAR-based firm-fixed-price purchase orders
- Multiple awards anticipated across topic
- Funding caps are estimates; more or less funding may become available
- DAF may award all, some, or none of proposals received
