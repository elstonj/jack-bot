## Executive Summary
This is a pre-award intellectual property (IP) allocation agreement between Black Swift Technologies (small business) and Old Dominion University (research institution) establishing the framework for allocating ownership rights, commercialization options, and royalty structures for intellectual property developed under a proposed Navy STTR Phase I project. The agreement clarifies that BST retains 100% ownership of developed innovations with commercialization rights, while both parties retain non-exclusive research rights.

## Key Agreement Terms

### Intellectual Property Allocation
- **Project IP Ownership**: SBC retains 100% ownership of all project intellectual property (inventions, patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, software) generated during the STTR project
- **Project IP Expenses**: SBC responsible for 100% of development and marketing costs
- **Background IP**: Neither party disclosed any existing background IP that will be used in the project (stated as "NONE")

### Commercialization Rights
- BST has exclusive first option to commercialize all project intellectual property from ODU
- For patentable inventions: Option period TBD (document shows blank fields) with extension option; SBC reimburses ODU patent prosecution costs
- For non-patentable Project IP: Option period extends until TBD months after ODU completion of relevant project phase
- If SBC exercises option: parties negotiate license terms with reasonable royalties to ODU and commercialization milestones/minimum royalties (terms to be negotiated)

### Government Rights
- Government retains irrevocable, royalty-free, nonexclusive license for any governmental purpose in all Project IP
- Compliant with 37 CFR § 401.14 (Patent Rights clause)

### Research & Development Rights
- Both parties may use each other's Project IP non-exclusively and without compensation for R&D activities and STTR reporting/Phase continuation proposals
- Joint IP ownership if co-developed (unless otherwise agreed); SBC has first option to perfect joint IP rights

### Subject Invention Disclosure
- Both parties must disclose patentable Subject Inventions per 35 U.S.C. § 205
- Disclosure timeline: TBD months after invention disclosure to patent contact (field blank in document)
- Disclosures to include sufficient detail and statutory bar identification

### Publication Rights
- Either party may publish results
- Publishing party must provide other party right of refusal and TBD-day review period with consideration of feedback
- Reviewing party may request deferral of up to TBD additional days for patent application filing

### Confidentiality
- Proprietary/confidential information received must be held in confidence and not used/disclosed without consent
- Standard carve-outs apply (information becoming public, independently developed, received from independent source)
- Confidentiality obligations survive agreement termination

### Liability & Indemnification
- Each party disclaims all warranties (merchantability, fitness, non-infringement) on information/results provided
- BST indemnifies ODU regarding commercialization claims
- Both parties indemnify Government regarding commercialization claims

### Termination
- Either party may terminate with 60 days written notice
- May also terminate for material breach
- Each party responsible for proportionate costs through termination date
- Confidentiality obligations survive termination
