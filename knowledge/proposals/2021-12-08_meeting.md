## Executive Summary
This meeting presentation updates flight testing progress on a novel aerial drone platform designed for Titan exploration. The team has transitioned to 9x6 propellers with improved stability and is characterizing the aircraft's speed range, hover power, and control authority through systematic flight testing.

## Technical Approach
- Propeller evaluation: Transitioned from 8x8 to 9x6 propellers due to ESC reboot issues with the larger size
- Flight envelope characterization: Testing across multiple angles of attack (AoA) to map stable operating regime
- Control modes: Angle-of-attack hold mode for precision control
- Data collection: Speed range, power consumption, altitude hold, pitch/roll stability at varying flight conditions

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Titan Exploration Aerial Drone Platform**
- Capable of sustained forward flight at 18–21 m/s airspeed
- Hover power requirement: ~180W (sustained)
- Stable flight envelope: 9x6 propellers effective at 10–15° AoA
- RPM operating range: 4,000–6,500 rpm
- Altitude control: Holds altitude during forward flight
- Control modes: Angle-of-attack hold, pitch control, orbital flight capability

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Titan atmospheric exploration**: Platform designed for operation in Titan's thick atmosphere (not Earth testing; presumably in simulation or Earth surrogate conditions)

## Flight Test Results

**Hover Testing**
- Sustained hover power: ~180W

**Forward Flight Stability**
- 9x6 propellers demonstrated superior stability vs. 8x8 configuration
- Optimal operating AoA: 10–15°
- Airspeed maintained: 18–20 m/s at nominal conditions

**Speed Range Characterization**
- Tested at 5 different angles of attack
- Slower speeds: α = 15–20°
- Nominal (cruise) speed: α = 11.5°, RPM 4,000–6,500 rpm, airspeed 19–21 m/s
- Orbital flight: Transitions tested at ~11.5° AoA, 19–21 m/s

**Control Performance**
- Good control authority across tested RPM range
- All critical tests conducted in angle-hold mode (indicating closed-loop control capability)
