- Key Personnel: Maciej Stachura (Principal Investigator)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies completed Phase I development of a low-cost small unmanned aircraft system (sUAS) integrated with a passive L-band microwave radiometer for soil moisture mapping at agricultural plot scales. The system operates at 15-30m altitude to measure volumetric soil moisture content at 5-20cm depths with ~15m resolution, providing a novel platform for Earth science research and precision agriculture applications.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed to tightly integrate a passive L-band microwave radiometer with the Tempest sUAS airframe and avionics to enable precise low-altitude flight control required for accurate soil moisture mapping. Key technical work included:

1. Detailed design of soil moisture sensor integration with Tempest airframe
2. Selection and design of laser altimeter interface for altitude above ground level (AGL) measurement
3. Accuracy analysis of aircraft trajectory and orientation error impacts on sensor performance
4. Tempest airframe and powerplant modifications to accommodate payload
5. Endurance and sensing resolution vs. usability tradeoff analysis
6. User interface modifications for soil moisture mapping missions
7. Sensor validation experiment design
8. FAA Certificate of Authorization (COA) application preparation

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Tempest sUAS
- Platform for integrating L-band microwave radiometer
- Modified airframe and powerplant to accommodate soil moisture sensor payload
- Capable of sustained low-altitude flight (15-30m AGL) required for sensor accuracy
- Integrated laser altimeter for AGL measurement
- Tight integration of sensor with avionics and airframe enables precise flight control

### Soil Moisture Sensor
- Passive L-band microwave radiometer
- Measures volumetric soil moisture content
- Coverage: agricultural plot-sized areas
- Depth penetration: 5-20cm
- Spatial resolution: ~15m
- Claimed as only commercial system capable of mapping soil moisture at critical depths in low-cost, easy-to-operate package

### Auxiliary Systems Designed/Built
- NDVI/thermal circuit boards
- Data logger
- Power management boards
- Integration of sensing payload into Tempest

## Use Cases & Applications

### NASA Applications
- Calibration and augmentation of satellite missions (SMAP, Aquarius) with higher resolution, local-scale data
- Follow-on work for Airborne Microwave Observatory of Subcanopy and Subsurface (AMOSS) program
- Supporting other NASA Earth observation missions

### Non-NASA Commercial Applications
- Precision agriculture
- Flood planning, management, and mitigation
- Any commercial application with similar payload and mission requirements

## Phase I Accomplishments
1. Created requirements and validation techniques for soil moisture mapping sUAS
2. Determined soil moisture payload configuration including mission profile and interface specifications
3. Completed Tempest UAS sensor interface design, airframe/powerplant modification tradeoff study, and prepared COA application
4. Designed, built, and tested all auxiliary circuit boards (NDVI/thermal, data logger, power) and integrated sensing payload into Tempest

## Notable Details
- Proposed as inexpensive alternative to satellite-only approaches for soil moisture monitoring
- Unique capability: claimed as only commercial system for field-scale soil moisture mapping at critical depths
- System designed for ease of operation by non-specialist users (agricultural extension service, farmers)
- FAA regulatory approval process initiated (COA application)
- Direct support to specific NASA missions (SMAP, Aquarius) with demonstrated capability gap
