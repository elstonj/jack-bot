## Executive Summary

Black Swift Technologies is seeking a $250,000 Colorado Advanced Industries grant to commercialize the S2 Methane Leak Detection (MLD) system—a UAS-based platform integrating a laser spectrometer sensor from partner Quanta 3. The system can survey 100+ well sites per day at $35 per site (vs. $500+ for competing methods) with <10 SCFH detection sensitivity. With matching NASA funding of $750K for technology development and $779,465 in confirmed funds, BST projects the methane detection service business will generate $19M in annual revenue by 2025 and create 75 direct FTEs in Colorado.

## Technical Approach

**Core Technology:**
- Ultra-low-mass laser spectrometer sensor co-developed with Quanta 3 (Colorado-based, Longmont)
- Integrated into BST's S2 fixed-wing UAS platform
- Detection sensitivity: 2 parts per billion (ppb)
- Sensor-directed flight control avionics combining methane readings with 3D wind sensors to characterize plumes in real-time
- Advanced post-processing software to create actionable data within hours of flight

**Key Innovation:**
Pairing high-sensitivity laser spectroscopy with BST's advanced UAS avionics and algorithms to automatically find and localize methane leaks with minimal operator input. The system uses reactive flight controls to locate leak sources and estimate leak magnitude, allowing operators to prioritize remediation efforts.

**Development Status:**
- Laser spectrometer bench-tested and integrated into S2
- First test flights completed as of application date
- Field testing planned at Methane Emissions Technology Evaluation Center (METEC) at Colorado State University and with PDC Energy
- S2 platform is mature and requires no further development for field testing
- Sensor-directed flight control is a NASA-funded development initiative for final production system

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 Methane Leak Detection (MLD) System
- **What it is:** Fixed-wing UAS platform with integrated laser spectrometer, sensor-directed flight control, and post-processing software
- **Deployment capability:** 100+ well sites per day; 50+ wells per day with data delivery
- **Cost:** $35 per well surveyed vs. $500+ for competing methods
- **Detection capability:** <10 SCFH (standard cubic feet per hour) leak rate vs. >300 SCFH for satellite/manned aircraft
- **Data delivery:** Actionable leak location and severity data within hours of flight
- **Pricing model:** $300 per well head scanned initially
- **Fleet requirement:** 25 platforms at $60K per unit = $1.5M capital investment retained in Colorado

### S2 Fixed-Wing UAS Platform
- Mature, proven airborne platform (introduced 2018)
- All-electric propulsion
- Range/speed approximately 10x that of conventional quadrotors
- Demonstrated Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) operations up to 20 miles in severe atmospheric conditions
- Equipped with 3D wind sensors and advanced avionics

### Quanta 3 Laser Spectrometer
- Industry-leading detection to 2 ppb
- Ultra-low mass package suitable for small UAS
- Intellectual property owned by Quanta 3; BST establishing exclusive licensing agreement for use in systems and service business
