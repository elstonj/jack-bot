# Letter of Support - Durlo

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: USDA (Small Business Innovation Research program)
- Program/Solicitation: USDA SBIR proposal - "A Ruggedized UAS System for Management of Wildfires on Forest Lands"
- Date: 2017-10-05
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: UAS system (unspecified model)
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston, Dr. Wardell (BST); Grzegorz Durlo (University of Agriculture in Cracow)

## Executive Summary
A professor from the University of Agriculture in Cracow, Poland provides institutional support for Black Swift Technologies' USDA SBIR proposal on a ruggedized UAS system for wildfire management. Durlo endorses BST's technology as uniquely suited to address increasing forest fire risks in Europe and emphasizes the value of airborne wildfire emissions monitoring capabilities.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed a ruggedized UAS system capable of:
- Monitoring a wide range of emissions downwind of wildfires
- Operating in areas that do not conflict with active fire-fighting operations
- Collecting airborne data to support wildfire modeling, forecasting, and management

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Ruggedized UAS System for Wildfire Management**
- Proposed application: Real-time or near-real-time monitoring of wildfire emissions
- Unique capability: Ability to safely monitor downwind of fires without interfering with firefighting operations
- Target utility: Support for emissions modeling, forecasting, and hazard management

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Wildfire monitoring and management** in European forests (particularly Poland and broader EU)
- **Airborne emissions monitoring** for wildfire impact assessment
- Support for forest ecosystem protection and sustainability management

## Notable Details
- Letter emphasizes growing wildfire risk in Europe due to climate change, droughts, and forest health deterioration
- Endorsement specifically highlights BST technology as "unique" and "needed capability"
- Suggests commercial market potential in the EU once system is commercialized
- Supportive letter from established academic institution validates technical approach and market need
