## Executive Summary
The XPRIZE Wildfire is an $11 million, 4-year global competition designed to incentivize innovation in wildfire detection and rapid response technologies. The competition features two parallel tracks: Track A (Space-Based Wildfire Detection & Intelligence, running April 2023–August 2025) and Track B (Autonomous Wildfire Response, running April 2023–July 2026). Teams compete to solve critical challenges in early detection accuracy, rapid suppression, and operability in extreme weather conditions.

## Document Purpose & Structure
This document is the official competition guidelines for participating teams. It outlines:
- Registration procedures and team requirements
- Two distinct competition tracks with different timelines and deliverables
- Prize purses totaling $11 million
- Health, safety, and environmental compliance requirements
- Competition structure, rounds, and judging criteria
- Roles of advisory boards, judges, and competition partners

## Competition Tracks

### **Track A: Space-Based Wildfire Detection & Intelligence**
- **Timeline:** April 2023 – August 2025 (22 months)
- **Challenge:** Detect all fires across landscapes larger than entire states/countries in 1 minute; characterize and report data within 10 minutes with minimal false positives
- **Key Requirement:** Observations must be obtained from space at altitude ≥100 km (Kármán Line)
- **Teams Not Required To:** Own or deploy their own satellite; may partner with existing space-based platforms or acquire third-party data
- **Three Rounds:**
  1. Qualifying Technical Submission (Feb 2024)
  2. Semifinals System Technical Verification (April 2025)
  3. Finals Testing using prescribed fire in outdoor environment (Aug 2025)

**Judging Criteria for Track A:**
- High-Resolution Detection: Detect fires ≥10m² down to ~1m², reduce false positives to 5%
- Live Fire Behavior Intelligence: Comprehensive characterization including perimeter, spread direction/rate, and intensity
- Global and Continuous Coverage: Detection across biomes, through smoke/cloud, day and night
- Fast and Universal Data Access: Analyze and transmit data to decision-makers within 10 minutes
- Integrability with Systems: Technical compatibility with emergency services data systems
- Scalability: Cost-effective global deployment

### **Track B: Autonomous Wildfire Response**
- **Timeline:** April 2023 – July 2026 (36-42 months)
- **Challenge:** Autonomously detect and suppress a high-risk fire in a 1,000 km² environmentally challenging area within 10 minutes, leaving decoy fires untouched
- **Key Requirement:** "Human-On-the-Loop" autonomy (systems operate without human input during testing, but humans supervise and can override)
- **Three Rounds:**
  1. Qualifying Technical Submission (May 2024)
  2. Semifinals: System Technical Verification (March 2025) + System Testing with prescribed fire (Oct 2025)
  3. Finals: System Technical Verification (Feb 2026) + System Testing (July 2026)

**Judging Criteria for Track B:**
- Fully Autonomous Integrated Solutions: End-to-end autonomous detection, response, suppression
- Smart Detection: Recognize and ignore decoy fires (false positives like water vapor, clouds, low-intensity non-moving fires)
- Safety: No harm to personnel; non-toxic suppression materials per NFPA standards
- Functionality in High Winds: Operate at 30 km/h minimum, evaluated for 100 km/h capability
- Functionality in Complex Terrain: Operate effectively on steep (≥45% slope) or difficult terrain
- Connectivity: Demonstrate 2 types of connectivity for redundancy
- Scalability: Cost-effective deployment

### Team Composition & Collaboration
- Teams may include experts in various fields (aerospace, computer science, fire management, forestry, ecology, emergency management, etc.)
- Teams may recruit new members at any time
- Teams may merge (must notify XPRIZE 10 days prior); mergers must designate one legal entity and team leader
- Multiple competition entries per track permitted if each represents distinct technology approach with separate team profile
- Teams must own or hold appropriate license rights to all submitted technologies and IP

### Eligibility
- Any person/entity may participate unless prohibited by US law or subject to US Treasury sanctions
- Non-US residents must obtain US authorization license if ordinarily resident in sanctioned destinations
- As of submission date, teams must own or hold license rights to all technologies, methods, resources, and IP included

## Health, Safety & Environmental Requirements

Teams must:
- Research and obtain necessary permits for operations in Competition Area
- Comply with all environmental, health, and safety regulations
- Comply with NFPA Wildfire Codes/Standards or comparable international standards
- Not employ, influence, or harm any form of life; minimize detrimental effects on conservation values
- Ensure solutions are fully functional and pose no risk to competitors, judges, safety personnel, or observers
- Recover deployed equipment; declare and justify any disposable portions to judges

**XPRIZE Discretion:** Reserves right to disqualify teams operating unsafely or unethically; to adjust guidelines based on latest scientific/legal information; to determine acceptable operating conditions

## Competition Ecosystem

### Advisory Board
- Panel of topical experts and thought leaders appointed by XPRIZE
- Independent of XPRIZE, sponsors, and all teams
- Duties include: assisting with judge qualifications, recommending judging panel members, developing testing protocols and judging criteria, providing input on guidelines
- All members must disclose conflicts of interest

### Judging Panel
- Independent subject matter and technical experts
- Proposed by XPRIZE, recommended by Advisory Board
- Independent of XPRIZE, sponsors, and all teams
- **Authority:** Judging Panel decisions are final and binding; sole discretion over methodology, accuracy/error tolerances, tie-breaking, and winner determination
- Teams have no right to observe other teams' testing or be informed of other teams' results unless publicly released
- Duties include: evaluating team compliance and Competitor Agreement adherence; awarding points and selecting advancing teams

### Competition Partners
- May include government entities, businesses, non-profits, coalitions
- Provide industry knowledge, in-kind services, discounted products
- Collaboration encouraged but optional

## Notable Details & Context

### Core Problems Addressed
The competition targets six major inhibitors to wildfire management progress:

1. **Time:** Detection-to-suppression averages 60 minutes; extreme fires spread in moments. Current best-case from detection to suppression is 60 minutes
2. **Detection Accuracy & Precision:** Space-based detection shows ~1 km² resolution per pixel with up to 40% false positive rate. Ground vehicles limited to 40% slopes; aerial vehicles limited by weather, time of day
3. **Accessibility:** Hard-to-reach fire start areas; flame height thresholds limit attack methods (>1.2m: no hand tools; >2.4m: no aerial attack)
4. **Fragmented Field:** Detection, response, and suppression split across many uncoordinated actors
5. **Extreme Weather Operability:** Climate-induced extreme fires spread faster, making current detection/response/suppression methods obsolete
6. **Global Availability:** Advanced technology often inaccessible to many countries/communities (e.g., satellite remote sensing, supertanker planes, local firefighting units)

### Environmental Context
- Only ~3% of wildfires are extreme, but drive >80% of total damages and increasing percentage of global greenhouse gas emissions
- Wildfire is integral to ecosystem health (clears dead vegetation, encourages growth, essential for giant sequoia/Eucalyptus survival)
- Wildland-Urban Interface (WUI) expansion increases risk; changing climate compounds threat
- Need for balance: safely manage destructive fires while preserving ecologically beneficial fire
