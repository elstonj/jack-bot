## Executive Summary
This document is a **template/framework** for submitting a Technical Volume (Volume 2) for an Air Force SBIR Phase I proposal. It is not a completed BST proposal but rather guidance on how to structure a dual-use technology proposal that adapts commercial solutions for Air Force needs. The template demonstrates the required narrative structure, key sections, and evidence types for SBIR Phase I applications.

## Document Purpose & Structure
This is a **proposal template**, not a completed proposal. It provides:
- Section-by-section guidance on what information to include
- Examples of the types of evidence to present (screenshots, testimonials, investment data)
- A sample work plan structure
- Instructions for demonstrating technical merit, commercial traction, and defense applicability

## Key Sections Required (Per Template)

### 1. **Technical Abstract & Overview**
Should describe:
- The Air Force's vital/urgent need being addressed
- How the proposed platform/solution enables rapid deployment of applications
- Capabilities being gained (cost reduction, battlefield solutions, etc.)
- Technical merit and technology capability
- Team qualifications and experience

### 2. **Technical Merit**
Guidance to "show, don't tell" using:
- Screenshots/pictures of solution in use
- Sales numbers from similar commercial solutions
- Customer testimonials from non-defense users
- Investment from credible venture capital firms demonstrating technological viability
- Evidence of actual implementation combining existing technologies

### 3. **Significance of Problem/Opportunity**
Must define:
- The specific technical problem or opportunity
- Proposed solution and how it's superior to current alternatives
- Why users should care (value proposition, not technical depth)

### 4. **Commercialization Potential**
Two components:
- **Non-Defense**: Current commercial contracts, revenue pipeline, customer base, market growth projections
- **Defense**: Strategy to transition solution through SBIR Phase III, partnerships with AFRL, Air Combat Command, Platform One, etc.

### 5. **Team's Ability to Perform**
Should include:
- Headshots and summaries of key personnel
- Demonstration of team's ability to conduct R&D and commercialize
- Evidence of relevant expertise

### 6. **Defense Need**
Must demonstrate:
- Knowledge of prospective Air Force end-users and realistic use cases
- Comparison to current alternatives
- Previous DoD funding for related work (AFOSR grants, etc.)
- Direct user/organizational benefits expected from implementation
- Reference to Air Force Strategic Capability areas where applicable
- Screenshots/citations from defense news articles validating use case assumptions

### 7. **Phase I Objectives**
Six primary objectives outlined:
1. Validate product-market fit between solution and DAF stakeholder
2. Deeply explore problem/benefit areas to understand end-user impact
3. Define clear objectives and measurable key results for potential trial
4. Describe how solution differs from non-defense commercial offering
5. Describe cost and feasibility of integration with mission-specific products
6. Describe applicability to other DoD/governmental customers

### 8. **Work Plan (Non-Proprietary)**
Typical structure includes 6 tasks over 90-day Phase I period:
- **Task 01**: Preliminary Report (Days 0-5)
- **Task 02**: Department of Air Force Customer Discovery (Days 0-60)
- **Task 03**: Problem Refinement via site visits (Days 15-60)
- **Task 04**: Technical Adaptation/Modification Study (Days 40-80)
- **Task 05**: Definition of Solution Trial with DAF End-User (Days 40-90)
- **Task 06**: Final Report with required forms (Days 60-90)

### 9. **Transition Plan**
Should outline:
- **Defense Transition Strategy**: Phase I validation → Phase II R&D → SBIR supplemental funding (TACFI/STRATFI) → Phase III sole source award
- **Defense Stakeholders**: PEOs and key organizations for transition
- **Commercial Stakeholders**: VC firms, advisors, board members, existing customers

### 10. **Related Work (Dual-Use)**
Must describe:
- Previous work by PI, firm, consultants directly related to proposal
- Coordination with outside sources
- Non-defense customer base and interviews conducted
- Defense customers and interviews conducted
- Competitive landscape analysis
- Focus on attaching defense solutions to pace of non-defense technological change
