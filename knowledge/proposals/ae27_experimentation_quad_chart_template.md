## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to conduct an experimentation campaign using its S0-VTOL UAS to collect high-resolution atmospheric data in the arctic region to improve combat weather forecasts. The experiment will demonstrate the S0's reliability in harsh arctic conditions and validate improved operational weather forecasting capabilities, with deployment planned for March 2026.

## Technical Approach
- Deploy S0-VTOL UAS for targeted atmospheric data collection in arctic environment
- Conduct multiple flights (~60 minutes total) at various altitudes: transit below 400' AGL, data collection blocks up to 1 km × 1 km at 4,000' AGL
- Transmit/collect data on standard 2.4 GHz and 900 MHz bands
- Transfer collected atmospheric data via CD/DVD to host unit's weather forecasting models
- Self-funded by BST using internal R&D funds; no direct government funding for this experimentation

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0-VTOL UAS**
- Vertical takeoff and landing UAS
- Specifically designed to operate in harsh conditions including arctic environment
- Capable of atmospheric/weather data collection
- System components fit in hard case (50" × 30" × 18"): aircraft, ground station, batteries, battery charger
- Uses standard 2.4 GHz and 900 MHz communication bands
- Does not currently have an airworthiness certificate
- Can be assembled/launched/landed in any outdoor location clear of obstructions within 30'

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Arctic weather data collection**: Address scarcity of fixed weather data collection stations in arctic region
- **Combat weather forecasting**: Enable improved operational weather forecasts for military operations in data-sparse arctic environment
- Supports expeditionary and forward-deployed combat weather squadron operations
