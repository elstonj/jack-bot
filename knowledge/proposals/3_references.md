## Executive Summary
This is a bibliography/references section from a NASA ROSES 2024 A.59 Step 2 proposal. The references focus on remote sensing, soil moisture monitoring, live fuel moisture, and wildfire dynamics in western US ecosystems—particularly California. The document provides scientific grounding for research on using remote sensing and soil moisture data to predict or assess wildfire risk.

## Technical Approach & Research Focus
The references indicate the proposal likely addresses:
- **Remote sensing for soil/fuel moisture monitoring**: Multiple papers on L-band and P-band SAR (Synthetic Aperture Radar) retrieval of soil moisture
- **Live fuel moisture as fire predictor**: Research on relationships between soil moisture, live fuel moisture (LFM), and wildfire activity
- **Microwave remote sensing**: Focus on passive and active microwave sensors for plant water content retrieval
- **Regional wildfire dynamics**: Emphasis on California fire regimes and climate-driven changes

## Key Research Areas Covered

### Soil Moisture Remote Sensing
- SMAP (Soil Moisture Active Passive) satellite L-band SAR observations
- NISAR and UAVSAR campaign data (Kim et al., 2024 in prep)
- L-band radar backscattering models for soil moisture retrieval
- Root-zone soil moisture from AirMOSS P-band data (Tabatabaeenejad et al., 2020)
- Forest canopy soil moisture retrieval across incidence angles (Kurum et al., 2020)

### Live Fuel Moisture & Wildfire Risk
- Critical LFM thresholds in chaparral ecosystems (Dennison & Moritz, 2009)
- Lagged relationship between SMAP soil moisture and live fuel moisture in California (Jia et al., 2020)
- LFM forecasting for *Adenostema fasciculatum* (California chaparral) (Park et al., 2022)
- Soil moisture as proxy for live fuel moisture monitoring (Qi et al., 2012)

### Wildfire Trends & Climate
- Increasing wildfire trends in western US despite fuel constraints (Abatzoglou et al., 2021)
- Lengthening fire season in Southern California (Dong et al., 2022)
- Climate-driven intensified burn severity (Huang et al., 2020)
- Large wildfire trends 1984-2011 and Sierra Nevada trends 1984-2010 (Dennison et al., 2014; Miller & Safford, 2012)

### Ecohydrology & Plant-Water Relations
- Leaf-level flammability thresholds vs. live fuel moisture and water potential (Boving et al., 2023)
- Microwave remote sensing of plant water content for physiology (Konings et al., 2019)
- Soil moisture-plant interactions (Wang et al., 2019)
- Landslide region soil moisture mapping (Liao et al., 2021)

## Notable Details
