# SBIR/STTR Phase II Customer Memorandum Template

## Document Metadata
- Type: Regulatory/Compliance Template - Customer Memorandum Form
- Client/Agency: Department of the Air Force (DAF), Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL), AFWERX
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR/STTR Phase II - Open Topic (AFVentures)
- Date: Document created circa 2023 (based on file metadata)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None (this is a blank template)
- Key Personnel: None named in template itself

## Executive Summary
This is a mandatory Department of the Air Force Customer Memorandum template that SBIR/STTR Phase II applicants must complete and submit with their proposals. The form documents Defense mission need, stakeholder commitments, technical points of contact, security/cybersecurity requirements, and Phase III transition strategy. All fields must be completed or marked N/A; proposals without a signed memorandum following this template are non-responsive and will not be considered.

## Document Purpose
This template establishes formal requirements for companies proposing Phase II SBIR/STTR efforts under the Air Force AFWERX AFVentures Open Topic program. It ensures DAF organizational buy-in and commitment before award.

## Key Sections Required

### Section 1: Purpose
- Documents DAF organization interest in working with AFWERX and the Phase II awardee
- Establishes authority under 15 U.S.C. §638 and SBIR/STTR Program Policy Directive

### Section 2: Defense Mission Need
- Requires description of national Defense-related mission need
- Requires separate justification for US Air Force vs. US Space Force applicability
- Requires identification of up to 5 measurable goals with clear metrics

### Section 3: Project Timeline
- Company must specify project execution period aligned with solicitation requirements
- Option to complete in shorter timeframe than specified

### Section 4: Phase II Funding Commitment
- Non-SBIR/STTR Government funding is optional (not required)
- If committed, DAF partner must submit DRAFT funding documents (Form 9s/MIPRs) within 45 days of solicitation closing
- CERTIFIED funding documents due within 5 business days of selection letters
- If certified documents not provided, effort proceeds without commitment consideration
- Commitment does not constitute obligation; subject to funds availability

### Section 5: Additional Agreement
- Requires consultation with Air Force legal, contracting, and program office personnel
- Address Government-Furnished Equipment/Information (GFE/I) needs
- Plan for Government facility access, testing, licenses, environmental considerations
- Post-award Phase III contract pathway discussion

### Section 6: Phase III Interest
- DAF may transition successful Phase II innovations to Phase III contracts/subcontracts
- Subject to acquisition planning, funds availability, security assessments, Small Business/Legal/Contracting review
- **Does not obligate DAF to commit Phase III funding**

### Section 7: Milestones
- Attachment 1.1 required: Schedule of Milestones
- Milestones must provide value to end-users/customers

### Section 8: Stakeholders (Critical)
**Primary End-User Organization:**
- Organization that will use/benefit from solution
- Requires named point of contact with rank/grade, duty title, email

**Primary Customer Organization:**
- Organization managing future procurement/sustainment
- Manages funding, contracting, logistics for Phase III
- Requires named point of contact with rank/grade, duty title, email

**Phase II Technical Points of Contact (TPOCs):**
- **MANDATORY:** Primary TPOC must be DAF civilian or military member
- Alternate TPOC also designated
- Format: Name; Rank/Grade; Organization; Email; Phone
- TPOCs must be Government personnel (not support contractors or SETA contractors)
- Responsibilities: receive/accept/reject deliverables, provide performance feedback, overcome execution barriers, drive Phase III transition

**Strategic Stakeholders:**
- Name of MAJCOM or HQ function most directly impacted
- Name of PEO or Center most directly impacted

**Staff Function Concurrence Required:**
- Security Office
- Information Assurance/Cybersecurity
- Finance
- Contracting
- Strategy, Integration, and Requirements
- Small Business Office
- Commander
- Legal Counsel
- Engineering/SPO/Test
- Public Affairs
- Additional stakeholders as applicable

### Section 9: Phase III Transition Strategy
- Core SBIR/STTR program objective
- Must tailor strategy to specific organization and solution (not boilerplate)
- Address minimum requirements:
  - Phase III funding strategy
  - Requirements personnel role (if applicable)
  - Contract vehicle
  - Integration plan
- Resources available at afwerx.com

### Section 10: Security
- Specify if company WILL/WILL NOT require:
  - Military installation/facility access
  - Common Access Cards (CACs) for DoD network access
  - Classified accesses documented via DD254
- TPOC/Customer coordinates with visitor centers or security offices
- Security office confirms processing capability for CACs and DD254 documentation

### Section 11: Cybersecurity
- Specify if solution DOES/DOES NOT involve software/IT connecting to DoD networks/systems or utilizing CUI data
- If yes, Information Assurance/Cybersecurity office will:
  - Determine if Authorization to Operate (ATO) or Interim Authorization To Test (IATT) required
  - Submit required paperwork
  - Ensure NIST SP 800-171 Rev 1 and DFARS PGI 239.76 compliance
  - Consider Platform One DevSecOps environments

### Section 12: Flight Testing or UAS Utilization
- Specify if company WILL/WILL NOT require flight testing or UAS
- If yes: coordinate immediately after award with TPOC, AFRL/DO, and AFWERX test personnel
- Prepare flight test planning documents per AFRL/DO instructions
- Note: Blanket waiver exists for COTS UAS procurement for benign cyber/signature analysis, but "use waiver" may be required

### Section 13: Signatories
- **MANDATORY:** Two CAC-enabled digital signatures required
- DAF Customer signatory and DAF End-User signatory must be different individuals
- Signatories must be in positions to speak for their organizations (understand operational needs, requirements, funding)
- Describe suitability of each signatory

## Critical Compliance Notes
- **Non-responsive if incomplete:** Proposals without completed, signed Customer Memorandum following this template will NOT be considered
- **All fields required:** If question not applicable, must indicate N/A (cannot leave blank)
- **Signature requirement:** Memorandum without two CAC-enabled digital signatures will NOT be accepted
- **TPOC notification:** TPOCs must be notified of their duties prior to memo signature

## Attachments Required

- Cybersecurity and security requirements are pre-identified and must be addressed before/at award
