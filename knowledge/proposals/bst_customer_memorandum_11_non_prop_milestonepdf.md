## Executive Summary
This document outlines the Phase II milestone schedule for Black Swift Technologies' SBIR project to develop soil integrity mapping capabilities for the Air Force using modified L-band sensing instruments and UAS platforms. The $1.25M effort spans 21 months and includes sensor/UAS modifications, algorithm development, field testing, and validation at Tyndall Air Force Base.

## Technical Approach

**Overall Strategy:**
- Modify existing L-band radiometer sensing instrument for soil moisture measurement
- Integrate auxiliary sensors (surface temperature, NDVI) into UAS platform
- Develop algorithms to compute soil integrity (cone index) from L-band soil moisture data and soil type information
- Conduct ground-truthing with in-situ cone index measurements
- Refine models based on local testing results
- Validate at operational Air Force location (Tyndall AFB)

**Key Technical Components:**
1. L-band radiometer refinements and auxiliary sensor integration
2. UAS modifications to accommodate new sensor suite
3. Real-time soil moisture calculation capability
4. Cone index computation algorithms
5. Model refinement based on empirical validation

## Products & Capabilities Described

**L-Band Sensing Instrument**
- Existing radiometer being modified for this application
- Refinements to radiometer design
- Addition of auxiliary sensors for enhanced data collection
- Used to measure soil moisture as basis for soil integrity derivation

**UAS Platform**
- Modified to carry sensing payload
- Integration of surface temperature sensors
- Integration of NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) sensors
- Capability for real-time soil moisture calculations during flight
- Designated as MVP2 (Minimum Viable Product 2) in testing phase

**Soil Integrity Algorithm**
- Computes cone index (soil compaction/bearing capacity metric) from:
  - L-band derived soil moisture data
  - Soil type classification
- Documented with full technical specifications
- Validated against ground-truth measurements
