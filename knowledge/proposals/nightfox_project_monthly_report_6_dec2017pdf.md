## Executive Summary
The NightFOX project reached its initial instrument development goal by selecting candidate instruments meeting minimum requirements for nighttime fire observations using UAS. Key accomplishments include validating near-IR and mid-IR telecentric lens systems, demonstrating near-IR sensitivity, and identifying improved CO2 detection methods. Development and evaluation of remote sensing and in situ instruments continues, with prototype construction planned for the coming quarter.

## Technical Approach
- **Optical Systems:** Development and validation of telecentric lens systems for near-infrared (near-IR) and mid-infrared (mid-IR) spectroscopy to observe fires at night
- **Gas Sensing:** Integration of CO sensors and CO2 NDIR (nondispersive infrared) detector/amplifier combinations to reduce background noise
- **Aerosol Measurement:** Evaluation of options for coarse mode aerosol measurement
- **UAS Platform:** Acquisition of S2 UAS platform by CU (University of Colorado) for carrying payload
- **Data Integration:** Assessment of data assimilation needs and protocols for fire weather modeling; discussion of synthetic data set development

## Key Results
**Instrument Development Status:**
- Near-IR telecentric lens system: Optically validated; angular resolution adequate; demonstrated sufficient sensitivity
- Mid-IR telecentric scope: Under evaluation with encouraging initial results
- CO sensors: Two prototypes expected in December 2017 (delayed due to holidays)
- CO2 detection: Identified improved NDIR detector/amplifier combination to significantly reduce background noise
