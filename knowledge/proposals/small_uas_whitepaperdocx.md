## Executive Summary
This whitepaper proposes a proof-of-concept effort to develop and commercialize next-generation Small UAS (unmanned aircraft systems) for civilian applications. The proposal describes collaboration between RECUV (Research and Engineering Center for Unmanned Vehicles), industry partners including Black Swift Technologies and UASUSA, and NOAA to advance UAS technologies across command & control, avionics, airframes, and sensor integration.

## Technical Approach
The proposed effort focuses on developing and commercializing next-generation UAS based on technologies matured through prior collaborations. Key technical areas include:

- **Advanced mobile C3 and networking**: Hardware and middleware for command, control, and communications
- **Advanced avionics**: Autopilots, flight computers, and interfaces
- **Advanced airframes**: Design and development
- **Sensor development and integration**: Collaboration sought with NCAR Earth Observation Laboratory

## Products & Capabilities Described
While specific BST products are not named in this document, it identifies Black Swift Technologies as an industry partner company that:
- Was created as an entrepreneurial partner based on products from RECUV projects or expertise of former RECUV-educated students
- Develops UAS products and technologies for civilian applications

## Use Cases & Applications
Domestic UAS applications covered by the initiative include:
- Precision agriculture
- Law enforcement
- Emergency response
- Atmospheric science
- Other civilian applications

## Notable Details
- BST is identified as one of two new companies (along with UASUSA) created as entrepreneurial partners from RECUV research and expertise over the preceding five years
- RECUV is positioned as state and national leader in UAS education, research, technology development, and regulatory knowledge
- The proposal emphasizes critical need for reliable, affordable C3 technologies to satisfy FAA regulations for safe integration of UAS into National Airspace System (NAS)
- Partnership includes NOAA UAS Program Office (UASPO), indicating government agency collaboration
- Potential partnership with NCAR Earth Observation Laboratory for sensor development
