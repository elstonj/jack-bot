## Executive Summary

Black Swift Technologies received a $250,000 Colorado Advanced Industries grant to develop and commercialize methane leak detection capabilities for oil and gas operations using their S2 unmanned aircraft system. The project achieved successful field demonstrations at customer sites showing the ability to qualitatively map methane emissions across multi-km areas and identify leak sources. Development of an advanced "flux mapping" analysis technique using eddy covariance methodology extended the timeline into Q2 2024 but promises significantly improved product capability, with MVP deployment expected by mid-2024 and potential early revenue from Chevron and Crowheart Energy partners.

## Technical Approach

**Core System & Sensor Integration:**
- Integrated a secondary Q3 methane sensor into the S2 platform
- Initial successful demonstration at BST test facility outside Longmont in January 2023
- Field testing conducted at customer (PDCE, now Chevron) well sites in Q3 2023

**Advanced Analysis Development:**
- Developed "Flux Mapping" technique using eddy covariance analysis with "wind memory" methodology
- Allows survey of 4km × 4km areas with only 5-10 downwind passes, pinpointing leak sources
- Expected to provide measurements of up to 40 wells from a single flight
- BST invested additional $70K in IR&D funding (outside grant) to develop this analysis capability

**Technical Challenges Encountered:**
1. **Sensor Drift:** High-temperature-induced drift from disconnected heatsink within aircraft—addressed through design iteration
2. **Data Analysis Complexity:** More sophisticated analysis technique than originally planned required extended development
3. **Field Logistics:** Determining flight patterns, geographic limitations, and landing area requirements

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S2 Methane Leak Detection (S2MLD) Platform:**
- Autonomous UAV capable of flying survey patterns and detecting methane at required sensitivity
- Integrated methane sensor (specialized sensor acquired at $30K cost)
- Autonomous flight capability for survey pattern execution
- Enhanced to provide both leak rate detection and flux mapping visualization
- Can generate qualitative survey maps identifying methane sources across multi-km areas
- Supports 40+ well measurement capability from single flight

**Flux Mapping Capability:**
- New graphical presentation of data enabling visualization of methane leak locations
- Generated through eddy covariance analysis of sensor data
- Provides powerful tool for demonstrating to regulators and public which well sites have minimal methane leak impact
- MVP configuration expected early Q2 2024

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary (Oil & Gas Exploration/Production):**
- **Well Site Monitoring:** Characterization of methane emissions from individual well sites
- **Multi-well Surveys:** Ability to assess 40+ wells in single flight
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Demonstrating minimal methane impact of specific well sites to regulators and public
- **Surrounding Area Assessment:** Identifying off-site methane sources (e.g., wetlands) contributing to area emissions

**Secondary Applications Identified:**
- **Landfill Operations:** Methane leak detection and management (contract developed with Delta Climate, $60K evaluation)
- **Abandoned Coal Mines:** Carbon capture and methane leak detection from defunct mining operations
- **Mid-stream Operations:** Initial discussions with mid-stream customers (DCP)

**Geographic Focus:**
- Primary development in Colorado/Wyoming region
- High-altitude mountain testing capability demonstrated (Carbondale area)
