## Executive Summary
This document provides guidance for the FY2025 NOAA Testbeds competition, explaining NOAA's research-to-operations (R2O) transition framework across five operational testbeds. The document describes the objectives, technical maturity requirements (Readiness Levels 5-8), testing protocols, and specific focus areas for each testbed facility managed jointly by NWS and OAR.

## Technical Approach
The testbeds operate as quasi-operational environments where researchers and operational forecasters collaborate through:
- Direct engagement of operational forecasters running or utilizing experimental techniques during operational shifts
- Development of collaborative test plans (within 6 months of project start) defining what will be tested, schedule milestones, evaluation processes, and success metrics
- Development of R2O transition plans outlining implementation timelines and product improvement approaches
- Semiannual progress monitoring and reporting
- Testbed staff provision of access to infrastructure (staff, hardware, software, data)

## Use Cases & Applications
Five primary testbed focus areas:

**a) Climate Testbed (CTB)** - College Park, Maryland
- Sub-seasonal to seasonal (S2S) monitoring and prediction
- Temperature, precipitation, and hazard outlooks (2 weeks to seasonal timescales)
- Drought monitoring
- Global Ensemble Forecast System (GEFS) and Seasonal Forecast System (SFS) improvements
- Climate re-analysis products

**b) Fire Weather Testbed (FWT)** - Boulder, Colorado
- Wildland fire situational awareness
- Fire partner services and mitigation support
- Prescribed fire and wildfire management optimization
- Community hazard preparation

**c) Hazardous Weather Testbed (HWT)** - Norman, Oklahoma
- Severe convective weather forecasting and warning
- Two programs: Experimental Forecast Program (EFP) and Experimental Warning Program (EWP)
- Lead-time and accuracy improvements for weather/water forecasts
- Support for Storm Prediction Center and local forecast offices

**d) Hurricane and Ocean Testbed (HOT)** - Miami, Florida
- Tropical weather and marine phenomena
- Hazard observations/observing systems (wind, rain, storm surge, severe weather, waves, dangerous ocean conditions)
- New model guidance for tropical hazards
- Impact-based decision support services
- Located in William Lapenta Laboratory with both physical and cloud-based virtual infrastructure

**e) Hydrometeorology Testbed (HMT)** - College Park, Maryland (partnered with Boulder, Colorado)
- Extreme precipitation forecasting (convection, flash flooding)
- Winter weather prediction (snow, sleet, freezing rain, atmospheric rivers)
- Real-time and retrospective forecast experiments
- AI, NWP, and statistical model verification
- Data visualization strategies
- Three primary experiments: Flash Flood and Intense Rainfall Experiment, Winter Weather Experiment, Atmospheric River Experiment

## Key Requirements for Project Selection

**Technical Maturity (Readiness Levels):**
- Projects should be in the "demonstration" phase (RL 5-8 range)
- Projects starting at RL 4 are acceptable for Fire Weather Testbed only
- Ideal progression: RL 5-6 at start → RL 8 at completion
- Early-stage development or proof-of-concept projects (RL ≤4) are not appropriate for this funding
- Transition from RL 8 to 9 (full operational implementation) is beyond scope

**Project Duration & Deliverables:**
- Short project periods require mid-to-high RLs at project start
- Testing in testbed facilities during project performance is mandatory
- Collaborative test plan development required within 6 months of start
- R2O transition plan development required within 6 months of start
- Direct PI involvement in system adjustments and evaluation planning
- Projects must be ready for testbed demonstration during project period

**Success Criteria:**
- NWS-defined metrics for success
- Operational constraints: added value, ease of use, computational efficiency
- Potential for operational implementation by appropriate NWS offices post-project

## Notable Details

**Organizational Structure:**
- Jointly managed by National Weather Service (NWS) and OAR
- WPO supports USWRP Testbeds and Joint Technology Transfer Initiative Programs
- Testbed staff coordinate project administration, experimental schedules, and facility engagement

**R2O Transition Focus:**
- Goal is practical outcomes transitionable to NOAA operations within 2-5 years post-project
- Iterative information exchange between research and operational communities
- Operations-to-research feedback mechanisms built in
- Testbed staff facilitation of researcher-forecaster collaboration

**Cross-Testbed Collaboration:**
- Multi-testbed proposals are possible; letters of intent and proposals should clearly identify testbed partnerships and collaborative properties
