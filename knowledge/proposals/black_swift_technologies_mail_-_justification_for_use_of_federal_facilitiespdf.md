## Executive Summary
Beck Cotter seeks clarification on submitting a Navy SBIR Phase II proposal that includes participation in Tactical Collective Exercises (TCEs) at federal military facilities (Camp Lejeune). The email thread documents administrative guidance on federal facility justification requirements, base access protocols, and testing participation logistics for a planned April 2026 event.

## Technical Approach
BST initially proposed attending TCEs (Tactical Collective Exercises) 1 and 2 during 2027-2028 as part of Phase II execution. Contact clarified that:
- Ground testing is the appropriate initial approach
- Experimentation should follow incremental methodology: "Build - test - build" or "build - fail - build"
- Two to three primary objectives should be established with stretch goals
- Navy DON SBIR Experimentation Cell can assist in developing test plans

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **Bartington sensor**: Referenced as equipment to be shipped to Navy contact for testing/evaluation

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Tactical Collective Exercises (TCEs)**: Fleet-level military exercises at naval bases
- **Experimentation in support of warfighters**: Primary motivation for facility access and testing
- **Ground testing**: Initial testing phase planned for April 2026 event at Camp Lejeune

## Key Administrative Details
- **Federal Facility Justification**: Clarification provided that "facility access" for TCE participation is locally controlled through AT/FP (Antiterrorism/Force Protection) protocols, not the same as DOE/DOD laboratory facility access
- **Base Access Requirements**: Camp Lejeune access requires:
  - CAC (Common Access Card) or visitor pass application
  - Attendee list submission to event planners by March 27, 2026 deadline
  - Individual name registration for all personnel entering base
- **Classified Work Discussion**: Beck inquired whether classified work elements should be discussed at proposal stage or kept unclassified; guidance suggests close coordination with TPOC if classified

## Notable Details
- Proposal submission deadline pressure: BST lacked formal justification letters from federal facilities by Thursday submission date
- Navy contacts confirmed proposal can be submitted with commitment to provide formal justification letters after the fact
- Sponsor and justification requirements vary by individual TCE event
- Bartington sensor shipment coordination initiated with Navy technical contact
- DON SBIR Experimentation Cell (led by DeSimone) available for test planning support
- Emphasis on achievable vs. stretch goals for warfighter-relevant experimentation
