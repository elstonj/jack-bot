## Executive Summary
This is a consulting work plan for Dr. Gijs de Boer to support BST's SBIR Phase II project to modify and develop the S0 sUAS for meteorological data collection in difficult-to-operate environments for the USAF. De Boer will provide expert guidance on sensor performance, test campaign planning/execution, data evaluation, and reporting over a 15-month period (March 1, 2020 start).

## Technical Approach
The S0 platform development involves:
- **Sensor performance optimization**: Guidance on required accuracy of platform sensors, environmental operating conditions, and sensor specifications for USAF weather reconnaissance applications
- **Flight profile development**: Recommendations for effective sampling strategies to acquire key atmospheric information
- **Boundary layer sampling**: Expert guidance on efficient sampling of thermodynamic and dynamic quantities in different environmental regimes
- **Data validation**: Analysis of collected data comparing wind fields against other measurements and model output
- **Thermodynamic assessment**: Evaluation of measurement quality for accuracy and impact on localized meteorological forecasts

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 Small Unmanned Aircraft System (sUAS)
- Primary platform being developed for USAF meteorological reconnaissance
- Equipped with sensors for thermodynamic and dynamic atmospheric measurements
- Intended for operation in difficult-to-operate environments
- Target applications: weather reconnaissance and prediction support

## Use Cases & Applications
- **USAF Weather Reconnaissance**: Supporting Air Force operations through real-time atmospheric measurements
- **Meteorological Prediction**: Localized weather forecasting support
- **Boundary Layer Studies**: Collection of thermodynamic and dynamic data in various environmental regimes
- **Field Testing**: Evaluation of platform and sensor performance across multiple test campaigns
