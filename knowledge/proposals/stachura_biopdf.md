## Executive Summary
This is a biographical summary of Dr. Maciej Stachura, Chief Technology Officer at Black Swift Technologies, documenting his extensive background in aerospace engineering, UAS operations, regulatory compliance, and multi-aircraft cooperative flight experiments.

## Professional Background & Qualifications

**Education:**
- M.S. in Aerospace Engineering, University of Colorado Boulder
- Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering, University of Colorado Boulder

**Experience at CU Boulder:**
- Involved in over 300 flight experiments
- Participated in multi-aircraft cooperative flight experiments
- Key role in VORTEX2 field campaign (first-ever intercept of a tornadic supercell thunderstorm)

**Regulatory & Operational Expertise:**
- Secured and maintained over 140 FAA Certificates of Authorization (COA) for legal UAS operations across Colorado, Kansas, and Nebraska
- Led NASA Airworthiness Flight Safety Review Board (AFSRB) and Flight Readiness Review Board process for UAS operations in the National Airspace System

**Prior Research:**
- Principal Investigator for Phase I and Phase II NASA SBIR programs focused on soil moisture mapping UAS development, testing, and commercialization

**Doctoral Research:**
- Designed cooperative control algorithms for multi-UAS experiments
- Integrated sensing and communication into unified framework
- Conducted and evaluated successful multi-UAS flight experiments

## Research Publications & References
Stachura has authored or co-authored research on:
- UAS capabilities for new missions (AGU Fall Meeting 2015)
- L-band soil moisture mapping using small UAS for SMAP validation and calibration (IEEE IGARSS 2016)
- Cooperative planning for UAS-based sensor networks in realistic communication environments (2014 dissertation)
- Communication-aware information gathering with UAS (Journal of Field Robotics 2017)

## Notable Details
- Co-founder of Black Swift Technologies
- Extensive experience bridging academic research and operational UAS deployment
- Strong background in multi-agent systems, sensor networks, and regulatory compliance for autonomous operations
