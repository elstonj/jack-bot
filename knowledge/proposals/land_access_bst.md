## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies outlines land access strategy for field testing and methane detection studies to be conducted over multiple years of a project with SMU. BST identifies accessible orphaned well sites in Colorado and other locations, while pursuing partnerships with Indigenous tribes across multiple states to conduct detection studies on tribal lands.

## Technical Approach
- S2 UAS will conduct methane detection studies flying at altitudes lower than 14 CFR Part 107 requirements
- All BST pilots and technicians are Part 107 certified to operate in all conditions and altitudes
- Strategy prioritizes sites with public access to minimize permitting complexity
- Flexible site selection pending tribal partnership entrance

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S2 UAS**
- Uncrewed aerial system capable of methane detection operations
- Can operate at low altitudes below Part 107 minimum altitude criteria
- Suitable for systematic well site surveys

## Use Cases & Applications
- Orphaned well site methane detection and monitoring
- Environmental impact mitigation of methane emissions from abandoned wells
- Economic development demonstrations within Indigenous regions

## Specific Sites & Locations Identified
- San Luis Valley, Colorado: 50+ orphaned well sites with identified public access
- Pawnee National Grasslands, northeastern Colorado
- Southwestern Colorado (additional orphaned wells listed in attachment)
