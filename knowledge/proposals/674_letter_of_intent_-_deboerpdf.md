# Letter of Intent - Dr. Gijs de Boer

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Intent / Consultant Commitment Letter
- Client/Agency: US Air Force SBIR/STTR Office
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Phase II, Proposal #F2-12711
- Date: 29 October 2019
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 sUAS (small unmanned aircraft system)
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (BST); Dr. Gijs de Boer (CIRES/University of Colorado Boulder, consultant)

## Executive Summary
Dr. Gijs de Boer from CIRES/University of Colorado Boulder confirms his intention to serve as a consultant on Black Swift Technologies' Air Force SBIR Phase II proposal for an Atmospheric Sounding UAS. De Boer offers expertise in sUAS development, deployment, sensor calibration, and atmospheric profiling to support system evaluation and testing.

## Consultant Background & Qualifications
- Extensive experience developing and deploying small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS) for atmospheric research
- Field experience in high Arctic, Colorado, and tropical locations
- Expertise in aircraft platform development, sensor development/calibration/evaluation for sUAS, and flight operations planning
- Multiple peer-reviewed publications in the field
- Chair, International Society for Atmospheric Research using Remotely piloted Aircraft (ISARRA)
- Recipient of Presidential Early Career Award in Science and Engineering (PECASE) from President Barack Obama for innovative atmospheric technologies

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 sUAS
- Purpose-built vehicle for atmospheric profiling and atmospheric structure characterization
- Capability to provide wind and thermodynamic information
- Small form factor with standardized output
- Can be integrated into existing workflows with minimal overhead
- Supports complex mission profiles including sensor-reactive control
- Capable of obtaining unique data unavailable through current alternative technologies

## Use Cases & Applications
- Atmospheric profiling for weather reconnaissance and prediction in support of US Air Force operations
- Numerical weather prediction improvement through wind and thermodynamic data collection
- Research and field operations support

## Proposed Consultant Deliverables
De Boer committed to provide expert guidance in:
- Evaluating system capability to meet US Air Force weather reconnaissance and prediction needs
- Contributing to system development, design, and planning
- Planning and executing system testing, calibration, and evaluation
- Evaluating test campaign data sets and comparing measurements to current standards
- Preparing reports, presentations, and communications for dissemination of project results

## Effort & Budget
- Time commitment: Average 6 hours per week as needed
- Rate: $150/hour
- Travel budget: $1,500 to cover field testing, experiments, and project meeting participation

- Letter frames the S0 as a "purpose-built vehicle" with significant potential for atmospheric applications

- Emphasis on the system's ability to execute "sensor reactive control" and provide unique data capabilities
- Integration with existing Air Force workflows highlighted as competitive advantage
