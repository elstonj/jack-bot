## Executive Summary
This document provides Department of Navy guidance on how Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) and Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) contractors must mark and handle Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) in all deliverables, proposals, correspondence, and communications. The guidance specifies mandatory marking requirements, CUI categories applicable to Navy SBIR/STTR work, and examples of proper formatting across different document types.

## Document Purpose & Scope
This is a compliance resource document (not a substitute for actual CUI policy, FAR, or DFARS regulations) that provides practical guidance to SBIR/STTR contractors on:
- Identifying what constitutes CUI
- Properly marking CUI documents
- Understanding applicable CUI categories
- Secure transmission of deliverables

## CUI Definition & Requirements
**Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI):** Unclassified information that requires safeguarding or dissemination controls pursuant to applicable laws, regulations, or government-wide policies. Based on DoD Instruction 5200.48 (effective March 6, 2020).

**Key Access Requirement:** Only individuals with an authorized, lawful government purpose may access CUI. Contractors must control distribution as specified in SBIR/STTR Policy Directive.

**Transmission Requirement:** All DoN SBIR/STTR contract deliverables must be submitted through Navy's SBIR/STTR deliverable website (appropriate for CUI transmissions).

## Mandatory CUI Marking Requirements

### All Documents (Proposals, Deliverables, Correspondence, Emails)

**Cover Page/Page 1 Requirements:**
1. Include "CUI" acronym in both header and footer
2. Include CUI Designation Indicator Block with five components:
   - Line 1: Controlled by: Department of Navy (DoN)
   - Line 2: Controlled by: <SYSCOM identified in contract>
   - Line 3: CUI Category: <Applicable categories from Figure 2>
   - Line 4: Distribution/Dissemination Control: DISTRO B-F (per SBIR/STTR contract)
   - Line 5: POC: <TPOC name or COR and phone/email from contract>

**Subsequent Pages:**
1. Must include "CUI" acronym in both header and footer
2. Do NOT need to repeat full CUI Designation Indicator Block

### Email Markings
- Include "[Contains CUI]" indicator in subject line (best practice)
- Optional: Add CUI indicator to attachment filename
- CUI markings must be carried forward when email is replied to or forwarded

### Document-Specific Formatting (with examples provided)

**Word Documents:** CUI block on cover page with header/footer markings on all pages

**PowerPoint Presentations:** Title slide includes full CUI block; "CUI" in header/footer of all slides

**Excel Spreadsheets:** 
- Each tab with CUI must be labeled "CUI" followed by tab name
- First tab includes full CUI indicator box
- Subsequent tabs include CUI in header/footer

**Email:** CUI designation block embedded in message (HTML formatting restrictions acceptable)

## Determining Applicable CUI Categories

**Three-Step Process:**

1. Determine which CUI category represents the document content
2. Confirm if one or more Category Descriptions align with document contents
   - If NO: CUI markings not required
   - If YES: Proceed to step 3
3. Document applicable CUI categories on line 3 of CUI Designation Indicator Block
   - **Recommendation:** Maximum of three CUI categories per document

### Applicable CUI Categories for Navy SBIR/STTR

**Defense Controlled Technical Information (CTI)**
- Marking: CTI
- Description: Technical information with military or space application subject to controls on access, use, reproduction, modification, performance, display, release, disclosure, or dissemination. Meets criteria for distribution statements B through F.
- *Note: CTI is described as "one example of a common DoN SBIR/STTR deliverable CUI category marking"*

**Export Controlled Research (EXPTR)**
- Marking: EXPTR
- Description: Related to systematic investigation into and study of materials and sources to establish facts and reach new conclusions

**Small Business Research and Technology (SBIZ)**
- Marking: SBIZ
- Description: Information relating to SBIR Program and STTR Program information in government databases (per 15 USC 638(k)(2))

**General Proprietary Business Information (PROPIN)**
- Marking: PROPIN
- Description: Material and information relating to a company's products, business, or activities including financial information, data/statements, trade secrets, product R&D, and product designs/performance specifications

- Document is subject to change based on updates to referenced material
