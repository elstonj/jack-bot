## Executive Summary
This is a NASA SBIR Phase I proposal preparation instruction document outlining formatting requirements, submission guidelines, and evaluation criteria for small business applicants. It specifies proposal structure, page limits, required forms, technical content organization, and compliance requirements for both SBIR and STTR (Technology Transfer) proposals.

## Notable Details

### Proposal Structure Requirements
- **Total Page Limit:** 23 pages maximum (8.5" x 11", 1-inch margins)
  - Forms A, B, C: 1 page each (pages 1-3)
  - Technical content: 20 pages max for SBIR; 19 pages for STTR
  - Briefing chart: not counted toward limit
  - Firm-level forms (certifications, audit, prior awards, metrics survey): not counted toward limit
- **Type size:** Minimum 10-point font
- **Header/footer:** Must include firm name, proposal number, project title, and page numbers

### Required Components (in order)
1. Cover Sheet (Form A)
2. Proposal Summary (Form B) – public information, no proprietary data
3. Budget Summary (Form C) – maximum $125,000 Phase I funding
4. Technical Content (11 parts):
   - Part 1: Table of Contents
   - Part 2: Identification and Significance of Innovation
   - Part 3: Technical Objectives
   - Part 4: Work Plan (detailed description of R&D plan, schedules, milestones)
   - Part 5: Related R&D
   - Part 6: Key Personnel and Bibliography
   - Part 7: Relationship with Future R&D
   - Part 8: Facilities/Equipment
   - Part 9: Subcontracts and Consultants
   - Part 10: Potential Post Applications (Commercialization)
   - Part 11: Essentially Equivalent and Duplicate Proposals and Awards
5. Research Agreement (STTR only)
6. Briefing Chart (one page, non-proprietary)
7. Firm-level forms: Certifications, Audit Information, Prior Awards Addendum, Commercial Metrics Survey

### Key Submission Rules
- Maximum 10 proposals per firm per program (SBIR or STTR)
- NASA selection limit: no more than 5 SBIR and 2 STTR proposals from any single offeror
- Each proposal must be unique and limited to one subtopic
- Substantially equivalent proposals to multiple subtopics will result in rejection
- Electronic submission via Electronic Handbook (EHB) required
- Proposals with classified information are not accepted
- Website references, product samples, videotapes not considered during review

### Principal Investigator (PI) Requirements
- PI must make substantial commitment to project
- PI functions: planning/directing project, technical leadership, personal contributions, primary NASA contact
- PI must spend acceptable amount of time (to be determined by Contracting Officer)
- NASA has sole right to accept or reject PI based on qualifications, experience, demonstrated ability
- PI cannot work more than half-time for academic/nonprofit organization (with STTR exception allowing PI employed by either SBC or RI)

### Subcontracting Restrictions
**SBIR Phase I:** Subcontracts/consultants cannot exceed 33% of research/analytical work (calculated as subcontractor cost + G&A / total contract price less profit)

**STTR Phase I:** 
- Minimum 40% of work by SBC
- Minimum 30% of work by RI
- Other subcontracting (excluding RI work) cannot exceed 30% of research/analytical work

### Federal Facilities Policy
- NASA generally does not provide facilities or equipment for SBIR/STTR work
- Proposals requiring use of Federal facilities (non-laboratory) or personnel require SBA waiver and must include:
  - Letter from SBC official explaining necessity
  - Data verifying absence of non-Federal alternatives
  - Statement from Government official verifying facility availability
- Federal laboratory use requires justification letter from SBC and availability verification
- No SBIR/STTR funds may pay for Federal facility use or personnel; charges require alternative funding
- Selected contractors must provide executed facility use agreement within 10 business days of notification

### Deliverables
- Phase I reports must present: work accomplished, scientific/technical/commercial merit and feasibility, relevance to NASA needs, strategy for development and transition
- Deliverables may include demonstration, prototype, test unit, or product for NASA testing
- Submission via EHB in PDF or MS Word format

### Post-Award Requirements (if selected)
- Within 10 business days of selection notification:
  - Provide signed financial institution statement
  - Provide three references with contact information
  - Provide contractor responsibility information
  - STTR: Submit signed Allocation of Rights Agreement (ARA)

## Important Notes
- The Government is not responsible for monies expended before contract award
- Proposals not following formatting requirements subject to rejection during administrative screening
- All required 11 technical content parts must be included or proposal rejected as non-responsive
- No proprietary data on Forms B or briefing chart
- Classified information will result in rejection
