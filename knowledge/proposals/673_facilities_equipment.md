## Executive Summary
This document describes Black Swift Technologies' facilities, equipment, and testing resources available for the proposed SBIR Phase II project (Topic AF192-005). BST operates a 1,600 sq. ft. facility in Boulder, CO with full prototyping, simulation, and testing capabilities, along with access to multiple flight test areas ranging from preliminary sites to high-altitude test ranges.

## Facilities & Equipment

**Main Facility (Boulder, CO):**
- Size: 1,600 square feet
- Capabilities:
  - Prototyping, simulating, and testing electrical subsystems and avionics boards
  - Full suite of atmospheric and aircraft simulation software for mission scenario analysis
  - Multiple full autopilot systems available for testing electrical and software interfaces
  - Manufacturing capability and tools for integrating prototype and production aircraft
  - Sensor and avionics integration capability
  - Conference/meeting space for stakeholder meetings
- Composites work outsourced to Northwind Composites
- Computing resources: dedicated atmospheric and aircraft simulation tools

## Flight Test Areas

**1. Local Sod Farm**
- Distance: 20 minutes east of Boulder office
- Use case: Preliminary testing during Phase II; can be instrumented for calibrated ground truth measurements

**2. Pawnee National Grasslands**
- Size: 20 x 20 mile test area
- Regulatory status: Applied for 1,000 ft waiver
- Use case: Extended range testing

**3. San Luis Valley (High Altitude)**
- Altitude capability: Testing up to 15,000 ft MSL under current regulations
- Features: Multiple peaks over 14,000 ft elevation
- Use case: High-altitude test scenarios
- Personnel: Jack Elston confirmed conducting tests at this location

## Notable Details
- Company formation date: 2011
- All flight test areas located within reasonable proximity of Boulder headquarters
- Clear progression of test sites available for Phase II work (from simple preliminary tests to complex high-altitude scenarios)
- Ground truth instrumentation capability at sod farm site demonstrates commitment to data validation
- BST has prior experience with FAA/NASA Flight Safety Review Board procedures (referenced satellite imagery from past safety review)
