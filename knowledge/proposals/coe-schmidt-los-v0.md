# CoE-Schmidt-LoS-v0

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support (LoS)
- Client/Agency: NASA (via SBIR Phase II)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA Phase II SBIR - Improved Wildfire Forecast Models
- Date: November 14, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Uncrewed Aircraft System (UAS) with meteorological sensor payload
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (BST CEO), Brad Schmidt (Wildland Fire Projects Manager, Center of Excellence)

## Executive Summary
Brad Schmidt, Wildland Fire Projects Manager at Colorado's Center of Excellence for Advanced Technology Aerial Firefighting, provides institutional support for BST's Phase II SBIR proposal to use UAS-collected meteorological data to improve wildfire weather forecasting models. Schmidt confirms his participation and contribution of subject matter expertise on wildland fire management practices and real-time common operating picture technology.

## Technical Approach
BST proposes to:
- Fly a meteorological sensor payload on a UAS
- Collect atmospheric data from fire-affected areas
- Provide collected data to refine fire weather models developed by research partner NCAR
- Make data available in real-time to deployed firefighters via systems like ATAK (Android Tactical Assault Kit)

## Products & Capabilities Described
**BST UAS with Meteorological Sensor Payload**
- What it is: An uncrewed aircraft system designed and developed by BST equipped with meteorological instrumentation
- Proposed use: Collect weather data for wildfire forecasting model refinement
- Performance goal: Decrease uncertainty in fire weather prediction models

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary**: Wildland fire suppression planning and operations
- **Secondary**: Real-time data delivery to deployed firefighter teams for tactical decision support
- Supports wildfire prevention and response across federal (USFS, Department of Interior), state, and local agencies

## Notable Details
- Supported by research partnership with NCAR for weather model development
- Aligns with NASA initiatives: FireSense Project and ACERO (Advanced Capabilities for Emergency Response Operations)
