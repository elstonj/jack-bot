## Executive Summary
This is a NASA mishap contingency plan establishing procedures for responding to, investigating, and reporting aircraft mishaps at NASA GSFC's Wallops Flight Facility. The plan details roles and responsibilities of personnel, response protocols organized by mishap severity classification (Type A-D), procedures for evidence preservation and impoundment, and coordination requirements with external agencies including the NTSB.

## Technical Approach
The plan establishes a hierarchical response structure:
- **Initial Response Priority**: Protect human life → Protect resources → Preserve evidence → Timely reporting
- **Classification System**: Mishaps categorized as Type A (≥$2M damage or crewed aircraft hull loss), Type B ($500K-$2M), Type C ($50K-$500K), Type D ($1K-$50K), or Close Call (<$1K damage)
- **Incident Response Team (IRT)**: Multi-disciplinary team with defined roles including IRT Lead, Deputy Lead, Scene Security Coordinator, Witness Statement Coordinator, Handling and Impoundment Coordinator, Chain of Custody Custodian, and Office advisors
- **Evidence Management**: Standardized debris collection, tagging, photographing, and chain-of-custody procedures; data impoundment protocols for maintenance records, telemetry, communications, design documents, and personnel records

## Use Cases & Applications
The plan applies to four categories of NASA aircraft operated at WFF:
- **Lockheed Martin P-3 Orion**: Four-engine turboprop, 116' length, 100' wingspan, 14,700 lb payload, 100,000+ lb gross weight. Missions: sensor development, satellite sensor verification, Earth surface/atmosphere research
- **Cessna C-23B Sherpa**: Two-engine turboprop, 58' length, 74'10" wingspan, 7,200 lb payload, 27,100 lb max gross weight. Primary mission: Earth Venture CARVE mission support
- **Beechcraft King Air B-200**: Twin turboprop, 8-passenger capacity, 300+ MPH cruise, 12,500+ lb gross weight, 1,600 lb payload
- **Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS)**: Vehicles ranging from micro (inches) to large (30,000+ lbs), operating autonomously or remotely within restricted airspace off WFF

## Notable Details

**Regulatory Framework:**
- Compliance required with NPR 8621.1B (NASA Procedural Requirements for Mishap and Close Call Reporting, Investigating, and Recordkeeping) and GPR 8621.4 (GSFC Mishap Preparedness and Contingency Plan)
- NTSB notification mandatory within 24 hours for any aircraft mishap
- OSHA notification required within 8 hours for work-related fatalities or hospitalization of 3+ employees

**IRT Training Requirements:**
- National Incident Management System (IS-100) online training
- Four SATERN courses: Overview of Mishap Investigations (SMA-002-07), Mishap Investigation Roles and Responsibilities (SMA-002-08), Completing the Investigation and Mishap Report (SMA-002-09), Root Cause Analysis (SMA-002-10)
- Blood-Borne Pathogens training
- NASA IRT Course and video training
- Plan familiarization

**Impoundment Data Categories:**
Plan specifies 26 categories of data subject to impoundment, including checkout logs, test records, launch records, weather data, telemetry/audio/video tapes, time cards, training records, inspection records, problem reports, emails, maintenance records, design drawings, personnel records, and mission operations data.

**Deployed Operations:**
For off-site operations, Mission Manager develops supplemental "Science Mission Pre-Mishap Contingency Plan" and maintains emergency responder contact lists for the deployment location.

**Emergency Response Kit Contents (Annex F):**
Includes backpacks, marking flags, first aid kit, rain gear, measuring equipment, safety equipment (goggles, gloves, vests), hand tools (machete, hammer, wrecking bar), GPS unit, digital camera, two-way radios, flashlights, spray paint, and blood-borne pathogen kits. Total estimated cost: $1,858.79

**After-Hours Protocol:**
Aircraft Crash Recall Roster activated by Duty Desk Watch (301-342-1095) for emergencies occurring outside normal work hours; Emergency Command Post established in Building B-129.

**Specific Hazard Categories:**
Plan identifies potential hazards including chemicals, explosives, radioactive materials, biological agents, fuel, pressure vessels, compressed air, batteries, hydraulics, accumulators, igniters, oxygen bottles, flares, parachutes, composites, and tires.
