## Executive Summary
BST held an internal meeting to plan Phase II of a Navy SBIR project developing an expendable UAV launched from sonobuoys for anti-submarine warfare (ASW) cued search, detection, tracking, and classification. The team discussed cost management, simplified magnetometer integration, potential Navy collaboration on testing, and sensor/algorithm decisions.

## Technical Approach
- Expendable sonobuoy-launched unmanned aerial vehicle platform
- Magnetometer (MAD) integration as primary sensor
- Acoustic detection system (secondary)
- Jetson-based onboard processing for magnetometer and acoustic algorithms
- Planning simplified magnetometer integration without requiring completion of full acoustic system
- Flexibility to operate with one or two aircraft variants in Phase II

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Expendable Sonobuoy-Launched UAV
- Designed for ASW cued search, detection, tracking, and classification
- Can be deployed from sonobuoys
- Water landing capable (B.6 testing planned)
- Local and ocean test flights planned (B.2)

### Magnetometer (MAD) System
- Primary detection sensor for the platform
- Evaluating QuSpin sensor
- Considering alternative: Bartington magnetometer sensor for comparison
- Simplified integration approach being pursued for Phase II

### Acoustic System
- Secondary detection capability
- Status unclear; Phase II plan allows proceeding with simplified MAD integration without completing acoustic subsystem

### Jetson Computing Platform
- Onboard processing for MAD and acoustic algorithms
- Questions raised about algorithm source (Navy-provided vs. BST-developed)

## Use Cases & Applications
- Anti-submarine warfare (ASW) operations
- Cued search missions triggered by sonobuoy detections
- Target detection, tracking, and classification

## Phase II Planning Details
- **Cost Strategy:** Keep costs low, maximize flexibility
- **Testing:** Local + ocean test flights (B.2), water landing tests (B.6)
- **Variants:** Considering 2-variant approach; option to reduce to 1 aircraft if needed
- **Navy Collaboration:** Exploring possibility of testing with Navy; Royal Navy may offer test site using non-SBIR funds
- **Sensor Selection:** Comparing QuSpin and Bartington magnetometer options
- **Algorithm Development:** Unresolved whether Navy provides MAD and acoustic algorithms or BST develops for testing

## Key Questions/Unknowns
1. Is Navy testing participation possible/desirable?
2. Where would testing occur?
3. What is the Navy approval process?
4. Will Navy provide MAD and acoustic algorithms or should BST develop independently?

## Notable Details
- Phase I deliverables appear to be on track
- Royal Navy may provide testing location independent of SBIR funding, suggesting potential international collaboration pathway
- Flexibility in approach (simplified MAD integration, variable aircraft count) indicates cost-conscious, adaptable development strategy
- Project is actively evaluating multiple sensor vendors for magnetometer, suggesting technical optimization ongoing
