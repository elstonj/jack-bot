## Executive Summary
NASA's 2024 ROSES A.47 solicitation seeks innovative Earth observation-based solutions for wildland fire management across the fire life cycle (pre-, active, and post-fire phases). The program emphasizes resilience-building, beneficial fire enablement, and proactive decision-making, with $3.5M/year available for 10-12 proposals over 3 years.

## Key Program Details

### Timeline
- Notice of Intent (NOI) Due: April 8, 2024
- Full Proposal Due: May 24, 2024
- Project Duration: 3 years
- Funding: $3.5M/year for 10-12 proposals selected

### Seven Key Themes
1. Urgent New Approaches to address the wildfire crisis
2. Supporting Collaboration to improve partner involvement at every scale
3. Shifting from Reactive to Proactive planning and mitigation
4. Enabling Beneficial Fire to reduce catastrophic wildfire risk
5. Supporting and Expanding Workforce for wildland firefighting
6. Modernizing Tools for Informed Decision-making
7. Investing in Resilience through preventive spending

## Scope & Eligible Activities

### Core Requirements
- Demonstrate tangible benefits to partner organizations
- Show improved decision-making outcomes from Earth observations
- Enable measurable improvements in lives and livelihoods affected by partner decisions
- Partners: organizations managing decisions across wildland fire life cycle phases

### Eligible Topic Examples
- Fire risk assessment, prediction, and planning
- Fire mitigation activities and effectiveness assessment
- Fuel inventory, characterization, mapping, and treatment planning
- Post-fire ecosystem restoration and management
- Reactive-to-proactive management transition tools
- Fire emissions, smoke, and human health impacts
- Earth observation/modeling tools for beneficial fire deployment
- Community wildfire resilience enhancement
- Fire impact understanding, prediction, and mitigation to public/environmental health

### Required NASA Earth Observation Data
**Current missions:**
- GEDI, ICESat-2, TEMPO, ECOSTRESS, EMIT, MODIS

**Future missions:**
- NISAR, PACE, SBG

**Airborne datasets:**
- UAVSAR, LVIS, AVIRIS

## Notable Details
- Primary focus: wildfire resilience and beneficial fire; secondary consideration for disaster management and suppression operations
- Emphasis on problems and challenges faced by actual partner organizations
- Multi-phase approaches encouraged (spanning pre-, active, and post-fire activities)
- Transition and operationalization of tools expected
