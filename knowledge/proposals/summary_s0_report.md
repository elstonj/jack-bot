## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies' S0 small unmanned aircraft system (sUAS) conducted 19 flights across four hurricanes during the 2024 Atlantic hurricane season for NOAA's Hurricane Research Division. The S0 demonstrated operational capability in extreme weather environments, including Category 5 Hurricane Milton, with flights accumulating 20.5 total flight hours and achieving a maximum wind speed measurement of 209 knots.

## Technical Approach
- S0 platform deployed from NOAA aircraft (P3) for coordinated hurricane sampling missions
- Flights conducted in real-time coordination with manned aircraft (max range to P3: 191 miles)
- Data collection conducted across multiple hurricane intensity categories
- Communications maintained at ranges up to 308 km during flight operations

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 (sUAS Platform)
- **What it is:** Small unmanned aircraft system designed for atmospheric sampling and observation
- **Operational use:** Deployed for in-situ measurements within hurricane environments, including extreme wind conditions
- **Performance specifications:**
  - Maximum sustained flight duration: 105 minutes (single flight)
  - Total season flight hours: 20.5 hours across 19 missions
  - Maximum wind speed encountered: 209 knots (Hurricane Milton)
  - Maximum communication range: 308 km (in Helene operations)
  - Operational altitude range: sea level to 2,120 meters
  - Success rate: 16 good flights out of 19 attempts (84%)

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Hurricane intensity sampling:** Direct measurement of wind speeds and atmospheric conditions within active hurricanes
- **Multi-hurricane monitoring:** Sequential deployment across four separate hurricane systems (Ernesto, Francine, Helene, Milton)
- **Real-time coordination:** Simultaneous operations with NOAA P3 manned research aircraft for complementary data collection
- **Extreme weather operations:** Demonstrated capability in Categories 1-5 hurricane environments

## Notable Details
- S0 successfully operated in the most intense hurricane environment tested (Category 5 Hurricane Milton), achieving 209-knot wind speed measurement
- Demonstrated reliability across diverse hurricane conditions with 84% mission success rate
- Extended communication ranges (up to 308 km) enabled stand-off operations with manned research aircraft
- Platform achieved significant altitude penetration capability (2,120 m maximum), enabling multi-level atmospheric sampling
- Three failed or aborted missions (out of 19) with brief durations suggest environmental limits or operational constraints were occasionally encountered
- Integration with NOAA's existing P3 hurricane research platform demonstrates compatibility with operational hurricane research workflows
