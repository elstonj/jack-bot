## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies conducted Week 6 flight testing of the Mustang 2 aircraft for ByLight, collecting battery and power consumption data during a 24.7-minute flight. The aircraft performed well in relatively high winds, and data is informing the design of the next iteration (Mustang 2.1). Design work on the new fuselage continues with long-lead procurement items being ordered.

## Technical Approach

### Mustang 2 Flight Test (Week 6)
- Manual takeoff with automated landing
- Flight duration: 24.7 minutes
- Conditions: Thursday, 11/13, relatively high winds (speeds picked up during test, particularly during 30 m/s portion)
- Aircraft assessment: "significantly underweight" with room for improvement in performance and efficiency

### Power Analysis & Efficiency
Key findings from flight data:
- Aircraft was very efficient at most tested speeds except 30 m/s
- 30 m/s inefficiency attributed to:
  1. Aircraft pitch-down of -6 degrees (aircraft too light to hold altitude efficiently at this speed)
  2. Control surface options: 3 control surfaces with reflex capability on trailing edge for higher speed improvement
  3. Propeller overspeeding: 12x12 propeller; 13x13 and 15x13 propellers available for testing

### Efficiency Metrics (Cruise Performance)
- **Chilli Actual - Inefficient**: 300W cruise power at 30 m/s, 7.4 kg mass, 7.26 effective efficiency
- **Chilli Actual - Efficient**: 205.7W at 26 m/s, 7.4 kg mass, 9.18 effective efficiency
- **Chilli Final - Inefficient**: 567.6W at 30 m/s, 14 kg mass, 7.26 effective efficiency
- **Chilli Final - Efficient**: 449W at 30 m/s, 14 kg mass, 9.18 effective efficiency
- Mission capability: Aircraft can handle up to 600W cruise power and still accomplish mission parameters of 400 km range at 30 m/s

### Mustang 2.1 (New Fuselage Design)
**Battery System:**
- Amprius SA112 battery cells (2 packs ordered)
- Custom lattice network structure for terminal connections optimized for maximum current flow
- Battery assembly placed on supporting tail boom, locked in place when fuselage slides over internals
- Bus bar terminals laser-cut from copper

**Fuselage Specifications:**
- Current prototype: 4.375 inch diameter (increased from 4.25 inches)
- Material: Cross-woven fiberglass and epoxy blend (sourced from large-scale rocketry applications for prototype)
- Final version: Carbon fiber
- External fiberglass fuselage procured as long-lead item

**Design Status:**
- Next steps: Avionics placement, wiring diagrams, payload attachment mechanisms
- Aircraft target gross takeoff weight: 30 lbs

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Mustang 2 (Chilli) Aircraft
- **What it is**: Multi-motor electric aircraft with wing-mounted motors and 3 control surfaces
- **Configuration**: Wing pods containing motors and ESCs bonded to center wing; custom wing-mounted motors
- **Specifications**: 
  - Cruise speed: 26-30 m/s
  - Flight endurance: 25+ minutes
  - Target payload/operational weight: 30 lbs gross takeoff weight
  - Battery: Flight battery sized to meet manufacturer CG specifications
  - Center of Gravity: Achievable despite custom wing-mounted motors
- **Performance characteristics**: Efficient across speed range; underweight in current test configuration; responds well to high-wind conditions

### Mustang 2.1 (Next Generation)
- **What it is**: Redesigned aircraft with optimized fuselage, battery system, and power configuration
- **Key improvements**: Larger diameter fuselage (4.375"), custom Amprius SA112 battery packs, refined weight distribution
- **Target specifications**: 400 km range at 30 m/s cruise speed; ~30 lbs GTOW

2. **Incremental Weight Testing**: Next week will add 4 lbs steel plates iteratively to approach 30 lbs GTOW and measure efficiency improvements.

3. **Flight Conditions**: Next flight test dependent on propeller parts arrival (currently delayed).
