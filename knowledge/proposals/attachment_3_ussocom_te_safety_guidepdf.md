## Executive Summary
This is a USSOCOM safety guide establishing requirements and best practices for companies demonstrating equipment at USSOCOM technical experimentation events. It mandates safety risk identification through Deliberate Risk Assessment (DRA) worksheets and references MIL-STD-882E as the standard methodology for system safety analysis across DoD.

## Document Purpose & Context
This is a reference/compliance document provided to potential contractors (including BST) to understand safety requirements for equipment demonstrations at USSOCOM events. It is not a BST proposal or technical document, but rather a regulatory/procedural guide that BST would need to follow when submitting equipment for USSOCOM evaluation.

## Required Safety Submissions
The guide requires or strongly encourages submission of:
- Deliberate Risk Assessment (DRA) worksheet (DD Form 2977 preferred format)
- Safety Assessment Reports
- System/Subsystem Hazard Analyses
- Laser Safety Evaluations
- Interim/Final Hazard Classifications
- Energetic Material Sensitivity Analyses
- Fuse Safety Analyses
- Developmental Test Reports with Warnings/Cautions

## System-Specific Safety Requirements

### Laser Systems
- Nominal Ocular Hazard Distance (NOHD) with/without magnification
- Optical Density (OD) requirements
- Design wavelength
- Beam divergence
- Output power
- Independent verification records of laser output

### Energetic Materials (propellants, pyrotechnics, explosives)
- Interim Hazard Classification (IHC) record
- Novel materials evaluation (sensitivity test results)
- Fuzing/Safe & Arm/Ignition Safety system evaluation
- Recommended standoff distances, surface danger zones, range fans
- Summary of prior live fire testing

### Radio-Frequency/Magneto-Inductive Emitters
- Number of frequencies requested and band
- Transmitter output power and antenna gain
- Radiation Hazard (RADHAZ) analyses
- Established Safe Stand-off Distances (SSDs) for ordnance, personnel, fueling operations

## Key Reference Standards
- **MIL-STD-882E**: "Standard Practice: System Safety" — the primary DoD system safety standard
- **DD Form 2977**: Standard format for Deliberate Risk Assessment
- **sam.gov**: Source for specific solicitation requirements

## Notable Details
- USSOCOM encourages integration of system safety into technology development processes from the start
- Common DoD probability, severity, and risk level definitions should be used in hazard analyses
- Submission of safety artifacts beyond the minimum DD2977 form is "highly encouraged"
- Mitigations should be tailored to specific technology and testing scenario
