## Compliance Framework Described
The document outlines DoN's FWA prevention requirements under:
- **15 USC 638(k)(2):** Statutory requirement for fraud-related conviction/liability database
- **2019 SBA SBIR/STTR Policy Directive:** Agency obligation to evaluate fraud risks, monitor awards, and implement prevention policies

## Key Compliance Areas & Risk Areas

### Eligibility Requirements
- Must maintain ≥51% small business ownership
- Must meet SBA size requirements (<500 employees including affiliates)
- PI must have primary employment (≥51%) with BST at time of award and throughout
- Adequate facilities required to perform work

### Financial Compliance
- **Allowable costs only:** salary, equipment, subcontracting, travel directly related to project
- **Prohibited costs:** personal expenses, vacations, mortgages, childcare, personal vehicle repairs, jewelry, clothing
- Indirect costs cannot exceed percentage of legitimate direct costs
- Fixed-price awards are NOT free money; renegotiation possible if expenditures significantly underrun
- Award funds must be tracked separately from other company funds

### Recordkeeping Requirements
- Maintain timesheets (all employees, tracked separately by project)
- Retain all financial receipts, invoices, statements
- Document subcontractor/vendor quotations
- Keep laboratory notebooks and research facility agreements
- Maintain project status updates
- Report actual expenditures accurately in Phase II

### Work Performance Requirements (STTR-specific)
- ≥40% of work must be performed by BST
- ≥30% must be performed by single research institution partner
- Work performed at university labs does NOT count as company work unless formal agreement exists

### Proposal & Technical Content
- Must represent company's original work; plagiarism subject to criminal investigation
- Letters of support must be specific, dated, non-reusable, with verified signatory contact
- Fabricated or falsified letters are false statements
- No fabrication of personnel bios or publications

## Consequences of Fraud, Waste, and Abuse

### Criminal Prosecution
- **False Statements (18 USC §1001):** 5 years prison, forfeiture, $250K fine
- **Theft of Federal Property (18 USC §641):** 10 years prison, forfeiture, $250K fine
- **Wire Fraud (18 USC §1343):** 20 years prison, forfeiture, $250K fine

### Civil Liability
- **Treble damages:** 3x actual damages recoverable
- **False Claims Act (31 USC §3729-3733):** Up to $11,000 per false claim (multiple claims possible: proposal submission, payment requests, progress reports, cost certifications)
- **Qui tam whistleblower provision:** Informants can recover up to 30% of judgment award

### Administrative Remedies
- Contract termination
- Debarment from SBIR/STTR programs (typically 3 years)
- Prohibition from all federal contracting
- Prohibition of relevant personnel from federal subcontracting
