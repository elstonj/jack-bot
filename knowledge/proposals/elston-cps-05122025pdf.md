- **Proposal/Award Number:** FA864923P1145
- **Source:** DoD AFWERX
- **Period:** 09/2023 – 06/2025
- **Total Funding:** $1,249,724
- **Person Months:** 1.5 (2023), 6 (2024), 2.07 (2025)
- **Objectives:** Develop UAS-based L-band radiometer (developed with OMS, CET, and NASA) for mapping high-resolution soil moisture and soil integrity. Work conducted with AFRL.
- **Overlap Statement:** None
- **Key Capability:** L-band radiometer for soil moisture/integrity mapping
- **Applications:** Military airfield operations, unimproved surface landing assessment

### 7. Revolutionizing UAS Navigation: A Low SWaP-C Sensor Suite for Terrain Relative Autonomy
- **Proposal/Award Number:** A2.01-1010
- **Source:** NASA
- **Proposed Period:** 08/2025 – 01/2026
- **Total Anticipated Funding:** $149,072
- **Person Months:** 0.48 (2025), 0.1 (2026)
- **Objectives:** Enable autonomous return-to-base capability from distances exceeding 10km to within visual line-of-sight using extended vision system and processing hardware for terrain relative navigation. Employ YOLO algorithm for feature tracking (landmarks: towers, structures from map data). Anticipated to provide greater resilience to environmental variations (seasonal changes, snow cover) compared to SLAM-based techniques.
- **Overlap Statement:** None
- **Key Capabilities:** Terrain relative navigation, YOLO-based feature tracking, semantic segmentation, robust return-to-base from 10km+
- **Notable:** Low SWaP-C (Size, Weight, Power, and Cost) design emphasis
