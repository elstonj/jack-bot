## Executive Summary
This document is an unofficial working guide for Black Swift Technologies outlining the AFRL test management process and required documentation, approvals, and artifacts needed to conduct flight testing under AFWERX contracts. It covers the regulatory and safety review pathways, timelines, and responsibilities for obtaining airworthiness clearances, safety approvals, and operational authorizations from AFRL.

## Technical Approach
Not applicable—this is a process/requirements document, not a technical proposal. However, it establishes the framework BST must follow:

1. **Test Planning & Review Process:** Company-prepared test plan submitted for AFRL technical and safety reviews
2. **Government Engagement:** Multiple review boards (Technical Review Board, Safety Review Board) with iterative feedback
3. **Approval Chains:** Different approval authorities depending on risk level (TD Test Lead, AFRL/DO, Flight Operations Authority)
4. **Concurrent Approvals:** Parallel tracks for airworthiness, cyber assessment, environmental analysis, spectrum management, and operational risk

## Key Requirements & Deliverables BST Must Prepare

### Primary Documentation:
- **Test/Demonstration Plan** – Central document defining objectives, system configuration, test methods, airspace, data collection, safety mitigations; substantial effort required
- **Training Plan** – If contractor training of government personnel is contracted; must address proficiency in aircraft operations
- **Flight Test Planning Meeting (FTPM) briefing** – High-level project overview for AFRL specialists; can be scheduled early before test plan completion
- **Technical Review Board (TRB) slides** – Project briefing; 1-2 hours for meeting; potential 4+ hours for follow-up if plan changes required
- **Safety Review Board (SRB) slides** – Requires final draft test plan; 1-2 hours for meeting; potential 4+ hours for follow-up
- **Test Hazard Analysis (THA)** – AFRL Form 12; 2-4 hours per hazard to document causes, effects, mitigations, corrective actions; updates common for SRB closure

### Airworthiness & Flight Clearances:
- **Airworthiness Assessment** – Differs by aircraft type:
  - Manned: Airworthiness Assessment Questionnaire (AAQ) / Aircraft Airworthiness Data Package (AADP)
  - UAS: AFLCMC Airworthiness Circular AC-20-02 (31 July 2020)
- **Flight Clearances:**
  - COCO aircraft under FAA rules → Civil Aircraft Operations (CAO) verification letter from AFRL Delegated Technical Authority
  - Activities not fully meeting FAA rules → Military Flight Release (MFR) + Public Aircraft Operation (PAO) Declaration (more time-intensive)
  - Manned aircraft → Maintenance & Operations (M&O) inspection may be required on-site
- **Maintenance & Operations (M&O) Procedures** – Required for PAO operations; leverages existing company docs but requires significant supplementation to USAF standards

### Government Flight Representative (GFR) Requirements (if applicable):
- Assignment based on government ownership/operation of contractor aircraft
- Flight Operations Procedures (FOPs) and Ground Operations Procedures (GOPs) preparation
- Approvals must be complete before operations

### Airspace & Environmental:
- **Airspace Authorization** – Multiple locations can be proposed; each reviewed for safety, environmental, and compliance
- **Proper Use Memorandum (PUM)** – Required for any cameras or imaging sensors (US citizen privacy protection); 1-2 hours prep; AFRL has blanket PUMs for some locations
- **Environmental Assessment** – AF Form 813 Environmental Impact Analysis; coordination with AFRL Environmental POC for Categorical Exclusion disposition

### Spectrum & Cyber:
- **Spectrum Management/Frequency Authorization** – All transmitter documentation to AFRL/DOO; examples include FCC license, FCC unlicensed compliance, or DD 1494 Radio Frequency Allocation
- **Cyber Assessment** – Information from test plan and other docs; AFWERX Test POC prepares cyber pre-assessment worksheet; 3-4 weeks for COCO with no GFE; may require Interim Authorization to Test (IATT) or Authorization to Test (ATO) if GFE or government-owned aircraft involved

### Day-of-Test Operations:
- **Unexpected Event Worksheet (UEW)** – Contact info for test director, alternate POC, 24-hour availability; emergency services contacts
- **Operational Risk Management (ORM) Worksheet** – Daily readiness checklist; AFRL Forms 33A (UAS) or 33B (manned); or agreed equivalent
- **Test Cards** – Day-of-test procedures, system configuration, mission setup, Go-NoGo items; approval tied to risk level:
  - LOW risk: TD Test Lead approval, valid 30 days
  - MED risk: AFRL/DO approval, valid 14 days
  - HIGH risk: AFRL Flight Operations Authority (FOA) approval, valid 14 days

## Notable Details

1. **Process Flexibility:** Document notes this is an "informal working guide"; AFRL POCs assigned to support company through process execution
2. **Risk-Based Approach:** Many review and approval requirements can be "informal" (no convened meeting/briefing slides) if technical/safety risk is reasonably assessed as LOW
3. **Referenced Standards:**
   - AFRLI 61-103 (5 Oct 2020) & Vol 1, Interim Change 1 (2 Nov 2023)
   - AFLCMC AC-20-02 (31 July 2020) – SUAS Airworthiness Assessments
   - DCMA Instruction 8210-1D (6 Feb 23) – GFR requirements
   - 2020 NDAA Section 848 – Note: COTS UAS ETP no longer required for R&D activities per OSD guidance
4. **Timeline Expectations:** 
   - Test plan preparation may take "substantial effort"
   - Limited updates expected after technical/safety reviews
   - SRB requires final draft to avoid post-approval changes
   - Cyber assessment typically 3-4 weeks for straightforward COCO cases
5. **Parallel Review Tracks:** Multiple independent reviews (technical, safety, cyber, environmental, airspace, spectrum) that do not all follow the same schedule
6. **Iteration Expected:** Document emphasizes that 4+ hours of follow-up work should be expected if SRB or TRB require test plan changes
