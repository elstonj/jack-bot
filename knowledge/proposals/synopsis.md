## Executive Summary
NASA Langley issued an RFI to assess state-of-the-art "intelligent" autonomy technologies for small Unmanned Aircraft Systems (sUAS) integration into the National Airspace System. The RFI seeks information on autonomy technologies, their maturity levels, applications, supporting technology requirements, and developer willingness to collaborate with NASA on technology transfer and community deployment.

## RFI Focus Areas
NASA's UAS Integration in the NAS Project addresses four technical areas:
1. Sense and Avoid (SAA) performance standards
2. Command and Control (C2) performance standards
3. Human Systems Integration (HSI)
4. Integrated Test and Evaluation (IT&E)

Strategic emphasis on certification and sUAS efforts.

## Requested Technology Characteristics
NASA seeks vehicle system-centric technologies enabling "intelligent" autonomy through:
- Heuristics
- Neural networks
- Artificial intelligence
- Other autonomous control and decision-making mechanisms

Technologies can be:
- On-board or ground control station (GCS) located
- Applicable to line-of-sight (LOS) or beyond-line-of-sight (BLOS) operations
- For vehicles smaller than typical manned aircraft, operating at lower altitudes
- Not limited to sUAS under 55 pounds

## Information Requested
Respondents must address six topic areas:

### 2.1 Technology Description
- What the technology is and how it works
- Problem/issue being solved
- Benefits over current state-of-the-art
- How it enables/benefits sUAS missions and NAS operations
- Commercial availability and cost

### 2.2 Applications and Operations
- Specific mission applications benefiting from intelligent autonomy
- Technology shortfalls preventing current implementation

### 2.3 Required Supporting Technologies
- Computational hardware requirements (Supercomputer, FPGA, PC/workstation, smartphone, embedded/microcontroller)
- External information/sensor inputs (ADS-B, EO/IR, radar, etc.)
- On-board vs. GCS location
- Lost-link impact assessment

### 2.4 Technology Maturity
- Technology Readiness Level (TRL)
- Testing/validation environment (simulation, flight-tested, etc.)

### 2.5 Technology Availability
- Licensing model (Open, Open Source, FRND, proprietary)
- Path to bring technology to sUAS community

### 2.6 NASA Collaboration and Technology Transfer
- Willingness to collaborate with NASA
- Need for NASA assistance in maturation
- Intellectual property rights availability
- Mechanism for community deployment
