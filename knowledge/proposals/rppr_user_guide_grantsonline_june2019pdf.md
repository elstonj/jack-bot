## Executive Summary
This is an administrative user guide for NOAA grant recipients on how to prepare, submit, and manage Research Performance Progress Reports (RPPRs) through the Grants Online system. It provides step-by-step instructions for locating awards, completing required reporting sections, uploading attachments, and submitting reports to the federal agency.

## Notable Details

**Report Deadlines & Status Tracking:**
- Annual RPPR due: 30 days after 9-month performance period ends
- Final RPPR due: 90 days after award end date
- Report status categories: Not Delinquent, Delinquent, Submitted, Accepted, Withdrawn, Replaced
- Reports become "Delinquent" if not submitted within 30 days (interim) or 90 days (final)

**Required Reporting Sections:**
1. Accomplishments (4,000 character limit, boxes 24–28)
2. Products (4,000 character limit, all boxes required)
3. Participants & Other Collaborating Organizations (4,000 character limit)
4. Impact (4,000 character limit, boxes 37–43; box 44 requires percentage)
5. Changes/Problems (4,000 character limit)
6. Project Outcomes (4,000 character limit)

**Key Personnel & Notifications:**
- PI/PD and Key Personnel must match award application names
- Changes require pre-approval via Award Action Request
- Automated reminders sent to PI/PD or Authorized Representatives
- Report notifications cannot be turned off

**Report Replacement:**
- Only accepted reports on open awards can be replaced
- No limit on replacement frequency
- Closed awards cannot have reports replaced
- Replacement requires withdrawal + new report submission through same workflow
