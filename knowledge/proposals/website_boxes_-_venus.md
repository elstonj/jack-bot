## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes a deployable unmanned aircraft system (UAS) based on dynamic soaring technology to enable long-duration atmospheric sampling in Venus's upper atmosphere. The system will exploit atmospheric wind shear to extract energy continuously (even at night), survive in the corrosive cloud-top environment, and be small enough to deploy multiple aircraft (up to 8) from a standard aeroshell. The Phase II work aims to advance the technology from TRL 3 to TRL 5 through simulation, controller development, and Earth-based demonstrations.

## Technical Approach

**Core Innovation:** Dynamic soaring—a proven method of extracting energy from atmospheric wind shear without active propulsion. This allows long-endurance flight without relying on solar power (which is unavailable at night on Venus) or fuel.

**Phase II Work Plan:**
1. **High-fidelity simulation**: Integrate Venus atmospheric models (from Phase I) into Gazebo simulation environment with CFD backing
2. **Dynamic soaring controller development**: Mature Phase I controller designs and conduct Monte Carlo testing in simulation
3. **Earth-based autonomous soaring demonstration**: Flight test autonomous dynamic soaring algorithms using Phase I-built aircraft
4. **Deployable UAS for Earth testing**: Build and flight-test deployment mechanisms to validate the concept in realistic conditions
5. **Venus-specific design**: Develop materials for corrosive atmosphere survival, turbine power generation, aeroshell deployment mechanisms, and evaluate emergency solar propulsion options

**Technology Readiness Progression:**
- TRL Start: 3 (analytical and experimental proof of concept)
- TRL End: 5 (component/breadboard validation in relevant environment)

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Dynamic Soaring UAS/Glider:**
- Fixed-wing unmanned aircraft system designed for Venus upper atmosphere operations
- Target mass: just under 10 kg fully instrumented
- Deployable from Pioneer aeroshell (up to 8 aircraft per aeroshell)
- Autonomous flight capability with dynamic soaring controller
- Designed to continuously extract energy from wind shear, including during nighttime
- Based on proven dynamic soaring platforms (adapted for Venus environment)

**Key Technical Features:**
- Harnesses atmospheric wind shear for propulsion-less flight
- Capable of long-endurance atmospheric sampling missions
- Corrosion-resistant materials for cloud-top environment
- Compact, modular design allowing multiple deployments
- Potential for emergency solar-powered propulsion capability
- Turbine-based power generation for electrical energy

## Use Cases & Applications

**NASA Applications (Primary):**
- Venus upper atmospheric observation and sampling missions
- Hurricane sampling UAS missions (Earth)
- Severe storm sampling (Earth)
- Volcanic plume measurement (Earth)

**Non-NASA Applications:**
- NOAA: Hurricane observations and fire weather observations
- USGS: Volcanic emissions monitoring and extended observations
- General applications: Ridge-line observations and severe convective storm monitoring

## Notable Details

- The dynamic soaring approach represents a significant innovation over traditional Venus mission concepts (dirigibles and solar-powered heavier-than-air vehicles), which struggle with high wind speeds
- Key advantage: Energy extraction works continuously, even at night (critical for Venus where solar power is unavailable at cloud top)
- Scalability: Up to 8 aircraft can be deployed from a single aeroshell, enabling multi-platform missions
- This work builds on Phase I foundation (previous dynamic soaring research and controller design)
- Earth demonstration serves as validation for Venus deployment
- Corrosive Venusian atmosphere requires specialized materials selection and construction methods (to be finalized in Phase II)
