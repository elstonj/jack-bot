## Executive Summary
This is a master CRADA framework between USSOCOM SOF AT&L-ST and Black Swift Technologies (Collaborator name redacted in document) for cooperative development of Special Operations peculiar technologies to address USSOCOM technology capability shortfalls. The agreement establishes terms for collaborative R&D, intellectual property rights, classified information handling, and deliverables under the Federal Technology Transfer Act of 1986.

## Technical Approach
The CRADA establishes a framework for:
- Joint development of innovative technologies, materials, components, software, modeling/simulations, and systems for SOF applications
- Regular technical meetings between USSOCOM SOF AT&L-ST and Collaborator subject matter experts (SMEs) to review capability needs and assess technology maturity
- Guidance from USSOCOM on threats, requirements, and priorities at classified levels
- Access to Government Furnished Equipment (GFE) including software and specialized facilities
- Security billets to facilitate classified work
- Leveraging Collaborator's internal R&D investment to reduce government acquisition cost and schedule

## Intellectual Property & Rights

**Invention Ownership:**
- Sole inventions owned by inventing party's employees
- Joint inventions owned jointly by both parties
- BST (Collaborator) has option to obtain title to inventions made by USSOCOM employees
- BST granted non-exclusive license in USSOCOM inventions
- USSOCOM granted Special Purpose License (non-exclusive, irrevocable, royalty-free) for government research/purposes

**Copyright:**
- BST owns copyrights in works created by its employees
- USSOCOM granted Special Purpose License in published BST copyrighted works
- BST must furnish copies of published works to USSOCOM at no cost

**Patent Prosecution:**
- BST has first option to file patent applications (2-month notice window, 6-month filing window)
- USSOCOM may file in jurisdictions where BST declines
- BST responsible for patent costs unless otherwise agreed
- Patent applications must include government interest statement

**Proprietary Information:**
- BST computer software treated as Proprietary Information
- USSOCOM granted Limited Rights license to BST Proprietary Information (use for CRADA purposes only, no manufacture or external disclosure without permission)
- Restricted Access Information (data generated by either party) protected from FOIA release for up to 5 years

## Security & Classified Information

**Facility Requirements:**
- BST must maintain active/valid Facility Clearance (FCL)
- DD Form 254 (Contract Security Classification Specification) required for classified work
- Security Classification Guide (SCG) governs classification of system/program information

**Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence (FOCI):**
- If BST is under FOCI, must have approved Special Security Agreement (SSA) in place
- Classified, critical unclassified, and export restricted information handled through US-only channels per SSA
- FOCI mitigation requires tailored agreement preserving foreign owner's board representation while denying unauthorized classified access

**Information Protection:**
- All Background Technology and Protected Information must be conspicuously marked with CRADA number
- Failure to mark does not waive rights
- CUI and classified information marked per DoDI 5200.48
- Information may not be disclosed in violation of export controls or executive orders
