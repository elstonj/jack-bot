## Executive Summary
The NightFOX project is developing a UAS-based system for nighttime wildfire observations, integrating multiple remote sensing and in situ instruments onto Black Swift Technologies' S2 platform. As of April 2018, the team had completed scanning mechanisms for infrared sensors, built prototype CO2 sensors, and was coordinating instrument integration with BST for July test flights. The S2 platform acquisition was delayed to May due to NightFOX-specific upgrades for nighttime operation capability.

## Technical Approach
- **Platform**: BST S2 small UAS platform being equipped with nighttime operation lighting
- **Remote Sensing**: Telecentric scanning mechanisms for SWIR (shortwave infrared) and mid-IR sensors with prototype controller; software development ongoing for digital input and scan angle reading
- **In Situ Sensors**: 
  - Prototype CO2 sensor (built, initial tests "very encouraging")
  - Prototype CO sensors (delayed delivery expected December, awaiting shipment)
  - Aerosol sampler (successfully tested)
- **Integration**: Coordination between CSD, CU-IRISS, and BST on payload integration; initial payload layout assembled
- **Operations Strategy**: Development of preliminary observation strategies for both in situ and remote sensing flights

- Expected delivery: May 2018 (delayed from earlier schedule)

- Role: BST manufacturer involved in payload integration process
- Purpose: Carry fire observation instruments to higher altitudes and for BVLOS (Beyond Visual Line of Sight) operations

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Mission**: Nighttime wildfire observations during active fires
- **Specific Applications**:
  - Monitoring fire dynamics at night (when daytime systems cannot operate)
  - In situ and remote sensing data collection over wildfires
  - Fire weather modeling data assimilation
  - Prescribed burn observation and monitoring (late summer/fall 2018 planned)
- **Geographic Focus**: Wildfire incidents across US (with focus on higher altitude operations and TFR access)
- **Testing Venues**: July 2018 ISARRA meeting Flight Week; late summer/fall 2018 prescribed burns in Boulder area
