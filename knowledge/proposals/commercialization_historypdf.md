## Executive Summary
This document provides a comprehensive record of Black Swift Technologies' funded research projects and commercialization activities from 2012-2020, spanning 11 major contracts/grants across NASA, NOAA, and USAF. Total award amount across all projects: $3,979,380. The company received $568,088 in total sales/revenue and $390,000 in total investment during this period.

## Funded Projects & Contracts

### 1. Improved UAS Robustness through Augmented Onboard Intelligence
- **Agency/Contract**: NASA NNX17CD08P
- **Award Amount**: $873,577
- **Year of Award**: 2017
- **Phase**: I SBIR
- **Award Period**: June 9, 2017 – December 8, 2017
- **Investment**: $100,000 (VC - Venture Capital)
- **Description**: Development of avionics subsystem and machine learning algorithms for early warning of critical subsystem failures on small UAS. Modular networked monitoring nodes with distributed machine learning capabilities. Objectives include identifying subsystems prone to failure, designing sensors/algorithms for fault prediction, creating compact monitoring nodes, and designing onboard communication network.
- **Key Technical Details**: 
  - Computationally powerful yet size/weight/power (SWaP) constrained monitoring boards
  - Networked nodes sharing information for vehicle-wide health awareness
  - Machine learning-based prognostics/diagnostics
  - Integration with autopilot and operator interfaces

### 2. A Ruggedized UAS for Scientific Data Gathering in Harsh Environments (Phase II)
- **Agency/Contract**: NASA NNX17CP13C
- **Award Amount**: $723,117
- **Year of Award**: 2017 (Phase II)
- **Phase**: II SBIR
- **Award Period**: June 5, 2017 – June 4, 2019
- **Sales/Revenue**: $32,640
- **System**: SuperSwift XT
- **Description**: Development of novel small UAS specifically designed for volcanic ash cloud monitoring with integrated airframe, avionics, and sensors. Based on commercial SuperSwift airframe and SwiftCore Flight Management System. Designed for high-altitude flights through strong winds and damaging airborne particulates.
- **Scientific Applications**: 
  - Volcanic ash cloud characterization
  - Measurement of ash particle size distribution, cloud height, thickness, and spatial/temporal variability of ash concentration
  - Atmospheric dispersion modeling validation for VATD (Volcanic Ash Transport and Dispersion) models
  - Air traffic management support
  - Broader applications: pollution alerts, toxic releases, dust storms, wildfire smoke monitoring
- **Design Improvements Over Phase I**:
  - Modified fuselage design (removable nose cone for sensor payload)
  - Twin-engine configuration on wings
  - Simplified antenna integration and manufacturing of control linkages
  - More easily removable/replaceable soil moisture electronics payload
- **Key Feature**: Well-documented power and data interfaces enabling sensor integration across multiple Earth observing missions

### 3. A Ruggedized UAS for Scientific Data Gathering in Harsh Environments (Phase I)
- **Agency/Contract**: NASA NNX16CP42P
- **Award Amount**: $124,849
- **Year of Award**: 2016
- **Phase**: I SBIR
- **Award Period**: June 10, 2016 – December 9, 2016
- **System**: SuperSwift XT (prototype/design phase)
- **Description**: Phase I feasibility study for SuperSwift XT targeting in situ atmospheric measurements of volcanic phenomena. Addressed need for enhanced in-field data collection capability complementing satellite-based remote sensing and dropsondes. Focus on ash cloud parameter measurement for VATD model validation.
- **Technical Challenge Addressed**: Dearth of detailed spatial/temporal atmospheric data from harsh environments; satellite data limitations (small spatial density, limited measurement frequency)
- **Regulatory Approach**: Strong working knowledge of FAA regulatory environment for sUAS operations

### 4. Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS (Phase II)
- **Agency/Contract**: NASA NNX14CG09C
- **Award Amount**: $874,751
- **Year of Award**: 2014
- **Phase**: II SBIR
- **Award Period**: April 11, 2014 – January 10, 2017
- **Sales/Revenue**: $190,000
- **Investment**: $290,000 (OI - Other Investment)
- **System**: SuperSwift with passive microwave radiometer
- **Description**: Continued development, testing, validation, and delivery of SuperSwift sUAS integrated with passive microwave radiometer for soil moisture measurement. Tight integration of sensor with avionics and airframe for precise flight control at low altitude (15-30m AGL).
- **Capability/Performance**:
  - Full coverage soil moisture mapping: 400 acres per flight
  - Measurement depth: ~5 cm
  - Spatial resolution: up to 15 m
  - Low-altitude precision flight control capability
- **System Components**:
  - Modified Tempest airframe (original tornado sampling design)
  - SwiftPilot avionics (autopilot board, wireless link, ground station, tablet-based UI)
  - Passive microwave sensor (L-band)
- **Design Modifications** (from Phase I):
  - Modified fuselage with removable nose cone for sensor payload housing
  - Twin-engine wing-mounted configuration (vs. fuselage-mounted)
  - Improved manufacturing of control linkages
  - Simplified sensor payload integration

### 5. Advanced Technologies for Coordinated In-Situ Atmospheric Sensing
- **Agency/Contract**: NASA NNX14CP21P
- **Award Amount**: $116,931
- **Year of Award**: 2014
- **Phase**: I SBIR
- **Award Period**: June 20, 2014 – December 19, 2014
- **System/Project**: Coordinated Atmospheric Profiling System (CAPS)
- **Description**: Design and validation of multi-aircraft system for coordinated atmospheric profiling. Each aircraft tightly integrated with airframe, avionics, and sensors for measuring temperature, pressure, humidity, and 3D winds. Cost-effective, rugged, easy-to-operate platform for atmospheric boundary layer study.
- **Scientific Need Addressed**: Continuity of atmospheric processes across multiple scales (meter to kilometers) poorly quantified; directly impacts weather prediction, pollution transport/diffusion modeling
- **Technical Approach**:
  - Removable nose cone with documented power/data interfaces
  - Simple autopilot and onboard computer integration
  - Intelligently optimized multi-aircraft data gathering and coordination
  - Extensibility to other Earth observing missions

### 6. Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS (Phase I)

- **Award Period**: May 23, 2013 – November 23, 2013

- **Regulatory Consideration**: Leverage PI's knowledge of FAA regulatory environment for sUAS operations in national airspace

## Products & Capabilities Summary

### Primary Airframes
- **SuperSwift**: Main commercial platform; proven field-tested design for scientific missions
- **SuperSwift XT**: Ruggedized variant for harsh environments (volcanic ash, high-altitude, strong winds, abrasive particulates)
- **Tempest**: Robust original airframe designed for tornado sampling; basis for soil moisture variant

### Avionics/Flight Management
- **SwiftCore Flight Management System**: Tightly integrated flight control system
- **SwiftPilot**: Autopilot board, wireless communication, command & control, ground station, tablet-based UI
- **Onboard Computing**: Networked monitoring nodes with machine learning capabilities

### Sensor Integration Approach
- Removable nose cone design with documented power and data interfaces
- Low-altitude precision flight control (15-30m AGL capable)
- Passive microwave sensor integration (L-band)
- Multi-sensor fusion algorithms
- Modular, easily replaceable payload design

1. **Small UAS Design & Integration**: Proven ability to tightly integrate airframes, avionics, and scientific sensors
2. **Low-Altitude Flight Control**: Specialized in terrain-following and low-altitude precision flight for sensor optimization
3. **Harsh Environment Operations**: Airframe hardening, high-altitude capability, strong-wind operation, particulate resilience
4. **Machine Learning & Prognostics**: Onboard diagnostics, fault prediction, predictive maintenance, distributed ML algorithms
5. **Multi-Aircraft Coordination**: Networked platforms for coordinated atmospheric profiling
6. **Regulatory Knowledge**: Strong understanding of FAA sUAS regulatory environment
