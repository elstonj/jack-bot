## Notable Details

### Intellectual Property Rights & Ownership
- **Sole Inventions**: Each party owns inventions made solely by its employees
- **Joint Inventions**: Jointly owned by both parties
- **Collaborator's Option**: Can obtain title to inventions made by USSOCOM employees with a Special Purpose License retained by Government
- **Patent Filing**: Collaborator has first option to file patent applications (2-month notice, 6-month filing deadline)
- **Government Rights**: USSOCOM retains a Special Purpose License for government research and purposes (including competitive procurement, but not commercial use)
- **Copyright**: Collaborator owns copyrights in works created under the agreement; Government receives Special Purpose License for published works

### Information Protection & Security
- **Classified Information**: Can be transmitted if Collaborator has active Facility Clearance (FCL) and DD Form 254
- **FOCI Consideration**: Special provisions for Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence situations (requires approved Special Security Agreement)
- **Proprietary Information**: Protected with Limited Rights to Government; limited to use within Government, not for manufacture or external disclosure without written permission
- **Restricted Access Information**: Can be protected from FOIA release for up to 5 years after development
- **Background Technology**: Identified in JWP/IWP; receiving party has no rights beyond use for this Agreement unless specifically stated

### Financial & Administrative
- **Cost Sharing**: Each party bears its own expenses unless otherwise stated in JWP/IWP (no Government funding specified)
- **No Warranties**: Parties make no express or implied warranties regarding research, inventions, or products
- **Liability Limitations**: No punitive, exemplary, or consequential damages; Government retains sovereign immunity except under Federal Tort Claims Act
- **Term**: Commences on Effective Date (last required signature) and terminates at date specified in Section III; either party may terminate with 30 days written notice
- **Modifications**: Changes within scope require mutual written agreement; changes outside scope require Amendment signed by Reviewing Official

### Dispute Resolution
- Parties attempt resolution between themselves
- Unresolved disputes referred to USSOCOM Reviewing Official for decision within 60 days
- 30-day appeal window to ORTA after Reviewing Official decision
- Final agency decision appealable in federal court

### Publication & Disclosure
- 30-day advance notice required before publication or public disclosure
- 90-day objection period; silence constitutes assent
- Publications must acknowledge CRADA unless waived in writing
- Parties coordinate on public announcements (except Congressional/DoD presentations)

### Government Participation & Oversight
- Other Government agencies (USSOCOM components, service labs, DOE labs, federal agencies) may contribute
- SETA support contractors may be used with nondisclosure agreements as needed
- USSOCOM ORTA (Office of Research and Technology Application) serves as technology transfer focal point and records keeper
- Reviewing Official provides final authority on disputes and signature authority

### Operational Constraints
- No sole-source preference established; agreement does not preclude Collaborator from future competitive source selections
- Solution developed under CRADA intended to compete against other approaches
- Similar CRADA offered to other qualified industry partners with similar qualifications
- If FOCI applies, classified/critical unclassified/export-restricted information handled in US-only channels per approved SSA
- Collaborator receives no preferential information for current/planned solicitations
