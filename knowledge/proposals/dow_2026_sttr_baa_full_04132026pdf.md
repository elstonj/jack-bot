## Executive Summary
This is the Department of War's 2026 Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) Program Broad Agency Announcement, establishing guidelines, eligibility requirements, proposal procedures, and evaluation criteria for small business concerns seeking federal R&D funding. The BAA releases topics in six monthly tranches from April through October 2026, supporting innovation across defense-related scientific and engineering challenges aligned with critical technology areas and military service priorities.

## Use Cases & Applications

### Program Structure
- **Three-Phase Approach**: Phase I (6-12 months) for feasibility/merit determination; Phase II (24 months) for continued R&D development; Phase III for commercialization with non-STTR funding
- **Primary Goal**: Stimulate technological innovation by small businesses, deliver technology to warfighters, grow defense industrial base through commercialization

## Key Results / Notable Details

### Eligibility Requirements
1. **Small Business Concern (SBC)** status required per 13 C.F.R §§ 701-705
2. **Work Distribution Requirements**:
   - Minimum 40% of project effort by small business concern
   - Minimum 30% of effort by single research institution
   - Measured by direct and indirect costs; deviations not allowed
3. **Personnel Requirements**:
   - Principal Investigator must have primary employment (>50% of 40-hour week) with SBC or research institution at time of award
   - SBC must have at least one management-level employee whose primary employment is with SBC (not research institution)
4. **Performance Location**: All R&D work must be performed in the United States
5. **Joint Ventures**: Permitted if entity qualifies as small business and meets ownership/size requirements; must disclose foreign relationships

### Performance Benchmarks for Experienced Firms
- Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate benchmark must be maintained
- Commercialization Benchmark requirement addresses transition past Phase II toward commercialization
- Non-compliance may result in cap of 80 total Phase I/Direct-to-Phase II awards from DoW, distributed as:
  - Army: 20 awards
  - Navy: 20 awards
  - Air Force: 20 awards
  - All other DoW Components combined: 20 awards

### Registration & Certification Requirements
1. **System for Award Management (SAM)** registration required (generates Unique Entity ID and CAGE code)
2. **SBA Company Registry** registration required
3. **Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP)** registration required for all proposal submitters
4. **DSIP Access**: Only accessible from United States (50 states, territories, Puerto Rico, Marshall Islands, Micronesia, Palau, DC)
5. **Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC)** compliance required:
   - Level 1: Basic Safeguarding of FCI (annual self-assessment)
   - Level 2: Broad Protection of CUI (self-assessment or independent C3PAO assessment every 3 years)
   - Level 3: Higher-Level Protection against Advanced Persistent Threats (DCMA DIBCAC assessment every 3 years)
6. **Fraud, Waste & Abuse Training** certification required

### Security & Risk Assessment
- **Due Diligence Program**: 15 U.S.C. §638(vv) requires risk-based review assessing:
  - Cybersecurity practices
  - Patent analysis
  - Employee analysis and foreign ownership
  - Financial ties and obligations (surety, equity, debt)
  - Ties to foreign countries/persons/entities
- **Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence (FOCI)**: DoW partnered with Project Spectrum to provide online FOCI training
- **Unacceptable Security Risk**: Proposals deemed to pose unacceptable national security risks ineligible for award regardless of technical merit

### Intellectual Property & Rights
- **Written Agreement Required**: SBC must have written agreement with research institution allocating IP rights and rights to carry out follow-on R&D/commercialization (Model Agreement referenced in SBA SBIR/STTR Policy Directive and DFARS 252.227-7040)
- **Invention Reporting**: Required during award lifecycle
- **Technical Data Rights**: Subject to DFARS 252.227-7040
- **Copyrights**: Government acquires copyright rights per standard FAR/DFARS procedures
- **Patents**: Standard patent rights provisions apply

### Export Control Requirements
- **ITAR Compliance**: If work subject to International Traffic in Arms Regulations, must submit DD Form 2345 (Military Critical Technical Data Agreement)
- **EAR Compliance**: If work subject to Export Administration Regulations, must obtain proper export licenses through Department of Commerce SNAP-R system
- Topics may be identified as ITAR/EAR subject; SBCs encouraged to review SBIR|STTR ITAR tutorial

### Human/Animal Subjects & Specialized Research
- **Human Subject Research (HSR)**: Requires federal assurance, IRB approval, personnel training; average IRB approval 1-3 months; DoW review 3-6 months
- **Animal Subject Research**: Requires IACUC approval and secondary DoW veterinarian approval; no funding until all approvals granted
- **Recombinant DNA**: Requires institutional biosafety committee approval
- Submitters proposing human/animal use encouraged to segregate these tasks to avoid award delays

### Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI)
- **FAR 9.5 Compliance Required**: Must identify and disclose all facts relevant to potential OCIs involving proposing SBC and team members
- **OCI Mitigation Plan**: Required; must describe actions taken/intended to prevent conflicting roles and unfair competitive advantage
- **Supplemental DoW Policy**: Contractors cannot concurrently provide SETA, A&AS, or similar support while being technical performer
- **Disclosure Requirements**: If providing SETA/A&AS to DoW office(s) under current or past award (within 1 calendar year), must disclose DoW office name, contract number, team member providing support, and OCI mitigation plan

### Proposal Submission Requirements
- **Volumes**: Cover Sheet (Vol. 1), Technical Volume (Vol. 2), Cost Volume (Vol. 3), Company Commercialization Report (Vol. 4), Supporting Documents (Vol. 5), Fraud/Waste/Abuse Training (Vol. 6), Foreign Affiliations Disclosures (Vol. 7)
- **Technical Volume Format**: Single PDF file, no password protection/encryption, no embedded active media
- **Page Limits**: Service/Component-specific; SBC responsible for verifying compliance after upload
- **Proprietary Information**: Must mark restricted data with specific legends; DoW assumes no liability for unmarked data
- **Promotional Materials**: Discouraged; external links, CDs, DVDs, videos not accepted

### Classified Work
- **Classified Proposals**: Not accepted under STTR Program
- **Phase II Classified Work**: If required, SBC must have facility clearance (DCSA procedures apply)

### Award Information & Contract Types
- Multiple awards anticipated per topic; individual independent evaluation without regard to other submissions
- **Potential Contract/Award Types**:
  - FAR-based contracts: Firm-Fixed-Price, Firm-Fixed-Price Level of Effort, Labor Hour, Time & Material, Cost-Plus-Fixed-Fee
  - Other Transactions (OT) under 10 U.S.C. 4021 (Research Projects) or 4022 (Prototype Projects)
  - Follow-on production OTs under 10 U.S.C. 4022(f) if conditions met
  - Awards under 10 U.S.C. 4023 (Procurement for Experimental Purposes)
- **No Obligation**: DoW under no obligation to make Phase I awards; offerors responsible for all proposal costs

### Additional Support Programs
- **Phase II Enhancement Policy**: Some Services/Components offer additional Phase II funding if SBC matches with non-STTR DoW or private sector funds
- **Commercialization Readiness Program (CRP)**: Available through Army, Navy, Air Force
- **OSW Accelerated Research for Transition (ART)**: Technology maturation/transfer program; contact osd.ncr.ousd-r-e.mbx.sbir-sttr-tech-transition@mail.mil
- **State Assistance Programs**: Vary by state; may include matching funds, administrative support

### Open Topics
- Released under this BAA, clearly identified in title/objective
- Generalized mission requirements vs. specific technical objectives
- SBC limited to one proposal per open topic; duplicates marked nonresponsive

### Federal Acquisition Supply Chain Security Act (FASCA)
- Must review SAM.gov at least quarterly for covered articles/FASCA orders
- Proposer represents conduct of reasonable inquiry and non-use of prohibited covered articles
- Must report to contracting officer any covered articles used during performance

### BlueUAS Certification
- As of December 2025, transitioned from Defense Innovation Unit (DIU) to Defense Contract Management Agency (DCMA)
- Not required for STTR competition unless specifically stated in topic
- Proposing companies not reimbursed for BlueUAS certification costs
