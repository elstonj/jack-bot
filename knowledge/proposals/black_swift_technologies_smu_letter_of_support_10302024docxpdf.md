# Black Swift Technologies SMU Letter of Support

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Commitment/Support
- Client/Agency: Department of Energy (DOE), Southern Methodist University (SMU)
- Program/Solicitation: DE-FOA-0003343, "Area of Interest 3 - Long-Term UOW Monitoring"
- Date: October 29, 2024
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 UAS, Quanta3 methane spectrometer, S2 Methane Leak Detection (MLD) solution
- Key Personnel: Saeed Salehi (SMU), Stefan Metzger (PhD, subcontractor), Stuart Stein and Jeff Stein (Hyde Park West Telecommunications, project manager), CEO of Black Swift Technologies

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies commits to subcontract with Southern Methodist University on a DOE-funded project to develop and test advanced methane emissions monitoring technologies for Undocumented Orphaned Wells (UOWs). BST will provide drone-based methane detection services using their S2 UAS platform paired with a Quanta3 spectrometer, conducting at least 50 flights over three years with a total subcontract value of $388,546.

## Technical Approach
- **Primary System**: S2 UAS integrated with Quanta3 methane spectrometer for methane leak detection and quantification
- **Methodology**: Custom eddy covariance techniques derived from flux tower methodologies for precise gas exchange measurements
- **Validation Strategy**: Results will be validated against Xplorobot measurements (full 3 years) and Licor Methane Systems evaluation (6 months during Year 2)
- **Supporting Infrastructure**: Subcontracted continuous flux tower monitoring (TRL-5) with Dr. Metzger for Years 2 and 3, including 7-meter tower, sensors, data logger, annual maintenance, and data analytics

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS (Unmanned Aerial System)
- **What it is**: Rugged, high-endurance fixed-wing aircraft designed for environmental monitoring
- **Flight endurance**: Up to 90 minutes
- **Wingspan**: 3 meters
- **Payload capacity**: 2 kg
- **Operating conditions**: Capable of flying in demanding environments including rugged terrain and low temperatures
- **Proposed use**: Mount Quanta3 methane spectrometer for methane leak detection in hard-to-reach locations (remote oil and gas fields, mines, landfills, agricultural areas)

### S2 Methane Leak Detection (MLD) Solution
- **What it is**: Integrated platform combining S2 UAS with custom-built Quanta3 methane spectrometer
- **Capabilities**: Detecting and quantifying methane emissions with high sensitivity
- **Proposed use**: Field trials for cost-effective long-term well monitoring

### Custom Methane Sensor (Quanta3 spectrometer)
- **Proposed enhancement**: Budget includes funds for purchase of an additional custom methane sensor in Year 1 to maximize flight days and prevent equipment failure delays

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary focus**: Methane emissions detection and monitoring from Undocumented Orphaned Wells (UOWs)
- **Secondary applications**: Remote oil and gas fields, mines, landfills, agricultural areas
- **Mission type**: Long-term, cost-effective monitoring and remediation of orphan well emissions and other toxic gases

- **Planned activities**: At least 50 drone flights over three years with data analysis
- **Key personnel costs**: Stefan Metzger, PhD (data analysis subcontractor to BST)
