# Customer Memorandum - Required CAO 19MAR2023

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Template/Form Instructions for SBIR/STTR Phase II Proposal
- **Client/Agency:** Department of the Air Force (DAF), Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL), AFWERX
- **Program/Solicitation:** SBIR/STTR Phase II Open Topic Program (AFVentures)
- **Date:** March 19, 2023
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None (this is a form template, not a BST-specific document)
- **Key Personnel:** Not applicable (template document)

## Document Purpose
This is a mandatory template and form instructions document for companies submitting SBIR/STTR Phase II proposals to the Air Force under the AFVentures Open Topic program. It establishes the required format and content for a "Customer Memorandum" that must accompany Phase II proposals, signed by Air Force stakeholders.

## Key Sections & Requirements

### 1. Defense Mission Need (Section 2)
- Must articulate specific national defense-related mission need
- Must address mission impact on USAF and/or USSF
- Must include measurable goals (up to 5 required)
- Describes how proposed technology development contributes to future mission fulfillment

### 2. Project Timeline (Section 3)
- Must specify start date and period of performance
- Can propose shorter timeframe than solicitation allows

### 3. Phase II Funding Commitment (Section 4)
- Non-SBIR/STTR Government funding is optional but can supplement award
- If committed: DRAFT funding documents (Form 9s/MIPRs) due within 45 days of solicitation closing
- CERTIFIED funding documents due 5 business days after selection letters issued
- Commitment does not constitute obligation; subject to funds availability
- If certified documents not provided, effort proceeds without commitment consideration

### 4. Stakeholders (Section 8) - Critical Requirements
**Primary End-User Organization:** The organization that will use/benefit from the solution
- Must provide: Name, rank/grade, duty title, email

**Primary Customer Organization:** Will manage future procurement, sustainment, funding, contracting for Phase III
- Must provide: Name, rank/grade, duty title, email

**Primary Phase II Technical Point of Contact (TPOC):** MANDATORY - Must be DAF civilian or military member
- Must provide: Name, rank/grade, organization, email, duty phone
- Will receive/accept deliverables, provide feedback, overcome execution barriers, drive Phase III transition

**Alternate Phase II TPOC:** Secondary contact with same information requirements

**Strategic Stakeholders:** Name of MAJCOM/HQ function and PEO/Center most directly impacted

**Staff Functions Providing Concurrence:** (as applicable)
- Security Office
- Information Assurance/Cybersecurity
- Finance
- Contracting
- Strategy, Integration, and Requirements
- Small Business Office
- Commander
- Legal Counsel
- Engineering/SPO/Test
- Public Affairs

### 5. Phase III Transition Strategy (Section 9)
- Must provide tailored narrative (not boilerplate)
- Core SBIR/STTR objective: increasing likelihood of transition to Phase III
- Must address:
  - Phase III funding strategy
  - Requirements personnel role (if applicable)
  - Contract vehicle
  - Integration plan
- Transition subject to: acquisition planning, funds availability, security assessments, SB/Legal/Contracting review
- No obligation for DAF to fund Phase III

### 6. Security Requirements (Section 10)
- Company must specify if military installation/facility access required
- If yes: TPOC will work with visitor centers/security offices to sponsor personnel
- Company must specify if Common Access Cards (CACs) required for DoD network access
- If yes: Security Office will process documentation to issue CACs
- Company must specify if classified accesses (DD254) required
- If yes: Security Office works with TPOC to draft DD254 and obtain required accesses

### 7. Cybersecurity (Section 11)
- Applies if solution involves software/IT connecting to DoD networks/systems or using CUI data
- If applicable: Information Assurance/Cybersecurity Office determines if Authorization to Operate (ATO) or Interim Authorization To Test (IATT) required
- Requirements based on: NIST SP 800-171 Rev 1 and DFARS PGI 239.76
- May utilize established DevSecOps environments within Platform One
- AFVentures does not manage ATO/cybersecurity processes—customer/TPOC responsible

### 8. Flight Testing or Unmanned Aerial Systems (Section 12)
- Company must specify if flight testing or UAS utilization required
- If yes: Company must coordinate with TPOC, AFRL/DO, and AFWERX Test Personnel
- Must prepare flight test planning documents per AFRL/DO instructions
- Blanket waiver exists for AFRL/DO to procure COTS UAS for cyber analysis, signature, system check-out, profile data
- Contractors proposing UAS purchases must pursue "use waiver" with Government TPOC
- Contract execution not dependent on use waiver completion

### 9. Signatories (Section 13) - Critical Requirement
- **Two CAC-enabled digital signatures MANDATORY**
- DAF Customer signatory: Must understand and influence operational needs, requirements, or funding
- DAF End-User signatory: Must understand and influence operational needs, requirements, or funding
- **DAF Customer and DAF End-User cannot be the same person**
- Without two valid signatures: Customer Memorandum NOT accepted; proposal disqualified

### 10. Required Attachments
1. **Attachment 1.1:** Schedule of Milestones (required)
2. **Attachment 1.2:** Non-Proprietary Work Plan (required)
3. **Attachment 1.3:** TPOC Designation Letter (informational; not included in signed memo)
4. **Attachment 1.4:** SBIR/STTR Definitions and Suitability Resources (informational; not included in signed memo)

## Compliance Critical Points
- Use of this template is MANDATORY
- All elements MUST be present and complete
- All fields must be filled in; use "N/A" only if truly not applicable
- Proposals without completed and signed Customer Memorandum will be determined **non-responsive** and **not considered for award**
- No boilerplate language acceptable; tailor all sections to specific organization and solution
- TPOC must be notified of duties prior to memo signature

## Notable Details

### AFWERX Resources
- Applicants directed to https://afwerx.com/afventures-overview/ for videos, sample applications, additional resources
- AFVentures does not manage security, ATO, cybersecurity, or flight testing processes—customer/TPOC are responsible

### Key Definitions from Instructions
- **Flight Testing:** Any set of related events where vehicle moves through air with controlled/maneuvering aerial transport (includes balloon, aircraft, UAS, air taxis, ground test, cockpit evaluations, etc.). Does NOT include solely ballistic flight paths.
- **End-User vs. Customer:** End-User uses/benefits from solution; Customer manages future procurement, sustainment, funding, contracting

### Financial Process Timeline
- Solicitation closes → 45 days: DRAFT funding documents due to AFRL/RGF
- Selection letters issued → 5 business days: CERTIFIED funding documents due
- Contact: AFRL.SBIR.STTR@us.af.mil for funds transfer initiation
