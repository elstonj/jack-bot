## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is planning a proposal to NSF's CO-WY Climate Engine for a translational research project focused on methane emissions analysis. The proposal will leverage existing BST capabilities in aerial sensing and cloud-based data delivery, partnering with multiple institutions including Colorado State University (CSU), Murray State, and Environmental Defense Fund (EDF) to meet grant matching requirements.

## Technical Approach
- Deploy S2 aircraft equipped with CH4 (methane) detection payload for emissions monitoring
- Utilize cloud-based computing and data delivery infrastructure (AWS ~$430/month current bill)
- Focus on surveying abandoned sites on rural/tribal lands for methane emissions
- Development of "flux mapping" capability for methane sources
- Training of deployment teams and partner institutions (CSU) on S2 operations

## Products & Capabilities Described

- Small unmanned aircraft system
- Equipped with CH4 detection payload
- Proposed use: methane emissions surveying and monitoring
