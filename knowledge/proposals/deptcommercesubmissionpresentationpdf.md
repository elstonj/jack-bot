## Executive Summary
This is a procedural training presentation for Department of Commerce grant applicants on how to submit and track federal grant applications through the Grants.gov and eRA Commons systems. It covers the complete workflow from organization registration through application submission, status tracking, and post-submission monitoring.

## Content Summary
**Note:** This document contains no information about Black Swift Technologies, its products, capabilities, or proposed work. It is purely instructional material on federal grant administration processes.

The presentation covers:
- **Registration Requirements**: eRA Commons setup for organizations, signing officials, and project directors/principal investigators (PD/PIs)
- **Finding Opportunities**: Locating Notices of Funding Opportunity (NOFOs) on Grants.gov
- **Application Preparation**: Completing required forms (SF-424) with PD/PI username and UEI
- **Submission Process**: Submitting applications via Grants.gov (not directly through eRA Commons)
- **Tracking Mechanisms**: 
  - Via Grants.gov tracking number
  - Via eRA Commons Status module (accessible to SO, AO, PD/PI roles)
- **Post-Submission Steps**: Monitoring application status after 48-hour validation window; addressing errors; following peer review process
- **Support Resources**: Links to eRA Service Desk, Grants.gov Support Center, and FAQ documentation

## Notable Details
- Applications must be submitted via Grants.gov; eRA Commons is used for tracking only
- Applicants receive a tracking number upon successful submission
- Error-free applications proceed to awarding agency (DOC) after 48-hour validation window
- Emphasis on periodic manual status checks rather than relying on email notifications
- Recommendation to submit early and address validation errors promptly
