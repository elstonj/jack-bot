## Executive Summary
This document outlines Black Swift Technologies LLC's security procedures and compliance requirements for managing NASA IT resources under SBIR/STTR contract 80NSSC25C0155. BST commits to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of NASA electronic information, including Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), in accordance with NASA FAR Supplement clause 1852.204-76 and NIST/NASA IT security standards.

## Technical Approach
BST's IT security approach centers on:
- **NASA System Access:** Remote access limited to SBIR/STTR Awardee Firm ProSAMS (https://prosams.nasa.gov/) for submission of deliverables and invoices
- **Access Management:** Through NASA Account Management System (NAMS) requiring Agency User ID and password via Identity and Access Management System (IdMAX)
- **User Registration:** Limited to personnel involved in contract negotiation and administration
- **Training Requirement:** All registered personnel must complete NASA Online Annual IT Security Training
- **Information Protection:** Protection of confidentiality, integrity, and availability of NASA electronic information and prevention of unauthorized disclosure

## Use Cases & Applications
This plan governs BST's handling of:
- Unclassified electronic information and Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)
- Contract deliverables and technical reports
- Invoices and billing documentation
- Sensitive information as defined under the Computer Security Act of 1987

## Key Compliance Obligations

### Sensitive Information Protection (per 1852.237-72)
BST shall:
1. Use sensitive information only for contract performance purposes
2. Safeguard sensitive information from unauthorized use and disclosure
3. Limit access to employees with need-to-know
4. Preclude access and disclosure to outside persons/entities
5. Train employees on authorized uses and mandatory protections
6. Obtain written affirmation of training compliance from each employee
7. Administer monitoring processes for compliance and report any breaches to the Contracting Officer (CO) and Contracting Officer's Representative (COR)

### General Rules of Behavior (15 rules)
BST personnel must:
- Use NASA systems only for lawful, official, authorized purposes per current NASA IT security requirements
- Protect all NASA information including PII and CUI
- Report known or suspected security incidents immediately to firm help desk, security manager, or supervisor
- Encrypt all NASA data on mobile devices and removable media when transported outside the organization
- Read NASA privacy and security notices before accessing NASA networks
- Screen-lock/log off computers when leaving work areas
- Verify proper clearance, authorization, and need-to-know before providing access to NASA information
- Minimize information on IT systems while traveling
- Properly mask and label classified, sensitive, and proprietary documents per NASA IT Security Policies
- Adhere to Separation of Duties principles
- Avoid anonymizer sites that bypass agency security mechanisms
- Protect IT equipment and media from destruction, damage, loss, theft, or data compromise
- Ensure supervisors adequately instruct, train, and supervise employees
- Meet minimum password requirements
- Never share passwords; protect them at highest classification/sensitivity level

### Incident Response
BST must immediately notify NASA Security Operations Center and the CO/COR of any:
- Intentional or inadvertent information security incidents affecting confidentiality, integrity, or availability
- NASA IT system or data compromise, misuse, distortion, loss, or destruction
- Issues affecting contract deliverables
