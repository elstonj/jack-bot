## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies demonstrated 24-hour persistent wildfire monitoring and airspace situational awareness using the S2 small uncrewed aircraft system integrated with NOAA's NightFOX infrared payload. The project successfully fused real-time ADS-B, Remote ID, and software-defined radio (SDR) detections with live IR data delivery into WFTAK incident command systems, proving the S2 as both an advanced sensor platform and communications relay for wildfire aviation safety operations.

## Technical Approach
- **Core Platform**: S2 fixed-wing UAS maintained 24-hour persistent flight station over wildfire areas
- **Payload Integration**: NOAA NightFOX IR/FRP (Fire Radiative Power) mapping payload for high-resolution thermal imaging
- **Airspace Surveillance**: Live fusion of three aircraft detection methods:
  - ADS-B (cooperative aircraft)
  - Remote ID (drone detection regulatory compliance)
  - SDR/AI (software-defined radio with AI algorithms for non-cooperative aircraft detection)
- **Data Relay**: S2 served dual role as sensor platform and communications relay, transmitting fused airspace picture to WFTAK (Wildfire Tactical Awareness Kit) in real-time
- **Calibration**: Produced calibration-verified IR data for operational use

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Use Case: Wildfire Airspace Management
- **Mission**: Identify non-participating (non-cooperative) aircraft within Temporary Flight Restrictions (TFRs) established for wildfire firefighting operations
- **Operational Integration**: Live airspace deconfliction and aircraft detection relayed to incident commanders and field units via WFTAK
- **Safety Application**: Prevents aerial collisions and airspace conflicts during firefighting operations

### Sensor & Intelligence Application
- Persistent IR monitoring of fire behavior, intensity, and spread
- Real-time situational awareness products for incident command
- Calibration-verified thermal data for fire characterization

## Significance
This project bridges critical operational needs in wildfire aviation safety—persistent airspace awareness and fire intelligence—into a single, deployable, uncrewed system. The successful integration of thermal imaging, multi-modal aircraft detection, and real-time incident command connectivity demonstrates the S2's potential as a force multiplier for wildfire incident management, with clear pathways to agency adoption and commercial deployment across disaster response sectors.
