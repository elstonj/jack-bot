## Executive Summary
This proposal seeks funding to develop the TTwistor Advanced Small Unmanned Aircraft System, a next-generation electric-powered UAS derived from the successful Tempest UAS platform. The TTwistor leverages lessons learned from Tempest deployments and integrates Black Swift Technologies' SwiftPilot Pro autopilot and advanced networking software. The system addresses critical needs for cost-effective, high-performance small UAS in emerging civil/commercial markets, with NOAA identified as the first customer and investor.

## Technical Approach

**Airframe Design:**
- Twin-motor configuration with motor-propellers mounted on each wing (vs. fuselage-mounted in Tempest)
- Enables centerline instrument mounting with forward-facing probes into undisturbed airflow
- Eliminates need for counterweights and in-flight trimming, improving aerodynamic performance
- Supports swappable, modular "nosecones" customized for specific instruments/payloads
- Wing-mounted propellers increase local circulation for improved takeoff lift
- Manufactured by UASUSA, Inc.

**Autopilot & Control:**
- Black Swift Technologies SwiftPilot Pro autopilot integration
- Advanced over Tempest's original autopilot systems

**Communications:**
- 900-MHz phased-array antenna system with electronic beam steering (under development, not included in this proposal scope)
- Prototype developed by CU senior project team (2013); second prototype in development
- Superior to original mechanically-steered antenna used in Tempest deployments
- Partnership with Boulder-based First RF Corp. for development and commercialization

**Software:**
- Advanced command, communications, control, and data networking software (proposed BST subcontract)
- Evolution of NetUAS architecture originally developed by Jack Elston for VORTEX2 campaign

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift Technologies SwiftPilot Pro Autopilot
- Primary autopilot system for TTwistor aircraft
- Represents advancement over earlier Tempest autopilot generation
- Enables autonomous flight control and integration with ground station communications

### BST Command/Control/Communications/Data Networking Software
- Advanced networking architecture building on proven NetUAS platform
- Enables distributed command, control, and data management across aircraft-ground station network
- Proposed for $30,000 development subcontract

### TTwistor Aircraft (Airframe + Autopilot + Software Integration)
- Small electric-powered UAS platform
- Designed for atmospheric sensing, environmental monitoring, emergency response
- Enhanced payload flexibility compared to Tempest through centerline mounting architecture
- Market positioning: high-performance small UAS niche

## Use Cases & Applications

**Demonstrated Applications (Tempest Heritage):**
- Severe weather research / tornado genesis studies (VORTEX2 campaigns, 2009-2010)
- Emergency/first response operations
- Precision agriculture
- Surveying/mapping
- Education/science missions
- Customized applications

**Proposed TTwistor Applications:**
- NOAA environmental monitoring missions addressing gaps between surface instruments and satellite data
- Operations in dangerous/remote environments (poles, oceans, wildlands, volcanic islands, wildfires)
- Cost-effective observing capacity increases for civil agencies
- First responder and law enforcement operations

## Market Context & Justification

**Market Size:**
- AUVSI 2013 report projects $82.1 billion economic impact of UAS integration (2015-2025)
- 34,000+ manufacturing jobs and 70,000+ new jobs projected in first three years
- Precision agriculture and public safety identified as most promising commercial/civil markets

**Customer Base:**
- Tempest UAS: 50+ aircraft sold to universities (Nebraska, MIT), US Navy Research Lab, Raytheon, Lockheed Martin, Airware
- NOAA UASPO identified as "First Customer and Investor" with stated vision to revolutionize observing strategies
- Public market growth ongoing through government agencies and universities with FAA Certificates of Authorization/Waivers (COAs)
- Commercial market anticipated to expand significantly once FAA publishes regulations for small UAS operations (target: November 2014; referenced FAA Modernization and Reauthorization Act of 2012)

**Regulatory Context:**
- FAA directed to integrate civil UAS into National Airspace System (NAS) by September 30, 2015
- Small UAS Aviation Rulemaking Committee recommendations published April 2009, awaiting implementation
- Proposed timing advantageous for market entry once regulatory framework established
