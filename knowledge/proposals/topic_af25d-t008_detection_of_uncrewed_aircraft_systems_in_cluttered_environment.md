## Executive Summary
This Air Force SBIR topic solicits novel sensor technology to detect small uncrewed aircraft systems (UAS) flying low through cluttered environments (trees, buildings) where traditional electro-optical, infrared, radar, and lidar systems fail. The program seeks cost-effective solutions that can achieve 80% detection effectiveness with <20% false alarms in Phase II, scaling to 95% effectiveness with <5% false alarms in Phase III, while integrating into layered defense architectures protecting military installations.

## Technical Approach
The Air Force identifies emerging acoustic and RF/wireless signal sensors combined with artificial intelligence and machine learning as promising approaches to "see through" clutter. Solutions should:
- Detect low-flying, small, relatively slow UAS threats obscured by foreground clutter
- Distinguish threats from false alarms (e.g., birds)
- Operate cost-effectively across large military installation perimeters
- Use mesh networking to trade range for coverage
- Integrate with traditional sensor systems in layered defense architecture
- Not require UAS type/size determination in Phase II

## Use Cases & Applications
- Detection of autonomous/semi-autonomous UAS threats infiltrating military installations
- Low-altitude, low-speed UAS detection through trees and between buildings
- Defense against coordinated UAS swarms using intelligent avoidance and detection evasion
- Large-scale perimeter defense for typical military installations
- Integration into Air Base Air Defense (ABAD) operations

## Technology Areas & Priorities
- Primary: Sensors
- Modernization Priorities: Emerging Threat Reduction; Integrated Sensing and Cyber
- Keywords: UAV, UAS, counter-UAV, counter-UAS

## Phase Breakdown

**Phase I:** Feasibility study identifying limitations of current sensor technology, evaluating novel sensor approaches, and proposing development pathway.

**Phase II:** Prototype delivery demonstrating ≥80% detection effectiveness, <20% false alarms, TRL 6 maturity, with preliminary concept of operations for large-scale military installation coverage.

**Phase III (Dual-Use):** Fully-functional product achieving ≥95% detection effectiveness, <5% false alarms, TRL 9 maturity, with roadmap for integration with traditional sensor arrays, counter-measure guidance, and UAS type/size determination.
