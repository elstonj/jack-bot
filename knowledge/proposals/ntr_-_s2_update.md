## Executive Summary
The BlackSwift S2™ is a purpose-built UAS designed for scientific payloads in demanding atmospheric environments (high-altitude, corrosive particulates, strong turbulence). The S2 offers larger payload capacity, longer endurance, higher ceiling, and greater range than comparable airframes, with autonomous launch/flight/landing capability for remote mountainous operations. The system features field-swappable payload design with standardized data and power interfaces.

## Technical Approach

**Design Philosophy:**
- Simplified operation to minimize ongoing operational costs
- Modular composite airframe with removable nose cone for payload accommodation
- Pusher design that leaves nose in free stream for instrument mounting
- RF noise mitigation across large frequency swath to protect payload electronics
- Scalable production design

**Key Design Features:**
- Fully composite airframe construction
- Large payload volume: up to 5 lbs of payload
- Packable into case for commercial airline shipping
- Field-swappable payload system with common power, data, and mechanical interface
- No specialized tools required for payload changes
- Complete sensor enclosure within nose cone for clean atmospheric measurements

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Mission (Soil Moisture Mapping):**
- Soil moisture mapping (SMM) payload for satellite calibration/validation
- Demonstrated at two test sites with significant interest from precision agriculture community

**NASA Applications:**
- **MALIBU Project:** Multispectral payload flown in sequence with SMM payload; field-swappable capability reduces operational overhead
- **NOAA FIREX Project:** Meteorological parameter measurement including relative humidity, particle number density, trace gases (CO, CO₂) for combustion efficiency determination using both in-situ and remote sensing payloads

**Precision Agriculture:**
- Aerial soil moisture sensing to optimize water and fertilizer application
- Demonstrated >15% water savings at IRF test site in Colorado while maintaining crop yields
- Overcomes limitations of in-situ probes (difficult, expensive installation)
- Compatible with existing GPS-driven farm machinery
- Addresses resource optimization (water, fertilizer, pesticides)

**General Scientific Applications:**
- Remote sensing and atmospheric science
- Volcanic plume detection heritage
- Deployable to other atmospheric measurement missions
