## Executive Summary
NOAA is conducting market research to identify vendors capable of providing Uncrewed Aircraft Systems (UAS) services across multiple mission areas including marine resource surveys, meteorological/oceanographic observations, ocean exploration, and cryospheric/hydrographic mapping. This Sources Sought Notice requests capability statements from interested vendors to inform future procurement strategy, with responses due by February 6, 2026.

## Mission Focus Areas
NOAA seeks UAS services for the following operational domains:

1. **Living Marine Resource Surveys and Research**: Marine mammal and protected species observations; fisheries stock assessments
2. **Meteorological and Oceanographic Observations**: Air-sea interaction; atmospheric flux measurements; long-range, mid- to high-altitude measurements for forecasting and climate research
3. **Ocean Exploration and Characterization**: Harmful algal bloom characterization; ocean color mapping; satellite calibration/validation data collection
4. **Land, Cryospheric, and Hydrographic Mapping**: High-resolution shoreline verification; shallow water bathymetry for nautical charting, coastal resilience, and habitat modeling
5. **Other Congressional Priority Areas**: As identified through appropriations process

## Required Capability Demonstrations

Vendors must demonstrate capabilities across five technical areas:

### 1. Atmospheric Data Services
- In-situ meteorological data collection (pressure, temperature, humidity, wind)
- Aerosol and trace gas sampling from boundary layer to high altitudes
- Required specification: Maximum altitude for in-situ data collection and trace gas sampling

### 2. Remote Sensing Services (Imagery/Lidar)
- High-resolution RGB, Multispectral, Hyperspectral, and Thermal Infrared (IR) imagery
- Lidar data collection for mapping and bathymetric characterization
- Orthomosaic map generation
- Required demonstrations: 
  - Examples of high-resolution Lidar and hyperspectral bathymetric data
  - Examples and details of orthomosaic maps generated from aerial imagery
  - Specific sensor types used for RGB, multispectral, and thermal IR land surface mapping

### 3. Visual Data & Scientific Observation
- High-resolution video and optical imaging for biological classification (biota detection and quantification)
- Ground truthing and environmental monitoring
- Integrated multi-sensor chemical parameter collection: eDNA, dissolved oxygen, CO₂, nutrients, methane, salinity
- Integrated multi-sensor physical parameter collection: temperature, pressure, currents, wave features, ocean energy
- Required demonstrations:
  - Multi-sensor integration methodologies for simultaneous chemical and physical parameter collection
  - Ground truthing methodologies for biological classification

### 4. Extreme Environments & Specialized Capabilities
- Arctic operations (high wind, freezing temperatures)
- Tropical cyclone eyewall operations
- Very high-altitude operations
- Required demonstrations:
  - Demonstrated experience in extreme conditions (Arctic or tropical cyclone environments)
  - Technical modifications enabling high-wind operations

### 5. Data Processing & Analytics
- Post-processing workflows: orthomosaics, point clouds, classified detection data
- Sensor calibration to national/international standards (metrological best practices)
- Machine-to-machine services for real-time data retrieval by NOAA
- Required demonstrations:
  - Raw data post-processing workflows for complex derived products
  - Calibration methodology and traceability to standards
  - Real-time data delivery capabilities

## Regulatory & Operational Requirements

### FAA Compliance
- Strict adherence to Federal Aviation Regulations (FAR) Part 107
- Ability to obtain FAA waivers and Special Government Interest (SGI) authorizations
- Spectrum frequency approvals and airspace authorization acquisition

### Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) Operations
- Detect and Avoid (DAA) technology capability OR alternative safety mitigation measures
- Compliance with FAA waivers for BVLOS in National Airspace System (NAS)

### Operational Flexibility
- Real-time adaptation to mission courses and locations during field operations
- Operations capability over land, water, and ice at various altitudes

## Response Requirements

Vendors must submit statement of capabilities not exceeding 20 pages including:

1. **Company Profile**: Name, address, UEI, NAICS code(s), GSA FSS schedules/SINs, SBA small business status, primary and secondary contacts

2. **General Platform Information**: 
   - Types of UAS platforms owned (fixed wing, VTOL, hybrid VTOL-fixed wing, etc.)
   - Endurance specifications for each platform (hours/minutes)

3. **Capability Demonstrations**: Responses to structured questions under each of five capability areas (listed above)

4. **Compliance & Regulatory Demonstration**: FAA Part 107 experience, waiver examples, BVLOS/DAA capabilities, spectrum/airspace authorization processes
