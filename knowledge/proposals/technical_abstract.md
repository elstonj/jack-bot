## Executive Summary
BST proposes an end-to-end soil integrity measurement system combining an existing Soil Moisture Mapping (SMM) small unmanned aircraft system (sUAS) with soil integrity processing software integrated with Army data services. The system uses an L-band radiometer to provide high-resolution soil moisture imaging, enabling the Army to assess soil conditions for heavy vehicle trafficking.

## Technical Approach
- **Core Technology**: L-band radiometer validated for commercial agriculture applications
- **Sensing Platform**: SMM sUAS (small unmanned aircraft system)
- **Data Processing**: Soil integrity algorithms and processing software
- **Integration**: Software interfaces with Army data services
- **Development History**: Technology developed under NASA grant in collaboration with University of Colorado
- **Spatial Capability**: 15-meter spatial resolution imaging
- **Measurement Depth**: ~10 cm soil depth penetration

- **What it is**: An unmanned aircraft system equipped with L-band radiometer for soil moisture measurement
- **Proposed use**: Assessment of soil integrity for Army ground operations
- **Specifications**: 
  - 15-meter spatial resolution
  - ~10 cm measurement depth
  - L-band radiometer payload
- **Performance claim**: Technology "significantly improves Army capabilities to assess soil conditions for heavy vehicle trafficking"

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Heavy Vehicle Trafficking Assessment**: Determining soil capacity to support military vehicle movement
- **Soil Strength Evaluation**: Assessing spatial heterogeneity of soil moisture, which directly correlates to soil strength
- **Field Operations Planning**: Enabling tactical decisions about vehicle deployment based on soil conditions

## Technical Justification
Soil moisture distribution is identified as the key variable determining soil strength. Increased soil moisture increases slipperiness and stickiness while decreasing soil strength. The high spatial resolution (15m) of the SMM sUAS is required to capture spatial heterogeneity of soil moisture across a terrain—capability unavailable through lower-resolution alternatives.
