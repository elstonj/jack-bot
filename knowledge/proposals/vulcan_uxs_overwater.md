## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes the S2 unmanned aerial system for USINDOPACOM J85's overwater logistics demonstration and field experimentation program. The S2 meets all technical requirements for delivering 5+ lb payloads over 30+ mile ranges in demanding Indo-Pacific maritime environments, with particular emphasis on ease of operation and maintenance by non-specialist personnel.

## Technical Approach
BST proposes using the **Black Swift S2 UAS** to meet INDOPACOM J85's capability requirements:

- **Autonomous Operations:** The S2 executes fully autonomous flight including takeoff, climbout, and landing without manual intervention
- **Control System:** Android-based tablet with waypoint-based mission planning, accessible to operators of varying experience levels
- **Launch/Recovery:** No constructed runway required; capable of launch and recovery in varied environments
- **Payload Delivery:** Tool-free modular nose cone compartment allows rapid loading/unloading of secure payloads in the field
- **Maritime Durability:** Airframe engineered for salt spray corrosion resistance and high-wind operations; proven BVLOS (Beyond Visual Line of Sight) reliability in extreme conditions

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift S2 UAS
- **What it is:** Purpose-built unmanned aerial system designed for demanding atmospheric environments (high-altitude, corrosive particulates, turbulence)
- **Specifications:**
  - Minimum 5 lb payload capacity
  - Minimum 30-mile round-trip range (exceeds requirement)
  - Tool-free payload compartment access
  - Autonomous launch/flight/landing
  - Operates reliably in conditions: 70-120°F (21-43°C), sustained winds ≤15 mph with gusts ≤21 mph, rainfall ≤2 inches/hour
  - Electric or hybrid propulsion
  
- **Proposed Use in Context:** Delivery of medicine and small supply payloads to dispersed island communities in Indo-Pacific littoral zones; demonstration at POST 2026 (Oahu, Hawaii) on March 11, 2026; field experimentation in Oceania summer 2026

- **Key Differentiators:**
  - Intuitive Android tablet interface suitable for non-specialist operators
  - Field maintainability: component removal/replacement with basic hand tools only
  - Most non-electric and non-flight-surface components can be 3D printed for rapid repairs
  - Significantly lower cost than comparable systems
  - Proven operational reliability in extreme conditions (Greenland, Alaska, Costa Rica deployments)
