# D2P2 Technical Volume Template (Topic X236-ODCSO2)

## Document Metadata
- Type: SBIR/STTR proposal template (Direct-to-Phase II)
- Client/Agency: U.S. Department of Defense / Department of the Air Force (DAF)
- Program/Solicitation: Topic X236-ODCSO2 - Direct-to-Phase II Call for Innovative Defense-Related Dual-Purpose Technologies/Solutions with a Clear Air Force Stakeholder Need
- Date: March 28, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None (this is a template document)
- Key Personnel: Not applicable (template only)

## Executive Summary
This is a template document for Black Swift Technologies (or any SBIR contractor) to use when submitting a Direct-to-Phase II (D2P2) proposal to the U.S. Air Force. It provides the required structure, section headings, compliance questions, and evaluation criteria for proposals adapting non-Defense commercial solutions to meet Air Force end-user needs, with funding up to $1.25M and 21 months period of performance.

## Technical Approach
The template guides contractors to:
- Describe adaptation of an existing non-Defense commercial solution to meet DAF needs
- Demonstrate Phase I-type feasibility work conducted independently (since D2P2 skips Phase I)
- Propose 3-5 clear technical objectives with measurable key results
- Develop prototype hardware/software and conduct testing
- Define work plan with specific milestones, deliverables, and acceptance criteria
- Demonstrate end-user and customer commitment through signed Customer Memoranda

## Products & Capabilities Described
Not applicable—this is a blank template. However, the example milestone schedule embedded in the template references a medical device project (EVAC and ARS wireless integration for physiologic testing), which appears to be illustrative only and not a BST project.

## Use Cases & Applications
The template accommodates multiple use case categories:
- Air Force Technology Strategic Capabilities: Air Superiority, ISR, Rapid Global Strike, Command and Control, etc.
- Space Force Technology Strategic Capabilities: Space Superiority, Combat Power Projection, Space Mobility and Logistics, etc.
- Dual-purpose (defense + commercial) technologies

## Compliance & Regulatory Requirements
The template includes mandatory compliance checklist covering:
- Flight testing (indoor/outdoor, manned/unmanned)
- Hardware/software delivery and Mil-Std-882E hazard analysis
- Government personnel, facilities, and equipment use
- Weapons, munitions, explosives testing
- Directed energy devices and RF radiation
- Hazardous materials
- Animal testing
- Human subject research (32 CFR 219)
- Toxins and recombinant DNA
- Human interaction and data use

## Key Deliverables (Required for all Phase II awards)
- Final Report (draft within 30 days of technical completion; includes single-page non-proprietary summary)
- Status Reports (quarterly minimum)
- Technical Interchange Meetings (quarterly minimum with end-user)
- Phase II Summary Report (≤700 words)
- Software documentation, engineering drawings, operation/maintenance docs
- Preliminary Hazard Analysis (if hardware delivered)
- Hazardous Material Reports (if applicable)

## Proposal Structure
1. **Technical Summary** – Problem/opportunity, Air Force/Space Force strategic fit, non-Defense commercial baseline, proposed adaptation, answers to 7 specific commercialization questions
2. **Phase I-Type Feasibility Results** – Technical research, customer exploration methods, empowered/committed end-users and customers (with mandatory signed Customer Memoranda)
3. **Compliance & Regulatory Activities** – 13-point checklist
4. **Phase II Technical Objectives & Key Results** – 3-5 objectives tied to trials, 3-5 measurable key results per objective
5. **Phase II Work Plan** – Scope, task outline, milestone schedule with deliverables and acceptance criteria, payment amounts
6. **Commercialization Strategy** – Company info, customer/competition analysis, market sizing, IP strategy, financing, mentoring plans, answers to 10 specific questions

## Notable Details

**Mandatory Requirements:**
- Signed Customer Memorandum from DAF end-user and customer (mandatory; proposals without it will not be considered)
- Proposals must follow the prescribed template format
- At least one DAF/Joint end-user and customer required (non-DAF government end-users permitted as secondary)
- Clear distinction between SBIR-funded and private investment-funded tasks in work plan

**Funding & Duration:**
- Not-To-Exceed (NTE): $1.25M
- 21-month period of performance (18 months technical + 3 months reporting)
- Milestone payments tied to deliverables with defined acceptance criteria

**Commercial Baseline Requirements:**
- Proposed solution must be based on a non-Defense commercial solution with evidence of commercial sales/adoption
- Must answer questions about commercial similarity, supplier modifications for non-government customers, essential use changes, and COTS/modification details
- Must include 12-month revenue from non-Defense commercial sales

**Foreign National Restrictions:**
- All foreign nationals/dual citizens must be identified with country of origin, visa/permit type, and task assignment
- When subject to export control, foreign citizens limited to public domain work
- Foreign citizens cannot be in key positions (PI, Senior Engineer, etc.)
- Tasks assigned must not reveal project's overall objectives

**Key Personnel Requirements:**
- Technical resume for PI required, including publications
- Education, experience, citizenship information for all key personnel
- Emphasis on both RDT&E and commercialization qualifications

**Non-Defense Commercial Customer Evidence:**
- Past revenue from commercial customers (mandatory documentation)
- Potential revenue-generating projects (ongoing/completed preferred over potential)
- Active users, subscriptions, pre-sales, or other commercial traction indicators
