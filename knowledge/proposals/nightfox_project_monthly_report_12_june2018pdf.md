## Executive Summary
NightFOX is a NOAA-funded project to develop a nighttime fire observation UAS system. As of June 2018, the project had completed payload layouts and begun assembly of both in situ and remote sensing instruments. The team took delivery of the S2 UAS platform this month and is preparing for integrated flight testing planned for July 2018.

## Technical Approach
The project integrates multiple sensor payloads (in situ and remote sensing) onto the S2 UAS platform for wildfire observation operations:
- **Remote sensing payload**: Software development mostly complete; instruments bench-demonstrated with initial assembly underway
- **In situ payload**: Central data system under development; includes prototype CO and CO2 sensors
- **Integration approach**: Custom carbon fiber frames designed for payload mounting per CSD specifications; BST responsible for fabricating these frames
- **Testing strategy**: Engineering and payload evaluation test flights planned; integration and EMI testing with BST and CU-IRISS

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS Platform
- Delivery taken by CU in June 2018
- Serves as the primary platform for NightFOX payloads
- Platform TRL assessed at Level 6 (Technology demonstrated in relevant environment)

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Nighttime fire observations**: Monitoring wildfires at night when traditional imagery is limited
- **Fire weather research**: Participation in USFS-NOAA Fire Weather Research MOU
- **Wildfire access operations**: Planned operations to higher altitudes and beyond visual line of sight (BVLOS) under FAA COA (Certificate of Authorization)
- **Prescribed burns**: Testing opportunities identified for late summer/fall 2018 in Boulder area

## Technical Development Status

### Instruments in Development
- **CO sensors**: Two prototype units ordered, delivery expected in December 2017 (delayed status as of June)
- **CO2 sensor**: Prototype testing and improvement ongoing
- **IR telecentric scopes**: Prototype evaluation pending
- **Aerosol instruments**: Commercial coarse mode instruments under evaluation

### Payload TRL Assessment
- UAS platform TRL: **Level 6** (Technology demonstrated in relevant environment)
- UAS payload TRL: **Level 5** (Technology validated in relevant environment)
- UAS observation system TRL: **Level 3** (Experimental proof of concept)
