## Executive Summary
This is the Department of the Air Force's Small Business Innovation Research Program Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO) X22.4 solicitation document, published with six amendments between April and June 2022. The solicitation invites small business concerns to submit Phase I proposals ($75,000, 3 months) or Direct-to-Phase-II proposals ($1,250,000, 21 months) for innovative, defense-related dual-purpose technologies/solutions addressing Air Force or Space Force stakeholder needs. The document establishes requirements, eligibility criteria, proposal instructions, and evaluation procedures for this competitive procurement.

## Technical Approach
The solicitation follows standard SBIR/STTR program structures:
- **Phase I**: Feasibility and concept demonstration ($75,000, 3 months maximum)
- **Phase II**: Principal R&D effort producing well-defined prototype ($1,250,000, 21 months maximum)
- **Direct-to-Phase-II**: Streamlined pathway for mature concepts ($1,250,000, 21 months)
- **Phase III**: Commercialization using non-SBIR/STTR funding sources

Proposals must address one of five Air Force S&T Strategic Capabilities or propose "BLUE SKY" innovative solutions:
1. Global Persistent Awareness
2. Resilient Information Sharing
3. Rapid, Effective Decision-Making
4. Complexity, Unpredictability, and Mass
5. Speed and Reach of Disruption and Lethality

## Key Requirements for Offerors

### Eligibility:
- Small Business Concern (SBC) status: ≤500 employees, >50% U.S. citizen/permanent resident ownership and control
- Primary employment of Principal Investigator with SBC (>50% time)
- Must be registered in Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP) and System for Award Management (SAM)
- Unique Entity ID (UEI) from SAM required (DUNS numbers deprecated as of April 4, 2022)

### Work Performance:
- **Phase I**: Minimum 2/3 of R&D work performed by prime contractor
- **Phase II**: Minimum 1/2 of R&D work performed by prime contractor
- All R&D work must be performed in the United States
- No classified proposals accepted

### Proposal Submission:
- Must submit through DOD SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP): https://www.dodsbirsttr.mil/
- Phase I submissions: Slide deck format (15 pages reference in Amendment 4)
- Direct-to-Phase-II submissions: White Paper format (15 pages)
- Deadline: August 9, 2022, 12:00 p.m. ET (no exceptions)

### Compliance & Regulatory Activities:
Proposers must disclose whether their project involves:
- Flight testing (manned/unmanned, indoor/outdoor)
- Hardware/software delivery to government
- Government personnel, facilities, or equipment use
- Weapons/munitions testing
- Explosives, propellants, or ammunition
- Directed energy devices or RF radiation
- Hazardous materials
- Animal or human subject research
- Toxins or recombinant DNA
- Interaction with or collection of human data

### Key Program Features:
- **CSO Authority**: Uses 10 U.S.C. § 879 (NDAA FY2017) allowing competitive procurement of innovative commercial items/technologies
- **Prototype Projects**: May result in Other Transaction (OT) awards for prototype development with potential noncompetitive follow-on OT for Production
- **Foreign Ownership Disclosure**: Offerors must self-report and disclose foreign investment in ownership or control per DoD Instruction 5000.83
- **Technology Protection**: DoD risk-based measures to protect systems from adversarial exploitation

### Performance Benchmarks:
Offerors with 20+ Phase I awards in past 5 years must maintain 4:1 ratio of Phase I to Phase II awards. Offerors with 15+ Phase II awards in past 10 years must demonstrate either:
- Average of ≥$100,000 sales/investments per Phase II award, OR
- Patents resulting from SBIR work ≥15% of number of Phase II awards received

Failure to meet benchmarks: ineligibility for new Phase I awards for one year (but can continue ongoing work and pursue Phase II/III awards).
