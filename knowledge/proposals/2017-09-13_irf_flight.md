# 2017-09-13 IRF Flight Report

## Document Metadata
- Type: Flight Report
- Client/Agency: NASA (MALIBU program)
- Program/Solicitation: MALIBU
- Date: 2017-09-14 (report prepared); flight conducted 2017-09-13

- Key Personnel: Maciej Stachura (last editor)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies conducted a single flight test of the SuperSwift aircraft on September 13, 2017, near the Irrigation Research Foundation north of Yuma, Colorado. The flight lasted 30.7 minutes and successfully captured 487 images with no operational issues.

## Technical Approach
- Deployed SuperSwift fixed-wing aircraft for aerial imaging mission
- Aircraft equipped with dual camera system (master camera and secondary camera)
- Onboard IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit) systems for attitude measurement
- Cameras mounted at 52° roll angle

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SuperSwift Aircraft:**
- Fixed-wing UAS platform used for aerial surveillance and imaging
- Capable of sustained flight operations (30+ minutes demonstrated)
- Equipped with redundant camera systems and IMU instrumentation
- Can operate from field locations with minimal infrastructure

## Use Cases & Applications
- Agricultural research/monitoring (irrigation research application)
- Multi-camera aerial image collection for ground truth/validation
- Precision agriculture assessment (corn crop monitoring visible in flight area)

## Key Results
- **Flight Duration:** 30.7 minutes
- **Images Captured:** 487 images
- **Operational Status:** No operational issues
- **Camera Performance:** IMU data shows camera roll angle tracking performed well in flight, with minimal deviation from intended 52° mounting angle
- **Trajectory:** Complete flight path and photo locations documented and mapped

## Notable Details
- Flight conducted in agricultural region (corn crops documented in images)
- Dual camera system with independent IMU monitoring enabled attitude verification
- Successful demonstration of multi-camera coordination and data collection capability
- Document includes trajectory maps and comparative roll angle analysis between camera systems
- Minimal operational issues suggests mature platform capability
