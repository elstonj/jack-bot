## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies received a $250,000 OEDIT ESCR grant to develop methane leak detection capabilities using their UAS platform (S2). The project involved building a second-article Q3 sensor, integrating it with the S2 platform, conducting field demonstrations with anchor customer PDCE (later merged with Chevron), and developing commercial products for oil & gas and environmental applications. As of August 2023, the company had completed initial demonstrations and was advancing product development while securing follow-on venture capital funding.

## Technical Approach
- **Sensor Development**: Built second-article Q3 methane sensor with improved sensing and analysis capabilities
- **Platform Integration**: Integrated new sensor to S2 UAS platform
- **Testing & Analysis**: Conducted field testing at well sites; partnered with Battelle Corporation for advanced eddy-covariance analysis of sensor data
- **Key Technical Challenges**: Methane sensing and analysis proved more challenging than prior trace gas surveying work; sensor provider delivery delays and complexities in real-time analysis required timeline adjustments
- **Innovation Areas**:
  - Absolute three-axis wind speed measurement from BST UAS platform
  - Real-time flight control updating based on sensor inputs
  - VTOL fixed-wing UAS capability development
  - Application of eddy-covariance analysis to BST UAS sensor data
