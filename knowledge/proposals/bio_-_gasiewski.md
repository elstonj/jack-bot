## Overview
Curriculum vitae for Professor Albin J. Gasiewski, a prominent remote sensing researcher and Principal Investigator for a 2018 USDA proposal. Gasiewski brings extensive expertise in microwave radiometry, atmospheric sensing, and instrument development spanning three decades.

## Professional Background

**Current Position:**
- Professor, Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Colorado at Boulder (since 1/06)
- Director, NOAA-CU Center for Environmental Technology (CET) (since 1/06)

**Prior Experience:**

## Research Expertise
Primary interests in:
- Passive and active remote sensing of atmospheric and oceanographic processes
- Radiative transfer and electromagnetic theory
- Signal detection, estimation, and data assimilation
- Microwave instrumentation, calibration, and metrology
- Development of surface-based, airborne, and spaceborne sensing systems for meteorology, hydrology, and climatology
- Continuum electrodynamics, statistical and quantum physics

## Leadership & Professional Activities
- Past-President, Geoscience and Remote Sensing Society (GRSS) of IEEE (2006-2008)
- Co-founding member, Executive Committee of IEEE Committee on Earth Observations (ICEO)
- Member, IEEE GRSS Seniors Council
- Immediate Past Chair, USNC/URSI Commission F
- Principal faculty member, CU/ECEE Remote Sensing Group
- Developer of three-course core graduate sequence in remote sensing at CU

## Selected Research Focus Areas
(Based on publication themes)

**Microwave Radiometry & Radiative Transfer:**
- Unified microwave radiative transfer models for stratified media
- Fast algorithms for Mie scattering calculations (hydrometeors)
- Millimeter-wave radiometry applications (Arctic, tropical precipitation)

**Remote Sensing Applications:**
- Cloud liquid water field tomographic retrieval
- Snow cover mapping (airborne polarimetric microwave imaging)
- Soil moisture estimation and mapping
- Ocean surface wind vector field measurement
- Temperature and humidity profiling
- Precipitable water vapor measurement

**Field Campaigns & Data Collection:**
- NASA Cold Lands Processes Experiment (CLPX) - snow cover imaging
- SMEX02/SMEX03 (Soil Moisture Experiments) - soil moisture observations
- Southern Great Plains experiments
- TRMM Microwave Imager data analysis

## Notable Instrumentation/Techniques

- Ground-based millimeter-wave radiometry
- 1DVAR retrieval methods
- Multistream scattering-based Jacobian methods for radiance assimilation
