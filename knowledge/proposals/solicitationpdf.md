# ERIS Master Program Solicitation

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Open Call / Program Solicitation
- **Client/Agency:** DARPA (Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency), managed by The Applied Research Institute (ARI)
- **Program/Solicitation:** DARPA Expedited Research Implementation Series (ERIS)
- **Date:** December 1, 2024
- **Open Submission Period:** December 9, 2024 – May 30, 2025
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None (this is the solicitation announcement, not a BST proposal)
- **Key Personnel:** Not applicable

## Executive Summary
DARPA's Expedited Research Implementation Series (ERIS) is an open, continuous call for disruptive technology solutions from U.S. and foreign entities. ERIS provides a low-barrier entry point for companies, academia, and individuals to submit video pitches of novel technologies, which are assessed monthly by peer panels and, if rated "awardable," become eligible for rapid acquisition and award through Other Transaction Authority. The program seeks breakthrough solutions addressing DARPA's strategic focus areas, particularly detecting/tracking objects, overcoming sensor limitations (SWaP-C), and advanced antenna technologies.

## Program Structure & Goals
- **Purpose:** Establish centralized marketplace for disruptive technologies relevant to DARPA's national security mission
- **Target Problems:** 
  - Detecting and tracking elusive objects across air, land, and space domains
  - Overcoming limitations of current sensing systems (SWaP-C, performance)
  - Developing advanced antenna technologies for improved performance and reduced size
- **Assessment Authority:** Peer panel of government, industry, and academic SMEs; final decision by Government Selecting Official
- **Eligibility:** U.S. companies (traditional/nontraditional, large/small business), universities, nonprofits, individuals, and foreign universities/research institutions (if export-law compliant)

## Submission Process & Timeline
- **Monthly Collection Periods:** Submissions accepted continuously; each month's submissions assessed in the following month
- **Deadline per Month:** Last day of month, 12:00 PM Eastern Time
- **Assessment Notification:** Within 30 days of end of collection period (subject to volume)
- **Resubmissions:** Allowed at any time; unsuccessful submitters receive scorecards, assessor comments, and access to weekly feedback sessions

## Technical Requirements for Video Submission

### Format Requirements:
- **Length:** Maximum 7 minutes (primary rejection reason if exceeded)
- **Resolution:** HD 1280x720
- **File Format:** .mp4, under 1 GB
- **Color Space:** Rec709 or sRGB
- **Markings:** No restrictive markings except valid copyright notice; export-controlled and proprietary information prohibited

### Required Content (Four Elements):
1. **Define the Problem & Current State of the Art:** Scope of problem(s) addressed; thorough explanation of existing solutions and gaps
2. **Advancing the State of the Art:** What is "new" in proposed approach; why it advances the field; barriers foreseen
3. **Team Capability:** Demonstrate unique vision, expertise, and experience of key personnel and why team is best-positioned for success
4. **Defense/Commercial Market Use Case & Impact:** Specific applications and impact in defense and/or commercial markets; articulate the "so what"

## Submission Form Requirements
Submitters must complete automated form with:
- Submitting Entity Name (match SAM.gov if applicable)
- Website address
- Unique Entity ID (UEI) if available
- Business Size (per SBA 13 CFR Part 121)
- Traditional/Nontraditional Defense Contractor status (per 10 USC 3014)
- Socioeconomic designations (match SAM.gov)
- Country
- Principal place of business address
- Topic area selection (no impact on assessment; used for categorization)
- Technology Readiness Level (TRL) per Appendix A (not used for awardable/non-awardable determination; helps match with customer funding types)
- Minimum 5 relevant keywords
- Video submission POC, email, phone
- Submission Title (< 128 characters)
- Abstract Description (1,500 characters max)
- White Space Chart (Appendix B)

## Assessment Process & Criteria
- **Scoring Rubric:** Published in Appendix C
- **Panel Composition:** Minimum 3-member peer panel (government, industry, academic representative)
- **Assessment Output:** Written initial assessment by each panelist; recommendations to Government Selecting Official
- **Rating Categories:** "Awardable" or "Non-Awardable"
- **Post-Competition Status:** Solutions rated awardable qualify as "post-competitive" under 10 USC 4021, 4022, 4023; eligible for award via Other Transaction Authority
- **Compliance Check:** Performed within 1 business day; non-compliant submissions returned for revision

## Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI) Mitigation
- **OCI Safeguards:**
  - SME assessors prohibited from evaluating their own solutions or those in which they hold financial interest
  - Assessors required to complete government ethics training and execute NDAs
  - Assessors must certify recognition of conflict-of-interest prohibitions
  - Government monitors scoring consistency across assessors to identify biases
  - Minimum 3-member peer panel plus Government Selecting Official authority reduces competitor-evaluation bias

## Award & Negotiation Terms
- **Awardable ≠ Guaranteed Award:** Assessment as awardable and placement in ERIS does not guarantee current or future award
- **Negotiable Terms:** Payment, intellectual property rights, data rights licenses, schedule, milestones, pricing
- **Duration & Price:** No proscriptive price ceilings or time limits; multiple awards possible with single customer or across multiple customers from single submission
- **Award Pathways:** Research, development, prototyping, test and evaluation, and procurement for experimental purposes
- **Contract Authority:** Other Transaction Authority

## Submission Portal & Support
- **Platform:** DARPAConnect.us / darpaconnect.us/ERIS
- **No Physical Submissions:** Hard copy/CD/DVD/Blu-Ray submissions not accepted
- **Resubmission Workflow:** Mark as "ERIS Resubmission" vs. "New ERIS Submission" (non-compliant corrections remain "New")
- **Feedback Sessions:** Weekly office hours available; conducted by ERIS Manager (ARI) and Government Agreement Officer
- **Questions:** ERIS@darpa.mil

## Data Handling & IP Licensing
Submitters grant government the following licenses in video and submission data:

1. **Assessment & Procurement License:** Governmentwide, nonexclusive, fully paid-up, non-assignable, non-royalty-bearing license to view, review, research, comment, share video and data within government and covered contractors for assessment, market research, requirements development, procurement planning, negotiation, and award—valid while video/data active in ERIS

2. **Perpetual Process-Improvement License:** Government and covered contractors retain perpetual license to use video/submission data to improve/revise submission, assessment, selection processes, criteria, scoring, or user interface, regardless of awardable/non-awardable status

3. **Transcription License:** Government and covered contractors may transcribe video for search functionality, accessibility, solution marketing, government reporting, market research, and requirements development; government retains perpetual license in transcription data itself

4. **Training License:** Government and covered contractors may use video solutions and data for government training on ERIS planning, implementation, execution, administration, assessment, feedback, and award processes

## Important Information for Participants
- **Cost Responsibility:** All costs of preparing and submitting solutions borne by submitting entity; not eligible for government reimbursement
- **Public Listing:** Company/individual names, solution names, and strategic focus areas published on DARPAConnect.us and ERIS media; entities may opt out by written notice to ERIS@darpa.mil
- **Multiple Submissions:** Solution providers may submit one or multiple video solutions while announcement remains open
- **Scoring & Feedback:** Provided for both awardable and non-awardable submissions; submitters may request feedback sessions via www.darpaconnect.us/ERIS
- **Dispute Resolution:** Scoring disputes addressed to Government Selecting Official
- **Assessor Privacy:** ERIS does not provide SME assessor names or contact information

## Notable Details
- **Low-Barrier Entry:** ERIS explicitly designed to address major barrier to small businesses and nontraditional defense contractors
- **Rapid Assessment Timeline:** 30-day notification target (flexible for high-volume periods)
- **Continuous Open Call:** Unlike traditional solicitations, ERIS remains open throughout stated period with monthly rolling assessment cycles
- **Resubmission Support:** Extensive feedback and coaching provided to improve non-awardable submissions
- **Statutory Compliance:** Leverages 10 USC 4021, 4022, 4023 (Other Transaction Authority) to enable streamlined procurement pathways
- **Non-Proprietary Submission:** All submissions must be unclassified, non-proprietary, non-export-controlled, and non-CUI to facilitate broad government access and future procurement
- **Video-First Format:** Emphasis on video pitch (max 7 min) plus structured data to democratize access and reduce submission burden
