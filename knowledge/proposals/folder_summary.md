## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed the Coordinated Atmospheric Thermodynamics Sampling System (CATSS) to NOAA—a team-based uncrewed aircraft system (UAS) platform enabling multiple small VTOL aircraft to cooperatively collect detailed atmospheric data. The system targets improved understanding and prediction of severe weather events including supercell thunderstorms, derechos, and fire weather conditions.

## Technical Approach
- **Multi-aircraft coordination**: Development of hardware and software to operate up to 10 aircraft simultaneously
- **Sensor integration**: SwiftFlow sensors for atmospheric measurements
- **Inter-aircraft communication**: Mesh network enabling data sharing between aircraft
- **Flight coordination**: Coordinated flight patterns and algorithms for team operations
- **User interface**: Intuitive control and management system for multi-aircraft teams
- **Platform**: VTOL S0 aircraft as the primary airframe

## Products & Capabilities Described

### CATSS System
- Multi-UAS cooperative platform
- Supports up to 10 simultaneous aircraft operations
- Mesh network communication between aircraft
- Coordinated sampling for atmospheric thermodynamics data collection

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Severe convective storm monitoring**: Supercell thunderstorms and derechos
- **Fire weather assessment**: Understanding fire-weather interactions
- **Atmospheric data collection**: In-situ measurements of atmospheric conditions during extreme weather events
