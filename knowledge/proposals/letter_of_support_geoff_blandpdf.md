# Letter of Support from NASA Goddard Space Flight Center

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: NASA Goddard Space Flight Center, Wallops Flight Facility
- Program/Solicitation: DOD Phase II submission (2020.3)
- Date: June 8, 2021
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Machine Learning (ML) based predictive maintenance tool for UAS
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (BST), Geoff Bland (NASA Wallops - signatory)

## Executive Summary
NASA Goddard's Wallops Flight Facility endorses Black Swift Technologies' proposed development of a Machine Learning-based predictive maintenance tool for Unmanned Aircraft Systems. The letter highlights BST's expertise in miniaturization, UAS design/manufacturing/operations, and technology commercialization, noting that the ML-based system will enhance UAS safety and reliability for NASA Earth science research and broader applications.

## Technical Approach
- Development of ML-based predictive maintenance tool for UAS
- Real-time diagnostics and performance monitoring
- Capability to identify small changes in system performance and predict failures in advance
- Recommended maintenance actions and real-time corrective actions
- Focus on sensitivity and reliability exceeding human operator capabilities

## Products & Capabilities Described
**ML-Based Predictive Maintenance Tool for UAS:**
- Early detection of system performance degradation
- Predictive failure identification
- Maintenance action recommendations
- Real-time corrective action capability
- Built on BST's demonstrated expertise in ML applications

**BST Specialties Noted:**
- Miniaturization of on-board systems
- High-speed diagnostics
- Extensive UAS design, manufacturing, and operations experience
- Maintenance experience
- Ability to bring new concepts to market and commercialize technologies

## Use Cases & Applications
- Earth science research support
- UAS safety enhancement across multiple platforms
- Remote sensing operations
- In-situ observations
- General commercial UAS applications

## Notable Details
- Letter emphasizes that the system represents "significant advancement of the state-of-the-art"
- Endorsement positions the tool as beneficial to multiple UAS manufacturers and users
- Highlights commercialization potential as important benefit beyond NASA's immediate needs
- Addresses expansion of "practical utilization envelop" for diverse UAS missions
- Signatory is from NASA's Atmospheres Laboratory at Wallops
