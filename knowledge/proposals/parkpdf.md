## Executive Summary
Dr. Hyoshin (John) Park is an Associate Professor at Old Dominion University with expertise in physics-informed machine learning, adaptive AI for autonomous systems, and decentralized multi-agent coordination. He holds a Ph.D. in Civil & Environmental Engineering and brings two decades of experience in autonomous vehicle systems, particularly for maritime and hazardous weather applications. His current research is directly aligned with Navy STTR work on expendable air-sea profiling in adverse conditions.

## Key Research Focus
- **Physics-informed, data-driven learning**: Real-time sensor data integrated with hydrodynamic models to characterize uncertainty in complex ocean environments
- **Temporal Multimodal Multivariate Learning (TMML)**: Adaptive AI fusing acoustic, visual, and flow-field data to predict environmental and threat evolution
- **Regret-based ergodic patrolling algorithms**: Optimizes coverage, minimizes risk, balances energy efficiency across multiple fleet
- **Decentralized ASV coordination**: Heterogeneous autonomous surface vehicles using asynchronous communication and epistemic planning
