# Wayne Letter - University of Nebraska-Lincoln Letter of Support

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of support/endorsement
- Client/Agency: Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator Program Review Committee
- Program/Solicitation: Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator Program (appears to be a funding/accelerator review)
- Date: 2015-02-27
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: First generation autopilot and ground station; next generation system; soil moisture mapping sUAS
- Key Personnel: Wayne Woldt (University of Nebraska-Lincoln, Associate Professor of Biological Systems Engineering and Environmental Engineering)

## Executive Summary
University of Nebraska-Lincoln faculty member Wayne Woldt endorses Black Swift Technologies' sUAS products based on his research collaboration with BST over multiple years. He highlights the effectiveness of BST's first-generation control system for precision agriculture applications and expresses confidence in their upcoming next-generation system and soil moisture mapping capabilities developed in partnership with NASA.

## Technical Approach
- BST's first-generation autopilot system with ground station and user interface software for sUAS operations
- Development of soil moisture mapping sUAS in collaboration with NASA
- Focus on usable, reliable control systems for sUAS fleet management and data collection

## Products & Capabilities Described

### First Generation Autopilot System
- Provides flight control, ground station, and user interface software
- Designed for ease of use in managing flight operations
- Suitable for precision agriculture applications
- Successfully tested in agricultural research at University of Nebraska-Lincoln

### Next Generation System
- Expected availability: "later this year" (2015)
- Anticipated to address market needs in agriculture applications

### Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS
- Developed in collaboration with NASA
- Capability: Soil moisture measurements
- Application: Precision agriculture and environmental engineering
- Use case: Informing farmers on water use, drainage/irrigation issues, crop application decisions
- Current limitation: Restricted to research fields due to regulations
- Future potential: Commercial applications anticipated "in a couple of years" (2017 timeframe) when commercial flight approval granted

## Use Cases & Applications
- Precision agriculture data collection
- Soil moisture mapping for irrigation and drainage management
- Agricultural decision support (water use, crop applications)
- Environmental engineering applications

## Notable Details
- Woldt has published 2 papers on sUAS for agriculture and speaks regularly at agriculture industry gatherings
- Partnership with NASA on soil moisture mapping demonstrates credibility and access to significant research programs
- Letter emphasizes both current product performance and future market potential
- Recognition of regulatory barriers to commercial sUAS operations as constraint (near-term limitation)
- Strong endorsement of BST's products addressing "market need for reliable and easy-to-use control systems"
