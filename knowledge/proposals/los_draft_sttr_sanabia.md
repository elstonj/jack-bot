- Type: Letter of Support

- Program/Solicitation: Navy STTR Topic N25A-T025, "Expendable Air-Sea Profiling Observations in Hazardous Weather Conditions" (Phase II)

- Key Personnel: Elizabeth "Beth" Sanabia (writer, UW); Jack Elston (last editor, BST)

## Executive Summary
This letter of support from an academic partner endorses Black Swift Technologies' Phase II Navy STTR proposal to develop the S0 expendable UAS for hazardous weather operations. The writer, based on direct technical discussions with BST, affirms that the S0 addresses a critical observational gap in marine atmospheric boundary layer measurements and is well-positioned for integration with Navy and Air Force C-130 platforms.

## Technical Approach
BST proposes to refine and mature the S0 expendable UAS for operational deployment from large crewed aircraft (C-130-class platforms). Phase II work focuses on:
- Launch interface refinement for aircraft integration
- Cold-weather and icing resilience
- Automated sampling behaviors
- Data assimilation into Navy forecasting systems (COAMPS-TC)

The approach emphasizes practical integration considerations: mechanical and electrical compatibility, operational workflows, crew burden reduction, and safety constraints in hazardous weather environments.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 Expendable UAS
- **What it is:** An expendable, aircraft-launched atmospheric sensing system designed for deployment in extreme weather conditions
- **Measurements:** Winds, pressure, temperature, and humidity in the marine atmospheric boundary layer
- **Key capability:** Persistent, targeted profiling in the lowest atmospheric layers where air-sea interaction processes occur
- **Form factor/deployment:** Designed for autonomous operation and integration with large crewed aircraft platforms
- **Maturity level:** Demonstrated robustness through use in recent hurricane seasons

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Hazardous weather operations:** Storm intensity assessment, icing risk characterization, aircraft hazard identification
- **Hurricane operations:** Proven deployment during recent hurricane seasons
- **Naval operations:** C-130-based Navy and Air Force missions
- **Data integration:** Assimilation into Navy forecasting systems (COAMPS-TC) for improved situational awareness and forecasting skill

## Notable Details
- The letter emphasizes the S0's unique advantage over traditional expendables: it fills a long-standing observational gap in the marine atmospheric boundary layer
- BST is characterized as understanding practical operational constraints (crew burden, safety, weather severity) and designing solutions within these constraints rather than around them
- The writer (Sanabia) has had "multiple technical discussions" with the BST team and direct familiarity with aircraft platform integration efforts
- The proposal is positioned as supporting broader joint force adoption of aircraft-launched sensing systems
- Data usability and operational transition are highlighted as key strengths of BST's approach
- This is a Phase II proposal, indicating prior Phase I completion
