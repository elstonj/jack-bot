# Proposal Evaluation Debrief: Navy STTR Phase I – Expendable Air-Sea Profiling Observations in Hazardous Weather

## Document Metadata
- Type: Proposal evaluation debrief / scoring feedback
- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy (STTR Program)
- Program/Solicitation: Navy STTR Topic N25A-T025
- Proposal Number: N25A-T025-0009
- Date: April 23, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Uncrewed Aircraft System (UAS) platform with air-sea profiling and sensing capabilities
- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies submitted a Navy STTR Phase I proposal to develop an expendable air-sea profiling UAS capable of operating in hazardous weather conditions. The proposal leverages BST's existing platform with sensing capabilities that can be deployed as expendable probes or operated as VTOL systems from land or ship. Evaluators found strong technical maturation and academic partnerships but noted that Phase I technical details and competitive positioning could be more thoroughly developed.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed to:
- Utilize an existing UAS platform with mature sensing capabilities
- Develop air-sea profiling observation capabilities for hazardous weather environments
- Focus Phase I efforts on **calibration and validation** of sensors against established reference systems (NOAA dropsondes, DoE ARM sites)
- Improve sensing capabilities for high-order air-sea interaction measurement
- Conduct field experimentation and sensor maturation in hazardous weather conditions
- Distinguish between Phase I base efforts and Phase I option efforts

## Products & Capabilities Described
**BST UAS Platform:**
- Existing platform with related sensing already integrated
- Dual operational modes: expendable deployment from aircraft OR VTOL operation from land/ship
- Capable of operating in hazardous weather conditions
- Previously validated against NOAA dropsondes and DoE ARM (Atmospheric Radiation Measurement) reference sites
- Environmental sensor integration on aircraft

## Use Cases & Applications
- Air-sea profiling observations in hazardous weather
- Marine boundary layer observation
- Air-sea interaction research and measurement
- Atmospheric profiling (temperature, pressure, humidity, etc. inferred from comparisons to dropsondes)
- Government and private sector meteorological observing
- Hazardous weather monitoring and research

## Evaluation Strengths (by Criteria)

**Technical Merit (Criteria A):**
- Existing platform with proven sensing and dual deployment modes (expendable or VTOL)
- Strong contextual background on limitations of existing Vaisala platforms
- Excellent prior validation work against NOAA dropsondes and DoE ARM sites
- Well-scoped Phase I focus on calibration/validation and capability enhancement for high-order air-sea measurement terms
- Clear delineation between Phase I base and option tasks

**Team Qualifications (Criteria B):**
- Excellent relevant experience in UAS development, sensor maturation, and validation
- Demonstrated expertise in hazardous weather field experimentation
- Strong knowledge of air-sea interaction and marine boundary layer observation
- Environmental sensor engineering expertise
- **Very strong academic partnerships** with University of Miami, Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University, and Old Dominion University

**Commercialization (Criteria C):**
- Good market analysis and understanding of meteorological observing sectors (government and private)
- Clear commercialization strategy focused on growth in hazardous weather observing

## Evaluation Weaknesses

**Technical Merit (Criteria A):**
- Phase I technical details are insufficient; proposal lacks specifics on how research will be performed
- Limited explanation of why specific Phase I objectives were prioritized
- Insufficient detail on R&D risks and mitigation strategies

**Commercialization (Criteria C):**
- Lacks analysis of competing vendors in the air-sea profiling/hazardous weather niche
- Unclear competitive differentiation and unique positioning versus other vendors
- Ambiguous strategic focus: unclear whether BST positions itself primarily as an environmental observing specialist or as a broader platform developer

## Notable Details
- Proposal demonstrates maturity through prior validation against established reference systems (NOAA, DoE)
- Strong university partnerships indicate deep domain expertise access and credibility
- Evaluators acknowledge the viability of the existing platform but request more granular technical planning
- Market opportunity exists in government and commercial sectors for hazardous weather meteorological observations
- Evaluation suggests BST has competitive advantages but needs clearer articulation of market positioning and vendor differentiation
