## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to support NASA's AEROKATS and ROVER Education Network, a Science Activation project promoting STEM learning and outreach. BST will contribute personnel (engineers, technical consultants, and a senior technician) to develop educational workshop materials using kites for environmental exploration, while also evaluating the commercial feasibility of AeroPod and Aerovator products. All work will be conducted remotely from Denver, Boulder, and Onancock.

## Technical Approach
- **Personnel contributions:** Named Co-Investigator (Aerospace Engineer Kay Rufty), two Technical Consultants (Jack Elston, Maciej Stachura), and a Senior Technician
- **Work scope:** Planning, writing, design, fabrication of wood and plastic parts, and shipping of educational workshop materials
- **Product evaluation:** Assessment of feasibility for "Aeropod" and "Aerovator" commercial product development
- **Prototyping:** Evaluation of new systems by team members as available
- **Execution:** Remote work from locations in Denver/Boulder, CO and Onancock, VA; no NASA facility access or IT system integration required
