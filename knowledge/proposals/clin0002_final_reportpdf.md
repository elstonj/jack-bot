## Executive Summary

This Phase I effort demonstrates rapid, field-deployable manufacturing of unmanned aircraft structural components through a partnership between Curable Composites and Black Swift Technologies. Using advanced thermoset resin systems that cure without ovens or autoclaves, the team successfully fabricated a thin-walled hollow composite cylinder (150 mm height, 60 mm inner diameter, 1.3 mm wall thickness) in approximately 20 minutes under ambient conditions. The report details Black Swift's S2 airframe construction and proposes modular, rapidly producible manufacturing alternatives using hollow composite cylinders, flat laminates, and precision fiber layups. The technology addresses DAF priorities under the Replicator Initiative and Agile Combat Support by enabling on-demand, distributed production of Group 1 and Group 2 UAS components in contested or austere environments.

## Technical Approach

### Core Manufacturing Technology
Curable Composites developed a proprietary thermoset resin system that remains stable at room temperature but cures rapidly when exposed to localized heat. Key capabilities include:

- **3D printing of continuous/discontinuous FRPCs:** Low-power laser heats carbon fibers via photothermal conversion, triggering in-situ curing. Achieves fiber volume fractions up to 70% with minimal void content (0–2%).
- **Digital lamination:** Rapid layup of woven or unidirectional carbon fabric plies on surfaces using robotic systems with one-step curing.
- **One-step winding and curing:** Patent-pending filament winding with photothermal heating enabling cure in under 10 minutes.
- **Untethered manufacturing:** Mobile robots deposit and cure discontinuous carbon FRPCs on uncontrolled surfaces without ambient condition control.

### Phase I Demonstrator Process
For the hollow cylinder proof-of-concept:

1. **Support structure:** 3D-printed PETG/PLA mandrel with four axial slits (500 µm–1 mm wide) for easy post-cure removal
2. **Fiber placement:** Continuous carbon fiber tows (Hexcel AS4, 12K) impregnated in real-time through a resin reservoir and wound at ~60° to the axis (simulating combined hoop and axial reinforcement)
3. **Automation setup:** Custom 2-axis system using rotary actuator (mandrel spinning) and linear actuator (axial nozzle movement)
4. **Curing:** Low-power laser source applied asynchronously; total cycle time ~20 minutes
5. **Support removal:** Mandrel segments easily extracted post-cure with minimal effort due to weak bonding between thermoset resin and thermoplastic

### Material Properties Achieved
- **Fiber volume fraction:** ~39% (tunable via resin viscosity and fiber tensioning)
- **Degree of cure:** ~94% (validated by DSC; residual heat of reaction ~17.3 J/g vs. total 292.4 J/g)
- **Resin system:** Cost-effective, thermally stable base with tunable glass transition temperature (Tg 110–160 °C); mechanical properties comparable to aerospace-grade epoxies
- **Resin characteristics:** Exceptional fracture toughness, impact resistance, low moisture uptake, low density (~0.9 specific gravity), low cure shrinkage

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift S2 UAS
- **Description:** Modular, fixed-wing unmanned aircraft system
- **Specifications:**
  - 90-minute endurance
  - Up to 2 kg payload capacity
  - Carbon composite fuselage, foam-core composite wings, carbon tube tail boom
  - Removable V-tail empennage, interchangeable nosecones
  - Ground rail launch system
  - Servo-integrated control surfaces, custom wiring harnesses
- **Current manufacturing:** Hand-laid carbon fiber over molds with vacuum bagging; semi-automated assembly
- **Components:** Fuselage (hand-laid carbon over molds), tail boom (carbon tube epoxied into keyed socket), foam-core wings with embedded wiring/servo mounts, V-tail empennage with integrated servos, 3D-printed battery housing with carbon reinforcement straps
- **Proposed manufacturing adaptations:**
  - **Fuselage:** Two-piece hollow composite shell using Curable's cylinder technique; wall thickness ~1.2–1.3 mm with integrated insert locations and mounting threads
  - **Tail boom:** Hollow cylinder with tailored wall thickness and integrated internal features (anti-rotation keys, cable guides); field-tunable for mission-specific stiffness
  - **Wings:** Foam-core composites with embedded wiring, pitot/static tubes, servo mounts (foam core remains as final part, not removed)
  - **Empennage:** Hollow shell layups with integrated mounting points for servos; standard COTS components for control horns/clevises
  - **Battery housing:** Carbon composite resin with modular rail integration, reducing need for additional strapping
  - **Payloads/nosecones:** Mission-specific carbon composite alternatives enabling rapid adaptation to new sensors

## Key Results

### Manufacturing Demonstration
- **Part produced:** Hollow composite cylinder, 150 mm height, 60 mm inner diameter, 1.3 mm wall thickness
- **Time to completion:** ~20 minutes (fiber winding + laser curing)
- **Fiber architecture:** ~60° winding angle to axis (combined hoop and axial reinforcement)
- **Support removal:** Successful extraction of slitted PETG mandrel with no composite damage or deformation
- **Final geometry:** Maintained circularity confirmed post-removal

### Material Characterization Results
- **Fiber volume fraction:** 39% (calculated from mass-based analysis; tunable via resin viscosity and fiber tensioning)
- **Degree of cure:** 94% (DSC analysis confirms full cure under rapid, low-energy conditions)
- **Resin residual heat:** 17.3 J/g measured; total resin heat of reaction 292.4 J/g
- **Processing conditions:** Ambient temperature, non-laboratory environment, minimal resource requirements

### Technical Maturity Assessment
- **Curable's resin platform:** TRL 5–6 (well-developed, extensively tested across 3D printing, digital lamination, filament winding)
- **Panel/flat laminate manufacturing:** Near commercial readiness with consistent results and scalable workflows
- **Fuselage-specific hollow cylinder process:** TRL 3–4 (novel adaptation requiring new hardware; successfully demonstrated at proof-of-concept scale)

### Validation Evidence
- Core process capabilities validated: fiber placement, in-situ curing, mandrel removal
- Custom, low-cost automation setup proven feasible
- Operation under ambient, non-laboratory conditions confirmed
- Mechanical performance: Material-level properties previously validated for similar constituents; structural-scale testing (compression, flexure, impact) planned for Phase II
- Tensile testing at CSU (3D printed composites using similar materials) confirmed expected performance per rule of mixtures

## Notable Details

### Dual-Use Technology
- **Defense adaptations:** Modular payload integration, enhanced durability (up to 150 °C Tg), structural reinforcement for harsh environments, command/control system compatibility
- **Commercial adaptations:** Broader material flexibility, fixed industrial robotic systems (not field-deployable), integration with existing digital manufacturing workflows, certification-focused quality control
- **Platform flexibility:** Same underlying manufacturing process serves both sectors with sector-specific tailoring

### Design & Manufacturability Innovations

**Removable Mandrel Concept:**
- 3D-printed support with axial slits enabling easy post-cure extraction
- Weak resin-thermoplastic bonding allows minimal-effort removal
- Opens path for scalable, recyclable support systems

**Next-Generation Mandrel Concepts (Under Development):**
1. **Mechanically actuated collapsible mandrels:** Multi-material 3D printing with rigid internal scaffold and compliant elastomer outer skin; collapse mechanism releases composite post-cure (inspired by catheter/stent technology)
2. **Modular, reconfigurable assemblies:** Disassembled/reused components via threaded features, push-twist locks, snap-fit joints; supports integrated access ports, embedded jigs, internal channels

### Development Risks & Mitigation Strategies

| Risk | Mitigation Plan |
|------|-----------------|
| **Support removal in complex geometries** | Phase II prototype advanced support concepts (collapsible, multi-part assemblies); evaluate topologically optimized supports or lightweight foam cores remaining in final part |
| **Mechanical performance validation** | Phase II full-scale component testing under relevant loading (axial compression, flexure, impact); benchmark against Group 2 UAS performance criteria |
| **Fiber placement on concave/non-axisymmetrical surfaces** | Multi-axis robotic arm with custom printhead (compaction roller + localized curing); license commercial fiber path planning software (Autodesk/Siemens) |
| **Integration of modular features** | Explore co-fabrication and insert placement during fiber layup; characterize bonded interface performance; evaluate subtractive techniques if required |
| **Laser-based curing safety/control** | Evaluate compact IR heaters as alternative (broader thermal footprint, inherently safer, simplified non-planar geometry handling); Phase II integration with robotic platforms |
