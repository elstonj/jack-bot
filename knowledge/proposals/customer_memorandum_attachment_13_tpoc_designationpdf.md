## Executive Summary
This is a template memorandum for designating a Technical Point of Contact (TPOC) from AFRL/RGK for AFWERX Phase II SBIR contracts. The TPOC serves as the government's primary liaison responsible for monitoring contractor performance, ensuring deliverable acceptance, and managing contract execution issues.

## Purpose & Authority
- Formal designation of TPOC authority per Federal Acquisition Regulations (FAR 2.101) and Defense Federal Acquisition Regulations Supplement (DFARS Part 201.602-2)
- Mandatory document that must be completed and signed by the designated TPOC after award selection
- Signed as PDF and submitted to p2@afwerx.af.mil

## TPOC Responsibilities
The designated TPOC is responsible for:

1. **Performance Monitoring**: Track cost, technical, and schedule performance; promptly report marginal/unsatisfactory performance or delivery failures to Phase 2 PM and Contracting Officer (CO)

2. **On-site Personnel**: Brief contractor personnel at Government facilities on required training (security, emergency management, accountability procedures)

3. **Deliverable Acceptance**: Receive and accept contract deliverables to enable timely contractor payment

4. **Liaison & Coordination**: Maintain close contact with Phase 2 PM, CO, and facility organizations

5. **Approvals & Waivers**: Work with contractor, installation, and local Government organizations to obtain required approvals/waivers for:
   - Security requirements (CACs, DD254 coordination)
   - Program protection compliance
   - Cybersecurity compliance
   - Flight testing
   - UAS regulations
   - Safety measures

6. **Documentation**: Maintain comprehensive project records including contract, modifications, designation memorandum, correspondence, inspection records, and all documented actions

7. **TPOC Replacement**: If unable to perform, identify replacement TPOC and notify PM to initiate transition; replacement must sign new memorandum

## Authorization Limits
The TPOC is **NOT authorized to**:
- Make commitments or changes affecting price, quality, quantity, delivery schedule, scope of work, place of performance, or contract terms/conditions
- Obligate Government funds or execute contract changes (only the CO may do this)
- Discuss performance or contract matters until award is finalized
- Provide advance information advantaging one contractor over another
- Instruct contractor on performance methods (only contract requirements)
- Participate in contractor hiring/termination decisions

**Note**: The TPOC may be personally liable for unauthorized acts.

## Required Training
Training must be completed within 30 days of award selection:
- **Required**: AFWERX-specific TPOC training (online)
- **Required**: Additional training based on contract activities and regulatory requirements

**Optional courses** (Defense Acquisition University):
- ACQ 1010: Fundamentals of Systems Acquisition Management (8 hrs)
- CLC 106: Contracting Officer's Representative with a Mission Focus (3 hrs)
- CLC 011: Contracting for the Rest of Us (2 hrs)

## Standards of Conduct & Conflicts of Interest
TPOC must comply with:
- DOD 5500.7-R (Joint Ethics Regulation)
- FAR 3.2 (Contractor Gratuities to Government Personnel)
- Avoid conflicts of interest or appearance thereof
- Restrictions apply to TPOC and family members
