## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes an integrated small UAS system to improve situational awareness for wildland firefighting operations through infrared imaging, wind estimation, communications relay, and soil moisture monitoring. The system leverages BST's flight management capabilities, prior research partnerships (University of Colorado, NASA), and extensive FAA Certificate of Authorization experience to deliver a field-ready, multi-aircraft capable platform for regional deployment.

## Technical Approach

**Infrared Imaging**: Integration of state-of-the-art lightweight thermal camera with BST-developed customized gimbal and onboard computing for image compression, aggregation, and real-time transmission to incident commanders and field personnel. Capability available on both fixed-wing and multi-rotor airframes.

**Wind Estimation**: Deployment of in situ wind measurement algorithms (previously validated in severe storm sampling and M2AV project research) combined with accurate instrumentation to provide localized wind field data unavailable through traditional forecasting methods.

**Communications Relay**: Implementation of relay algorithms to optimize aircraft positioning for maximum bandwidth and connectivity in terrain-challenged environments (e.g., mountainous areas). Builds on prior University of Colorado research demonstrating sUAS effectiveness for inter-party communication chains.

**Multi-Aircraft Operations**: Black Swift Flight Management System enables single-operator control of multiple simultaneous sUAS (fixed-wing, multi-rotor, or blended fleets) for increased coverage, data frequency, and communication redundancy.

**Soil Moisture Sensing**: End-to-end system in NASA testing phase; provides 15 m (45 ft) resolution soil moisture data for wildfire susceptibility assessment and post-fire hazard prediction (landslide, flash flood).

## Use Cases & Applications

- **Wildland firefighting situational awareness**: Real-time multispectral imagery and wind data for incident commanders and deployed firefighters
- **Fire weather forecasting**: Wind estimation providing previously unavailable localized atmospheric conditions
- **Personnel and equipment tracking**: Via sUAS-enabled communications relay in mountainous/terrain-challenged areas
- **Wildfire susceptibility assessment**: Soil moisture mapping pre-deployment
- **Post-fire monitoring**: Soil moisture data for landslide and flash flood risk prediction
- **Regional rapid-response deployment**: Lower cost and simplified operations compared to national-level platforms (e.g., NASA Ikhana)
