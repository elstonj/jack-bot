## Executive Summary
This is a brief official Q&A document from the NOAA SBIR 2018-1 solicitation clarifying that Subtopic 8.2.12 welcomes novel upper air observing systems, including small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS), in addition to surface-based weather observing systems.

## Technical Approach
N/A - This is a solicitation clarification document, not a technical proposal.

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **Small Unmanned Aircraft Systems (sUAS)**: Clarified as an eligible approach for this subtopic, specifically for gathering upper air observations over data-sparse areas.

## Use Cases & Applications
- Upper air observations over data-sparse areas
- Weather observing (general category)

## Notable Details
- This Q&A clarifies that the subtopic is not limited to surface-based systems
- sUAS technology is explicitly welcomed as a viable approach
- The document emphasizes that all communication must route through the Contracting Officer (CO) to ensure fair competition and equal information distribution to all potential offerors
- The deadline for questions was December 5, 2017

## Significance
This document is significant as it indicates NOAA's openness to unmanned aircraft solutions for weather observation gaps, suggesting this SBIR topic was receptive to the type of capabilities Black Swift Technologies would offer with its UAS platforms.
