## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies received a single airframe from ByLight on October 1, 2025, for quality assurance and intake inspection as part of the Mustang Program. The aircraft was found to have surface damage throughout the fuselage and wing sets, missing critical components (battery, radio handset, ground station), and no visible serial numbers for inventory tracking. The report documents structural issues, material composition, dimensional measurements, and propulsion testing to assess airworthiness before flight testing.

## Technical Approach
BST conducted a systematic intake and QA process including:
1. **Physical Inspection** – structural condition assessment, surface finish evaluation, material verification
2. **Measurements** – weight breakdown, center of gravity audit, geometric documentation
3. **Propulsion Bench Run** – motor/ESC testing, propeller assessment
4. **Avionics & Electrical Systems** – installed systems audit, power system verification
5. **Compliance Review** – airworthiness risk identification, specification compliance checking

## Products & Capabilities Described
No BST-specific products are described. The report documents a **ByLight aircraft** being evaluated, with the following specifications:

### ByLight Mustang Aircraft
- **Airframe Construction:** Fiberglass and composite fuselage with plaster outer shell; aluminum wing and tail spars (friction fit)
- **Current Weight:** 4.6 kg (no battery or payload)
- **Battery:** Tenergy 6s Li-ion, 21.6V, 518.4Wh, 24Ah capacity; XT90 connector
- **Propulsion:** Hargrave ESC and brushless motor (spares provided)
- **Propellers:** 12 propellers supplied (4 packages of 3)
- **Control Surfaces:** Wing ruddervators (non-locking); right ruddervator has damaged servo linkage
- **Avionics:** Installed but not fully documented; capabilities unclear from report

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Mission Objective:** 400–500 km endurance flight with 35 lb (≈16 kg) gross takeoff weight (GTOW) target
- **Launch Method:** Car launcher mechanism provided by ByLight

## Key Findings

### Critical Issues Identified
1. **Structural Damage:**
   - Right wing: damage near fuselage on both top and bottom surfaces; top surface reinforced with duct tape
   - Left wing: propagating in-line crack from trailing edge (TE) forward to leading edge (LE), approximately halfway along wing
   - Right ruddervator: damaged servo linkage requiring repair before operations

2. **Design/Manufacturing Deficiencies:**
   - Wing and tail surfaces lack locking mechanisms for secure attachment
   - Shoddy surface finish with frequent holes in outer shell
   - Surface chips and scratches throughout
   - No visible serial markings for aircraft identification

3. **Missing Documentation/Components:**
   - No wiring or autopilot specifications provided
   - No battery documentation
   - No payload weight specifications
   - Battery shipped separately; radio handset and ground station not included at intake

4. **Electrical/Power System:**
   - Tenergy 6s Li-ion battery (1.85 kg), XT90 connector
   - Power system configuration and safety redundancy not fully documented

### Items Requiring Action Before Flight Testing
- Repair/replace left wing (structural crack assessment needed)
- Repair right wing damage and remove duct tape reinforcement
- Repair right ruddervator servo linkage
- Add locking mechanisms to wing/tail attachment points
- Surface finish remediation (holes, chips)
- Obtain complete avionics, battery, and payload specifications
- Establish serial number tracking
- Complete CG audit and weight breakdown tables
- Conduct and document propulsion bench testing (thrust vs. throttle/power)
