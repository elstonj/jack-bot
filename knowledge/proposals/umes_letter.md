## Executive Summary
UMES commits $15,000 in matching funds to support further development and commercialization of the NASA SBIR Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS project. The funding will enable personnel training, system deployment testing, and evaluation of the technology's viability in challenging crop environments.

## Technical Approach
The project uses a two-tier platform approach:
- **miniSwift**: A smaller UAS serving as a training platform for UMES personnel, capable of collecting photogrammetric data
- **SuperSwift**: The larger deployment platform for operational soil moisture mapping missions

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **miniSwift**: Smaller training UAS; to be provided to UMES in summer 2017 for personnel training and photogrammetric data collection
- **SuperSwift**: Full-scale soil moisture mapping sUAS; planned for deployment at UMES test farm in 2018
- **Soil Moisture Mapping (SMM) sUAS**: Uncrewed aerial system for measuring soil moisture; designed as a commercial product

## Use Cases & Applications
- Soil moisture measurement over various crops
- Agricultural testing, particularly over corn (identified as a challenging crop due to plant density)
- Evaluation of system viability in penetrating dense plant canopies to measure soil moisture beneath

## Notable Details
- UMES provides $15,000 in direct funding to Black Swift Technologies
- UMES will host testing at their test farm
- Corn cultivation testing is identified as a critical proof-of-concept to determine market viability
- Document indicates concern about the system's ability to penetrate dense crop canopies—a potential technical limitation being addressed
- Personnel training flights scheduled for summer 2017; operational deployment planned for 2018
