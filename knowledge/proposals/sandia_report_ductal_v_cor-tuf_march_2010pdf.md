## Executive Summary
This Sandia report documents ballistic impact experiments conducted on high-performance concrete samples using a 50-caliber powder gun with M33 ball ammunition at nominal velocity of 914 m/s. Testing compared penetration resistance of three concrete types: ERDC ultra-high performance concrete (UHPC/rpc "Cor-Tuf"), Ductal® concrete with fibers, and Ductal® without fibers. Results were measured using electronic velocity systems, radiography, and high-speed photography to characterize projectile residual velocity and penetration mechanisms.

## Technical Approach

**Test Configuration:**
- Single-stage powder gun: 50-caliber bore, 1.83 m barrel with military rifling
- Projectile: M33 ball ammunition (50 BMG), modified propellant (13.4 grams vs. standard 15.1 grams to achieve 914 m/s)
- M33 ball composition: Copper full-metal jacket over steel core, 41.98 g total weight
- Flight range: Evacuated to ~1 torr absolute pressure

**Diagnostic Methods:**

1. **MAVIS (Magnetic Induction Velocity System):**
   - Two monitoring stations spaced 150 mm apart, located ~5.5 m before target impact
   - Permanent magnet and copper pickup coil at each station
   - Measurement accuracy: 0.2%
   - Average impact velocity across all tests: 912 m/s (std dev 23 m/s)

2. **Flash Radiography:**
   - Scandiflash XT-150 x-ray system firing at 150 kV, 20 ns pulse duration
   - Kodak Flex GP digital imaging plates (200 × 300 mm), 16-bit grayscale
   - 60-100 micron/pixel spatial resolution along line of flight
   - Pre-shot static radiographs with threaded rod calibration references
   - Multiple radiographs (overhead and side) for projectile pitch/yaw measurement and residual velocity calculation
   - Spatial measurement uncertainty: ±0.96 mm; timing uncertainty: ±0.010 µs; total velocity uncertainty: 1.1%

3. **High-Speed Photography:**
   - Two Vision Research Phantom v12 monochromatic cameras
   - Single impact/multi-hit tests: 150,000 fps on both cameras (side-on and rear surface observation via mylar mirror)
   - Confined cylinder tests: 150,000 fps impact recording + 100 fps confinement housing monitoring

## Key Results

### Single Impact Panel Tests

**UHPC/rpc (Cor-Tuf) Performance:**
- 7 shots conducted
- Average impact velocity: 930 m/s
- Average residual velocity: 460 m/s
- Average penetration time through target: 104.8 µs
- **Energy dissipation:** 18.1 kJ → 2.6 kJ (15.5 kJ absorbed)
- **Performance:** HIGHEST penetration resistance; most kinetic energy dissipated

**Ductal with Fibers Performance:**
- 5 shots conducted
- Average impact velocity: 920 m/s
- Average residual velocity: 590 m/s
- Average penetration time: 141.7 µs
- **Energy dissipation:** 17.8 kJ → 4.3 kJ (13.5 kJ absorbed)
- **Performance:** MODERATE penetration resistance (middle tier)

**Ductal without Fibers Performance:**
- 2 shots conducted
- Average impact velocity: 913 m/s
- Average residual velocity: 625 m/s
- Average penetration time: 136.5 µs
- **Energy dissipation:** 17.5 kJ → 4.9 kJ (12.6 kJ absorbed)
- **Performance:** LEAST penetration resistance; lowest energy dissipation

**Projectile Damage:**
- In all penetration experiments, M33 ball copper jacket completely stripped from steel core
- Residual velocities calculated for steel core only
- Average recovered M33 core weight: 24.9 g (std dev 0.35 g)

### Multi-Impact Tests (Grid Configuration)

**Test Setup:** 4×4 grid of 68.26 mm square concrete cells (Ductal with fibers) separated by 6.35 mm steel frames; central four cells shot sequentially

**Grid-1 (Bare Steel Frame):**
- First shot caused one side of frame to blow off; significant frame deformation
- Subsequent shots covered penetrated cells with aluminum sheet to contain debris
- Average impact velocity: 899 m/s
- Average residual velocity: 567 m/s
- Individual residual velocities: 528, 639, 533, n/a m/s

**Grid-2 (Enamel-Painted Steel Frame):**
- Frame remained intact; deformation observed after each shot
- No dramatic failure like Grid-1
- Average impact velocity: 902 m/s
- Average residual velocity: 618 m/s
- Individual residual velocities: 616, 593, 610, 651 m/s

**Key Finding:** No significant drop-off in penetration resistance with cumulative damage; enamel-painted frame showed slightly higher residual velocity (618 vs. 567 m/s) but more controlled testing needed for statistical validation.

### Confined Ballistic Penetration Tests

**Configuration:** 101.6 mm diameter × 203.2 mm long cylinders in steel confinement fixture for post-mortem study

**Test Materials & Results:**

| Shot | Material | Impact Velocity (m/s) | Notes |
|------|----------|----------------------|-------|
| cyl-1 to cyl-3 | UHPC/rpc | 894–905 | High-speed photography on cyl-4 & cyl-5 only |
| cyl-4 to cyl-5 | Ductal with fibers | 894–904 | High-speed imaging conducted |
| cyl-6 to cyl-7 | Ductal with fibers + olivine at quarter points | 886–901 | Embedded mineral variant |
| cyl-8 | Ductal with fibers + quartz at quarter points | 891 | Embedded mineral variant |
| cyl-9 | Ductal without fibers | 947 | Highest impact velocity |

**Purpose:** Samples recovered for post-mortem analysis and characterization at outside laboratories to examine ballistic damage mechanisms under confinement.

## Notable Details

**Material Origins:**
- Ductal® is patented and commercialized by Lafarge Group in partnership with Bouygues and Rhodia (Chimie)
- UHPC/rpc "Cor-Tuf" is proprietary ERDC in-house formulation
- Fiber types: Dramix brass-coated high-carbon steel (Ductal) and Dramix ZP305 (UHPC)

**Experimental Rigor:**
- All 31 tests successfully documented with radiography and/or high-speed photography
- Independent velocity measurements (MAVIS) for projectile impact confirmed
- Strict spatial calibration (threaded rod references) enabled sub-millimeter accuracy
- Multiple diagnostic approaches (electronics, radiography, optics) provided redundancy

**Observations on Fiber Reinforcement:**
- Fiber presence notably affects penetration resistance and energy dissipation
- Ductal with fibers shows ~130 m/s lower residual velocity than without fibers (590 vs. 625 m/s)
- Fibers extend penetration time by ~5 µs despite similar material composition

**Comparative Performance Ranking (Best to Worst Penetration Resistance):**
1. UHPC/rpc (Cor-Tuf): 460 m/s residual velocity
2. Ductal with fibers: 590 m/s residual velocity
3. Ductal without fibers: 625 m/s residual velocity
