## Executive Summary
This document outlines subcontractor tasks for Black Swift's NASA SBIR Phase I volcano monitoring proposal, detailing work to be performed by Arapahoe SciTech (sensor integration) and the University of Colorado (CFD and wind tunnel testing).

## Technical Approach

**Arapahoe SciTech:**
- Identification and assessment of commercially available sensors for harsh environment volcanic plume analysis
- Sensor types: mass spectrometers, gas sensors, vacuum pump systems
- Integration of selected sensor suite into Black Swift's proposed UAS
- Environmental mitigation: addressing adverse effects of harsh volcanic environments on sensor performance
- Airframe modifications to enable flight in harsh airspace environments

**University of Colorado (CU):**
- CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics) analysis examining MHP mounting and mechanical design issues
- Preliminary wind tunnel testing to validate MHP design and provide calibration data
- Thrust performance analysis via wind tunnel testing
- High-altitude feasibility characterization
- Full propeller performance characterization
- SuperSwift airframe design iteration and testing
- Lead: Dr. Argrow with one graduate student

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **SuperSwift airframe**: Early-stage UAS airframe undergoing design validation and modification
- **MHP (Modular Hover Platform)**: Mechanical system requiring CFD analysis, mounting validation, and wind tunnel testing
- **Sensor suite**: Mass spectrometers, gas sensors, vacuum pump systems for volcanic plume sampling

## Use Cases & Applications
- Volcanic plume analysis and monitoring
- Harsh environment flight operations
- High-altitude operations capability assessment
