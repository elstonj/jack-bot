# Genesis Mission Phase I Letter of Commitment

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Commitment
- Client/Agency: Brookhaven National Laboratory (Dr. Gijs De Boer, Environment, Biology, Nuclear Science & Nonproliferation)
- Program/Solicitation: The Genesis Mission: Transforming Science and Energy with AI, Topic 11-A
- Date: April 14, 2026
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: UAS (Unmanned Aerial Systems) swarm
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (CEO, Black Swift Technologies)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies commits to participating as a team member in a DOE Genesis Mission project focused on AI-enabled mobile autonomous laboratories for real-time adaptive experimentation in dynamic environmental systems. BST will integrate the proposed system on a swarm of its UAS platforms and evaluate the adaptive workflow against non-adaptive flight strategies.

## Technical Approach
- Integration of the Genesis Mission proposed system onto a swarm of BST UAS platforms
- Comparative evaluation of adaptive flight strategies vs. non-adaptive baseline flight strategies
- Testing conducted in both simulation and field-relevant environments
- Focus on autonomous, adaptive decision-making in dynamic environmental sampling scenarios

## Products & Capabilities Described
**BST UAS Swarm:**
- Multi-platform unmanned aerial system capable of carrying scientific payloads
- Suitable for deployment in dynamic environmental research
- Capable of both simulated and field-relevant testing
- Can be coordinated as a swarm for coordinated data collection

## Use Cases & Applications
- Real-time adaptive experimentation in dynamic environmental systems
- AI-enabled autonomous laboratory operations
- Environmental monitoring and sampling

## Notable Details
- This is a commitment letter confirming BST's role as a team member, not the full proposal
- BST's contribution centers on platform integration and comparative testing
- Project emphasizes AI-enabled adaptive strategies vs. traditional pre-planned flight approaches
- Brookhaven National Laboratory is the lead organization for this DOE Genesis Mission effort
