## Executive Summary
This technical letter provides standardized criteria and procedures for evaluating the structural integrity and load-bearing capability of military airfields using conventional field assessment methods. It supersedes previous guidance from Army and Air Force field manuals and is intended for use by all Air Force organizations responsible for airfield evaluations, including AFCESA pavement teams, RED HORSE squadrons, Air Mobility Operations Groups, and Special Tactics Teams. The document establishes protocols for determining allowable aircraft loads and pavement classification numbers for various runway surfaces and subsurface conditions.

## Technical Approach

The evaluation methodology follows an eight-step structural assessment process:

1. **Define Initial Airfield Layout**: Survey and inventory all operational surfaces; divide pavement systems into features based on common characteristics (type, thickness, condition, construction history)

2. **Collect Field Data**: Determine pavement structural properties through:
   - Visual identification and thickness measurement of pavement layers
   - Pavement coring (when possible) to verify layers in wheel paths
   - Soil layer classification using Unified Soil Classification System (USCS)
   - Measurement of layer thicknesses

3. **Obtain Mission Data**: Document aircraft types, loads, traffic volume, and ground operations requirements

4. **Conduct Surface Condition Assessment**: Visual evaluation of structural distresses using Pavement Condition Index (PCI)

5. **Refine Airfield Layout**: Select representative soil profiles; compile Physical Property Data (PPD)

6. **Determine Allowable Gross Loads (AGLs) and Passes**: Calculate for unsurfaced, flexible (asphalt concrete), rigid (Portland cement concrete), and composite pavements

7. **Determine Aircraft Classification Numbers (ACNs) and Pavement Classification Numbers (PCNs)**: Use standardized conversion charts

8. **Prepare Evaluation Report**: Document findings and operational capabilities

### Key Testing Methods

**Soil Strength Assessment:**
- **Dynamic Cone Penetrometer (DCP)**: Preferred method; measures soil strength 1-100 CBR; uses 8 kg hammer dropped 575 mm; 16 mm diameter rod with 60° cone; penetration measured between hammer drops
- **Airfield Cone Penetrometer (ACP)**: Portable probe instrument; range 0-18 CBR; uses X-pattern with five penetrations at each location, averaged for results; measures to 24 inches depth
- **Automated Dynamic Cone Penetrometer (ADCP)**: Motorized versions with automated data collection
- **Electronic Cone Penetrometer (ECP)**: Hydraulically pushed to 5-7 feet; measures cone/sleeve pressures; provides friction ratio data; truck-mounted or air-transportable by C-130, C-141, C-5, C-17
- **Small Aperture CBR Test**: Performed through core holes in pavement surface
- **USCS Correlation**: Empirical CBR estimates based on soil type classification (least accurate method)

### Pavement Types Evaluated
- Flexible asphalt concrete (AC)
- Rigid Portland cement concrete (PCC)
- AC over PCC (flexible overlay on rigid base)
- PCC over PCC (rigid overlay on rigid base)
- PCC over AC over PCC (composite)
- Reinforced PCC (RPCC)
- Double bituminous surface treatment (DBST)
- AM-2 Mat
- Semi-prepared (gravel/unsurfaced)

### Traffic Area Classification
- **Type A**: Channelized, full design weight (runways first/last 1,000 feet, primary taxiways, semi-prepared surfaces)
- **Type B**: Non-channelized, full design weight (aprons)
- **Type C**: Non-channelized, 75% design weight (runway center, secondary taxiways, overruns)

## Use Cases & Applications

- **Contingency Airfield Evaluation**: Assessment of forward-deployed or austere airfields for military operations
- **Peacetime Operations**: Structural evaluation of permanent Air Force installations
- **Humanitarian Relief Operations**: Rapid assessment of civilian airfields for airlift support
- **Aircraft Operational Requirements Assessment**: Determination of payload/loading limits for specific aircraft types (A-10, C-5, C-17, C-130H, C-141, F-15E, F-16C/D, KC-10, KC-135)
- **Pavement Condition Monitoring**: Sustainment and expedient evaluation protocols for existing airfield pavements
- **Landing Zone (LZ) Assessment**: Evaluation of unprepared or semi-prepared surfaces for tactical operations
- **Runway Suitability Determination**: Establishment of Airfield Suitability and Restrictions Reports (ASRR) for flight operations

### Key Technical Specifications
- **CBR Measurement Standards**: California Bearing Ratio used as empirical soil strength measure (0-100% scale)
- **K-Value Conversion**: CBR values converted to modulus of soil reaction (K) for rigid pavement evaluation
- **Rigid Pavement Strength**: Flexural strength assumed 700 psi (high quality control) or 600 psi (uncertain quality control)
- **Wheel Path Offsets**: Specific offset distances from runway centerline for different aircraft types (4.5-18 feet depending on aircraft)
- **PCI Rating Scale**: Pavement Condition Index used for surface distress assessment

### Critical Quality Control Notes
- Evaluation reliability is questionable when in-field time is limited; contact AFCESA before reporting capability in constrained situations
- Weak soil areas should be tested first, as weakest conditions often control pavement evaluation
- Visual indicators of weak soils: wet areas, discolored soil, drainage problems, animal burrows, previously forested areas with subsurface roots

This document is foundational guidance for Air Force airfield engineering practices, particularly for contingency and expedient evaluation scenarios where rapid assessment of austere airfield suitability is required for force projection.
