## Executive Summary
This document provides standardized instructions for the Research Performance Progress Report (RPPR) format, which federal agencies use to collect progress reports from grant and cooperative agreement recipients. The RPPR standardizes reporting across federal agencies to reduce administrative burden and enable comparison of research outcomes and impacts across government-funded programs. This is a procedural/instructional document rather than a project-specific proposal or report.

## Notable Details

**Reporting Structure:**
- Cover page data elements required for all awards
- Mandatory "Accomplishments" category (all agencies)
- Optional additional categories tailored by individual agencies
- Agencies may develop supplementary program-specific requirements with OMB approval

**Significant Contributor Definition:**
- PD/PI or individual working ≥1 person-month per year on the project, regardless of compensation source

**Training & Professional Development:**
- Reporting encouraged on opportunities provided to project participants
- Definition: Activities where experienced professionals assist others in developing greater proficiency, or individuals increase knowledge/skills in their area of expertise

**Dissemination Requirements:**
- Results distribution to communities of interest
- Outreach activities to enhance public understanding
- Increased interest in STEM learning and careers

**Participant Reporting:**
- Names and roles of all funded personnel
- Project roles identified: PD/PI, Co-PD/PI, Faculty, Technician, Graduate/Undergraduate students, Postdoctoral fellows, Consultants, etc.
- State/territory/country of residence
- International collaboration tracking
- Demographics collection (voluntary): Gender, ethnicity, race, disability status per Privacy Act guidelines

**Impact Assessment Categories:**
- Development of principal discipline
- Impact on other disciplines
- Human resource development (training, retention, access for underrepresented groups)
- Teaching and educational experiences
- Physical, institutional, and information infrastructure
- Technology transfer (commercialization, startups, new practices)
- Societal impact beyond science and technology
- Foreign country impacts

**Budget Reporting:**
- Uses SF-R&R form family formats (identical to Grants.gov implementation)
- Updated as Grants.gov forms are modified

**Project Outcomes (Final Reports):**
- Cumulative summary ≤5,000 characters
- Written for general public (non-technical language)
- Suitable for electronic public dissemination
- Excludes proprietary/confidential information
