## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed the SuperSwift, a small unmanned aircraft system equipped with a passive microwave radiometer for mapping soil moisture across agricultural plots and watersheds. The system combines a lightweight, electric-powered airframe with integrated sensing technology to enable rapid deployment and autonomous missions for regional-scale soil moisture assessment at 15m resolution, supporting agricultural management and flood prediction applications.

## Technical Approach
The SuperSwift integrates a highly capable passive microwave radiometer sensor directly into the airframe for soil moisture mapping. Key technical features:
- **Flight envelope**: 15m-30m above ground level (AGL) for optimal sensor accuracy
- **Coverage**: Up to 400 acres per flight
- **Measurement depth**: Soil moisture mapping down to ~5cm depth
- **Resolution**: Up to 15m spatial resolution
- **Airframe**: 6 kg total weight (including payload); 3.2m wingspan
- **Propulsion**: Electric (enabling rapid deployment)
- **Flight operations**: Fully autonomous from takeoff to landing
- **Control**: Android tablet with intuitive map-based interface for mission planning and execution
- **Avionics**: Built on Black Swift's SwiftPilot Pro autopilot and ground station architecture

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift sUAS
- **What it is**: A small unmanned aircraft system specifically designed for soil moisture mapping missions
- **Proposed use**: Regional-scale agricultural assessment; flood prediction and watershed management; calibration/validation of satellite soil moisture missions (ESA SMOS, NASA SMAP)
- **Key specifications**: 
  - Weight: 6 kg (payload-inclusive)
  - Wingspan: 3.2m
  - Operating altitude: 15-30m AGL
  - Coverage per mission: 400 acres
  - Measurement resolution: 15m
  - Measurement depth: ~5cm soil depth
  - Autonomous operation; tablet-based control

### SwiftPilot Pro
- **What it is**: Black Swift's autopilot, ground station, and user interface suite
- **Proposed use**: Primary avionics system for SuperSwift; designed to minimize operator training and enable use by non-UAS-specialist personnel
- **Key advantage**: Specifically designed for simplicity of operation and usability

### Passive Microwave Radiometer
- **What it is**: Compact, lightweight sensing payload uniquely developed with NASA funding
- **Proposed use**: Direct measurement of soil moisture content at critical depths
- **Competitive claim**: Smaller in weight and size than any existing radiometric sensor applicable to soil moisture mapping

## Use Cases & Applications

1. **Agriculture Sector**: 
   - Mapping soil moisture for agricultural plots
   - Supporting agricultural assessment and decision-making
   - Informing land use and water conservation efforts

2. **Flood Prediction and Flood Control**:
   - Mapping soil absorption capacity at critical watershed scales
   - On-demand regional measurements to complement global satellite data
   - Calibration/validation of satellite missions (SMOS, SMAP)
   - Enhanced prediction of flash floods with radiometric data
   - Supporting federal and local flood mitigation strategies

3. **Scientific/Research**:
   - Calibration and validation tool for global satellite soil moisture missions
   - Higher-resolution complementary data to satellite systems

## Competitive Advantages & Innovation Claims

1. **Unique sensing capability**: Only commercial system capable of mapping soil moisture at critical depths in a low-cost, easy-to-operate package
2. **Sensor innovation**: Proprietary passive microwave radiometer significantly smaller and lighter than existing radiometric sensors
3. **Usability focus**: SwiftPilot Pro avionics specifically designed for minimal operator training and accessibility to non-specialists
4. **Rapid deployment**: Electric propulsion and small team requirements reduce deployment costs and timelines
5. **Accessibility**: Enables local authorities and budget-constrained organizations (e.g., agricultural extension services) to access soil moisture data previously available only through satellite systems or expensive manned platforms

## Notable Details
- Document references complementarity with NASA satellite missions (SMOS, ESA; SMAP, NASA)
- Emphasis on economic viability through low-cost operation and rapid deployment for commercial market
- System designed to serve both immediate user needs (farmers, local flood management) and scientific research (satellite validation/calibration)
- The microwave radiometer sensor was developed with prior NASA funding, indicating continued partnership/evolution of technology
- Flight altitude requirement (15-30m AGL) is stated as a deliberate design choice to achieve 15m resolution soil moisture mapping—a key differentiator from higher-altitude platforms
