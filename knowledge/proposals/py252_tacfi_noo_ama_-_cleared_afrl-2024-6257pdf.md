## Executive Summary
This is an official Notice of Opportunity (NOO) for the Air Force's PY25.2 Tactical Funding Increase (TACFI) program, which provides Phase II continuation funding ($375K–$2M) for small business contractors with active or recently-completed SBIR/STTR Phase II efforts. The program aims to bridge the capability gap between Phase II R&D and Phase III scaling/commercialization, catalyzing relationships between Air Force/Space Force end-users, innovators, and investors.

## Program Purpose & Goals
- Catalyze relationships between DAF end-users, acquisition professionals, private-sector innovators, and investors
- Bridge the capability gap between current SBIR/STTR Phase II efforts and Phase III scaling efforts
- Facilitate delivery of strategic capabilities for the Department of the Air Force

## Eligibility Criteria
All criteria must be met:
- Company must be a Small Business Concern (SBC) and eligible for SBIR/STTR award
- Must be on an active SBIR/STTR Phase II or completed within 2 years of Capability Package submission
- The subject effort has not already been awarded a Sequential Phase II
- At least 90 days have passed since Phase II execution began
- Not executing a prior STRATFI effort at time of submission
- Anticipated work to be performed in the United States
- Able to achieve minimum matching/investment requirements
- Able to obtain required End-User & Customer Collaboration Agreement

## PY25.2 TACFI Structure & Timeline
- **Funding Range:** $375K–$2M
- **Submission Window:** Open window through March 31, 2025 (Noon ET)
  - Two selection cycles: Winter 2025 & Spring 2025
  - Companies may submit at any point during the NOO window
- **Award Timeline:** 90 calendar days from selection notification to contract award

## Matching Requirements
- **SBIR/STTR Match:** Up to $2M increased matching requirement
- **Government Matching:** Phase III/Contract awards within six months of submission may count; SBIR funds do not count toward government matching; subcontracts may count
- **Private Matching:** Eligible third-party funds received after Phase II start and within 24 months of submission count; all funds must be received prior to TACFI award

## Submission Requirements & Documentation

### Capability Package Elements:
- **Company Information:** Company description/profile, key personnel, shareholders, ownership profile, private funding cap table, SBC registration, supply chain
- **Contract Information:** Award history, pending contracts, Phase II overview
- **White Paper:** Summary of Phase II technology solution, TACFI technology solution summary, transition strategy, benefit to warfighter
- **Additional Documents:** 
  - SBC Participation Memorandum
  - Foreign Disclosure Form
  - Spend Plan & Milestones Worksheet
  - Matching Funds Letters (private and government)
  - Customer/End-User Collaboration Agreement (with quad chart, white paper, non-proprietary work plan, DAF PK exemption request as attachments)
  - Finance MOU
  - Contracting MOU
  - Phase II Contract (including modifications)
  - Phase III Contract (if used as funds match)

## Government Roles & Responsibilities
- **End-User:** DAF organization that is and/or will be the primary user/supporter of solution
- **Customer:** Organization that will procure and/or sustain the effort/solution
- **TPOC (Technical POC):** Works closely with company during TACFI execution to achieve project success
- **FM (Financial Management):** Supporting financial manager responsible for transmitting matching funds to executing contracting office; facilitates fund utilization from AFWERX and other contributing offices
- **PK (Contracting Office Symbol):** Supporting contracting office responsible for RFP creation, contract award, and contract administration

## Contracting & Financial Management Process
**Contracting Responsibility:**
- Supporting contracting office acknowledges responsibility to award Phase IIb or Sequential TACFI within 90 calendar days of selection notification
- Customer FM/PK coordinate incoming SBIR/STTR and non-SBIR/STTR matching funds

**Award Steps (90-day timeline):**
1. SBC & Government POCs receive selection notification
2. Government POCs receive Funding Assurance Letter
3. Customer FM/PK validate matching funds in Funding Letters
4. Customer PK builds acquisition strategy (determine modification vs. new award)
5. Pre-RFP requirements documented (SOO/SOW, IGCE per local acquisition policies)
6. RFP released
7. Upon proposal receipt, contracting officer screens for completeness
8. TPOC evaluates proposal (if deemed complete)
9. Negotiations begin (if approved by CO and TPOC)
10. **Prior to award:** Certified PR google form request submitted to Ventures FM
11. Ventures/FM validates info and issues direct-site Certified PR or MIPR
12. Execute modification/award new contract; send files to AFWERX team

**Funding Process:**
- Funds Assurance Memo for RFP release provided to functional POCs in selection notification
- Certified funding document (direct site PR for DAF or MIPR external) provided to awarding PK after final proposal, negotiations, and google PR form submission
- AFRL/RGF reviews final proposal for propriety of funds to ensure scope alignment with 3600 RDT&E funds
- Form 9 Purchase Request (PR) sent by AFRL/RGF to awarding PK office
- AFRL/RGF reviews contract award posting in Accounting System

## Common Submission Mistakes
- Missing elements of capability package
- Inaccurate/ineligible Phase II contract
- Document integrity issues (inconsistent information across documents)
- Missing End-User/Customer/TPOC/SIPR/Staff Function POC information
- Incorrect POCs
- Inaccurate funds match (Phase IIIs, ineligible third-party, etc.)
- Incomplete/inaccurate government/private funding letters
- Funding alignment issues (TACFIs funded upfront)
- Incorrect document signatories
- Contracting acceptance/contract award responsibility unclear
- Using previous edition forms (must use PY25.2 versions)
