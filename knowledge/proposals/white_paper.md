## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes developing a miniaturized payload for airborne monitoring of hazardous wildfire emissions (chemical and particulate matter plus IR/EO imagery) integrated onto the S2 UAS platform. The system would enable firefighter managers to assess airborne health hazards, improve burn planning, and evaluate pre-fire conditions while leveraging the S2's proven heritage in in situ and remote sensing campaigns, including selection for NOAA's FIREX nighttime wildfire observation program.

## Technical Approach
- **Payload Development**: Create a miniaturized UAS payload targeting hazardous airborne wildfire emissions monitoring (both chemical constituents and particulate matter) combined with infrared and electro-optical (EO) imagery
- **Sensor Integration**: Integrate commercially available handheld air monitors as intermediate-step solution while developing full miniaturized multi-sensor payload:
  - Dust monitors (capable of measuring PM1.0, PM2.5, total particulate)
  - Photoionization detectors (for volatile organic compounds/VOCs)
  - Mercury vapor monitors
  - Single integrated monitoring unit constrained to under 6 lbs
- **Platform**: Integration on Black Swift S2 UAS
- **Validation Approach**: Test and validate against established handheld instruments already familiar to firefighter managers

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift S2 UAS
- Proven platform with heritage in both in situ and remote sensing campaigns
- Selected for NOAA FIREX campaign (nighttime wildfire observation experiment - "NightFOX")
- Capable of carrying miniaturized sensor payloads
- Designed for wildfire reconnaissance and monitoring applications

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Wildfire Emissions Monitoring**: Real-time assessment of chemical and particulate hazards to firefighters and public during active fire operations
- **Burn Planning**: Evaluation of burn conditions (dryness, fuel load conditions, fuel species composition) to improve control burn planning
- **Pre-Fire Hazard Assessment**: Assessment of forest conditions prior to wildfire events
- **Nighttime Wildfire Operations**: Capability demonstrated through NOAA FIREX participation for after-dark fire monitoring
- **Air Quality Monitoring**: Data collection on airborne hazards from wildfires for public information and modeling

## Key Technical Justifications
- **Health Hazard Context**: Wildfires emit mercury, lead, and trace metals comparable to industrial/urban pollution; release three times more fine particulate matter than accounted for in US National Emissions Inventory
- **Sensor Advancement**: Miniaturized sensor technology advancing, particularly electrochemical and IR sensors suitable for small UAS integration
- **Operational Readiness**: Handheld dust monitors have proven track record in aircraft; mercury vapor monitors and PID sensors offer novel application when airborne
- **Rapid Response**: Integration of sensors already in firefighter use enables validated, familiar tools with rapid deployment capability
