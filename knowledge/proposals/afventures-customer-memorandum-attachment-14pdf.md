## Executive Summary
This is an internal reference document providing regulatory definitions and compliance guidance for SBIR/STTR proposal development. It clarifies what constitutes eligible Research, Development, Testing, and Evaluation (RDT&E) activities versus ineligible commercial purchases, and explains how COTS/NDI items are treated under SBIR/STTR funding rules.

## Document Purpose
This attachment provides BST proposal teams with authoritative definitions governing SBIR/STTR eligibility to ensure Phase I and Phase II proposals comply with federal regulations (15 USC 638, SBA Policy Directive, DoD FMR). It is explicitly marked as "Info only; do not include in Memo."

## Key Regulatory Definitions

### Phase I and Phase II Conditions
- **Phase I**: Conduct feasibility-related experimental/theoretical research to determine scientific and technical merit and feasibility
- **Phase II**: Continue R&D from Phase I; includes special testing and evaluation authority
- **Direct-to-Phase II**: Requires agency determination that offeror's idea has sufficient scientific and technical feasibility despite skipping Phase I

### Research or Research and Development (R/R&D) - Eligible
Includes systematic studies directed toward:
1. Greater knowledge/understanding of subject
2. Applying knowledge/innovation to meet unmet needs
3. Production of useful materials, devices, systems, or methods (including design, development, improvement of prototypes and new processes)

Covers R&D in all fields: physical sciences, engineering, etc.

### COTS and NDI Treatment - Not Eligible
- Ready-to-use products purchased directly from commercial sources without alteration are NOT suitable for SBIR/STTR funding
- Examples: ready-to-use products, training services, software licenses, SaaS
- First article purchases and minor adjustments not funded
- Commercial items acquired for testing without RDT&E engineering/design/integration effort not funded

**Exception**: COTS/NDI required for RDT&E test purposes may be funded.

### Modified COTS/Modified NDI - Eligible (with conditions)
- Commercially available items modified to satisfy user requirements ARE eligible
- Eligible costs: first article, modification of first article, first article testing
- Follow-on purchases not eligible
- Quantity limited to what's needed for RDT&E acceptance tests
- Modification must significantly change performance envelope (differs from incremental technology refreshment)

**Distinction**: Continuous technology refreshment (incremental improvement during maintenance) is NOT funded; developmental modification is.

### Developing COTS or Commercial Product
- Offeror must explain intended modifications to COTS or commercial product
- Proposal must identify scientific/technical baseline from literature
- Must describe RDT&E efforts advancing this baseline

## Proposal Requirements

### For Direct-to-Phase II Awards
- Provide sufficient information for agency to determine scientific and technical feasibility and merit per 15 USC 638(cc)
- Team discussion must address:
  - Qualifications for conducting RDT&E
  - Commercialization pathway

### Tie to Defense Acquisition Program
- Offeror must identify any Defense Acquisition Program tied to proposal (if known)
- Used for tracking in Federal Procurement Data System (FPDS)
- If unknown, state so in proposal
