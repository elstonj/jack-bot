## Executive Summary
This is a NASA SBIR final report documenting Black Swift Technologies' work on developing a ruggedized uncrewed aircraft system (UAS) for scientific data collection in harsh environments. The work emphasizes the need for automated flight capabilities in difficult conditions to improve atmospheric model validation and safety systems related to volcanic ash aviation hazards, pollution, and other atmospheric phenomena. Previous Phase II-e work demonstrated small UAS capability for volcano monitoring but revealed that autonomous operation in challenging conditions requires significant automation improvements.

## Technical Approach & Problem Statement
The report identifies a critical gap in UAS operational capability: while instrumented UAS can theoretically operate in airspaces too hazardous for crewed aircraft, the lack of autonomous/automatic operation in all weather conditions limits widespread adoption and safety validation. 

Key issues addressed:
- Current atmospheric models rely on limited ground station data, satellite observations (with gaps from cloud masking, infrequent coverage, resolution limits), and occasional in situ measurements
- Previous volcano monitoring missions required months of planning and real-time wind observation data to ensure safe battery margins and navigation
- Routine hazardous-environment missions require significant human expertise and cannot easily scale worldwide

The proposed solution involves automating much of the flight planning and real-time decision-making that previously required expert human operators.

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Small UAS for Scientific Missions**
- Demonstrated capability during Phase II-e volcano monitoring operations
- Achieved "only visual observations of the target volcano during that calendar year" (implying operational success in conditions that prevented other observation methods)
- Ability to operate in airspaces too difficult or hazardous for crewed aircraft
- Can carry instrumentation for atmospheric science measurements

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Volcano monitoring**: Primary demonstrated use case from Phase II-e; collecting data from volcanic regions
- **Atmospheric model validation**: In situ data collection to improve weather and dispersion models
- **Volcanic ash aviation hazards**: Improving warnings and safety systems
- **Pollution alerts and toxic release monitoring**: Supporting air quality and hazard notification systems
- **Dust storms and wildfire smoke hazards**: Data collection for improved forecasting
- **Wind and temperature measurement**: Basic atmospheric profiling in remote/hazardous locations

## Key Results
- Phase II-e successfully demonstrated small UAS for routine data gathering in harsh volcanic environments
- Achieved unique observational capability during a challenging year when other methods could not obtain visual observations
- Revealed critical need for automation: successful missions required:
  - Months of flight planning and preparation
  - Continuous expert monitoring and wind observation during flight
  - Custom software to manage battery margins and safe return planning
  - Highly trained, knowledgeable flight team
