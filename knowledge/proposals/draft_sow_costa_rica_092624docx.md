# Draft Statement of Work: Data Collection over Costa Rica Volcanoes

- **Type:** Statement of Work (Draft)
- **Client/Agency:** NASA HQ and NASA Ames Research Center (ARC), with USGS involvement
- **Program/Solicitation:** Joint NASA HQ/NASA ARC partnership effort
- **Date:** September 26, 2024
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** Black Swift S2 (fixed-wing, catapult-launched UAS)
- **Key Personnel:** Meredith Needham (last editor)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies will deploy its S2 uncrewed aircraft system to conduct multiple survey missions over active volcanoes in Costa Rica, acquiring high-resolution infrared and visible imagery along with in-situ gas sampling data. The project is a collaborative effort between NASA HQ, NASA Ames Research Center, and USGS, with flights planned for Q1 2025.

## Technical Approach
- **Aircraft Platform:** Black Swift S2 fixed-wing UAS (catapult-launched)
- **Flight Pattern:** Pre-determined transects for wide-area plume imagery and single-location orbits
- **Operational Model:** BST flight crew (employees/subcontractors) with NASA/USFS observer personnel present; optionally, NASA ARC's own S2 (if airworthiness cleared) crewed by NASA personnel
- **Compliance:** Project follows NASA NPR 7900, including Airworthiness Review, Flight Readiness Review, and Mission Readiness Review as applicable
- **In-Country Operations:** BST responsible for coordinating local services and obtaining Costa Rican airspace access via on-site personnel

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift S2
- **What it is:** Fixed-wing, catapult-launched uncrewed aircraft system
- **Proposed Use:** Volcano survey platform for IR/visible imaging and in-situ gas sampling
- **Operational History:** Currently operated by NASA ARC and Langley Research Center (LaARC); previously deployed operationally by USGS for volcano caldera mapping and gas sampling
- **Payload Capacity:** IR/visible camera systems and in-situ gas sampling instruments

## Use Cases & Applications

**Volcanic Monitoring:** High-resolution IR imagery and gas quantification over active volcano plumes

**Target Volcanoes (Costa Rica):**
- Turrialba
- Rincón de la Vieja
- Poás

**Mission Type:** Low-intensity volcano surveillance with continuous monitoring capability

## Deliverables
1. **Payload and aircraft state data** – delivered to on-site staff within 48 hours of each flight
2. **Final flight report** – including recommendations and lessons learned, delivered 90 days post-mission
3. **Data archiving** – all data products made available to science community via DAAC or NASA Ames website
4. **Open Science compliance** – all aircraft state and payload data released per NASA's Open Science Data Policy

- **Flight Window:** Q1 2025 (weather and daylight permitting)
