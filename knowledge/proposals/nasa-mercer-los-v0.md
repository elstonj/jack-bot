# NASA Letter of Support for BST Wildfire SBIR Phase II

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: NASA Ames Research Center
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Phase II (Wildland Fire topic)
- Date: November 14, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Uncrewed Aircraft System (UAS) with meteorological sensor payload
- Key Personnel: Joey Mercer (NASA Ames, Human Systems Integration Division); Jack Elston (BST, last editor)

## Executive Summary
NASA Ames Research Center provides formal support for Black Swift Technologies' Phase II SBIR proposal to use a UAS equipped with meteorological sensors to collect data that will refine weather models for wildland fire forecasting and operational response. The letter emphasizes the importance of this capability for improving fire suppression planning and confirms NASA's willingness to provide advisory support and deployment opportunities.

## Technical Approach
- BST-developed UAS platform carries a meteorological sensor payload
- Data collected used to improve weather models developed by National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR)
- Goal: decrease uncertainty in fire weather modeling
- Real-time data delivery to field personnel via systems like ATAK (Android Tactical Assault Kit) proposed as additional benefit
- Validation planned through deployment during NASA field campaigns

## Products & Capabilities Described

**UAS with Meteorological Sensor Payload**
- Purpose: Collect atmospheric data for fire weather model refinement
- Application: Wildland fire suppression and operational forecasting
- Integration: Data can be transmitted in real-time to deployed firefighters

## Use Cases & Applications
- Wildland fire suppression planning and response
- Operational fire weather forecasting
- Support to U.S. Forest Service, Department of Interior, and state/local fire response teams
- Integration with NASA's FireSense Project and Advanced Capabilities for Emergency Response Operations (ACERO)

## Notable Details
- NCAR partnership for weather model development
- NASA commitment includes advisory support on data delivery to meet both NASA data needs and NCAR modeling requirements
- Use case consultation and field campaign deployment opportunities offered
- Letter emphasizes BST's track record of translating innovation to practice and organizational "vision, nimbleness, and commitment to exceptional performance"
- Framed as supporting multiple federal agencies and emergency response stakeholders
