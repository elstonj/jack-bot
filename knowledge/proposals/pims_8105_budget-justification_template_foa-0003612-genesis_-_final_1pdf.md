- Date: 2026-04-27
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Black Swift UAS platform
- Key Personnel: Gijs de Boer (BNL PI), Nathan Urban (BNL co-I), Andrew McMahon (BNL team engineer), Fernando Jose Llorente (BNL postdoctoral researcher)

## Executive Summary
This budget justification supports a DOE Genesis Mission proposal to develop AI-enabled autonomous laboratories using Black Swift UAS platforms for real-time adaptive experimentation in dynamic environmental systems. The project integrates AI decision-making frameworks with BST's autonomous aircraft to enable optimal adaptive sampling, with a focus on atmospheric science applications including boundary layer demonstrations.

## Technical Approach
The project proposes to:
1. **Integrate AI systems with Black Swift UAS platform** – Andrew McMahon will support integration of AI systems with the aircraft, including development of communication protocols between surface-based computing systems and aircraft navigation
2. **Develop mathematical framework for autonomous flight decision-making** – Fernando Jose Llorente will create the mathematical framework supporting AI-based decision making to guide the aircraft during autonomous flight for optimal sampling
3. **Coordinate AI framework development** – Nathan Urban will oversee intellectual input into the AI framework development and supervise mathematical framework work
4. **Establish autonomous flight operation limits** – Work with BST to define constraints on aircraft movement during autonomous operations

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift UAS Platform
- **What it is:** Autonomous unmanned aircraft system operated by Black Swift Technologies
- **Proposed use:** Platform for deploying AI-enabled autonomous laboratories capable of real-time adaptive experimentation in dynamic environmental systems
- **Integration focus:** Communication protocols between surface-based computing and aircraft navigation; autonomous flight operation constraints

## Use Cases & Applications
- Boundary layer atmospheric science demonstrations
- Real-time adaptive environmental sampling
- Dynamic environmental system monitoring
