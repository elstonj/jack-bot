## Executive Summary

The Department of Interior seeks multiple IDIQ contracts for on-call Unmanned Aircraft Systems (UAS) to provide non-emergency resource flight services throughout the contiguous 48 states. Services include survey and mapping, video, LiDAR, thermal operations, and logistical/reconnaissance activities in support of public safety and resource management. Successful offerors must provide fully contractor-operated and maintained fixed-wing or VTOL UAS with dual-sensor (EO/IR) gimbals, certified pilots, maintenance capabilities, and support personnel. Contractors must demonstrate FAA and DOI compliance and be available 14 hours daily for mission operations.

## Technical Approach & Requirements

### UAS Aircraft Requirements (Section B2.1)
- **Platform Type**: Fixed-wing with assisted launch/landing OR VTOL unmanned aircraft system
- **Altitude Capability**: 12,000 feet Density Altitude (DA); 8,500' launch site DA for VTOL at max payload
- **Endurance**: Sufficient fuel/batteries for 16-hour shift duration
- **Wind Performance**: Capable of takeoff/landing in 20-knot winds; sustained operations in 25-knot winds
- **Operations**: Day and night capable with anti-collision lighting visible from ≥3 statute miles
- **Minimum Quantity**: Two (2) UAS required per contractor; three or more may be offered for consideration

### Sensor Requirements (Section B2)
- One stabilized dual sensor (EO/Infrared) gimbal per aircraft
- Capable of capturing still EO and IR images with geo-tagging (latitude/longitude)
- Payload operator must have minimum 25 logged flight hours on offered UAS make/model/GCS

### Command and Control (Section B2.2)
- **Secure Uplink**: Digital data uplink frequency-hopping at 2.4, 5.8, or 900 MHz ISM band or proprietary spectrum with WPA2 or 128/192/256-bit AES encryption (FIPS compliant)
- **Secure Downlink**: Digital data downlink frequency-hopping at 2.4, 5.8, or 900 MHz ISM band or proprietary spectrum with WPA2 or 128/192/256-bit AES encryption (FIPS compliant)
- **Loss of Link Capability**: Autonomous LOL capability to return to designated location and terminate flight
- **Ground Station**: Programmable VHF AM and VHF FM radios (FCC compliant) for communication with incident and aviation resources
- **Compliance**: Operations must comply with DOI, FAA policy, and Executive Order 13981

### Regulatory & Certification Requirements (Section B2.1.1)
- All aircraft and personnel must comply with DOI OPM-11 and 14 CFR Part 107
- UAS pilots must possess FAA remote pilot certificate
- Pilots must be approved by DOI Office of Aviation Services (OAS)
- Aircraft must be FAA airworthy certified (or equivalent DOD/NASA flight release)

### Maintenance & Airworthiness (Section B5-B6)
- All maintenance by manufacturer-authorized personnel
- Routine/preventive maintenance before/after scheduled daily use or as COR-approved
- Contractor must maintain list of field-replaceable components
- Functional maintenance check flights required after installation, overhaul, major repair, or engine/flight control changes
- Immediate notification required for any major component changes or repairs following incidents/accidents

## Personnel Requirements

### Pilot Qualifications (Section B13.3)
- FAA Part 107 Remote Pilot Certification required
- Minimum 25 logged flight hours on offered UAS make/model and GCS
- Subject to government flight evaluation if deemed necessary
- Must demonstrate proficient operation of all aircraft equipment
- FAA airworthiness action notification required

### Duty & Flight Hour Limitations (Section B11)
- **Daily Duty Limit**: Maximum 14 consecutive duty hours per assigned duty period
- **Rest Requirements**: 
  - Minimum 2 calendar days off-duty within any 14 consecutive calendar days
  - Minimum 8 consecutive hours rest prior to assigned duty period
- **Flight Hour Limits**:
  - Maximum 8 flight hours per pilot per duty period
  - Maximum 42 flight hours in any consecutive 6-day period
  - When 36+ hours flown in 6-day period, pilot gets 1 calendar day off before new cycle

### Personnel Availability
- Contractor must provide 14-hour daily availability for all calendar days as required
- Missions may include weekends and holidays
- Government will assist with acquiring personnel passes, IDs, and vehicle permits as needed
- Training required: 8 hours incident/aviation operations orientation (contractor cost)

## Inspection & Approval Process (Section B13)

### Initial Inspection Timeline
- Scheduled within 30 calendar days of award
- Conducted at designated base, contractor facility, or mutually agreed location
- Monday-Friday, 0730-1630 local time (unless otherwise agreed)
- Written confirmation required; 10-day advance notice allows rescheduling if workload permits

### Aircraft Inspection
- Verification of compliance with all contract requirements
- In-flight dynamic testing of aircraft systems may be required (no cost to government)
- FAA Airworthiness Certificate or DOD/NASA flight release required
- Offerors without certification must participate in OAS airworthiness review at their expense

### Pilot Evaluation
- FAA Part 107 certification verification
- Flight evaluation conducted at contractor expense using same make/model/series aircraft as awarded
- Interim evaluations permitted throughout contract performance
- Government makes final determination on pilot competency

### Equipment Approval
- Issuance of Interagency Aircraft Data Card and Pilot Qualification Card upon approval
- Cards detail authorized activities; must be kept with pilots at all times
- Quarterly submission of additional payloads (01/15, 04/15, 07/15, 10/15) for approval

### Non-Conformance
- If equipment, personnel, records, or documents are non-conforming, inspection may be suspended
- Reinspection scheduled for later date; contractor may be charged reinspection costs
- Failure to have approved aircraft/pilots may result in exclusion from orders, removal from master listing, or contract termination
