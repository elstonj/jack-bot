# Evaluation of Routine Atmospheric Sounding Measurements using Unmanned Systems (ERASMUS)

## Document Metadata
- Type: DOE Pre-Proposal / Campaign Plan
- Client/Agency: Department of Energy (DOE), ARM Climate Research Facility (ACRF)
- Program/Solicitation: ARM program (Atmospheric System Research)
- Date: February 6, 2013 (document creation)
- Campaign Dates: April 15, 2015 to April 30, 2015
- Site: Oliktok Point, Alaska (AMF3 deployment site)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: DataHawk, Tempest
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (University of Colorado, co-investigator); Gijs de Boer (University of Colorado/CIRES, lead scientist); co-investigators from NASA, Sandia, NCAR, and University of Colorado

## Executive Summary
ERASMUS is a two-week field campaign to evaluate the feasibility of routine UAS operations for atmospheric profiling at the DOE's high-latitude Oliktok Point site in Alaska. The campaign will test two aircraft types (DataHawk and Tempest) to assess their capability for providing long-term, autonomous atmospheric measurements of temperature, humidity, wind, and aerosol/cloud properties under Arctic conditions, addressing key logistical constraints including icing, nighttime operations, profiling frequency, and altitude coverage.

## Technical Approach

**Campaign Strategy:**
- Two-week experimental period testing multiple profiling strategies
- Parallel operation of two UAS types to explore different measurement capabilities and operational regimes
- Real-time data evaluation on site with post-campaign laboratory analysis
- Integration with AMF3 (ARM Mobile Facility 3) ground instrumentation for context and validation

**Profiling Frequency Testing:**
- Evaluation of operator requirements: 24-hour profiling with 2-4 operators vs. single-aircraft operations
- Assessment of profiling frequency as function of cloud base height
- Testing alternative flight plans combining vertical profiling with horizontal transects near clouds and surface

**Altitude Coverage Assessment:**
- Determination of maximum profiling altitude under Arctic conditions
- Evaluation of cloud presence/height impact on vertical extent
- Documentation of restricted airspace constraints beyond FAA regulations

**Icing Characterization:**
- In-cloud operation testing under various temperature/humidity regimes
- Correlation with AMF3-derived liquid water path (LWP), cloud altitude, and thickness
- Detection of icing through flight performance monitoring

**Operational Environment Testing:**
- Nighttime/polar night operations feasibility assessment (lighting availability, polar bear safety protocols)
- Documentation of aircraft, instrument, and operator performance across environmental conditions
- Spring or fall deployment for weather variability testing

## Products & Capabilities Described

### DataHawk
- **Description:** Miniature (700g), foam-construction electric pusher-prop UAS with autonomous operation and GPS autopilot
- **Operational Characteristics:** 
  - Requires 2-person crew
  - Low cost (budgeted at $1,000 per airframe)
  - Ideal for limit testing and expendable operations
  - Can be pre-programmed for accurate, consistent flight patterns
- **Measurement Capabilities:**
  - Temperature
  - Wind speed and direction
  - Humidity
  - Atmospheric turbulence
  - Enhanced nighttime operation variants
- **Proposed Use:** Primary platform for extended routine profiling demonstrations; multiple aircraft (4 suggested) for relay-staging and redundancy
- **Data Output:** Real-time telemetry, stored flight performance data; high temporal resolution (~seconds); climb rate 2.5-5 m/s providing high spatial resolution profiles

### Tempest
- **Description:** Larger, electric-powered aircraft with greater payload capacity and longer range than DataHawk
- **Operational Characteristics:**
  - Autonomous operation with GPS navigation and ground station software
  - Longer range enabling extended data collection without landing
  - Requires cold-weather modifications for Arctic deployment
- **Measurement Capabilities:**
  - Temperature
  - Wind speed and direction
  - Broadband shortwave radiation
  - Aerosol particle counter (number concentration and size)
  - Cloud particle imager (hydrometeor imaging)
  - Potential for sophisticated probe deployment and testing
- **Proposed Use:** Demonstration platform for advanced payloads and measurement technologies; evaluation of icing tolerance under in-cloud conditions
- **Data Output:** Same temporal/spatial resolution as DataHawk; emphasis on sophisticated multi-parameter datasets

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Application:** Arctic Atmospheric Monitoring at Oliktok Point
- Long-term routine measurements of atmospheric profiles in high-latitude environment
- Integration with ground-based ARM facility (AMF3) for comprehensive atmospheric characterization
- Support for 2 nautical mile radius restricted airspace operations

**Scientific Research Domains Addressed:**

1. **Cloud Life Cycle (ASR Cloud Life Cycle Working Group):**
   - Stratification and cloud/precipitation role in Arctic lower troposphere
   - Cloud phase determination (supercooled liquid, ice, mixed-phase)
   - Response of Arctic clouds to climate change (sea ice loss, temperature, circulation)
   - Ice nucleation in cold clouds
   - Moisture inversion evolution in cloud formation

2. **Aerosol Life Cycle (ASR Aerosol Life Cycle Working Group):**
   - Cloud processing of aerosols (aqueous chemistry, coalescence, wet removal)
   - Aerosol aging effects (size, composition, mixing state, distribution)
   - CCN spectrum and ice nuclei concentration determination
   - Optical properties parameterization as function of size, morphology, and humidity
   - Black carbon and absorbing aerosol radiative effects

3. **Cloud-Aerosol-Precipitation Interactions (CAPI Working Group):**
   - Anthropogenic aerosol role in nucleation, solar absorption, and cloud radiative properties
   - Vertical transport in updrafts/downdrafts and scavenging processes
   - CCN background concentration and sources
   - Entrainment effects on aerosol-cloud interactions
   - Anthropogenic influence on ice crystal nucleation

## Key Results / Expected Final Products

This is a pre-proposal for a planned campaign, not a completed study. Expected deliverables include:

1. **Final Report:** Detailed outcome assessment with recommendations for enhanced Oliktok Point operations and feasibility assessment for long-term routine UAS sampling under tested scenarios and conditions

2. **Scientific Publication:** High-profile peer-reviewed paper documenting the experiment and providing datasets examples

3. **Archived Datasets:** Quality-controlled, formatted datasets for the two-week campaign to be submitted to ARM archive (with no planned in-proposal analysis, but expectation of future research utility)

## Notable Details

**Operational Constraints & Tests:**
- High-latitude operations present unique challenges: extended darkness (nighttime/polar night), icing conditions, polar bear hazard mitigation, extreme cold impacts on equipment
- Sandia National Laboratories personnel involvement for FAA coordination and safety/bear protection
- Restricted airspace coordination at Oliktok Point required

**Integration with ARM Infrastructure:**
- Coupling with AMF3 provides ground-truth data for real-time flight planning and UAS performance assessment
- Data to be quality-controlled and integrated into ARM archive
- Alignment with ARM stated priorities: exploitation of Recovery Act capabilities, multi-year high-latitude cloud/aerosol characterization, grid-scale product development for model improvement

**Budget Considerations:**
- Primary UAS-specific costs: 4 DataHawk aircraft at ~$1,000 each; Tempest modifications for cold weather operation
- Personnel costs for flight planning, instrument testing, and data QC algorithm development
- Personnel travel and equipment transport to Oliktok Point
- DataHawk ground station provided at no cost (loaned equipment)

**Scientific Rationale:**
- Addresses critical ARM/DOE BER mission gap in understanding clouds, aerosols, and their interactions—among Earth system's most poorly understood components
- High-latitude focus supports climate prediction advancement and environmental science priorities
- Positions UAS as enabling technology for future multi-year operational programs at Oliktok and similar sites
