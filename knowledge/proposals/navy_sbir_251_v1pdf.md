# Navy SBIR 25.1 Proposal Submission Instructions

## Document Metadata
- Type: Program announcement / proposal submission instructions
- Client/Agency: Department of the Navy (DoN)
- Program/Solicitation: Navy Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) 25.1; topics N251-001 through N251-073
- Date: 2024 (current cycle)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None mentioned
- Key Personnel: Not applicable (this is DoN guidance document)

## Executive Summary
This document provides comprehensive instructions for submitting Phase I proposals to the Navy SBIR 25.1 program, which encompasses 73 research and development topics spanning advanced materials, AI/ML, maritime systems, aviation, weapons systems, and other DoD priorities. The document details submission requirements, evaluation criteria, compliance rules, funding limits, and post-award procedures for small business applicants.

## Document Structure & Content Overview
This is an administrative/procedural document, not a technical proposal or report. It serves as the official guidance for small businesses seeking to respond to Navy SBIR topics. It does not describe any specific BST capabilities or products.

## Key Administrative Requirements for Applicants

### Submission Process
- All proposals must be submitted via the DoD SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP)
- Registration required prior to submission
- Electronic certification by Corporate Official required by BAA close date
- Encrypted or password-protected documents will be rejected

### Proposal Structure (7 Volumes Required)
1. **Volume 1**: Proposal Cover Sheet (compliance reviewed)
2. **Volume 2**: Technical Proposal - 10-page maximum, 1-inch margins, 10-point minimum font, single-column, single-spaced
   - Must include Phase I Base (6 months) and Phase I Option (6 months) clearly identified
3. **Volume 3**: Cost Volume - compliance reviewed only (not evaluated for selection)
   - Phase I Base: maximum $140,000
   - Phase I Option: maximum $100,000
   - Minimum 2/3 of work must be performed by proposing small business (both Base and Option)
   - No cost sharing accepted
4. **Volume 4**: Company Commercialization Report (required for DSIP submission)
5. **Volume 5**: Supporting Documents (administrative materials)
6. **Volume 6**: Fraud, Waste and Abuse Training Certification
7. **Volume 7**: Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Technical merit**: most important
- **Qualifications of key personnel**: equal importance with commercialization potential
- **Commercialization potential**: equal importance with key personnel qualifications
- Cost is NOT an evaluation criterion; only compliance review of Volume 3
- Evaluation is NOT comparative (proposals not compared to each other)

### Funding & Contract Structure

**Phase I:**
- Total maximum: $240,000 (Base $140,000 + Option $100,000)
- Three milestone payments: 50% at day 15, 35% at day 90, 15% at day 180
- Typical contract types: Firm Fixed Price (FFP) or small purchase agreement; may use Basic Ordering Agreements (BOAs)
- TABA (Technical and Business Assistance) available up to $6,500 (additional to award amount)

**Phase II:**
- Maximum: $2,000,000 (may be lower per individual SYSCOM)
- Limit of one sequential Phase II award per small business per topic
- Typical contract type: Cost Plus Fixed Fee; may use Other Transaction Agreements (OTAs)
- TABA available up to $25,000 per award (up to $50,000 total per Phase II project)
- Awardees receiving TABA are excluded from Navy STP and DoN Forum for SBIR/STTR Transition

**Phase III:**
- Any work derived from, extending, or completing Phase I/II SBIR/STTR effort funded from non-SBIR/STTR sources
- Awardees receive SBIR/STTR Data Rights on noncommercial technical data and software developed under Phase I/II

### Special Requirements & Restrictions

**NIST SP 800-171 Compliance:**
- Required for award consideration
- Current assessment must be uploaded to Supplier Performance Risk System (SPRS)

**Fundamental Research Disclosure (DFARS 252.204-7000):**
- If proposing fundamental research, must complete DoN Fundamental Research Disclosure form
- Eliminates need for prior approval of public disclosure
- Approval by Government Contracting Officer required

**Majority Ownership Certification:**
- Small businesses >50% owned by venture capital operating companies (VCOC), hedge funds (HF), or private equity firms (PEF) are eligible
- Must register with SBA Company Registry Database
- Must submit Majority-Owned VCOC, HF, and PEF Certification in Volume 5

**System for Award Management (SAM):**
- Registration strongly encouraged before BAA close
- Must be active at time of award (registered for contracts, not just grants)
- Address in SAM must match proposal address

**ITAR & Foreign National Restrictions:**
- Topics marked as ITAR-restricted require disclosure of foreign nationals
- Export control limitations may apply
- Universities/non-profit institutions may be restricted beyond basic research level
- Proposers must segregate basic research from classified/export-controlled work

**Not Recommended for Phase I:**
- Human subjects research (IRB approval can take 6-12 months; may delay start or terminate award if not obtained within 2 months of selection)
- Animal testing
- Recombinant DNA protocols
- Government Furnished Equipment (GFE) - may be considered weakness if unavailable

### Selection & Award Process
- Compliance review precedes evaluation
- Proposals not meeting submission requirements are rejected without evaluation
- DoN reserves right to limit number of awards per topic
- Debrief requests must be made within 15 calendar days of notification
- Debriefs typically provided in writing within 60 days
- Protests can be filed per FAR Subpart 33.1

### Post-Award
- Contract deliverables typically include kick-off brief, progress reports, final report
- Deliverables uploaded to https://www.navysbirprogram.com/navydeliverables/
- Phase II awardees not receiving TABA must participate in virtual Navy STP Kickoff (no travel costs)

## Topic-Specific Details (Sample)

### N251-001: Directional High Front to Back Ratio Low Frequency Antenna
**ITAR Restricted**
- **Objective**: Develop compact (<6ft), lightweight antenna operating in HF spectrum (1.5-90 MHz) with directional gain capability
- **Key Requirements**:
  - 30 dB front-to-back ratio (threshold); 60+ dB (objective)
  - Non-hazardous materials
  - Material thickness <2" (threshold); <0.25" (objective)
  - Compatibility with fielded HF radio connectors
- **Phase I**: Define concepts, conduct modeling/simulation, determine feasibility, recommend materials, provide Phase II plan
- **Phase II**: Develop prototype, test performance, refine design, prepare Phase III transition plan
- **Dual Use**: Commercial amateur radio and communications interference reduction
- **Critical Technology Area**: Advanced Materials; Microelectronics

### N251-002: Amphibious Combat Vehicle Weight Reduction
- **Objective**: Develop lightweight components using advanced materials or innovative design for ACV variants (ACV-P, ACV-C, ACV-30, ACV-R)
- **Target Components**: Engine access covers, drive shafts, ramp door, hatches, fuel tanks, armor, suspension, turret panels, bow plane
- **Key Requirements**:
  - Address salt-water corrosion resistance
  - Minimum 2,000 lbs total reduction
  - Maximum production cost: $20/lb weight savings
  - Improve fuel efficiency, lower center of gravity
- **Phase I**: Review drawings, identify components, produce preliminary design, conduct FEA
- **Phase II**: Fabricate and validate prototypes, conduct lab and on-vehicle testing
- **Critical Technology Area**: Advanced Materials

### N251-003: Amphibious Combat Vehicle Improved HVAC System
- **Objective**: Develop HVAC system meeting space, weight, power (SWaP) limitations, noise requirements, maintenance standards
- **Requirements by Variant**:
  - ACV-P: 320 cfm fresh air, ~55,000 BTU cooling, maintain <90°F in 110°F ambient with 1,120 W/m² solar load
  - ACV-C: 200 cfm fresh air, ~55,000 BTU cooling (includes computer/server heat)
  - ACV-30: Filtration to prevent noxious fumes, 220 cfm fresh air
  - All: Must not exceed MIL-STD-1474 hearing protection guidelines
- **Current Issues**: Inadequate cooling, uneven temperature distribution, refrigerant maintenance burden during engine removal
- **Phase I**: Site visit, conduct feasibility study, develop concept, demonstrate compliance through modeling/simulation
- **Phase II**: Fabricate and validate prototype, conduct on-vehicle testing
- **Critical Technology Area**: Human-Machine Interfaces
