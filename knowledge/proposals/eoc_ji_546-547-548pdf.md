## Executive Summary
Datasheet for three variants of silicon photodiodes with integrated low-noise JFET amplifiers (JI 546, JI 547, JI 548) designed for radiation/light measurement applications. Three models offer different feedback resistances and corresponding responsivity levels, operating across visible and near-infrared spectral ranges.

## Products Described

### JI 546 Photodiode
- Feedback resistor: 1 MΩ
- Max spectral responsivity: 0.6 mV/nW
- Rise time: 3 μs
- Bandwidth: 120 kHz
- Dark offset voltage: ±0.5 mV

### JI 547 Photodiode
- Feedback resistor: 10 MΩ
- Max spectral responsivity: 6 mV/nW
- Rise time: 15 μs
- Bandwidth: 25 kHz
- Dark offset voltage: ±0.5 mV

### JI 548 Photodiode
- Feedback resistor: 100 MΩ
- Max spectral responsivity: 60 mV/nW
- Rise time: 35 μs
- Bandwidth: 10 kHz
- Dark offset voltage: ±2 mV

## Technical Specifications (Common to All Variants)

**Optical Performance:**
- Active area: 4.8 mm²
- Spectral range: 400–1100 nm (VIS and NIR)
- Peak spectral response: 850 nm
- Opening angle: ±50°
- Very low offset and drift parameters
- High dynamic range

**Electrical Performance:**
- Operating voltage: ±5 to ±15 V
- Operating temperature: -25°C to +85°C
- Storage temperature: -40°C to +100°C
- Current consumption: 2.2 mA (typical), 2.6 mA (max)
- Short circuit current: ±45 mA
- Saturation voltage (RL = 2 kΩ): -14.8 V typical, -14.5 V max

**Mechanical:**
- Package: Hermetically sealed TO-5
- Assembly isolated to ground
- RoHS and WEEE compliant
- Replacement for JI 4, JI 8 (not pin-compatible)

## Applications Listed
- General light/radiation measurement
- Detection of low radiation intensities with high signal-to-noise ratio
- Spectroscopy
- Medical diagnostics

## Notable Details
- "Decadic staggered responsivity" design allows selection of appropriate gain for different measurement scenarios (three models with 10× responsivity differences)
- Integrated feedback resistor and capacitor reduce component count
- Dual power supply configuration (±V)
- Three variants cover range from fastest/lowest-gain (JI 546) to slowest/highest-gain (JI 548) operation
