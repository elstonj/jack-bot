## Executive Summary
This is the USGS Innovation Center for Earth's FY2016 Call for Proposals to the Innovation Fund, soliciting seed funding for technological innovations that address national-level science problems. The program will fund 8-10 projects from a $500,000 fund, with individual proposals targeting $40,000-$70,000. Proposals must demonstrate novel/innovative approaches to significant science problems and include meaningful technology partner involvement.

**Note: This document does not contain any Black Swift Technologies (BST) content. It is a USGS solicitation announcement with one example proposal included as a template.**

## Document Contents

### Part 1: Solicitation Rules & Requirements

**Submission Requirements:**
- Maximum 3 pages of text and budget, plus 2 additional pages for figures/captions
- PDF format with filename: YourLastName_ICE2016.pdf
- Email to: jstock@usgs.gov
- Deadline: February 5, 2016 (15:00 HAST / 20:00 EST)
- Notification of selected proposals: before end of March 2016

**Funding Details:**
- Total Innovation Fund: $500,000 for FY16
- Expected projects funded: 8-10
- Target budget per proposal: $40,000-$70,000 (smaller proposals accepted)
- Fund type: FY16/17 appropriated, two-year funds
- Duration: One year at a time; subsequent years can be proposed separately
- Reporting requirement: Mid-year update (2 PowerPoint slides) + minimum 3-page end-of-year results report

**Priority Criteria:**
Priority given to proposals that:
- Address national-level science problems with novel methods
- Leverage external technology partner expertise (defined as direct innovation producer, not just collaborator)
- Are aligned with USGS interests or represent significant growth opportunities
- Have technological innovation as centerpiece

**Activities NOT Funded:**
- Purchase or rental of commercially available instrumentation/software
- Workshops or data integration efforts
- Conventional software engineering
- Publication costs

### Part 2: Scoring Criteria (5-point scale for each)

The ICE Advisory Group (USGS senior scientists) evaluates proposals on:

1. **Scientific Importance** (1-5 points)
   - Scale: from "little generality or impact" to "national generality and great impact"

2. **Approach Innovation** (1-5 points)
   - Scale: from "conventional tools on well-understood problem" to "novel/innovative tool on challenging problem"

3. **Criticality of Innovation Funding** (1-5 points)
   - Scale: from "elaboration on previously-funded research" to "highly innovative science and tools"

4. **Technology Transfer Value** (1-5 points)
   - Scale: from "no new capability" to "capabilities leading to growth with potential for new area growth"

5. **Resource Sufficiency & Cost-Effectiveness** (1-5 points)
   - Scale: from "insufficient resources, no technology partner" to "substantial resources, cost-effective, strong committed technology partner"

**Review Process:**
- Panel composed of USGS senior scientists representing geographic and disciplinary breadth
- Includes Office of Science Quality and Integrity (OSQI) participation
- Written feedback provided to all PIs
- ICE Director facilitates but does not score

### Part 3: Budget & Administrative Guidance (FAQs)

**Funding Source:**
- FY16/17 appropriated two-year funds
- Functional Area to be announced
- Science Support funds have restrictions on assessments and carryover

**External Partner Funding:**
- Contracts/POs require two or more identified sources for competition
- Cooperative agreements don't require competition if partners do research/data collection
- All external partner costs calculated as gross cost (including their overhead)

**Cost Center Overhead:**
- Apply Cost Center burden rate (not reimbursable rate) to USGS internal costs
- Example: $25,000 science subtotal + 25% overhead rate = $6,250 additional = $31,250 total
- Validate budgets with Center Director and Administrative Officer before submission

**Other Administrative Notes:**
- Letters of support from technology partners recommended
- Mendenhall Fellows may serve as PIs if succession plan confirmed
- All funds must be obligated before end of fiscal year (September) except allowable carryover
- Cooperative agreement development handled through cost center

## Example Proposal Included

The document includes a template example proposal for reference:

### "Real-time tests of destabilizing subsurface storm-flows using the San Francisco Bay Area landslide monitoring network"

**Problem Statement:**
Testing whether lateral subsurface stormflow can destabilize hillslopes during large storms to improve landslide susceptibility models and forecasts.

**Technical Approach:**
- Deploy remotely-triggered servo-mechanism to inject sodium bromide tracer into upper piezometers at four USGS landslide monitoring sites
- Detect tracer with conductivity meters at lower piezometers
- Measure time-lag to determine if lateral subsurface flow occurs at storm timescales
- Use near real-time pore pressure data to trigger injections remotely

**Key Objectives:**
1. Design and test wireless, telemetered servo-mechanism (1L sodium bromide injection)
2. Couple with telemetry and write code for remote activation
3. Install logged conductivity meters to measure lateral flow timescales

**Technology Partner:**
- University of Irreproducible Results (UIR) Engineering Department
- Contribution: Machining/lab facilities, 1 semester faculty oversight (John Go)
- Expected technology transfer: General-purpose telemetered servo-mechanism applicable nationwide for groundwater studies

**Budget Example (Total $30,988):**
- Equipment/supplies: $5,000
- Student support (1 semester): $20,000 (includes university overhead)
- Cell phone contract: $1,200
- Overhead (18.27%): $4,788

**Alignment:**
- Checked for sharing with Mission Area Associate Directors
- Applicable to Natural Hazards and Water Mission Areas

## Notable Details

1. **Technology Partner Definition:** Documents emphasize that true "technology partners" must be directly involved in producing innovation technology, not merely serving as collaborators or cooperators.

2. **Innovation Focus:** Fund explicitly rejects conventional research without technological innovation centerpiece.

3. **Growth Potential:** Preference given to proposals demonstrating potential for national-scale growth and capability sharing across USGS community.

4. **Flexibility on Follow-up:** Year-two proposals evaluated using same criteria but with expectation of progress evidence. Multi-year work possible through sequential annual proposals.

5. **Review Panel Composition:** Independent advisory group ensures rigorous, fair evaluation with geographic and disciplinary representation.
