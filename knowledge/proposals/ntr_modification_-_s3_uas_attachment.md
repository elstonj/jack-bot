## Executive Summary
This document presents design modifications to the S3 UAS platform, transitioning from a five-motor configuration with twin tail booms to a streamlined three-motor design with a single tail boom. The update improves payload modularity, yaw control, and operational flexibility while maintaining the vehicle's core capabilities for atmospheric and geological monitoring missions.

## Technical Approach

### S3 Design Evolution
- **Previous Iteration**: Five motors, twin tail boom configuration
- **Updated Design**: Three-motor configuration with single tail boom
- **Motor Design**: Pivoting front rotors provide thrust vectoring for enhanced yaw control
- **Avionics Architecture**: Moved from payload housing integration to standalone small pylon under wing, enabling standard payload interface
- **Battery Integration**: Battery pylon colocated with pivoting motor assembly

### Key Technical Improvements
1. **Thrust Vectoring**: Pivoting front rotors replace torque-based yaw control (quad lift frame approach)
2. **Yaw Authority**: Particularly important improvement for fixed-wing aircraft with high moment of inertia in yaw axis
3. **Payload Modularity**: Separation of avionics from payload housing improves flexibility
4. **Structural Simplification**: Reduction from twin to single tail boom
