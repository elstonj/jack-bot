# Statement of Work (SOW) for Black Swift Technologies: Long-Range Fixed-Wing Evaluation & DoD Autopilot Enablement (BYL-LRFE)

## Document Metadata
- Type: Statement of Work (SOW)
- Client/Agency: ByLight (Bryan Sparling)
- Program/Solicitation: BYL-LRFE (Long-Range Fixed-Wing Evaluation & DoD Autopilot Enablement)
- Date: Created 2025-10-03, Modified 2025-10-06
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: SwiftCore (flight control system), Mustang Unit 3, Mustang Unit 4
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (BST), Bryan Sparling (ByLight)

## Executive Summary
This SOW defines Effort 1 of a two-part engagement where Black Swift Technologies will integrate SwiftCore into Mustang Unit 3, conduct truck launch operations, and collect video and flight data over a 2-week period (10/6 - 10/17/2025) to demonstrate reliable flight operations. Effort 2 outlines development of a next-generation Mustang platform with full electric propulsion, 55lb GTOW, and 400-450km range capability.

## Technical Approach

**Effort 1 (This SOW):**
- Integration of SwiftCore flight control system into Mustang Unit 3
- Truck launch operations
- Collection of ground and air video documentation
- Flight data collection and analysis
- Timeline: 2-week completion (Week 1: integration; Week 2: launch, video, and data analysis)

**Effort 2 (Referenced):**
- Design and development of Generation 2 (G2) Mustang UAS platform meeting specifications
- Source and procure commercial-off-the-shelf (COTS) wings, tail, and test fuselage
- Flight validation using SwiftCore FC and software with truck launch
- Fuselage solution determination (Unit 4 fuselage vs. new design)
- Progressive weight testing to 55 lbs GTOW using payload simulants
- Battery draw testing and range estimation
- Integration of government-owned (GOV) components: EchoMAV flight control, payload, gimbal, cameras
- Validation with government-provided launcher

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SwiftCore**
- Flight control system (FC) / autopilot
- Described as BST's core enabling technology for autonomous flight
- Proposed for primary integration in Mustang Unit 3 for Effort 1
- Also proposed as foundation for G2 Mustang development in Effort 2
- Appears to support truck launch operations

**Mustang Platform**
- Fixed-wing UAS platform
- Unit 3: Current platform for Effort 1 integration and demonstration
- Unit 4: Referenced as potential fuselage source for G2 development
- Target specifications (G2): <55 lb GTOW, 400-450 km range, full electric propulsion

## Use Cases & Applications
- Long-range fixed-wing operations
- DoD autopilot enablement and evaluation
- Extended-range unmanned surveillance/reconnaissance missions (400-450 km range)
- Integration with government flight control systems (EchoMAV FC)
- Truck launch capability for rapid deployment
