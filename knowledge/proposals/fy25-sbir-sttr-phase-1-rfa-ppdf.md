## Executive Summary
This is the USDA NIFA FY 2025 Request for Applications for Phase I Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) and Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) funding. The RFA provides $13,000,000 for SBIR and $2,500,000 for STTR programs, with individual awards ranging from $125,000–$181,500 and an application deadline of September 17, 2024 at 5:00 PM ET. The program supports small businesses in developing innovative, disruptive technologies with commercial and/or societal benefit across ten research topic areas aligned with USDA's strategic goals.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Applicant Eligibility:
- Must be a small business concern (SBC)
- Must meet SBA size standards
- For STTR: must have formal cooperative R&D agreement with partnering research institution detailing IP rights

### Foreign Ownership & National Security Requirements:
Applicants must disclose and comply with 15 USC 638(vv) requirements regarding:
1. Identity of owners/covered individuals in foreign talent recruitment programs
2. Joint ventures or subsidiaries in/funded by foreign countries of concern
3. Contractual/financial obligations with foreign state/entities
4. Wholly foreign-owned operations
5. Venture capital/institutional investment with foreign affiliations
6. Technology licensing/IP sales to foreign countries of concern (5-year lookback)
7. Foreign business entities/offshore entities related to the SBC

**Foreign Countries of Concern Include:** China, North Korea, Russia, Iran, or other countries designated by the Secretary of State

### Cost Sharing:
Not required

## Program Features

### SBIR vs. STTR Comparison:
- **SBIR:** 8-month project period, small business is primary performer
- **STTR:** 12-month project period, requires minimum percentage of research by partnering research institution (university, federal lab), formal cooperative agreement on IP rights required

### Phase Structure:
- **Phase I (this RFA):** Demonstrates technical feasibility; typically 8 months (SBIR) or 12 months (STTR); max $125,000–$181,500
- **Phase II:** Comprehensive development and commercialization (typically announced December, deadline March); for prior Phase I recipients only
- **Phase III:** Commercialization funded by non-SBIR/STTR sources (private sector)

### Technical and Business Assistance (TABA):
Additional $6,500 available if applicant elects to participate; brings total max awards to $181,500 (SBIR) or higher for STTR
