## Executive Summary
This is an AFWERX SBIR Phase II proposal template and example for Black Swift Technologies' soil moisture mapping project. BST proposes to adapt its existing NASA-validated L-band radiometer system mounted on UAS to measure soil integrity (cone index) for U.S. Air Force C-130 operations on unimproved landing zones, working with AFRL as the government customer and end-user.

## Technical Approach
BST, in partnership with OMS and CET, will:
1. Modify the existing L-band radiometer sensing instrument (refinements to radiometer, auxiliary sensors)
2. Adapt the UAS platform with new sensors for surface temperature and NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) to enable real-time soil moisture calculation
3. Develop algorithms to compute soil integrity/cone index as a function of L-band soil moisture data and soil type
4. Conduct local field testing to gather in situ cone index and soil moisture measurements
5. Refine the soil integrity model based on field results
6. Execute flight campaign testing at Little Rock AFB/Normandy Landing Zone or other customer-defined locations

## Products & Capabilities Described

### L-Band Radiometer
- Existing commercial system developed and validated in conjunction with NASA
- Measures high-resolution soil moisture
- To be modified with auxiliary sensors for this application
- Key capability: Radiometric measurement of soil moisture at L-band frequencies

### UAS Platform
- Carrying the modified L-band radiometer
- To be modified with surface temperature and NDVI sensors
- Will enable real-time calculation of soil moisture
- Integration planned for AFRL's extensive UAS remote sensing operations

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Use Case: C-130 Landing Operations
- Mapping soil integrity (measured as cone index) on unimproved landing zones
- Provides forward air controllers critical data for decision-making on C-130 landing operations
- Addresses key Air Force need: soil moisture is essential to understanding soil integrity, as moisture increases slipperiness/stickiness while decreasing strength

### Test Location
- Little Rock AFB, Normandy Landing Zone (or other Air Force-defined locations)
