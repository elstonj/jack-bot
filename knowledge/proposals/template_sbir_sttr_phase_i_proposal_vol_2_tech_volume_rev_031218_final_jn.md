## Executive Summary
This is a **template and instruction document** for the DOD SBIR/STTR Phase I Technical Volume submission. It provides formatting requirements, page limits, and guidance on the 13 required sections that small businesses must complete when proposing Phase I research and development work to the Department of Defense.

## Document Purpose & Structure
This is not a BST proposal or report, but rather the official DOD template that contractors (including BST) would use to format their Phase I proposals. It establishes:

**Format Requirements:**
- Maximum 20 pages (all content counts toward limit)
- Single column, single-spaced
- Times New Roman font, minimum 10-point
- 1-inch margins on all sides
- Standard 8.5" x 11" paper

**Mandatory Sections (with suggested lengths):**

1. **Identification and Significance of the Problem or Opportunity** (~1 page)
   - Define the technical problem/opportunity and its importance

2. **Phase I Technical Objectives** 
   - Enumerate specific objectives and feasibility questions

3. **Phase I Statement of Work** (~10-12 pages total with section 2)
   - Detailed description of approach, tasks, schedule, deliverables
   - For Army/Navy/DARPA/MDA: Include Phase I Option details (4-6 month tasks, NTE $25K-$125K depending on DoD Component)
   - Note any human/animal subjects or recombinant DNA research protocols

4. **Related Work** (~1 page)
   - Prior activities by PI, firm, consultants
   - State of art awareness
   - Client POC information and dates of performance

5. **Relationship with Future Research** (~0.5 page)
   - Anticipated Phase I results
   - Foundation for Phase II
   - Phase II clearances, certifications, approvals needed

6. **Commercialization Strategy** (~1-2 pages)
   - Market need and market size
   - DoD/Federal/private sector commercialization path
   - Quantitative commercialization schedule

7. **Key Personnel** 
   - Principal Investigator: school, degree, year
   - Relevant experience description
   - Awards/patents related to topic
   - Publications
   - Repeat for all key personnel

8. **Foreign Citizens**
   - Table identifying all foreign nationals/dual citizens
   - Country of origin, visa/work permit type, level of involvement
   - Required for compliance; failure to identify is common error

9. **Facilities/Equipment** (~0.5 page)
   - Available instrumentation and physical facilities
   - Equipment purchase justification
   - Compliance with environmental laws (airborne emissions, waterborne effluents, radiation, noise, waste, hazmat handling)

10. **Subcontractors/Consultants** (~0.5 page)
    - **SBIR**: Minimum 2/3 of research/analytical work (by direct+indirect costs) must be done by small business firm
    - **STTR**: Minimum 40% by small business, minimum 30% by single Research Institution
    - Federal Laboratory/FFRDC involvement noted on Cover Sheet (no waiver required as of this date)
    - Universities cannot publicly release Export Control/ITAR restricted information

11. **Prior, Current or Pending Support** (~0.5 page)
    - Identify if proposal is substantially same as one previously funded, currently funded, or pending elsewhere
    - Provide: Federal Agency name/address, submission/award date, proposal title, PI name/title, BAA number/date, contract number if awarded, applicable topics
    - Alternative: state "No prior, current, or pending support"

12. **Technical Data Rights**
    - Government obtains royalty-free license for Government use only for 5 years post-completion
    - Data marked with DFARS 252.227-7018 restrictive legend
    - After 5 years, Government has unlimited rights
    - Proposers may assert restrictions via DFARS 252.227-7017
    - Supporting assertions can go in Volume 5 (Supporting Documents)

13. **Identification and Assertion of Restrictions on Technical Data/Software**
    - Table format for restricted technical data or software
    - Requires: identification, basis for assertion, asserted rights category, name of person/organization asserting restrictions
    - Proposal submission constitutes signature

## Notable Details

**Compliance Notes:**
- Non-compliance with format requirements results in proposal rejection (20-page limit is strict, regardless of content)
- Disclosure statements required if proposal contains proprietary/restricted data
- Phase I Options typically exercised upon Phase II selection
- Section length guidance is suggested only, not absolute
- All resume content counts toward 20-page limit
- DOD Components with Phase I Option requirements:
  - Army: 4 months NTE $50K (SBIR only)
  - Navy: 6 months NTE $125K (SBIR) / NTE $100K (STTR)
  - DARPA: 4 months NTE $50K (both)
  - MDA: 6 months NTE $50K (SBIR) / NTE $25K (STTR)
