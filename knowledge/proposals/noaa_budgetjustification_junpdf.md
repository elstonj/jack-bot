# NOAA 2025 RFP Budget Justification – Tropical Cyclone UAS Sampling

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Budget Justification (subcontract proposal component)
- **Client/Agency:** NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration), OMAO UxS Operations Center
- **Program/Solicitation:** NOAA 2025 RFP from OMAO UxS Operations Center
- **Date:** June 2025 (submitted)
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** UAS (Uncrewed Aerial Systems) platform(s) for tropical cyclone sampling
- **Key Personnel:** Dr. Jack Elston (PI), Dr. Maciej Stachura (System Engineer)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes a subcontracted effort to improve NOAA forecasts by advancing UAS capabilities to sample tropical cyclones across altitudes from the ocean surface to the stratosphere. The three-year project is led by Dr. Jack Elston with Dr. Maciej Stachura as System Engineer, with a total contract value of $327,952.

## Technical Approach
- Dr. Elston directs overall project operations, maintains project plans/schedules, manages budget, and organizes stakeholder meetings
- Dr. Stachura leads design, development, and implementation of modifications, working closely with platform systems, controls, and sensor hardware
- Work includes algorithm generation and system integration across multiple teams
- Focus on modifications to enable UAS to sample tropical cyclones across a wide vertical range (ocean surface to stratosphere)

## Products & Capabilities Described
**UAS Platform(s):**
- Unspecified UAS platform(s) proposed for tropical cyclone sampling
- Intended capabilities: vertical sampling from ocean surface to stratosphere
- Modifications to platform systems, controls, and sensor hardware planned

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Tropical Cyclone Sampling:** Primary use case is sampling tropical cyclones to improve NOAA weather forecasts
- **Vertical Profiling:** Capability to collect data from sea surface through the stratosphere, enabling comprehensive atmospheric characterization in severe weather environments
