## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies developed the S0-AD, a low-cost, air-deployed small UAS platform designed for sampling the lower boundary layer of tropical storms and hurricanes. This report documents commercialization efforts and follow-on funding resulting from the NOAA SBIR Phase II project, including plans to develop a VTOL variant (S0 VTOL) for broader atmospheric science applications and the transition to Phase III activities valued at $476K over 2021-2023.

## Technical Approach

### S0-AD Design Philosophy
- **Purpose-built for air deployment** from NOAA P-3 Orion aircraft with parachute/drag chute deployment system
- **Simplified architecture:** Single swiveling wing design to reduce complexity, weight, and manufacturing costs
- **Weight optimization:** Leverages BST's proprietary certified autopilot, ground station, and integrated thermodynamic/kinematic sensing to consolidate subsystems
- **Target cost:** ~$5,000 per unit (vs. single-use aircraft costing orders of magnitude more)
- **Design philosophy mirrors radiosondes** to reduce operator workload and maintain current NOAA operational concepts (CONOPS)

### Key Technical Capabilities
- **3D Wind Measurement:** Multi-hole probe (MHP) with 5-hole design for accurate wind speed, direction, and 3D wind profiling
- **Thermodynamic Sensing:** Temperature, humidity, pressure, sea surface temperature
- **Additional Sensors:** Laser altimeter for height above water, thermal infrared sensor
- **Autonomous Control:** Pressure isobar feedback allows aircraft to autonomously position itself within desired storm regions
- **Data Integration:** Compatible with NOAA's AVAPS telemetry protocol for seamless data collection matching dropsonde formats

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0-AD (Fixed-Wing Air-Deployed)
- **Purpose:** Hurricane boundary layer research for NOAA
- **Key Features:**
  - Single-use, air-deployed platform from P-3 Orion
  - Drag chute deployment system for safe deployment
  - 1-2 hours cruise endurance
  - Designed for turbulent convective environments
- **Sensing Package:** MHP + temperature/humidity/pressure + laser altimeter + thermal IR
- **Status:** Final development phase, deployment testing planned

### S0 VTOL (Vertical Takeoff/Landing - Ground-Based Variant)
- **Purpose:** Commercial atmospheric sampling replacing weather balloons/radiosondes
- **Key Specifications:**
  - **Max Takeoff Weight:** 4 lbs
  - **Wingspan:** 54.6 inches
  - **Flight Ceiling:** 15,000 ft AGL
  - **Flight Time:** 60 minutes nominal
  - **Cruise Speed:** 40 mph
  - **Max Speed:** 100 mph
  - **Max Winds Endured:** 50 mph
  - **Payload Capacity:** 100 grams (1.8 in. diameter nose cone)
- **Sensor Options:** 3D wind, temperature, dewpoint, pressure, laser altimeter, thermal IR, EO/IR cameras, laser target designators
- **Endurance:** Up to 15,000 ft AGL with pre-programmed flight patterns
- **Market Target:** $12,000 per system (Cost of Goods Sold: $4,800)

### SwiftCore FMS-L (Flight Management System - Lightweight)
- Lightweight, low-cost version of SwiftCore FMS autopilot
- Originally designed for S0, high potential for future commercialization
- Possible applications: glider variants, balloon-borne sondes with return capability

### Multi-Hole Probe (MHP)
- 5-hole probe for wind speed/direction, 3D wind profiling, temperature, pressure, humidity
- Small form factor, originally developed under NASA SBIR
- Improved version for S0-AD being commercialized separately
- Proven commercial sales track record

### Intelligent Flight Control Algorithms
- Sensor-reactive control using pressure isobar feedback from MHP
- Autonomous guidance for targeted sampling with minimal human intervention
- Potential applications across NOAA and other agencies/commercial users

### Communications Package
- Uses existing NOAA telemetry protocols for atmospheric data collection
- Matches dropsonde data collection procedures
- Potential application: new dropsonde glider concept seamlessly integrated into NOAA operations

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary (NOAA)
- **Tropical Cyclone/Hurricane Research:** Lower boundary layer sampling to improve intensity forecasts and models
- **Targeted In Situ Measurements:** More frequent flights per storm (vs. single-digit limit with previous systems)
- **Temporal/Spatial Sampling:** Enhanced grid sampling for improved atmospheric models and forecasts

### Potential Commercial/Research Applications
- **Universities:** Atmospheric science research programs

### Comparison to Alternatives
- **vs. Weather Balloons:** $395+ per balloon; radiosondes unrecoverable; $1,000/day costs for 2 diurnal launches; S0 VTOL provides reusable, horizontal sampling, lower annual amortized cost
- **vs. Dropsondes:** Higher cost per flight; limited observation time
- **vs. Competing UAS:** Coyote and Aerosonde platforms (higher complexity, cost, broader mission requirements); two competitors also developing Coyote replacements
- **vs. Ground/Satellite Data:** Ground stations = single-point measurement; satellite = low spatial density, low resolution, limited measurement frequency

## Key Results & Market Analysis

### Commercialization Status
- **S0-AD:** In final development, deployment testing upcoming on NOAA P-3
- **S0 VTOL:** Under development with Air Force funding (separate SBIR, completed); civilian variant in early commercialization planning
- **Market Research:** BST initiating studies to gauge customer interest, identify regulatory hurdles, define performance requirements

### Economic Analysis
- **S0 VTOL COGS:** $4,800
- **Target Commercial Price:** $12,000 per full flight system with technical support
- **Value Proposition:** Weather balloon program ($300,000+/year) vs. amortized S0 VTOL cost (~$6,000/year for 2 flights/day) = comparable to research weather station cost
- **Market Size:** Estimated robust global demand; potential uncertainty about U.S.-only market sustainability

### Key Competitive Advantages
1. **Purpose-built design:** Optimized for specific mission, not ISR or other objectives
2. **Cost reduction:** Order of magnitude cheaper than existing air-deployed platforms
3. **Simplified operations:** Mirrors radiosonde workflows for minimal operator learning curve
4. **Integrated subsystems:** BST's proprietary autopilot, ground station, and sensing suite eliminate integration complexity
5. **Autonomous sampling:** Sensor-reactive control enables targeted, intelligent data collection
6. **Data compatibility:** AVAPS/dropsonde protocol integration for seamless NOAA operations
7. **Reusability:** Unlike balloons/dropsondes, recoverable and reusable
