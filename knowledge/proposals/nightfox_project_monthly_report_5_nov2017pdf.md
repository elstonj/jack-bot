# NightFOX Project Monthly Report - November 2017

## Document Metadata
- Type: Monthly Progress Report
- Client/Agency: NOAA
- Program/Solicitation: FIREX (Fire Influence on Regional to Global Environments and Air Quality); FASMEE field mission (coordinated for 2019)
- Date: November 2017 (Month 5 of reporting period)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 UAS
- Key Personnel: Ru-Shan Gao (Principal Investigator), Troy Thornberry, Schranz, Mandel, Kocharski

## Executive Summary
The NightFOX project is developing a UAS-based observational system for nighttime fire observations to support NOAA's FIREX mission. As of November 2017, the S2 UAS platform delivery remained on schedule for January 2018, with multiple sensor prototypes (near-IR camera, mid-IR and SWIR telescopes, CO sensors, aerosol instruments) under development and evaluation. The project was progressing toward field operations in 2018-2019.

## Technical Approach
- **Platform**: Black Swift Technologies S2 UAS as primary platform; CU Mistral (S2 backup) under construction
- **Instrument Development**: Multiple sensor payloads being developed in parallel:
  - Coarse-mode aerosol instrument (similar to POPS design) - concept validated, requiring stronger light source
  - Near-IR camera prototype - mechanical parts manufactured, testing scheduled for December
  - Near-IR cross-track-scanning telescope - parts ordered, prototype testing in December
  - CO sensors - two prototypes expected early December
  - CO2 NDIR source/detector - ongoing testing
  - Mid-IR and SWIR telescopes - prototypes to be completed/evaluated
- **Observation Strategy**: Preliminary strategy developed; model aspects and data needs prioritized
- **Infrastructure**: CU mobile UAS command/control vehicle being outfitted

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S2 UAS:**
- Primary UAS platform for NightFOX
- On schedule for delivery January 2018
- Intended for higher altitude and BVLOS (Beyond Visual Line of Sight) operations (discussions with FAA ongoing)
- Expected to carry multiple sensor payloads for fire observation

**CU Mistral (S2 Backup):**
- Newly constructed UAS based on S2 design
- CU expected to have four constructed by June 2018
- Serves as backup/additional platform

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Mission**: Nighttime fire observations during FIREX campaign
