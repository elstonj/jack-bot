## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes a DOE Genesis project titled "AI-Enabled Mobile Autonomous Laboratories for Real-Time Adaptive Experimentation in Dynamic Environmental Systems" running from July 1, 2026 to March 31, 2027. The project involves developing and testing AI-enabled autonomous systems for environmental monitoring, with a subcontract partnership with Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL) focusing on atmospheric science applications.

## Technical Approach
- Development of AI-enabled autonomous laboratory capabilities for real-time adaptive experimentation in dynamic environmental systems
- Boundary layer demonstration activity aligned with atmospheric science community needs
- AI framework coordination and development (led by BNL's Dr. Nathan Urban)
- Testing and validation of three S0-VTOL UAS platforms
- Close collaboration with BNL on project milestones and tasks

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0-VTOL UAS
- Vertical takeoff and landing uncrewed aerial system
- Three (3) units to be purchased for testing and validation
- Cost: $54,000 total ($18,000 per unit implied)
- Proposed use: Testing and validation platform for AI-enabled autonomous laboratory systems

## Use Cases & Applications
- Boundary layer atmospheric science demonstrations
- Real-time adaptive experimentation in dynamic environmental systems
- Atmospheric monitoring and data collection
