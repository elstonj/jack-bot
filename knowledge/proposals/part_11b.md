## Executive Summary
This disclosure document explains Black Swift Technologies' prior related research and clarifies why a new 2016 volcano monitoring proposal constitutes distinct work from a previous Phase I SBIR, despite both involving atmospheric sampling. BST distinguishes the scientific objectives and system requirements between the two efforts to address federal anti-fraud requirements regarding "essentially equivalent work."

## Related Prior Work
**Previous SBIR Award:**
- Title: "Advanced Technologies for Coordinated In-Situ Atmospheric Sensing"
- Proposal Number: 14-1 S3.04-9888
- Status: Past Phase I SBIR (completed/inactive by 2016)
- Focus: Atmospheric data measurement (3D winds, pressure, temperature, humidity)
- Application Context: Weather and atmospheric phenomena

## Current Proposal Differentiation
**New 2016 Volcano SBIR:**
- Primary Scientific Objective: Volcanic plume sampling
- Key Technical Distinction: System ruggedization for harsh volcanic environments
- Relationship to Prior Work: Leverages and builds on atmospheric sampling component, but constitutes "significantly different system"

## Key Distinction
BST explicitly argues the new volcano proposal differs "greatly" from the 14-1 S3.04-9888 effort in:
1. **Scientific objectives** - volcanic plume sampling vs. general weather/atmospheric phenomena
2. **System design** - environmental ruggedization requirements unique to volcanic operations
