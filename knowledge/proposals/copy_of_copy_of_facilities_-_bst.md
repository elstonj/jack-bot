## Executive Summary
This document describes Black Swift Technologies' facility infrastructure, manufacturing capabilities, and testing resources in support of UAS design, prototyping, and operations. BST operates a 3,000 sq. ft. facility in Boulder, CO with full vertical integration for aircraft design and manufacturing, complemented by multiple regional testing areas ranging from low-altitude sites to high-altitude mountain locations.

## Facilities & Infrastructure

**Primary Facility:**
- Location: Boulder, Colorado
- Size: 3,000 sq. ft.
- Contents: Office space, workspace, electronics benches, shop facilities

**Computing Resources:**
- Full suite of atmospheric and aircraft simulation tools
- Mission scenario analysis capability
- Multiple full autopilot systems available for testing electrical and software interfaces

**Manufacturing & Design Capabilities:**
- Vertically integrated designer and manufacturer of fixed-wing and multirotor UAS
- Full in-house control of critical systems:
  - All electronics (autopilot, power boards, GNSS)
  - Software and firmware
  - Mechanical design and integration
  - Ground control stations
  - Support equipment
- Prototype and production integration capability for aircraft, sensors, and avionics
- Outsourced partners: Northwind Composites (airframe composite work), local Denver-area board houses (circuit board manufacturing)
- Supply chain flexibility: demonstrated ability to adapt designs during electronic parts shortages through redesigns and microcontroller substitution

## Testing & Operations Areas

**Regional Testing Locations:**

1. **Sod Farm Test Site** - 20 minutes east of Boulder office
   - Used for operational testing (referenced with S2 aircraft photo)

2. **Pawnee National Grasslands** - 20 x 20 mile test area
   - Current authorization: 1,000 ft waiver

3. **San Luis Valley** - High altitude testing
   - Peaks over 14,000 ft elevation
   - Current testing capability: up to ~15,000 ft MSL under existing regulations

4. **Alaska Operations** - Remote BVLOS missions
   - Established relationship with Alaska DOT
   - Participating in UAS Activity Areas initiative for beyond-visual-line-of-sight operations in remote locations
