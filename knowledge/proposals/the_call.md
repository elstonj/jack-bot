# Tactical Resolution Soil Moisture Modeling (ARMY25-COE02)

- Program/Solicitation: ARMY25-COE02

- Key Personnel: John Eylander (Technical Point of Contact, Army)

## Executive Summary
The U.S. Army issued a request for information seeking a capability to analyze and predict surface soil moisture and soil strength at sub-100-meter spatial resolution to support tactical-level predictive mobility modeling for ground forces. The requirement calls for integration with Army Geospatial Enterprise and Standard and Sharable Geospatial Foundation standards, interoperability with DoD systems, and ingestion of operational weather products from the Air Force 557th Weather Wing.

## Technical Approach
The requirement calls for:
- **Resolution Target:** Sub-100-meter spatial resolution (significant improvement over existing DOD geographic information)
- **Data Processing:** Analyze and predict surface soil moisture and soil strength
- **Integration:** Weather-based soil moisture downscaling using operational weather products from US Air Force 557th Weather Wing
- **Standards Compliance:** Must operate within Army Geospatial Enterprise (AGE) and Standard and Sharable Geospatial Foundation (SSGF) standards
- **Interoperability:** Standardized interfacing to integrate with other DoD technologies
- **Network Compatibility:** Must function across all Combatant Commands (COCOMs) and support users on Army networks
- **Data Ingestion:** Capable of ingesting 557WW data transmission formats

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Mission:** Predictive mobility modeling for U.S. Army ground forces operations
- **Supporting Models:** Input to mobility models, energy balance models, and biological process models
- **Operational Level:** Tactical-level decision support (vs. current coarse-resolution strategic/operational level data)

## Key Requirements Addressed
1. Overcome spatial resolution limitations of existing DOD soil moisture data
2. Support tactical-level decision aids for ground force mobility
3. Enable interoperability across Army networks and DoD systems
4. Comply with Army geospatial standards and Air Force weather data formats
5. Support all geographic combatant commands
