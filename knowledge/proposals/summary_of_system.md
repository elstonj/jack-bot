## Executive Summary
This document describes the Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS, an integrated system combining BST's SuperSwift aircraft with a passive microwave radiometer for autonomous soil moisture measurement. Developed under NASA SBIR, the system provides L-band brightness temperature measurements over 400 acres per flight at resolution as fine as 15 meters, comparable to SMAP satellite data but at higher resolution.

## Technical Approach
- **Integration Strategy**: Tight integration of passive microwave radiometer sensor with aircraft avionics, sensors, and airframe to enable precise low-altitude flight control
- **Operational Altitude**: 15-30 meters above ground level for detailed measurements
- **Measurement Type**: L-band brightness temperature acquisition
- **Resolution**: As fine as 15 meters (compared to SMAP satellite capabilities)
- **Coverage**: 400 acres per flight
- **Precision Landing**: Laser altimeter enables precision landing and low-altitude terrain-following flight

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift Aircraft
- **Configuration**: 10-foot wingspan, fully electric propulsion
- **Endurance**: 120-minute flight time
- **Performance**: 40 mph cruise speed, flight ceiling over 20,000 ft
- **Operations**: Fully autonomous
- **Special Features**: 
  - Laser altimeter for precision landing system
  - Modular field-swappable payload
  - Certified under multiple NASA flight safety reviews
  - Demonstrated cold-weather operational capability

### Passive Microwave Radiometer
- **Purpose**: L-band brightness temperature measurement for soil moisture mapping
- **Integration**: Tightly integrated with SuperSwift avionics and airframe

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Application**: Soil moisture mapping over agricultural areas
- **Scientific Mission**: Validating and complementing SMAP satellite soil moisture data at higher spatial resolution (15m vs. coarser satellite resolution)
- **Geographic Capability**: Multiple flight campaigns conducted; cold weather operations demonstrated

## Key Results
- Two separate flight campaigns completed at time of writing
- Additional flight campaigns planned for summer 2017
- Successfully acquired L-band brightness temperature data (example map shown for agricultural site)
- System demonstrated capability to operate in cold weather conditions

## Notable Details
- System specifically designed for low-altitude operations (15-30m AGL), enabling resolution improvements over satellite-based systems
- Modular payload design allows sensor swapping for different applications
- Multiple NASA flight safety certifications indicate rigorous testing and validation
- Partnership with CET indicates collaborative development approach
