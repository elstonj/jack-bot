- Key Personnel: Dr. Gijs de Boer (consultant), Dr. Adam Houston (consultant), Jack Elston (BST team member, last editor)

## Executive Summary
This document outlines the consulting agreements for two leading atmospheric scientists to support a Black Swift Technologies SBIR Phase II project to develop the S0 sUAS for meteorological data collection in difficult-to-operate environments. Dr. Gijs de Boer will provide meteorological validation and model interface expertise ($60k over 15 months), while Dr. Adam Houston will conduct aircraft testing and CONOPS (Concepts of Operations) validation.

## Technical Approach

**Dr. Gijs de Boer's Contributions ($60k, ~6 hours/week plus travel):**

1. **Expert Guidance on USAF Requirements**: Provide performance specifications for the S0 platform including sensor accuracy requirements, environmental operating ranges, endurance specifications, and optimal flight profiles for weather reconnaissance and prediction applications.

2. **Test Campaign Planning & Execution**: Advise on efficient boundary layer sampling strategies (thermodynamic and dynamic quantities) across different environmental regimes; recommend sampling locations and verification data sources; potentially attend field test sites to support configuration and execution.

3. **Data Evaluation & Quality Assessment**: Review S0 field test data and instrumentation performance; analyze wind field measurements relative to other datasets and model output; assess thermodynamic data quality and accuracy for meteorological forecasting applications.

4. **Reporting & Communication**: Produce formalized reports documenting work performed, time allocation, analysis results, platform/instrumentation utility assessments, and maintain regular communication with BST team throughout the 15-month project period.

**Dr. Adam Houston's Contributions:**

- Provide guidance on S0 performance through calibration/validation tests and research-grade atmospheric profiling missions
- Conduct operator training and provide feedback on training quality
- Participate in regular meetings and contribute to interim and final reports

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0 Small Unmanned Aircraft System (sUAS)**
- Purpose: Collect meteorological data in difficult-to-operate environments for the USAF
- Application: Weather reconnaissance and prediction
- Intended capabilities: Atmospheric boundary layer profiling; measurement of thermodynamic and dynamic quantities; multi-environment sampling (implied: Arctic, complex terrain, severe weather)
- Testing phase: Performance validation through field campaigns with industry-standard instrumentation

## Use Cases & Applications

- **Weather Reconnaissance**: USAF weather reconnaissance and atmospheric prediction
- **Boundary Layer Research**: Sampling atmospheric boundary layer structure across diverse environmental regimes
- **Atmospheric Profiling**: Collection of research-grade atmospheric profiles (thermodynamic and dynamic quantities)
- **Verification & Validation**: Testing against standard meteorological measurements and model output
