# Community Airspace Safety through Eddy Dissipation Measurement (CASED)

## Document Metadata
- Type: SBIR Phase I Briefing Chart
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR 2021, Topic A2.03-1798
- Date: January 6-8, 2021
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 (fixed-wing UAS)
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (PI)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to develop CASED, a system to measure winds and turbulence in the lower 5,000 feet of atmosphere by adapting existing Eddy Dissipation Rate (EDR) algorithms to small fixed-wing UAS. The system leverages BST's historical flight data and partners with NCAR to fill critical data gaps needed for safe integration of Advanced Air Mobility (AAM) and UAS into national airspace through UAS Traffic Management (UTM) systems.

## Technical Approach
- Adapt existing EDR (Eddy Dissipation Rate) algorithms to work on small fixed-wing UAS platforms
- Utilize BST's extensive historical flight dataset to validate algorithm performance on UAS-scale data
- Identify and document hardware, sensor, and processing requirements for accurate EDR measurements
- Implement EDR algorithm on BST's S2 fixed-wing UAS
- Perform bench-top demonstration and validation before Phase II flight testing
- Develop Interface Control Document (ICD) for system integration

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- BST's fixed-wing UAS platform
- Proposed as testbed for CASED system
- Historical flight data available for algorithm development and validation
- Proposed to carry CASED hardware/sensors for Phase II flight testing

### CASED System
- Module to be added to any UAS to measure and report winds and turbulence
- Includes EDR algorithm implementation, sensors, processing hardware, and communications interfaces
- Designed to operate with minimal computational requirements
- Output: near real-time wind and turbulence data for distribution to AAM/UAS operators

## Use Cases & Applications

### NASA Applications
- Fill critical data void in wind and turbulence observations in lowest 5,000 feet of atmosphere
- Support UAS Traffic Management (UTM) system accuracy by providing 3D wind/turbulence profiles
- Enable safe integration of commercial AAM and small UAS into national airspace
- Advance short-term Numerical Weather Prediction
- Scale measurement capability with AAM industry growth at minimal cost

### Commercial Applications
- Direct subscription service to UAS/UAM operators
- Third-party data distribution services
- Scientific research applications
- Commercial operator safety and efficiency improvements

## Phase I Objectives & Deliverables

**Objective 1:** Adapt EDR algorithm to small fixed-wing UAS using BST's historical flight data; assess whether existing UAS flight data meets EDR requirements

**Objective 2:** Identify and document system requirements (processing power, sensors, communications interfaces) for accurate EDR measurements

**Objective 3:** Bench-top demonstration of CASED on Phase II flight-ready hardware to validate computational requirements

**Tasks:**
