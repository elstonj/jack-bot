# Budget Justification - Rev. 1 (NASA Balloon Observations)

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Budget justification for revised proposal
- **Client/Agency:** NASA
- **Program/Solicitation:** NASA Balloon Observations (implied from folder structure)
- **Date:** Created 2025-06-23; Modified 2025-06-24
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** S0 UAS, launch tube system, integrated avionics
- **Key Personnel:** Jack Elston (Principal Investigator), Maciej Stachura (Principal Engineer/Aerospace Engineer)

## Executive Summary
This budget revision increases project costs from ~$43,000 to ~$61,000 in direct personnel labor, primarily to support platform integrations and flight testing in partnership with Urban Sky's balloon platform. The total project budget reaches $237,349, reflecting expanded engineering support for mechanical/electrical integration, communication systems, regulatory preparation, and a risk-reduction flight test.

## Technical Approach
BST proposes to:
1. Develop mechanical and electrical integration designs for a balloon-launch system
2. Create communication and command systems for launch operations
3. Develop UI and data integration capabilities
4. Prepare regulatory documentation and conduct flight demonstration
5. Procure and integrate one S0 UAS as a validation and demonstration platform
6. Subcontract with Urban Sky to conduct risk-reduction flight testing

**Key Integration Points:**
- Thermal/electrical interface with Urban Sky balloon platform
- Launch tube and mounting system
- Avionics cabling and heater elements for high-altitude operation
- Integration rig for pre-flight validation testing

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS
- **What it is:** Black Swift's small UAS platform
- **Application:** Full system validation, limited flight testing, and flight demonstration
- **Configuration:** Equipped with sensor suite measuring 3D winds, pressure, temperature, humidity, surface temperature, and terrain/wave height
- **Budget Allocation:** $18,000 for procurement and integration
- **Role:** Validates the balloon-launch system before and during integration with Urban Sky platform

### Launch Tube System
- **What it is:** Single-launch tube with thermal/electrical interfaces
- **Integration Point:** Custom interface with Urban Sky balloon platform
- **Supporting Hardware:** Partial integration rig for system testing, simplified mounting strategies

### Avionics & Communication
- **Integrated avionics** for balloon-launch support
- **Launch command system** for mission operations
- **EO/IR camera (Hadron)**, radio systems, custom cabling and housings

## Use Cases & Applications
- **High-altitude balloon observations** (implied from program name and sensor requirements)
- **Atmospheric science measurements** (3D winds, pressure, temperature, humidity profiles)
- **Surface observations** (terrain/wave height monitoring from altitude)

## Notable Details

**Revision Drivers:**
- Expanded engineering hours required for **platform integration challenges** (80 hours for Urban Sky flight test integration, 40 hours for ground systems, 80 hours for balloon system integration)
- PI commitment increased from planning/initial design to 2-month engagement
- Additional engineer engagement increased from initial scope to 1.75 months
- Budget revision reflects move from "critical development only" to inclusive flight testing and integration validation

**Cost Structure:**
- Overhead rate (46.67%) covers indirect engineering, facilities, lab access, and project management
- G&A rate (18.32%) supports contracting, legal, compliance, and NASA/Urban Sky coordination
- 7% profit margin
- No equipment procurement beyond S0 UAS and basic integration hardware—emphasizes integration of existing platforms rather than full system development

**Partnership Model:**
- **Urban Sky subcontract relationship** ($64,500) indicates BST provides avionics/sensor integration while Urban Sky provides balloon platform and flight operations
- S0 UAS serves dual role: **validation platform** and **atmospheric measurement capability** in integrated system

**Technical Risk Mitigation:**
- Partial integration rig for pre-flight validation reduces risk of in-flight system failures
- Compressed (6-month) development timeline supported by experienced PI and engineering team
- Multiple testing phases (component, rig, flight) before operational deployment
