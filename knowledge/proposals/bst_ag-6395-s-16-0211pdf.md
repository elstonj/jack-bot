## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed the sale and delivery of four SwiftTrainer unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) to the USDA for animal damage management operations. The proposal emphasized the SwiftTrainer's low cost of ownership, ease of operation, reduced training time, robust performance in various weather/terrain conditions, and industry-leading accuracy in geo-tagging sensor data to enable rapid decision-making by field personnel.

## Technical Approach
BST offered a turn-key SwiftTrainer system specifically designed as a survey and mapping platform. The system includes:
- Hand-launched aircraft with autonomous belly landing capability
- Fully autonomous operation from launch to landing
- Advanced user interface enabling operator training and successful flight in less than one day
- SwiftCore Flight Management System (proprietary BST design, USA-made) comprising SwiftPilot autopilot, SwiftStation ground station, and SwiftTab tablet interface

Optional EO/IR video upgrade adds:
- FLIR Vue 640x512 thermal camera with 19mm lens and 30Hz update rate
- Dual-operator capability: one managing aircraft, one monitoring video feed
- Independent radio transmission to separate command and video feed

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SwiftTrainer UAS
**Specifications:**
- Coverage per flight: 600 acres
- Ground sample distance: 1 inch/pixel at 400 feet altitude
- Mapping camera: 24 megapixel
- Photo interval: <1 second
- Maximum flight time: 60 minutes
- Cruise speed: 33 mph
- Weight: 6 lbs
- Wingspan: 5.5 ft
- Propulsion: Electric motor
- Operational ceiling: Up to 14,000 ft (demonstrated in Colorado)

**Key Competitive Advantages:**
1. **Accurate Ground Tracks**: SwiftTrainer flies extremely accurate flight lines compared to competing systems (Pteryx, Falcon Unmanned), maintaining regularly spaced grids critical for consistent image overlap
2. **Accurate Geo-tags**: SwiftPilot autopilot achieves 1-2m correction versus >10m for Pixhawk-based competitors; significant R&D invested in this capability
3. **Fast Image Acquisition**: Camera trigger rate <1 second vs. 3.8s for Sensefly Ebee; critical for maintaining >60% overlap at 400ft or below and operating in high wind conditions

### SwiftCore Flight Management System
- Entirely designed and manufactured in USA by BST
- Approved and used by NASA for major scientific missions
- Deployed by NOAA
- Utilized by growing list of commercial survey companies
- Robust, reliable, easy to use, provides industry-leading performance

### EO/IR Video Upgrade
- FLIR Vue 640x512 thermal camera
- 19mm lens, 30Hz update rate
- Minimum resolution 10cm/pixel at 400ft (5cm/pixel preferred at 200ft)
- Dual tablet/screen system on ground
- Independent radio link following FAA crew management recommendations
