# The S0 UAS: An Air-Deployed Solution for Boundary Layer Observations in Turbulent Environments

- Program/Solicitation: NOAA SBIR 2018; Subtopic 8.2.13 "Developing a Cost Effective Air-Deployed Unmanned Aircraft System (UAS) for Use in Turbulent Environments"
- Date: January 31, 2018

- Key Personnel: Jack S Elston (CEO, Principal Investigator)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed development and flight validation of the S0, a low-cost, air-deployed unmanned aircraft system designed for boundary layer observations in tropical storms and turbulent environments. Building on prior successes with Aerosonde and Coyote platforms, the S0 aims to deliver an order-of-magnitude cost reduction while maintaining measurement performance and endurance for gathering 3D wind, thermodynamic, and sea surface data.

## Technical Approach
The S0 was designed with the following key objectives:
- **Cost target:** Near $5,000 per unit
- **Deployment method:** Air deployment (launched from manned aircraft)
- **Measurement capabilities:** 
  - 3D wind measurements
  - Pressure, temperature, humidity (PTH) measurements
  - Sea surface temperature
  - Vehicle height above water via laser altimeter
  - Thermal infrared sensor for sea surface characterization
- **Endurance:** 2 hours of flight time
- **Telemetry:** AVAPS (Airborne Vertical Atmospheric Profiling System) compatible
- **Interface:** Simple pre-programming interface for mission setup

**Design Philosophy:** Reduce complexity and weight compared to existing platforms (Aerosonde, Coyote) while maintaining performance and measurement quality.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS
- **What it is:** A commercial, air-deployed unmanned aircraft system for lower boundary layer sampling
- **Design focus:** Low-cost alternative to existing platforms with comparable or superior performance
- **Proposed use:** Tropical storm and boundary layer observations; kinematics and thermodynamics sampling in the lower boundary layer
- **Key specifications:**
  - Price: ~$5,000
  - Endurance: 2 hours
  - Sensor suite: 3D wind, PTH, sea surface temperature, laser altimeter, thermal IR
  - Telemetry: AVAPS-compatible
  - Deployment: Air-launched from manned aircraft

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Tropical storm observation:** Targeted data collection for improving forecast models
- **Boundary layer profiling:** Detailed measurements of wind kinematics and thermodynamic structure
- **Sea surface characterization:** Temperature and height measurements for oceanographic/meteorological applications
- **Lower altitude operations:** Focused sampling of lower atmospheric layers in turbulent conditions

## Challenges Addressed
- **Regulatory obstacles:** Air deployment circumvents some land-based flight restrictions
- **Intercept difficulty:** Launched from manned aircraft eliminates need for land-based platform repositioning
- **Cost barriers:** Order-of-magnitude reduction vs. Aerosonde/Coyote platforms
- **Observation time:** Extended endurance (2 hours) improves data collection windows
- **Operational complexity:** Simple user interface reduces training requirements
