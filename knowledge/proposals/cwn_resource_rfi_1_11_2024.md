## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies responded to a Department of the Interior RFI seeking contractor-operated and maintained UAS flight services for natural resource missions. BST provided compliance documentation demonstrating that its systems meet Executive Order 13981 requirements (no adversary country components), offered three aircraft platforms (S2, E2, S0) with multiple payload options, and documented extensive past experience with federal agencies including NASA, NOAA, and USGS.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed a contractor-operated service model providing:
- Full transportation, system setup, mission execution, storage, and teardown
- Support vehicles and personnel (certified operator and technician)
- Daily availability with potential operations up to 14 hours/day for 14 consecutive days
- Maximum 8-hour flight time per pilot per day
- All aircraft compliant with 14 CFR Part 107 and Executive Order 13981

## Products & Capabilities Described

### BST S2 UAS
- **Description**: Fixed-wing aircraft with 10-foot wingspan and swappable payload bay
- **Proposed Use**: Primary platform for DOI missions
- **Available Payloads**:
  - Soil moisture sensor
  - FLIR Vue Pro R thermal camera
  - Sony a5011 and a7r cameras
  - Micasense Altum multispectral camera
  - Trace gas sensors (CO₂, H₂S, CH₄, SO₂) and nephelometer
  - Trace gas (CH₄) sensor with <2 ppb sensitivity
  - 3D winds, pressure, temperature, humidity sensors
  - LiDAR (various vendors)

### BST E2 UAS
- **Description**: Multirotor platform with swappable payload
- **Available Payloads**:
  - FLIR Vue Pro R
  - Sony a5100
  - Micasense Altum
  - Soil Moisture Sensor
- **Proposed Use**: Complementary platform for missions requiring hover/VTOL capability

### BST S0 UAS
- **Description**: Optionally VTOL or air-deployed fixed-wing aircraft
- **Specialized Capability**: High-resolution atmospheric thermodynamic measurements
- **Available Payloads**:
  - 3D winds and turbulence measurement
  - Pressure
