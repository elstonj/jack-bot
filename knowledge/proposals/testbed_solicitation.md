## Executive Summary
NOAA's Weather Program Office is soliciting proposals for its Testbeds grant competition with approximately $4.2 million in total annual funding (13-15 projects expected at ~$300K per project maximum per year). The competition funds applied research and development projects demonstrating and testing new forecasting capabilities in NOAA's quasi-operational environment through collaboration with one of five testbeds: Climate Testbed (CTB), Fire Weather Testbed (FWT), Hazardous Weather Testbed (HWT), Hurricane and Ocean Testbed (HOT), and Hydrometerology Testbed (HMT). Projects should target Readiness Levels 5-8 and transition to operational use within 3-5 years.

## Technical Approach
The Testbeds Program supports technology transition from applied research to NOAA operations through close collaboration with designated testbeds. Key technical focus areas vary by testbed:

**Climate Testbed (CTB):** Data assimilation improvements (including AI/ML), Seasonal Forecast System (SFS) acceleration, S2S outlook methods, probabilistic forecasts
- Priority CTB-1: Data assimilation including JEDI integration, coupled GDAS development, reanalysis enhancements
- Priority CTB-2: SFS developmental activities, physics parameterizations, stochastic perturbations, ensemble applications
- Priority CTB-3: S2S post-processing, probabilistic forecasts, verification tools (METplus compatible)

**Fire Weather Testbed (FWT):** Verification datasets, fire environment prediction, social science/decision support
- Priority FWT-1: Fire weather verification tools, operational environmental parameters, microscale-to-mesoscale condition analysis
- Priority FWT-2: Social science frameworks for end-user communication, probabilistic messaging, impact-based decision support

**Hazardous Weather Testbed (HWT):** Convection-allowing ensemble forecasts, post-processing/verification, high-resolution observations, social science integration
- Priority HWT-1: Ensemble forecast system performance for tornado/hail/wind/lightning from rapid-update to multi-day
- Priority HWT-2: Post-processing and probabilistic guidance development
- Priority HWT-3: High-resolution in-situ and remote-sensed observation datasets, 3D objective analysis techniques
- Priority HWT-4: Social/behavioral science integration for forecaster/end-user communication

**Hurricane and Ocean Testbed (HOT):** Near-real-time TC analysis, supplemental observing systems, TC guidance, forecaster tools, NWP diagnostics
- Priority HOT-1: Improved 2D analysis of near-surface wind/wave fields in tropical cyclones
- Priority HOT-2: Supplemental observing system strategies, flight planning software
- Priority HOT-3: TC guidance (0-7 days) improvements using ensemble/AI/ML/statistical approaches; rapid intensity change prediction; TC genesis
- Priority HOT-4: Tools for forecaster interpretation of disparate observational data (cross-validation, probabilistic satellite classification, LES)
- Priority HOT-5: NWP model diagnostic tools for hurricane model characteristics

**Hydrometeorology Testbed (HMT):** Extreme precipitation/flash flood forecasting, winter precipitation, post-processing/verification, forecaster communication, atmospheric forcing for hydrologic models
- Priority HMT-1: Probabilistic flash flood and winter precipitation forecasting using convection-allowing ensembles
- Priority HMT-2: Post-processing and verification techniques for flash flood/winter precipitation
- Priority HMT-3: Forecaster use of probabilistic information, uncertainty communication, social/behavioral science
- Priority HMT-4: Atmospheric forcing improvements for hydrologic models

## Products & Capabilities Described
This is a solicitation document, not a description of BST products. However, it specifies that funded projects should produce:

- Tools, software applications, algorithms, technologies

- Training materials, user manuals, guidance, documentation

All products must progress through defined Readiness Levels (RL5-RL8 during project duration) with clear pathway to operational implementation.

## Use Cases & Applications

**Climate Prediction (CTB):** Sub-seasonal-to-seasonal (S2S, 2 weeks to 2 years) monitoring and prediction; drought monitoring/prediction; ocean, sea ice, land data assimilation; coupled modeling systems

**Fire Weather (FWT):** Wildland fire hazard forecasting; rapid fire spread prediction (minute-to-hour timescales); end-user communication for firefighting operations; long-lead (days to weeks 3-6) fire weather guidance

**Severe Weather (HWT):** Tornado, large hail, damaging wind, lightning prediction from next-hour through week-two forecasts; rapid-update very short-term guidance; severe hazard threat guidance

**Tropical Cyclones (HOT):** TC track, intensity, size, and structure forecasting; rapid TC intensity change prediction; TC genesis forecasting; pre-genesis disturbance forecasting; two-dimensional surface wind field and wave height prediction

**Extreme Precipitation (HMT):** Flash flooding forecasting; winter precipitation (snow, sleet, freezing rain) forecasting; atmospheric river prediction; precipitation type forecasting; multi-day threats; forecast timing and duration uncertainty

## Key Readiness Level Guidance
Projects should generally fall within RL5-RL8 range (demonstration to deployment) and show clear pathway to operational implementation in NOAA's Unified Forecast System (UFS) or existing operational frameworks within 2-5 years. Incremental improvements to existing NOAA technologies preferred over revolutionary new approaches.

## Notable Details

1. **Multi-Institution Collaborations:** Each institution must submit identical project narratives and separate budget documents; identical title pages must list all PIs and funding. Lead PI responsible for additional multi-institution coordination documents.

2. **Testbed Collaboration Required:** Close collaboration with at least one testbed is mandatory. If proposing multiple hazards across multiple testbeds, must clearly identify each participating testbed and explain collaborative aspects.

3. **DEIA Emphasis:** Proposals strongly encouraged to address broadening participation of underrepresented groups through partnerships with HBCUs, Tribal Colleges, Minority-Serving Institutions, NOAA Cooperative Science Centers, and accessibility/universal design commitments.

4. **AI/ML Integration:** Multiple priorities explicitly encourage application of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning methods (particularly for data assimilation, post-processing, probabilistic forecasting, and operational tool development).

5. **Transition Focus:** Projects must clearly identify ultimate operational recipients (e.g., local weather forecast offices, National Weather Service, operational forecast centers) and provide metrics/success indicators for transition.

6. **Probabilistic Forecasting Emphasis:** Cross-cutting priority for all testbeds to advance probabilistic forecasting and decision support services; includes development of uncertainty communication and impact-based decision support tools.

7. **Pre-Award Spending:** 90-day pre-award spending authorized but at recipient's risk if award not made or is inadequate.

8. **No Duplicate Funding:** Identical or predominantly identical proposals submitted to multiple NOAA competitions will result in rejection of all but the first submission.
