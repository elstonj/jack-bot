# Microstrip Colinear (MiCo) Antenna Array

## Executive Summary
This disclosure describes a novel Microstrip Colinear (MiCo) antenna array designed specifically for integration into a small unmanned aircraft system (sUAS) for soil moisture sensing. The lightweight L-band antenna operates at 1.4135 GHz and connects to the LDCR radiometer, enabling high-resolution soil moisture measurements at 15-meter resolution compared to satellite-based systems at 40 km resolution.

## Technical Approach

**Antenna Design Specifications:**
- L-band frequency: 1400-1427 MHz (SMAP frequency band), operating frequency 1.4135 GHz
- Configuration: 2x2 rectangular microstrip antenna array (prototype 2x1 validated, full 2x2 investigated)
- Element type: 5-segment microstrip colinear patch antenna per element
- Element pattern: Omnidirectional radiation pattern
- Array arrangement: Half-wave horizontal separation, quarter-wave vertical separation
- Quarter-wave vertical separation provides 90° phase shift for quadrature-phased LDCR receiver channels
- Radiation pattern: Horizontal radiation at horizon is canceled; broad end-fire beams realized in nadir and zenith directions
- Construction: PCB-based design (low-cost, scalable manufacturing)
- Mechanical stability: Styrofoam blocks used for mechanical support and resonant frequency tuning

**Design Development Process:**
- 11/03/2013: Initial concept design and simulation
- 05/04/2014: Refined design including aircraft fuselage in simulations
- 07/01/2014: Initial build and lab test of antenna array
- 10/04/2014: Integration with fuselage, testing, and tuning
- 04/01/2015: Outdoor testing fully integrated into airframe
- 09/08/2015: First flight test over instrumented field (Canton, OK)

**Key Technical Studies:**
- Impedance matching and input impedance validated through HFSS simulation vs. prototype measurement
- Mutual coupling analysis for 2x2 array configuration
- Optimal vertical separation distance identified to minimize mutual coupling and maximize main-to-back lobe ratio
- Resonant frequency tuning methodology developed

## Products & Capabilities Described

**MiCo Antenna Array:**
- **What it is:** A 2x2 L-band microstrip colinear antenna array optimized for sUAS integration
- **Use in this context:** Primary antenna for the soil moisture mapping sUAS, interfacing with the LDCR radiometer receiver
- **Key specifications:** 1.4135 GHz operating frequency, downward-pointing beam pattern suitable for nadir/zenith observations, lightweight design to fit within sUAS constraints, PCB-based construction

**LDCR (Lobe Differencing Correlating Radiometer):**
- **What it is:** A lightweight L-band microwave radiometer with lobes viewing both nadir and zenith
- **Integration:** The MiCo antenna was specifically designed to connect with and support LDCR operations
- **Capability:** Passive microwave soil moisture sensing

## Use Cases & Applications

1. **SMAP Mission Validation & Augmentation:**
   - SMAP satellite provides ~40 km resolution soil moisture data
   - sUAS with MiCo antenna provides 15-meter resolution validation data
   - Supports calibration and validation plan for SMAP spacecraft operations

2. **High-Resolution Local Soil Moisture Mapping:**
   - Watershed monitoring
   - Drought area assessment
   - Local soil moisture characterization in support of broader SMAP science

3. **Flood Risk Assessment and Warning:**
   - Support for FEMA in understanding flash flood vulnerability
   - Historical precedent: 2007 NASA-funded effort by Prof. Gasiewski mapped soil moisture in North Texas and Oklahoma flood region, providing alert/warning data for flash flood-susceptible areas
   - Cost-effective alternative to manned aircraft operations for vulnerable watersheds nationwide

## Notable Details

**Unique Features:**
- Specifically dimensioned to fit sUAS constraints (minimal weight/volume priority)
- PCB-based manufacturing enables rapid large-scale production if needed
- Novel quarter-wave vertical separation approach provides required 90° phase shift for quadrature receiver channels
- Substantial advancement in the art (per technology significance assessment)

**Performance Advantage:**
- Delivers 26.7x finer resolution than SMAP satellite (15 m vs. 40 km)
