## Executive Summary
This section describes Black Swift Technologies' facilities, equipment, and flight test locations for a proposed Phase II project to develop volcano monitoring capabilities. BST outlines its 6,700 sq. ft. Boulder facility, manufacturing partnerships, and three planned flight test locations ranging from grasslands to high-altitude mountains to active volcanoes.

## Facilities & Infrastructure

**BST Primary Facility (Boulder, CO)**
- Location: 6,700 sq. ft. facility in Boulder, Colorado
- Founded: 2011
- Capabilities:
  - Prototyping, simulation, and testing of electrical subsystems and avionics boards
  - Full suite of atmospheric and aircraft simulation tools for mission scenario analysis
  - Multiple complete autopilot systems available for testing
  - Integration of prototype and production SuperSwift XT aircraft
  - Sensor and avionics integration
  - Meeting space for collaborative work

**Manufacturing Partnership**
- Partner: Northwind Composites LLC (since May 2011)
- Services: Low-volume production, repair, custom composite fabrication
- Capabilities: Basic wet layup, VARTM, oven cure, assembly, tooling, advanced aerospace repair
- Use: Airframe composite work for SuperSwift XT

**Wind Tunnel Testing Partnership**
- Partner: Research and Engineering Center for Unmanned Vehicles (RECUV) at University of Colorado
- Facility: Wind tunnel operated by University Corporation for Atmospheric Research (UCAR)
- Use: Sensor calibration and nose cone design testing
- Existing research agreement in place for continued use
- Equipment tested: BST multi-hole probe

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SwiftCore Flight Management System (FMS)**
- Components: SwiftPilot autopilot, SwiftStation ground station, SwiftTab tablet-based user interface, communications hardware
- Use: Core flight control system for all BST aircraft
- Capability: Multiple complete systems available for testing electrical/software interfaces

**SuperSwift XT Aircraft**
- Proposed capability: Operation up to 14,500 ft altitude
- Integration: Sensors and avionics can be integrated at BST facility
- Proposed use: Volcano profiling and high-altitude scientific missions

## Flight Test Locations & CONOPS Validation

**Site 1: Pawnee National Grasslands (Colorado)**
- Characteristics: Open public lands, minimal trees/structures
- Elevation: Low/moderate
- Proximity: Close to BST facility in Boulder
- Prior use: Two previous NASA-approved missions, multi-aircraft experiments, atmospheric phenomenon sampling
- Purpose: Initial Phase II testing, simplified logistics
- Flight approval: NASA flight safety review board required

**Site 2: Alamosa Region (Colorado)**
- Elevation range: 7,500 ft to 14,000+ ft (contains Colorado's tallest mountains)
- Terrain: High altitude, mountainous
- FAA COA Status: Existing FAA Certificates of Authorization from prior commercial projects
- Purpose: Full CONOPS validation, test SuperSwift XT altitude capability (target: 14,500 ft), guide design decisions for commercial volcano system
- Benefit: Similar to intended volcano deployment conditions

**Site 3: Active Volcano Deployment (Two Options Under Consideration)**

*Option A: Turrialba Volcano, Costa Rica (Primary)*
- Science Team Lead: Dr. Jorge Andres Diaz (confirmed experience flying at this site)
- Prior Aircraft: Vector Wing 100 flown at Turrialba
- Regulatory: Flight permissions confirmed as simple and straightforward
- Support: Strong endorsement from Costa Rican authorities and University of Costa Rica
- NASA oversight: David Pieri (NASA TM) involved in final site selection

*Option B: Volcano National Park, Hawaii (Backup)*
- Advantage: Located in United States (simplified NASA flight permissions)
- Concern: National Park flying restrictions
- Mitigation: BST's recent Great Sand Dunes National Park flights and Pieri's DragonEye platform work in National Parks
- Status: Under consideration

## Notable Details

- **Flight Safety**: All flights at designated sites will be conducted under NASA flight safety review board
- **Team Expertise**: Science team includes researchers with prior volcano survey experience
- **Regulatory Path**: Multiple researchers (Pieri, Diaz) have confirmed operational feasibility at proposed sites
- **Prior Success**: BST documented successful National Park operations (Great Sand Dunes) with deliverables including orthomosaics and elevation profiles from UAS data
- **Phased Approach**: Site selection between Costa Rica and Hawaii to be finalized during Phase II, after consultation with NASA TM and science team subcontractors
