## Executive Summary
The Department of War is soliciting responses for the Drone Dominance Program (DDP), a multi-phase prototype acquisition initiative designed to procure one-way attack (OWA) small unmanned aerial systems (sUAS) at scale with resilient U.S. supply chains. Phase I will conduct a competitive "Gauntlet" event in February 2026, with up to 12 winning vendors receiving fixed-price prototype delivery awards (1,000–2,500 drones each) valued at $150 million total across 30,000 units at $5,000 per unit. The program represents a fundamental shift from requirements-based to capability-based acquisition.

## Technical Approach
**Gauntlet Evaluation Model:**
- Up to 25 qualifying vendors invited to participate in Phase I Gauntlet (February–July 2026)
- Vendors provide $25,000 to attend; carry additional costs independently
- Two-hour operator training period per vendor, followed by military operator evaluation
- Vendors bring minimum 11 sUAS (10 for missions, 1 for potential NDAA tear-down)
- Vendors provide 10 removable 2.0 kg inert payloads per system

**Mission Requirements:**
- **Mission #1:** Find and strike stationary target 10 km away in open field; second run with multiple simultaneous launches
- **Mission #2:** Find and strike stationary targets 1–5 km away in urban environment
- **Mission #3 (Optional):** Integrated live munition demonstration with Government approval; requires 3+ additional drones; vendor or military operator controlled with military oversight; 500m strike distance

**Scoring Criteria (Three Factors):**
1. **Gauntlet Performance:** Mission completion (find, fix, finish), setup time, time-to-target, accuracy, system technical capability beyond Phase I requirements
2. **Military Operator Evaluation:** Ease of use, setup/flight simplicity, military utility, structured feedback collected after each mission
3. **Supply Chain/Production Capabilities:** Scaling ability, Blue UAS List/NDAA compliance, U.S./allied sourcing, supplier relationships, financial stability, production maturity indicators

**Scoring Methodology:** Underlying calculations not shared; results posted on public leaderboard at www.dronedominance.mil

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Small Unmanned Aerial Systems (sUAS) – General Requirements:**

*Phase I OTA Delivery Specifications:*
- Full sUAS system capable of OWA missions (Find, Fix, Finish) at 10 km range
- Integrated inert payload minimum 2.0 kg (no Electronic Safe & Arm Detonation Devices required for Phase I)
- Controllers, antennas, display systems included as complete package
- Durable system components provided at 40:1 ratio (40 sUAS per 1 ground control station/antenna/etc.)
- 5% sparing of identified common failure components (propellers, chargers, batteries)
  - *Example:* 2,000-unit order = 2,000 core drones + 50 controllers/antennas + 100 spares
- Flight modes/settings for nationwide U.S. operation without special FAA/FCC approval (CONUS commercial compliance)
- Lost link/failsafe behavior included; beacons/line-of-sight FAA requirements NOT required
- Storage/transportation solution provided
- Blue UAS List compliant; NDAA compliant (FY 2024 Section 848, FY 2023 Section 817, 2024 American Security Drone Act)

*Delivery Schedule & Pricing Structure:*
- Two-batch minimum delivery with incentive pricing structure:
  - **First half:** NLT 2.5 months after award → $4,000 per unit (20% below reference price of $5,000)
  - **Second half:** NLT 5 months after award → $6,000 per unit (20% above reference price)
  - **Late deliveries (after 5 months):** $4,000 per unit; maximum 8-month timeline with potential cancellation
- Early delivery bonus pool: 6,600 of 30,000 units (22%) reserved for vendors demonstrating capacity and early delivery

*Phase I Award Quantities (Table 2):*
- 1st place: 2,500 drones
- 2nd place: 2,400 drones
- 3rd–12th place: 2,300–1,400 drones (decreasing)
- Total baseline: 23,400 units; bonus pool: 6,600 units = 30,000 total

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Use Case:** One-Way Attack (OWA) Missions for Ground Combat Forces
- Target acquisition and strike in open field environments (10 km range)
- Urban target engagement (1–5 km range)
- Multi-drone simultaneous operations
- Integration with military unit operations (fielding to DoW units)

**Mission Environment Evolution:**
- **Phase I:** Daylight operations; open field and urban environments; stationary targets
- **Phase II onwards:** Comprehensive counter-UAS (cUAS) application; night operations; more complex targets; denied communications/navigation scenarios; cold/heat/altitude environmental challenges; moving targets
- **Future Phases:** Actual explosive components; Government Safety Board review requirements

**End-User Context:** Delivery to military units within Department of War for operational evaluation and field use

## Notable Details

**Acquisition Innovation:**
- **Advanced Market Commitment Model:** Government posts quantities and prices up front; awards fixed-price prototype delivery to winners
- **Competition-Based Acquisition:** Shift from paper requirements to demonstrated live-fire capabilities
- **Production Scale Testing:** Each phase increases unit volumes and decreases unit costs, with winners reducing from 12 vendors (Phase I) → 10 (Phase II) → 7 (Phase III) → 5 (Phase IV)
- **No Progress Payments:** Vendors bear development/manufacturing risk; Government pays fixed price only for acceptable delivered units
- **Multi-Phase Budget:** $1.1 billion total across four phases (Feb 2026–Jan 2028); Phase I: $150M

**Supply Chain & Industrial Base Strategy:**
- **U.S. Manufacturing Preference:** Executive Order 14307 (June 2025) and Secretary of War memo "Unleashing U.S. Military Drone Dominance" favor American sourcing
- **NDAA Compliance Mandatory:** Blue UAS List accession required; no payment without Blue UAS List verification
- **Phase II Escalation:** Motors and battery systems from covered countries will be precluded (more restrictive than current NDAA)
- **Army Drone Marketplace:** Phase I participants offered opportunity for inclusion in planned Army drone marketplace with Gauntlet results and feedback documented

**Military Operator Focus:**
- Ease of use heavily weighted in scoring (reduces training burden)
- Training and field maintenance materials required with deliveries
- Vendors expected to provide SME support and tech support at CONUS unit fielding locations
- Delta packages required for any design changes affecting operation/field maintenance within a phase

**Success Criteria for Phase I:**
- On-time delivery of mission-capable OWA drones
- Compliance with OTA sUAS Delivery Requirements
- NDAA compliance verified through Blue UAS List
- End-user evaluation confirming delivered units consistently achieve/surpass capabilities and quality benchmarks established during Gauntlet

**Production Follow-On Path:**
- Successful completion may transition prototype to production without additional competition
- May occur prior to all four phases' completion, enabling selective production transitions

**Eligibility & Security Requirements:**
- **10 U.S.C. § 4022 Eligibility:** At least one nontraditional defense contractor OR all participants are small businesses/nontraditional contractors OR one-third of costs from non-Federal sources
- **CMMC Requirements:** Level 1 (Self) for proposal; Level 2 (Self) required prior to prototype delivery award
- **Section 889 Compliance:** No covered telecommunications equipment (Huawei, ZTE, Hytera, Hikvision, Dahua) or services
- **ITAR/EAR Compliance:** Strict adherence required; technology developments constitute national defense enhancement
- **Data Rights:** Limited rights standard (Government may use for emergency repair, release to covered support contractors, or foreign governments for evaluation; restrictions apply)

**Submission Requirements:**

- Evaluation considers technical merit, implementation feasibility, and total project risk
- Incomplete or unclear responses may be rejected as ineligible
- Debriefings not provided; unsuccessful proposals retained for future consideration
