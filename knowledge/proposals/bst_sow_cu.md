## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies will develop, integrate, and test advanced UAS technologies for atmospheric research in collaboration with the University of Colorado. BST's work focuses on online sensor calibration algorithms, communication protocols for coordinated UAS operations, and comprehensive flight testing in extreme weather conditions to support high-resolution meteorological data collection.

## Technical Approach

**Online Calibration & Wind Sensing (Objective A1):**
- Design and develop online calibration algorithm for real-time UAS sensor data accuracy
- Integrate calibration algorithm into UAS systems
- Flight test validation across various atmospheric conditions
- Collaborate with CU on wind sensing solution evaluation in extreme weather

**4D Mapping & Coordination (Objective A2):**
- Flight test evaluation of CU-designed 4D mapping algorithms
- Validate spatio-temporal data accuracy and operational efficiency
- (CU leads algorithm design; BST provides flight test support)

**Communication System Development (Objective A3):**
- Design standardized data format for seamless communication across UAS platforms
- Design communication protocols governing data exchange and UAS coordination
- Implement protocols within UAS systems
- Conduct comprehensive flight tests of communication system in real-world scenarios
