## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to develop a Sonobuoy-Launched Unmanned Aircraft System (SL UAS) capable of magnetic anomaly detection (MAD) and passive acoustic sensing for Navy anti-submarine warfare (ASW) operations. The Phase I option period will validate sensor integration feasibility, modify the S0 platform to accommodate payload constraints, conduct controlled environment testing, and develop data fusion algorithms to combine MAD and acoustic outputs for enhanced underwater target detection and classification.

## Technical Approach

### Phase I Option Period Objectives:

**Objective 1: Sensor Integration Feasibility**
- Evaluate noise mitigation strategies to minimize platform-induced interference
- Ensure hydrophone meets Navy sensitivity, frequency range, and depth requirements

**Objective 2: Platform Modification for Sonobuoy Launch Constraints (SLC)**
- Modify existing S0 UAS platform to accommodate payload volume and weight constraints
- Design for tube-launch compatibility

**Objective 3: Sensor Performance Validation**
- Laboratory simulations and bench testing of magnetometer noise performance
- Characterization of hydrophone acoustic sensitivity
- Compliance verification with noise spectral density requirements

**Objective 4: Data Fusion Algorithm Development**
- Design and implement algorithms combining MAD and acoustic sensor outputs
- Test for underwater target detection and classification enhancement

### Work Plan Summary:

1. **Requirements Analysis and Preliminary Design:** Validate selected magnetometer and hydrophone sensors; plan compute module integration; optimize for SWAP (Size, Weight, and Power)

3. **Sensor Integration and Testing:** Test magnetometer compliance; continue validation using larger UAS currently flying QuSpin magnetometer

4. **UAS Prototype Construction:** Modify S0 UAS to accommodate sensors and Navy mission requirements

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS
- **What it is:** BST's baseline unmanned aircraft system, tube-launchable platform
- **Current state:** Existing platform being modified for Navy mission
- **Proposed modifications:** Integration of magnetometer and hydrophone sensors; payload accommodation; electronics redesign for compute module; airframe size/shape modifications possible to trade battery capacity for additional payload
- **Key capability:** Sonobuoy-launched deployment from P-8 aircraft

### Magnetometer Sensor
- **Type:** QuSpin QTFM Gen-2 magnetometer (primary option under evaluation)
- **Current testing:** Already flown in BST larger UAS during Phase I proposal work
- **Sensitivity requirement:** Must comply with Navy noise spectral density requirements
- **Challenge:** Maintaining noise performance during flight (mitigation: rapid modification capability of S0 design)
- **Phase I option work:** Comparison with lower-cost alternative sensors

### Hydrophone (Passive Acoustic Sensor)
- **Requirements:** Must meet Navy sensitivity, frequency range, and depth specifications
- **Testing partner:** Ultra Maritime (Paul DeMond – SME)
- **Validation approach:** Laboratory testing and bench characterization

### Data Fusion Algorithms
- **Capability:** Combine MAD and acoustic sensor outputs for integrated underwater target detection and classification
- **Development approach:** Design, implement, and test during Phase I option

## Notable Details

### Challenges and Risk Mitigation:

1. **Magnetometer noise requirements during flight**
   - Mitigation: Prior test flights with QuSpin sensor and S0 UAS; BST designs all S0 aspects enabling rapid modification

2. **Fitting sensors and compute into tube-launched platform**
   - Mitigation: CAD design validation during Phase I; flexibility to modify S0 airframe size/shape and trade batteries for payload

3. **Achieving requirements within $10K price point**
   - Mitigation: Comparing lower-cost sensors; improving production efficiencies in Phase II
