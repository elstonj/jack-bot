## Executive Summary
This Q&A document clarifies the WWF's Wildlife Crime Technology Project RFP requirements for potential suppliers. The project seeks aerial surveillance systems to detect wildlife crimes across four international deployment sites (Namibia, Nepal, and two additional Asia/Africa locations). The RFP was flexible regarding system type (not limited to fixed-wing UAVs), emphasizing integration with SMART database and animal tracking technologies, with initial field deployment expected October 2013.

## Technical Approach & Requirements

**System Specifications:**
- Fixed-wing electrical UAV preferred but alternative solutions accepted
- Minimum 60-minute endurance with video transmission and continuous telemetry
- Operating temperature range: primarily 10-30°C (acceptable under -5°C to +40°C in emergencies)
- Launch capability in winds up to 16 m/s (with flexible takeoff direction)
- Landing area: 100m x 100m with potential 20m obstacles at edges
- Emergency landing probability: ≤1% per 100 flights (technical failure only, excluding pilot error)

**Ground Control Station (GCS) Requirements:**
- Display of UAV position, line-of-sight (LOS), and coordinates on moving map
- Operation in daylight and nighttime conditions with readable outdoor sunlight visibility
- Real-time emergency landing location selection capability
- "Camera guide mode" - automatic flight around target locations
- Drawing function for marking boundaries, areas of interest, event detections, suspicious areas
- Flexible hardware approach (PC, tablet, or ruggedized case) welcome

**Payload & Sensors:**
- Gimbal-preferred EO/IR sensor (fixed acceptable if justified)
- Continuous optical zoom in EO camera preferred (non-optical zoom acceptable if cost-justified and requirements met)
- Detection/recognition requirements in all light conditions (evaluation primarily in full daylight and moonlight conditions)
- Simple "training camera" for field training separate from primary mission payload
- Small fire detection capability (objects <1 meter diameter)
- Vehicle classification: distinguish family cars from pickup trucks from semi-trucks

**Data & Mission Requirements:**
- Mission data = flight parameters ("black box") stored in UAV and GCS (separate from video recording)
- Data elements: UAV path, coordinates, targets, altitude, indicated airspeed (IAS), temperature
- Post-mission SMART database integration via event flagging (location, time, event type/violation)
- Minimal acceptable solution: event export as Excel file or basic location/time/event data
- Video mosaic optional but evaluated

**Durability & Field Performance:**
- Rugged transport in vehicles over rough terrain
- High durability target: 98% of landings require only field-level (O) maintenance; only 2% require intermediate (I) level maintenance
- Protective coatings for sensitive electrical components
- Battery performance: can operate 3 flights/week at 2 hours per flight; acceptable to be <60 minutes in low temperatures if documented
- Data rate for storage: supplier to recommend

**Operational Constraints:**
- Detection capability with lights off
- Multiple systems in same area: two systems operating one UAV each within 50km x 50km area (two teams minimum, 2+ personnel each team)
- GCS operation in moving vehicles permitted (optional mode; UAV launched in static mode first)
- Modem reception antenna access in moving vehicles (not confined)
- Training location flexible (preferred on-site but open to alternatives)

## Use Cases & Applications

**Mission Context:** Wildlife crime detection and prevention at four protected sites over 3 years
- **Primary Location (2013):** Namibia
- **Secondary Location (pending):** Nepal  
- **Future Sites:** One additional Asia site, one additional Africa site
- **Site Coverage:** Highly variable (10,000 sq mi to <200 sq mi); system not expected to cover entire area but deployed in high-threat zones efficiently

**Operational Tempo:**
- Expected 3-4 days/week operations
- 1-2 missions per day
- 1-2 hour missions
- Theoretical lifetime: duration of 3-year project minimum

**Integration Requirements:**
- SMART database integration (post-mission event data export minimum; preferably real-time integration)
- Animal tracking device compatibility with multiple scenarios:
  - Passive RFID tags (≤1km detection range)
  - GSM-communicating tags (in areas with poor cellular reception)
  - Satellite-based tags (potentially independent of aerial system)
  - UHF-communicating tags
  - Other emerging solutions

## Contractual & Procurement Approach

**Acquisition Strategy:**
- Initial procurement: one system for first pilot site
- Second system purchase likely to follow if first system successful
- No budget ceiling disclosed for aerial systems component
- Open to multiple procurement models: purchase, lease, or service contracts
- Proposals may include multiple options (sale/lease/service) for selection flexibility
- Full-service solutions (hourly rate provision) acceptable alternative to asset provision

**System Deployment:**
- Systems will NOT be transferred between countries
- Target replication: selected system intended to serve as model for other organizations with similar missions
- Multiple awardees possible for evaluation phase; final contract with single provider preferred

**Contract Terms Expected:**
- Training
- On-the-job training (OJT)
- Long-term maintenance

## Notable Details

**Technical Flexibility:**
- RFP explicitly positioned as "conceptual document" - suppliers encouraged to propose alternative solutions outside stated parameters if justified by CONOPS requirements
- Not limited to fixed-wing UAVs despite RFP language
- SMART and RFID/GSM specifications incomplete; suppliers asked to respond based on current understanding and proposed integration approaches
- Supplier responsible for all export/trade control compliance and obtaining necessary licenses for deployment to Namibia, Nepal, and other international locations

**Evaluation Approach:**
- Emphasis on technical capability, flexibility of ideas, and system integration strength
- Field evaluation expected October 2013
- NIIRS rating acceptable for image quality assessment
- Durability, ruggedness, and field operability prioritized

**Operational Philosophy:**
- Emphasis on field-sustainable solutions (minimal maintenance in theater)
- Training approach flexible but preferably on-site with SMEs/trained operators potentially working with end users
- GCS hardware/interface approach flexible ("we welcome any idea you see as appropriate")
