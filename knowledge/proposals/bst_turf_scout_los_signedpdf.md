# BST Turf Scout Letter of Support

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: U.S. Air Force (AFWERX)
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Phase II (Topic 22.4 - Soil Moisture Mapping); Proposal F2-16588
- Date: 2023-05-03
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: L-band radiometer, UAS (unmanned aircraft system)
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor at BST); Dana Sullivan (President/Owner, TurfScout)

## Executive Summary
TurfScout provides a letter of support endorsing Black Swift Technologies' SBIR Phase II effort to map soil integrity from a UAS using an improved L-band radiometer. TurfScout highlights the commercial importance of BST's technology improvements and references successful collaboration on a 2022 pilot project mapping soil moisture on golf courses.

## Technical Approach
BST's proposed approach involves:
- Improvements to an L-band radiometer (core technology)
- Integration of the radiometer with a UAS platform
- Mapping soil integrity/moisture from the UAS platform
- Technology refinement to enable wider commercial adoption

## Products & Capabilities Described

**L-band Radiometer**
- Core sensor technology for soil moisture/integrity measurement
- Being improved as part of Phase II effort
- Enables remote sensing capabilities from UAS platform

**UAS (Unmanned Aircraft System)**
- Platform for deploying the L-band radiometer
- Subject to improvements during Phase II
- Used for aerial soil moisture mapping

## Use Cases & Applications

**Golf Course Soil Moisture Mapping**
- 2022 pilot project with Toro partnership
- Mapped soil moisture on golf courses
- Data compared against ground-based sensors
- Application: reducing water usage on golf courses through accurate soil moisture monitoring

## Notable Details
- TurfScout brings expertise in remote sensing, GIS, and data analysis
- TurfScout emphasizes data integrity and analyst empowerment in their approach
- Successful 2022 pilot demonstrated feasibility of the mapping concept
- Commercial application identified for water management in turf industry (golf courses)
- Toro (major turf equipment company) involved in pilot project partnership
