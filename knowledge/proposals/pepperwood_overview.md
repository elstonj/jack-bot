- Date: Created December 1, 2023; last modified April 25, 2025

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies partnered with USGS to develop and demonstrate soil moisture mapping capabilities using uncrewed aircraft systems equipped with L-band radiometer technology. The pilot study deployed the S2 fixed-wing UAS over Pepperwood, California to map surface soil moisture across large areas, generating multiple data products to support water resource management during wildfire season, droughts, and floods.

## Technical Approach
- **Platform**: S2 fixed-wing UAS (also mentions multirotor option for higher resolution)
- **Primary Sensor**: Patented L-band radiometer (similar to NASA SMAP technology) that penetrates vegetation to measure average soil moisture through top soil layers
- **Auxiliary Sensors**: Multispectral cameras capturing visible, thermal, and near-infrared (NIR) imagery for NDVI and thermal data
- **Data Processing**: Automated flight path generation; raw radiometer data combined with auxiliary camera data and soil type information
- **Operational Capacity**: Up to 6 flights per day per S2 system

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS with Soil Moisture Mapping (SMM)
- **What it is**: Fixed-wing uncrewed aircraft equipped with L-band radiometer and multispectral camera suite
- **Proposed use**: Rapid mapping of surface soil moisture over large areas to inform land and water resource management
- **Key specifications**:
  - Coverage: 600 acres (0.5 sq miles) per flight
  - Resolution: Moisture maps up to 5-meter resolution
  - Accuracy: Within 3% of true volumetric soil moisture
  - Altitude capability: Demonstrated at 10,000 ft elevation
  - Data products: Soil moisture map, orthomosaic map, NDVI map, thermal map
- **Technology**: Patented radiometer technology penetrates vegetation; accuracy validated against ground probe measurements

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Wildfire season monitoring**: Assessing surface soil moisture conditions during fire risk periods
- **Drought and flood response**: Water resource management during extreme hydrological events
- **Agriculture**: Optimize water use and minimize runoff
- **Construction**: Validate soil integrity for heavy construction projects
- **Golf course management**: Optimize water usage
- **Military operations**: Remotely determine soil integrity for operational planning
- **Watershed mapping**: Noted follow-on work with NOAA for mountain terrain mapping

## Key Results
- **Pepperwood pilot deployments**: 4 total deployments
- **Fixed-wing flights**: 8 flights with S2 UAS over 1.5 days
- **Coverage demonstrated**: Mapped 10 km² with soil moisture, thermal, NDVI, and EO (electro-optical) data
- **Validation**: Good correlation observed between ground probe measurements and UAS-based radiometer measurements
- **Follow-on work**: Flights planned in mountainous terrain at 10,000 ft altitude; NOAA watershed mapping initiative

## Notable Details
- Partnership framed around improving water measurement capabilities during critical periods (wildfire season, droughts, floods)
- Two UAS platform options offered: fixed-wing for coverage efficiency, multirotor for resolution/precision
- Patented L-band radiometer technology provides competitive advantage over conventional multispectral approaches
- Ground truth validation demonstrates scientific credibility for USGS applications
- Broad applicability across civilian and defense sectors
- USGS pilot study publicly documented (linked to California Water Science Center website)
