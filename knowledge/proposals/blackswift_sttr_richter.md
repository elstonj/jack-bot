# Black Swift Technologies STTR Letter of Support - Navy Hazardous Weather Program

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support (for STTR proposal)
- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy / Office of Naval Research (ONR)
- Program/Solicitation: NAVY STTR 25.A-T025 "Expendable Air-sea Profiling Observations in Hazardous Weather Conditions"
- Date: January 17, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 UAS (unmanned aircraft system)
- Key Personnel: Dr. David Richter (University of Notre Dame - author, not BST staff)

## Executive Summary
Dr. David Richter from the University of Notre Dame offers support for Black Swift Technologies' STTR proposal for expendable atmospheric profiling systems in hazardous weather. Richter endorses BST's S0 UAS based on its proven reliability in hurricane operations, data accuracy through rigorous calibration/validation, and cost efficiency compared to competitors, aligning with his ONR MURI program on air-sea coupling.

## Technical Approach
The proposed work involves:
- Enhanced data processing capabilities for the S0 UAS
- Integration of alternative wave measurement technologies
- Addressing icing challenges for cold/hazardous environment operations
- Addressing communication challenges for at-sea deployments
- Validation methodology: rigorous calibration and validation through instrumented towers and dropsondes from NOAA P-3 aircraft

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS (Unmanned Aircraft System)
- **What it is**: An expendable unmanned aircraft system designed for atmospheric profiling in hazardous weather conditions
- **Key capabilities**: 
  - Operates in turbulent/hazardous marine environments
  - Delivers superior wind measurement accuracy
  - Proven deployment record in hurricane observations
- **Performance attributes**: 
  - High data quality for air-sea interaction studies
  - Cost-effective design compared to competing platforms (Raytheon Coyote, Area-I/Anduril Altius)
  - Emphasis on simplicity and affordability enabling frequent, widespread deployment

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Hurricane observations** and tropical cyclone sampling
- **Air-sea interaction research**, specifically wind and turbulence measurements in high-wind conditions
- **Wave measurement** integration for hazardous weather conditions
- **Meteorological profiling** in cold/icing environments
- **Navy operational applications** in hazardous marine environments

## Supporting Research Partnership
