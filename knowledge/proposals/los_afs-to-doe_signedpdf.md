# Letter of Support: AtmoFacts to DOE for Undocumented Orphaned Well Characterization

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: U.S. Department of Energy, National Energy Technology Laboratory (NETL)
- Program/Solicitation: DE-FOA-0003343 - Undocumented Orphaned Well Characterization and Remediation
- Date: November 7, 2024
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Unmanned Aircraft Systems (UAS)
- Key Personnel: Stefan Metzger (AtmoFacts CEO/Founder)

## Executive Summary
AtmoFacts provides a letter of support for a collaborative proposal led by Southern Methodist University (SMU) with Black Swift Technologies as a subawardee. The project aims to develop comprehensive solutions for detecting and quantifying methane emissions from undocumented orphaned wells by integrating AtmoFacts' FluxMapper technology with BST's UAS platforms and other environmental monitoring equipment.

## Technical Approach
The collaboration integrates three complementary technologies:
1. **AtmoFacts FluxMapper™**: High-resolution eddy-covariance and remote sensing capabilities for methane leak detection and flux quantification
2. **Black Swift Technologies UAS**: Unmanned aircraft systems for deployment of monitoring sensors
3. **LI-COR flux towers**: Ground-based flux measurement infrastructure
4. **SMU methods**: Advanced environmental monitoring approaches

Combined, these systems aim to provide precision methane measurement and emissions quantification capabilities.

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **BST UAS**: Deployed to carry and position sensing equipment for methane detection; uncrewed aerial platform for environmental monitoring
- **AtmoFacts FluxMapper™**: High-resolution eddy-covariance and remote sensing system for methane leak detection and flux quantification

## Use Cases & Applications
- Detection and characterization of methane emissions from undocumented orphaned wells
- Methane flux quantification
- Emissions monitoring and mitigation support

- Addresses DOE priorities around environmental stewardship and greenhouse gas emission reduction
