## Executive Summary
Notes from a DARPA ERIS solicitation webinar capturing submission guidance and evaluation criteria. DARPA emphasized the importance of selling a company trajectory and value proposition rather than just a product, with a focus on authentic boundary-pushing innovation. Submissions take the form of 7-minute videos reviewed by the defense/intelligence community.

## Submission Format & Evaluation Criteria

**Video Requirements:**
- Exactly 7 minutes maximum (7:01+ will not be reviewed)
- Professional quality not required; iPhone video acceptable if content demonstrates planning and thoughtfulness
- Reviewable by anyone with .mil email address (buy-side decision makers)
- All submissions receive feedback within 30 days
- Videos can be resubmitted after incorporating feedback

**Heilmeier Framework Emphasis:**

1. **"What are you trying to do?"** - Communicate mission statement without jargon; sell the company trajectory, not just the product
2. **Value Proposition** - Position as disruption to existing market; articulate what "doesn't currently exist"
3. **Authenticity** - Push boundaries while understanding risk tolerance
4. **Trajectory Over Product** - DARPA is "buying a TRAJECTORY" not a "thing"
5. **Novel Future State** - Demonstrate "a life for this that doesn't currently exist"

**Technology Readiness Level (TRL):**
- TRL 4-6 preferred
- Earlier TRL favored over later-stage development
- Later TRL perceived as more constrained/defined than preferred

## Current Topic Areas (In Scope)
1. Detecting and tracking elusive objects of interest across air, land, and space domains
2. Overcoming limitations of current sensing systems (SWaP-C: size, weight, power, cost; performance)
3. Developing advanced antenna technologies for improved performance and reduced size

## Key Submission Strategy
- Bridge the "Valley of Death" through persistence
- Use feedback cycles to refine submissions
- Focus messaging on problem/opportunity rather than mature solution

## Notable Details
- Implicit acknowledgment of challenge in securing early-stage funding ("Valley of Death")
- Emphasis on authenticity suggests DARPA skeptical of over-polished or incremental pitches
- .mil audience gatekeeping means submissions must appeal to defense/intelligence procurement decision-makers
