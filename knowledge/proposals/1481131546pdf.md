## Executive Summary
The SuperSwift XT is a ruggedized modification of the SuperSwift airframe designed to operate in harsh volcanic environments. Key innovations include sealed airframe design for ash protection, a redundant airspeed measurement system, and enlarged control surfaces for high-altitude turbulence. The aircraft carries 5 lbs of payload with 1.5+ hour flight time capability and can operate at elevations up to 20,000 ft.

## Technical Approach

### Airframe Modifications
- **Ash Protection**: Motor encased in protective cowling that maintains cooling while preventing ash ingress
- **Hatching/Sealing**: Permanent gasket solution on control rod access hatches positioned away from airflow direction (replacing field tape solutions)
- **Control Surfaces**: Increased length and span of ailerons and flaps based on aerodynamic analysis for volcanic turbulence

### Airspeed Measurement Redundancy
- **Primary**: New large-opening (1/4 inch) pitot tube designed to prevent water droplet bridging in rain/wet conditions
- **Backup 1**: Multi-hole probe (also subject to clogging)
- **Backup 2**: Nephelometer - laser-based particle measurement sensor providing independent airspeed confirmation

### Propulsion & Configuration
- Electric-powered pusher configuration with V-tail design
- Motor cooling design critical to harsh environment operation

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift XT Aircraft
- **What it is**: Ruggedized modification of the original SuperSwift airframe (originally developed for soil moisture mapping)
- **Specifications**:
  - Payload capacity: 5 lbs
  - Flight endurance: 1.5+ hours
  - Operating altitude: Up to 20,000 ft
  - Configuration: Electric pusher with V-tail
- **Novel Features**:
  - Modular, field-swappable nose cone for scientific payloads
  - Sealed airframe design minimizing mass penalty
  - Multiple redundant airspeed sensors
  - Ruggedization for ash, moisture, and high-altitude turbulence

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Mission
- Volcanic sampling and ash collection in harsh volcanic environments
- Operations in mountainous terrain with difficult landing sites

- Wildlife research
- Wildfire detection and monitoring
- Weather monitoring
