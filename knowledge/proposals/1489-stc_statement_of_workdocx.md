## Executive Summary
This Statement of Work describes Dr. Bullock's role in defining science goals and instrumentation for a fixed-wing Venus atmospheric exploration mission. His contribution focuses on using advanced cloud microphysical modeling (CARMA) to optimize atmospheric sampling strategies and identify high-priority measurement regions in Venus' cloud layers.

## Technical Approach
- **Modeling Focus**: Use CARMA (state-of-the-art chemical microphysical model) to simulate Venus' cloud layers at meter-scale resolution
- **Model Inputs**: GCM (General Circulation Model) calculations to define model domain boundaries
- **Analysis Goals**: Identify vertical winds, cloud aerosol distributions, and chemical gradients in regions of scientific interest
- **Output Application**: Use modeling results to optimize sampling parameters (vertical, horizontal, and temporal) for fixed-wing platform operations
