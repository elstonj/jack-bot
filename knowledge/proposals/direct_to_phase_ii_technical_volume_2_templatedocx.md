## Executive Summary
This document is a DARPA-issued instruction and template guide for Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Direct to Phase II proposals. It provides detailed formatting requirements, page limits, and content guidance for two alternative submission formats: (1) the Standard Format with Feasibility Documentation and Technical Proposal, and (2) the White Paper & Slide Deck Format. It is not itself a BST proposal or project document.

## Document Purpose and Content Structure

This is an **official DARPA template and instruction document** that establishes requirements for Direct to Phase II submissions. It does not describe a specific Black Swift Technologies project, proposal, or capability.

### **Part I: Standard Format Requirements**

**Technical Volume Structure (Two Parts):**
- **Part One: Feasibility Documentation** (max pages per DARPA Instructions)
  - Documentation substantiating Phase I feasibility achievement
  - Technical reports, test data, prototype designs, performance results
  - Commercial applications and DoD insertion opportunity analysis
  - One-page Commercialization Potential summary (required)

- **Part Two: Technical Proposal** (max pages per topic)
  - Problem/Opportunity identification and significance
  - Phase II Technical Objectives
  - Phase II Statement of Work (detailed approach, schedule, deliverables)
  - Phase II Option Statement of Work (if applicable)
  - Related Work (state-of-art awareness, previous relevant projects)
  - Relationship with Future Research/Phase III commercialization
  - Key Personnel (resumes, education, experience, publications)
  - Foreign Citizens disclosure (if applicable)
  - Facilities/Equipment description
  - Subcontractors/Consultants (minimum 50% work by prime contractor)
  - Prior/Current/Pending Support disclosure
  - Commercialization Strategy (5-page limit, doesn't count toward page limit)
  - Technical Data Rights assertions

**Commercialization Strategy Requirements (5 pages, not counted in page limit):**
- Phase I transition/commercialization activities and Technology Readiness Level (TRL) achieved
- Problem/Need Statement (DoD and/or private sector relevance)
- Product(s) and System Application(s) description
- Business Model(s)/Procurement Mechanism(s)
- Target Market analysis and competitive landscape
- Funding Requirements and history
- Transition and Commercialization Risks and mitigation
- Team Expertise/Qualifications and Company Readiness
- Anticipated Transition/Commercialization Results (quantitative schedule)
- Optional: Advocacy Letters and Letters of Intent/Commitment

**Optional Submission Elements:**
- Advocacy letters (substantiating customer interest)
- Letters of Intent/Commitment (from commercial, DoD, or prime contractor partners)

### **Part II: White Paper & Slide Deck Format**

**White Paper (max 20 pages):**
- Goals and Impact
- Phase I Feasibility documentation
- Technical Plan (objectives, metrics, milestones at 1-month or less intervals)
- Management and Capabilities (key personnel, team expertise, IP, facilities, BSL requirements)
- Transition and Commercialization Plan

**Slide Deck (max 15 slides, converted to PDF):**
- Topic relevance and proposed deliverables
- Technology approach and competitive landscape
- Technical and commercial value proposition
- Technical and commercial risks
- Market analysis
- Cost, schedule, and milestones
- Management overview
- Technical Summary Quad Chart (using SBIR template)
- Commercialization Summary Quad Chart (using DARPA TCSP template)

## Formatting Requirements

**General Format:**
- Standard 8.5" x 11" paper, single-spaced, single column
- Times New Roman font, minimum 10-point (smaller font allowable only for headers, footers, tables, figures, graphics)
- One-inch margins on all sides
- All content counts toward page limit unless otherwise specified
- Proprietary data must be marked with disclosure legend

**File Submission (White Paper & Slide Deck Format):**
- Single PDF file combining white paper and slide deck
- Consecutive page numbering
- Header: company name, topic number, proposal number
- Virus scanned before upload
- No locked/encrypted files
- No embedded videos or moving media

## Supporting Documentation Requirements

**Volume 1 (Cover Sheet):** Completed in DSIP system
- Proposal Summary (duration, abstract, commercial application, keywords)
- Proposal Certifications (% of work calculated from budget)
- Contact Information (PI, Corporate Official, Authorized Contract Negotiator)

**Volume 3 (Cost Volume):** Excel template required
- Personnel costs (by name and hours)
- Equipment and materials (with justification and market research)
- Travel (justified and project-related)
- Subcontractor and consultant costs (detailed substantiation)
- All costs require documentation of derivation

**Volume 4 (Company Commercialization Report):** Required for Phase I and Direct to Phase II
- Reporting of funding outcomes from prior SBIR/STTR awards

**Volume 5 (Supporting Documents):** MANDATORY uploads
- Contractor Certification Regarding Prohibition on Certain Telecommunications/Video Surveillance Services
- Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations/Relationships
- Disclosure of Funding Sources
- DARPA Milestones Template
- Technical Data Rights Assertions (table and supporting documentation)
- Letters of recommendation, CVs, and supplemental materials

**Volume 6 (Fraud, Waste & Abuse Certification):** Required for Phase I and Direct to Phase II
- Annual FWA training review and certification

## Key Submission Rules

- **Minimum 50% of work** (by direct and indirect costs) must be performed by proposing small business firm (unless waiver approved by Contracting Officer)
- Federal Laboratories and FFRDCs may be used without waiver; use must be certified on Cover Sheet
- Universities may be subcontractors; must certify that they will not publicly release Export Controlled/ITAR restricted information
- Cost sharing is permitted but not required
- Phase II Option Statement of Work (if applicable per topic)
- All proposed costs must include substantiation (quotes, market research, vendor justification)

## Technical Data Rights (Class Deviation 2020-O0007)

- Contractor retains rights to technical data and software developed under the contract
- Government obtains royalty-free license for Government purposes only during period from contract award through 20 years after project completion
- During 20-year license period, Government may not release/disclose SBIR data except: (1) for evaluation purposes, (2) with contractor permission, or (3) for emergency repair/overhaul
- After 20-year period, Government has unlimited rights
- Proposers may assert restrictions using Class Deviation 2020-O0007 framework
- All assertions must be identified in proposal; contract cannot be awarded until assertions approved

## Submission Portal and Process

**Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP):**
- Online submission system for all DoD SBIR/STTR proposals
- Training materials and job aids available at https://www.dodsbirsttr.mil/submissions/learning-support/training-materials
- Registration required
- "Start New Proposal" workflow for Direct to Phase II submissions
- Sequential volume uploads (Volumes 1-6)

## Notable Details

- **Advocacy letters and Letters of Intent/Commitment** are optional; should only be submitted if substantiating commercialization claims; do not count against page limits
- **Government personnel letters** are NOT considered during evaluation per DoD 5500.7-R Joint Ethics Regulation
- **Privacy Act protection** applied to foreign national information
- **Embedded Entrepreneurship Initiative (EEI)** eligibility may be determined separately following SBIR award selection based on commercialization strategy content
- **Compliance with environmental laws** (federal, state, local) must be addressed for proposed facilities
- **Technology Readiness Level (TRL)** progression must be demonstrated and tracked
