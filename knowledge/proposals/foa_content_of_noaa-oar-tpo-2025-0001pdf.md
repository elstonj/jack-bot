## Executive Summary
This is NOAA's FY2025 Phase I Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) solicitation inviting small business concerns to submit research proposals addressing six research topics aligned with NOAA's mission. The program provides up to $190,000 for Phase I feasibility research (6-month period), with anticipated awards of up to 35 grants. Phase I awardees may advance to Phase II (~$690,000, 24 months) and Phase III (commercialization with non-SBIR funding).

## Program Structure

### Three-Phase Program
- **Phase I (Feasibility Research)**: Up to $190,000, 6-month period; ~35 awards anticipated
  - Focus: Determine scientific, technical, commercial merit and feasibility
  - Requirement: Minimum two-thirds of R&D/analytical effort performed by awardee
  - Constraint: No-cost extensions prohibit Phase II advancement if requested
  
- **Phase II (R&D/Prototype Development)**: Up to $690,000, 24-month period
  - For Phase I awardees demonstrating commercial potential
  - Phase II NOFO released ~30 days before Phase I period ends
  
- **Phase III (Commercialization)**: Non-SBIR funded follow-on work derived from or extending SBIR research

## Eligibility Requirements

### Applicant Qualifications
- Must qualify as Small Business Concern (SBC) at time of award per 13 CFR 121.701-121.705
- Principal Investigator (PI) must have primary employment (≥51% time) with SBC at time of award and during project conduct
- No full-time employment with other organizations allowed for PI
- PI replacement requires prior written NOAA Grants Officer approval
- R&D work must be performed in the United States (exemptions require prior written approval)
- Minimum two-thirds of Phase I research/analytical effort must be performed by awardee
- Total consultant fees, facility leases, usage fees, and subcontract/subaward agreements cannot exceed one-third of total award
- Only one Phase I application per applicant to this NOFO
- Venture capital operating companies (VCOCs), hedge funds, and private equity firms prohibited from participation
- Applicants with current SBA Phase I-to-Phase II Transition Rate assessment must meet Performance Benchmark requirements to be eligible

### Company Registry & Certifications
- Must register in SBA Company Registry prior to submission at https://app.www.sbir.gov/company-registration/overview
- Must save registration as .pdf and append to SF-424 form
- Certification of completion of DOC OIG mandatory Fraud, Waste, and Abuse training required
- SBIR Funding Agreement Certifications required at award and life-cycle

## Application Requirements

### Project Narrative (15-page limit, including references)
Pages 1-2: SBIR Cover Page (Appendix D fillable form)
Page 3: Project Abstract (one page, ≤200 words technical + ≤200 words commercial applications)
Pages 4-15: Technical Content with required sections:

1. **Identification and Significance of Problem/Opportunity**: Clear statement of research problem, innovativeness, commercial potential, importance, alignment with specific research topic and NOAA mission
2. **Phase I Technical Objectives**: Specific objectives and technical questions to determine feasibility
3. **Phase I Work Plan**: Detailed description of feasibility research, methods, locations, approach to achieve objectives
4. **Related R&R&D**: Previous significant efforts directly related, including prior PI/SBC research; awareness of key recent R&R&D by others
5. **Key Individuals and Bibliography**: Identify key Phase I personnel with relevant education, experience, publications (summaries preferred); no PII
6. **Relationship with Future R&R&D**: Phase I significance as foundation for Phase II; anticipated results if Phases I & II successful
7. **Facilities and Equipment**: Detailed description, availability, location of instrumentation and physical facilities
8. **Consultants, Contracts, and Sub-awards**: 
   - Clear identification of third-party research assistance
   - Letters of Commitment required from consultants, subawardees, subcontractors signed by appropriate official
   - Must match budget tables and narrative
   - Minimum two-thirds effort by awardee; outside involvement encouraged but not required
   - Total outside costs capped at one-third of award
   - Consultants/contractors cannot have had role in suggesting/developing/reviewing NOAA research topic or received non-public NOAA information
9. **Potential Commercial Application**: Detail commercial potential, commercialization approach, product/service use; include market opportunity, technology/competition analysis, financing strategy, non-SBIR funding commitments
10. **CRADA Relationship**: State if applicant is current CRADA partner (include agreement number/title); SBIR statement of work cannot overlap with existing CRADA
11. **Guest Researcher Status**: State if applicant or consultants/subcontractors are NOAA guest researchers (name sponsoring laboratory)
12. **Cost Sharing**: Not required; not evaluated; if included, describe in narrative only
13. **Similar Applications or Awards**: Disclose if identical/substantially equivalent applications under consideration or funded by other Federal agencies (required statement if none exists)
14. **Prior SBIR Phase II Awards**: If SBC received >15 Phase II awards in prior 5 fiscal years, must submit: awarding agency, award date, funding agreement number, amount, topic, follow-on agreement details, commercialization status (not counted toward 15-page limit)

## Special Research Considerations

### Human Subjects Research
- Must comply with Common Rule (15 C.F.R. Part 27)
- NOAA makes independent determination of whether research involves human subjects
- IRB approval from OHRP-registered IRB with valid FWA required before award
- NOAA generally does not fund foreign human subjects research (limited exceptions for pre-existing tissue/cells/data under specific criteria)
- Retroactive approvals not permitted
- Must provide: institution names, IRB names/numbers, FWA numbers, approval dates, estimated start dates if pending
- Embryonic stem cell lines must comply with NIH Guidelines; only NIH-approved lines funded
- Additional documentation may be requested: signed IRB-approved protocols, approval letters, special clearances documentation, protocol excerpts
- IRB education documentation required from Organizational Official for all IRB members and key personnel

### Live Vertebrate Animals Research
- Must comply with U.S. Government Principles for Utilization and Care of Vertebrate Animals
- Must meet Animal Welfare Act (7 U.S.C. § 2131 et seq.), AWA rules (9 C.F.R. Parts 1, 2, 3), and Good Laboratory Practice standards (21 C.F.R. Part 58) as appropriate
- NOAA makes independent determination of animal involvement
- IACUC approval required before award; no retroactive approvals permitted
- Must provide: institution names, IACUC assurance type/number, approval dates, estimated start dates if pending
- Additional documentation may include: signed IACUC-approved Animal Study Proposal (ASP), IACUC approval documentation, non-duplication-of-funding letters if multi-funded
