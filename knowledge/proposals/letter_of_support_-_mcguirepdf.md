# Letter of Support - McGuire

- Client/Agency: Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator (grant application)

## Executive Summary
Michael McGuire, a methane emissions monitoring expert with experience across multiple detection technology companies, provides a letter of support for Black Swift Technologies' Advanced Industries grant application. McGuire endorses BST's S2 Methane Leak Detection platform as a unique solution that fills a critical gap in autonomous, regional-scale emissions monitoring for Oil and Gas producers.

## Technical Approach
Not detailed in this letter, but McGuire acknowledges BST's S2 MLD as capable of autonomous detection, location, and quantification of emissions across regional assets.

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S2 Methane Leak Detection (S2 MLD) Platform**
- Proposed use: Autonomous detection, location, and quantification of methane emissions across multiple Oil and Gas regional assets
- Positioning: Addresses gap not filled by existing technologies (satellite, manned aircraft, rotary drones, OGI cameras)
- Market advantage: Can operate at scale in a timely and cost-effective manner across all regional assets autonomously

## Use Cases & Applications
- Oil and Gas emissions monitoring and detection
- Regional asset monitoring across multiple sites
- Compliance with Colorado emissions regulations (AIMM approval mentioned)

## Notable Details
- **Supporter's Background**: McGuire brings credibility from multiple angles:
  - Former research associate at CSU Energy Institute's METEC facility (ARPA-e funded methane research center)
  - VP of Operations at SeekOps Inc (competing methane detection technology company)
  - Co-founder of Field Sync Services (infrastructure services company)

- **Competitive Context**: McGuire explicitly contrasts S2 MLD against existing technologies:
  - Satellite: Region-wide scanning but only detects largest emitters
  - Manned aircraft: Hundreds of pads per flight but logistically challenging and expensive
  - Rotary drones & OGI cameras: Granular detection but single-site, labor-intensive
