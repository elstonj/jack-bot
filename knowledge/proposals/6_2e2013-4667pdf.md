## Executive Summary
This paper presents a dynamic data-driven hybrid sampling strategy for mapping coherent atmospheric structures (plumes, thermals, clouds, storms) using small unmanned aircraft systems (UAS). The approach combines remote sensing-based tracking with adaptive in-situ sampling using multiple coordinated aircraft, employing Scale-Cascaded Field Alignment (SCA) for tracking and Gaussian Process/MCMC methods for adaptive mapping. Real-world experiments demonstrate the technique's viability for plume mapping applications.

## Technical Approach

### Core Strategy: Two Feedback Loops
1. **Tracking Loop (Slow Time-Manifold)**: Remote observations of coherent structures are tracked using an improved Scale-Cascaded Field Alignment (SCA) algorithm that handles complex life-cycle events (merges, splits, births, deaths).
2. **Adaptive Mapping Loop (Fast Time-Manifold)**: In-situ measurements from UAS following adaptive sampling plans are analyzed to produce maps (spatial analyses), which then adapt future sampling plans.

### Key Technical Components

**Tracking (Scale-Cascaded Field Alignment - SCA)**
- Uses deformation fields expressed as weighted sums of turbulent motion basis components: q = Σᵢ wᵢqᵢ
- Weights (wᵢ) estimated recursively in Bayesian filtering formulation
- Improved algorithm handles events classical methods cannot:
  - Storm cell merges and splits
  - Birth/death of new structures
  - Vertical motion

**Improved SCA Algorithm for Complex Events**:
1. Segment organizing fields (e.g., pressure) using Otsu's method
2. Link basin centers between frames using probabilistic correspondence
3. Align and transport vorticity fields with SCA
4. Propagate storm labels through time using deformation vectors
5. For unlabeled segments, construct probability distribution from shared organizing field labels
6. Inherit labels from parent organizing field

**Adaptive Mapping**
- Uses **Gaussian Process (Kriging) model** for spatial reconstruction
- Employs **Markov-Chain Monte-Carlo (MCMC)** for adaptive sampling plan generation
- Bayesian formulation: P(πₙ₊₁|Xₙ₊₁) ∝ P(Xₙ₊₁|πₙ₊₁)P(πₙ₊₁)
- Metropolis-Hastings variant to sample from posterior distribution of sampling plans
- Balances exploration vs. exploitation through proposal distribution acceptance

### Remote Sensing Component
- Small UAS equipped with specialized imaging optics to detect atmospheric phenomena
- Example: UV imaging to detect SO₂ absorption around 307 nm for volcanic plumes
- Dense remote-sensed spatial fields provide context for tracking without requiring full numerical weather models

## Products & Capabilities Described

### UAS Platforms Used
- **Multiplex Merlin**: Small fixed-wing UAS with modified autopilots
- **Skywalker X8**: Small fixed-wing UAS with kevlar wing modifications, rudders, brakes, robust vertical tail

**Performance Specifications**:
- Altitude capability: 3,700 m ASL
- Range: 5 km
- Endurance: 1 hour
- Top speed: 30 m/s

**Operational Capabilities**:
- Autonomous flight via MavLink Ground Control Station (256 kbps digital modem)
- First Person Video (FPV) flight capability
- Line-of-sight RC radio operation with Pilot-in-Command
- Provably safe finite-state machine autopilot control
- Autonomous mode with command and guidance from planner

### Data Processing & Analysis
- **Heteroskedastic Gaussian Process reconstruction** with scale-parametrized monotone basis functions
- **Vertical kinetic energy estimation** from aircraft glideslope variations
- **Non-negative matrix factorization** for dominant mode extraction
- **Kernel estimation** from sparse measurements

## Use Cases & Applications

### Demonstrated Applications
1. **Volcanic Plume Mapping**: 
   - Kilauea volcano's Halema'uma'u crater experiment
   - SO₂ detection and tracking
   - Vertical kinetic energy estimation

2. **Smokestack Plume Studies**:
   - Queretaro, Mexico experiment over 80m smokestack
   - Vertical kinetic energy reconstruction at single horizontal level
   - Plume localization and cross-section measurement

3. **Storm System Tracking**:
   - Winter storm tracking over western Europe (WRF simulations)
   - Multi-cell storm system organization tracking
   - Vorticity-based storm mapping

## Key Results

### Real-World Experiment Results

**Kilauea Plume Experiment**:
- Successfully detected SO₂ plume from small UAS using UV imaging
- Continuous plume tracking demonstrated

**Queretaro Smokestack Experiment #1**:
- Successfully localized plume with small UAS
- Reconstructed vertical kinetic energy
- Identified plume localization: ~40m cross-section, ~50m away from chimney, ~30m above source

**Queretaro Smokestack Experiment #2**:
- Adaptive sampling successfully implemented
- Initial radial spoke pattern (uninformative prior)
- Gaussian Process reconstruction clearly resolved plume at sampled level
- Adapted sampling paths localized to plume region
- Radial distribution of vertical kinetic energy extracted
- **Key finding**: Maximum kinetic energy ~50m from chimney
- **Validation**: Reconstruction identified two vents, verified by resident meteorologists

### Algorithm Validation
- Winter storm tracking algorithm validated against human estimates for track consistency and storm counts
- SCA improvements successfully handled storm cell merges, splits, and birth/death events
- Tracking labels propagated consistently through complex life-cycle events

## Notable Details

### Methodological Advantages
- **Hybrid approach avoids pitfalls of pure Eulerian sampling**: Dense Eulerian sampling shown impractical (example: mapping 1×1×1 km cloud at 100×100×50m resolution requires 6 aircraft; at 50×50×25m resolution requires 48 aircraft)
- **Improves on Lagrangian sampling**: Can handle flow barriers and complex circulations
- **Nowcasting alternative**: More accurate than full numerical models for <2hr predictions; faster to simulate

### Computational Scalability
- MCMC approach computationally scalable
- Scales effectively with increased number of UAS platforms
- Can incorporate fixed assets (NWP, satellite, radar, in-situ measurements)

### Innovations
- First proposal to map coherent structures using this dynamic data-driven hybrid approach (per authors)
- Improved SCA tracking using organizing field context (pressure basins, etc.)
- Integration of remote sensing, tracking, and adaptive sampling in closed feedback loops

### Operational Expertise
- Authors announce ability to operate multiple aircraft autonomously for various atmospheric tasks
- Established test site in Queretaro, Mexico
- Preparations underway for flights at Popocatepetl volcano (Mexico)

### Supporting Theory
- Bayesian approach to handling position/shape errors (not just amplitude errors) in coherent structure inference
- Field Alignment and Coalescence algorithms for data assimilation and uncertainty quantification
- Parsimony principle allows regularization through sparse observations
