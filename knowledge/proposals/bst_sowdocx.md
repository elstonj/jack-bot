- Type: Statement of Work (SOW)
- Client/Agency: University of Colorado / CIRES (Cooperative Institute for Research in Environmental Sciences)

- Date: Created 2020-11-06; Last modified 2022-01-11
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 aircraft, Lobe Differencing Correlation L-band Radiometer (LDCR), Multihole Probe
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
BST was contracted to deploy its S2 UAS equipped with soil moisture and vegetation mapping sensors to the East River Basin in Colorado as part of the SPLASH initiative. The aircraft would conduct repeated mapping flights between May and August across a ~10 km² study area to monitor evolving soil moisture conditions and provide data to NOAA's National Weather Service and regional forecast centers.

## Technical Approach
- Deploy S2 aircraft with two primary payloads:
  - **Lobe Differencing Correlation L-band Radiometer (LDCR)**: primary sensor for soil moisture mapping
  - **Surface imaging sensors**: vegetation monitoring and NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) data collection
  - **Multihole Probe** (optional but desirable): wind speed, direction, and thermodynamic measurements to characterize atmospheric properties during flights
- Establish rapid data processing pipeline to deliver results within 1-2 days of flight
- Conduct repeated flights over in-situ soil moisture sensor networks for validation and uncertainty characterization
- Operate over upper East River Basin, primarily north and southeast of Rocky Mountain Biological Laboratory

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 Aircraft
- Multi-sensor capable UAS platform
- Equipped with LDCR radiometer for L-band soil moisture sensing
- Capable of carrying surface imaging sensors for vegetation mapping
- Compatible with environmental characterization instrumentation (Multihole Probe)

### LDCR (Lobe Differencing Correlation L-band Radiometer)
- Primary instrument for soil moisture mapping
- L-band passive microwave sensor
- Deliverable output: gridded soil moisture data files

### Surface Imaging Sensors
- Purpose: vegetation monitoring and NDVI mapping
- Deliverable output: geo-located NDVI maps

### Multihole Probe
- Wind and thermodynamic measurement capability
- Provides atmospheric characterization data during mapping flights
- Optional but recommended for campaign

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Hydrometeorology monitoring**: Study of precipitation and lower atmosphere properties in Colorado mountain basin
- **Soil moisture mapping**: Quantify spatial and temporal evolution of soil moisture during snowmelt and summer seasons
- **Vegetation monitoring**: Track NDVI changes over the study period
- **Weather forecasting support**: Data delivery to National Weather Service Colorado River Basin Forecast Center (CRBFC) and Grand Junction Weather Forecast Office (GJT WFO) to improve regional forecasting
- **Sensor validation**: Coordinate with NOAA Physical Sciences Laboratory in-situ soil moisture sensor deployment for retrieval algorithm validation and uncertainty characterization

## Geographic & Temporal Scope
- **Study Area**: East River Basin (ERB), Colorado, north of Crested Butte; approximately 10 km² focus area
- **Deployment Period**: Year 1: Four 1-week field campaigns; Year 2: Three 1-week field campaigns
- **Campaign Window**: May through August (snowmelt through late summer)
- **Flight Focus**: Upper East River Basin, primarily north and southeast of Rocky Mountain Biological Laboratory

## BST Responsibilities
1. Pre-campaign aircraft and payload preparation and testing
2. Development and validation of data processing pipeline for rapid delivery (1-2 day turnaround)
3. Field team deployment with aircraft operators and support equipment
4. Real-time and post-campaign data processing
5. Delivery of gridded soil moisture data files
6. Delivery of geo-located NDVI maps
7. Activity reporting to CU and NOAA sponsors

## Notable Details
- Partnership with NOAA Physical Sciences Laboratory for coordinated in-situ soil moisture measurements
- Data delivery to multiple National Weather Service offices for operational forecasting use
- Emphasis on rapid processing timeline to support near-real-time operational applications
- Multi-year commitment: 7 total field campaigns across 2 years
- Close coordination required with CIRES and primary sponsor (NOAA) for reporting and validation activities
