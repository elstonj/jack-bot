## Executive Summary
This is a master services agreement between University of Miami and Black Swift Technologies establishing the legal framework for BST to provide services to the University. The agreement covers a 2-year initial term with provisions for renewal, sets liability and insurance requirements, and includes standard compliance, confidentiality, and intellectual property provisions. ## Key Terms & Conditions

**Term & Termination:**
- Initial term: 2 years from effective date
- Renewable for consecutive 1-year terms by mutual written agreement
- University can terminate for material breach (20 days' notice to cure) or immediately for insolvency, bankruptcy, confidentiality breaches
- Either party may terminate immediately if Force Majeure Event exceeds 90 days

**Insurance Requirements:**
- Commercial General Liability: $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate, naming University as additional insured
- Workers' Compensation: Statutorily prescribed amounts
- All policies must include waiver of subrogation in favor of University of Miami
- Evidence of coverage required before execution

**Reporting:**
- Services reported to: Jun Zhang
- University has right to examine all pertinent records for 3 years after final payment

**Compliance Requirements:**
- FCPA (Foreign Corrupt Practices Act)
- ITAR/EAR (export control regulations)
- FERPA (education records privacy)
- GDPR (if processing personal data)
- HIPAA (if handling protected health information)
- Title IX (sexual misconduct reporting)
- Non-discrimination and diversity principles
- University policies and protocols

**Confidentiality:**
- BST must protect all University confidential information to commercially acceptable standards
- Must report unauthorized use/disclosure within 5 business days
- Obligation survives agreement termination
- Exception: University may share pricing/purchase data with GPO and benchmarking providers

**Intellectual Property:**
- Indemnification clause covers BST liability for infringement of patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, or other IP rights

**Conflict of Interest:**
- BST required to disclose any University employees/faculty receiving compensation from BST or holding ownership interests
- Failure to accurately report constitutes material breach allowing immediate termination

## Fees & Payment
- Payment upon submission of detailed invoice with taxpayer ID
- Original receipts required, subject to University approval
- Reporting requirements must be met before payment
- Additional services require advance written approval
- Compensation changes require mutual written agreement

## Notable Details

**Address:**
- BST principal place of business: 2840 Wilderness Place, Suite D, Boulder, CO 80301

**Governing Law:**
- State of Florida law without conflict of law provisions
- Exclusive jurisdiction: State and federal courts in Miami-Dade County, Florida

**Subcontracting:**
- Any subcontracting requires advance written University approval
- Subcontracts worth $10,000+ over 12 months require access clauses for federal auditing

**Record Access:**
- University, U.S. Secretary of Health and Human Services, and U.S. Comptroller General have 4-year post-termination access to records

**Independent Contractor Status:**
- BST is independent contractor; no employee/employer relationship created

**Force Majeure:**
- Includes hurricanes (specifically mentioned as potentially requiring evacuation near University premises), natural disasters, war, government actions, embargoes, public health emergencies, strikes, and other exceptional events

**Conflict of Interest Disclosures (Appendix B):**
- BST must disclose any University employees receiving compensation exceeding $75 annually
- BST must disclose any University employees with direct or indirect ownership interest
