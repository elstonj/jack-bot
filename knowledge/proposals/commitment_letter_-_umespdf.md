# Commitment Letter - UMES

## Document Metadata
- Type: Commitment letter
- Client/Agency: University of Maryland Eastern Shore (UMES)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR contract NNX14CG09C; CCRPP proposal (Commercialization, Collaboration, and Regional Partnership Program)
- Date: 2017 (letter file modified 2017-03-31)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Soil Moisture Mapping (SMM) sUAS, MiniSwift, SuperSwift
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (BST CEO), Chris Hartman (UMES Aviation Science Program Coordinator)

## Executive Summary
UMES commits $15,000 in matching funding to support the continued development and commercialization of Black Swift Technologies' Soil Moisture Mapping (SMM) sUAS system under NASA SBIR contract NNX14CG09C. The commitment focuses on training and field testing activities designed to enhance the system's commercial viability and market readiness.

## Technical Approach
The project leverages a tiered approach using two BST platforms:
1. **MiniSwift** - A smaller sUAS designated as a training platform for UMES personnel; capable of collecting photogrammetric data
2. **SuperSwift** - The primary operational system for soil moisture mapping; targeted for deployment in advanced field conditions

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Soil Moisture Mapping (SMM) sUAS
- **Purpose**: Measure soil moisture from aerial platforms
- **Application Context**: Agricultural productivity enhancement; commercial product development
- **Proposed Testing**: Includes sub-canopy soil moisture measurement in corn (a challenging crop due to high plant density)
- **Status**: Under development with commercialization focus

### MiniSwift
- **Type**: Smaller sUAS platform
- **Intended Use**: Training and personnel development; photogrammetric data collection
- **Deployment**: To be provided to UMES for summer 2017 operation and continuous use

### SuperSwift
- **Type**: Larger, primary SMM platform
- **Intended Use**: Operational soil moisture mapping missions
- **Planned Deployment**: UMES test farm in 2018

## Use Cases & Applications

1. **Agricultural Soil Moisture Monitoring** - Primary commercial application to enhance agricultural productivity
2. **Corn Crop Assessment** - Critical test case for sub-canopy soil moisture measurement; corn identified as a difficult but important market opportunity due to plant density challenges
3. **Training & Operational Capacity Building** - Development of trained UMES personnel as end-users and potential regional operators

## Key Commitments
- **UMES Funding**: $15,000 direct contribution to BST
- **Funding Use**: Commercialization enhancements; training delivery; field testing
- **Timeline**: MiniSwift training flights summer 2017; SuperSwift deployment at UMES test farm 2018

## Notable Details
- UMES identifies the corn-growing viability assessment as a "key factor to determine if measuring soil moisture in corn is viable and a market that can be pursued in earnest"
- The commitment represents institutional partnership combining NASA matching funds, CCRPP support, and UMES contribution
- Project explicitly framed as moving technology from development to commercial product stage
- Training component deemed "important activity and capability to bring the project to market"
