## Executive Summary
This document is guidance from the Navy SBIR/STTR PMO to small businesses preparing full Phase II proposals. It identifies common submission issues and provides specific hints and tips to minimize back-and-forth corrections, accelerate cost negotiations, and reduce time to award. The guidance covers templates, compliance, deliverables, cost structure, and administrative requirements.

## Notable Details

### Formatting & Templates
- Technical volume must adhere to provided formatting/numbering structure
- Cost volume must use Navy's Phase II Cost Volume template exclusively; no alternative templates accepted
- Templates available at: https://www.navysbir.com/links_forms.htm

### ITAR/EAR Restrictions
- Question 7 (SBIR) or 8 (STTR) on Volume I Proposal Certification in DSIP asks about ITAR/EAR data
- Check original topic description in BAA for ITAR/EAR-restricted status
- Contractor's DSIP response must match Government's understanding; resolve discrepancies with TPOC

### Deliverables Section
- Must not be deleted from Volume II Technical Proposal
- Must list all items produced and delivered throughout effort (progress reports, test reports, manuals, etc.)
- Must include tangible/productized deliverable (prototype/hardware/software)

### Government Participation (GFP/GFI)
- If none requested, write "No GFP, GFI or Facilities/Personnel requested at this time"
- If resources needed, clearly articulate what is needed and why
- Must explain necessity of resources

### Security Classification
- Section must remain in Volume II even if work is unclassified
- Must identify whether work anticipated to be classified or unclassified
- Consult TPOC if uncertain

### Data Rights Assertions
- Question 11 (SBIR) or 12 (STTR) on Volume I asks about DFARS 252.227-7017 assertions
- If "yes" answered, corresponding assertions table required in Technical Proposal
- **No need to assert rights on data from previous contracts under same topic** or data developed during upcoming Phase II
- Assert rights only on technical data from external contracts or privately funded development
- Descriptions must clearly distinguish pre-contract data from execution-phase development
- If answering "no," write "none" rather than deleting section

### Equipment
- Complete Equipment tab in Cost Proposal with maximum detail
- Must identify equipment type
- **No fee applied to Plant Equipment (PE) or General Purpose Equipment (GPE)/Contractor Acquired Property (CAP)**
- Must complete all table sections: description, vendor/source, country of origin, basis of estimate
- If CAP proposed, **Property Management System Approval (PMSA) Letter from DCMA required**
- If PMSA letter doesn't exist, contact cognizant DCMA office and provide confirmation to NAVAIR contracting officer (Dina Marinelli: dina.m.marinelli.civ@us.navy.mil)
- DCMA office locator: https://www.dcma.mil/DCMA-Pricing-Support/

### TABA (Technical and Business Assistance)
- **Must be included within NTE amount, not in addition to it**
- Maximum $25,000 per Phase II award; $50,000 per project (all phases combined)
- Reviewed and approved by DON SBIR/STTR Program Office
- Final report with TABA report required: http://www.navysbirprogram.com/navydeliverables/

**TABA Requirements if proposed:**
- TABA provider firm name
- Provider point of contact, email, phone
- Explanation of provider's unique qualifications
- Detailed tasks to be performed (purpose/objective)
- Total cost, hours, labor rates (blended rate acceptable)

**TABA Prohibitions:**
- No profit/fee by SBIR proposer
- Cannot be the SBIR proposer
- Cannot be affiliate of SBIR proposer
- Cannot be investor of SBIR proposer
- Cannot be subcontractor/consultant otherwise required for paid research effort

**TABA Consequences:**
- If awarded TABA, firm eliminated from DON SBIR/STTR Transition Program (STP), Forum for SBIR/STTR Transition (FST), and other Phase II assistance
- If NOT awarded TABA, must participate in virtual DON STP Kickoff in first or second year (up to full day, no travel costs)
- **Failure to include TABA details in Volume 5 results in disapproval**

### Period of Performance (PoP)
- Standard: 30-month base + 12-month option
- Deviations require written justification from Government TPOC
- Base and option periods can overlap
- **Goal: minimize no-cost extensions**—propose sufficient time to complete base and option tasking
- All PoP dates must match across technical volume sections
- Cost volume: break base and option into yearly increments (calendar or fiscal years)
- Year-to-year breakdown must cover entire PoP and show obvious labor rate escalation

### Teaming Partners/Subcontractors
- Question 13 (SBIR) or 14 (STTR) on Volume I asks about teaming/subcontractors
- Answer "yes" only if subcontractors proposed; consultants alone don't require "yes"
- Complete consultant and subcontractor tabs in cost volume with maximum detail
- Volume 5 must include competitive quotes or agreement letters for subcontractors/consultants with agreed-upon rates/costs for **this specific** Phase II effort
- Do not rely on past history or old estimates

### Labor Category
- All key personnel must be in cost volume with labor category, rate, and hours
- PI labor category: "Principal Investigator"

### Human/Animal Testing
- Research involving human, animal, or recombinant DNA must be proposed for **Option period only**
- If proposed during base period, contract cannot be awarded until review board approvals completed
- **This may significantly delay contract award**

### Contract Type & Fee
- NAVAIR generally awards Phase II as **Cost-Plus-Fixed-Fee (CPFF)**
- **Must include fee in cost volume**
- Firm Fixed Price (FFP) requires justification; CPFF more appropriate for R&D due to uncertainty and flexibility
- CPFF shifts financial risk to government, allows funding increases based on transition potential

### Labor Escalation
- If proposing escalation, "Labor&Ind Rates" tab must be thoroughly completed
- For 42-month PoP, expect **minimum 3 escalation instances** across 3+ calendar/fiscal years
- Year-to-year breakdown required so escalation can be verified
- While not required, escalation is typical and expected
- If no escalation proposed, Government will request confirmation this was intentional

### Materials/Vendor Quotes
- **Strongly recommend domestic materials and vendors** per SBIR/STTR Policy Directive and Buy American clause
- Foreign vendors/materials: if proposed, must identify domestic source or provide justification if domestic unavailable or cost-prohibitive
- Foreign materials require funding agreement officer review/approval—takes additional time, approval not guaranteed
- **Basis of estimate required for all materials, supplies, equipment**
- Provide support documentation in Volume 5: vendor quotes, correspondence, website screenshots
- Complete Materials-Supplies tab with detail: country of origin, reference document numbers, unit/total price
- Arrange quotes in order they appear on table if possible
