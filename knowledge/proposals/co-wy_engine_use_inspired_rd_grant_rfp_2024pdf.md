# CO-WY Engine Use-Inspired R&D Grant RFP 2024

## Document Metadata
- Type: Request for Proposals (RFP) / Program Guidelines
- Client/Agency: Colorado-Wyoming Climate Resilience Engine (CO-WY Engine), funded by National Science Foundation (NSF)
- Program/Solicitation: NSF Regional Innovation Engines initiative; CO-WY Engine Use-Inspired R&D Grant Program
- Date: 2024 (RFP created/modified August 2, 2024)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None mentioned
- Key Personnel: Mark Gorham (contact)

## Executive Summary
The CO-WY Engine, an NSF Regional Innovation Engines awardee, is issuing a call for use-inspired research and development proposals in climate resilience and community resiliency across Colorado and Wyoming. The program seeks to fund innovative data, technology, or practice solutions in designated focus areas, with up to $300K per project and anticipated 1:1 matching requirement from project teams.

## Program Overview
**Funding Source & Scope:**
- CO-WY Engine has received $15M with potential for up to $160M in NSF funding over 10 years
- Focus on translating environmental monitoring technologies and predictive analytics into decision support systems for climate change mitigation
- Regional collaboration across diverse sectors: small businesses, industry, higher education, national labs, NGOs, government, tribal nations

**Four Core Goals:**
1. Enhance R&D pathway for climate resilience solutions
2. Bridge funding/resource gaps for accelerated R&D
3. Support growth of R&D climate innovation ecosystem
4. Foster inclusivity, particularly for underserved populations and tribal nations

## Funding Focus Areas (2024 Cycle)
Six approved challenge/solution areas eligible for funding:
1. Complex Earth Sensing
2. Soil Carbon Capture Data & Analytics
3. Methane Emissions Analysis
4. Extreme Weather Modeling
5. Wildfire Risk & Prediction
6. Water Availability Prediction

## Funding Details
**Available Resources:**
- Anticipated funding: $300,000 per project (direct + indirect costs)
- Anticipated awards: 4 projects per cycle
- Project period: 12 calendar months with possible 6-month no-cost extension (not guaranteed)
- Matched/leveraged resource requirement: 1:1 ratio (Engine to non-Engine funding) with exceptions for tribal/underserved community projects and higher expectations for corporate partners

**Application Cycles:**
- First cycle: Opens 07/22/2024; Due 09/03/2024 at 5:00 PM MT
- Second cycle (if budget available): Expected to open 01/15/2025; Due 02/28/2025 at 5:00 PM MT
- Selection notifications: 4-6 weeks after deadline
- Project start: ~10 weeks after deadline

## Eligibility Requirements

**Lead Institution (required):**
Must be one of seven Colorado and Wyoming institutions of higher education:
- Colorado School of Mines
- Colorado State University
- Metro State University
- University of Colorado Boulder
- University of Colorado Denver
- University of Northern Colorado
- University of Wyoming

**Principal Investigator (PI):**
- Must be tenure-track or non-tenure-track faculty member, research scientist/scholar, center/institute director, dean, department head, or equivalent

**Team Composition:**
- One PI per project (though PI may serve in other roles on other projects)
- No limit on proposals per organization
- Project team members may come from any discipline
- Cross-sector partnerships strongly encouraged (private entities, universities, federal labs, NGOs)
- All subaward partners must meet NSF eligibility and be able to receive federal funding

**Eligible Costs:**
- Senior personnel, postdoctoral fellows, graduate and undergraduate students
- Equipment advancing R&D capabilities
- Domestic travel
- Materials and supplies
- Consultant services
- Other direct costs and indirect costs (using federally-negotiated rates or 10% de minimis MTDC for partners without agreements)
- Unspent funds must be returned to CO-WY Engine at project end

## Leveraged/Matching Resources
Examples of acceptable matching support:
- Cash or in-kind contributions (volunteered time, donated equipment, lab/office space, datasets, industrial testbeds)
- Collaborations and partnerships (local government, nonprofits, industry)
- Leveraging existing programs and initiatives
- Integration with existing research and data
- Innovative technology utilization
- Internal organizational capacity building
- Public-private partnerships
- Community assets (libraries, schools, community centers)
- Non-federal award stacking

## Evaluation Criteria
Reviewers (CO-WY Engine team + external technical, academic, business experts) will assess:

1. **Technical Viability:** Novelty of R&D products, likelihood of success, approach to market/industry/community-defined need, competitive advantages, IP strategy strength
2. **Project Viability:** Appropriateness of scope, project plan, milestones, and budget
3. **Translation Potential:** Likelihood of translational outcomes (startup, industry/community partnerships)
4. **Team Readiness:** Strength of qualifications, management structure, execution record
5. **Engagement and Partnerships:** Inclusivity of engagement, strength and breadth of partnerships, matched/leveraged resources
6. **Potential Impact:** Potential for broad economic and community impacts to climate resiliency

## Accountability & Reporting
- Awardees must participate in entrepreneurship programs if anticipating startup development
- Regular Engine convenings required to support use-inspired research teams
- Progress updates and evaluations every 6 months (brief)
- Six-month and final reports required, including:
  - Summary of activities and impact evaluation
  - Outcomes assessment (metrics, timelines, current/projected outcomes)
  - Expenditure summary
- Unspent funds returned to Engine at project end
- Eligible for consideration for follow-on funding through Engine's Translation Grant Program based on demonstrated progress

## Notable Details

**Alignment with Climate Tech Economy:**
The program explicitly frames R&D in service of climate technology commercialization and workforce development, not basic research discovery.

**Inclusivity Focus:**
Strong emphasis on diversity, equity, inclusion, and accessibility throughout—from team composition to stakeholder engagement to impact on marginalized/tribal communities. Less than 1:1 matching allowed for projects serving socially/economically disadvantaged partners.

**Regional Ecosystem Building:**
Part of broader NSF initiative to build 10 regional innovation hubs; positioning Colorado-Wyoming as significant player in climate tech innovation nationally and globally.

**Follow-on Funding Pathway:**
Explicitly designed as stepping stone to Engine's Translation Grant Program; successful projects can be fast-tracked for additional support to commercialize or scale solutions.
