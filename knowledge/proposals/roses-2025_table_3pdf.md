# ROSES-2025 Table 3: Solicited Research Programs

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Reference/Index Document
- **Agency:** NASA
- **Program/Solicitation:** ROSES-2025 (Research Opportunities in Space and Earth Sciences)
- **Date:** February 21, 2025
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None
- **Key Personnel:** None named

## Executive Summary
This is a comprehensive index table of NASA's FY2025 research solicitations organized by scientific division (Earth Science, Heliophysics, Planetary Science, Astrophysics, Biological and Physical Sciences, and Cross-Division). It lists 120+ individual program opportunities with their Notice of Intent (NOI) and proposal due dates, indicating which programs are active this year and which are not being solicited.

## Document Content Overview
This document is purely administrative/reference material—a deadline calendar and program listing for NASA's major solicitations. It contains:

- **Section A (Earth Science):** 70 programs covering land cover/use, oceanography, atmospheric composition, cryospheric science, hydrology, remote sensing, disaster monitoring, and applications
- **Section B (Heliophysics):** 18 programs addressing solar physics, space weather, and heliospheric research
- **Section C (Planetary Science):** 25 programs for solar system exploration, lunar science, exobiology, and planetary protection
- **Section D (Astrophysics):** 18 programs including general investigator cycles for space telescopes (Swift, Fermi, NuSTAR, TESS, NICER, IXPE, XRISM)
- **Section E (Biological and Physical Sciences):** 4 programs for microgravity research
- **Section F (Cross-Division):** 20 programs for exoplanets, citizen science, lunar payloads, and STEM initiatives

## Relevance to Black Swift Technologies
**This document has no direct relevance to BST.** It is a NASA administrative scheduling document. However, it may be useful context for identifying potential NASA funding opportunities that could align with BST's airborne sensor capabilities (e.g., Earth Venture Suborbital programs, atmospheric composition missions, tropical weather observation).

## Notable Details
- Programs marked "Not Solicited This Year" are inactive for FY2025
- Programs with "TBD" dates have not yet published specific deadlines
- Programs with "No Due Date" can receive proposals continuously until March 31, 2026
- Two-step review processes use either NOI/Step-1 or NOI/Step-2 structures
- Some programs have mandatory Notices of Intent (marked as such)
