# Advancing Wildfire and Prescribed Fire Forecasting and Assessment Tools

## Document Metadata
- Type: Solicitation/Call for Proposals
- Client/Agency: Department of Defense (DoD) via ESTCP (Environmental Security Technology Certification Program)
- Program/Solicitation: ESTCP FY 2026, Natural Hazards Adaptation Program Area
- Date: Released January 7, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None explicitly mentioned
- Key Personnel: J. Kevin Hiers (ESTCP Program Manager for Natural Hazards Adaptation)

## Executive Summary
This is a DoD ESTCP solicitation seeking demonstrations of tools that forecast wildfire activities and prescribed fire opportunities at military installation scales for 1-10 year time horizons. The goal is to improve wildfire risk management and optimize prescribed burn planning by incorporating weather-to-climate forecasting data with high-resolution fuel and vegetation inputs.

## Technical Approach
Proposed solutions should:
- Incorporate fire environmental conditions from weather-to-climate forecasting models to assess wildfire exposure and identify prescribed burn windows
- Demonstrate forecasting capability for extreme fire weather conditions (drought, excessive heat, extreme energy release components) at 1-10 year horizons
- Validate modeling accuracy against historical fire activities
- Accurately model prescribed burning windows using 1-10 year weather-to-climate forecasting tools
- Incorporate high-resolution fuel and vegetation condition data at least annually to update future hazard forecasts
- Operate at DoD installation-relevant spatial scales
- Show dynamic skill in projecting fire activities over the "weather-to-climate continuum"

## Use Cases & Applications
- Wildfire risk reduction and management at DoD installations
- Prescribed fire opportunity forecasting and optimization
- Budget and resource planning for fire management (1-10 year planning horizons)
- Military mission planning considering fire risk exposure
- Habitat management for threatened and endangered species (fire-dependent species)
- Ordnance-caused wildfire reduction planning

## Key Context
- DoD applies approximately 800,000 acres of prescribed fire annually across all military Services
- Prescribed fire is the primary treatment for wildfire risk reduction on DoD lands
- Current wildfire risk assessment tools lack validation at installation scales and show limited skill at 1-10 year forecasting horizons
- Many existing tools are static or infrequently updated for fuel/vegetation data
- Emerging weather-to-climate forecasting and AI approaches offer new potential solutions

## Notable Details
- Emphasis on interannual forecasting capability—a gap in current operational tools
- Integration of multiple data streams (weather, climate, fuel conditions, vegetation dynamics)
- Focus on actionable, operationally relevant assessments for resource managers
- Recognition that emerging AI and advanced forecasting methods warrant evaluation for this application
- Program manages significant acreage (800,000 acres/year prescribed fire) requiring improved planning tools
