## Executive Summary
This is a USDA NIFA Request for Applications soliciting Phase I proposals for the Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) and Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) programs. The RFA announces $13 million in SBIR and $2.5 million in STTR funding available for FY 2024, with similar funding anticipated for FY 2025. Phase I awards range from $125,000 to $181,500 and support proof-of-concept research with commercial potential across ten topic areas focused on agriculture, natural resources, and rural development.

- **Three-Phase Structure:** Only Phase I RFA shown; Phase II and III follow after successful Phase I completion
- **Letter of Intent:** Not required
- **Cost Sharing:** No cost sharing or matching requirement specified in this document
- **TABA Option:** Technical and Business Assistance (TABA) available, increases maximum awards to $181,500 for most topics

### National Security Requirements (New)
The SBIR and STTR Extension Act of 2022 requires all FY 2024 Phase I awardees to complete foreign country of concern certifications prior to award, disclosing:
1. Ownership ties and involvement in foreign talent recruitment programs of countries of concern (China, North Korea, Russia, Iran, or others designated by State Department)
2. Joint ventures or subsidiaries in foreign countries of concern
3. Contractual/financial obligations with foreign state-owned enterprises
4. Foreign ownership status
5. Venture capital/institutional investment from entities with foreign affiliations
6. Technology licensing or IP sales to countries of concern (5-year lookback)
7. Foreign business entities or offshore entities related to the small business

## Eligibility Criteria (Key Points)

### Applicant Eligibility
- Must be a small business concern (SBC) per SBA guidelines
- For SBIR: Principal investigator must be employed by the small business
- For STTR: Requires formal cooperative agreement with nonprofit research institution (university, federal laboratory)
- Foreign ownership limitations apply; foreign country of concern certifications required
- U.S. institutions eligible to apply; international collaboration permitted if benefits to U.S. demonstrated

### STTR-Specific Requirements
- Minimum percentage of R&D work must be conducted by nonprofit research institution partner
- Formal cooperative R&D agreement required detailing IP rights and commercialization rights allocation

## Program Philosophy & Focus

### Mission Statement
Unlike fundamental research funding, the SBIR/STTR programs support:
- **Disruptive technologies** with commercial potential and/or societal benefit
- **Early/seed-stage R&D** with equity-free funding
- **Entrepreneurial support** from conception through early commercialization
- **Technology transfer** from lab to market

### Key Evaluation Criteria
- **Technical feasibility** demonstration (proof of concept)
- **High technical risk** (novel approaches, never before attempted)
- **Commercial impact and/or societal benefit** (market disruption potential, product-market fit validation, barrier removal, societal benefit through sustainable business model)
- **Commercialization readiness** (Phase II requires detailed commercialization strategy)
