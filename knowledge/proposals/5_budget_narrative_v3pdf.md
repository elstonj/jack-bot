## Executive Summary
This budget narrative details BST's personnel, fringe benefits, equipment, and cost structure for a NOAA SBIR Phase II project. The project involves a team of five personnel (two full-time engineers, one technician, one part-time intern, and a project coordinator) working 10 months on platform design, prototype development, and sensor integration, with direct costs of $359,755.28 and total project cost including indirect expenses of $395,601.32.

## Notable Details

1. **American-Made Commitment**: Document explicitly notes that "all supplies, equipment, and products are American made."

2. **Team Composition**: Mix of experienced engineers (Stachura as CTO, Elston as PI) with technical support staff (technician, intern) reflects both development and operational/production capability.

3. **Fringe Benefit Scaling**: Document notes expectation that full-time employee fringe benefits will increase to 22%-24% in years three and four of project continuation.

4. **Sensor Integration Focus**: Equipment selection emphasizes vision (DepthAI for optical flow/SLAM) and radio frequency capabilities (MangOH Red, LimeSDR for signals of opportunity), suggesting autonomous navigation and RF sensing as core technical approaches.

5. **De Minimis Rate**: Use of 10% de minimis indirect rate indicates BST may not have had a provisional or final indirect cost rate negotiated with a cognizant federal agency at this time.
