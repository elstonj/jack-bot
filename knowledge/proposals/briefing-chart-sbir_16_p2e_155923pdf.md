## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes development of a VTOL (vertical takeoff and land) variant of the S2 XT UAS specifically designed for sampling volcanic ash clouds and measuring atmospheric properties in harsh environments. Building on Phase II work that demonstrated the fixed-wing S2 XT platform, Phase II-E will add vertical takeoff/landing capability in winds up to 30 mph and conduct a demonstration flight campaign at Makushin Volcano in Alaska.

## Technical Approach

### VTOL Enhancement of S2 XT
The core technical approach involves three primary work elements:

1. **Airframe Design**: Modification of the existing S2 XT fixed-wing airframe to integrate 4 rotors for VTOL capability while maintaining the fixed-wing performance characteristics that enable harsh environment operations.

2. **High-Wind VTOL Algorithms**: Development of hybrid landing algorithms that combine rotor control with fixed-wing aerodynamic surfaces to enable landing in winds up to 30 mph. This hybrid approach preserves the S2 XT's inherent capability to operate in severe environmental conditions.

3. **Demonstration Flight Campaign**: Flight operations at Makushin Volcano in Alaska to validate VTOL S2 XT performance and collect scientific data, including deployment of new real-time data visualization capability.

### Scientific Measurement Capabilities
The integrated system will measure:
- Trace gases
- Particulate sizing and density
- Pressure, temperature, humidity
- 3D winds
- EO (electro-optical) and thermal imagery and video

## Technical Rationale

The document emphasizes that the VTOL capability enhancement does **not sacrifice** the S2 XT's proven ability to operate in harsh environments through the hybrid algorithm approach. This is positioned as a key innovation—maintaining forward-flight performance characteristics (which enable extended range and rough-air penetration) while adding VTOL flexibility for deployment in remote locations without prepared landing sites.
