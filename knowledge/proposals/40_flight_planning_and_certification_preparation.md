## Executive Summary
This section documents BST's planning for Phase II flight testing and regulatory certification of the SuperSwift XT aircraft with integrated volcanic plume sampling sensor suite. The team selected three test sites (Pawnee National Grasslands in Colorado for baseline testing, San Luis Valley in Colorado for high-altitude mountainous validation, and a volcano site for scientific deployment), and completed NASA Airworthiness Flight Safety Review Board (AFSRB) and Flight Readiness Review Board (FRRB) documentation ahead of schedule to minimize Phase II delays.

## Technical Approach
- **Test Site Strategy**: Three-tier testing approach progressing from simple to complex environments
  - Site 1: Pawnee National Grasslands (90 miles NE of Boulder) for early validation of procedures and sensor performance
  - Site 2: San Luis Valley, Colorado (7,500-14,000 ft elevation) for high-altitude and mountainous CONOPS validation
  - Site 3: Volcano deployment (Turrialba Volcano, Costa Rica or Volcano National Park, Hawaii) for final scientific validation
  
- **Certification Strategy**: Leveraging prior successful AFSRB approvals; preparing documentation for submission immediately upon Phase II contract announcement to avoid delays

- **FAA Coordination**: Utilizing CU (University of Colorado) partnership for COA (Certificate of Airworthiness) submission to enable flights above BST's current 400' AGL commercial exemption limit

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift XT
- **What it is**: Ruggedized UAS platform designed for harsh environments and high-altitude operations
- **Proposed use**: Primary airframe for volcanic plume sampling mission in Phase II
- **Performance claims/specs**: 
  - Capable of testing to 14,500' altitude (validated in San Luis Valley testing)
  - Designed for mountainous terrain and high-altitude scientific deployments
  - Compatible with MALIBU sensor payload

### SuperSwift
- **What it is**: Earlier version of ruggedized UAS (or alternate platform designation)
- **Previous deployments**: Flown under NASA COA at Pawnee National Grasslands for Soil Moisture Mapping project; flown with MALIBU payload at Table Mountain Test Site (summer 2016)

### Tempest
- **What it is**: Another BST aircraft platform
- **History**: Successfully completed 4 prior AFSRB approvals across 3 test sites with 2 different payloads from 2 NASA centers (2015-2016)

### MALIBU Payload
- **What it is**: Volcanic plume sampling sensor suite (Unmanned Aerial Mass Spectrometer System for in-situ volcanic plume analysis)
- **Integration**: Being integrated with SuperSwift XT for Phase II volcanic missions
- **Prior validation**: Successfully tested with both Tempest and SuperSwift aircraft at Pawnee National Grasslands and Table Mountain Test Sites under NASA GSFC approval (2016)

## Use Cases & Applications

### Volcanic Plume Sampling
- Primary mission: In-situ scientific data collection from volcanic plumes
- Target environments: High-altitude, mountainous terrain with hazardous atmospheric conditions
- Scientific objectives: Chemical analysis and characterization of volcanic emissions

### High-Altitude Mountainous Operations
- Testing at elevations up to 14,500 feet
- Validation of aircraft performance in thin-air, complex terrain environments
- Demonstration of CONOPS (Concept of Operations) in realistic volcanic geography

### National Park Operations
- BST successfully conducted first legal UAS flight in a National Park (Great Sand Dunes, Colorado) post-new restrictions
- Demonstrated capability to operate in protected areas with proper authorization

## Key Results

### Test Site Selection Completed
1. **Pawnee National Grasslands** (Baseline): 90 miles NE of BST Boulder facility; 20×20 mile area with previously approved NASA COA for Soil Moisture Mapping SuperSwift
2. **San Luis Valley** (High-altitude validation): Elevation range 7,500-14,000 ft; existing FAA COAs from prior commercial operations; successful Great Sand Dunes NP test flight completed
3. **Volcano Site** (Scientific deployment): Two candidates identified:
   - **Turrialba Volcano, Costa Rica**: Simpler flight permissions; strong local support from authorities and University of Costa Rica; experience team (Dr. Diaz) available
   - **Volcano National Park, Hawaii**: Advantage of US location for NASA permissions; potential concerns from National Park restrictions (mitigated by BST's recent NP experience)
