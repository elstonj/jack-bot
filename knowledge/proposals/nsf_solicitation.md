# NSF Small Business Innovation Research Program Phase I Solicitation FY-2014

## Document Metadata
- Type: NSF Program Solicitation
- Client/Agency: National Science Foundation (NSF) - Directorate for Engineering, Industrial Innovation and Partnerships
- Program/Solicitation: NSF Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase I, FY-2014; Solicitation NSF 13-546
- Date: June 2, 2013 (document created/modified); Proposal deadline: June 11, 2013
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None
- Key Personnel: Multiple NSF program officers listed (Prakash Balan, Juan E. Figueroa, Steven Konsek, Glenn H. Larsen, Rajesh Mehta, Muralidharan S. Nair, Benaiah Schrag, Ruth M. Shuman, Jesus V. Soriano)

## Executive Summary
This is the official NSF SBIR Phase I solicitation for FY-2014, issued under Public Law 112-81 (SBIR/STTR Reauthorization Act of 2011). The program aims to stimulate technological innovation in the private sector by funding small business research and development projects with high commercial potential and high-risk research across four technical topic areas. NSF expects to fund approximately 200 Phase I awards with a total budget of $30 million, with individual Phase I awards up to $150,000 for six-month feasibility studies.

## Program Structure & Scope

### Eligible Topic Areas
- Biological and Chemical Technologies (BC)
- Education Applications (EA)
- Electronics, Information and Communication Technologies (EI)
- Nanotechnology, Advanced Materials, and Manufacturing (NM)

### Award Information
- **Phase I Award Amount:** Up to $150,000 per proposal
- **Project Duration:** 6 months
- **Expected Number of Awards:** ~200
- **Total Anticipated Funding:** $30,000,000
- **Award Effective Date:** January 1, 2014
- **Notification Timeline:** 4-6 months from submission deadline

## Eligibility Requirements

### Organization Requirements
- Must qualify as a small business concern per SBA definitions (13 CFR 121)
- Cannot be majority-owned by venture capital operating companies (VCOCs), hedge funds, or private equity firms
- Socially and economically disadvantaged and women-owned small businesses particularly encouraged
- Joint ventures and partnerships permitted if entity qualifies as small business concern
- Research collaborations with universities, national laboratories, and research institutions encouraged

### Research Performance Requirements
- Minimum 2/3 of research budget (as measured by budgeted funds) must be performed by small business concern
- Maximum 1/3 may be outsourced to consultants or subcontractors
- All research must be performed in the U.S.

### Principal Investigator (PI) Requirements
- Primary employment must be with small business concern (51% employment) at time of award
- Minimum 1 calendar month commitment to Phase I project
- Employment elsewhere cannot exceed 19.6 hours per week
- No person may serve as PI for more than one proposal submitted to this solicitation

### Proposal Limits
- Organizations may submit maximum 2 Phase I proposals total (combined SBIR/STTR) during this cycle
- Deadline window: May 11, 2013 - June 11, 2013
- Proposals outside window returned without review

### Administrative Requirements
- Registration in System for Award Management (SAM) required prior to submission
- Dun and Bradstreet (DUNS) number required
- Registration with SBA Company Registry required prior to submission
- Fraud, Waste, and Abuse notification requirement
- SBIR/STTR Funding Agreement Certification required upon award recommendation

## Project Requirements

### Acceptable Project Scope
Projects must provide evidence of:
- Commercially viable product, process, device, or system
- Address important social or economic need
- High potential commercial payback
- High-risk efforts
- Research tools meeting significant commercial market needs
- Applications with multipurpose commercially viable functions

### Unacceptable Objectives (Returned Without Review)
- Systems studies
- Market research
- Commercial development of existing products or proven concepts
- Straightforward engineering design for packaging
- Laboratory evaluations
- Incremental product or process improvements
- Evolutionary optimization of existing products
- Evolutionary modifications to broaden scope of existing product

### Phase I Deliverable
- Technical report summarizing experimental and theoretical accomplishments
- Report serves as basis for Phase II proposal

## Proposal Format & Content Requirements

### Cover Sheet Components
- Topic and subtopic designation (one only)
- Electronic signature requirement
- Proprietary information marking (if applicable)
- Certification of previous SBIR/STTR Phase II awards (if applicable)
- Declaration of equivalent or overlapping proposals/awards to other agencies

### Project Summary (Maximum 1 page)
- Box 1: Overview, key words, and subtopic (200 word limit)
  - Summary of potential outcomes
  - Statement of objectives and methods
  - List of technical expertise areas
  - Target application areas
  
- Box 2: Intellectual Merit (200 word limit)
  - Problem/opportunity identification
  - Research objectives
  - Research description
  - Anticipated results
  - No proprietary information
  
- Box 3: Broader/Commercial Impact (200 word limit)
  - Potential commercial value
  - Societal impact
  - Enhanced scientific and technological understanding

### Project Description (Maximum 15 pages)
**Part 1: Identification and Significance of Innovation**
- Clear statement of research innovation
- Explanation of relevance to subtopic needs

**Part 2: Background and Phase I Technical Objectives**
- List and explanation of key Phase I objectives
- Questions to be answered regarding technical and commercial feasibility
- How customer needs will be met if successful
- Commercial potential considerations

**Part 3: Phase I Research Plan**
- Detailed description of research approach
- Technical discussion of proposed concept
- What is planned and implementation approach
- Plan to achieve each objective
- Sequence of experiments, tests, and computations
- Measurement of objectives

**Part 4: Commercial Potential** (typically 3-5 pages)
Must address:
- **Market Opportunity:** Target market/segments, customer profile, customer needs addressed, market size estimate, barriers to entry
- **Company/Team:** Origins, employee count, anticipated job creation, revenue history (past 3 years), personnel experience and credentials, team's ability to take product to market, alignment with company objectives, technology fit within company mission
- **Product/Technology and Competition:** Competitive landscape, main competition, value proposition, IP protection strategy, critical milestones to market
- **Financing and Revenue Model:** Revenue assumptions, financing strategy for innovation

**Part 5: Consultants and Subawards/Subcontracts**
- Consultant expertise, affiliation, contribution, and agreed consulting rate (max $600/day)
- Signed consultant statements confirming availability, time commitment, role, and rate
- Subaward task descriptions and relation to overall project
- Separate budgets and justifications for each subawardee
- Letters of support from subawardees uploaded to budget justification, not supplementary documents
- Note: Routine analytical services and fabricated components not reportable as subawards
- FFRDC use requires SBA waiver

**Part 6: Equivalent or Overlapping Proposals or Awards**
- Certification of other federal submissions
- Information on related federal awards (ongoing/completed)
- Proposals submitted under other government solicitations
- Anticipated future submissions
- Details for each: agency name/contact, submission dates, solicitation details, performance period, PI/senior personnel info
- Must explicitly state "NONE" if no overlapping proposals

**Part 7: Lineage of Innovation**
- If connected to previously NSF-funded academic/non-profit research, include statement with:
  - Directorate Name
  - Division Name
  - Award Number
- Note: Previous research not limited to SBIR proposer's organization

### References Cited
- Comprehensive list of relevant references including patent and IP citations
- Upload required; indicate "No References Cited" if applicable

### Biographical Sketches
- Maximum 2 pages per person
- Required for PI and key personnel (including consultants and key subaward team members)

### Budget and Budget Justification

**Total Budget Limit:** $150,000

**Line Item Details Required:**

**Personnel (Line A/B):**
- List PI and senior personnel by name
- Time commitments in person-months
- Dollar amounts for performance period
- PI minimum: 1 month commitment
- Do NOT list company employees under Post-Doctoral Scholars line

**Subawards/Consultants (Line C/D):**
- Minimum 2/3 of budget must be small business work
- Maximum 1/3 available for outsourcing
- Consultant rates: Maximum $600/day (8-hour day)
- Consultant letters uploaded with budget justification (not supplementary documents)
- Signed consultant statements required

**Materials and Supplies (Line E):**
- Tangible personal property other than equipment
- Must cost less than $5,000 (or organization's lower threshold)
- Estimated cost for each line item

**Prohibited Expenditures:**
- Permanent equipment (Phase I cannot include equipment purchases)
- Patent expenses
- Foreign travel
- Tuition costs

**Required Expense:**
- **Grantees Conference Travel:** One domestic trip for up to 2 persons (normally PI + business operations representative) to 2-day DC-area workshop
- Required to attend grantee workshop
- Estimate: ~$2,000 per person
- Explicit statement of attendance required in budget justification

**Indirect Costs and Fringe Benefits:**
- Maximum 150% of direct salaries and wages
- Formula: (Line C + Line I) ≤ 150% of (Line A + Line B)
- NOT allowable in indirect cost pools:
  - Independent Research and Development (IR&D)
  - Patent and patent-related expenses
  - Sales and marketing expenses
  - Business development
  - Manufacturing and production expenses

**Reasonable Fees (Profit):**
- Maximum 7% of total indirect and direct project costs

### Current and Pending Support
- All current project support from any source (Federal, State, local, foreign government, foundations, commercial)
- All projects requiring PI/senior personnel time (even without salary support)
- Total award amounts and person-months per year
- All proposals submitted or submitted concurrently to other sponsors
- The NSF SBIR proposal itself considered "pending" and must be listed
- Anticipated submissions in coming year (excluding those listed as equivalent/overlapping)

### Facilities, Equipment and Other Resources
- Description of significant equipment, instrumentation, computers, and physical facilities
- Must specify availability and location
- Equipment NOT allowed as permanent purchase in Phase I (Line D should be empty)
- If not owned/leased by proposing firm, signed statement required from owner/lessor affirming:
  - Availability for research
  - Reasonable lease/rental costs
  - Associated costs
- Scanned statements should be uploaded

### Supplementary Documents (Limited to following if applicable)

**Required:**
- Data Management Plan (mandatory for all)
  - Must include statement: "All data generated in this SBIR Phase I project is considered proprietary."

- SBA Company Registry Documentation
  - PDF from registration at sbir.gov/registration
  - Required for all applicants

**Conditionally Required:**
- Company Commercialization History
  - Required if proposer certifies previous Phase II awards from any Federal agency
  - Must use NSF template exclusively
  - All items must be addressed in template format

- Letters of Support for Technology (maximum 3)
  - Market validation for proposed innovation
  - Evidence of stakeholder dialog (potential customers, strategic partners, investors)
  - Demonstrate legitimate business opportunity if technology proves feasible
  - Must contain signatory affiliation and contact information
  - NOT consultant/subcontractor letters (those go in budget justification)

- Post Doc Mentoring Plan (if applicable)
  - Required if funding postdoctoral scholars on budget line B.1 or subaward budgets
  - Describes mentoring activities for all postdoctoral researchers
  - Applies regardless of researcher location
  - Template available; separate from 15-page project description limit

- Human Subjects and Vertebrate Animals Documentation (if applicable)
  - IRB approval letters if human subjects involved
  - Plan for approval if not yet obtained (must be attainable within 6 weeks of award notification)
  - IACUC approval for animal research
  - Options: establish own IRB, use university/research institution IRB, use commercial company
