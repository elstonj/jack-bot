## Executive Summary
This is the preface document for the DoD FY2025.2 Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Program Broad Agency Announcement. It establishes the framework, eligibility requirements, certifications, and proposal preparation instructions for small business concerns (SBCs) seeking Phase I, Direct to Phase II, or Phase III SBIR funding from the Department of Defense. The BAA runs from April 23, 2025 (topics open) through May 21, 2025 (proposal deadline).

## Program Overview

### Objectives
The SBIR Program aims to:
- Stimulate technological innovation
- Strengthen small business role in meeting DoD R&D needs
- Foster and encourage minority and disadvantaged persons' participation in technological innovation
- Increase commercial application of DoD-supported research results

### Three-Phase Structure
- **Phase I:** 6-12 months; Determines scientific, technical, and commercial merit/feasibility; typically funded amount not specified in this document
- **Phase II:** ~24 months; Continues and further develops Phase I R&D effort; generally follows successful Phase I completion
- **Phase III:** Non-SBIR funded work deriving from or extending prior SBIR funding; focused on commercialization with private sector or other non-SBIR federal funding

### Direct to Phase II Program
Available under 15 U.S.C. §638(cc) as amended; allows SBCs to submit Phase II proposals without prior Phase I award if they provide feasibility documentation demonstrating Phase I-level technical merit. Feasibility work cannot be based on prior federally funded SBIR/STTR work.

### Open Topics Program
Established under 15 U.S.C. §638(ww); uses generalized mission requirements or technology areas to adapt commercial solutions rather than specifying exact technical objectives. Single submission per SBC allowed per open topic.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Small Business Concern (SBC) Qualification
- Must meet SBC definition per 13 C.F.R §§ 701-705 at time of award
- Must meet small business ownership and control requirements (13 CFR § 121.702)
- Must be U.S.-owned and controlled

### Work Performance Requirements
- **Phase I:** SBC must conduct minimum 2/3 of research and analytical work (measured by direct and indirect costs)
- **Phase II:** SBC must perform minimum 50% of research and analytical work
- Deviations may be permitted with Funding Agreement Officer's written approval after SBIR/STTR program manager consultation

### Principal Investigator (PI) Requirements
- PI's primary employment must be with proposing SBC at time of award and during performance
- Primary employment defined as >50% of time with SBC (based on 40-hour work week)
- Precludes full-time employment elsewhere
- Changes/deviations subject to Funding Agreement Officer approval

### Performance Location
- All R&D work must be performed in the United States by SBC and subcontractors

### Joint Ventures and Partnerships
- Permitted if entity qualifies as SBC per ownership and size requirements
- Must disclose relationships with foreign entities or foreign government-controlled companies

### Venture Capital/Private Equity Restrictions
- SBCs with multiple venture capital operating companies (VCOCs), hedge funds, or private equity firms as majority owners are generally ineligible unless Service/Component authorizes participation
- Single VCOC/hedge fund/private equity firm cannot own >50% unless that firm is itself a small business >50% directly owned and controlled by U.S. citizens or permanent resident aliens
- Foreign ownership thresholds: Must disclose foreign nationals, foreign entities, or foreign government ownership >5% (direct or indirect)

## Performance Benchmark Requirements

For SBCs with multiple prior SBIR/STTR awards:
- **Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate:** Extent of progression from Phase I to Phase II
- **Commercialization Benchmark:** Progress toward commercialization beyond Phase II work

Increased minimum performance standards apply per SBIR and STTR Extension Act of 2022 (Pub. L. 117-183).

**Consequences for firms failing either benchmark:**
Maximum 80 total Phase I and Direct to Phase II awards across DoD, allocated as:
- Army: 20 awards maximum
- Navy: 20 awards maximum
- Air Force: 20 awards maximum
- All other DoD Components combined: 20 awards maximum

## Certifications and Registrations Required

### System for Award Management (SAM)
- SBC must register in SAM to receive Unique Entity ID (UEI) and Commercial and Government Entry (CAGE) code
- Required for SBA Company Registry registration

### SBA Company Registry
- SBC must be registered; must provide SBC Control ID and Proof of Registration/Certification during proposal submission

### Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP)
- All individuals from proposing SBC must register in DSIP
- Uses Login.gov authentication
- Designated DSIP Firm Admin responsible for Firm PIN creation, user access control, and firm-level forms
- Account sharing/password sharing prohibited per Login.gov Terms of Use and DoD policy
- **Accessibility Note:** DSIP only accessible from within the United States (50 states, territories, possessions, Puerto Rico, Marshall Islands, Federated States of Micronesia, Palau, District of Columbia)

### Required Certifications
Per SBIR and STTR Extension Act of 2022, SBCs must certify at:
- Time of Phase I and Phase II award
- Prior to final payment on Phase I award
- Prior to receiving 50% of Phase II total award amount
- Prior to final payment on Phase II award

## Due Diligence Program - National Security Risk Assessment

Per 15 U.S.C. §638(vv), DoD must assess and mitigate national security risks from SBCs seeking federal funding.

### Assessment Areas
- Cybersecurity practices
- Patent analysis
- Employee analysis and foreign ownership
- SBC's financial ties and obligations (surety, equity, debt)
- Employee ties to foreign countries, persons, or entities
- Open-source analysis to confirm accuracy of disclosures

### Information Required
- "Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries" (Volume 7 of proposal)
- DoD may request copies of contractual/financial obligations or agreements with foreign state-owned or foreign entities (5-year lookback period)

### Risk Determination
- DoD may not make awards posing unacceptable national security risk
- If risk deemed unmitigable, proposal rejected regardless of technical merit review
- Reference: "Defense Small Business Innovation Research and Small Business Technology Transfer Due Diligence Program" memo (May 13, 2024) with Common Risk Matrix

### FOCI Training
DoD offers online course on Understanding Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence (FOCI) via Project Spectrum at projectspectrum.io

## Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI)

### FAR 9.5 Requirements
- SBCs must identify and disclose all facts relevant to potential OCIs
- Must include OCI mitigation plan describing actions taken/intended to:
  - Prevent conflicting roles biasing judgment
  - Prevent unfair competitive advantage
- Must address OCIs in context of FAR 9.505-1 through 9.505-4 limitations

### DoD Supplemental OCI Policy
- Contractors cannot concurrently provide SETA (Scientific Engineering Technical Assistance), A&AS (Advisory and Assistance Services), or similar support while being technical performer
- Must affirm whether SBC or proposed team members provide such support under:
  - Current awards/sub-awards
  - Past awards/sub-awards ending within one calendar year prior to submission
- If applicable, must disclose:
  - DoD Service/Component office receiving support
  - Prime contract number
  - Team member providing support
  - OCI mitigation plan per FAR 9.5

### Government Evaluation
- Per FAR 9.503, 9.504, 9.506, government evaluates OCI mitigation plans for proposals determined selectable and with funding available
- May require additional information to support OCI mitigation plan evaluation
- May reject proposals with failed full disclosure, missing government waiver, or failure to reasonably provide requested information

## Research Protections and Compliance

### Human Subjects Research (RIHS/HSR)
- Must comply with federal/state laws and agency policy for human subject protection
- Requires documentation of federal assurance of compliance (e.g., HHS Office for Human Research Protections federal-wide assurance)
- All subcontractors must also have valid assurance
- Personnel must provide documentation of completed human subjects protection training
- May be exempt if meeting criteria in 32 CFR 219.101
- Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval required; typical timeline 1-3 months
- DoD review of approved protocol: 3-6 months
- **No funding for human subject research until ALL approvals granted**
- Recommended: Separate human subject research tasks in technical and cost proposals

### Animal Subjects Research
- Must comply with federal and agency rules on animal acquisition, transport, care, handling, use
- Requires Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC) approval
- Requires secondary/headquarters-level approval from DoD veterinarian
- **No animal research using DoD funding until all approvals granted**
- Recommended: Separate animal use tasks in technical and cost proposals

### Recombinant DNA Research
- Must comply with federal/state law, regulation, and agency guidance
- Institutional biosafety committee approval required

## Proposal Preparation - Structural Requirements

### DSIP Proposal Volume Structure
- **Volume 1:** Proposal Cover Sheet
- **Volume 2:** Technical Volume
- **Volume 3:** Cost Volume
- **Volume 4:** Company Commercialization Report
- **Volume 5:** Supporting Documents
- **Volume 6:** Fraud, Waste and Abuse Training
- **Volume 7:** Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries

### Cover Sheet (Volume 1)
- Prepared in DSIP
- Brief technical abstract (max 3,000 characters)
- Anticipated benefits and potential commercial applications (max 3,000 characters)
- No proprietary or classified information
- May be publicly released if selected for award
- DSIP assigns proposal number upon save; may modify until BAA closes
- Includes Percentage of Work (POW) certification derived from cost volume entries
- If POW falls below eligibility requirements, Funding Agreement Officer must upload explanatory letter or approval

### Technical Volume (Volume 2)
**Format Requirements:**
- Single PDF file with graphics
- Must include virus check before upload
- No password protection, encryption, or locked files
- No active graphics (videos, moving pictures, etc.)
- File size and page limits per Service/Component-specific instructions
