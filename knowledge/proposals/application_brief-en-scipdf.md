## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies partnered with EN-SCI Technologies to develop a novel approach for horizontal atmospheric profiling of noxious gases (ozone, NOx) using the S2 fixed-wing UAS. Rather than traditional vertical profiling via multi-rotor drones or weather balloons, this system enables cost-effective horizontal volumetric sampling at multiple defined altitudes to capture broader atmospheric conditions and identify localized gas concentration anomalies.

## Technical Approach
- **Aircraft Platform**: BST S2 fixed-wing UAS with autonomous flight capability
- **Flight Software**: BST's proprietary flight management software (SwiftCore) enables autonomous operation with intuitive flight planning
- **Sampling Method**: Horizontal volumetric profiling at pre-determined altitudes; automatic grid-mapping pattern generation based on user-specified area of interest, altitude bounds, and flight track spacing
- **Sensor Positioning**: Sensors mounted in aerodynamically-optimized nose cone, positioned in front of propeller to ensure clean, uncontaminated atmospheric measurements
- **Payload System**: Modular, field-swappable design allowing rapid payload changes without specialized tools (four bolts for installation)

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Real-time horizontal ozone profiling**: Measuring ozone concentrations at precise altitudes across defined geographic areas
- **NOx detection**: Horizontal profiling of nitrogen oxide gases
- **Air quality assessment**: Understanding air quality impacts on human populations
- **Environmental monitoring**: Detecting localized pollution sources (e.g., well sites, industrial facilities)
- **Altitude-stratified sampling**: Grid mapping at multiple altitudes (200, 300, 400 feet AGL demonstrated)
- **Volumetric atmospheric analysis**: Capturing broader snapshots of atmospheric conditions vs. single vertical columns

## Key Results
EN-SCI and BST conducted demonstration flights performing grid mapping at 200, 300, and 400 feet AGL. Data analysis revealed a localized spike in gas concentrations in one corner of the survey area that correlated with proximity to a well site, demonstrating the capability to identify point-source pollution and localized contamination.
