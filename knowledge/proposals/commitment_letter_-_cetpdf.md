# Commitment Letter - CET (University of Colorado Center for Environmental Technology)

## Document Metadata
- Type: Commitment Letter (supporting material for SBIR proposal)
- Client/Agency: University of Colorado; Black Swift Technologies LLC
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Phase II/Phase II-E contract NNX14CG09C; CCRPP (Climate Change Research and Policy Program) project
- Date: March 28, 2017
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Soil Moisture Mapping (SMM) sUAS; SuperSwift sUAS
- Key Personnel: Prof. Albin J. Gasiewski (CU Center for Environmental Technology Director); Dr. Jack Elston (BST CEO)

## Executive Summary
Professor Albin Gasiewski of the University of Colorado's Center for Environmental Technology commits $49,090 in funding to Black Swift Technologies for continued development of the Soil Moisture Mapping (SMM) small Unmanned Aerial System, originally developed under a NASA SBIR contract. The funding will support summer 2017 flight campaigns, sensor validation, and agricultural applications focused on precision irrigation management.

## Technical Approach
- Further development of SMM sUAS system data processing pipeline
- Summer 2017 flight campaign and validation against in situ soil moisture sensors
- Focus on reducing processing time to generate soil moisture maps from combined radiometric, NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index), and thermal sensor data
- Goal: achieve same-day map generation to enable variable rate irrigation

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Soil Moisture Mapping (SMM) sUAS**
- Small unmanned aerial system for soil moisture mapping
- Uses radiometric, NDVI, and thermal sensors for data collection
- Developed during NASA SBIR Phase II and Phase II-E
- Target TRL 7 and MRL 4 for the soil moisture mapping sensor by end of CCRPP grant

**SuperSwift sUAS**
- Platform for SMM system
- Target TRL 9 and MRL 7 by end of CCRPP grant

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Precision Agriculture / Irrigation Management**: Primary commercialization target
- **Water Conservation**: Global agriculture uses ~70% of freshwater; demonstrated reductions in water usage of >15% while maintaining crop yield using accurate soil moisture data
- **Test Site**: Irrigation Research Foundation (IRF) site in Yuma, Colorado
- **Variable Rate Irrigation**: Enabling software and algorithms development for precision irrigation optimization
