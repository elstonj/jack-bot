# NSF FastLane Proposal Status - Wind Sensing STTR

## Document Metadata
- Type: NSF STTR Proposal Review / Panel Summary
- Client/Agency: National Science Foundation (NSF)
- Program/Solicitation: STTR (Small Business Technology Transfer)
- Date: Submitted/Reviewed October 24 - December 19, 2013
- Proposal Number: 1346510
- Academic Partner: University of Colorado at Boulder
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Small UAS platform for atmospheric measurement

## Executive Summary
This NSF STTR proposal sought to develop a small unmanned aircraft system (sUAS) to measure meteorological parameters in the atmospheric boundary layer for thunderstorm, hurricane, and fire monitoring and forecasting. The panel recognized the market potential but identified significant weaknesses in proposal rigor, intellectual property strategy, competitive differentiation, and technical feasibility justification.

## Technical Approach
- Small UAS platform designed to sample atmospheric boundary layer meteorological parameters
- System integration of commercially available sensors
- Long-endurance electric-powered aircraft
- Reconfigurable platform for multiple applications
- Intended for use by meteorology modelers and forecasters

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Small UAS for Atmospheric Sampling:**
- Purpose: Measure meteorological parameters in boundary layer
- Target Applications: Thunderstorm monitoring, hurricane sampling, fire monitoring
- Design Philosophy: Low-cost, easy-to-operate platform
- Configuration: Versatile, reconfigurable for different payloads
- Power System: All-electric (noted as technically challenging)
- Integration Strategy: Leverage commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) sensors

## Use Cases & Applications
- Thunderstorm monitoring and forecasting
- Hurricane characterization and forecasting
- Fire monitoring and behavior prediction
- General atmospheric boundary layer meteorological research

## Key Strengths (Panel Assessment)
- Large projected market for small UAS
- Support from National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) and National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR) via letters of endorsement
- Subcontractor experience in sensor development and appropriate facilities
- Versatile, reconfigurable platform design

## Key Weaknesses (Panel Assessment)
- Intellectual property strategy not clearly articulated
- Competitive advantages versus existing systems not adequately demonstrated
- Annual sales estimates not justified
- Proposal focused on strategic planning rather than feasibility demonstration
- Limited novelty—primarily integrating existing commercial sensors
- Long-endurance electric power system lacked technical detail and feasibility justification
- No clear justification for lower cost, better performance, or reusability versus similar instruments
- Insufficient market opportunity description

## Notable Details
- Panel noted this was primarily a strategic planning document rather than a technical feasibility study
- Letters of support from NREL and NCAR indicated recognized market need for small UAS in meteorology
- Proposal acknowledged commercial sensor integration approach but did not adequately address resulting lack of novelty
- Suggestions for strengthening: more detailed/rigorous technical plan, better market opportunity analysis

## Outcome
Document status suggests proposal was not selected for funding (categorized in "Inactive Projects" folder). Panel feedback focused on need for stronger technical rigor and clearer differentiation strategy.
