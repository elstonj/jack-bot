## Executive Summary
This Air Force Airworthiness Circular (AC-20-02) establishes the official procedure and template for conducting airworthiness assessments of Small Unmanned Aircraft Systems (SUAS) operating under U.S. Air Force authority. The document provides a standardized assessment methodology derived from the Range Commander's Council publication RCC 323-99 and is applicable when SUAS operations are identified as appropriate in Airworthiness Bulletin (AWB)-350.

## Technical Approach
AC-20-02 prescribes a structured assessment process consisting of six main sections:

1. **Program Information** – documenting program/project name, objectives, operating environment (Flight Test, Operational, or Other), operator type (USAF, Contractor, or Both), and airspace designation
2. **UAS Information** – comprehensive technical description including physical characteristics (dimensions, weight, materials), flight envelope/performance (speed, altitude, endurance, range, glide ratio), weather limitations, avionics specifications, power systems, computer architecture, ground control station configuration, and launch/recovery methods
3. **Causes of Loss of Control** – analysis of potential failure modes including loss of command link, vehicle position information, flight reference data, control responsiveness, propulsion, electrical power, and ground control station capability
4. **Common Safeguards** – description of system capabilities and mitigation procedures (degraded modes, return-home functions, geofencing, flight termination systems, fail-safe modes, collision avoidance, parachute recovery)
5. **Vehicle Safety History and Reliability** – compilation of flight history, safety program documentation, hazard analysis, failure modes/MTBF data, and hazardous materials assessment
6. **Risk Assessment** – systematic identification of hazards, severity classification, probability assessment, and residual risk determination using standardized matrices

## Notable Details

### Risk Assessment Methodology:
- Employs severity categories based on repair/replacement cost of air vehicle
- Probability assessment uses both qualitative and quantitative approaches
- Includes aggregate probability of mishap calculation: **(Total Aircraft Losses + 1) / Total Flight Hours**
- Uses standardized Risk Assessment Matrix (Table 6.3) to determine appropriate Risk Acceptance Authorities (RAAs)
- Severity/Probability determination references AWB-150 thresholds

### Key Assessment Areas:
- **Command & Control Robustness:** Backup transmitters/receivers, electromagnetic interference tolerance, operator awareness of command link loss
- **Navigation Redundancy:** Multiple position information sources, backup GPS capability, autopilot response to navigation loss
- **Flight Termination Systems:** Independent battery circuits, fail-safe activation on power loss, automatic activation criteria
- **Environmental Constraints:** Temperature, icing, dust effects on propulsion; wind limits for flight, launch, recovery
- **Battery/Power Management:** Essential bus prioritization, battery backup capacity verification, load shedding procedures
- **Operator Control:** Manual vs. automated control modes, situational awareness indicators (attitude, position, system health)
- **Collision Avoidance:** "See and avoid" procedures, radar/transponder integration
