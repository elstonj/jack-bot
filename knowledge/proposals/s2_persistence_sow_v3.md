## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to demonstrate continuous infrared imagery collection over simulated wildfires using multiple S2 fixed-wing UAS operating in coordinated handoff patterns. The effort aims to conduct a 10-12 hour persistence flight simulation with potential scaling to 24-hour operations, delivering near-real-time geo-rectified imagery to incident commanders.

## Technical Approach
- **Multi-aircraft synchronization**: Deploy 2-4 S2 UAS with synchronized launch and recovery cycles to maintain persistent overhead presence
- **Handoff protocol**: Maintain at least two aircraft over target during crew transitions to ensure continuous data coverage
- **Flight patterns**: Pre-determined transects for wide-area imagery or orbits over single locations
- **Data delivery**: Near real-time geo-rectified imagery transmission to accessible ground location
- **Regulatory compliance**: Operations under NASA NPR 7900, including potential Airworthiness Review, Flight Readiness Review, and Mission Readiness Review
- **COA**: Existing COA for S2 flights up to 2,000 feet at testing location; modifications for mission-specific requirements handled by vendor

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift S2
- Fixed-wing, catapult-launched UAS
- Previously deployed operationally by USGS for volcano caldera mapping and gas sampling
- Currently operated by NASA ARC and NASA Langley Research Center (LaRC)
- Suitable for extended-duration surveillance missions

### IR Imaging Payload Nosecones
- Previously flown for USGS and NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL)
- Integrates with S2 for thermal imagery collection
- Capable of geo-rectified imagery generation

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary**: Continuous high-resolution infrared surveillance of active wildfires
- **Secondary**: Monitoring of other ephemeral ground or atmospheric targets
- **Operational context**: Support to incident commanders with real-time data access
- **Expansion potential**: 24-hour persistence flight campaigns

## Deliverables
1. Flight report with recommendations and lessons learned for 24-hour operations (delivered 90 days post-mission)
2. Complete equipment list required for sustained operations (launchers, batteries, chargers, aircraft, ground stations, GSE)
3. All collected payload data released per NASA Open Science Data Policy
4. All aircraft state and payload data to science community via DAAC or NASA Ames website
5. Data archiving assignment to appropriate DAAC facility

## Project Schedule
- **Start**: No later than November 15, 2024
- **Duration**: 6 months from award to completion (including all imagery, telemetry, and reports)
- **Flight duration objective**: 10-12 hour demonstration (weather and daylight permitting)
