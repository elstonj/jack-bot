## Executive Summary
This is a Long Range Broad Agency Announcement from the Office of Naval Research inviting proposals for S&T research projects offering potential advancement and improvement of Navy and Marine Corps operations. The BAA remains open for approximately one year from publication, accepting proposals for basic research, applied research, advanced technology development, and advanced component development. Amendment 0002 makes administrative corrections to FAR/DFARS clauses related to unmanned aircraft systems procurement restrictions and policy requirements.

## Use Cases & Applications
The BAA seeks proposals addressing Navy and Marine Corps operational needs across a broad spectrum of science and engineering disciplines, including:

## Notable Details

### Eligibility & Participation
- Open to academia, industry, and research community worldwide
- Small and large businesses encouraged to compete
- HBCUs and minority institutions encouraged but not set-aside
- FFRDCs (including DOE National Labs) **not eligible** as prime awardees but may partner with eligible offerors
- Navy labs, military universities, warfare centers, and DoD/civilian agency labs **not eligible** as direct submitters (should contact ONR TPOC instead)
- UARCs eligible unless precluded by their DoD UARC contract
- Teaming encouraged with cooperation required on data/software exchange

### Award Instruments
Four award types available:
1. **Procurement Contracts** (acquisition for direct Government benefit)
2. **Grants** (no fee/profit; minimal Federal involvement)
3. **Cooperative Agreements** (no fee/profit; substantial Federal involvement expected)
4. **Other Transaction Agreements (OTAs)** for research (10 U.S.C. § 4021) and prototypes (10 U.S.C. § 4022); 50/50 cost share required to maximum extent practicable

### Submission Requirements
- **White Papers**: 5 pages max (excluding cover page); submitted via FedConnect for ONR/NavalX, direct email for MCWL/ONRG
- **Full Proposals**: Instructions in Appendices 1 (Grants/Coop Agreements) and 2 (Contracts/OTAs)
- **Non-proprietary Statement of Work** required with all proposals
- Classification: proposals expected unclassified; classified proposals permitted with unclassified SOW
- All offerors must register in SAM.gov with valid UEI and CAGE code prior to submission

### Key Dates
- Announcement remains open approximately **1 year from publication** (until replacement)
- Late proposal deadline: **30 September 2025** (or when successor BAA issued)
- White papers frequently desired; offerors should consult cognizant Program Officer

### Evaluation Criteria
Proposals evaluated on:
1. **Overall scientific/technical merit**: innovation, soundness of concept, state-of-art awareness, significance/originality, anticipated impact, PI/team qualifications and experience
2. **Naval relevance**: contribution to ONR/DoN mission; **Foreign Talent Recruitment Program risk assessment** (per NDAA FY2019 §1286, amended by FY2021 §1299C); IP protection; technology transfer risks; associations with denied entities; Confucius Institute hosting restrictions (per NDAA 2021 §1062)
3. **Fund availability** (not applicable to white papers)

Criteria weighted equally.

### Small Business Provisions
- Subcontracting plan required for contract awards exceeding $750,000 (not for small businesses)
- Plans must include realistic small business goals; if below 5% SDB goals, must provide written explanation
- ONR will post contact info of interested small businesses on website to facilitate subcontracting connections
- Apex Accelerators available for business assistance

### Security & IP Protection
- FOIA exemption: OT and Cooperative Agreement submissions exempt from disclosure under 5 U.S.C. §552 for 5 years (10 U.S.C. §4021(i))
- Support contractors utilized for evaluation may access proposals but must sign ONR Non-Disclosure Agreement with third-party beneficiary language
- Proprietary data marking required; restricted legend available for sensitive information
