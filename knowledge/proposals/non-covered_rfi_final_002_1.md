# Non-covered UAS - Department of Interior RFI Response

## Document Metadata
- Type: Request for Information (RFI) Response
- Client/Agency: U.S. Department of Interior (DOI), Office of Aviation Services (OAS)
- Program/Solicitation: Executive Order 13981 Compliance - Non-covered UAS
- Date: November 10, 2022 (submission deadline: December 9, 2022)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: SwiftPilot, SwiftStation, Gimbal
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (Primary Point of Contact)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies responded to a Department of Interior RFI seeking to identify UAS compliant with Executive Order 13981 requirements regarding adversary country component restrictions. BST confirmed that their UAS (under 55 lbs) and all critical electronic components meet compliance requirements, with all core systems manufactured or developed in-house in the United States.

## Compliance Response Summary
BST answered "No" to all five critical compliance questions:

1. **Manufacturing in Adversary Countries**: No
2. **Critical Electronic Components from Adversary Countries**: No
3. **Operating Software from Adversary Countries**: No
4. **Network Connectivity/Data Storage Outside US**: No
5. **Data Transmission Hardware/Software from Adversary Countries**: No

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SwiftPilot**: Flight controller system with all code and hardware developed in-house by BST in the United States.

**SwiftStation**: Ground control system/processor with all software code developed in-house by BST in the United States.

**BST Gimbal**: Gimbal system manufactured in-house by Black Swift Technologies in the United States.
