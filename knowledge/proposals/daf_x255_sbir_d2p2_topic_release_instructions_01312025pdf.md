# DAF X25.5 SBIR D2P2 Topic Release Instructions

## Document Metadata
- Type: Topic Release Instructions (solicitation guidance document)
- Client/Agency: Department of the Air Force (DAF) / U.S. Air Force
- Program/Solicitation: DoD X25.5 Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO), Direct-to-Phase-II (D2P2)
- Date: February 5, 2025 (topics open); Release 5 dated January 31, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None (this is a solicitation framework document, not a BST proposal)
- Key Personnel: None from BST (document names DAF contracting officers: Ryan DeBonis, Daniel Brewer)

## Executive Summary
This is the official topic release instructions document for the DAF X25.5 CSO Direct-to-Phase-II solicitation. It provides comprehensive guidance for small businesses submitting Phase II proposals to address Air Force and Space Force stakeholder needs using innovative dual-purpose (defense/commercial) technologies. Two topics are available: AFX255-DPCSO1 (Air Force) and AFX255-DPCSO2 (Space Force), each offering up to $1,250,000 in funding for 21-month performance periods.

## Key Solicitation Details

### Timeline
- **February 5, 2025**: Topics open; DoD begins accepting proposals in DSIP
- **February 20, 2025**: Topic Q&A closes (12:00 p.m. ET)
- **March 6, 2025**: Proposal deadline (12:00 p.m. ET)

### Funding & Duration
- **Maximum Funding**: $1,250,000 per award
- **Maximum Period of Performance**: 21 months
- **Number of Awards**: Multiple (2 topics available)
- **Submission Requirement**: One (1) proposal per applicant per topic release

### Eligible Applicants
- Small Business Concerns (SBCs)
- Note: DAF capped at 15% of SBIR budget for SBCs majority-owned by venture capital, hedge funds, or private equity firms

## Evaluation Criteria (in descending order of importance)

### 1. Technical Approach (Non-Cost Factor)
- Clarity of problem statement and technical merit
- Innovation and soundness of proposed approach
- Differentiation from current alternatives
- Qualifications of PI, staff, and consultants to execute approach

### 2. Defense Need (Non-Cost Factor)
- Appropriateness, relevance, and specificity of identified Defense Need
- **Mandatory Customer Memorandum** (signed by DAF end-user and customer) required
- Adequacy of proposed effort to fulfill identified need
- Level of defense mission impact and urgency
- Breadth of applicability of solution

### 3. Commercialization (Non-Cost Factor)
- SBC's track record of commercializing SBIR/research (pre-sales, pilots, sales, revenue, active users, subscriptions, downloads, traction)
- Market size and revenue potential
- Clarity of business plan
- Phase II follow-on commitments (government or private sources)
- Sound transition strategy documented in Customer Memorandum

### Cost/Price
- Secondary to technical, defense need, and commercialization factors
- Price reasonableness and realism considered when technical evaluations are essentially equal

## Proposal Structure & Requirements

### Volume 1: Cover Sheet
- Technical Abstract (non-proprietary, for public release via OSD)
- Anticipated Benefits/Potential Commercial Applications (specific format required)
- Maximum 8 keywords/phrases (SEO-style for categorization and identifying Phase III transition partners)

### Volume 2: Technical Volume (15-page limit)
**Required Sections:**

1. **Identification and Significance of Problem/Opportunity**
   - Specific problem/opportunity addressed in feasibility study
   - Relationship to Phase II effort

2. **DAF Operational Imperatives**
   - Which of seven DAF Operational Imperatives addressed, or reference "BLUE SKY"
   - Explanation of solution's capability to provide increased strategic capability

3. **Non-Defense Commercial Solution**
   - Description including use by non-Defense commercial customers
   - Technical specifications and qualitative/quantitative value
   - Must answer mandatory questions:
     - Novel standalone or modification/build-upon existing product?
     - Has it been sold, leased, or licensed to public? (with quantifiable data)
     - Non-defense market opportunity (quantified/described)?
     - Is it "of a type" customarily used by public/non-governmental entities?
     - End-user use cases and how solution fulfills them?
     - Differentiation from competitor solutions?
     - Technical risks and mitigation?
     - Technology Readiness Level (TRL) and previous viability results?
     - Evidence of commercial traction/adoption (pre-sales, pilots, sales, revenue, active users, subscriptions, downloads)?

4. **Proposed Adaptation of Non-Defense Commercial Solution**
   - Description of R/R&D adaptation or T&E to meet US Government end-user need
   - Must answer mandatory questions including similarity to commercial marketplace, production differences, DAF-unique modifications, quantitative benefits, COTS/commercial modification intent, and T&E details

5. **Phase I-Type "Feasibility Study" Results**
   - Scientific/technical R/R&D effort (research questions, methods, results, relevant literature)
   - DAF End-User and Customer Exploration Methods (at minimum: 1 in-depth interview with DAF end-user and 1 with DAF customer)
   - Empowered and Committed DAF End-Users (willing to support further R&D, testing, evaluation, trial)
   - Empowered and Committed DAF Customers (Program Managers willing to assist with Phase III transition)
   - **Mandatory Customer Memorandum** (signed, uploaded to Volume 5 "Letters of Support")
   - Note: Joint organizations acceptable as end-user/customer, but primary TPOC must be from DAF
   - Non-DAF government end-users acceptable, but at least one DAF/joint end-user and customer required

6. **Compliance and Regulatory Activities**
   - Completion of Attachment 3 (Regulatory Compliance Initial Safety Assessment form) required

7. **Phase II Technical Objectives and Key Results**
   - 3-5 objectives tied to specific Phase II trial tasks
   - Objectives must be qualitative, specific to participating DAF end-user(s), tied to time periods, tangible, unambiguous
   - 3-5 specific "key results" per objective (measurable, likely quantitative)
   - List of Required Stakeholders for Phase II completion (lawyers, test range officials, IA officials, etc.)

8. **Phase II Work Plan Outline** (see Table 2 details below)

9. **Commercialization Strategy** (see Table 3 details below)

10. **Military Applications/DoD Customers**
    - Existing potential military requirement
    - DoD agency/organization most likely to become customer
    - Statement of whether DoD agency expressed interest/commitment to non-SBIR federally funded Phase III or Phase II enhancement with matching SBIR funds
    - Relevant details: funding availability, fiscal year, funding type (RDT&E, O&M, etc.)
    - Agency POC names, emails, phone numbers

11. **Foreign Persons**
    - Identify all foreign persons, non-U.S. citizens, and dual citizens as direct employees, subcontractors, or consultants
    - Provide countries of origin, visa/work permit types, anticipated involvement level
    - For export-controlled technology: foreign nationals limited to public domain work, cannot be in key positions (PI, Senior Engineer, etc.)
    - ITAR-restricted work: only "Lawfully Admitted for Permanent Residence" or "Protected Individuals" may participate

12. **Key Personnel**
    - Identify all involved in project (subcontractors, consultants)
    - Address qualifications for RDT&E and commercialization
    - Include education, experience, citizenship
    - Technical resume for PI (including publications) required

13. **Non-Defense Commercial Customers**
    - Past revenue from commercial customers (clear list with revenue amounts)
    - Potential revenue-generating projects directly related to proposed solution
    - Distinguish between on-going/completed projects (preferred) and potential projects
    - Defense and non-Defense customer projects listed separately

14. **Investors and Partners**
    - Identify investors, shareholders, strategic advisors contributing significantly to Phase II
    - Explain how past experience/track record will support technical or commercialization efforts
    - Include leverage of portfolio companies or other capital sources

15. **Related Work**
    - Significant activities directly related to proposed effort
    - Previous programs by PI, SBC, consultants, others
    - Application to proposed project
    - List of reviewers providing state-of-the-art comments
    - Related government efforts (SBIR or otherwise): awarded, previously proposed, or intended for proposal

### Phase II Work Plan Outline (Table 2)

**Mandatory Features:**
1. Work stated in terms of outcomes/results, not performance methods
2. Measurable performance standards with assessment methods

**Required Sections:**

- **Scope**: Major requirements and specifications
- **Task Outline**: Work to be accomplished; if government funding commitments (non-SBIR), clearly identify SBIR-funded vs. government-funded tasks; indicate tasks fitting "Research Involving Human Subjects" (RIHS) definition
  - Note: IRB approval can take 1-3 months; DoD review can take 3-6 months; no funding can be used for human subject research until ALL approvals granted
  - Applicants encouraged to separate human/animal use tasks in technical and cost proposals to avoid award delays
  
- **Deliverables**: Specific sample/prototype hardware/software, data deliverables, schedules, quantities
- **Unique Item Identification (UID)**: DFARS 252.211-7003 may apply to items with unit acquisition cost ≥$5,000

**Required Reports (All Phase II awards):**

1. **Final Report**
   - SF 298 (Report Documentation Page) as first page
   - Include: purpose, brief description, potential results applications, objectives met, work completed, results obtained, technical feasibility estimates
   - Non-proprietary (may be published by DoD)
   - Maximum 25 pages

2. **Status Reports**
   - Quarterly minimum
   - Concise documents describing progress toward DAF end-user needs
   - Maximum 15 slides per report
   - Include progress toward Phase II objectives and key results
   - Include quantitative progress measure (0-1 scale) for each objective

3. **Phase II Summary Report**
   - Due at technical effort completion
   - Email to cognizant AF Contracting Officer/Agreement Officer (CO/AO)
   - Maximum 700 words
   - Include technology description and anticipated applications/benefits (government/private sector use)

**Safety-Related Deliverables:**
- Preliminary Hazard Analysis Report (if hardware developed)
- Hazardous Material Report (if hazardous materials used): materials identification, purpose, alternative procedures/safeguards
- Additional reporting may be required: software documentation, user manuals, engineering drawings, O&M documentation, safety hazard analysis (hardware development), updated commercialization results

**Additional Requirements:**
- Investor funds expenditure accounting in company format (final Phase II status report)
- Mutually agreed upon milestone schedule from signed Customer Memorandum
- Each milestone = completion of required deliverable

**Milestone Schedule Requirements:**

- Minimum 8 CLINs (Contract Line Item Numbers), maximum 12 CLINs
- Format: CLIN (4-digit, e.g., 0009), Delivery Date (Award + X months), CLIN Description, Payment Amount (whole dollars)
- Final delivery date cannot exceed maximum 21-month performance period
- CLIN Description must include: Task (title), Deliverables (specific items), Acceptance Criteria (measurable)

**CLIN 0001 (Mandatory):**
- Delivery Date: Award + 1 month
- Deliverable: Kick-off meeting presentation (mandatory template via Virtual Contract Management Site [VCMS] in PDF)
- Acceptance Criteria: TPOC confirmation of completeness and accuracy
- Payment: Not to exceed 5% of total contract value
