# S0 UAS for Clear Air Testing

## Document Metadata
- Type: Statement of Work (SOW)
- Client/Agency: NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration)
- Program/Solicitation: 2025 NOAA 36K Clean Air Supp
- Date: January 29, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 UAS
- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
BST will provide two fully integrated S0 small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS) equipped with meteorological/oceanographic payloads for NOAA's tropical cyclone research program. The systems will undergo clear air testing prior to in-storm deployments, with delivery by June 30, 2025, under a $36,000 contract.

## Technical Approach
- **Platform Design**: Two S0 sUAS designed for deployment from NOAA WP-3D aircraft via free-fall chute with air-pressure-actuated launch tube and release mechanism
- **Sensor Integration**: Each platform equipped with METOC payloads measuring pressure, temperature, humidity, and three-dimensional wind components
- **Communication Architecture**: Radio line-of-sight command & control (C2) link with 150 nautical mile range; telemetry loss tolerance <1 minute
- **Autonomous Operations**: Full autonomous flight with three pre-programmed flight profiles; payload data transmission via C2 link
- **Testing Protocol**: Clear air calibration mission (CM) in low-wind conditions to validate operational parameters before storm deployment

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS
- **Configuration**: Fully integrated sUAS with METOC payloads
- **Sensors**: 
  - Pressure, temperature, humidity measurement
  - Turbulence probe (5Hz or greater sampling rate for 3D wind components)
- **Deployment Method**: Free-fall deployment from WP-3D aircraft at 5,000–12,000 feet altitude
- **Design Speed**: 250 KIAS nominal; demonstrated reliability at 210 KIAS
- **Endurance**: Minimum 1.5 hours flight duration with payload
- **Communication Range**: 150 nautical miles line-of-sight C2 link
- **Operational Philosophy**: Designed to be expendable and withstand severe turbulence

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Tropical Cyclone Observation**: Data collection within tropical cyclone boundary layer (currently under-sampled region)
- **Meteorological & Oceanographic Research**: Measurement of atmospheric parameters in harsh storm environments
- **Intensity Forecasting**: Data to improve NOAA operational models for tropical cyclone intensity change prediction
- **Situational Awareness**: Enhancement of storm awareness and forecasting capability

## Testing & Validation Requirements
- **Range Validation**: Confirm sUAS-WP-3D communication capability at 150 nautical miles
- **Endurance Test**: Validate minimum 1.5-hour flight duration with payload
- **Payload Performance**: Cross-check pressure, temperature, humidity, wind speed, and wind direction measurements against reference systems
- **Safety Compliance**: Emergency response and flight termination functionality verification
- **Data Integrity**: Validate payload data transmission against government-launched dropwindsondes
