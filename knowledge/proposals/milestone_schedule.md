## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies, in partnership with OMS and CET, is developing a UAS-based L-band radiometer system to map soil integrity via soil moisture measurements for the Department of the Air Force. The technology will enable forward air controllers to make informed decisions regarding C-130 aircraft operations on unimproved landing zones by providing high-resolution soil integrity data (cone index) derived from soil moisture measurements.

## Technical Approach
- **Foundation**: Existing UAS-based L-band radiometer previously developed and validated by BST, OMS, and CET in conjunction with NASA
- **Key Modifications**:
  - Refinement of L-band radiometer and integration of auxiliary sensors
  - Addition of surface temperature and NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) sensors to the UAS platform for real-time soil moisture calculation
- **Algorithm Development**: Creation of algorithms to compute cone index (soil integrity measure) as a function of L-band soil moisture data and soil type
- **Validation**: Local field testing followed by testing at Tyndall Air Force Base or other customer-specified locations with in situ cone index and soil moisture data collection

## Products & Capabilities Described

### UAS-Based L-Band Radiometer System
- **What it is**: An unmanned aircraft system equipped with L-band radiometric sensing for measuring soil moisture at high resolution
- **Proposed use**: Mapping soil integrity (cone index) for C-130 landing zone assessment
- **Key feature**: Existing technology being modified and refined for DAF-specific requirements

### Soil Integrity Mapping
- **Measurement basis**: Soil moisture data converted to cone index (measure of soil strength)
- **Application**: Determination of landing zone suitability for C-130 operations on unimproved surfaces
- **End users**: Forward air controllers, DAF operational planners

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary mission**: Mapping landing areas for C-130 operations on unimproved landing zones
- **Operational context**: Unimproved/austere airfield operations
- **Testing location**: Tyndall Air Force Base (primary) or other DAF-specified locations
