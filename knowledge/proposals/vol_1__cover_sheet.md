## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed a 3-month Phase I feasibility study ($50K) to develop machine learning algorithms for predicting UAS maintenance schedules and providing early warning of critical system failures. The system would use networked onboard monitoring nodes with a simple red/yellow/green diagnostic interface for non-technical operators, addressing the high catastrophic failure rate of small unmanned aircraft due to inadequate monitoring and maintenance practices.

## Technical Approach
- **Core concept**: Modular avionics monitoring system with networked onboard monitoring nodes
- **Technology**: Machine learning algorithms for subsystem state awareness and failure diagnostics
- **Interface design**: Red/yellow/green diagnostic display requiring no specialized mechanic training; delivers "decision-quality information to everyday end-users"
- **Data source**: Analysis of flight logs using unsupervised learning algorithms
- **Phase I scope**: Feasibility study of the monitoring system

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Avionics Monitoring System
- **What it is**: A modular, networked onboard monitoring system using machine learning for UAS health diagnostics
- **Proposed use**: Early warning and diagnostics of critical UAS subsystem failures
- **Deployment**: Small UAS platforms
- **User interface**: Simple red/yellow/green diagnostic system accessible to non-technical operators

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary mission**: Improving safety and reliability of small UAS operations in the national airspace
- **Target users**: UAS operators and maintenance personnel (both technical and non-technical)
- **Problem addressed**: Catastrophic UAS failure rates significantly higher than manned aircraft due to lack of onboard monitoring and systematic maintenance
