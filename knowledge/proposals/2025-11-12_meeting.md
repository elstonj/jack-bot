## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies presented Phase 1 status on a Navy SBIR contract to develop a sonobuoy-launched unmanned aerial vehicle for anti-submarine warfare (ASW) operations. The November 2025 meeting focused on ongoing magnetometer integration testing on the S0 UAS platform, including electromagnetic interference isolation work, shielding optimization, and concurrent hydrophone/acoustic sensor development.

## Technical Approach

### Magnetometer Integration Testing
BST is conducting a phased electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) testing approach:
1. **Isolation Testing**: Testing motor and batteries separately using a dedicated test rig with precise positioning (not full UAS initially)
2. **Shielding Strategy**: Modular MuMetal shielding material sourced and being tested
3. **Placement Goal**: Position magnetometer in nose or no more than 1 foot out the front of aircraft
4. **Filter Testing**: 10 Hz filter applied to frequency plots; further testing underway without filtering to characterize motor/battery EMI signatures
5. **Data Validation**: Clean 30-minute test conducted at "Sod Farm" location showing expected 60 Hz spike

### Key Findings from Testing
- Motor EMI cutoff range identified at 0-5 feet without shielding
- Uncertainty remains whether close-range interference is primarily motor or battery sourced
- Frequency domain analysis ongoing to optimize shielding placement

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS
- Experimental unmanned aerial system serving as development platform for sonobuoy deployment
- Equipped with BST SwiftCore autopilot for autonomous scuttling (automatic based on battery remaining, or commanded by operator)
- Carries integrated sensor payload (magnetometer, hydrophone, acoustic sensors)

### Magnetometer System
- **Objective**: Magnetic anomaly detection for submarine detection
- **Current Status**: Phase 1 integration and EMC testing
- **Depth Rating**: 200 feet operating depth (threshold); 400 feet objective
- **Deployment Time**: 120 seconds (threshold); 60 seconds objective
- **Operating Life**: 70 minutes (meets objective of 60-70 minute requirement)
- **Sensor Characteristics**: Omnidirectional (meets threshold; objective is higher gain/direction-finding)
- **Calibration Accuracy**: ±2 dB (meets Navy threshold; ±1 dB objective)
- **Range**: >20 nm line-of-sight (extending toward 50 nm objective)

### Hydrophone System
- **Frequency Coverage**: 1 Hz – 25 kHz (exceeds Navy objective of 0.001 Hz – 25 kHz on upper end; 1 Hz vs 0.01 Hz threshold on lower end)
- **Sensor Type**: Omnidirectional
- **Noise Performance**: Low-noise design, rated below sea-state zero conditions
- **Data Processing**: Whitened to environment for reduced uplink bandwidth; processing built into ADC
- **Calibration**: ±2 dB accuracy

### Acoustic Sensing (E-Field Sensors)
- Discussions ongoing with Ultra Maritime on integrating their new E-field sensors
- Technology covers low-end frequencies (DC – 3 kHz)
- Intended to complement hydrophone coverage

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Mission**: Anti-submarine warfare (ASW) cued search, detection, tracking, and classification
- Sonobuoy-launched aerial deployment from surface vessels
- Magnetic anomaly detection for submarine localization
- Acoustic/hydrophone sensing for contact classification
- Extended range detection capability (>20 nm, extending toward 50 nm)

## Notable Details

1. **Compliance with Navy Specs**: Document shows BST meeting or exceeding Navy thresholds on most sensor requirements; some areas still working toward objectives
2. **Modular Design Approach**: MuMetal shielding material being designed as modular between test rig and S0 UAS for efficient validation
3. **Iterative Testing Methodology**: Systematic isolation of noise sources (motor, batteries, airframe) to optimize sensor placement and shielding
4. **Uplink Bandwidth Management**: Data whitening strategy built into payload ADC to reduce transmit power/bandwidth requirements
5. **Autonomous Operations**: SwiftCore autopilot enables commanded or battery-life-based vehicle scuttling (sinking) per Navy requirement
6. **Sensor Integration Partners**: Collaboration with Ultra Maritime on E-field sensor integration suggests multi-vendor sensor fusion approach
7. **Phased Validation**: Plan includes return to full S0 UAS testing after isolated component testing and optimization complete
