## Professional Summary
Dr. Bullock is a planetary scientist specializing in planetary atmosphere origin and evolution, with particular expertise in Venus climate and atmospheric dynamics. His work combines spacecraft data analysis, climate modeling, laboratory experiments, and telescopic observations to understand how volcanism and surface-atmosphere interactions shape planetary climates.

## Expertise & Research Areas
- **Primary Focus**: Origin and evolution of planetary atmospheres (Venus, Earth, Mars, exoplanets)
- **Observational Work**: Infrared telescopic observations of Venus to determine winds and atmospheric composition
- **Experimental Work**: Laboratory simulations of Mars surface geochemistry and its climate signatures
- **Modeling**: Computer climate models for rocky planet atmosphere evolution
- **Mission Experience**: NASA Participating Scientist on Japanese Akatsuki mission at Venus; Co-Investigator on ROCKE-3D team (Goddard Institute for Space Studies) under NASA's Nexus for Exoplanet System Science Program (NExSS)
