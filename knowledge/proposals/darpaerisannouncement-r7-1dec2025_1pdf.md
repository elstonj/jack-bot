## Executive Summary
This is DARPA's master solicitation for the Expedited Research Innovation System (ERIS), a long-term, continuously open call for disruptive technology solutions relevant to national security. ERIS provides a streamlined submission pathway via video pitch for industry, academia, and individuals to present breakthrough technologies, with successful submissions becoming "post-competitive" and eligible for rapid acquisition awards through Other Transaction Authority. The program emphasizes fast-track assessment (30 days), negotiable terms, and potential for multiple awards from a single submission.

## Technical Approach
ERIS operates as a digital marketplace with the following process:
1. **Submission**: Unclassified video pitches (max 7 minutes, HD 1280x720 .mp4 format) with required supplemental slides submitted monthly through DARPAConnect.us
2. **Compliance Check**: Internal review (typically within 1 business day) for format, content, and submission completeness
3. **Assessment**: Peer panel of government, industry, and academic SMEs rates submissions as "awardable" or "non-awardable" within 30 days of monthly collection period close
4. **Marketplace Placement**: "Awardable" solutions posted for 12 months (6 months for Special Topic Areas)
5. **Negotiated Awards**: GSO-determined "awardable" solutions eligible for procurement without further competition under 10 USC 4021, 4022, 4023

## Use Cases & Applications
ERIS seeks solutions across six topic areas:
1. **Detection and tracking** of elusive objects across air, land, and space domains
2. **SWaP-C optimization** - overcoming limitations in size, weight, power, cost, and performance of sensing systems
3. **Strategic systems resilience** - technologies for critical infrastructure and military command-and-control networks/applications
4. **Chemical/biological defense** - surveillance, detection, diagnosis, countermeasures for human, agricultural, and bioeconomy threats
5. **Operational resilience** - human health/performance optimization across prepare-deploy-execute-return cycle; AI/ML convergence with chemistry/biology
6. **Complex systems metrology** - methods beyond reductive science for emergent/adaptive systems, including quantum-scale properties, material engineering, human-AI ecosystems (AI-only approaches excluded)

## Notable Details

### Eligibility & Participation
- Open to U.S.-based companies (traditional and nontraditional, large and small businesses), universities, non-profits, and individuals
- Foreign universities and research institutions may submit if compliant with international/export law
- No cost to prepare/submit; submission costs not reimbursable
- Continuous monthly collection cycles with 12:00 Noon ET cutoff on final day of each month

### Required Submission Elements
Four mandatory video content elements:
1. Problem definition and current state-of-the-art limitations
2. How solution advances state-of-the-art (innovation/uniqueness vs. current practice)
3. Team capability (key personnel vision, expertise, experience)
4. Defense/commercial market use case and impact

### Submission Requirements
- Technology Readiness Level (TRL) self-assessment (not used in evaluation, aids customer matching)
- Business size classification per SBA standards
- Traditional/non-traditional defense contractor status
- SAM.gov entity registration info
- 1,500-character abstract
- Minimum five keywords
- Four supplemental slides (ERIS Submission Info, Company Introduction, Submission Criteria Quad Chart, Solution Overview & DARPA White Space Chart)

### Assessment & Feedback
- Minimum 3-person peer panel (government, industry, academic)
- Government Selecting Official makes final awardable/non-awardable determination
- All submitters receive rating scorecards and assessor comments
- Feedback sessions available via ARI (Applied Research Institute) office hours to help unsuccessful submitters improve resubmissions
- Resubmissions allowed indefinitely while announcement remains active
- Non-government assessors operate under NDAs with ethics training required

### Organizational Conflict of Interest Mitigation
- Assessors prohibited from evaluating their own solutions or those of competitors in which they hold financial interests
- Three-member minimum peer panel and GSO oversight reduce competitive bias risk
- Consistency monitoring of assessor ratings to identify potential biases
- All assessments against published rubric on technical merit, not specific requirements

### Data Handling & IP
- Submitters retain ownership of video and submission data
- Submitters grant government non-exclusive, fully-paid, perpetual licenses for:
  - Viewing, reviewing, commenting, sharing within government and covered contractors
  - Transcription for search, accessibility, marketing, reporting, market research
  - Government training on ERIS processes and administration
- Videos remain on marketplace 12 months (enduring topics) or 6 months (Special Topics); can be removed by DARPA at any time
- Awardable status does not guarantee current or future awards
