## Phase II-E Program Overview

### Funding Structure
- Dollar amount: $25,000–$125,000 with 1:1 matching requirement
- External investor funds matched dollar-for-dollar by NASA SBIR/STTR funds
- Submission window: 12th month of Phase II through 60 days before Phase II end
- Period of performance: 6–12 months additional

### Key Eligibility Requirements
- Firm must secure an external investor partner
- Funds must be NEW funding, not previously obligated
- All external investor funds must transfer to the firm within 45 business days of Phase II-E selection notification
- Proof of fund transfer (legal binding document such as contract, not just PO) required within 45 business days or matching funds option expires
- Work must significantly advance Phase II development beyond original Phase II scope
- Request for Phase II extension may result in ineligibility for Phase II-E

### Application Components Required
1. **II-E Application Form**: SBIR contribution level, external investment amount(s), start/end dates, final TRL, justification if >12 months performance needed
2. **Letter of Commitment**: Must include investor contact info on official letterhead, commitment to provide funds within 45 days of approval, statement of how investment furthers Phase II technology or commercialization
3. **Statement of Work**: Detailed tasks, methods, schedules, milestones, deliverables; must show work separately funded by SBIR/STTR vs. external investor
4. **Cost Proposal**: Detailed budget broken into two sections (SBIR/STTR matching funds and external investment funds)
5. **Briefing Chart**: Non-proprietary overview of Phase II-E work
6. **Commercialization Plan**: Must address market feasibility, competitive advantages, key customers (including NASA), market size, time to market, management/technical personnel, production/operations plans, financial planning, and IP strategy

### Critical Timeline and Administrative Points
- No "typical" timeline from submission to decision; depends on Phase II contract status and submission timing relative to Phase I review cycles
- Firm receives "Notice of Intent" from NASA Shared Services Center (NSSC) if option exercised
- Work cannot commence until Phase II-E modification to contract is executed
- Start and end dates proposed by firm are dependent on Phase II contract end date; must be discussed with Phase II contract specialist
- Applications received after 60 days before Phase II end are deemed late and not considered

### Matching Investment Eligibility
- Can include Phase III contracts or modifications to existing contracts if awarded AFTER Phase II-E proposal submission
- Must be directly related to Phase II-E work (further R&D, manufacturing, marketing, etc.)
- Existing contracts/grants count only if directly tied to Phase II-E work and awarded post-submission

### Clarifications from NASA Officials
- External investor commitment must be made prior to award, but expenditure must be concurrent with Phase II-E performance
- NASA will NOT add investment funding to existing Phase II contract; must be separate funding vehicle
- Legal binding document (contract) required as proof; PO insufficient
- Funds must be truly for new development efforts, not completion of Phase II work
