## Executive Summary

This is mandatory compliance training for all SBIR applicants, produced by the DOC Office of Inspector General. The training covers SBIR program requirements, fraud definitions, compliance obligations (particularly around "essentially equivalent work" and Principal Investigator employment), consequences of fraud, and administrative/record-keeping best practices. Completion and certification of this training is required to be attached to any SBIR proposal submission.

## Content Overview

### SBIR Program Structure
The SBIR Program is a federal initiative with three phases:
- **Phase I**: $150K–$225K awards (up to 6 months) to determine feasibility
- **Phase II**: $1M–$1.5M awards (up to 2 years) for development of prototypes
- **Phase III**: Commercialization with no funding limits and potential for sole-source awards

### Eligibility Requirements
- For-profit Small Business Concern (≤500 employees)
- >50% owned by U.S. citizens or permanent resident aliens
- Principal Investigator must have primary employment (>50%) with the SBC
- Work must be performed in the U.S.
- Phase I: minimum 2/3 of effort by SBC; Phase II: minimum 1/2 of effort by SBC
- Cannot receive funding for essentially equivalent work already funded by federal government

## Fraud Definitions & Compliance Requirements

### Key Fraud Types Outlined

1. **Essentially Equivalent Work**: Submitting and accepting funding for substantially the same research from multiple federal agencies or funding sources
   - Defined as: Research objectives and design that are "the same or closely related" regardless of funding source
   - Submitting similar proposals to multiple agencies is permitted, but accepting multiple contracts for the same work is fraud
   - Must disclose if similar proposals are submitted elsewhere

2. **Principal Investigator Employment Violations**: PI claiming primary employment with SBC while employed full-time elsewhere

3. **Misrepresentations**: Regarding SBC size, facilities, employee counts, percentage of work performed by SBC, or references

4. **Fund Misuse**: Using SBIR funds for personal expenses

5. **Fabrication/Falsification**: In proposals or reporting results

6. **Undisclosed Self-Dealing**: Subcontracting to entities in which PI or family has financial interest

7. **False Certifications**: Certifying compliance with material facts when in violation

### Required Certifications at Proposal Submission
- SBC meets size eligibility (≤500 employees)
- >50% U.S. citizen/permanent resident ownership
- Required percentage of work will be performed by SBC (2/3 Phase I, 1/2 Phase II)
- PI is primarily employed by SBC
- SBC facilities located in U.S.
- No other federal contracts/grants for essentially equivalent work
- Disclosure of family/academic relationships with owners, employees, subcontractors

### Certifications During Award Lifecycle (Phase I & II)
- At time of award
- Before final payment (Phase I)
- Before receiving >50% of total award and before final payment (Phase II)
- Certifications address: PI primary employment, no essentially equivalent work, work completed in U.S., required percentage performed by SBC

## Consequences of Fraud

### Criminal Prosecution
- **False Statements** (18 U.S.C. § 1001): Up to 5 years imprisonment, forfeiture, $250K fine
- **Theft of Federal Property** (18 U.S.C. § 641): Up to 10 years imprisonment, forfeiture, $250K fine
- **Wire Fraud** (18 U.S.C. § 1343): Up to 20 years imprisonment, forfeiture, $250K fine
- Criminal forfeiture = full contract/grant amount; personal assets can be seized

### Civil Liability
- **False Claims Act** (31 U.S.C. §§ 3729-3733): Up to treble damages (3x actual) plus civil penalty up to $21,916 per false claim
- Includes payments where government relied on false proposal information, certifications, or reports
- Knowledge includes "deliberate ignorance" or "reckless disregard of truth"
- Whistleblowers can recover up to 30% of settlement amount

### Administrative Remedies
- Contract/grant termination
- Suspension/debarment of SBC, owner, and/or employees (typically 3 years, can be longer)
- Results in prohibition from federal contracts/grants and work as subcontractor/subgrantee

## Case Examples Provided

1. **Essentially Equivalent Work Case**: SBC owner applied for NASA funding after receiving Air Force funding for same work; subcontracted major portions in violation of terms
   - Outcome: Pled guilty to mail fraud and tax evasion; 12 months home confinement, 5 years probation; $1.4M restitution; 5-year ban from federal funding

2. **PI Employment Case**: Subject employed full-time at university falsely certified full-time employment with SBC
   - Outcome: Pled guilty to wire fraud; 3 years probation; $133,333 restitution

3. **False Certifications Case**: University director's wife owned SBC; falsely represented SBC facilities and PI employment; falsified work identity and used university personnel without consent
   - Outcome: Both convicted of conspiracy and wire fraud; 6 months prison + 3 years supervised release for subject; $390,252 forfeiture and $100,000 fine; similar penalties for wife

## Best Practices & Recommendations

### Record-Keeping Requirements
- Timesheets for all involved employees
- Financial receipts, invoices, and statements for project-related expenses
- Laboratory notebooks
- Agreements for use of research facilities (if applicable)
- Personnel logs for assistance from institutions/students

### Government Oversight
- Government retains right to examine contract/grant status at any time
- Status checks include site visits and requests for records
- Good documentation is key to successful audits

## Additional Compliance Items

### ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulation)
- 22 C.F.R. §§ 120.1-130.17
- Restricts exports of military-related goods and technology
- SBIR awardees must ensure policies/processes to protect ITAR information
- Disclosure of defense-related technical data to unauthorized persons (e.g., foreign students) violates ITAR

### Whistleblower Protection
- Federal employees, applicants, and federal contractor/grantee employees making protected disclosures are protected from retaliation
- DOC OIG receives and investigates whistleblower reprisal allegations
- Contact: wpo@oig.doc.gov, 202-482-1099, or http://www.oig.doc.gov/
