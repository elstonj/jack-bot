# Letter of Support - NASA (AFWERX SBIR Phase II Soil Moisture Mapping)

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support (draft)
- Client/Agency: Air Force Evaluation Team (for AFWERX SBIR Phase II evaluation)
- Program/Solicitation: Air Force SBIR Phase II - Soil Moisture Mapping (AFWERX)
- Date: April 25, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 sUAS, SwiftFlow, Soil Moisture Mapping System, L-band radiometer
- Key Personnel: Geoffrey L. Bland (NASA Wallops Flight Facility, Climate and Radiation Laboratory); Jack Elston (BST, document editor)

## Executive Summary
NASA's Geoffrey L. Bland provides official support for Black Swift Technologies' AFWERX SBIR Phase II proposal to develop a soil integrity mapping system for UAS platforms. Bland endorses BST's track record of translating NASA-developed core technology (L-band radiometer) into practical, operational systems currently in use across multiple NASA centers and federal agencies.

## Technical Approach
BST is proposing to map soil integrity from a UAS platform using core L-band radiometer technology developed in partnership with NASA. The system builds on previous NASA SBIR contracts and aims to create a commercializable soil moisture mapping capability.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 sUAS
- A ruggedized small UAS platform
- Developed under NASA contract NNX17CP13C
- Currently operational at two NASA centers: Langley Research Center and Ames Research Center
- Enabling agency-wide science and technology research

### SwiftFlow Atmospheric Probe
- An atmospheric measurement probe
- Developed under NASA contract NNX17CP13C
- Described as a novel, practical system supporting research efforts

### Soil Moisture Mapping System
- Microwave-based soil moisture sensor system
- Developed under NASA contract 80NSSC18C0002
- Uses L-band radiometer technology
- Proposed Phase II effort will develop soil integrity mapping variant

### L-band Radiometer
- Core technology developed in partnership with NASA
- Foundation for soil moisture/integrity mapping capability

## Use Cases & Applications
- Soil moisture and soil integrity mapping
- Potential commercial applications following SBIR development
- Support for NASA Science Mission Directorate (SMD) research
- Support for NASA Aeronautics Research Mission Directorate (ARMD) research
- Operations for USGS and NOAA
