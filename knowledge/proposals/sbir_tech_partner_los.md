## Executive Summary
This letter of support endorses Black Swift Technologies' Phase II SBIR proposal for developing an expendable, sonobuoy-launched unmanned aerial vehicle equipped with Magnetic Anomaly Detection (MAD) and passive acoustic sensors for Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW) operations. The letter confirms successful Phase I completion and commits unnamed technology partner support for Phase II prototype fabrication and validation.

## Technical Approach
- Integration of high-sensitivity payloads (MAD and passive acoustic sensors) into BST's S0 platform
- Design adherence to strict Size, Weight, and Power (SWaP) constraints of the LAU-126A Sonobuoy Launch Container
- Magnetic noise reduction through:
  - Magnetic shielding
  - Boom extension
- Edge processing via NVIDIA Jetson AGX Orin

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0 Platform**
- Expendable sonobuoy-launched UAV
- Capable of accommodating high-sensitivity payloads within LAU-126A container constraints
- Platform-induced magnetic noise floor: ~1.5 pT/√Hz at 5 feet separation (Phase I result)
- Meets Navy threshold requirement for magnetic noise
- Proposed to enable ASW target detection, tracking, and classification

**Sensors**
- Magnetic Anomaly Detection (MAD) sensor (specific model not named)
- Passive acoustic sensors (likely QuSpin QTFM Gen-2 and CRT Hydrophone based on bracketed examples)
- NVIDIA Jetson AGX Orin for edge signal processing

## Use Cases & Applications
- Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW) operations
- Cued search, detection, tracking, and classification of submarines
- Naval operations using sonobuoy launch platforms

## Key Results (Phase I)
- Feasibility of integrating MAD and passive acoustic sensors into S0 platform established
- Magnetic noise characterization: ~1.5 pT/√Hz at 5 feet separation distance
- Successfully demonstrated compatibility with LAU-126A Sonobuoy Launch Container SWaP requirements
- Performance met Navy threshold requirements
