## Professional Summary
Dr. Joshua B. Wadler is an Assistant Professor of Meteorology at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University with world-leading expertise in experimental design, turbulence measurements, and small uncrewed aerial systems (sUAS) operations in both low-wind and high-wind conditions. He holds a Ph.D. in Meteorology and Physical Oceanography from the University of Miami (2020) and has extensive operational experience with NOAA's Hurricane Field Program, including leadership of scientific missions and Category 5 hurricane observations.

## Key Research Areas & Expertise

### Primary Specializations
- **Atmospheric Boundary Layer Measurements**: Turbulence-quality measurements in both low-wind and high-wind environments
- **Small Uncrewed Aerial Systems (sUAS)**: Experimental design and validation using emerging sUAS technologies in extreme conditions
- **Tropical Cyclone Dynamics**: Air-sea interactions, boundary layer thermodynamics, rapid intensification processes, environmental wind shear effects
- **Field Operations**: Substantial experience developing and implementing operational field plans with NOAA Hurricane Field Program

### Specific Technical Focus Areas
- Air-sea exchange coefficients in low-wind conditions
- Tropical cyclone boundary layer thermodynamics and intensity change
- Environmental wind shear effects on hurricane intensity
- Downdrafts and boundary layer evolution in rapidly intensifying hurricanes
- Turbulent characteristics in hurricane eye and eyewall regions
- Wind measurement instrumentation (StreamSondes)
