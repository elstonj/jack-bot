## Document Metadata
- Type: SBIR CCRPP (Commercialization, Catalysts, and Restoration Program) Application Summary
- Client/Agency: NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration)
- Program/Solicitation: 2012 SBIR Program; CCRPP 2017 Phase II continuation
- Phase II Contract: NNX14CG09C
- Date: March 31, 2017
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: SMM sUAS (Soil Moisture Mapping small Unmanned Aircraft System), SuperSwift sUAS, L-band radiometer
- Key Personnel: 
  - Dr. Maciej Stachura (Principal Investigator/Project Manager)
  - Dr. Jack Steward Elston (Corporate/Business Official)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed a CCRPP commercialization effort focused on two core technologies from their Soil Moisture Mapping small UAS (SMM sUAS) project: an L-band radiometer and the SuperSwift sUAS platform. The project aimed to accelerate commercialization of these technologies across NASA programs and commercial markets, leveraging investments from four customer/investor entities and identified interest from six additional potential customers across satellite calibration/validation, precision agriculture, and wildland firefighting applications.

## Technical Approach
- Commercialization of L-band radiometer technology (radiometric soil moisture measurement capability)
- Development/refinement of SuperSwift sUAS as the operational platform
- Integration of these two core technologies into a marketable system
- Technology Readiness Level progression: TRL 6 (beginning) to TRL 9 (end)

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift sUAS
- Small unmanned aircraft system designed as platform for soil moisture mapping
- Represents key technology developed during Phase II/Phase II-E SBIR work
- Intended for multiple end-user applications

### L-band Radiometer
- Radiometric sensor for soil moisture measurement
- Core payload technology for the SMM sUAS
- Represents bulk of Phase II/Phase II-E development work

### SMM sUAS (Soil Moisture Mapping small UAS)
- Complete system integrating L-band radiometer and SuperSwift sUAS
- Operational soil moisture measurement platform

## Use Cases & Applications
1. **Satellite Calibration/Validation (Cal/Val)** - validating space-based soil moisture sensors
2. **Precision Agriculture** - soil moisture monitoring for agricultural management
3. **Wildland Firefighting** - soil moisture mapping for fire management and prevention

## Commercialization Strategy
- Four entities investing in project (also serving as customers)
- Six additional potential customers identified and engaged
- Customer/investor base directly steered technology development needs
- NASA investment supplemented by non-NASA matched funding aligned with NASA interests
- CCRPP funding intended to accelerate market expansion across all three application areas

## Notable Details
- Strong demonstrated customer demand identified
- Multi-stakeholder investment model (4 investor/customers + 6 potential customers)
- Clear pathway from NASA SBIR Phase II development to commercial deployment
- Represents technology commercialization and market expansion, not fundamental research
