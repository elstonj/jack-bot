# Uncrewed Aircraft System Data Assimilation for Improved Wildland Fire Fighting Decision Support

## Document Metadata
- Type: NASA SBIR Phase I Proposal Briefing Chart
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR 2023, Topic A3.02-1718
- Date: March 13, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 UAS
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (PI)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to improve wildland firefighting safety by developing algorithms and data assimilation methods to generate automated UAS sampling patterns for gathering accurate wind and turbulence profiles in the lowest 5000 ft of atmosphere. BST will partner with NCAR/RAL and Colorado's Center of Excellence for Advanced Technology Aerial Firefighting to integrate advanced meteorological products into ATAK (Army Tactical Assault Kit), with field validation on 1-2 high-risk fire days using the S0 UAS equipped with meteorological sensors.

## Technical Approach
- Deploy teams of small, purpose-built UAS (S0) to make targeted observations of winds and turbulence
- Use advanced data assimilation methods developed at NCAR to improve forecasting
- Develop algorithms for automatically generating UAS sampling patterns based on current forecast models and observations
- Integrate meteorological products into existing wildland firefighting decision support tools (ATAK)
- Plan for future integration of wind/turbulence sensing capabilities onto other UAS and crewed aircraft
- Create sequential S0 flights for validation of forecasting improvements over 3-12 hour periods

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS
- Small, purpose-built UAS designed for wildfire response operations
- Can be equipped with meteorological sensors for wind and turbulence measurement
- Capable of operating in hazardous environments with particulates and severe turbulence
- Intended for safe integration into airspace with manned aircraft through UTM-like systems

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Application
- **Wildland Firefighting**: Gathering low-level wind and turbulence data to improve safety of both airborne and surface-based firefighting assets; providing accurate 3D wind and turbulence profiles to inform tactical decisions

### Secondary Applications
- Volcano monitoring (hazardous particulate environments)
- Federal and commercial UTM applications
- NASA Earth Science applications including:
  - TCP (Tropospheric Composition Program)
  - Applied Sciences Air Quality Program
  - CALIPSO mission support
  - Aura mission support
  - CATS (Cloud-Aerosol Transport System)
  - OCO-3 (Orbiting Carbon Observatory 3)
  - Earth Ventures airborne field campaigns

## Technical Objectives & Specific Deliverables
1. Develop algorithms and methods for automatically generating UAS sampling patterns based on forecast models and observations
2. Create operational requirements, risk assessment, and mitigation documentation for operating S0 UAS in wildfire response
3. Gather meteorological data during 1-2 high-risk fire days from locations identified through data denial experiments
4. Demonstrate and quantify forecasting improvements for low-level wind fields over 3-12 hours post-sampling using sequential S0 flights

## Partnerships
- NCAR/RAL (National Center for Atmospheric Research/Research Applications Laboratory) - advanced data assimilation methods
- Colorado's Center of Excellence for Advanced Technology Aerial Firefighting

## Notable Details
- Critical gap identified: limited observations of winds and turbulence in lowest 5000 ft of atmosphere during wildfire operations
- Integration with existing tools (ATAK) rather than developing new platforms
- Focus on improving UTM safety for mixed manned/unmanned aircraft operations in wildfire airspace
- NASA Ames identified as primary customer with multiple other NASA Earth Science program applications
- Emphasis on hazardous environment capability (particulates, turbulence) as competitive advantage
