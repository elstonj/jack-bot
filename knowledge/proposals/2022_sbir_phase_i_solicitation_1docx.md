# 2022 NASA SBIR Phase I Solicitation

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Solicitation (Program Description & Instructions)
- **Client/Agency:** National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)
- **Program/Solicitation:** NASA Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Program, Fiscal Year 2022, Phase I
- **Date:** January 6 - March 9, 2022 (submission period)
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None mentioned in this document
- **Key Personnel:** Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is the official NASA SBIR Phase I solicitation for FY 2022. It provides comprehensive guidance for small businesses to submit proposals for early-stage R&D funding ($150,000 maximum for Phase I). The document outlines eligibility requirements, proposal submission procedures, evaluation criteria, and program structure including Phase II and Phase III opportunities.

## Key Program Details

### Funding & Timeline
- **Phase I Maximum Award:** $150,000 (or $156,500 with Technical and Business Assistance)
- **Phase I Period of Performance:** Up to 6 months
- **Expected Selection:** May 2022
- **Historical Award Rate:** 20-22% of eligible proposals
- **Phase II Maximum Award:** $750,000 (potentially up to $1,000,000 pending appropriations)
- **Phase III:** Commercialization phase, funded from non-SBIR sources

### Eligibility Requirements

**Small Business Concern (SBC) Requirements:**
- Combined with affiliates: maximum 500 employees
- SBC must be primary performer (minimum 67% of research effort in-house)
- Maximum 33% of effort may be performed by subcontractors/consultants
- All work must be performed in the United States (rare exceptions with written approval)
- American-made products/equipment preferred
- Cannot be majority-owned by multiple venture capital firms, hedge funds, or private equity firms

**Principal Investigator (PI) Requirements:**
- Full-time employment (40+ hours/week) OR half-time (19.9+ hours/week)
- Must direct project planning and lead technical effort
- Must make substantial personal contributions
- Subject to NASA approval
- Cannot exceed permitted concurrent commitments

**Registrations Required:**
- Small Business Administration (SBA) Firm Registry with SBC Control ID
- System for Award Management (SAM) registration under NAICS codes 541713 or 541715
- Unique Entity Identifier (UEI) - replacing DUNS number as of April 2022

### Subcontractor/Consultant Restrictions
- Combined subcontractor and consultant effort cannot exceed 33% of total R&D effort (calculated as: subcontractor cost plus G&A divided by total contract price less profit)
- Formula provided: If total price is $99,500 with $3,000 profit, subcontractor max is ~$30,975

### Proposal Submission Requirements

**Format Requirements:**
- Maximum 19 pages (8.5" x 11") including all graphics, tables, and table of contents
- 1.0 inch margins
- Minimum 10-point font
- Headers must include: firm name, proposal number, project title
- Footers must include: page number and proprietary markings
- No classified information permitted

**Required Technical Proposal Components (10 parts, must be in order):**
1. Table of Contents (0.5 page suggested)
2. Identification and Significance of the Innovation (5 pages suggested)
3. Technical Objectives (1 page suggested)
4. Work Plan (5 pages suggested) - must include detailed methods, task descriptions, schedules, resource allocations, task hours, milestones
5. Related R&D (1 page suggested)
6. Key Personnel and Bibliography (2.5 pages suggested)
7. The Market Opportunity (1 page suggested)
8. Facilities/Equipment (1 page suggested)
9. Subcontractors and Consultants (varies)
10. Related, Essentially Equivalent, and Duplicate Proposals and Awards (varies)

**Required Supporting Forms/Documents:**
- Proposal Contact Information
- Proposal Certifications (electronic endorsement required)
- Proposal Summary (public information, no proprietary data)
- Proposal Budget Form
- Briefing Chart (public information, no proprietary data)
- NASA Evaluation License Application (if using NASA Technology Available)
- Technical and Business Assistance (TABA) request (optional)
- I-Corps Interest Form
- Firm Certifications
- Audit Information
- Prior Awards Addendum
- Commercial Metrics Survey (CMS)

### Budget & Cost Considerations

**Travel:**
- Highly discouraged in Phase I due to short duration and limited budget
- Requires justification if needed
- Requires Contracting Officer and Technical Monitor approval

**Foreign Vendors:**
- Subject to policy review due to short Phase I timeframe
- Delays or non-award possible if foreign vendors proposed
- Requires "Request to Use a Foreign Vendor" form with justification
- Cannot use vendors from Designated Countries list

**Technical and Business Assistance (TABA):**
- Optional supplement of $6,500 for Phase I
- Can fund services such as: IP protection, market research, regulatory planning, manufacturing planning, technical problem-solving, commercialization strategy
- Approval not guaranteed; subject to Contracting Officer review

### Market Opportunity Section Requirements

Offerors must address:
- Business economics and market drivers in target industry
- Market validation approach
- Customer identification and go-to-market strategy
- Competitive landscape description
- Anticipated changes in competition by market entry
- Key commercialization risks
- Commercialization approach and strategy
- Potential economic benefits and revenue estimates with underlying assumptions
- Resources needed for commercialization implementation

### Use of NASA Technology

**NASA Technology Available (TAV):**
- Over 1,400 NASA patents available for licensing
- Over 1,000 software tools/applications in NASA Software Catalog
- Available "as is" with no guarantee of infusion or commercial viability
- Use of TAV is optional and not a selection factor
- Requires patent evaluation license or Software Usage Agreement (SUA)

**NASA Patent Licensing:**
- Nonexclusive, royalty-free evaluation license during SBIR Phase I
- Application through NASA's Automated Licensing System (ATLAS)
- Successful awardees can negotiate nonexclusive or exclusive commercialization licenses
- Access to inventors/knowledge transfer available with NDA/Space Act Agreement
- Knowledge transfer reimbursement may be required

**NASA Software:**
- Software Usage Agreement (SUA) required
- Nonexclusive, royalty-free basis
- Requested from NASA Center Software Release Authority after contract award

### Additional Programs & Support

**I-Corps™ Training:**
- Partnership between NASA and National Science Foundation
- Available to Phase I awardees
- Approximately 25 grants anticipated, up to $10,000 each for shortened I-Corps training
- Focuses on Business Model Canvas, Customer Development Interview process, customer discovery

**Mentor-Protégé Program (MPP):**
- Provides incentives for NASA contractors to mentor Phase II SBIR companies
- Award Fee Pilot Program for mentors
- Evaluation based on technology transfer and business development for TRL advancement

### Proposal Restrictions

- Each proposal must be based on unique innovation
- Limited to one subtopic per proposal
- Maximum 10 proposals per offeror per solicitation
- Maximum 5 awards per offeror under this solicitation
- Substantially equivalent proposals to multiple subtopics will be rejected
- NASA will NOT move proposals between subtopics

### Proposal Content Requirements

**Must Address:**
- Scientific, technical, and commercial merit and feasibility
- Relevance and significance to NASA interests
- Preliminary strategy for technical development, market validation, and business factors
- Competitive patent landscape review (USPTO search recommended)

**Must NOT Contain:**
- Classified information
- Submissions to other subtopics (substantially equivalent proposals)
- System studies, market research only, routine engineering, or modifications of existing products without substantive innovation
- References to unsubmitted related proposals

**Proprietary Information Handling:**
- Unsuccessful proposals remain property of applicant
- Government retains copies per records retention schedule
- Proprietary information treated confidentially if clearly marked with specific legends
- Contact Information and Proposal Summary are public information
- FOIA applies

### Compliance & Certifications

**Federal Registrations:**
- SAM registration required; active by proposal deadline
- Must validate mandatory information including UEI, DUNS (transitioning to UEI), CAGE code
- Representations and certifications must be completed electronically in SAM

**Certifications Required:**
- Small Business Concern certification
- Firm size, ownership, control certifications per 13 CFR §§ 121.701-121.705
- SBIR program-specific certifications
- Transition rate benchmarks apply to companies with 21+ Phase I awards in past 5 years (minimum 0.25 Phase II to Phase I ratio)

**Compliance Standards:**
- Software development must comply with NASA Procedural Requirements (NPR) 7150.2A
- Human/animal subjects: approved protocol required; NOT allowed for Phase I
- HSPD-12/PIV credentials: required if accessing Federal facilities/systems for 6+ consecutive months

### Oversight & Recourse

**Fraud/False Statements:**
- Violations subject to Federal Criminal False Statements Act penalties (up to 5 years imprisonment)
- Office of Inspector General (OIG) has access to all proposals
- OIG Hotline: 1-800-424-9183

**Procurement Ombudsman Program:**
- Available for concerns about solicitation terms, evaluation processes
- Must first address concerns with Contracting Officer
- Cognizant Ombudsman: Jason Detko, Deputy Assistant Administrator for Procurement
- Contact: agency-procurementombudsman@nasa.gov, 202-358-4483

### Program Administration

**Evaluation & Selection:**
- Electronic Handbook (EHB) system at https://sbir.gsfc.nasa.gov/submissions/login
- All submissions must be electronic through EHB
- Administrative screening for compliance with formatting requirements
- Noncompliant proposals declined without evaluation
- Historically 20-22% award rate

**Questions & Support:**
- Procurement-related questions deadline: March 2, 2022, 5:00 p.m. ET
- Only clarification questions answered during open period
- Subtopic intent questions not addressed
- NASA SBIR/STTR Help Desk: sbir@reisystems.com
- Submission platform support: https://sbir.gsfc.nasa.gov/submissions/login

**Phase I to Phase II Transition:**
- Only Phase I awardees eligible for Phase II
- Separate Phase II solicitation will be published
- Companies must meet transition rate benchmarks (0.25 Phase II per Phase I) if they have 21+ Phase I awards

### Post-Award Opportunities

- Phase II continuation (more comprehensive R&D, $750k-$1M+)
- Post-Phase II initiatives for extended development
- Phase III commercialization (non-SBIR funded)
- TABA supplemental funding for Phase I
- I-Corps training grants for Phase I awardees

## Notable Details

- **No commitment to fund:** NASA may elect to make no awards in any specific subtopic
- **Equity-free funding:** Program provides seed-stage funding without equity requirements
- **Mission alignment:** Focus on transforming scientific discovery into products with NASA infusion potential and commercial viability
- **Timeline:** Approximately 2 months from deadline (March 9) to selection negotiations (May 2022)
- **Place of performance:** All work must be in U.S. except in rare, approved circumstances
- **No system studies:** Awards limited to substantive innovations, not routine development or market studies
- **SBA oversight:** All SBIR/STTR programs governed by Small Business Administration with congressionally mandated participation metrics
