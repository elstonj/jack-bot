## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies developed a compact NDVI/Thermal sensor board during NASA SBIR Phase I for measuring vegetation indices and ground temperature to correct soil moisture measurements from L-band radiometers. The six-sensor board combines red and near-infrared optical sensing (nadir and zenith directions) with thermal temperature sensing, designed to integrate into small unmanned aircraft systems.

## Technical Approach
The NDVI/Thermal board uses the following components:
- **Optical Sensing**: Si-J146 photodiodes with amplifiers (Electro Optical Components), spectral range 400-1100 nm, with filters to sample red (550-700 nm) and near-infrared (700-1300 nm) wavelengths from both nadir and zenith directions
- **Thermal Sensing**: MLX90615 sensor for surface and cloud-bottom infrared (10 µm) temperature measurement
- **Data Processing**: STM32F103 Cortex-M3 microcontroller samples all sensors and outputs digital data packets

## Products & Capabilities Described

**NDVI/Thermal Sensor Board**
- **What it is**: A multi-sensor payload for small unmanned aircraft systems capable of measuring sky-corrected Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) and surface/cloud-bottom temperature
- **Proposed use**: Provide auxiliary measurements to correct L-band radiometer soil moisture data; support precision agriculture applications
- **Key specifications**:
  - Dual-direction sensing (nadir and zenith) for improved NDVI accuracy
  - Red band: 550-700 nm
  - Near-infrared band: 700-1300 nm
  - Thermal band: 10 µm wavelength
  - Small size, low weight, low power consumption for sUAS integration

## Use Cases & Applications

- **Soil Moisture Mapping**: Primary application is providing ground truth corrections for L-band radiometer soil moisture measurements via small unmanned aircraft
- **Precision Agriculture**: Identified as largest potential market for sUAS in US with 2.2+ million farms; NDVI measurements support crop health monitoring and field management decisions

## Notable Details

- **Novel features**: Combination of nadir and zenith-facing sensors with integrated thermal measurement; previously unavailable in compact, low-cost form factor
- **Competitive advantages**: Superior to low-cost EO cameras with filters (lacks scientific precision) and TetraCam sensors (higher cost, no thermal); small size and low power enable sUAS integration without significant payload penalty
- **Technology significance**: Classified as "Modification to Existing Technology" rather than breakthrough
- **State of development**: Prototype stage (11/2013), with planned Phase II flight testing using Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS to validate performance
- **Partnership**: Collaboration with University of Colorado Boulder's Electrical Engineering department (Albin Gasiewski involvement)
- **Market context**: References AUVSI research identifying precision agriculture as single largest sUAS market opportunity
