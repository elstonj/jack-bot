## Executive Summary
This is the Department of War's 2026 Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Broad Agency Announcement establishing the framework, eligibility requirements, proposal preparation instructions, and evaluation criteria for small business submissions across six quarterly releases throughout fiscal year 2026. The BAA solicits innovative R&D proposals in defense-related technology areas aligned with DoW modernization goals and Critical Technology Areas.

## Document Purpose & Structure
This is a solicitation framework document, not a technical proposal or capability brief. It provides:
- Program description and three-phase structure (Phase I, Phase II, Phase III)
- Eligibility and certification requirements for small business concerns (SBCs)
- Registration and compliance procedures (SAM, SBA Company Registry, DSIP)
- Detailed proposal preparation instructions
- Evaluation criteria and selection procedures
- Contract requirements and IP/patent policies

## Key Program Features

### Three-Phase Program Structure
- **Phase I**: 6-12 months; determines scientific/technical feasibility and commercial merit; generally $150K-$250K range (not specified in this excerpt)
- **Phase II**: 24 months; continues R&D development; available to Phase I awardees or Direct-to-Phase II applicants
- **Phase III**: Commercialization phase; funded by non-SBIR sources (private sector or other federal agencies)

### Critical Technology Areas (OUSW(R&E) CTAs)
- Applied Artificial Intelligence (AAI)
- Biomanufacturing (BIO)
- Contested Logistics Technologies (LOG)
- Quantum & Battlefield Information Dominance (QBID)
- Scaled Hypersonics (SHY)
- Scaled Directed Energy (SCADE)

### Component Technology Priority Areas (TPAs)
- FutureG, Trusted AI and Autonomy, Biotechnology, Advanced Computing and Software
- Integrated Sensing and Cyber, Directed Energy, Hypersonics, Microelectronics
- Quantum Science, Space Technology, Renewable Energy, Advanced Materials
- Human-Machine Interfaces, Combat Casualty Care, Military Operational Medicine
- Nuclear, Sustainment & Logistics (and others)

## Eligibility Requirements

**Small Business Concern (SBC) Must:**
1. Meet SBA definition per 13 CFR §§ 701-705 at time of award
2. Phase I: Conduct minimum 2/3 of research/analytical work
3. Phase II: Conduct minimum 50% of research/analytical work
4. Principal Investigator: Primary employment with SBC (>50% time) at award and during performance
5. All R&D work performed in United States
6. Joint ventures/limited partnerships permitted if qualifies as small business per 13 CFR 121.702

**Ineligibility:**
- SBCs with majority ownership by multiple venture capital firms, hedge funds, or private equity firms (unless Service/Component authorizes; foreign ownership disclosures required for >5% stakes)

## Performance Requirements for Experienced Firms

SBCs with multiple prior SBIR/STTR awards must meet minimum benchmarks:
- **Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate**: Minimum progression requirement
- **Commercialization Benchmark**: Advancement toward commercialization

Firms failing benchmarks subject to cap on new awards:
- Army: 20 Phase I/Direct-to-Phase II awards max
- Navy: 20 Phase I/Direct-to-Phase II awards max
- Air Force: 20 Phase I/Direct-to-Phase II awards max
- Other DoW Components combined: 20 Phase I/Direct-to-Phase II awards max

## Direct-to-Phase II Program

Eligible SBCs without prior Phase I award may submit Direct-to-Phase II proposals if they:
- Provide documentation demonstrating Phase I-level feasibility (not from prior federal SBIR/STTR funding)
- Demonstrate SBC/PI substantially performed the work
- Possess ownership or licensing of associated intellectual property

## Critical Certifications & Registrations Required

**System for Award Management (SAM)**: Must register to receive UEI and CAGE code

**SBA Company Registry**: Must register and provide Control ID and Proof of Registration at submission

**Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP)**: All users require individual Login.gov-authenticated accounts; Firm Admin must create Firm PIN and control access

**Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC)**: 
- Level 1: Annual self-assessment (15 FAR 52.204-21 requirements)
- Level 2: Annual affirmation + triennial assessment (110 NIST SP 800-171 Rev 2 requirements)
- Level 3: Level 2 completion + triennial DCMA assessment (24 NIST SP 800-172 requirements)

**Due Diligence Security Review**: DoW assesses national security risks via:
- Cybersecurity practices review
- Patent analysis
- Employee analysis and foreign ownership ties
- Financial obligations (debt, equity, surety)
- Open-source confirmation and verification

**Foreign Affiliations Disclosure**: Required; may request copies of contractual/financial obligations with foreign entities from 5-year preceding period

**OCI (Organizational Conflicts of Interest)**: Per FAR 9.5; disclosure and mitigation plans required; DoW may prohibit concurrent SETA/A&AS services and technical performance

**Human Subject Research (HSR)**: Requires federal assurance, IRB approval (1-3 month average), DoW Service/Component approval (3-6 months); no funding until all approvals granted

**Animal Subject Research**: Requires IACUC and DoW veterinarian approval; no funding until approved

**Recombinant DNA**: Requires institutional biosafety committee approval

**Export Control (ITAR/EAR)**: 
- ITAR-controlled: Complete DD Form 2345 certification
- EAR-controlled: Obtain Bureau of Industry and Security export licenses via SNAP-R system

**BlueUAS Certification**: Not required unless specified in topic; DoW does not reimburse certification costs

## Proposal Submission Structure (DSIP)

**Volume 1: Proposal Cover Sheet**
- Technical abstract (max 3,000 characters)
- Anticipated benefits and commercial applications (max 3,000 characters)
- Percentage of Work (POW) certification (auto-calculated from Volume 3)
- Certifications of SBC status and program requirements

**Volume 2: Technical Volume**
- Single PDF file with graphics
- Virus-checked, not password-protected or encrypted
- No embedded active media (videos, animations)
- Format specifications per Service/Component instructions

**Volume 3: Cost Volume**
- Budget detail and justification
- Drives POW calculations

**Volume 4: Company Commercialization Report**
- Commercialization strategy and capability

**Volume 5: Supporting Documents**
- OCI disclosures and mitigation plans
- DD Form 2345 (if ITAR-applicable)
- Joint venture representations (if applicable)
- Prior/current/pending support disclosures

**Volume 6: Fraud, Waste and Abuse Training**
- Completion certification required

**Volume 7: Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries**
- Required due diligence security review documentation

## Awards & Contracting

**Multiple awards anticipated** per topic; each proposal evaluated independently; DoW may award:
- FAR-based contracts (Firm-Fixed-Price, Level of Effort, Labor Hour, Time & Material, Cost-Plus-Fixed-Fee)
- Other Transactions (OTs) under 10 U.S.C. 4021, 4022, or 4023
- Phase II Enhancement funding (matching additional SBIR funds with non-SBIR DoW or private sector funds)

**Commercialization Readiness Program (CRP)**: Available through Army, Navy, Air Force; OSW Accelerated Research for Transition (ART) for DoW components

## Additional Requirements

**Fraud Prevention**: DoW Office of Inspector General Hotline: (800) 424-9098; violations of Federal Criminal False Statement Act (18 U.S.C. § 1001) punishable by up to $10,000 fine and 5 years imprisonment

**Contract Requirements Upon Award:**
- Copyrights, patents, and technical data rights per 5.0 section
- Invention reporting requirements
- Final technical reports required (Phase I through Phase III)
- Agency recovery authority for certain circumstances

**Evaluation Criteria**: Technical merit, feasibility, innovation potential, and commercialization potential

## Notable Details

1. **Non-Prescriptive Topic Approach**: Topics intentionally avoid prescribing specific technical solutions; encourages novel approaches aligned with DoW modernization

2. **Innovation Open Topics**: Special category using generalized mission requirements or technology areas to adapt commercial products to capability gaps

3. **Technical and Business Assistance (TABA)**: Discretionary; availability varies by Service/Component

4. **State SBIR/STTR Support**: Programs available in many states for matching funds and Phase III assistance

5. **DSIP Accessibility**: Only accessible from within U.S. (50 states, territories, Puerto Rico, Marshall Islands, Micronesia, Palau, D.C.)

6. **No Unsolicited Proposals**: Only topic-responsive proposals considered; unsolicited proposals rejected

7. **Commercialization Focus**: Phase II Enhancement policy encourages technology transition to DoW acquisition programs and private sector
