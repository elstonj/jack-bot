# N251-016 Expendable Sonobuoy-Launched Unmanned Aerial Vehicle for ASW Cued Search, Detection, Tracking, and Classification

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is developing an expendable sonobuoy-launched unmanned aerial vehicle (SLUAV) for anti-submarine warfare (ASW) applications, integrating a QuSpin magnetometer and CRT-designed hydrophone onto the S0 air-deployed platform. The Phase I effort focuses on subsystem requirements analysis, platform modifications to accommodate sensor payloads, and validation testing to meet Navy specifications for detection, tracking, and classification of submarine signatures.

## Technical Approach

### Primary Mission Concept
- Deploy S0 UAS via LAU-126A Sonobuoy Launch Container (SLC) from P-8 Poseidon maritime patrol aircraft
- Integrate three key sensor systems: QuSpin QTFM Gen-2 magnetometer, CRT hydrophone, and Jetson AGX Orin computing platform
- Magnetometer mounts on extended boom at aircraft nose to separate from power/propulsion electronics
- Hydrophone deploys via fiber-optic spool after water landing; sensor sinks to depth, transmits acoustic data back to UAS via fiber optic cable
- UAS broadcasts integrated sensor data via primary communications antenna to P-8

### Phase I Tasks
- **B.1 (Requirements Analysis & Preliminary Design):** Documented subsystem specifications, integrated requirements from QuSpin and CRT, established compute platform specs
- **B.2 (Platform Modifications):** Evaluated compatibility changes to S0 for SLC integration; ground and flight testing of magnetometer integration
- **B.3 (Sensor Integration & Testing):** In-progress; includes QuSpin magnetometer testing integrated into S0, CRT hydrophone testing and calibration
- **B.4 (Prototype Fabrication & Validation):** Planned; S0 compatibility testing with SLC launch conditions, hydrophone deployment validation

### Optional Tasks
- **O.1:** Local flight testing with integrated magnetometer and hydrophone payloads
- **O.2:** Certification paperwork for Phase II Navy flight testing
- **O.3:** Design modifications for Cartridge Actuated Device (CAD) launch

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 Air-Deployed UAS
**What it is:** Small, low-cost, modular, electric fixed-wing UAS originally developed under SBIR grant for air deployment from P-3 Orion via Common Launch Tube; hand-deployable from aircraft ramp/window; operationally deployed with NOAA; TRL 9.

**Specifications:**
- **Weight:** 2.75 lbs max takeoff weight
- **Dimensions:** 32.5-inch wingspan
- **Flight Performance:**
  - Cruise speed: 50 kts (dashes to 70 kts; planned modification for efficient 70 kts cruise)
  - Max speed: 100 mph
  - Flight time: 105-120 minutes nominal
  - Service ceiling: 15,000 ft
  - Max winds endured: 50 mph
  - Operating altitude: 30–10,000 ft (exceeds Navy requirement of 50–2,000 ft)
  - Range: 270 nm LOS (Navy requirement: 20 nm, extending to 50 nm)
  - Temperature tolerance: -40°C – 50°C
  - Weather: Heavy rain with zero visibility capability
- **Payload:** ~100 grams capacity; nose cone 1.8 inches diameter
- **Autonomy:** Pre-programmed waypoint flight with hooks for autonomous queuing (tested without MAD payloads); planned evolution toward autonomous target tracking cued by magnetometer

**Deployment Configuration:**
- Folds and stows within BST-designed deployment tube (5 in. × 37 in. currently, target 4.875 in. × 36 in.)
- Full assembly (UAV + deployment tube) = 15.6 lbs, fitting within LAU-126A SLC weight limit (39 lbs)
- Weight, size, and center of gravity match existing 36B BT-type expendable assets to ensure identical launch dynamics
- Design withstands MIL-L-81745B loading specification (~10G for 100ms)
- BST has successfully dropped 30+ systems via P-3 free-fall chute without separation concerns
- Shelf life: 5 years (dependent on temperature and pre-storage state of charge)

**Proposed Use:** Integration platform for Navy magnetometer and hydrophone sensors for ASW detection and classification; expansion use cases with USAF (KC-135 magnetic mapping), mining industry applications.

### QuSpin QTFM Gen-2 Magnetometer
**What it is:** Optically pumped quantum magnetometer for magnetic anomaly detection (MAD) of submarine propulsion signatures.

**Specifications:**
- **Measurement Bandwidth:** 500 Hz (exceeds Navy requirement of 100 Hz)
- **Measurement Range:** 150 μT (exceeds requirement of 100 μT)
- **Temperature Range:** -15°C – 55°C (exceeds requirement of 0°C – 50°C)
- **In-Air Noise Level:** 3 pT/√Hz (exceeds requirement of 20 pT/√Hz by 6.7x)
- **Heading Error:** <3 nT uncompensated (meets requirement)

**SWaP:**
- **Sensor Head:** 17.7 × 19.8 × 35.8 mm; 12 grams
- **Electronics/ECU:** 14.7 × 24.4 × 92.3 mm; 29 grams (6.5 g without housing)
- **Power:** 2.5 W nominal, 3.5 W during startup; +5V input via control board or 10-12V direct
- **Integration:** Sensor head mounts on small boom extending past S0 nose to isolate from power/propulsion electronics; ECU mounts in fuselage, connects directly to avionics

**Testing Results:**
- **S1 Flight Testing:** Preliminary in-air logging on larger S1 airframe revealed significant magnetic interference (120-150 nT heading error, 250-600 pT/√Hz platform noise—10-30x requirement)
- **S0 Ground Testing:** Magnetic signature measured at 0 ft, 5 ft, 10 ft, 20 ft, and 50 ft distances with aircraft off, idle, and at cruise throttle
  - Noise 5 ft and beyond: ~1.5 pT/√Hz (1x requirement, providing 2x margin)
  - 0 ft (close-in): 34.9 pT/√Hz (due to motor and electronics)
  - Peak noise at 0 ft correlated with 400MHz communications

**Mitigation Strategies:**
- MuMetal magnetic shielding material sourced for testing (demonstrated 10x reduction from electric motors in prior work)
- In-air calibration procedure developed: fly figure-8 patterns at multiple headings/orientations (~5 minutes, leaves ~70 minutes mission time on S0)
- Motor separation and repositioning under evaluation
- Communication filtering during noise analysis

### Jetson AGX Orin 64GB Single Board Computer (SBC)
**What it is:** NVIDIA AI compute platform for onboard sensor data processing and autonomous decision-making.

**Specifications (matching Navy requirements exactly):**
- **AI Performance:** 275 TOPS
- **GPU:** 2048-core CUDA + 64 Tensor cores; Max frequency 1.3 GHz
- **CPU:** 12-core @ 2.2 GHz
- **Memory:** 64 GB RAM
- **Power Consumption:** 15–60 W
- **Size:** 110 mm × 110 mm × 71.65 mm

**Challenge:** Largest component for S0 integration; fits within BST deployment tube but represents significant SWaP constraint. Document notes flexibility on SBC specs could enable lower-cost and more SWaP-efficient alternatives.

### CRT Hydrophone System
**What it is:** Lightweight underwater acoustic sensor for passive sonar detection, designed for fiber-optic deployment from S0.

**Concept Architecture:**
- Potted assembly containing: transducer, ADC, primary-chemistry battery (A23 cell: 12V, 55 mAh), fiber-optic transmission circuit
- Water-tight housing: 1" diameter × 2" length for sensor
- 200 ft fiber-optic spool for deployment and data transmission
- Water-activated switch triggers microphone, ADC, and fiber-optic data transmission
- Independent battery eliminates need for long tether power; reduces size/weight vs. copper conductor cable

**Specifications:**
- **Operating Life:** 70 minutes (exceeds Navy threshold of 60 min)
- **Max Operating Depth:** 200 ft (meets threshold; objective 400 ft not met)
- **Deployment Time:** 120 seconds (meets threshold; objective 60 s not achieved)
- **Scuttle:** Automatic (commanded by BST autopilot) plus on-command
- **Directionality:** Omnidirectional (meets threshold; objective higher gain/direction-finding not pursued)
- **Frequency Coverage:** 1 Hz – 25 kHz (meets objective of 0.001 Hz – 25 kHz for upper range; below 1 Hz not implemented)
- **Sensor Noise:** Low-noise design below sea-state zero
- **Data Shaping:** Whitened to environment for reduced uplink bandwidth; built into ADC
- **Calibration Accuracy:** ±2 dB (meets threshold)
- **Range:** >20 nm (meets requirement)
- **Power Consumption:** 30 mA @ 12 VDC
- **SWaP:** Sensor 1" dia. × 2" length; 200 ft cabling stored in S0 aft fuselage

**Deployment Sequence:**
1. S0 lands on sea surface
2. Hydrophone releases from UAS and sinks to depth under its own weight
3. Water-activated switch energizes hydrophone electronics
4. Acoustic data transmitted via fiber optic cable to UAS
5. UAS re-broadcasts data via primary communications antenna

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary: Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW)
- **Mission Profile:** Air-deployed from P-8 Poseidon maritime patrol aircraft via sonobuoy launcher
- **Operational Concept:** Magnetometer detects submarine propulsion signatures (magnetic anomaly); triggers autonomous vehicle to maintain contact, deploy hydrophone for acoustic classification
- **Detection Range:** >20 nm (LOS)
- **Autonomous Capability Evolution:** Pre-programmed waypoints in Phase I/II; autonomous target tracking cued by MAD system in later phases

### Secondary/Dual-Use Markets
- **U.S. Air Force:** Integration of S0 with magnetometer into KC-135 tanker aircraft for magnetic field mapping over ocean (navigation reference if GPS denied) and pilot training
- **Mining Industry:** Magnetic detection for mineral/resource exploration
- **Marine Mammal Monitoring:** Leveraging existing S0 NOAA deployment experience
- **GPS-Denied Navigation:** Detailed magnetic maps for ocean navigation reference
