# ARL BAA White Paper: Army-scale Data Assimilation and Remote Sensing for Boundary Layer and Artillery Met Applications

- **Date:** July 19, 2016 (draft); submitted August 2016

- **Key Personnel:** Maciej Stachura (Black Swift Technologies, CTO)

## Executive Summary
This white paper proposes a three-year Army-funded research effort (Jan. 1, 2017 – Dec. 31, 2019) to develop data assimilation and UAS remote sensing capabilities for soil moisture characterization at Army-relevant scales, with applications to boundary layer modeling, target recognition, trafficability assessment, and artillery meteorology. The work involves Colorado State University (prime), University of Colorado Boulder, and Black Swift Technologies as a subaward partner.

## Technical Approach

### Theme A: Boundary Layer Data Assimilation at Army-Relevant Scales
- Soil moisture data assimilation and surface energy balance studies to improve boundary layer characterization
- Year 1: Innovation vector analysis of soil moisture estimates using downscaled datasets; design Army-scale data assimilation components and experiments; apply analysis to Reynolds Creek, ID datasets
- Year 2: Create Army-scale innovation vector analysis capabilities; implement synthetic experiments
- Year 3: Extend and apply innovation vector analysis to White Sands, NM site in collaboration with ARL/BED

### Theme B: UAS Remote Sensing of Soil Moisture and Land Surface Characteristics
- Microwave (MW) sensor-equipped UAS for fine-scale soil moisture mapping
- Year 1: Prepare for Reynolds Creek, ID field experiments (summer 2018); develop MW sensor forward operator simulation capabilities
- Year 2: Conduct Reynolds Creek field experiment; intercompare calibration/validation (cal/val) results; characterize sensor errors and model initialization errors
- Year 3: Conduct White Sands, NM field experiment; continue innovation vector analysis intercomparisons; integrate characterized UAS sensor data into Army-scale data assimilation systems

### Theme C: UKMET Unified Model NWP Error Characterization
- Characterize numerical weather prediction (NWP) errors in the UKMET Unified Model for Army applications
- Focus on biases and error distribution as related to artillery meteorology and boundary layer applications
- Consider atmospheric profiling capabilities (potentially using ARL balloon soundings)

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift Technologies UAS Platform
- **What it is:** Uncrewed aerial system equipped with microwave remote sensing sensors
- **Proposed use:** Conduct cal/val of soil moisture sensors; collect fine-scale soil moisture data for integration with data assimilation systems; perform field experiments at Reynolds Creek, ID and White Sands, NM
- **Personnel:** Maciej Stachura (CTO) serving as technical lead
- **Experience:** Dr. Stachura has 10+ years of UAS experience, including FAA flight certification, system design, and over 500 flight experiments; previously PI on NASA Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS development (3 years prior)

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Boundary layer characterization** for improved atmospheric modeling at Army-relevant scales
- **Target recognition** applications enabled by enhanced environmental characterization
- **Trafficability assessment** using soil moisture and surface characteristics
- **Artillery meteorology** via improved NWP error characterization and boundary layer data assimilation
- **Fine-scale soil moisture mapping** for cal/val of satellite and model data

## Field Test Sites
- **Reynolds Creek, ID:** Primary Year 1-2 field experiment location
- **White Sands, NM:** Year 3 field experiment site; collaborative with ARL/BED
