## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed a small, atmospheric profiling UAS capable of real-time in situ data collection with 1+ hour endurance for Air Force applications. The system combines BST's proven 3D Wind Probe with an integrated atmospheric sensor suite at a significantly lower cost than competitors, with all U.S.-made components and no Chinese-sourced parts.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed developing an air-launched UAS optimized for low-level atmospheric profiling with the following key elements:

**Core Sensor Suite:**
- 3D Wind Probe (BST-designed): 100 Hz measurement rate, accuracy of ±0.5 m/s and ±1 degree
- Co-located IMU, magnetometer, and GPS
- Pressure, temperature, humidity sensors
- Trace gas measurement capability
- Particulate size and distribution measurement

**Operational Specifications:**
- Maximum cruise time: 2 hours
- Target endurance: 1+ hours for targeted observations
- Aircraft weight: under 2 lbs
- Target cost per aircraft: $5,000
- Fully automated with less than half-day training requirement
- Heritage from S0 Hurricane Sampler platform

**Software/Automation:**
- Automated sampling UI with scripting for custom sampling plans
- Real-time weather and plume monitoring
- Automated tracking of eyewall boundaries (hurricane applications)
- Integration with existing Air Force UAS platforms

### Atmospheric Sensor Suite
Integrated measurements of:
- 3D winds with small-scale turbulence

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Applications:**
1. **Hurricane Operations**: Low-level hurricane and sea-surface observations; automated eyewall boundary tracking; reliable low-cost capability for sustained operations
2. **Air Force Weather Profiling**: Vertical atmospheric profiling for real-time weather characterization
3. **Volcanic Plume Sampling**: Demonstrated capability to fly through active volcanic plumes with integrated sensor suite
4. **Scientific Research Campaigns**: Multi-week field deployments with automated profiling capabilities

**Extended/Future Applications:**
- Balloon-deployed variants for very high altitude sampling with controlled descent
- Integration onto other Air Force UAS platforms
- Wildfire applications (referenced NOAA NightFox work)
- Arctic atmospheric studies
