## Executive Summary
NREL proposes a two-year, $550,000 effort to develop a prototype unmanned aerial system (UAS) capable of measuring wind plant flow characteristics with high spatial and temporal resolution. The system would combine the flexibility of mobile platforms with the detailed three-component flow measurement capability typically limited to fixed tower sensors, generating publicly available wind farm aerodynamics data to support simulation tool validation and wind plant design optimization.

## Technical Approach

**Core Platform:**
- Small, lightweight, fixed-wing UAS (~1 m wingspan) or multi-rotor device
- Off-the-shelf components: remote-controlled aircraft, open-source programmable autopilot with GPS, fast multi-axis measurement probes
- Open-source autopilot firmware to facilitate sensor integration and station-keeping behavior

**Unique Capabilities:**
- Station-keeping capability: UAS designed to fly at wind/turbine wake speed, allowing it to remain fixed at one point in space for extended periods to gather detailed turbulence statistics (rather than traversing through flow at high speed like conventional UAS wind measurement systems)
- Traverse behavior: After dwelling at one location, UAS moves to another point in the flow field, functioning like a traverse-mounted probe in wind tunnel experiments
- Multi-axis measurement: Captures wind and turbulence information in all three coordinate directions
- Temperature and moisture measurement capability
- Modular, reconfigurable design using off-the-shelf components

**Validation & Support Infrastructure:**
- Testing at NREL's National Wind Technology Center (NWTC)
- Existing tower-mounted flow sensors for validation and augmentation of UAS measurements
- Access to modern multi-megawatt turbines

**Year 1 Deliverable:** Prototype UAS platform development and testing

**Year 2 Deliverable:** Initial data collection and peer-reviewed publication of findings

## Products & Capabilities Described

**UAS-Based Wind Flow Measurement Platform**
- What it is: A portable, station-keeping unmanned aerial system for measuring wind plant aerodynamics
- Design approach: Low-cost, modular construction using commercial components
- Key advantage: Combines portability and flexibility of mobile platforms with stationary measurement capability for detailed turbulence statistics
- Measurement capability: 3-component wind data, turbulence statistics, temperature, and moisture across arbitrary locations in the flow field

## Use Cases & Applications

1. **Wind Plant Aerodynamics Research**
   - Validation of NREL wind plant aerodynamics simulation tools
   - Understanding atmospheric boundary layer interactions, turbine wakes, and complex terrain effects
   - Supporting next-generation wind turbine and plant design

2. **Experimental Campaigns**
   - Crop Wind Energy eXperiment (CWEX) - follow-on projects
   - Turbine Wake and Inflow Characterization Study (TWICS)
   - DOE Complex Flow initiative projects

3. **Commercial Services**
   - Wind plant developers could contract NREL for in-situ measurements to improve proprietary simulation tools
   - Plant layout and operational strategy optimization

## Notable Details

**Competitive Advantages Over Existing Approaches:**
- Fixed tower sensors: Limited by coarse vertical resolution, high cost, immobility, cannot measure temperature/moisture effectively
- LIDAR remote sensing: Expensive, limited to two velocity components, cannot measure temperature/moisture
- Current UAS systems: Travel faster than wind, cannot obtain stationary turbulence statistics, limited dwell time at measurement locations

**Data Dissemination:**
- All measurements to be released to public domain
- Knowledge shared through peer-reviewed journal publications
- Benefits wind energy research community and industry

**Partnership Leverage:**
- Collaboration with University of Colorado's Research and Engineering Center for Unmanned Vehicles (RECUV)
- RECUV has FAA expertise for unmanned aircraft authorization
- Strengthens ties with local UAS research expertise

**Strategic Benefits to NREL:**
- Positions NREL as key player in DOE-prioritized complex flow research
- Demonstrates data-driven validation capability for simulation tools
- Opens opportunities for future funding (DOE Complex Flow, industry contracts)
- Advances NREL's wind plant aerodynamics simulation leadership
