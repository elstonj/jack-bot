## Executive Summary
This is a personnel and resources document supporting a 3-year NASA ROSES proposal to develop on-demand wildfire susceptibility mapping using fuel moisture (via L-band radiometry) and vegetation indices from UAS platforms. The team includes Black Swift Technologies (aircraft and systems), JPL (soil moisture retrieval algorithms), USGS (hydrology and fire science), Pepperwood Reserve (field site and validation), and Orbital Micro Systems (radiometer expertise). The project aims to provide fire managers with high-resolution, spatially-resolved soil and vegetation moisture data to predict fire risk.

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Application: Wildfire Susceptibility Mapping
- **Target Users**: Wildland fire managers; land management agencies; prescribed burn planning
- **Data Products**: On-demand maps of fuel moisture (via L-band radiometry) and vegetation indices
- **Geographic Focus**: California wildlands, particularly Pepperwood Preserve and surrounding regions
- **Fire Management Context**: 
  - Real-time fire danger assessment
  - Prescribed burn planning and timing
  - Post-fire recovery monitoring
  - Integration with fire behavior modeling

### Secondary Applications (Partner Expertise)
- **Soil Moisture Mapping**: Agriculture, water resource management, irrigation planning (Seungbum Kim's SMAP/SAR work)
- **Hydrologic Monitoring**: Watershed management, water availability assessment (Michelle Stern's USGS work)
- **Disaster Response**: Hurricane boundary layer sampling (Elston's active NOAA project); volcanic monitoring (Stachura's DOI project)
