## Executive Summary
The DOE Office of Science and partner offices (CMEI, EM, NE, OE, HGEO) are soliciting interdisciplinary team applications to address the Genesis Mission National Science and Technology Challenges. Teams will accelerate scientific discovery and R&D workflows using novel AI models and frameworks across 9+ topic areas (advanced manufacturing, biotechnology, critical materials, nuclear fission, nuclear fusion, quantum information science, semiconductors, discovery science, and energy). Total funding available: $293.76 million for FY26 Phase I ($500K-$750K per award, 9 months) and Phase II ($1.5-3.75M, 3 years).

## Program Structure

### Phased Approach
**Phase I (9 months, $500K-$750K):** Teams design and demonstrate clear research workflows incorporating AI with concrete evaluation of AI advantage. Success metrics may include increased predictive power, improved scientific insight from curated data, tighter data-experiment coupling, new model development, scaling metrics, accelerated experimental workflows, or other quantitative metrics showing trajectory toward transformative capability.

**Phase II (3 years, $1.5-3.75M):** Meritorious Phase I teams and new Phase II teams pursue promising directions. Level of effort envisioned at 3-5x Phase I. Receipt of Phase I award not prerequisite for Phase II; teams believing they've achieved Phase I goals may apply directly for Phase II.

### Team Requirements
- **Phase I:** Multi-institutional teams required with partners from at least 2 of 3 categories: (1) DOE/NNSA National Laboratory or Scientific User Facility, (2) Industry, (3) Institute of Higher Education/Non-profit/Other
- **Phase II:** Large teams with at least one partner from categories (1) and (2); category (3) strongly encouraged but not required
- Industrial partners may receive up to 20% of total requested budget
- Partners must provide intellectual contributions but don't require DOE funding

## Eligibility Requirements

### Eligible Applicants
- All types of domestic applicants except 501(c)(4) nonprofit organizations engaged in lobbying after December 31, 1995
- DOE/NNSA National Laboratories eligible as lead or team member (not as subrecipients)
- Non-DOE FFRDCs eligible in collaborative applications (not as subrecipients)
- Other Federal Agencies eligible in collaborative applications (not as subrecipients)

### Cost Sharing Requirements
- **Non-profit entities and academic institutions:** Cost sharing not required for basic and applied research
- **For-profit entities (prime or sub):** Minimum 20% cost share for R&D activities; 50% for demonstration/commercial tasks
- Cost share based on each for-profit entity's budget portion, minimum 20% of allowable R&D costs
- Cost share must originate from non-federal sources
- Institutional commitments (space, facilities, equipment, faculty release time, student scholarships, waived F&A) strongly encouraged but separate from cost share requirement
- Cost share not required from DOE/NNSA National Labs, other Federal agencies, or FFRDCs

### PI Requirements
- Must be in permanent or indefinitely extensible position (tenured, tenure-track, or staff appointment)
- Not eligible if in term-limited appointments (adjunct, visiting, fellow)
- Part-time permanent positions eligible
- Joint appointment eligible if work performed at applicant institution and PI on applicant's payroll
- Individuals receiving >50% salary from DOE/NNSA National Lab cannot be PI at other institutions
- Can be senior/key personnel on unlimited applications but lead PI on only one application
- Non-U.S. citizens and non-permanent residents eligible

## Key Challenge Areas/Topics

**Topic 1: Reenvisioning Advanced Manufacturing and Industrial Productivity**
- Participating Offices: SC-BES, CMEI-ITO, CMEI-AMMTO, SC-FES, SC-ASCR
- Challenge: Bridge "valley of death" between scientific discovery and commercial viability
- AI Solution: Agentic/generative AI to navigate multi-scale systems, integrate data into digital twins, enable automated decision support
- Focus Areas:
  - A. Agentic AI-Driven Chemical Manufacturing (BES)
  - B. AI-Driven Materials Processing (BES)
  - C. AI-Enabled Manufacturing for Extreme Energy Systems (FES)
  - D. Digitalization of Industrial Processes (ITO)
  - E. AI-Enabled Smart Manufacturing (AMMTO)
  - F. Energy Material Manufacturing (AFFO) - primarily National Lab led

**Topic 2: Scaling the Biotechnology Revolution**
- Participating Offices: SC-BER, CMEI-AFFO, SC-ASCR
- Challenge: Design biology on demand; overcome nonlinear complexity and long development cycles
- AI Solution: AI to integrate genomics, multi-omics, imaging, phenomics data; establish genotype-phenotype relationships; guide autonomous experimentation
- Focus Areas:
  - A. Biomolecular Science (BER)
  - B. Genotype to Phenotype (BER)
  - C. Predictive Engineering of Microbial Communities (BER)
  - D. Bio Design (BER)
  - E. AI-Enabled Biological Reaction Engineering, Bioreactor Design, Scale-up (AFFO)

**Topic 3: Securing America's Critical Minerals Supply**
- Participating Offices: CMEI-ASO, CMEI-AMMTO, CMEI-AMMPTO, SC-BES, SC-BER, SC-ASCR
- Challenge: Dependency on foreign supply chains for critical minerals threatens security and competitiveness
- AI Solution: Integrate geophysical data, science data, process optimization into connected system; physics-based AI for predictive capabilities
- Focus Areas:
  - A. Resource Mapping and Development (AMMPTO)
  - B. AI-Enabled Materials Discovery and Engineering (AMMTO)
  - C. Economic Modeling and Market Analysis (ASO)
  - D. Extraction and Processing Technologies (AMMPTO/AMMTO)
  - E. Geological Finders/Keepers (BES/BER)
  - F. Connections for Isolation (BES)
  - G. Biological Pathways to CMM (BER)

**Topic 4: Delivering Nuclear Energy that is Faster, Safer, Cheaper**
- Participating Offices: NE, SC-ASCR
- Challenge: Long development timelines and escalating costs limit affordable nuclear energy deployment
- AI Solution: Accelerate deployment with explainable AI solutions including surrogate models, agentic workflows, autonomous labs, digital twins. Goal: 2x schedule acceleration and >50% operational cost reductions
- Focus: Proof-of-concept demonstrations leveraging AI to accelerate nuclear engineering/licensing pipelines with quantifiable cost/schedule/regulatory uncertainty reductions

## Genesis Mission Integration Framework

All funded teams become part of the Genesis Mission and must contribute to relevant components in coordinated fashion. Core components include:

1. **American Science Cloud (AmSC):** Collaborative cloud ecosystem for scientific research, data sharing, and analysis
2. **Transformational AI Models Consortium (ModCon):** Curating data and building foundational, self-improving AI models for science
3. **American Science and Security Platform:** Collaborative effort between AmSC/ModCon and NNSA-funded teams

### Expected Contributions
- Host data, models, and artifacts on Genesis Mission platform with discoverable metadata
- Participate in coordination activities across Genesis Mission for data, models, infrastructure
- Provide information on project requirements, needs, and best practices as requested
- Leverage platform capabilities provided by DOE/NNSA National Laboratories and Genesis Mission Consortium members

## Genesis Mission Consortium

- Public-private partnership announced February 6, 2026
- Members contribute computing power, AI tokens, technical expertise, in-kind support
- Provides partnership service to facilitate team formation (not required for eligibility)
- Applicants encouraged to engage partnership service but not required
- Partnership service effectiveness decreases within one week of submission deadline

## Application Limitations

- **Institutional Submission Limits:** Maximum one application as lead institution per focus area for Phase I and Phase II combined. Phase II applications identify primary and secondary focus areas; primary determines limit. No limit on non-lead team member applications.
- **PI Limitations:** PI can be lead on only one application; can be senior/key personnel on unlimited applications. Phase I award recipients may submit Phase II as part of go/no-go decision.
- Excess submissions beyond limits may be declined without review
