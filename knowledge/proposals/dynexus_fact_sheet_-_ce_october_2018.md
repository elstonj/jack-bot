## Executive Summary
Dynexus has developed iRIS™ (inline Rapid Impedance Spectroscopy), an in-situ battery impedance spectrum sensor that performs real-time diagnostics on operating batteries. Licensed from Idaho National Laboratory's R&D 100 award-winning technology, the system enables battery health monitoring, thermal runaway prediction, cell qualification, and counterfeit detection.

## Technical Approach
**Technology Foundation:**
- Exclusive perpetual licensing agreement with US Department of Energy Idaho National Laboratory (INL), effective December 2016
- License includes 11 patents, algorithms, prototypes, test data, and access to INL battery projects

**Operating Principle:**
- Multi-sine excitation signal applied across broad frequency range
- Excitation signal comprises approximately 30 frequencies
- Requires minimum one period of lowest frequency
- Typical measurement range for Li-ion cells: 0.1 to 1638.4 Hz
- Standard measurement cycle: 10 seconds
- Captured battery response processed at each frequency to determine impedance spectrum

**Measurement Characteristics:**
- Non-invasive interrogation
- Benign and rapid measurement
- Operates under both no-load and load conditions
- Real-time, in-situ capability

## Products & Capabilities Described

**iRIS™ (inline Rapid Impedance Spectroscopy) Sensor:**
- **Current Form:** Rack-mounted unit with USB interface to laptop control software
- **Measurement Capability:** Broadband impedance measurements at point of need
- **Validated Correlations:** Research at INL demonstrates strong correlation between in-situ impedance data and standard state-of-health metrics (pulse resistance, capacity)
- **Safety Application:** Independent Sandia National Laboratories research validates iRIS utility for state-of-stability (safety) detection

## Use Cases & Applications
- Advanced battery diagnostics during operation
- Battery health analysis (present and predictive)
- Early warning detection for thermal runaway events
- Cell qualification and validation
- Battery pack integration verification
- Counterfeit battery detection
- Laboratory research for battery characterization
- Consumer electronics in-situ monitoring (proposed future application)
