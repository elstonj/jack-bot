## Executive Summary
This is a compliance guidance document for SBIR/STTR proposals submitted to DOD, specifying requirements for disclosing any foreign citizens or dual-citizen employees, subcontractors, or consultants who will be involved in the project. The document emphasizes that failure to disclose such individuals will result in proposal rejection.

## Key Requirements
- **Mandatory Disclosure**: All foreign nationals and dual-citizenship individuals expected to be involved (direct employees, subcontractors, consultants) must be identified
- **Required Information**: 
  - Country of origin
  - Type of visa or work permit under which they are performing
  - Explanation of anticipated level of involvement on the project
- **Definition Reference**: Foreign nationals are defined in Section 3.5 of the CSO (presumably Contracting Officer Source or similar guidance)
- **Consequences**: Proposals lacking this information will be rejected
- **Verification**: Offerors may be asked to provide additional information during contract negotiations to verify eligibility
- **Privacy Protection**: Supplemental information provided is protected under Privacy Act (5 U.S.C. 552a) and FOIA exemptions (5 U.S.C. 552(b)(6))

## Notable Details
- Document warns that offerors commonly fail to report individuals with dual citizenship or work permits, mistakenly assuming they are automatically eligible
