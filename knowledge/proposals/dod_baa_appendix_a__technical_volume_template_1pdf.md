## Document Purpose
This is a **template and instruction document**, not a proposal or technical narrative. It provides standardized formatting requirements and section guidance for companies submitting Phase I technical proposals to the DoD SBIR/STTR program.

## Key Sections Required in Technical Volume (Volume 2)

1. **Identification and Significance of the Problem or Opportunity** – Define specific technical problem/opportunity and importance
2. **Phase I Technical Objectives** – Enumerate specific objectives and feasibility questions
3. **Phase I Statement of Work** – Detailed approach, tasks, location, schedule, deliverables; must address how work determines technical feasibility
4. **Related Work** – Describe significant related activities; demonstrate state-of-art awareness; include client references with POC
5. **Relationship with Future Research or R&D** – Anticipated results; Phase II foundation; clearances/certifications needed
6. **Commercialization Strategy** – ~1 page on DoD/Federal/private sector commercialization; market need and size; quantitative commercialization timeline
7. **Key Personnel** – Education, experience, resumes for principal investigator and key personnel (resumes count toward page limit)
8. **Foreign Citizens** – Identify any foreign nationals/dual citizens; country of origin, visa type, involvement level
9. **Facilities/Equipment** – Available instrumentation and facilities; equipment justification; environmental compliance statement
10. **Subcontractors/Consultants** – SBIR: minimum 2/3 of work by small business; STTR: minimum 40% by small business, 30% by research institution
11. **Prior, Current or Pending Support** – Disclose substantially similar proposals to other Federal Agencies/DoD Components
12. **Restrictions on Government Use of Technical Data/Computer Software** – Assert any proprietary data restrictions using specified table and DFARS clauses

## Formatting Requirements
- Single column, single-spaced
- Standard 8.5" × 11" paper
- 1-inch margins on all sides
- Minimum 10-point font (smaller acceptable only for headers, footers, tables, figures, graphics with text)
- Header on each page with company name, topic number, DSIP-assigned proposal number
- No locked/encrypted files
- No embedded active graphics, videos, or moving media
- Page limits specified in Component-specific instructions (proposer responsibility to verify after upload)

## Important Compliance Notes
- **Disclosure Requirements:** Proposals containing proprietary data must be marked with specific legends on first page and data sheets
- **Human/Animal Subjects or Recombinant DNA:** Must identify applicable protocols; funds cannot be released until proper approvals obtained; proposers encouraged to separate these tasks
- **SBIR/STTR Eligibility:** Different cost-share requirements for SBIR vs. STTR
- **Export Control/ITAR:** Universities cannot publicly release Export Controlled/ITAR restricted information
- **Rights in Technical Data:** Government obtains royalty-free license for Government purposes for 20 years post-completion under DFARS 252.227-7018 Class Deviation 2020-O0007; unlimited rights thereafter
