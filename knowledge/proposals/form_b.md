# NASA SBIR Phase II Proposal: SuperSwift sUAS with Passive Microwave Radiometer for Soil Moisture Measurement

## Document Metadata
- Type: SBIR Phase II Proposal (Form B - Technical Abstract & Commercialization Applications)
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: 2012 SBIR Phase II
- Date: Submitted 2013 (created 2013-11-23; modified through 2017-12-01)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: SuperSwift sUAS, passive microwave radiometer payload
- Key Personnel: Maciej Stachura (last editor); Prof. Gasiewski referenced for prior work

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed Phase II development of the SuperSwift small Unmanned Aircraft System (sUAS) equipped with a passive microwave radiometer to measure soil moisture over 400-acre areas per flight at 15-meter resolution. The system operates at 15-30 meters above ground level and will advance from TRL 3 to TRL 7. Phase II addresses design issues from Phase I by redesigning the fuselage with a removable nose cone for sensor integration and converting to a twin-engine wing-mounted configuration.

## Technical Approach

### Airframe & System Design
- **Phase I platform**: Modified Tempest airframe (stock design with limitations)
- **Phase II redesign**: Custom fuselage featuring:
  - Modified removable nose cone to house entire sensor payload
  - Twin-engine propulsion design mounted on wings (replacing stock single-engine configuration)
  - Resolves Phase I integration challenges: external antenna placement, complex control linkage manufacturing, difficulty removing payload for testing/replacement

### Flight Operations
- Operational altitude: 15-30 meters above ground level (AGL)
- Coverage: 400 acres per flight
- Soil moisture measurement depth: ~5 cm
- Spatial resolution: 15 meters
- Regulatory approach: Leverages BST's "strong working knowledge of the regulatory environment surrounding sUAS" to inform design and concept of operations for safe, legal FAA-approved operation

### Sensor Integration
- Tight integration of passive microwave radiometer with sUAS avionics and airframe
- Precise flight control required for low-altitude missions
- Removable sensor payload design for easier testing and replacement

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift sUAS
- **What it is**: Small Unmanned Aircraft System with integrated passive microwave radiometer payload
- **Soil moisture measurement capability**:
  - Coverage: 400 acres per flight
  - Measurement depth: ~5 cm below surface
  - Spatial resolution: 15 meters
  - Operational altitude: 15-30 m AGL
- **Technology readiness**: Starting Phase II at TRL 3, target TRL 7 by completion
- **Passive microwave radiometer**: Custom-designed sensor requiring precise antenna placement and flight control

## Use Cases & Applications

### NASA/SMAP Mission Support
- **SMAP comparison**: NASA's SMAP satellite (Oct 2014 launch) provides ~40 km resolution; SuperSwift provides 15 m resolution
- **Validation**: Support calibration and validation program for SMAP spacecraft data
- **Resolution augmentation**: Provide fine-resolution data in:
  - Watershed regions
  - Drought areas
  - Regions requiring enhanced measurement frequency
- **Science data augmentation**: Complement global SMAP measurements with localized high-resolution data products

### FEMA/Flood Prediction
- Application to flash flood vulnerability assessment
- Historical precedent: 2007 NASA-funded soil moisture mapping in North Texas/Oklahoma flood-prone region (led by Prof. Gasiewski) used to provide flood alerts and warnings
- Proposed advantage: SuperSwift could provide flood prediction data at reduced cost, improving vulnerable watershed predictions nationwide

### Wildland Fire Monitoring
- Vegetation density and soil moisture mapping for wildland fire understanding, monitoring, and firefighting support
- Application evaluated through proprietary white paper submitted to state organizations
- Status: Concept developed with alternative sensor/system configurations

### Precision Agriculture
- Early interest from Colorado State University researchers
- CSU collaboration: Initial SuperSwift system deployment with expectation of future expansion as research validates additional applications beyond soil moisture measurement

## Technical Challenges Addressed in Phase II

**Phase I Issues with Tempest Airframe:**
- External antenna placement created integration challenges
- Complex manufacturing of rudder and elevator control linkages
- Soil moisture electronics payload deeply embedded in airframe
- Difficult payload removal for testing and replacement

**Phase II Solutions:**
- Redesigned fuselage with removable nose cone payload bay
- Twin-engine wing-mounted configuration
- Modular payload architecture enabling easier testing/replacement

## Notable Details

- **Regulatory expertise**: BST emphasized its "strong working knowledge of the regulatory environment surrounding sUAS" as a competitive advantage for FAA certification and national airspace integration
- **Partnership development**: Active collaboration with Colorado State University for precision agriculture applications
- **Commercialization pathways**: Identified multiple government (NASA, FEMA) and commercial (agriculture, fire management) customer segments
- **Technology maturation trajectory**: Clear path from TRL 3 (Phase II start) to TRL 7 (Phase II end)
- **Cost advantage**: Positioning SuperSwift as lower-cost alternative to satellite-based soil moisture measurement and manned aircraft campaigns
