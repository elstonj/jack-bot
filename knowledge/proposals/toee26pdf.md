## Executive Summary
This is a Navy RFI soliciting innovative technologies (TRL 5–7) from industry to support three operational areas: Counter Unmanned Systems (UxS) Swarm capabilities, Containerized Payloads for Unmanned Surface Vehicles, and Decision Superiority tools for Maritime Operations Centers. Selected technologies will participate in live experimentation events throughout FY 2026–2027, with potential government funding for travel, labor, and integration costs.

## Operational Topics

### Topic 1: Wave Breaker – Counter Unmanned Systems (UxS) Swarm
- **Focus:** Defeat of UxS swarms by Naval forces, including Marine Corps Stand-In Forces (SIF) in Expeditionary Advanced Bases (EAB) and lightly defended Navy surface assets in littoral regions
- **Sought Technologies:**
  - Active/passive sensors for detection, tracking, and identification of unmanned air, surface, and undersea systems operating in large numbers and in close proximity
  - Battle management aids and C2/common tactical picture (CTP) decision support tools
  - Kinetic and non-kinetic multi-domain effectors (air, surface, undersea) capable of defeating multiple threats cost-effectively
  - Deployment from small tactical vehicles, individual Marines, or lightly defended ships (e.g., Military Sealift Command vessels)
  - Survivable UxS swarms for red-cell testing in contested/congested environments

### Topic 2: Battle Box – Containerized Payloads for Unmanned Surface Vehicles
- **Focus:** Modular, remotely operated or autonomous payloads for long-endurance open-ocean unmanned surface vessels
- **Sought Technologies:**
  - Payloads controlled from unmanned operations centers or fully autonomous systems
  - Compatibility with vessels meeting specifications in Navy solicitation N00024-25-R-6314 (MASC Area of Interest)
  - Novel payloads enabling multi-vessel teaming or manned-unmanned platform teaming
  - All Navy mission areas considered

### Topic 3: Virtual Vanguard – Decision Superiority from Maritime Operations Center (MOC)
- **Focus:** Advanced decision support leveraging data analytics, automation, and AI for MOC operations
- **Sought Technologies:**
  - Course of action (COA) generation and ranking from near-real-time information and predictive adversary modeling
  - Automated planning and orders writing
  - Data visualization from Space to Seabed
  - Scalable tools capable of handling significantly increased future data/information streams

## Submission Requirements

### Format
- **Quad Chart** (PowerPoint format) – single-page visual summary
- **White Paper** (PDF, maximum 5 pages excluding cover) addressing:

1. **Operational Relevance:** How the technology addresses one or more of the three TOEE 26 topic areas
2. **Cost Estimates:** 
   - Estimated cost to mature to low-rate production
   - Not-to-exceed LRIP cost for 4–6 fieldable prototype units
3. **System/Sub-Systems Architecture:** Diagrams, architectural views, data architecture, interfaces, IP rights (open-competitive or proprietary) for major components; SysML/UML models encouraged
4. **Critical Technical Parameters:** Quantified performance in tabular format with test environment details; specification sheets, data sheets, and test reports required
5. **Dependencies & Special Considerations:** 
   - Required government-furnished information/equipment (interface specs, launch/recovery procedures, frequency allocation, intra-network connectivity, shore power, etc.)
   - Required certifications (Weapons Safety Review Board, Laser Safety, Li-ion Battery, etc.)
   - Feasible alternatives (virtual, constructive, simulation, static display) to mitigate risk
6. **Technology & Integration Readiness Levels:** Current TRL and IRL assessments with supporting documentation; basis of assessment (developmental lab/simulation vs. operational test range)
7. **Team:** Composition across industry, academia, government; subject matter expertise descriptions
