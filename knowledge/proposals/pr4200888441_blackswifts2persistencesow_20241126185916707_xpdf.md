# Black Swift S2 Persistence Demonstration Statement of Work

## Document Metadata
- Type: Statement of Work (SOW)
- Client/Agency: NASA Ames Research Center (ARC) / US Forest Service (USFS)
- Program/Solicitation: Persistence Demonstration (PR4200888441)
- Date: November 26, 2024 (submitted/finalized)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 (fixed-wing UAS)
- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies will demonstrate continuous infrared surveillance over simulated wildfires using multiple S2 aircraft in a coordinated launch-and-recovery pattern to maintain persistent overhead coverage for 10-12 hours. The effort aims to validate operational procedures and equipment requirements for eventual 24-hour persistent monitoring capabilities using geo-rectified imagery delivered in near real-time to incident commanders.

## Technical Approach
- **Persistent Coverage Strategy**: Deploy 2-4 Black Swift S2 UAS in synchronized launch/recovery operations to maintain continuous overhead presence; minimum of 2 aircraft simultaneously on station during handoff periods to ensure uninterrupted data collection
- **Flight Patterns**: Pre-determined transects over simulated fire line or orbital patterns over single locations
- **Payload**: IR imaging payload nosecones previously flown for USGS and JPL
- **Operations**: Black Swift employees or subcontractors conduct flights; NASA/USFS personnel observe
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Follows NASA NPR 7900 standards including Airworthiness Review, Flight Readiness Review, and Mission Readiness Review; COA (Certificate of Airworthiness) procurement is vendor responsibility

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 (Black Swift Fixed-Wing UAS)
- **System**: Small, fixed-wing, catapult-launched uncrewed aircraft
- **Current Operations**: Already operated by NASA ARC and Langley Research Center; deployed operationally by USGS for volcano caldera mapping and gas sampling
- **Use in This Project**: Primary platform for persistent IR surveillance; 2-4 aircraft required for continuous coverage demonstration
- **Capability Being Validated**: Ability to maintain persistent surveillance over dynamic ground targets through coordinated multi-aircraft operations

### IR Imaging Payload Nosecones
- Previously flown for USGS and NASA JPL
- Provides geo-rectified imagery
- Data transmitted for near real-time delivery to incident commanders

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary**: Wildfire surveillance and monitoring
- **Secondary**: Other ephemeral ground or atmospheric targets requiring persistent overhead observation
- **Operational Context**: Support to incident commanders with real-time imagery for operational decision-making

## Key Deliverables
1. **Geo-rectified IR imagery** delivered in near real-time to accessible location for incident commanders
2. **Flight report** (due 90 days post-mission) including:
   - Recommendations for transitioning to 24-hour flight campaigns
   - Lessons learned
   - Complete equipment list (launchers, batteries, chargers, aircraft, ground stations, GSE)
3. **All aircraft state and payload data** made available to science community per NASA Open Science Data Policy
4. **Data archival** through designated DAAC (Distributed Active Archive Center) or NASA Ames website

## Notable Details
- **Location**: Colorado site selected to minimize costs
- **Duration**: 10-12 hour flight demonstration (simulating eventual 24-hour capability), weather and daylight permitting
- **Project Timeline**: 
  - Start: No later than December 1, 2024
  - Completion: Within 6 months of award (including all imagery, telemetry, and reports)
- **Personnel**: Flight crew consists of BST employees or subcontractors; NASA and/or USFS observers present
- **Data Accessibility**: All data open science policy compliant; delivered to science community for broad research use
- **Partnership Context**: Joint NASA ARC / USFS effort demonstrating UAS capability for operational wildfire monitoring
