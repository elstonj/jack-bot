# Support Letter - Black Swift Technologies UAS SBIR Phase II

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support / Memorandum
- Client/Agency: Air Force Life Cycle Management Center (AFLCMC/HBAW), Hanscom Air Force Base, Massachusetts
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Phase II - Commercial Solutions for Upper Air Weather Observations
- Date: 31 October 2019
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Upper Air UAS solution
- Key Personnel: Bruce A. Lambert (Chief Engineer, Weather Systems Branch, AFLCMC)

## Executive Summary
The Air Force Life Cycle Management Center (AFLCMC) issued a formal letter of support for Black Swift Technologies' SBIR Phase II proposal to develop a commercial UAS solution for upper air weather observations. The letter indicates AFLCMC/HBAW's interest in collaborating with BST to develop a tactical upper air measurement tool supporting Air Force, Army, and Special Operations Forces (SOF) operations.

## Technical Approach
- Development of a UAS-based solution for upper air weather observations
- Feasibility study and prototype development under SBIR Phase II funding
- Technology testing aligned with Air Force Weather operational needs

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Upper Air UAS Solution**
- Unmanned aircraft system designed for upper atmospheric weather measurements
- Proposed to support tactical operations requiring upper air data
- Addresses current unfunded Air Force requirement for upper air measurement capabilities

## Use Cases & Applications
- Air Force Weather operations requiring upper air observations
- Support for Air Force operations (ACC - Air Combat Command)
- Army operations
- Special Operations Forces (SOF) operations
- Tactical weather intelligence and atmospheric sounding

## Notable Details
- AFLCMC explicitly identifies this as an "unfunded requirement for ACC/A5W," indicating recognized operational need
- Letter emphasizes this is contingent on SBIR Phase II selection (not a direct funding commitment)
- Positioning as collaboration opportunity for feasibility/prototyping work
- Addresses multi-service need across Air Force, Army, and SOF
