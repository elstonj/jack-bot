## Executive Summary

Black Swift Technologies proposes a novel approach to persistent Venus upper atmospheric observation using a deployable fixed-wing unmanned aircraft system (UAS) that harvests energy through dynamic soaring rather than solar power or traditional propulsion. The concept leverages proven dynamic soaring techniques combined with high-strain composite deployable structures to fit multiple aircraft within a standard aeroshell, enabling long-duration missions on Venus's dark side while performing targeted atmospheric sampling at 50-60 km altitude.

## Technical Approach

### Core Innovation: Dynamic Soaring for Venus
- **Energy Source:** Extract energy from atmospheric wind shear rather than relying on solar power or propulsion systems
- **Baseline Design:** 4.3-meter wingspan glider, ~10 kg all-up weight including instruments and communications
- **Deployment Configuration:** Up to 8 aircraft can fit in a Pioneer aeroshell ("Venus Flytrap" concept); scalable to Pathfinder or Flagship missions
- **Operating Altitude:** 50-60 km above Venus surface (above cloud tops)
- **Mission Duration:** Persistent flight including traversal of dark side of Venus (which completes one rotation every ~90 hours)

### Atmospheric Energy Sources Identified
1. **Upward winds** near low latitudes (vertical atmospheric movement)
2. **High wind shear** above cloud interface and above topographic features (mountain waves)
3. **Gravity waves** generated by interaction with planetary terrain

The design focuses on wind shear exploitation, linking dynamic soaring cycles together to achieve both persistent altitude maintenance and directed lateral motion for targeted sampling.

### Key Technical Advantages
- **No propulsion system needed:** Eliminates complexity of sealing electric motors against sulfuric acid atmosphere; reduces onboard energy requirements to control surfaces and avionics only
- **Deployable rigid structure:** Faster deployment than inflatable systems; tested deployment techniques from tube-launched UAS used in hurricane research
- **Redundancy and flexibility:** Multiple aircraft enable different sensor suites, inter-vehicle coordination for locating optimal atmospheric conditions, and redundancy for long missions

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SwiftCore Flight Management System
- **What it is:** BST's highly capable autopilot system anchoring flight operations
- **Application in this context:** Foundation for dynamic soaring algorithm implementation; will be adapted for Venus atmospheric models
- **Characteristics:** Has been proven in multiple atmospheric sampling campaigns and scientific research missions

### BST Simulation Environment
- **What it is:** Open-source Gazebo-based platform with modular atmospheric modeling
- **Proposed Use:** 
  - Create Venus atmospheric model module to replace standard Earth atmosphere
  - Integrate aircraft aerodynamic models accounting for Venus conditions (lower gravity, different gas composition, altered Reynolds numbers)
  - Implement and test dynamic soaring control algorithms
  - Simulate full deployment sequences and flight profiles
- **Advantage:** Can run Earth analogs simultaneously for validation and flight test planning

### High-Strain Composite (HSC) Deployables (Roccor partnership)
- **What it is:** Composite structures that deform during deployment; examples include slit-tube booms and tape-spring hinges
- **Design for Venus:** Z-fold wing configuration with "dog-bone" patterns cut into composite tubes at hinge points
- **Performance:** Wings fold to compact configuration for aeroshell packing; unfold to full 4.3m span with strain energy
- **Key Feature:** Single-part design with low part count reduces corrosion concerns and complexity

## Technical Objectives for Phase I

### 1. Venus Atmospheric Model Development
- Compile data from Pioneer and Venera probes
- Construct vertical structure model including pressure, temperature, 3D winds
- Account for Venus gravity (~90% Earth's), CO₂/N₂ composition, viscosity effects
- Model small-scale winds and turbulence in expected operating region

### 2. Simulated Flight Validation
- Integrate atmospheric model into BST simulation environment
- Model aircraft aerodynamics under Venus conditions (adjusted Reynolds numbers, pressure, gravity)
- Implement dynamic soaring algorithms targeting:
  - Persistent altitude maintenance in 50-60 km band
  - Directed navigation for targeted sampling
  - Operation across dark side of planet
- Run extensive simulations across range of expected atmospheric conditions to prove robustness

### 3. Deployable Structure Development
- Design high-strain composite wing hinges using tape-spring concept
- Validate hinge performance under realistic aerodynamic loads
- Achieve z-fold configuration fitting 8+ aircraft in Pioneer aeroshell
- Address wire harness routing and control surface integration
- Develop passive sealing methods for hinge regions post-deployment

### 4. Materials Assessment for Corrosive Environment
- Survey composite matrix materials resistant to sulfuric acid (candidate materials: Cyanate Ester, Polyimide, Silicone, Viton)
- Literature review of exposure test data
- Trade study of material options vs. strain performance requirements
- Propose barrier film approach (e.g., Viton coating over Cyanate Ester composite) if needed
- Risk mitigation plan for Phase II testing

## Notable Details

### Unique Competitive Advantages
- **First heavier-than-air dynamic soaring concept for Venus:** Existing Venus concepts rely on dirigibles (limited by high winds) or solar-powered aircraft (limited on dark side)
- **Energy harvesting approach:** Unlike prior solar-powered concepts that struggle on dark side, dynamic soaring provides consistent propulsive energy from wind shear day or night
- **Proven technologies applied creatively:** Dynamic soaring has propelled world's fastest small-scale aircraft and enabled albatross-like ocean crossings; deployment mechanisms proven in hurricane research
- **Redundancy and scalability:** Multiple small aircraft approach enables sensor diversity, inter-vehicle coordination, and mission flexibility

### Key Technical Insights
- Venus upper atmosphere circulates every ~90 hours, creating unique operational challenges
- Wind shear exists in multiple regions: above cloud interface, above topographic features, near equatorial upwelling
- Removal of propulsion system dramatically simplifies corrosion protection (no motor seals required)
- Rigid deployable structures deploy faster than inflatable systems

### Partnership & Subcontracting
- **Roccor LLC:** Specializes in High-Strain Composite deployables; bringing flight heritage in small satellite solar array deployment and extensive HSC design experience
- BST provides simulation environment, flight control systems, and UAS integration expertise

### Commercialization Path
