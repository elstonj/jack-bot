## Executive Summary
This letter of support from NOAA's Joseph Cione endorses Black Swift Technologies' Phase II Navy STTR proposal for the S0 expendable profiling platform. Cione attests that the S0 has transitioned from developmental concept to proven operational asset through 42 successful deployments in the 2024-2025 hurricane seasons, demonstrating the capability to collect continuous atmospheric boundary layer data in extreme conditions including Category 5 hurricanes.

## Technical Approach
The S0 platform is designed to collect continuous, targeted data in the marine atmospheric boundary layer (MABL), specifically at the air-sea interface in the lowest 2 km where storm intensification occurs. Phase II technical objectives include:
- Assimilation of S0 data into operational forecasting models (NOAA HAFS by June 2026; Navy COAMPS-TC model)
- Icing protection enhancements
- AI-based autonomous sampling capabilities

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 Platform
- **What it is:** Expendable air-sea profiling observation system designed for hazardous weather conditions
- **Capabilities:** Collects 3D wind, pressure, temperature, and humidity data continuously for up to two hours
- **Performance specifications:**
  - Operational in Category 5 hurricane conditions (horizontal winds up to 209 knots, vertical velocities up to 30 m/s)
  - Survived and reported data during Hurricane Milton
  - Cost: approximately $15,000 per deployment
- **Operational history:** 
  - 19 deployments from NOAA WP-3D aircraft in 2024 hurricane season
  - 23 deployments in 2025 hurricane season
  - All deployments described as "successfully gathering" required data
- **Advantages over existing alternatives:** Continuous data collection at air-sea interface versus single-point, episodic AVAPS dropsonde deployments; cost-effective alternative delivering similar temporal resolution

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary:** Hurricane/tropical cyclone atmospheric profiling and storm intensification research
- **Proposed expansion:** Polar and winter storm environments (via icing protection enhancements)
- **Military/Defense:** Navy situational awareness and operational safety in hazardous weather conditions
- **Civilian/Dual-use:** NOAA hurricane research and operational forecasting
