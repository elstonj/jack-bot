## Document Overview
This document is the NASA Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Program Phase I solicitation for Fiscal Year 2025. It is not a BST proposal, report, or capability document. Rather, it is the official government solicitation document that Black Swift Technologies (or any other small business) would respond to when submitting a Phase I SBIR proposal to NASA. The document establishes the rules, requirements, deadlines, evaluation criteria, and submission procedures for SBIR Phase I awards.

## Key Changes from Amendment P00004
The most significant amendment revised the proposal submission deadline:
- **Original deadline:** March 10, 2025, 5:00 p.m. ET
- **Revised deadline:** May 21, 2025, 5:00 p.m. ET

Other notable changes:

## Program Structure

### Phase I
- **Maximum Contract Value:** $150,000
- **Period of Performance:** 6 months
- **Objective:** Demonstrate technical feasibility of proposed innovation and potential for use in NASA programs/missions and/or commercial markets

### Phase II
- **Maximum Contract Value:** $850,000
- **Maximum Period of Performance:** 24 months
- **Eligibility:** Only Phase I awardees

### Phase III
- **Funding Source:** NASA programs, other government agencies, or private sector
- **Scope:** Further development or use of technologies from Phase I or II awards

## Funding and Awards
- NASA does not commit to fund any proposal or make a specific number of awards
- **Maximum submissions per firm:** 5 proposals (reduced from 10 in prior years)
- **Maximum awards per firm:** 3 contracts (reduced from 5 in prior years)
- Awards determined based on appropriated funding and evaluation scores

## Eligibility Requirements

### Small Business Concern (SBC)
- Must meet SBA definition of small business at proposal submission and contract award
- Combined with affiliates: not more than 500 employees
- Must be primary performer: at least 67% of effort; subcontractors/consultants may perform up to 33%

### Principal Investigator (PI)
- Must be primarily employed with the SBC (more than 50% of time, based on 40-hour workweek)
- Primary employment must be maintained at time of award and during project conduct
- Co-PIs not allowed
- Misrepresentation of qualifications will result in proposal decline or contract termination

### Registration Requirements
- **SAM.gov registration** required with valid Unique Entity Identifier (UEI)
- **SBA Company Registry registration** required with SBC Control ID
- Must list "All Awards" as purpose of registration in SAM
- NAICS code 541713 or 541715 required at contract award (not proposal submission as of amendment)

### Foreign Affiliation Disclosures
- All firms must complete "Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries" form
- Must disclose:
  - Owners/covered individuals in foreign talent recruitment programs (especially China)
  - Joint ventures or subsidiaries with foreign affiliations
  - Contractual or financial obligations to foreign states/entities
  - Ownership in foreign countries of concern
  - Venture capital investment with foreign affiliations
  - Technology licensing or IP sales to foreign countries (5-year period)
  - Any foreign-related entities
- Failure to disclose may result in award denial
- Foreign involvement does not automatically disqualify but must be disclosed

### Other Restrictions
- Venture-capital-owned businesses (majority-owned by multiple VC operating companies, hedge funds, or private equity firms) not eligible
- Joint ventures and limited partnerships permitted if entity qualifies as SBC
- No human or animal subjects allowed in Phase I

## Proposal Requirements

### Technical Proposal Format
- **Maximum length:** 15 pages (standard letter 8.5" × 11")
- **Margins:** 1.0 inch
- **Type size:** 10 point minimum
- **Header:** SBC name, proposal number, project title on each page
- **Footer:** Page number and proprietary legend (if applicable)

### Technical Proposal Structure (9 Parts)
1. **Identification and Significance of Innovation** (~3.5 pages)
   - Describe innovation relative to current state of the art
   - Address relevance to NASA interests

2. **Technical Objectives** (~1 page)

3. **Work Plan** (~3 pages)

4. **Related R/R&D** (~1 page)

5. **Key Personnel and Bibliography of Directly Related Work** (~2.5 pages)

6. **The Market Opportunity** (~1 page)
   - Preliminary commercialization strategy
   - Market and business factors

7. **Facilities/Equipment** (~1 page)

8. **Subcontractors and Consultants** (~1 page)
   - If subcontractors involved, distribution/management of workload

9. **Related, Essentially Equivalent, and Duplicate Proposals and Awards** (~1 page)

### Complete Proposal Package Components
- Proposal Contact Information Form
- Proposal Certifications Form
- Proposal Summary Form
- Proposal Budget Form
- Technical Proposal (15 pages max)
- Briefing Chart
- NASA Evaluation License Application (if using NASA Technology Available)
- Request for Technical and Business Assistance (TABA) Supplement (optional)
- I-Corps Interest Form (optional)
- SBC Level Forms
- Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries form

## Submission Requirements

### Submission System
- **Platform:** ProSAMS (Proposal Submissions and Award Management System)
- **URL:** https://prosams.nasa.gov/
- **Method:** Electronic submission only
- **Deadline:** Wednesday, May 21, 2025, 5:00 p.m. Eastern Time

### Administrative Review
All proposals undergo administrative review for:
- Format compliance
- Completeness of certifications and registrations
- Eligibility verification
- Foreign affiliation disclosures

Proposals failing administrative review will not be evaluated technically.

## Evaluation Criteria

### Technical Evaluation Categories
1. **Technical Merit and Feasibility:** Innovation quality and technical soundness
2. **Relevance and Significance to NASA:** Alignment with NASA priorities and needs
3. **Commercialization Potential:** Market opportunity and business viability
4. **Cost Reasonableness**

### Scoring and Weighting
(Details in Appendix A of full solicitation)

### Important Note
- **Responsiveness to subtopic critical:** Firms must carefully select correct subtopic; NASA will not move proposals between subtopics

## Evaluation Restrictions
- **Classified information:** Any proposal containing classified information will be declined
- **Proprietary data:** Non-NASA reviewers may be used; confidential business information protection provided

## Supporting Programs and Opportunities

### I-Corps™ Program
- Partnership with National Science Foundation
- Available to Phase I awardees
- Customer discovery and business plan development
- **Funding:** Up to $10,000 for SBIR awardees
- Award made separately via contract modification

### Technical and Business Assistance (TABA)
- **Phase I:** Up to $6,500 supplement
- **Phase II:** Up to $50,000 supplement
- Funds contracted to external vendors for:
  - Technical decisions and problem-solving
  - Risk minimization
  - Commercialization support
  - IP protection
  - Market research/validation
  - Regulatory/manufacturing planning
- Approved vendors: commercialization assistance providers, ESOs, IP/licensing professionals
- TABA funds cannot support internal company activities

### NASA Technology Available (TAV)
- Over 1,400 NASA patents available for licensing (nonexclusive, royalty-free)
- Over 1,000 software applications/tools available
- **NASA Patent Use:** Evaluation license application required before proposal submission
- **NASA Software Use:** Software Usage Agreement (SUA) required after award; clause 48 C.F.R. 1852.227-88 applies
- Inventor knowledge transfer available (with NDA; may require reimbursement)

## Notable Solicitation Features

### Place of Performance
- Congress intends SBIR funding should support American-made equipment and products
- Foreign vendor justification required via Foreign Vendor Form if U.S. sources unavailable
- NASA will not approve purchases from Designated Country list vendors
- Foreign vendor proposal may delay or prevent award

### Restrictions on Work
- **No routine engineering or development of existing products** without substantive innovation
- **System studies and market research alone** not acceptable Phase I focus
- **Proven concepts or unsubstantiated modifications** not fundable

### Research Subtopics
- List of FY 2025 Phase I research subtopics in Chapter 9
- Organized by Mission Directorate and Technology Taxonomy
- Varies year-to-year to maintain alignment with NASA interests

## Regulatory Compliance

### Required Standards
- **Software Development:** NPR 7150.2D (NASA Software Engineering Requirements)
- **Flight Safety:** NPR 8079.1 (Spacecraft Conjunction Analysis and Collision Avoidance)
- **Airworthiness:** AFOP-7900.3-027, AFOP-7900.3D-023, NPR 7900.3 (Commercial Air Service and NASA Airworthiness)
- **Facility Access:** HSPD-12 and FAR 52.204-9 (Personal Identity Verification if accessing federal facilities 6+ months)

### Fraud and Compliance
- NASA Office of Inspector General has full access to all proposals
- False statements may be felony under 18 U.S.C. § 1001 (up to 5 years imprisonment)
- Fraud hotline: 1-800-424-9183; Cyber hotline available at https://oigforms.nasa.gov/wp_cyberhotline.html

## Award Process

### Contract Type
- Fixed-price or cost-reimbursable (determined during negotiation)

### Reporting and Deliverables
- Final report required at Phase I completion
- I-Corps awards made separately via contract modification
- TABA awardees must deliver description of services and results

### Rights in Data
- Rights in data developed under SBIR specified in contract
- Copyrights retained by awardee with government use rights
- Invention reporting required; election of title and patent filing obligations apply

### Performance Benchmarks
- More experienced firms (21+ Phase I awards) must meet commercialization benchmarks
- SBA notifies companies and agencies of benchmark failures
- Details: https://www.sbir.gov/performance-benchmarks
