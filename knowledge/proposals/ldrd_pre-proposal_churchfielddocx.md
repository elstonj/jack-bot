## Executive Summary
NREL proposes a two-year effort to develop an unmanned aerial system (UAS)-based platform for high-quality flow measurement in and around wind plants. The system would combine tower-mounted instrument quality with the mobility to measure turbulence and wake interactions at any location within a wind plant's flow field, leveraging access to NWTC (National Wind Technology Center) for validation.

## Technical Approach
- **Core Innovation**: UAS capable of flying at wind/turbine wake speed, allowing it to remain fixed at a single point in space long enough to obtain detailed turbulence measurements (not just traverse the flow field)
- **Components**: Off-the-shelf components including inexpensive remote-controlled aircraft, open-source programmable autopilots, and measurement probes
- **Validation**: Data would be validated against existing accurate mast-mounted flow sensors at NWTC
- **Analysis Support**: Data analysis expertise from NWTC and Computational Science Center

## Use Cases & Applications
- Wind plant aerodynamic flow measurement and characterization
- Wake-turbine-terrain-atmospheric boundary layer interaction studies
- Understanding power production and fatigue load drivers
- Validation and improvement of wind plant aerodynamics simulation tools
- Support for next-generation wind turbine and wind plant development

## Problem Statement Addressed
- Current flow measurement limited to fixed tower instruments or LIDAR/SODAR with limited turbulence information
- Wind plant aerodynamic interactions poorly understood, limiting turbine development and simulation tools
- DOE recognized complexity of complex flow in wind plants (Workshop on Complex Flow referenced)

## Notable Details
- Proposal emphasizes leveraging local UAS expertise in NREL vicinity
- Data intended to be released freely to research community
- Would position NREL as key player in Complex Flow research area
- NWTC provides real-world validation environment and existing measurement infrastructure
- Focus on practical, affordable components rather than custom/expensive systems
