# Commitment Letter - IRISS (University of Colorado)

## Document Metadata
- Type: Commitment Letter (supporting material for SBIR proposal)
- Client/Agency: University of Colorado (CU); supporting NASA SBIR Phase II continuation
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Phase II Contract NNX14CG09C; CCRPP proposal (Climate Change Research Pilot Program)
- Date: 20 March 2017
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: SuperSwift sUAS, modular nose cone, SMM (Soil Moisture Mapper) payload
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (BST CEO), Brian M. Argrow (CU Professor, Director of IRISS)

## Executive Summary
University of Colorado commits $22,390 in funding to support further development of the SuperSwift small unmanned aircraft system under NASA SBIR Phase II contract. CU intends to use the enhanced SuperSwift for wildland firefighting research under the FIREX project with NOAA, viewing the platform as critical to their ongoing research priorities.

## Technical Approach
The committed funds will support:
1. Adaptation of SuperSwift's modular nose cone for FIREX mission requirements
2. Technical enhancements to improve key performance indicators for broader utilization
3. Integration of SMM (Soil Moisture Mapper) payload for fuel assessment and wildfire spread modeling
4. Flight testing and validation by CU as part of FIREX campaign

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SuperSwift sUAS:**
- Small unmanned aircraft system developed under NASA SBIR Phase II
- Modular platform designed for scientific data gathering missions
- Being enhanced for wildland firefighting research applications
- Features modular nose cone adaptable for different mission payloads
- Expected to reach TRL (Technology Readiness Level) 9 and MRL (Manufacturing Readiness Level) 7 by end of CCRPP grant

**SMM Payload:**
- Soil Moisture Mapper payload
- Proposed use: assess available fuel and model wildfire spread
- Applicable to wildland firefighting research

## Use Cases & Applications
- **FIREX Project**: Wildland firefighting research conducted jointly by CU and NOAA
- Fuel assessment for wildfire spread modeling
- Applications to Western U.S. wildfire management and economic burden reduction

## Notable Details
- CU providing direct funding commitment: $22,390
- Funds payable within 45 business days of CCRPP award announcement
- Delivery deadline: CU expecting to receive SuperSwift for FIREX operations by 1 October 2017
- CU positioned as future customer pending additional FIREX project funding
- Demonstrates market validation and customer commitment to commercialization
- SuperSwift represents commercialization opportunity beyond original SBIR Phase II scope
- IRISS (Institute for Research in Sensing and Imaging Science) endorsement from CU Aerospace Engineering Sciences department
