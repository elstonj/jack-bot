## Executive Summary
The NightFOX project is developing a UAS-based nighttime fire observation system for NOAA, with Black Swift Technologies manufacturing the S2 platform and providing payload integration support. As of February 2018, most instrument sensors have been selected, scanning mechanisms for IR optics are in manufacturing, and the S2 platform acquisition is expected in May after NightFOX-specific upgrades for nighttime lighting operations.

## Technical Approach
NightFOX integrates multiple sensor types on a UAS platform (S2) for fire observation:
- **Remote sensing**: SWIR and mid-IR sensors with telecentric scanning mechanisms
- **In situ measurements**: AlphaSense particle counters for coarse-mode aerosol measurements, CO sensors (prototype delivery delayed to March)
- **Flight operations**: Development of observation strategies for both in situ and remote sensing flights, with coordination between CSD (NOAA), CU-IRISS, and BST for instrument integration
- **Regulatory pathway**: Working with FAA for COA approval for higher altitudes and BVLOS operations

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 (UAS Platform)
- **What it is**: Black Swift Technologies' unmanned aircraft system being procured by CU for NOAA
- **Proposed use**: Primary airframe for NightFOX nighttime fire observation missions
- **Status/Specifications**: 
  - Expected delivery May 2018 (delayed from earlier timeline)
  - NightFOX-specific upgrades include lighting systems for nighttime operation
  - BST serving as manufacturer providing integration support for payloads
- **TRL**: Platform assessed at TRL 4 (Technology Demonstration)

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Nighttime wildfire observation**: Core mission to observe fires at night using thermal and spectral sensors
- **Specific applications**: 
  - Fire weather research in coordination with USFS-NOAA Fire Weather Research MOU
  - Prescribed burn monitoring (summer/fall 2018 tests planned in Boulder area)
  - Data assimilation for fire weather modeling
  - Higher altitude operations (specific altitudes not stated but under FAA negotiation)
  - BVLOS (Beyond Visual Line of Sight) operations over wildfires

## Technical Readiness Levels
- UAS Platform (S2): TRL 4 (Technology Demonstration)
- UAS Payload: TRL 5 (Technology Validation)
- UAS Observation System: TRL 3 (Experimental Proof of Concept)
- UAS Observation Strategy: TRL 3 (Experimental Proof of Concept)
