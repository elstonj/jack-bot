## Executive Summary
This is a mandatory template form for SBIR/STTR Phase II proposals submitted to the Air Force SBIR/STTR Program through AFVentures. The Customer Memorandum documents the Air Force organization's defense mission need, interest in working with a small business awardee, and commitment to supporting Phase II execution and potential Phase III transition.

## Document Purpose & Structure
This is a **blank template form**, not a completed proposal or technical document. It serves as the official mechanism for:
1. DAF organizations to formally express mission needs and support for SBIR/STTR Phase II projects
2. Establishing stakeholder engagement and governance structure
3. Defining security, cybersecurity, and flight testing requirements
4. Documenting Phase II funding commitments and Phase III transition strategies

## Key Required Elements

### Section 1: Purpose
Documents DAF organization interest in working with SBIR/STTR Phase II awardees on proposed efforts.

### Section 2: Defense Mission Need
- Organizations must describe specific national defense-related mission needs
- Separate justification required if both US Air Force AND US Space Force missions apply
- Requires up to five measurable goals with clear success criteria

### Section 3: Project Timeline
Identifies expected start date and period of performance based on solicitation requirements.

### Section 4: Phase II Funding Commitment
- Non-SBIR/STTR Government funding is optional but may supplement the award
- Draft funding documents (Form 9s/MIPRs) due within 45 days of solicitation closing
- Certified funding documents required within 5 business days of selection letters
- Funds contingent on availability at contract execution

### Section 5: Additional Agreements
DAF organization commits to consulting with Air Force SBIR/STTR Legal, Contracting, and Program Office personnel to formalize cooperation and accommodate specialized requirements (GFE/I, facility access, licenses, environmental considerations).

### Section 6: Phase III Interest
Establishes DAF commitment to consider Phase III contract transition if Phase II is successful, subject to acquisition planning, funds availability, security assessments, and review requirements.

### Section 7: Milestones
References Attachment 1.1 (Schedule of Milestones) to provide value to end-users and customers.

### Section 8: Stakeholders (Critical)
Required identification of:
- **Primary End-User Organization**: The organization that will use or primarily benefit from the solution
- **Primary End-User Point of Contact**: Name, rank/grade, duty title, email
- **Primary Customer Organization**: Organization managing future procurement, sustainment, funding, contracting, and logistics
- **Primary Customer Point of Contact**: Name, rank/grade, duty title, email
- **Primary Phase II Technical Point of Contact (TPOC)**: MUST be DAF civilian or military member (not contractor or SETA support contractor); includes name, rank/grade, organization, email, phone
- **Alternate Phase II TPOC**: Backup with same requirements
- **Strategic Stakeholders**: MAJCOM/HQ functions and PEO/Center impacted
- **Staff Functions Concurrence**: Security, IA/Cybersecurity, Finance, Contracting, Strategy/Integration/Requirements, Small Business Office, Commander, Legal Counsel, Engineering/SPO/Test, Public Affairs

**TPOC Responsibilities:**
- Receive and accept/reject deliverables
- Provide performance and progress feedback
- Overcome tactical execution barriers (base access, waivers, certifications)
- Drive Phase III transition if successful

### Section 9: Phase III Transition Strategy
DAF organizations must tailor transition strategy narrative addressing:
- Phase III funding strategy
- Requirements personnel role (if applicable)
- Contract vehicle
- Integration plan
- Must not use boilerplate language; must be specific to organization and solution

### Section 10: Security
Company must indicate if it will require:
- **Military installation/facility access**: TPOC works with visitor centers or security offices to sponsor individuals
- **Common Access Cards (CACs)**: Security office processes documentation for CAC issuance
- **Classified accesses (DD254)**: Security office drafts DD254 and facilitates classified access clearances

### Section 11: Cybersecurity
If proposed solution involves software/IT connecting to DoD networks/systems or utilizing Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI):
- Information Assurance/Cybersecurity office determines if Authorization to Operate (ATO) or Interim Authorization To Test (IATT) required
- Company works with Authorizing Official or Platform One
- Compliance with NIST SP 800-171 Rev 1 and DFARS PGI 239.76 required
- AFVentures does not manage this process; customer/TPOC responsible

### Section 12: Flight Testing or UAS Utilization
If company requires flight testing or Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS) operations:
- Blanket waiver available for commercial COTS UAS for cyber analysis, signature, system checkout, or profile data in benign/controlled RF environments
- Company must pursue "use waiver" with Government TPOC
- Contract execution not dependent on use waiver completion, but early coordination essential
- Company coordinates immediately after contract award with TPOC, AFRL/DO, and AFWERX Test Personnel on flight test planning per AFRL/DO instructions

**Definition of Flight Testing:** Any set of related events where a vehicle moves through air using controlled/maneuvering aerial transport (balloon, aircraft, UAV, air taxi, ground test, cockpit evaluation). Excludes purely ballistic flight paths.

### Section 13: Signatories
- **Two CAC-enabled digital signatures required** (mandatory for acceptance)
- DAF Customer signatory and DAF End-User signatory MUST be different individuals
- Signatories must understand and exert influence over operational needs, requirements, or funding
- Memorandum without two signatures will NOT be accepted
