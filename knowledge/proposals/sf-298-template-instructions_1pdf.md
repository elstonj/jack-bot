## Executive Summary
This document is a template and instruction guide for completing the Standard Form 298 (SF-298), the Report Documentation Page required by the Department of Defense. It provides definitions and guidance for all 19 data fields on the form, which is used to standardize the documentation and metadata for technical reports, research, and project deliverables submitted to government agencies.

## Document Purpose
The SF-298 is a mandatory administrative form used by DoD and other federal agencies to collect standardized metadata about technical reports. The form establishes uniform documentation practices across government-sponsored research and development projects.

## Form Fields & Instructions

1. **Report Date** - Full publication date (DD-MM-YYYY format, Year 2000 compliant)
2. **Report Type** - Category (final, technical, interim, progress, quarterly, etc.)
3. **Dates Covered** - Time period during which work was performed
4. **Title and Subtitle** - Full report title with volume/part numbers if applicable
5. **Identification Numbers** - Contract, grant, program element, project, task, and work unit numbers
6. **Author(s)** - Names of persons responsible for writing/research (format: Last, First, Middle Initial)
7. **Performing Organization** - Name and address of the organization conducting the work
8. **Performing Organization Report Number** - Unique alphanumeric identifier assigned by performing organization
9. **Sponsoring/Monitoring Agency** - Name and address of financially responsible organization
10. **Sponsor/Monitor Acronym(s)** - Agency abbreviations (e.g., BRL, ARDEC, NADC)
11. **Sponsor/Monitor Report Number(s)** - Report identifier assigned by sponsoring agency
12. **Distribution/Availability Statement** - Public availability or distribution restrictions (RD/FRD, PROPIN, ITAR, copyright info)
13. **Supplementary Notes** - Additional information (cooperative arrangements, translations, superseded editions)
14. **Abstract** - Brief factual summary (approximately 200 words) of significant information
15. **Subject Terms** - Keywords or phrases identifying major concepts
16. **Security Classification** - Classification level (U, C, S, etc.) for report, abstract, and page
17. **Limitation of Abstract** - Distribution limitation code (UU = Unclassified Unlimited; SAR = Same as Report)
18. **Number of Pages** - Total page count
19. **Responsible Person Contact** - Name and telephone number of point of contact
