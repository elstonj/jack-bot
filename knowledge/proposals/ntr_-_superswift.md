## Executive Summary
The SuperSwift is a novel small unmanned aircraft system (sUAS) with a 10-foot wingspan specifically designed to carry an L-band radiometer payload for soil moisture mapping missions. The aircraft features a modular, field-swappable nose cone to accommodate scientific payloads, pusher propeller configuration for safety and payload accessibility, electric propulsion enabling 1.5+ hours flight time, and hand-launchable capability with payload capacity of 3-5 lbs.

## Technical Approach

### Airframe Design Philosophy
The SuperSwift design was driven by specific constraints from the soil moisture payload:
- L-band radiometer antenna must be surrounded by fiberglass (minimum thickness) with no conducting materials
- Payload must be housed in a removable nose cone
- Aircraft must be hand-launchable for operation in unimproved areas
- Must achieve minimum 1.5 hours flight time
- Payload capacity: 3 lbs nominal, 5 lbs maximum

**Design Solutions:**
- **Pusher configuration with low tail boom**: Keeps nose clear and easily removable; provides structural barrier between operator hand and propeller for safe hand launch
- **V-tail design**: Reduces structural weight, simplifies mechanical design, enables faster field setup/teardown; uses NACA 0012 airfoils with 1° positive incidence angle to operate near zero-lift condition
- **Wing sizing**: Driven by hand-launch requirement (target wing loading ~20 oz/ft²); uses MH 70 airfoil (11.08% thickness-to-chord ratio) selected for high coefficient of lift (Cmax ~1.5), smooth stall transition, thick cross-section, and low pitching moment (Cm ~-0.05)

### Aerodynamic Specifications
- **Wing area**: 1,100 in² (10 ft² approximately)
- **Wing span**: 120 in (10 ft)
- **Root chord**: 10 in; Tip chord: 7.5 in
- **Mean aerodynamic chord**: 9.24 in
- **Aspect ratio**: 13.09
- **Taper ratio**: 0.84
- **Wing configuration**: High-wing, no dihedral angle, straight leading edge (0.6° forward sweep)
- **Maximum coefficient of lift (wing)**: 1.23
- **Stall speed** (at 6,000 ft, standard atmosphere): 33.5 ft/s
- **Wing loading**: 22 oz/ft²

### Tail Configuration
- **V-tail airfoil**: NACA 0012
- **Root chord**: 7.0 in; Tip chord: 5.75 in
- **Distance from main wing leading edge**: 40 in
- **Horizontal tail volume**: 0.48
- **Vertical tail volume**: 0.015
- **Predicted downwash angle at tail**: 1.3°

### Weight & Balance
- **Empty weight**: 7.5 lbs
- **Gross takeoff weight (GTOW)** with 3 lb payload: 10.5 lbs
- **Maximum GTOW** with 5 lb payload: 12.5 lbs
- **Neutral point**: 4.25 in aft of wing leading edge
- **Center of gravity (CG)**: 2.0 in from wing leading edge
- **Static margin**: 21% (0.5 in forward of wing root ¼-chord)

### Propulsion
- Electric propulsion system
- Motor and propeller selection completed by 01/10/2015

### Manufacturing & Assembly
- Modular design: field-swappable nose cone and removable subsystems
- Field assembly without tools
- Laser-cut payload tray for rapid prototyping of sensor mounts
- Quick field setup/teardown

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift sUAS
**What it is:**
A small unmanned aircraft system with 10-foot wingspan designed as a dedicated platform for aerial scientific sensing missions. It combines electric propulsion, hand-launch capability, modular payload architecture, and extended flight duration in a single integrated system.

**Key Features:**
- Hand-launchable electric aircraft
- Modular nose cone (removable, field-swappable)
- Pusher propeller configuration (safe launch, payload flexibility)
- V-tail configuration (light weight, simplified assembly)
- Extended flight time for electric sUAS (~1.5+ hours)
- Payload capacity 3-5 lbs
- Operation in unimproved areas with minimal support equipment
- Tool-free field assembly

**Specifications:**
- Wingspan: 10 ft (120 in)
- GTOW: 10.5 lbs (with 3 lb payload); up to 12.5 lbs (with 5 lb payload)
- Empty weight: 7.5 lbs
- Cruise stall speed: 33.5 ft/s (at 6,000 ft altitude)
- Wing loading: 22 oz/ft²
- Flight time: Minimum 1.5 hours

**Soil Moisture Mapping Configuration:**
- Designed to carry L-band radiometer in removable nose cone
- Radiometer antenna housed in fiberglass-only nose (no conducting materials)
- Well-defined mechanical and electrical interface for third-party sensor integration

## Notable Details

### Novel Features
1. **Modular, field-swappable nose cone**: Crucial for soil moisture payload tuning/maintenance and enables rapid sensor package changes for different scientific objectives
2. **Integrated system design**: Combination of features (field assembly without tools, pusher propeller allowing payload integration in nose, long electric flight time, hand-launchability with 5 lb payload capacity, laser-cut payload tray for rapid prototyping) represents substantial advancement over existing commercially available platforms

### Design Rationale
- **Detailed searches and assessments** of commercially available platforms found none meeting all requirements
- Specific design choices made to balance competing requirements: pusher configuration vs. hand safety, V-tail for weight reduction vs. manufacturing simplicity, airfoil selection for stall behavior and pitching moment characteristics

### Commercialization Strategy
- SuperSwift positioned as foundational platform with user-friendly interfaces and low operational costs
- Modularity enables broad appeal by allowing customization of sensor packages for specific scientist, researcher, and commercial customer needs
- Broad market potential identified across agriculture, environmental monitoring, and scientific research applications

### Organizational Innovation
The document emphasizes that while individual features (pusher prop, V-tail, electric propulsion, hand-launch) are not novel individually, **their specific combination in a single system optimized for scientific payload integration represents the innovation**. This platform approach enables rapid deployment of new scientific sensing capabilities without requiring new airframe development.
