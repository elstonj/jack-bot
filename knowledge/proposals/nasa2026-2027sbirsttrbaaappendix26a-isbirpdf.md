## Executive Summary
This NASA SBIR Phase I solicitation announces funding opportunities for small businesses to develop innovative technologies across 12 subtopics organized into 10 technical themes. Phase I awards are capped at $225,000 for up to 6 months, with proposals due May 21, 2026. The BAA invites proposals for advanced materials, in-space logistics and robotics, EVA suit architectures, propulsion heaters, lightweight structures, power systems, instruments, detector technologies, space weather applications, and in-space manufacturing capabilities.

## Research Subtopics (12 Total)

### 1. **CARITL.1.S26A: Development of Hydrazine-Compatible Elastomeric Materials for Long-Duration Spaceflight**
- **Lead Center:** KSC
- **Participating Center(s):** JSC
- **Problem:** Heritage AF-E-411 rubber formulation no longer viable due to unavailable precursors. Critical gap for NASA missions requiring long-duration hydrazine exposure in space.
- **Scope:** Develop replacement elastomer from currently available precursors maintaining chemical compatibility with hydrazine for multi-year exposure, mechanical properties comparable to AF-E-411, manufacturability, and reliable performance under spaceflight conditions (vacuum, thermal cycling, radiation).
- **Expected TRL at Completion:** 2–4
- **Phase I Deliverables:** Trade studies identifying candidate elastomer formulations; preliminary hydrazine compatibility testing (NASA-STD-6001B Test 15); initial mechanical property characterization; down-select materials for Phase II optimization and full Test 15 evaluation
- **Phase II Deliverables:** Optimized formulation; extended aging tests under simulated spaceflight conditions; manufacturing processes and prototype components; validated material specification and test data package
- **Applications:** Propulsion system seals, gaskets, diaphragms for spacecraft and planetary landers; long-duration hydrazine propellant storage for deep-space missions; replacement parts for legacy systems
- **Need Horizon:** Near-Term (Less than 5 years)
- **Key References:** NASA Technical Memorandum on Long-Time Dynamic Compatibility of Elastomeric Materials with Hydrazine (1973); DTIC Report on Hydrazine Compatibility with Ethylene-Propylene Elastomers (1969)

### 2. **EXPAND.1.S26A: Flight Demonstrations of Commercial In-Space Logistics, Robotic Manipulation, and Automation Systems for Future Space Operations**
- **Lead Center:** GSFC
- **Participating Center(s):** ARC, JSC, LaRC
- **Problem:** Need innovative concepts for flight demonstration of commercial in-space logistics, robotic manipulation, and automation systems to enable advanced exploration, science, and in-space production.
- **Scope:** Must include plan for flight demonstration with launch within 5 years, preferably using NASA STMD Flight Opportunities program or low-cost alternatives. Must advance space logistics and/or robotic manipulation capabilities applicable to defined target applications. Orbital flight technology demonstration required; planetary surface demos acceptable only if orbital relevance demonstrated or achievable at LEO funding levels.
  
**In-Space Logistics Systems (3 primary functions):**
  1. Delivery systems for materiel transportation to pre-existing space assets
  2. Aggregation systems for collection, sorting, temporary storage of materials/components
  3. Earth-return systems for packaging/returning products, samples, equipment (NOT reentry system technology itself, only in-space systems supporting reentry operations)

**Robotic Manipulation and Autonomous System Architectures (6 categories):**
  1. Mobile robotic systems (walking, climbing, free-flyers)
  2. Multi-degree-of-freedom manipulators, end effectors, tools
  3. Pick-and-place automation systems
  4. Hybrid and novel configurations
  5. Multi-agent proliferated robotics
  6. Surface-mobile platforms (rover-mounted manipulation in scope, not rover technology itself)

**Target Applications (5 key operational areas):**
  1. In-Space Servicing Operations (ORU delivery/installation, propellant transfer, maintenance, derelict spacecraft removal, surface mobility asset maintenance)
  2. In-Space Assembly and Construction Operations (structural component delivery, integration, outfitting, robotic assembly of platforms/structures, surface infrastructure construction)
  3. Automated Science Operations (glovebox-equivalent robotic manipulation, experiment automation, sample handling/preparation, commercial LEO science support)
  4. In-Space Production Support (consumable delivery/management, robotic manufacturing, analytical tool handling, material restocking, product packaging)
  5. Commercial Platform and Human Spaceflight Support Operations (inspection, inventory management, workspace setup, intravehicular handling, human-robot operations, habitat setup, cargo handling)

- **Expected TRL at Completion:** 3–6
- **Phase I Deliverables:** Integrated system concept with preliminary design (cost-effectiveness, modularity); comprehensive feasibility analysis (technical/economic risk); mission scenario definition with detailed CONOPS; cost-effectiveness analysis vs. existing solutions; technology maturation roadmap with TRL advancement plan; detailed flight demonstration mission plan with identified flight opportunity and 5-year launch timeline; for planetary surface applications, justification of orbital demonstration relevance; Phase II development plan with key milestones and commercialization pathway
- **Phase II Deliverables:** Matured Phase I technology; ground demonstration of in-space logistics/robotic manipulation/automation systems; mission analysis; commercialization plans with potential mission partners; subsystems ready for integration into suborbital flight or relevant space environment testing; detailed flight demonstration mission plan; detailed risk assessment and mitigation plan
- **Participating Technologies Mentioned:** Canadarm2, Japanese Module Remote Manipulator System, OSAM-1, Robotic Servicing of Geosynchronous Satellites (RSGS), Automated Reconfigurable Mission Adaptive Digital Assembly Systems (ARMADAS), NASA Precision Assembled Space Structure (PASS), Tall Lunar Tower (TLT), Robot Operating System (ROS), Astrobee, Space Robot Operating System (SpaceROS), Cold Operable Lunar Deployable Arm (COLDArm), Ingenuity
- **Critical Gaps:** Flight-rated component development; robotic system integration for assembly capabilities; affordability for future missions and proliferated commercial implementation
- **Need Horizon:** Near-Term (Less than 5 years)
- **Numerous NASA shortfall references (25 total) addressing modular design, observatory instrument installation, robotic hardware supply chains, cooperative interfaces, robotic assembly/construction, autonomous operations, health monitoring, free-flying systems, manipulation, autonomous coordination, robotic actuation, mobile manipulation, sensing for autonomous operations**

### 3. **EVA.1.S26A: Advanced Spacesuit Architectures, Technologies, and Designs for Mars Exploration**
- **Lead Center:** JSC
- **Problem:** EVA suits for Mars require mass reduction (approx. 1/3 reduction from xEMU), enhanced durability and performance, thermal control optimization, and designs balancing mass reduction with maintainability.
- **Scope:** Technologies and concepts for advanced EVA suit architectures optimized for Martian surface environment. Areas of interest: mass reduction strategies for Mars gravity, enhanced durability/performance for Mars conditions, thermal control for Mars thermal environment, design approaches balancing mass with maintainability.
  
**High-value targets include:**
  - Lightweight upper torso architectures
  - Advanced bearing and disconnect interfaces
  - Materials/components providing necessary thermal control
  - Technologies offering enhanced durability/performance per EVA Development Roadmap
  - Possible proposal scopes: Low-pressure (1-2 psid) additively manufactured suit platform for rapid architectural evaluation; flexible/adaptable architecture suit concepts; bonded/roved/simplified hard/soft interface designs; advanced lightweight bearing designs; integrated structural/thermal materials for partial atmosphere; variable emissivity radiators; heat rejection/isolation technologies; durability-enhancing technologies; in-situ printable suit components; EVA-borne planetary protection; active dust mitigation; enhanced in-helmet communication; dust-tolerant bearings

- **Expected TRL at Completion:** 1–4
- **State of the Art:** EMU (waist entry) with MXB7701/7781 epoxy/fiberglass prepreg for Hard Upper Torso (HUT) and stainless-steel bearings; xEMU (rear entry) government reference design with options: Aluminum 6061-T6 or lightweight fiberglass composite (HexForce 6781HT/Patz PMT-F4A) for HUT, Titanium 6AL4V bearings/disconnects
- **Phase I Deliverables:** Analysis/testing of candidate architectures, materials, designs against requirements; prototype material coupons, sub-scale or full-scale Pressure Garment Subsystem (PGS) components in sufficient quantity for vendor testing; final report
- **Phase II Deliverables:** Analysis of Phase I results; material/design optimization; Manufacturing and NDE plan; sub-scale/full-scale PGS component prototype for NDE and destructive testing; testing results and analysis; final report
- **Critical Gaps:** Suit mass reduction for Mars gravity; thermal control of full-suit system in Mars environment; development of solution-space evaluation platform; bearing/disconnect lightweighting; new materials/technologies for EVA Development Roadmap gaps
- **Need Horizon:** Near-Term (Less than 5 years)
- **References:** EVA Roadmap ICES papers (2025); NASA Advanced Space Suit Pressure Garment System Status and Development Priorities (2022-2025)
