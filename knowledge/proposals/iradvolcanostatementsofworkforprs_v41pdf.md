- **Type:** Statement of Work / Procurement Specification
- **Client/Agency:** NASA Ames Earth Science Division
- **Program/Solicitation:** NASA SBIR Phase III; IRAD-2021 Proposal titled "sUAS Tracking of Subtle Volcano Emissions"
- **Date:** 2021 (proposal year; document version dated internally)
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** S2 UAS (unmanned aerial system), ground control system, launcher
- **Key Personnel:** Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This statement of work details NASA Ames' procurement requirements for a Black Swift Technologies S2 UAS platform equipped with multiple sensor suites to conduct volcanic gas monitoring and photogrammetry over active volcanoes. The system builds on prior SBIR Phase II development and references a parallel USGS Phase III SBIR effort (NASA Phase IIe contract NNX17CP13C) that demonstrated the volcano flux sensor integration.

## Technical Approach
The procurement specifies a complete sUAS system designed to enable measurements over a 15 km diameter circle. The S2 is a Category 1 UAS with:
- Gross takeoff weight: <55 lbs (including payload)
- Maximum payload capacity: 10 lbs
- Minimum endurance: 1 hour
- Autonomous flight capable with full avionics integration
- Compatible with multiple field-swappable sensor suites

The system includes a launch system and ground control station. NASA requires support for integration of agency-provided/specified instruments and pilot training.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- **What it is:** Small unmanned aerial system (Category 1) with integrated airframe, avionics, and autonomous flight capability
- **Proposed use:** Primary platform for volcanic gas monitoring and photogrammetry missions over active volcanoes
- **Specifications:** <55 lbs GTOW, 10 lb payload capacity, 1+ hour endurance, 15 km diameter survey capability

### Volcano Flux Sensor Suite
- **What it is:** Integrated gas analysis payload duplicating design implemented by BST for USGS Phase III SBIR (NASA Phase IIe, contract #NNX17CP13C)
- **Components:**
  - CO₂ sensor
  - SO₂ sensor
  - H₂S sensor
  - Spectrometer with collimating optics
  - Pressure, temperature, humidity sensor(s)
- **Proposed use:** Direct measurement of volcanic gas emissions for flux calculations
- **Field deployment:** Integrated onto S2 platform

### Photogrammetry Sensor Suite
- **What it is:** Field-swappable nose cone with imaging sensors
- **Components:**
  - 24 MP RGB camera
  - FLIR Vue Pro R thermal camera
  - Extra battery for extended flight time
- **Proposed use:** Simultaneous visible and thermal imaging of volcanic features and terrain

### Iridium Communications Package
- **What it is:** Satellite backup communications system integrated with ground station and aircraft
- **Proposed use:** Redundant command/data link for operations in remote volcanic regions with limited terrestrial connectivity

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary mission:** Tracking of subtle volcanic gas emissions and flux measurements
- **Geographic scope:** Active volcano monitoring over 15 km diameter survey areas
- **Secondary capability:** Thermal and photographic documentation of volcanic features and terrain changes

## Notable Details
- **Prior development:** S2 designed through an earlier SBIR Phase II effort
- **Proven sensor design:** Volcano flux sensor suite replicates successful design from parallel USGS Phase III SBIR (NASA Phase IIe, contract #NNX17CP13C)—demonstrating both prior BST work and NASA recognition of the approach's viability
- **Sole-source procurement:** NASA intended to pursue this as a sole-source SBIR Phase III purchase, indicating confidence in BST's platform and proven capabilities
- **Modular architecture:** Field-swappable sensor suites allow mission flexibility without redesigning the platform
- **Regulatory compliance:** Category 1 classification indicates compliance with FAA small UAS regulations
