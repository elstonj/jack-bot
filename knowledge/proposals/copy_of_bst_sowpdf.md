## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies will collaborate with CIRA during 2025 to integrate fire detection and soil moisture monitoring data into wildfire modeling and prediction systems. BST will provide rapid deployment of its S2 aircraft equipped with the NOAA NightFOX payload, along with sensor data processing and fuel loading analysis to support WRF-SFIRE model development.

## Technical Approach
The project involves four primary work tasks:
1. **Data Format Conversion**: Converting NOAA NightFOX hotspot detections and BST Soil Moisture Sensor data into formats compatible with the WRF-SFIRE (Weather Research and Forecasting - Stochastic Fire Dynamics) model
2. **Sensor Data Collection**: Gathering soil moisture and NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) data over areas of interest to examine fuel loading characteristics
3. **Rapid Deployment Operations**: Deploying an S2 aircraft equipped with the NOAA NightFOX payload based on satellite-detected lightning activity, with real-time guidance from CIRA
4. **Project Support**: Contributing to meetings, reports, and other collaborative activities
