## Executive Summary
TurfScout provides a letter of support for Black Swift Technologies' SBIR Phase II proposal to map soil integrity using an L-band radiometer mounted on a UAS. The letter validates BST's technology based on a 2022 pilot project and emphasizes the commercial importance of soil moisture mapping for water conservation in golf course management.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed to improve an L-band radiometer and integrate it into a UAS platform for soil moisture mapping. The approach involves aerial remote sensing to detect soil moisture conditions with improved accuracy and reliability compared to existing methods.

## Products & Capabilities Described

**L-band Radiometer**
- Core technology for soil moisture sensing
- Mounted on a UAS platform
- Being improved for Phase II effort
- Designed for commercial adoption and wider use

**UAS (Uncrewed Aircraft System)**
- Carries the L-band radiometer payload
- Purpose-built platform for soil moisture mapping
- Improvements planned during Phase II

## Use Cases & Applications

**Golf Course Soil Moisture Mapping**
- Primary commercial application identified
- Used to reduce water usage on golf courses
- Enables data-driven irrigation management decisions

## Key Results (Pilot Project - 2022)
- BST conducted a day of mapping with TurfScout and Toro partnership
- Mapped soil moisture on golf courses
- Compared radiometer data against ground-based sensors
- Pilot validated the approach and sensor accuracy
