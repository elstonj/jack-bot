## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies submitted this RFI response to demonstrate its portfolio of small unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) compliant with NDAA requirements for the Department of the Interior. BST proposes demonstrations of four primary systems (S0, E2, S2 UAS platforms and SwiftCore FMS) across multiple mission applications including wildfire monitoring, hurricane tracking, volcano observations, methane detection, soil moisture mapping, and infrastructure inspection. All proposed systems are NDAA 2020, 2023, and 2024 compliant.

## Technical Approach
BST's approach centers on modular, ruggedized electric-powered UAS platforms built on proprietary SwiftCore Flight Management System avionics. The company develops end-to-end solutions including aircraft design, manufacturing, payload integration, testing, and field operations. Systems emphasize extreme environment capability, long endurance, autonomous operations, and rapid customization. Unlike competitors relying on open-source avionics, BST guarantees quality and supply of critical components and has developed advanced autonomy features including cooperative multi-vehicle control, predictive maintenance, safe-to-land algorithms, and sensor-based autonomous flight path modification.

### SwiftCore Flight Management System (FMS)
Proprietary modular avionics suite consisting of three integrated systems:

**SwiftPilot Control Board:**
- Size: 6.7cm x 4.0cm x 1.8cm, Weight: 50 grams
- 9 DOF IMU, 10 Hz GPS, 1kHz altitude update
- Cortex-A8 processor (1 GHz Gumstix Overo)
- Interfaces: CAN, UART, I2C, SPI, USB, Ethernet
- Industry-leading sensor-based control minimizing operator workload
- Autonomous flight capability in extreme conditions
- Modifies flight path based on sensor inputs to improve data quality

**SwiftTab Operator Interface:**
- Android-based tablet with gesture and multi-touch interface
- Real-time telemetry display
- Mission planning and control of autopilot, aircraft, and payload functions
- Intuitive design abstracting operational clutter
- Designed for minimal training requirements
- SwiftTab seamlessly integrates with modular payload system

**SwiftStation Ground Station:**
- Size: 15cm x 30cm x 35cm, Weight: 0.9 kg
- Tripod-mounted with 900MHz and GPS antenna
- Links SwiftTab to SwiftPilot for autonomous launch-to-landing control
- Multiple switchable data links for maintained connectivity
- RTK base station capability for centimeter-level accuracy for navigation and payload data tagging

**Advanced Capabilities:**

*Predictive Maintenance:*
- Real-time diagnostics and predictive analytics
- Continuous monitoring of critical aircraft components for early wear/failure detection
- Example: Early detection of propulsion system degradation, preventing in-flight failures
- Detects anomalies in power distribution networks
- Extends maintenance intervals and reduces downtime
- Enables targeted inspections and repairs

*Safe-to-Land System:*
- Machine learning algorithms utilizing onboard sensor data
- Autonomous identification and selection of safe landing zones
- Hazard detection and environmental condition assessment
- Real-time processing (once per second on-board)
- Tested successfully on S2 UAS platform
- Currently low TRL but targeted for commercial inclusion within next year
- Ability to detect obstacles, assess wind conditions, execute safe landings in challenging scenarios

*Cooperative Control (Multi-Vehicle Swarming):*
- NSF and Air Force funded project demonstrating autonomous UAS path planning and optimization
- Autonomous RF emission localization and tracking with mesh network communications
- Successfully tested with WiFi (2.4GHz) and 433MHz emitter tracking
- Field experiments achieved ~20m localization accuracy
- Automatic relay aircraft selection outside direct communication range
- Demonstrated across different small fixed-wing UAS platforms
- Being updated for NOAA 2025 season multi-UAS hurricane operations

*Advanced Autonomy:*
- Proprietary estimation and control schemes outperforming Dronecode options, particularly in high-wind environments
- Distributed avionics system with networked nodes on robust bus
- Enables redundancy, field maintenance, automated part lifetime tracking, expandability, third-party component integration
- Not Dronecode clone but maintains open interfaces supporting multiple protocols
- Provides interoperability while avoiding single-point-of-failure vulnerabilities

**Innovation:** One of few proprietary avionics systems (not open-source); distributed architecture enabling redundancy and field maintenance; advanced control algorithms for extreme wind performance; integrated sensor-based autonomous modifications; real-time onboard processing.
