## Executive Summary
This is a foundational guidance document for NASA's 2024 Earth Science Division solicitation. It introduces NASA's new Earth-Science-to-Action (ES2A) ten-year strategy, designed to increase the impact of Earth science research by connecting scientific knowledge, observations, modeling, and applications to address societal challenges. The document outlines six interdisciplinary science focus areas, program structure (R&A, Earth Action, enabling capabilities), and open science requirements for all funded proposals.

## Technical Approach
The document describes NASA's end-to-end Earth Science Research Program approach:
- Development of observational techniques and instrument technology
- Testing in laboratory and across in situ, surface, ship, balloon, aircraft, and space-based platforms
- Incorporation of results into complex computational models for Earth system characterization
- Partnership development with national and international organizations for environmental forecasting and policy/business decision-making
- Integration of satellite data analysis with process studies and modeling

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Earth Science Division Structure
- **Research and Analysis (R&A)**: Development of new scientific knowledge, satellite mission science teams, interdisciplinary science, calibration/validation
- **Earth Action (EA)**: Applied research and applications through partnerships with end-user organizations; bridge between data/modeling and government/business/organizational decision-making
- **Enabling Capabilities**: Programmatic elements with broad contribution potential, including education support, data/information management, airborne science, and technology development

### Six Interdisciplinary Science Focus Areas
1. **Carbon Cycle and Ecosystems** - Understanding global ecosystems, land cover/use change, biogeochemical cycles, biodiversity
2. **Water and Energy Cycle** - Global water cycle dynamics, energy transfer, terrestrial hydrology, seasonal snow cover
3. **Climate Variability and Change** - Ocean circulation, ice cover changes, sea level, climate-relevant processes and interactions
4. **Atmospheric Composition** - Air quality, ozone recovery, greenhouse gases, aerosol particles, atmospheric chemistry
5. **Weather and Atmospheric Dynamics** - Weather prediction improvement, extreme weather, atmospheric measurements, convection
6. **Earth Surface and Interior** - (referenced but details not included in excerpt)

## Key Policies & Requirements

### Open Science and Data Management Plan (OSDMP)
All proposals must include comprehensive plans addressing:

**Data Requirements:**
- Data needed to validate peer-reviewed publications must be publicly available at publication time
- Remaining scientifically useful data released by award end
- Machine-readable formats with robust metadata
- Digital Object Identifiers (DOI) for citation
- Compliance with ESD Open Data, Services, and Software Policy
- No exclusive access periods
- Community-endorsed repositories or NASA DAACs (Distributed Active Archive Centers)

**Software Requirements:**
- Software for publication validation available at publication time
- Remaining scientifically useful software available by award end
- Open-Source Software (OSS) development in publicly accessible, version-controlled platforms
- Permissive software licensing
- Algorithm Theoretical Basis Documents and user guides publicly available
- Calibration information and simulated products shared

**Publications Requirements:**
- As-accepted manuscripts publicly available at publication time
- Options: upload to NASA PubSpace, publish in CHORUS/ADS/NASA Science Explorer indexed journals
- SMD encourages open access publication
- Conference presentations and technical documents must be publicly available

### Personnel Requirements
- PIs and Co-Is must provide digital persistent identifier (ORCID) via NSPIRES

### Funding Constraints
- Awards to non-governmental organizations via grants or cooperative agreements (not contracts)
- Proposals aiming to fund litigation against government agencies are non-responsive

## Notable Details

### Strategic Context
- **ES2A Strategy**: New ten-year strategy expected release late FY2024, emphasizing translation of research across knowledge layers to actionable information
- **Decadal Survey Alignment**: Based on "Thriving on our Changing Planet: A Decadal Strategy for Earth Observation from Space" (2018)
- **Climate Strategy Priorities**: NASA's 2023 Climate Strategy emphasizes four priorities—innovate, inform, inspire, partner
- **Interagency Coordination**: Major contributor to U.S. Global Change Research Program (USGCRP), Federal Framework for Climate Services, and bilateral efforts with NOAA and USGS
- **International Engagement**: Contributes to National Climate Assessment (NCA5) and IPCC reports

### Program Elements Not Solicited in 2024

- Carbon Monitoring System
- Ocean Salinity Science Team
- Sea Level Change Team
- SWOT Science Team
- Early Career Investigator Program
- Various Earth Action programs (Water Resources, Agriculture, Community Action for Equity/Environmental Justice)
- Instrument development programs (Incubator, Component Technology, In-Space Validation)
- Advanced Information Systems Technology
- Technology Development for Wildfire Science

### Competitive Advantages Emphasized
- Unique capability to provide near-global coverage at temporal/spatial scales necessary to detect change
- Integration of space, airborne, balloon, and ground-based measurements
- End-to-end approach from instrument design through data analysis, interpretation, and forecasting
- Strong operational partnerships enabling transition of research to forecasting/decision-support
