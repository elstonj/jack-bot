## Executive Summary
Jun A. Zhang is a Scientist at the University of Miami specializing in tropical cyclone (hurricane) boundary layer physics, atmospheric dynamics, and in-situ aircraft observations. His extensive publication record and leadership roles in major hurricane research programs (NOAA, ONR, NASA) position him as a subject matter expert for Navy hazardous weather research involving unmanned aircraft systems and hurricane observations.

## Research Expertise

**Primary Focus Areas:**
- Hurricane boundary layer structure and thermodynamics
- Tropical cyclone intensity change and forecasting
- Turbulent characteristics in hurricane low-level regions
- Enthalpy flux measurements in hurricane boundary layers
- Effects of environmental wind shear on hurricane asymmetry
- Planetary boundary layer (PBL) parameterization improvements
- Small unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) for hurricane observation

**Key Technical Contributions:**
- First direct measurements of enthalpy flux in hurricane boundary layer (CBLAST results, 2008)
- Turbulent characteristics analysis of Hurricanes Allen (1980) and Hugo (1989)
- Development of observation-based hybrid EDMF and EDMF-TKE PBL schemes
- Large-eddy simulation framework for tropical cyclone boundary layer
- Dropsonde composite analysis relating boundary layer structure to vertical wind shear
