This is a draft response to NASA ARMD's Aeronautics Flight Accelerator RFI seeking partnerships for rapid, cost-shared flight testing of advanced aeronautics technologies. Black Swift Technologies is responding with the S3 Nomad platform. ## RFI Key Requirements & Scope
NASA is seeking:
- **Revolutionary (not evolutionary) technologies** enabling major capability leaps
- **Cost-shared partnerships** with Government, industry, and academia
- **Flight testing within 3 years** (by FY28 at latest)
- Coverage of: propulsion, airframes, integrated systems, power, GNC, avionics, sensors, autonomy, airspace operations, communications across all flight regimes
- Subscale demonstrations acceptable; multiple spirals acceptable

**Potential Partnership Mechanisms:**
- Funded Space Act Agreements (FSAA)
- SBIR/STTR Phase III
- Similar partnership mechanisms
- NASA may contribute in-kind resources (expertise, flight assets)

**Airworthiness & Safety:**
- Respondents to identify whether using NASA, FAA, or DOD for airworthiness approval
- Specify safety oversight organization
