# Intellectual Property Agreement Letter - University of Miami

## Document Metadata
- Type: IP Agreement / Authorization Letter
- Client/Agency: University of Miami, Office of Research Administration
- Program/Solicitation: Navy STTR (Small Business Technology Transfer)
- Date: February 3, 2026
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None specifically named
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (BST CEO), Deborah Musgrove (UM Asst. Executive Director)

## Executive Summary
University of Miami requires Black Swift Technologies to acknowledge and agree to negotiate in good faith for licensing of any UM background intellectual property that may be incorporated into a proposed Navy STTR application for "Expendable Air-Sea Profiling Observations in Hazardous Weather Conditions via Small Uncrewed Aircraft System." This is a prerequisite authorization letter before UM can formally submit the proposal with BST as the prime contractor.

## Key Agreement Terms
Black Swift Technologies agrees that:
- If any UM background intellectual property is used or incorporated in the application, BST will negotiate in good faith to enter into a license agreement with UM upon award
- IP is broadly defined as "all forms of intellectual property, patentable or not, under the laws of any state or country including, but not limited to, inventions, discoveries, copyrightable works, mask works, trademarks, service marks, trade secrets and proprietary data"

## Notable Details
- University of Miami serves as a subrecipient in this STTR application
- The project involves expendable air-sea profiling observations in hazardous weather using small uncrewed aircraft
- BST CEO Jack Elston signed and acknowledged agreement on 2/3/2026
- This letter was required authorization before UM could submit the proposal
- Contact: UM Office of Research Administration (mra@med.miami.edu)
