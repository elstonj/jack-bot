# AF X24E STTR Phase I Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO)

## Document Metadata
- Type: Solicitation with Amendment 1
- Client/Agency: Department of the Air Force (DAF), in partnership with AFWERX
- Program/Solicitation: STTR AFX24E Phase I, Topic AFX24E-TPCSO1
- Date: Solicitation opened September 11, 2024; proposals due October 10, 2024
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None
- Key Personnel: Not applicable (this is the solicitation itself, not a BST proposal)

## Executive Summary
This is a Department of the Air Force Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) Phase I Commercial Solutions Opening seeking innovative, dual-purpose commercial technologies adaptable to meet Air Force stakeholder needs. The solicitation is "open topic" (agnostic of industry and technology), seeking feasibility studies and prototype-validated concepts under accelerated schedules with maximum funding of $110,000 and 90-day performance period. Multiple awards are anticipated across varying technical approaches.

## Key Solicitation Details

### Funding & Timeline
- **Maximum Phase I Award**: $110,000
- **Maximum Performance Period**: 90 calendar days
- **Proposal Submission Deadline**: October 10, 2024, 12:00 p.m. ET
- **Submission Portal**: Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP) at https://www.dodsbirsttr.mil/

### Eligible Technology Areas (OUSD R&E Critical Technologies)
FutureG; Trusted AI and Autonomy; Biotechnology; Advanced Computing and Software; Integrated Sensing and Cyber; Directed Energy; Hypersonics; Microelectronics; Integrated Network Systems of Systems; Quantum Science; Space Technology; Renewable Energy Generation and Storage; Advanced Materials; Human-Machine Interfaces; Advanced Infrastructure & Advanced Manufacturing; Combat Casualty Care; Emerging Threat Reduction; Military Infectious Diseases; Military Operational Medicine; Mission Readiness & Disaster Preparedness; Nuclear; Sustainment & Logistics

### Air Force Operational Imperatives (Primary Focus Areas)
1. Space Order of Battle
2. Operationally Focused Air Battle Management Systems (ABMS)
3. Moving Target Engagement
4. Tactical Air Dominance
5. Resilient Basing
6. Global Strike
7. Readiness to Deploy and Fight

*Note: Proposals not fitting above categories can reference "BLUE SKY" with explanation of strategic capability.*

## Evaluation Criteria (Equal Importance)

### 1. Commercialization Potential
- Applicant's prior record of commercializing SBIR/STTR or other research (pre-sales, pilots, sales, revenue, active users, subscriptions, downloads, traction/adoption)
- Size of market and revenue potential
- Clarity of business plan
- Evidence of Phase II follow-on commitments (DAF Customer Memorandum or letters of commitment from private sources)

### 2. Defense Need
- Appropriateness, relevance, and specificity of identified Defense Need
- Signed letters of support from Government personnel (favorable but not required)
- Adequacy of proposed effort and relationship to fulfilling Defense Need
- Level of defense mission impact and urgency
- Breadth of applicability across DoD/Government customers

### 3. Technical Approach
- Clarity of problem statement and technical approach
- Soundness and technical merit of approach
- Innovation and differentiation from current alternatives
- Principal Investigator's/Project Manager's and supporting staff qualifications
- Technical approach must represent applied R&D (not "front-end" basic research)

**Cost/Price**: Significantly less important than above three factors combined; considered when technical evaluations are essentially equal.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Business Size & Structure
- Small Business Concern (SBC) with SBC + affiliates ≤ 500 employees
- More than 50% directly owned and controlled by U.S. citizens or permanent resident aliens
- Joint ventures and venture capital-backed entities have specific approval pathways
- **NOT eligible**: SBCs majority-owned by multiple venture capital firms, hedge funds, or private equity funds

### Security & Foreign Affiliation Restrictions
DAF will **deny awards** if applicant has:
- Owner or covered individual in malign foreign talent recruitment program
- Business entity, parent company, or subsidiary in People's Republic of China or "country of concern"
- Owner or covered individual with foreign affiliation to entity in China or "country of concern"
- Foreign affiliations that interfere with capacity, create duplication, present conflicts of interest, were not disclosed, violate Federal law, or pose national security risk

### Work Location & Performance Requirements
- **Location**: R&R&D work must be performed in United States (rare exceptions possible)
- **Work Distribution**: Minimum 40% of R&R&D by Small Business Concern; minimum 30% by single Research Institution (RI)
- **Research Institution Definition**: Non-profit institution or Federally-funded R&D center (FFRDC) with U.S. presence
- **Principal Investigator**: Must have primary employment (>50% time) with SBC or single partnering RI at award and during performance

### Registration & Compliance
- Must be registered in System for Award Management (SAM.gov) with current Unique Entity ID (UEI), formerly DUNS
- Must have Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP) account linked to Login.gov
- Must complete NIST SP 800-171 Self-Assessment and ensure score posted to Procurement Integrated Enterprise Environment (PIEE) Supplier Performance Risk System (SPRS) module
- Failure to complete NIST assessment or post to PIEE/SPRS = automatic disqualification

### Performance Benchmarks (for Repeat Awardees)
Companies with multiple prior SBIR/STTR awards must meet minimum:
- **Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate**: Progress percentage toward commercialization
- **Commercialization Benchmark**: Progress toward post-Phase II commercialization

Companies exceeding DoD award limits (more experienced firms cap: 80 total awards across all DoD, with 20-award caps per service):
- Army: 20 Phase I/Direct-to-Phase II awards
- Navy: 20 Phase I/Direct-to-Phase II awards
- Air Force: 20 Phase I/Direct-to-Phase II awards
- Other DoD: 20 combined

Applicants must self-attest eligibility in DSIP.

## Phase I Objectives

### Feasibility Study Goals
1. Clearly identify potential stakeholders for adapted solution solving Air Force need(s)
2. Describe pathway to integrating into Air Force operations, including:
   - Core technology development approach
   - Navigation of applicable regulatory processes
   - Integration with other relevant Air Force systems/processes
3. Describe if/how solution can be used by other DoD or Government customers

### Ideal Proposal Characteristics
- Non-defense commercial solution adapted to meet Air Force need
- High probability of keeping pace with technological change
- Closely tied to commercial technologies and solutions
- Clear product-market fit between solution and Air Force stakeholder
- Applied research and development (not basic "front-end" R&R&D)
- Concept demonstrations, pilots, and agile development of commercial technologies

## Phase II (if awarded)

Phase II proposals should include:
1. Development, installation, integration, demonstration, and/or test and evaluation of prototype system
2. Evaluation against proposed objectives and measurable key results
3. Detailed description of how adapted solution differs from non-defense commercial offering; scaling pathway for wide adoption
4. Clear transition path with input from end-users, engineering, sustainment, contracting, finance, legal, cyber security
5. Integration with current and future solutions
6. Sustainability and supportability; identification of other DoD/Government customers

## Phase III (Potential Pathway)
Some solutions may advance directly from Phase I to Phase III upon product-market fit verification, transitioning the adapted non-Defense commercial solution for broader mission capability and Governmental/civilian user applications.

## Common Disqualifiers

Proposals will be disqualified for:
- SAM.gov not properly updated or not eligible for ALL AWARDS
- Minimum Performance Percentage of Work not allocated properly
- Work not meeting definition of Research/Research & Development
- Proposal submitted after deadline
- Exceeding maximum $110,000 funding
- Exceeding 90-day performance period
- Applicant not meeting STTR eligibility (ownership/size)
- Prior award for Essentially Equivalent Work
- Failure to submit NIST SP 800-171 Assessment Documentation
- Failure to ensure NIST score published in PIEE/SPRS module and provide DFARS 252.204-7019 information
- Proposal not formatted as single submission
- Missing required proposal volumes
- Multiple proposals from same SBC (only most recent submission evaluated)

## NIST Cybersecurity Compliance (Amendment 1 Addition)

**Critical Requirement - Automatic Disqualification if Not Met**:

Applicants must:
1. Complete NIST SP 800-171 Self-Assessment at https://www.projectspectrum.io/#/cyberreadiness-Check
2. Login/register and navigate to "NIST 800-171" section
3. Save self-assessment score and submission date
4. Email WEBPTSMH.fct@navy.mil with required DFARS 252.204-7019(d)(1) information
5. **Confirm** with above email that firm's NIST score is posted to PIEE's SPRS module
6. Failure to ensure publication in PIEE/SPRS OR failure to provide additional requested information = **automatic proposal disqualification**
