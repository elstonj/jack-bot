## Executive Summary
This is a Form B2 outline/template for a CCRPP application proposing to mature soil moisture mapping technology using small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS). The document provides the required framework and instructions for describing customer objectives, commercialization strategy, and implementation approach to transition SBIR/STTR technology toward market readiness with investor backing.

## Document Character
**Note:** This is primarily a **template and instructional document** rather than a completed proposal. It provides the Form B2 outline structure, requirements, and guidance for applicants to follow when submitting CCRPP applications to NASA. The document includes detailed instructions for each section but minimal actual technical content or proposal specifics from Black Swift Technologies.

## Key Sections & Requirements Outlined

### 1.0 Customer Objectives
Applicants must describe:
- Primary and target customers (NASA programs or non-NASA customers aligned with NASA interests)
- Customer's acquisition plan for the technology
- Whether customer and investor are the same entity
- Investor description and relationship to customer
- Program/project/customer alignment and expected impacts/benefits
- Technology need dates

### 2.0 Additional Commercialization Information
Required elements:
1. Market feasibility and competition strategy (target markets, competitive advantages, key customers, market size, time to market, anticipated competition)
2. Commercialization strategy and company's 5-year business plans
3. Key management, technical personnel, and organizational structure
4. Financial strategy (committed resources, financial requirements, evidence of financial condition)
5. Intellectual property plans and status

### 3.0 Implementation Approach
- Technology maturation strategy with identified tasks
- Technology transition approach to achieve initial capability
- Integration, test, and certification steps
- Supporting programs and research efforts
- Funding application and contingency planning
- Detailed schedule (Gantt chart and milestones)

### 3.2-3.5 Detailed Strategy Sections
- **Procurement Strategy:** Subcontracts and procurements as line items
- **Technical Strategy:** Implementation funding, technology issues, integration issues, test/evaluation, environmental/safety/health considerations
- **Logistics Strategy:** Design interfaces, reliability requirements, maintenance, training, facilities, asset management
- **Manufacturing Strategy:** Production readiness demonstration, supply sources, lean manufacturing, industrial capabilities

### 3.6 Readiness Levels
Required documentation of:
- **Technology Readiness Levels (TRL):** Current and projected TRL with justification
- **Manufacturing Readiness Levels (MRL):** Current and projected MRL (if applicable)
- **Integration Readiness Levels:** Minimum acceptable and projected final levels

## Appendix A: Manufacturing Readiness Levels (MRL 1-10)

Detailed definitions spanning from MRL 1 (Basic manufacturing implications identified) through MRL 10 (Full Rate Production demonstrated with lean practices in place), organized by phases:
- Material Solutions Analysis (MRL 1-3)
- Technology Development (MRL 4-6)
- Engineering and Manufacturing Development (MRL 7-8)
- Production and Deployment (MRL 9-10)

## Appendix B: Technology Readiness Levels (TRL 1-9)

Comprehensive TRL definitions with hardware and software descriptions, exit criteria:
- **TRL 1:** Basic principles observed and reported
- **TRL 2:** Technology concept and/or application formulated
- **TRL 3:** Analytical and experimental proof of concept
- **TRL 4:** Component/breadboard validation in laboratory environment
- **TRL 5:** Component/breadboard validation in relevant environment
- **TRL 6:** System/subsystem prototype demonstration in relevant environment
- **TRL 7:** System prototype demonstration in operational environment
- **TRL 8:** Actual system completed and flight qualified
- **TRL 9:** Actual system flight proven through successful mission operations

Includes definitions of key terms: Proof of Concept, Breadboard, Brassboard, Prototype Unit, Engineering Unit, Laboratory/Relevant/Operational Environments.

## Notable Details
- **Investor Letter Required:** A letter of commitment from an investor is mandatory entrance criterion for CCRPP application
- **Procurement Sensitivity:** Information provided is considered procurement sensitive and protected by government
- **Customer Pull:** Technology acceleration must be clearly tied to strong customer demand at project completion
- **Flexibility:** MRL may not be relevant for all customers; applicants may leave blank at discretion
- **Compliance Requirement:** All parts of application must be submitted through NASA SBIR/STTR Electronic Handbook (EHB)
