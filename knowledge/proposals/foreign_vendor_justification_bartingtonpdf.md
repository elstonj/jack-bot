# Foreign Vendor Justification—Bartington Instruments

## Document Metadata
- Type: Justification letter for use of foreign-sourced component
- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy / NAVAIR Program Management Office
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Topic No. N251-016; Phase II Contract No. N6833525C0492
- Date: 2026-04-02
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Magnetometer system (under development)
- Key Personnel: Angel Ruiz-Reyes (Navy TPOC); Beck Cotter (BST, last editor)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies justifies the use of a magnetic sensor manufactured by Bartington Instruments (UK) in a Navy Phase II SBIR magnetometer project. The justification explains that Bartington is the sole source for the required sensor configuration, and that the Navy has directed a comparative evaluation between the Bartington sensor and a domestic alternative from QuSpin Inc. to assess performance and cost trade-offs.

## Technical Approach
- **Comparative evaluation**: BST will test and evaluate the Bartington magnetic sensor alongside a QuSpin (U.S.-made) sensor to assess performance characteristics and cost trade-offs.
- **Sensor selection rationale**: Bartington Instruments manufactures a specific sensor configuration with unique design, performance characteristics, and form factor that cannot be replicated by domestic vendors.
- **No domestic equivalent**: No U.S. vendor can provide the same sensor design and specifications required for the Navy's objectives.

### QuSpin Inc. Magnetic Sensor (U.S.)
- Domestic alternative sensor
- Being compared against Bartington sensor for performance and cost trade-offs

## Use Cases & Applications
- Navy magnetometer applications
- Potential Royal Navy interoperability and collaboration
- Joint capability development with allied partners

## Notable Details
- **Government direction**: The Navy explicitly directed inclusion of the comparative evaluation between foreign and domestic sensors.
- **Allied interoperability**: The Bartington sensor has garnered interest from the Royal Navy, which has engaged in related technical efforts with the U.S. Navy. Evaluating this sensor supports broader interoperability and collaboration considerations.
- **Justification rationale**: Procurement is necessary to fulfill Phase II technical objectives, is aligned with Government direction, and cannot be satisfied through domestic sources alone.
