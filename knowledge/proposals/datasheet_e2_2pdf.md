## Executive Summary
The Black Swift E2 is a compact, fully autonomous UAS designed for automated industrial inspections in extreme environments. It features a forward-mounted payload configuration, advanced autonomous flight capabilities, and rapid deployment via proprietary flight planning software, delivering real-time data collection and analysis.

## Technical Approach
The E2 employs proprietary carbon fiber and polyamide composite airframe construction with advanced navigation technology supporting completely autonomous flights. The system leverages computer vision and machine learning for intelligent autonomous operation around complex structures. Flight planning utilizes BST's proprietary Flight Planning User Interface accessible via Android tablet running SwiftTab software, with gesture-based controls for minimal operator training.

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Black Swift E2 UAS**
- Compact autonomous quadrotor specifically designed for industrial inspections
- Forward-mounted payload bay (quick-change design, tool-less swaps)
- Adjustable battery placement for payload weight optimization
- Completely autonomous flight with precision navigation around complex structures
- Real-time actionable data delivery to operator

**SwiftTab Software**
- Handheld Android tablet-based mission planning and monitoring interface
- Tab-driven, intuitive flight planning UI
- Gesture-based controls
- Mapping and mission monitoring capability
- Rapid mission setup (minutes to plan)

## Specifications

**Vehicle Characteristics**
- Airframe: Proprietary carbon fiber and polyamide 12
- Frame weight (empty): 1.42 kg (3.13 lbs)
- Electronics and battery: 3.83 kg (8.44 lbs)
- Maximum payload capacity: 1.99 kg (4.39 lbs)
- Maximum takeoff weight (MTOW): 8.78 kg (19.36 lbs)
- Motors: 4x 360Kv
- Battery: Custom Li-ion 41,400 mAh (6 cells)
- Propellers: 4x 18.5 inches
- RC frequency: 2.4 GHz (FASST)
- Telemetry frequency: 900 MHz (ISM)

**Mission Capabilities**
- Flight time (full payload): 20 minutes
- Max winds endured: 15 m/s (30 knots)
