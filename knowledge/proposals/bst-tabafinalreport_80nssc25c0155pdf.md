## Executive Summary
This Phase I TABA report documents Black Swift Technologies' completion of two primary tasks: (1) development of a Phase II Needs Assessment through market research on UAS autonomy and swarming software, and (2) development of a Phase II Commercialization and Business Plan. The work focused on market research across multiple application domains and identification of potential customers and partners for BST's modular autonomy platform for unmanned aircraft systems.

## Technical Approach
The TABA effort employed two complementary research strategies:

1. **Secondary Market Research**: Comprehensive analysis of uncrewed aircraft systems (UAS) markets, drone software (autonomy and swarming capabilities), and application markets including:
   - Remote sensing
   - Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (ISR)
   - Environmental monitoring
   - Emergency response & public safety
   - Infrastructure inspection
   - Precision agriculture
   - Advanced air mobility

2. **Primary Market Research**: Direct outreach to prioritized customer segments operating large heterogeneous UAS fleets, with focus on establishing collaboration relationships for Phase II.

- **Military/Defense**: DoD large UAS fleets, Navy and Marine Small Tactical UAS programs

- **Research Institutions**: MIT Lincoln Laboratory, Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory, Air Force Academy Center for UAS Research, multiple university drone centers and autonomy research programs
