## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed to design and develop a ruggedized UAS platform with specialized payload for volcanic plume observation, building on Phase I work that identified operational concepts, payload requirements, and airframe modifications. The Phase II effort aims to construct prototype hardware, integrate multiple scientific sensors, perform flight testing, and validate the system at an active volcano site. This system would address a critical NASA need for in-situ atmospheric sampling in harsh volcanic environments.

## Technical Approach

### System Architecture
- **Base Platform:** SuperSwift XT airframe (NASA-funded design previously used for supercell operations)
- **Mission Profile:** Autonomous UAS designed to operate safely near and within active volcanic eruptions
- **Flight Management:** SwiftCore FMS (Flight Management System) with autonomy capabilities
- **Operational Concept:** Multi-vehicle capable with coordinated flight patterns specifically designed for volcanic plume sampling

### Phase I Accomplishments (Foundation for Phase II)
- Identified specific Concept of Operations (CONOPS) with flight patterns uniquely designed for volcanic sampling
- Selected candidate scientific payload suite based on prior UAS missions
- Designed mechanical, electrical, and data interfaces for payload integration
- Identified required SuperSwift airframe design changes for high-altitude volcano operations:
  - High density altitude capability (20,000 ft ASL)
  - High turbulence tolerance
  - Significant icing mitigation
  - High-temperature environment resilience
- Constructed and tested multi-hole atmospheric probe for sUAS (cost-optimized for scientific use)
- Generated FAA flight permissions paperwork ready for submission upon award

### Phase II Technical Objectives
1. **Payload Construction:** 
   - Acquire all sensors for volcanic plume observation
   - Design and iterate mechanical interfaces to UAS
   - Create self-contained, integrated payload
   - Design second payload for mass spectrometer integration (candidate sensors identified; potential for lighter/smaller sensor being developed with JPL's Dave Pieri)

2. **Airframe Development:**
   - Construct SuperSwift XT based on Phase I design refinements
   - Implement ruggedization for harsh volcanic environment
   - Integrate power and data interfaces for payload hot-swapping capability

3. **Systems Integration:**
   - Design common power and data interface for field-swappable payloads
   - Develop UI/mission planning software allowing operators to generate atmospheric science flight patterns in field
   - Design for multi-vehicle operations

4. **Validation & Testing:**
   - Flight testing to validate airframe modifications
   - In-situ payload calibration and performance evaluation
   - Field testing at candidate active volcano site (multiple volcanic sites identified as backup options to secure permissions)

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Use Case: Volcanic Monitoring
- **Mission Type:** In-situ atmospheric sampling during active volcanic eruptions
- **Advantages Over Existing Approaches:**
  - No requirement for improved runway infrastructure
  - Direct access to plume interior (different from satellite remote sensing)
  - Capability to resolve vertical gradients and concentration profiles (satellites cannot)
  - On-demand deployment vs. scheduled satellite overpasses
  - Operational in high turbulence, high temperature, icing conditions

### Secondary/Derivative Applications (Mentioned for Commercialization)
- **Wildfire monitoring:** Plume and atmospheric chemistry observation
- **Operational safety:** Air traffic safety during volcanic ash events (historical precedent: Iceland 2010)
- **Extreme weather sampling:** Tornado observation and measurement
- **Infrastructure inspection:** Gas leak detection, environmental monitoring
- **Atmospheric composition research:** General trace gas and aerosol studies

### Specific Scientific Goals Addressed
- Earth science research on volcanic hazards and crustal processes
- Volcanic aerosol and gas plume phenomenology
- First responder support for natural and man-made hazards
- Dynamic natural event investigation
