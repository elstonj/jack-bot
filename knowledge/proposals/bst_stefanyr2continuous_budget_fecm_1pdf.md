# TBDDOE FECM - Orphaned Wells PoP: 3-Year Budget

## Document Metadata
- Type: Budget proposal (3-year Period of Performance)
- Client/Agency: DOE FECM (Department of Energy, Fossil Energy and Carbon Management)
- Program/Solicitation: Orphaned Wells PoP (Proof of Concept)
- Date: 2024
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 (unmanned aircraft system)
- Key Personnel: Maciej Stachura (Principal Investigator), Stefan Metzger/Atmofacts (Data Analysis consultant), Beck Cotter (budget completion)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies submitted a three-year budget proposal to DOE FECM for an Orphaned Wells Proof of Concept project. The project involves conducting aerial surveys and data analysis over two geographic regions: the San Luis Valley near Alamosa, Colorado (Years 1-2) and Osage Nation Lands in Oklahoma (Year 3). Total project cost across three years is approximately $298,546 (direct costs) plus indirect costs.

## Technical Approach
The project employs BST's S2 unmanned aircraft system for aerial surveys. Key components:
- **Year 1:** 10 flights with 2 travelers per flight in San Luis Valley
- **Year 2:** 20 flights with 2 travelers per flight in San Luis Valley
- **Year 3:** 20 flights with 2 travelers per flight in Oklahoma (Osage Nation Lands)
- Data analysis conducted by Stefan Metzger/Atmofacts consultant
- New sensor procurement budgeted for Year 1

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 Unmanned Aircraft System
- Primary operational platform for the project
- Rental cost: $64,194/year × 2 years = $128,388 total
- Used for aerial survey missions over target regions

### Sensor(s)
- Year 1 sensor purchase budget: $44,000
- Additional sensor cost shown in contribution summary: $35,572
- Purpose: Detection/monitoring of orphaned wells (implied by project name and oil/gas context)

## Use Cases & Applications

### Orphaned Wells Monitoring
Primary application: Detection and characterization of orphaned oil and gas wells
- **San Luis Valley, Colorado (Years 1-2):** 30 total flights for well survey/monitoring
- **Osage Nation Lands, Oklahoma (Year 3):** 20 flights for well survey/monitoring
- Likely application: Methane detection, well identification, environmental compliance monitoring
