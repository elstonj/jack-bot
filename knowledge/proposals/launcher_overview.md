## Executive Summary
This document provides a technical overview of Black Swift Technologies' S2 pneumatic launcher system, detailing its mechanical design, operational capabilities, and safety features. The launcher successfully accelerates the S2 aircraft to flight-capable velocities (17.5 m/s demonstrated, 13 m/s minimum required) using pneumatic pressure and has demonstrated durability through 60+ test firings with minimal wear.

## Technical Approach

**Launcher Design:**
- Single-piece extruded aluminum (80-20) rail with center slot for piston
- Pneumatized system using compressed air pressure
- Bipod mount with machined aluminum components
- Impact-absorbing springs at rail end (iteratively refined for durability)
- Minimized custom machining; maximized COTS (commercial off-the-shelf) components
- Contract manufacturer (Geotech Environmental) handles CNC machining and assembly for scalability

**Pneumatic System:**
- Main tank: 145 psi operating pressure
- Pneumatic switch tank: 125 psi operating pressure
- One-way valves on all tanks prevent accidental discharge if disconnected
- Passive blow-off valves set 5 psi above operating pressure for safety
- Automatic pump maintains pressure continuously (compensates for temperature fluctuations)
- Pressure switches prevent firing until tanks are fully charged

**Launch Acceleration:**
- Average acceleration: 12 G's
- Aircraft fuselage designed to withstand these forces with minimal risk of damage
- Carbon fiber launch rail interface within S2 fuselage, secured with Kevlar pulp

**Control System:**
- Three-button interface: Arm, Fire, Vent
- Minimal operator training required
- Operating sequence:
  1. Power on launcher (auto-charges tanks to target PSI)
  2. Hold Arm button for 3 seconds
  3. Press Fire button to launch
  4. System automatically vents both tanks and returns to safe state post-launch
- Vent button allows operator to abort after charging/arming

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S2 Pneumatic Launcher:**
- **What it is:** A ground-based catapult system for rapid aircraft deployment using compressed air
- **Demonstrated performance:** Successfully launched S2 at 17.5 m/s using 16 lbs aircraft weight, 145 psi tank pressure
- **Predicted capability:** Capable of launching up to 21 lbs S2 aircraft at below 150 psi pressure
- **Durability:** One unit test-fired 60 times with little to no visible wear or damage
- **Safety:** Three-stage interlock system (full tank requirement, arm hold duration, separate fire button); automatic venting after launch
- **Portability:** Cart-mounted system with minimal setup

## Use Cases & Applications
- Rapid deployment of S2 small UAS from unprepared surfaces
- Field operations requiring quick aircraft launch without traditional runway infrastructure
- Applications where launcher portability and quick turnaround are beneficial

## Notable Details

**Design Philosophy:**
- Emphasis on COTS components to minimize cost and manufacturing complexity
- Scalable production via contract manufacturer arrangement
- User-friendly three-button interface minimizes training burden

**Safety Features:**
- One-way valves on all tanks
- Dual pressure switches prevent premature firing
- Passive blow-off valves
- 3-second arm button hold requirement prevents accidental firing
- Automatic safe-state return post-launch
- Abort capability via Vent button

**Aircraft Integration:**
- Custom machined aluminum bipod mount
- Flush-mounted launcher rail slot on S2 fuselage
- Carbon fiber and Kevlar reinforcement for launch rail interface
- Aircraft structural design accommodates 12 G acceleration loads
