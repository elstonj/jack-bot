## Executive Summary
This document outlines a consulting agreement between Dr. Gijs de Boer and Black Swift Technologies for contribution to a NASA SBIR Phase I project developing an advanced tethersonde system for atmospheric observations. De Boer will provide expert guidance on state-of-the-art tethersonde wind sensing, contribute to instrument testing and data evaluation, and support Phase II proposal development over one year at $165/hour (estimated 120 hours, $19.8k total including travel).

## Technical Approach
The tethersonde development project follows this structure:
1. **Expert guidance phase**: Establish performance characteristics and measurement requirements based on current state-of-the-art tethersonde systems and community atmospheric observation needs
2. **Testing phase**: Laboratory and surface-level testing to confirm basic measurement capability
3. **Data evaluation phase**: Analysis of test campaign data comparing measurements to other data sources and assessing utility for meteorological modeling
4. **Phase II planning**: If Phase I successful, detailed recommendations for flight testing configuration and optimal sampling strategies

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Tethersonde System
- **What it is**: An advanced tethered balloon-based system for lower-atmospheric observations
- **Proposed use**: Atmospheric measurements in lower boundary layer, including wind structure, thermodynamic properties, and turbulence characterization
- **Development stage**: Phase I (NASA SBIR) - laboratory and surface testing phase
- **Performance goals**: To be defined through de Boer's expert guidance on industry standard instrumentation capabilities and limitations

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Lower-atmosphere wind structure and turbulence observation** to support safe operation of remotely-piloted aircraft and autonomous vehicles
- **Boundary layer sampling** in different regimes with guided selection of sampling locations
- **Verification and validation** of wind field measurements against other reference data
- **Meteorological model guidance** - assessment of tethersonde measurement utility for weather prediction and forecasting
- **Potential Phase II flight testing** in relevant operational environments

## Key Deliverables & Work Breakdown

1. **Expert Guidance Report**: Performance characteristics, measurement accuracy requirements, environmental sampling considerations, effective lower-atmospheric wind observation methods, known issues/limitations of existing tethersonde systems

2. **Testing Support**: Guidance on efficient boundary layer sampling strategies, sampling location selection, site verification data availability, possible on-site testing assistance

3. **Data Analysis & Evaluation**: Review of laboratory and field test data, quality assessment, comparison with other wind measurements, utility assessment for meteorological models

4. **Phase II Planning Support**: Detailed recommendations on flight-testing configuration and optimal sampling strategies

5. **Reporting & Communication**: Formalized final report with work performed breakdown, time allocation, analysis conclusions, overall assessment of instrumentation utility; interim reports as requested; regular communication with BST team
