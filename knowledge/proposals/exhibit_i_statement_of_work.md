# Engineering Feasibility for Integrating JPL's P-band Reflectometry Instrument to an Unmanned Aircraft System

## Document Metadata
- Type: Statement of Work
- Client/Agency: Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL)
- Program/Solicitation: Engineering feasibility study (not a full flight campaign)
- Date: October 4, 2017
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 airframe
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies (BST) was contracted by JPL to conduct an engineering feasibility study for integrating JPL's P-band reflectometry instrument (a snow remote sensing technology) onto BST's S2 unmanned aircraft system. The work encompasses design, integration, bench testing, and preparation of a flight test plan for regulatory approval, but does not include full flight campaign execution.

## Technical Approach
- **Integration onto S2 airframe**: Incorporate P-band receiver antenna (designed and built by Ohio State University) into the nose cone section with minimal design changes
- **Mechanical design**: Construct scaffolding to house antenna, frontend, and logging hardware
- **Power trade study**: Determine optimal power source—either separate battery system or power from aircraft propulsion pack
- **Telemetry & control analysis**: Examine requirements for real-time payload telemetry and ground-based payload control during operations
- **Integration work**: Construct mechanical, electrical, and software interfaces
- **Testing**: Conduct initial bench testing at BST offices in Boulder, Colorado; additional testing at local facilities as needed
- **Flight approval**: Prepare Flight Test Plan for Armstrong review and approval; prepare for and conduct review

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 Airframe
- BST's unmanned aircraft platform being adapted for scientific payload integration
- Nose cone section modified to accommodate P-band receiver antenna
- Can supply power to payload via propulsion pack or separate battery
- Supports real-time telemetry and ground-based payload control

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary application**: Airborne testing of P-band reflectometry instrument for remote sensing of snow over varied terrains
- **Purpose**: Maturation of reflectometry concept following ground-based receiver testing

## Notable Details
- **Partnership**: Collaboration between JPL (technology developer), Ohio State University (antenna design/build), and BST (airborne integration platform)
- **Deliverable**: Final nose cone design and integrated instrument to be delivered to JPL post-experiments for future campaigns
- **Approval pathway**: Design requires JPL researcher approval; flight operations require Armstrong approval via Flight Test Plan review
- **Scope limitation**: Engineering study only; full flight campaigns explicitly excluded from this scope
