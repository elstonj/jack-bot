## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes a novel architecture for long-duration atmospheric exploration of extraterrestrial bodies using secondary spacecraft powered by USNC-Tech's Chargeable Atomic Batteries (CABs). The innovation addresses the fundamental limitation of current power systems by leveraging radioisotope energy density (~1 million times greater than chemical batteries) to enable exceptionally long-lived flight profiles in extreme atmospheric environments where traditional power solutions are not viable.

## Technical Approach

**Major Tasks:**
- CAB feasibility assessment, including radioisotope manufacture (targeting TRL advancement from 1→3)
- Launch safety and radiation analysis
- Power system analysis and design to support the radioisotope power source design

**Key Innovation:**
USNC-Tech's Chargeable Atomic Battery (CAB) represents a breakthrough in radioisotope power systems manufacturing:
- Novel manufacturing process producing high-performance, non-traditional radioisotope materials at lower cost and faster production than traditional Pu-238 systems
- Based on USNC's patented FCM (Fully Ceramic Microencapsulated) fission nuclear fuel technology
- Radioactive material encapsulated in silicon carbide matrix, preventing environmental release and providing resilience to radiation, high temperatures, and chemical diffusion
- Can be manufactured using non-radioactive feedstock in standard facilities, then "charged" (activated) in a fission reactor to desired power level
- Multiple strong containment barriers provide safety in launch failure scenarios
- Packaging requires no chemistry or separation steps

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Secondary Spacecraft (BST)**
- **What it is:** Diversely-capable secondary craft designed for deployment from a primary exploratory vehicle
- **Proposed use:** Atmospheric exploration of extraterrestrial bodies with exceptionally long mission durations
- **Key advantage:** Power is no longer the limiting factor; craft remains in flight as long as other systems remain operational
- **Power capability:** Designed to support 20-30W continuous power draw (entirely for payloads, communications, etc.)
- **Unique design flexibility:** On gas giants, propulsion may not be necessary; craft can leverage atmospheric dynamics to remain aloft

**Chargeable Atomic Battery (CAB) - USNC-Tech Technology**
- **Energy density:** Approximately 1 million times greater than chemical batteries and fuel cells
- **Performance parameters:** Operating lifetime, shielding requirements, form-factor, and charging needs tailored to specific radioisotope selection
- **Radioisotope options:** Multiple radioisotopes available; examples mentioned include Co-60 and Eu-152
- **Cost advantage:** Significantly lower cost than Pu-238 systems (Pu-238 costs $60-200M per kWth); CAB manufacturing avoids expensive special nuclear materials and multiple processing steps
- **Shielding:** Co-60 or Eu-152 CABs still require x-ray shielding for transportation, but environmental release risk is nearly zero
- **Safety profile:** Fully encapsulated in ceramic matrix

## Use Cases & Applications

**Mission Contexts Identified:**

1. **Titan**
   - Long-duration atmospheric flight in methane/ethane atmosphere
   - Supports extended scientific exploration beyond current capability

2. **Gas Giants** (Jupiter, Saturn, etc.)
   - Power requirement: 20-30W
   - Unique architecture: Propulsion not necessarily required; craft uses atmospheric dynamics for sustained flight
   - Enables novel, long-duration flight profiles previously impossible

3. **Venus**
   - Flight range: 20km in extremely hot, corrosive atmosphere
   - Possible mission profiles: guided drop deployment or balloon launch with guidance systems
   - Addresses extreme environmental challenges (600+ K temperatures, high pressure)

**Scientific Payloads Enabled:**
- Atmospheric science measurements
- Large-scale mapping
- Ground targeting and reconnaissance

## Notable Details

**Innovation Positioning:**
The proposal explicitly positions this as a "breakthrough" rather than an incremental improvement. The core innovation is reframing power as non-limiting for atmospheric flight missions—a fundamental shift from previous architectures where battery depletion or solar collection limitations constrained mission duration.

**State-of-the-Art Comparison:**
- Radioisotope power is not new (Pu-238 heritage in space exploration)
- CAB innovation is in manufacturing: enables use of alternative radioisotopes (Co-60, Eu-152) that are cheaper, faster to produce, and safer to handle than Pu-238
- Traditional Pu-238 systems cost-prohibitive for missions requiring hundreds of kWth; CAB approach makes such missions economically viable

- Identified as infusion opportunity for follow-up missions
