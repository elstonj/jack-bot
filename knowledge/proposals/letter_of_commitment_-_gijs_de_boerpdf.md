# Letter of Commitment - Gijs de Boer

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support/Commitment
- Client/Agency: NASA (SBIR/STTR program); also references NOAA Weather Program Office
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR/STTR Civilian Commercialization Readiness Pilot Program (CCRPP); Phase II contract #NNX17CP13C
- Date: 10 January 2022
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 uncrewed aircraft system (UAS)
- Key Personnel: Dr. Gijs de Boer (CIRES/University of Colorado); Dr. Jack Elston (Black Swift Technologies)

## Executive Summary
Dr. Gijs de Boer of the University of Colorado's Cooperative Institute for Research in Environmental Sciences (CIRES) provides a letter of support for Black Swift Technologies' NASA CCRPP Phase II application. The letter confirms an existing $148,729 NOAA-funded subcontract with BST for S2 UAS operations in complex terrain near Crested Butte, Colorado, and endorses BST's proposed enhancements to the S2 platform.

## Technical Approach
The letter references a Phase II SBIR contract (#NNX17CP13C) proposal titled "A Ruggedized UAS for Scientific Data Gathering in Harsh Environments." The described work aims to enhance the S2 UAS for safer and more efficient operation in mountainous/complex terrain environments.

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S2 Uncrewed Aircraft System (UAS)**
- Primary platform under development and testing
- Equipped with soil moisture payload
- Proposed enhancements target: safe and efficient operation in mountainous regions
- Being advanced to higher readiness levels for potential NOAA operations

## Use Cases & Applications
- Soil moisture data collection in complex terrain (specifically near Crested Butte, Colorado)
- Earth science data gathering in harsh/mountainous environments
- Potential support for NOAA missions related to weather and water prediction in complex terrain
- University and government research supporting Earth system understanding

## Key Details
- Existing NOAA funding: $148,729 subcontract to University of Colorado for BST work
- Flight operations scheduled: Spring and summers of 2022 and 2023
- Letter confirms this NOAA work is separate from (not matching funds for) the NASA CCRPP proposal
- Letter expresses confidence that CCRPP-funded enhancements could enable future deployments for various Earth science missions
- Signatory is Research Scientist III at CIRES and Associate Director for Science of the Integrated Remote and In Situ Sensing (IRISS) program
