## Executive Summary
This is a Broad Agency Announcement from the Naval Air Warfare Center Aircraft Division seeking proposals for basic and applied research in airborne systems, unmanned platforms, advanced sensors, computing/processing, and air system security. The BAA solicits mid- to high-TRL (Technology Readiness Level 4-9) research and development with emphasis on rapid demonstration and transition to warfighter capabilities. Multiple awards are anticipated across various research areas supporting Navy aviation missions.

## Technical Approach
The BAA seeks research in five primary focus areas:
1. **Platform-related**: UAS/USV/UUV autonomous operations, powertrain improvements, noise dampening, payload delivery, expendable systems, survivability enhancements
2. **Payload-related**: Anti-submarine warfare (ASW) sensors, magnetic sensors, EO/IR systems, LIDAR, hyperspectral systems, EW/cyber capabilities, RF/radar technologies, communications, lightweight sense-and-avoid, edge computing
3. **Advanced Computing/Processing**: Signal/information processing, AI/ML insertion into sensors, multi-source data fusion, geolocation, autonomy techniques
4. **Air System Security/Protection**: Signature reduction (RF, visual, infrared, acoustic), countermeasure techniques, cyber hardening, secure communications
5. **Improved Naval Capabilities**: Technologies supporting distributed maritime surveillance, Air Undersea Warfare, counter-air, close air support, C-UAS, anti-surface warfare

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Target Systems (not BST products)
- **Unmanned Air Systems (UAS)**: Group 1-4 platforms; modifications to existing larger manned/unmanned aircraft
- **Sensors**: Acoustic, EO/IR, radar, magnetic, special sensors; air-deployed via manned/unmanned aircraft or ground/surface/undersea deployed
- **Computing/Processing**: Edge computing, AI/ML, signal processing, multi-platform collaborative solutions
- **Communications**: Data link technologies, networking, multi-domain interoperability

## Use Cases & Applications

### Specific Areas of Interest (2.2.1-2.2.15):
1. Collaborative multi-sensor payloads with onboard data fusion for disadvantaged communication links
2. Enhanced manned/unmanned payloads for maritime situational awareness (reduced SWAP, lower total ownership cost)
3. COTS UAS modifications for military application (ruggedization, secure data links, hybrid engines, cyber-safe coding)
4. UAS solutions with reduced launch/recovery footprint
5. New/unique UAS capabilities for warfighter
6. Precision geolocation and network timing in contested environments
7. Persistent air/ground/ship communications data links (line-of-sight and Over-The-Horizon)
8. Minimally invasive roll-on/bolt-on enhancements to existing naval platforms
9. Technologies improving operational availability and reducing total ownership cost
10. Manned/unmanned systems with advanced data processing, improved detection, reduced SWAP
11. Low-cost expendable air/ship/ground-launched UAS and sensor technologies
12. Human-machine teaming, autonomy, and machine learning for reducing warfighter threats
13. Immersive training, maintenance, and operational simulation environments
14. Rapid fielding approaches with minimal platform intrusion
15. Mission/communication systems with cyber resilience and open systems architecture

## Key Requirements

### Submission Process:
- **Phase I**: Proposal abstract submission (5 pages + 1-page Quad Chart) at any time until 09 April 2026
- **Phase II**: Full technical/cost proposal by invitation only (25-page technical proposal limit)
- Minimum TRL of 4 at proposal start
- Email submission to robert.a.megill2.civ@us.navy.mil and truman.b.harris3.civ@us.navy.mil
- SAM registration required (with active DUNS number)

### Evaluation Criteria:
**Primary (Equal Importance):**
1. Scientific and technical merit of proposed research
2. Potential contributions to DoD/Navy including: relevance, operational utility, rapid field-testing capability, warfighting experiment suitability, affordability of transition, logistics/supportability, data rights

**Additional (Lesser but Equal Importance):**
1. Offeror capabilities, experience, personnel qualifications, facilities, techniques
2. Cost realism and reasonableness (including proposed cost sharing)

### Award Information:
- Multiple awards anticipated; funding levels not predetermined
- Awards may be structured as:
  - Procurement contracts (FAR Part 35)
  - Research Other Transactions (OTs) per 10 U.S.C. § 4021
  - Prototype OTs per 10 U.S.C. § 4022 (with options for follow-on production)
- No pre-award funding responsibility
- Socio-economic considerations: subcontracting opportunities with HBCUs, minority institutions, small/disadvantaged businesses, veteran-owned businesses evaluated

### Technology Readiness Levels Expected:
- Proposals must begin at TRL 4 minimum
- Expected progression through TRL 9 (proven through mission operations) by completion
- Government response time: ~60 days after submission
