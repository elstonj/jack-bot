# Subcontractor OMS Letter of Commitment for SBIR Phase II Soil Moisture Mapping

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Commitment (subcontractor commitment letter)
- Client/Agency: US Air Force
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Phase II Topic AFX224-OCSO1 / Proposal #F2-16588
- Date: May 3, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: L-band microwave radiometer (Lobe Differencing Correlation Radiometer - LDCR)
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (CEO, BST), Dr. Maciej Stachura (CTO, BST), Michael A. Hurowitz (CEO, Orbital Micro Systems)

## Executive Summary
Orbital Micro Systems (OMS) commits as a subcontractor to support Black Swift Technologies' US Air Force SBIR Phase II effort to develop a runway integrity validation capability using L-band microwave radiometer technology for soil moisture measurements from small UAS platforms. OMS will handle sensor design refinement, manufacturing, testing, and data analysis over a 24-month period for a fixed cost of $569,433.16.

## Technical Approach
**Primary Task Focus:**
1. Refinement of L-band radiometer design from RevC2 (current baseline) to RevC3 (Phase II optimized version)
2. PCB design and layout for RevC3 including EMI mitigation features
3. Manufacturing of 20 RevC3 sensors with 20 antenna modules for flight testing and laboratory use
4. Laboratory and field testing/validation of RevC3 LDCR sensors with antenna modules
5. Data analysis support and soil integrity algorithm development

The approach centers on transitioning the existing RevC2 radiometer design to an optimized Phase II configuration suitable for small UAS deployment.

## Products & Capabilities Described

**L-band Microwave Radiometer (Lobe Differencing Correlation Radiometer - LDCR):**
- Device type: L-band microwave radiometer for soil moisture measurement
- Current state: RevC2 baseline design
- Planned evolution: RevC3 optimized design for Phase II
- Proposed use: Mounted on small UAS for runway integrity validation through soil moisture sensing
