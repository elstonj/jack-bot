# Enhancing Tropical Cyclone Forecasts through High-Frequency Uncrewed Aircraft System Observations and Cross-Validation with Operational Observational Systems

- Date: Not explicitly stated (created/modified 2024-11-22 in system)

## Executive Summary
This SOW establishes a collaborative project between Black Swift Technologies, Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University (Dr. Joshua Wadler), and NOAA's National Hurricane Center to integrate UAS observations into operational hurricane forecasting. The work focuses on data evaluation, wind speed standardization, and operational integration through testbed activities.

## Technical Approach
- Evaluation of UAS-collected data for operational use by NHC forecasters
- Research and standardization of wind data to 10-meter altitude reference level (standard meteorological measurement height)
- Participation in testbed activities to familiarize NHC forecasters with UAS observation capabilities and data products

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Black Swift UAS:**
- Capability: High-frequency in-situ observations in hurricane environments
- Proposed Use: Collect wind and other meteorological data during tropical cyclone events
- Application: Provide observations for cross-validation with existing operational observational systems (e.g., dropsondes, aircraft, satellites)

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Tropical Cyclone/Hurricane Forecasting:** Enhancing National Hurricane Center forecast accuracy and decision-making
- **Operational Integration:** Transitioning research-grade UAS data into routine NHC operations
- **Forecaster Training:** Enabling NHC operational forecasters to understand and trust UAS observation data
