## Executive Summary
This is the Department of War's 2026 SBIR Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO) solicitation framework document. It establishes procedures, eligibility requirements, evaluation criteria, and proposal preparation instructions for small businesses seeking SBIR awards across three phases. The CSO is designed to acquire innovative R&D solutions that address DoW capability gaps, with particular focus on emerging technologies and non-traditional defense contractors.

## Technical Approach
The CSO enables DoW to acquire a full spectrum of R&D activities under FAR Parts 35 and DFARS Part 235, extending beyond basic research to include prototype demonstrations and system-level applications. The solicitation emphasizes:

- **Non-prescriptive topic structures** encouraging varied technical and scientific approaches
- **Commercial solutions framework** treating proposed technologies as commercial items (not requiring pre-existing COTS availability)
- **Three-phase program model:** Phase I (6-12 months, feasibility proof), Phase II (24 months, continued development), Phase III (commercialization via non-SBIR sources)
- **Direct-to-Phase II option** for companies demonstrating feasibility without prior Phase I award
- **Firm-Fixed-Price contracting** under FAR Part 12, with flexibility for cooperative agreements or Other Transactions

## Key Requirements & Eligibility

### Small Business Concern (SBC) Requirements:
- Must qualify as SBC under 13 C.F.R §§ 701-705 at time of award
- Phase I: SBC must perform minimum 2/3 of research/analytical work (direct + indirect costs)
- Phase II: SBC must perform minimum 50% of research/analytical work
- Principal Investigator must have primary employment (>50% time) with proposing SBC at award and during performance
- All R&D work must be performed in the United States
- Joint ventures permitted if entity qualifies as SBC under ownership/size requirements

### Performance Benchmarks (for experienced firms):
- Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate requirement
- Commercialization Benchmark requirement
- Companies failing benchmarks limited to maximum 80 awards across DoW (20 each for Army, Navy, Air Force; 20 for other components combined)

### Funding Restrictions:
- SBIR/STTR funds **cannot** be used for COTS/NDI purchases without modification
- **Modified COTS/NDI** allowed when modifications significantly alter performance envelope to meet requirements
- Design and development for COTS/NDI integration is allowable
- Continuous technology refreshment (sustainment) is **not** allowable

## Critical Compliance Requirements

### Registrations Required:
1. **SAM (System for Award Management)** - generates UEI and CAGE code
2. **SBA Company Registry** - with SBC Control ID and Proof of Registration
3. **DSIP (Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal)** - individual user accounts authenticated via Login.gov
4. **Firm-level DSIP forms** - completed by designated Firm Admin before proposals submitted

### Security & Foreign Affiliations:
- **Due Diligence Review** (15 U.S.C. §638(vv)): Assesses cybersecurity, patents, employee analysis, foreign ownership, financial ties/obligations; uses open-source analysis to verify disclosures
- **Foreign Ownership/Control/Influence (FOCI) disclosure** required; partnerships with foreign entities must be disclosed
- Venture Capital/Hedge Fund/Private Equity majority ownership generally prohibited unless Service/Component authorizes
- No awards made that pose unacceptable national security risks

### Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification (CMMC):
- **Level 1:** Annual self-assessment, compliance with 15 FAR 52.204-21 requirements
- **Level 2:** Self-assessment or C3PAO assessment every 3 years, compliance with 110 NIST SP 800-171 Rev 2 requirements
- **Level 3:** Annual affirmation, assessment by DCMA's DIBCAC every 3 years, compliance with 24 NIST SP 800-172 requirements
- **BlueUAS Certification:** Not required unless specified in topic; companies responsible for certification costs

### Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI):
- Must identify and disclose all facts relevant to potential OCIs (FAR 9.5)
- Must provide OCI mitigation plan addressing FAR 9.505-1 through 9.505-4 limitations
- Must disclose if providing/providing SETA, A&AS, or similar support to DoW offices currently or within past year
- Government evaluates OCI mitigation plans only for selectable proposals with available funding

### Export Control Compliance:
- **ITAR:** Requires fully certified DD Form 2345 (Military Critical Technical Data Agreement) for ITAR-subject efforts; obtain via JCP Portal
- **EAR:** Must obtain proper export licenses through Department of Commerce's SNAP-R system
- Failure to obtain licenses timely may delay award, cause cancellation, or trigger contract termination
- Contractors must review SAM.gov at least quarterly for new FASCA orders

### Research Involving Human/Animal Subjects:
- Must comply with federal and state laws for human subject protection
- Requires current federal assurance of compliance (e.g., HHS federal-wide assurance)
- Personnel must provide documentation of completed human subject protection training
- IRB approval required; approval process typically 1-3 months
- DoW review of IRB protocol typically 3-6 months
- **No funding authorized until ALL approvals granted**
- Animal research requires IACUC approval plus secondary/headquarters approval from DoW veterinarian
- Recombinant DNA research requires institutional biosafety committee approval

## Proposal Submission & Evaluation

### Proposal Volumes:
1. Volume 1: Proposal Cover Sheet
2. Volume 2: Technical Volume
3. Volume 3: Cost Volume
4. Volume 4: Company Commercialization Report
5. Volume 5: Supporting Documents
6. Volume 6: Fraud, Waste and Abuse Training
7. Volume 7: Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries

### Phase I Proposal Requirements:
- Focus on Research or Research & Development to prove scientific/technical feasibility and commercialization potential
- Must demonstrate minimum 2/3 of work performed by SBC
- Service/Component-specific page limits and cost caps apply

### Phase II Proposal Requirements:
- Continues and further develops Phase I results
- Must demonstrate minimum 50% of work performed by SBC
- **Commercialization Strategy section required** - addresses path to private sector or military commercialization
- **Adequate Accounting System** required - capable of tracking costs, labor, materials

### Phase II Enhancement Policy:
- Certain DoW Services/Components provide additional Phase II funding if SBC matches with non-SBIR funds from DoW acquisition programs or private sector

### Evaluation Criteria:
- Technical merit and feasibility
- Commercialization potential
- Alignment with DoW modernization goals and capability needs
- Innovation and novel approaches

### Award Information:
- Multiple awards anticipated per topic; each proposal evaluated independently
- Funding decisions made without regard to other submissions
- Services/Components may fund several or none of proposals received
- All awards subject to risk-based due diligence security review and funding availability
- DoW has no obligation to make Phase I or Direct-to-Phase II awards
- Offerors bear all costs of proposal preparation; DoW will not reimburse

### Key Policy Distinctions:
- **CSO vs. BAA:** CSO permits full spectrum R&D (FAR Part 35) including prototypes and system-level applications; BAA typically limited to basic/applied research
- **Commercial Solutions Framework:** Technologies not required to be pre-existing or COTS; early commercial offerings, concept demonstrations, and agile development acceptable
- **Three-Phase Structure:** Phase I (~$300K typical, 6-12 months), Phase II (~$2M typical, 24 months), Phase III (non-SBIR funded commercialization)

### Program Support Services:
- **Technical and Business Assistance (TABA):** Discretionary; availability determined by Service/Component
- **Commercialization Readiness Program (CRP):** Available through Army, Navy, Air Force
- **OSW Accelerated Research for Transition (ART):** DoW-level technology maturation and transfer program
- **Project Spectrum:** Online resources on FOCI understanding and cybersecurity readiness (projectspectrum.io)

### Intellectual Property:
