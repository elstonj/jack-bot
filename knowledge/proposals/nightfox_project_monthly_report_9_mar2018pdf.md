## Executive Summary
The NightFOX project, a NOAA-funded initiative for nighttime fire observations, is developing remote sensing and in-situ instruments for wildfire monitoring. As of March 2018, the project has progressed toward all FY18 milestones with sensor development underway and the S2 UAS platform acquisition scheduled for May delivery. The project is advancing toward field testing participation in summer 2018 and continues FAA coordination for expanded operational approvals.

## Technical Approach
- Integration of multiple sensor types (SWIR and mid-IR sensors, CO2 sensors, CO sensors, aerosol instruments) on a UAS platform
- Development of scanning mechanisms with telecentric scopes for infrared sensors
- Telescope scanning control system development
- Preliminary observation strategies for both in-situ and remote sensing flight operations
- Data product assimilation for fire weather modeling
- Planned participation in ISARRA meeting Flight Week (July) for system testing

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS Platform
- Acquisition by CU expected May 2018 (originally scheduled earlier)
- Being modified with nighttime operation capabilities (lighting upgrades) by manufacturer BST
- Payload integration coordinated with BST
- Current TRL: 4

### Sensor Suite
- **SWIR and mid-IR sensors**: Scanning mechanism and telecentric scopes manufactured; scanning control development ongoing
- **CO2 sensor**: Design phase initiated
- **CO sensor**: Two prototypes expected March 2018 (delayed from December 2017)
- **Aerosol instruments**: Commercial coarse mode instruments under evaluation

## Use Cases & Applications
- Nighttime wildfire observations and monitoring
- Fire weather research in coordination with USFS-NOAA MOU
- Potential prescribed burn observation opportunities (late summer/fall 2018 in Boulder area)
- Data assimilation for fire weather modeling
- Demonstration flights for incident command officials (airboss) regarding NightFOX UAS wildfire operations
