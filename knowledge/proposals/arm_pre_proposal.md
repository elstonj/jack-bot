- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (Black Swift Technologies, Co-Investigator); Dr. Gijs de Boer (CU-Boulder, Lead Scientist)

## Executive Summary
OPTIMUS proposes a two-week summer 2015 field campaign using the BlackSwift Tempest unmanned aircraft system to map high-resolution terrestrial surface properties at Oliktok Point in the Arctic tundra. The campaign would measure soil moisture, soil temperature, NDVI, surface elevation, and visible imagery to bridge the gap between point-scale ARM measurements and satellite-scale observations, improving understanding of atmosphere-surface interactions.

## Technical Approach

**Primary Platform:** BlackSwift Tempest UAS (3-meter wingspan, small unmanned aircraft system)

**Measurement Strategy:**
- Multiple mapping passes over two-week deployment period
- Coarse-resolution mapping of entire land-covered airspace
- High-resolution passes at lower altitudes near AMF3 instrumentation for cross-validation
- Data collection timing: Mid-July (post-snowmelt period), repeated over three years

**Sensor Suite & Specifications:**
1. **Soil Moisture:** Passive L-band microwave radiometer
   - Depth: ~5-20 cm
   - Resolution: 15 m at 15 m flight altitude
   
2. **Soil Temperature:** Infrared camera
   
3. **NDVI:** Near-infrared/red camera system
   
4. **Surface Elevation:** Onboard laser altimeter, EO camera, post-processing
   
5. **Visible Imagery:** Onboard EO (electro-optical) camera

## Products & Capabilities Described

**BlackSwift Tempest UAS:**
- Small UAS platform (3-meter wingspan)
- Originally developed with support from NASA SBIR program for making surface property measurements
- Designed to carry multiple sensor packages for earth observation
- Capable of sustained low-altitude operations for high-resolution mapping
- Portable and deployable to remote field sites

**Data Deliverables:**
- Maps of measured terrestrial properties
- Datasets archived with ARM Data Archive within 6 months of campaign completion
- Final report for ARM community
- Integration with publications using AMF3 and related campaign data

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Application:** Arctic tundra terrestrial monitoring
- **Site:** Oliktok Point, Alaska (AMF3 site)
- **Spatial Focus:** Land-covered portion of R-2209 restricted airspace region
- **Scale Bridging:** Provides measurements at 10s of meters resolution between single-point ARM measurements and kilometer-scale satellite observations
- **Scientific Purpose:** Evaluate representativeness of point measurements to larger regions; characterize surface heterogeneity influencing atmospheric measurements

**Specific Surface Features to Map:**
- Distance to major features (lakes, rivers, vegetation type changes)
- Developed areas associated with USAF and oil industry activities
- Upstream surface characteristics influencing air masses passing ECOR and Ameriflux instrumentation under different wind regimes

**Integration with Related Programs:**
- Complementary to AMF3 eddy-correlation flux system (ECOR) and surface radiation (GNDRAD) measurements
- Coordinated with ERASMUS (Evaluation of Routine Atmospheric Sounding Measurements using Unmanned Systems) DataHawk operations
- Coordinated with ACME-V (ARM Airborne Carbon Measurements) G-1 flights
- Acts as integrator between atmospheric (ARM) and land surface (NGEE - Next Generation Ecosystem Experiments) communities

## Relevance to ARM Mission

Addresses three key scientific gaps:
1. **Scale Bridging:** Fills measurement scale gap between point observations and satellite data (kilometers)
2. **Representativeness:** Evaluates how well single-point measurements represent larger regions
3. **Atmosphere-Surface Linking:** Provides information necessary for linking atmospheric observations to underlying surface properties and upstream influences
