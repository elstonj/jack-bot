# NREL LDRD Proposal: UAS Wake Measurement System

## Document Metadata
- Type: LDRD (Laboratory Directed Research and Development) proposal - brainstorming/planning document
- Client/Agency: NREL (National Renewable Energy Laboratory)
- Program/Solicitation: 2013 LDRD
- Date: April 3, 2013
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Not explicitly named, but BlackSwift contracted for UAS development
- Key Personnel: Matt Churchfield, Lee Jay Fingersh, Scott Schreck, Kenny Gruchalla, Jason Roadman, Julie Lundquist, Brian Argrow, Mark Whitehorn (NREL); BlackSwift Technologies (contractor)

## Executive Summary
This is a two-year LDRD proposal to develop a prototype unmanned aircraft system (UAS) capable of measuring wind turbine wake characteristics at NREL's National Wind Technology Center. The project aims to collect synchronized wake measurements from turbines alongside existing met mast and SCADA data, validate these measurements against high-performance computing simulations, and document lessons learned for future iterations.

## Technical Approach
- **FY14 (Hardware Development & Integration):**
  - Q1: Select aircraft hardware, autopilot, GPS, and measurement devices; begin COA (Certificate of Airworthiness) process
  - Q2-Q4: Component testing, GPS/autopilot integration, airframe and instrumentation integration with static testing on meteorological tower, flight testing planning
  
- **FY15 (Flight Testing & Analysis):**
  - Q1-Q4: Conduct flight testing with parallel measurement campaigns, high-performance computing simulations, and data analysis
  - Produce working prototype capable of high-quality, reliable wake measurements

## Products & Capabilities Described
**BlackSwift UAS System:**
- Contracted to provide/develop the core UAS platform
- Intended purpose: autonomous wake measurement collection at wind facilities
- Budget allocation: $20K (FY14) and $20K (FY15)
- Integrated with: custom GPS/autopilot systems, measurement instrumentation, airframe modifications

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Application:** Measuring wind turbine wake characteristics at NREL's National Wind Technology Center (NWTC)
- **Data Integration:** Synchronized with inflow data from meteorological masts and turbine SCADA/existing instrumentation
- **Validation Use:** Compare UAS-collected measurements against CFD/HPC simulations to validate simulation models and improve wind energy research

## Project Outcomes & Deliverables
1. Working prototype UAS system for wake measurement with documented methodology
2. Journal paper on measurement/aircraft integration methods
3. Initial set of synchronized wake measurements from NWTC turbines
4. Journal paper comparing UAS data with HPC simulation results
5. Documented lessons learned, including hardware integration challenges, what worked/failed, and recommendations for improved second iteration
6. Clear understanding of COA (airworthiness certification) process for future UAS deployments
