## Executive Summary
BST proposes the S3, a Group 2 tactical UAS designed for low-altitude persistent monitoring through solar-powered extended endurance (4-6 hours per flight), automated machine vision landing, and terrain-aware flight planning. The system enables month-long automatic monitoring campaigns without extensive pre-planning.

## Technical Approach
The S3 employs several integrated capabilities:
- **Solar power system**: Solar panels integrated on wings for extended flight endurance (4-6 hours per flight)
- **Ground recharging**: Between-flight recharging capability enabling month-long continuous campaigns
- **Automated landing**: Machine vision-based autonomous landing system eliminates need for pre-planned landing zones
- **Terrain-aware flight planning**: On-board elevation models allow low-altitude flight in rugged terrain with terrain-following capability
- **Wind-aware flight planning**: Active wind condition assessment to avoid infeasible flight paths and perform energy harvesting to extend endurance
- **Environmental hardening**: Ingress protection rated for extended temperature range (-40°F to +120°F)

- Emphasis on reducing operational complexity through automation (no need to pre-plan landing areas beyond identifying general monitoring regions)
- Designed for harsh environmental conditions (wide temperature range)
- Wind and terrain awareness suggests capability for challenging operational environments
