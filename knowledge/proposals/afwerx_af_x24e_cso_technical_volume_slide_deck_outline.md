## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes developing an autonomous, all-weather VTOL UAS based on the S3 platform for base perimeter security at U.S. Air Force installations, with a focus on JBER (Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson). The system will integrate advanced machine vision algorithms, gimbaled EO/IR sensors, and seamless interoperability with Air Force command-and-control systems (ATAK, COP) to significantly reduce operator workload while enabling continuous monitoring in extreme weather conditions. The S3's proven capabilities in harsh environments position it as a superior alternative to current "drone-in-a-box" systems with limited range, weather resilience, and high human oversight requirements.

## Technical Approach

**Platform Foundation:**
- Leverages Black Swift Technologies' S3 VTOL (vertical takeoff and landing) platform
- System currently at TRL 6; Phase II will advance to TRL 7 for operational deployment
- Builds on proven S2 platform heritage (wildfire monitoring, hurricane observation, Arctic surveys)

**Key Technical Components:**
1. **Autonomy System:** SwiftCore flight management system minimizes operator workload; enables rapid customization for mission-specific needs
2. **Machine Vision & Navigation:** University of Colorado (ARPG) developing real-time algorithms for:
   - GPS-denied navigation
   - Obstacle avoidance
   - Real-time target recognition and tracking
3. **Sensor Payload:** Gimbaled EO/IR (electro-optical/infrared) sensor for high-resolution, real-time surveillance and target detection
4. **Command Integration:** Seamless interoperability with Air Force infrastructure:
   - Common Operational Picture (COP)
   - Android Tactical Assault Kit (ATAK)
   - Real-time data transmission and control interfaces

**All-Weather Capability:**
- Designed to operate in extreme cold, high winds, and heavy precipitation
- Addresses current limitations at facilities like JBER (temperature limits, high wind speed restrictions prevent existing systems from operating consistently)
- Target: Nearly 100% uptime for perimeter monitoring

**Phase I Plan (6 months):**
- Initial design and integration of autonomy, machine vision, and EO/IR sensor systems
- Collaboration with University of Colorado to develop and refine machine vision algorithms
- Preliminary integration of sensor payload with ATAK and COP
- Prepare documentation for Phase II field testing permissions at JBER

**Phase II Plan (12 months):**
- Complete hardware and software adaptations for all-weather operations
- Extensive field testing at JBER under extreme weather conditions
- Refinement of operational procedures with Air Force personnel
