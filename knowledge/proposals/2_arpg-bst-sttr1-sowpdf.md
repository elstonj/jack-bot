## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies will partner with the Autonomous Robotics and Perception Group (ARPG) at University of Colorado Boulder to develop a technical approach for transitioning existing BST technologies to Department of the Air Force capabilities. The Phase I effort focuses on technical feasibility and identifying DAF operational needs and potential customers for Phase II development.

## Technical Approach
Phase I efforts include:
- Technical approach development for applying existing technologies to three identified capabilities
- Sensor selection and evaluation
- Algorithm selection and customization
- Integration with existing unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) platforms
- Detailed plan of work for Phase II implementation

Dr. Heckman (ARPG) will provide expertise and guidance throughout the technical planning process.

## Products & Capabilities Described
The document identifies three capabilities under consideration but does not provide details on specific BST products. The three capabilities are:
1. **Autonomous Landing** - enabling UAVs to land without external positioning references
2. **GPS-denied Visual Navigation** - enabling flight autonomy in GPS-denied environments using onboard vision
3. **Obstacle Avoidance for Terrain-following Flight** - enabling low-altitude operations with terrain clearance

## Use Cases & Applications
- Department of the Air Force operational missions
- Unmanned aerial vehicle operations in GPS-denied environments
- Low-altitude terrain-following missions
