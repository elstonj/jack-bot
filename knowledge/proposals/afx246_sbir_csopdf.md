# AFX24.6 SBIR Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO)

## Document Metadata
- **Type**: Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Program solicitation - Direct-to-Phase-II (D2P2)
- **Client/Agency**: Department of the Air Force (DAF)
- **Program/Solicitation**: AFX24.6 CSO; Topics: AFX246-DPCSO1 (Air Force stakeholder need) and AFX246-DPCSO2 (Space Force stakeholder need)
- **Date**: Submission deadline 23 May 2024 at 12:00 PM ET (proposals accepted beginning 24 April 2024)
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced**: None mentioned in this document
- **Key Personnel**: Daniel J. Brewer (Air Force SBIR/STTR Program Contracting Officer); Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is a Department of the Air Force Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO) solicitation for Direct-to-Phase-II (D2P2) SBIR awards seeking innovative defense-related dual-purpose technologies/solutions addressing Air Force or Space Force stakeholder needs. The solicitation offers approximately 131 awards with a maximum of $1,250,000 per proposal and 21-month performance period, with emphasis on commercial innovation, dual-use capabilities, and technological advancement within Air Force Operational Imperatives.

## Program Overview & Key Parameters

### Award Structure
- **Maximum Funding**: $1,250,000 per proposal
- **Maximum Duration**: 21 months total (no distinction between technical and reporting periods)
- **Anticipated Awards**: Approximately 131 awards government-wide
- **Award Type**: Firm-Fixed-Price contracts or Other Transaction Agreements
- **Funding Agreement Types**: Only contracts and OTA agreements used by DAF (no grants or cooperative agreements)

### Submission Requirements
- **Submission Portal**: Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP) only; https://www.dodsbirsttr.mil/
- **Submission Deadline**: 23 May 2024 at 12:00 PM ET (no exceptions)
- **One Proposal Per Firm**: Small businesses may submit only one proposal to this CSO (covers both topics)
- **Technical Volume Limit**: 15 pages maximum (cover sheet, glossary, table of contents do not count)
- **Technical Volume Format**: White Paper

### Proposal Structure (Direct-to-Phase-II Format)
- **Volume 1**: Proposal Cover Sheet
- **Volume 2**: Technical Volume (15 pages max) including:
  - Non-Proprietary Phase II Work Plan
  - Commercialization Plan
- **Volume 3**: Cost Volume
- **Volume 4**: Company Commercialization Report
- **Volume 5**: Supporting Documents (including mandatory foreign disclosure attachments)

## Eligibility & Certifications

### Small Business Concern Requirements
Must meet 13 C.F.R. § 121.702 criteria at time of award:
- Organized for profit with U.S. place of business
- Operates primarily in U.S. or makes significant contribution to U.S. economy
- Legal form: individual proprietorship, partnership, LLC, corporation, joint venture, association, trust, or cooperative
- More than 50% owned and controlled by U.S. citizens or permanent resident aliens
- No more than 500 employees (including affiliates)

### Joint Ventures
- Eligible if entity qualifies as small business under Small Business Act (13 U.S.C. § 121.701)
- Must disclose any joint ventures with foreign entities or foreign government-controlled companies (Attachment 8)
- Must submit representation statement (Attachment 10)

### Majority Ownership by Multiple VCOCs/Hedge Funds/Private Equity
- Eligible to submit but must identify:
  - All foreign nationals, foreign entities, foreign persons, or foreign governments holding >5% equity
  - All ultimate parent owners
  - All entities/individuals owning >5% equity in chain of ownership

### Performance Benchmarks (for Prior SBIR/STTR Awardees)
Applicants with prior awards must meet benchmarks as determined by SBA on 1 June annually:
1. **Phase I Track Record** (>20 Phase I awards in past 5 fiscal years, excluding most recent): Phase II to Phase I award ratio must be ≥0.25
2. **Phase II Track Record** (>15 Phase II awards in past 10 fiscal years, excluding last 2 years): Must have average of ≥$100,000 sales/investments per Phase II award OR received patents equal to/greater than 15% of Phase II awards received during period

**Consequence**: Failure to meet benchmarks results in ineligibility for new Phase I proposals for one year. Companies may continue current work, apply for Phase II awards, and are not prohibited from Phase III awards.

### SAM.gov Registration
- Required before DAF can award contract
- Firms must register with Unique Entity ID (UEI/SAM); DUNS numbers no longer valid
- Registration must reflect "ALL AWARDS" for purpose of registration
- Firms registered for grants only will be ineligible

### DSIP Registration
- Individual firm members must register
- DSIP only accessible from within U.S. (50 states, territories, possessions, Puerto Rico, Marshall Islands, Micronesia, Palau, D.C.)
- All users require individual accounts authenticated by Login.gov
- Email used for Login.gov should match DSIP account email
- Sharing accounts/passwords violates Terms of Use and DoD policy

## Technical Approach & Requirements

### Dual-Purpose Technologies Focus
Solicitation seeks innovative commercial technologies that:
- Create new DAF solutions or potential new capabilities
- Fulfill requirements, close capability gaps, provide technological advancements
- Feature varying technical/scientific approaches and new technologies
- Support transition into DAF

### Research and Development (R&D) Definition
Activities classified as R/R&D must be:
- Systematic, intensive study directed toward greater knowledge/understanding
- Systematic study directed toward applying new knowledge to recognized need
- Systematic application of knowledge toward production of useful materials, devices, systems, or methods (including design, development, improvement of prototypes and new processes)

### Performance of Work Requirement
- **Minimum 50%** of research and/or analytical work must be performed by proposing firm
- Percentage measured by direct and indirect costs (excluding profit)
- Deviations possible only with written government approval after proposal selection for negotiations

### Prototype Projects
CSO may result in prototype projects including:
- Commercially available technologies fueled by commercial or strategic investment
- Concept demonstrations
- Pilots
- Agile development activities improving commercial technologies or government-owned capabilities
- Concepts for broad Defense application(s)

## Air Force Operational Imperatives Alignment

Proposals should align with one of seven identified Air Force Operational Imperatives:
1. Space Order of Battle
2. Operationally Focused Air Battle Management Systems (ABMS)
3. Moving Target Engagement
4. Tactical Air Dominance
5. Resilient Basing
6. Global Strike
7. Readiness to Deploy and Fight

**Alternative**: Proposals not fitting above categories may reference "BLUE SKY" with explanation of how solution provides increased strategic capability within AF, national, and global constraints.

## Compliance & Special Requirements

### Foreign Affiliations & Relationships Disclosure
- **Mandatory**: All proposing small business concerns must complete Attachment 8: "Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries"
- **Requirement**: Proposals without completed Attachment 8 in Volume 5 will be deemed non-compliant and not evaluated
- **Scope**: Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence (FOCI) assessment; DoD partnership with Project Spectrum provides online training on FOCI

### Work Location
- All research/R&D must be performed in U.S. by small business concern and subcontractors
- Rare exceptions possible for specific items/materials/supplies not available in U.S. (requires written approval)
- Exceptions only considered after proposal selection for negotiations

### Export Control
- International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR), 22 CFR Parts 120-130 apply
- Export Administration Regulations (EAR), 15 CFR Parts 730-799 apply
- Applies to projects with military or dual-use applications developing beyond fundamental research
- Fundamental research = basic and applied research ordinarily published and shared broadly within scientific community
- **Note**: Applicants remain liable for all ITAR/EAR compliance; must inform Government of potential restrictions

### Classified Work
- **Classified proposals NOT accepted** in response to this CSO
- If Phase II work requires classified effort, applicant must have or obtain facility clearance
- Additional information: Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency (https://www.dcsa.mil/)

### Research Involving Human Subjects (RIHS)
Compliance required with:
- 32 C.F.R. § 219 "The Common Rule"
- 10 U.S.C. § 980 "Limitation on Use of Humans as Experimental Subjects"
- DoDI 3216.02 "Protection of Human Subjects and Adherence to Ethical Standards"
- Applicable federal/state law and DAF guidance

**"Human Use" Protocols Apply To**:
1. Any research involving intervention or interaction with living person not otherwise occurring or occurring differently without this research
2. Any research involving identifiable private information (data/specimens collected from living individuals with known or readily ascertainable identity)

**Requirements Before Award**:
- Documentation of current Federal Assurance of Compliance (e.g., HHS Office for Human Research Protections Federalwide Assurance)
- Documentation of appropriate training completion for human subject protection

**Before Performance**:
- Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval or exemption determination (32 CFR 219.101)
- Evidence of appropriate investigator training
- Detailed protocol including: research plan, study population, risks/benefits, recruitment/consent process, data collection/analysis
- Expected timeline: IRB review 1-3 months; DoD review 3-6 months
- **No funding for human subject research until ALL approvals granted**

### Research Involving Animal Subjects
Compliance required with:
- DoDI 3216.01 "Use of Animals in DoD Programs"
- 9 C.F.R. Parts 1-4 "Animal Welfare Regulations"
- National Academy of Sciences "Guide for the Care & Use of Laboratory Animals"
- Animal Welfare Act (7 U.S.C. §§ 2131-2159)
- Applicable federal/state law

**Requirements**:
- Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC) approval
- Secondary/headquarters DoD veterinarian approval (trained/experienced in laboratory animal medicine/science)
- **No animal research allowed until all DoD approvals granted**

### Research Involving Recombinant DNA
- Compliance with applicable federal/state laws and regulations
- Institutional Biosafety Committee approval required

### Unmanned Aircraft Systems (UAS)
- Compliance with DoD Memo "Guidance for Domestic Use of UAS in U.S. National Airspace" (18 August 2018)
- Compliance with DoD Memo "Delegation Authority to Approve Exemptions for COTS UAS" (16 November 2018)
- Contractor personnel must comply with all laws, regulations, guidance for UAS operation in U.S. National Airspace
- Specific programs require exemption for operational use
- DoD exemption approval process can require ~2 months
- **No funding for contracted UAS operations until ALL approvals granted**
