## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies, founded in 2011 and based in Boulder, Colorado, is a small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS) avionics startup seeking to capitalize on the emerging commercial UAS market. The company provides customizable flight management systems and end-to-end sUAS solutions to research institutions, government agencies, and NASA, positioning itself to serve agriculture, mapping, and scientific applications once FAA commercial regulations are finalized in 2016-2017.

## Technical Approach
BST's competitive advantage rests on proprietary control and estimation algorithms that deliver high-level autonomy and system optimization using affordable commercially available sensors (similar to those in consumer electronics). The technical team holds three Doctorates in Aerospace Engineering focused on sUAS automation, applications, and design. Products feature fully customizable user interface software with modular capabilities—simple operation for basic users alongside advanced features for skilled operators—enabling single operators to theoretically manage multiple sUAS simultaneously.

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SwiftCore Flight Management System**
- Core operating system differentiating BST's sUAS from competitors
- Proprietary control and estimation algorithms for high performance and resiliency
- Modular software architecture allowing customization by skill level
- Sold to integrators at approximately $10k per unit
- Enables third-party companies to develop sUAS applications

**SuperSwift Aircraft**
- End-to-end turn-key sUAS platform
- Priced in the $20k-100k range depending on configuration

**Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS**
- Specialized system developed through customization work
- Demonstrates BST's ability to develop mission-specific sensors and systems

## Business Model & Revenue Streams

BST operates four primary business areas:

1. **Custom Development**: Customized sUAS technologies including unique sensors; past work includes Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS
2. **Turn-Key Systems Sales**: End-to-end sUAS systems ($20k-100k per unit)
3. **SwiftCore Licensing**: Flight management system sales to integrators (~$10k per unit)
4. **Consulting Services**: FAA and NASA flight approval consulting

## Competitive Advantages
- Unmatched technical team of early innovators with three Aerospace Engineering PhDs specialized in sUAS
- Proprietary algorithms integrated into fully customized end-to-end systems
- Superior usability and reliability for high-value applications
- Reduced operational complexity: single operator can theoretically manage multiple aircraft versus competitor systems requiring crews of up to 12 personnel
- Existing customer base providing ongoing feedback and validation
- Extensive experience obtaining FAA Certificates of Authorization (COAs)

## Market Context
- UAS market (excluding consumer/commercial) estimated at $6.6B in 2012, projected to grow to $11.4B by 2022 (Teal Group estimate)
- Commercial UAS market expected to be significantly larger; AUVSI estimates $13.6B economic impact in US for 2015-2017 period
- Current market limited to public entities (universities, law enforcement, government) operating under FAA COAs
- Commercial market expected to open in late 2016 or early 2017 when FAA regulations finalize

## Marketing Strategy
**Current (2015)**: Direct engagement at trade shows and industry events; focus on early adopter relationships with research institutions; lead tracking through web-based management system; emphasis on customer referrals and word-of-mouth from satisfied customers.

**Future (post-2017)**: Wide-scale marketing campaign to educate commercial users once FAA regulations permit. Distribution through established channels and new channels serving emerging markets.

## Intellectual Property Strategy
- Proprietary control and estimation algorithms maintained as trade secrets
- Patent protection filed on key user interface technologies
- Standard copyright protection for software designs
- Competitive protections for hardware and circuit board designs
- Strategy emphasizes innovation and superior execution over litigation

## Management & Staffing
- Leadership has strong technical capabilities and aerospace commercialization experience
- Business experience from entrepreneurial training programs
- Company actively seeking experienced management team members in marketing and sales to build a dedicated business development function
- Stated goal: strengthen capabilities in sales, marketing, and business management

## Key Agreements
Pursuing agreements with airframe manufacturers to establish BST avionics as standard offerings. Manufacturers have proven effective at generating new sales.

## Notable Details
- Company positioned as "ready" with trusted, reliability-demonstrated products ahead of FAA commercial regulations
- Current problem being solved: traditional UAS require expensive crews (up to 12 personnel) with extensive training; BST systems reduce operational complexity and cost through automation and simplified interfaces
- Compelling need validation: potential customers in agriculture, mapping, and scientific applications have directly contacted BST to acquire systems and consulting services
- Seeking Colorado state investment to fund business development, marketing, and sales team building
