## Executive Summary
USGS Volcano Science Center sought Black Swift Technologies to deploy an intermediate-range UAS platform equipped with specialized gas monitoring and imaging payloads to conduct volcanic observation surveys at Nevado del Ruiz volcano in Colombia. The mission addresses the need for safe, remote volcano monitoring during active volcanic crises where ground-based approaches are too hazardous, with capabilities for gas emissions characterization and topographic mapping via photogrammetry.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed to:
- Modify and deploy the previously developed S2 UAS platform with enhanced payload endurance for long-distance, high-altitude, harsh-environment operations
- Conduct missions from safe staging areas 10-15 km from volcanic centers, climbing to 10,000-20,000 feet altitude
- Integrate real-time data streaming to ground operations team to enable adaptive observation strategies
- Perform multi-day deployment campaigns with continuous data collection and safe return with all equipment and sensors
- Handle all logistics, flight operations, regulatory compliance, and data management

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- **What it is:** Intermediate-range unmanned aircraft system designed for volcano monitoring applications
- **Proposed use:** Primary platform for Nevado del Ruiz volcano monitoring mission
- **Specifications/Payload:**
  - Gas concentration monitoring: CO2, H2S, SO2
  - UV spectrometer (upward-looking) for scattered sunlight radiance measurement to determine vertical SO2 column
  - Environmental sensors: wind speed, wind direction, relative humidity, temperature, pressure
  - RTK-stamped nadir imagery for photogrammetry processing and topographic mapping
  - 3D wind velocity measurement capability
  - GPS tracking
  - Enhanced endurance for long-distance operations
  - Real-time data streaming capability

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Mission:**
- Nevado del Ruiz volcano, Colombia (very high threat volcano with ongoing volcanic activity)
- Safe observation during active volcanic crises when ground approach is impossible

**Observation Objectives:**
- Characterization of volcanic gas emissions (H2O, CO2, SO2, H2S) during eruptions
- Quantification of SO2 emission rates and volatile species emission rates
- Topographic mapping and repeat surveys to capture:
  - Volcanic deformation rates
  - Lava emission volume and extent
  - Fundamental eruption metrics

**Operational Profile:**
- Launch from safe distance (10-15 km from volcanic center)
- Climb to high altitude (10,000-20,000 feet) in harsh environmental conditions
- Extended flight duration for comprehensive observation
- Real-time telemetry to support adaptive mission planning
- Safe return with all data and sensors intact

## Notable Details

**Scope of Work:**
- BST responsible for securing flight permissions and regulatory compliance
- BST handles all logistics (equipment and personnel transport to site)
- BST conducts all flight operations and data collection
- VSC retains documentation for future campaigns

**Mission Context:**
- Addresses critical gap in volcano monitoring—most small UAS operations limited to close-range observations due to payload and range constraints
- Intermediate-range capability enables safe observation of explosive, unpredictable volcanism in Cascades, Alaska, and globally where ground approach poses unacceptable hazards
- Multi-sensor approach (gas speciation + topographic imaging) allows comprehensive eruption characterization simultaneously
- Real-time data streaming critical for crisis response decision-making with limited pre-crisis operational information
