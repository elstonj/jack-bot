## Executive Summary
This document outlines Black Swift Technologies LLC's IT security procedures and policies for performing work under NASA SBIR/STTR contract 80NSSC25CA052. The contract requires remote access to NASA's ProSAMS system for electronic submission of deliverables and invoices. BST commits to protecting NASA information assets, including Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) and personally identifiable information (PII), in accordance with NIST and NASA security requirements.

## Technical Approach
**NASA System Access:**
- Single NASA IT system required: SBIR/STTR Awardee Firm ProSAMS (https://prosams.nasa.gov/)
- Access managed through NASA Account Management System (NAMS)
- Authentication via Identity and Access Management System (IdMAX)
- Registration limited to personnel involved in contract negotiation and administration
- All registered personnel required to complete NASA Online Annual IT Security Training

**No additional NASA IT systems or agency data access required.**

## Notable Details

**Regulatory & Compliance Framework:**
- Compliance required with NASA FAR Supplement clause 1852.204-76
- Adherence to NIST and NASA IT Security requirements per www.nasa.gov/offices/ocio/itsecurity/index.html
- Computer Security Act of 1987 (PL 100-235) compliance
- Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986 and National Information Infrastructure Protection Act of 1996 awareness

**Sensitive Information Handling Commitments:**
BST agrees to:
1. Use sensitive information only for contract performance, not competitive advantage
2. Safeguard information from unauthorized use and disclosure
3. Limit access to employees with need-to-know
4. Preclude external access and disclosure
5. Train all employees with access to sensitive information
6. Obtain written affirmation of compliance from each employee
7. Monitor compliance and report breaches immediately to Contracting Officer and Contracting Officer's Representative (COR)

**General Rules of Behavior (15 requirements):**
- Lawful, authorized use only
- Protect PII and CUI data
- Report suspected security incidents immediately
- Encrypt NASA data on mobile/transportable devices
- Screen-lock computers when unattended
- Verify clearance, authorization, and need-to-know before sharing information
- Minimize sensitive data during travel
- Properly label classified, sensitive, and proprietary documents
- Maintain Separation of Duties principles
- Prohibit use of anonymizer sites
- Protect IT equipment from damage, theft, or compromise
- Supervisor instruction and training responsibility
- Minimum password requirements compliance
- No password sharing

**Incident Response:**
- Immediate notification to NASA Security Operations Center if any security incident occurs
- Notification to Contracting Officer and COR if NASA IT systems, data, or deliverables are compromised, misused, distorted, lost, or destroyed

**Consequences:**
- Unauthorized contract use may result in contract termination for default or contractor debarment
- Legal penalties under applicable federal law

**No additional IT security management procedures required.**
