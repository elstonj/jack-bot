# Data Collection over Costa Rica Volcanoes

## Document Metadata
- Type: Statement of Work (SOW)
- Client/Agency: NASA HQ and NASA Ames Research Center (ARC)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA contract 80NSSC25CA052
- Date: January 22-23, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 (fixed-wing UAS)
- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift will conduct multiple overflight missions over active Costa Rican volcanoes (Turrialba, Rincon, Poas) using the S2 UAS to collect high-resolution IR imagery and in-situ gas sampling data. Missions are planned for Q1 2025, with data delivery to the science community via NASA DAAC or website within 6 months of project start.

## Technical Approach
- **Platform**: Black Swift S2 fixed-wing, catapult-launched UAS
- **Operations**: Pre-determined transects for wide-area imagery over/near volcano plumes, or single-location orbits
- **Crew**: Black Swift employees or subcontractors (with optional NASA ARC S2 crewed by NASA personnel if airworthiness cleared)
- **Data Delivery Timeline**: Aircraft/payload state data to on-site staff within 48 hours of each flight; final flight report within 90 days
- **Regulatory Compliance**: NASA NPR 7900 compliance, including Airworthiness Review, Flight Readiness Review, and Mission Readiness Review as applicable
- **Logistics**: BST responsible for coordinating in-country services and Costa Rican airspace access via on-site personnel

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 (Fixed-Wing UAS)
- **Application**: Continuous high-resolution IR imagery and in-situ gas sampling over active volcanoes
- **Launch Method**: Catapult-launched
- **Operational History**: Currently operated by NASA ARC, NASA Langley Research Center, and USGS for volcano caldera mapping and gas sampling
- **Payloads**: IR/visible camera and in-situ gas sampling equipment

## Use Cases & Applications

**Mission Type**: Volcano monitoring and gas quantification
- **Target Volcanoes**: Turrialba, Rincon, Poas (Costa Rica)
- **Volcano Classification**: Active, low-intensity volcanoes
- **Scientific Objectives**: 
  - Wide-area mapping data collection via transects
  - Volcanic plume quantification
  - Gas sampling from volcanic emissions

## Notable Details

- **Partnership**: NASA HQ, NASA ARC, NASA LaRC, and USGS already operating S2 for similar volcano monitoring missions
- **Project Timeline**: Start NET January 31, 2025; all deliverables within 6 months of award
- **Data Accessibility**: All data products to be released to science community per NASA Open Science Data Policy; archiving via NASA DAAC
- **International Operations**: BST assumes full responsibility for in-country coordination, contracting, airspace permitting, and regulatory compliance with Costa Rican laws and policies
- **Multi-Agency Observation**: NASA and/or USFS personnel will observe operations
