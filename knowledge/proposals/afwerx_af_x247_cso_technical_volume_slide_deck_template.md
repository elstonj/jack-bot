## Executive Summary

Curable Composites and Black Swift Technologies propose to integrate advanced additive manufacturing of carbon fiber composites with Black Swift's proven UAS expertise to develop rapidly deployable, modular Group 2 UAS platforms for the Air Force's Replicator Initiative. The solution enables on-site, 24/7 manufacturing of durable carbon composite UAS components without traditional tooling or autoclaves, addressing critical DoD needs for cost-effective, adaptable platforms capable of real-time mission customization in extreme battlefield environments.

---

## Technical Approach

### Core Integration Strategy
- **Curable Composites Role:** Refine additive manufacturing (3D printing) process for lightweight yet structurally sound carbon composite fuselage and components
- **Black Swift Role:** Integrate composite components into modular UAS platform; conduct material testing, flight simulations, and proof-of-concept demonstrations

### Manufacturing Innovation
- **Edge Manufacturing:** On-site, distributed additive manufacturing of carbon fiber composites at point of need
- **No Traditional Tooling:** Eliminates need for expensive, labor-intensive autoclave curing processes
- **Autoclave-Free Production:** Uses Curable's proprietary resin and 3D printing process
- **24/7 On-Demand Manufacturing:** Enables rapid component production with significantly reduced lead times (days vs. months)
- **Near Net-Shape Preforms:** Reduces material scrap and post-machining requirements

### Design Approach
- **Modular Architecture:** Enables rapid payload integration and mission-specific customization
- **Rapid Reconfiguration:** Components can be reprinted/modified in field for evolving combat requirements
- **Real-Time Adaptability:** UAS can be modified without lengthy production cycles

## Competitive Landscape & Differentiation

### Current State-of-the-Art Baseline
- **UAS Limitations:** Weight-sensitive designs; long production lead times; single-mission designs; limited modularity
- **Carbon Composite Manufacturing:** Complex, multi-step processes; labor-intensive; time-consuming; expensive tooling; requires autoclave curing
- **Thermoplastic Alternatives:** Gaining traction in defense 3D printing but lack strength and durability required for combat-ready, high-performance platforms; inadequate for extreme battlefield conditions

### Competitive Advantage
Curable Composites' ability to 3D-print carbon fiber composites (vs. thermoplastics) provides:
- **Superior Strength-to-Weight Ratio:** Supports supersonic and high-endurance UAS designs
- **Battlefield-Deployable Quality:** Materials withstand rigors of high-speed flight and extreme environmental stress
- **Unique Manufacturing Capability:** On-site carbon composite production currently unavailable from competitors attempting distributed drone manufacturing

### Comparison to Current DoD Alternatives
- Current UAS rely on thermoplastics (inferior strength/durability for combat scenarios)
- Long production and maintenance lead times
- Rigid, non-modular designs unsuitable for dynamic payload swapping
- **Proposed Solution Advantages:** Faster production, real-time field adaptability, increased durability, cost-effective modularity, field-ready customization
