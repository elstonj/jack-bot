- Key Personnel: Joshua Fromm (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This report documents the 2024 hurricane season operations of Black Swift Technologies' S0 uncrewed aerial system (sUAS) deployed by NOAA's Emerging Technology Team. The S0 conducted 19 flights across 4 major hurricanes (Ernesto, Francine, Helene, and Milton), accumulating 20.5 total flight hours and demonstrating successful operations in extreme wind conditions up to 209 knots.

## Technical Approach
The S0 system was deployed in hurricane research missions with the following operational profile:
- Deployed from P3 aircraft during hurricane sampling missions
- Remote operation via line-of-sight communication
- Operated across varying hurricane intensity categories and altitudes
- Conducted atmospheric measurements and observations in extreme wind environments

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 (Small Uncrewed Aerial System)
- **What it is:** A small UAS designed for hurricane research and extreme weather operations
- **Operational performance achieved:**
  - Maximum recorded wind speed: 209 knots (during Hurricane Milton)
  - Maximum communication range: 308 km (during Hurricane Helene)
  - Maximum single flight duration: 105 minutes
  - Flight altitude range: capable of operating from surface level (10 m) to 2,120 m altitude
- **Deployment context:** Operated from NOAA P3 research aircraft, with maximum documented range to P3 of 191 miles
- **Survivability:** Demonstrated operations in Category 5 hurricane conditions (3 flights) and Category 4 conditions (2 flights)

## Use Cases & Applications

### Hurricane Research Missions
- Real-time atmospheric measurement and observation during active hurricanes
- High-wind condition data collection to support hurricane understanding and forecasting
- Multi-aircraft coordination (S0 deployed from P3)

## Key Results

### Operational Success
- **Total flights:** 19 missions
- **Success rate:** 16 of 19 flights classified as "Good Flights" (84% success rate)
- **Total flight hours:** 20.5 hours across all 4 hurricanes

### Performance Highlights
- **Hurricane Milton (Cat. 5):** S01 achieved 209 knots maximum wind speed measurement at 500 m altitude
- **Hurricane Helene (Cat. 4):** S02 recorded maximum communication range of 308 km (flight duration 104 minutes)
- **Extended range capability:** Multiple flights exceeded 180 km communication range
- **Extreme altitude operations:** S03 in Francine operated at 1,900 m altitude; S01 in Helene at 2,120 m

### Flight Duration Data
Maximum individual flight duration: 105 minutes (S02 in Ernesto, 102 kt wind speed)
- Ernesto: 4 flights, 66-77 min duration
- Francine: 1 very short flight (7 min), others 39-100 min
- Helene: Flights 77-104 min (excluding one 9 min flight)
- Milton: 3-87 min range

## Notable Details

1. **Extreme Weather Capability:** The S0 demonstrated proven ability to operate in Category 4-5 hurricane conditions, representing significant capability advancement for uncrewed systems in severe weather research

2. **Communication Range:** Extended communication ranges (up to 308 km) suggest advanced telemetry and control systems capable of maintaining link integrity in noisy RF environments

3. **Partnership:** Integration with NOAA's existing P3 hurricane research platform indicates successful operational integration with manned research aircraft

4. **Data Quality:** High percentage of successful flights (84%) even in extreme conditions demonstrates reliability and robustness

5. **Scientific Value:** Collection of wind speed and altitude data in real-time hurricane environments provides research data previously difficult to obtain safely
