## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is designing the SuperSwift airframe specifically for NASA soil moisture payload missions, with extensibility to other science and commercial applications. The primary innovation is a removable nose cone that simplifies payload integration, reduces costs, and enables field-swappable capabilities while maintaining a man-portable, hand-launched platform.

## Technical Approach
SuperSwift is designed around a modular payload integration concept centered on a removable nose cone:
- **Removable nose cone** with openly published mechanical and electrical interfaces
- Field-swappable without tools
- Well-specified weight and moment envelopes to ensure payload CG and weight compliance
- Electrical interface provides both power and autopilot telemetry for payload data tagging
- Twin-engine design positioning payload bay forward and battery pack aft for rapid swaps
- Hand-launched capability for deployment over unimproved surfaces

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SuperSwift Airframe:**
- Man-portable system with tool-less setup and 5-minute nominal deployment time from case
- Twin-engine design with removable nose cone
- Hand-launch capable (no catapult or runway required)
- 1.5-hour flight time on electrical power
- Payload capacity: 3 lbs (nose cone), scalable with larger airframe versions
- Target cost: ≤$10K per airframe
- Planned hybrid power system upgrades for extended endurance

**Removable Nose Cone:**
- Removable component cost in hundreds of dollars (vs. entire airframe cost)
- Openly published mechanical and electrical interface specifications
- Field-swappable with no tools required
- Mechanical interface includes weight/moment envelopes for integrator reference
- Electrical interface provides power and autopilot telemetry to payload

## Use Cases & Applications
- NASA soil moisture measurement missions (primary)
- Future extensions to other science missions
- Commercial applications (unspecified)

## Notable Details
- Design prioritizes scientist/payload-integrator accessibility through open interfaces and simplified swapping
- Reduces friction between payload development and flight deployment
- Addresses typical scientific application requirements: portability, rapid deployment, limited infrastructure needs
- Twin-engine configuration optimizes center of gravity and operational flexibility with forward payload bay and swappable rear battery
- Future hybrid power system planned to significantly extend mission endurance beyond current 1.5-hour electric flight time
