## Key Topics Covered

### Three Points Where Fraud Can Occur
1. During the application process
2. Within the proposal itself
3. When the award is given

### Four Main Types of Fraud
1. **Misrepresentations or factual omissions** - false certifications, inaccurate labor rates, falsified letters of reference, ineligible PIs
2. **Misrepresentation of expended funds** - misrepresenting how funds were used, work done, results achieved; includes research recycling
3. **Misuse of award funds** - using funds for non-research purposes, funneling to personal accounts, falsifying timesheets
4. **Duplication of funds** - receiving multiple awards from same/different agencies for same research

Additional fraud types: self-dealing, failure to comply with federal cost principles

### Consequences of Fraud
**Criminal Penalties:**
- False Statements: up to 5 years prison, $250,000 fine
- Theft of Federal Property: up to 10 years prison, $250,000 fine
- Wire Fraud: up to 20 years prison, $250,000 fine
- False Claims: up to $250,000 (individuals) or $500,000 (companies)

**Civil Liabilities:**
- Treble damages (3x actual damages)
- $11,000 fine per false claim

**Administrative Remedies:**
- Contract termination
- Debarment (3+ years) - bans from federal contracts/subcontracts

### Prevention Strategies
- Maintain meticulous records (timesheets, receipts, invoices, lab notebooks)
- Document facility use agreements and personnel logs
- Provide regular project status updates
- Maintain open communication with government
- Follow established SBIR/STTR rules and policies
- Provide required certifications regarding company eligibility and non-duplication

## Notable Details
- Based on 2011 SBIR/STTR Reauthorization and 2014 SBA Policy Directive
- GAO report (April 2017) identified need for additional FWA prevention implementation
- All SBIR solicitations require disclosure of "Similar or Duplicative Proposals or Awards"
- It is permissible to submit identical proposals to multiple agencies *with proper notification*, but unlawful to accept multiple contracts for essentially equivalent work
