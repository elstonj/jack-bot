- Program/Solicitation: DoD X25.5 CSO D2P2 (Hurricane Sampling)

- Key Personnel: Dr. Jun Zhang (University of Miami PI); Beck Cotter (BST contact/last editor)

## Executive Summary
This subcontract defines University of Miami's role in a project to advance air-deployed small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS) for hurricane sampling from C-130 aircraft. The project aims to address tropical cyclone (TC) boundary layer measurement gaps by integrating high-frequency UAS data into operational forecasts and real-time data transfer to NOAA's National Hurricane Center and Environmental Prediction Center.

## Technical Approach
- Advance technologies for air-deployed UAS optimized for hurricane sampling operations
- Develop tools to enable real-time sUAS data collection and transfer from sUAS to C-130 aircraft
- Conduct field programs with C-130 deployments in both clear air and hurricane conditions
- Process and format P3 and sUAS observational data for research use
- Design experiments and analyze collected atmospheric data
- Enable real-time data transfer from sUAS to operational forecasting centers

## Products & Capabilities Described
**sUAS (Small Unmanned Aircraft System)**
- Air-deployed platform for atmospheric data collection
- Designed for deployment from C-130 aircraft
- Capable of operation in both clear air and hurricane conditions
- Real-time data transfer capability to host aircraft and ground stations

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Tropical Cyclone Forecasting**: Fill boundary layer measurement gap during hurricane operations
- **C-130 Operations**: Integrate sUAS as operational payload for NOAA hurricane reconnaissance flights
- **Operational Meteorology**: Provide high-frequency atmospheric data to:
  - NOAA National Hurricane Center
  - NOAA Environmental Prediction Center
  - Environmental Modeling Center
  - AOML Hurricane Research Division
