## Executive Summary
This document provides formatting and content requirements for the Technical Volume slide deck submission for an Air Force SBIR Phase I proposal (AFX23.1). The slide deck is limited to 25 slides and must include 11 required slides plus 6 conditional slides covering technical merit, commercialization potential, team capabilities, defense needs, and transition planning.

## Document Purpose
This is an instruction/guidance document, not a BST proposal or report. It outlines the structure and content expectations for SBIR Phase I Technical Volume submissions to the Air Force, serving as a template for how proposers should present their solutions.

## Required Slide Content (11 Slides)

1. **Overall Summary Slide** - Company name and proposal overview
2. **Technical Merit Summary** - Scientific merit of solution, baseline comparison, advancement of state-of-the-art
3. **Concept and Significance of Problem/Opportunity** - Specific technical problem addressed, importance to customers, differentiation from alternatives, R&D focus (not procurement)
4. **Commercialization Potential** - Ability to commercialize in DoD and commercial markets, pilots, revenue, customer interest, competitive landscape, scalability
5. **Team's Ability to Perform** - Team identification, relevant education/experience, capability for development and adaptation to AF customer needs
6. **Defense Need** - Knowledge of AF end-users, use cases, differentiation from current alternatives, previous DoD funding history
7. **Objectives** - Success measurement plan, stakeholder identification beyond end-users (safety, test/certification, cybersecurity, acquisition specialists)
8. **Plan Outline** - Work approach, task descriptions, conduct location, schedule, deliverables including kickoff (within 30 days), preliminary report, and final report with SF 298 and DD Form 882
9. **Transition Plan** - Strategy to transition solution into Air Force, integration with existing AF customer efforts
10. **Related Work (Dual-Use)** - Significant related activities by PI, firm, consultants; state-of-the-art awareness; similar previous/parallel work with descriptions, POC information, completion dates; Defense customer interviews
11. **Clearances/Certifications/Approvals/Registrations** - DUNS number, CAGE code, SBA SBIR/STTR ID number (CAGE code required or proposal may be disqualified)

## Conditional Slides (Include If Applicable)

12. **Foreign Citizens** - Identify foreign citizens/dual citizenship holders, country of origin, visa/work permit type, assigned tasks
13. **Facilities/Equipment** - Available instrumentation, physical facilities, environmental law compliance (airborne emissions, waterborne effluents, radiation, noise, waste disposal, hazardous materials handling)
14. **Prior, Current, or Pending Support** - Similar proposals to other AF organizations, DoD components, or Federal agencies; requires entity name, topic number, submission/award dates, titles, PI names, solicitation details, contract numbers
15. **Technical Data Rights (Assertions)** - Intellectual property assertions per DFARS 252.227-7018; Government obtains royalty-free license for 5 years post-completion, unlimited rights after 20-year restrictive period
16. **Supplemental Information on Direct Labor Costs** - Justification for notable labor costs (significant hours, high hourly wages, overhead/fringe rates)
17. **Supplemental Information for Direct Material Costs** - Itemized list of materials, parts, supplies with types, quantities, prices, purposes, and cost bases (historical data, vendor quotes, catalogs)
18. **Detailed Travel Costs** - Number of trips, travelers per trip, specific locations, purposes, basis for flight/lodging/per diem/ground transportation costs; note that travel is likely required for Phase I feasibility study
19. **Supplemental Information for Special Tooling and Test Equipment** - Justification for specialized equipment purchases; must be determined advantageous to Government and directly related to effort; title vests with Air Force unless transfer deemed more cost-effective

## Formatting Requirements

- **File Format**: Single PDF file, virus-checked, not locked or encrypted
- **Length**: 25-slide maximum; slides exceeding limit will not be reviewed
- **Layout**: Consecutively numbered slides; each slide must include company name, topic number, and proposal number (assigned by DSIP at cover sheet creation)
- **Media**: No embedded active graphics, videos, moving pictures, or similar media
- **Mandatory Slides**: Proposals without all 11 required slides will not be considered

## Key Compliance Notes

- CAGE code is mandatory at submission or proposal may be disqualified
- Foreign citizen participation requires detailed reporting or proposal will be rejected
- RDT&E (Research, Development, Test & Evaluation) focus required; non-RDT&E procurement of commercial solutions inappropriate for SBIR
- Scientific and technical literature searches required per AFI 61-201 and AFRL/CA guidance
- Phase I should determine technical feasibility of proposed concept
- Foreign citizens must be reported; firms frequently fail to disclose dual citizenship holders, risking rejection

## Notable Details

- Kickoff meeting required within 30 calendar days of contract start
- AF recognizes actual travel costs may differ from estimates; estimates aid contracting officer fairness and reasonableness determination
- Government maintains rights to SBIR data with specific restrictive legends and disclosure limitations during license period
- Document emphasizes customer interviews and customer interest evidence as critical for commercialization slide
- Strategic Capability Area reference applicable if relevant to defense need
