## Executive Summary
The NightFOX project is developing a UAS-based system for nighttime fire observations, leveraging the S2 platform from Black Swift Technologies. As of May 2018, the project had received delivery of the S2 aircraft and was progressing on payload integration design, sensor development (including CO2 and CO sensors), and coordination for summer 2018 field testing in the San Luis Valley and participation in the ISARRA Flight Week.

## Technical Approach
- Integration of remote sensing and in situ sensor payloads onto the BST S2 UAS platform
- Development of custom nosecone design for the S2 to accommodate specialized optics and instrumentation
- Software development for digital input and scan angle reading for remote sensing payload
- Prototype development and testing of CO2 and CO sensor technologies
- Payload layout design with emphasis on optics protection (hatch cover design ongoing)
- Preliminary observation strategies being developed for both in situ and remote sensing flight modes

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- Delivery status: Delivered to University of Colorado (CU) in May 2018
- Platform TRL: Level 6 (system/subsystem model or prototype demonstration in a relevant environment)
- Role: Primary airborne platform for NightFOX payload integration
- Custom integration: Working with BST on custom nosecone design and payload layout

### Remote Sensing Payload
- Status: Integration design completed (with BST approval); awaiting hatch cover for optics protection
- Components: IR telecentric scopes (prototype evaluation ongoing)
- Payload TRL: Level 5 (component and/or breadboard validation in relevant environment)
- Software: Digital input and scan angle reading software in development

### In Situ Payload
- Status: Layout design begun (following completion of remote sensing payload design)
- Instruments in development: CO2 sensor (prototype testing ongoing), CO sensors (delivery delayed, expected December; prototype elements to undergo evaluation upon arrival)
- Commercial instruments: Coarse mode aerosol instruments to be assessed

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Mission:** Nighttime fire observations in wildfire environments

**Planned Test Sites & Operations:**
- San Luis Valley (test flight site survey completed; sites selected)
- Participation in ISARRA (International Society for Aerial Remote Sensing and Applications) Flight Week, July 2018
- Prescribed burn observation opportunities in Boulder area (late summer/fall 2018)
- Operational wildfire sampling (subject to FAA coordination and TFR access)

**Operational Requirements Being Addressed:**
- Higher altitude flight operations (FAA COA coordination ongoing)
- Beyond visual line of sight (BVLOS) operations (FAA discussions underway)
- Temporary flight restriction (TFR) access protocols for wildfire incidents
