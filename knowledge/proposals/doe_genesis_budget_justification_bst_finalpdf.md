## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes a DOE-funded project titled "AI-Enabled Mobile Autonomous Laboratories for Real-Time Adaptive Experimentation in Dynamic Environmental Systems" running from July 1, 2026 through March 31, 2027. The project will develop and validate AI-enabled autonomous unmanned aircraft systems for real-time environmental monitoring and adaptive field experimentation.

## Technical Approach
The technical approach centers on leveraging Black Swift's S0-VTOL UAS platform equipped for atmospheric measurements. Key elements include:
- Development of AI-enabled autonomous laboratory capabilities on mobile platforms
- Real-time adaptive experimentation protocols for dynamic environmental systems
- Integration of adaptive control systems to respond to environmental conditions in real-time
- Validation through field testing with three S0-VTOL systems

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0-VTOL UAS
- **What it is**: A fully assembled vertical takeoff and landing unmanned aircraft system designed for atmospheric thermodynamic measurements
- **Capabilities**: Measures wind, pressure, temperature, and humidity
- **Use in project**: Three units purchased for testing and validation at $18,000 per unit
- **Total equipment budget**: $54,000 for three aircraft

## Use Cases & Applications
- Atmospheric thermodynamic measurement and monitoring
- Dynamic environmental system characterization
- Real-time adaptive field experimentation in variable conditions
- Collaboration with Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL) on DOE Genesis program objectives
