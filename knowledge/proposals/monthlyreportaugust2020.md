# Monthly Progress Report - August 2020

## Document Metadata
- Type: Monthly progress report
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: AREN (Airborne Research and Education Network)
- Date: September 1, 2020 (reporting period: August 2020)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: AeroPod, Aerotube, PocketLab Air
- Key Personnel: Geoff Bland (Task Manager), Kay Rufty (Employee)

## Executive Summary
This August 2020 progress report documents BST's work on the NASA AREN program, including assembly and shipment of equipment to partners, construction and testing of low-cost $2 kites, PocketLab Air sensor integration and flight testing, and continued development of AeroPod and Aerotube components for the MHP (Mobile High-altitude Platform or similar project).

## Technical Approach
- **Low-cost kite development**: Construction and feasibility testing of $2 kites built from Dollar Store supplies with bottle weights, following Glenn's design specifications
- **Sensor integration**: Experimentation with PocketLab Air instrument, including 3D-printed mount design and kite-based flight testing
- **Data collection and analysis**: Flight log submission to mission mapper; sensor data uploaded to mission mapper and plotted in CODAP (Common Online Data Analysis Platform)
- **Hardware redesign**: AeroPod battery repositioning away from GNSS antenna; sensor returned to BST for calibration
- **CAD modeling**: Continued Aerotube design work in Onshape, including GNSS mount and sensor board mount additions

## Products & Capabilities Described

### AeroPod
- Airborne platform with GNSS and sensor payload capability
- Battery positioning being optimized to avoid interference with GNSS
- Undergoing refinement with sensor board integration

### Aerotube
- Airborne platform under development in Onshape
- Incorporating GNSS mount and sensor board mount in current design iteration
- Part of broader airborne research capability

### PocketLab Air
- Environmental sensor instrument
- Successfully tested for kite-based atmospheric data collection
- Data can be uploaded to mission mapper and analyzed in CODAP

## Use Cases & Applications
- Kite-based atmospheric monitoring for AREN educational/research missions
- Sensor data collection for air quality and atmospheric parameters
- Educational outreach through low-cost kite platforms
- Multi-site coordination (CU collaboration in Denver and Boulder)
