# Autonomous Wildfire Detection and Monitoring System

## Document Metadata
- Type: SBIR Phase I Funding Decision Notice
- Client/Agency: NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration), Technology Partnerships Office
- Program/Solicitation: NOAA-OAR-TPO-2025-0001, FY 2025 Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase I
- Date: 2025 (decision issued; proposal submitted for FY2025 cycle)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S3 UAS platform
- Key Personnel: Not named in this document (review panel comments only)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed an autonomous wildfire detection and monitoring system leveraging uncrewed aircraft systems with lightweight, high-resolution payloads. The proposal was not recommended for funding despite receiving a high aggregate score (93/100) from peer reviewers, due to competitive rankings and limited available funding.

## Technical Approach
- Deployment of an uncrewed aircraft system (UAS) equipped with lightweight, high-resolution payload
- Use of passive and active remote sensing instruments for detection and monitoring
- Focus on enhancing wildfire detection capabilities through aerial reconnaissance
- Proposed timeline and budget characterized as reasonably scoped for Phase I work

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S3 UAS Platform
- Lightweight uncrewed aircraft system capable of carrying remote sensing payloads
- Described as having "clear commercial potential"
- Suitable for operating in extreme environments
- Can accommodate both passive and active remote sensing instruments

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Wildfire Detection and Monitoring**: Real-time or near-real-time identification and tracking of active wildfires
- **Emergency Response Support**: Assisting wildfire management agencies in tactical decision-making
- **Societal Impact**: Life-saving potential through early detection, property protection, and economic impact mitigation

## Evaluation Results
**Funding Decision**: Not recommended for funding

**Aggregate Scores** (Total 93/100):
- Criterion 1 - Scientific Merit & Technical Approach: 36/40
- Criterion 2 - Innovation: 20/20 (perfect score)
- Criterion 3 - Commercial & Societal Impacts: 18/20
- Criterion 4 - Facilities & Key Investigators: 19/20

**Reviewer Assessment Summary**:
- Scientific merit deemed sound with good foundation for Phase II work
- Innovation highly praised as creative solution to significant national challenge
- Commercial potential well-articulated; S3 platform has identifiable customer base
- Team qualifications excellent with relevant experience in UAS operations for extreme environments and NOAA collaboration
- Facilities and instrumentation adequate for successful completion

## Notable Details
- Proposal included letter of support from a potential customer beyond NOAA, demonstrating market interest
- Team has prior experience with similar work leveraging UAS solutions and existing NOAA collaborations
- While the proposal scored highly (93/100) across all criteria, funding was unavailable due to competitive ranking and budget constraints
- Reviewers provided constructive feedback suggesting the work was merit-worthy but not funded due to external factors rather than technical deficiencies
