## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to support a University of Iowa-led USDA NIFA precision agriculture research project by conducting three annual flight campaigns (2025, 2026, 2027) using its S2 fixed-wing UAS to carry and integrate commercial and proprietary nitrous oxide (N2O) sensors over corn fields during high-rate soil denitrification periods. The work includes payload integration, mission support, and data collection over 3 acres at 400 feet altitude during April-May of each project year.

## Technical Approach
- **Aircraft**: Black Swift S2 (S2UAS) fixed-wing drone
- **Flight Operations**: Three separate campaigns, each 3 days on-site in Eastern Iowa (Cedar Rapids airport)
- **Sensors**: 
  - Year 1: Commercial N2O sensor (Venus Solo, LSE 4450, or Sensiron option TBD by UI)
  - Years 2-3: University of Iowa's proprietary N2O sensor
- **Flight Parameters**: 400 feet altitude; 3 acres area coverage per campaign
- **Data Handling**: Onboard DAQ system and storage device; post-flight retrieval and processing (no in-flight data relay required)
- **Payload Integration Work**: Mechanical interface design, electrical system integration, firmware development, assembly, testing

## Products & Capabilities Described

- Fixed-wing UAS platform
- Used for: Low-altitude agricultural sensor missions
- Rental cost per mission: $1,000
- Can carry sensor payloads requiring custom integration

- BST offers payload integration services including:
  - Mechanical interface design and manufacturing (S2 nosecone, scaffold modifications, custom 3D prints, carbon fiber plates)

## Use Cases & Applications
**Primary Application**: Precision agriculture research - monitoring nitrous oxide emissions from corn fields during planting season

**Specific Mission Parameters**:
- Timing: Mid-April to mid-May (optimal for post-rain denitrification events)
- Geography: Eastern Iowa corn fields
- Crop: Corn during sowing season
- Environmental monitoring focus: N2O soil emissions (greenhouse gas/environmental monitoring)
