## Executive Summary
This is the DoD SBIR 25.4 Annual Program Broad Agency Announcement preface document that establishes the framework, eligibility requirements, certifications, proposal preparation instructions, and submission guidelines for small business concerns (SBCs) to submit Phase I, Direct to Phase II, or Phase III proposals throughout fiscal year 2025. The BAA runs on a rolling monthly release schedule with 12 releases from October 2024 through October 2025.

### Three-Phase Program Structure

**Phase I:**
- Duration: 6-12 months
- Purpose: Determine scientific, technical, and commercial merit/feasibility
- Deliverable: Proof of concept and commercialization potential

**Phase II:**
- Duration: 24 months
- Purpose: Continue and develop R&D from Phase I results
- Eligibility: Based on Phase I results and Phase II proposal merit
- Alternative: Direct to Phase II available (without prior Phase I) with feasibility documentation

**Phase III:**
- Funding Source: Non-SBIR (private sector, non-SBIR federal sources)
- Purpose: Commercialize previously SBIR-funded technology into viable product/service

## Eligibility Requirements

### Small Business Concern (SBC) Definition
SBCs must meet requirements in 13 C.F.R §§ 701-705 at time of award.

### Work Performance Requirements
- **Phase I:** SBC must conduct minimum 2/3 of research/analytical work (measured by direct and indirect costs)
- **Phase II:** SBC must perform minimum 50% of research/analytical work
- **Deviations:** Possible with Funding Agreement Officer written approval after SBIR/STTR Program Manager consultation

### Principal Investigator Requirements
- Primary employment must be with proposing SBC at time of award and during proposed effort
- Primary employment = >50% of time spent with SBC (based on 40-hour work week)
- Precludes full-time employment at another organization
- Changes subject to Funding Agreement Officer approval

### Work Location
- **Phase I & II:** All R&D work must be performed in the United States
- **Applies to:** SBC and all subcontractors

### Ownership Requirements
- SBC must meet Small Business Ownership and Control per 13 CFR § 121.702
- **Joint Ventures/Limited Partnerships:** Permitted if entity qualifies as small business; foreign entity relationships must be disclosed

### Venture Capital/Private Equity Restrictions
- Unless noted in Service/Component instructions, SBCs majority-owned by multiple venture capital operating companies (VCOCs), hedge funds, or private equity firms are **ineligible**
- If Service/Component authorizes such participation, foreign nationals/entities/governments with >5% equity stake must be identified
- Single VCOC, hedge fund, or PE firm cannot own >50% unless it qualifies as SBC with >50% direct ownership by US citizens or permanent residents

## Performance Benchmarks for Experienced Firms

Per SBIR and STTR Extension Act of 2022 (Pub. L. 117-183):

**Two Key Metrics:**
1. **Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate:** Extent awardee progresses from Phase I to Phase II
2. **Commercialization Benchmark:** Extent awardee moves toward commercialization post-Phase II

**Consequences of Failure:**
SBCs failing either benchmark are subject to DoD award caps:
- Army: 20 total Phase I and Direct to Phase II awards
- Navy: 20 total Phase I and Direct to Phase II awards
- Air Force: 20 total Phase I and Direct to Phase II awards
- All other DoD Components combined: 20 total Phase I and Direct to Phase II awards
- **Total limit:** 80 awards across all DoD

## Required Certifications and Registrations

### 1. System for Award Management (SAM)
- Required for all SBCs
- Generates Unique Entity ID (UEI) and Commercial and Government Entry (CAGE) code
- Must be current and active

### 2. SBA Company Registry
- Required registration
- Must verify with SBC Control ID and Proof of Registration/Certification at proposal submission

### 3. Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP)
- All proposal preparation and submission through DSIP
- Individual user accounts required (authenticated via Login.gov)
- Designated Firm Admin controls firm PIN and user access
- **Geographic Restriction:** Only accessible from within the United States (50 states, territories, possessions, Puerto Rico, Marshall Islands, Federated States of Micronesia, Palau, District of Columbia)
- **Support:** DoDSBIRSupport@reisystems.com (Mon-Fri, 9am-5pm ET)

### 4. Program Certifications
At proposal submission and during award lifecycle (Phase I award, prior to Phase I final payment, at 50% Phase II payment, prior to Phase II final payment):
- SBC meets size, ownership, and SBIR program requirements
- Per Pub. L. 117-183 additional certifications required

## Due Diligence Program to Assess Security Risks

### Statutory Requirement (15 U.S.C. §638(vv))
DoD, in coordination with SBA, conducts risk-based due diligence review of:
- Cybersecurity practices
- Patent analysis
- Employee analysis and foreign ownership
- SBC financial ties and obligations (surety, equity, debt)
- Employees' ties to foreign countries/persons/entities
- Open-source analysis verification of disclosed information
- Assessment of non-disclosed foreign affiliations

### Information Collected
SBCs must complete "Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries" (Volume 7 of proposal submission).

### Potential Additional Requests
DoD may request true copies of contractual or financial obligations/agreements with foreign state-owned enterprises or foreign entities from past 5 years.

### Award Decision
- If due diligence concludes unacceptable national security risk that cannot be mitigated, proposal will NOT be considered for award regardless of technical merit
- **Reference:** "Defense Small Business Innovation Research and Small Business Technology Transfer Due Diligence Program" memo dated May 13, 2024, Attachment 2: Common Risk Matrix

## Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI) Requirements

### FAR 9.5 Compliance
Proposing SBCs must identify and disclose all facts relevant to potential OCIs involving:
- Proposing SBC's organization
- Any proposed team members (sub-awardees, consultants)

**Required Disclosure:**
- OCI mitigation plan describing actions taken/intended to prevent conflicting roles
- Prevention of unfair competitive advantage
- Specific discussion of disclosed OCI in context of FAR 9.505-1 through 9.505-4

### DoD Supplemental OCI Policy
SBCs/team members providing scientific/technical assistance (SETA), advisory and assistance services (A&AS), or similar support to any DoD Service/Component office cannot concurrently be technical performers.

**Disclosure Required If:**
- Currently providing such support under active award/sub-award, OR
- Provided support within one calendar year prior to proposal submission

**If Applicable, Proposal Must Include:**
- Name of DoD Service/Component office receiving support
- Prime contract number
- Identification of team member providing support
- OCI mitigation plan per FAR 9.5

## Research Involving Human Subjects (RIHS/HSR)

### Requirements
- Must comply with applicable federal/state laws and agency policy/guidelines
- All institutions must provide documentation of current federal assurance of compliance (e.g., HHS Office for Human Research Protections federal-wide assurance)
- All subcontractors must have valid assurance
- Personnel must provide documentation of completed human subjects protection training
- **Exception:** Exemption from assurance requirement if research meets exemption criteria in 32 CFR 219.101

### IRB Approval Process (If Selected)
- Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval required OR determination that work meets exemption criteria
- All investigators must show appropriate training (evidence accompanies protocol)
- Protocol (separate from proposal) must include: research plan, study population, risks/benefits, recruitment/consent, data collection/analysis
- **Timeline:** IRB review typically 1-3 months; DoD review 3-6 months
- **Funding Restriction:** NO funding for human subject research until ALL approvals granted

### Proposal Preparation
- Clearly segregate human subject research activities from other R&D
- Encouraged to separate these tasks in technical and cost proposals to avoid contract award delays

## Research Involving Animal Subjects

### Requirements
- Must comply with federal and agency rules on acquisition, transport, care, handling, use
- Institutions must have Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC) approval
- Secondary/headquarters-level approval required from DoD veterinarian trained in laboratory animal medicine/science
- **Funding Restriction:** NO animal research conducted until all appropriate DoD office(s) grant approval

### Proposal Preparation
- Briefly describe plans for IACUC review and approval
- Encouraged to separate animal use tasks in technical and cost proposals to avoid contract award delays

## Research Involving Recombinant DNA Molecules

### Requirements
- Must comply with applicable federal/state law, regulation, and additional agency guidance
- Institutional biosafety committee must approve the research

## Proposal Structure

### Volume 1: Proposal Cover Sheet (DSIP-prepared)
- Brief technical abstract (max 3,000 characters)
- Anticipated benefits and potential commercial applications discussion (max 3,000 characters)
- Percentage of Work (POW) certification - derived from cost volume (Volume 3) data
- **Note:** If POW falls below eligibility requirements, Funding Agreement Officer must upload explanatory letter or approval
- **Deviations:** Some DoD Services/Components will not accept POW deviations; check Service/Component instructions
- **Can be modified** until BAA closes

### Volume 2: Technical Volume
**Format Requirements:**
- Single PDF file with graphics
- Virus check required before upload
- No password protection, encryption, or locked files
- No embedded active media (videos, animations, etc.)
- Must verify page limit compliance after DSIP upload

**Layout Requirements:**
- Consecutive page numbering
- 10-point type minimum on standard 8.5" x 11" paper
- 1-inch margins (including header)
- Header: SBC name
