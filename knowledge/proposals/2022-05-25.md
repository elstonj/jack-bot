## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies conducted 40 flight tests of a novel aerial drone platform designed for exploration of Titan, evaluating both hover and forward flight capabilities. Testing focused on optimizing propeller configurations and motor canting angles to maximize power efficiency for a 1.1 kg system operating at high altitude conditions.

## Technical Approach
The drone platform was tested with multiple propeller and motor configurations to identify optimal power efficiency:
- Two propeller sizes evaluated: 9x6 and 8x8
- Two cruise speeds tested: 17 m/s and 19 m/s
- Motor canting angle optimization: 7° cant angle implemented to reduce roll power requirements during forward flight
- Test location: Sod Farm site, Colorado (elevation 5,000+ feet) to simulate thin atmosphere conditions relevant to Titan operations

## Key Results

**Power Consumption by Configuration:**
- 9x6 at 17 m/s: 112.8 W thrust power
- 9x6 at 19 m/s: 140.4 W thrust power
- 8x8 at 17 m/s: 107.8 W thrust power
- 8x8 at 19 m/s: 113.0 W thrust power

**Motor Canting Benefits (7° cant angle):**
- Roll power reduction: 45-55% savings during cruise flight
- Thrust power penalty: <1% increase
- Total power improvements with canting applied to all configurations tested

**Efficiency Data Example (9x6 at 19 m/s):**
- Straight configuration total power: 182.2 W
- Canted configuration total power: 163.6 W
- Power savings: 18.6 W (~10% overall reduction)

## Notable Details
- Testing conducted at high elevation (5,000+ feet) to simulate low-density atmosphere conditions relevant to extraplanetary deployment
- Motor canting proved highly effective for reducing lateral control power while maintaining thrust efficiency
- Platform designed for extreme environment exploration with minimal mass budget (1.1 kg constraint)
- Project marked as "Completed/Inactive" in BST file system, indicating SBIR phase completion
- 8x8 propeller configuration showed comparable or superior efficiency to 9x6 despite larger blade size
