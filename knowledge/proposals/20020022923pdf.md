# Exploring Venus by Solar Airplane

## Executive Summary
This paper proposes a solar-powered airplane for atmospheric exploration of Venus at cloud-top altitudes (45-60 km). The author argues Venus is uniquely suitable for long-duration solar flight due to high solar constant (2.6 kW/m²), Earth-like atmospheric pressure, slow rotation enabling continuous sunlight, and high albedo (90%) that allows power generation from both wing surfaces.

## Technical Approach

### Atmospheric Environment Exploited
- **Altitude range**: 45-60 km above surface
- **Pressure**: 2 bars (45 km) to 0.2 bars (60 km) — comparable to Earth conditions
- **Temperature**: ~80°C at 45 km, decreasing to -35°C at 60 km
- **Solar intensity**: 2.62 kW/m² exoatmospheric (1.9x Earth's 1.37 kW/m²), attenuated but still strong at cloud level
- **Surface gravity**: 90.7% of Earth's

### Design Challenges & Solutions

**Wind Dynamics:**
- Planet rotates every 117 Earth days (retrograde), extremely slow rotation
- Ground speed to remain at subsolar point: only 13.4 km/hr at equator
- **Critical problem**: Clouds circulate in 4-5 days; wind speeds are 45 m/sec (160 km/hr) at 45 km altitude, increasing to 100 m/sec (360 km/hr) at 60 km
- Three design approach options:
  1. Design for 100 m/sec airspeed
  2. Fly at lower altitudes with reduced wind (but less solar intensity)
  3. Operate at near-polar latitudes where winds are lower (but high sun angles)

**Chemical Hazard:**
- Sulfuric acid clouds require all surfaces coated with impervious materials (glass and Teflon coatings)
- Solar arrays must be fully encapsulated with no exposed openings
- Sulfuric acid handling technology well-developed; no fundamental barrier to indefinite flight

### Deployment Architecture
- **Folding-wing design** to fit inside Pioneer-Venus aeroshell (1.2-meter diameter)
- Wings fold in two places; unusually wide chord to maximize solar array area
- Deployment sequence:
  1. Heat-shield separation
  2. Parachute deployment and descent stabilization
  3. Parachute drop at 65-70 km altitude
  4. Airplane unfolds and descends to desired flying altitude
  5. Begins observations

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Venus Solar Airplane:**
- Solar-powered aircraft designed for continuous operation in Venus's upper atmosphere
- Folding-wing configuration for aeroshell integration
- Both-surface solar power generation (high albedo exploitation)
- Indefinite operational duration (no day-night cycle requirement)
- Position control advantage over balloon probes; can maintain station over specific locations

## Use Cases & Applications

### Scientific Objectives
1. **Climate science**: "Atmospheric truth" measurements for greenhouse effect understanding and general circulation models
2. **Atmospheric composition**: Investigate ultraviolet absorber identity, mode-three particles (30-58 km altitude), atmospheric superrotation
3. **Geology/Volcanology**: 
   - Radar probing of surface (with orbital transmitter) for order-of-magnitude resolution improvement
   - Chemical detection of volcanic plume signatures to answer whether Venus has active volcanism
   - Surface probe deployment at geologically interesting locations
4. **Formation flying**: Two or more aircraft measuring correlation of atmospheric properties by altitude/position

### Additional Values
- **Public engagement**: First airplane flown on another planet
- **Pathfinding**: Demonstrates approach applicable to Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, and Titan solar aircraft

## Notable Details

- **Comparison to other planets**: Venus is described as "perhaps the easiest planet in the solar system for flight of a long-duration solar airplane" compared to Mars (thin atmosphere) or other outer planets

- **Reference mission**: Pioneer Venus small-probe aeroshell (1.2-meter) used as baseline constraint for folding-wing design

- **Historical precedent**: Vega mission (1985) demonstrated balloon operations in Venus atmosphere; airplane offers superior position control

- **Engineering maturity**: Sulfuric acid materials technology is "well developed," suggesting no fundamental showstoppers
