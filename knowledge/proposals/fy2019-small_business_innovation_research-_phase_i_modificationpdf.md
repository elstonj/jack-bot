## Executive Summary
This document is a USDA NIFA SBIR Phase I solicitation for FY2019 announcing research funding opportunities for small businesses to develop innovative agricultural and food-related technologies. The RFA provides 13 topic areas with specific research priorities, offering Phase I grants up to $100,000 for 8 months to demonstrate technical feasibility of proposed innovations with commercial potential.

## Document Purpose & Context
**This is NOT a Black Swift Technologies document.** It is a government Request for Applications (RFA) from the USDA National Institute of Food and Agriculture (NIFA) announcing the FY2019 Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase I program. The document outlines eligibility, funding, application procedures, and 13 research topic areas that small businesses can apply to.

## SBIR Phase I Program Structure

### Funding & Award Details
- **Phase I Award Amount:** Up to $100,000
- **Phase I Duration:** Normally not to exceed 8 months
- **Purpose:** Demonstrate technical feasibility of proposed innovation
- **Phase I Focus:** Research that proves scientific/technical feasibility as prerequisite to Phase II
- **Phase II:** Continuation of R&D toward commercialization (only open to Phase I recipients)
- **Phase III:** Commercialization phase funded by sources other than SBIR

### Eligibility & Requirements
- Small business applicants must be registered with SBA
- Projects must demonstrate:
  - High degree of technical risk (novel, unproven approach)
  - Potential for significant commercial impact and/or societal benefit
  - Good product-market fit (validated by customers)
  - Path to disrupt market or remove barriers to entry
- Applicants encouraged to contact National Program Leaders (NPLs) for each topic area before applying

## SBIR Phase I Topic Areas (13 Total)

### Topic Area 8.1: Forests and Related Resources
**NPL:** Dr. Charles Cleland (ccleland@nifa.usda.gov)

**Research Priorities:**
1. Growth and Yield - tree regeneration, pest/pathogen monitoring and control
2. Increasing Utility of Forest-Grown Material - lumber, pulp yield improvement
3. Reducing Ecological Damage by Forest Operations - erosion/water quality mitigation
4. Urban Forestry - greenways, parks, urban tree programs
5. Climate Change - ecosystem adaptation, carbon sequestration, decision support tools
6. Wildfire Management Technology - detection, fuel reduction, firefighter tools, communications
7. Sustainable Bioenergy and Value-Added Products - cellulosic biofuels (ethanol, biobutanol, jet fuel), wood composites, local energy conversion

**Key Guidance:** Biofuels from non-woody crops should go to Topic 8.8; genetic improvement of woody biomass feedstock goes here; applications strongly encouraged to contact NPL.

### Topic Area 8.2: Plant Production and Protection (Biology)
**NPL:** Dr. Robert Nowierski (rnowierski@nifa.usda.gov)

**Research Priorities:**
1. Plant Improvement - molecular biology, mutagenesis, genomics, tissue culture, embryogenesis to develop crops with improved quality, yield, agronomic traits
   - Sub-focus: Commercial floriculture production improvement
   - Sub-focus: Development of new crops
2. Pollinators and Crop Production - health and success of domesticated and natural pollinators
3. Plant Protection Against Abiotic/Biotic Stress
   - Sub-focus: Improved plant disease diagnostics (rapid, cost-effective identification)
   - Sub-focus: Bio-based approaches for pest/disease protection (organic and conventional crops)

**Key Guidance:** Transgenic crops should include regulatory/commercialization pathway; non-biological engineering technologies go to Topic 8.13; algae genetic improvement goes to Topic 8.7; woody biomass genetic improvement goes to Topic 8.1.

### Topic Area 8.3: Animal Production and Protection
**NPL:** Dr. Robert M. Smith (rsmith@nifa.usda.gov)

**Research Priorities:**
1. Improve Production Efficiency - fertility, feed efficiency, genomic applications
2. Improve Safety/Quality of End Products - pre-harvest technologies
3. Improve Animal Health and Well-being - rapid diagnostics, therapeutics, vaccines, biosecurity, traceability, alternatives to antibiotics
4. Improve Productivity in Modified/Alternative Production Systems - non-confinement, pasture-based, organic systems
5. Mitigate Impacts on Natural Environment - reduce greenhouse gas emissions, reduce phosphorus/nitrogen excretion (excluding manure management)

**Key Guidance:** Post-harvest technologies go to Topic 8.5; aquaculture species go to Topic 8.7.

### Topic Area 8.4: Conservation of Natural Resources
**NPL:** Dr. Karelyn Cruz (karelyn.cruz@nifa.usda.gov)

**Research Priorities:**
1. Water Quality and Quantity - farm/watershed-scale management, groundwater/surface water monitoring, nontraditional water sources (wastewater, agricultural return flows, produced water), improved irrigation technologies
2. Soil Health - physical/chemical/biological property measurement, microbial functional activity, soil remediation, erosion monitoring/prevention
3. Air Resources - monitor air quality, reduce agricultural air pollution (manure/livestock emissions)
4. Nutrient Management - macro/micronutrient practices to maximize productivity while minimizing environmental impact

**Key Guidance:** Animal manure/carcass waste for biofuels goes to Topic 8.8; food-borne hazard detection goes to Topic 8.5; should demonstrate innovation/non-redundancy with existing technologies; software/data-intensive applications encouraged.

### Topic Area 8.5: Food Science and Nutrition
**NPL:** Dr. Jodi Williams (jwilliams@nifa.usda.gov)

**Research Priorities:**
1. Food Safety - detection/mitigation of foodborne hazards (microorganisms, chemicals, toxins) during pre/post-harvest processing/distribution
2. Food Quality-Engineering - processing/packaging technologies/materials to reduce losses, maintain safety/quality, extend shelf life
3. Food Quality-Science - understand physical/biological/chemical interactions to develop food ingredients/formulations
4. Nutrition-Education - effective programs for educators/parents to foster healthy choices, combat childhood obesity
5. Nutrition-Science - improve functionality of foods, nutrients, bioactive components
6. Nutrition, Food Safety and Quality Data Tools - software tools to collect/analyze data for producers, consumers, retailers

**Key Guidance:** New detection tests should detect ≥1 cfu/25g without enrichment, allow large volume sampling; novelty screening methods need Federal agency letters of support; focus on functional foods/value-added products encouraged; avoid nutritional supplements (not food ingredients); include appropriate field expert collaborations.

### Topic Area 8.6: Rural and Community Development
**NPL:** Dr. Toija Riggins (toija.riggins@nifa.usda.gov)

**Research Priorities:**
1. Services, Information, and Managerial Systems - educational programs (virtual/apps/gaming), big data in planning, housing design, improved healthcare delivery (mobile/virtual), transportation/communication innovations, rural marketing
2. Technologies for Environmental Protection + Economic Development - renewable energy, big data for rural knowledge, food loss/waste reduction, energy conservation, wind/solar (not biofuels)
3. Reducing Rural Community Vulnerabilities - hazard mitigation (food-borne illness, contamination, drought, wildfire, hurricane, climate variability), preparation/forecast/warning/response/rebuilding
4. Technologies for Vulnerable Rural Populations - youth, elderly, disabled, veterans, low-income; human capital development, workforce development, food security/access
5. Employment and Income Generation - creative place-making, recreational economies, rural tourism, agri-tourism, e-commerce, off-farm value-added agriculture
6. Expanding Broadband Access - white space spectrum, increased bandwidth/speed/reach, adoption/diffusion initiatives, infrastructure investment

**Key Guidance:** Community letters of support highly encouraged; must assess environmental/socio-economic impacts; must provide evidence of rural market; identify commercialization barriers; on-farm production research goes to Topic 8.12; biofuel development goes to Topic 8.8.

### Topic Area 8.7: Aquaculture
**NPL:** Dr. Gene W. Kim (Gene.W.Kim@nifa.usda.gov)

**Research Priorities:**
1. Reproductive Efficiency - control of maturation/ovulation/fertilization, gamete/embryo storage, larval rearing, broodstock performance, cryopreservation, sex determination
2. Genetic Improvement - sex determination mechanisms, inheritance of commercial traits (growth, temperature tolerance, disease susceptibility), molecular biology/genomics integration, crossbreeding/hybridization
3. Integrated Aquatic Animal Health Management - stress management, genetic/environmental/nutritional components, predation control, vaccines/immunization, disease diagnostics, treatment methods
4. Improved Production Systems and Management Strategies - system design/models, water quality enhancement, effluent characterization/treatment, harvesting methods, recirculation efficiency
5. Algal Production Systems - species identification/improvement, bioreactor technology, biomass harvesting

**Key Guidance:** Aquaponics with current technology goes to Topic 8.12; new food products from aquaculture go to Topic 8.5; algae biomass conversion/products go to Topic 8.8.

### Topic Area 8.8: Biofuels and Biobased Products
**NPL:** Dr. Charles Cleland (ccleland@nifa.usda.gov)

**Research Priorities:**
1. New Non-food Biobased Products from New Industrial Crops - economically competitive, environmentally sustainable alternatives to petroleum products
2. New Processes for Industrial Products, Chemicals, or Biofuels - sustainable conversion of crops/agricultural residues into conventional/cellulosic/advanced biofuels or coproducts; minimize environmental impacts (energy, water, harmful byproducts, life cycle carbon)

**Key Guidance:** Forest biomass biofuels go to Topic 8.1; small farm bioenergy goes to Topic 8.12; genetic improvement of non-woody biomass feedstocks goes to Topic 8.2; woody biomass genetic improvement goes to Topic 8.1; engineering aspects of biomass handling go to Topic 8.13; algae genetic improvement goes to Topic 8.7; animal manure/carcass waste acceptable; microbial approaches must show credible path to industrially-relevant conversion rates/yields/titers.

### Topic Area 8.12: Small and Mid-Size Farms
**NPL:** Dr. Denis Ebodaghe (debodaghe@nifa.usda.gov)

**Research Priorities:**
1. New Agricultural Enterprises - small-scale specialty farm products (plant/animal), direct marketing (farmers markets, cooperatives), organic/natural foods, specialty products (free-range poultry, natural beef, medicinal herbs), value-added products
2. Development of New Marketing Strategies - assessment of consumer demand, product characteristics, packaging/processing innovations, efficient assembling/packing/advertising/shipping for local/regional/national/international markets
3. Farm Management - tools/skills for efficiency/profitability, farm safety, risk management tools, life-cycle assessment tools for resource efficiency and ecosystem services quantification, agro-tourism
4. On-Farm Natural Resources and Renewable Energy - energy efficiency, scaled technologies for wind/solar/geothermal energy
5. Urban Farming - rooftop/abandoned building/vacant lot farming; energy efficiency, environmental sustainability, profitability, appropriate crop selection

**Definition:** Small farms = <$250,000 annual sales; Mid-size farms = <$500,000 annual sales

**Key Guidance:** Conservation/renewable energy technologies go to Topic 8.4; aquaponics with technological improvements go to Topic 8.7; emphasize rural community benefits and dissemination to small farm community.
