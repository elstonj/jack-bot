## Executive Summary
This agreement allocates intellectual property rights between Black Swift Technologies and Old Dominion University Research Foundation for a Navy STTR Phase II project focused on "Expendable Air-sea Profiling Observations in Hazardous Weather Conditions" (N2-9533). The agreement establishes ownership, licensing, publication, and commercialization rights for background IP and project-generated IP, with ODU retaining educational/non-commercial research rights and BST receiving first option to commercialize innovations.

## Notable Details

### Intellectual Property Allocation Structure:

**Background IP:**
- BST Background IP: None declared
- ODU Background IP Available to BST: U.S. Patent 17/467,046 (Inventors: H. Park, M. Ono, K. Otsu, L. Folsom; Title: "Systems and Methods for Efficient Navigation of Robotic Devices") - may be used nonexclusively by BST at no compensation for this STTR project

**Project IP (Generated During STTR):**
- **Solely created by ODU/Old Dominion University:** Ownership remains with FOUNDATION
- **Solely created by BST:** Ownership remains with BST
- **Jointly created:** Joint ownership by BST and FOUNDATION/ODU
  - ODU assigns its interest to FOUNDATION prior to licensing
  - ODU retains irrevocable, perpetual, royalty-free rights for:
    - Publication of general scientific findings
    - Non-commercial research, teaching, and educational use
    - Ability to share with other nonprofit institutions for non-commercial/educational purposes

**Commercialization Rights:**
- **6-month exclusive option** for BST to negotiate licenses on jointly-created IP
- 60-day negotiation period for exclusive licenses
- BST must provide business plan, financial projections, performance milestones, and contingency plans for approval by FOUNDATION Executive Director or Board (depending on transaction type)
- Royalty-bearing licenses required; royalty rates to be negotiated
- FOUNDATION responsible for patent prosecution; BST reimburses patenting expenses during option period

**Government Rights:**
- Government (ONR) retains irrevocable, royalty-free, nonexclusive license for all Project IP for governmental purposes

### Key Operational Provisions:

**Confidentiality:**
- 3-year confidentiality period from last signature date
- 30-day publication review period; parties may request up to additional 60 days deferral for patent filing
- ODU subject to Virginia Freedom of Information Act

**Export Controls:**
- Both parties must comply with U.S. Export Administration Regulations and ITAR
- BST must notify FOUNDATION before transferring export-controlled information

**Data Access:**
- BST has full access to all data, information, and inventions created for internal R&D purposes

**Dispute Resolution:**
- Virginia state courts and U.S. District Courts in Virginia have exclusive jurisdiction
- Virginia law governs

**Termination:**
- Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice
- Confidentiality obligations survive termination for 3 years

### Strategic Implications:
- Patent 17/467,046 (robotic navigation systems) appears relevant to autonomous profiling vehicle operations in hazardous conditions
- BST positioned as lead commercialization entity with strong first-mover advantage on innovations
- ODU retains significant academic/research dissemination rights
- Structure allows BST to maintain competitive advantage while supporting academic research mission
