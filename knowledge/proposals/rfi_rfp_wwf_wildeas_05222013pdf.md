## Executive Summary
WWF issued this RFI/RFP seeking unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) systems and integrated technologies to combat wildlife poaching of rhinos, elephants, and tigers in Africa and Asia. The project, funded by a Google Global Impact Award (December 2012), aimed to create an integrated network of four technologies: affordable animal tracking systems (GSM-based), aerial survey systems (UAVs), ground ranger patrol tools (SMART software), and data correlation with wildlife trafficking intelligence. The RFI sought preliminary proposals from suppliers to be evaluated for possible field deployment and testing by October 2013.

## Technical Approach
WWF proposed a multi-component system designed to support field personnel (park rangers, law enforcement) in anti-poaching operations:

1. **Unmanned Aerial Systems:** Autonomous UAVs capable of day/night surveillance with minimal operator training (2-person teams)
2. **Animal Tracking Integration:** Systems capable of detecting and communicating with animal tags (RFID, GSM, or satellite-based)
3. **Ground Control Stations (GCS):** Mission planning, real-time video display, telemetry monitoring, and data recording
4. **Data Integration:** Seamless export of UAV flight data and target detections to SMART (Spatial Monitoring and Reporting Tool) conservation software
5. **Operational Autonomy:** Emphasis on automated takeoff, flight, and landing to reduce crew burden in field conditions

**Key Operational Requirements:**
- Minimum 1-hour flight time (ideally 4 hours)
- 5 km line-of-sight video downlink
- Autonomous flight with preprogrammed waypoints
- Deployment within 20 minutes by 3-person crew
- Turnaround time of <30 minutes between flights
- Operation in day/night conditions across diverse habitats (savanna, forest, tropical rainforest)
- 2-year operational lifespan with field-level O&I maintenance
- Cost-effective for conservation organizations

### UAV System Requirements (Expected Supplier Capabilities)
The RFI outlined detailed specifications for suppliers to address:

**Platform:**
- Maximum takeoff weight (MTOW): ≤10 kg in operational configuration
- Maximum indicated airspeed (IAS): ≥20 m/s
- Operating altitude: 0–10,000 feet ASL (terrain at 6,000 feet ASL)
- Modular structure enabling parts interchange
- Navigation lights (optional switchable)
- Visual/audio detectability: not detectable nearer than 700m at 1,000 feet AGL

**Payload (Sensors):**
- Electro-optical (EO) camera with continuous optical zoom
- Infrared (IR) camera with 640×480 pixel minimum resolution, black-hot/white-hot polarity, continuous or 2-field zoom
- Automatic and manual focus in both EO and IR
- Payload stabilization against UAV movement
- Requirements for vehicle/pedestrian/fire detection at specified ranges:
  - Vehicle identification: 300m slant range
  - Pedestrian identification: 100m slant range
  - Vehicle detection: 2,000m slant range
  - Pedestrian/animal detection: 1,000m slant range
  - Fire detection: 2,000m slant range

**Flight Capabilities:**
- Autonomous flight with automatic takeoff and landing
- Preprogrammed waypoint flying
- Multiple flight modes: Link Loss, Home, Navigate-to-Coordinate, Hold, Camera Guide (optional)
- Mission planning with designated navigation points
- Target accuracy: CEP 100m using EO/IR cameras at 1,000 feet AGL
- Emergency landing capability with safety lock

**Data Link:**
- Control range: minimum 10 km line-of-sight (15–30 km as optional)
- Live video transmission to GCS
- 0–2 km operation without manual antenna rotation
- Radio frequency compliance with end-user regulations
- Antenna weight: <10 kg including all equipment
- Optional: operation from moving GCS (vehicle/vessel)

**Ground Control Station (GCS):**
- Standard/picture maps with DTED/DTED2 terrain models
- Map scales: 1:25,000, 1:50,000, 1:100,000 with zoom in/out
- Display of UAV location, track, camera field of view (FOV)
- Real-time video display with optional annotations (reticle coordinates, date/time, north arrow)
- Video recording capability for ≥10 hours (MPEG preferred, ≥24 FPS)
- Mission playback with simultaneous flight route and video display
- Drawing/marking and distance measurement tools
- Coordinate system support: WGS84 datum, GEO format; optional UTM and other datum

**Environmental Operating Conditions:**
- Temperature: –5°C to +40°C (broader range preferred)
- Humidity: 0–90%
- Wind speed: 0–16 m/s (preferably more)
- Rainfall: ≤4 mm/hour (tropical rainfall solutions preferred)
- Dust/sand operation capability
- Protective coating on vulnerable materials

**Power & Batteries:**
- AC input: 230V nominal (110V adapters acceptable)
- DC input: 12–30V DC
- Optional DC car charger (cigarette lighter compatible, <8A fuse)
- All rechargeable batteries with safety documentation
- Second batch of batteries included for 2-year operation
- Stand-by or 3 flights/week (2-hour missions) operating profile

**System Package Requirements:**
- Minimum configuration: GCS, 2 UAVs with batteries, day/night cameras, wildlife tag detector (optional), training, maintenance package
- Shock-proof waterproof hard cases, <30 kg packed weight (preferably <20 kg)
- Two systems capable of simultaneous operation in same area
- Training camera (fixed or moving) included
- Language: English for operations and maintenance; other languages for manuals

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Mission: Anti-Poaching Operations**
- Detection and deterrence of poaching of rhinos, elephants, and tigers
- Routine surveillance of threat areas
- Boundary surveillance for illegal human/vehicle/fire activity
- Night operations for intruder detection, ambush intercept, and suspect tracking
- Response to poaching incidents with real-time suspect surveillance until arrest

**Geographic Deployment:**
- 4 preliminary sites planned: 2 in Asia, 2 in Africa
- Diverse habitat types: sparse shrub/woodlands, savannahs, temperate broadleaf/mixed forests, tropical dry forests, tropical rainforests
- Terrain altitudes up to 6,000 feet ASL

**Operational Tempo:**
- Activated 3–5 times per week depending on weather
- Capable of double daily missions
- Day and night operations

**Integration with Existing Systems:**
- Seamless data export to SMART (Spatial Monitoring and Reporting Tool) database
- Export of UAV flight trails and target detection lists to SMART
- Potential integration with wildlife tag detection (RFID, GSM, satellite)
- Correlation with TRAFFIC wildlife trade monitoring network

## Notable Details

**Project Context:**
- Google Global Impact Award funding (December 2012)
- Response to escalating wildlife crime: 30,000 elephants killed in Africa in 2012; 668 rhinos poached in South Africa in 2012 (vs. 13 in 2007); estimated 3,200 wild tigers remaining
- Emphasis on ranger safety: wildlife rangers increasingly facing well-resourced criminal elements with rising injury/death rates

**WWF's Operational Role:**
- WWF would not directly operate systems but provide support, software, consulting, guidance, training, and hardware to country governments
- End users: national park authorities and law enforcement in partner countries

**Evaluation Timeline:**
- RFI publication: May 23, 2013
- Question deadline: June 13, 2013
- Submission deadline: June 21, 2013
- Evaluation results: July 2013
- RFQ to qualified suppliers: July 2013
- Final supplier selection: August 2013
- Field deployment and training: October 2013

**Compliance & Controls:**
