# Navy STTR 25A Proposal Submission Instructions & Topic Guide

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** STTR solicitation guidance document with 28 topic descriptions
- **Client/Agency:** U.S. Navy Department of the Navy (DoN), across multiple Systems Commands (NAVAIR, NAVSEA, ONR)
- **Program/Solicitation:** Navy 25.A Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) BAA
- **Date:** December 2024 (created/modified)
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None
- **Key Personnel:** Multiple Navy SYSCOM Program Managers listed (Kristi DePriest, Jason Schroepfer, Steve Sullivan)

## Executive Summary

This is the Navy's 25.A STTR solicitation guidance document establishing submission instructions and requirements for Phase I proposals across 28 technical topics. The document outlines compliance requirements, evaluation criteria, cost limits ($140K base + $100K option for Phase I), work percentage requirements (40% small business, 30% research institution minimum), and post-award processes for Navy SBIR/STTR funded research and development.

## Technical Approach & Submission Requirements

### Phase I Requirements:
- **Duration:** Exactly 6 months base period + exactly 6 months option period
- **Technical Volume (Volume 2):** Maximum 10 pages, single column, single-spaced, 10-point minimum font, 1-inch margins
- **Cost Volume (Volume 3):** 
  - Phase I Base: Not to exceed $140,000
  - Phase I Option: Not to exceed $100,000
  - No cost sharing accepted
- **Work Distribution:**
  - Small business concern: minimum 40% (direct + indirect costs)
  - Research institution partner: minimum 30% (direct + indirect costs)
- **Evaluation Criteria (in order of importance):**
  1. Technical merit (most important)
  2. Key personnel qualifications (equal importance with below)
  3. Commercialization potential (equal importance with above)
  - Cost is NOT an evaluation criterion

### Phase II Guidelines:
- Evaluation uses same criteria as Phase I
- Typically awards Cost Plus Fixed Fee contracts
- Maximum award: $2,000,000 (unless adjusted by individual SYSCOMs)
- Structure can include Base + Options for increased funding based on transition potential
- Limited to one sequential Phase II award per small business concern per topic

### Required Submission Volumes:
1. **Volume 1:** Proposal Cover Sheet
2. **Volume 2:** Technical Proposal (10 pages max)
3. **Volume 3:** Cost Volume
4. **Volume 4:** Company Commercialization Report
5. **Volume 5:** Supporting Documents (administrative materials, cost details, certifications)
6. **Volume 6:** Fraud, Waste and Abuse Training Certification
7. **Volume 7:** Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries

### Key Submission Requirements:
- All proposals submitted via DoD SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP)
- Proposals NOT encrypted, password protected, or locked
- Must be certified electronically by Corporate Official before BAA Close
- Registration in SAM (System for Award Management) required before award
- NIST SP 800-171 assessment must be uploaded to Supplier Performance Risk System (SPRS)
- Minimum registration deadline: 60 days before BAA Close or verification of active existing registration

## Notable Details

### Funding & Award Information:
- **Payment Schedule (Phase I):** 
  - Day 15: 50% of Base or Option amount
  - Day 90: 35% of Base or Option amount
  - Day 180: 15% of Base or Option amount
- **Technical and Business Assistance (TABA):**
  - Phase I: Up to $6,500 (in addition to award amount)
  - Phase II: Up to $25,000 per award; up to $50,000 per project total
  - TABA cannot be used for G&A calculation
  - TABA providers cannot be the proposing small business, affiliates, investors, or required subcontractors
- **Contract Types:** 
  - Phase I: Firm Fixed Price (FFP) or small purchase agreement; may use Basic Ordering Agreements (BOAs)
  - Phase II: Cost Plus Fixed Fee typical; may use Other Transaction Agreements (OTAs) or BOAs

### Research Institution Requirements:
- Must be a single research institution (university or equivalent)
- Naval Academy, Naval Postgraduate School, and military academies qualify as research institutions
- DoN laboratories do NOT qualify as research partners (may be proposed IN ADDITION to research institution)

### Compliance & Security Requirements:
- **Due Diligence Program:** Foreign affiliation/relationship disclosures reviewed for security risk assessment (per SBIR and STTR Extension Act of 2022)
- **NIST SP 800-171:** All awardees must implement controls for Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) in non-federal systems
- **DFARS 252.204-7000:** Fundamental research disclosure required if proposing to eliminate prior approval requirement for public disclosure
- **ITAR Restrictions:** Some topics indicate ITAR or classification restrictions; must segregate basic research from export-controlled work
- **Human Subjects/Animal Testing/Recombinant DNA:** Not recommended for Phase I due to approval timelines (6-12 months for IRB approval can delay Phase I award); if required, must be approved within 2 months of selection notification or award decision may be terminated
- **GFE (Government Furnished Equipment):** Not recommended for Phase I due to lengthy approval processes

### Evaluation & Selection Process:
- **NOT FAR Part 15 evaluation** — proposals not compared to each other; evaluated on own merits
- **Compliance review** occurs before technical evaluation
- Non-compliant proposals rejected without technical review
- Cost is not evaluated; only compliance review of cost volume
- Limited funding means DoN reserves right to limit number of awards per topic
- Selection does not guarantee award; selected proposals sent to Contracting Officer for compliance review and eligibility confirmation

### Post-Award Processes:
- **Contract Deliverables:** Kick-off brief, progress reports, final report (uploaded to navysbirprogram.com/navydeliverables/)
- **Debriefs:** Requests must be made within 15 calendar days of selection notification; typically provided in writing within 60 days
- **Phase III:** Any work derived from/extending SBIR/STTR Phase I or II (funded by non-SBIR/STTR sources) receives SBIR/STTR Data Rights for noncommercial technical data and software developed under Phase I/II

### Navy SBIR Transition Program (STP):
- 1% of SBIR/STTR funding set-aside for Commercialization Readiness Program
- Phase II awardees not receiving TABA funds MUST participate in virtual Navy STP Kickoff during first or second year of Phase II
- STP provides non-financial resources and transition support
- STP participation mandatory for Phase II awardees (unless they received TABA, which makes them ineligible)
