# BAEDOC USGOVSERV - General Clauses for Subcontracts/Purchase Orders

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Standard Terms and Conditions / General Clauses Document
- **Client/Agency:** BAE Systems subcontractors and suppliers performing work under U.S. Government prime contracts
- **Program/Solicitation:** U.S. Government prime contracts (DoD, DoD Support agencies, and other federal agencies)
- **Date:** 2024 (Final Update)
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None
- **Key Personnel:** Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is BAE Systems' standard terms and conditions template for subcontracts and purchase orders issued to suppliers performing work on U.S. Government prime contracts. It establishes comprehensive contractual requirements covering administrative procedures, legal compliance, quality control, payment, intellectual property, security, and termination rights applicable to all BAE Systems subcontractors and vendors.

## Document Purpose & Scope
This document serves as BAE Systems' master contractual framework for managing supplier relationships on government-funded work. It is not specific to any particular product, program, or technical capability but rather establishes baseline legal and administrative requirements that flow down to all suppliers working under BAE Systems government contracts.

## Key Sections & Requirements

### Section A: General/Administrative Clauses
**Contract Administration:**
- Supplier must register in BAE Systems' HICX portal (https://baesystems.hicx.net)
- Only BAE Systems Procurement Representative has authority to issue Change Orders
- BAE Systems appoints Technical Representative; Supplier appoints Lead Supervisor
- Claims process: Suppliers have NO recourse directly with U.S. Government; must work through BAE Systems

**Payment Terms:**
- Net 45 days from latest of: accurate invoice receipt OR service acceptance
- Small business concerns may invoice/be paid every 2 weeks
- All prices include federal, state, local taxes (listed separately on invoice)
- Travel reimbursement: coach class airfare, no alcohol, per diem per Federal Travel Regulations
- BAE Systems right of setoff against any payments owed

**Cost-Type Contracts (Special Provisions):**
- Reimbursement for recorded costs actually paid
- Progress payments to subcontractors under similar cost standards
- Final indirect cost rates per FAR Subpart 31.2
- Billing rates pending final overhead rate establishment
- Quick closeout procedures available per FAR Subpart 42.7
- Audit rights for BAE Systems and U.S. Government at any time
- Interim billings with indirect rate adjustments required

### Section B: Laws and Regulations
**Compliance Requirements:**
- Full compliance with all applicable laws, orders, rules, regulations
- Priority rating compliance
- Gratuities/Kickbacks prohibition and ethical conduct requirements
- Export Control compliance
- Occupational Safety and Health compliance
- Foreign Corrupt Practices Act compliance

### Section C: BAE Systems Information Protection
**Critical Security & Confidentiality Requirements:**
- 5-year confidentiality period post-contract termination
- No transfer of BAE Systems Information to Cloud or external systems without written approval
- No storage outside U.S. without advance written approval
- No remote access to BAE Systems networks without authorization and business security screening
- 48-hour incident notification requirement for unauthorized disclosure, cyber-attacks, or breaches
- Supplier must implement reasonable written security program with administrative, technical, organizational, and physical safeguards
- Compliance with all U.S. Government laws regarding safeguarding government covered information
- Cyber Security Verification Form (CSV) completion required upon request
- Records of incidents must be maintained and made available to BAE Systems and Customers
- Supplier bears costs of remedial steps for incidents

### Section D: Personnel & Premises Access
**Background Screening & Access Control:**
- Non-U.S. citizens/non-permanent residents require written BAE Systems approval
- Unescorted facility access requires pre-employment background screening including:
  - I-9/E-Verify verification
  - Criminal conviction check (7-year minimum)
  - Education verification (degreed positions)
  - 3-year employment history verification
- Zero tolerance for harassing behavior
- BAE Systems/Customer can remove personnel from premises at sole discretion
- All personnel, property, vehicles subject to monitoring/search upon entry/exit

**On-Premises Conduct Requirements:**
- No weapons
- No controlled substances or alcohol
- No hazardous materials (except personal computing devices with lithium batteries)
- Remain in authorized areas only
- No solicitation of BAE Systems employees
- No unauthorized sales/marketing
- Cannot remove BAE Systems assets without written authorization
- Cannot share user credentials or access information
- BAE Systems may monitor all communications and data on its networks

### Section E: Records & Audit
**Record Retention:**
- Minimum 5 years from final payment (or longer if specified)
- Records include: financial, proposal, procurement, specifications, inspection, test, certifications, time records, expense reports, travel receipts, employee clearances
- BAE Systems and Customer have audit access during normal business hours with reasonable notice
- BAE Systems has audit rights on all performance reports except proprietary indirect cost data
- Defense Contract Audit Agency (DCAA) or Defense Contract Management Agency (DCMA) may conduct indirect cost audits

### Section F: Intellectual Property & Software
**IP Ownership & Rights:**
- BAE Systems Information remains BAE Systems property
- Supplier must comply with proprietary markings and restrictive legends
- Cannot remove proprietary markings
- Non-exclusive, non-transferable, royalty-free license to use BAE Systems Information only for contract performance
- Restrictions on Open Source and Restricted Software usage
- Supplier responsible for declaring and managing software compliance

### Section G: Termination Rights
**Termination for Convenience:**
- BAE Systems may terminate at any time after 30 days from effective date with written notice
- Immediate effect possible
- Procedures per FAR 52.249-4 (or 52.249-6 for cost-reimbursable work)

**Termination for Default:**
- Failure to perform within specified time
- Failure to perform material provisions with 8-day cure period after written notice
- Default on other BAE Systems contracts
- Bankruptcy or insolvency
- BAE Systems may acquire replacement services; Supplier liable for excess costs
- BAE Systems may require transfer of materials, parts, tools, plans, drawings, subcontract assignments

### Section H: Dispute Resolution
**Litigation & Jury Waiver:**
- Disputes must be filed in court of competent jurisdiction within the state from which contract was issued
- Both parties consent to personal jurisdiction and venue in that state
- **Both parties WAIVE RIGHTS TO JURY TRIAL** (to extent permitted by law)
- Patent actions may be initiated at U.S. Patent and Trademark Office or International Trade Commission
- Until dispute resolution, Supplier must diligently proceed with contract performance

### Section I: Additional Requirements
**Non-Solicitation:**
- During contract and 1 year after expiration/termination: Cannot recruit/solicit BAE Systems technical or professional employees supporting the contract
- Exception: Non-targeted recruiting efforts permitted
- BAE Systems entitled to injunctive relief for violations

**Subcontractors:**
- Supplier must bind subcontractors to substantially similar terms
- Supplier assumes liability for subcontractor acts/omissions
- Upon BAE Systems request, must provide: subcontractor names, supply locations, sourcing, supply categories, financial health, performance issues
- Cannot use cost-plus-percentage-of-cost subcontracts
- Subcontractor fee limitations per FAR 15.404-4(c)(4)(i)

**Business Continuity Planning:**
- Supplier must establish disaster recovery and business continuity plan (BCP)
- BCP must include vital records protection
- Must activate BCP upon necessity
- If unable to perform >10 consecutive days, BAE Systems may terminate for default without penalty

**Stop Work Orders:**
- Supplier must stop work upon written notice
- Must minimize cost incurrence during stoppage
- BAE Systems will then notify continuation or termination

**Assignment:**
- Any assignment of contract rights/delegation of duties void without BAE Systems written consent
- Change of control constitutes impermissible assignment
- Exception: May assign payment rights to financing institutions after furnishing signed assignment
- BAE Systems may freely assign contract

## Notable Details & Compliance Implications

**Governing Law:**
- State law of state from which contract issued (excluding choice of law rules)
- Exception: FAR/DFARS-incorporated provisions governed by U.S. federal common law of government contracts

**Order of Precedence (for conflicts):**
1. FAR/DFARS mandatory flow-down clauses
2. Statement of Work or Purchase Order
3. Master Service Agreement
4. Representations and Certifications
5. Supplemental Terms and Conditions
6. These General Provisions
7. Specifications or Drawings

**Language & Standards:**
- All communications, reports, documentation in English
- American Standard English interpretation controls
- U.S. standard weights and measures (NIST) required

**Severability:**
- Each provision severable; if one declared invalid, remainder remains in full force

**Survival Clauses:**
- Following provisions survive contract expiration/termination: Applicable Laws, Definitions, Disputes/Jury Waiver, Export Control, Property Rights, Independent Contractor Status, Indemnification, Liability Limitation, BAE Systems Information Protection, Intellectual Property, Premises Access, Records Maintenance, Non-Solicitation, Warranties, corresponding FAR/DFARS flow-down provisions

**Independent Contractor Status:**
- Supplier is independent contractor; no agency/partnership/joint venture relationship
- Supplier personnel are employees of Supplier, not BAE Systems
- Supplier responsible for all insurance, benefits, workers' compensation
- BAE Systems has no liability for Supplier personnel
- Former BAE Systems employees require approval for assignment

**Indemnification & Liability:**
- Document contains indemnification and limitation of liability provisions (detailed in Section D)
- Defective cost/pricing data liability
- Failure to perform liability
- Insurance requirements

**References to External Documents:**
- Supplemental Terms and Conditions at: https://www.baesystems.com/en-us/what-we-do/suppliers/united-states/us-terms-and-conditions
- Includes USGOVA (DoD) and USGOVB (Other Agencies) FAR/DFARS flow-down provisions
- Interim Terms and Conditions changes incorporated as they occur
