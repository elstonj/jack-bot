# BST Letter of Commitment - NASA SBIR Phase II (Virtual Redundancy for Safety Assurance)

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Commitment (subcontractor commitment letter)
- Client/Agency: NASA (via prime contractor Barron Associates, Inc.)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Phase II - "Virtual Redundancy for Safety Assurance in the Presence of Sensor Failures"
- Date: December 16, 2014
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Fixed-wing UAS (small unmanned aircraft systems)
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (President and CEO, Program Lead)
- DUNS Number: 078359543

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies committed to participate as a subcontractor on a NASA SBIR Phase II program led by Barron Associates, Inc. BST would integrate Barron's virtual sensor redundancy technology into BST's fixed-wing UAS platforms to improve safety assurance and enable integration into the national aerospace system.

## Technical Approach
- Integration of Barron Associates' virtual sensor redundancy control technology with BST's fixed-wing unmanned aircraft systems
- Leveraging BST's in-house designed, modular plug-and-play technologies for rapid system integration
- Focus on safety assurance through redundancy in the presence of sensor failures

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Black Swift Fixed-Wing UAS:**
- Small unmanned aircraft systems
- High-capability platforms
- Equipped with modular, plug-and-play in-house designed technologies
- Rapid integration capability to meet customer requirements

## Use Cases & Applications
- Integration into the national aerospace system
- Safety-critical applications requiring sensor redundancy and fault tolerance
- Enablement of advanced control technologies for UAS operations

## Notable Details
- Subcontract value: $40,000
- Prime contractor: Barron Associates, Inc. (Charlottesville, VA)
- BST characterized the partnership as "synergistic with our company goals"
- Emphasis on BST's rapid prototyping and integration capabilities
- Positioned as advancement in aerospace control technology
