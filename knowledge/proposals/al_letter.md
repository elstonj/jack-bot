# AI Letter: Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator Program Support

- Client/Agency: Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator Program Review Committee

- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Soil moisture mapping sUAS
- Key Personnel: Maciej Stachura (BST); author is Director of Center for Environmental Technologies at CU Boulder, Professor of Electrical and Computer Engineering

## Executive Summary
A CU Boulder faculty member and researcher who has collaborated with Black Swift Technologies since June 2013 provides a letter of support for BST's application to the Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator Program. The letter emphasizes BST's technical capabilities and commercialization potential in developing a miniaturized soil moisture mapping sensor for small unmanned aircraft systems.

## Technical Approach
- Miniaturization of soil moisture sensing technology from manned aircraft platforms to small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS)
- Sensor development conducted in collaboration between CU Boulder researchers and BST team
- Technology builds on prior work: author led a 2007 NASA flight experiment using a larger soil moisture sensor on a manned aircraft in northern Texas to collect data ahead of storms
- Current effort focuses on adapting this proven concept to smaller, more deployable platforms

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS**
- A small unmanned aircraft system equipped with a miniaturized soil moisture sensor
- Enables deployment from small platforms for rapid, localized soil moisture data collection
- Can provide real-time or near-real-time information for applications requiring soil moisture data

## Use Cases & Applications
- Flash flood prediction and early warning
- Scientific soil moisture research
- Safety applications
- Commercial applications (specific markets not detailed)
- Storm monitoring and prediction

## Notable Details
- Strong academic partnership: collaboration between CU Boulder's Center for Environmental Technologies and BST
- Demonstrated interest from "early adopters in the soil moisture community" including NASA scientists
- Author's extensive background: 8 years with NOAA's Earth System Research Laboratory (Chief of Microwave Systems Development Branch), faculty at Georgia Tech and MIT, currently at CU Boulder
- Primary challenge identified: producing flight results and communicating results to user community
- Accelerator program support seen as critical to commercialization timeline and market introduction
