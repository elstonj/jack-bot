## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies successfully developed a commercially viable, ruggedized unmanned aircraft system capable of routine flights into volcanic plumes to measure trace gases, particulates, atmospheric parameters, and imagery. The Phase II effort resulted in two complete S2 XT airframes optimized for harsh environments, demonstrated in Hawaii at Fissure 8 with comprehensive data collection across multiple sensor types simultaneously.

## Technical Approach
- Modified and ruggedized S2 UAS platform for volcanic environment operations
- Designed sub-2.5 kg integrated payload for simultaneous measurement of multiple atmospheric parameters
- Implemented dust and particulate exclusion measures for volcanic conditions
- Expanded operational altitude capability to 20,000 ft MSL
- Integrated atmospheric sampling cassettes for lab analysis
- Modified UI for simplified mission planning and sampling operations
- Developed integrated sensor package combining in-situ and remote sensing data collection

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 XT UAS
- **What it is**: Ruggedized unmanned aircraft system optimized for harsh environment operations
- **Use in this project**: Primary platform for volcanic plume sampling and atmospheric data gathering
- **Specifications/Claims**:
  - Sub-2.5 kg integrated payload capacity for multi-parameter measurement
  - Operational ceiling: 20,000 ft MSL
  - Payload capability: trace gas sensors, particulate sizing instruments, pressure/temperature/humidity sensors, 3D wind measurement, electro-optical (EO) camera, thermal imaging, forward video, atmospheric sampling cassettes
  - Can simultaneously collect remote sensing imagery and in-situ measurements

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary (Demonstrated)
- **Volcanic plume monitoring**: Fissure 8, Hawaii
  - Trace gas concentration measurement
  - Particulate size and distribution analysis
  - 3D wind field mapping
  - Atmospheric thermodynamic measurements (pressure, temperature, humidity)
  - RGB orthomosaic generation
  - Thermal mapping
  - Atmospheric sampling for lab analysis
  - 3D fissure and lava flow modeling

### Secondary/Planned
- USGS Makushin volcano campaign (Aleutian Islands)
- Beyond-Visual-Line-of-Sight (BVLOS) volcano operations
- Wildland fire monitoring (NOAA operations noted as actively using improvements)
- IAVCEI (International Association of Volcanology and Chemistry of the Earth's Interior) calibration campaign participation

## Key Accomplishments & Results

### Deliverables
- Two complete S2 XT airframes designed, tested, and assembled
- Integrated sub-2.5 kg payload for volcanic environment operation
- Modified UI for mission creation and sampling
- Dust/particulate ingestion protection measures
- Altitude expansion to 20,000 ft MSL

### Demonstrated Capabilities
- Successfully conducted comprehensive flights at Fissure 8 location
- Collected background-level trace gas baseline data
- Generated detailed fissure characterization including:
  - Trace gas concentrations
  - Particulate size/distribution
  - 3D wind vectors
  - Thermodynamic atmospheric profiles
  - RGB orthomosaic and thermal maps
  - Lab analysis samples
  - 3D fissure/flow model

### Key Finding
Unique combination of simultaneous in-situ and remote sensing data collection provided insights not previously possible through individual measurement approaches.
