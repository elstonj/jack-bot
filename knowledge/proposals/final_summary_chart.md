## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies conducted a Phase I effort to demonstrate how UAS observations can improve wind and turbulence forecasts around wildland fires to enhance firefighting safety. The work focused on optimizing and quantifying the impact of targeted UAS measurements on meteorological predictions, establishing operational access protocols with fire agencies, and identifying valuable data products for integration into Team Awareness Kit (TAK).

## Technical Approach
- Conducted experiments comparing UAS-enhanced meteorological simulations against non-UAS baseline simulations
- Leveraged NCAR's ESA (Ensemble Sensitivity Analysis) methods to identify optimal sites for gap-filling observations
- Developed software tools to convert raw UAS meteorological data into WMO standard data and PREPBUFR formats compatible with modeling centers
- Performed continuous-flying and multi-UAS atmospheric data collection flights to verify data formatting processes
- Implemented UAS Data Assimilation methodology into meteorological prediction models

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 VTOL UAS
- **What it is:** A vertical takeoff and landing unmanned aerial system
- **Modifications:** Prototype underwent significant hardware revisions optimized for mountainous terrain and wildland fire conditions
- **Proposed use:** Collect targeted atmospheric measurements in fire zones to improve wind and turbulence forecasts; designed as small-size, severe-weather capable platform
- **Capability claim:** Demonstrated significant improvement in meteorological predictions when utilizing data from S0 observations

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Wildland firefighting support:** Enhanced wind and turbulence forecasts to improve operational safety for manned firefighting aircraft and ground crews
- **Data gap-filling:** Targeted observations in areas poorly represented by existing meteorological networks
- **Severe-weather operations:** Mountain terrain and wildfire environment operations

## Key Results
1. Demonstrated capability of UAS Data Assimilation to significantly improve meteorological predictions
2. Established that NCAR's ESA methods can determine optimal observation sites using current forecasts
3. Successfully conducted continuous-flying and multi-UAS flights collecting atmospheric data
4. Verified data formatting processes and conversion protocols to WMO/PREPBUFR standards
5. Established operational pathways and risk-mitigation protocols with fire agencies for conducting UAS operations alongside manned firefighting efforts
