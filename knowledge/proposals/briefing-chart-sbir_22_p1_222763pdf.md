## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes the S3 UAS, a next-generation uncrewed aircraft evolved from the S2-VTOL platform, designed for all-weather operations and long-duration unattended environmental monitoring missions lasting days to months. The innovation centers on severe weather capability (high-speed performance, icing/precipitation resistance) and autonomous persistent operations (onboard solar charging enabling multi-day missions without return to base).

## Technical Approach

### Extreme Weather Redesign
- Complete redesign of S2-VTOL airframe for severe weather operations
- New aerodynamic design optimized for high dash speeds to overcome strong winds
- Improved climb performance for strong downdrafts
- Structural design and control geometry for safe operation in strong turbulence
- Heavy precipitation and icing-resistant modifications

### Systems Modifications
- **Pitot/Static System:** New design rated for heavy precipitation and icing environments
- **Waterproofing:** Extended waterproofing of aircraft with minimal mass increase
- **Onboard Intelligence Monitoring:** Multiple cameras for:
  - Environmental and terrain monitoring
  - Wing icing detection
  - Propulsion system icing monitoring

### Long-Duration Capability
- Autonomous landing system
- Onboard battery recharge via integrated solar panels in wings
- Persistent operations model: aircraft remains deployed and cycles through multiple flights over extended periods rather than returning to base after each sortie

### Phase I Work Plan
- Complete new wing and tail aerodynamic design/analysis
- Structural CAD modeling of redesigned components
- System design modifications for precipitation/icing environments
- Hardware/sensor selection for onboard intelligence monitoring system
- Requirements evaluation for icing detection systems

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S3 UAS
- **Description:** Next-generation small uncrewed aircraft evolved from S2-VTOL platform
- **Key Innovation:** Combination of extreme weather resilience and autonomous persistent operations
- **Proposed Capabilities:**
  - All-weather operation (severe turbulence, heavy precipitation, icing)
  - High dash speeds for wind penetration
  - Strong downdraft climb performance
  - Autonomous recharge via solar power
  - Multi-day to multi-month mission endurance without operator presence
  - Onboard environmental monitoring and health monitoring cameras

### Referenced Legacy Systems
- **S2:** Earlier generation UAS with proven deployment record
- **S2-VTOL:** Vertical takeoff/landing variant; basis for S3 redesign; currently used for volcano monitoring and satellite calibration

## Use Cases & Applications

### NASA Applications
1. **Volcano Monitoring:** Expansion of existing S2/S2-VTOL missions with enhanced all-weather capability and multi-week deployment potential
2. **Satellite Calibration:** MALIBU project; S3 enables larger area coverage and demonstration of weeks-to-months persistent mission concepts

### Non-NASA Applications
1. **Pipeline Methane Leak Detection:** 
   - Inspection of hundreds of miles of pipeline per day
   - Autonomous operation eliminates per-flight human operator costs
   - Significant greenhouse gas monitoring capability
   - Partnership established with sensor company for delivery

## Notable Details

- **Competitive Advantage:** S3 represents a "revolutionary change in capabilities of small UAS for Earth observing" by combining extreme weather resilience with persistent autonomous operations—a capability gap in current small UAS market
- **Leveraging Heritage:** Proposal builds on BST's proven S2/S2-VTOL deployment record across difficult missions
- **Funding TRL Statement:** "TRL Estimated" header present but TRL value not populated in this briefing chart
- **Commercial Partnership:** BST already partnered with sensor company for methane detection payload integration
- **Operational Model Shift:** The persistent operations concept (autonomous recharge, multi-day deployment, no return-to-base requirement) represents a fundamental change in how Earth observation UAS missions are conducted

## Technical Risks/Challenges Implicit in Proposal
- Icing protection and detection in heavy precipitation environments
- Structural integrity for sustained turbulence operations
- Solar panel integration weight/performance balance
- Autonomous landing reliability in extreme conditions
- Multi-week power management and system reliability
