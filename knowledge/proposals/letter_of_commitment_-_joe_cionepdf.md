## Executive Summary
NOAA commits $500,987.14 in matching funds to support Black Swift Technologies' NASA SBIR Phase II project developing a ruggedized small unmanned aircraft system (S0) for scientific data gathering in harsh environments, specifically tropical cyclones and severe storms. The funding supports payload integration, hurricane performance enhancement, full mission automation, and real-time data capabilities for NOAA meteorological and oceanographic research.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed to develop and deliver a multi-hour duration small UAS (S0) capable of:
- Safe air-deployment from NOAA WP-3D aircraft
- Continuous meteorological and oceanographic data collection in tropical cyclone boundary layers
- Extended command and control range up to 150 nautical miles from launch platform
- Operation in severely turbulent, severe storm environments as expendable/unrecoverable technology
- Integration of laser altimeter and Multi-Hole Turbulence Probe (MHTP) payloads
- Communication and data management ensuring continuous data collection with robust command/control
- Full mission automation based on scientific measurement goals
- Real-time data delivery meeting NOAA KARMA and WMO HDOBS requirements

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 (Small UAS)
- **What it is:** Ruggedized small unmanned aircraft system designed for harsh environment operations
- **Proposed use:** Deployment from NOAA WP-3D aircraft for in-storm tropical cyclone data collection
- **Specifications/claims:**
  - Multi-hour endurance
  - 150 nm command and control range
  - Capable of operating in severe turbulence and tropical cyclone environments
  - Designed as expendable/unrecoverable technology
  - Payload integration with laser altimeter and MHTP

### S0 VTOL Variants
- **What it is:** Vertical takeoff and landing variants of the S0
- **Proposed use:** Ground-based meteorological observations from surface stations
- **Deliverable:** 2 units by September 26, 2022

### Multi-Hole Turbulence Probe (MHTP)
- **What it is:** Sensor payload for turbulence and atmospheric measurements (developed in collaboration with Area-I)
- **Proposed use:** Integration on S0 and Area-I Altius 600 platform
- **Development focus:** Enhanced design to better handle heavy precipitation in hurricane environments
- **Deliverables:** Multiple units (8 MHTPs by June 30, 2023)

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary use case:** Tropical cyclone (hurricane) research and operations
- **Specific applications:**
  - In-storm meteorological and oceanographic data collection within TC boundary layer
  - Turbulence measurements in severe storm environments
  - Real-time data support for NOAA operational requirements
  - Replacement of manned aircraft risk in extreme weather

## Notable Details
- **Funding commitment:** $500,987.14 in unencumbered matching funds, transferable within 45 business days of CCRPP selection
- **Strategic significance:** NOAA identified this technology as potentially becoming a "staple for data collection" in difficult hurricane environments
- **Operational goal:** Achieve routine NOAA capability for TC boundary layer data collection using sUAS
- **Technology maturation:** Project aims to advance sUAS technology to higher technical maturity level for operational deployment
- **Partnerships:** Coordination with NOAA Aircraft Operational Center, Ground Payload Center (GPC), and User Payload Center (UPC); collaboration with Area-I on MHTP integration for Altius 600
- **Key research goals:** Measurable increases in sUAS duration, communication robustness (150 nm continuous range), and enhanced payload data reliability
- **Data standards:** Real-time data delivery meeting NOAA KARMA (Kinetic Analysis and Real-time Monitoring Advanced system) and WMO HDOBS (World Meteorological Organization Hurricane Data and Observation Specifications) requirements
