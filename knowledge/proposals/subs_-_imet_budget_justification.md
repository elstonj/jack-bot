## Executive Summary
This document justifies iMet's subcontractor costs (~$26,510) for a NOAA SBIR Phase I project involving sensor integration, calibration methodology development, and transmitter design for atmospheric measurement instrumentation to be deployed on BST platforms.

## Technical Approach
iMet's scope involves:
1. **Sensor Suite Integration** - Integration of sensor suite with BST's multi-hole probe (8 hours conceptualization)
2. **Calibration Development** - Development of calibration methodologies and procedures for all sensors excluding the multi-hole probe:
   - Conceptualization with drawings (40 hours)
   - Calibration procedure writing (40 hours)
   - Test fixture design (80 hours)
3. **Transmitter Design** - Design of transmit interface including conceptualization/research, TX specifications development, and transmitter design/support (60 hours total)

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **BST Multi-hole Probe**: Atmospheric measurement probe requiring sensor suite integration and calibration
- **Air-Deployed Sonde**: Referenced for deployment recommendations (no-charge consulting)

## Use Cases & Applications
- Atmospheric profiling / meteorological data collection via air-deployed platforms
- Integration of multi-sensor suite for comprehensive environmental measurements
