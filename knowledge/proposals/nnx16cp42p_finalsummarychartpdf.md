## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed design and development of the SuperSwift XT small Unmanned Aircraft System (sUAS) with integrated scientific sensor suite for harsh environment operations. The project aimed to enhance NASA's airborne science capabilities by enabling durable, easy-to-operate collection of in situ atmospheric data from volcanic plumes and other hazardous environments. Phase I focused on concept of operations definition, airframe ruggedization design, sensor integration planning, and flight permission preparation.

## Technical Approach

### Core Development Objectives
1. **Definition of Concept of Operations (CONOPS)**: Established mission profiles and sensing requirements for volcanic plume observation
2. **Airframe Ruggedization**: Modified SuperSwift design to withstand harsh environmental conditions including:
   - Motor protection
   - Airframe sealing
   - Sensor redundancy
   - Aerodynamic modifications for high-altitude, high-turbulence flight
3. **Sensor Integration**: Modified SuperSwift and SwiftCore FMS to support multiple scientific sensors
4. **Flight Testing Preparation**: Assembled flight permission documentation for three test sites

### Key Technical Innovations
- **Multi-hole probe design**: New 3D wind measurement sensor specifically engineered for sUAS atmospheric science applications
  - Prototype constructed with integrated firmware
  - Validated through wind tunnel testing
- **Sensor mechanical, electrical, and data interfaces**: Custom-designed to accommodate trace gas, particulate, pressure, temperature, humidity, and wind sensors

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift XT
- **Description**: Ruggedized small UAS platform purpose-built for harsh environment scientific data collection
- **Proposed Use**: Volcanic plume observation and in situ atmospheric sampling in extreme conditions
- **Key Modifications**:
  - Motor protection systems
  - Environmental sealing
  - Redundant sensor architecture
  - High-altitude, high-turbulence flight capability
  - Multi-sensor payload integration

### SwiftCore FMS
- **Description**: Flight Management System (BST's core flight control software)
- **Proposed Use**: Modified to support multiple scientific sensors and manage payload data integration
- **Capability**: Enables integration of complex sensor suites while maintaining flight stability and safety

### Scientific Sensor Suite
- Trace gas measurement
- Particulate detection
- Pressure sensing
- Temperature measurement
- Humidity measurement
- Wind measurement (including new 3D wind probe)

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Mission (Phase I Focus)
- **Volcanic Plume Observation**: Multi-year observation campaigns of volcanic emissions at high altitude
- **Partnership Reference**: Jet Propulsion Laboratory's CARTA-UAS project and partners already conducting volcano observation

### Secondary Applications Identified
- **Wildfire smoke characterization**: Assessment of atmospheric aerosol structure and composition
- **Smog and coherent atmospheric structure monitoring**: Urban and regional air quality research
- **Smokestack exhaust analysis**: Industrial compliance monitoring for EPA-permitted pollutant levels
- **Chemical fire assessment**: Hazard composition and danger evaluation at refineries and chemical facilities
- **Forest humidity monitoring**: Environmental and fire prevention applications

## Key Milestones Achieved (Phase I)

1. Identified flight pattern set for volcano observation from UAS
2. Selected scientific payloads and designed mechanical, electrical, and data interfaces
3. Defined airframe design changes required for high-altitude volcanic operations
4. Constructed and wind-tunnel tested multi-hole probe for 3D wind measurement in sUAS
5. Generated flight permission documentation for three test sites (ready for submission upon contract award)
