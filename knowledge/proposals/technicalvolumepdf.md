## Executive Summary
This proposal seeks DOE funding to develop, validate, and deploy an integrated methane detection and monitoring system specifically designed for orphaned and undocumented orphan wells (UOWs). The system combines advanced drone-based episodic monitoring (Black Swift S2 UAS with Quanta3 methane sensor) with continuous ground-based eddy-covariance tower systems enhanced by FluxMapper™ technology, plus real-time remote connectivity solutions. The project aims to advance methane detection sensitivity from the current 2 kg/h baseline to 0.2 kg/h while increasing Technology Readiness Level (TRL) from TRL-4/5 to TRL-6 over three years.

## Technical Approach

### System Architecture
The proposed system integrates three primary monitoring components:

1. **Drone-Based Episodic Monitoring**: Black Swift S2 UAS equipped with Quanta3 methane spectrometer, operating beyond visual line of sight (BVLOS) with autonomous flight path adjustment based on real-time methane and wind data.

2. **Continuous Ground-Based Monitoring**: LI-COR eddy-covariance (EC) flux towers enhanced with FluxMapper™ software for high-resolution spatial methane mapping and continuous flux measurement.

3. **Remote Data Connectivity**: HOO-nah device from Hyde Park West Telecommunications for real-time data transmission from remote well sites.

### Key Technical Features
- **Sensitivity Enhancement**: Target detection capability of 0.2 kg/h (improvement from 2 kg/h baseline)
- **Spatial Resolution**: Decameter-resolution FluxMaps™ with automated hotspot identification
- **Autonomous Operation**: S2 UAS adjusts flight patterns in real-time based on methane concentration gradients and wind fields
- **Multi-Scale Coverage**: S2 UAS capable of monitoring up to 50 sites in a single day; EC towers provide continuous benchmark data
- **3D Wind Field Measurement**: UAS simultaneously measures three-dimensional wind profiles enabling in-flight eddy covariance analysis
- **Data Integration**: Raw high-frequency methane and wind data converted to actionable FluxMaps™ through proprietary algorithms

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift S2 UAS
- **Platform**: Fixed-wing, unmanned aircraft system with 3-meter wingspan
- **Range**: 100 km operational range
- **Operational Mode**: Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) capable with FAA-certified operators
- **Primary Sensor**: Quanta3 methane spectrometer
- **Sensitivity**: Detects methane concentrations down to 3 parts per billion (ppb)
- **Unique Capability**: Autonomous flight path adjustment based on live methane concentration readings and wind measurements
- **Scale**: Can conduct episodic surveys of up to 50 sites per deployment day
- **Proposed Use**: High-resolution spatial mapping of methane emissions from dispersed orphan well sites; provides three-dimensional plume characterization
- **Performance Claims**: Substantially improved sensitivity compared to traditional optical gas imaging (OGI) cameras or manned aircraft with LIDAR; dramatically more cost-effective than ground crew-based inspections

### Quanta3 Methane Spectrometer
- **Detection Capability**: 3 ppb sensitivity threshold
- **Integration**: Custom-built sensor integrated onto S2 UAS platform
- **Data Output**: Real-time methane concentration measurements
- **Autonomous Response**: Enables UAS flight path optimization during mission execution

### FluxMapper™ Software (AtmoFact)
- **Function**: Advanced data analysis platform for eddy covariance measurements
- **Output**: High-resolution spatial FluxMaps™ identifying methane emission hotspots
- **Resolution**: Decameter-scale spatial resolution
- **Sensitivity**: Achieves 0.2 kg/h detection threshold
- **Temporal Capability**: Processes high-frequency methane and wind data into actionable insights
- **Integration**: Works with LI-COR flux tower systems to enhance emissions quantification

### LI-COR Flux Tower Systems
- **Function**: Continuous ground-based methane flux measurement using eddy covariance methodology
- **Data Type**: High-frequency methane concentration and wind velocity measurements
- **Role**: Serves as validation benchmark for UAS-based measurements and provides long-term continuous monitoring
- **Integration**: Enhanced with FluxMapper™ software for spatial precision

### HOO-nah Device (Hyde Park West Telecommunications)
- **Function**: Remote connectivity solution for data transmission from isolated well sites
- **Capability**: Enables real-time data transfer and continuous monitoring
- **Target Reliability**: 95% data transmission reliability in remote, low-connectivity areas
- **Application**: Overcomes connectivity barriers at dispersed orphan well sites

### Xplorobot Laser Optical Gas Imaging (OGI) System
- **Function**: Handheld sensor platform with advanced computer vision for methane detection
- **Capability**: Precise methane emission data capture with visualization
- **Integration**: Cloud-based software for autonomous data processing and analysis
- **Role**: Complements UAS and tower-based monitoring; provides supplemental episodic measurements

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Application: Orphaned Well Monitoring
- **Target Sites**: Over 50 publicly accessible orphan wells in Colorado's San Luis Valley and Pawnee National Grasslands (northeastern Colorado); discussions ongoing for access to lands managed by Osage, Sioux, Crow, and Navajo nations
- **Mission Type**: Episodic methane detection surveys and continuous flux monitoring at dispersed well pads
- **Specific Challenge Addressed**: Monitoring low-emission, difficult-to-access well sites with improved sensitivity and reduced cost per inspection

### Operational Scenarios
1. **Dispersed Well Surveys**: S2 UAS deployed to rapidly assess methane emissions across multiple widely-separated orphan well sites in remote locations
2. **Site Characterization**: Combined UAS episodic flights with ground-based EC tower deployments to establish baseline emissions and identify hotspots
3. **Continuous Monitoring**: EC towers with FluxMapper™ provide sustained emissions tracking at high-priority sites for trend analysis
4. **Real-Time Response**: HOO-nah connectivity enables immediate data access and supports rapid mitigation decision-making
5. **Validation Studies**: Tower-based measurements serve as gold-standard benchmarks for validating UAS system accuracy

### Broader Potential Applications
- Oil and gas field methane monitoring (active and abandoned)
- Landfill emissions characterization
- Agricultural methane source identification
- Wetland and permafrost methane flux studies

## Key Results (Project is Proposal, Not Yet Executed)

This is a forward-looking proposal with no completed results. However, the proposal cites prior research validating core components:

### Prior Research Cited
- **Elston et al. (2017)**: Demonstrated effectiveness of fixed-wing UAS with eddy covariance sensors for large-scale methane detection across variable landscapes; validated flight endurance and range for extensive monitoring areas
- **Baldocchi (2014), Nemitz et al. (2018)**: Established eddy covariance tower methodology as "gold standard" for long-term greenhouse gas flux measurement with high temporal resolution
- **Metzger et al. (2013, 2021)**: Demonstrated UAS-based eddy covariance capabilities and advanced data processing methods for enhanced methane leak detection
- **Peltola et al. (2014)**: Validated solar-battery power systems for continuous field monitoring without conventional power grid
- **Kohnert et al. (2017), Rey-Sanchez et al. (2022), Xu et al. (2020)**: Published peer-reviewed validation of high-resolution spatial flux mapping and data mining approaches

### Go/No-Go Decision Criteria (Planned)

**Year 1 Milestones:**
- UAS system detects methane within 3 ppb sensitivity; data alignment within <10% variance between UAS and ground sensors
- FluxMapper™ produces spatial maps with clear emission hotspot identification
- HOO-nah device achieves 95% data transmission reliability with low latency
- Documentation of interest from ≥3 well operators and successful community engagement

**Year 2 Milestones:**
- Consistent methane detection across varying conditions with <15% variance threshold
- FluxMapper™ optimization enables <10% variance across repeated tests with high-resolution hotspot identification
- HOO-nah 95% reliability verified across multiple remote sites
- Preliminary Techno-Economic Analysis (TEA) shows positive Net Present Value (NPV)

**Year 3 (Final):**
- System achieves TRL-6 ready for broader deployment
- Comprehensive Life Cycle Analysis (LCA) demonstrates net reduction in carbon footprint
- Complete techno-economic study for scaling to nationwide orphan well monitoring
