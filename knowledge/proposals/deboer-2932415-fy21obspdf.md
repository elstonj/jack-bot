# NOAA FY2021 Weather Program Office Funding Decision: Uncrewed Aircraft Observations for Complex Terrain

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Funding decision letter with merit review comments
- **Client/Agency:** NOAA Office of Atmospheric Research (OAR), Weather Program Office (WPO)
- **Program/Solicitation:** NOAA-OAR-WPO-2021-2006592 (FY2021 Observations Program)
- **Date:** May 14, 2021 (decision); reviews dated 05/12/2021
- **Proposal Number:** 2932415
- **Applicant Institution:** The Regents of the University of Colorado
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** S2 (L-band radiometer for soil moisture measurement)
- **Key Personnel:** de Boer, White (Principal Investigators); Borensteinn (mentioned in budget context)

## Executive Summary
This document contains NOAA's recommendation for funding a University of Colorado proposal to deploy uncrewed aircraft systems to characterize land surface and lower atmosphere interactions in complex terrain for improved water and weather prediction. The proposal was recommended for funding with an overall score of 84.3/100. The project integrates the Black Swift Technologies S2 platform (equipped with an L-band radiometer) with other UAS platforms and ground instrumentation during the SPLASH and SAIL field campaigns in the Crested Butte area.

## Technical Approach
**Primary Methodology:**
- Deploy uncrewed aircraft systems during intensive field campaigns (SPLASH and SAIL) to collect coordinated observations of soil, land surface, and boundary layer processes over complex mountainous terrain
- Integrate data from multiple observation platforms to characterize land-atmosphere interactions and data gaps in boundary layer observations
- Assimilate collected UAS data into numerical weather prediction (NWP) models to evaluate boundary layer parameterizations and sensitivities
- Leverage co-located observations from diverse sensors to develop physical understanding supporting advanced prediction of weather and water

**Data Collection Timeline:** Fall 2021 through spring 2023 (multi-year field campaign period)

**Key Technical Challenges Identified:**
- Project depends on securing parallel funding for two University of Colorado UAS platforms (RAAVEN and HELiX) beyond the WPO proposal scope
- Many proposed sensors and non-UAS instruments are contingent on "additional funds" from sources outside the current WPO proposal
- Risk that data collection gaps would materialize if parallel funding does not materialize
- Unclear success metrics for evaluating whether the project achieves its goal of developing a capability for operational transition within 2-5 years of completion

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift Technologies S2
- **System:** L-band radiometer platform
- **Proposed Use:** Mapping soil moisture measurements across the study domain
- **Integration:** Proposed for integration with uncrewed aircraft operations alongside CU's RAAVEN and HELiX platforms
- **Operational Status:** Noted as "familiar to NOAA through activities conducted by other laboratories and is being considered for operational transition for boundary layer observation"
- **Funding Status in WPO Proposal:** S2 is THE ONLY UAS platform included in the WPO line of funding; it is the sole BST system being directly funded by this award

### Supporting UAS Platforms (not BST, but part of integrated system)
- **RAAVEN (CU):** Proposed for thermodynamic, kinematic, and turbulence measurements; contingent on additional funding
- **HELiX (CU):** Proposed for up/down-welling radiation and surface albedo mapping; contingent on additional funding

### Integrated Sensor Suite
- Soil moisture observations (S2 L-band radiometer)
- Boundary layer thermodynamic measurements
- Kinematic and turbulence data
- Radiation measurements (upwelling/downwelling)
- Ground comparison soil sensors (included in budget)
- Surface albedo mapping

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Study Domain:**
- Crested Butte, Colorado area with complex mountainous terrain
- SPLASH (Submesoscale Processes in Landslides And Slope Hillslopes) and SAIL (Soil-moisture, Atmosphere, and Land surface-atmosphere Interactions) field campaigns

**Operational Applications:**
- **Short-term (2021-2023):** Support day-to-day operations and forecasting activities of local NOAA field offices:
  - Colorado Basin River Forecast Center (CBRFC)
  - Grand Junction Weather Forecast Office (GJT WFO)
- Near real-time data sharing with NWS for immediate incorporation into operational forecast decision-making

**Long-term Research & Development:**
- Fill critical data gaps in boundary layer observations over complex terrain
- Improve understanding of coupling between land surface models and atmospheric models
- Evaluate numerical weather prediction tools and boundary layer parameterizations
- Develop methods/updates to improve NWP forecast models for water and weather prediction
- Support calibration of flood forecast models

**Data Application:** Assimilate UAS-collected data into high-resolution modeling systems for evaluation of impact on forecast skill

## Key Findings & Review Evaluation

**Overall Scoring (84.3/100):**
- Technical/Scientific Merit: 28.7/35 (Good)
- Importance/Relevance: 25.6/30 (Good)
- Applicant Qualifications: 14.3/15 (Outstanding)
- Project Costs: 6.7/10 (Adequate)
- Outreach/Education & D&I: 9.0/10 (Outstanding)

**Reviewer Consensus Strengths:**
1. **Scientific Merit:** Proposal identified a clear, urgent need for boundary layer observations in complex terrain. Reviewer #3 noted "the proposing team has aptly connected these dots, putting together a solid proposal to fill this gap."
2. **Team Qualifications:** "Well-known high-performing team" with PhDs in meteorology, atmospheric science, aerospace engineering; strong publication record; extensive credentials and institutional resources
3. **Collaboration:** Multiple institutional partners with signed letters of commitment (UCAR, NCAR, CU, BST, CIRES, NOAA Physical Sciences Lab, NWS)
4. **Outreach/Education:** Strong commitment including summer internship program for Colorado Community College students, K-12 engagement, community open houses, NOAA Hollings Program participation
5. **Diversity & Inclusion:** Good gender balance on core team, engagement with CIRES director of D&I, inclusion of under-represented backgrounds

**Reviewer Concerns:**
1. **Funding Fragmentation (Critical Issue):** Only the S2 platform is included in WPO funding; the other two critical UAS platforms (RAAVEN, HELiX) depend on parallel, unfunded sources. Reviewer #3 flagged this as "a not so insignificant risk to the success of this wholly dependent, currently proposed WPO project."
2. **Incomplete Scope:** Multiple sensors and instruments beyond UAS are marked as "pending availability of additional funds," creating potential large gaps in data collection
3. **Operational Transition Path:** Limited articulation of how model improvements would transition into NWS operational forecasting. Reviewer #3 noted concern that the project "could also end up capping out at RL8" (proven demonstration) without a clearer pathway to RL9 (operational implementation)
4. **NWS Operational Integration:** Proposal lacks representation from NWS operational NWP modeling community (as opposed to research and local forecast offices). Reviewer #3 stated: "it is desirable to have personnel on the list of contributors possessing background affiliations in the NWS operational NWP community"
5. **Cost-Benefit Ratio:** For a large funding request focused on "UAS mapping" and "UAS data analysis," only one UAS platform is funded through this proposal. Reviewer #3 noted: "the involvement of just one, single UAS platform (as proposed for the WPO line of funding) seems off balance with the total proposed funding amount"
6. **Equipment Sustainability:** Most equipment is not being purchased; continuation of operations beyond the campaign would require additional capital investment
7. **Success Metrics:** "Little to no information provided regarding explicit measures to evaluate whether or not the overall project will have resulted in success or failure to meet the goals" (Reviewer #3)

**Operational Relevance Assessment:**
Reviewers noted that while research benefits are clear, the proposal's short-term operational benefits "do not seem to support any long-term transition goals for NWS use" and that small-scale UAS platforms are "not practical to consider...the lasting benefits...to be the transition...into operations to support these same types of data collection for large-scale, long-term benefit to NWS operations." The more impactful contribution is viewed as improved understanding of boundary layer processes for model parameterization refinement.
