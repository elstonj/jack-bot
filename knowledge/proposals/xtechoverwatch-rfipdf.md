## Executive Summary

The U.S. Army xTechOverwatch competition seeks innovative concepts from eligible small businesses for advanced autonomous unmanned systems (UAVs, UGVs, and/or sensor networks) capable of conducting overwatch and terrain-shaping tasks using AI, robotics, secure networking, and real-time data processing. The three-round competition offers up to $1 million in cash prizes with the top 20 winners eligible to submit Direct to Phase II Army SBIR proposals worth up to $2 million each (total SBIR funding pool: $40 million).

## Technical Approach & Problem Statement

The Army seeks well-defined prototype technology solutions for autonomous unmanned platforms integrating:
- Multi-modal sensor fusion with AI-driven target recognition
- Secure communications and adaptive decision-making
- Systems capable of identifying and occupying advantageous overwatch positions with minimal human intervention

**Key Technical Areas of Interest (from Appendix A):**

1. **Autonomous Unmanned Systems (UAVs, UGVs, Stationary Sensors)**
   - UGVs with self-adjusting mobility, real-time terrain navigation, and automated repositioning
   - UAVs with adaptive flight control for ISR and dynamic mission execution
   - Stationary sensor networks with low-power, persistent surveillance capabilities integrated with mobile platforms

2. **Sensor Fusion, Data Integration, and AI-Driven Target Recognition & Classification**
   - AI-powered sensor fusion integrating EO/IR, LiDAR, acoustic, and RF sensors
   - Real-time threat detection, classification, and tracking
   - Robust performance in cluttered, low-visibility, or GPS-denied environments

3. **Secure Communications and Networking**
   - Encrypted, resilient, scalable communications using software-defined radios (SDRs), mesh networking, low-power long-range protocols
   - Network redundancy and self-healing architectures
   - Real-time data sharing across multiple autonomous systems without traditional infrastructure

4. **Human-Machine Interface (HMI) for Effective Decision-Making**
   - Advanced visualizations (augmented reality [AR])
   - AI-driven decision-support systems to reduce operator cognitive burden
   - Adaptive UIs tailored to mission context and operator workload

5. **Edge Computing for Onboard Processing**
   - High-performance, low-power embedded AI accelerators
   - Real-time onboard processing of sensor data and AI inference
   - Low-latency threat detection in disconnected/contested environments

6. **Resilient Robotics for Adaptive Terrain Navigation**
   - Mobility adaptation solutions (variable tire inflation, articulated suspensions, AI-driven path planning)
   - Biomimetic robotic adaptations
   - Terrain-aware decision-making for obstacles, mud, snow, unstable ground

7. **Automated Self-Recovery Mechanisms**
   - Self-righting mechanisms, robotic manipulator arms, cooperative recovery protocols
   - AI diagnostics for predictive maintenance
   - Autonomous fail-safe capabilities for rollovers, entrapment, hardware failures

8. **Collaborative/Mission-Focused Autonomy**
   - Decentralized AI-based mission execution using multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL)
   - Distributed decision-making and swarm robotics
   - Autonomous coordination with minimal human intervention

9. **UGV and UAS Payloads**
   - Modular ISR payloads for reconnaissance
   - Deployable terrain-shaping tools (automated barrier placement, excavation)
   - Electronic warfare (EW) payloads for disrupting adversary sensors/communications

10. **Modular & Open Architecture**
    - Plug-and-play hardware/software interfaces per Army Interoperability Profile (IOP), NATO STANAGs
    - Standardized data protocols and API-based integration
    - Future upgradability and cross-platform compatibility

## Competition Structure & Phases

**Part 1: Concept White Paper (Deadline: May 21, 2025, 5 p.m. ET)**
- Three-page white paper submission via template
- Evaluated by Army/DoD S&T, Warfighter, acquisition, and R&D subject matter experts
- **Scoring Criteria:**
  - Introduction (3%)
  - Army Benefits (15%)
  - Technical Approach (35%)
  - Programmatic Potential (20%)
  - Commercial Potential (25%)
  - Proposal Quality (2%)
- **Prize:** Up to 40 winners receive $15,000 each

**xTech Collider Event (August 2025, tentatively Austin, TX, Fed Supernova)**
- Optional networking event for Part 1 winners
- Designed to facilitate teaming and collaboration among finalists and industry partners
- No government role in team formation; collaboration optional and non-binding

**Part 2: Finals (October 27-29, 2025, tentatively Bush Combat Development Center, Bryan, TX)**
- In-person pitch and demonstration to Army/DoD expert panel
- Scheduled around Army Human-Machine Integration (HMI) Summit
- **Prize:** Up to 20 final winners receive $20,000 each

**Direct to Phase II (DP2) Army SBIR Proposals (Submission period: November 12-25, 2025)**
- Only Part 2 finalists eligible
- Up to $2 million per award, not to exceed 18 months
- Must demonstrate sufficient solution maturity and feasibility
- Total Army SBIR funding pool: $40 million (up to 20 awards possible)

## Eligibility Requirements

**Eligible Entities:**
- Small, for-profit, independent U.S. businesses
- Meet small business requirements in 13 C.F.R. § 121.702
- Majority (>50%) equity directly owned and controlled by:
  - U.S. citizens or permanent resident aliens, OR
  - Other for-profit SBCs (similarly owned), OR
  - Combination thereof
- Not more than 500 employees (including affiliates)
- Only one submission per eligible entity

**Submission Requirements:**
- Work must be substantially performed by the proposing firm
- Cannot be based on prior or ongoing federally funded Army SBIR/STTR work
- Disqualified if attestation cannot be made

## Use Cases & Applications

The competition targets autonomous systems for:
- **Overwatch missions:** Advanced situational awareness and threat detection
- **Terrain-shaping operations:** Adaptive positioning and environmental modification
- **GPS-denied and contested environments:** Autonomous navigation and decision-making without reliance on traditional infrastructure
- **Persistent surveillance:** Long-duration intelligence gathering via distributed sensor networks
- **Minimal human intervention missions:** Decentralized autonomous coordination and swarm operations

## Notable Details

1. **Collaboration Encouraged:** SBCs can partner with other SBCs or entities; the competition explicitly encourages teaming to deliver comprehensive solutions. The xTech Collider Event facilitates strategic partnerships.

2. **Specialist Submissions Welcome:** Firms with expertise in specific technical areas are encouraged to submit even if not providing full-spectrum solutions; the Army intends to integrate complementary technologies.

3. **Feedback and Transition Support:** Beyond cash prizes, participants receive detailed feedback reports designed to accelerate technology transition to Army end-users.

4. **Flexible Mechanism:** While the prize competition operates under 10 U.S.C. § 4025, other Army, DoD, or USG organizations may express interest in funded participants for separate funding opportunities (e.g., Broad Agency Announcements).

5. **Confidentiality:** All submissions treated as privileged information; disclosed only to government employees or designated support contractors for evaluation and program support.

6. **Risk Waiver:** Participants must assume all risks and waive claims against the USG and related entities (except in cases of willful misconduct) for any injury, death, damage, or loss arising from competition participation.

7. **Interoperability Standards:** Emphasis on modular, open architecture compatible with:
   - Army Ground IOP (NAMC RAS-G)
   - NATO Standardization Agreements (STANAGs)
   - Army Standards & Specifications
   - Modular Mission Payload Standard

8. **Timeline:**
   - Registration and submission: April 2 – May 21, 2025
   - Finalists announced: June 30, 2025
   - Collider event: August 2025
   - Finals pitch: October 27-29, 2025
   - Final winners announced: October 29, 2025
   - DP2 SBIR submissions: November 12-25, 2025
