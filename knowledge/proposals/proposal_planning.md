## Executive Summary
This planning document outlines BST's Phase II-E proposal to develop the S2 VXT™ (a variant of the S2 platform) specifically optimized for volcano monitoring operations. The goal is to create a simplified solution for accessing difficult-to-reach volcanic environments and enable deployment of volcano monitoring systems in remote, dangerous locations.

## Technical Approach
**S2 VXT™ Development Focus:**
- Enhanced takeoff/landing capability at high altitude (10,000 feet elevation)
- Extended operational range with volcano-specific payload
- High-wind operation capability
- Integration of volcano monitoring payload and sensors
- Path toward semi-autonomous remote deployment capability for hazardous conditions

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 VXT™
- **Description**: Variant of the S2 platform optimized for volcano monitoring
- **Key Specifications**:
  - Takeoff/landing altitude capability: 10,000 feet
  - Flight time with volcano payload: 1 hour at 5,000 feet
  - Wind tolerance: Up to 30 mph takeoff/landing
- **Proposed Use**: Access to remote, rugged volcanic terrain with difficult access, high altitude, and dense vegetation
- **Long-term Vision**: Semi-autonomous volcano monitoring system with remote launch capability for dangerous deployment scenarios

## Use Cases & Applications
1. **Primary Mission**: Scientifically useful flight operations in Alaska volcanic regions (difficult to access with conventional fixed-wing aircraft)
2. **Existing BST Mission Integration**: 
   - MALIBU (unspecified volcanic science mission)

3. **Secondary Applications**: 
   - Pipeline inspection (with gas sensor payload)
   - Other industrial inspection in difficult terrain
