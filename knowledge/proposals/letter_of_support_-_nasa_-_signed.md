# Letter of Support - NASA for Black Swift Technologies SBIR Phase II

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: NASA Goddard Space Flight Center, Wallops Flight Facility
- Program/Solicitation: Air Force SBIR Phase II - Soil Moisture Mapping (AFWERX)
- Date: April 25, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 (sUAS), SwiftFlow, Soil Moisture Mapping System, L-band radiometer
- Key Personnel: Geoffrey L. Bland (NASA Climate and Radiation Laboratory); Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
NASA's Geoffrey Bland provides strong institutional support for Black Swift Technologies' proposed SBIR Phase II effort to develop a soil integrity mapping UAS system. Bland endorses BST's track record of translating innovative technologies into practical systems, particularly the L-band radiometer core technology developed in partnership with NASA, and emphasizes the company's successful deployment of the S2 sUAS and SwiftFlow probe across multiple NASA research centers.

## Technical Approach
BST's Phase II effort proposes to map soil integrity from an Unmanned Aerial System (UAS), building on the L-band radiometer technology co-developed with NASA. The approach leverages proven microwave-based soil moisture sensor technology and established UAS platforms.

## Products & Capabilities Described

**L-band Radiometer**
- Core technology developed in partnership with NASA
- Microwave-based soil moisture measurement capability
- Foundation for soil moisture mapping system

**S2 sUAS (Small Unmanned Aerial System)**
- Ruggedized small UAS platform
- Currently in operational use at NASA Langley Research Center and NASA Ames Research Center
- Supporting agency-wide science and technology research

**SwiftFlow Atmospheric Probe**
- Atmospheric measurement system
- Deployed under NASA contract NNX17CP13C
- Supporting multiple research efforts

**Soil Moisture Mapping System**
- Developed under contract 80NSSC18C0002
- Microwave-based sensor system for soil moisture measurement

## Use Cases & Applications
- Soil integrity mapping from UAS platforms
- Supporting NASA Science Mission Directorate (SMD) research efforts
- Supporting NASA Aeronautics Research Mission Directorate (ARMD) research efforts
- Supporting USGS and NOAA agency research needs
- Agency-wide science and technology research interests
