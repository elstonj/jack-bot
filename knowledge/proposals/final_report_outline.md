## Executive Summary
This is an internal planning document outlining the structure and content for BST's NASA SBIR Phase I final report on using UAS for wildfire weather observation. The document details technical work completed during Phase I, planned Phase II activities, and the major report sections needed to comprehensively describe the project's advancement of autonomous aircraft for real-world fire team operations.

## Technical Approach

### Phase I Completed Activities:
1. **Ensemble Sensitivity Analysis & Pre-Data-Collection Steps** – Advanced modeling to optimize data collection strategy
2. **Fire Team Integration Pathway Development** – Safety-focused coordination with fire teams for both testing and real-world operations
3. **Flight Operations & Data Validation** – Testing data handoff techniques and assimilation methods
4. **Multi-UAS Flights** – Coordinated operations with multiple vertical-takeoff aircraft
5. **S0 VTOL Design Improvements** – Enhanced performance in fire weather and mountainous conditions
6. **Data Output & Visualization Pipeline Development** – User-focused data delivery systems with safety emphasis

### Phase II Planned Activities:
- **Continued S0 VTOL Development**: Tool-less improvements (latching wings/tail with connectors), night operations (strobes/LED winglets), deployment carry case design
- **COTAK Integration**: Work with Center of Excellence to integrate BST data outputs with COTAK (Common Operational Picture Architecture) when available; develop BST plugin for COTAK
- **Real-World Fire Operations**: Deploy with local and NASA fire teams during actual wildfire events
- **Extended Flight Testing**: Additional S0 VTOL operations in critical sampling locations with multiple aircraft

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 VTOL
- **What it is**: Primary autonomous vertical-takeoff UAS platform for fire weather data collection
- **Phase I improvements**: Redesigned for better performance in fire weather and mountainous conditions
- **Phase II development focus**: 
  - New design features including improved fuselage, tail motor/prop selection, primary motor/prop selection
  - ESC heatsink with thermal testing
  - Battery revisions and testing
  - Integrated laser and precision mount for exact positioning
  - Updated sonde design for environmental sensing
  - Improved display board with weatherproofing and simplified wiring
  - Enhanced CAN boards and wiring to address RF interference
  - Video system integration for testing and flight ops
  - Toolless assembly improvements: latching wings and tail with connectors
  - Night operation capability (strobes, LED winglets)
  - Carry case design for field deployment

## Use Cases & Applications

### Wildfire Operations
- **Primary Application**: Real-time wind and turbulence data collection to support wildland firefighting decision-making
- **Deployment Context**: Operations in or near active fire zones and mountainous fire terrain
- **Fire Team Integration**: Direct coordination with local fire teams and NASA fire operations teams
- **Data Users**: Wildland firefighting managers requiring wind guidance information

### Key Research Questions Being Addressed:
1. Can strategically located UAS significantly improve wind information needed for wildland firefighting?
2. What optimal sampling strategies maximize impact (number of UAS, flight hours per day, observation timing)?
3. Can UAS flight parameters and wind information be conveyed through ATAK/COTAK to fire managers?
