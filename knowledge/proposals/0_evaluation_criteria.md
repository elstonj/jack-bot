# Evaluation Criteria for DOD/CSO D2P2 Extreme Environment Operations SBIR Proposal

## Document Metadata
- Type: Solicitation evaluation criteria document
- Client/Agency: Department of the Air Force (DAF), Chief Scientist Office (CSO)
- Program/Solicitation: D2P2 Extreme Environment Operations (Topic AF234-ODCSO20); also references Orbital Prime submissions
- Date: 2023-10-23 (created/modified)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None explicitly mentioned in this document
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This document outlines the evaluation criteria that the Air Force will use to peer-review and select SBIR proposals submitted in response to the D2P2 Extreme Environment Operations solicitation. Proposals will be judged on commercialization potential, defense need appropriateness, and technical approach, with technical merit weighted significantly more heavily than cost.

## Evaluation Criteria (Primary)

### 1. Commercialization Potential (Equal Weight)
- **Commercial track record**: Evidence of pre-sales, pilots, sales, revenue, active users, subscriptions, downloads, or other adoption metrics from previous SBIR or research commercialization efforts
- **Follow-on commitments**: Phase II follow-on funding commitments from Government (documented via DAF Customer Memorandum) or private sources (letters of commitment)
- **Transition strategy**: Sound transition plan detailed in DAF Customer Memorandum demonstrates commercialization potential

### 2. Appropriateness & Relevance of Defense Need (Equal Weight)
- **Customer Memorandum**: Required for AF234-ODCSO20 submissions; optional for Orbital Prime. Must use mandatory template and be signed by appropriate end-user/customer to demonstrate defense need specificity
- **Letters of support**: Signed support letters from other Government personnel serve as indicators of appropriateness and relevance
- **Effort adequacy**: Proposed effort must be adequate and directly related to fulfilling the identified Defense Need

### 3. Technical Approach (Equal Weight)
- **Technical merit and innovation**: Soundness and innovation of proposed technical approach
- **Differentiation**: How the approach differs from current customer alternatives
- **Team qualifications**: Principal Investigators', Project Managers', supporting staff, and consultants' qualifications to execute the approach

## Weighting & Decision Rules
- **Technical criteria combined are significantly more important than cost/price**
- Cost/price considered only when technical evaluations are essentially equal in merit
- **Price reasonableness and realism** evaluated as appropriate
- Peer reviewers base conclusions **only on information in proposals** (cannot assume familiarity with firm or prior work)

## Special Ownership & Investment Restrictions
- Air Force will accept proposals from SBCs with majority ownership by multiple venture capital, hedge funds, or private equity firms
- However, **maximum 15% of SBIR budget can go to such SBCs** (per 15 U.S.C. 638(dd))
- Once selected proposals from such SBCs reach 15% threshold, no further such proposals will be considered
- **Investment by VC/PE/hedge funds cannot be used as award criterion**

## Additional Factors in Selection Process
- National security concerns
- Executive Order 13329: High priority for U.S. small business manufacturing innovation
- Energy Independence and Security Act (P.L. 110-140): High priority for energy efficiency or renewable energy R&D projects
- Eligible U.S.-owned SBCs conducting biomedical research in energy efficiency/renewable energy encouraged to apply

## Notable Details
- This solicitation emphasizes **Government or private sector commercialization potential** as a primary evaluation factor, requiring evidence of real-world adoption or credible transition plans
- Proposals must include a signed Customer Memorandum from an end-user to be competitive, indicating this is a **customer-pull solicitation** rather than exploratory research
- The D2P2 (Developing, Demonstrating, and Proving Prototypes) program structure suggests focus on mature prototypes ready for demonstration/transition
