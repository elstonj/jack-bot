# BST Statement of Work - NSF CO-WY Climate Engine Project

## Document Metadata
- Type: Statement of Work (SOW)
- Client/Agency: Cooperative Institute for Research in the Atmosphere (CIRA), Colorado State University
- Program/Solicitation: NSF CO-WY Climate Engine (Colorado-Wyoming)
- Date: August 26, 2024
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 (aircraft), Soil Moisture Sensor, NightFOX payload
- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor); addressed to Dr. Kyle Hilburn (CIRA)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies will collaborate with CIRA over 2025 to support wildfire monitoring and prediction efforts by integrating soil moisture and hotspot detection data into the WRF-SFIRE model, collecting remote sensing data on fuel loading, and conducting rapid deployment of an S2 aircraft equipped with NOAA's NightFOX payload for lightning-triggered response.

## Technical Approach
The project involves four key work streams:
1. **Data Integration**: Convert NOAA NightFOX hotspot detection data and BST Soil Moisture Sensor measurements into formats compatible with the WRF-SFIRE (Weather Research and Forecasting - Stochastic Induced Regeneration Fire Model) for fire behavior modeling.
2. **Remote Sensing Collection**: Collect soil moisture and NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) data over designated areas of interest to assess fuel loading conditions.
3. **Rapid Response Operations**: Deploy an S2 aircraft equipped with the NOAA NightFOX payload, with deployment decisions based on satellite-detected lightning signatures and CIRA guidance.
4. **Project Support**: Participate in meetings, prepare reports, and conduct other collaborative activities.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 (Aircraft)
- **What it is**: BST's small unmanned aircraft system
- **Proposed use**: Platform for carrying the NOAA NightFOX hotspot detection payload during rapid-response wildfire monitoring missions
- **Deployment trigger**: Satellite-based lightning detections

### Soil Moisture Sensor (BST)
- **What it is**: BST ground-based or airborne soil moisture measurement system
- **Proposed use**: Collect soil moisture data for fuel loading assessment and integration into WRF-SFIRE model for fire prediction
- **Output**: Data converted to WRF-compatible format

### NightFOX Payload (NOAA)
- **What it is**: NOAA hotspot detection system (identifies active fire thermal signatures)
- **Proposed use**: Mounted on S2 for rapid fire detection and characterization
- **Integration**: Data converted to WRF-SFIRE model format

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Wildfire Monitoring & Prediction**: Integration of real-time hotspot detection with fuel loading and atmospheric data to improve fire behavior modeling
- **Fuel Loading Assessment**: Using soil moisture and NDVI measurements to characterize vegetation state and flammability
- **Lightning-Triggered Response**: Rapid S2 deployment following satellite lightning detection to monitor emerging fire conditions
- **Fire-Weather Modeling**: Supporting WRF-SFIRE simulations for Colorado and Wyoming regions
