# FY2025 Weather Program Office Research Programs - Testbeds Solicitation

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO)
- **Client/Agency:** NOAA Weather Program Office (WPO), Oceanic and Atmospheric Research (OAR)
- **Program/Solicitation:** NOAA-OAR-WPO-2025-28601; Weather Research and Forecasting Innovation Act of 2017 (Public Law 115-25)

- **Key Personnel:** Not explicitly named in this solicitation document

## Executive Summary

NOAA's Weather Program Office is soliciting proposals for the Testbeds grant competition to support applied research and development that demonstrates and tests new technologies in NOAA's quasi-operational forecasting environments. The competition has approximately $4.2M in annual funding available for 13-15 projects, with a maximum of $300K per project per year, supporting three-year projects beginning August 1, 2025. The program focuses on mature projects (Readiness Levels 5-8) addressing weather, water, and earth system forecasting innovations in five NOAA testbeds: Climate Testbed (CTB), Fire Weather Testbed (FWT), Hazardous Weather Testbed (HWT), Hurricane and Ocean Testbed (HOT), and Hydrometeorology Testbed (HMT).

## Program Objectives & Structure

### Core Mission
The Testbeds Program aims to transition applied research to NOAA operations and services through close collaboration with testbeds. Projects should address improvements to NOAA's existing or near-term future technologies (e.g., Unified Forecast System components) rather than entirely revolutionary approaches, with transition expected within 3-5 years to the National Weather Service (NWS).

### Maturity Requirements
- **Target Readiness Levels:** RL5-RL8 during project duration (demonstration level)
- Projects should be nearly ready or ready for testing in quasi-operational forecasting environments
- Focus on operational implementability within existing NOAA frameworks

### Five Participating Testbeds

**1. Climate Testbed (CTB)** - Manages subseasonal-to-seasonal (S2S, 2 weeks to 2 years) prediction
- **Location:** Climate Prediction Center (CPC) and Environmental Modeling Center (EMC), College Park, MD
- **Priority CTB-1:** Data assimilation improvements including AI/ML methods for ocean, sea ice, land data assimilation using JEDI; enhancements to coupled GDAS for GFS/GEFS/SFS
- **Priority CTB-2:** Seasonal Forecast System (SFS) development; AI/ML methods; physics parameterizations; Common Community Physics Package (CCPP) improvements; stochastic perturbations for ensemble applications
- **Priority CTB-3:** S2S outlook improvements through post-processing, statistical/ML techniques, probabilistic forecasts; diagnostic/verification tools (METplus compatible); multi-model ensemble combinations

**2. Fire Weather Testbed (FWT)** - Transitions advanced technologies to operational platforms
- **Location:** OAR Global Systems Laboratory, Boulder, CO
- **Priority FWT-1:** Fire weather forecast verification (datasets, success metrics, tools); operational environmental parameters; microscale-mesoscale condition research for rapid fire spread
- **Priority FWT-2:** Social science integration for decision support; end-user comprehension of probabilistic/intensity/timing information; long-lead (days to week 2) and subseasonal (weeks 3-6) forecasting support

**3. Hazardous Weather Testbed (HWT)** - Severe convective weather forecasting
- **Location:** OAR National Severe Storms Laboratory, NWS Storm Prediction Center (SPC), NWS Norman Weather Forecast Office, National Weather Center, Norman, OK
- **Priority HWT-1:** Convection-allowing/resolving ensemble forecast improvements; rapid updating, very short-term through few-days forecasting for tornado, hail, damaging wind, lightning
- **Priority HWT-2:** Post-processing and verification techniques for probabilistic thunderstorm and severe hazard guidance (next hour through week 2)
- **Priority HWT-3:** High temporal/spatial resolution in-situ and remote-sensed observation datasets; 3-D objective data analysis for detection/tracking/analysis of severe convective phenomena
- **Priority HWT-4:** Social/behavioral science integration for forecaster decision-making and end-user messaging

**4. Hurricane and Ocean Testbed (HOT)** - Tropical weather and marine phenomena
- **Location:** NWS National Hurricane Center (NHC), OAR Atlantic Oceanographic and Meteorological Laboratory, Miami, FL
- **Priority HOT-1:** Near real-time analysis of 2-D surface wind and wave fields in tropical cyclones (TC); center location, maximum sustained winds, radius of maximum winds, wind radii, significant wave height
- **Priority HOT-2:** Observing system strategies (crewed/uncrewed platforms, supplemental rawinsonde, satellite targeting); enhanced/automated flight planning software
- **Priority HOT-3:** TC guidance (0-7 days) improvements; post-processing and AI/ML models; consensus techniques; full 2-D surface wind field and wave height prediction; rapid TC intensity changes; TC genesis forecasting
- **Priority HOT-4:** Tools for interpreting and integrating disparate real-time observational data; cross-validation of measurement sources using statistical/AI/ML techniques; probabilistic satellite-based classification
- **Priority HOT-5:** NWP model diagnostic tools; initialization, vortex tilt, surface wind field structure assessment

**5. Hydrometeorology Testbed (HMT)** - Extreme precipitation and winter weather
- **Location:** NWS Weather Prediction Center, College Park, MD; OAR Physical Sciences Laboratory, Boulder, CO
- **Priority HMT-1:** Probabilistic flash flood and winter precipitation forecasting; convection-allowing/resolving ensemble improvements for snowfall, flashflooding, ice accumulation, atmospheric rivers
- **Priority HMT-2:** Post-processing and verification for flash flood and winter precipitation forecasts across scales
- **Priority HMT-3:** Forecaster use of probabilistic information; messaging uncertainty; social/behavioral science frameworks; existing tools like Dynamic Ensemble-based Scenarios for IDSS (DESI)
- **Priority HMT-4:** Atmospheric forcing improvements for hydrologic models; data assimilation methods; high-resolution forecast guidance evaluation

## Funding Information

- **Total Annual Funding:** $4.2 million
- **Expected Number of Projects:** 13-15 funded projects
- **Maximum Per-Project Per-Year:** $300,000
- **Maximum Award Length:** 3 years
- **Recommended Start Date:** August 1, 2025
- **Award Type:** Cooperative agreements (NOAA staff involvement expected due to testbed requirements)

## Eligibility

### Eligible Applicants
- Public or private corporations, partnerships, or associations
- State, tribal government agencies or officers
- Non-NOAA federal agencies may NOT receive funding, but can participate as unfunded collaborators
- Federal scientists may serve as uncompensated co-PIs
- Federal labs can provide expertise/facilities but cannot be compensated

### Registration Requirements
Applicants must complete three registrations before application submission:
1. SAM.gov (System for Award Management)
2. Grants.gov
3. eRA Commons
- Complete process takes 4-6 weeks; applicants should begin 6 weeks before deadline

### Federal Collaborator Restrictions
- Federal employee salary costs are ineligible
- Allowed: Project-critical travel, equipment, indirect costs, infrastructure, testbed-related costs
- NOT allowed: Conference/workshop travel
- Requested costs for NOAA collaborators cannot exceed 20% of total project cost

## Application Requirements & Deadlines

### Key Deadlines
- **Letter of Intent (LOI):** October 2, 2024, 5:00 PM ET (strongly encouraged, non-binding feedback expected by October 18)
- **Full Application:** December 2, 2024, 5:00 PM ET
- **Award Notification:** Expected June 2025
- **Pre-award spending:** Authorized 90 days prior to award start date

## Evaluation Criteria

**Reviewed against:**
- Alignment with testbed program objectives and stated priorities (Section I.B)
- Technical approach feasibility
- DEIA considerations and broadening participation
- Data management and software management plans
- Outreach and education plans

## Notable Requirements & Policies

### Human Subjects Research
- Must complete Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval
- OMB/PRA clearance required if collecting information
- Include IRB timeline in project schedule

### High-Performance Computing (HPC)
- Applicants must demonstrate available HPC access sufficient for project success

### Testbed Collaboration
- Testbed Collaboration Form must be completed by NOAA Testbed Manager or GSL director (for DESI use)
- Close collaboration with testbeds is critical and required

### Data & Software Management
- Data must be made publicly available per plan
- Separate Software Management Plan required if software is developed
- Comply with institutional data storage/sharing policies

### Cost-Sharing
- No cost-sharing required

### Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, Accessibility (DEIA)
- Required statement describing how project broadens participation of traditionally underrepresented groups
- Should include partnerships with HBCUs, TCUs, Minority-Serving Institutions, NOAAs Cooperative Science Centers
- Commitments to accessibility for people with disabilities
- Training on sexual assault/sexual harassment (SASH) awareness and prevention
- Proposed to measure WPO's progress on nurturing diverse research community (optional information collection)

### Funding Restrictions
- No federal employee salary costs
- No duplicate work across different federally-funded projects
- NOAA will not accept deliverables more appropriate for procurement; planned transitions to NOAA should be follow-on work, not deliverables
- Funding beyond Year 1 dependent on satisfactory performance
- NOAA not responsible for proposal preparation costs
