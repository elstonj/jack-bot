## Executive Summary
This document is an official Department of the Navy template and instructions for completing Phase I Technical Volume (Volume 2) submissions under the DoD SBIR/STTR program. It specifies format requirements, content sections, and submission guidelines for Navy SBIR/STTR proposals.

## Document Purpose & Scope
This is NOT a Black Swift Technologies proposal or technical document. Rather, it is a **blank submission template** provided by the Navy for companies to use when responding to Navy SBIR/STTR topics. It contains no information about BST's products, capabilities, or specific proposals.

## Template Structure Required
The template prescribes the following sections for Phase I Technical Volumes:

1. **Description of Proposed Phase I Technical Effort**
   - 1.1 Phase I Technical Objectives
   - 1.2 Phase I (Base and Option) Statement of Work
   - 1.3 Related Work

2. **Key Personnel**
   - Summary table with qualifications, employer, publications
   - Foreign national restrictions noted for ITAR-controlled topics

3. **Commercialization/Transition Plan Summary**
   - Market strategy for DoD, Federal Agencies, and private sector
   - Market need and size information

4. **Facilities/Equipment**
   - Description of instrumentation and physical facilities
   - Environmental compliance statement
   - Equipment justification and pricing

5. **Letters of Support**
   - Optional inclusion within 10-page limit

## Format Requirements (Mandatory)
- **Maximum length:** 10 pages (all content counted, no exceptions)
- **Layout:** Single column, single-spaced
- **Paper:** Standard 8.5" x 11"
- **Margins:** 1 inch on all sides (headers/footers allowed in margin)
- **Font:** Minimum 10-point (exceptions: headers, footers, table/figure text, references)
- **Rejection criteria:** Non-compliance with format results in automatic rejection

## Phase I Restrictions
- NO human subjects testing
- NO animal testing
- NO recombinant DNA research
- Human/animal subject protocols may be planned for Phase I Option, execution in Phase II
- Phase II funding cannot be released for human/animal/recombinant DNA work until approvals obtained

## Notable Administrative Details
- Proposals may include proprietary data with restriction legends on first page and marked sheets
- Foreign nationals on ITAR-restricted topics must hold Permanent Resident Card or be "Protected Individual" per 8 U.S.C. 1324b(a)(3)
- Resumes count toward 10-page limit
- Phase I Option required to bridge funding gap to Phase II and demonstrate preparation for Phase II
- Program Manager identifies if human/animal subjects or recombinant DNA required by topic
