# Letter of Commitment: AtmoFacts to Black Swift Technologies

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Commitment
- Client/Agency: Black Swift Technologies (BST)
- Program/Solicitation: US Department of Energy (DOE) Fossil Energy and Carbon Management (FECM), "Undocumented Orphaned Well Characterization and Remediation" (DE-FOA-0003343)
- Date: October 31, 2024
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 platform
- Key Personnel: Stefan Metzger (AtmoFacts Founder and CEO), Beck Cotter (last editor, BST)

## Executive Summary
AtmoFacts commits to providing $128,999 in services to support Black Swift Technologies' response to a DOE proposal on undocumented orphaned well characterization and remediation. Services include FluxMapper™ characterization and analysis of BST's S2 platform, as well as deployment of eddy-covariance flux tower hardware for methane leak detection and atmospheric flux monitoring.

## Technical Approach
AtmoFacts will provide high-resolution FluxMapper™ analyses to support methane leak detection and atmospheric flux monitoring. The approach includes:
- Characterization of BST S2 platform data (Phases 2a, 3a, 3b)
- Generation of 60 FluxMapper™ scenes from fully characterized S2 data
- Deployment of eddy-covariance flux tower hardware with integrated FluxMapper™ analyses

## Products & Capabilities Described

### BST S2 Platform
- Platform for collecting data to be characterized and analyzed
- Data subjected to FluxMapper™ characterization in multiple project phases
- Used in context of methane emission quantification from orphaned wells

### AtmoFacts FluxMapper™
- High-resolution atmospheric flux analysis and visualization tool
- Capable of processing S2 platform data
- Used for methane leak detection and atmospheric flux monitoring
- Supports scene generation from characterized data

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Undocumented orphaned well characterization**: Detection and quantification of methane emissions from abandoned oil/gas wells
- **Atmospheric flux monitoring**: Measurement of methane leaks using eddy-covariance flux tower methodology
- **Emissions quantification and visualization**: Supporting "Quantify Emissions. Visualize Impact" mission

## Service Breakdown & Costs
- Phase 2a, 3a, 3b FluxMapper characterization of S2 platform: $21,250
- 60 FluxMapper scenes from characterized S2 data: $17,749
- Two 3-month deployments of eddy-covariance flux tower hardware with FluxMapper analyses: $90,000
- **Total Commitment: $128,999**

- Partnership demonstrates BST S2 platform integration with third-party atmospheric analysis expertise

- Proposal targets environmental remediation and emissions monitoring for DOE FECM program
