## Executive Summary
This is an official NOAA SBIR 2018-1 Q&A document providing clarifications to potential proposers on program eligibility, personnel requirements, and technical scope for Subtopic 8.1.1 (GPS-RO balloon payload development). The document clarifies that startups are encouraged, addresses university participation pathways, and defines Phase I vs. Phase II scope for balloon-based GPS Radio Occultation systems.

## Technical Approach - GPS-RO Balloon Payload (Subtopic 8.1.1)

**Phase I Scope:**
- Design and develop a prototype GPS Radio Occultation (GPS-RO) balloon payload
- Partial testing including ground and inflight data collection
- Focus on measurements and calculations of bending angle and refractivity
- Demonstrate basic principles of how meteorological variables could be derived
- Operational demonstrations of balloon integration

**Phase II Scope (future):**
- Extensive testing, data collection, and retrievals
- Calibration and validation with mature system
- Complex mission architecture design

**Data Quality Focus:**
- Phase I should prioritize bending angle and refractivity measurements/calculations over direct phase delay analysis
- Actual meteorological variable retrievals are out of Phase I scope

## Use Cases & Applications
- GPS Radio Occultation data collection via balloon platforms
- Atmospheric sensing and meteorological data collection
- Climate and weather monitoring applications

## Program Eligibility & Structure
- Startups explicitly encouraged alongside established small businesses
- Universities may participate as subcontractors (limited to ⅓ of total project cost)
- Principal Investigator qualification required for key personnel
- Proposals must comply with SBIR employment and structure rules

## Notable Details
- Document emphasizes fair competition—all communications must route through Contracting Officer (CO) to ensure uniform information distribution to all potential offerors
- SBIR consortium was conducting outreach to encourage university-small business partnerships on this topic
- Universities cannot directly submit but can partner with small businesses as subcontractors
