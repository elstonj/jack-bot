## Executive Summary
NOAA expresses strong support for Black Swift Technologies' Navy STTR Phase II proposal for the S0 expendable platform to profile the marine atmospheric boundary layer in hazardous weather. The letter attests that S0 has transitioned from concept to proven operational asset through successful hurricane deployments, addressing a critical gap in lower-atmosphere observational capability.

## Technical Approach
- **S0 platform**: Expendable air-sea profiling system designed to operate in extreme weather conditions
- **Data collection**: Continuous 3D profiles of wind, pressure, temperature, and humidity in the lowest 2 km (air-sea interface)
- **Operational enhancement plan**: Phase II objectives include:
  - Data assimilation into Navy COAMPS-TC forecasting model (NOAA already assimilating into HAFS model)
  - Icing protection enhancements
  - AI-based autonomous sampling capability
  - Expansion into polar and winter storm environments

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 Platform
- **What it is**: Expendable atmospheric profiling system for hazardous weather deployment
- **Deployment method**: Dropped from NOAA WP-3D aircraft
- **Performance claims**:
  - Survived and reported data in Category 5 hurricane conditions
  - Hurricane Milton: operated in horizontal winds up to 209 knots, vertical velocities to 30 m/s
  - Provides up to 2 hours of continuous data
  - Cost: ~$15,000 per deployment (vs. high cumulative cost of multiple dropsondes)
- **Operational status**: Transitioned to proven operational asset (not developmental)
