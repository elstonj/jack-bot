# Ainstein - Letter of Support

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of support (for NASA SBIR Phase II proposal)
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Phase II - "Sensor Fusion and Algorithms for Mapping at Low Altitude Above Rugged Terrain"
- Date: January 29, 2019
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Terrain-following UAS platform for low-altitude mapping
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (Black Swift Technologies)

## Executive Summary
Ainstein provides a letter of support committing to supply radar sensors and engineering support for BST's NASA SBIR Phase II project focused on developing sensor fusion and autonomous mapping algorithms for UAS operating at low altitude over rugged terrain.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed to develop integrated sensor fusion and algorithms enabling UAS to autonomously map terrain while maintaining safe low-altitude flight over challenging topography. Ainstein's radar sensors would provide critical terrain-awareness capabilities for collision avoidance and altitude measurement.

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Ainstein µSharp Patch Collision Avoidance Radar**
- Purpose: Collision avoidance for low-altitude flight
- Quantity committed: 2 units
- Application: Integration into BST's terrain-following UAS

**Ainstein LR-D1 Radar Altimeter**
- Purpose: Precise altitude measurement above terrain
- Quantity committed: 2 units
- Application: Terrain-following capability and sensor fusion input
