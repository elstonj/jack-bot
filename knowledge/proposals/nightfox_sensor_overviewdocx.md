## Executive Summary
This document provides an overview of the NightFOX remote sensing sensor and its capabilities for fire detection and monitoring. It demonstrates NightFOX's ability to measure fire radiative power (FRP) and detect active fire hotspots with higher spatial resolution than satellite-based systems like VIIRS, with applications in wildfire monitoring using small unmanned aircraft systems (UAS).

## Technical Approach
NightFOX is a remote sensing sensor designed for fire detection and thermal characterization. The sensor operates at 4 µm wavelength for detecting thermal signatures. The technical approach involves:
- Deployment on small UAS platforms for persistent fire monitoring
- Comparison and validation against satellite-based fire detection systems (VIIRS)
- Real-time hotspot identification and fire perimeter tracking

## Products & Capabilities Described

### NightFOX Sensor
- **What it is:** A remote sensing instrument for fire radiative power (FRP) measurement and fire detection
- **Wavelength:** 4 µm thermal infrared band
- **Spatial resolution:** ~28 m pixel resolution (compared to VIIRS ~375 m pixels)
- **Temperature measurement capability:** Detects apparent temperatures ranging from ~48°C to 600°C+ (maximum observed 873 K)
- **Proposed use:** Deployment on small UAS for continuous fire perimeter coverage and hotspot monitoring

## Use Cases & Applications

### Wildfire Monitoring
- **Active fire detection:** Real-time identification of fire hotspots and active burning areas
- **Fire perimeter mapping:** Continuous coverage of fire boundaries using small UAS teams
- **Thermal characterization:** Detailed temperature mapping of fires for fire behavior analysis

### Validation Case Study
- **Mission:** FIREX-AQ campaign (Granite Gulch fire, August 7, 2019)
- **Platform:** NOAA Twin Otter aircraft
- **Performance:** Good spatial agreement with VIIRS satellite detections; NightFOX provided much finer spatial detail (28 m vs. 375 m pixels)

## Key Results
- **Temperature measurements:** Mean fire pixel apparent temperature of 357°C (630 K) with maximum of 600°C (873 K) observed by NightFOX at 4 µm
- **Satellite comparison:** VIIRS mean fire pixel temperature was 48°C (321 K), indicating NightFOX's superior sensitivity to hot fire signatures
- **Spatial validation:** Confirmed good agreement between NightFOX and VIIRS fire locations, validating sensor accuracy despite different spatial resolutions

## Notable Details
- **Platform flexibility:** Demonstrated on both NOAA Twin Otter aircraft and intended for deployment on small UAS teams for persistent coverage
- **Operational context:** Part of the FIREX-AQ (Fire Influence on Regional to Global Environments and Air Quality) campaign, a NOAA-led initiative
- **Comparative advantage:** Superior spatial resolution and thermal sensitivity compared to operational satellite systems (VIIRS)
