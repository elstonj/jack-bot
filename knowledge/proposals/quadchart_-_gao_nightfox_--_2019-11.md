## Executive Summary
This project developed small UAS observation systems (sUASOS) for in situ and remote sensing wildfire measurements, with the S2 platform deployed for nighttime fire observations during the NOAA/NASA FIREX-AQ field campaign (2017-2019). The work aimed to generate wildfire data to inform and improve fire weather modeling.

## Technical Approach
- Leveraged Black Swift Technologies S2 UAS as the primary platform for carrying integrated instrument payloads
- Developed dual payload suites: remote-sensing package and in-situ measurement package
- Designed for nighttime operations and beyond-visual-line-of-sight (BVLOS) flight capabilities
- Used pneumatic launch system; recovery posed ongoing technical challenges
- Integrated observations into fire weather forecasting models

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- **What it is**: Small unmanned aircraft system with pusher propeller design
- **Specifications**: 5 lb maximum payload capacity; ~2 hour endurance; nose payload bay ideal for sensor placement
- **Use in project**: Carrier platform for wildfire observation payloads during day and nighttime operations
- **Readiness Levels**: Beginning RL 4 (platform); Current RL 8 (platform) by project end

## Payloads & Sensors

### Remote-Sensing Payload
- 1.6 µm scanner
- 4 µm scanner
- FLIR Duo R thermal imager
- 1.6 µm imager
- Laser range finder
- Readiness Levels: Beginning RL 3, Current RL 7

### In-Situ Payload
- POPS (Particle Optical Spectrometer)
- CO2 cell
- CO (carbon monoxide) sensor
- OPC (Optical Particle Counter)
- Filter sampler
- Readiness Levels: Beginning RL 3, Current RL 8

### Observation System Overall
- Readiness Levels: Beginning RL 2, Current RL 6 by project end

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Nighttime wildfire measurements**: Primary mission to collect fire observations during night operations when conventional methods are limited
- **Wildfire remote sensing**: Thermal and spectral imaging of active fires
- **Emissions measurements**: In-situ sampling of combustion products (CO, CO2) and particles
- **Fire weather modeling**: Data used to inform, test, and improve fire forecasting models
- **FIREX-AQ field campaign**: Participated in major NOAA/NASA coordinated field mission (August 31, 2019 participation date)

## Known Issues & Mitigation Strategies

### Flight Permissions (Night, Altitude, BVLOS Operations)
- **Challenge**: Complex regulatory environment for wildfire operations
- **Mitigation**: Ongoing dialogue with USFS UAS program; seeking FAA and local airspace control guidance; pursuing step-wise increase in local (CU) FAA Certificate of Authorization (COA) permissions to demonstrate operational competence

### Complex UAS Operations Environment
- **Challenge**: Difficult launch and recovery conditions in wildfire terrain
- **Mitigation**: UAS range flexibility for location selection; transitioned to pneumatic launch; recovery remains potential challenge due to terrain hazards
