## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes an L-band based soil moisture mapping approach for rapid airfield evaluation and soil integrity assessment. The system leverages soil moisture measurement as the key variable to determine soil strength (Cone Index), addressing inefficiencies in current manual and localized assessment methods used by the Air Force.

## Technical Approach
- **Core Measurement**: L-band based soil moisture sensing with 5-7cm measurement depth
- **Key Principle**: Soil moisture is the primary unknown variable needed for soil integrity mapping; soil type knowledge is also required
- **Calculation Method**: Cone Index (CI) calculated using formula incorporating:
  - mg = percent moisture content (expressed as weight of soil)
  - a, b = coefficients specified for each USCS (Unified Soil Classification System) type
- **Platform**: UAS-based deployment for automated, efficient coverage vs. manual point measurements

## Products & Capabilities Described
**L-band Soil Moisture Sensor**
- Measurement depth: 5-7 cm
- Technology type: L-band radar
- Application: Rapid, automated soil integrity mapping
- Advantage: Replaces inefficient manual methods and localized point measurements (Dynamic Cone Penetrometer, Rapid Assault Zone Terminal Evaluation Kit)

## Technical Justification
- Related peer-reviewed research demonstrates that L-band soil moisture measurement with soil type knowledge is sufficient for trafficability assessment
- Two cited studies validate the approach:
  - Flores et al. (2013) on hillslope-scale soil moisture data assimilation for military trafficability
  - Carranza et al. (2019) on monitoring field trafficability using Sentinel-1 radar
