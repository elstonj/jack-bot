# SBIR N251-016 Letter of Commitment - Ultra Maritime USA

- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy (SBIR Program)
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Topic N251-016
- Date: 27 March 2026
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: A-size sonobuoy-launched UAV
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (BST), Todd A. Schill (Ultra Maritime Director of Contracts)

## Executive Summary
Ultra Maritime USA commits to serve as a subcontractor to Black Swift Technologies on a Navy SBIR Phase II project aimed at integrating expendable acoustic sensors into an A-size, sonobuoy-launched UAV. Ultra Maritime will provide design consultation, prototype fabrication, and engineering support for the acoustic and electric field sensor package, with anticipated total contract value of $420,000 (base $300K + options $120K).

## Technical Approach
The project proposes integrating an expendable acoustic sensor package into a sonobuoy-launched UAV platform. Key technical workstreams include:

1. **Design Consultation**: Finalize specifications for acoustic and E-field sensors (frequency, range, sensitivity, deployment requirements)
2. **Physical Architecture Development**: Design suspension, packaging, and deployment scheme; estimate weight and power requirements
3. **Electronics & Software Design**: Develop acoustic sensor electronics, electric field sensor electronics, data acquisition electronics, COTS computing/processing, and data acquisition/ethernet software
4. **Fabrication**: Build 2 prototype sensor systems for integration and testing
5. **System Testing**: Conduct testing at Ultra Maritime lab, field testing in Lapel, and Black Swift site testing with data analysis

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift UAV Platform
- **What it is**: A-size sonobuoy-launched unmanned aerial system (UAS)
- **Proposed use**: Delivery and deployment platform for expendable acoustic sensor package
- **Role in project**: Primary vehicle for sensor payload integration and field testing

### Acoustic Sensor Package
- **What it is**: Expendable acoustic sensing system with associated electronics
- **Components**: Acoustic sensor, electric field sensors, suspension system, data acquisition electronics, COTS computing/processing elements
- **Specifications**: To be finalized during Phase II (frequency, range, sensitivity)
- **Power/Weight**: To be estimated during design phase
