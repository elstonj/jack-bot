## Executive Summary
This report documents Black Swift Technologies' use of a $240,000 Colorado Advanced Industry Accelerator Grant (July 2015 - May 2017) to reduce market entry barriers and accelerate deployment of the SuperSwift sUAS, SwiftCore Flight Management System, and soil moisture mapping radiometer. BST successfully focused efforts on manufacturability improvements, system demonstration across multiple application domains, and expansion of SwiftCore to support third-party applications. The grant resulted in 5.0 FTE jobs retained in Colorado and generated $316,924 in follow-on funding from NASA and university partners.

## Technical Approach

### SuperSwift sUAS Development
- Focused on transitioning from prototype to commercially viable system through improved manufacturing processes and supplier qualification
- Worked with multiple composite manufacturers to achieve desired cost, consistency, and reliability targets
- Modified airframe design to accommodate new sensor packages (nose cone redesign completed for new applications)
- Implemented modular payload architecture to enable rapid adaptation to different mission requirements

### L-band Radiometer Manufacturing
- Second-generation design completed to enable scaled production
- Addressed manufacturability issues from first version that required extensive manual work, testing, and tuning
- Improved design-for-manufacturing approach implemented with ongoing field validation planned for summer 2017

### SwiftCore Flight Management System Expansion
- Adapted system to accommodate additional airframes, flight modes, and sensors
- Tailored features and testing specifically for Colorado's mountainous, high-altitude terrain challenges
- Enabled integration with third-party aircraft platforms

## Products & Capabilities Described

- **What it is**: Small unmanned aircraft system designed for scientific research and commercial applications
- **Capabilities**:

## Use Cases & Applications

### Satellite Calibration and Validation (Sat Cal/Val)
- Multi-Angle Imaging Bidirectional Reflectance Distribution Function (MALIBU) payload integration with SuperSwift
- Partnership with Dr. Miguel O. Román at NASA GSFC
- Planned worldwide deployment to assist with satellite validation
- Upgraded MALIBU payload: transitioning from Tetracam (1.3 MP, ~1 fps) to MAPIR Kernel camera array (3.0 MP, 11 fps capture rate)
- Benefits: Doubled resolution, 1 lb weight reduction, improved capture rate, automatic geo-tagging, open-source linux control architecture

### Precision Agriculture (Project Drought)
- Partnership with University of Colorado at Irrigation Research Foundation (IRF) in Eastern Colorado
- Goal: Use soil moisture data to optimize variable-rate irrigation
- Benefit: Reduce water usage while maintaining/improving crop yields
- Potential customers identified: Oklahoma State University, St. Louis University, University of Bristol, Colorado State University, Monash University (Australia), National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency

### Wildland Firefighting / Wildfire Monitoring
- Partnership with University of Colorado on NOAA FIREX project
- Planned integration of swappable wildfire monitoring payload
- Proof-of-concept flight campaign funding secured
- Path to expanded NOAA funding through end of FIREX project (2019)

### Lidar Surveying and Mapping
- Partnership with Wohnrade Civil Engineers, Inc. (Broomfield, CO)
- Requirements met:
  - Terrain crawling and collision avoidance
  - Survey-grade accuracy (1 cm XYZ)
  - 100 acres/flight mapping area
  - High-resolution orthomosaic generation
  - In-flight image geotagging
  - Future expansion: uranium detection, thermal imaging

### Avalanche Monitoring and High-Altitude Operations
- Engagement with Colorado Avalanche Information Center (CAIC)
- Operational requirements specified:
  - Operation to 15,000 MSL or higher
  - Temperature range: -20°F to 80°F
  - Wind capability: 50+ mph
  - Endurance: 1.5+ hours
  - Payload: 5+ lbs minimum
  - Use case: Aerial reconnaissance and avalanche damage assessment
