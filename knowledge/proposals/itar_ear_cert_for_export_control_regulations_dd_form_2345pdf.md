# ITAR/EAR Certification for Export Control Regulations DD Form 2345 - Instructions

## Document Metadata
- **Type**: Instructional/Regulatory Guidance Document
- **Subject**: DD Form 2345 completion instructions for Joint Certification Program (JCP)
- **Regulatory Authority**: DoD Directive 5230.25, International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR), Commerce Control List (CCL)
- **Date**: 2020 (modified 2020-09-14, created 2021-02-03)

## Executive Summary
This is a comprehensive instruction guide for completing DD Form 2345 (Militarily Critical Technical Data Agreement), required by entities seeking to access, handle, or exchange DoD unclassified export-controlled technical data. The document applies to both U.S. and Canadian vendors and covers certification requirements, data custodian responsibilities, and compliance obligations under ITAR/EAR regulations.

## Purpose of JCP Certification
Certification under DoD Directive 5230.25 is required to:
1. Handle/access DoD/DND unclassified export-controlled technical data
2. Respond to DoD solicitations involving export-controlled data
3. Attend conferences/meetings with export-controlled material
4. Exchange export-controlled technical data between vendors
5. Obtain RFP details with export-controlled data
6. Participate in DoD R&D projects involving export-controlled data
7. Participate in Directly Arranged Visits (DAV) between Canada and U.S.

## Key Form Requirements

### Block 1: Type of Submission
- **Initial Submission**: First-time JCP certification request
- **Revision**: Updates to existing certification (entity name, address, CAGE code, data custodian, contact info, business activity, certifications)
- **Five-Year Renewal**: For certifications expiring or expired (may submit up to 120 days prior to expiration)

### Block 2: Entity/Individual Data (Mandatory)
- Legal business name (must match CAGE/NCAGE database exactly)
- Physical address (P.O. Box not acceptable; must match CAGE records)
- CAGE Code (must be active status prior to JCP submission)
- Company must have DUNS number
- Entity must have current registration in System for Award Management (SAM)

**Verification Links**:
- U.S. CAGE verification: https://cage.dla.mil
- Canadian NCAGE verification: https://eportal.nspa.nato.int/AC135Public/scage/CageList.aspx

### Block 3: Data Custodian or Export Control Security Manager (Mandatory)
- One individual per physical CAGE location
- Sole responsibility for downloading, receiving, disseminating, or sharing DoD unclassified export-controlled technical data
- Must provide: Name, telephone number, title, email address
- **Citizenship restriction**: Canadian citizen cannot be U.S. vendor's data custodian; U.S. citizen cannot be Canadian vendor's data custodian
- Any change of data custodians requires completion of "Introduction to Proper Handling of DoD Export-Controlled Technical Data" training by new custodian

### Block 4: Description of Relevant Business Activity

**For U.S. Vendors** (11 required elements):
1. Prime Contractor or Sub-Contractor status (if applicable, include Prime's CAGE code)
2. Business type (manufacturer, distributor, data broker, consultant, university, other)
3. Equipment/material/service types with NAICS/FSC codes
4. For non-manufacturers: CAGE and company names of manufacturers
5. USML (United States Munitions List) or CCL (Commerce Control List) applicability with category
6. DDTC registration status (required if handling USML items under ITAR)
7. Export Control License possession and status
8. System access requirements (Beta.SAM.gov, DIBBS, DTIC, or none)
9. Purpose for JCP certification access (bidding, data exchange, conference attendance, R&D, other)
10. Who is requiring certification (Prime, government contract specialist, etc.)
11. Specific systems requiring access and user IDs if applicable

**For Canadian Vendors** (9 required elements):
1. Prime Contractor or Sub-Contractor status
2. Business type
3. Equipment/material/service types with NAICS/FSC codes
4. Registration with Canadian Controlled Goods Program (CCGP) if applicable, with registration number
5. USML or CCL applicability with category
6. Export Control License status
7. Purpose for JCP certification
8. System access requirements
9. Who is requiring certification

**Important**: All information must fit in Block 4 space; attachments concerning Block 4 not allowed; no proprietary, personal, or entity-sensitive information.

### Block 5: Citizenship/Residency Status & Contractor Certifications (Mandatory)
Entity must certify:
- **5a**: Location (United States or Canada)
- **5b**: Data used only per DoD Directive 5230.25 or Technical Data Control Regulations (TDCR); needed to bid/perform government contracts
- **5c**: Acknowledgment of responsibility under all applicable export control laws (U.S. and Canadian); violation risks revocation of JCP certification and severe civil/criminal penalties
- **5d**: Access limited to employees/eligible persons meeting citizenship requirements; no public disclosure without authorization
- **5e**: No employees with access have been debarred, suspended, or violated Canadian/U.S. export control laws
- **5f**: Organization not debarred, suspended, or ineligible; has not violated export control laws/regulations

### Block 6: Contractor Certification (Mandatory)
- **6a**: Typed name of individual who can legally obligate enterprise
- **6b**: Individual's title (for universities, must show relationship to Office of Research and Grants)
- **6c**: Original handwritten signature (stamped or digital signatures NOT acceptable)
- **6d**: Date signed

## Data Custodian Training Requirement (Mandatory)
- "Introduction to Proper Handling of DoD Export-Controlled Technical Data" training mandatory before JCP certification approval
- Available at: https://www.dla.mil/HQ/LogisticsOperations/Services/JCP/ (DLA Requirements section)
- Or direct link: https://www.dla.mil/Portals/104/Documents/J3LogisticOperations/EnhancedValidation/J3_expctrltrainin g_191004.pdf
- Data Custodian must complete training, fill out "Certification Statement of Export Control Compliance," sign, and scan to PDF (verify citizenship box checked in item #4)

## Supporting Documentation Requirements
- **U.S. companies**: No longer required, BUT if company possesses Export Control License, must submit it
- **Canadian companies**: Required to prove registered business entity status with government agency
- Documentation must include entity name and issuing state/province, county, or city
- **W-9 forms NOT acceptable**
- **Prohibited**: No personal information (SSN, passport, bank account numbers, etc.)

**Acceptable supporting documentation**:
- Sales tax license
- Business license
- Filed articles of incorporation (with date stamp or letter confirming filing with state)

## DDTC/Export License Documentation
Per 22 CFR §120-130 (International Traffic in Arms Regulations):

**Required declarations**:
1. Is equipment/material/service related to USML or CCL? If yes, state category per 22 CFR §121.1
2. Are you registered with DDTC? If yes, provide registration code and expiration date
3. Do you possess Export Control License? If yes, provide documentation, license number, and expiration date

**Regulatory Resources**:
- DDTC Public Portal: https://www.pmddtc.state.gov/
- U.S. Munitions List: https://www.ecfr.gov/cgi-bin/retrieveECFR?gp=&SID=70e390c181ea17f847fa696c47e3140a&mc=true&r=PART&n=pt22.1.121

## Submission Process
1. Complete fillable DD Form 2345 (Blocks 1-6b)
2. Print form
3. **Wet sign and date** (Blocks 6c and 6d) - original handwritten signature required
4. Scan signed form as PDF
5. Email package with DD Form 2345 PDF and supporting documentation to: **JCP-ADMIN@DLA.MIL**
   - Subject line: Include CAGE and indicate "New" or "Renewal"
   - For expedited service, mark subject line accordingly
6. No longer accepting hard copy documents via USPS, FedEx, or UPS

## Certification Validity
- Data Custodian receives electronically signed approval letter with certification number
- Unless specifically annotated in Block 7C, certification valid for **five years**
- Renewal requests may be submitted up to 120 days prior to expiration

## Notable Compliance Points
- Any changes to entity status/data during certification period require REVISION submission with supporting documentation
- Violations of export control laws subject to severe civil and criminal penalties
- Violation of agreement provisions may result in:
  - Revocation of JCP certification eligibility
  - Administrative action by contracting authority
  - Severe penalties for unauthorized export
- Entity responsible for ensuring SAM registration remains current per FAR Subpart 4.11 and 2 CFR §25.310
