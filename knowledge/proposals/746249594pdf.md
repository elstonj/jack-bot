## Executive Summary
This is a 2013 Geological Society special publication compiling peer-reviewed chapters on remote sensing techniques for volcano monitoring and volcanic process observation. It covers multiple remote sensing approaches including InSAR, thermal infrared, satellite-based gas detection, and airborne sampling methods for volcano deformation, thermal anomalies, volcanic gas emissions, and eruption forecasting.

## Technical Approach
The publication covers diverse technical approaches to volcano remote sensing:
- **InSAR (Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar)**: Ground deformation monitoring at tropical and mid-latitude volcanoes
- **Thermal infrared remote sensing**: Detection and monitoring of volcanic thermal anomalies and hotspots
- **Satellite instruments**: OMI (Ozone Monitoring Instrument) for volcanic SO₂ degassing; ASTER and MODVOLC for thermal monitoring
- **Airborne thermal sensing**: Aircraft-based thermal imaging of lava domes
- **In situ airborne sampling**: Unmanned aircraft for direct collection of volcanic emissions

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **InSAR systems**: Multi-year deformation monitoring (e.g., 15-year time series at Nyamulagira)
- **Thermal satellites**: ASTER, MODVOLC for detecting volcanic hotspots across large geographic areas (2000-2010 Central/Southern Andes survey)
- **OMI**: Global volcanic SO₂ detection and quantification
- **Airborne platforms**: NASA/UCR unmanned aircraft for in situ volcanic gas and emission sampling

## Use Cases & Applications
- Volcano deformation monitoring and eruption forecasting
- Thermal anomaly detection and tracking

- Volcanic plume and ash property characterization
- Lava dome thermal monitoring (Volcan de Colima, Mexico case study)
