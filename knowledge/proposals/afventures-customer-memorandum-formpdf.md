# AFVentures Customer Memorandum Form

## Document Metadata
- Type: Form template / administrative guidance
- Client/Agency: U.S. Department of the Air Force (DAF), Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL)
- Program/Solicitation: AFVentures SBIR/STTR Phase II Open Topic Program
- Date: Document created 2023-10-23; modified 2023-02-10
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is a mandatory template and instruction document for the Air Force's AFVentures SBIR/STTR Phase II Open Topic program. It provides the Customer Memorandum form that small businesses (including BST if applying under this program) must complete and obtain DAF signatory approval for when pursuing Phase II funding. The form documents defense mission needs, project timelines, funding commitments, stakeholder roles, transition strategies, and security/cybersecurity requirements.

## Document Purpose
This is a blank form template with comprehensive instructions for applicants. It is not a proposal or report on a specific BST project, but rather the administrative framework BST would use if submitting a Phase II proposal under AFVentures.

## Key Sections & Requirements

### Defense Mission Need (Section 2)
- Applicant must articulate specific national defense-related mission need
- Must describe impact on U.S. Air Force and/or U.S. Space Force
- Must identify measurable goals (up to 5+)
- Critical for determining appropriate funding appropriation

### Project Timeline (Section 3)
- Start and end dates must align with solicitation guidance
- May be shorter than solicitation-specified period

### Phase II Funding Commitment (Section 4)
- Non-SBIR/STTR Government funding is optional but may be committed
- If yes, partner must provide DRAFT funding documents (Form 9s/MIPRs) within 45 days of solicitation closing
- CERTIFIED funding documents due within 5 business days after selection letters sent
- Commitment does not obligate funds; subject to availability at contract execution
- Email AFRL.SBIR.STTR@us.af.mil to initiate funds transfer

### Stakeholders (Section 8)
Required stakeholder designations:
- Primary End-User Organization (will use/benefit from solution)
- Primary Customer Organization (will manage procurement, funding, contracting)
- Primary Phase II Technical Point of Contact (TPOC) – **must be DAF military or civilian**
- Alternate TPOC
- Strategic Stakeholders (MAJCOMs, PEOs, Centers)
- Staff Functions Concurrence: Security, IA/Cybersecurity, Finance, Contracting, Strategy/Requirements, Small Business, Commander, Legal, Engineering/SPO/Test, Public Affairs

**TPOC Responsibilities:**
- Receive/accept/reject deliverables
- Provide performance and progress feedback
- Overcome tactical execution barriers (base access, waivers, certifications)
- Drive Phase III transition if successful

### Phase III Transition Strategy (Section 9)
- Core SBIR/STTR objective is transitioning small business innovations to Phase III
- Applicant must describe tentative transition strategy assuming successful Phase II
- Must address: Phase III funding strategy, requirements personnel role, contract vehicle, integration plan
- Must be tailored to specific organization/solution (no boilerplate language)
- Phase IIIs subject to acquisition planning, funds availability, security assessments, SB/Legal/Contracting review

### Security Requirements (Section 10)
Three security elements to address:
1. **Military Installation Access:** Will contractor need installation/facility access?
   - TPOC will work with visitor centers/security offices to sponsor individuals
2. **Common Access Cards (CACs):** Will contractor need CAC access to DoD networks?
   - Security Office confirms it will process CAC documentation if awarded
3. **Classified Accesses (DD254):** Will contractor require classified information access?
   - Security Office drafts DD254; works with company to obtain required accesses

### Cybersecurity (Section 11)
- If solution involves software/IT connecting to DoD networks/systems or utilizing CUI data, must complete this section
- IA/Cybersecurity Office will determine if Authorization to Operate (ATO) or Interim Authorization To Test (IATT) required
- Requirements based on NIST SP 800-171 Rev 1 and DFARS PGI 239.76
- May utilize Platform One DevSecOps environments
- **AFVentures does not manage cybersecurity processes** – customer/TPOC responsible

### Flight Testing / UAS (Section 12)
- Defines flight testing: any controlled/maneuvered aerial transport including ballistic trajectories excluded
- Includes: balloon flight, manned/unmanned aircraft, air taxis, cockpit evaluations, ground tests
- Blanket waiver exists for AFRL/DO to procure COTS UAS for cyber analysis, signature analysis, system checkout, profile data in benign/controlled RF environments
- Contractors must also pursue "use waiver" through TPOC
- Contract execution not dependent on waiver completion, but early coordination essential

### Signatories (Section 13)
- **Two CAC-enabled digital signatures required** – memorandum without both signatures will NOT be accepted
- DAF Customer signatory and DAF End-User signatory must be different individuals
- Both must be in positions to speak for their organizations (understand/influence operational needs, requirements, or funding)
- Brief justification of signatory suitability required for each

## Critical Compliance Notes
- **ALL elements MUST be present and complete**
- All fields must be filled in; if not applicable, enter "N/A"
- Use of this template is **MANDATORY**
- Proposals without completed, signed memorandum per this template = **non-responsive and not considered for award**
- Form instructions span pages 10-16 with detailed guidance on each section

## Attachments Required
1. Attachment 1.1: Schedule of Milestones (required)
2. Attachment 1.2: Non-Proprietary Work Plan (required)
3. Attachment 1.3: TPOC Designation Letter (informational; not included in signed memo)
4. Attachment 1.4: SBIR/STTR Definitions and Suitability Resources (reference only)

## Notable Details
- **AFVentures does not manage security, cybersecurity, or ATO processes** – customer/TPOC responsible
- Small Business Office should advise on contracting/subcontracting opportunities
- No obligation for Phase III follow-on contracts
- Flexible timeline: applicant can propose shorter period than solicitation specifies
- Funding documents template and guidance available via automated response from AFRL.SBIR.STTR@us.af.mil
