## Executive Summary
This is a **template document** for Black Swift Technologies or other SBIR applicants to follow when preparing a Direct-to-Phase II Technical Volume (Volume II) proposal in response to an Air Force SBIR solicitation. The document provides comprehensive instructions, formatting requirements, section outlines, compliance questionnaires, and example milestone schedules for Phase II proposals addressing Air Force end-user needs.

## Document Structure & Content

### Mandatory Proposal Sections

**1. Technical Summary**
- Problem/opportunity identification and relationship to Phase I feasibility
- Identification of Air Force or Space Force beneficiary
- Description of non-Defense commercial solution with technical specs and adoption metrics
- Proposed R&D adaptation of commercial solution to meet government needs
- Phase I feasibility study results including AF end-user and customer exploration
- Compliance and regulatory activities questionnaire (flight testing, hardware delivery, hazardous materials, human subjects research, etc.)
- Phase II technical objectives (3-5) with measurable key results (3-5 per objective)
- Stakeholder requirements and trial completion dependencies

**2. Phase II Work Plan**
- Scope: Major requirements and specifications
- Task outline with clear delineation between SBIR-funded and private-investment funded tasks
- Milestone schedule with deliverables, acceptance criteria, and payment amounts
- Deliverables (hardware, software, data, schedules, quantities)
- Unique Item Identification requirements (IAW DFARS 252.211-7003)
- Required reports (Final Report, Quarterly Status Reports, Phase II Summary Report, Safety Documentation)

**3. Commercialization Strategy**
Must address:
- Company information (core competencies, product sales history, federal/non-federal funding, regulatory experience)
- Customer and competition analysis
- Market analysis (milestones, market size, target market share)
- Intellectual property protection strategy
- Financing plans
- Technical or business assistance/mentoring needs
- Specific answers to 10 key commercialization questions (first product to market, customer identification, market size, competitors, private funding sources, foreign investment, Phase III commitments, commercial indicators, revenue from non-Defense solutions, transition plans)
- Military applications and DoD customer identification
- Foreign national disclosures

**4. Key Personnel**
- Principal Investigator and key personnel with:
  - Education credentials (degree, major, university)
  - Relevant experience (2-3 sentences emphasizing commercialization)
  - Citizenship status
  - Technical resumes for PI including publications
- Subcontractors and consultants

**5. Related Work**
- Significant related activities and programs by PI, firm, consultants
- Prior commercialization results
- State-of-the-art knowledge
- Related Government efforts (SBIR or otherwise)

## Formatting Requirements
- Type: Minimum 10-point font
- Paper: Standard 8.5" × 11"
- Margins: One-inch minimum
- Page limit: 15 pages maximum for Technical Volume
- Status reports: Maximum 15 slides per quarterly report
- Phase II Summary Report: 700 words maximum

## Compliance & Regulatory Requirements
Proposal must address 13 compliance questions covering:
1. Flight testing (indoor/outdoor, manned/unmanned)
2. Test facility/location
3. Hardware/software delivery to government
4. Government personnel/facilities/equipment use
5. Weapons/munitions testing
6. Explosives/propellants/ammunition use
7. Directed energy devices or RF radiation
8. Hazardous materials
9. Animal testing
10. Human subject research (32 CFR 219)
11. Toxins, human-derived materials, recombinant DNA
12. Human interaction/intervention
13. Access to human data/biospecimens

## Safety Requirements
- Preliminary Hazard Analysis required if hardware developed
- Hazardous Material Report if applicable (identification, purpose, alternatives/safeguards)
- Compliance with Mil Std-882E requirements
- Government safety review required for use of GFE (approximately 4-week review timeline)

## Required Deliverables & Reports
1. **Final Report:** Draft due 30 days after Phase II completion; includes single-page non-proprietary summary, objectives met, results, technical feasibility
2. **Quarterly Status Reports:** Minimum quarterly; 15-slide maximum; must include progress metrics toward objectives (quantified 0-1 scale)
3. **Phase II Summary Report:** At technical completion; 700 words maximum; submitted to AF CO/AO
4. **Additional Documentation:** Software documentation, engineering drawings, O&M documentation, safety hazard analysis, updated commercialization results
5. **For Private Funding:** Accounting of investor fund expenditures in final status report

## Customer Memorandum Requirements (Mandatory)
- **Required:** Signed Customer Memorandum from empowered, committed Air Force end-users and customers
- **Template:** Must follow mandatory AF template (Attachment 1)
- **Minimum:** One AF/Joint end-user AND one AF/Joint customer
- **Alternative:** US non-AF Government end-users acceptable but AF commitment mandatory
- **Content:** Must clearly articulate validated Air Force need and mutual agreement on milestone schedule
- **Consequence:** Proposals without signed Customer Memorandum will not be considered

## Key Commercialization Questions (Must Address)
1. First product to incorporate technology
2. Customer identification and estimated market size; funding needed; funding source
3. Company marketing expertise or acquisition plan
4. Competitor analysis and price/quality advantages
5. Private sector funding commitments (VC, angel, state/local, internal)
6. Foreign investment disclosure
7. Phase III Government or commercial follow-on commitments
8. Other commercial indicators (pre-sales, pilots, revenue, users, downloads, adoption)
9. Last 12 months' non-Defense commercial solution revenue
10. Phase III transition plan with potential Government customer

## Notable Details
- **Boilerplate Template:** This is a template/instruction document with sample milestone schedule; actual technical content varies by proposing company
- **Flexibility:** Numbered sections show areas for company-specific insertion (proposal number, topic number, CAGE code, company logo)
- **Emphasis on End-User Engagement:** Strong emphasis on AF end-user validation, commitment, and involvement throughout Phase II
- **Dual-Use Focus:** Proposals must demonstrate both Defense (AF/Space Force) and non-Defense commercial market potential
- **Foreign National Restrictions:** Export-controlled projects restrict foreign nationals from key positions; public domain work only
- **Cost Share Options:** Proposals may include private investment cost match with clear task delineation
