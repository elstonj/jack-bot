## Executive Summary
This document outlines NASA's role in supporting the Next Generation Air Transportation System (NextGen), a comprehensive modernization initiative to transform the U.S. National Airspace System (NAS). Written in the context of NextGen's development timeline (Epochs 1-3, spanning 2006-2025), the paper describes how NASA's Aeronautics Research Mission Directorate (ARMD) contributes to NextGen's key characteristics and capabilities through three core research programs: Airspace Systems, Aviation Safety, and Fundamental Aeronautics.

## Technical Approach
NASA's approach is structured around three core principles:
1. Mastering core competencies in aeronautics for the Nation across all flight regimes
2. Focusing research in areas appropriate to NASA's unique capabilities
3. Directly addressing fundamental NextGen research needs while collaborating with JPDO partner agencies

The research portfolio is organized to address air traffic management, environmental compliance, efficiency, and safety challenges. NASA contributes approximately $18 million annually in labor and procurement to JPDO planning and development activities, including staffing of the JPDO Deputy Director and division leadership roles.

### NASA Programs and Their Focus Areas:

**1. Airspace Systems Program (Two Projects)**
- **NGATS ATM-Airspace Project:**
  - Develops fundamental concepts for optimal allocation of ground/air automation
  - Focus: Where, when, how, and extent of automation application
  - Key research areas:
    - Trajectory-based operations with 4D trajectory accuracy
    - Trajectory prediction, synthesis, and uncertainty management
    - Separation assurance and automated sequential processing/merging
    - Traffic Flow Management (TFM) with 2-3x current capacity handling
    - Dynamic airspace configuration
    - Performance-based services
    - Super-density operations near congested airports
    - System analysis and design tools

- **NGATS ATM-Airportal Project:**
  - Develops trajectory-based automation for surface operations
  - Key research areas:
    - Safe and efficient all-weather surface operations
    - Runway incursion minimization
    - Arrival/departure separation reduction
    - Metroplex (regional inter-airport) operations optimization
    - Coordinated arrival/departure operations
    - Airportal transition and integration management

**2. Fundamental Aeronautics Program (Three of four projects contribute to NextGen)**
- **Subsonic Fixed Wing Project:**
  - Noise reduction technologies (conventional and unconventional configs)
  - Emissions reduction (NOx, CO2, particulates)
  - Alternative fuels development
  - Noise and emissions prediction capabilities
  
- **Subsonic Rotary Wing Project:**
  - Similar environmental focus for helicopter/rotorcraft applications
  
- **Supersonics Project:**
  - Low-boom, low-drag supersonic configurations
  - Advanced aircraft concepts for enhanced capacity

**3. Aviation Safety Program (Four Projects)**
- **Integrated Vehicle Health Management (IVHM):**
  - Advanced health management technologies
  - Self-diagnostic and self-correcting onboard systems
  - Data mining tools for distributed aviation-safety data sources
  - Prognostic safety analysis capabilities

- **Integrated Intelligent Flight Deck (IIFD):**
  - Crew workload optimization
  - Situational awareness adaptation for NextGen environment

- **Integrated Resilient Aircraft Control (IRAC):**
  - Aircraft flight control automation/autonomy
  - Loss-of-control prevention
  - Fault, damage, and upset tolerance

- **Aircraft Aging and Durability (AAD):**
  - Advanced diagnostic and prognostic capabilities
  - Aging-related hazard detection and mitigation

## Use Cases & Applications

### NextGen Operational Scenarios:
- **Capacity Growth:** Handling 2-3x current traffic demand by 2025
- **Trajectory-Based Operations:** Gate-to-gate 4D commitments including gate identification, pushback time, takeoff time, complete airspace trajectory, touchdown time, gate arrival time
- **Mixed Fleet Operations:** Accommodating airline operations, air taxi operations, general aviation, and unmanned air vehicles
- **Dynamic Airspace:** Real-time airspace reconfiguration to balance workload, capacity, and demand
- **Weather Integration:** Integrated digital, probabilistic weather information in automation and decision-support tools
- **Super-Density Airport Operations:** Increased throughput at congested hub airports through reduced separation minima
- **Surface Operations:** All-weather runway complex optimization with efficient gate, taxiway, runway, and final approach management
- **Environmental Compliance:** Noise and emissions management for en route and airport operations
- **Safety-Critical Operations:** Enhanced aircraft health monitoring and prognostic safety analysis for higher-volume operations

## Notable Details

### NextGen Vision & Timeline:
- **Three Epochs:** Epoch 1 (FY06-11 develop, FY10-15 implement), Epoch 2 (FY12-17 develop, FY14-19 implement), Epoch 3 (FY18-21 develop, FY20-25 implement)
- **NextGen Key Characteristics:** User focus, distributed decision-making, human/automation balance, scalability, robustness/resiliency, integrated safety management, environmental framework
- **NextGen Key Capabilities:** Network-enabled information access, performance-based operations, weather assimilation, layered adaptive security, PNT services, trajectory-based operations, equivalent visual operations, super-density operations

### Critical Research Challenges Requiring Resolution:
- Whether separation assurance should be performed by ground-based navigation service provider or aircraft
- Human vs. automation management and control responsibilities
- Uncertainty impacts on traffic flow management (particularly weather)
- Stability of aircraft gate-to-gate 4D trajectories
- Development of prognostic analysis to identify inadvertent safety impacts of NextGen concepts
- Aircraft efficiency and performance within environmental compliance constraints

### Current System Problems Addressed:
- **Capacity Limitations:** Current system already strained; forecast demand increase of 2-3x by 2025 unsustainable with existing infrastructure
- **Cost Impact:** Projected shortfall estimated at $9.2-23 billion annually in lost productivity, increased operational costs, higher fares, and eliminated flights
- **Rigidity:** Current airspace structure is rigid, restricts flexibility, increases queuing delays during weather
- **Mixed Fleet Challenges:** Cannot currently accommodate diverse aircraft types and equipage levels
- **Environmental Issues:** Noise and emissions constraining airport growth; en route emissions becoming problematic
- **Safety Approach:** Currently reactive rather than proactive; based on prescriptive rule compliance

### Organizational Structure:
- **JPDO Member Agencies:** DOT, DOD, DOC, DHS, FAA, NASA
- **NASA's ARMD Structure:** Three primary programs (Airspace Systems, Aviation Safety, Fundamental Aeronautics) plus supporting research
- **NASA Personnel in JPDO:** Deputy Director, Systems Engineering and Analysis Division Director, Enterprise Architecture and Engineering Division Director, representatives on executive council and multiple Independent Product Teams

### Technology Transition Approach:
Document emphasizes close collaboration between NASA researchers and FAA/user community throughout development to ensure effective transition from R&D to operational implementation. Notes tension between pressure to show quick near-term results (Epochs 1-2) versus necessity of long-term research investment (Epoch 3) for transformational NextGen capabilities.
