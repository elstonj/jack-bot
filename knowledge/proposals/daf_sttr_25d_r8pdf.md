## Notable Details

### Proposal Structure Requirements
- **Volume 1**: DoD Proposal Cover Sheet with technical abstract
- **Volume 2**: Technical Volume (max 20 pages for most topics, 6 months PoP for T007-T011)
- **Volume 3**: Cost Volume with detailed itemization
- **Volume 4**: Company Commercialization Report
- **Volume 5**: Supporting Documents
- **Volume 6**: Fraud, Waste, and Abuse Training Certification
- **Volume 7**: Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries

### Funding & Performance Parameters
- **Phase I Maximum Value**: $200,000
- **Phase I Performance Period (PoP)**: 3-6 months depending on topic
- **Phase II**: 18-20 months (typical)
- **STTR Performance Requirements**: Minimum 40% of work by small business concern; minimum 30% by single partnering research institution

### Key Submission Requirements
- All work must be performed in the United States (with rare exceptions requiring written DAF approval)
- Firms with majority ownership by multiple venture capital, hedge funds, or private equity firms are ineligible
- Technical volume must use 10-point font, 1-inch margins on 8.5" x 11" paper
- No proprietary information in technical abstracts (will be publicly released if selected)
- Foreign citizens must be identified with visa/work permit type and explanation of involvement level
- Estimated evaluation timeline: ~90 calendar days from solicitation close

### Security & Due Diligence
- DAF will conduct security risk assessments on all applicants per 15 USC 638(vv) and Deputy Secretary of Defense Memorandum (May 13, 2024)
- Assessment includes cybersecurity practices, patent analysis, employee analysis, and foreign ownership review
- DAF reserves right to reject proposals based on security risk assessment

### Support Infrastructure
- AF SBIR/STTR One Help Desk: usaf.team@afsbirsttr.us
- DoD SBIR/STTR Help Desk (DSIP system questions): dodsbirsupport@reisystems.com
- Contracting Officer: Mr. Daniel J. Brewer, Daniel.Brewer.13@us.af.mil

### Notable Administrative Details
- Proposals may be handled administratively by support contractors (TEC Solutions, APEX, Oasis Systems, Riverside Research, Peerless Technologies, HPC-COM, Mile Two, Montech, Wright Brothers Institute, MacB/Alion Company)
- Only Government employees and FFRDC personnel (MITRE, Aerospace Corporation) may evaluate proposals technically
- DAF does not participate in Discretionary Technical and Business Assistance (TABA) Program
- AF uses Phase I work plan as draft for Phase I Statement of Work; proprietary information should not be included
- Firms must maintain current email/mailing addresses; DAF not responsible for notification failures due to address changes
