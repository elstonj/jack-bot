# Letter of Commitment - Matt Fladeland

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Commitment / Funding Confirmation
- Client/Agency: NASA Ames Research Center
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR/STTR Civilian Commercialization Readiness Pilot Program (CCRPP), Application #2022-1-1118
- Date: January 10, 2022
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 UAS
- Key Personnel: Matt Fladeland (NASA Ames, Airborne Science Manager); Jack Elston (Black Swift Technologies)

## Executive Summary
NASA Ames Research Center commits $129,793 in Phase III grant funding for the acquisition and future use of Black Swift's S2 uncrewed aircraft system. The funding supports continued development and refinement of the S2 platform, which has demonstrated capability for gas measurements and IR mapping of remote volcanic systems in Alaska, with future applications to hazardous environments including volcanoes and wildfires.

## Technical Approach
- S2 UAS originally developed under NASA SBIR Phase II contract (NNX17CP13C) for "A ruggedized UAS for scientific data gathering in harsh environments"
- Current CCRPP funding focuses on system refinements to enable operations in high winds and improved data collection
- Platform proven for gas measurements and infrared (IR) mapping capabilities

## Products & Capabilities Described
**S2 UAS**
- Ruggedized uncrewed aircraft system designed for harsh environmental conditions
- Demonstrated capabilities: gas measurements, infrared (IR) mapping
- Recent validation: remote volcanic system monitoring in Alaska

## Use Cases & Applications
- Volcano monitoring and measurements (primary demonstrated use)
- Wildfire monitoring
- Operations in hazardous environments
- Remote scientific data gathering in harsh conditions
- High-wind operations (target for refinement)
