## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes deploying long-endurance UAS equipped with advanced methane sensing and wind measurement capabilities to detect and characterize methane emissions from oil and gas infrastructure. The project will partner with the Choctaw Nation Oklahoma (CNO) to demonstrate the technology and build capacity in rural and Indigenous communities, leveraging CNO's expanded Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) authorization under the FAA's UAS Integration Pilot Program.

## Technical Approach
- **Core Platform**: S2 UAS equipped with sophisticated wind measurement capabilities
- **Methane Sensor**: Highly sensitive methane sensor developed in collaboration with **Quanta3**
- **Data Analysis**: Innovative eddy covariance-based analysis in partnership with **Atmofacts** for:
  - Real-time methane plume characterization
  - Source pinpointing
  - Emission quantification
- **Advantage**: Bridges sensing gap between satellite, crewed aviation, and terrestrial measurements; provides enhanced data resolution and cost-effective leak localization

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- Long-endurance uncrewed aircraft system
- Equipped with wind measurement and methane detection sensors
- Proposed for BVLOS operations under Choctaw Nation's expanded authorization

### Methane Detection System
- Integrates Quanta3 methane sensor with S2 platform
- Capable of precision leak detection
- Can quantify natural methane emissions to reduce uncertainties

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Oil and gas infrastructure monitoring**: Routine monitoring and leak detection
- **Pipeline following**: Currently restricted operations that would be enabled by BVLOS authorization
- **Orphaned well detection**: Scanning large areas to detect leaking abandoned wells
- **Infrastructure inspections and environmental monitoring**: Enabled by expanded Choctaw Nation BVLOS operations
- **Natural methane emissions quantification**: Helps reduce uncertainties in emissions systems

## Project Partnerships & Capacity Building
- **Choctaw Nation Oklahoma (CNO)**: Primary operational partner
  - Will deploy S2 UAS equipped with Quanta3 sensor
  - Provides matching resources and infrastructure
  - Benefits from technology training and capacity building
  - Leverage of BEYOND program (continuation of FAA UAS Integration Pilot Program)
  - Access to expanded BVLOS authorization covering extensive operational area

## Matching Resources (Choctaw Nation Commitment)

- Radar (Harrier S200d BLOS) x2: $490,000 ($245,000 each)
- CNO C2 (Command & Control): $60,000
- **Total CNO Matching: $614,194**
- Additional: BST covering ~3-5 days travel to Boulder, CO for CNO team training

## Funding Requirements
- Project requires 1:2 Engine to non-Engine matching resources
- Exception: Teams collaborating directly with tribal or underserved communities may document less than 1:2 matching support
- Budget structure implications discussed regarding CNO designation as vendor vs. partner

- BVLOS capability expansion is critical for economic scaling—S2 can now cover much larger areas per flight, enabling routine monitoring operations previously restricted

- Integration of multiple third-party capabilities (Quanta3 sensors, Atmofacts analytics) demonstrates collaborative technical approach
- Emphasis on environmental benefit: enabling detection and quantification of methane emissions from both active and orphaned oil/gas infrastructure
