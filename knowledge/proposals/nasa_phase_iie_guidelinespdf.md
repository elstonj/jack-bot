# NASA Phase II-Enhancement Contracting Handbook

## Document Metadata
- Type: Contracting handbook / program guidelines
- Client/Agency: NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration)
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR/STTR Phase II-Enhancement (Phase II-E) Program
- Date: November 18, 2014
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None
- Key Personnel: Carlos Torrez (SBIR/STTR Business Manager, NASA Ames Research Center)

## Executive Summary
This NASA handbook provides comprehensive guidelines for small businesses with active Phase II SBIR/STTR contracts to apply for Phase II-Enhancement (Phase II-E) funding. The program matches external investor funds with NASA SBIR/STTR funds to extend Phase II projects for a minimum of 4 months, encouraging technology transition into NASA acquisition programs, other government agencies, and the private sector.

## Program Overview
The Phase II-Enhancement Option encourages small businesses to secure external investment (from venture capital, angel investors, other companies, or non-SBIR government programs) in their SBIR/STTR technologies, with NASA providing matching funds. The goal is to facilitate R&D advancement, technology infusion, and commercialization beyond the base Phase II contract.

## Key Program Parameters

**Eligibility & Timing:**
- Only active NASA SBIR/STTR Phase II contracts may apply
- Applications accepted anytime after the 13th month of Phase II performance through 60 days before contract expiration
- No extensions of Phase II period of performance allowed (results in ineligibility for Phase II-E)
- No guarantee of option exercise due to limited designated funds

**Funding & Matching:**
- NASA provides dollar-for-dollar matching of external investor funds up to applicable matching levels
- No upper limit on external investor contributions
- Minimum external investment required per current matching levels (varies by solicitation)
- Period of performance commensurate with total NASA and external investor funding
- Minimum 4-month extension period
- Phase II-E funding cannot cover incomplete or unsuccessful Phase II work

**External Investor Definition:**
Must be external to the NASA SBIR/STTR Program and may include:
- Other companies
- Venture capital firms
- Angel investors (individuals)
- Non-SBIR/non-STTR government programs
- Combinations of the above

**Excluded as External Investors:**
- Small business owners and family members
- Affiliates (as defined in 13 C.F.R. Section 121.103)
- SBIR/STTR project subcontractors
- The small business itself contributing internal funds

## What Qualifies as Investment (Detailed Examples)

**Acceptable Investment Forms:**
- Cash investment in exchange for equity, royalty share, technology rights, profit percentage, or combination
- Convertible loans from public entities (e.g., state agencies)
- Convertible loans from private entities if actually converted to equity before Phase II-E option exercise
- Existing contracts or grants directly related to Phase II-E work, if awarded after Phase II-E proposal submission (e.g., Phase III contracts for same technology)
- Multiple investors acceptable

**Non-Qualifying Arrangements:**
- Company's own internal funds
- Loans (non-convertible)
- In-kind contributions
- Purchase orders or procurement-based payments
- Investments from family members or affiliated entities
- Mutual/reciprocal investments between two small businesses
- Investments into university partners (funds must go to small business; company can then subcontract to university)
- Investments made during Phase I (must occur within commitment window around Phase II-E selection)

**Important Subtraction Rule:**
When external investor serves multiple roles (e.g., investing + subcontracting), only the net cash investment counts. Subcontracting payments and service fees are deducted from total investment amount.

## Application Requirements & Submission

**Submission Timeframe:**
- Applications submitted through NASA EHB Submission website during eligible contract period

**Required Application Components:**

1. **Phase II-E Application Form** (electronic submission):
   - Anticipated SBIR/STTR contribution amount
   - Total external investment amount
   - Proposed start and end dates
   - Justification for period of performance if longer than 12 months
   - NASA vs. non-NASA investment source identification
   - Mission Directorate supported (if NASA source)
   - Program/Project/Application identification
   - List of non-NASA investor(s)
   - Estimated final Technology Readiness Level (TRL)
   - Additional comments

2. **Letter of Commitment from External Investor:**
   - On official letterhead
   - Investor contact information (address, title, email, phone)
   - Statement of willingness to verify commitment
   - Total investment amount with acknowledgment it references specific Phase II effort
   - Acknowledgment that entire investment will be available within 45 business days of approval notification, without constraints
   - Statement of investment use:
     - For government programs: brief statement on technology integration into acquisition program and funding mechanism
     - For private sector: brief statement on how investment furthers Phase II technology or commercialization

3. **Statement of Work (SOW)** (PDF):
   - Specific Phase II-E objectives
   - Detailed work plan with specific tasks and methods
   - Performance schedules and project milestones
   - Deliverables
   - Clear separation of SBIR/STTR-funded work from externally-funded work
   - Demonstration of significant improvement over Phase II work

4. **Cost Proposal** (PDF with detailed breakdowns):
   - Section 1: Budget summary for SBIR/STTR matching funds
   - Section 2: Budget summary for external investment funds
   - Detailed cost elements consistent with company's cost accounting system

5. **Briefing Chart:**
   - Updated, non-proprietary information
   - May be freely distributed by Government

6. **Commercialization Plan** (PDF) addressing:
   - Market Feasibility and Competition: target markets, competitive advantages, key customers (including NASA and prime contractors), market size projections, time to market, 5-year market share estimate, competitive threats
   - Commercialization Strategy: relationship to company business plans (next 5 years), NASA mission infusion as option
   - Key Management and Personnel: skills/experience in commercialization, current organizational structure, plans for obtaining necessary expertise
   - Production and Operations: development status, milestones, production timeline, resource acquisition plans
   - Financial Planning: private financial resources committed, projected requirements, capital/funding sources, current financial statements including cash flow
   - Intellectual Property: plans and status for securing IP rights (patents, copyrights, trade secrets) for investment attraction, competitive advantage, and commercialization

## Exercise & Funding Process

**If Option Exercised:**
- Small business receives "Notice of Intent" from NASA Shared Services Center (NSSC)
- Notice does not commit Government funds or obligate exercise
- Small business must provide proof of external investor funds transfer within 45 business days of approval notification
- If proof not received within 45 days, Phase II-E matching funds option expires
- Modification executed via bilateral contract modification identifying option period and amount
- Work cannot commence until modification is executed

**If Option Not Exercised:**
- Firm receives notice from NSSC
- No debriefing or explanation provided (contract option clause, per FAR 52.217-9)

## Deficiency Remediation
- Small businesses notified of application/proposal deficiencies have 45 calendar days to remedy
- Failure to cure within timeframe results in non-selection

## Notable Details
- Matching levels and post-Phase II initiatives detailed at http://sbir.nasa.gov/content/post-phase-ii-initiatives
- NASA guidance heavily references 13 C.F.R. Section 121.103 (affiliation definitions) but reserves discretion in determining external investor eligibility
- Venture capital firms can invest additional funds if they don't control the company (own <50% pre-investment)
- Government investments require full funding within separate funding vehicle from base Phase II contract; funds must be transferred within 45 business days of notification
- All Phase II contractual rules and regulations apply to Phase II-E continuation
- Emphasis on external third-party validation of commercial potential as program rationale
