## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to adapt its soil moisture mapping sensor payload (proven with NASA and NOAA) for deployment on small UAS platforms (S2 fixed-wing or E2 multi-rotor) to provide high-resolution soil moisture data for USAF landing zone assessment and runway integrity validation globally. This capability enables forward air controllers to de-risk aircraft landing operations in unconventional locations worldwide by providing sub-15m pixel resolution soil strength measurements that were previously only available at low resolution from manned aircraft or satellites.

## Technical Approach
- **Platform**: Adaptation of BST's S2 (fixed-wing) or E2 (multi-rotor) UAS platforms, specifically designed for sensitive payload integration
- **Payload**: Soil Moisture Radiometer sensor (previously proven in NASA, NOAA, and commercial operations)
- **Data Resolution**: Sub-15m per pixel spatial resolution with high temporal resolution
- **Processing**: Machine learning-based algorithms to convert raw soil moisture measurements into soil strength models and actionable landing zone fidelity assessments
- **Integration**: Custom avionics, firmware, and payload interfaces tailored to AFRL/RQQ requirements
- **Technology Readiness**: Several foundational UAS technologies at TRL-9

## Use Cases & Applications

### Defense Applications
- **Primary**: Runway Integrity Validation / Landing Zone Assessment
  - Support for aircraft landing operations in unconventional, globally dispersed locations
  - De-risking USAF landing operations
  - Enhanced operational capability in potentially unstable terrain (riverine, wetlands, coastal regions)
  - Damage assessment post-thunderstorm or heavy precipitation
- **Secondary**: Army Corps of Engineers soil condition assessment for heavy vehicle tracking
- **Expansion Potential**: AFSOC/Special Operations applications with AFLCMC/SOF-ISR

### Non-Defense Commercial Applications
- Wildfire mitigation (USGS collaboration)
- Watershed characterization (NOAA collaboration)
- Turf/sod health monitoring (Turf Scout commercial partner)
- Construction operations (avoiding delays with heavy machinery on weak soil)
- Railway operations (preventing derailments from track movement when soil saturated)
- Flash flood and landslide prediction
- Emergency response operations
- Insurance risk assessment

### Soil Moisture-to-Soil Strength Connection
Document emphasizes that soil moisture is key variable for soil strength assessment: as soil moisture increases, soil becomes slippery and sticky while decreasing in load-bearing strength—critical for landing zone assessment and heavy vehicle operations.
