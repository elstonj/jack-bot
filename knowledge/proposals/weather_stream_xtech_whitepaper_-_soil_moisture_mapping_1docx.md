## Executive Summary
Weather Stream, in partnership with the University of Colorado Center for Environmental Technology and Black Swift Technologies, has developed patented remote sensing technology for measuring soil moisture and soil integrity from UAV, UGV, handheld, and stationary platforms. The technology combines a novel L-band microwave radiometer with COTS vegetation and thermal sensors plus a proprietary retrieval algorithm to produce high-resolution soil moisture maps, filling a critical Army meteorological data gap currently unmet by satellite systems or ground-based alternatives.

## Technical Approach

### Core Technology
- **L-band microwave radiometer**: Novel design developed by world-leading sensor development team
- **Sensor fusion**: Integrates radiometer data with commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) vegetation sensors and thermal sensors
- **Retrieval algorithm**: Physics and machine learning-based environmental algorithms to invert microwave thermal signals plus ancillary data (vegetation health, surface temperature) into soil moisture and soil integrity assessments

### Scientific Basis
- Built on decades of microwave remote sensing expertise (team has >100 years combined experience)
- Previous development funded by NASA and U.S. Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL)
- Operational deployments funded by NOAA and U.S. Geological Survey (USGS)
- Extensive experience managing critical sensor design considerations: system losses, amplifier noise, EMI, drift, and calibration stability

### Key Technical Risks & Mitigation
- Environmental hardening of sensor
- Potential EMI degradation (mitigation: adaptive spectrum techniques to find quiet spectrum regions)
- Antenna operating bandwidth enhancement
- Ground-based platform adaptation
- On-board processing and sensor fusion optimization
- All identified as Phase II development tasks targeting TRL 8
