## Executive Summary
BST contracted with Creare to develop a flight simulation environment for both Earth and Titan atmospheres, design flight control systems for Creare's Titan Ringlet drone, integrate avionics into a terrestrial prototype, and conduct flight testing to validate performance in multiple flight modes and autonomous mission scenarios.

## Technical Approach

**Flight Simulation Models**
- Extend BST's existing planetary flight simulation environment to model Titan and Earth atmospheres
- Develop unmanned aircraft models of Creare's ringlet drones (Titan variant and terrestrial prototype)

**Simulation and Flight Controls**
- Design and develop flight controller with tuned gains for takeoff, landing, and mission execution
- Customize existing attitude control algorithms for high-stability multi-rotor and tail-sitter aircraft
- Support multiple flight modes: vertical hovering (takeoff), transition to horizontal flight, horizontal flight, precision landing
- Use simulation to develop and tune flight controls for both Titan and terrestrial variants

**Hardware Integration**
- Integrate avionics into terrestrial prototype (provided by Creare: airframe, motors, speed controllers)
- Supply autopilot hardware and ground station hardware for integration

**Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) Simulation**
- Validate algorithms using actual hardware in simulated environment before physical flight testing

**Terrestrial Prototype Flight Testing**
- Develop detailed flight test plan with Creare
- Obtain flight permissions through NASA Airworthiness and Flight Safety Review Board and Flight Readiness Review Board
- Validation flights using existing sub-scale ring-wing prototypes at local sites with comprehensive data logging
- Final demonstration flights in Boulder, CO, validating all flight modes and executing autonomous mission analog to Titan operations
