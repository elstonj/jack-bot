## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies will serve as a subcontractor to BAE Systems (Information and Electronic Systems Integration Inc.) on the DARPA Albatross program, a 24-month effort to develop and demonstrate autonomous aircraft soaring capabilities. BST will provide a fleet of small uncrewed aircraft systems (s-UAS) and support integration of weather-informed mission planning tools, real-time wind sensing, and autonomous soaring control systems to harness wind energy and extend aircraft range and endurance.

## Technical Approach

**Program Philosophy**: Albatross is NOT an airframe design effort. Instead, it integrates planning tools, sensors, and control solutions on existing or quickly-assembled small uncrewed aircraft to autonomously identify and respond to soaring conditions.

**Core Technical Components**:

1. **Pre-flight Planning Perk (PF2P)**
   - Integrate terrain, land cover, weather, solar, and oceanic forecast data
   - Develop models to predict harvestable energy by geologic feature type and UAV performance
   - Design user interface (ground station + Android map-based UI)
   - Export functionality for in-flight plan use

2. **Sensing and Harnessing Control System (SHCS)**
   - Real-time flow condition detection sensors and processing
   - Integration with PF2P for balancing energy harvesting with mission objectives
   - Soaring location detection algorithms
   - Obstacle avoidance subsystem
   - Real-time flight safety implementation

3. **Soaring Platform (BST Primary Responsibility)**
   - Select appropriate s-UAS platforms for test events and practice test events
   - Procure/build/modify s-UAS to meet program goals and government specifications
   - Provide both baseline and Albatross-enabled s-UAS for metrics comparison
   - Ensure MAV Link compatibility and government range certification
   - Maintain adequate quantities to account for hardware failures and flight incidents

4. **Integration, Test, and Evaluation**
   - System component integration
   - Simulation-based integration testing
   - Test event readiness reviews
   - Multi-location flight test execution and logistics
   - System performance evaluation against program metrics

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Small Uncrewed Aircraft Systems (s-UAS) Fleet**
- What it is: A fleet of small UAS platforms selected and adapted to demonstrate autonomous soaring
- Use in program: Baseline platforms to integrate soaring planning and control systems; demonstration of autonomous soaring in multiple operational domains
- Specifications/Requirements:
  - Must meet government specifications per solicitation
  - Must comply with MAV Link interface requirements
  - Must obtain US government range flight certification
  - Must support both baseline avionics payload and Albatross-enhanced payloads
  - Redundant quantities needed to account for flight incidents and hardware failures

## Use Cases & Applications

**Operational Domains & Test Locations** (across 24-month period):
- **CONUS Test Events (12 MAC)**: Fort Irwin, California; Yuma Proving Ground, Arizona
- **OCONUS Test Events (18 MAC)**: Pacific Missile Range Facility (Hawaii); San Clemente Island (California); Roosevelt Roads (Puerto Rico)
- **Extended Test Events (24 MAC)**: Vandenberg Space Force Base (California); Kodiak (Alaska); South of Agadir (Morocco)

**Mission Capability**: Autonomous soaring to "actively harvest energy from the natural environment's atmospheric flow field to reduce onboard power demand and enable increased range and/or endurance"
