## Executive Summary
This document provides comprehensive Phase II proposal preparation and submission instructions for the Air Force's SBIR Commercial Solutions Opening (AF203-CSO1). It details eligibility requirements, proposal structure, technical and cost volume specifications, and evaluation criteria for small businesses seeking to advance innovative solutions addressing Air Force needs through adaptation of non-defense commercial technologies.

## Document Type & Purpose
This is an official solicitation instruction document outlining mandatory requirements for Phase II SBIR proposals. It is not a proposal submission from Black Swift Technologies but rather the regulatory framework governing how proposals should be submitted and what they must contain.

## Key Administrative Details

### Submission Requirements
- **Deadline:** June 3, 2021, 12:00 p.m. Eastern Time (for Phase I awardees with deliverables due by June 2, 2021)
- **Portal:** Department of Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP)
- **Maximum Funding:** $750,000 per award
- **Performance Period:** 15 months maximum (12 months technical + 3 months reporting)
- **Award Type:** Firm-fixed-price contracts or Other Transactions for Prototype
- **Anticipated Awards:** ~60 awards
- **Review Timeline:** ~90 days from proposal submission

### Eligibility Requirements
- Must be a qualified small business concern (13 CFR §§ 701-705)
- Minimum 50% of Phase II R&D must be conducted by the offeror
- Principal Investigator must have primary employment with small business (>50% time)
- All R&D work must be performed in the United States
- Must meet SBA commercialization benchmarks
- No classified proposals accepted
- No majority ownership by venture capital, hedge funds, or private equity
- Must be registered in SAM.gov and DSIP

## Proposal Structure & Volume Requirements

### Volume 1: DoD Proposal Coversheet
- Technical abstract (max 200 words)
- Problem solved, advantage provided, end-user organization, commercialization potential
- Maximum 8 keywords
- Anticipated benefits/potential commercial applications
- Statement of prior/current/pending similar proposals

### Volume 2: Technical Volume (15 pages maximum)
Required sections:
- **Table of Contents** (not counted toward page limit)
- **Glossary of acronyms** (not counted)
- **Technical Summary:** Non-defense commercial solution description, problem scope, commercial history
- **Commercialization Potential:** Company info, customer/competition analysis, market analysis, IP protection, financing plans, assistance/mentoring
- **Non-Defense Commercial Customer Discovery:** Revenue history, potential projects
- **Proposed Adaptation of Non-Defense Commercial Solution:** Technical modifications, military applications, DoD customer identification
- **Phase I Feasibility Study Results:** R&D effort, DAF end-user/customer exploration methods, documented feasibility
- **Phase II Technical Objectives and Key Results:** 3-5 objectives with measurable key results; stakeholder interactions; compliance/regulatory activities
- **Phase II Work Plan:** Outcomes, deliverables, specifications, regulatory accommodation
- **Task Outline:** Work breakdown
- **Schedule of Milestones:** Max 15 milestones with delivery dates, deliverables, acceptance criteria
- **Deliverables:** Specific sample/prototype/hardware/software; data deliverables
- **Safety Requirements:** Preliminary hazard analysis (hardware); hazardous material reports
- **Required Reports:** Final report, quarterly status reports, technical interchange meetings, Phase II summary report
- **Key Personnel:** Names, education, experience, citizenship; PI resume required
- **Investors and Partners:** Identification and track record
- **Related Work:** Previous programs and state-of-the-art knowledge
- **Non-U.S. Citizens:** Complete identification and visa/work permit status
- **Supply Chain Integrity:** Joint ventures, suppliers, IP licensing, foreign customers/investors, FOCI mitigation, CFIUS compliance, Bayh-Dole compliance

### Volume 3: Cost Volume
- Direct labor costs (by person/category with rates)
- Direct material costs (itemized with quantities and prices)
- Other direct costs (specialized services, equipment leases, travel, subcontracts, consultants)
- Overhead and G&A
- Profit (minimum required; zero-profit proposals not acceptable)
- **Constraint:** Consultant fees + facility leases + subcontract costs ≤ 1/3 of total contract price
- Must be adequate for price analysis and cost reasonableness determination

### Volume 4: Company Commercialization Report
- Required for firms with prior SBIR/STTR awards
- New/startup companies can select "No"
- Must be completed in SBIR.gov database and uploaded

### Volume 5: Supporting Documents
- Signed Customer Memorandum (mandatory, using provided template) from DAF customer AND end-user
- Letters of support from end-users, partners, investors
- Lifecycle Certification (signed)
- Prior/current/pending support disclosure
- Foreign citizen table (if applicable)
- Supplemental cost information
- Key personnel resumes
- Compliance certifications
- Additional facilities/equipment information
- Additional subcontractor information

### Volume 6: Fraud, Waste, and Abuse Training
- Required documentation of completion

## Critical Mandatory Requirements

### Customer Memorandum
- **MANDATORY** for proposal consideration
- Must use provided template (Attachment 1)
- Must be signed by a DAF Customer AND End-User (if same person, sign twice)
- Required from at least one DAF/Joint organization
- If multiple DAF end-users, consolidate into single coherent memorandum signed by lead DAF Phase II PM
- Proposals without compliant Customer Memorandum will NOT be considered for award

### Commercialization Plan Requirements
Must address:
1. Company information (objectives, core competencies, previous funding, regulatory experience)
2. Customer and competition (key technology objectives, advantages vs. competitors, innovation hurdles)
3. Market analysis (milestones, target dates, market size, share estimates)
4. Intellectual property (patent status, protection plan, competitive advantage timeline)
5. Financing (plans for securing Phase III funding)
6. Assistance and mentoring (sources of technical/business support)
7. Specific questions answered:
   - What is the anticipated first product incorporating the technology?
   - Who are probable customers and what is estimated market size?
   - How much funding needed and how will it be raised?
   - Does firm have marketing expertise?
   - Who are probable competitors and what price/quality advantage?
   - Are there private sector/non-SBIR commitments demonstrated?
   - What past 12-month non-Defense commercial revenue?
   - What are Phase III transition plans?

### Phase I Feasibility Study Results Requirements
Must demonstrate:
- Specific problem/opportunity from Phase I
- Scientific/technical R&D effort (research questions, methods, results, literature)
- DAF end-user and customer exploration methods (both successful and unsuccessful)
- Documentation of at least one in-depth interview with DAF end-user
- Specific DAF end-users willing to support Phase II testing/trials (via Customer Memorandum)
- Specific AF customers assisting with Phase II transition/Phase III (via Customer Memorandum)
- Other US Government end-user commitments (via Customer Memorandum)

### Non-Defense Commercial Solution Requirements
Must address:
- Specific problem/opportunity addressed in Phase I
- Non-Defense commercial solution description (use by commercial customers, technical specifications, qualitative and quantitative value)
- Scope and importance of technical problem
- Whether item is "of a type" (similar to commercial items used by public/non-governmental entities)
- Whether item has been sold/leased/licensed or offered for sale
- Revenue, unit sales, user adoption, pre-sales, investments (where applicable)

### Adaptation to DAF Needs Requirements
Must describe:
- Scope and importance of technical problem
- Similarity of modified item to items sold to non-Defense customers
- Whether supplier performs similar modifications for non-Defense customers
- Whether DAF-unique modifications change product's essential use/purpose
- Differences in production/manufacturing/delivery processes vs. non-Defense customers
- Quantitative benefits expected for Air Force end-users

## Technical Objectives and Key Results Specifications

Phase II proposals must include 3-5 R&D objectives that are:
- Tied to specific Phase II tasks
- Qualitative but specific to participating DAF end-users
- Tied to specific time periods
- Tangible and unambiguous
- Describing end-state outcomes rather than processes
- Each accompanied by 3-5 specific, measurable "key results"
- Key results likely quantitative (e.g., "deliver X widgets to DAF end-user")
- Non-quantitative key results must be clearly measurable

## Work Plan and Milestone Specifications

- **Maximum 15 milestones/deliverables** including:
  - Refined Work Plan (due CA + 30 days)
  - Final technical report
  - Intervening milestones at offeror discretion
- Each milestone must include:
  - Expected delivery date (in months after contract award)
  - Deliverable of value
  - Clear acceptance criteria
- Status reports cannot count as milestones
- Fixed-price payments tied to negotiated milestones and deliverables

## Compliance and Regulatory Activities

Proposals must identify whether effort includes:
- Air flight (including taxi)
- Human subject research
- Animal research
- Directed energy devices or RF radiation
- Explosives, propellants, ammunition
- Hazardous materials
- Infectious agents, human-derived materials, or recombinant DNA

Special requirements if applicable:
- **Human subjects:** Must have IRB approval, Federal Assurance documentation, human subject protection training (1-3 months processing)
- **Animal subjects:** Must have IACUC approval plus DoD veterinarian approval (no funding until approvals granted)
- **Recombinant DNA:** Must have Institutional Biosafety Committee approval
- **UAS operations:** Must obtain UAS operational use exemption (up to 3 months processing)
- **Cyber security:** IT products must have RMF assessment and/or ATO; must meet NIST SP 800-171 and DFAR PGI 239.76 requirements

## Key Personnel Requirements

- Principal Investigator:
  - Primary employment with small business at award and throughout performance
  - Primary employment = >50% time with small business
  - Precludes full-time employment with another organization
  - Technical resume required including publications
- All key personnel must be identified with education, experience, citizenship
- Non-U.S. citizens must be identified with countries of origin, visa/work permit types, and anticipated involvement levels
- Non-U.S. citizens, if eligible, may be limited to work in public domain and non-key positions if subject to export control

## Supply Chain Integrity Requirements

Must disclose and verify:
- Joint ventures, partnerships, technology development agreements, teaming arrangements
- Main component, software, and other suppliers; licensed IP used
- Entities utilizing firm's IP
- Significant non-US customer or investment base (>5% of revenue)
- Foreign sovereign wealth funds or government instrumentalities as customers/investors
- Foreign interests with power to direct or control company management, IP, or operations (FOCI mitigation measures if applicable)
- Classified information needs and approving Federal agencies
- Owner, investor, and PI compliance with 13 CFR § 121.701-705
- Compliance with DFARS 252.225-7048 (Export-Controlled Items)
- CFIUS mandatory/voluntary reviews or eligibility (especially for emerging/foundational technologies: quantum information/computing, AI, machine learning, advanced sensors/networks)
- Compliance with Bayh-Dole Act (35 USC § 204) U.S. technology commercialization/manufacturing requirements
