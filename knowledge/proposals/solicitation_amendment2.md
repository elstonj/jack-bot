# AFX24.E Amendment 2 - STTR Phase I Solicitation

## Document Metadata
- **Type**: Solicitation Amendment (Commercial Solutions Opening - CSO)
- **Client/Agency**: Department of the Air Force (DAF), in partnership with AFWERX
- **Program/Solicitation**: STTR AFX24.E Phase I (Small Business Technology Transfer)
- **Solicitation Topic Number**: AFX24E-TPCSO1
- **Date**: Amendment 2 issued October 2024 (modifies Amendment 1)
- **Key Personnel**: Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
Amendment 2 clarifies applicant responsibilities regarding NIST SP 800-171 cybersecurity assessments, rescinding previous requirements from Amendment 1. The Department of the Air Force is soliciting innovative, dual-purpose (defense/commercial) technologies adaptable to Air Force needs through a Phase I feasibility study program with up to $110,000 in funding and a maximum 90-calendar-day performance period.

## Key Solicitation Details

### Funding & Timeline
- **Maximum Funding**: $110,000 per Phase I award
- **Maximum Duration**: 90 calendar days
- **Proposal Submission Deadline**: October 10, 2024, 12:00 p.m. ET
- **Submission Portal**: DOD SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP) at https://www.dodsbirsttr.mil/
- **NIST Assessment Deadline**: January 6th, 2025 (for completion and upload to SPRS module)

### Topic Scope
**AFX24E-TPCSO1**: "Phase I Open Call for Innovative Defense Related Dual-Purpose Technologies/Solutions with a Clear Air Force Stakeholder Need"

This is a truly "open" topic (agnostic of industry, technology, and problem area) seeking non-defense commercial solutions adapted to meet Air Force stakeholder needs.

## Evaluation Criteria (Equal Importance)

### 1. **Commercialization Potential**
- Track record of commercializing SBIR/STTR or other research (pre-sales, pilots, sales, revenue, active users, subscriptions, downloads)
- Market size and revenue potential
- Clarity of business plan
- Evidence of Phase II follow-on commitments (Government or private sector letters of commitment)

### 2. **Defense Need**
- Appropriateness, relevance, and specificity of identified Defense Need (letters of support from Government personnel are helpful indicators)
- Adequacy of proposed effort relative to fulfilling the Defense Need
- Level of defense mission impact and urgency
- Breadth of applicability of the solution

### 3. **Technical Approach**
- Clarity of problem statement and technical approach
- Soundness and technical merit of proposed approach
- Innovation and differentiation from current customer alternatives
- Qualifications of Principal Investigator/Project Manager, supporting staff, and consultants

## Air Force Operational Imperatives (Referenced Context)
Solutions should align with at least one of:
- Space Order of Battle
- Operationally Focused Air Battle Management Systems (ABMS)
- Moving Target Engagement
- Tactical Air Dominance
- Resilient Basing
- Global Strike
- Readiness to Deploy and Fight
- **BLUE SKY option**: Proposals addressing other strategic capabilities may be considered with justification

## Phase I Objectives
1. Determine scientific and technical merit and feasibility of ideas with potential for Air Force transition
2. Validate product-market fit between proposed solution and Air Force stakeholder
3. Define clear, actionable integration pathway with Air Force customer
4. Clearly identify potential stakeholders
5. Describe pathway to integrating into Air Force operations, including core technology development, regulatory navigation, and system integration
6. Describe applicability to other DoD/Government customers

## Critical Eligibility Requirements

### Small Business Concern (SBC) Eligibility
- **Size**: Combined with affiliates, not more than 500 employees
- **Ownership**: 
  - More than 50% directly owned and controlled by U.S. citizens or permanent resident aliens, OR
  - More than 50% owned by multiple venture capital operating companies (VCOCs), hedge funds, or private equity firms (if agency-authorized), OR
  - Joint venture meeting above requirements
- **Ineligibility**: SBCs owned in majority by multiple VCOCs, hedge funds, or private equity firms are **NOT eligible**

### Performance of Work Requirements
- **Minimum 40%** of R&R&D work must be performed by the Small Business Concern
- **Minimum 30%** of R&R&D work must be performed by a single partnering Research Institution
- Percentages measured by direct and indirect costs, excluding profit
- **No exceptions to these requirements**

### Research Institution (RI) Definition
Must be U.S.-located and either:
1. Non-profit institution operated exclusively for scientific/educational purposes, OR
2. Federally-funded R&D center (FFRDC) identified by NSF

### Principal Investigator (PI) Requirements
- Primary employment must be with SBC or single partnering RI (>50% time)
- Only one PI/Project Manager per proposal
- Precludes full-time employment elsewhere

### Location of Work
- **R/R&D work must be performed in the United States**
- Rare exceptions for unavailable supplies/materials require pre-submission approval from DAF

### National Security Assessments
DAF will **deny awards** if SBC has:
- Owner/covered individual party to malign foreign talent recruitment program
- Business entity, parent company, or subsidiary in People's Republic of China or foreign country of concern
- Owner/covered individual with foreign affiliation to such entities (unless properly mitigated per due diligence)

## Proposal Submission Requirements

### Seven (7) Required Volumes
1. **Volume 1**: Proposal Cover Sheet
2. **Volume 2**: Technical Volume (max 25 slides, PDF format)
3. **Volume 3**: Cost Volume
4. **Volume 4**: Company Commercialization Report (CCR)
5. **Volume 5**: Supporting Documents
6. **Volume 6**: Fraud, Waste, and Abuse Training certification
7. **Volume 7**: Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries

### Volume 1 - Cover Sheet Requirements
- Brief technical abstract (max 200 words) describing R&D project
- Discussion of anticipated benefits and potential commercial applications (max 200 words)
- No proprietary or classified information
- System auto-assigns proposal number upon save
- Modifiable until solicitation closes

### Volume 2 - Technical Volume Format
- **Single PDF file** with graphics (virus-checked, not encrypted)
- **Maximum 25 slides** (excess slides not evaluated)
- Minimum 10-pt font
- Consecutively numbered pages
- Each slide must include: company name, topic number (AFX24E-TPCSO1), proposal number
- No embedded videos, moving pictures, or active media

## Amendment 2 Changes (NIST SP 800-171)

### What Was Rescinded from Amendment 1
- Requirement for NIST SP 800-171 score publication in PIEE's SPRS module as disqualification trigger
- All references to NIST Assessment Documentation in Volume 5 "Supporting Documents"

### New Requirement (Section 4(p))
- **NIST SP 800-171 Assessments must be completed and uploaded to SPRS no later than January 6th, 2025**
- Does **NOT** require submission of NIST documentation as part of proposal Volume 5
- Assessments must be "current" per DFARS 252.204-7020
- SBC must provide access to facilities, systems, and personnel for Government Medium/High assessments if required

### NIST Assessment Process
- **Basic Assessment**: Self-assessment via https://www.projectspectrum.io/#/cyberreadiness-check
- **Submission Methods**:
  - (Preferred) Email to WEBPTSMH.fct@navy.mil with required DFARS 252.204-7019(d)(1) information
  - Submit directly to SPRS module in PIEE
- **Deadline**: January 6th, 2025 (DAF recommends early completion)

## Common Disqualifiers

Proposals will be rejected for:
- SAM.gov registration not properly updated or not current at submission/award
- Incorrect allocation of Minimum Performance Percentage of Work
- Work not meeting R/R&D definition
- Submission after deadline
- Exceeding maximum $110,000 funding amount
- Exceeding maximum 90-calendar-day duration
- Not meeting STTR eligibility requirements
- Prior award for Essentially Equivalent Work
- NIST SP 800-171 assessment not submitted by January 6th, 2025

## Additional Requirements & Restrictions

### Submission Practices
- All proposals via DSIP only; submissions by other means disregarded
- **One proposal per SBC** under this solicitation (only most recent certified submission evaluated)
- Classified proposals **NOT accepted**
- Early submission recommended (system traffic intensifies near deadline)
- No responsibility for missed submissions due to system lag or applicant-caused delays

### Registration Requirements
- **SAM.gov**: Active registration with current UEI (Unique Entity ID, replacing DUNS Number)
- **DSIP**: Login.gov account required; must link to DSIP account
- **Account Sharing**: Prohibited under Login.gov Terms of Use and DoD policy

### Export Control Compliance
- Review U.S. Munitions List (22 CFR Part 121) for ITAR applicability
- If ITAR-restricted: Submit certified DD Form 2345 (Military Critical Technology Agreement)
- Non-U.S. citizens must be "Lawfully Admitted for Permanent Residence" or "Protected Individual"
- Proposal may be rejected if non-U.S. citizen on ITAR work doesn't meet above categories

### Essentially Equivalent Work
- Identical or substantially equivalent proposals may be submitted to multiple Federal solicitations
- **Unlawful** to enter into contracts with essentially equivalent effort
- Applicants must disclose prior, current, or pending support of similar proposals/awards to DAF early

## Other Selection Factors
