## Document Summary
This is a compliance and guidance document outlining technical data rights assertions required for DOD SBIR proposal submissions. It explains contractor IP protections, government licensing terms, and documentation requirements under DFARS clauses for SBIR programs.

## Key Provisions

### Technical Data Rights Framework
- **Ownership**: Contractor retains rights to technical data and software developed under SBIR contract
- **Government License**: Government obtains royalty-free license limited to Government purposes only
- **License Duration**: 20-year period from contract award
- **Post-20-Year Rights**: Upon expiration, Government gains Government Purpose Rights in SBIR data
- **Restrictions During License Period**: Government cannot release or disclose SBIR data to third parties except:
  1. For evaluation purposes
  2. With explicit contractor permission
  3. For emergency repair or overhaul of Government-operated items

### Required Markings & Documentation
- Technical data must be marked with restrictive legend specified in DFARS 252.227-7018
- Proposals must include data rights assertion table (available at JHU/APL vendor forms link)
- If proposer plans to submit assertions per DFARS 252.227-7017, assertions must be:
  - Identified in proposal
  - Include use, release, or disclosure restrictions
  - Submitted with proposal
  - **Approved before contract award**

## Applicable Regulations
- DFARS 252.227-7018 (Rights in Noncommercial Technical Data and Computer Software – SBIR Program)
- DFARS 252.227-7017
