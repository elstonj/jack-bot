## Executive Summary
This datasheet highlights the Black Swift Technologies S2 unmanned aircraft's field-swappable payload system, which enables rapid sensor integration without specialized tools while maintaining clean, uncontaminated atmospheric measurements. The S2's modular design supports diverse scientific applications from soil moisture mapping to volcanic gas monitoring.

## Technical Approach
The S2 features a **field-swappable payload system** with the following design philosophy:
- **Complete enclosure**: Sensor suite and associated hardware fully enclosed within the nose cone to ensure clean, uncontaminated atmospheric measurements
- **Rapid field swaps**: Common power, data, and mechanical interfaces enable payload changes without specialized tools
- **Well-documented interfaces**: Power and data interfaces are thoroughly documented to facilitate third-party sensor integration
- **Modular architecture**: Designed to integrate diverse sensors for multiple application areas

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 (Swift Surveillance II)
- **What it is**: Medium-altitude, long-endurance (MALE) unmanned aircraft system with enclosed payload bay
- **Key capability**: Field-swappable modular payload system designed for atmospheric research
- **Operating parameters**: Demonstrated operation at temperatures near -20°C and altitudes up to 14,000 feet
- **Coverage capability**: Soil moisture mapping up to 600 acres per flight

## Use Cases & Applications

1. **Soil Moisture Mapping**: L-band radiometer measuring soil moisture content up to 10 cm below ground surface, including under dense canopy crops; coverage of up to 600 acres per flight

2. **High-Altitude Atmospheric Research**: Greenland ice sheet operations measuring water vapor above ice sheets at -20°C, 14,000 feet altitude to understand climate impacts

3. **Snow Water Equivalent (SWE) Measurement**: P-band reflective signals for mountainous environments

4. **Satellite Calibration**: Multispectral camera arrays for Landsat-8 OLI and S-NPP VIIRS instrument calibration in partnership with NASA Goddard Space Flight Center

5. **Coastal Monitoring**: Thermal and hyperspectral payload integration

6. **Volcanic Gas & Plume Monitoring**: 
   - Airborne measurements of CO2, SO2, CH4, and H2S
   - Orthomosaic, thermal, and 3D data products generation
   - Nephelometer for volcanic particle size and distribution assessment
   - Atmospheric probes measuring pressure, temperature, humidity, and 3D wind patterns
   - Partnership with NASA/JPL for improved eruption planning and warning

7. **Wildfire Monitoring**:
   - Nighttime in situ measurements of wildfire plumes
   - Remote measurements of wildfire properties
   - Research-quality measurements of CO2, CO, aerosol, relative humidity, pressure, and temperature
   - Multispectral high-resolution fire mapping
   - Application to fire weather modeling and forecasting
