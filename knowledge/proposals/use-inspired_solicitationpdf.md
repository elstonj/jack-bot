## Executive Summary
The Colorado-Wyoming Climate Resilience Engine is a 10-year, up-to-$160M NSF-funded initiative that has received its initial $15M allocation. The Use-Inspired R&D Grant Program solicits collaborative research projects addressing climate resilience challenges in Colorado and Wyoming through an "Advanced Sensing and Computation for Environmental Decision Making" (ASCEND) framework. The program offers up to $500,000 per project (3-4 awards per cycle) for early-stage R&D that leverages regional environmental sensing assets, applies AI/ML analytics, and develops actionable decision-support tools.

## Technical Approach
Projects must demonstrate alignment with the ASCEND framework by:
1. **Leveraging/advancing environmental sensing assets** in the CO-WY region
2. **Transforming sensing data** through advanced data analytics, including AI/ML
3. **Developing actionable decision-support tools** in one of four core use-case areas

The program also considers applications that enhance previous CO-WY Engine projects or provide foundational assets (testing infrastructure, computational support).

## Use Cases & Applications
Core focus areas for R&D:
- **Wildfire Preparedness and Response**
- **Soil Health**
- **Water Quality and Availability**
- **Air Quality**

Applications addressing climate resilience broadly within these domains are encouraged.

## Funding & Requirements

### Budget
- **Funding per project:** Up to $500,000 total (direct + indirect costs) over 12 calendar months
- **Anticipated awards:** 3-4 per cycle
- **Matched/leveraged resources:** Generally 1:1 ($1 Engine : $1 non-Engine) expected, though teams working with tribal or underserved communities may document less; projects with established corporate partners expected to provide more than 1:1
- **Cost share flexibility:** Not voluntary but expected to be provided and expended

### Leveraged/Matched Resource Examples
- Cash or in-kind contributions (personnel, equipment, lab space, datasets, industrial testbeds)
- Collaborations and partnerships (local government, nonprofits, industry)
- Integration with existing initiatives and programs
- Existing research, data, or studies
- Existing technology and edge computing solutions
- Internal organizational capacity building
- Public-private partnerships
- Community assets (libraries, schools, community centers)
- Stacking of non-federal awards from federal, public, and private sources

## Eligibility

### Who May Submit
- Entities located and doing business in Colorado or Wyoming (institutions of higher education, for-profit and non-profit organizations)
- Must have existing operations in CO/WY or establish operations as result of grant
- Cross-sector collaborations (funded or unfunded) encouraged

### Principal Investigator Requirements
- Must be tenure-track or non-tenure-track faculty; academic, federal lab, or corporate research scientist/scholar; center/institute director; dean; department head; or equivalent
- Project team members may come from any discipline
- Non-university members (private entities, community, state, and NGOs) encouraged
- One PI may lead only one project but may serve in other roles on other teams
- No limit on number of proposals per organization
- For institutions of higher education and national labs: proposals must be routed through and submitted by institutional sponsored programs office (no direct PI submissions accepted)

### Eligible Costs
Funds advance specific R&D aims and milestones:
- Proof of concept or pilot studies
- Intellectual property protection
- Prototypes and technical validation
- Market assessment
- Equipment advancing R&D capabilities

Allowable costs (must align with NSF standards):
- Senior personnel, postdoctoral fellows, graduate and undergraduate students
- Equipment, domestic travel, materials and supplies
- Consultant services, other direct costs, and indirect costs
- Federally-negotiated indirect cost rates or de minimis 10% MTDC for partners without negotiated rates

## Submission Requirements

### Letter of Intent (Required)
Single PDF document including:
- **Page 1 (Cover):** Project title, PI/key personnel and affiliations, partners and affiliations
- **Page 2 (Content):** Topic area and ASCEND use-case alignment, specific problem addressed, proposed goals and impact, approach summary, leveraged resources potential

### Full Proposal Components
- **Cover Page** (1 page): Project title, lead institution, PI/co-PI info with demographics, total funds requested
- **Executive Summary** (1 page): Use-inspired R&D basis, value proposition, problem statement, proposed solution, regional economic development contribution
- **Project Description** (5 pages): Background and problem definition, user needs, proposed R&D products/solutions, objectives and activities, stakeholder/market analysis, competitive landscape, IP potential, funding path differentiation, challenges and risk mitigation, potential short/long-term climate resilience benefits, regional engagement and broader community impact
- **References** (4 pages): NSF format
- **Project Plan and Milestones** (1 page): High-level timeline with major activities and key milestones (including follow-on funding targets)
- **Team Composition and Management Plan** (1 page): Team structure, leadership, technical expertise, R&D/translational experience, individual roles, project management approach
- **Partnerships and Matched/Leveraged Resources** (2 pages): Partnership narrative, matched/leveraged resource details, justification for less than 1:1 if applicable
- **Biographical Sketches** (3 per bio): SciENcv-generated NSF-approved sketches for PI, co-PIs, and senior/key personnel
- **Budget** (N/A pages): Line-item budget by category using provided worksheet; separate budgets/justifications for subawards; federally-negotiated rates or 10% MTDC de minimis
- **Budget Justification** (5 pages): NSF-style format
- **New and Existing Resources** (N/A pages): Using provided template with resource types and values
- **Letters of Support** (1 page per letter, 2-3 total): From stakeholders describing support/interest
- **Letters of Resource Commitment** (1 page per letter): From all partners committing matched/leveraged resources

## Evaluation Criteria
External technical, academic, and business experts will evaluate proposals on:
- **Technical Viability:** Novelty of R&D products, likelihood of success, market/industry/community-defined problem approach, competitive advantages, IP strategy strength
- **Project Viability:** Appropriateness of scope, project plan, milestones, budget
- **Translation Potential:** Likelihood of translational outcomes (start-up, industry/community partnerships)
- **Team Readiness:** Strength of qualifications, management structure, execution record
- **Engagement and Partnerships:** Strength and breadth of partnerships and matched/leveraged resources
- **Potential Impact:** Potential for broad economic and community impacts

## Accountability & Reporting
- Anticipated awardees encouraged to participate in entrepreneurship programs (for start-up development outcomes)
- **Required participation:** Regular Engine convenings to develop effective use-inspired research teams and prepare for follow-on funding
- **Progress reporting:** Brief updates and evaluations every 6 months including:
  - Milestones evaluation
  - Budget expenditure tracking
  - Summary of activities and impact assessment
  - Current and projected outcomes, metrics, and timelines
  - Expenditure summary report
- **Unspent funds:** Must be returned to CO-WY Engine at end of project period
- **Follow-on funding:** Successful projects receive consideration for CO-WY Engine Translation Grant Program and other awards programs

## Competitive Advantages & Notable Details

### CO-WY Engine Context
- One of ten inaugural NSF Regional Innovation Engines
- Aims to boost innovation capacity, create sustainable innovation ecosystems, and demonstrate economic growth
- Regional ecosystem includes small businesses, industry leaders, higher education institutions, federal research laboratories, community groups, and public sector agencies
- Explicitly supports both translation of innovation to practice and workforce development

### Program Positioning
- Use-Inspired R&D program targets **early-stage innovations** not yet requiring a commercialization entity
- Translation Grant Program is more appropriate for projects with existing commercial entities
- Program explicitly values building on previous CO-WY Engine projects and providing foundational assets to enhance their success
