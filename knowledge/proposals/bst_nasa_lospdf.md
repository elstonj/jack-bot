# NASA Letter of Support for Black Swift Technologies - AFWERX SBIR Phase II

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: NASA Goddard Space Flight Center / Wallops Flight Facility (in support of Air Force AFWERX evaluation)
- Program/Solicitation: AFWERX SBIR Phase II (Topic 22.4 - Soil Moisture Mapping); Contract 80NSSC18C0002 (Soil Moisture Mapping System); Contract NNX17CP13C (S2 UAS and SwiftFlow)
- Date: April 25, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 UAS, SwiftFlow, Soil Moisture Mapping System, L-band radiometer, Soil Integrity Mapping UAS
- Key Personnel: Geoffrey L. Bland (NASA Climate and Radiation Laboratory, Technical Monitor)

## Executive Summary
Geoffrey Bland, a NASA SBIR Technical Monitor and Sub-Topic manager, provides strong institutional support for BST's Phase II SBIR effort to map soil integrity from a UAS. Bland testifies that BST has successfully developed and commercialized core technologies (L-band radiometer) in partnership with NASA, with demonstrated adoption across multiple NASA centers and federal agencies.

## Technical Approach
BST's Phase II effort focuses on mapping soil integrity from an Unmanned Aerial System (UAS), building on L-band radiometer technology previously developed in partnership with NASA. The approach leverages:
- Microwave-based soil moisture sensor system
- S2 UAS platform (ruggedized)
- Integration with atmospheric measurement capabilities

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- Ruggedized unmanned aerial system
- Currently operational at two NASA centers: Langley Research Center and Ames Research Center
- Enables agency-wide science and technology research
- Contract: NNX17CP13C

### Soil Moisture Mapping System
- Microwave-based sensor system
- Developed under SBIR contract 80NSSC18C0002
- Enables soil moisture and integrity mapping from UAS platforms
- Phase II effort focused on commercialization and operationalization

### SwiftFlow
- Atmospheric measurement probe
- Ruggedized design
- Contract: NNX17CP13C
- Currently in use at NASA centers

### L-Band Radiometer
- Core technology developed in partnership with NASA
- Forms foundation for soil moisture mapping capabilities
- Previously supported by NASA SBIR programs

## Use Cases & Applications
- Soil integrity mapping from UAS platforms
- Multi-agency science research (NASA SMD and ARMD)
- USGS and NOAA applications
- Agency-wide science and technology research at NASA field centers
