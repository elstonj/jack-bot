# ERDC Broad Other Transaction Authority Announcement (BOTAA) 25-01

## Document Metadata
- Type: Broad Other Transaction Authority Announcement (BOTAA) / Solicitation
- Client/Agency: U.S. Army Corps of Engineers (USACE) Engineer Research and Development Center (ERDC)
- Program/Solicitation: ERDC BOTAA 25-01
- Date: October 2024
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None mentioned in this document
- Key Personnel: N/A (this is a government solicitation, not a BST proposal)

## Executive Summary
This is an open, continuously available solicitation from the ERDC seeking prototype projects from nontraditional defense contractors (NDCs), traditional defense contractors, and nonprofit research institutions. The announcement covers two broad focus areas (Military Engineering and Engineered Resilient Systems) with multiple sub-focus areas, using Other Transaction authority under 10 U.S.C. 4022 for flexible prototype development.

## Process Overview
The BOTAA operates in stages:
1. **White Paper Submission** (max 3 pages): Continuous submission through ERDCWERX platform
2. **Government Evaluation** (60-90 days): Assessment against technical merit and eligibility criteria
3. **Selection**: If advantageous, government may request solution pitch, technical demonstration, or issue Request for Prototype Proposal (RFPP)
4. **Proposal Development**: Collaborative scope-of-work development with government
5. **Award & Performance**: Firm fixed-price basis with milestone payment schedules

## Eligibility Requirements
To qualify for award, at least ONE of these must be present:
- At least one NDC or nonprofit research institution participating significantly, OR
- All significant participants (other than Federal Government) are small businesses or NDCs, OR
- At least one-third of total project cost paid by non-Federal sources

## Focus Areas

### Military Engineering (ME)
1. **Protective Structures and Systems**: Force protection infrastructure, planning tools, TTPs for perimeter/port/border security
2. **Protection and Survivability**: Detection, defeat of threats; infrastructure hardening, camouflage, vulnerability assessment
3. **Geophysical Sensors and Data Fusion**: Multi-sensor integration, automated fusion for Common Operating Picture, sub/super-surface monitoring
4. **Vulnerability Assessment, Detection, and Analytics**: Strategic/operational/tactical analysis frameworks for force protection posture, population vulnerability, infrastructure disruption
5. **Geoscience, Environmental, Geospatial, and Material Modeling and Simulation**: Materials-by-design, computational modeling of sensors, structural response prediction, environmental impact modeling
6. **Enhance and Assess Mobility of Manned and Unmanned Systems**: Autonomous systems, obstacle clearing/breaching, terrain interaction, dynamic mobility hazard detection, M&S for mobility prediction
7. **Engineering and Engineering Enabling Technologies**: Combat engineering, materials, manufacturing, modeling/simulation, autonomous systems, engineer mobility/counter-mobility
8. **Global Access Engineering**: Remote sensing, tactical/experimental methods for assessing soils, ice/snow, pavements, bathymetry, port depths, coastal regions, airfields, infrastructure
9. **Infrastructure Maintenance, Repair and Reconstruction**: Advanced physics-based assessment, rapid repair technologies, prototype kits for technology transition
10. **Engineering Analysis Tools and Decision Aid Prototype Software**: Software integrating advanced algorithms for evaluation, design, repair of critical infrastructure

### Engineered Resilient Systems (ERS)
1. **Cybersecurity**: Protection of ICS/SCADA/PLC systems and networks; networked-, host-, and behavior-based defense approaches
2. **High Performance Computing Enabled Advanced Manufacturing**: 3D printing, multi-physics computational approaches, optimization, trade-space analytics, machine learning, HPC-enabled process control, large-scale component fabrication
3. **High Performance Data Analytics**: Techniques for examining large/complex datasets; heterogeneous computing resources; AI/ML algorithms
4. **System Engineering Techniques**: Design trade-space development, Condition Based Maintenance, massive data processing, AI/ML algorithms
5. **HPC Enabled Development of Surrogate Models and Data Analytics**: Computationally efficient models; multi-physics interactions; CFD/FEA applications; anomaly detection; physics-informed machine learning
6. **Leveraging HPC Capabilities for On-the-Edge Computing**: HPC at point of need; electro-optical/thermal/radar/acoustic sensor processing; terrain characterization; sensor fusion; anomaly detection
7. **Computational Material Testing and Validation**: Numerical tools connecting advanced manufacturing techniques to engineering mechanics simulations

## White Paper Requirements
**Format**: Max 3 pages (cover page not counted)
- 12pt Times New Roman, single-spaced, 1-inch margins
- PDF format, <20 MB
- No classified information

**Content**:
- Cover page (auto-generated from submission form): POC details, company info, business status, focus area(s), title, description, DoD purpose, ROM price
- Technical requirements: Background/benefits, technical approach, data rights assertions
- ROM pricing with supporting documentation

## Proposal Requirements (if white paper selected)
- Cover page with white paper ID, title, company info, submission date
- Statement of Work: background, safety/environmental/security requirements, technical objectives, success criteria, reporting/deliverables, timeline, Government-furnished resources
- Milestone Payment Schedule: firm fixed-price events with descriptions and target timeframes
- Security clearance requirements assessment
- Data rights assertions
- Key personnel resumes (Program Manager and others); commitment terms if not currently employed
- Significant participation rationale (if applicable)
- Detailed pricing: labor rates, materials/equipment list, indirect costs with supporting calculations

## Evaluation Criteria

**White Papers**:
- Degree to which solution meets government problem/need/capability gap in identified focus area
- ROM price commensurate with solution
- Government funding availability
- Demonstration of required participation (NDC, nonprofit, small business, or cost share)

**Proposals**:
- Scientific/technical merit: feasibility, clarity, state-of-the-art approach, clear objectives, innovative methodology
- Key personnel qualifications
- Significant participation extent (new technology/product, novel application, material performance increase, significant effort contribution, cost/schedule reduction)
- Budget/price: fair, reasonable, fully justified, commensurate with complexity

## Key Administrative Details
- Registration in System for Award Management (SAM) required prior to award
- Subject to International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR) and Export Administration Regulations (EAR)
- NOT subject to Competition in Contracting Act or FAR regulations
- Proposals may be disclosed to subject matter experts for evaluation
- Proposals subject to Freedom of Information Act (FOIA); confidential information should be marked
- Follow-on production available after successful prototype completion using non-competitive procedures
- U.S. Government does not fund proposal preparation; no reimbursement for pre-agreement costs
