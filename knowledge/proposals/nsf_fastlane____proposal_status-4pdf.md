- Client/Agency: National Science Foundation (NSF)
- Program/Solicitation: STTR Phase I

- Date: 2013-10-24 (created); 2013-12-19 (modified)
- Organization: University of Colorado at Boulder
- Key Personnel: Jack S. Elston (Principal Investigator)

## Executive Summary
This NSF STTR Phase I proposal by Black Swift Technologies (through University of Colorado Boulder) proposed developing a cost-effective, reliable small UAS platform for atmospheric boundary layer sampling. The proposal received a "Good" rating but raised questions about differentiation from existing platforms and clarity regarding sensor adaptation versus development.

## Technical Approach
- Development of a versatile, reconfigurable small UAS platform for atmospheric boundary layer sampling
- Design intended to be easily reconfigurable for different mission types
- Approach included adapting existing sensing schemes (as discussed in research plan) versus developing entirely new sensing platforms
- Focus on improving data quantity and quality for atmospheric research applications

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Small UAS Platform for Atmospheric Sampling**
- Cost-effective platform for atmospheric boundary layer data collection
- Versatile design allowing reconfiguration for multiple mission types
- Potential to improve atmospheric models, climate pattern analysis, and weather predictions
- Proposed applications beyond atmospheric science (agriculture, land surveying)

## Use Cases & Applications
- Atmospheric boundary layer sampling and characterization
- Data collection to improve atmospheric models and weather prediction
- Climate pattern research
- Potential secondary applications in agriculture and land surveying

## Notable Details
- Proposal included support letters from National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) and National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR)
- Reviewer concerns centered on competitive differentiation and market clarity rather than technical feasibility
- Reviewer recommended clarifying distinction between adapting existing sensors versus developing new sensing platforms
- Document notes proposal contained typos that should be corrected

## Review Outcomes
**Rating:** Good

**Key Reviewer Concerns:**
- Lack of clarity on competitive advantages versus existing small UAS platforms
- Uncertainty about scope of work (sensor adaptation vs. sensor development)
- Undefined target market segment
- Unclear market size for atmospheric research UAS applications

**Positive Assessment Elements:**
- Recognized value of cost-effective, reliable platform for atmospheric sampling
- Sufficient detail in research plan
- Strong institutional support (NREL, NCAR endorsements)
- Recognition of broader impact potential across multiple scientific domains
