## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to develop an all-weather, multi-mission unmanned aircraft system based on its S3 platform to address operational needs at Department of Air Force bases globally. The system would provide 24/7/365 capability for perimeter security, civil engineering surveys, emergency response, and CBRN sensing in extreme weather conditions (winds to 45 mph, temperatures -40°F to 110°F, heavy precipitation) where current small UAS platforms fail. The Phase I focuses on feasibility research and customer discovery with identified DAF end-users at Elmendorf AFB (AK), Grand Forks AFB (ND), Thule AB (Greenland), and other Pacific Theater bases.

## Technical Approach
BST proposes to adapt its existing S3 VTOL fixed-wing UAS platform—designed and proven for extreme atmospheric research missions—for DAF base operations. The approach emphasizes:

**Core Technical Capabilities:**
- End-to-end UAS intellectual property (user interface, ground station, communications protocol, avionics, control algorithms, vehicle design)
- Intelligent wind and terrain-aware control enabling high-wind VTOL operations
- Advanced vision and radar-based systems for obstacle and terrain avoidance
- Machine learning-based fault prediction and detection for preventative maintenance
- Distributed, service discovery-based subsystems providing redundancy and fault tolerance
- Airdata systems capable of mitigating ice and heavy precipitation
- Vision-based GPS-denied navigation and automated emergency landing
- Tool-less assembly designed for gloved operations with IP-rated aircraft design
- Ingress protection from abrasive volcanic ash and corrosive gases
- Energy extraction through dynamic soaring to extend mission duration
- Energy-aware warning systems for rapidly evolving flight conditions

**Performance Specifications:**
- Maximum dash speed: 100 mph
- Cruise speed: 45 mph
- Default flight time: 90 minutes at 45 mph with maximum payload
- Extended capability: 4+ hours with smaller payloads and/or integrated solar system
- All-weather operation: Heavy precipitation (snow/rain), winds to 45 mph, temperatures -40°F to 110°F
- 24/7/365 operational capability at most global air bases
- Ready to launch in minutes with automated pre-flight and planning

**Phase I Tasks (90-day duration):**
