## Executive Summary
This is an undergraduate course syllabus for a 3-credit remote sensing operations course at UMES that covers unmanned remote sensing platform operations, data processing using GIS, and application of remote sensing to scientific endeavors. The course includes both theoretical instruction and hands-on laboratory work, culminating in a student-executed remote sensing project.

## Course Overview
**Prerequisites:** AVSC 390 or MATH 210 or instructor permission

**Meeting Times:** Tuesdays & Thursdays, 2:00pm–3:15pm, EASC 1067

**Textbook:** Jensen, John R., *Remote Sensing of the Environment: An Earth Resource Perspective*, 2nd Edition, Prentice Hall, 2007

## Course Learning Objectives
By course completion, students should be able to:
1. Describe the principle of Remote Sensing
2. Describe the functions and components of a Geographical Information System (GIS)
3. Identify the challenges, limitations, and regulations of gathering RS data in different environments
4. Design experiments to facilitate the collection of RS data
5. **Safely operate aerial, aquatic, and terrestrial RS platforms** to collect RS data
6. Analyze and extract thematic information from RS data
7. Produce tangible RS data products using GIS software
8. Apply RS data in various fields
9. Execute a project using RS environmental data

## Curriculum Content
**Major topic areas covered:**
- History of Aerial Photography and RS Platforms
- Aerial Photography and Aerial Platform Operations (Week 3)
- Introduction to GIS and GIS Lab (Week 4)
- Elements of Visual Image Interpretation and Photogrammetry (Weeks 5-6)
- Design of Experiments and Statistical Methods (Week 6-7)
- Multispectral RS Systems and Data Lab (Week 9)
- Remote Sensing of Terrestrial Surfaces and Vegetation (Week 10)
- Remote Sensing of Aquatic Ecosystems (Week 11)
- Contemporary Issues in Remote Sensing (Week 15)

## Hands-On Laboratory Work
Students complete labs in:
- Aerial Platforms
- GIS operations
- Photogrammetry and Data Interpretation
- Multispectral Data processing
- Terrestrial Remote Sensing
- Aquatic Remote Sensing

## Remote Sensing Semester Project
**Weight:** 30% of final grade

Students work in teams to design, execute, analyze, and report on an instructor-assigned remote sensing project. Project assessment criteria:
- Intellectual merit
- Adherence to experimental design principles
- Project execution
- Statistical analysis
- Project write-up and presentation
