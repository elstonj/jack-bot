## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed and executed a pilot study to demonstrate the value of collecting UAS observations near active wildland fires and assimilating that data to improve low-level wind and turbulence forecasting. The work addressed a critical safety gap exposed by a fatal 2021 nighttime air tanker crash potentially caused by low-level turbulence, with the goal of enabling safer and more efficient wildland firefighting operations, including expansion to nighttime operations.

## Technical Approach
- **Observation Collection**: BST would deploy UAS to collect targeted atmospheric observations near wildland fires in complex terrain
- **Data Assimilation**: Partnership with NCAR/RAL to perform data quality assessments and assimilation experiments using an Ensemble Kalman Filter approach specifically tailored to assimilate UAS observations
- **Outcome Metrics**: Quantify improvements in accuracy of low-level winds and turbulence representation; determine critical wind/turbulence criteria required for safe operation of crewed and uncrewed aerial firefighting assets

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Black Swift Technologies UAS**
- Purpose: Collecting low-level atmospheric observations (wind, turbulence) in extreme fire environments
- Application Context: Near active wildland fires in complex terrain where traditional sensors unavailable
- Performance Goal: Provide accurate estimates of current low-level wind field and evolution over 3-12 hour windows

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Wildland Firefighting Operations**: Improved decision support for air tanker operations (both crewed and uncrewed)
- **Nighttime Operations**: Enabling safer nighttime aerial firefighting by providing real-time wind/turbulence data
- **Complex Terrain**: Areas where terrain-induced low-level turbulence poses hazards to conventional firefighting aircraft
- **Extreme Environments**: Operations in high-temperature, smoke-laden, turbulent conditions near active fires

## Partnerships & Collaborations
- **NCAR/RAL (National Center for Atmospheric Research / Research Applications Laboratory)**: Data assimilation and ensemble Kalman filtering expertise
- **Colorado Center of Excellence (CoE) for Advanced Technology Aerial Firefighting**: Coordination of field operations and permissions to collect data near active fires

## Notable Details
- **Triggering Event**: Fatal 2021 air tanker crash during nighttime operations, attributed potentially to strong low-level turbulence—established critical need
- **Safety Focus**: Project explicitly aims to improve safety of mixed (crewed and uncrewed) flight operations in extreme conditions
- **Data Assimilation Innovation**: Ensemble Kalman Filter approach tailored specifically for UAS observations (non-standard for this application)
- **Operational Envelope Expansion**: Goal to extend wildland firefighting operations to nighttime with improved situational awareness
