## Executive Summary
Dr. Joshua P. Schwarz is a leading atmospheric scientist at NOAA specializing in airborne and ground-based measurements of aerosol particles, black carbon, and bioaerosols. He has participated in 25+ field campaigns measuring atmospheric composition globally, authored 108+ peer-reviewed publications (h-index 46), and received multiple awards including NOAA Administrator's Award and Bronze Medal. His expertise centers on single particle soot photometer (SP2) measurements and understanding black carbon's role in climate.

## Key Scientific Instruments & Methods

### Single Particle Soot Photometer (SP2)
- Primary instrument used across all campaigns
- Measures black carbon particles at individual particle level
- Expertise includes:
  - Detection efficiency quantification
  - Trigger deadtime corrections ("invisible bias")
  - Incandescent signal extrapolation
  - Hygroscopicity measurements
  - Fluorescence calibration methods

### Measurements Expertise
- Black carbon vertical profiles (from surface to lower stratosphere)
- Aerosol mixing state and light-scattering properties
- Black carbon in snow, rain, and precipitate
- Bioaerosol fluorescence
- Coatings on black carbon particles
