## Executive Summary
AFVentures, a division of AFWERX established in 2018, leverages commercial innovation to deliver improved capabilities to the warfighter faster by matching operator needs with private sector solutions through the SBIR/STTR Open Topic program. From FY18–FY20, AFVentures awarded 2,299 contracts totaling $710 million to 1,433 companies, achieving a 5.8:1 return on investment and generating $2.21 billion in post-award private sector investment. The program successfully transitioned 538 capabilities to testing or prototyping with over 300 Air Force/DoD units, while accelerating average contracting timelines to 72 days.

## Technical Approach
AFVentures operates through a three-phase SBIR/STTR structure designed to accelerate technology transition:

- **Phase I**: $50,000 short-term contracts for concept development, feasibility testing, and customer identification
- **Phase II**: Up to $750,000 for prototyping, research, development, test and evaluation based on Air Force operator needs
- **Phase II Supplemental Funding Pilot Program (SFPP)**: Up to $15M in matched funding (requiring 3rd-party private or government co-investment) for scaling
- **Phase III**: Commercialization phase funded through non-SBIR/STTR sources (FAR-based contracts)

The program inverts the traditional acquisition model by enabling any qualifying commercial innovator to propose openly, rather than pursuing pre-determined, government-specified requirements. This decentralized, user-need-driven approach prioritizes early linkage between technology developers and DAF end-users to ensure subsequent funding addresses real Air Force problems.

Key process innovation: AFVentures achieved 100+ contract awards in 40 hours (November 5-9, 2019), reducing typical 6-month evaluation timelines to under 1 week evaluation + 1 week to contract award.

## Use Cases & Applications
AFVentures portfolio companies address diverse Air Force mission sets across multiple domains:

**By Major Command:**
- Air Force Materiel Command (AFMC): 134 Phase II projects
- Air Combat Command (ACC): 83 Phase II projects
- Air Force Special Operations Command (AFSOC): 74 Phase II projects
- Air Force Education and Training Command (AETC): 58 Phase II projects
- United States Space Force (USSF): 42 Phase II projects
- Air Mobility Command (AMC): 28 Phase II projects
- Air Force Global Strike Command (AFGSC): 20 Phase II projects

**Geographic Distribution**: 538 testing/prototyping projects supported across 300+ offices/units spanning Pacific Air Forces (PACAF), U.S. Air Forces in Europe (USAFE-AFAFRICA), Air Force Reserve Command (AFRC), Air National Guard (ANG), and Air Forces Central Command (USAFCENT).

**Example Case Study—Vita Technologies**: Load Stability System (LSS) for helicopter payload/personnel stabilization. First Phase I contract (October 2018) for chaotic motion control system feasibility. Success led to $1M+ angel investment within 20 days and multiple follow-on Phase II contracts.

**Example Case Study—BrainGU, LLC**: Received first SBIR/STTR award (August 2019), followed by $12.3M sole-source follow-on contract from Cryptologic and Cyber Systems Division (Lackland AFB, November 2019) with 4-month commercialization timeline.

## Key Results (Report Data)

**Portfolio Metrics (FY18–FY20):**
- 2,299 contracts awarded
- $710M total AFVentures funding
- 1,433 companies (average 9 years old, 18 average employees)
- 72-day average time from proposal to contract
- 4,850 proposals received

**Return on Investment:**
- **5.8:1 ROI** (defined as post-award private investment + post-award government contracts + matched funding)
- $357M private matched funding
- $169M government matched funding
- Post-award private investment: $2.21 billion
- Post-award government contracts: $1.42 billion
- 29% of portfolio companies receiving venture capital (vs. 10% baseline across all DoD SBIR/STTR recipients since 2015)

**Venture Capital Integration:**
- 281 portfolio companies with VC backing
- VC funding distribution: 37% Seed stage, 19% Series A, 10.3% Series B, 6.8% Series C, 1.8% Series D+
- 10 companies achieved $500M+ valuations

**Commercialization & Transition:**
- 538 capabilities tested/prototyped by warfighter
- 300+ Air Force/DoD units supported
- Average 6 months to government commercialization
- 53% of $1.42B post-award government funding went to companies receiving first Air Force SBIR/STTR award through AFVentures

**Industrial Base Growth:**
- 75% of 1,433 companies received first Air Force SBIR/STTR award through AFVentures
- 18% had prior Air Force prime contracts before program
- 68% of companies had fewer than 5 federal prime contracts before AFVentures

**Demographic Diversity:**
- 37.6% self-certified small disadvantaged businesses
- 11.9% minority-owned
- 7.0% disabled veteran-owned
- 83.6% have 25 or fewer employees

**Space Portfolio (FY18–FY20):**
- $90.7M awarded to 215 companies across 292 contracts (12% of total portfolio)
- $107.5M post-award private investment
- $133.9M private matched funding
- $390M post-award government contracts
- $31.7M government matched funding
- Top states: California ($31.3M), Colorado ($9.6M), Massachusetts ($5.4M)

## COVID-19 Response (FY20)
AFVentures pivoted to pandemic response:
- **COVID-19 Industry Portal**: Received 3,740 proposals (single largest RFI in DoD history)
- Top submission sources: California (391), Virginia (185), Texas (177)
- **COVID-19 Commercial Solutions Offering (CSO)**: $300M in industrial base expansion (screening, testing, diagnostics, pharmaceuticals, PPE)
- **FEMA Rapid Response Evaluation**: 230+ Air/Space Force volunteers evaluated 300+ proposals; resulting in $640M in contracts delivering 2B+ articles of PPE
- Generated novel internal programs: DAF Rapid Agile Manufacturing Platform (AFRAMP), DAF Telework Solutions Team (AFTEST), Pandemic Case Management Suite (PCMS)

## Notable Details

**Competitive Advantages of AFVentures Model:**
1. **Speed**: 72-day average to contract vs. traditional 6-month timelines; demonstrated 40-hour capability for 100+ awards
2. **Open Innovation**: Inverted traditional RFP model; companies propose solutions rather than responding to pre-determined requirements
3. **Venture Capital Alignment**: 3x higher VC engagement (29% vs. 10% DoD baseline) indicates commercial market validation
4. **Private Capital Leverage**: 5.8:1 return on taxpayer investment; $2.21B private capital follow-on vs. $710M government investment
5. **Non-Traditional Supplier Growth**: 75% of awardees were first-time Air Force contractors; 68% had minimal prior federal experience

**Strategic Focus Areas:**
- AI/ML: 18.06% of funding (above commercial investment proportion)
- Aerospace: 8.98% of funding (above commercial baseline)
- Space technologies: Coordinated with newly established SpaceWERX for strategic alignment

**Measurement Methodology:**
- Crunchbase data mining for venture investment tracking (694/1,433 companies matched; 48% coverage)
- USASpending.gov DUNS matching for federal contract follow-on
- DUNS & Bradstreet for company demographics
- Identified 288 undisclosed funding rounds, suggesting conservative Phase III commercialization estimates
