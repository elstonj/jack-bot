## Executive Summary
This is the official DoD SBIR 25.1 Program Broad Agency Announcement (BAA), establishing the framework for small business innovation research proposals across three phases. It outlines eligibility requirements, proposal preparation instructions, evaluation criteria, and administrative procedures for companies seeking federal R&D funding. The program aims to stimulate technological innovation by small businesses, strengthen their role in meeting DoD R&D needs, and promote commercialization of defense-related research.

## Document Purpose & Scope
This document serves as the governing solicitation framework for the DoD SBIR 25.1 BAA. It is **not** a Black Swift Technologies proposal, report, or technical document. Instead, it is the government solicitation that establishes the rules and processes under which companies like BST would submit proposals to DoD SBIR opportunities.

## Program Structure

### Three-Phase Program
1. **Phase I (6-12 months):** Determines scientific, technical, and commercial merit and feasibility; generally awards funds to prove concept viability
2. **Phase II (24 months):** Continues Phase I R&D development based on Phase I results; focuses on technical advancement
3. **Phase III:** Commercialization phase funded by non-SBIR sources (private sector or other federal agencies); focuses on bringing SBIR-funded technology to market

### Direct to Phase II Program
- Allows qualified SBCs to advance directly to Phase II without Phase I award
- Requires documentation demonstrating Phase I-level feasibility
- Cannot be based on prior federally funded SBIR/STTR work
- Must demonstrate ownership or licensure of underlying IP

## Eligibility Requirements

### Small Business Concern (SBC) Requirements
- Must meet Small Business Administration (SBA) definitions per 13 C.F.R §§ 701-705 at time of award
- Must be U.S.-owned and controlled per 13 CFR § 121.702
- SBC must be registered in SAM (System for Award Management) with valid UEI
- SBC must be registered in SBA Company Registry

### Work Performance Requirements
- **Phase I:** SBC must conduct minimum 2/3 of research and analytical work (measured by direct and indirect costs)
- **Phase II:** SBC must perform minimum 50% of research and analytical work
- **Principal Investigator (PI):** Must have primary employment with proposing SBC (>50% time) at time of award and during performance
- **Location:** All R&D work must be performed in the United States

### Joint Ventures
- Permitted if entity qualifies as small business per SBA size and ownership requirements
- Must disclose relationships with foreign entities or foreign government-controlled companies
- Must submit representations for small business status per FAR provisions

### Ownership Restrictions
- Proposing SBCs majority-owned by multiple venture capital firms, hedge funds, or private equity firms are **ineligible** (unless Service/Component authorizes)
- No single venture capital, hedge fund, or private equity firm may own >50% unless that entity itself qualifies as a small business

## Performance Benchmark Requirements
- **Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate:** Addresses extent to which awardee progresses from Phase I to Phase II
- **Commercialization Benchmark:** Addresses extent to which awardee moves past Phase II toward commercialization
- **Penalty for Non-Performance:** Experienced firms failing benchmarks limited to maximum of 80 total Phase I and Direct to Phase II awards across DoD:
  - Army: 20 awards maximum
  - Navy: 20 awards maximum
  - Air Force: 20 awards maximum
  - All other DoD Components combined: 20 awards maximum

## Proposal Submission Requirements

### Required Registrations & Certifications
1. **SAM (System for Award Management):** Must register to obtain Unique Entity ID (UEI)
2. **SBA Company Registry:** Must register using UEI; provide Control ID and Proof of Registration at submission
3. **DSIP (Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal):** All proposal preparation and submission through DSIP
4. **Login.gov Account:** Required for DSIP access; account sharing prohibited

### Required Certifications at Proposal Submission
- SBC size, ownership, and program requirement compliance
- Certification of work percentages per SBIR requirements
- Disclosure of foreign affiliations/relationships
- FOCI (Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence) compliance
- Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI) disclosure per FAR 9.5
- SETA/A&AS conflicts disclosure if applicable
- FASCA (Federal Acquisition Supply Chain Security Act) compliance

### Security & Due Diligence Review
- DoD conducts risk-based due diligence to assess national security risks
- Reviews cybersecurity practices, patent analysis, employee analysis, foreign ownership/ties
- May request documentation of contractual/financial obligations with foreign entities
- May disqualify proposals posing unacceptable national security risk regardless of technical merit

## Proposal Volumes & Content

### Volume 1: Proposal Cover Sheet
- Prepared in DSIP
- Brief technical abstract (max 3,000 characters)
- Anticipated benefits and commercial applications (max 3,000 characters)
- Certification of SBC eligibility and work percentage compliance
- Topic number and proposal number (auto-assigned)

### Volume 2: Technical Volume (Phase I)
**Required sections in order:**
1. Identification and Significance of Problem/Opportunity
2. Phase I Technical Objectives
3. Phase I Statement of Work
4. Related Work
5. Relationship with Future Research or R&D
6. Commercialization Strategy
7. Key Personnel
8. Foreign Citizens
9. Facilities/Equipment
10. Subcontractors/Consultants
11. Prior, Current, or Pending Support
12. Identification and Assertion of Restrictions on Government Use, Release, or Disclosure

**Format Requirements:**
- Single PDF file (graphics embedded)
- No password protection, encryption, or active media
- Type size minimum 10-point
- Standard 8.5" x 11" paper with 1-inch margins
- Consecutive page numbering
- Header on each page with SBC name, topic number, and DSIP proposal number
- Page limit set by Service/Component (SBC responsible for verifying compliance)

### Volume 3: Cost Volume
- Completed using DSIP cost form
- List all key personnel with hours dedicated to project
- Detail special tooling, test equipment, materials
- Justify travel costs and relation to project
- Detail all subcontractor and consultant costs at prime contractor level
- Equipment title vests with DoD unless cost-effectiveness favors contractor transfer

### Volume 4: Company Commercialization Report (CCR)
- Required for SBCs with prior SBIR/STTR awards
- Firm admin must download CCR PDF from SBIR.gov
- Upload to DSIP "Firm Forms" (applies to all firm's proposals)
- **Warning:** Updating CCR in DSIP reopens all active proposals under open BAAs/CSOs for recertification
- Not required for first-time SBIR applicants

### Volume 5: Supporting Documents
- Joint venture representations (if applicable)
- OCI disclosure and mitigation plans
- Additional cost substantiation
- Export control compliance (DD Form 2345 for ITAR/EAR topics)
- Foreign affiliation/relationship disclosures

### Volume 6: Fraud, Waste and Abuse Training
- Certification of completion

### Volume 7: Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries
- Identify foreign nationals, entities, government entities with >5% equity stake
- Identify ultimate parent owners
- Identify other entities/individuals with >5% equity stake
- Used for national security risk assessment

## Special Requirements & Considerations

### Export Control
- ITAR/EAR-marked topics require certified DD Form 2345 or evidence of application submission
- DD Form 2345 approval required if ITAR/EAR proposal receives award

### Classified Proposals
- **Not accepted** under DoD SBIR Program
- If Phase II requires classified work, proposing SBC must possess facility clearance
- Facility/personnel clearance info: DCSA website (dcsa.mil)

### Human Subjects Research (RIHS/HSR)
- Must comply with federal/state law and agency policy
- Require HHS federal-wide assurance (FWA) or equivalent
- Personnel must complete appropriate training
- IRB approval required (exemptions possible per 32 CFR 219.101)
- Approval timeline: 1-3 months IRB + 3-6 months DoD review
- **No funding for human subjects research until ALL approvals granted**

### Animal Subjects Research
- Comply with federal/agency rules on animal care, handling, use
- IACUC approval required
- Secondary/HQ approval from DoD veterinarian required
- No animal research funding until all approvals granted

### Recombinant DNA Research
- Institutional biosafety committee approval required

### Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI)
- FAR 9.5 compliance required
- Full disclosure of potential OCIs for proposing SBC and team members
- OCI mitigation plan required addressing FAR 9.505-1 through 9.505-4
- Disclosure required if providing SETA, A&AS, or similar support to DoD offices (current or within 1 year)
- Government evaluates and may grant waiver if in government interest
- Failed disclosure or waiver may result in proposal rejection

## Intellectual Property & Rights

### Patents
- SBIR awardees receive patent rights per standard SBIR provisions
- Details covered in resulting contract

### Technical Data Rights
- Government retains rights to use technical data as specified in contract

### Copyrights
- SBIR awardees may retain copyright in works developed under SBIR funding
- Government retains unlimited rights to data produced under SBIR

### Invention Reporting
- All inventions must be reported per SBIR/STTR requirements

## Award Information & Contract Terms

### Award Limitations
- DoD not obligated to make awards under Phase I, II, or III
- All awards subject to risk-based due diligence security review and funds availability
- DoD not responsible for costs incurred before award issuance
- Proposals must conform to BAA terms
- No obligation to fund any proposal or specific number per topic

### Phase II Enhancement Policy
- Some DoD Services/Components offer Phase II Enhancement
- Additional Phase II funding if awardee matches with non-SBIR DoD or private sector funds
- Details in Service/Component instructions

### Commercialization Readiness Program (CRP)
- Army, Navy, and Air Force each have CRP
- OSD Transitions SBIR Technology (OTST) Pilot Program available
- Info: osd.ncr.ousd-r-e.mbx.sbir-sttr
