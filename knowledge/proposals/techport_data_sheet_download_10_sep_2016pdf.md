## Executive Summary
IDI (Innovative Dynamics, Inc.) developed a miniature optical Nephelometer sensor integrated with a commercial SO2 sensor for measuring volcanic ash and cloud properties in the upper atmosphere. The probe was designed as an expendable dropsonde for initial deployment and intended for future integration into the SCAN EAGLE UAV to provide hazard awareness for aircraft operating near volcanic plumes.

## Technical Approach
- **Phase I:** Development of miniature Nephelometer sensor prototype demonstrating ability to measure small ash and water particulates with discrimination between particle types
- **Phase II:** Integration of Nephelometer + commercial SO2 sensor into radiosonde package; tethered field testing at NASA Wallops facility; eventual deployment near active volcano in Central America
- **Packaging design:** Sized for compatibility with SCAN EAGLE UAV payload specifications and other payload recovery vehicles
- Technology Area: TA 8 (Science Instruments, Observatories, and Sensor Systems), TA 8.3 (In-Situ Instruments and Sensors)

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Volcanic Ash Nephelometer Probe**
- Miniature optical sensor for in-situ measurement of cloud particulates
- Provides cloud properties, hazardous volcanic ash detection, and icing information
- Capability to measure particulate size and density in upper atmosphere
- Intended as low-cost, expendable probe
- SO2 sensing capability via integrated commercial sensor
- Designed for deployment from high-altitude UAVs and dropsondes

**SCAN EAGLE UAV Integration**
- Proposed as eventual platform for Nephelometer probe payload
- Probe packaging designed to be upwardly compatible with SCAN EAGLE payload specifications and weight/size constraints

## Use Cases & Applications
1. **Volcanic ash monitoring:** Detection and characterization of volcanic ash plumes in commercial airspace (specifically referenced: North Atlantic near Iceland)
2. **Aircraft safety:** Hazard awareness for pilots regarding icing conditions and volcanic ash hazards that have caused engine damage
3. **Atmospheric research:** NASA earth sciences and high-altitude atmospheric research missions
4. **Commercial radiosondes:** Integration into operational weather radiosondes (900+ launched daily worldwide) for aircraft icing avoidance data

## Anticipated Benefits
- **To NASA:** Low-cost expendable probe for high-altitude UAS atmospheric research; particulate characterization capability for earth sciences programs
- **To commercial space industry:** 900+ daily radiosonde launches could be equipped with cloud property measurement; unique market niche due to affordability; high-volume application for aircraft icing avoidance
