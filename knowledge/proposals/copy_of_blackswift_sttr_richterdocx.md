# Letter of Support for Black Swift Technologies STTR Phase II Proposal

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- From: Dr. David Richter, Associate Professor, Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering and Earth Sciences, University of Notre Dame
- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy (Office of Naval Research)
- Program/Solicitation: NAVY STTR 25.A-T025: Expendable Air-sea Profiling Observations in Hazardous Weather Conditions (Phase II)
- Date: January 17, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 UAS (unmanned aircraft system)
- Related Programs: ONR MURI "SASCWATCH: Study on Air-Sea Coupling with Waves, Turbulence, and Clouds at High Winds"

## Executive Summary
Dr. David Richter from the University of Notre Dame offers institutional support for Black Swift Technologies' STTR Phase II proposal to develop expendable air-sea profiling systems for hazardous weather conditions. The letter emphasizes BST's S0 UAS platform as a cost-effective, reliable solution for delivering high-quality meteorological and turbulence measurements in challenging marine environments.

## Technical Approach
BST proposes to enhance the S0 UAS platform through:
- Enhanced data processing capabilities
- Integration of alternative wave measurement technologies
- Addressing icing challenges and communication improvements
- Application to Navy-specific operational requirements

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0 UAS (Unmanned Aircraft System)**
- Expendable/cost-effective atmospheric profiling platform
- Proven capability for operation in hazardous/turbulent conditions
- Successfully deployed in hurricane observations
- Provides atmospheric profiling and wind measurements
- Demonstrates superior data quality compared to comparable systems

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Air-sea interaction research** in high-wind conditions
- **Hurricane observations** and operations
- **Hazardous weather profiling** for Navy applications
- **Meteorological and turbulence measurements** in marine environments
- **Wave measurement** applications in extreme conditions

## Validation & Performance Claims
- Wind measurement accuracy validated against:
  - Instrumented tower measurements
  - NOAA P-3 aircraft dropsonde deployments
- Rigorous calibration and validation techniques employed
- Proven reliability in turbulent maritime conditions

## Competitive Positioning
BST's S0 UAS cost structure is positioned as more affordable than:
- Raytheon Coyote
- Area-I/Anduril Altius

## Key Institutional Support
- University of Notre Dame endorsement
- Alignment with ONR MURI "SASCWATCH" research objectives
- Support from full SASCWATCH research team
- Addresses critical need identified by leading atmospheric and air-sea interaction researchers

## Notable Details
- Letter emphasizes BST's design philosophy of "simplicity and affordability"
- Cost efficiency enables more frequent/widespread deployments and richer datasets
- Recognized commitment to data quality through rigorous validation
