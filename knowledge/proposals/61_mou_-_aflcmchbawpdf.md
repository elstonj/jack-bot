# MOU - AFLCMC/HBAW Letter of Support

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support / Memorandum of Understanding
- Client/Agency: Air Force Life Cycle Management Center (AFLCMC/HBAW), Hanscom Air Force Base, Massachusetts
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Phase II - Commercial Solutions for Upper Air Weather Observations
- Date: 31 October 2019
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Upper Air UAS solution
- Key Personnel: Bruce A. Lambert (Chief Engineer, Weather Systems Branch, AFLCMC/HBAW)

## Executive Summary
AFLCMC/HBAW expresses interest in collaborating with Black Swift Technologies on a feasibility and prototype project to develop an upper air weather observation UAS system. The Air Force identifies this as addressing a current unfunded requirement for tactical upper air measurement to support Air Force, Army, and Special Operations Forces (SOF) operations.

## Technical Approach
- Proposed feasibility/prototype project under SBIR Phase II framework
- Development of tactical upper air measurement tool using UAS platform
- Technology testing aligned with Air Force Weather operational needs

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Upper Air UAS Solution**
- Designed to conduct upper air weather observations
- Intended application: tactical weather measurement for military operations
- Addresses Air Force Weather requirements for atmospheric sounding data

## Use Cases & Applications
- Air Force Weather operations
- Army operations support
- Special Operations Forces (SOF) operations
- Tactical upper air atmospheric measurement and sounding

## Key Requirements Addressed
- Current unfunded requirement identified by ACC/A5W (Air Combat Command, Requirements Division)
- Need for tactical upper air measurement capability within Air Force Weather community

## Notable Details
- This letter explicitly states it "does not constitute a promise of funding"
- Contingent on Black Swift receiving SBIR Phase II award
- AFLCMC willing to serve as collaborative partner post-award
- Indicates Air Force Weather stakeholder interest and operational need validation
