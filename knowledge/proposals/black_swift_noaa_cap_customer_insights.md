## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies conducted targeted customer discovery to validate market demand for the S0, a low-cost, lightweight UAS platform designed for air deployment to gather atmospheric measurements. The effort identified key customer personas, developed interview questions for meteorological researchers and data processors, and compiled contact lists from NOAA webinar attendees and strategic partners in agtech.

## Technical Approach
The S0 is proposed as an air-deployable UAS platform that builds on proven concepts (Aerosonde, Coyote) with significant improvements:
- **Key design innovation**: Reduced complexity and weight versus existing platforms
- **Cost reduction**: Order of magnitude decrease compared to predecessors
- **Core capabilities maintained**: Endurance and measurement quality preserved despite lower cost
- **Deployment method**: Airborne deployment to overcome land-based limitations (regulations, storm interception distance)

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 (Proposed Platform)
- **Purpose**: Commercial UAS for atmospheric measurements during tropical storm sampling
- **Measurement capability**: 3D wind measurements at 100 Hz sampling rate
- **Additional sensors**: PTH (Pressure, Temperature, Humidity), sea surface temperature, vehicle height above water
- **Operational specs**: 
  - Endurance: ~40 minutes
  - Maximum altitude: 15,000 ft AGL
  - Designed for lower altitude boundary layer observations
- **Design philosophy**: Simplification to reduce weight and cost while maintaining measurement quality
- **Flexibility**: Programmable atmospheric sampling flight patterns (spiral ascent/descent, volumetric measurement, long baseline vertical flux measurement)

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Use Case: Tropical Storm/Hurricane Observation
- Gather targeted meteorological data during evolving storm systems
- Focus on boundary layer and sea surface observations
- Improve forecast models and predictive capabilities
- Air deployment approach overcomes regulatory and geographic obstacles faced by land-based platforms

### Secondary Applications (inferred from customer survey):
- Atmospheric science research requiring 3D wind and thermodynamic measurements
- Real-time data ingestion for modeling
- Multi-vehicle coordinated operations (suggested in survey questions)

## Customer Discovery Approach

### Target Customer Personas:
1. **Field research meteorologists** - collecting meteorological data during storm systems
2. **Data processors and modelers** - ingesting and processing atmospheric observations

### Key Survey Questions Developed:
1. How would an aircraft with 100 Hz sampling of pressure, temperature, humidity, and winds; 40-minute endurance; and 15,000 ft AGL capability benefit your current work?
2. What price range would you be looking for?
3. Would simultaneous multi-vehicle coordination be useful for your operations?
4. What customizations would you require? Are programmable flight patterns (spirals, volumetric, vertical flux measurement) sufficient?
