## Executive Summary
This document outlines Black Swift Technologies' IT security procedures and compliance framework for NASA SBIR/STTR Contract 80NSSC19C0181. It establishes policies for protecting NASA electronic information and IT resources, including Sensitive But Unclassified (SBU) data, in accordance with NASA FAR Supplement clause 1852.204-76 and NIST/NASA IT security requirements.

## Scope of IT Access
- **Primary NASA System:** SBIR/STTR Awardee Firm Electronic Handbook (EHB) at https://ehb8.gsfc.nasa.gov/contracts/public/firmHome.do for electronic submission of contract deliverables, invoices, and technical reports
- **Access Management:** NASA Account Management System (NAMS) with NASA Agency User ID and password via Identity and Access Management System (IdMAX)
- **Additional NASA Systems:** None applicable beyond EHB
- **Registration Limitations:** Limited to personnel involved in contract negotiation and administration

## Compliance Requirements
BST personnel must:
- Protect confidentiality, integrity, and availability of NASA Electronic Information
- Understand and adhere to NIST and NASA IT Security requirements per www.nasa.gov/offices/ocio/itsecurity/index.html
- Complete NASA Online Annual IT Security Training
- Comply with Sensitive But Unclassified (SBU) information handling protocols

## Sensitive Information Protection Obligations
Per clause 1852.237-72, BST committed to:
1. Use sensitive information only for contract performance, not competitive advantage
2. Safeguard from unauthorized use and disclosure
3. Limit access to employees with documented need-to-know
4. Preclude access/disclosure to external persons and entities
5. Train all personnel on authorized uses and mandatory protections
6. Obtain written employee affirmation of training compliance
7. Administer monitoring processes and report breaches to Contracting Officer and COTR

## General Rules of Behavior
All BST personnel must comply with 15 mandatory security rules:
1. Use systems only for lawful, official, authorized purposes
2. Protect all NASA information including PII and SBU data
3. Report security incidents immediately
4. Encrypt NASA data on mobile/transportable devices and removable media
5. Review NASA privacy/security notices before system access
6. Screen-lock/log off when away from workstation
7. Provide access only to authorized personnel with proper clearance and need-to-know
8. Minimize and destroy sensitive data while traveling
9. Properly mask/label classified, sensitive, and proprietary documents
10. Adhere to Separation of Duties principles
11. Do not use anonymizer sites that bypass security mechanisms
12. Do not engage in behavior endangering IT equipment or data integrity
13. Supervisors must adequately instruct, train, and supervise employees
14. Adhere to minimum password requirements
15. Do not share account passwords; protect at highest classification level

## Incident Response Protocol
- Immediate notification required to NASA Security Operations Center for any security incident affecting confidentiality, integrity, or availability
- Immediate notification required if NASA IT systems, data, or contract deliverables are compromised, misused, distorted, lost, or destroyed
- Notification must include Contracting Officer (CO) and Contracting Officer's Technical Representative (COTR)

## Notable Details
- Document explicitly references potential sanctions under Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986 and National Information Infrastructure Protection Act of 1996
- Emphasis on SBU information handling (broadly defined as unclassified information pertinent to U.S. national interest requiring special handling and disclosure protection)
- Unauthorized attempts to upload or change information on NASA servers are strictly prohibited
- Sanctions enforced commensurate with infraction level
