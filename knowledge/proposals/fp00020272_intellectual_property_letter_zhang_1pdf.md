## Executive Summary
This is a pre-submission IP agreement letter from the University of Miami requiring Black Swift Technologies to acknowledge and agree to negotiate in good faith for licensing of any University of Miami background intellectual property that may be used or incorporated in a Navy STTR Phase II proposal on hazardous weather air-sea profiling observations using small uncrewed aircraft systems.

## Program Details
- **Sponsoring Agency:** Department of Navy
- **Program Type:** STTR Phase II (Small Business Technology Transfer)
- **Project Title:** Expendable Air-Sea Profiling Observations in Hazardous Weather Conditions via Small Uncrewed Aircraft System
- **UM Role:** Subrecipient

## Key Agreement Terms
BST commits to:
1. Review and agree to UM's background IP terms before proposal submission
2. Negotiate in good faith to enter into a license agreement with UM for any Background IP used or incorporated if the application is awarded

**IP Definition:** Includes all forms of intellectual property (patentable or not) including inventions, discoveries, copyrightable works, mask works, trademarks, service marks, trade secrets, and proprietary data under laws of any state or country.
