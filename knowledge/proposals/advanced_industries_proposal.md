# Advanced Industries Proposal: TTwistor Advanced Small Unmanned Aircraft System

- Type: Technology commercialization proposal / proof-of-concept application
- Client/Agency: Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator Program (TTO Proof of Concepts Program)
- Program/Solicitation: Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator Program

- Key Personnel: Brian Argrow (PI, RECUV), Jack Elston (BST founder, former RECUV PhD candidate)

## Executive Summary
This proposal describes the TTwistor UAS, an advanced small unmanned aircraft system derived from the successful Tempest UAS heritage. The system combines an improved twin-motor airframe design (manufactured by UASUSA) with Black Swift Technologies' SwiftPilot Pro autopilot to enable superior payload integration, flight performance, and mission flexibility for civil and commercial applications. NOAA UAS Program Office committed as first customer and investor.

## Technical Approach

**Airframe Design:**
- Twin-propeller configuration with motor-propeller mounted on each wing (vs. single fuselage-mounted motor in Tempest)
- Enables instruments/probes to be mounted near airframe centerline with extensions into undisturbed airflow
- Incorporates swappable modular "nosecones" custom-designed for specific instruments or payloads
- Propeller airflow increases local circulation to improve lift during takeoff
- Manufactured by UASUSA Inc.

**Autopilot:**
- BST SwiftPilot Pro autopilot integrated for autonomous flight control
- Derived from proven Tempest UAS deployments in severe weather research

**Communications:**
- Prototype 900-MHz phased-array antenna system under development (not included in this proposal)
- Electronic beam-steering technology superior to mechanically-steered alternatives
- Second prototype in development with First RF Corp.
- Designed for improved communication between aircraft and ground station autopilot

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SwiftPilot Pro Autopilot:**
- Advanced autopilot system developed by Black Swift Technologies
- Provides autonomous flight control for TTwistor UAS
- Evolved from lessons learned in Tempest UAS operations
- Supports modular payload configurations

**TTwistor UAS (overall system):**
- Small electrically-powered unmanned aircraft system
- Designed for public/civil applications requiring cost-effective solutions
- Improved over Tempest with expanded capabilities
- Carries meteorological sensors, cameras, communications hardware, and other payloads
- Targets performance advantages in small electric UAS class

## Use Cases & Applications

**Proven Applications (from Tempest Heritage):**
- Severe weather research (tornado genesis studies, supercell sampling)
- Atmospheric sensing and meteorological research
- Emergency/first response operations
- Precision agriculture
- Surveying and mapping
- Education and science research
- Mobile communications experiments

**NOAA-Specific Applications:**
- Monitoring dangerous/remote areas (poles, oceans, wildlands, volcanic islands, wildfires)
- Bridging observational gap between ground instruments and satellite data
- Advanced atmospheric research and forecasting
- Routine operational environmental monitoring

## Commercial Market Context

**Existing Customer Base:**
- Tempest UAS has sold >50 units to customers including:
  - University of Nebraska
  - MIT
  - US Navy Research Lab
  - Raytheon
  - Lockheed Martin
  - Airware

**Market Projections (AUVSI 2013):**
- Precision agriculture and public safety identified as most promising markets
- Predicted $13.6 billion economic impact in first three years of UAS integration into National Airspace System (2015 estimate)
- $82.1 billion cumulative impact projected 2015-2025
- 34,000 manufacturing jobs and 70,000 additional jobs in first three years
- 103,776 total jobs estimated by 2025
- $482 million in state tax revenue 2015-2025

**Regulatory Context:**
- FAA Modernization and Reauthorization Act of 2012 mandated integration plan for civil UAS into NAS by September 30, 2015
- Small UAS rules targeted for November 2014 release (at time of proposal)
- Currently only "public" entities (government agencies, state universities) have easy NAS access
- Commercial market expected to expand significantly once regulations published

## Notable Details

**Heritage and Pedigree:**
- TTwistor descends directly from Tempest UAS proven in VORTEX2 field campaign (spring 2009-2010)
- Tempest originally built by Skip Miller Models for NSF-funded tornado research
- Dr. Jack Elston was primary developer of Tempest NetUAS communications/command/control architecture; later founded BST in 2012
- Multiple co-authors of peer-reviewed journals on Tempest performance

**First Customer:**
- NOAA Unmanned Aircraft Systems Program Office (UASPO) serving as first customer and investor
- Aligns with NOAA vision of UAS revolutionizing observing strategies
- Addresses NOAA UASPO goals: increase observing capacity, develop high science-return missions, transition cost-effective solutions to routine operations

**Competitive Advantages:**
- Proven airframe design with decade of operational history
- Advanced autopilot from specialized UAS vendor (BST)
- Modular nosecone design enables rapid payload customization
- Twin-motor configuration solves payload mounting and aerodynamic efficiency problems inherent in single-motor Tempest
- Timing advantageous with pending FAA regulatory approvals for commercial UAS
