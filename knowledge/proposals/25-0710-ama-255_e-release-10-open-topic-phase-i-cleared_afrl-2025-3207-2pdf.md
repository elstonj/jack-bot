## Executive Summary
This document is a presentation overview of the Department of the Air Force's Open Topic Phase I SBIR/STTR solicitation (25.5/E Release 10). It provides guidance to small business concerns (SBCs) on solicitation requirements, proposal structure, submission procedures, and evaluation criteria for Phase I awards of up to $75K (SBIR) or $110K (STTR) with a 3-month period of performance.

## Solicitation Overview

### Key Dates
- Solicitation Open: 2 July 2025
- Solicitation Close: 5 August 2025 (Noon ET)
- Expected Contract Award: December 2025
- Solicitation Questions Deadline: 15 July 2025 (4:00 PM ET)
- Consolidated Q&A Response: ~18 July 2025

### Award Parameters
- **SBIR Phase I**: Up to $75,000 per award
- **STTR Phase I**: Up to $110,000 per award
- **Period of Performance**: 3 months
- **Expected Awards**: ~700 per year across all open topics

### Eligibility Requirements
SBCs must meet all three criteria:
1. Organized for profit with a place of business located in the United States
2. More than 50% owned and controlled by a U.S. entity
3. 500 employees or less

### STTR-Specific Requirements
- Requires one qualifying research institute (university, non-profit, or FFRDC) to perform 30-60% of work
- Principal Investigator (PI) may be employed by either the research entity or small business, but must spend at least 40% of time with the SBC
- Leverages close ties with Federal R&D and operational communities

## Solicitation Focus & Desired Solutions

The Open Topic solicitation seeks:
- **Innovative, dual-use technology solutions** applicable to both defense and non-defense markets
- **Clear potential to solve a DAF mission need** (USAF or USSF)
- **Capability to prototype under accelerated schedule**
- **Ability to scale and grow the industrial base for defense**
- **Applied Research/R&D or Test and Evaluation (RDT&E) efforts** (not basic research or COTS resale)
- **Commitment from an empowered DAF end-user customer** for Phase II

## Proposal Structure & Requirements

### Volume Organization (7 Volumes Required)
1. **Volume 1**: Proposal Cover Sheet (SAM, UEI, CAGE, Abstract) - prepared on DSIP
2. **Volume 2**: Technical Volume - Using **Required Proposal Template** (DQ if not used)
3. **Volume 3**: Cost Volume - Online form in DSIP
4. **Volume 4**: Company Commercialization Report (CCR)
5. **Volume 5**: Supporting Documents (6 attachments required)
6. **Volume 6**: Fraud, Waste, and Abuse Training - Module in DSIP
7. **Volume 7**: Disclosure of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships - Online form in DSIP

### Required Attachments (Volume 5)
- Att 1: Phase I Proposal Template (required for Vol 2)
- Att 2: SBIR/STTR Funding Agreement Certification
- Att 3: SBIR/STTR Funding Agreement Certification - Life Cycle Certification
- Att 4: Identification and Assertion of Use, Release or Disclosure Restrictions
- Att 5: SBIR applicants must submit Majority-Owned Multiple Venture Capital/Hedge Fund/Private Equity Certification; STTR applicants must submit Contractor Certification Regarding Preaward Requirements

### Proposal Template Requirements
- **Mandatory Use**: Failure to use the template is a disqualifying (DQ) offense
- **Page Limit**: 13 pages (plus optional Glossary)
- **Content Location**: All content must appear on designated pages per Table of Contents; evaluators only review content on designated pages
- **Blank Pages Acceptable**: Proposals may have blank pages if section is completed on earlier designated pages
- **Instructions**: Template includes [bold, blue, italicized, bracketed] instructional text that must be deleted before submission

## Evaluation Criteria (In Descending Order of Importance)
1. **Defense Need**: Clear proposed benefit to government customer
2. **Technical Merit**: Feasibility and technical approach
3. **Commercialization**: Path to commercialization and market potential

## Common Reasons for Disqualification or Non-Selection

- Exceeding cost or duration limits
- Inactive or not updated SAM.gov registration (must be active prior to award)
- Uncertain/unclear defense need
- Proposed work not appropriate for SBIR funding (COTS resale proposals not eligible)
- Wrong topic number submitted
- Unaddressed requirements (missing volumes, attachments, or incomplete responses to evaluation criteria)
- Failure to use required proposal template

## Submission Rules

- **One Proposal Per Solicitation**: A business may only submit one proposal per solicitation
- **Topic Selection Restriction**: Prohibited from submitting proposals under both SBIR and STTR topics within a single solicitation
- **Component Restriction**: If multiple proposals submitted, only the most recent certified proposal prior to deadline is eligible
- **No Component Crossing**: Cannot submit to both AFX255 (25.5 Release) and AFX25E (25.E Release) topics
