## Executive Summary
This SOW outlines Black Swift Technologies' proposed development and deployment of a specialized payload for the S2 UAS to conduct volcano monitoring missions for the USGS. The payload would integrate multiple gas sensors, spectroscopy equipment, and imaging systems to characterize volcanic emissions and topography from a safe distance of 10-15 km. BST would design the payload, develop real-time telemetry systems, conduct validation flights, and deploy the system to Makushin Volcano in Alaska for operational monitoring.

## Technical Approach

### Payload Development & Integration
- Design one or two nosecones for the S2 UAS capable of housing specialized sensors
- Identify communication interfaces, power requirements, and weight/volume optimization for each sensor
- Develop data recording system with geographic and temporal tagging for post-processing
- Create calibration procedures and pre/post-flight protocols in collaboration with USGS
- Conduct bench testing and characterization of sensor rise time and critical properties
- Perform local validation flights (near BST headquarters in Colorado) to verify performance during flight

### Telemetry & Ground Systems
- Develop high-data-rate, long-distance real-time telemetry system for video and sensor streams
- Create improved base-station display software capable of:
  - Displaying video alongside real-time sensor data
  - Color-coded flight route visualization
  - Real-time spectroscopic analysis of UV spectral data

### Regulatory & Operational
- Secure necessary FAA flight permissions and operational authority
- Provide documentation for future USGS and Department of Interior Office of Aviation Services approval
- Plan mission series with USGS and handle logistics
- Conduct flight operations with NASA staff assuming Operational Control
- Coordinate with USGS scientists and NASA observers on-site

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- **What it is:** Black Swift's intermediate-range unmanned aircraft system
- **Proposed use:** Volcano monitoring platform with 10-15 km range capability, capable of climbing to 10,000-15,000 feet summit elevations
- **Enhancement:** Development of "more robust S2 platform for low-temperature environments available to procure"

### Payload Sensor Suite
Nosecone(s) integrating:
- **Gas sensors:** CO2, SO2, H2S concentration measurement
- **Spectroscopy:** Upward-looking UV spectrometer measuring scattered sunlight radiance to determine vertical column SO2 (used to calculate total SO2 emission rates)
- **Environmental sensors:** 3D wind velocity, temperature, relative humidity, pressure
- **Positioning & Imaging:** GPS with real-time kinematic (RTK) geotagging capability
- **Camera systems:** Nadir photogrammetry camera for digital elevation models and deformation detection; gimbaled or forward-looking video camera for observations

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Mission: Volcano Gas Emissions Monitoring
- **Objective:** Characterize gas composition and emission rates during volcanic activity
- **Measurements:** Relative abundances of H2O, CO2, SO2, H2S; total SO2 emission rates; emission rates of all primary volatile species
- **Advantage over manned operations:** Eliminates need for costly helicopter support and allows safe observation from 10-15 km distance

### Secondary Mission: Topographic & Deformation Monitoring
- High-resolution baseline topographic models via photogrammetry
- Repeat surveys to characterize deformation rates, volumes, and extents of lava emissions
- Detection of subsurface magma presence indicators

### Target Volcano: Makushin, Alaska
- **Location:** Northern end of Unalaska Island, eastern Aleutians; 25 km west of Dutch Harbor
- **Characteristics:** Active volcano, rated "Very High Threat" in National Volcano Early Warning System (NVEWS)
- **Justification:** 
  - 2018 gas survey showed hundreds of tons SO2 per day emissions (indicating shallow magma)
  - Follow-up survey planned for summer 2019 but restricted by helicopter availability
  - Summit elevation 1800 m accessible via intermediate-range UAS
  - Close proximity to Dutch Harbor (accessible via commercial flights)
  - Archetype for revolutionizing USGS volcano monitoring capability
  - Baseline monitoring possible at least once per year

## Key Deliverables

1. **Customized nosecone(s)** with integrated gas sensors (CO2, SO2, H2S), UV spectrometer, 3D wind sensor, environmental sensors, RTK-enabled photogrammetry camera, and video camera
2. **Enhanced S2 UAS platform** optimized for low-temperature environments
3. **High-data-rate long-distance telemetry system** with real-time video streaming capability
4. **Base-station display software** with video overlay, real-time sensor data visualization, flight route mapping, and spectroscopic analysis
5. **Bench test and validation flight data** (Colorado-based testing) with documentation
6. **Mission deployment data** from target volcano operations
7. **Final reports** documenting deployment activities and aircraft characteristics for future DOI Office of Aviation Services procurement/approval

### Competitive Advantage Highlighted
- Intermediate-range capability (10-15 km) exceeds typical commercial sUAS limitations
- Enables safe observation of explosive, unpredictable volcanoes during active phases
- Eliminates need for close-range operations in hazardous environments
- Real-time telemetry and decision-making capability during observation missions
- Integrated multi-parameter monitoring (gas composition + topography) in single platform
