## Executive Summary
This document establishes the formal evaluation rubric for DARPA ERIS proposals. It defines four primary evaluation factors with weighted scoring, establishing how DARPA will assess submissions across innovation impact, team capability, problem definition clarity, and commercial/defense market viability.

## Evaluation Framework

### Factor 1: Advancing the State of the Art
- **Weight:** 40% (40 points possible)
- **Assessment Focus:** Degree to which successful solution will advance current state of the art in relevant field(s)
- **Rating Scale:**
  - Unsatisfactory (0): Will not advance state of the art
  - Marginal (10): Will slightly advance state of the art
  - Satisfactory (20): Will adequately advance state of the art
  - Highly Satisfactory (30): Will significantly advance state of the art
  - Superior (40): Will exponentially advance and/or redefine state of the art

### Factor 2: Team Capability (Key Personnel Vision, Expertise, and Experience)
- **Weight:** 30% (30 points possible)
- **Assessment Focus:** Capability of proposing team's key personnel and human resource planning to drive project to successful outcome
- **Rating Scale:**
  - Unsatisfactory (0): Team has not demonstrated capability
  - Marginal (8): Team somewhat capable
  - Satisfactory (16): Team capable
  - Highly Satisfactory (22): Team more than capable with retained and proposed personnel
  - Superior (30): Team resoundingly capable with retained and proposed personnel

### Factor 3: Defining the Problem & Current State of the Art
- **Weight:** 20% (20 points possible)
- **Assessment Focus:** Clarity and comprehensiveness in defining: (1) scope of problem(s) to be addressed, and (2) current state of art in field of potential solutions
- **Rating Scale:**
  - Unsatisfactory (0): Neither problem scope nor state of art clearly/comprehensively defined
  - Marginal (5): Somewhat clear and comprehensive definitions of both
  - Satisfactory (10): Adequate clarity and comprehensiveness of both
  - Highly Satisfactory (15): Very clear and comprehensive definitions of both
  - Superior (20): Exceptional clarity and comprehensiveness of both

### Factor 4: Defense and/or Commercial Market Use Case/Impact
- **Weight:** 10% (10 points possible)
- **Assessment Focus:** Proposed solution's impact to current commercial and/or defense use cases
- **Rating Scale:**
  - Unsatisfactory (0): No demonstrated impact or viable applications
  - Marginal (2): Marginal impact or somewhat viable applications
  - Satisfactory (4): Sufficiently demonstrated impact or viable applications
  - Highly Satisfactory (6): Significant impact or applications
  - Superior (10): Exceptional impact or applications

## Scoring Summary
- **Total Points Possible:** 100
- **Dominant Factor:** "Advancing the State of the Art" (40% weight) is the primary evaluation driver
- **Secondary Factor:** "Team Capability" (30% weight)
- **Supporting Factors:** Problem definition (20%) and market impact (10%)
