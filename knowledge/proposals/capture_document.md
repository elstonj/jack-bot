# Early-Stage Capital and Retention (ESCR) Grant Proposal - Colorado AI Accelerator Program

## Document Metadata
- Type: Grant proposal / program application
- Client/Agency: Colorado Advanced Industries (AI Accelerator Program)
- Program/Solicitation: Early-Stage Capital and Retention (ESCR) Grants - Phase III Early Stage Development
- Date: February 25, 2015
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: SwiftCore FMS (Frequency Modulated Soil Moisture System), SuperSwift, soil moisture sensor system
- Key Personnel: Frew, Wayne, Al, Robin (listed as support letter providers; specific roles not defined)

## Executive Summary
BST proposed a $250,000 ESCR grant to commercialize its soil moisture sensing system and related products (SwiftCore FMS and SuperSwift). The grant would be leveraged with existing SBIR Phase II ($425k) and Phase E ($150k) funding, with total project budget of approximately $825,000 over two years. The strategy focuses on sales, distribution, marketing, and business growth activities to position BST for private investment and expanded market capture.

## Technical Approach
- **Sensor development and prototype design/testing**: to be conducted using NASA funds in partnership with University of Colorado (CU)
- **ESCR grant allocation**: Support for commercialization activities (sales, distribution, marketing, business growth) rather than primary technical development
- **Staffing**: 2 FTE positions for 2 years dedicated to sales/distribution and business growth
- **Budget structure**: 
  - SBIR Phase II: $425,000 (already allocated)
  - SBIR Phase E: $150,000 (technical development at CU and BST)
  - AI/ESCR funding: $250,000 (sales, distribution, marketing, 2 FTE for 2 years, external commercialization costs)

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SwiftCore FMS (Frequency Modulated Soil Moisture System)**
- Soil moisture sensing technology
- Proposed for both near-term government/research applications and long-term commercial agricultural/infrastructure uses

**SuperSwift**
- Related product in soil moisture/sensing system family
- Commercialization target alongside SwiftCore FMS

**Soil Moisture Sensor System**
- Core technology platform for both products
- Supports multiple applications: wildfire prediction, landslide monitoring, flood detection

## Use Cases & Applications

**Near-term (Government/Research Focus)**
- Support state and local government agencies
- Wildfire monitoring and prediction
- Landslide detection and monitoring
- Flood forecasting and early warning
- Researcher support

**Long-term (Commercial Focus - contingent on FAA approval)**
- Agriculture and ranching applications
- Commercial infrastructure monitoring
- Commercial surveying operations

## Key Program Goals
By end of ESCR grant period, BST would achieve:
- Strong positioning for non-state investment (angel, venture capital, SBIR follow-on)
- Ability to capture new private and public contracts
- Significantly increased company valuation and stability
- Market share in both commercial and government sectors
- Manufacturing, sales, and distribution infrastructure in Colorado

## Notable Details
- **Interstate/State Requirement**: All resulting products/services must be manufactured or performed in Colorado
- **Maximum Grant Amount**: ESCR grants capped at $250,000
- **Leveraged Funding Strategy**: Combined SBIR Phase II and Phase E with state grant for optimal commercialization support
- **Dual-Track Approach**: Near-term government/research applications fund early revenue while long-term commercial applications await regulatory approval (FAA)
- **University Partnership**: CU collaboration for technical development; letters of support requested from Frew, Wayne, Al, Robin
- **Industry Classification**: Aerospace (primary), Electronics and Infrastructure (secondary)
