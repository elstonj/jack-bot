## Executive Summary
This document justifies the selection of Dr. Joshua Wadler at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University as the sole source subcontractor for a Navy STTR Phase II effort on hazardous weather. The justification emphasizes Dr. Wadler's specialized expertise in tropical cyclone research and his continuity from Phase I work.

## Expertise & Qualifications
- **Specialized Knowledge**: Tropical cyclone structure and intensity change; boundary layer thermodynamics
- **Platform Experience**: Demonstrated experience with emerging sampling platforms, specifically small UAS for tropical cyclone observation
- **Prior Experience**: Background with NOAA and University of Miami
- **Research Community Standing**: Noted as having "unique insights and technical approaches that are not widely available in the research community"

## Continuity Rationale
- Phase I foundation: Dr. Wadler's involvement ensures continuation of work begun in Phase I
- Access to experimental frameworks and validated methodologies integral to proposed Phase II work
- Avoiding re-onboarding costs and schedule delays that would result from engaging a different researcher
- Risk mitigation: Competing the effort to other sources would not yield comparable technical alignment

## Key Argument
Dr. Wadler and ERAU are presented as "the only responsible source capable of continuing, and meeting the project's requirements" due to:
1. Specialized expertise unavailable elsewhere
2. Continuity from Phase I
3. Established experimental frameworks and methodologies
4. Cost and schedule efficiency

## Notable Details
- This is a follow-on Phase II effort, indicating BST was successful in Phase I competition
- Focus area is hazardous weather observation, likely hurricane/tropical cyclone sampling
- The small UAS platform capability (referenced in context of cyclone observation) appears relevant to BST's core unmanned systems business
