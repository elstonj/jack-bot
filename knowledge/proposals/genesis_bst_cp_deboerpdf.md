## Executive Summary
This is a DOE Current and Pending Support disclosure form for Gijs de Boer, Interim Associate Laboratory Director at Brookhaven National Laboratory (Environment, Biology, and Nuclear Science and Nonproliferation). The form documents de Boer's current and pending research projects involving atmospheric transport modeling, agricultural security, power-grid resilience, and autonomous systems for environmental sampling (2026-2031).

## Technical Approach
The projects involve:
1. **WRF-Chem simulations** for atmospheric transport and transformation of airborne contaminants with particle dispersion/deposition mapping
2. **Physics-Informed Neural Networks** for high-resolution wind prediction using sparse data networks
3. **AI/ML techniques** to enable fully-autonomous UAS flight operations for environmental sampling
4. **Predictive modeling** for biogenic aerosol transport

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **WRF-Chem modeling**: Atmospheric simulation tool for local-scale redistribution of airborne materials, accounting for emission sources and meteorological influences
- **Physics-Informed Neural Networks**: Framework for wind prediction in power-grid resilience applications
- **Autonomous UAS systems**: Unmanned aircraft capable of real-time adaptive experimentation in dynamic environmental systems

## Use Cases & Applications

2. **Agricultural Defense** – C-AIR project for predictive transport of biogenic aerosols
3. **Power-Grid Resilience** – Wind prediction for extreme weather event impacts
4. **Environmental Monitoring** – Autonomous mobile laboratories for real-time sampling
