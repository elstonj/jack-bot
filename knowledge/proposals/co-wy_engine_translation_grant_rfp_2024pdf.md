## Executive Summary
The Colorado-Wyoming Climate Resilience Engine, an NSF Regional Innovation Engines awardee, is soliciting proposals for its Translation Grant Program to accelerate commercialization of innovations addressing climate resilience in Colorado and Wyoming. The program will award $250K-$750K per project (3 awards per cycle) to advance technologies from translational phase to startup phase, with potential for up to $160M in NSF funding over ten years across the regional innovation ecosystem.

## Program Overview
The CO-WY Engine received $15M in initial NSF funding and is positioned to receive up to $160M over ten years to build a technology-driven innovation ecosystem for climate solutions and community resilience. The initiative aims to: 1) Boost innovation capacity; 2) Create sustainable innovation ecosystems; and 3) Demonstrate inclusive economic growth. The Engine comprises small businesses, industry leaders, universities, national laboratories, NGOs, community groups, government entities, and policy makers.

## Funding Focus Areas
The Translation Grant Program prioritizes projects in the following challenge/solution areas:
- Complex Earth Sensing
- Soil Carbon Capture Data & Analytics
- **Methane Emissions Analysis**
- Extreme Weather Modeling
- Wildfire Risk & Prediction
- Water Availability Prediction

(Note: These areas may change in future funding cycles)

## Program Goals & Priorities

**Goal 1: Enhance the Translation Pathway of Climate Resilience Solutions at Speed and Scale**
- Priority: Projects in translational or early startup phase with scalable solutions and clear pathways to real-world implementation within short timeframes

**Goal 2: Bridge the Funding/Resource Gap for Accelerated Translation**
- Priority: Projects with strong translation interest, startup development resources, and roadmaps for securing additional translational funding

**Goal 3: Support Growth of the Translational Climate Innovation Ecosystem**
- Priority: Cross-sector collaborative approaches, stakeholder engagement, long-term sustainability strategies, workforce development, and economic growth

**Goal 4: Foster Inclusivity**
- Priority: Clear inclusion strategies, diverse team composition, and impact on marginalized communities including tribal nations

## Funding Details

**Anticipated Funding Amount:** $250K-$750K per project (direct plus indirect costs) over 12-month project period
**Anticipated Number of Awards:** 3 per cycle
**Project Period:** 12 calendar months, with potential for formal no-cost extensions up to 6 months (not guaranteed)

**Matched/Leveraged Resources Requirements:**
- General expectation: 1:2 ($1 Engine to $2 non-Engine) leveraged or matching resources
- Exception: Teams working directly with tribal or underserved communities may provide less than 1:2
- Teams with established corporate partners expected to provide more than 1:2

Acceptable matched/leveraged resources include:
- Cash or in-kind contributions (personnel time, equipment, space, datasets)
- Collaborations and partnerships
- Integration with existing programs/initiatives
- Existing research and data
- Existing technology deployment
- Internal organizational capacity building
- Public-private partnerships
- Community assets utilization
- Non-federal award stacking

## Eligibility

**Who May Submit:**
- Startup ventures (must have received <$20M from grants and third-party investors since inception; annual revenue <$2M; substantial operations in CO or WY)
- Faculty/researchers from eligible Colorado and Wyoming institutions of higher education:
  - Colorado School of Mines
  - Colorado State University
  - Metro State University
  - University of Colorado Boulder
  - University of Colorado Denver
  - University of Northern Colorado
  - University of Wyoming

Underlying technology may have been developed in whole or part with institutions of higher education in the two states or regional Federal Laboratories.

**PI/Project Team Member Requirements:**
- No restrictions on PI from startup ventures
- PIs from institutions must be: tenure-track/non-tenure-track faculty, research scientist/scholar, center/institute director, dean, department head, or equivalent
- Team members may come from any discipline; non-university members encouraged (private entities, universities, federal labs, NGOs)

**Proposal Submission Limits:**
- No limit on number of proposals per organization
- PI can lead only one project but may serve in other roles on other teams
- All proposals from institutions must be routed through institutional sponsored programs office; direct PI submissions will not be accepted

## Eligible Costs
Funds must advance toward:
- Minimum viable product (MVP) development
- MVP pilot with partners
- Startup formation
- Team expansion
- Intellectual property strengthening
- Business plan development
- Capital/alternative funding procurement

**Allowable costs:** Senior personnel, postdoctoral fellows, graduate and undergraduate students, equipment, domestic travel, materials and supplies, consultant services, other direct costs, indirect costs

**Requirements:**
- Participation in incubator or accelerator required for startup applicants
- All teams must participate in regular Engine convenings
- Use federally-negotiated indirect cost rates or de minimis rate of 10% MTDC if no negotiated rate exists

## Accountability & Reporting
- Incubator/accelerator participation required for startups
- Mandatory participation in regular Engine convenings
- Progress updates and evaluations every 6 months
- Final report required including: activity summary with impact evaluation, outcomes assessment with metrics/timelines, expenditures summary
- Unspent funds must be returned to CO-WY Engine

## Evaluation Criteria
Review panel will include CO-WY Engine team and external technical, academic, and business experts. All applications reviewed; funding consideration based on proposal completeness, likelihood of success, and potential climate resilience impact through scaled commercial opportunities/practices.

**Review Criteria:**
- **Technical Viability:** Novelty, likelihood of success, technology strength, competitive advantages, IP strategy
- **Project Viability:** Scope, translation plan, milestones, budget appropriateness
- **Scalability:** Likelihood products will scale and achieve adoption/practice
- **Market Analysis:** Market opportunity level
- **Funding Path & Business Model:** Strength of plans and likelihood to achieve market impact
- **Team Readiness:** Qualifications, management structure, execution record
- **Engagement & Partnerships:** Inclusivity, partnership strength/breadth, matched/leveraged resources
- **Potential Impact:** Potential for broad economic and community impacts to climate resiliency
