# High Data Rate Compact Logger

## Document Metadata
- Type: NASA Technology Disclosure Form (Form 1679)
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Grant NNX13CG33P
- Date: 2013-11-23
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: High Data Rate Compact Logger (microSD card array-based)
- Key Personnel: Maciej Stachura (CTO), Jack Elston, Eric McIntyre (University of Colorado Boulder)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies developed a compact, lightweight high-rate data logger capable of sampling at 180 mega-samples per second (MS/s) using a microSD card array, designed to fit within the size, weight, and power constraints of small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS). This represents a substantial advancement over comparable systems, which typically max out at 0.2 MS/s.

## Technical Approach
The innovation employs an array of 36 microSD cards sequentially written to by an FPGA operating at 720 MHz clock speed (4x the desired sampling rate). Key technical specifications:
- **Sampling Rate**: 180 MS/s
- **Data Rate**: 810 MB/s
- **ADC**: AD9643-210 ADC (210 MS/s sampling capability)
- **Resolution**: 16-bit analog-digital converter channels
- **Processing**: FPGA-based sequential writing with post-processing data reconstruction
- **Prototype Status**: Lower-rate prototype built and tested at ~1 kS/s using single microSD card; software written to extract binary data

## Products & Capabilities Described

**High Data Rate Compact Logger**
- What it is: A miniaturized data acquisition system using microSD card arrays for high-speed logging
- Proposed use: Logging microwave radiometer system data on sUAS platforms
- Unique advantage: Achieves 180 MS/s sampling in a form factor suitable for sUAS (addressing severe size/weight/power constraints where comparable systems only manage 0.2 MS/s)
- Key innovation: Uses low-cost microSD cards in parallel array configuration rather than single high-speed storage device

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary**: Microwave radiometer data collection on small unmanned aircraft
- **Market context**: General sUAS sensor advancement; part of broader civil UAS market projected at $2.4 billion annual sales by 2020 (AUVSI estimate)
- **Follow-on possibilities**: Advanced sensing systems in sUAS beyond initial radiometer application

- **Technology Significance**: Marked as "Substantial Advancement in the Art"
- **Collaborative Development**: Joint effort between Black Swift Technologies and University of Colorado Boulder
- **Component Roles**: Stachura (overall design), Elston (PCB assembly/firmware), McIntyre (PCB design)
- **Prior Art Gap**: Innovators unable to locate comparable alternative systems meeting both the sampling rate and sUAS size/weight/power requirements
- **Leverage Point**: Utilizes low-cost, readily available microSD card components in innovative array configuration rather than custom high-speed logging hardware
