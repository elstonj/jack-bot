# Part 09: Subcontracts and Consultants

## Document Metadata
- Type: SBIR Phase II Proposal - Subcontracts and Consultants Section
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: 2016 SBIR Phase II
- Date: December 2-9, 2016
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: SuperSwift XT
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jorge Diaz (NTCR Consulting), Dr. Lois Wardell (Arapahoe SciTech LLC)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes two subcontractors for Phase II volcano monitoring work: Dr. Jorge Diaz of NTCR Consulting and Dr. Lois Wardell of Arapahoe SciTech LLC, both with extensive experience in volcanic plume characterization and UAS operations. Combined commitment totals 40 hours/month over 18 months with a combined budget of $143,150.

## Subcontractor Details

### Dr. Jorge Diaz (NTCR Consulting)
**Responsibilities:**
- Sensing payload design and integration
- Calibration techniques development
- Flight pattern design
- UI design review
- Operational permissions coordination for Costa Rica
- Field deployment leadership at Turrialba volcano in Costa Rica
- Ground equipment operation for SuperSwift XT validation and data collection
- Deployment support in San Luis Valley, Colorado

**Qualifications:**
- Extensive experience with volcanic plume characterization
- Participated in multiple SBIR efforts developing instruments and UAS/sondes
- Established reputation and past experience operating unmanned aircraft near volcanoes

**Commitment:** 20 hours/month for 18 months plus deployment time  
**Budget:** $84,150

### Dr. Lois Wardell (Arapahoe SciTech LLC)
**Responsibilities:**
- Results analysis and comparison of flight data with satellite data
- Volcanic data cataloging
- Flight pattern design contribution
- UI design review
- Payload design, integration, and calibration
- Initial lab testing of scientific payload in Boulder
- Full sensor payload flights in Pawnee National Grasslands

**Qualifications:**
- Extensive experience with volcanic plume characterization
- Participated in multiple SBIR efforts developing instruments and UAS/sondes
- Prior Phase I subcontract work with BST

**Commitment:** 20 hours/month for 18 months  
**Budget:** $59,000

## Use Cases & Applications
- Volcanic plume characterization and monitoring at Turrialba volcano, Costa Rica
- Ground-truth validation of SuperSwift XT airborne measurements
- Satellite data comparison and correlation

## Notable Details
- Both subcontractors bring prior SBIR experience in volcanic instrumentation and UAS operations
- Diaz will provide ground equipment for independent data validation alongside SuperSwift XT flights
- Wardell provided Phase I support and was continued for Phase II
- Diaz was identified during Phase I and selected for his volcano-specific UAS operations expertise
- Work spans testing locations in Colorado (San Luis Valley, Pawnee National Grasslands) and Costa Rica (Turrialba)
