## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to design, develop, and validate a modular, open-architecture avionics system and cost-effective demonstrator aircraft for advanced multi-vehicle cooperative algorithm research and swarming operations. Building on the proven SwiftCore FMS and S0 platform, this effort will create a turn-key capability enabling coordinated UAS missions in extreme environments while addressing the USAF's strategic need for large numbers of collaborative systems providing synergistic and multidomain effects.

## Technical Approach

**Core Architecture:**
- Leverage BST's SwiftCore Flight Management System—one of the only truly modular autopilot systems with proven performance in extreme environments
- Design an open, modular architecture with well-defined interfaces to enable rapid development and adaptation of cooperative control algorithms
- Develop inexpensive yet capable small UAS demonstrator platforms with networked command and control
- Establish open interfaces and modular design in partnership with AFRL researchers

**Demonstrator Platform:**
- Based on BST's VTOL S0 and air-deployable S0 variants
- Original S0 developed under NOAA SBIR for low-cost, air-deployed hurricane monitoring
- Extended with USAF support to create land-based VTOL variant with vertical takeoff/landing capability
- Includes unique flush-air system embedded in nose cone measuring 3D inertial winds, pressure, temperature, and humidity

**Sensor Capabilities:**
- Well-characterized sensor suite for accurate atmospheric measurements
- EO/IR imagery collection
- 3D wind measurement and meteorological data collection
- Designed for extreme environment operation

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SwiftCore Flight Management System (FMS)
- **What it is:** Modular, ground-up avionics and flight management system with well-defined interfaces
- **Capabilities:** Handles avionics, communications, ground station, and UI in modular fashion
- **Proven in:** Extreme environments with NOAA and NASA
- **Application in proposal:** Foundation for open-architecture cooperative control platform; enables development of advanced multi-vehicle algorithms and simulations across wide variety of USAF platforms

### SwiftPilot FMS
- Extremely capable autopilot system developed from ground up by BST
- Integrated into SwiftCore ecosystem

### S0 UAS Platform
- **Variants:** Air-deployable (NOAA version) and VTOL land-based (USAF version)
- **Original development:** NOAA SBIR for P-3 based hurricane monitoring
- **Extension:** USAF-supported development of VTOL variant for simplified operations
- **Sensors:** Integrated flush-air system for 3D inertial winds, pressure, temperature, humidity measurement
- **Proposed use:** Swarming demonstrator platform base; capable of cooperative missions in extreme conditions

### S2 Aircraft
- Developed from soil moisture mapping SBIR
- Selected for NASA MALIBU (Multi-AngLe Imaging Bidirectional Reflectance Distribution Function UAS) project
- Operational with JPL in Costa Rica for ELEVATE project (trace gas sensing)
- Larger payload capacity (>5 lbs)
- Extended flight times and range vs. multi-rotors
