## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is subcontracted by BAE Systems to provide a fleet of small uncrewed aircraft systems (s-UAS) and develop autonomous soaring capabilities for the DARPA Albatross program. The 24-month effort focuses on integrating weather forecast-informed mission planning, real-time onboard wind sensing, and control systems to autonomously harness wind energy for extending flight range and endurance across multiple operational domains.

## Technical Approach
Albatross is NOT a dedicated airframe design effort. Instead, BST will:

1. **Apply existing or quickly-assembled small UAS platforms** to demonstrate autonomous soaring capabilities
2. **Develop integrated solutions** combining:
   - Weather forecast-informed preflight planning tools
   - Additional aircraft sensors for real-time wind condition detection
   - Unique control solutions for autonomous energy harvesting
3. **Harness natural atmospheric energy** to reduce onboard power demand and offset flight power requirements
4. **Enable autonomous response** to constantly evolving soaring conditions

The core innovation is independent active harvesting of energy from natural atmospheric flow fields to extend range/endurance.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Preflight Planning Perk (PF2P)
- **What it is:** Software tool for planning and test execution
- **Proposed use:** Support identification of data sources (terrain, land cover, weather, solar, oceanic forecasts); predict harvestable energy per geologic feature; design user interface
- **Deliverables:** Baseline ground station, Android map-based UI for ground control, export functionality for in-flight data use
- **Scope:** Data source identification, energy prediction modeling, UI design and implementation

### Sensing and Harnessing Control System (SHCS)
- **What it is:** Real-time sensor suite and control system for detecting and responding to wind conditions
- **Proposed use:** Detect real-time flow conditions, work in tandem with PF2P to balance energy harvesting with mission objectives, identify potential soaring locations, provide obstacle avoidance
- **Key functions:** 
  - Real-time flow condition detection in operational domain
  - Potential soaring location identification
  - Obstacle avoidance subsystem for flight safety
  - Real-time flight safety requirement enforcement

### Soaring Platform (s-UAS Fleet)
- **What it is:** Fleet of small uncrewed aircraft systems
- **Proposed use:** Demonstrate autonomous soaring in multiple environments
- **Scope:**
  - Select s-UAS appropriate to test events, practice test events (PTEs), and test events (TEs)
  - Procure, build, adapt, or modify s-UAS to meet program goals
  - Provide baseline AND Albatross-enabled s-UAS for metrics evaluation
  - Include baseline avionics payloads
  - Ensure compliance with government specifications (flight safety, MAVlink interface compatibility)
  - Produce certification documentation for US government range flight approval
  - Procure adequate quantities to account for hardware failures and flight incidents

## Notable Details

1. **Contract Structure:** BAE Systems (prime) subcontracts BST; BAE is the primary interface to government; BST manages technical and financial performance as subcontractor.

2. **Dual System Provision:** BST must provide both baseline s-UAS (unmodified reference) AND Albatross-enabled s-UAS for direct performance comparison and metrics evaluation.

3. **Regulatory Compliance:** SOW explicitly requires:
   - Flight safety compliance with government specifications
   - MAVlink (microAir vehicle link) interface compatibility
   - Certification documentation for US government range flight approval

4. **Test Cycle Rhythm:** After 6-month system checkout, PTE/TE cycles occur every 2 months through end of performance, enabling rapid iteration and validation.

5. **Geographic Escalation:** Test locations progress from continental US (Months 12) to Pacific island ranges (Month 18) to Arctic and international operations (Month 24, including Alaska and Morocco), suggesting increasing mission complexity and environmental diversity.

6. **Milestone Payment Structure:** 12 payment milestones tied to deliverable completion; pricing should be estimated for April 2025–March 2027 period of performance.

7. **Quality & Risk Management:** Project management scope includes risk management, opportunity management, contractual change management, and strategy execution for quality performance.
