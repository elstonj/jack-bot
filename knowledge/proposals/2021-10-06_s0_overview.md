## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies presents the S0-ISR, a small electric modular VTOL/fixed-wing UAS designed for landfalling hurricane measurement and atmospheric profiling. The aircraft combines low cost and ease of operation with advanced avionics (SwiftCore) and intuitive user interface (SwiftTab), demonstrating field-validated performance exceeding Air Force requirements for atmospheric science missions.

## Technical Approach
BST developed two operational variants of the S0 platform:
1. **Air-Deployable Variant**: Purpose-built for deployment from NOAA P-3 Orion "Hurricane Hunter" aircraft; autonomous flight for boundary layer measurements in hurricane eyewalls
2. **VTOL Fixed-Wing Variant**: Autonomous atmospheric profiling for cargo drop support and weather forecasting

Both use SwiftCore distributed avionics (networked nodes for redundancy and field maintainability) and SwiftTab GUI (mission-focused data gathering interface with fault tree diagnostics and simulator training capability).

Wind sensing employs 3D printed probe components calibrated via 9th-order polynomial fitting with comprehensive tunnel testing (~106 total measurements across 900 points).

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0-ISR UAS
**Platform Specifications:**
- Max takeoff weight: 4 lbs
- Wingspan: 54.6 inches
- Flight ceiling: 15,000 ft AGL
- Flight time: 60 min nominal
- Cruise speed: 40 mph
- Max speed: 100 mph
- Max winds endured: 50 mph
- Nose cone diameter: 1.8 inches
- Payload capacity: 100 grams

**Sensor Suite Options:**
- Atmospheric: air temperature, 3D wind speed/direction, dewpoint, atmospheric pressure, laser altimeter
- Optical: thermal IR sensor, lightweight EO/IR cameras, laser target designators

**Design Features:**
- Electric, modular, ruggedized
- VTOL or tube-launched fixed-wing capability
- Modern manufacturing for economic construction, operation, and maintenance
- Suitable for low-cost swarm solutions

### SwiftCore Flight Management System
- Distributed avionics architecture (networked nodes on robust bus)
- Developed entirely in-house by BST (not Dronecode-based)
- Capabilities: redundancy, field maintenance, automated part lifetime tracking, expandability, third-party component integration
- Superior fixed-wing control performance vs. open-source autopilots (per UC Boulder endorsement)
- Open interfaces supporting multiple protocols while avoiding single-point ecosystem failures

### SwiftTab User Interface
- Proprietary GUI focused on data gathering
- Mission-centric operator experience
- Integrated safety: electronic centralized aircraft monitor-like fault tree system
- Stand-alone simulator with fault injection for training
- Advanced flight plan creation: terrain-aware, pattern generation
- Supports common data formats: BUFR, GRIB, netCDF

## Key Results (Field Testing Summary)

### Test Locations & Duration
- **SGP (Southern Great Plains):** 12 flights, 5.8 hours total
- **OSU site (comparative testing):** 4 flights, 1.7 hours total

### Measured Performance
- **Endurance:** Extrapolated max time-of-flight 78-100 min; 62-80 min with VTOL landing
- **High wind capability:** Flown in up to 38 mph wind with no control/stability issues
- **Vertical profiling (Air Force mission profile):**
  - Most efficient at 6 m/s
  - 12.7-15 min climb to 15,000 ft at 6 m/s
  - 19 min climb at 4 m/s (recommended for wind measurement accuracy)

### Sensor Validation (Surface-Based Comparisons)
**Temperature bias:**
- S0-1: +1.03°C to +1.70°C
- S0-2: +1.16°C to +1.67°C

**Relative humidity bias:**
- S0-1: -1.69% to ~0%
- S0-2: -11.34% to +2.5%

**Static pressure bias:**
- S0-1: -0.25 to -1.96 hPa
- S0-2: -1.94 to -2.13 hPa

### Overall Assessment
- Aircraft exceeds Air Force performance requirements (time-of-flight, max ceiling, high wind capability)
- CONOPS validated; UI and data file formats confirmed
- Ongoing wind measurement calibration work with NCAR near Boulder

## Notable Details

### VTOL S0 Design Requirements
- Simple to operate (<1 day training)
- Low cost (<$5K full setup)
- 10 ft recovery footprint
- Vertical takeoff/landing with "safe height" capability
- 40+ min flight time for profiling missions
- Field-chargeable with custom battery

### Competitive Positioning
- Only 1 of "very few companies" developing integrated avionics, control systems, payload interfaces, payloads, aircraft designs, user interfaces, and ground stations in-house
- Distributed avionics approach avoids single-point ecosystem failures inherent in Dronecode-based systems
- Superior fixed-wing handling vs. open-source alternatives
- Advantages over multirotor systems: better duration, range, inclement weather ops, loiter capability, 100+ mph dash speed for rapid deployment/recovery
