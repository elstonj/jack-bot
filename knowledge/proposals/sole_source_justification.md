- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy (SBIR Program)
- Program/Solicitation: Navy SBIR Topic N251-016, Phase II
- Phase I Contract Number: N6833525C0492
- Date: 2026-03-23
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0-Acoustic (airframe)
- Key Personnel: Paul DeMond (Ultra Maritime); Beck Cotter (BST, last editor)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies justifies the selection of Ultra Maritime (UM) as sole-source subcontractor for Phase II development of acoustic and Electric-field (E-field) sensor payloads on a Sonobuoy-Launched Unmanned Aerial System (SL UAS). The justification centers on UM's unique, mature sensing technologies and direct integration with BST's Phase I airframe design, making replacement impractical.

## Technical Approach
**Sensor Integration Architecture:**
- BST's S0-Acoustic airframe was custom-engineered around Ultra Maritime's specific sensor architecture during Phase I
- UM defined precise physical dimensions, weight profiles, and electrical interface requirements
- BST designed complex features to accommodate UM's payload: elastically-loaded belly compartment, cable compliance assembly, and gravity-assisted deployment mechanism

**SWaP and Performance Constraints:**
- Development constrained by Navy's stringent Size, Weight, and Power (SWaP) requirements
- Acoustic sensitivity thresholds specified by Navy requirements

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Ultra Maritime Acoustic Sensors
- **Calibrated Omnidirectional (CO) sensor**: Modified version derived from UM's Q53H production sonobuoy
- **Performance improvement**: Self-noise reduction and acoustic performance improvement of 6-8 dB achieved using textured ceramic materials
- **Development precedent**: Technique successfully demonstrated during Digital Vertical Line Array (DVLA) 2.0 sonobuoy development

### Ultra Maritime E-Field Sensing
- **Frequency range**: 0.01 Hz – 5 Hz (low-frequency)
- **Specialized bands**: Extremely Low Frequency Electromagnetic (ELFE) and Ultra Low Frequency (ULF)
- **Navy validation**: Demonstrated under two relevant PMA-264 SBIRs:
  - N091-019: Multi-Modal Sensor for Tactical Marine Surveillance / EA Buoy (UM as large business partner with Information Systems Lab)
  - N192-060: Multi-Sensor Sonobuoy (MSS) (UM as exclusive large business partner)

### Black Swift S0-Acoustic Airframe
- Custom-engineered to integrate UM sensor suite
- Features elastically-loaded belly compartment for payload accommodation
- Cable compliance assembly for sensor integration
- Gravity-assisted deployment mechanism

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Mission**: Sonobuoy-Launched Unmanned Aerial System (SL UAS)
- **Operational Context**: Navy Air Anti-Submarine Warfare Systems (ASW), specifically under Program Executive Officer for Air Anti-Submarine Warfare Systems (PMA-264)
- **Sensing Applications**: Acoustic and electric-field detection for submarine/marine surveillance

## Key Justification Arguments
1. **Specialized Technology Maturity**: UM possesses flight-ready, production-derived acoustic hardware (Q53H/DVLA-derived) and Navy-validated E-field technology
2. **Recent Relevant Performance**: Direct past performance developing identical sensing modalities under PMA-264 program
3. **Design Integration Lock-in**: S0-Acoustic airframe was engineered specifically around UM's sensor specifications; replacing UM would require:
   - Discarding validated Phase I CAD models
   - Complete re-engineering of S0-Acoustic airframe
   - Massive duplication of engineering effort
   - Unacceptable schedule delays to Phase II
4. **Unique Qualification**: No known alternative vendor possesses the combination of flight-ready Q53H/DVLA-derived acoustic hardware, PMA-264 validated E-field technology, and pre-integrated Phase I form factors
