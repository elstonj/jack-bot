## Notable Details

**Regulatory Framework:**
- Implements 15 USC 638 (SBIR statute), SBA SBIR/STTR Policy Directive (Oct 2020), and DoD FMR Vol. 2A Chapter 1
- Applies to both Phase I and Phase II awards

**Eligible RDT&E Work:**
- Systematic studies directed toward knowledge, understanding, or applied innovation to meet unmet needs
- Design, development, and improvement of prototypes and new processes
- Testing and evaluation of developmental items

**COTS/NDI Treatment (Key Constraints):**
- Off-the-shelf items purchased without alteration are **not** SBIR-fundable
- **Modified COTS/NDI** can be funded if modification represents technology refresh that significantly changes performance envelope
- First article, modification, and acceptance testing of modified items are fundable; follow-on purchases are not
- COTS items required for RDT&E test purposes can be funded

**Direct-to-Phase II Requirements:**
- Offeror must demonstrate sufficient scientific and technical feasibility and merit without Phase I
- Must provide scientific/technical baseline based on relevant literature
- Must describe RDT&E efforts advancing that baseline
- Team discussion must address RDT&E qualifications and commercialization
- Must identify any tie to Defense Acquisition Program (for FPDS tracking)
