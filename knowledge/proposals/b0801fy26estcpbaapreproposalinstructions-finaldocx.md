# ESTCP FY 2026 BAA Pre-Proposal Instructions

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Solicitation Guidance / Pre-Proposal Instructions
- **Client/Agency:** U.S. Department of Defense (DoD); U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, Humphreys Engineer Center Support Activity (HECSA)
- **Program/Solicitation:** Environmental Security Technology Certification Program (ESTCP) Broad Agency Announcement (BAA), FY 2026
- **Date:** January 7, 2025 (BAA announced); Instructions finalized and last edited March 13, 2025
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None
- **Key Personnel:** Aimee N. Johnson, Shannon N. Benson, Monique Holmes (HECSA contacts)

## Executive Summary
This document provides instructions for private sector organizations to submit pre-proposals to the ESTCP FY 2026 Broad Agency Announcement. ESTCP is DoD's demonstration and validation program for environmental and installation energy technologies. The announcement solicits innovative technology demonstrations that address DoD's urgent environmental and installation energy needs and are projected to deliver cost savings, improved efficiencies, or improved outcomes. Pre-proposals are limited to 5 pages and must be submitted via the SERDP and ESTCP Management System (SEMS) by March 6, 2025, at 2:00 p.m. Eastern Time.

## Technical Approach / Program Requirements

**ESTCP expects:**
- Technologies that have successfully completed laboratory testing and initial small-scale field testing
- Demonstrations conducted under operational conditions at DoD facilities
- Projects that generate supporting cost and performance data for technology acceptance/validation
- Development of guidance, design, and protocol documents to support future implementation
- Engagement with regulatory community when applicable
- Technology transfer planning and execution
- Multi-Service or DoD-wide applicability (prioritized)

**Projects must accomplish three key objectives:**
1. Execute technology demonstration to validate performance and operational costs
2. Transfer the technology through user community engagement, guidance documents, peer-reviewed publications, and identified implementation pathways
3. Provide data and support to achieve regulatory and end-user acceptance

## Pre-Proposal Content Requirements

Pre-proposals must include (5-page limit, 11-point font minimum, 1-inch margins):

1. **Proposal Number** (system-generated via SEMS)
2. **Proposal Title**
3. **Lead Principal Investigator**
4. **ESTCP Topic Area**
5. **Lead Organization** (contact details)
6. **Problem Statement** – DoD relevance, current approaches and shortcomings
7. **Technology Description** – including:
   - Technical objectives
   - Theory, functionality, operation, schematic
   - Innovation discussion and state-of-the-art comparison
   - Specific performance issues requiring validation
   - **Technology Maturity** – evidence of readiness for demonstration, references, funding history, required development work
   - **Technical Approach** – tasks, performance objectives with metrics, data requirements, success criteria (table format required per Demonstration Plan Guidance), test design overview, proposed site(s) or desired characteristics, scale of tests
   - **Technical Risks** – potential issues, assumptions, risk mitigation, scale-up issues if not full-scale
   - **Related Efforts** – similar projects and funding sources

8. **Expected DoD Benefit** – impact, cost reduction, per-site and aggregate benefits, deployment projections, adoption enablers, ROI and payback estimates, life-cycle cost advantages
9. **Schedule of Milestones** – Gantt chart with start/end dates, demonstrations, deliverables, permits, development work, testing, commissioning
10. **Technology Transition** – specific plan beyond conferences/publications; user guides, web tools, guidance documents; audience-specific methods; regulatory/implementation barriers and recommendations; timeline to product/service suitable for DoD acquisition
11. **Performers** – lead person(s) per organization with contributions; one-page CV each (not counted in 5-page limit)
12. **Costs** – total costs by year (labor, materials, travel, burdens, profit/fee), funding by calendar year (project start assumed June 1, 2026), representative field demonstration cost estimate, reporting and travel funds, other funding sources with points of contact. Proposals exceeding $2M must provide certificate of current cost/pricing data per FAR 15.403-4(a)(1).

**Required Appendices (not counted in page limit):**
- Abbreviated CV (1 page each for PI and significant performers with addresses, phone, email)
- List of Acronyms
- Literature Citations (if cited)
- Supporting Technical Data (optional, up to 3 pages)
- Existing Support description (optional, ½ page per relevant effort)

## Evaluation Criteria (Pre-Proposal Stage)

**Pass/Fail Threshold Criteria:**
1. **ESTCP Relevance** – Does submission respond to DoD environmental requirement in topic area?
2. **Technology Maturity** – Is technology appropriate for demonstration/validation? Must have completed proof-of-concept, show capability evidence, be deployable within one year of project initiation. Excludes basic research, exploratory development, or commercially standard approaches already deployed at DoD.

**Scored Criteria (Technical Merit weighted more heavily):**
1. **Technical Merit** – Sound methodology, innovative state-of-the-art technology, well-characterized technical risks, qualified technical team
2. **Cost/Benefit of Technology** – Significant projected cost savings/risk reduction, reasonable benefits consistent with technology, payoffs commensurate with costs/risks
3. **Transition Potential** – Well-defined DoD user, clearly identified transition activities, implementable within DoD
4. **Cost of Proposal** – Realistic and reasonable costs per FAR 35.016(e), appropriate and traceable to level of effort

## Submission Instructions & Key Dates

**Submission Process:**
- Submit via SEMS only (no email, fax, hardcopy)
- **Deadline:** March 6, 2025, 2:00 p.m. Eastern Time
- Login to SEMS → Enter proposal details (saveable/editable) → Add system-generated proposal number to first page → Create single PDF with all sections → Upload → Submit
- No signed cover page required for pre-proposals
- Only "Submitted" status proposals reviewed; "Saved" status ignored
- Proposers may revise and reupload until deadline; last version retained
- Email confirmation sent upon submission; proposer must verify receipt

**Evaluation Schedule:**
- Pre-proposals evaluated per schedule
- Proposers notified whether to submit full proposal or not
- No debriefs provided for pre-proposals not invited to full proposal stage
- Full proposal packages sent to contracting in Q1 FY2026
- Contract award expected Q3 2026 (not guaranteed)

**Full Proposal Next Steps:**
- Only invited proposers submit full proposals (outside pre-proposal process not accepted)
- Proposers without DoD partner/site assigned DoD liaison for demonstration site identification
- Oral presentation to ESTCP Technical Committee required (proposer bears costs, not included in proposal budget)
- Selection based on written proposal and oral presentation
- Only Contracting Officer can legally commit Government
- ESTCP reserves right to recommend any, all, or none of proposals and/or portions thereof for award

## Full Proposal Requirements (if selected)

**Revised Proposal:** May require revisions post-selection (schedule, tasks, funding, deliverables)

**Subcontracting Plan:** Required if contract exceeds $750,000 (per FAR Subpart 19.7); must address small business goals with explanation if zero percent goals claimed. Must be reviewed/negotiated with Contracting Officer; subject to DoD Office of Small Business and SBA review pre-award.

**Accounting & Auditing:** ESTCP projects generally cost-type contracts requiring approved accounting system and accepted government audit. Contractors lacking requirements may convert to firm fixed-price if appropriate. DCAA and DCMA details required.

**Mandatory Clauses & Compliance:**
- DFARS 252.204-7012 Safeguarding Covered Defense Information and Cyber Incident Reporting (mandatory, unalterable)
- FAR 52.204-24, 52.204-25, 52.204-26 (Covered Telecommunications Equipment restrictions)
- Service Contract Reporting (SCR) – contracts exceeding $3M must report manpower data in SAM per DFARS 252.204-7023
- System for Award Management (SAM) registration required with UEI and current representations/certifications; contracts not issued to non-compliant contractors

**Cost and Pricing Data Backup Documentation Required:**
- Recent audit report (for cost-type requests) or firm fixed-price submission if lacking
- Payroll documents showing wages/fringe
- DCAA/FPRA rate agreement or three years actual cost data
- Materials/Supplies/Consumables details with quotes and suppliers
- Subcontract service quotes for ODCs
- Travel specifics (who, where, when, purpose, mode); GSA per diem maximums apply; conference fees with source data; **no fee/profit on travel**
- Subcontractor cost sheets and backup if subcontract ≥$50K

**Contract Execution:**
- All contracts require bilateral execution in counterparts
- Contractors must not begin performance until fully executed contract received from Government Contracting Officer
- Performance begun prior to execution is at contractor's own cost and risk

## Notable Details

- **No ITAR, EAR, limited distribution statements, CUI, or proprietary markings accepted** in proposals; only government employees/DoD contractors with NDAs access ESTCP proposals
- **Private sector BAA only** – DoD organizations use separate FY26 DoD Call; Federal agencies outside DoD use separate call
- **No full-scale cleanup demonstrations** – focus on validation and transition to commercialization
- **Multi-Service/DoD-wide benefit prioritized** over single-installation solutions
- **Technology readiness:** Must be beyond proof-of-concept but not commercially standard; must be demonstrable within one year of project initiation
- **Regulatory engagement:** Required upfront for technologies needing approval; state, interstate, EPA coordination encouraged
- **DoD liaison assignment:** Government provides liaison if full proposal selected, assisting with site ID, cost/performance validation, regulatory interface, technology transfer support
- **Cost considerations:** Assume June 1, 2026 project start; include all ESTCP reporting and travel costs; major equipment purchases by ESTCP identified; leverage and other funding sources noted with POCs
