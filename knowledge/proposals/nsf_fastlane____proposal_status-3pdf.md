# NSF STTR Phase I Proposal: Small UAS for Atmospheric Boundary Layer Sampling

## Document Metadata
- Type: NSF FastLane Proposal Review (Review #3)
- Client/Agency: National Science Foundation (NSF)
- Program/Solicitation: STTR Phase I
- Date: Created 2013-10-24; Modified 2013-12-19
- Proposal Number: 1346510
- Organization: University of Colorado at Boulder
- Key Personnel: Jack S. Elston (Principal Investigator)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed to develop a small unmanned aircraft system (UAS) as an in situ platform for measuring meteorological parameters in the atmospheric boundary layer. The proposal aimed to integrate commercially available sensors on a small UAV to provide a more flexible, cost-effective alternative to conventional balloon sondes for atmospheric research.

## Technical Approach
- **Platform**: Small unmanned aircraft (UAS/UAV)
- **Measured Parameters**: Temperature, pressure, humidity, 3D winds in the atmospheric boundary layer
- **Sensor Integration**: Implementation of various commercially available sensors on small UAS
- **Application**: In situ atmospheric sampling platform

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **Small UAS Platform**: Proposed as a cost-effective, flexible alternative to balloon sondes for meteorological data collection
- **Operational Flexibility**: Capability to provide more flexible operation than conventional balloon-based atmospheric sampling

## Use Cases & Applications
- Atmospheric boundary layer sampling and characterization
- Meteorological data collection for modeling and forecasting
- Support to meteorology research community (National Center for Atmospheric Research expressed supporting interest)

## Reviewer Assessment

**Strengths:**
- UAV platform offers genuine operational and cost advantages over conventional balloon sondes
- Clear end-user need articulated by supporting letter from National Center for Atmospheric Research (co-located in Boulder)
- Potential market niche in meteorological community

**Weaknesses & Criticisms:**
- **Low Innovation/IP**: Project criticized as primarily integrating existing commercial sensors; no novel intellectual merit
- **No Patent Protection**: No protected intellectual property; vulnerability to market erosion by other drone manufacturers
- **Incomplete Business Plan**: No pricing strategy included; vague revenue estimate of $400,000 annual sales by 2016 with unclear basis
- **Poor Evaluation Plan**: No quantifiable metrics for project evaluation; evaluation plan deferred to post-funding phase (Task 5)
- **Undefined Market**: Targeted market and specific customers not clearly identified

## Overall Rating
**Fair** - NSF reviewer concluded the proposal lacked critical components including innovative intellectual merit, protected IP, concrete commercialization strategy, and clear market definition, despite addressing a genuine atmospheric science need.

## Notable Details
- This represents an early-stage BST effort in UAS-based atmospheric sensing
- Reviewer feedback highlights classic STTR Phase I challenges: balancing technical feasibility with innovation and commercialization viability
- Project appears to have been unfunded based on fair rating and noted weaknesses
