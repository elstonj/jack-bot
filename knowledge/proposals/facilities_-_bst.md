## Executive Summary
This document describes Black Swift Technologies' 3,000 sq. ft. facility in Boulder, CO, including manufacturing capabilities, computing resources, and testing infrastructure. BST maintains vertical integration across all critical UAS design and manufacturing aspects, with access to multiple flight testing locations including high-altitude sites and remote Alaska operations areas.

## Facility Description

**Main Office & Workshop**
- Location: Boulder, CO
- Size: 3,000 sq. ft.
- Infrastructure: Electronics benches, shop facilities, meeting space
- Computing: Full suite of atmospheric and aircraft simulation tools for mission scenario analysis
- Autopilot Systems: Multiple full autopilot systems available for testing electrical and software interfaces with sensors

**Manufacturing & Design Capabilities**
- Vertically integrated designer and manufacturer of fixed-wing and multirotor UAS
- Full control over: autopilots, power boards, GNSS, software, firmware, mechanical design, airframes, ground control stations, support equipment
- Integration capability: prototype and production UAS, sensor integration, avionics installation
- Manufacturing partners: Northwind Composites (airframe composite work), local Denver-area circuit board houses
- Supply chain resilience: Successfully managed recent electronic parts shortages through redesigns and component substitutions

## Testing & Flight Operations Infrastructure

**Local Testing Areas**
- Sod farm: 20 minutes east of office (primary test site for S2)
- Pawnee National Grasslands: 20 x 20 mile area with 1,000' altitude waiver
- San Luis Valley: High-altitude testing location with peaks over 14,000 ft MSL, enabling testing up to ~15,000 ft MSL under current regulations

**Remote Testing**
- Alaska operations: Established relationship with Alaska DOT for BVLOS (Beyond Visual Line of Sight) missions in remote locations under new UAS Activity Areas initiative

## Notable Details
- BST maintains design authority over all critical supply chain components, providing flexibility during parts shortages
- Geographic proximity to diverse testing environments (sea-level sod farm to 15,000+ ft high altitude)
- Regulatory infrastructure established for advanced operations (1,000' waivers, Alaska BVLOS authorization)
- Full in-house capabilities reduce dependency on external partners for core UAS functions
