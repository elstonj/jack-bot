## Executive Summary
This is Section M of an Air Force solicitation for multiple-award Indefinite Delivery/Indefinite Quantity (IDIQ) contracts under the AMAC program. The document outlines the evaluation criteria and award methodology for both unrestricted and small business pools of contractors. Awards will be made to all qualifying offerors meeting technical acceptability thresholds; no cost/price evaluation will occur at the IDIQ level.

## Technical Approach
**Award Structure:**
- Multiple-award IDIQ contracts under competitive source selection (FAR 15, supplemented by DFARS and DAFFARS)
- Two competition pools: Unrestricted and Small Business
- Awards to "each and all qualifying offerors" meeting defined criteria
- No cost/price evaluation for IDIQ award (pricing evaluated at task order level)
- Only U.S. companies eligible as prime contractors (per NISPOM)

**Evaluation Process:**
- Two-factor evaluation with pass/fail ratings (Acceptable/Unacceptable)
- Factor 1: Technical Experience (mandatory minimum score)
- Factor 2: Small Business Participation Commitment Document (SBPCD)
- Government reserves right to award without discussions
- Grossly deficient proposals may be excluded without evaluation
- "Correction potential" of deficiencies considered at Government's discretion

## Evaluation Criteria Details

### Factor 1: Technical Experience (Mandatory Acceptable Rating Required)
**Scoring Mechanism:**
- Offerors must achieve cumulative validated score of **1,000 points minimum** across four Areas of Interest (AOI) domains
- Self-scoring spreadsheet used by offerors; Government validates claimed points
- Once 1,000 points validated, further spreadsheet review stops
- No partial points awarded; no additional credit for exceeding 1,000-point threshold
- Government validates using Volume II proposal information and contract documents
- Points not validated if supporting documentation does not demonstrate required relevant experience

**Four Technical Domains with Bonus Point Requirements:**

1. **Subfactor 1.1 – Air Domain** (600 required bonus points; 500 bonus given)
   - 12 technical areas, each weighted at 10 points:
     - Human Performance Optimization
     - Aerospace Medicine and Operational Health
     - Warfighter Survivability and Protection
     - Cognitive and Behavioral Sciences
     - Advanced Air Vehicles
     - Air Propulsion Systems
     - Aerodynamics and Flight Sciences
     - Hypersonics
     - Advanced Weapons Technologies
     - Energetics and Propellants
     - Guidance Navigation and Control
     - Munitions Survivability and Countermeasures

2. **Subfactor 1.2 – Space Domain** (500 required bonus points; 500 bonus given)
   - 8 technical areas, each weighted at 10 points:
     - Space Access and Orbital Systems
     - Spacecraft and Satellite Technologies
     - Space Power and Propulsion
     - On-Orbit Operations and Autonomy
     - Space Situational Awareness
     - Resilient and Survivable Space Architecture
     - Space Environment and Effects
     - Position, Navigation, and Timing (PNT) from and for Space

3. **Subfactor 1.3 – Cyberspace Domain** (700 required bonus points; 500 bonus given)
   - 17 technical areas, each weighted at 10 points:
     - Cyber Operations and Cybersecurity
     - Communications and Networks
     - Electronic Warfare and Spectrum Operations
     - Quantum Technologies
     - Electro-Optical and Infrared Sensors
     - Radar and Radio Frequency Sensors
     - Multispectral and Hyperspectral Sensing
     - Signal Processing and Sensor Fusion
     - Quantum Sensing and Advanced Properties
     - Directed Energy Sensing and Countermeasures
     - High-Energy Laser Systems
     - Photonics
     - High-Power Microwave Technologies
     - Beam Control and Adaptive Optics
     - Directed Energy Effects and Countermeasures
     - Cybersecurity for Digital Environments
     - Navigation Warfare and Counter PNT

4. **Subfactor 1.4 – Cross Cutting Domains** (800 required bonus points; 500 bonus given)
   - 19 technical areas, each weighted at 10 points:
     - Human Systems Integration
     - Human-Machine Teaming and Interfaces
     - Training and Simulation Technologies
     - Rapid Multi-Domain Integration
     - Advanced Systems Engineering
     - Integrated Vehicle Health Management
     - Power and Thermal Management
     - Advanced Materials Development
     - Additive Manufacturing
     - Materials Processing and Fabrication
     - Materials Characterization and Testing
     - Environmentally Responsive and Smart Materials
     - Microelectronics
     - Model-Based Systems Engineering, Digital Engineering, and Digital Thread
     - Advanced Simulation and Virtual Prototyping
     - Data Analytics and Artificial Intelligence
     - Additive Manufacturing Digital Integration
     - Low Cost, Size, Weight, and Performance (C-SWAP) Alternative PNT Systems for Attritable Systems
     - Modular Open Architecture Approaches to Resilient PNT

### Factor 2: Small Business Participation Commitment Document (Mandatory Acceptable Rating Required)
- Offerors must submit complete SBPCD per instructions in Section L, 4.2
- Acceptable if SBPCD meets Section L requirements
- Unacceptable if SBPCD not submitted or does not meet requirements
- Both factors must rate Acceptable for contract award eligibility

## Award Structure & Key Requirements
- **Minimum Initial Delivery Order:** $500 for post-award conference (video/teleconference, max 60 minutes)
- **Qualifying Offeror Definition:** Must be:
  1. Determined responsible source (FAR Part 9)
  2. Conform to all solicitation requirements, terms, conditions, certifications, small business subcontracting plan
  3. Likely to offer fair and reasonable pricing at task order level
  4. Rated Acceptable for Factor 1
  5. Rated Acceptable for Factor 2
- **Eligibility Restrictions:** Excludes foreign-owned or foreign-influenced companies unless approved as U.S. subsidiary by Defense Security Service (DSS)
- **Prime Contractor Flexibility:** Prime IDIQ holders may form subcontracting teams at task order level with entities not holding AMAC IDIQ contracts

## Notable Details
- This is a **multiple-award IDIQ** (not a single-award contract), meaning all qualifying offerors will receive contract awards
- **No price competition at IDIQ level**—pricing competition deferred to task order stage
- Competitive range may be reduced for efficiency, but all technically acceptable and responsible offerors should receive awards
- Government has broad discretion on "correction potential"—deficiencies may be correctable or cause elimination
- Bonus point thresholds vary significantly by domain (Air: 600 required, Space: 500, Cyberspace: 700, Cross-Cutting: 800), but actual bonus points awarded are capped at 500 per domain
- No incremental credit for exceeding 1,000-point minimum threshold (pass/fail at 1,000 points)
