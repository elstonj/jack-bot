# Letter of Intent - Houston (S0 UAS Atmospheric Profiling)

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Intent (supporting material for proposal)
- Client/Agency: National Science Foundation (NSF)
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Phase II - Atmospheric Sounding (2019.2)
- Date: October 31, 2019
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 UAS platform
- Key Personnel: Dr. Adam L. Houston (University of Nebraska-Lincoln)
- Last Editor: Jack Elston (BST)

## Executive Summary
Dr. Adam L. Houston, a leading atmospheric science researcher with 10+ years of UAS experience, commits to supporting BST's development of the S0 UAS platform as a purpose-built atmospheric profiling vehicle. Houston brings extensive experience in UAS-based atmospheric research and operational meteorological data collection.

## Technical Approach
The S0 platform is being developed specifically for atmospheric profiling applications. The work builds on BST's "industry-leading innovations and expertise" in UAS development, with the goal of creating capabilities that "exceed the requirements for both atmospheric research and operational meteorological data collection."

## Products & Capabilities Described
**S0 UAS Platform:**
- Purpose-built vehicle for atmospheric profiling
- Represents evolution of prior BST platforms used in atmospheric research
- Intended for both research-grade atmospheric science and operational meteorology applications

## Use Cases & Applications
- Atmospheric profiling and sounding
- Operational meteorological data collection
- Atmospheric science research
- Field campaign operations

## Notable Details
- Houston has served as field coordinator for UAS operations in 10 field campaigns since 2009
- He has been an investigator on $13M+ in UAS-for-atmospheric-science projects, some including BST team members
- Houston serves on the steering committee for the International Society for Atmospheric Research using Remotely-piloted Aircraft (ISARRA)
- This letter represents high-level institutional support and demonstrates BST's established track record with leading atmospheric researchers
