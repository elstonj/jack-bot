- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston, Dr. Maciej Stachura

## Executive Summary
This commercialization plan describes BST's strategy to bring a VTOL-capable S2 XT unmanned aircraft system equipped with volcanic plume sensors to market. The system addresses multiple scientific and commercial markets including volcanic emissions monitoring, weather observation, and remote sensing in difficult environments. BST projects sufficient market demand from NASA, NOAA, NSF, USGS, and DOE to justify development investment, supported by existing customer relationships and demonstrated technology maturity.

## Technical Approach
The Phase II-E project will develop:
- **VTOL capability** for the S2 XT platform with high-wind takeoff/landing algorithms
- **Modular payload interface** with field-swappable nose cone for different sensor configurations
- **SwiftCore FMS integration** supporting up to 128 sensors with onboard data logging
- **Autonomous takeoff/landing** features via SwiftPilot autopilot
- **3D multi-hole probe** wind sensor (low-cost, lightweight replacement for commercial alternatives)
- **Rugged airframe design** for harsh environments (cold operations tested to -40°C in Greenland)

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 XT (VTOL variant)
- VTOL fixed-wing hybrid UAS for atmospheric sampling in extreme conditions
- Capable of operations from minimal landing areas
- Modular sensor architecture allowing rapid payload reconfiguration
- Heritage from existing S2 platform with modifications for harsh-environment flight
- Built-in redundancy enabling safe beyond-visual-line-of-sight operations
- Fully commercial (non-military) design enabling EAR export classification rather than ITAR

### SwiftCore Flight Management System
- Can interface with up to 128 unique sensors simultaneously
- Onboard data logging capability
- Supports modularity through standardized interfaces
- Previously proven on multiple platforms

### SwiftPilot Autopilot
- Enables autonomous takeoff and landing
- Reduces pilot skill requirements vs. manual fixed-wing systems
- Simplifies operations for broader user base

### SwiftTab User Interface
- Simplified operation interface
- Enables wider range of end-users

### Multi-Hole Probe (3D Wind Sensor)
- Significant cost and weight reduction vs. existing commercial systems
- Maintains or improves measurement accuracy
- Already built, validated through wind tunnel testing, operationally deployed

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Markets
1. **Atmospheric Composition and Plume Measurement**
   - Volcanic plume monitoring and sampling (extreme temperature/ash environments)
   - Wildfire activity monitoring and evolution tracking
   - Coordination with satellite systems (e.g., NASA ASTER)
   - Volcanic ash detection for aviation safety
   - Vertical gradient measurements in plumes (satellite-unobservable)

2. **Weather Observation, Model Validation, and Early Warning Systems**
   - 3D in situ measurements of temperature, pressure, humidity, winds
   - Local and global weather model inputs
   - Small-scale dynamic phenomenon validation
   - Tornado/supercell thunderstorm development and evolution studies
   - Reduced operational overhead vs. fixed-wing alternatives

3. **Remote Sensing in Difficult Areas**
   - Operations in extreme conditions and nomadic locations
   - Emergency descent capability for safety
   - Supports safe BVLOS operations and future regulatory compliance

### Target Customers
- **NASA**: Earth Science program (Tropospheric Chemistry Program, Applied Sciences Air Quality, CALIPSO, Aura, CATS, OCO-2/3, Earth Ventures airborne campaigns)
- **NOAA**: Weather operations and validation
- **USGS, DOE, NSF**: Various atmospheric and environmental monitoring
- **UCAR, NCAR**: Research institutions (NCAR and USGS already customers of S2 XT)
- **Wildland firefighting operations**
- **Oil/gas monitoring activities**

- **No direct commercial competition**: VTOL atmospheric sampling system with this capability not currently commercially available
- **Complementary to existing technologies**: Satellite remote sensing, manned aircraft, radiosondes/ground stations
- **Key advantages over alternatives**:
  - Reusable vs. expendable drop sondes
  - Persistent/frequent sampling vs. satellite frequency
  - In situ sensing vs. remote-only methods
  - Cost-effective risk tolerance capability

### Time to Market
- Phase II-E conclusion expected to align with market entry (2019-2020)
- Design for manufacturability and scalability integrated into development

## Competitive Advantages

1. **Ruggedness**: Designed for harsh environments (corrosive particulates, high altitude, turbulence, extreme cold)

2. **Modularity**: 
   - Field-swappable nose cone for sensor configurations
   - SwiftCore FMS supports up to 128 sensors
   - Rapidly reconfigurable for emerging mission needs (demonstrated in Phase II)

3. **Takeoff/Landing Capability**: VTOL enables operations from minimal landing areas vs. traditional fixed-wing requirements

4. **Flight Management System**: SwiftCore FMS and SwiftPilot autopilot provide autonomous operation and user simplicity

5. **Export Classification**: Commercial development (not military-designed) enables export under EAR rather than ITAR
   - **Strategic advantage for NASA**: Allows foreign entities in NASA missions to participate in science gathering with reduced administrative burden
   - Current licensing obtained; additional classification for sensor payloads to be pursued

6. **Operational Maturity**: Hundreds of successful UAS deployments and proven commercialization track record

## Commercialization Strategy

### Core Strategy: Demonstration
- Deploy systems, learn from customers, iterate rapidly
- Infuse technology into ongoing NASA programs first, then government and commercial missions
- Government demonstrations expected to generate commercial customer demand

### Marketing Approach
- Target thought leaders in emerging segments (NASA, NOAA, USGS researchers)
- Leverage existing distribution channels: manufacturing partners, in-house sales, airframe resellers
- Bring on dedicated sales support as commercialization becomes imminent
- Targeted marketing emphasizing performance superiority demonstrated in comparative studies

### Development & Deployment Timeline (2019-2020)
- Q3-Q4 2019: Conclusion of Phase II, Phase II-E work begins, VTOL development
- Q1-Q4 2020: High-wind takeoff/landing algorithms, flight campaigns
- Parallel: Customer discovery, material generation for Earth Science market, commercial sales

### Post-Phase II-E Production
- Utilize existing S1/S2 XT assembly processes with minimal modifications
- Multiple suppliers identified for key components enabling efficient scale-up
- Existing inventory tracking and customer management systems in place
- Leverage Phase II-E field test data to refine designs for production
