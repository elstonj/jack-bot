## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to support University of Iowa's USDA NIFA project on precision agriculture by conducting three annual flight campaigns (2025, 2026, 2027) using the S2 fixed-wing UAS to collect nitrous oxide (N2O) emissions data over corn fields during the critical spring sowing season (April-May). The work includes integration of commercial N2O sensors in Year 1 and University of Iowa's proprietary N2O sensor in Years 2-3.

## Technical Approach
- **Platform**: S2 fixed-wing UAS for sensor deployment
- **Flight Parameters**: 
  - Altitude: 400 feet
  - Coverage area: 3 acres per mission
  - 3-day flight campaigns per year
- **Data Management**: Onboard DAQ system with storage device; post-flight data retrieval and processing (no in-flight data collection anticipated)
- **Payload Integration Work**: Mechanical interface design, electrical system integration, firmware development, assembly, and testing for N2O sensor compatibility

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 Fixed-Wing UAS
- **Application**: Precision agriculture environmental monitoring
- **Deployment**: Aircraft rental at $1,000/day for 3-day missions
- **Operational Requirements**: Pilot in Command (8 hrs @ $250/hr per mission day) and Technician support (8 hrs @ $175/hr per mission day)
- **Payload Compatibility**: Designed to accommodate both commercial and proprietary N2O sensors through custom integration

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Application**: Measurement of nitrous oxide (N2O) emissions from agricultural soils
- **Target Conditions**: Post-rain events during corn sowing season (mid-April to mid-May) when soil denitrification rates are highest
- **Geographic Focus**: Eastern Iowa corn fields (Cedar Rapids airport region)
- **Scientific Purpose**: Support precision agriculture research on N2O emission monitoring and soil management practices
