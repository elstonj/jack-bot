## Executive Summary
The University of Colorado commits $22,390 in funding to Black Swift Technologies to further develop the SuperSwift unmanned aircraft system (sUAS) originally developed under NASA SBIR Phase II. CU intends to use the enhanced SuperSwift for wildland firefighting research as part of its NOAA FIREX project partnership, with the platform adapted for modular payload delivery and fuel assessment capabilities.

## Technical Approach
- Adapting the modular nose cone of the SuperSwift for FIREX mission compatibility
- Integration of SMM (Soil Moisture Measurement) payload to assess available fuel for wildfire spread modeling
- Enhancement of key performance indicators to broaden operational utilization
- Development toward flight testing by CU as part of FIREX campaign

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SuperSwift (sUAS)**
- Originally developed under NASA SBIR Phase II (contract NNX14CG09C)
- Designed as common platform for scientific data-gathering missions
- Features modular nose cone for payload adaptation
- Intended for wildland firefighting research applications

**SMM Payload**
- Soil Moisture Measurement capability
- Proposed use: assessing available fuel for wildfire modeling and spread prediction

## Use Cases & Applications
- **FIREX Project**: Wildland firefighting research in partnership with NOAA and University of Colorado
- Fuel assessment for wildfire management in Western States
- Scientific data gathering missions (general platform capability)
