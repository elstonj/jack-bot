## Document Type & Purpose
This is a **blank template/model agreement** for allocating intellectual property rights between a Small Business Concern (SBC) and a Research Institution (RI) participating in STTR-funded projects. It is not filled out with specific BST details but was filed as a supporting document for BST's Soil Moisture Mapping SBIR Phase I proposal to AFWERX.

## Key Provisions

### Background Intellectual Property (Section 2)
- Each party retains ownership of pre-existing IP developed independently of the agreement
- Either party may use the other's Background IP nonexclusively and without compensation for R&D activities under the STTR project
- SBC may use RI's Background IP for commercialization under specified conditions, potentially with royalty payments (percentage to be negotiated)

### Project Intellectual Property (Section 3)
- Ownership vests in the party whose personnel conceived or first reduced to practice the subject matter
- Jointly developed IP is jointly owned unless otherwise agreed; SBC has first option to perfect rights in jointly developed IP
- **Critical provision**: Parties agree to disclose all Subject Inventions to each other and the awarding agency within ___ months of initial disclosure
- Government receives irrevocable, royalty-free, nonexclusive license for any governmental purpose in all Project IP
- SBC has exclusive option to commercialize RI's Project IP (both patentable inventions and other forms)

### Commercialization Rights (Section 3(f))
**For potentially patentable RI inventions:**
- SBC has exclusive license option for initial period of ___ months after invention disclosure
- Option renewable for additional ___ months with written notice
- SBC reimburses RI patent prosecution expenses during option period (within ___ days of invoice)
- Upon exercise, SBC negotiates license terms including: reasonable royalties on sales, reimbursement of patent maintenance expenses, and (for exclusive licenses) commercialization milestones/minimum royalties
- Patent expense reimbursement may offset up to ___ % of accrued royalties

**For non-patentable RI Project IP:**
- SBC has exclusive license option extending until ___ months after RI completes the relevant project phase
- Negotiated license includes reasonable royalties and commercialization terms

### Confidentiality & Publication (Section 5)
- Proprietary information held in confidence unless disclosed with permission
- Either party may publish results, but publishing party must:
  - Give right of first refusal to other party
  - Provide ___ day review period with consideration of comments
  - Defer publication up to ___ additional days to allow patent application filing

### Liability & Indemnification (Section 6)
- Each party disclaims all warranties (merchantability, fitness for purpose, non-infringement)
- SBC indemnifies RI for claims arising from SBC's commercialization
- Both parties indemnify Government for commercialization claims

### Termination (Section 7)
- Either party may terminate on ___ days written notice
- Confidentiality obligations survive termination
- Each party responsible for its proportional costs through termination

## Notable Details

### Blank Fields Requiring Completion
This template includes multiple unfilled blanks for:
- Option periods (in months)
- Royalty percentages
- Review/deferral periods for publication
- Termination notice periods
- Disclosure deadlines
- Expense reimbursement timelines

### Government Rights
- Government retains irrevocable, royalty-free, nonexclusive license for governmental purposes in all Project IP
- Consistent with 37 CFR 401.14 Patent Rights clause
- Both parties must indemnify Government for commercialization claims

### Key Protections for SBC
- First option to commercialize RI-developed inventions
- Ability to extend option periods for patent prosecution
- Right to offset patent expenses against royalties
- Exclusive licensing rights if SBC elects to commercialize

### Key Protections for RI
- Retains ownership of Background IP
- Receives royalties on SBC commercialization of RI Background IP
- Receives royalties on commercialization of RI Project IP
- Patent prosecution expenses reimbursed by SBC during option periods
