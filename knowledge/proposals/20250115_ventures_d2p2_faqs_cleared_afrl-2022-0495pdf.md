## Executive Summary
This FAQ document provides guidance for small businesses applying for SBIR Direct to Phase II (D2P2) awards through AFWERX. D2P2 allows companies that have completed Phase I-like feasibility studies with non-SBIR funding to apply directly for Phase II awards. The document clarifies requirements around Customer Memoranda, funding, evaluation criteria, and customer discovery resources.

## Key Content Areas

### Direct to Phase II Program Overview
- D2P2 topics enable small businesses with completed Phase I-equivalent feasibility work (funded outside SBIR/STTR) to apply for Phase II awards
- AFWERX D2P2 Open Topic proposals require a signed Customer Memorandum from DAF Customer and End-User(s)
- Other DoD D2P2 topics under Air Force CSOs and DoD SBIR BAAs may not require Customer Memoranda

### Funding & Financial Requirements
- Non-SBIR Government funding (Second Phase Funding Commitments) is **not required** for D2P2 proposal or award
- Customer/End-User organization funding commitments are not essential
- Venture capital, private equity, or hedge fund investments are **not prohibited** but cannot be used as award criteria or evaluation factor (per 15 USC 638(dd)(7))
- Fixed-price awards do not require DCAA-certified accounting systems
- AFWERX D2P2 and Phase II awards are issued as fixed-price Other Transactions (OTs) for Prototype or FAR-based purchase orders

### Regulatory & Compliance
- Regulatory approvals (ATOs, TMAP certifications) are **not prerequisites** for proposal submission or award
- However, all IT within the Air Force must eventually obtain Risk Management Framework (RMF) assessment and Authority to Operate (ATO)
- RMF assigns Impact Levels (IL-2 through IL-5) for cloud security
- DevSecOps must be established in secure cloud environment
- NIST SP 800-171 Rev 1 and DFAR PGI 239.76 outline cybersecurity control requirements
- CMMC (Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification) measures cybersecurity preparedness
- SBIR funds cannot be used to pay for Platform One services (requires Government source funding)

### Customer Memorandum Requirements
- Only **one Customer Memorandum per Phase II proposal** allowed
- Primary End-User operationally benefits from solution; Primary Customer acquires on their behalf
- No rank/grade requirement for signatories, but Primary Technical Point of Contact (TPOC) must be DAF military or civilian
- TPOC is appointed by Customer/End-User and serves as technical on-site government representative
- Joint organizations may serve as End-User/Customer but must clearly articulate valid DAF need
- Previously signed memoranda must include written confirmation of continued commitment (case-by-case consideration)
- Letters of support may demonstrate interest from other DAF/government organizations

### Proposal Evaluation
- Proposals evaluated on three general criteria: Commercialization, Defense Need validity, and Technical Merit
- Specific evaluation criteria detailed in individual solicitation documents
