# Letter of Support for Black Swift Technologies – Navy STTR Hazardous Weather

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support (academic endorsement)
- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy (Office of Naval Research)
- Program/Solicitation: NAVY STTR 25.A-T025: Expendable Air-sea Profiling Observations in Hazardous Weather Conditions
- Date: January 16, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 (unmanned aircraft system/UAS)
- Key Personnel: Dr. David Richter (University of Notre Dame, letter author); Beck Cotter (BST, last editor)
- Supporting Institution: University of Notre Dame, Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering and Earth Sciences

## Executive Summary
Dr. David Richter from Notre Dame provides institutional support for Black Swift Technologies' Navy STTR Phase I proposal to develop expendable air-sea profiling capabilities in hazardous weather. The letter endorses BST's S0 UAS platform as a cost-effective, reliable solution for atmospheric profiling in challenging marine environments, citing proven hurricane deployment experience and rigorous data validation practices.

## Technical Approach
BST's proposed work under this STTR includes:
- Enhancement of data processing capabilities
- Integration of alternative wave measurement technologies
- Addressing icing challenges in harsh marine conditions
- Improving communication systems for hazardous weather operations
- Leveraging the proven S0 UAS platform as the foundation

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 Unmanned Aircraft System (UAS)
- **What it is:** An expendable unmanned aircraft designed for atmospheric profiling in hazardous conditions
- **Proven capability:** Successfully deployed in hurricane observations
- **Key strengths cited:**
  - Operates reliably in turbulent conditions
  - Capable of accurate wind measurements in challenging marine environments
  - Design emphasizes simplicity and affordability
  - Demonstrated ability to gather data in hazardous weather conditions
- **Data quality:** Wind measurements validated against instrumented towers and NOAA P-3 aircraft dropsondes

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Hurricane observations:** Proven deployment history in hurricane environments
- **Air-sea interaction research:** Supporting understanding of complex atmospheric-oceanic processes
- **Navy operational applications:** Expendable profiling observations in hazardous weather at sea
- **Atmospheric profiling in marine environments:** Cost-effective alternative to manned aircraft for Navy applications

## Key Advantages Highlighted
1. **Platform Reliability:** Demonstrated through successful hurricane deployments
2. **Data Accuracy:** Rigorous calibration and validation practices; measurements validated against established sources (instrumented towers, NOAA P-3 dropsondes)
3. **Cost Efficiency:** More affordable than comparable state-of-the-art platforms (specifically referenced: Raytheon Coyote, Area-I/Anduril Altius), enabling more frequent and widespread deployments

## Notable Details
- The letter specifically compares BST's cost-effectiveness favorably against commercial competitors (Raytheon Coyote and Area-I/Anduril Altius)
- Academic validation of data quality is emphasized as a competitive advantage
- Dr. Richter references expertise in air-sea interaction as the basis for his endorsement
- The proposal aims to address specific technical challenges relevant to Navy operations: icing, communication, and wave measurement integration
- The support emphasizes the potential for "richer dataset for research and operational purposes" through wider, more frequent deployments enabled by cost savings
