## Executive Summary
The NightFOX project is developing a nighttime fire observation system using an unmanned aircraft platform to measure aerosols, gases, and thermal signatures from wildfires. In September 2017, the team completed approval of the SuperSwift UAS purchase order, accumulated 7 hours of flight experience with a loaned system, and made progress on instrument development including aerosol sensors and infrared telescopes for remote sensing.

## Technical Approach
- **UAS Platform**: SuperSwift acquired through CU purchase order (delivery pending); team gained operational experience with loaned SuperSwift system
- **In Situ Instruments**: 
  - POPS (Particle Optical Properties Spectrometer) for accumulation-mode aerosol measurements—one assembled, second near completion
  - Coarse-mode aerosol instrument under development (similar to POPS design)
  - CO sensor elements expected in October 2017
- **Remote Sensing**: Development of near-IR and mid-IR cross-track-scanning telescopes; preliminary optical designs completed; mechanical prototype designs begun
- **Regulatory**: FAA COA (Certificate of Authorization) amendment approved for nighttime flight operations; assessing UAS lighting requirements
- **Modeling**: Integration with fire weather modeling, data assimilation assessment, and potential synthetic data set development
