## IT Security Requirements & Procedures

### NASA System Access
- **Single NASA IT system access required**: SBIR/STTR Awardee Firm ProSAMS (https://prosams.nasa.gov/)
- **Purpose**: Electronic submission of contract deliverables, invoices, and technical reports
- **Access management**: Via NASA Account Management System (NAMS) using NASA Agency User ID and profile password through Identity and Access Management System (IdMAX)
- **Personnel limitation**: Registration limited to those involved in contract negotiation and administration
- **Mandatory training**: All registered personnel must complete NASA Online Annual IT Security Training
- **No additional NASA system access** required during contract performance

### Information Protection Requirements
BST must:
1. Protect confidentiality, integrity, and availability of NASA Electronic Information and IT resources
2. Prevent unauthorized disclosure of NASA Electronic Information
3. Understand and adhere to NIST and NASA IT Security requirements, regulations, policies, and guidelines
4. Comply with Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) handling requirements

### Sensitive Information Handling (FAR 1852.237-72)
BST personnel must:
1. Use sensitive information only for authorized contract purposes, not competitive advantage
2. Safeguard sensitive information from unauthorized use and disclosure
3. Restrict access to employees with operational need-to-know
4. Prohibit access and disclosure to external persons/entities
5. Train all employees with potential sensitive information access on obligations and protections
6. Obtain written affirmation from each employee confirming training compliance
7. Implement monitoring processes to ensure compliance and report breaches immediately

## General Rules of Behavior

All personnel must comply with 15 mandatory security behaviors:
1. Use NASA systems only for lawful, official, authorized purposes
2. Protect all NASA information including PII and CUI
3. Report known or suspected security incidents immediately
4. Encrypt all NASA data on mobile/transportable devices and removable media
5. Read and understand NASA website privacy, security notices, and disclaimers
6. Screen-lock or log off computers when leaving work area
7. Grant NASA information access only to authorized personnel with proper clearance and need-to-know
8. Minimize sensitive information on IT systems while traveling; destroy copies when no longer needed
9. Properly mark and label classified, sensitive, and proprietary documents
10. Adhere to Separation of Duties principles
11. Do not use anonymizer sites that bypass Agency security
12. Protect IT equipment and media from destruction, damage, loss, theft, or compromise
13. Supervisors must adequately instruct, train, and supervise employees
14. Adhere to minimum password requirements
15. Never share account passwords; protect passwords at highest classification level of applicable system

## Incident Response Procedures
- **Immediate notification required** for any intentional or inadvertent information security incident affecting confidentiality, integrity, or availability
- **Notification recipients**: NASA Security Operations Center and appropriate NASA officials including Contracting Officer (CO) and Contracting Officer's Representative (COR)
- **Reportable incidents**: Compromise, misuse, distortion, loss, or destruction of NASA IT systems, data, or contract deliverables

## Legal Acknowledgments
BST acknowledges that:
- Unauthorized attempts to upload or change information on NASA servers are strictly prohibited
- Violations may be punishable under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986 and National Information Infrastructure Protection Act of 1996
- Compliance will be enforced through sanctions commensurate with infraction severity
