- **Key Personnel:** Dana Naeher (NOAA UAS Division)

## Executive Summary
NOAA is procuring 26 Black Swift S0 uncrewed aircraft systems to operationalize routine hurricane research missions, along with comprehensive operator/observer and instructor training course development and delivery. The S0 was selected as the best overall platform after NOAA's ~$2M evaluation of multiple sUAS providers, being 10x more cost-effective than competing systems while surpassing all performance criteria for sampling critical air-sea boundary measurements during tropical cyclones.

## Technical Approach
**Platform Deployment Model:** Air-launched from NOAA's WP-3D (P-3) Hurricane Hunter aircraft to conduct low-altitude measurements in hurricane conditions where crewed aircraft cannot safely operate.

**Operational Concept:** 
- Operators and observers deploy aboard the P-3 from Lakeland, FL (or meet aircraft in Barbados, St. Croix, or Aruba)
- S0 platforms launched during hurricane operations to collect near-surface meteorological/oceanographic data
- Real-time data transmission back to P-3 aircraft for forecaster situational awareness and TC forecast model assimilation

**Training Development:** Three-tier training program:
1. Operator and Observer course (draft due 120 days from award)
2. Instructor training course (draft due 150 days from award)
3. Initial training delivery to NOAA UASD and AOC employees at Lakeland, FL

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift S0 Platform
**What it is:** A small uncrewed aircraft system (sUAS) designed for air-launch from crewed aircraft to conduct meteorological and oceanographic measurements in severe weather.

**Specifications:**
- Lightweight, portable design suitable for P-3 deployment
- Endurance: 60+ minutes
- Cruise speed: 35+ knots
- Dash speed: 90+ knots
- Communication range: 100+ miles in hurricane conditions
- Payload: In situ sensors for highly accurate atmospheric measurements (pressure, temperature, humidity, 3D winds, surface temperature, wave height)
- Includes charger, cables, protective case for transport/storage

**Performance Claims:**
- Only system capable of sampling near air-sea boundary during tropical cyclones
- Surpassed all other evaluated systems on performance criteria
- 10x more cost-effective than next closest competing platform

**Robustness:** Designed as "robust, simple to operate scientific platform" suitable for routine operations in hurricane conditions

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Mission:** Hurricane Research and Operational Support
- Real-time data collection during active hurricane/tropical cyclone (TC) operations (June 1 – November 30, 2025 hurricane season)
- Data supports forecaster decision-making and situational awareness
- Measurements assimilated into operational TC forecast models
- Tier 1 Validated Earth Observation Data requirement

**Specific Measurements:**
- Air-sea boundary conditions (energy/momentum exchange with sea surface)
- Wind speed/direction at critical altitudes during storm landfall
- Atmospheric pressure, temperature, humidity profiles
- Surface temperature and wave height data

**Operational Context:** Fills critical gap in data collection—no crewed system can safely sample these measurements during active hurricane conditions; manned aircraft cannot operate in the severe weather where sUAS can deploy.

## Key Results
Not applicable—this is a procurement SOW, not a results/findings report. However, it documents NOAA's conclusion from a prior ~$2M evaluation effort:

- Selection based on performance criteria evaluation + cost-effectiveness (10x advantage)
- Represents transition from R&D phase to operational deployment phase
