## Executive Summary
This is a blank Form B2 template for NASA's Civilian Commercial Readiness Pilot Program (CCRPP), which governs Phase III commercialization applications for SBIR/STTR technology projects. The form requires applicants to detail customer objectives, commercialization strategy, implementation approach, and technology readiness levels for maturation and transition of SBIR/STTR innovations to operational use or commercial markets.

## Document Purpose & Context
This is not a Black Swift Technologies proposal or report, but rather a standardized government application template. It serves as a reference document for understanding the structure and requirements of NASA CCRPP Phase III applications.

## Key Required Sections

### 1.0 Customer Objectives
- Overview of primary/target customer (NASA program or non-NASA aligned with NASA interests)
- Customer acquisition plan
- Relationship between customer and investor (investor letter of commitment required)
- Program/project alignment with customer needs and goals
- Expected impacts and benefits (mission enablement, new capabilities, performance/reliability improvements, cost savings)
- Technology need dates (Quarter and Fiscal Year specificity required)

### 2.0 Commercialization Information
Applicants must provide:
- **Market Feasibility & Competition Strategy**: Target markets, competitive advantages, key customers/applications, market size projections, time-to-market, estimated market share within 5 years, anticipated competition
- **Commercialization Strategy**: Product commercialization approach, alignment with SBC business plans (5-year horizon), commercialization experience, existing/projected commitments (Phase III funding, industry investment)
- **Key Management & Technical Personnel**: Skills/experience in commercialization, organizational structure, plans for obtaining commercialization expertise
- **Financial Strategy**: Private financial resources committed, current investment/sales/licensing indicators, projected financial requirements, evidence of financial condition (cash flow statements), fit within broader commercialization planning
- **Intellectual Property**: Plans and status for securing patents, copyrights, trade secrets necessary for competitive advantage and commercialization

### 3.0 Implementation Approach

#### Maturation Strategy
- Technology maturation tasks with descriptions
- Approach to transitioning technology to user from maturation stage to initial capability achievement
- Required integration, testing, and certification steps
- Support or research efforts required
- Relationship to other NASA programs/projects
- Financial contingency planning

#### Schedule
- Detailed tentative schedule (Gantt charts required)
- Milestone table (must align with budget Form C)

#### Procurement Strategy
- All applicable procurements and subcontract relationships as separate line items

#### Technical Strategy
Factors to address:
- Implementation funding requests
- Technology issues
- Integration issues
- Test and evaluation concept and implementation
- Environmental, safety, and occupational health (if required)

#### Logistics Strategy (as needed)
- Design interface
- System reliability requirements
- Technical data
- Maintenance planning
- Computer resources support
- Manpower and personnel
- Supply support
- Support equipment requirements
- Facilities
- Asset Management
- Environmental Issues
- Training and training support

#### Manufacturing Strategy (if applicable)
- Demonstration of production readiness
- Sources of product production/supply
- Lean manufacturing and manufacturing development initiatives
- Industrial capabilities

#### Readiness Levels
- Table 2 required: Technology Readiness Levels (TRL) and Manufacturing Readiness Levels (MRL)
- Minimum requirement: describe TRL increases
- MRL optional depending on customer

## Technology Readiness Level (TRL) Definitions Provided

**TRL Scale (1-9):**
1. **Proof of Concept** - Analytical and experimental demonstration of hardware/software concepts
2. **Breadboard** - Low fidelity functional demonstration only
3. **Brassboard** - Medium fidelity functional unit addressing scaling issues
4. **Prototype Unit** - Form, fit, and function at representative scale in simulated operational environment
5. **Engineering Unit** - High fidelity demonstration of engineering processes, closely resembles final product
6. **Mission Configuration** - Final architecture/system design for operational environment

## Environment Definitions Provided
- **Laboratory Environment**: Demonstrates underlying principles without addressing operational environment
- **Relevant Environment**: Specific subset of operational environment addressing critical "at risk" aspects
- **Operational Environment**: Actual environment where final product will operate (space, airborne, ground-based, or platform-specific for software)

## Notable Details
- **Investor Commitment Required**: Letter of commitment from investor is mandatory entrance criterion
- **NASA Alignment**: Non-NASA customers acceptable only if application "strongly aligns with and furthers NASA interests"
- **Page Limit**: 30 pages for Form B2
- **Submission Method**: Must submit through NASA SBIR/STTR Electronic Handbook (EHB) at https://sbir.gsfc.nasa.gov/submissions/public/login
- **Confidentiality**: Information is procurement-sensitive and protected by government
- **Strong Customer Pull**: Project acceleration must be clearly tied to demonstrated customer demand at project completion
