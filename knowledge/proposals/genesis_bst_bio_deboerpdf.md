## Executive Summary
This is a DOE biographical sketch for Gijs de Boer, Interim Associate Laboratory Director at Brookhaven National Laboratory. De Boer is an atmospheric scientist with 25+ years of experience in climate science, remote sensing, and unmanned aircraft research, including collaborative work with Black Swift Technologies on atmospheric measurement campaigns.

## Professional Background
- **Current Position (as of 2025):** Interim Associate Laboratory Director for Environment, Biology, and Nuclear Science and Nonproliferation at Brookhaven National Laboratory
- **Previous Key Role:** Associate Director for Science, Integrated Remote and In Situ Sensing (IRISS) at University of Colorado Boulder (2021–2025)
- **Education:** PhD in Atmospheric and Oceanic Sciences (University of Wisconsin–Madison, 2009); MS in same field; BS in Soil, Crop and Atmospheric Science (Cornell, 2002)
- **Postdoctoral Experience:** Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (climate science, 2009–2011)

## Products & Capabilities Described
De Boer's publication record includes five peer-reviewed papers on uncrewed aircraft systems (RPAS/UAS) for atmospheric research:

1. **Observational data from uncrewed systems over Southern Great Plains** (2022) – Demonstrates coordinated fleet operations and data collection
2. **Assimilation of a Coordinated Fleet of Uncrewed Aircraft System Observations in Complex Terrain** (2022) – Shows integration of UAS data into weather forecasting models
3. **University of Colorado and Black Swift Technologies RPAS-based measurements of the lower atmosphere during LAPSE-RATE** (2021) – Direct collaboration with BST; atmospheric profiling campaign
4. **Development of Community, Capabilities, and Understanding through Unmanned Aircraft-Based Atmospheric Research: The LAPSE-RATE Campaign** (2020) – Community-wide atmospheric research initiative using UAS
5. **Advancing Unmanned Aerial Capabilities for Atmospheric Research** (2019) – Overview of UAS capabilities for atmospheric science
