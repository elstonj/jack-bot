## Executive Summary
The NightFOX project is developing a UAS-based system for nighttime fire observations, integrating multiple sensor types including infrared and aerosol instruments. As of January 2018, most instrument technologies have been selected or identified, the CU Mobile Command Center vehicle has been received, and platform acquisition (S2 UAS) is expected mid-February.

## Technical Approach
The project is assembling a multi-sensor payload for wildfire observation with the following components:
- **Optical Systems**: Scanning mechanism for telecentric lenses designed for SWIR (shortwave infrared) and mid-IR (midwave infrared) sensors; prototype near-IR camera assembled and tested with satisfactory results
- **Gas Sensors**: CO (carbon monoxide) sensors under development (prototype delivery delayed from December)
- **Aerosol Instruments**: Commercial coarse-mode aerosol instruments ordered
- **Platform**: CU to acquire S2 UAS platform (expected mid-February 2018)
- **Integration**: Discussions ongoing between CSD (NOAA), CU, and Black Swift Technologies for instrument integration

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS Platform
- **What it is**: CU's UAS platform being acquired for NightFOX
- **Proposed use**: Carry multi-sensor fire observation payload; operate to higher altitudes and BVLOS (beyond visual line of sight) per FAA discussions
- **Status**: Acquisition expected mid-February 2018

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Wildfire monitoring**: Nighttime fire observations during active wildfires
- **Prescribed burns**: Planned testing during summer/fall 2018 prescribed burns in Boulder area
- **Data assimilation**: Fire weather modeling and synthetic dataset development
- **Agency coordination**: Working with FAA on Certificate of Authorization (COA), USFS, NOAA, and state/federal wildfire agencies on access through Temporary Flight Restrictions (TFR)
