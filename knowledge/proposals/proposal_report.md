## Executive Summary
This report examines how Black Swift Technologies' S2 unmanned aircraft system can be redesigned for rapid, edge-manufacturable composite fabrication using Curable Composites' advanced rapid-curing, tool-free additive manufacturing methods. Nearly all structural components of the S2 can be adapted for carbon fiber 3D printing without sacrificing strength-to-weight performance. The integration enables significant reductions in lead time, enhanced field adaptability, and scalable production of Group 2 UAS for defense and environmental applications.

## Technical Approach

**Core Strategy:** Redesign S2 airframe components from traditional hand-laid carbon fiber and foam composites to Curable Composites' printed carbon fiber hollow cylinders and flat composite laminates using frontal polymerization techniques.

**Manufacturing Paradigm Shift:**
- Replace fixed molds and autoclaves with autoclave-free, tool-light additive manufacturing
- Integrate mounting points, fasteners, and routing paths directly into printed components
- Achieve near-net-shape production to minimize waste and machining
- Enable rapid changeover between mission-specific designs without retooling

**Key Technical Capabilities Leveraged:**
- Hollow cylindrical tube fabrication with adjustable wall thickness, fiber orientation, and diameter
- Tailored fiber layups for specific load-bearing requirements
- Digital design compatibility enabling rapid geometry modification
- Post-cure integration of metal inserts, adhesive bosses, and modular interfaces

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 Unmanned Aircraft System
**What it is:** A modular, fixed-wing Group 2 UAS with 90-minute endurance and 2 kg payload capacity. Deployed in extreme weather conditions including wildfires, volcanic monitoring, and supercell sampling.

**Current Construction:**
- Carbon composite fuselage with integrated launch tubes and payload interfaces
- Foam-core composite wings with embedded wiring and servo pockets
- Carbon fiber tube tail boom with internal cable routing
- Removable V-tail empennage with embedded servos
- Interchangeable nosecones for payload integration
- 3D-printed battery housing with carbon reinforcement and QR latch system

**Proposed Redesign Using Curable Composites:**

**Fuselage** – Currently hand-laid carbon fiber over mold with vacuum bagging. Proposed as two-piece printed carbon fiber hollow cylinder with wall thickness of ~1.2–1.3 mm, tailored fiber orientation for torsional and bending stiffness, and integrated or post-bonded inserts for launch tubes, servo connectors, and payload mounts.

**Tail Boom** – Currently carbon fiber tube with internal wiring and SMA antenna routing. Proposed as Curable-printed hollow cylinder with integrated anti-rotation keys, cable guides, and threaded metal inserts for V-tail mounting. Enables field-fabricated variants with mission-specific stiffness.

**Wings** – Currently foam-core composite panels with embedded wiring, pitot/static tubes, and servo mounts. Proposed as printed composite skins with embedded spar sockets, servo mounts, pitot holders, and hinge pockets. Goal: standardized wing molds with replaceable, reconfigurable field-producible components.

**Empennage (V-Tail)** – Currently two symmetric control surfaces with embedded servos, linkages, and horns. Proposed as printed hollow shells using lightweight layups with integrated servo mounting points, alignment keys, and joiners. Control horns and clevis remain COTS.

**Battery Housing** – Currently 3D-printed with carbon straps, QR button integration, and cable routing. Proposed as Curable-printed carbon composite with modular rail integration, embedded strain reliefs, and thermal isolation ribs. Reduces need for additional carbon strapping.

**Servo Pockets and Control Interfaces** – Currently laser-cut composite trays with silicone bonding. Proposed as directly printed composite trays with pre-tapped mounting hardware, wiring guides, and alignment tabs using Curable's flat-sheet panel methods.

**Payloads and Nosecones** – Mission-specific, currently 3D-printed. Proposed as Curable-printed carbon composite components enabling rapid adaptation to new sensors while maintaining structural consistency and aerodynamic form.

- Frontal polymerization techniques for hollow cylinders and flat panels
- Adjustable wall thickness and fiber orientation
- Digital design integration for rapid changeover
- Near-net-shape production minimizing waste

## Use Cases & Applications

- **Extreme weather operations:** Wildfires, volcanic monitoring, supercell sampling
- **Tactical edge manufacturing:** Rapid repair and replication in austere, distributed operational environments
- **Field customization:** Real-time mission reconfiguration with modular component swaps
- **Defense and environmental applications:** Group 2 UAS for contested or remote operations
- **Payload adaptation:** Rapid integration of EO/IR, RF, or weather sensor payloads

## Key Results

This is a Phase I report presenting feasibility analysis and design concepts rather than test results. No prototype testing data is provided. The report identifies all major S2 components as suitable for Curable Composites' manufacturing approach without loss of structural performance.

## Notable Details

**Edge Manufacturing Advantages:**
- **Rapid Production:** Critical components fabricated on-site within hours vs. weeks
- **Modular Reconfiguration:** Independent removal and replacement of fuselage, wing, tail sections supporting payload swaps and environmental adaptation
- **No Specialized Tooling:** Eliminates dependency on fixed molds and autoclaves; enables agile response to new manufacturing requirements
- **Reduced Cost and Waste:** Material minimization through near-net shaping; pre-integration of fasteners and routing paths eliminates rework
- **Distributed Scalability:** Tactical edge fabrication reduces dependency on centralized logistics chains and enhances resilience in contested environments

**Recommended Next Steps:**
1. Prototype and test modular composite components (fuselage tubes, empennage structures)
2. Digitize and parametrize S2 designs for mission-specific CAD adjustment
3. Develop deployable production toolkits with printers, resins, molds, and design libraries
4. Validate printed composites for impact, UV, and moisture degradation under fielded conditions
5. Integrate modular parts with transport cases, field connectors, and sustainment chains

**Strategic Significance:** Report positions joint BST-Curable approach as establishing new standard for "responsive aerospace systems" that are high-performance, reconfigurable, and manufacturable at tactical edge—supporting defense resilience in distributed and contested environments.
