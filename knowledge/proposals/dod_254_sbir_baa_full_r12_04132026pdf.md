## Executive Summary
This is the DoD Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Program 25.4 Annual BAA document, establishing the framework and requirements for small business concerns to submit Phase I and Direct-to-Phase II proposals across 12 monthly releases throughout fiscal year 2025. The document outlines eligibility criteria, certifications, proposal preparation instructions, evaluation processes, and compliance requirements for defense-related R&D projects.

## Notable Details

**Program Structure:**
- Three-phase program: Phase I (6-12 months), Phase II (24 months), Phase III (commercialization with non-SBIR funding)
- 12 monthly release windows throughout FY2025, with specific pre-release, open, and close dates
- Direct-to-Phase II option available for qualified SBCs with demonstrated feasibility
- Amendment 1 clarified Section 2.5 on Due Diligence Program to Assess Security Risks

**Eligibility Requirements:**
- SBCs must meet SBA small business size, ownership, and control standards (13 CFR §§ 121.702-705)
- Majority venture capital/hedge fund/private equity ownership prohibited unless authorized by specific DoD component
- Phase I: SBC must conduct minimum 66.7% of research/analytical work
- Phase II: SBC must conduct minimum 50% of research/analytical work
- Principal investigator must have primary employment (>50% time) with proposing SBC at award and during performance
- All R&D work must be performed in the United States

**Performance Benchmarks:**
- SBCs with multiple prior SBIR/STTR awards must meet Phase I-to-Phase II transition rate and commercialization benchmarks
- Companies failing benchmarks limited to 80 total Phase I and Direct-to-Phase II awards from DoD across all components

**Security & Compliance Requirements:**
- Risk-based due diligence review assessing cybersecurity, patent analysis, employee analysis, foreign ownership, and FOCI (Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence)
- DoD may reject proposals posing unacceptable national security risk regardless of technical merit
- SBCs must disclose foreign affiliations, relationships, financial obligations to foreign entities, and employee ties to foreign countries
- FASCA (Federal Acquisition Supply Chain Security Act) compliance required

**Proposal Requirements:**
- Submission through Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP)
- Multiple certifications required: SAM registration, SBA Company Registry, DSIP registration
- Proposals must include: technical volume, cost volume, company commercialization report, supporting documents, fraud/waste/abuse training certification, foreign affiliations disclosures
- OCI (Organizational Conflicts of Interest) disclosure and mitigation plans required per FAR 9.5
- Human subjects research, animal research, and recombinant DNA research require specific approvals and assurances

**Funding & Awards:**
- Phase I awards generally $150K-$225K range (typical, not specified in this excerpt)
- Phase II awards generally $600K-$1M range (typical, not specified)
- Phase III requires non-SBIR funding (private sector or other federal sources)
- Cost sharing permitted but not required for Phase I
- No guarantee of awards; DoD may fund multiple or zero proposals per topic

**Notable Policies:**
- Commercialization Readiness Program (CRP) available through Army, Navy, and Air Force
- TABA (Technical and Business Assistance) discretionary; availability varies by component
- Phase II Enhancement available if company matches SBIR funds with DoD acquisition or private sector funds
- Three-month SBIR/STTR extension act (Public Law 117-183) benchmark requirements apply

**Related Support:**
- SBA offers online FOCI training through Project Spectrum (projectspectrum.io)
- State SBIR/STTR support offices available
- DoD Office of Inspector General hotline (800-424-9098) for fraud/waste reporting
