- Type: Internal planning document / submission checklist

## Executive Summary
This is an internal planning document outlining requirements and submission procedures for Black Swift Technologies' NOAA Phase II SBIR proposal. The document consolidates program specifications, formatting requirements, and submission component checklists to guide proposal preparation.

## Program Specifications & Requirements

### Award Details
- **Funding**: $400,000
- **Duration**: 24 months
- **Decision Timeline**: Within 90 days of February 19th (decision expected mid-May 2019; awarded June 2019)

### Submission Format Requirements
- Unlimited page count (internal max recommended to avoid reviewer fatigue)
- All pages numbered (including cover, summary, main text, references, resumes, attachments)
- Paper size: 8.5" × 11"
- Font: ≥10 pt
- Margins: ≥0.984" (2.5 cm)
- Email and attachments: ≤20 MB total
- All attachments: PDF format only

### Work Requirements
- Minimum 50% of work must be performed by BST
- All R&D must be conducted within the USA

## Required Submission Components

### Technical Proposal (single PDF file)
1. Cover Page (required form)
2. Project Summary (required form)
3. Phase I Summary (1 page max, summarizing Phase I activities and findings)
4. Technical Content (no page limit)
5. Proposed Budget (required form)

### Supplemental Budget and Documentation (individual PDF files)
1. Supplemental Budget documentation
2. SBIR Funding Agreement Certification
3. Company Registry screenshot from SBIR.gov
4. SBIR Fraud, Waste, and Abuse Training Certification
5. Commitment Letter (on company letterhead, dated, signed by authorized officer; for corporations/LLCs must list individuals with binding authority)

### Commercialization Plan (separate PDF file)
Required section with no specified format constraints

## Program Emphasis & Guidance

### NOAA Priorities
- High priority given to manufacturing-related R&D
- Proposal should emphasize how BST's innovations result in commercial production (hundreds of units per year)
- Commercialization scope includes both Government and private sector markets

### Phase Structure Guidance
- Phase II primarily focused on prototypes
- Phase III focused on final versions
- Document notes uncertainty about strategic advantage of proposing final version completion within Phase II

### Proposal Strategy Considerations
- Proposal must be responsive to specific NOAA program interests (Section 8 of solicitation)
- Proposal must serve as basis for technological innovation leading to new commercial products, processes, or services
- Recommendation to set internal page limit despite unlimited official allowance

## Notable Details
- Decision expected in June 2019 based on 90-day timeline from February 19 submission deadline
- Document emphasizes compliance with formatting and submission procedures
- Clear emphasis on commercialization pathway for government and private markets
- Strong focus on manufacturing scalability as competitive advantage
