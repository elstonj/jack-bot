## Executive Summary
Budget justification for a one-year DOD SBIR Phase II atmospheric sounding project led by PI Adam Houston at UNL, detailing personnel costs, field operations, travel, and indirect costs for testing a new system in collaboration with Black Swift Technologies.

## Budget Components

### Senior Personnel
- PI (Adam Houston): 1 month of summer funding requested
- Fringe benefits at 30% rate

### Other Personnel
- One graduate research assistant (GRA) funded for full project duration
- GRA will participate in field operations and potentially conduct thesis research using collected data
- Fringe benefits for GRA: 38% + health benefits (actual costs only charged)

### Travel

**Field Operations:**
- Duration: 10 days of system testing operations
- Personnel lodging: $120/night/person
- Subsistence: $60/day/person
- Fleet mileage for SUVs (including fuel)

**Meetings:**
- Two meetings in Boulder, CO with BST personnel
- Three participants per meeting
- Cost per person per meeting: $1,150
  - Airfare: $400
  - Lodging: $450
  - Subsistence: $300

### Communication, Printing, and Supplies
- Annual support: $1,000
- Covers conference calls, poster printing, routine communication

### Indirect Costs
- UNL's federally negotiated rate: 55.5% MTDC (Modified Total Direct Costs) for organized research

## Use Cases & Applications
Field testing of new atmospheric sounding system; potential thesis research applications utilizing collected data.

## Notable Details
- Project involves direct collaboration with BST personnel in Boulder, CO
- Field testing component spans 10 days with multi-person team
- Partnership between academic institution (UNL) and BST for technology development/validation
- Emphasis on involving graduate student in both field operations and research outcomes
