## Executive Summary
This document describes the Impedance Measurement Box (IMB), a battery diagnostic tool developed by Idaho National Laboratory researchers that measures battery state of health in 10-15 seconds using wideband impedance spectroscopy. The technology is being commercialized through a licensing agreement with Dynexus Technology for applications ranging from electric vehicle batteries to military and telecommunications backup systems.

## Technical Approach
The IMB diagnostic system operates through five key steps:
1. Generation of input signal consisting of strategically-spaced sinusoids summed together
2. Injection of combined signal into the energy storage device (battery)
3. Capture of response via data acquisition system
4. Data processing and analysis
5. Display of results

**Key capability**: Measures wideband impedance spectrum in seconds during battery operation with no significant impact on service life, replacing older methods requiring 10+ minutes of laboratory testing.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Impedance Measurement Box (IMB)
- **What it is**: A hardware and control software diagnostic tool that measures battery impedance and state of health
- **Performance**: Generates multipoint impedance spectra in as little as 10 seconds
- **Technical basis**: Measures impedance (opposition to alternating electrical current flow), which correlates with resistance and power capability
- **Generations**: Third-generation device capable of assessing 50-volt systems (for battery modules with multiple cells)
- **Advantage over prior methods**: Previous standardized techniques required expensive, delicate laboratory equipment; IMB enables in-place evaluation during operation
