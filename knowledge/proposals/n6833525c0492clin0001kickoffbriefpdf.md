## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to develop a Sonobuoy-Launched Unmanned Aircraft System (SL UAS) integrating magnetic anomaly detection (MAD) and passive acoustic sensing capabilities for Navy anti-submarine warfare (ASW) operations. Phase I will demonstrate sensor integration feasibility, modify the existing S0 UAS platform to meet sonobuoy launch constraints, validate sensor performance in controlled environments, and develop data fusion algorithms combining MAD and acoustic outputs for enhanced underwater target detection and classification.

## Technical Approach

### Phase I Objectives:

**Objective 1: Sensor Integration Feasibility**
- Evaluate noise mitigation strategies to minimize platform-induced interference
- Ensure hydrophone meets Navy sensitivity, frequency range, and depth requirements

**Objective 2: Platform Compatibility with Sonobuoy Launch Constraints (SLC)**
- Modify existing S0 UAS platform to accommodate payload volume and weight constraints of sonobuoy launcher

**Objective 3: Sensor Performance Validation**
- Conduct laboratory simulations and bench testing
- Characterize magnetometer noise performance
- Characterize hydrophone acoustic sensitivity

**Objective 4: Data Fusion Algorithm Development**
- Design, implement, and test algorithms combining MAD and acoustic sensor outputs
- Enhance underwater target detection and classification capabilities

### Key Technical Details:
- Magnetometer: QuSpin QTFM Gen-2 (BST has already flown this sensor on similar UAS during proposal phase)
- Hydrophone: Cetacean Research Technology partnership for acoustic sensor integration and testing
- Computing: Onboard sensor processing module to be integrated into S0 airframe
- Design approach emphasizes Size, Weight, and Power (SWAP) optimization while maintaining required capabilities

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS Platform
- **Description:** Existing small UAS platform developed by BST; tube-launched capable
- **Proposed Use:** Base platform for integration of MAD and acoustic sensing for Navy sonobuoy-launched operations
- **Modifications Required:** 
  - CAD and electronics design updates to accommodate sensors and compute module
  - Potential modifications to airframe size/shape
  - Battery/payload trade-off optimization
  - Integration of noise-mitigation components for magnetometer performance
- **Design Flexibility:** BST designs all aspects of S0 and can rapidly modify components to address technical challenges
- **Key Specification:** Must fit within tube-launch constraints with full sensor and compute payload; cost target of ~$10K per unit

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary (Phase I Focus):

- **Stratospheric Missions:** NASA high-altitude balloon gondola deployment; wildfire and disaster response applications
