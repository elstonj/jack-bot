## Executive Summary
This document describes Black Swift Technologies' facilities, manufacturing capabilities, and testing locations in Boulder, Colorado. BST operates a vertically integrated UAS design and manufacturing operation with 3000 sq ft of workspace, including prototyping, simulation, testing, and production capabilities. The company has access to multiple flight testing areas ranging from local sites to high-altitude locations in the San Luis Valley.

## Facilities & Infrastructure

**Main Facility (Boulder, CO)**
- 3000 sq ft facility
- Location: Boulder, Colorado
- Includes: office space, electronics benches, shop facilities, meeting space
- Computing resources: full suite of atmospheric and aircraft simulation tools for mission scenario analysis
- Testing equipment: multiple full autopilot systems for electrical and software interface testing
- Available systems: autopilot, ground station, user interface, communications hardware

## Products & Capabilities Described

**UAS Design & Manufacturing**
- Vertically integrated designer and manufacturer of fixed-wing and multirotor UAS
- Full in-house control of critical design aspects:
  - Electronics (autopilot, power boards, GNSS, etc)
  - Software and firmware
  - Mechanical design and manufacturing
  - Aircraft airframes
  - Ground control stations
  - Support equipment
- Manufacturing capability for both prototype and production versions
- Sensor and avionics integration capability
- Supply chain management: manages component sourcing and has ability to redesign around parts shortages (successfully navigated recent electronic parts shortages with microcontroller substitutions)

**Outsourced Manufacturing Partners**
- Northwind Composites: airframe composite work
- Denver-area board houses: circuit board manufacturing

## Testing & Operations Capabilities

**Flight Testing Locations**

1. **Local Sod Farm Test Site**
   - Location: 20 minutes east of Boulder office
   - Current use: S2 testing documented

2. **Pawnee National Grasslands**
   - Size: 20 x 20 mile test area
   - Authorization: 1000' waiver from FAA

3. **San Luis Valley High-Altitude Testing**
   - Terrain: peaks over 14,000 ft elevation
   - Testing capability: up to nearly 15,000 ft MSL under current regulations

4. **Alaska Operations**
   - Relationship: established with Alaska DOT
   - Capability: BVLOS (Beyond Visual Line of Sight) missions in remote locations
   - Program: UAS Activity Areas initiative

## Notable Details
- BST demonstrates vertical integration across full UAS supply chain, providing control over critical components during parts shortages
- Proximity to diverse testing environments (plains, high altitude, remote operations)
- Established regulatory relationships (FAA waivers, Alaska DOT partnership)
- Infrastructure supports both development and production activities
- Emphasis on in-house expertise across software, electronics, and mechanical engineering
