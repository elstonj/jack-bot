## Executive Summary
This is a standard federal SBIR/STTR life cycle certification form that Black Swift Technologies completed as a Phase II awardee. The document certifies BST's compliance with Small Business Administration requirements regarding principal investigator time commitment, work performance percentages, work location, and the absence of duplicate funding across federal agencies.

## Document Content
This is a template-based compliance certification form rather than a substantive technical or business document. It contains 11 certification checkboxes and statements addressing:

### Key Certification Requirements Covered:
1. **Principal Investigator/Project Manager Time Commitment**: Requirement that PI/PM spend >50% time as an employee of the awardee (or approved deviation)
2. **Essentially Equivalent Work**: Certification that work hasn't been submitted for duplicate federal funding
3. **Work Performance Percentage (Pre-Completion)**: SBIR Phase II requires awardee to perform at least 50% of the research
4. **Work Performance Percentage (Completion)**: Same 50% threshold upon award completion
5. **Management Control**: N/A for SBIR (applies to STTR only)
6. **U.S. Performance Location**: Work must be performed in the United States unless waived
7. **Facility Location**: Work performed at awardee's facilities by awardee employees per approved application
8. **Notification Requirement**: Must notify agency if work subsequently funded by another federal agency
9. **Information Disclosure**: Understanding that information may be shared with federal/state/local agencies
10. **Authorized Signatory**: Officer certifying is authorized to represent the business concern
11. **Change Notification**: Company must notify contracting officer if information changes between proposal receipt and award

## Notable Details
- This is a required certification document for all SBIR/STTR Phase I and Phase II awardees, completed at times set forth in the funding agreement
- Signatories face potential criminal, civil, or administrative sanctions for false information, including prosecution under 18 U.S.C. § 1001, False Claims Act liability, and debarment from federal contracts
