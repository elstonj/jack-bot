# DTRA SBIR 25.2 Solicitation Instructions

## Document Metadata
- Type: SBIR solicitation instructions and topic descriptions
- Client/Agency: Defense Threat Reduction Agency (DTRA)
- Program/Solicitation: DoD 2025.2 Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Program
- Date: FY25.2 (2025)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None
- Key Personnel: Ms. LaTricia Price (DTRA SBIR/STTR Program Manager)

## Executive Summary
This document provides instructions and guidelines for the Defense Threat Reduction Agency's FY25.2 Small Business Innovation Research program, along with detailed descriptions of three available research topics. The solicitation seeks innovative solutions in AI-driven plume tracking for UAS operations, predictive threat modeling for force protection, and displacement damage modeling for satellite components. Black Swift Technologies is not mentioned in this document.

## Document Content Overview
This is a standard government solicitation document containing:
- General SBIR program administration procedures and contact information
- Phase I, Phase II, and Phase III requirements and funding parameters
- Proposal submission guidelines via the Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP)
- Evaluation criteria and selection procedures
- Export control and security requirements
- Three specific research topics with detailed technical objectives

## Key Program Parameters

### Funding and Duration
- **Phase I**: 7 months, up to $200,000 (plus optional $6,500 TABA)
- **Phase II**: 24 months, up to $1,300,000 (plus optional $50,000 TABA)
- **Phase III**: No specified funding (seeks private/other government sources)

### Proposal Requirements
- Phase I Technical Volume: 20-page maximum
- Phase II Technical Volume: 40-page maximum
- Cost Volume: Itemized Excel spreadsheet (DTRA template)
- Company Commercialization Report (CCR) as Volume 4
- Supporting Documents as Volume 5
- All submissions via DSIP portal only

### Important Constraints
- DTRA does not participate in Direct to Phase II (DP2) program
- Phase II proposals only from Phase I awardees
- No deviations to Percentage of Work (POW) requirements normally accepted
- Proposers must comply with ITAR and EAR export control regulations
- Foreign nationals must be disclosed with visa/work permit status and task assignments
- Cyber security requirements mandatory (DFARS 252.204.7012)
- Travel costs must be justified and project-related

## DTRA SBIR 25.2 Available Topics

### DTRA252-001: Plume Localization Using Machine-learning for Environmental Sensing (PLUMES)

**Critical Technology Area**: Trusted AI and Autonomy

**Objective**: Develop an AI-driven solution for autonomous detection, tracking, and guidance of unmanned aerial systems (UAS) through airborne plumes generated during explosive tests.

**Technical Requirements**:
- Platform and sensor-agnostic algorithms for multi-sensor data fusion
- Real-time AI/ML-based plume identification and classification
- Capability to distinguish plumes from background interference (dust, clouds, smoke)
- Autonomous UAS flight control for real-time plume tracking and sampling
- Robust data security and transmission capabilities
- Adaptability to various UAS platforms and integration into existing test frameworks
- Integration of LIDAR, radar, EO/IR, and hyperspectral imaging data

**Phase I Deliverables**:
- Technical roadmap for AI/ML-driven plume tracking integration with UAS
- Computer vision model for real-time plume identification
- Framework for integration of additional sensor data (LIDAR, radar)
- Security data framework
- Proof-of-concept report and feasibility study
- Preliminary AI/ML model
- System architecture plan
- Scaling plan for Phase II requirements

**Phase II Deliverables**:
- Scaled-down working prototype with live-scale testing in controlled environment
- Integration of AI/ML algorithms, onboard sensors, and real-time UAS control
- AI performance metrics and optimization approaches
- Demonstrations in various plume conditions and weather scenarios
- Data collection and transmission systems (UAS to ground control)
- Fully functioning prototype (performer choice of UAS platform)
- Live demonstration results
- Detailed AI model evaluation
- Sensor integration report
- Phase III scaling design plan

**Phase III Applications**:
- Full-scale testing at DTRA explosive test sites or similar environments
- Integration with existing test range infrastructures
- Commercialization pathways for hazardous material detection, industrial emissions monitoring, emergency response

**Export Control**: ITAR-restricted topic; foreign nationals must be disclosed

### DTRA252-002: Predicting Force Protection Threats against DTRA from Foreign Adversarial Messaging

**Critical Technology Area**: Trusted AI and Autonomy

**Objective**: Develop predictive models to determine when online messaging from foreign adversaries may "break out" into broader information environments or manifest as physical threats against DTRA personnel or facilities.

**Technical Focus**:
- Predict transitions between parts of foreign information environment (news, social media, websites)
- Gauge overall threat level based on messaging content and spread
- Focus on predicting threat escalation patterns, not individual threat actors
- Leverage publicly available information (PAI)
- Integration with force protection and public affairs posturing

**Phase I Deliverables**:
- Review of prior work on predicting physical threats from online messaging
- Proof-of-concept model(s) using publicly available information
- Historical case study validation
- Subject matter expert validation
- Initial models scoped to specific foreign populations, information environment topics, or threat sets

**Phase II Deliverables**:
- Refined model(s) with explanatory capabilities
- Demonstration of model generality across different foreign populations and threat types
- Model accuracy assessment against ground truth data
- Improved transparency, explainability, and portability

**Phase III Applications**:
- Broader application to multiple online information environments and media types
- Dual-use applications for industrial security envisioned

### DTRA252-003: Displacement Damage Modeling for Satellite Component Design, Evaluation, and Validation

**Critical Technology Areas**: Microelectronics; Space Technology

**Objective**: Develop specialized time-dependent computing code to model displacement damage effects in critical semiconductor components of satellites exposed to particle radiation.

**Technical Scope**:

*Radiation Sources*:
- Primary focus: Fast neutrons (with energy, uniformity, fluence/dose/dose-rate considerations)
- Secondary: Protons, heavy ions, alpha particles, beta particles

*Materials of Interest*:
- Silicon bipolar technologies, bipolar transistors, integrated circuits
- Diodes, MOS technologies, silicon imaging arrays
- Silicon particle detectors, LEDs, laser diodes, solar cells, infrared detectors
- Germanium, silicon-germanium, silicon carbide, gallium nitride, gallium arsenide
- Considerations: dimensionality, doping, polycrystalline substances

*Environmental Considerations*:
- Static and time-varying environments
- Temperature effects on conductivity changes
- Multiple surrounding materials (voids, housings, interfaces)
- Annealing processes

*Computational Requirements*:
- Radiation transport modeling
- Energy deposition calculations
- Displacement damage modeling
- Ray tracing visualization

*Required Outputs*:
- Primary knock-on atoms (PKA) per unit volume
- Multiple models for displacements per PKA with corrections
- Atomic displacement rate density and rate
- Ray tracing visualization
- Model performance/fidelity statistics
- Displacement damage cascade descriptions
- Particle collision analysis
- Time and temperature-dependent annealing changes
- Graphical depictions of particle rest positions

**Phase I Deliverables**:
- Proof of concept identifying suitable models, processes, algorithms
- Identification of radiation transport computation approaches
- Energy deposition and ray tracing visualization methods
- Displacement damage modeling processes
- Output specifications (PKA, atomic displacement rates, statistics)
- Report detailing advantages, disadvantages, limitations
- Preliminary proof-of-concept demonstration
- Confidence metrics

**Phase II Deliverables**:
- Mature models, processes, and algorithms
- Improved model performance and fidelity
- Demonstration of developed code
- Comprehensive documentation
- Technical and computational details report
- Development approach documentation
- Certification, validation, and verification

**Phase III Applications**:
- Finalization and commercialization for government, satellite designers, customers
- Potential expansion beyond exoatmospheric environments
- Broader semiconductor material scope
- Additional particle radiation types

**Export Control**: ITAR-restricted topic; foreign nationals must be disclosed

## Evaluation and Selection Process
- Single Evaluation Authority (EA) for all proposals
- Decision-making based on review results, funding limitations, and investment balance across topics
- Lower-rated proposals in one topic may be selected over higher-rated proposals in different topics
- Selection notification within 90 days of BAA closing date
- Debriefing available for non-selected proposals via email request
- Selected proposals proceed to contracting; awards issued as fixed-price purchase orders
- No opportunity for revision required if proposal is complete and reasonable
