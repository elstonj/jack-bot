## Executive Summary
This is a press release announcing an exclusive licensing agreement between Idaho National Laboratory and Dynexus Technology (Boulder, Colorado) to commercialize INL's embedded wideband impedance technology for battery health diagnostics and forecasting. The technology enables real-time monitoring of battery aging, health, and safety characteristics across multiple industries.

## Technical Approach
**Embedded Wideband Impedance Technology:**
- Developed by INL's Energy Storage Group with DOE Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy funding
- Won R&D 100 Award in 2011 (Impedance Measurement Box/IMB)
- Provides in-depth diagnostic insights previously only available in laboratory settings
- Enables continuous embedded monitoring of battery health and remaining life throughout the battery's lifecycle
- Delivers operational data on battery state of health, aging characteristics, safety bounds, and performance degradation

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Embedded Wideband Impedance Technology (licensed to Dynexus):**
- What it is: Advanced battery health diagnostic system based on broad-spectrum impedance measurement
- How used: Embedded continuous monitoring integrated into battery management systems
- Capabilities: 
  - Analyzes and forecasts battery health, aging, and safety characteristics
  - Monitors remaining useful life
  - Functions across entire battery lifecycle
  - Provides "smart meter" functionality for battery condition assessment

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Electric Vehicles (EVs):** Assessment of resale value, remaining life estimation, safety insights
- **Battery Repurposing:** Secondary market applications after EV batteries fade below 80% capacity
- **Drones:** Battery health monitoring for unmanned systems
- **Utility Energy Storage:** Grid-scale battery system monitoring
- **Telecommunications:** Battery backup systems
- **Medical Devices:** Life-critical battery applications
- **Military Systems:** Defense applications requiring battery reliability
- **General:** Any application requiring predictable battery performance, personal safety assurance, and waste reduction

## Notable Details
- **Exclusive licensing agreement:** Dynexus holds exclusive commercial rights
- **Environmental benefit:** Technology enables economic viability of battery repurposing/second-life applications
- **Value chain:** Applicability across entire battery value chain from manufacturers to end-users
- **Dynexus profile:** Emerging growth company specializing in sensor-based enterprise data and condition monitoring for critical infrastructure; founders have expertise in advanced sensors, distributed sensor networks, and data science
- **Problem addressed:** Growing consumer/industry/military need for timely battery health insights as expectations increase and failure consequences become more serious
