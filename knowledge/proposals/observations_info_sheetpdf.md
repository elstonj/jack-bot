## Executive Summary
This document outlines NOAA's FY2025 Observations Program competition, which seeks innovative sensor and observing technologies to advance weather observation systems. The program will allocate up to $2,100,000 across approximately 7 projects (up to $300,000/year per project, 2-year maximum), targeting technologies that improve accuracy, reliability, spatial coverage, cost-effectiveness, and deployability of surface-through-troposphere observations for the operational weather enterprise.

## Program Objectives
- Develop, demonstrate, and analyze innovative sensor and observing technologies with high potential to advance integrated, adaptable, and affordable observation systems
- Focus on surface and planetary boundary layer/tropospheric observations lacking high-resolution coverage
- Support eventual use by NOAA, National Mesonet Program, private sector, and other government agencies
- Exclude satellite-based sensors except as secondary calibration/validation objectives
- Encourage operational weather and commercial sector participation

## Proposal Evaluation Criteria
Strongest proposals should:
1. Include substantial collaboration with operational weather stakeholders who will benefit from the work
2. Collaborate with relevant R&D partners to accelerate results
3. Clearly document linkage to operational weather needs
4. Demonstrate potential to transition to operations, applications, commercialization, or final product
5. Explain how NOAA will sustain observational capabilities post-project (if applicable)

## Technology Readiness Level Requirements
Projects appropriate for this competition range from Readiness Level (RL) 4 to RL7 with potential to transition within 3–6 years.

## FY2025 Program Priorities

### OBS-1: Weather Buoy Mitigation Vehicle
**Objective:** Develop and demonstrate high-endurance, modular, portable Unmanned Surface Vehicle (USV)
- **Key Features:**
  - Artificial Intelligence (AI) User Interface for automated station keeping
  - Easy payload integration capability
  - **Use Case:** Rapidly mitigate weather buoy outages to reduce observational data gaps until permanent moored buoy solutions are available
- **Problem Addressed:** Buoy outages create observational data gaps critical to maritime weather monitoring and prediction

### OBS-2: Rainfall Estimate Improvement
**Objective:** Develop improved Specific Differential Phase (KDP) weather radar product
- **Technical Approach:** 
  - Separate propagation contributions from backscattered components of total differential phase
  - Filter measurement variance to remove unwanted signals while retaining resolution
  - Identify and correct return data corrupted by artifacts
- **Applications:** Rainfall rate estimation (particularly heavy rainfall), hail detection, ice crystal growth identification, thunderstorm downdraft location
- **Scientific Basis:** KDP is a critical dual-polarization weather radar parameter

### OBS-3: SFMR Improvement
**Objective:** Develop and demonstrate software to process Stepped Frequency Microwave Radiometer (SFMR) data
- **Current SFMR Capabilities:** Provides surface wind speed and rain rate estimates along reconnaissance aircraft tracks around/within tropical cyclones
- **Proposed Improvements:**
  - Process SFMR brightness temperature measurements more reliably
  - Deliver more accurate and trustworthy rain rates and surface wind speeds
  - Apply to all SFMR instruments on reconnaissance aircraft
  - Leverage other in-situ and remotely sensed tropical cyclone observations for validation
- **Problem Addressed:** Need for improved understanding and processing of SFMR retrievals to provide trustworthy data to forecasters and decision makers

### OBS-4: Tropical Cyclone Reconnaissance Data Advancements
**Objective:** Utilize NOAA Unified Forecast System (UFS) including Global Forecast System (GFS) and Hurricane Analysis and Forecast System (HAFS)
- **Specific Activities:**
  - Improve use of existing airborne reconnaissance observations through methods such as super-obbbing of high-resolution data
  - Assimilate existing experimental tropical cyclone reconnaissance data not in operational data stream
  - Assimilate new/experimental in-situ and remotely sensed data from poorly sampled regions
  - Develop improved quality assurance/quality control (QA/QC) procedures for tropical cyclone airborne reconnaissance data
- **Scientific Approach:** Coordinate new/experimental data with tropical cyclone airborne reconnaissance data to improve forecasts

## Notable Details

### Funding Details
- **Total Annual Allocation:** Up to $2,100,000 (pending FY2025 fund availability)
- **Per-Project Budget:** Up to $300,000/year
- **Project Duration:** Up to 2 years
- **Number of Projects:** Approximately 7 to be funded

### Strategic Focus Areas
- Weather enterprise integration (NOAA, private sector, other government sectors)
- Transition pathways to operations within 3–6 years
- High-impact, disruptive weather observation improvement
- Operational relevance and sustainability

### Program Context
- Addresses critical gap in high-resolution surface and tropospheric observations limiting weather prediction skill
- Emphasizes collaboration with National Weather Service (NWS) and operational weather stakeholders
- Part of broader NOAA strategy to improve life/property protection and national economic benefit through enhanced weather forecasting

### Exclusions
- Satellite-based sensors (except as secondary calibration/validation tools)
