## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to enhance wildland firefighting operations by deploying uncrewed aircraft systems (UAS) to collect targeted atmospheric observations in data-sparse regions, improving wind and turbulence forecasting for incident commanders. Working with NCAR on modeling and meteorology, BST will develop software tools and operational systems to integrate UAS-gathered data with existing fire operations infrastructure, advancing the technology from TRL 5 to TRL 7 by end of Phase II.

## Technical Approach

**Four primary technical objectives:**

1. **Ensemble Sensitivity Analysis (ESA) & Data Assimilation**: Optimize ESA methods to identify critical observation gaps; conduct Observing System Experiments with targeted UAS observations to refine data assimilation techniques and quantify improvements in wind forecasting.

2. **Multimodal Site Selection**: Operationalize an automated site-selection tool that weighs multiple factors (ESA results, accessibility, network coverage, terrain) with variable weighting to tailor deployment decisions to specific fire scenarios.

3. **Team Awareness Kit Integration**: Develop a plugin that displays both real-time BST UAS flight data (position, heading, speed, altitude) and meteorological outputs in a unified interface for incident commanders.

4. **Multi-UAS Operations Demonstration**: Conduct field operations around active wildfires or prescribed burns, coordinating with researchers and fire agencies to demonstrate operational impact.

**Division of Labor**: NCAR handles modeling and meteorology components; Black Swift handles software tools and UAS development, building on Phase I prototypes.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 VTOL UAS
- **What it is**: Vertical takeoff and landing uncrewed aircraft
- **Proposed use**: Atmospheric sensing platform for targeted weather observations around active wildfire zones; capable of continuous monitoring when deployed in multiples
- **Capabilities**: Can operate in mountainous, data-sparse terrain; equipped for autonomous deployment to fill observational gaps

### Team Awareness Kit Plugin
- **What it is**: Software integration tool for existing fire operations command systems
- **Proposed use**: Unified interface displaying UAS flight telemetry and meteorological forecasts to incident commanders
- **Data types**: Real-time UAS position, heading, speed, altitude; wind and turbulence forecasts
- **Future vision**: Broadcast hazard information to nearby UAS, crewed aircraft, and UTM Traffic Management Systems

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Mission**: Wildland firefighting operations, especially in:
- Mountainous terrain with sparse weather observation coverage
- Nighttime operations
- Data-sparse regions where existing models are inaccurate
- Conditions with rapid wind shifts or intense turbulence zones

**Operational Impact**: 
- Enhanced incident commander strategic planning through accurate, timely wind/turbulence forecasts
- Improved safety for firefighting aircraft operations
- Fire spread prediction and tracking support
- Potential integration with advanced air mobility (AAM) systems

**Commercialization Path**: Standalone system deployable by firefighting agencies and first responders during active operations.

## Notable Details

- **Problem Context**: Current aviation weather services fail to capture detail needed for wildland fire operations; lack of data in mountainous terrain creates dangerous forecasting gaps
- **NASA Alignment**: Proposal connects to NASA FireSense (Earth sciences + operational agencies), NASA ACERO (UAS safety applications), and broader Advanced Air Mobility research
- **Data Dissemination**: Wind/turbulence forecasts will be delivered through existing data pathways (Team Awareness Kit) to minimize operational friction
- **Spatio-Temporal Innovation**: System aims to provide unprecedented continuous coverage of wind/turbulence measurements and predictions—both observational data and model outputs
- **Safety Focus**: Emphasis on preventing "deadly consequences" from sudden wind shifts and turbulence in high-risk operations
