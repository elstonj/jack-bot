## Executive Summary
Dr. Jack Elston is BST's CEO and co-founder with extensive expertise in designing and operating unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) for in-situ atmospheric measurements in hazardous conditions. He leads all avionics work at BST, including development of the SwiftCore Flight Management System, and has directed multiple SBIR projects including Venus upper-atmosphere sampling and hurricane reconnaissance missions.

## Background & Expertise
**Academic Foundation:**
- Ph.D. from University of Colorado Boulder focused on meshed network UAS and control algorithms for tornadic supercell sampling
- NSF Atmospheric and Geospace Sciences Postdoctoral Research Fellowship for "Improved In Situ Thermodynamic Sampling of Severe Storms with Unmanned Aircraft Systems Through Improved Wind Estimation and Energy Harvesting"

**Key Achievements:**
- Designed the Tempest UAS, which conducted the first-ever unmanned aircraft intercept of a tornadic supercell thunderstorm (VORTEX2 project, NSF and NOAA funded)
- Demonstrated simultaneous multi-vehicle operations and vertically stacked formation flight through gust fronts
- Published over 100 COA (Certificate of Airworthiness) applications
- Conducted hundreds of flight experiments including Arctic deployments

## Technical Leadership at BST
- Technical lead for all avionics work
