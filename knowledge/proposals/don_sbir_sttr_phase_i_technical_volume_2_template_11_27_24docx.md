# DON SBIR/STTR Phase I Technical Volume 2 Template

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Template/Guidance Document (Instructions for proposal preparation)
- **Client/Agency:** Department of the Navy (DON) SBIR/STTR Program
- **Program/Solicitation:** DoD SBIR/STTR Broad Agency Announcement (BAA)
- **Date:** November 27, 2024
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None (this is a blank template)
- **Key Personnel:** None named in template itself

## Executive Summary
This is an official Department of the Navy instruction document and template for completing Phase I Technical Volumes (Volume 2) for SBIR/STTR proposals submitted through the DoD BAA. It specifies formatting requirements, content expectations, and structural guidance for Navy small business applicants.

## Document Purpose
This is **not a BST proposal or report**, but rather a government-provided template document that BST would use as guidance when preparing a Phase I Technical Volume for a Navy SBIR/STTR submission. The document contains no information about BST capabilities, products, or technical approaches.

## Key Formatting Requirements
- **Maximum Length:** 10 pages (strict limit; exceeding results in rejection)
- **Format:** Single column, single-spaced, standard 8.5" × 11" paper
- **Margins:** 1 inch on all sides
- **Font:** Minimum 10-point (exceptions for headers, footers, tables/figures)
- **Content:** Both Phase I Base and Phase I Option tasks must be included within the 10-page limit

## Required Sections

1. **Description of Proposed Phase I Technical Effort**
   - Demonstrate understanding of Navy requirement
   - Present technical approach
   - Describe innovation to solve Navy problem

2. **Phase I Technical Objectives**
   - Enumerate specific objectives
   - Identify research questions to address feasibility

3. **Phase I Statement of Work (Base and Option)**
   - Outline planned tasks, execution location, schedule
   - Define final deliverables
   - Demonstrate technical feasibility

4. **Key Personnel**
   - Identify Principal Investigator (PI) and key team members
   - Include employees, subcontractors, consultants
   - Note ITAR restrictions for Foreign Nationals on restricted topics

5. **Commercialization/Transition Plan Summary**
   - Strategy for DoD, Federal, and private sector markets
   - Market need and market size information
   - Pathway to Phase III and Navy acquisition

6. **Facilities/Equipment**
   - Describe available instrumentation and facilities
   - Justify equipment purchases
   - Certify compliance with environmental laws and regulations

7. **Letters of Support**
   - Optional; must remain legible
   - Count toward 10-page limit if included in Volume 2

## Important Restrictions & Notes

- **No Human Subjects, Animal Testing, or Recombinant DNA** in Phase I (can be planned for Phase II)
- **Disclosure Requirements:** Proposers must mark pages containing restricted data and include required legend if data must not be disclosed outside Government
- **Foreign National Restrictions:** Non-U.S. passport holders may be restricted on ITAR-controlled topics unless they hold Permanent Resident Cards or are "Protected Individuals"
- **Volume 5 Not Evaluated:** Other supporting documents are not reviewed by evaluators

## Notable Details

- This is a flexible template; proposers may adapt format but must meet strict requirements
- The document emphasizes that any non-compliance with format requirements will result in automatic rejection
- Resumes of key personnel count toward the 10-page limit
- Both Phase I Base and Phase I Option work must be clearly delineated within single Volume 2
