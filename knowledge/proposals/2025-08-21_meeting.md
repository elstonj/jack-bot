## Executive Summary
This Phase I SBIR meeting presents BST's plan to integrate a magnetometer, hydrophone, and single-board computer (SBC) onto the S0 Air-Deployed UAS platform for Navy anti-submarine warfare (ASW) cued search, detection, tracking, and classification missions. The project involves requirements analysis, platform modifications, sensor integration and testing, and prototype fabrication and validation, with optional tasks including flight testing and launch mechanism certification.

## Technical Approach

### Base Tasks (Phase I)
- **B.1 Requirements Analysis & Preliminary Design**: Integrate QuSpin magnetometer per their specifications; incorporate CRT acoustic sensor specifications for hydrophone integration
- **B.2 Platform Modifications**: Evaluate necessary changes to S0 UAS for compatibility with Sonobuoy Launch Container (SLC)
- **B.3 Sensor Integration & Testing**: Integrated testing of QuSpin magnetometer on S0; CRT hydrophone testing and calibration
- **B.4 Prototype Fabrication & Validation**: Validate S0 platform compatibility with SLC launch conditions, hydrophone deployment, and other integration risks

### Optional Tasks (Phase II consideration)
- **O.1 Local Flight Testing**: Drop tests with integrated magnetometer and CRT payloads
- **O.2 Certification for Phase II**: Navy flight testing authorization paperwork
- **O.3 Design for CAD Launch**: Modifications to support Cartridge Actuated Device (CAD) launch

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 Air-Deployed UAS
**Overview**: Small, low-cost, modular, electric fixed-wing UAS originally developed via SBIR for air deployment from P-3 Orion aircraft through Common Launch Tube; can also be hand-deployed from aircraft ramp or window.

**Platform Specifications**:
- **Weight**: 2.75 lbs maximum takeoff weight
- **Wingspan**: 32.5 inches
- **Flight Ceiling**: 15,000 ft
- **Flight Time**: 105 minutes nominal
- **Cruise Speed**: 40 mph
- **Max Speed**: 100 mph
- **Max Wind Endurance**: 50 mph
- **Payload Capacity**: 100 grams maximum
- **Nose Cone Dimensions**: 1.8 inches diameter

**Key Characteristics**: All-weather capability; low cost; modular design; ideal for ISR at tactical edge

**Avionics Configuration**: Newest S0 avionics routes high-powered wiring to back-half of airframe, leaving nose area available for magnetometer mounting (isolates magnetic interference)

### QuSpin Magnetometer
- Three-component system being integrated onto S0
- **Integration Questions Identified**:
  - Which components could be integrated into BST avionics for space/weight savings?
  - Could power input and filtering board integrate directly into BST avionics?
  - Optimal nose-cone mounting location relative to avionics magnetic interference?

### CRT Hydrophone
- Navy-specified sensor for acoustic detection
- **Integration Considerations**:
  - Size/weight/power requirements for Navy-compliant hydrophone unclear
  - Feasibility of miniaturization for small S0 platform
  - Proposed tail-mounted configuration (replacing atmospheric sonde)
  - Deployment from landed/floating aircraft
  - Depth requirements for operational effectiveness unknown
  - Communications link requirements to aircraft TBD

### Single-Board Computer (SBC)
- **Requirement**: NVIDIA Jetson Orin (Navy-mandated; variations exist)
- **Integration Issues**: 
  - Large relative to airframe scale
  - Computationally over-specified for S0 platform
  - Navy resistance to alternative SBC options noted
  - Size and weight optimization challenging

## Major Challenges & Risks Identified

1. **Magnetometer Placement**: Finding locations isolated from magnetic interference on compact airframe; avionics configuration may/may not provide suitable nose-cone location
2. **Hydrophone Miniaturization**: Whether Navy-compliant hydrophone can be reduced to size/weight suitable for S0 platform
3. **SBC Size/Weight**: NVIDIA Jetson Orin is large and computationally excessive; Navy-mandated specification limits alternatives
4. **Sonobuoy Launch Container Compatibility**: S0 modifications required for SLC integration; launch condition stresses on platform
5. **Power and Communications**: Integration of new sensors with S0 power budget and communication architecture
6. **Price Point**: $10k cost target for integrated system
