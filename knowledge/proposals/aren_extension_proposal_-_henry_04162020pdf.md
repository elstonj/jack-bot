# AREN Extension Proposal

## Document Metadata
- Type: Invited extension proposal
- Client/Agency: NASA (Science Activation Collective/SciAct)
- Program/Solicitation: AREN (AEROKATS and ROVER Education Network) - Program Years 6-10
- Date: April 16, 2020 (submission date); covers PY6-PY10 (2021-2025)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Aeropod, kite systems, sensor platforms
- Key Personnel: Paul (Andy) Henry (PI, Wayne RESA); Kay Rufty and Jack Elston (BST); Geoff Bland (NASA GSFC co-inventor of Aeropod/ROVER)

## Executive Summary
AREN is a NASA-funded experiential STEM education program that trains the next generation of scientists and engineers to observe Earth using novel low-cost kite-borne remote sensing technologies (Aeropods) and remotely operated vehicles (ROVERs). This invited extension proposal seeks to expand and deepen AREN's reach from 17 states to all 50 states while increasing participation from underserved populations, developing GLOBE-integrated learning activities, establishing sustainable hardware distribution, and advancing technology development through Years 6-10 (2021-2025).

## Technical Approach

### Overall Strategy
AREN employs experiential, hands-on learning centered on authentic NASA Earth science concepts and operational practices. The approach includes:
- Kite-borne sensor platforms (Aeropods) for remote sensing education
- ROVER platforms for spatially variable in-situ measurements
- Engagement across K-12, undergraduate, informal, and community science settings
- Integration with GLOBE Program protocols and data systems
- Engineering design activities (wind tunnels, sensor package development, kite design)
- Data analysis and visualization tools development
- Dissemination through online training, lending libraries, and distributed 3D printing

### Key Technical Areas for PY6-10:
1. **Activity & Curriculum Development**: Nine-week middle school course in progress; simplified introductory materials (MiniKites, "Middle Kite" in development); Wind Direction activity at 10-50m altitude; GIS data visualization; ROVER pilot activities
2. **ROVER Platform Evolution**: ROVER X7 and X10 (kit-based) versions with glue-free construction; integration of Arduino-based sensor systems; Vernier-compatible sensor packages
3. **Aeropod Development**: Multiple new designs tested and deployed; 3D printing enabled local production at multiple sites; "Thermopod" (thermal imager) kit development; enhanced online content
4. **Wind Tunnel Development**: UMES wind tunnel design/fabrication/testing for aerodynamic understanding; team-based learning component
5. **Data Infrastructure**: Working with GLOBE to develop array-based (multidimensional) data capabilities for transect/profile data; moving beyond single-point observations typical of GLOBE protocols; image/raster data support

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Aeropod
- **What it is**: Miniaturized, low-cost NASA remote sensing instrument for kite-borne deployment
- **Status**: Multiple stable designs developed; 3D printing enables distributed production; licensed to GLOBE and American Kitefliers Association
- **Proposed use in AREN**: Core platform for introducing remote sensing and in-situ observation concepts; used in formal and informal educational settings
- **Key milestones**: GLOBE license acquisition (2020/21); commercial production (2022/23); integration with GLOBE protocols
- **Related products**: Thermopod (thermal infrared imager variant) for Urban Heat Island studies and engineering design

### ROVER (Remotely Operated Vehicles)
- **What it is**: Remotely operated platform for spatially distributed in-situ measurements, particularly water quality
- **Versions**: ROVER X7 (current stable); ROVER X10 (new kit-based version)
- **Design features**: Glue-free construction for classroom assembly; multiple dissemination options (plan-built with suggestions, kits-in-a-box, fully assembled)
- **Sensor packages**: Vernier-based standard package; custom Arduino-based alternatives in development
- **Proposed use**: Highlight spatially variable measurements; water quality monitoring; integration with GLOBE water quality protocols
- **Key milestones**: ROVER X10 kits available (2021/22); ROVER GLOBE Learning Activity (2022/23); GLOBE license acquisition (2023/24)

### Kite Systems
- **Great Winds "Frustrationless Fliers"**: Introductory training kites
- **"Middle Kite"**: New student-built design in development with Great Winds Kites (2022); designed for meaningful lift capacity for small instruments
- **MiniKites**: Simplified dissemination approach in development
- **Wind Direction instrument** ("Windirator"): For measuring wind direction at altitudes ≤50m
- **Supplier partnership**: Into The Wind (kite vendor); Great Winds Kites (manufacturer)

### Data Tools & Platforms
- **MapKnitter** (Public Lab): Enhanced tool for stitching aerial images; ongoing investment by commercial partner
- **Fish-Eye GL Barrel Distortion Removal tool**: Image processing from AREN 1.0
- **Image Sequencer**: Image processing tool developed under AREN 1.0
- **AEROKATS.org website**: New platform for hosting training modules (2021 onward)
- **GLOBE Database enhancement**: Working to support multidimensional array data; raster/image data capabilities

## Use Cases & Applications

### Educational Settings
1. **K-12 Formal Education**: Cohort-based training for educators; implementation in classrooms and units; standards-aligned learning experiences
2. **Middle School**: Nine-week course in Remote Sensing using AREN technologies (in development)
3. **University Level**: Semester courses in aviation and engineering design; freshman engineering labs with Aeropod/ROVER design activities; junior-level aerial operations in remote sensing
4. **Informal Science Education**: Family Science Nights, public events, environmental centers, community science groups
5. **Teacher Professional Development**: Multi-day summer institutes and successive online webinars

### Research & Real-World Applications
- **Assateague Island Mapping**: Implemented and supported by National Park Service (highlighted as "real-world research" example)
- **Water Quality Monitoring**: ROVER platform for creek/water body assessment
- **Urban Heat Island Studies**: Thermopod deployment
- **Atmospheric Profile Measurement**: Kite-borne profiling with multiple sensors
- **Arctic Communities**: Alaskan villages using AREN remote sensing to identify physical pathways to food sources

### Target Populations
- **Underserved Urban**: Detroit Public Schools (81% free lunch eligibility); 64% economically disadvantaged in Wayne County; 60+ languages spoken; largest Arab American population; 82% African American in Detroit
- **HBCUs**: University of Maryland Eastern Shore (1890 Land Grant); Tennessee State University; potential with other HBCUs
- **Native American Communities**: Montana (community schools partnerships); Alaska Arctic villages (UAF); Northwest Earth and Space Sciences Pipeline (NESSP) project
- **First-Generation College Students**: CU pre-college engineering program
- **Rural Audiences**: Montana State University focus on rural youth
- **Female Learners**: MSU Girls STEM Collaborative; gender-specific outreach initiatives
