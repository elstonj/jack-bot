## Executive Summary
The Colorado Division of Fire Prevention and Control solicited vendor-provided and operated long-endurance vertical take-off and landing UAS on a call-when-needed basis for wildfire suppression, search and rescue, and other public safety incidents throughout Colorado and neighboring states. The contractor must provide fully crewed and equipped aircraft with defined performance specifications, payload capabilities, crew certifications, and operational compliance with federal and state regulations.

## Technical Approach
This is a performance-based statement of work outlining requirements for a vendor to provide on-call UAS services. Key operational framework:

- **Service Model**: Call-when-needed contracting with no guaranteed minimum orders; State retains exclusive 24/7 control of aircraft during ordered periods
- **Crew Requirements**: FAA Remote Pilot Certificate (14 CFR 107) minimum; duty limits of 14 consecutive hours with 2 calendar days rest within any 14-day period; minimum 10 consecutive hours rest before any duty period
- **Response Time**: Personnel must be ready for takeoff within 15 minutes of dispatch (or longer if authorized for flight planning)
- **Operational Period**: Standard 14-hour operational periods with optional extended periods up to 24 hours at negotiated rates

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Aircraft Requirements
- **Type**: Fixed or rotor-wing vertical take-off and landing (VTOL) UAS
- **Flight Performance**:
  - Capable of flying 12,000 feet density altitude including takeoff and landing
  - 2 hours continuous flight with fuel/batteries sufficient for 12-hour shift
  - Takeoff/landing capability in 20-knot winds; sustained operation in 25-knot winds
  - Day and night operations
- **Command & Control**:
  - Mode C transponder minimum (higher capability acceptable)
  - Two-way radio communication with incident ground and aviation resources
  - Ground Control Station (GCS) capable of connecting to 50-inch video monitor displaying live payload feed and contextual mapping

### Payload Requirements
- **Video Camera**: 1280x720 resolution, 10x zoom lens, live transmission to GCS
- **Infrared Sensor**: 640x480 resolution, live transmission to GCS
- **Capability**: Both sensors capable of tracking stationary point of interest (POI)
- **In-flight Re-tasking**: Capable of responding to unplanned payload tasking within 30 minutes
- **Data Standards**: 
  - MISB (Motion Imagery Standards Board) compliant video as multicast/unicast stream for EO and IR, compatible with TAK (Team Awareness Kit)
  - Real-time Cursor on Target (CoT) data as multicast and TLS stream to TAK Server
  - Still image capture with geo-tagging (lat/lon, DDD MM.MM format)
  - Fire perimeter mapping capability using target cursor with SPI (Sensor Point of Interest) coordinates (~100 feet horizontal accuracy); export as KML, SHP, or compatible format

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Wildland fire operations**: Real-time situational awareness, fire perimeter mapping, payload video collection
- **Search and rescue (SAR)**: Real-time video reconnaissance, EO/IR capability for personnel location
- **Emergency management**: General incident support
- **Other public safety missions**: As directed by incident command

**Geographic Scope**: Throughout Colorado with potential for missions in neighboring states

## Notable Details

### Operational Control & Authority
- State maintains exclusive control of aircraft 24/7 during ordered periods
- Incident Commander or designee provides tasking and point of contact
- UAS Manager has full contract administration authority including suspension of operations and pilot suspension upon flight safety concerns
- Data Standards Integration: Contractor must deliver data compliant with DoI/USFS standards (MISB, CoT, TAK Server) for interoperability with federal and state incident management systems

### Safety & Compliance Requirements
- All personnel must hold applicable FAA certifications and maintain compliance with relevant Certificates of Waiver or Authorization (COA)
- Mandatory NTSB and DFPC notification for accidents/incidents
- SAFECOM reporting required within 2 days of any aviation safety concerns
- Preservation of evidence and aircraft following mishaps until DFPC authorization
- Full cooperation with federal agencies in mishap investigations
- Contractor bears all costs for search, rescue, salvage, and reassembly following incidents not caused by DFPC negligence
- Personnel replacement mandated for non-compliance with facility rules, unsafe flying, or unsatisfactory performance (within 24 hours if directed)

### Payment Structure
- **Daily Availability Rate**: Per Exhibit B (prices not included in this document)
- **Flight Time**: Paid per hour for authorized revenue flights; measurement begins when aircraft moves under own power for flight, ends when comes to rest
- **Proficiency Flights**: Authorized by UAS Manager, paid as revenue flights
- **Mobilization/Demobilization**: Actual drive time from home base or agreed point to assigned location and return
- **No Guarantees**: Minimum/maximum flight hours not guaranteed
- **Deductions**: 1/28th of 14-hour rate or 1/48th of 24-hour rate deducted per 30 minutes of unavailability
- **Per Diem**: Not paid; lodging and subsistence included in daily rate
- **Fuel**: Contractor responsible for all fuel costs

### Data Ownership
All payload data acquired is property of the agency managing the incident and must be stored per agency/incident management direction.
