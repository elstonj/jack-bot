# Customer Memorandum CAO 29MAR23

## Document Metadata
- Type: Template / Form Instruction Document
- Client/Agency: Department of the Air Force (DAF), Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL)
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR/STTR Phase II – AFVentures Open Topic Program
- Date: 29 March 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None (this is a template document, not a completed proposal)
- Key Personnel: None named (template for completion by others)

## Executive Summary
This is a mandatory template and instruction document for Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR)/Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) Phase II proposals submitted to the Air Force under the AFVentures Open Topic program. It establishes the required format and content for Customer Memoranda that document Air Force organizational interest in partnering with awarded Phase II contractors on proposed efforts.

## Document Purpose
The Customer Memorandum serves to:
1. Document Department of the Air Force organization interest in working with AFWERX and an SBIR/STTR Phase II awardee
2. Establish Defense mission needs and measurable goals
3. Identify stakeholders, technical points of contact (TPOCs), and transition strategy
4. Address security, cybersecurity, and flight testing requirements
5. Formalize Air Force commitment to Phase II and potential Phase III efforts

## Required Sections & Content Areas

### Defense Mission Need (Section 2)
- Description of national Defense-related mission need
- Impact statements for US Air Force mission-need fulfillment
- Impact statements for US Space Force mission-need fulfillment (if applicable)
- Up to five measurable goals for the project
- Critical: Applicant must specify whether need applies to AF, SF, or both (determines appropriation funding)

### Project Timeline (Section 3)
- Expected execution period from award to completion
- Note: Applicants may propose shorter completion timeframes than specified in solicitation

### Phase II Funding Commitment (Section 4)
- Optional non-SBIR/STTR Government funding from DAF partners
- If yes: requires amount, type of funds, partner finance office concurrence
- Draft funding documents (Form 9s/MIPRs) due within 45 days of solicitation closing
- Certified funding documents due 5 business days after selection letters sent
- Commitment does not obligate Government; subject to funds availability at contract execution

### Additional Agreement (Section 5)
- Prior to/after award: DAF will consult with Air Force SBIR/STTR Legal, Contracting, and Program Office
- Identify specific clauses needed (GFE/I, facility access, licenses, environmental considerations)
- After award: guidance for Phase III contract transition

### Phase III Interest (Section 6)
- If Phase II successful: DAF may transition innovations to Phase III contracts, subcontracts, or other instruments
- Consideration given to Phase III transition preference for SBIR/STTR funded technologies
- Subject to Government acquisition planning, funds availability, security assessments, and reviews
- **No obligation for DAF to commit Phase III funding**

### Milestones (Section 7)
- Attachment 1.1 required: Schedule of Milestones providing value to end-users/customers

### Stakeholders (Section 8)
**Primary End-User Organization:** Organization that will use the developed solution or primarily benefit
- Requires point of contact with rank/grade, title, email

**Primary Customer Organization:** Organization managing future procurement, sustainment, funding, contracting, logistics
- Requires point of contact with rank/grade, title, email

**Primary Phase II Technical Point of Contact (TPOC):** *Must be DAF civilian or military member*
- Includes rank/grade, organization, email, duty phone
- Responsibilities: receive/accept/reject deliverables, provide feedback, overcome tactical barriers, drive Phase III transition

**Alternate TPOC:** Secondary point of contact with same information requirements

**Strategic Stakeholders:** Name of MAJCOM or HQ function and PEO or Center most directly impacted

**Staff Functions Requiring Concurrence:**
- Security Office
- Information Assurance/Cybersecurity Office
- Finance
- Contracting
- Strategy, Integration, and Requirements
- Small Business Office
- Commander
- Legal Counsel
- Engineering/SPO/Test
- Public Affairs
- Additional stakeholders as applicable

**Critical Note:** Support contractors and SETA contractors may NOT serve as Primary TPOC; must be Government personnel. TPOCs do not need to be from End-User or Customer organization but must be DAF military (Active, Guard, Reserve) or civilian.

### Phase III Transition Strategy (Section 9)
- Required narrative addressing transition assuming Phase II success
- Must address: Phase III funding strategy, requirements personnel role, contract vehicle, integration plan
- Should not be boilerplate; tailor to specific organization and solution
- Consult AFVentures SBIR/STTR resources at afwerx.com
- Small Business Office should advise on potential contracting/subcontracting opportunities

### Security (Section 10)
**Installation Access:** Indicate if contractor will require access to military installations/facilities
- If yes: TPOC or Customer confirms they will work with visitor centers/security offices to sponsor individuals

**Common Access Cards (CACs):** Indicate if contractor requires CACs to access DoD networks
- If yes: Security Office/Trusted Agent confirms they will process required documentation

**Classified Access (DD254):** Indicate if contractor requires classified accesses
- If yes: Security Office will work with TPOC and Customer to draft DD254 and obtain required accesses

**Note:** AFVentures does not manage security processes; customer and TPOC are responsible

### Cybersecurity (Section 11)
**Scope:** Applies if solution involves software/IT connecting to DoD networks/systems or utilizing CUI data
- If applicable: IA/Cybersecurity Office will work with contractor and Authorizing Official/Platform One
- Determine if Authorization to Operate (ATO) or Interim Authorization to Test (IATT) required
- Compliance with NIST SP 800-171 Rev 1 and DFARS PGI 239.76 requirements
- **AFVentures does not manage ATO or cybersecurity processes; customer/TPOC responsible**

### Flight Testing / Unmanned Aerial Systems (Section 12)
**Scope:** Applies if contractor requires flight testing or UAS utilization
- Flight testing definition: Vehicle moving through air using controlled/maneuvering aerial transport (includes balloon, attributable aircraft, manned/unmanned aircraft, air taxis, ground test, cockpit evaluations, etc.)
- Does NOT include solely ballistic flight paths

**Process:** After contract award, contractor must immediately coordinate with TPOC, AFRL/DO, and applicable AFWERX Test Personnel
- Prepare flight test planning documents per AFRL/DO instructions
- Blanket waiver exists for AFRL/DO to procure COTS UAS for cyber analysis, signature, checkout, profile data in benign/controlled RF environment
- Contractors must pursue additional "use waiver" with Government TPOC
- Contract execution not dependent on use waiver completion, but early initiation important for milestone scheduling

### Signatories (Section 13)
**Two Required CAC-Enabled Digital Signatures:**
1. **DAF Customer:** Must be in position to speak for organization regarding operational needs, formal requirements, or funding
2. **DAF End-User:** Must be in position to speak for organization regarding operational needs and benefit from solution
- **Critical:** DAF Customer and DAF End-User cannot be the same person
- Must provide suitability descriptions for each signatory
- **Memorandum without two signatures will NOT be accepted**

## Required Attachments
- Attachment 1.1: Schedule of Milestones (required)
- Attachment 1.2: Non-Proprietary Work Plan (required)
- Attachment 1.3: TPOC Designation Letter (informational; do not include in signed memo)
- Attachment 1.4: SBIR/STTR Definitions and Suitability Resources (do not include in signed memo)

## Key Requirements & Critical Notes

### Mandatory Compliance
- Use of template is **MANDATORY**; all elements must be present and complete
- All fields must be filled in; if not applicable, must state "N/A"
- Proposals without completed and signed memoranda in accordance with template will be determined **non-responsive** and not considered for award

### Disqualifying Issues
- Missing required sections
- Incomplete information
- Failure to obtain two CAC-enabled digital signatures
- Same person serving as both Customer and End-User

### Timeline for Funding Documents
- DRAFT documents: Due within 45 calendar days after solicitation closing
- CERTIFIED documents: Due 5 business days after selection letters sent
- If not provided by deadline, effort proceeds without Phase II funding commitment consideration

### Responsibility Distribution
- **AFVentures:** Does not manage security, ATO, cybersecurity, or flight test processes
- **Customer/TPOC:** Responsible for security clearances, CAC processing, cybersecurity compliance, flight test coordination
- **Contractor:** Must immediately coordinate with TPOC and AFRL/DO after award for flight test planning
