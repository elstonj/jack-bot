# Alerion Letter of Support - UAS Predictive Maintenance

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of support/interest
- Client/Agency: Alerion (industrial infrastructure inspection company)
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Phase II (Proposal #F2-14757)
- Date: 2021 (document modified June 9-10, 2021)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Predictive maintenance software system (unnamed in this excerpt)
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (BST), Oier Peñagaricano Muñoa (Alerion CEO)

## Executive Summary
Alerion, an industrial infrastructure inspection company, expresses strong interest in Black Swift Technologies' proposed machine learning-based predictive maintenance system for small UAS. The letter confirms market demand for this technology and identifies it as critical for safe operations in harsh industrial environments.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed to develop a predictive maintenance system using unsupervised machine learning algorithms to:
- Provide early warning of potential critical system failures on small UAS
- Deliver diagnostic information to support maintenance decisions
- Enable safer, more routine flight operations without risk to persons or property

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Predictive Maintenance Software System:**
- Uses unsupervised ML algorithms
- Targets small UAS platforms
- Provides early warning and diagnostics for critical failures
- Addresses a gap in current state-of-the-art maintenance approaches

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Application**: Industrial infrastructure inspections
- **Operational Context**: Extended operations in harsh environments
- **Safety Critical Missions**: Beyond-line-of-sight (BLOS) operations and flights over crowds

## Notable Details
- Alerion identifies predictive maintenance as becoming a necessity as UAS applications advance toward safety-critical operations
- The letter emphasizes that reliable predictive maintenance is essential for routine operations without risk to people or property in industrial inspection contexts
- Market need validation comes from an active industry player (Alerion) engaged in infrastructure inspection
