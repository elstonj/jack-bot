## Executive Summary
NOAA is procuring Black Swift S0 uncrewed aircraft systems (UAS) platforms, operational support, and comprehensive training to operationalize the S0 for routine hurricane research operations during the 2025 field season. Following approximately $2M in evaluation across multiple commercial sUAS providers, NOAA selected the S0 as the best overall platform—surpassing all competitors on performance criteria while being 10x more cost-effective. The procurement includes 26 aircraft, course development, operator/observer/instructor training, and optional operational support for hurricane missions.

## Technical Approach
Black Swift Technologies will:
1. **Supply S0 Platforms**: Deliver 26 fully-equipped S0 units by July 1, 2025, with chargers, cables, and protective cases
2. **Course Development**: Develop operator/observer training curriculum (draft by 120 days post-award) and instructor training curriculum (draft by 150 days post-award), working with NOAA to define standards and safety requirements
3. **Training Delivery**: Conduct initial operator, observer, and instructor training sessions in Lakeland, FL before hurricane season
4. **Operational Support (optional)**: Provide qualified operators and observers deployable on short notice June 1–November 30, 2025 to fly aboard NOAA's P-3 Hurricane Hunter aircraft, with support for pre-season testing flights
5. **Travel Support**: Cover all travel for training, operations, and testing to deployment sites (Lakeland, FL; Barbados; St. Croix; Aruba)

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift S0 Uncrewed Aircraft System
**What it is**: A lightweight, air-launched small uncrewed aircraft system designed for atmospheric and oceanographic measurement in tropical cyclone (hurricane) conditions.

**Specifications & Performance Claims**:
- Weight: Lightweight (exact specs not stated in SOW)
- Endurance: 60+ minutes
- Cruise speed: 35+ knots
- Dash speed: 90+ knots
- Range: 100+ miles communication link in hurricane conditions
- Payload: Highly accurate in situ sensors for pressure, temperature, humidity, 3D winds, surface temperature, and wave height
- Data transmission: Real-time transmission of scientific data to P-3 aircraft in hurricane conditions
- Deployment: P-3 air-launchable platform
- Operability: Robust, simple to operate

**How Proposed to Be Used**: 
- Launched from NOAA's WP-3D (P-3) Hurricane Hunter aircraft
- Measures air-sea boundary conditions (energy/momentum exchange) during tropical cyclone operations
- Provides near real-time data to improve forecaster situational awareness
- Data assimilated into operational tropical cyclone forecast models
- Classified as Tier 1 Validated Earth Observation Data

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Mission**: Hurricane/tropical cyclone research and operations
- Sampling near the air-sea boundary during storms
- Collecting meteorological/oceanographic (METOC) data in conditions no crewed system can access
- Supporting severe wind forecasting and landfall prediction affecting population centers
- Real-time data transmission to forecasters and operational modeling centers

**Deployment Locations**:
- Lakeland, FL (primary base)
- Forward deployment to: Barbados, St. Croix, Aruba (subject to change)

**Operational Window**: June 1–November 30, 2025 (hurricane season)

## Notable Details

**Competitive Selection Rationale**: NOAA evaluated multiple commercial sUAS providers over ~10 years of development with ~$2M investment. S0 selected because it:
- Surpassed all competitors on performance criteria
- Is 10x more cost-effective than the next closest system
- Meets critical unmet operational need (no crewed system can accomplish near-surface TC sampling)

**Operational Significance**: 
- Data is Tier 1 Validated Earth Observation Data (critical requirement)
- Real-time data improves forecaster situational awareness and impacts operational TC forecast models
- Directly affects hurricane preparedness, evacuation decisions, and property/life safety for millions
