## Executive Summary
This Phase I proposal develops an AI-enabled autonomous sensing system using unmanned aerial systems (UAS) to adaptively sample atmospheric boundary layers in real time. Black Swift Technologies and Brookhaven National Laboratory will create a closed-loop workflow integrating a physics-constrained probabilistic estimator, an uncertainty-aware adaptive planner, and autonomous flight control to demonstrate that AI-guided sampling outperforms traditional non-adaptive measurement strategies. The system targets longstanding observational gaps in capturing transient, spatially heterogeneous atmospheric processes critical to understanding weather, air quality, and hazardous material transport.

## Technical Approach

### Core Architecture (Four-Layer System)
The proposed autonomous agent operates through four integrated layers:

1. **Model Layer:** Hybrid physics-data-driven representation using a compact, low-dimensional latent state to encode local atmospheric vertical structure. Encodes full spatiotemporal temperature fields through a learned decoder that maps latent states to vertical profiles.

2. **Inference Layer:** Probabilistic predictor of atmospheric boundary layer (ABL) structure. Uses sequential Bayesian filtering (e.g., ensemble Kalman filters) to assimilate streaming temperature observations and produce posterior estimates of planetary boundary layer height (PBLH) and latent atmospheric state.

3. **Decision Layer:** Uncertainty-aware action policy using greedy receding-horizon optimization. Selects next waypoint/trajectory segment to maximize expected uncertainty reduction in boundary-layer map, subject to flight-time, energy, and safety constraints.

4. **Control Layer:** Flight planner and controller that executes policy decisions. Incorporates model predictive control (MPC) to optimize trajectories while maintaining adherence to operational constraints.

### Three Primary Tasks

**Task 1: Real-Time Probabilistic Estimation of ABL Structure**
- Develop hybrid digital twin assimilating streaming observations of temperature
- Model latent atmospheric state **z** that enables decoder to generate full vertical temperature profiles
- State-space model: latent state evolves under dynamics; observations are samples from profiles generated by latent state at measurement location/time
- Compare interpretable physics-informed latent model vs. expressive AI-enhanced latent representation
- Employ sequential Bayesian filtering for online posterior updates
- Extract boundary-layer estimates (PBLH) from posterior samples of decoded profiles
- **Target metrics:** ≥10% improvement over baseline in estimation accuracy, uncertainty calibration, or real-time latency

**Task 2: Constraint-Aware Adaptive Sensing and Path Planning**
- Define local region, forecast horizon, and discrete set of trajectory primitives
- Score candidate trajectories using pre-posterior analysis from Task 1 posterior samples
- Estimate updated posterior under each candidate action and quantify uncertainty reduction in boundary-layer map
- Enforce flight-time, energy, and safety constraints through construction of feasible trajectory subsets
- **Target metrics:** ≥15% improvement in uncertainty reduction per unit time vs. non-adaptive strategies

**Task 3: Closed-Loop Integration and Demonstration**
- Integrate all layers into autonomous closed-loop system
- Evaluate against non-adaptive baselines (raster scans, fixed vertical profiling)
- Primary Phase I demonstration in simulation and/or hardware-in-the-loop
- **Evaluation metrics:** Prediction accuracy of boundary-layer map, uncertainty reduction per unit time, resource efficiency at fixed budget
- **AI Advantage Metrics:**
  - Time-to-solution: ≥20% reduction in time to reach target RMSE/posterior interval threshold
  - Resource-to-solution: ≥20% reduction in flight minutes, samples, distance, or energy for target threshold
  - Accuracy-at-budget: ≥10% improvement in RMSE, MAE, or calibration score at same flight budget
  - Empirical resource-performance scaling law

- **Target phenomena:** Planetary boundary layer height (PBLH), boundary layer transitions, cloud edges, aerosol plumes, regions of strong thermodynamic gradients
- **Scientific value:** Resolve processes such as entrainment at boundary layer top, plume dispersion/dilution, coupling between thermodynamics and cloud formation
- **Applications:** Weather forecasting, air quality prediction, transport/dispersion modeling, environmental hazard response

## Key Results / Milestones (9-Month Phase I)

### Task 1: Real-Time Probabilistic Estimation
- **Months 1-2 (Milestone 1):** Curated benchmark dataset and baseline estimator established
  - Assemble and quality-control historical UAS, radiosonde, and meteorological datasets
  - Define held-out benchmark for PBLH estimation
  - Implement baseline estimator from vertical temperature profiles
  - Success: Benchmark completed, baseline produces reproducible PBLH estimates

- **Months 2-4 (Milestone 2):** Compact latent-state estimator developed
  - Low-dimensional latent-state model mapping sparse temperature to posterior estimates of local profiles and PBLH
  - Success: ≥10% improvement in held-out PBLH estimation accuracy/uncertainty calibration (RMSE, MAE, or calibration score)

- **Months 4-6 (Milestone 3):** Online sequential inference demonstrated
  - Streaming/sequential Bayesian updating for latent state using simulated or replayed flight measurements
  - Success: Posterior updates at latency <5 seconds per cycle, maintaining acceptable accuracy and calibrated uncertainty

### Task 2: Constraint-Aware Adaptive Sensing
- **Months 3-5 (Milestone 4):** Simulation testbed for adaptive sensing established
  - Define feasible waypoint/trajectory primitives, operational constraints, evaluation scenarios
  - Success: Planner evaluates candidate actions and produces feasible next-step recommendations under constraints

- **Months 5-6 (Milestone 5):** Prototype uncertainty-aware planner outperforms non-adaptive baselines
  - Score candidate trajectories by expected uncertainty reduction in boundary-layer map
  - Success: ≥15% improvement in uncertainty reduction per unit time vs. raster scan or fixed vertical profiling

### Task 3: Closed-Loop Integration
- **Months 6-8 (Milestone 6):** Closed-loop software stack integrated
  - Integrate latent-state estimation, uncertainty-aware planning, vehicle/control interfaces
  - Success: End-to-end execution in simulation/hardware-in-the-loop for ≥10 consecutive closed-loop cycles without manual intervention

- **Months 8-9 (Milestone 7):** Phase I end-to-end demonstration
  - Evaluate integrated system against non-adaptive strategies on representative scenarios
  - Success: Demonstrate quantitative AI advantage in uncertainty reduction rate, map accuracy, or sampling efficiency

## Decision Gate Metrics

### Feasibility & Usefulness Metrics
1. **Estimation feasibility:** Task 1 inference pipeline recovers PBLH from sparse observations with ≥10% improvement over baseline on held-out data
2. **Uncertainty usefulness:** Posterior uncertainty is well-calibrated and informative; uncertainty-aware policies outperform uncertainty-agnostic variants
3. **Real-time feasibility:** Task 2 online updates and candidate-action scoring completed within 5-second time budget

### AI Advantage Metrics (vs. non-adaptive open-loop baselines)
1. **Time-to-solution:** ≥20% reduction in time from mission start to first estimate meeting RMSE/posterior interval threshold
2. **Resource-to-solution:** ≥20% reduction in flight minutes, number of samples, distance traveled, or energy needed
3. **Accuracy-at-budget:** ≥10% improvement in RMSE, MAE, or uncertainty-calibration score when both methods given same flight budget
4. **Empirical resource-performance scaling law:** Characterize boundary-layer estimation error as function of sensing budget, offline training data, and planning compute

### Research Vision for ASCR Audience
Proposal emphasizes that methodological novelty lies in **scientific state compression**—replacing high-dimensional online field estimation with posterior inference over a learned, physically-structured reduced state whose dynamics are simple enough to support real-time filtering and informative control. This positions the work as advancing computational and algorithmic capabilities for real-time perception, planning, and manipulation under uncertainty—core to ASCR's focus on advanced robotics for dynamic environments.

### Competitive Advantages
- **Physics-informed AI:** Hybrid approach combining domain knowledge with data-driven flexibility, enabling real-time operation and interpretability
- **Tight data-decision coupling:** Closed-loop integration of measurement and inference supports transformative discovery paradigm
- **Multi-agent foundation:** Architecture generalizable to coordinated fleets of autonomous platforms
- **Quantifiable AI advantage:** Explicit comparison against non-adaptive baselines with well-defined scaling metrics
