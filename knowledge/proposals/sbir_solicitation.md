## Executive Summary
This SBIR solicitation calls for the development of a small, low-cost unmanned aircraft system (UAS) designed specifically for atmospheric sampling with high autonomy and ease of operation. The system must measure wind velocity, pressure, temperature, and humidity while being capable of autonomous flight, rapid deployment by a single operator, and accommodation of additional sensor payloads.

## Technical Approach

**Phase I - Design and Requirements Development:**
- Develop conceptual models, functional requirements, and component architectures
- Select or design airframe with capability for:
  - Fully autonomous flight including take-off and landing
  - Rapid assembly and deployment by single operator
  - Ruggedized construction for unimproved surfaces
- Design avionics system including:
  - Atmospheric sensor suite selection
  - Autopilot system interfaces
  - Multi-aircraft user interface for mission execution and monitoring
  - Autonomous sampling algorithms
- Develop test plan to experimentally validate sensing systems

**Phase II - Prototype Development and Validation:**
- Build and demonstrate functional prototype
- Implement and test autonomous sampling algorithms
- Gain airspace access and conduct atmospheric data validation experiments

**Phase III - Commercialization:**
- Prepare software, hardware, and documentation for commercial release

## Key Parameters & Requirements
- **Primary measurements:** Wind velocity, pressure, temperature, humidity
- **Operational requirements:** Low cost, easy to operate, high autonomy
- **Deployment:** Single operator capable, rapid assembly
- **Airframe:** Ruggedized for unimproved surfaces, autonomous capable
- **Interface:** Multi-aircraft simultaneous control capability
- **Payload flexibility:** Designed to accommodate additional sensors (particulate detection/classification)

## Use Cases & Applications
- Atmospheric sampling and meteorological measurements
- Wind velocity profiling
- Environmental monitoring with expandable sensor suites
