## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is executing a Navy SBIR Phase I contract to develop a Sonobuoy-Launched Unmanned Aircraft System (SL UAS) integrating magnetic anomaly detection (MAD) and passive acoustic sensing capabilities for underwater target detection and classification. The effort focuses on demonstrating sensor integration feasibility and platform compatibility with sonobuoy launch constraints.

## Technical Approach

### Phase I Objectives:

**Objective 1: Demonstrate Sensor Integration Feasibility**
- Evaluate noise mitigation strategies to minimize platform-induced interference
- Ensure hydrophone meets Navy sensitivity, frequency range, and depth requirements

**Objective 2: Develop Platform Compatible with Sonobuoy Launch Constraints**
- Modify existing S0 UAS platform to accommodate magnetometer and acoustic sensor payload volume and weight constraints

**Objective 3: Validate Sensor Performance in Controlled Environments**
- Conduct laboratory simulations and bench testing
- Characterize magnetometer noise performance
- Characterize hydrophone acoustic sensitivity

**Objective 4: Develop and Evaluate Data Fusion Algorithms**
- Design, implement, and test algorithms combining MAD and acoustic sensor outputs
- Enhance underwater target detection and classification capability

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS Platform
- **What it is:** Existing unmanned aircraft system operated by BST
- **Application in this project:** Platform being modified to serve as a sonobuoy-launched delivery system
- **Key constraint:** Must accommodate magnetometer and hydrophone payload within sonobuoy launch constraints (SLC) for volume and weight
- **Technical challenge:** Noise mitigation required to prevent platform-induced interference with sensitive sensors

### Sensor Suite
- **Magnetic Anomaly Detection (MAD) Sensor:** Submarine/underwater target detection capability
- **Passive Acoustic Hydrophone:** Underwater acoustic detection and classification
- **Data Fusion Algorithms:** Integration layer combining both sensor streams for enhanced detection/classification
