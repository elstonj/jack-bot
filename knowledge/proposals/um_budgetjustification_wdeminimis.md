## Executive Summary
This budget justification supports Black Swift Technologies as a subcontractor to the University of Miami for a NOAA-funded project to advance UAS capabilities for sampling tropical cyclones from ocean surface to stratosphere. The three-year project budgets $327,952 total in labor, fringe, and indirect costs.

## Budget Summary
- **Total Contract Value:** $327,952 over 3-year Period of Performance
- **Labor (A/B):** $118,917
- **Fringe Benefits:** $23,139 (24.16% rate)
- **Indirect Costs:** $38,351 (Overhead 15%, G&A 15%)
- **Equipment, Travel, Other Direct Costs:** $0 (N/A)

## Cost Structure Details

### Labor Rates
- Based on current salaries, competitive for education/experience and local market
- 3% cost-of-living increase factored into Years 2 and 3

### Fringe Benefits (24.16% rate)
Components include:
- Employer-paid health insurance
- Life and AD&D insurance
- Paid time off and sick time
- 401(k) matching retirement plan
- State and Federal worker's compensation and unemployment insurance

### Indirect Costs (Year 1 fully invoiced; Years 2-3 itemized)
- **Overhead:** 15% of (Total Direct Labor + Fringe) base
- **G&A:** 15% of Total Direct Costs base
- **Year 2:** $12,366 (Overhead) + $14,221 (G&A) = $26,587
- **Year 3:** $5,471 (Overhead) + $6,292 (G&A) = $11,763

## Technical Context
The project focuses on advancing UAS capabilities to sample tropical cyclones across altitude ranges from ocean surface to stratosphere, supporting improved NOAA forecasting. BST's role involves design and system engineering modifications, though specific systems and technical approaches are not detailed in this budget document.
