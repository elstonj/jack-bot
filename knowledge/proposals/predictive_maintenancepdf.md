# Predictive Maintenance for UAS

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** SBIR Phase II Proposal - Supporting Material
- **Client/Agency:** U.S. Air Force (USAF)
- **Program/Solicitation:** 2019.2 SBIR - Atmospheric Sounding topic
- **Date:** Submitted 2019 (document modified June 2, 2021)
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** On-Board Augmented Intelligence (avionics subsystem with machine learning algorithms)
- **Key Personnel:** Jack Elston PhD (CEO), Bill Nickerson

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes development of a machine learning-based anomaly and fault detection system for UAS that predicts system failures before they occur. The On-Board Augmented Intelligence subsystem continuously monitors for common flight hazards (icing, propeller damage, wireless communications failure) to enable early warning and diagnostics of critical failures, reducing USAF mission failure risk and improving UAS operational viability in the National Airspace System (NAS).

## Technical Approach
- **Core Technology:** Machine learning-based avionics subsystem integrated with fault detection algorithms
- **Continuous Monitoring:** Real-time detection of specific fault modes including:
  - Icing conditions
  - Propeller damage
  - Wireless communications failures
- **Automation Advantage:** Automates detection of common flight hazards, reducing operator experience requirements from hundreds of hours to enable equivalent expert-level diagnostics during flight
- **Predictive Capability:** Alerts users to faults requiring maintenance or operational envelope restrictions before critical failures occur

## Products & Capabilities Described

**On-Board Augmented Intelligence**
- Highly capable avionics subsystem with integrated machine learning algorithms
- Provides intelligent fault detection and early warning capability
- Enables real-time diagnostics during flight operations
- Automates detection of three primary fault categories: icing, propeller damage, communications failure

## Use Cases & Applications
- **USAF Drone Operations:** Early warning and diagnostics of potential critical system failures on USAF UAS
- **Beyond-Line-of-Sight Operations:** Enables safe BLOS flights through improved system reliability
- **Autonomous Operations:** Supports fully autonomous operations without direct human oversight
- **Operations Over Populated Areas:** Increases safety viability for UAS operations in populated areas
- **NAS Integration:** Improves overall reliability and robustness required for ubiquitous UAS use in National Airspace System

## Key Problem Addressed
Document cites statistic that UAS failure rates significantly exceed manned aircraft accident rates, creating mission failure risk. USAF investment in preventative maintenance technology is critical to address this gap.

## Notable Details
- **Competitive Context:** Proposal positions this as one of several required technology advancements needed alongside collision avoidance, GPS-denied navigation, and system reliability improvements for ubiquitous UAS adoption
- **Operational Impact:** Technology removes requirement for hundreds of hours of operator experience to detect equivalent faults, democratizing expert-level diagnostics
- **Safety & Mission Assurance:** Dual benefit of reducing mission failure risk while increasing safety for operations in contested airspace and populated areas
