# X224-OCSO1 Phase II RFP - Air Force SBIR Soil Moisture Mapping

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Request for Proposal (RFP) / Solicitation Guidance
- **Client/Agency:** Department of the Air Force (DAF), AFWERX
- **Program/Solicitation:** SBIR Phase II Topic AFX224-OCSO1 (Soil Moisture Mapping); based on successful Phase I completion
- **Date:** April 5, 2023
- **Submission Deadline:** May 4, 2023 at 12:00 PM ET
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None explicitly mentioned in this document
- **Key Personnel:** Daniel J. Brewer (Air Force SBIR/STTR Contracting Officer, AFRL/RGK AFWERX)

## Executive Summary
This is a Phase II SBIR RFP issued by the Air Force to companies that successfully completed Phase I awards under topic AFX224-OCSO1 (Phase I Open Call for Innovative Defense-Related Dual-Purpose Technologies/Solutions). Companies are invited to submit Phase II proposals for continued development of their technologies, with a maximum funding amount of $1,250,000 over 21 months, evaluated on commercialization potential, defense need relevance, and technical merit.

## Document Purpose & Scope
This is a comprehensive RFP guidance document outlining submission requirements, evaluation criteria, and administrative procedures for Phase II proposals. It is **not** a technical proposal but rather the solicitation framework and instructions for applicants.

## Key RFP Parameters

### Funding & Duration
- **Maximum SBIR Funding:** $1,250,000
- **Maximum Performance Period:** 21 months
- **Minimum Work Requirement:** At least 50% of research/analytical effort must be performed by awardee (measured by direct and indirect costs, excluding profit)

### Technical Requirements
- **Technical Volume Format:** White Paper (15-page limit, single PDF)
- **Page Limit:** Strictly 15 pages; pages in excess will not be reviewed
- **Evaluation Basis:** Peer review by engineers/scientists; each proposal judged on its own merits

## Evaluation Criteria (Equal Importance)

### Criteria A: Commercialization Potential
- Small business's record of commercializing SBIR or other research (pre-sales, pilots, sales, revenue, active users, subscriptions, downloads, traction/adoption)
- Phase II follow-on commitments and funding commitments from Government or private sources
- Sound transition strategy documented in Air Force Customer Memorandum

### Criteria B: Defense Need Appropriateness & Relevance
- Complete and compelling Air Force Customer Memorandum (signed by appropriate end-user/customer) required
- Adequacy of proposed effort relative to fulfilling identified Defense Need
- Evidence of previous investment (basic research grants, etc.) supporting the proposed effort

### Criteria C: Technical Soundness & Innovation
- Soundness, technical merit, and innovation of proposed approach
- Differentiation from current customer alternatives
- Incremental progress toward fulfilling identified Defense need
- Qualifications of proposed Principal Investigators/Project Managers, supporting staff, and consultants

**Note:** Technical and merit criteria combined are significantly more important than cost/price.

## Proposal Content Requirements

### Volume 1: Proposal Cover Sheet
- CAGE Code, EID, certification questions, business information
- **Technical Abstract:** Non-proprietary; may be publicly released by OSD
- **Anticipated Benefits/Anticipated Applications:** Must follow specific format: "We solved [DAF problem] by providing [advantage], to help [DAF end-user organization] accomplish [goal]. We will recognize profit by charging [non-Defense commercial customers] to get [non-Defense commercial benefit]."
- **Keywords:** Maximum 8 keywords/phrases describing project (used for categorization and Phase III transition partner identification)

### Volume 2: Technical Volume (15 pages maximum)
**Required Sections:**
1. **Table of Contents** (does not count against 15-page limit)
2. **Glossary of Acronyms/Abbreviations** (does not count against limit)
3. **Technical Summary** including:
   - Identification and significance of problem/opportunity
   - Air Force or Space Force Technology Strategic Capabilities alignment
   - Non-Defense commercial solution description and specifications
   - Proposed adaptation of commercial solution to meet Government end-user need
   - Compliance and regulatory activities (flight testing, hardware delivery, Government personnel/facilities use, weapons/munitions testing, explosives, hazardous materials, animal testing, human subject research, toxins, human data)

4. **Phase II Technical Objectives and Key Results** (3-5 objectives)
   - Tied to specific proposed Phase II trial tasks
   - Qualitative and specific to participating DAF end-user(s)
   - Time-bound, tangible, unambiguous regarding anticipated results
   - Each accompanied by 3-5 specific, measurable key results
   - List required stakeholders needed to accomplish Phase II

5. **Phase II Work Plan** (see Table 5 outline below)
6. **Commercialization Strategy** (see Table 6 outline below)
7. **Military Applications/DoD Customers:** Existing potential military requirement, DoD agency/organization likely to become customer, Phase III funding commitments
8. **Non-US Citizens:** Identification of all non-U.S. citizens/dual citizens with countries of origin, visa/work permit types, involvement level
9. **Key Personnel:** Qualifications for RDT&E and commercialization, education, experience, citizenship; technical resume for PI with publications
10. **Non-Defense Commercial Customers:** Past revenue from commercial customers; potential revenue-generating projects
11. **Investors and Partners:** Company investors, shareholders, strategic advisors; their experience/track record
12. **Related Work:** Significant related activities, previous programs, consultant involvement, state-of-the-art reviewer comments, related Government efforts

### Phase II Work Plan Outline (Non-Proprietary)
**Mandatory Features:**
- Work stated in terms of outcomes/results, not performance methods
- Measurable performance standards with assessment methods

**Work Plan Components:**
1. **Scope:** Major requirements and specifications
2. **Task Outline:** Work to be accomplished; clearly identify SBIR-funded vs. Government/private-funded tasks
3. **Milestone Schedule:** From signed Customer Memorandum; each milestone marks completion of required deliverable (prototype, test plan, final report, etc.)
4. **Deliverables:** Specific sample/prototype hardware/software to be delivered; data deliverables, schedules, quantities; Unique Item Identification (IAW DFARS 252.211-7003) may be required

**Required Reports (ALL Phase II awards):**
- **Final Report:** Draft due 30 days after technical effort completion; includes single-page project summary (may be published, no proprietary info), project objectives met, work completed, results obtained, technical feasibility estimates
- **Status Reports:** Quarterly minimum; concise (≤15 slides); describe progress toward selected DAF end-user needs; include quantitative measure (0-1 scale) of progress toward each objective/key result
- **Phase II Summary Report:** At technical effort completion; ≤700 words; description of technology and anticipated applications/benefits

**Safety-Related Deliverables:**
- Preliminary Hazard Analysis (if hardware developed; IAW MIL STD-882E requirements)
- Hazardous Material Report (if hazardous materials used): materials identification, purpose, alternatives/safeguards

**Additional Reporting:**
- Software documentation and user manuals
- Engineering drawings
- Operation and Maintenance documentation
- Updated commercialization results

### Commercialization Plan (Included in Volume 2)
**Required Components:**
1. **Company Information:** Focused objectives/core competencies, specialization area(s), products with significant sales, history of Federal/non-Federal funding, regulatory experience, commercialization results
2. **Customer and Competition:** Key technology objectives, current competition, advantages vs. competitors, hurdles to acceptance
3. **Market:** Milestones, target dates, market size analysis, estimated market share (Year 1 and Year 5), plan to obtain market share
4. **Intellectual Property:** Patent status, technology lead, trade secrets, plan for competitive advantage
5. **Financing:** Plans for securing further funding
6. **Assistance and Mentoring:** Plans for technical/business assistance (mentoring, partnering, State programs, Small Business Development Centers, FFRDCs, Manufacturing Extension Partnership, DAF Mentor-Protégé Program)

**Specific Questions to Address:**
- First product into which technology will be incorporated
- Customers and estimated market size
- Funding needed to bring to market and how it will be raised
- Marketing expertise possessed or to be obtained
- Competitor companies and firm's price/quality advantage
- Private sector/non-SBIR funding commitments (venture capital, angel investors, state/local programs, internal funding)
- Foreign investment sources and amounts
- Phase III (Government or commercial) follow-on commitments
- Other commercial potential indicators (pre-sales, pilots, sales, revenue, active users, subscriptions, downloads, traction, commercial signals)
- Last 12 months' total revenue from non-Defense commercial solution sales
- Phase II anticipated end results and plans to transition to Phase III with potential Government customer

### Volume 3: Cost Volume
- Submitted via online DSIP form
- **Direct Labor Costs:** Name of key personnel if possible or labor category; number of hours, labor overhead/fringe benefits, hourly rates
- **Direct Material Costs:** Itemized list (types, quantities, price, purpose); vendor quotes, historical costs as justification
- **Other Direct Costs:** Specialized services (machining, milling, testing, analysis), temporary equipment use
  - **Travel:** Must relate to project needs; break out by trip, travelers, airfare, per diem, lodging; recommend budgeting at least one trip to managing Air Force location
  - **Subcontracts:** Include subcontractor/consultant costs; proposed total of all consultant fees, facility leases, and subcontract/purchase agreements may not exceed one-half total contract price/cost (unless otherwise approved in writing by CO); support with copies of agreements
  - **Consultants:** Separate agreement letter for each; brief statement of services, hours required, hourly rate
  - **Special Tooling/Test Equipment/Material:** Carefully reviewed for need/appropriateness; title to Government-furnished property vests with AF unless title transfer deemed more cost-effective

**Measurable Milestones:** Fixed-price payments tied to measurable milestones agreed with Government
