# DARPA ERIS Master Program Solicitation

## Document Metadata
- Type: Program Solicitation / Open Call Announcement
- Client/Agency: DARPA (Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency), in conjunction with The Applied Research Institute (ARI) as marketplace manager
- Program/Solicitation: ERIS (Expedited Research Implementation Series)
- Date: December 1, 2024 (R3 Updated February 1, 2025)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None mentioned in this document
- Key Personnel: Not applicable (this is a program announcement, not a BST submission)

## Executive Summary
The DARPA ERIS is an open, continuous call for innovative technology solutions from U.S. and foreign entities (companies, academia, individuals) seeking to advance disruptive research and development relevant to national defense. ERIS provides a marketplace where vetted "awardable" solutions can be rapidly procured through Other Transaction Authority pathways, with submissions assessed monthly and rated as either "awardable" or "non-awardable" based on technical merit.

## Program Structure & Key Features

### Purpose
- Establish centralized location for DARPA to assess disruptive technological solutions
- Provide low-barrier entry point for industry, academia, and nontraditional defense contractors
- Enable rapid acquisition pathways from idea → research → prototype → test & evaluation

### Eligibility
- All U.S.-based companies (large/small, traditional/nontraditional), universities, nonprofits, and individuals
- Foreign universities, research institutions, and entities may participate if compliant with export laws
- No restrictions on number of submissions per entity

### Benefits of Participation
- Direct exposure to DARPA program offices
- Fast-track assessment (results within 30 calendar days of collection period end)
- Streamlined submission process
- Fully negotiable award terms (payment, IP, data rights, schedule, pricing)
- Potential for multiple awards from single submission
- No price ceilings or duration limits
- Feedback and resubmission opportunities

## Topic Areas
ERIS seeks solutions in four primary areas:
1. **Detecting and tracking elusive objects** across air, land, and space domains
2. **Overcoming sensing system limitations** (size, weight, power, cost/SWaP-C, performance)
3. **Advanced antenna technologies** for improved performance and reduced size
4. **Resilience, efficiency, effectiveness of strategic systems** (critical infrastructure, military command-and-control across strategic, operational, tactical edges)

## Submission Process

### Timeline & Deadlines
- **Open Period**: December 9, 2024 – May 30, 2025
- **Submission Cycles**: Monthly collections (e.g., Dec 1-31, Jan 1-31, etc.)
- **Cutoff**: Last day of month at 12:00 PM ET (Noon)
- **Assessment Timeline**: Results provided within 30 days of collection period end
- **Compliance Check**: Typically within 1 business day of submission

### Submission Format Requirements

**Video Specifications:**
- Maximum length: 7 minutes (exceeding 7:00 is primary rejection reason)
- Resolution: HD 1280x720 minimum
- Format: .mp4 file, under 1 GB
- Color space: Rec709 or sRGB
- No restrictive markings (except valid copyright notice)
- Must be unclassified, non-proprietary, no export-controlled or CUI information

**Submission Form Requirements:**
1. Legal entity/individual name (matching SAM.gov if applicable)
2. Website
3. Unique Entity ID (UEI) from SAM.gov (if available; "NA" if not)
4. Business size (small/large per SBA 13 CFR Part 121)
5. Nontraditional/traditional status per 10 USC 3014
6. Socioeconomic designations (must match SAM.gov)
7. Country of origin
8. Principal place of business address
9. Topic area selection
10. Technology Readiness Level (TRL) - specific level, not range
11. Minimum 5 relevant keywords (comma-separated)
12. Video POC name, email, phone
13. Submission title (less than 128 characters)
14. Abstract description (up to 1,500 characters)
15. ERIS Supplemental Slides (Appendix B format required)

### Supplemental Slides (4 required slides, Appendix B format)
1. **ERIS Submission Information** – Submission and company details
2. **Company Introduction** – One-slide overview of company/individual and motivation to engage DARPA
3. **ERIS Submission Criteria Quad Chart** – Summary of four video content criteria
4. **Solution Overview & DARPA White Space Chart** – What you're doing, how, with custom metrics showing impact

### Video Content Requirements (4 Required Elements)
1. **Problem Definition & Current State of Art** – Clearly define problem scope and existing solutions/limitations
2. **Advancing State of Art** – Explain what's "new" in approach vs. current practices; identify barriers
3. **Team Capability** – Demonstrate unique vision, expertise, experience; explain why team is best suited
4. **Defense/Commercial Market Use Case/Impact** – Identify specific applications and "so what" value proposition

### Submission Portal
- All submissions through DARPAConnect.us/ERIS
- No physical/hard copy submissions accepted
- "Create Submission" workflow

## Compliance Review Process

ERIS internal team conducts compliance checks including:
1. Video length verification (max 7:00)
2. Video playback and audio quality
3. Graphics/charts legibility
4. Prohibited markings check
5. Video format/resolution/color space compliance
6. Required submission criteria presence
7. Submission form completeness and accuracy
8. Business size and nontraditional status verification (via SAM.gov)
9. ERIS topic area fit verification
10. TRL consistency check
11. Supplemental Slides format compliance (Appendix B)

**Outcome:** Compliant submissions placed in assessment queue; non-compliant submissions returned for correction with notification by email.

## Assessment & Scoring Process

### Assessment Panel
- Peer panel of government, industry, and academic SMEs
- Final decisions by Government Assessment Authority
- Three-member minimum peer panels (government, industry, academic representation)
- Non-government SMEs operate under NDAs

### Scoring & Rating
- Assessed against published rubric (Appendix C)
- Rated as "awardable" or "non-awardable" for given assessment round
- Written initial assessment by panel
- Recommendations presented monthly to Government Selecting Official
- Scoring and assessor comments provided for both awardable and non-awardable submissions

### Contact During Assessment
- Government/SMEs may contact submitters to clarify information

## Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI) Mitigations

**OCI Identified: SMEs evaluating own solutions**
- Mitigation: Exclude assessors from evaluating own solutions or competitor solutions where assessor holds financial interest
- All assessors required to certify recognition of prohibition
- Government ethics training required
- NDAs required

**OCI Identified: SMEs evaluating competitor solutions**
- Lesser risk due to evaluation characteristics (non-proprietary submissions, published rubric, merit-based assessment, multi-member panels, government decision authority, viability vs. requirement-specific assessment)
- Mitigations: Government monitoring of assessor scoring consistency; ethics training; NDAs

## Resubmission Process

- Non-awardable solutions may be resubmitted at any time while announcement active
- Feedback provided: Monthly rating scorecard with assessor comments and improvement suggestions
- Weekly feedback sessions available (ERIS office hours) to review assessor comments and develop resubmission strategy
- Resubmission strongly recommended before second attempt
- Mark as "ERIS Resubmission" in submission workflow (not for technical deficiency corrections, only peer-assessed non-awardable submissions)

## Important Provisions

- All submission preparation costs borne by submitter (no government reimbursement)
- "Awardable" status does not guarantee award
- Company list to be published on DARPAConnect.us (name, solution, focus area) unless written objection sent to ERIS@darpa.mil
- Multiple submissions per entity permitted
- Submitter retains ownership of video and submission data; may request removal at any time
- Disputes regarding scoring addressed to Government Selecting Official
- SME assessor names/contact info not provided

## Data Handling & Government Licenses

By participating, submitters grant the government the following non-exclusive, fully paid-up, non-assignable, non-royalty-bearing licenses:

1. **Assessment & Market Research License** – Government and covered support contractors may view, review, research, comment, share for assessments, market research, requirements development, procurement consideration/planning, negotiation, award (active while video in ERIS)

2. **Perpetual Improvement License** – Government/support contractors may view, review, research, share for improving submission/assessment/selection processes, criteria, scoring, or UI/UX (perpetual, whether awardable or non-awardable)

3. **Transcription License** – Government/support contractors may transcribe videos for search functionality, accessibility, solution marketing, government reporting, market research, requirements development (perpetual license in transcription data itself)

4. **Training License** – Government/support contractors may use videos and submission data for ERIS-related training (planning, implementation, execution, administration, processes, criteria, scoring, feedback, awards)

## Notable Details

- This is a **continuously open solicitation** designed to be a standing marketplace rather than a traditional one-time competition
- **Low barrier to entry** is intentional design; emphasis on streamlined, simplified submission vs. traditional proposals
- **Technology Readiness Level (TRL)** specified by submitter to indicate maturity level; NOT used in awardability determination, but helps match solutions to customer needs
- Assessment against **published rubric** promotes transparency and consistency
- **Other Transaction Authority** (10 USC 4021, 4022, 4023) enables "post-competitive" rapid procurement pathways
- Solutions must be **unclassified and non-proprietary** to be viewable across government
- Resubmission feedback is structured and supportive (office hours, scorer comments)
- Document emphasizes **nontraditional defense contractors** and small businesses as key target audience
- **Foreign participation allowed** with export compliance requirement
