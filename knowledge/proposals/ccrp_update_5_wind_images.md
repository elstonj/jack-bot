## Executive Summary
This presentation documents wind field imaging and simulated trajectory data for an autonomous aircraft system (Eyewall Controller) designed to navigate and sample hurricane structures. The materials show typical meteorological profiles within hurricanes and demonstrate simulated flight paths through three sequential turns within the hurricane eyewall.

## Technical Approach
The Eyewall Controller system is designed to autonomously navigate through hurricane structures while collecting meteorological data. The presentation demonstrates:
- Simulated flight trajectories through hurricane eyewall regions across three sequential maneuvers
- Integration of wind speed, atmospheric pressure, and reflectivity data to inform autonomous navigation decisions
- Time-segmented trajectory planning (Turn 1: 0-49 min; Turn 2: 49-89 min; Turn 3: 89-127 min)

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Eyewall Controller**
- Autonomous flight control system for hurricane sampling missions
- Capable of navigating complex wind fields and pressure gradients within hurricane structures
- Uses real-time meteorological data (wind speed, pressure, reflectivity) to inform navigation decisions
- Demonstrated capability for multi-turn trajectories within hurricane eyewalls

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Hurricane sampling and reconnaissance**: Autonomous in-situ measurement of hurricane structure, wind fields, and precipitation
- **Meteorological data collection**: Profiling wind speed, atmospheric pressure, and reflectivity across hurricane diameters
- **Hazardous weather operations**: Designed to operate in extreme conditions unsuitable for manned aircraft
