## Executive Summary
This is a Department of Navy guidance document explaining how contractors on DoN SBIR/STTR contracts must mark and handle Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) in all deliverables, proposals, and correspondence. It outlines the mandatory CUI designation indicator block format, marking requirements for different document types (Word, PowerPoint, Excel, email), and procedures for determining applicable CUI categories.

## Key CUI Marking Requirements

### Cover Page (Page 1)
- Must include "CUI" acronym in header and footer
- Must include CUI Designation Indicator Block with five components:
  1. "Controlled by: Department of Navy (DON)"
  2. "Controlled by: <SYSCOM>" (Systems Command from contract)
  3. "CUI Category: <category>" (see categories below)
  4. "Distribution/Dissemination Control: DISTRO <B-F>"
  5. "POC: <TPOC Name or COR, Phone or email>"

### Subsequent Pages
- Must include "CUI" acronym in header and footer
- Do not require full CUI Designation Indicator Block

### CUI Categories (Applicable to DoN SBIR/STTR)
- **CTI (Controlled Technical Information)**: Technical information with military or space application subject to access/use/dissemination controls; meets criteria for distribution statements B-F
- **EXPTR (Export Controlled Research)**: Related to systematic investigation, study of materials to establish facts and reach conclusions
- **SBIZ (Small Business Research and Technology)**: Information related to SBIR/STTR programs in government databases (15 USC 638(k)(2))
- **PROPIN (General Proprietary Business Information)**: Company products, business, activities, financial information, trade secrets, R&D, product designs and specifications

**Note:** Maximum three CUI categories per document recommended; CTI is noted as a common DoN SBIR/STTR marking.

### Document Type-Specific Guidance
- **Word Documents**: Include full CUI block on page 1; "CUI" in header/footer on all pages
- **PowerPoint**: Include CUI block on cover slide; "CUI" on all slides
- **Excel**: Label each tab with "CUI" + tab name; include CUI block on first tab only
- **Email**: Include "[Contains CUI]" in subject line; include CUI block in email body; carry forward markings when forwarding

## Notable Details
- CUI must be submitted through the Navy's SBIR/STTR deliverable website, which is appropriate for CUI transmissions
- Document states it is not a substitute for reading full CUI policy guidance (DoDI 5200.48) or FAR/DFARS included in contracts
- Based on DoD Instruction 5200.48 (effective March 6, 2020) and DoD Manual 5200.01
- Determination of whether information requires CUI marking follows a three-step process documented in the guidance
- No individual may access CUI without an authorized, lawful government purpose
