## Executive Summary
This is a brief technical update documenting modifications to the camera mounting scheme for the MALIBU project. BST received sensor hardware from NASA GSFC and redesigned the internal mounting configuration to accommodate a new camera orientation, while maintaining proper center of gravity and ensuring all components fit within the aircraft fuselage.

## Technical Approach
- Received detailed sensor/camera units from NASA GSFC
- Modified original mounting plan due to change in camera orientation (cameras rotated from forward-aligned to 26° offset from center axis)
- Designed new 3D mounting models accounting for:
  - 26° angle orientation of cameras relative to fuselage centerline
  - Clearance for wire bending on aircraft back/aft section
  - Connector plug geometry (modeled as simple extrusions)
  - Center of gravity (CG) positioning requirements
- Provided adjustable range for camera positioning to enable CG tuning

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Aircraft Platform (unnamed):**
- Carries camera sensor payload from NASA GSFC
- Internal volume sufficient for multiple cameras and wiring
- Capable of CG adjustment through variable component positioning

## Notable Details
- Original plan called for cameras aligned lengthwise with fuselage forward direction; this was changed
- The new 26° orientation still allows successful integration with adequate clearance margins
- Mounting design provides flexibility ("play in location") to adjust camera position for achieving target center of gravity
- Document includes 5 technical figures showing 3D models, internal layout, and orientation views (front and top perspectives showing maximum aft and forward positioning ranges)
