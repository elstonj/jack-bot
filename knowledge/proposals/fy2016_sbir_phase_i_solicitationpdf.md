## Executive Summary
This is the official FY2016 NOAA SBIR Program solicitation document outlining the Phase I, Phase II, and Phase III structure for federally-funded R&D projects. It establishes eligibility requirements, proposal submission guidelines, evaluation criteria, and funding parameters for small business concerns seeking innovation research contracts with NOAA.

## Technical Approach
This document describes a three-phase federal funding model rather than proposing a specific technical approach:

**Phase I – Feasibility Research**
- Purpose: Determine scientific, technical, and commercial merit and feasibility
- Funding: Up to $120,000
- Duration: Up to 6 months
- Requirement: 2/3 of research/analytical effort must be performed by awardee
- Focus: Proving feasibility as prerequisite for Phase II

**Phase II – Research and Development**
- Purpose: Prototype development and comprehensive R&D
- Funding: Up to $400,000
- Duration: Up to 24 months
- Requirement: 1/2 of research/analytical effort must be performed by awardee
- Requirements: Detailed commercialization plan, possible presentation to NOAA SBIR Selection Committee

**Phase III – Commercialization**
- Purpose: Move prototype to viable product/service
- Funding: Non-SBIR sources (private sector, other government sources)
- Scope: Product development, production, services, or combination thereof

## Notable Details

### Eligibility Requirements
- Small Business Concern (SBC) status required: ≤500 employees
- Principal Investigator (PI) must spend >50% time as employee of awardee
- Work must be performed in the United States (waiver possible for rare/unique circumstances)
- Minimum Phase I-to-Phase II transition rate of 0.25 for companies with >20 Phase I awards in past 5 years
- Cannot be majority-owned by venture capital operating companies, hedge funds, or private equity firms

### Compliance & Certifications
- Requires SBIR Funding Agreement Certification at award and during life cycle
- Human subjects research must comply with Common Rule (15 C.F.R. Part 27) and require IRB approval
- Vertebrate animal research must comply with Animal Welfare Act and National Research Council guidelines
- Proprietary rights in technical data, patents, and copyrights protected under standard SBIR provisions

### Key Program Restrictions
- Unsolicited proposals not accepted
- No venture capital firm participation permitted
- Cannot have development role, prior information access, or DOC assistance in proposal preparation
- No CRADA agreements allowed with awardees on awarded subtopic
- Phase II awards made in June (stated goal)

### Competitive Fairness
- Prospective awardees and their consultants/subcontractors cannot participate in topic selection or proposal review
- Restriction on communications about technical topics with NOAA outside designated contacts
