## Executive Summary
This document establishes standard general conditions that apply to all bids and contracts issued by Murray State University's Procurement Services. It outlines requirements for bid submission, compliance with state and federal law, delivery obligations, contract changes, dispute resolution, equal employment requirements, and termination provisions. These conditions are mandatory for all vendors bidding on University contracts and become part of every resulting contract.

## Notable Details

**Bid Submission Requirements:**
- Bids must be legible, signed in ink, and submitted on official forms
- Must specify brand name, trademark, model, and catalog number
- Only one price and one product per item unless multiple bids are expressly invited
- Prices quoted on delivered prepaid basis (F.O.B. destination)
- Technical specifications and deviations from specified brands must be provided prior to bid opening
- Federal excise taxes must be excluded (University is tax-exempt)
- Bids must arrive before scheduled opening; time measured by Procurement Services reception desk clock
- Late bids only considered if postmarked before opening and are the only responsive bid received

**Compliance & Legal:**
- Contracts governed by Kentucky law
- University may cancel contracts obtained by fraud, collusion, or conspiracy
- Contractor must comply with Kentucky Civil Rights Act (KRS 45.550 to 45.640)
- Contractor must comply with Presidential Executive Order 11246 regarding equal employment
- No discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, age, handicap, or national origin

**Delivery & Performance:**
- All deliveries must be made by end of University's fiscal year in which contract is awarded (unless otherwise specified)
- Deliveries during normal working hours (8:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., Monday-Friday)
- Contractor pays all transportation charges unless contract specifies University pays; if University pays, contractor must prepay and invoice
- Adequate commercial packing required; no charges for standard containers unless bid specifies memorandum charge
- Contractor must provide spare parts and maintenance service for 5 years after equipment acceptance at current user prices

**Contract Changes & Disputes:**
- University may make changes within general scope of contract without surety notice
- Contractor must assert claims for cost/time adjustments within 30 days of change notification
- Disputes decided by Director of Procurement Services (written decision); appeals to Vice President final unless fraudulent
- Contractor must proceed with performance while dispute is pending

**Termination:**
- Termination for Default: University may terminate if contractor breaches terms, fails to deliver on time, or fails to perform per specifications
- Contractor and surety (if applicable) jointly and severally liable for all losses resulting from default
- Termination for Convenience: University may terminate for its own convenience; compensation limited to proportionate profit margin based on out-of-pocket expenses incurred to date of termination
- Contractor bears burden of proving compensation amount through cost data and evidence of expenses

**Payment Terms:**
- Bills must be paid within 30 working days of receipt of correct invoice AND receipt of goods/services in satisfactory condition
- Penalty of 1% per month charged for each full or partial month payment exceeds 30 days
- Bidders encouraged to offer cash discounts for early payment (under 30 days)

**Bond Requirements:**
- Performance bond may be required; must be signed by bidder as principal and by surety authorized in Commonwealth of Kentucky
- If surety loses Kentucky authority, bidder must obtain replacement surety
- Bonds condition full performance and permit University recovery of all damages from bidder and/or surety

**Workforce Compliance:**
- Successful bidder may be required to submit workforce breakdown (race, sex, age, position, county/state of residence, employment date)
- If workforce data shows minority underutilization, contractor must submit affirmative action plan with recruitment/hiring goals and timetables
- Failure to comply with KRS 45.550-45.640 is material breach of contract

**Other Provisions:**
- No assignment of contract rights/duties without University written consent (claims for payment may be assigned to financial institutions)
- If University closed on non-holiday, bid openings postponed to next normal working day
- Samples submitted become University property if not requested back within 30 days; bidder pays all sample shipping costs
- University may request financial records and references to verify contractor ability to perform
