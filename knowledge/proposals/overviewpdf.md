# AFWERX STRATFI/TACFI Program Overview

## Document Metadata
- Type: Program solicitation guidance/eligibility overview
- Client/Agency: Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL), Department of the Air Force
- Program/Solicitation: STRATFI (Strategic Funding Increase) / TACFI (Tactical Funding Increase)
- Date: 2024 (PY24 results shown; document created/modified 2025-02-12)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None (this is program guidance, not BST-specific)
- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This document outlines the AFWERX STRATFI and TACFI programs, which provide follow-on funding to small businesses completing SBIR/STTR Phase II efforts. The programs aim to bridge the "valley of death" between Phase II completion and Phase III scaling by catalyzing relationships between Air Force/Space Force end-users, private innovators, and investors, with total matching funding from government and private sources.

## Program Structure

### Funding Levels
- **STRATFI (Strategic)**: $3M–$15M
- **TACFI (Tactical)**: $375K–$2M

### PY24 Results
- 158 companies selected (30 STRATFI / 128 TACFI)
- $583M in SBIR funds allocated
- $702M in matching government funds
- $619M in matching private funds

## Eligibility Requirements
Small Business Concerns (SBC) must meet ALL criteria:
1. Classified as SBC and eligible for SBIR/STTR award
2. Currently executing or completed SBIR/STTR Phase II within 2 years of Capability Package submission
3. Associated Phase II effort has NOT already received a sequential Phase II award
4. Minimum 90 days elapsed since Phase II execution began
5. Not currently executing a prior STRATFI effort at submission time
6. Anticipated work performed in the United States

## Matching Options
- **TACFI**: 1:1 matching (SBIR/STTR vs. Government OR SBIR/STTR vs. Private)
- **STRATFI**: 1:2 matching (SBIR/STTR vs. Government) OR 1:1:2 matching (SBIR/STTR vs. Government vs. Private)

## Strategic Purpose
The program serves two primary objectives:
1. Catalyze relationships between DoAF end-users/acquisition professionals and private-sector innovators/investors
2. Bridge funding gap between Phase II completion and Phase III contract awards, enabling scaling of strategic capabilities

## Notable Details
- Program explicitly addresses the "valley of death" funding gap in SBIR/STTR commercialization pathway
- Emphasizes dual-use vs. defense-only capability development options
- Includes both government and private sector matching mechanisms
- Government distribution marking: DISTRIBUTION A (approved for public release, unlimited distribution)
