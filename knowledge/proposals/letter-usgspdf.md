# USGS Letter of Support for S2 UAS Volcano Monitoring Project

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support / Funding Commitment
- Client/Agency: USGS National Innovation Center (U.S. Geological Survey)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Phase II, Contract NNX17CP13C, Topic: "A Ruggedized UAS for Scientific Data Gathering in Harsh Environments"
- Date: June 24, 2019
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 UAS
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (BST CEO), Jonathan Stock (USGS National Innovation Center Director)

## Executive Summary
The USGS National Innovation Center committed $100,000 in funding to support Black Swift Technologies' continued development and field deployment of the S2 UAS system for volcano hazard assessment. This co-investment, matched by NASA, would enable further technical improvements to the Phase II system and expanded data collection campaigns to demonstrate the capability for volcanic monitoring applications.

## Technical Approach
The S2 UAS and associated sensing system were originally developed under NASA SBIR Phase II contract NNX17CP13C. The USGS funding was designated to support:
- Field missions using the S2 system
- Further development and technical improvements to Phase II technologies
- Extended operational campaigns or increased on-station time
- Demonstration of new capabilities for volcano hazard assessment

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S2 UAS**
- Ruggedized unmanned aerial system designed for harsh environments
- Equipped with sensing systems for scientific data gathering
- Purpose: Volcano monitoring and hazard assessment
- Developed under NASA SBIR Phase II contract

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Volcano Hazards Assessment**: Primary application for USGS funding; characterizing volcanic hazards across the Nation
- **Harsh Environment Operations**: Designed for "dull, dirty and dangerous" missions
- **Potential Expansion**: Letter references opportunity for broader adoption in other hazard assessment and water resources missions

## Notable Details
- Co-funding arrangement: USGS ($100,000) + NASA matching funds
- Positions S2 as enabling technology for hazard assessment missions traditionally difficult for conventional platforms
- USGS emphasis on improving "the Nation's ability to assess volcano hazards" suggests national-level priority application
- Letter indicates strong institutional support for transition from SBIR research to operational use
