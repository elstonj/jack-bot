## Executive Summary
This document provides standardized instructions for completing a Research Performance Progress Report (RPPR) for Federal grant and cooperative agreement recipients. It establishes uniform reporting requirements across federal agencies to reduce administrative burden and enable consistent comparison of research outputs and outcomes. The RPPR is used for both interim and final progress reports.

## Document Purpose & Scope
This is a template/instruction document issued by the Department of Commerce for SBIR Phase I award recipients (including Black Swift Technologies on their NOAA 2019 SBIR Phase I project). It does not contain project-specific information but rather defines the standardized format and categories that recipients must use when submitting progress reports to federal agencies.

## Reporting Categories & Required Information

### Mandatory Section: ACCOMPLISHMENTS
Recipients must address:
- Major goals and objectives of the project
- What was accomplished under these goals
- Training and professional development opportunities provided
- How results were disseminated to communities of interest
- Plans for the next reporting period

### Optional Sections (as required by agency):
- **PRODUCTS**: Publications, conference papers, presentations, websites, technologies, inventions, patents, software, databases, educational materials, instruments, etc.
- **PARTICIPANTS & OTHER COLLABORATING ORGANIZATIONS**: Personnel involved, changes in active support, partner organizations, international collaborations
- **IMPACT**: Effects on the discipline, other disciplines, human resources development, teaching/education, infrastructure, technology transfer, society, and foreign countries
- **CHANGES/PROBLEMS**: Modifications to approach, delays, expenditure impacts, changes to human subjects/animal protocols, performance site changes
- **SPECIAL REPORTING REQUIREMENTS**: Award-specific requirements
- **BUDGETARY INFORMATION**: Financial data using standard forms
- **PROJECT OUTCOMES**: Cumulative findings (final report only, max 8,000 characters, written for general public)

## Cover Page Data Elements Required
- Federal agency and organization element
- Federal grant/identifying number
- Project title
- PD/PI name, title, contact information
- Submitting official information (if different from PD/PI)
- Submission date
- DUNS and EIN numbers
- Recipient organization name and address
- Project/grant period dates
- Reporting period end date
- Report frequency
- Final report designation (Yes/No)
- Submitting official signature

## Key Reporting Standards
- Recipients must obtain prior written approval for significant changes to project objectives or scope
- "Nothing to Report" option available when no significant activity in a category
- Instructions emphasize shift from reporting activities to reporting accomplishments as project progresses
- Results should be disseminated and evaluated based on excellence/significance, not quantity of publications
- Language should be accessible to lay audiences where possible

## Demographic Information (Optional)
Agencies may collect voluntary demographic data on significant contributors:
- Gender (Male/Female/Decline)
- Ethnicity (Hispanic/Non-Hispanic/Decline)
- Race (American Indian, Asian, Black, Native Hawaiian, White, Decline)
- Disability status (Yes/No/Decline)

Data collection must be direct from individual contributors and protected under Privacy Act requirements.

## Notable Details
- RPPR replaced previous agency-specific reporting formats to create uniformity
- Originally implemented FY2013, revised based on lessons learned
- Format applies to both interim and final progress reports
- Agencies may supplement with program-specific instructions only where necessary, subject to OMB clearance
- A "significant contributor" is defined as a PD/PI or individual working ≥1 person-month per year (approximately 160 hours) on the project
- Special emphasis on infrastructure development, technology transfer, and societal impact beyond traditional academic metrics
- International collaboration and foreign spending must be tracked and reported
