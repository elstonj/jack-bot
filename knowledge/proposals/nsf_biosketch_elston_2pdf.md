## Professional Background

**Education:**
- BS in Electrical Engineering, University of Colorado Boulder (2003)
- MS in Electrical Engineering, University of Colorado Boulder (2005)
- MS in Aerospace Engineering, University of Colorado Boulder (2007)
- PhD in Aerospace Engineering, University of Colorado Boulder (2011)

**Career Timeline:**
- August 2011-Present: Founder and owner of Black Swift Technologies LLC, Boulder, CO
- October 2012-May 2014: NSF AGS Postdoctoral Fellow - "Improved In Situ Thermodynamic Sampling of Severe Storms Through Improved Wind Estimation and Energy Harvesting"
- November 2010-July 2011: Professional research assistant on MIZOPEX (Marginal Ice Zone Observations and Processes EXperiment)
- August 2011-September 2012: Consultant on UAS for Forge Aeronautics
- August 2007-May 2011: Graduate student research on tornado penetration, networking/control of heterogeneous UAS teams, and embedded hardware design for aircraft

## Research Focus & Expertise

Dr. Elston's work centers on **small unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) for atmospheric sampling**, with particular emphasis on:
- Severe storm penetration and tornadic supercell observation
- In situ thermodynamic sampling
- Networked command and control of heterogeneous UAS teams
- Wind field navigation and energy harvesting
- Semi-autonomous flight systems for hazardous environments
