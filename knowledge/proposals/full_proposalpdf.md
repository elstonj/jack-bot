## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies seeks $240,000 in Colorado state funding to accelerate commercialization of the Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS—an end-to-end autonomous flight system developed in collaboration with CU Boulder that measures volumetric soil moisture at 15-meter resolution from 15 meters altitude. The grant will support sales, marketing, and business development activities to reach scientific, government, and commercial customers 18-24 months sooner than bootstrap funding would allow, with matching funds from NASA SBIR Phase II contract and potential Phase II-E program funding.

## Technical Approach

**Core System Architecture:**
The Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS integrates three key technologies:

1. **L-Band Passive Microwave Radiometer Sensor**
   - Passive (non-transmitting) radiometer for volumetric soil moisture measurement
   - Measures soil moisture to ~20 cm depth
   - 15-meter spatial resolution
   - Miniaturized design through years of development at CU Boulder's Center for Environmental Technology
   - Unique in cost, size, and soil moisture measurement capability

2. **SuperSwift Airframe**
   - Robust, field-serviceable flight platform weighing ~5 kg
   - Designed for scalable manufacturing and safety-approved operations
   - Reflects 30+ years of operational sUAS experience
   - Composite construction (sourced from Ability Composites, Loveland, CO)
   - Designed for remote autonomous operations

3. **SwiftCore Flight Management System**
   - Fully integrated autopilot, ground station, and tablet-based user interface
   - Enables terrain-following flight required for soil moisture collection
   - Leverages commercially available consumer electronics sensors (cell phone-grade components)
   - Advanced control and estimation algorithms providing high autonomy and system resiliency
   - Next-generation command and control for sUAS
   - Enables operations by users with minimal training/experience
   - Commercially available for third-party integrators

**Operational Specifications:**
- Flight altitude: 15 meters above ground
- System weight: ~5 kg
- Soil moisture measurement depth: ~20 cm
- Spatial resolution: 15 meters
- Measurement type: Volumetric soil moisture maps
- Regulatory approvals: FAA and NASA flight approvals obtained; planned test flights March 2015

**Technical Development Status:**
- Working prototype completed
- FAA and NASA flight approvals obtained
- First flight deployments planned for end of March 2015
- Two planned flight deployments over scientifically instrumented test sites
- First deployment will use prototype aircraft with prototype sensor
- Second deployment will use SuperSwift airframe with production-ready sensor and commercial support software/hardware

### Competitive Position
- **vs. In Situ Sensors:** Less fidelity and depth than in situ, but flexible, responsive, larger coverage area; in situ are fixed infrastructure with high overhead
- **vs. Manned Aircraft:** Lower cost, more deployable, fewer airspace/scheduling constraints; manned aircraft offer demonstrated utility but very expensive to operate
- **vs. Satellites (SMAP):** Complementary rather than competitive; satellites provide global coverage at 3 km resolution; sUAS provides 15-meter resolution for high-variability areas and smaller regions
- **Competitive Advantages:** Advanced algorithms enabling high autonomy with affordable sensors; usable system enabling non-expert operations; modular design for scalability; regulatory heritage and FAA/NASA approvals
