# Commitment Letter - MALIBU

## Document Metadata
- Type: Commitment letter
- Client/Agency: NASA Goddard Space Flight Center (GSFC)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR contract NNX14CG09C; CCRPP (Collaborative Program) proposal
- Date: March 20, 2017
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: SuperSwift aircraft/sUAS, SMM sUAS (Soil Moisture Mission)
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (CEO, Black Swift Technologies); Dr. Miguel O. Román (Research Physical Scientist, NASA GSFC)

## Executive Summary
NASA GSFC commits $90,677 in funding to Black Swift Technologies to further develop the SuperSwift unmanned aircraft system (initially developed under Phase II SBIR work) for integration with the MALIBU instrument package for satellite calibration and validation. The funding will support enhancements to the SuperSwift platform's payload capabilities and flight validation campaigns through spring 2018.

## Technical Approach
- Enhancement of SuperSwift aircraft's modular swappable payload tray to accommodate MALIBU instrument packages
- Development of a specialized camera array for payload integration
- System validation through additional flight campaigns
- Emphasis on quick field-swappable payload capability to enable dual-mission use (soil moisture mapping + MALIBU imaging)

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift UAS
- Originally developed during Phase II of soil moisture mission SBIR
- Modular platform designed for soil moisture missions and broader scientific applications
- Key capability: swappable payload tray enabling rapid field reconfiguration
- Expected performance upon CCRPP completion: TRL 9 (Technology Readiness Level) and MRL 7 (Manufacturing Readiness Level)
- Timeline: Flight operations planned summer 2017 through spring 2018

## Use Cases & Applications
- **MALIBU Project**: Multi-Angle Imaging Bidirectional Reflectance Distribution Function measurements for satellite calibration and validation
- **Dual-mission capability**: Simultaneous soil moisture mapping and MALIBU payload deployment in same flight area
- Broader scientific mission platform with multiple payload configurations

## Key Commitments
- NASA GSFC funding: $90,677 directly to BST
- Funds available within 45 business days of CCRPP award announcement
- Funds dedicated to payload tray enhancements, camera array development, and flight validation
- Expected operational flights: summer 2017 through spring 2018

## Notable Details
- This letter demonstrates NASA GSFC's confidence in SuperSwift as a commercializable technology platform
- NASA GSFC identified as "current and potential future customer"
- Letter emphasizes the competitive advantage of SuperSwift's modular, quick-swap payload capability versus other unmanned systems
- Coordination between SBIR Phase II development, CCRPP proposal funding, and GSFC co-investment to accelerate commercialization
- The soil moisture mission capability enables enhanced value proposition for MALIBU work through integrated data collection
