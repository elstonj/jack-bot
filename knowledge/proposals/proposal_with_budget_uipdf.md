## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to support a three-year USDA NIFA-funded University of Iowa project on precision agriculture by conducting aerial surveys over corn fields using its S2 fixed-wing UAS. The project will measure nitrous oxide (N2O) emissions during soil denitrification events in spring planting seasons, utilizing both commercial N2O sensors (Year 1) and University of Iowa's proprietary N2O sensor (Years 2-3).

## Technical Approach

**Platform:** S2 UAS (fixed-wing drone)

**Mission Parameters:**
- Three separate flight campaigns: April-May 2025, April-May 2026, April-May 2027
- Flight altitude: 400 feet above ground
- Area coverage per mission: 3 acres
- Duration per campaign: 3 days
- Ideal flight timing: Mid-April to mid-May, preferably after rain events when soil denitrification rates are high

**Data Handling:**
- Data stored onboard via DAQ (Data Acquisition) system and storage device during flight
- Post-flight data retrieval and processing
- No in-flight data transmission anticipated

**Sensor Integration (Years 2-3):**
- N2O sensor integration support required for both 2026 and 2027 campaigns
- Integration includes mechanical interface design, electrical system integration, firmware development, and full testing

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S2 UAS (S2 Fixed-Wing Drone)**
- Role: Primary platform for aerial N2O measurements
- Specifications: Operates at 400 feet altitude, capable of 3-acre coverage per flight
- Usage: Equipped with sensor payload, carries DAQ and storage systems
- Rental cost per mission: $1,000
- Integration support available for custom payloads

## Use Cases & Applications

**Precision Agriculture / Soil Emissions Monitoring**
- Measuring nitrous oxide (N2O) emissions from corn fields during planting season
- Monitoring soil denitrification rates following rain events
- Comparing commercial N2O sensors (Year 1: VenusSolo, LSE4450, or Sensirion options) with proprietary University of Iowa sensor (Years 2-3)
- Supporting data-driven agricultural practices and environmental monitoring
