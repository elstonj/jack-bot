## Executive Summary
This is the official NASA SBIR Phase II solicitation document for Program Year 2025. It provides guidelines, eligibility requirements, proposal preparation instructions, and evaluation criteria for Phase I award recipients seeking Phase II funding to continue research and development with a goal of commercialization. Only companies that received FY2025 NASA SBIR Phase I awards are eligible to submit Phase II proposals.

## Key Program Details

### Award Information
- **Maximum Phase II Award Amount:** $1,275,000
- **Maximum Period of Performance:** 24 months
- **TABA Funds:** Up to $50,000 (included within total award amount)
- **Submission Period:** April 15, 2026 - May 15, 2026, 5:00 p.m. ET
- **Submission System:** ProSAMS (Proposal Submissions and Award Management System)

### Eligibility Requirements
- Only FY2025 Phase I awardees are eligible to submit Phase II proposals
- Small Business Concern (SBC) status required
- Combined with affiliates: maximum 500 employees
- Minimum 50% of research/analytical effort must be performed by the offeror (maximum 50% by subcontractors/consultants)
- All work must be performed in the United States (limited exceptions for unique circumstances)
- Principal Investigator must be primarily employed with the SBC and spend >50% time on the project
- Cannot have Co-PIs
- Venture-capital-owned businesses (owned in majority by multiple VCOCs, hedge funds, or private equity firms) are not eligible

### Registration & Certification Requirements
- SBA SBIR Company Registry registration required (with unique SBC Control ID)
- System for Award Management (SAM) registration required under NAICS codes 541713 or 541715
- Must complete Firm Certifications and Proposal Certifications in ProSAMS
- Must complete "Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries" form (failure to submit results in declination)
- Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) certifications required
- If proposing human/animal subject research, approved protocol required
- If proposing spacecraft delivery, must comply with NPR 8079.1
- HSPD-12 compliance required for those needing 6+ months access to federal facilities

### Foreign Affiliation Disclosures
Each offeror must disclose:
- Identity of owners/covered individuals in foreign talent recruitment programs of foreign countries of concern
- Joint ventures or subsidiaries based in/funded by/affiliated with foreign countries of concern
- Current or pending contractual/financial obligations with foreign state-owned or foreign entities
- Whether wholly owned by foreign country of concern
- Percentage of venture capital from entities with foreign affiliations
- Technology licensing or IP sales to foreign countries of concern (5-year lookback)
- Any foreign, offshore, or non-U.S. entities related to the business

A "covered individual" is defined as someone who substantively contributes to scientific development/execution of the proposed R&D project or has access to the proposed technology development.

## Technical & Business Assistance (TABA)
- Optional funding up to $50,000 available (included in total award)
- Can be used to contract with vendors for:
  - Better technical decision-making
  - Solving technical problems
  - Minimizing technical risks
  - Commercializing the product/process
  - Intellectual property protections
- Vendors may include commercialization assistance providers, business development services, entrepreneurial support organizations, IP/licensing professionals

## Phase II Purpose & Expectations
Phase II is intended to:
- Continue R&D developed under Phase I
- Move innovation closer to transition into NASA programs/missions or commercialization
- Require comprehensive proposal with detailed effort plan
- Include commercialization strategy
- Advance technology toward Federal or commercial market application

NASA recognizes Phase I and II awards may be insufficient for complete R&D and commercialization; completion may require Post-Phase II opportunities or Phase III funding.

## Evaluation & Selection
- Selection based on Phase II evaluation criteria (detailed in section 4.2)
- NASA conducts risk-based due diligence review on cybersecurity, patents, employees, and foreign ownership/affiliations
- Proposals administratively screened for format compliance
- No commitment to fund any specific number of awards
- Awards based on appropriated funding level for FY 2026

## Reporting & Compliance
- All Phase II contractors must complete life cycle certifications at contract midpoint and end of period
- Must report inventions and elect title per federal requirements
- Patent application filing required per regulations
- Export control compliance required
- Government-furnished property requirements apply
- Ongoing reporting required

## Funding Sources & Opportunities
- Phase III available for continued technology commercialization (funded outside SBIR program)
- May be awarded without further competition
- Includes development for transition to NASA, other Government agencies, or private sector

## Additional Support Programs
- NASA Technology Available (TAV): Firms may access NASA-developed technology via:
  - Nonexclusive, royalty-free software usage agreements
  - Nonexclusive, royalty-free evaluation licenses for NASA patents
  - Opportunity to negotiate commercialization licenses post-award
  - Access to NASA personnel knowledgeable about patents (requires NDA)

- NASA Mentor-Protégé Program (MPP): Mentors eligible for award fees for assisting Phase II contractors

- Prohibition on plagiarism and false claims
- False statements are federal felony under 18 U.S.C. § 1001 (up to 5 years imprisonment)
- Office of Inspector General has full access to all proposals
- Fraud/waste/abuse reporting to NASA OIG encouraged: 1-800-424-9183 or https://oig.nasa.gov/hotline/
