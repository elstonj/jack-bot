# CU Letter of Commitment for NASA SBIR Phase II - Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Commitment
- Client/Agency: NASA (SBIR Program)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Phase II, Topic S.305 "Unmanned Aircraft and Sounding Rocket Technologies"
- Date: November 21, 2013
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: sUAS (small Unmanned Aircraft System)
- Key Personnel: Professor Albin J. Gasiewski (CU Center for Environmental Technology, Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Colorado at Boulder)

## Executive Summary
University of Colorado at Boulder commits to partnering with Black Swift Technologies on a Phase II NASA SBIR project to develop and test a soil moisture mapping unmanned aircraft system (sUAS). CU brings three decades of expertise in microwave radiometry and airborne remote sensing of soil moisture through its Center for Environmental Technology.

## Technical Approach
CU will support implementation and testing of BST's proposed soil moisture mapping UAS. The project leverages CU's technology capabilities in:
- Microwave radiometry
- Airborne remote sensing
- Remote sensing of soil moisture

Support will come from the CU Center for Environmental Technology (CET) and students associated with the existing Lobe Differencing Correlation Radiometer (LDCR) project, which is in application development phase.

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS**
- Unmanned aircraft system designed for soil moisture remote sensing applications
- To incorporate microwave radiometry sensing technology

**CU Center for Environmental Technology Capabilities**
- 30+ years of expertise in microwave radiometry and airborne remote sensing
- Lobe Differencing Correlation Radiometer (LDCR) technology platform under development

## Use Cases & Applications
- Soil moisture mapping via remote sensing
- Microwave radiometric measurement from airborne platform

## Notable Details
- CU commitment budget: $185,369
- Partnership leverages existing CU research infrastructure and the LDCR project
- Demonstrates BST's ability to attract university research partners with relevant expertise in remote sensing and microwave instrumentation
