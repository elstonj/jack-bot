## Executive Summary
The USCG Robotics and Autonomous Systems PEO held an industry day to communicate upcoming cUAS (counter-Unmanned Aerial Systems) mission requirements and solicitation strategy. The USCG is seeking integrated solutions for detecting, tracking, identifying, and mitigating unauthorized UAS across fixed and mobile operations, with a full RFP anticipated in Q1-Q2 2026.

## USCG Mission Requirements & Focus Areas

### Core Mission
- Accelerate development, acquisition, fielding, and sustainment of RAS capabilities
- Enhance mission effectiveness and operational readiness
- Enable agile acquisition and rapid technology evolution

### Counter-UAS (cUAS) Capability Pillars
1. **Detect, Track, and Identify (DTI)** - Five modalities specified:
   - Visual/Optical/Infrared
   - Radar
   - Passive Radio Frequency
   - Acoustics
   - Multi-domain integration

2. **Mitigation** - Systems to neutralize or deter hostile UAS

3. **Integrated DTI + Mitigation** - Combined solutions

### Mission Types
1. Fixed Location (e.g., Super Bowl, World Cup)
2. Mobile Response (e.g., disaster operations)
3. Special Mission Support (highlighted as particular priority)

## Key Requirements & Stated Needs

**Operational Requirements:**
- Detect, track, and identify Group 1-3 UAS
- Neutralize and deter within urban/city landscape
- Special emphasis on World Cup security support

**Suitability:**
- Mobile and ruggedized for maritime environment
- Effective performance in city/urban environments

**Usability:**
- Easy to operate
- Easy to calibrate
- Minimal training burden

**Compliance & Standards:**
- FAA, FCC, DHS regulations compliance
- Legal and regulatory adherence
- Cybersecurity requirements

**Sustainability & Lifecycle:**
- Long-term sustainability focus
- Energy efficiency
- Environmentally responsible operations
- Incorporated lifecycle management

**Interoperability & Collaboration:**
- Compatible with federal, state, and local agency systems
- Enable cross-agency coordination and response

**Scalability:**
- Solutions must scale to meet future operational evolution

## Acquisition Strategy & Timeline

- **Approach**: Open to flexible strategies enabling rapid acquisition
- **RFP Release Window**: Q1 or Q2 2026
- **Contracting Philosophy**: Willing to work with vendors on various acquisition models (hardware, services, software, licensing)

## Notable Details

- Large industry engagement: 285 participants at industry day indicates significant government interest
- Special focus on World Cup security reflects near-term, high-profile mission need
- Maritime + urban environment requirements suggest coastal city operations (consistent with USCG mission)
- Early transparency on timeline and requirements aligns with "agile acquisition" philosophy
- Emphasis on multi-domain DTI suggests single-sensor solutions may not be sufficient
