# NASA SBIR Phase II Letter of Support for Black Swift Technologies

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: NASA Headquarters, Goddard Space Flight Center
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Phase II (Wildfire topic)
- Date: November 14, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Uncrewed Aircraft System (UAS) with meteorological sensor payload
- Key Personnel: Haris Riris (NASA Airborne Science Program); Jack Elston (BST, last editor)

## Executive Summary
This letter from NASA's Airborne Science Program endorses Black Swift Technologies' SBIR Phase II proposal to use UAS-mounted meteorological sensors to collect data that refines weather models for wildland fire suppression operations. The effort partners with NCAR to reduce fire weather model uncertainty and supports multiple federal agencies including USFS and Department of Interior.

## Technical Approach
- BST develops a Uncrewed Aircraft System (UAS) platform
- Carries a meteorological sensor payload designed by BST
- Collects meteorological data in fire zones
- Data feeds into fire weather models developed by research partner NCAR to decrease uncertainty
- Potential for real-time data delivery to deployed firefighters via systems like ATAK (Android Tactical Assault Kit)

## Products & Capabilities Described

**UAS Platform with Meteorological Sensor Payload:**
- Purpose: Collect atmospheric data in wildfire environments
- Application: Improve fire weather forecasting accuracy
- Deployment model: Operational validation during NASA field campaigns
- Data integration: Real-time delivery to firefighting teams

## Use Cases & Applications
- Wildland fire suppression planning
- Operational fire weather forecasting
- Real-time support to deployed firefighters
- Integration with fire response command systems (ATAK)

- **Research Partners:** NCAR (National Center for Atmospheric Research)
- **Supporting Programs:** NASA FireSense Project; Advanced Capabilities for Emergency Response Operations (ACERO)
