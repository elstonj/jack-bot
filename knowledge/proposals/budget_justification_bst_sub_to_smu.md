# Budget Justification - Black Swift Technologies Subcontract to SMU

## Document Metadata
- Type: Budget justification document
- Client/Agency: Southern Methodist University (SMU) - subcontract lead
- Program/Solicitation: DOE FECM (Fossil Energy and Carbon Management), Area of Interest 3 - Long-Term UOW (Undisturbed Observation Well) Monitoring
- Date: Created 2024-10-31, Modified 2024-11-06
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Custom methane sensor (via Quanta3)
- Key Personnel: Dr. Maciej Stachura (Subcontract Lead), unnamed UAS Pilot/Technician

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes a three-year subcontract to Southern Methodist University for long-term methane emissions monitoring in remote oil and gas fields and agricultural areas using UAS (unmanned aerial systems). The work involves field detection flights in Colorado, Oklahoma, and potentially other Native American lands, supported by data analysis and continuous monitoring services.

## Budget Summary

- Senior Personnel: $107,626.00

- Travel: $11,531.00

- Indirect Costs (Overhead & G&A): ~$95,790.00

## Technical Approach

**Field Testing Operations:**
- Year 1: 10 detection flights (Alamosa, CO area)
- Year 2: 20 flights (Alamosa, CO area)
- Year 3: 20 flights (Native American lands - Osage Nation, OK; with potential access to Sioux lands SD, Crow lands MT, Navajo lands AZ)
- Each field deployment: 5-7 days with PI and pilot/technician

- UAS Pilot/Technician: Responsible for UAS operations and data optimization
  - Year 1: 60 hours @ $75/hr

  - Year 3: 100 hours @ $75/hr
