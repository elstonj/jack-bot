## Executive Summary
This three-year Army-funded research project (Feb. 2017 – Jan. 2020, $3.33M total) proposes to improve Army operational capabilities in soil moisture characterization, numerical weather prediction error quantification, and land surface data assimilation at high spatial resolutions (1-30 m). The work integrates small UAS remote sensing systems (operated by Black Swift Technologies and CU) with satellite microwave data to support Army applications including ISR, artillery meteorology, boundary layer analysis, and vehicle/robotics mobility assessment. The project leverages existing CSU soil moisture downscaling capabilities and the recently developed Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS.

## Technical Approach

The project comprises three integrated theme areas:

**Theme A: Met Office UM NWP Error Characterization ($674,916 total)**
- Compare Met Office Unified Model output from USAF sources against in situ measurements
- Create statistical analyses to identify performance bottlenecks and mismatches in NWP model inputs for Artillery Met systems
- Team training with Met Office data assimilation team in May-June each year
- Collaborate with ARL/BED NWP and Artillery Met scientists

**Theme B: Soil Moisture Remote Sensing Data Assimilation ($967,070 total)**
- Perform innovation vector/perturbation sensitivity analysis of soil moisture and land surface properties using downscaled data
- Design Army-relevant scale data assimilation system with design experiments and tests
- Years 1-2: Testing at USDA Reynolds Creek, ID site using observational datasets
- Year 3: Extend to White Sands Missile Range (WSMR) site with ARL/BED collaboration
- Leverage existing CSU Equilibrium Moisture from Topography, Vegetation, and Soil (EMT+VS) soil moisture downscaling system—a high-resolution Army GIS-compatible framework created through prior Army Phase 2 SBIR

**Theme C: Collaborative Remote Sensing Cal/val Field Studies ($1,686,183 total)**
- Year 1: Prepare for Reynolds Creek field experiment (summer 2018); develop sUAS and satellite microwave sensor forward operator simulation capabilities
- Year 2: Conduct Reynolds Creek field experiment; intercompare, calibrate, and validate results; characterize sensor and model initialization errors
- Year 3: Replicate experiment at WSMR, New Mexico to test portability and characterize microwave sUAS sensor data for Army-scale data assimilation

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Soil Moisture Mapping (SMM) sUAS**
- What it is: A small unmanned aircraft system developed over three years through NASA funding (partnership between Black Swift Technologies and University of Colorado) for high-resolution soil moisture measurement
- Capabilities: Equipped with L-band radiometer and MiCo antenna for accurate soil moisture measurements; includes soil moisture extraction algorithm
- Proposed use: Primary sensing platform for field calibration/validation studies at Reynolds Creek (2018) and WSMR (2019-2020); high-resolution observational data collection to validate satellite microwave sensors and test data assimilation systems
- Deployment history: Successfully deployed at NASA Cal/Val site in Canton, OK and precision agriculture site in Eastern Colorado; both included in situ validation

**L-Band Radiometer & MiCo Antenna**
- Designed and tested by CU team (Prof. Gasiewski's lab)
- Key enabling technologies for accurate soil moisture measurements from small UAS

**CSU EMT+VS Soil Moisture Downscaling System**
- What it is: High-resolution soil moisture downscaling capability operating at 1-30 m spatial resolution
- Status: Created through prior Army Phase 2 SBIR (CSU with Technology Services Corporation); currently in Subsequent Phase 2 activity
- Features: Army GIS-compatible framework
- Proposed use: Foundation for developing high-fidelity soil moisture data assimilation framework in Theme B
