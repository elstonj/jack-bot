## Executive Summary
This document presents DARPA's evaluation criteria for the ERIS 2026 solicitation. It defines four assessment factors with weighted scoring systems that will be used to evaluate proposals submitted to this program.

## Evaluation Factors & Scoring

### 1. Advancing the State of the Art
**Weight: 40% | Points Possible: 40**
- Assesses degree to which a successful solution will advance the current state of the art in its relevant field(s)
- Rating scale (0-40 points):
  - Unsatisfactory (0): Will not advance state of the art
  - Marginal (10): Slightly advance state of the art
  - Satisfactory (20): Adequately advance state of the art
  - Highly Satisfactory (30): Significantly advance state of the art
  - Superior (40): Exponentially advance and/or redefine state of the art

### 2. Team Capability (Key Personnel Vision, Expertise, and Experience)
**Weight: 30% | Points Possible: 30**
- Assesses capability of proposing team's key personnel and human resource planning to drive project to successful outcome
- Rating scale (0-30 points):
  - Unsatisfactory (0): Not demonstrated capable
  - Marginal (8): Somewhat capable
  - Satisfactory (16): Capable of successful outcome
  - Highly Satisfactory (22): More than capable with retained and proposed key personnel
  - Superior (30): Resoundingly capable

### 3. Defining the Problem & Current State of the Art
**Weight: 20% | Points Possible: 20**
- Assesses degree to which submission clearly and comprehensively defines:
  1. Scope of problem(s) the solution addresses
  2. Current state of the art in the field of potential solutions
- Rating scale (0-20 points):
  - Unsatisfactory (0): Neither clearly nor comprehensively defined
  - Marginal (5): Somewhat clear and comprehensive definitions
  - Satisfactory (10): Adequate clarity and comprehensiveness
  - Highly Satisfactory (15): Very clear and comprehensive definitions
  - Superior (20): Exceptional clarity and comprehensiveness

### 4. Defense and/or Commercial Market Use Case/Impact
**Weight: 10% | Points Possible: 10**
- Assesses proposed solution's impact to current commercial and/or defense use cases
- Rating scale (0-10 points):
  - Unsatisfactory (0): No demonstrated impact or viable applications
  - Marginal (2): Marginal impact or somewhat viable applications
  - Satisfactory (4): Sufficiently demonstrated impact or viable applications
  - Highly Satisfactory (6): Significant impact or significant applications
  - Superior (10): Exceptional impact or exceptional applications

## Scoring Summary
- **Total Points Possible: 100**
- State of the Art advancement carries highest weight at 40%
- Team capability weighted at 30%
- Problem definition weighted at 20%
- Commercial/defense impact weighted at 10%
