## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies seeks $240,000 in Colorado state funding to accelerate commercialization of its Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS—a turn-key unmanned aircraft system developed in collaboration with the University of Colorado Boulder and NASA. The system combines BST's SwiftCore Flight Management System and SuperSwift airframe with an innovative L-band microwave radiometer sensor to map soil moisture at 15-meter resolution from 5 kg airframe flying at 15 meters altitude. Current funding is restricted to technical development; state funds will support accelerated marketing, sales, and business development activities, positioning the company for government and commercial sales 18-24 months sooner, with matching funds available from NASA's Phase II-E SBIR program.

## Technical Approach

### Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS Core System
- **Aircraft:** SuperSwift airframe (~5 kg), field-serviceable, designed for scalable manufacturing and safety-approved operations; incorporates 30+ years of operational experience
- **Sensor:** Passive L-band microwave radiometer miniaturized through years of development in collaboration with CU Boulder's Center for Environmental Technology (CET); measures volumetric soil moisture at depths as low as 20 cm with 15-meter spatial resolution
- **Flight Management:** SwiftCore Flight Management System with integrated autopilot, ground station, tablet-based user interface; enables terrain-following flight and autonomous operations; permits safe flight by minimally trained operators
- **Operational Profile:** Remote autonomous operations at 15 meters above ground altitude

### Key Technical Capabilities
- Volumetric soil moisture mapping with 15-meter resolution
- Depth measurement capability to ~20 cm
- Fully autonomous terrain-following flight
- FAA and NASA flight approvals obtained (as of proposal date, flights planned for March 2015)
- SwiftCore leverages commercial sensors with advanced control and estimation algorithms (maintained as trade secrets)

### IP Strategy
- **Trade Secrets:** Control and estimation algorithms providing high autonomy and performance using affordable commercial sensors (e.g., cell phone components)
- **Patent Strategy:** User interface technologies targeted for patent protection
- **Protection:** Software under copyright/licensing; hardware/circuit board designs under competitive protection
- **Competitive Focus:** Superior execution and quality rather than litigation-based protection; emphasis on innovation to remain competitive as technology evolves rapidly

## Products & Capabilities Described

### 1. **Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS (SMM sUAS)**
- **What it is:** Complete, turn-key unmanned aircraft system for volumetric soil moisture measurement
- **Unique aspects:** First passive L-band radiometer successfully miniaturized for sUAS deployment; novel data-type previously only available from expensive manned aircraft or satellites
- **Performance:** 5 kg weight, 15-meter ground resolution, 20 cm depth measurement capability, autonomous terrain-following flight at 15 m altitude
- **Regulatory status:** Has obtained FAA Certificates of Authorization (COAs) pathway and NASA flight approvals
- **Use context:** Proposed for government/scientific initial deployment with commercial expansion following FAA Part 107 rules (expected 2016-2017)

### 2. **SwiftCore Flight Management System**
- **What it is:** Next-generation autopilot, ground station, and command-and-control system with tablet-based user interface
- **Technology foundation:** Advanced algorithms for estimation and control; leverages commercial off-the-shelf sensors
- **Key differentiators:** Terrain-following capability, high usability enabling minimal training, robust autonomous flight, advanced circuit board design
- **Commercial model:** Licensed to integrators at ~$10,000 per unit; enables third-party development of sUAS applications
- **Scalability:** Designed as platform for presenting multiple data-types and sensors to end users through common interface

### 3. **SuperSwift Aircraft**
- **What it is:** Composite airframe for small unmanned systems
- **Design heritage:** 30+ years combined operational experience
- **Manufacturing:** Sourced from Colorado-based Ability Composites (Loveland)
- **Key characteristics:** Robust, field-serviceable, designed for scalable manufacturing and safety-approved operations

### 4. **L-Band Microwave Radiometer Sensor**
- **What it is:** Passive radiometer for volumetric soil moisture measurement
- **Development:** Collaboration with CU Boulder's Center for Environmental Technology
- **Innovation:** Represents direct transition of scientific advancements into commercial system; miniaturized through years of development
- **Specifications:** Measures at depths to ~20 cm; operates at 15-meter spatial resolution; cost-effective compared to alternatives

## Use Cases & Applications

### Scientific/Research Applications
- **SMAP Validation:** Direct validation and monitoring of NASA's SMAP satellite mission (3 km resolution) through high-resolution (15 m) local measurements in areas of high variability
- **Soil Moisture Science:** Support for researchers studying soil moisture impacts on environmental processes
- **Key Customers:** NASA scientists, academic soil moisture researchers (noted engagement with Tom Jackson (SMAP calibration lead) and Mahta Moghaddam (USC))

### Government/Disaster Response
- **Natural Disaster Monitoring:** Landslide, wildfire, and flash flood prediction and monitoring
- **Soil Integrity Assessment:** Army Corps of Engineers interest for monitoring soil integrity during heavy equipment deployment on unimproved terrain
- **First Responder Support:** Inquiries following past landslides for providing soil integrity information to emergency response teams
- **Key Contact:** Robin Murphy (Texas A&M, Roboticists without Borders)

### Commercial/Agricultural Applications
- **Precision Agriculture:** Crop monitoring and optimization
- **Water Use Optimization:** Agricultural water management and irrigation planning

### Competitive Positioning
**Primary Competitors & BST Advantages:**
1. **In situ sensors:** Fixed, require infrastructure/maintenance; BST offers flexibility and larger coverage area
