## Executive Summary
University of Colorado at Boulder proposes to partner with Black Swift Technologies on a Phase I NASA SBIR project to develop and demonstrate a soil moisture mapping system using a Lobe Difference Correlating Radiometer (LDCR) integrated onto a BST small UAS. CU will contribute microwave radiometry expertise and sensor development while BST provides the aircraft platform, with a planned field demonstration over an agricultural area in the Colorado-Wyoming-Kansas region.

## Technical Approach
- **Sensor System**: CU will develop and integrate the LDCR (Lobe Difference Correlating Radiometer), a passive microwave radiometer, for soil moisture mapping
- **Key Development Tasks**:
  - Design and develop LDCR data acquisition system
  - Development of digital sampling and archival module (~1TB capacity) for standalone LDCR operation
  - LDCR antenna design and power supply
  - Integration of LDCR into BST sUAS
  - Field mission support and data processing
- **Technology Basis**: Leverages three decades of CU/CET experience in passive microwave remote sensing of soil moisture
- **Integration Approach**: CU provides sensor/payload; BST provides UAS platform for integrated flight demonstration

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Lobe Difference Correlating Radiometer (LDCR)
- **What it is**: A passive microwave radiometer developed by CU for remote sensing of soil moisture
- **Proposed Use**: Integration onto BST sUAS for airborne soil moisture mapping of agricultural areas
- **Components**: 
  - Microwave module (existing)
  - Radiometer antenna (to be designed)
  - Power supply (to be developed/adapted)
  - Digital sampling and archival module (new development, ~1TB capacity)
- **Heritage**: First airborne polarimetric imaging radiometer; first NOAA remote sensing payload on unmanned aerial system

### BST Small UAS
- **What it is**: Small unmanned aerial system platform
- **Proposed Use**: Carry LDCR payload for soil moisture mapping demonstration
- **Specifications**: Not detailed in this document

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Application**: Soil moisture mapping of agricultural regions
- **Geographic Area**: Agricultural areas in Colorado-Wyoming-Kansas region
- **Mission Type**: At least one field demonstration flight planned during Phase I (June-December 2013)
