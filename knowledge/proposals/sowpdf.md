# Demonstrating Persistent IR Measurements Over Wildfires Using Small, Fixed Wing Uncrewed Aircraft

## Document Metadata
- Type: Statement of Work (Draft)
- Client/Agency: US Forest Service (USFS) and NASA Ames Research Center (ARC)
- Program/Solicitation: Joint USFS-NASA effort (referred to as "PersistenceDemo")
- Date: Draft created October 18, 2024
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Black Swift S2 fixed-wing UAS
- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This SOW describes a demonstration project where Black Swift will use multiple S2 fixed-wing UAS equipped with IR imaging payloads to provide continuous, persistent surveillance over active wildfires. The effort will demonstrate 10-12 hours of coordinated flight operations with synchronized launch/recovery cycles to ensure uninterrupted data collection and near-real-time delivery to incident commanders, with the goal of developing a 24-hour operational capability.

## Technical Approach
- **Operational Concept**: Synchronized launch and recovery of multiple S2 aircraft to maintain continuous overhead presence over target area; at least two aircraft during handoff periods to ensure uninterrupted coverage
- **Flight Patterns**: Pre-determined transects for wide-area imagery collection over simulated fire lines, or orbits over single locations
- **Data Delivery**: Geo-rectified imagery in near real-time accessible to incident commanders
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Operations will follow NASA NPR 7900, including Airworthiness Review, Flight Readiness Review, and Mission Readiness Review as applicable; COA (Certificate of Airworthiness) acquisition is vendor responsibility

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift S2
- **Description**: Small, fixed-wing, catapult-launched uncrewed aircraft system
- **Proposed Use**: Primary platform for persistent IR surveillance; 2-4 aircraft required for demonstration
- **Deployment History**: Currently operated by NASA ARC and Langley Research Center (LaRC); operationally deployed by USGS for volcano caldera mapping and gas sampling
- **Payload Capability**: Compatible with IR imaging payload nosecones previously flown for USGS and NASA JPL

### IR Imaging Payload Nosecones
- **Description**: Infrared imaging payload compatible with S2 aircraft
- **Previous Experience**: Already flown for USGS and NASA JPL applications
- **Quantity Required**: 2 or more for demonstration project

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary**: Continuous overhead surveillance of active wildfires
- **Secondary**: Other ephemeral ground or atmospheric targets
- **Historical Context**: S2 previously used for volcano caldera mapping, gas sampling, and other scientific survey missions
- **End-User**: Data for incident commanders and wildfire management personnel

## Project Deliverables
1. Flight report including recommendations and lessons learned for 24-hour flight campaigns (due 90 days post-mission)
2. Equipment list: launchers, batteries, chargers, aircraft, ground stations, ancillary GSE required for mission execution
3. All data products (imagery, telemetry, aircraft state data) made available to science community via DAAC or NASA Ames website per NASA Open Science Data Policy
4. Assignment to appropriate DAAC for long-term archiving

## Schedule
- **Start Date**: No later than November 15, 2024
- **Duration**: 6 months from award to completion (including all imagery, telemetry, and reports)
- **Demonstration Duration**: 10-12 hour simulation of eventual 24-hour capability (weather and daylight permitting)

## Notable Details
- **Location**: Colorado (selected for lowest cost effort)
- **Flight Crew**: Black Swift employees or subcontractors
- **Observers**: NASA and/or USFS personnel present during operations
- **Data Policy**: All collected data subject to NASA Open Science Data Policy; immediate availability to science community
- **Goal Statement**: Transition from 10-12 hour demonstration to operational 24-hour persistent surveillance capability
- **Equipment Scope**: Project responsible for all infrastructure (launchers, power systems, ground stations, etc.) required for execution
