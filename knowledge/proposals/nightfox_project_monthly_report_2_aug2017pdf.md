## Executive Summary
The NightFOX project is developing a UAS-based remote sensing and in situ measurement system for nighttime fire observations, with emphasis on fire weather modeling applications. This month 2 report documents ongoing acquisition of the SuperSwift platform, development of multiple sensor suites (CO2, aerosol, mid-IR), and early engagement with potential field sampling opportunities.

## Technical Approach
- **Platform Acquisition**: SuperSwift UAS being procured through CU (Colorado University) partnership
- **Sensor Development Strategy**: Multi-instrument approach with both remote sensing and in situ measurement packages:
  - CO2 measurement: Evaluating 2004 nm laser and NDIR (non-dispersive infrared) sensor options with associated detectors
  - Aerosol measurements: Assembly of 2 POPS instruments for accumulation-mode aerosol; coarse-mode aerosol instrument development begun
  - Mid-IR sensing: Candidate sensors acquired, optical filters (near- and mid-infrared) identified and ordered
  - CO sensor: Candidate prototypes under negotiation with developer
  - SWIR imager: Continued evaluation underway
- **Modeling Integration**: Engaged modelers on payroll working on data assimilation needs and fire weather modeling; coordinating access to NOAA supercomputer
