# Letter of Commitment for Inficon PSTAR Proposal

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Commitment
- Client/Agency: INFICON Inc. (Research Manager: Dr. Andres Diaz, Intelligent Sensor Solutions)
- Program/Solicitation: ROSES 2023 PSTAR (NRA NNH23ZDA001N-PSTAR)
- Date: September 12, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: SwiftCore (certified flight management system), Heavy-lift UAS (multicopter)
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (CEO, Black Swift Technologies)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies commits to collaborating with INFICON on a NASA-funded project for in-situ plume gas analysis using airborne quadrupole ion trap mass spectrometry (QIT-MS). BST will procure, develop, modify, and operate a heavy-lift UAS platform for volcanic plume sampling deployments across multiple field sites.

## Technical Approach
BST will deliver and manage a heavy-lift multicopter UAS platform based on their SwiftCore flight management system. The approach includes:
- Procurement or development of heavy-lift multicopter frame/system
- Modification for autonomous operation
- Ruggedization for volcanic plume operations
- Integration support for INFICON's MS payload
- Flight readiness testing and field preparations
- Coordination with NASA designated center for operational control and safety

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SwiftCore Flight Management System**
- Certified flight management system for UAS
- Track record with NASA missions
- Previously employed for volcanic observations

**Heavy-Lift UAS (Project-specific)**
- Multicopter platform (frame or full system)
- Modified for autonomous operation
- Ruggedized for volcanic environment operations
- Capable of carrying INFICON QIT-MS payload

## Use Cases & Applications

**In-situ Plume Gas Analysis**
- Direct sampling and analysis of volcanic plume gases using airborne mass spectrometry

**Deployment Locations & Schedule**
- Year 2: Iceland (1 deployment, 2 weeks, 10 operational days)
- Year 3: Kilauea, Hawaii (2 weeks, 10 operational days)
- Year 3: Volcano, Italy (2 weeks, 10 operational days)

## Key Tasks Committed

1. Provide heavy-lift UAS with modifications and ruggedization for volcano operations
2. Conduct flight readiness tests and prepare for NASA Flight Readiness Review and Risk Flight Assessment
3. Assist with obtaining UAS flight permits in USA
4. Execute three field deployments with 10 operational days each across Iceland, Hawaii, and Italy
