## Executive Summary
This letter justifies the use of NASA Glenn's Ice Research Tunnel (IRT) for Phase II Option Period testing under Task O3. BST requires the IRT's unique capability to simultaneously control temperature, airflow, and icing conditions to validate prototype performance in extreme cold and icing environments representative of Navy operational scenarios.

## Technical Approach
BST proposes to conduct controlled, instrumented testing of its Phase II prototype at the NASA Glenn Ice Research Tunnel to:
- Expose the prototype to sustained sub-freezing temperatures with calibrated icing conditions
- Simultaneously control temperature, airflow, and icing parameters
- Generate repeatable, traceable test data with high measurement fidelity
- Quantify performance degradation, failure modes, and recovery behavior
- Correlate models and reduce risk prior to later-phase demonstrations

Testing will be executed by a small technical team traveling to the facility during the Option Period.

- Navy hazardous weather operations in extreme cold environments
- Icing condition exposure and performance monitoring

## Technical Justification
The document asserts that no other domestic facility provides:
- Combined environmental control of temperature, airflow, and icing
- Required measurement fidelity
- Operational relevance to Navy requirements
- Ability to meet schedule and risk posture constraints

Alternative approaches (laboratory freezers, environmental chambers, field testing) would introduce unacceptable uncertainty, increased cost, or extended timelines.
