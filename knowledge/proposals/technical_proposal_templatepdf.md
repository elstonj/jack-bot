# Technical Proposal Template

- Type: Template / Instructional Document

- Date: 2023-11-14
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None

## Executive Summary
This is a blank NASA SBIR Phase II technical proposal template that provides detailed formatting requirements, structural guidelines, and content specifications for firms submitting Phase II proposals. It does not contain substantive information about any specific project, innovation, or technical approach—it is purely instructional documentation.

## Document Structure & Requirements
The template mandates ten required sections in a specific order:

1. **Table of Contents** (0.5 pages)
2. **Identification and Significance of the Innovation and Results of Phase I Award** (15 pages max)
3. **Technical Objectives** (2 pages max)
4. **Work Plan** (10 pages max)
5. **Related R/R&D** (1 page max)
6. **Key Personnel and Bibliography** (5 pages max)
7. **Commercialization and Business Planning** (2-8 pages, required minimum)
8. **Facilities/Equipment** (2 pages max)
9. **Subcontracts and Consultants** (2 pages max)
10. **Related, Essentially Equivalent, and Duplicate Proposals and Awards** (0.5 pages max)

**Total page limit:** 46 pages maximum (8.5" x 11" with 1-inch margins)

## Formatting Specifications
- Minimum font size: 10 point (smaller fonts cause rejection)
- Header requirements: Firm name, proposal number, project title
- Footer requirements: Page number and proprietary markings
- All pages must be consecutively numbered at bottom
- Non-applicable sections must be included and marked as such
- Proposals omitting any required part are considered non-responsive

## Key Content Requirements by Section

### Part 2: Phase I Results & Phase II Proposed Work
Must include:
- Summary of Phase I results
- Description of proposed innovation
- Relevance and significance to subtopic needs
- Position relative to state of market, state of art, and feasibility
- Note: Evaluators may verify accuracy against Phase I final technical report

### Part 3: Technical Objectives
Must state:
- Specific objectives of Phase II R/R&D relative to problem statement
- Innovation types requested by subtopic manager
- Proposed deliverables (contract deliverables, test reports, software, hardware)
- If using NASA IP: description of planned developments with NASA Intellectual Property and submission of NASA Evaluation License Application

### Part 4: Work Plan
Must include:
- Detailed description of Phase II R/R&D plan
- What will be done, where, and how
- Detailed methods for achieving each task/objective
- Task descriptions, schedules, resource allocations
- Estimated task hours for each key personnel
- Planned accomplishments and project milestones
- **Critical requirement:** Estimated task hours must be consistent with Proposal Budget form
- For joint ventures/partnerships: statement of workload distribution, management, and charging

### Part 6: Key Personnel
Must identify:
- All key personnel essential to Phase II success
- Biographical information with directly related education and experience
- **Principal Investigator (PI) requirements:**
  - PI is essential to project success and must make substantial commitment
  - Functions: planning/directing project, technical leadership, substantial personal contributions, primary NASA contact, ensuring compliance with contract agreements
  - Must describe nature of PI activities and time commitment
  - Qualifications and basis for selection must be clearly presented
  - NASA has sole right to accept/reject PI based on education, experience, competence
  - PI eligibility requirements apply (cannot work more than half-time for academic/nonprofit organization for SBIR; exception for STTR)
  - If Phase II PI differs from Phase I PI: must provide rationale for change

### Part 7: Commercialization and Business Planning
**Strict requirement:** Minimum 2 pages, maximum 8 pages. Firms not meeting this requirement may be considered non-responsive and not scored.

Must validate:
- Firm has knowledge and ability to commercialize innovation
- Company's future viability and financial viability
- Quantitative market analysis completed
- Convincing value proposition
- Pro forma financial projections developed and validated

**Market Feasibility and Competition Strategy:**
1. Describe market segment and total addressable market (TAM)
   - How market was validated and assumptions used
   - Market size in dollars if possible
   - Market segmentation/TAM in dollars if possible
   - Projected market share percentage in 2-3 years post-entry
2. Describe target customers (NASA, Federal agency, commercial)
3. Describe competitive landscape
   - Identify potential competitors by company name
   - Discuss barriers to entry and timeline for competitor entry
   - How innovation differs from current/future competitors

**Commercialization Strategy:**
1. Commercial development details
   - Timeline to market
   - Business model (spin-out, license, OEM, etc.)
   - Distribution channels (direct sales, distributors, etc.)
   - Pro forma 2-3 year revenue projections
   - Follow-on development plans (>5 years)
2. Risks and mitigations

**Financial Strategy:**
1. Current and future company capitalization
   - Pro forma forecast (income statements, balance sheets, cash flow statements)
   - Operations/manufacturing and business staff utilization
   - Physical plant, facilities, capital equipment, tooling, test equipment
   - Consultants, incubators, research institutions to be utilized
   - Production entry approach and capitalization changes
2. Approach from past SBIR/STTR commercializations (if applicable)

**Intellectual Property:**
1. IP protection strategy
   - Actions for temporary competitive advantage
   - Company's prior IP record
   - Strategy to build sustainable business through IP protection

**Assistance and Mentoring:**
1. Business relationships (partnerships, joint ventures, licensing agreements)
2. Plans for securing technical/business assistance through mentoring, partnering, SBDCs, federally-funded labs, Manufacturing Extension Partnerships, other providers
3. Any TABA (Technical Assistance and Business Assistance) needs assessment requests

**Funding Plan (one or more required):**
1. Letter of commitment for follow-on funding/product sales
2. Letter of commitment for matching Phase II-E funding
3. Letter of capital commitment (CEO/CFO signed) for conditional funding upon success
4. Specific plan to secure Phase III funding

Note: Capital commitment letters submitted in Capital Commitments Addendum do not count toward page limits.

### Part 8: Facilities/Equipment
If requesting Government-furnished laboratory equipment, facilities, or services: must describe why necessary and not reasonably available from private sector.

### Part 9: Subcontracts and Consultants
Must describe:
- All subcontracting and business arrangements
- Relevant organizations/individuals
- Expertise provided
- Functions, services, number of hours, labor rates
- Costs documented in Proposal Budget form
- Supporting documentation required

### Part 10: Related, Essentially Equivalent, and Duplicate Proposals
**Critical legal warning:** Identical or substantially equivalent proposals may be submitted to multiple Federal agencies with proper notification, but it is unlawful to enter into funding agreements requiring essentially equivalent work.

If submitting identical/equivalent proposals elsewhere, must disclose:
1. Name and address of agencies
2. Submission date or award date
3. Solicitation title, number, and date
4. Applicable research topics
5. Project titles
6. PI/project manager name and title for each proposal

Must also summarize this information on Proposal Certifications form.
