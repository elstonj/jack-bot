## Executive Summary
This is a Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO) for the FY 2020 NOAA SBIR Phase II program. It invites Phase I awardees from FY 2019 to submit Phase II applications for research and development funding up to $400,000 over approximately 24 months. The solicitation outlines eligibility requirements, application procedures, evaluation criteria, and program rules for small business concerns interested in commercializing federally-funded research innovations.

## Scope & Program Description
- **Eligibility**: Restricted to small business concerns (SBCs) that received NOAA SBIR Phase I awards under NOFO NOAA-OAR-OAR-TPO-2019-2005899 with performance periods between January 1, 2020 and September 30, 2020
- **Funding**: Phase II awards up to $400,000 per award
- **Duration**: Typically no more than 24 months; anticipated start date February 1, 2021
- **Funding Mechanism**: Grant (not contract)
- **Participation**: NOAA elects NOT to allow venture capital operating companies (VCOCs), hedge funds, or private equity firms to participate

## SBIR Program Structure
The document describes the three-phase SBIR program:
1. **Phase I**: Feasibility research to demonstrate scientific and technical merit
2. **Phase II**: Research and development (R&D) or prototype development (this solicitation)
3. **Phase III**: Commercialization using private sector or non-SBIR government funding

## Key Eligibility Requirements

### Small Business Concern (SBC) Qualifications
- Must meet 13 CFR § 121.702 requirements
- At time of award, not time of application
- Must be U.S.-based concern with appropriate ownership structure

### Principal Investigator (PI) Requirements
- Primary employment must be with the SBC at time of award and during project
- Primary employment defined as >50% of PI's time as SBC employee (not contractor/consultant)
- Precludes full-time employment elsewhere

### Work Performance Requirements
- Minimum 50% of research and analytical effort must be performed by the applicant SBC
- Total cost for all consultant fees, facility leases, usage fees, and other contract/subcontract agreements may not exceed 50% of total award
- All work must be performed in the United States (50 states, US territories, DC, Marshall Islands, Federated States of Micronesia, Palau)
- Rare waivers available for supplies/materials unavailable in US (must request 14+ days before deadline with detailed justification)

### Performance Benchmark Ratings
- Only applicable to Phase I applicants; provided for reference
- Firms with 21+ Phase I awards in past 5 years must maintain Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate of at least 0.25
- Assessment occurs June 1 annually; ineligible companies cannot submit Phase I for one year (June 1 - May 31)
- Does not affect Phase II/Phase III eligibility

## Company Registry Requirements
- All applicants must register in SBA Company Registry at sbir.gov/registration
- Must update information before each SBIR application submission
- Must save registration as PDF and append to SF-424 form
- Registry tracks ownership, affiliation, and compliance requirements

## Application Requirements

### Phase II Project Narrative (Technical Content) Must Include:
1. Title page with project title, company name, company representative, PI full name, title, organization, telephone, mailing address, email, and NOAA SBIR Phase I award information
2. All standard sections as specified (with N/A if not applicable)
3. Self-contained, peer-review quality document
4. Direct, concise, and informative writing
5. Sufficient information demonstrating sound approach to important scientific/engineering innovation
6. Responsiveness to original Phase I subtopic NOAA program interests
7. Basis for technological innovation leading to new commercial products/processes/services

### Format Requirements
- Standard 8.5" × 11" pages
- 2.5 cm margins minimum
- 10-point font minimum
- All units in metric system
- PDF format required
- No total page limit specified
- Promotional material should be omitted

### Required Forms and Documents (Section 8.1)
- SF-424 form with attached Company Registry PDF
- SBIR Funding Agreement Certification (At Time of Award) - Appendix A
- SBIR Funding Agreement Certification (Life-Cycle) - Appendix B
- Completed Fraud, Waste, and Abuse training certification
- SBIR cover page and checklist - Appendix D
- Additional certifications as required

## Evaluation and Selection

### Phase II Screening Criteria (Section 4.2)
Detailed in Section 4.2; insufficient scientific/technical information or missing required forms will result in rejection without review.

### Phase II Evaluation Criteria (Section 4.3)
- Scientific and technical merit
- Potential for commercial success
- Alignment with NOAA program interests

### Special Priorities
- **Manufacturing-Related**: Executive Order 13329 requires high priority for manufacturing-related R&D projects
- **Energy Efficiency/Renewable Energy**: Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007 (P.L. 110-140) requires high priority

These priorities will not override core selection criteria but will be considered where feasible.

## Certifications Required

### Funding Agreement Certifications
- Size, ownership, and SBIR program compliance at time of award
- Life-cycle certification required before receiving >50% of award and before final payment

### Research Involving Human Subjects
- Must comply with Common Rule for Protection of Human Subjects (15 CFR Part 27)
- IRB approval required; must be registered with OHRP
- Requires FWA (Federal-wide Assurance) on file
- Human embryonic stem cell lines must be NIH-approved
- Foreign tissue/cells allowed only if unique and equivalent US source unavailable

### Research Involving Live Vertebrate Animals
- Must comply with U.S. Government Principles for Utilization and Care of Vertebrate Animals
- IACUC (Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee) approval required
- Animal Welfare Act (7 USC § 2131 et seq.) compliance required
- Preexisting animal images and animal materials from commercial processors exempt

### Federal Felony and Tax Compliance
- Pre-award certifications regarding federal felony convictions, criminal tax convictions, unpaid federal tax assessments, delinquent federal tax returns

### Fraud, Waste, and Abuse Certification
- Mandatory DOC Office of Inspector General training required
- Print, sign, and submit certification of completion

## Notable Details

- **No Unsolicited Proposals**: SBIR is not a substitute for unsolicited proposal mechanisms; unsolicited proposals not accepted
- **No Obligation to Fund**: NOAA not obligated to make awards; subject to fund availability
- **Unilateral Selection**: NOAA retains right to select research topics and awardees; may select several or no awards under given subtopic
- **Federal Awarding Agency Risk Review**: NOAA may conduct independent risk assessment of applicants (Section 4.5, details not provided)
- **Application Review**: NOAA may not forward applications lacking sufficient scientific/technical information or missing required forms; applications failing screening criteria rejected without further review
- **Personal/Business Information**: Applications may be reviewed by DOC employees, other federal employees, federal agents/contractors, and non-federal personnel under confidentiality agreements; applicants expected to support program reviews by submitting required information timely
- **FOIA Considerations**: Applications subject to FOIA requests; confidential business information should be clearly marked
- **U.S. Performance**: Strong requirement for U.S.-based work; rare waivers available with substantial justification
