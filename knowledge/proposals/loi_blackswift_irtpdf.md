# Black Swift Technologies S2 Icing Test – NASA GRC Letter of Intent

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Letter of Intent (LOI)
- **Client/Agency:** NASA Glenn Research Center (GRC)
- **Program/Solicitation:** NASA Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) – Extreme Environments topic
- **Date:** January 23, 2023
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** S2 (uncrewed aircraft system)
- **Key Personnel:** Dr. Jack Elston (CEO, Black Swift Technologies); Dennis Eck (IRT Facility Manager, NASA GRC); Bryan K. Smith (Director, Facilities, Test and Manufacturing, NASA GRC)

## Executive Summary
NASA GRC offers in-kind support for Black Swift Technologies' icing research testing on the S2 uncrewed aircraft system, providing one day of occupancy in the Icing Research Tunnel (IRT) valued at $49,660. The testing is intended to evaluate icing sensors and mitigation techniques developed under NASA SBIR funding.

## Technical Approach
- **Test Facility:** Icing Research Tunnel (IRT) at NASA Glenn Research Center
- **Duration:** 1 day of facility occupancy
- **Scope:** Testing of icing sensors and mitigation techniques mounted on the S2 uncrewed aircraft system
- **Funding/Support:** NASA GRC providing facility use, staffing, and supporting services

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S2 Uncrewed Aircraft System:**
- Developed through NASA SBIR efforts
- Equipped with sensors and mitigation techniques for icing detection/prevention
- Intended for testing in the IRT facility

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Icing Research:** Development and validation of sensors and mitigation techniques for uncrewed aircraft operating in icing environments
- **Extreme Environment Operations:** Implied capability for aircraft to operate in icing conditions

## Notable Details
- **Timeline:** Proposed testing window: January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024
- **Estimated Cost:** $49,660 (NASA GRC in-kind contribution, subject to change)
- **Legal Status:** Non-binding letter of intent; contingent on proposal selection, internal NASA reviews (legal and export control), and fund availability
- **Next Step:** Formalization would require "appropriate subsequent agreement" between BST and NASA GRC

---

**Note:** This is a brief but substantive LOI confirming NASA's willingness to support BST's icing research with S2. It demonstrates validation of BST's SBIR-funded work and NASA's commitment to in-kind testing resources.
