## Executive Summary
The NightFOX project achieved a prototype remote sensing payload test flight in August 2018 aboard the S2 UAS, though the flight was brief due to aircraft instability issues. The in situ payload (excluding CO sensor) is ready for testing, and multiple instrument evaluations are ongoing. A controlled burn sampling mission is planned for October 2018 near Pueblo, Colorado.

## Technical Approach
- Development and evaluation of dual-payload capability on S2 UAS platform: remote sensing and in situ sensing
- Remote sensing payload includes 4 µm telescope (noted as not requiring reflected sunlight capability)
- In situ payload includes CO2 sensors (long and short pathlength versions), CO sensor, and aerosol instrumentation (POPS instrument with planned flow dilution modification)
- Staged testing approach: prototype validation → local engineering flights → operational fire plume sampling

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- Test flights conducted by CU in August 2018
- Platform for both remote sensing and in situ payloads
- Aircraft stability issues identified and being actively resolved by BST and CU

### Remote Sensing Payload
- Prototype version tested August 23, 2018
- Includes 4 µm telescope (for thermal/infrared fire observations)
- Performed adequately overall; generated data for initial algorithm development
- Status: awaiting 4 µm telescope completion/evaluation

### In Situ Payload
- CO2 sensors (long pathlength for low CO2 environments, short pathlength for high CO2 environments) - under evaluation
- CO sensor - prototype received, requires firmware modification for preferred communication mode
- POPS aerosol instrument - requires flow dilution implementation for high-loading fire plume sampling
- Status: mostly assembled and ready for test flight (CO sensor pending modifications)

## Use Cases & Applications
- Nighttime fire observations during active wildfires
- Fire plume characterization (aerosols, CO, CO2, thermal signature)
- Controlled burn sampling at Security and Emergency Response Training Center (Pueblo, CO) - planned for week of October 8, 2018
- Integration with fire weather modeling and USFS-NOAA coordination
- Support for FIREX research program
