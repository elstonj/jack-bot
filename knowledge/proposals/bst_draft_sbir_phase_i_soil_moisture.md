## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to adapt its proven soil moisture mapping payload—currently used in NASA, NOAA, and commercial operations—for USAF runway integrity validation and global landing zone assessment. The Phase I will conduct feasibility research, customer discovery, and define Phase II development milestones to deliver high-resolution (~15m) soil moisture data that reduces landing operation risk globally.

## Technical Approach

### Core Concept
- Adapt existing soil moisture mapping sensor payload to BST's S2 (fixed-wing) or E2 (multi-rotor) UAS platforms
- Deliver high-resolution (~15m per pixel) soil moisture data for landing zone assessment
- Focus on rapid collection and dissemination with low-latency processing
- Provide spatial and temporal resolution superior to manned aircraft and satellite alternatives

### Phase I Work Plan (90 days)
1. **Preliminary Report** (Days 0–5): 15-slide research approach document
2. **Department of Air Force Customer Discovery** (Days 0–60): Outreach to identified DAF stakeholders; conference attendance; program office discussions

### Key Technical Features
- Machine Learning-based algorithm design for soil condition analysis
- GPS-Denied Navigation capability
- Sensor-directed flight control

- Real-time or near-real-time data processing

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 (Fixed-Wing UAS)
- Proprietary design suitable for sensitive payloads
- Proven platform selected for NASA MALIBU project
- Currently operating with JPL trace gas sensors in Costa Rica (ELEVATE project)
- Used successfully in soil moisture mapping operations

### E2 (Multi-Rotor UAS)
- Alternative platform option for soil moisture payload adaptation
- Designed for specialized payload accommodation

### Soil Moisture Radiometer / Soil Moisture Mapping Payload
- Measures soil moisture critical for assessing soil integrity and landing zone suitability
- Currently field-proven in USGS wildfire mitigation work, NOAA watershed characterization, and commercial turf health monitoring
- Sub-15m resolution capability (significantly higher than existing satellite/manned alternatives)
- Can assess slipperiness, stickiness, and bearing strength as soil moisture increases

### SwiftPilot Flight Management System (FMS)
- In-house developed autopilot system
- Demonstrates BST's end-to-end UAS development capability

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary (Defense)
- **Runway Integrity Validation:** Assess landing zone soil conditions for USAF aircraft operations in unconventional locations globally
- **Warfighter Support:** De-risk landing operations and extend USAF global reach with on-demand environmental awareness
- **Unstable Terrain Assessment:** Rapid diagnosis of soil drainage and moisture dynamics in rivers, wetlands, coastal regions, and precipitation-prone areas
- **Special Operations:** Potential applications with AFSOC/WIN-ISR Division and AFLCMC/SOF-ISR

### Secondary (Dual-Use Commercial)
- **Wildfire Mitigation:** USGS soil moisture measurements for fire prevention planning
- **Watershed Characterization:** NOAA hydrological studies
- **Turf/Sod Health Monitoring:** Ongoing work with Turf Scout
- **Construction:** Heavy machinery site assessment; road construction planning
- **Railway Operations:** Predict/prevent track movement and derailments from soil saturation
- **Flash Flood/Landslide Prediction:** Early warning systems
- **Insurance Risk Assessment:** Property and infrastructure vulnerability evaluation
