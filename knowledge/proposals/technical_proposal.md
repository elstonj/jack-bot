## Executive Summary
This is a NASA SBIR Phase II technical proposal template and instructions document. It provides the required structure, formatting guidelines, and content requirements for firms submitting Phase II proposals to NASA's Small Business Innovation Research program. The document specifies ten mandatory sections, page limits, formatting standards, and detailed guidance on commercialization planning.

## Document Structure & Requirements

### Formatting Requirements
- **Total page limit:** 46 standard 8.5 x 11 inch pages (single submission)
- **Margins:** 1.0 inch (2.5 cm)
- **Font size:** Minimum 10 point for text and tables
- **Header:** Firm name, proposal number, project title
- **Footer:** Page number and proprietary markings (if applicable)

### Ten Required Sections

**Part 1: Table of Contents** (0.5 page)
- Index of all proposal sections with page numbers

**Part 2: Identification and Significance of the Innovation and Results of the Phase I Award** (15 pages suggested)
- Summary of Phase I results
- Description of proposed Phase II work
- Proposed innovation overview
- Relevance and significance to subtopic needs
- Innovation relative to state of market, state of art, and feasibility
- Note: Evaluators may review Phase I final technical report for verification

**Part 3: Technical Objectives** (2 pages suggested)
- Specific objectives related to problem statement and subtopic
- Proposed deliverables (contract deliverables, test reports, software, hardware)
- If using NASA IP: description of planned developments with the IP and NASA Evaluation License Application attachment

**Part 4: Work Plan** (10 pages suggested)
- Detailed description of Phase II R&D plan to meet technical objectives
- What, where, and how work will be done
- Detailed methods for each task/objective
- Task descriptions, schedules, resource allocations
- Estimated task hours for key personnel
- Planned accomplishments and project milestones
- Consistency requirement: task hours in work plan must match Proposal Budget form
- For joint ventures/limited partnerships: statement of workload distribution, management, and charging

**Part 5: Related R/R&D** (1 page suggested)
- Description of significant current/previous R&D directly related to proposal
- R&D conducted by PI or offeror
- How it relates to proposed effort
- Planned coordination with outside sources
- Demonstrate awareness of key recent R&D by others in subject area

**Part 6: Key Personnel and Bibliography of Directly Related Work** (5 pages suggested)
- Identification of all key personnel essential to Phase II success
- Biographical information, directly related education and experience
- Resume/vitae summaries focusing on relevant experience or publications

**Principal Investigator (PI) Requirements:**
- PI considered key to project success; must make substantial commitment
- Functions: planning and directing project; technical leadership; substantial personal contributions; primary NASA contact; ensure work proceeds per contract
- Qualifications: clearly presented in proposal; education, experience, demonstrated ability, and competence
- NASA has sole right to accept/reject PI based on evaluation factors
- Time commitment: must be acceptable to Contracting Officer
- Eligibility: establish and confirm PI eligibility; indicate extent to which existing projects commit PI time concurrently
- Restriction: PI cannot work more than half time for academic/nonprofit organization; circumventing this restriction by substituting ineligible PI results in proposal rejection
- STTR exception: PI can be primarily employed by either SBC or RI
- If Phase II PI differs from Phase I PI: provide rationale for change

**Part 7: Commercialization and Business Planning** (Minimum 2 pages; Maximum 8 pages)
- **Mandatory section:** Firms not meeting minimum 2-page requirement may be considered non-responsive and not scored under Factor 4
- Must validate knowledge and ability to commercialize innovation
- Must validate company's future and financial viability
- Capital Commitments Addendum letters do not count against page limit

**Required subsections:**

1. **Market Feasibility and Competition Strategy**
   - Market segment and total addressable market (TAM)
   - Market validation method and assumptions
   - Market size in dollars
   - Market segmentation/TAM in dollars
   - Projected market share in 2-3 years post-entry
   - Target customers (NASA, other Federal agency, commercial enterprise)
   - Competitive landscape by company name
   - Barriers to entry; years for competitor entry (capitalization, technology, people)
   - Differentiation from current and future competitors

2. **Commercialization Strategy**
   - Commercial development timeline to market
   - Business model (spin-out, license, OEM, etc.)
   - Distribution channels (direct sales, distributors, etc.)
   - Pro forma 2-3 year revenue projections based on market penetration
   - Follow-on development plans (long-term > 5 years) to expand market presence
   - Risks to commercial development plan and mitigations

3. **Financial Strategy**
   - Current and future company capitalization efforts
   - Pro forma forecast: income statements, balance sheet(s), statement of cash flows
   - Forecasts showing current and projected revenues, expenses, as percentage of future sales
   - Operations/manufacturing and business staff utilization
   - Physical plant description (facilities, capital equipment, tooling, test equipment) and commercialization use
   - Consultants, incubators, research institutions to be utilized
   - Innovation production path (in-house, licensee, etc.) and capitalization changes needed
   - Past commercialization approach, path to market, and revenues from SBIR/STTR awards (disclosed in Commercial Metrics Survey)
   - Note: Companies with no SBIR/STTR awards or only recent awards not penalized on past performance

4. **Intellectual Property (IP)**
   - IP protection strategy
   - Actions to attain temporary competitive advantage
   - Company's prior IP record
   - Strategy to build sustainable business through IP protection

5. **Assistance and Mentoring**
   - Existing and future business relationships (formal partnerships, joint ventures, licensing agreements)
   - Plans for securing technical or business assistance (mentoring, partnerships, SBDCs, Federally-funded research labs, Manufacturing Extension Partnership centers, Federal programs)
   - TABA needs assessment for assistance/mentoring (reviewed separately from Commercialization Plan)

6. **Follow-on Funding Plan** (one or more required)
   - Letter of commitment for follow-on funding and/or product sales
   - Letter of commitment for matching funding for future Phase II-E application
   - Letter of capital commitment (signed by CEO, CFO, etc.) indicating commitment to provide funding/product sales if Phase II successful and market need exists
   - Specific plan to secure Phase III funding
   - Note: Letters submitted in Capital Commitments Addendum do not count toward page limits

**Part 8: Facilities/Equipment** (2 pages suggested)
- If requesting Government-furnished laboratory equipment, facilities, or services: describe why necessary and not reasonably available from private sector
- Narrative description of resources supporting proposed approach and Proposal Budget form documentation

**Part 9: Subcontracts and Consultants** (2 pages suggested)
- Description of all subcontracting and business arrangements
- Identification of relevant organizations/individuals
- Detailed expertise provided by entities
- Functions, services, hours, and labor rates
- Offeror responsible for verifying availability during proposed time periods
- Subcontract costs documented in Proposal Budget form with supporting documentation
- Narrative description supporting proposed approach and Proposal Budget form documentation

**Part 10: Related, Essentially Equivalent, and Duplicate Proposals and Awards** (0.5 page)
- **WARNING:** Permissible to submit identical/essentially equivalent proposals to multiple Federal solicitations with proper notification; unlawful to enter funding agreements requiring essentially equivalent work
- If submitting identical or substantially equivalent proposals to other Federal solicitations, must include statement with:
  - Name and address of agencies
  - Proposal submission date or award date
  - Title, number, and date of solicitations
  - Specific applicable research topics
  - Research project titles
  - PI/project manager name and title for each proposal/award
- Offerors at risk for submitting essentially equivalent proposals; strongly encouraged to disclose to soliciting agency before award
- Summary of essentially equivalent work information also required on Proposal Certifications form (if applicable)

## Key Evaluation Factors Referenced
- Factor 4: Commercialization and Business Planning (scoring based on compliance with Part 7 requirements)
- Evaluators may review Phase I final technical report to verify Phase II accuracy

## Notable Requirements
1. **Non-responsive proposals:** Omitting any of the 10 parts results in non-responsive status and possible rejection during administrative screening
2. **Minimum page requirement for Part 7:** Failure to meet minimum 2-page commercialization section may result in no score for Factor 4, leading to overall lower proposal score
3. **PI time commitment verification:** Work plan task hours for key personnel must be consistent with Proposal Budget form
4. **NASA IP usage:** Must declare planned use and submit NASA Evaluation License Application if applicable
5. **Essentially equivalent work disclosure:** Offerors must proactively disclose to mitigate risk

## Document Notes
This is a standardized template and instructions document—not a specific proposal. It contains no technical content, product information, or project-specific details. It serves as guidance for all firms submitting Phase II proposals to NASA SBIR in this submission cycle.
