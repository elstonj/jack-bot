## Executive Summary
This is the official DoD Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) X25.5 Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO) solicitation framework document. It establishes the rules, requirements, and procedures for small businesses to submit proposals in response to DoD innovation topics released throughout fiscal year 2025. The document covers three-phase SBIR awards, eligibility requirements, proposal submission procedures via the Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP), and mandatory certifications and compliance requirements.

## Program Structure

### Three-Phase SBIR Program
- **Phase I**: 6-12 months (max 12 months). Determines scientific, technical, and commercial merit/feasibility. Minimum 67% of research work must be conducted by the proposing SBC.
- **Phase II**: Generally 24 months. Principal R&D effort producing well-defined deliverable prototype. Minimum 50% of research work must be performed by proposing SBC. May receive one additional sequential Phase II award.
- **Phase III**: Non-SBIR funded commercialization. SBC must obtain funding from private sector, non-SBIR government sources, or both to develop prototype into viable product/service.

### Direct-to-Phase II (D2P2) Awards
- Available via clearly-designated D2P2 topics only
- Provides Phase II funding without requiring prior Phase I award
- Applicants must adequately document completion of Phase I feasibility requirements
- No mandatory second-phase commitments or cost-share required
- Emphasizes dual-use applications and commercialization potential

## Eligibility Requirements

### Small Business Concern (SBC) Qualification
- Must meet definition under 13 C.F.R §§ 701-705 at time of award
- Must meet Small Business Ownership and Control requirements (13 CFR § 121.702)
- Only one proposal per SBC per open topic (additional submissions marked nonresponsive)

### Performance of Work
- Phase I: Minimum 67% research/analytical work by proposing SBC (measured by direct and indirect costs)
- Phase II: Minimum 50% research/analytical work by proposing SBC
- Deviations must be approved in writing by Funding Agreement Officer

### Principal Investigator (PI) Requirements
- Primary employment with proposing SBC at time of award and during performance
- Primary employment = more than 50% of time with SBC
- Precludes full-time employment elsewhere

### Location of Work
- All R&D work must be performed in United States by SBC and subcontractors
- Rare exceptions may be approved by DoD for items/materials unavailable in U.S.

### Performance Benchmarks for Experienced Firms
- SBCs with multiple prior SBIR/STTR awards must meet minimum performance standards:
  - Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate
  - Commercialization Benchmark
- Failure to meet benchmarks results in cap of 80 total Phase I and D2P2 awards from DoD:
  - Army: 20 awards maximum
  - Navy: 20 awards maximum
  - Air Force: 20 awards maximum
  - All other DoD Components combined: 20 awards maximum

### Venture Capital/Private Equity Ownership
- SBCs owned in majority by multiple VCOCs, hedge funds, or private equity firms are eligible
- Must disclose all foreign nationals, foreign entities, foreign persons, or foreign governments holding >5% equity stake
- Must identify all ultimate parent owners and entities holding >5% equity in chain of ownership

## Proposal Submission Requirements

### DSIP (Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal)
- Mandatory submission platform; proposals submitted by other means will be disregarded
- All users require individual accounts authenticated by Login.gov
- Application accessible only from within United States territory (50 states, territories, possessions, Commonwealth of Puerto Rico, Marshall Islands, Federated States of Micronesia, Palau, District of Columbia)
- Firm Admin responsible for creating Firm PIN and maintaining firm-level forms
- Early submission strongly recommended to avoid system lag near deadline

### Submission Deadlines
- Hard deadline: No exceptions
- Proposals cannot be submitted after close date listed in Topic Release Instructions
- Proposals in "In Progress" or "Ready to Certify" status will NOT be considered submitted even if all volumes completed
- DoD not responsible for missed submissions due to system lag or inaccessibility

### Proposal Volumes

**Volume 1: Proposal Cover Sheet**
- Technical abstract (max 3,000 characters)
- Anticipated benefits and potential commercial applications discussion (max 3,000 characters)
- Must NOT include proprietary or classified information (may be publicly released if selected)
- Includes Percentage of Work (POW) certification derived from Cost Volume data
- Includes certifications on SBC eligibility and SBIR program requirements

**Volume 2: Technical Volume**
- Format and content instructions provided in Topic Release Instructions
- For D2P2 proposals: must demonstrate sufficient technical progress/problem-solving to warrant Phase II R&D

**Volume 3: Cost Volume**
- Format and instructions provided in Topic Release Instructions
- Must clearly allocate costs to show SBC meets minimum percentage of work requirements

**Volume 4: Company Commercialization Report (CCR)**
- Required for all firms with prior DoD and/or non-DoD Phase I and/or Phase II SBIR/STTR awards
- PDF must be downloaded from SBIR.gov and uploaded to DSIP Firm Forms
- Not required for firms with no prior SBIR/STTR awards
- Warning: Uploading new CCR reopens all previously certified proposals under open BAAs/CSOs; applicant must recertify/resubmit

**Volume 5: Supporting Documents**
- Letters of support
- Additional cost information
- Funding agreement certification
- Technical data rights assertions
- Lifecycle certification
- Allocation of rights
- Joint venture eligibility verification
- DD Form 2345 (Military Critical Technical Data Agreement) if applicable

**Volume 6: Fraud, Waste, and Abuse (FWA) Training**
- 3-page PDF training material (review required once per year)
- Must be thoroughly reviewed by designated Proposal Owner
- Covers FWA definition, common mistakes, penalties, and prevention

**Volume 7: Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries**
- Webform completion mandatory (PDF uploads no longer accepted)
- Corporate official cannot certify/submit proposal until fully completed
- Must not be uploaded as supporting document in Volume 5

## Registrations and Certifications

### System for Award Management (SAM) Registration
- Required before DoD contract award
- Generates Unique Entity ID (UEI) and Commercial and Government Entry (CAGE) code
- Must reflect ALL AWARDS for Purpose of Registration; grants-only registration ineligible
- Active registration and current representations/certifications required to avoid award delays

### SBA Company Registry
- Proposing SBCs must be registered
- Must provide SBC Control ID and Proof of Registration/Certification during submission

### DSIP Registration
- Individual accounts required for all SBC personnel preparing/submitting proposals
- First-time submitters must register
- Recommended to register early upon opportunity identification

### Required Certifications
- SBCs must certify at proposal submission they meet SBIR program requirements
- Certifications required at Phase I award, Phase II award, prior to final payment on Phase I, prior to 50% Phase II payment, and prior to final Phase II payment
- Certifications required under SBIR and STTR Extension Act of 2022 (Public Law 117-183)

## Security, Compliance, and Legal Requirements

### Due Diligence Program to Assess National Security Risks
- DoD conducts risk-based due diligence review per 15 U.S.C. §638(vv)
- Uses proposal and "Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations" responses
- Assessment areas:
  - Cybersecurity practices
  - Patent analysis
  - Employee analysis and foreign ownership
  - SBC's financial ties and obligations (surety, equity, debt)
  - SBC employees' ties to foreign country/person/entity
- DoD may use open-source analysis and analytical tools to confirm accuracy of disclosures
- DoD may request true copies of contractual/financial obligations with foreign-owned enterprises
- Unacceptable national security risks result in proposal rejection regardless of technical merit
- Project Spectrum provides online course: "Understanding Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence (FOCI)"

### Export-Controlled Technologies
- Proposals with solutions under ITAR or EAR must include:
  - Certified DD Form 2345 (Militarily Critical Technical Data Agreement), OR
  - Evidence of DD Form 2345 application submission
- PI must be U.S. citizen or lawful permanent resident for export-controlled proposals
- DFARS 252.225-7048 (Export-Controlled Items) clause included in all resulting awards

### Classified Proposals
- Classified proposals NOT permitted
- If Phase II requires classified work, applicant must have or obtain facility clearance
- Facility and personnel clearance procedures available at Defense Counterintelligence and Security Agency (http://www.dss.mil/)

### Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI)
- SBCs required to identify and disclose all facts relevant to potential OCIs per FAR 9.5
- Must include OCI mitigation plan addressing:
  - Conflicting roles that might bias judgment
  - Unfair competitive advantage prevention
  - Discussion in context of FAR 9.505-1 through 9.505-4 limitations
- Must disclose if SBC or team members provide SETA, A&AS, or similar support to DoD under:
  - Current awards/sub-awards, OR
  - Past awards/sub-awards ending within one calendar year of submission
- Disclosure must include: DoD Service/Component office name, prime contract number, team member identification, and OCI mitigation plan
- Government evaluates mitigation plans and may require additional information or grant waiver
- Failure to adequately disclose or mitigate OCIs may result in proposal rejection

### Research Involving Human Subjects
- Must comply with applicable federal/state laws and agency policy
- Requires documentation of current federal assurance of compliance (e.g., HHS Office for Human Research Protections Federalwide Assurance)
- All personnel must complete appropriate human subjects protection training
- Exemption criteria under 32 CFR 219.101 do not require federal assurance
- IRB approval required (unless exemption criteria met)
- Average IRB approval process: 1-3 months
- DoD review of protocol and IRB determination: 3-6 months
- No funding for human subject research until ALL approvals granted

### Research Involving Recombinant DNA Molecules
- Addressed in solicitation but details deferred to Topic Release Instructions

### Use of Unmanned Aircraft Systems (UAS)
- UAS use addressed in solicitation; details in Topic Release Instructions

### Joint Ventures
- Eligible if created entity qualifies as SBC per 13 U.S.C. § 121.701
- Must disclose foreign entity/foreign government-controlled company relationships
- Must submit required representations to Volume 5

### Conflicts of Interest for Government Employees
- Contract awards to SBCs owned by or employing current/previous federal employees may create conflicts of interest
- Potential violation of federal law

## Technology and Treatment of Commercial Items

### Commercial Off-the-Shelf (COTS) and Non-Developmental Items (NDI)
- Items purchased from commercial source without alteration/modification not suitable for SBIR/STTR funding
- Includes ready-to-use products, training services, ready-to-use software licenses
- COTS/NDI first article and associated testing not fundable
- Exception: If end item requires design/development to accept COTS/NDI, design/development effort may be fundable
- COTS/NDI for RDT&E test purposes may be fundable

### Modified COTS/Modified NDI
- Commercially available items requiring modification to satisfy user requirements eligible
- First article, modification of first article, and first article testing fundable
- Follow-on purchases NOT fundable
- Quantity of "modified" first articles bought with SBIR funds limited to R
