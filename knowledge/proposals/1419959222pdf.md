## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies developed the CAPS Multi-hole Probe during Phase I of a NASA SBIR program to measure 3D winds, pressure, temperature, and humidity from small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS). The innovation combines a compact multi-hole pressure probe with integrated thermodynamic sensors in a lightweight, robust package specifically designed for hand-launched sUAS operations.

## Technical Approach
The CAPS Multi-hole Probe employs the following technical design:

- **Probe Head**: 1-inch radius hemispherical design with 5 pressure ports
- **Wind Measurement**: Center hole measures total pressure; comparison with static pressure determines airspeed; four offset ports (45-degree spacing) measure angle of attack and sideslip angle
- **Pressure Sensors**: 
  - SM9541 sensors in 20cm H₂O version for airspeed measurement
  - SM9541 sensors in 10cm H₂O version for increased resolution on angle of attack/sideslip
- **Additional Measurements**: MS5607 sensor provides high-resolution static pressure, temperature, and humidity
- **Manufacturing**: Laser-cut scaffolding guides drilling for accurate port placement
- **Electronics**: Custom sensor board with integrated electronics; outputs on CAN bus for autopilot/data logger integration

## Products & Capabilities Described

### CAPS Multi-hole Probe
- **What it is**: A compact atmospheric sensor package measuring 2" × 4" and weighing 1.5 oz
- **Measurements**: 3D wind vectors (airspeed, angle of attack, sideslip), static pressure, temperature, humidity
- **Integration**: Modular nose cone design for field swapping in sub-5-lb sUAS
- **Design advantage**: Blunt, integrated design doesn't protrude from airframe, improving robustness and reducing ground-handling damage risk
- **Key components**: SM9541 dynamic pressure sensors, MS5607 thermodynamic sensor, custom PCB

## Use Cases & Applications
- Local and global weather model validation
- Dynamic small-scale atmospheric phenomenon measurement
- Severe weather prediction (6-24 hour forecasts for convection-driven events)
- High-risk missions where sUAS loss is probable (low-cost replacement important)
- Arctic and other unimproved field operations

**Target customers identified**: NASA, NOAA, NSF, UCAR/NCAR (specifically Mesoscale Predictability Experiment - MPEX)
