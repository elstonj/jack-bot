# NOAA FY25 NOFO General Information Sheet

## Document Metadata
- Type: Guidance document / Information sheet
- Client/Agency: NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration), Weather and Water Prediction (WPO) Program
- Program/Solicitation: FY2025 Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO)
- Date: 2024 (created/modified September 30, 2024)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None
- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is a comprehensive guidance document from NOAA's Weather and Water Prediction (WPO) program clarifying and expanding upon the FY2025 Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO). It provides applicants with detailed instructions on proposal preparation, submission requirements, registration processes, and special program initiatives to support federal funding competition for weather and water prediction research.

## Document Content Overview

This document does **not contain information about Black Swift Technologies or BST products/capabilities**. Instead, it is a general informational guide for applicants to the NOAA FY25 NOFO covering:

### Key Sections:

**Administrative & Submission Guidance:**
- Federal institution eligibility and cost restrictions (max 20% federal overhead)
- Cooperative Institutes (CI) treatment as university employees
- Required system registrations: SAM.gov, Grants.gov, eRA Commons
- eRA validation errors and common submission issues
- Proposal title page and abstract requirements
- Standard federal forms (SF-424, SF-424A, SF-424B, CD-511, SF-LLL)

**NOAA Testbeds:**
- NOAA Testbeds allow research outputs (new observing systems, data products, forecast techniques) to be evaluated for operational implementation in NWS forecast offices
- Applicants must coordinate with appropriate Testbed managers prior to submission
- Testbed Collaboration Forms required if leveraging Testbed resources

**Research Requirements:**
- Readiness Levels (RL1-RL9) ranging from basic research to deployment
- NOAA Readiness Levels used to evaluate project maturity
- Research Transition Plan required for projects at RL4+ or planning post-award transition
- Unified Forecast System (UFS) focus for forecast model improvement projects
- High-Performance Computing (HPC) resource availability and guidance
- DESI (Dynamic Ensemble-based Scenarios for Impact-based Decision Support) tool information

**Special Initiatives & Compliance:**
- Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and Accessibility (DEIA) requirements - formal statement mandatory
- Minority Serving Institutions (MSIs) emphasis and definitions (HBCUs, HSIs, TCCUs, AANAPISIs, ANNHSIs)
- Student opportunity encouragement
- Human subjects research (IRB approval required)
- OMB Paperwork Reduction Act (PRA) clearance requirements
- Invention disclosure to NOAA Technology Partnerships Office

**Project Products & Outputs Examples:**
- Instruments, sensors, observing platforms
- Tools, widgets, technologies
- Model codes, software, algorithms
- Published datasets/databases
- Reports and recommendations
- Methodologies
- Inventions and patents
- Audio/video products
- Outreach and training events
- Publications and presentations

**Expected Operational Outcomes:**
- Improvements in detection, accuracy, reliability, coverage, latency, leadtime, skill, processing speed, efficiency, cost, knowledge

**Societal Benefits:**
- Increased scientific literacy
- Improved weather hazard awareness
- Better access to weather information
- Life-saving information delivery
- Enhanced disaster resilience and preparation
- Informed decision-making during extreme weather
- National security improvements
- Economic competitiveness

### Notable Details:
- Emphasis on meaningful engagement with operational collaborators (NWS forecasters, emergency managers)
- Letter of Intent submission deadline: September 18, 2024 at 5:00 PM ET
- Multiple validation checks (Grants.gov + eRA) required before successful submission
- eRA Commons registration can take weeks to approve
- Common technical issues: PDF requirements, file sizes, file naming conventions
- Three business day minimum for validation/rejection after submission
- All registrations (SAM, Grants.gov, eRA) must be in place by application deadline
