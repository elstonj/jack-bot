# Model Agreement for the Allocation of Rights

## Document Metadata
- Type: Template/Model Agreement
- Program/Solicitation: Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) Program
- Date: September 14, 2020 (last modified); February 3, 2021 (created)
- Context: BST Phase I proposal submission, 2020.3

## Executive Summary
This is a model agreement template from the STTR program designed to govern intellectual property rights allocation between a small business concern (SBC) and a research institution (RI) collaborating on an STTR-funded project. The template establishes frameworks for handling background IP, project IP ownership, commercialization rights, and follow-on development.

## Document Purpose & Applicability
This is a blank template document provided by the STTR program to guide parties in negotiating IP rights allocation agreements. It is not a completed agreement specific to Black Swift Technologies, but rather a supporting document for BST's Phase I STTR proposal submission.

## Key Sections & Provisions

### Background Intellectual Property (Section 2)
- Defines pre-existing IP developed before or independent of the agreement
- Allows SBC to use RI's Background IP nonexclusively in R&D activities without compensation
- Allows SBC to use RI's Background IP in commercialization with negotiated royalty rates (percentage of net sales/leases)
- Template includes blank fields for specifying which Background IP is available and royalty percentages

### Project Intellectual Property (Section 3)
- Defines IP created during STTR project performance
- Ownership vests in the party whose personnel conceived or first reduced to practice the invention
- Jointly created IP is jointly owned; SBC has first option to perfect rights in joint IP
- Template provides blanks for allocating ownership percentages and development/marketing expense percentages
- Government receives irrevocable, royalty-free, nonexclusive license for governmental purposes
- SBC has exclusive option to commercialize RI's Project IP

### SBC Commercialization Options (Section 3(f))
**For patentable inventions:**
- Initial option period of [blank] months from invention report
- Extendable for additional [blank] months with written notice
- SBC reimburses RI for patent prosecution/maintenance expenses within [blank] days of invoice
- If exercised, leads to negotiated license for making/using/selling products
- License includes reasonable royalties, patent expense reimbursement, and (for exclusive licenses) commercialization milestones

**For non-patentable Project IP:**
- Exclusive option period extends until [blank] months after RI completes that phase
- Similar negotiation and licensing framework
- Includes reasonable royalties and commercialization milestones for exclusive licenses

### Multi-Royalty Management (Section 3(3))
- Parties agree to negotiate in good faith to cap or discount total royalties if multiple royalty streams threaten commercial viability

### Follow-on Work (Section 4)
- All future licenses, contracts, subcontracts, and arrangements must preserve IP rights allocated in this agreement
- Must ensure PARTIES and Government obtain/retain granted rights

### Confidentiality & Publication (Section 5)
- Disclosing party's proprietary information protected from use/disclosure
- Standard exceptions apply (public domain, independent development, independent source)
- Publishing party grants right of refusal to other party
- [Blank] day review period before publication
- Additional [blank] day deferral available for patent application filing

### Liability & Indemnification (Section 6)
- Each party disclaims all warranties (merchantability, fitness, non-infringement)
- SBC indemnifies RI regarding commercialization claims
- Both parties indemnify Government for commercialization-related claims

### Termination (Section 7)
- Either party may terminate with [blank] days written notice
- Termination also available for material breach
- Each party responsible for its proportional share of costs
- Confidentiality obligations survive termination

## Notable Details
- This is a **blank template** with numerous unfilled blanks for option periods, royalty percentages, notification timelines, and allocation percentages
- The document references 37 CFR 401 (federal patent regulations applicable to STTR awards)
- Emphasizes good faith negotiation between parties on key IP matters
- Protects government's interest through mandatory license rights
- Designed to balance SBC commercialization incentives with RI's IP protections
- Includes provisions for both technical IP (patents, software) and business IP (trade secrets, trademarks, mask works)

## BST Context
This template was included as supporting documentation in BST's 2020.3 Phase I STTR proposal submission. It would be used by BST if partnering with a research institution on an STTR project, establishing the IP framework between BST (as the SBC) and an academic or non-profit RI partner.
