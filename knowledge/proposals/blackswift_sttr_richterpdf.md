# Navy STTR Phase II Letter of Support: Expendable Air-Sea Profiling Observations in Hazardous Weather

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy / Office of Naval Research (ONR)
- Program/Solicitation: Navy STTR 25.A-T025: "Expendable Air-sea Profiling Observations in Hazardous Weather Conditions"
- Date: December 29, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 UAS (unmanned aircraft system)
- Key Personnel: Dr. David Richter (University of Notre Dame, Letter author); Beck Cotter (Last editor of BST document)

## Executive Summary
Dr. David Richter from the University of Notre Dame provides institutional support for Black Swift Technologies' Navy STTR Phase II proposal to develop expendable atmospheric profiling systems for hazardous weather operations. The letter emphasizes BST's proven S0 UAS platform reliability, data accuracy commitment, and cost-effective design as critical advantages for meeting Navy and ONR research objectives in air-sea interaction studies.

## Technical Approach
BST's proposed work focuses on:
- Enhancing data processing capabilities for the S0 UAS
- Integrating alternative wave measurement technologies
- Addressing icing challenges in hazardous environments
- Improving communication systems for remote operations
- Leveraging the S0 platform's proven ability to operate in turbulent conditions

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0 UAS (Unmanned Aircraft System)**
- **What it is:** Expendable atmospheric profiling aircraft system designed for hazardous weather operations
- **Proven capabilities:** Successful deployment in hurricane observations; proven reliability in turbulent conditions
- **Key attributes:** Designed with emphasis on simplicity and affordability
- **Measurement focus:** Wind measurement accuracy and atmospheric profiling in marine environments
- **Data validation:** Measurements validated against instrumented towers and dropsondes from NOAA P-3 aircraft

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Air-sea interaction research:** Meteorological and turbulence measurements in hazardous marine environments
- **Hurricane observations:** Demonstrated operational use in hurricane sampling and monitoring
- **Navy operational support:** Cost-effective alternative for frequent and widespread deployments in challenging conditions
- **Academic research:** Support for ONR MURI "SASCWATCH: Study on Air-Sea Coupling with Waves, Turbulence, and Clouds at High Winds"

## Notable Details
- **Research Alignment:** Proposal directly aligns with ONR's MURI program "SASCWATCH" led by Dr. Richter
- **Competitive Positioning:** Letter explicitly compares BST S0 favorably to Raytheon Coyote and Area-I/Anduril Altius platforms in terms of cost-effectiveness
- **Data Quality Commitment:** BST has demonstrated rigorous approach to characterizing and mitigating measurement uncertainties through calibration and validation
- **Institutional Support:** University of Notre Dame and full SASCWATCH team offer support for the work
- **Cost Advantage:** Design philosophy emphasizing simplicity and affordability enables more frequent deployments than competitor systems, yielding richer research datasets
