## Executive Summary
This is Amendment 1 to the DAF STTR AFX24.E Phase I Commercial Solutions Opening solicitation. The amendment clarifies applicant responsibilities regarding NIST SP 800-171 cybersecurity assessments and adds disqualification language related to failure to publish assessment scores to the Supplier Performance Risk System (SPRS) in PIEE. The solicitation seeks innovative, commercial dual-purpose technologies adaptable to Air Force needs with clear commercial viability and defense applications.

## Document Type & Scope
This is a solicitation document (not a Black Swift Technologies proposal or report). It outlines the requirements for companies seeking STTR Phase I funding from the Department of the Air Force. **This document does not contain information about BST's products, capabilities, or proposals.**

## Amendment 1 - Key Changes

### NIST SP 800-171 Assessment Requirements
Added detailed instructions for mandatory cybersecurity self-assessment:
- Applicants must complete NIST SP 800-171 self-assessment via https://www.projectspectrum.io/#/cyberreadiness-Check
- Must save assessment document with date submitted
- Must submit confirmation email to WEBPTSMH.fct@navy.mil with required information per DFARS 252.204-7019(d)(1)
- Must confirm assessment score is published in PIEE's SPRS module
- **Failure to comply results in proposal disqualification**

### Eligibility & Performance Requirements

**Company Size:**
- Small Business Concern (SBC) with affiliates: ≤500 employees

**Ownership Requirements:**
- Must be >50% directly owned and controlled by U.S. citizens/permanent residents, small businesses, or Indian tribes
- Majority ownership by multiple venture capital/private equity/hedge funds is prohibited
- Single VCOC/hedge fund/PE firm ownership allowed only if they themselves are eligible SBCs

**Work Performance Requirements (Both Phase I & II):**
- Minimum 40% R/R&D work performed by Small Business Concern
- Minimum 30% R/R&D work performed by single Research Institution partner
- Work must be performed in the United States (rare exceptions may be granted for unavailable supplies/materials)

**Principal Investigator:**
- Must have primary employment (>50% time) with SBC or partnering Research Institution
- Only one PI per proposal
- Primary employment precludes full-time work elsewhere

**Research Institution Definition:**
- Non-profit organization (owned/operated for scientific or educational purposes, no private benefit)
- OR Federally-Funded R/D Center (FFRDC) identified by NSF
- Must have U.S. place of business and operate primarily within U.S. or significantly contribute to U.S. economy

## Evaluation Criteria (Equal Weight)

### a. Commercialization Potential
- Company's history of commercializing SBIR/STTR or other research (pre-sales, pilots, revenue, users, subscriptions, downloads, adoption metrics)
- Market size and revenue potential
- Clarity of business plan
- Existence of Phase II follow-on or funding commitments (from Government or private sources documented via letters)

### b. Defense Need
- Appropriateness, relevance, and specificity of identified Defense Need
- Signed letters of support from Government personnel strengthen this criterion
- Adequacy of proposed effort in relation to fulfilling the Defense Need
- Level of defense mission impact and urgency
- Breadth of applicability of solution

### c. Technical Approach
- Clarity of problem statement
- Soundness and technical merit of approach
- Innovation and differentiation from current alternatives
- Qualifications of Principal Investigator, Project Manager, supporting staff, and consultants

## Disqualification Triggers

Common reasons for proposal disqualification include:
- Improper SAM.gov registration at submission/award
- Incorrect allocation of minimum Performance Percentage of Work
- Work does not meet R/R&D definition
- Late submission
- Exceeds maximum funding amount ($110,000)
- Exceeds maximum duration (90 days)
- Does not meet STTR eligibility requirements (ownership/size)
- Previous award for "Essentially Equivalent Work"
- **Failure to submit NIST SP 800-171 Assessment Documentation**
- **Failure to ensure NIST score is published in PIEE's SPRS module per DFARS 252.204-7019**

## Foreign Ownership & Security Considerations

### Denial of Award Criteria
DAF will not award if applicant:
- Has owner/covered individual in malign foreign talent recruitment program
- Has business entity, parent company, or subsidiary in PRC or foreign country of concern
- Has owner/covered individual with foreign affiliation to PRC or foreign country of concern
- AND these relationships:
  - Interfere with DoD-supported activity capacity
  - Create duplication with DoD-supported activities
  - Present conflicts of interest
  - Were not appropriately disclosed
  - Violate Federal law or contract terms
  - Pose risk to national security

### Non-U.S. Citizen Work Restrictions
For ITAR-restricted work, non-U.S. citizens may work only if they are:
- "Lawfully Admitted for Permanent Residence" (per 8 U.S.C. 1101(20)), OR
- "Protected Individuals" (per 8 U.S.C. 1324b(a)(3) - includes U.S. citizens, permanent residents, refugees, asylees, those with temporary protected status)

Proposals with non-qualifying non-U.S. citizen participants may be rejected.

## Phase I vs. Phase II Objectives

### Phase I (90 days, $110,000 max)
- Determine scientific/technical merit and feasibility
- Validate product-market fit with Air Force stakeholder
- Clearly identify potential stakeholders
- Describe pathway to AF integration (technology development, regulatory navigation, systems integration)
- Describe applicability to other DoD/Government customers

### Phase II
- Development, installation, integration, demonstration, test/evaluation of prototype
- Evaluate solution against proposed objectives and measurable key results
- Detail how solution differs from commercial offering and can be scaled
- Identify clear transition path with stakeholder input (users, engineering, sustainment, contracting, finance, legal, cybersecurity)
- Specify integration with current/future solutions
- Describe sustainability and supportability
- Identify other DoD/Government customer opportunities

### Phase III
- Transition adapted non-Defense commercial solution to provide expanded mission capability
- Serve Governmental and civilian users with alternate mission applications

## Additional Key Requirements

**Research/Development Definition:**
Work must constitute R/R&D: systematic, intensive study directed toward greater knowledge; systematic study to apply new knowledge to recognized need; or systematic application of knowledge toward production of useful materials, devices, systems, methods, including design, development, improvement of prototypes and processes.

**Essentially Equivalent Work:**
- Applicants may submit identical proposals to multiple Federal solicitations with proper notification
- However, it is unlawful to enter into multiple contracts/awards for essentially equivalent effort
- Prior, current, or pending similar support must be disclosed early

**Export Control Compliance:**
- Proposed work may be ITAR-restricted
- Applicants must review U.S. Munitions List (22 CFR 121)
- If applicable, must submit certified DD Form 2345 (Military Critical Technology Agreement)
- Export control compliance statements do not remove applicant liability for ITAR/EAR compliance

## Selection Methodology

- **Peer Review:** Proposals screened for responsiveness, then peer reviewed by engineers/scientists
- **Multiple Awards Planned:** DAF may award all, some, or none of proposals received
- **Independent Merit Evaluation:** Each proposal evaluated on its own merits regardless of other submissions
- **Due Diligence Security Review:** DoD assesses security risks using open-source analysis, patent analysis, employee analysis, foreign ownership review, and financial ties to foreign entities
- **Mitigation or Rejection:** If risks identified, DoD may create mitigation plan or reject proposal based on totality of circumstances

## Notable Details

1. **Open Topic Nature:** This is a truly "open" solicitation (agnostic of industry, technology, problem area) seeking varying technical/scientific approaches and new technologies adaptable to AF needs.

2. **Commercial-to-Defense Focus:** Explicitly seeks non-defense commercial solutions to be adapted for Air Force use—not new development of defense-specific technologies.

3. **Accelerated Schedule:** Aimed at applied R/D with feasibility studies and prototype-validated concepts under accelerated Phase I/II schedules, not "front-end" basic R/R&D.

4. **High Priority Areas:** Per Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007, high priority given to energy efficiency or renewable energy R/D projects.

5. **Manufacturing Priority:** Per Executive Order 13329, high priority for proposals advancing innovation in U.S. small business manufacturing.

6. **No Classified Proposals:** Classified proposals are NOT accepted.
