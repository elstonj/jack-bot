# BNSF Railway Company Certificate of Waiver or Authorization (CoW)

## Document Metadata
- Type: FAA Certificate of Waiver or Authorization (CoW)
- Client/Agency: BNSF Railway Company; FAA (General Aviation and Commercial Division, AFS-800)
- Program/Solicitation: FAA Section 333 Exemption Petition (Docket FAA-2014-0704-0010)
- Date: Issued August 29, 2016; Effective through August 31, 2020
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None
- Key Personnel: Todd Graetz (BNSF Responsible Person)

## Executive Summary
This FAA Certificate of Waiver authorizes BNSF Railway Company to operate small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS) under conditions that would normally be prohibited, including night operations, beyond visual line of sight of the remote pilot (but within visual observer line of sight), and in reduced visibility/weather conditions. Operations are confined to BNSF property and right-of-ways at or below 200 feet AGL.

## Technical Approach
Not applicable. This is a regulatory authorization document, not a technical proposal. However, it references BNSF's "unique Concept of Operations and Operational Risk Assessment" based on prior flight testing, operational history, and flight experience that established adequate safety mitigations for the proposed operations.

## Regulatory Waivers Granted
The following CFR sections were waived:
- **14 CFR §107.29** (Daylight operation requirement) – allows night operations
- **14 CFR §107.31** (Visual line of sight requirement) – allows operations beyond pilot VLOS if within visual observer VLOS
- **14 CFR §107.33(b)** (Visual observer specification) – relieves pilot of ensuring observer can see aircraft per §107.31 requirements
- **14 CFR §107.51(c) and (d)** (Operating limitations) – allows reduced visibility and reduced cloud clearance minimums

## Operational Parameters Authorized

**Geographic/Airspace Restrictions:**
- Operations limited to BNSF property and right-of-ways only
- Class G airspace only (unless additional FAA authorization obtained)
- Maximum altitude: 200 feet AGL (unless within 400-foot radius of structure, then up to 200 feet above structure)
- Emergency exceptions: Operations over non-BNSF property permitted only in response to emergencies involving BNSF property/nearby persons

**Night Operations (Section B):**
- Requires one or more visual observers
- sUA must have anti-collision lighting visible from minimum 3 statute miles
- Minimum visibility: 3 statute miles
- Cloud clearance: minimum 500 feet below or 2,000 feet horizontally from clouds

**Beyond Visual Line of Sight Operations (Section C):**
- sUA must remain within visual line of sight of at least one visual observer at all times (except takeoff/landing)
- Remote pilot must determine correct number and placement of visual observers
- sUA must have high visibility exterior features
- Command and Control system must have demonstrated performance capabilities for planned range

**Reduced Weather Minimums (Section D):**
- Minimum visibility: 1 statute mile (reduced from standard 3 statute miles)
- sUA must have high visibility features and anti-collision lighting (3 statute mile visibility)
- Must remain clear of clouds; visual line of sight not to be hampered by clouds

## Operational Requirements & Provisions

**Common Requirements (Sections A & ongoing):**
- Todd Graetz holds direct responsibility for safety
- All pilots must maintain current remote pilot certificates; list must be maintained and presented for FAA inspection
- All sUA must be registered; registration numbers must be maintained and presented for inspection
- All flight participants must be trained on contingency profiles and emergency procedures (documented)
- Copy of waiver must be available during operations
- Waiver is non-transferable and may be canceled at any time by FAA if safety concerns arise or terms are violated

**Personnel Training Requirements:**
- Night vision training: Personnel must be trained to recognize and overcome visual illusions caused by darkness and understand physiological conditions degrading night vision
- Communication protocols: Continuous communication between visual observer and remote pilot must allow sufficient time for pilot to maneuver aircraft and yield right-of-way per §107.37
- Emergency procedures: All participants must be trained and documented

## Notable Details

**Regulatory Context:**
- This waiver represents approval under Part 107 (Small Unmanned Aircraft Systems Rule) rather than Section 333 exemption authority, issued to expedite approval while ensuring compliance with 14 CFR §107.200 safety requirements
- FAA determined that BNSF's operational risk assessment and prior flight testing data established adequate safety mitigations
- Effective period: August 29, 2016 – August 31, 2020 (4-year authorization)

**Operational Significance:**
- Grants BNSF broad authority for advanced sUAS operations (night, BVLOS, reduced weather) unusual for 2016 regulatory environment
- Reflects FAA's recognition of BNSF's operational maturity and safety culture based on demonstrated experience
- Likely intended for railroad inspection, monitoring, and emergency response operations along BNSF's extensive right-of-way network
