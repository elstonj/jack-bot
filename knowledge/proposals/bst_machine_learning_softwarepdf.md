# BST Machine Learning Software for Predictive UAS Maintenance

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Interest/Support
- Client/Agency: U.S. Air Force, 60th Security Forces Squadron (AMC), Travis AFB
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Phase II (Proposal #F2-14757)
- Date: June 9, 2021
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Predictive maintenance system for small UAS
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (Black Swift Technologies); Kenneth M. Perkins (USAF S5: Chief, Plans and Programs)

## Executive Summary
This is a letter of support from the 60th Air Mobility Wing UAS Working Group endorsing BST's proposed predictive maintenance software system for small unmanned aircraft systems. The letter validates market need and emphasizes the system's importance for enabling safety-critical UAS operations including beyond-visual-line-of-sight (BVLOS) flights and operations over crowds.

## Technical Approach
BST proposed to develop a predictive maintenance system utilizing **unsupervised machine learning algorithms** to:
- Provide early warning of potential critical system failures on small UAS
- Deliver diagnostic capabilities for maintenance prediction
- Enable data-driven maintenance scheduling to improve aircraft availability and safety

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Predictive Maintenance Software System:**
- Uses unsupervised ML algorithms for early failure detection
- Provides diagnostics for small UAS
- Represents improvement over current state-of-the-art
- Target: small unmanned aircraft systems (UAS)

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Beyond-Visual-Line-of-Sight (BVLOS) Operations:** System enables safer BVLOS flights by providing maintenance assurance
- **Operations Over Crowds:** Safety assurance needed for UAS flights in proximity to people
- **Military Airfield Operations:** Aircraft maintenance inspections and health monitoring for operations near military aircraft
- **Installation-Wide UAS Scaling:** Support for 60th Air Mobility Wing's expansion of UAS operations across Travis AFB

## Key Requirements Addressed
- **Safety Enhancement:** System addresses critical need to increase safety as UAS operations scale
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Supports movement toward safety-critical applications by providing technical justification for BVLOS operations
- **Maintenance Rigor:** Aligns with rigorous safety practices required for airfield operations in close proximity to military aircraft
- **Operational Necessity:** Letter states predictive maintenance will become "a necessity" for routine UAS operations without risk to persons or property

## Notable Details
- The 60th Air Mobility Wing UAS Working Group had specifically discussed this need with BST prior to the proposal submission
- Current BVLOS restrictions mentioned as limiting operational capability
- Letter emphasizes this is a "valid market need" and "significant improvement upon current state-of-the art"
- Focus on small UAS platform
- This is a Phase II SBIR proposal, indicating Phase I work had been completed previously
