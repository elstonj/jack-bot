# QuSpin Letter of Support for Navy Phase II SBIR: Sonobuoy-Launched UAS Magnetometer

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support (for SBIR proposal)
- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy
- Program/Solicitation: Navy Phase II SBIR - Sonobuoy-Launched Unmanned Aerial Vehicle for ASW (Anti-Submarine Warfare)
- Date: December 18, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 platform, SL UAS (Sonobuoy-Launched UAS)
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (BST); Jeff Orton (QuSpin Lead QTFM Engineer)
- Partner Organization: QuSpin Inc. (Louisville, CO)

## Executive Summary
QuSpin Inc. provides technical support for Black Swift Technologies' Navy Phase II SBIR proposal to develop an expendable sonobuoy-launched unmanned aerial vehicle equipped with Magnetic Anomaly Detection (MAD) and passive acoustic sensors for anti-submarine warfare. Following successful Phase I feasibility work, QuSpin commits to providing their Gen-2 QTFM magnetometer and integration support for full-scale prototype development.

## Technical Approach
- Integration of QuSpin's Gen-2 QTFM magnetometer into the S0 platform (sonobuoy-launched UAV)
- Integration of passive acoustic sensors alongside MAD capability
- QuSpin to provide technical expertise for sensor integration, laboratory validation, and data processing optimization
- Target performance: achieve Navy requirement of 20 pT/√Hz noise spectral density in 0.01–100 Hz frequency range

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Gen-2 QTFM Magnetometer (QuSpin)
- **What it is**: State-of-the-art quantum tuned flux magnetometer
- **Specifications**: Noise spectral density <1 pT/√Hz
- **Proposed Use**: Integration into SL UAS for Magnetic Anomaly Detection in maritime surveillance
- **Design Features**: Robust construction demonstrated in laboratory and field settings; compatible with mobile platforms; reliable under harsh conditions

### S0 Platform (BST)
- **What it is**: Sonobuoy-launched expendable unmanned aerial vehicle
- **Proposed Use**: Persistent maritime surveillance for anti-submarine warfare
- **Sensor Integration**: Equipped with MAD (via Gen-2 QTFM) and passive acoustic sensors

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Mission**: Anti-submarine warfare (ASW) - persistent maritime surveillance
- **Cost-effectiveness**: Described as cost-effective solution for maritime operations
- **Deployment Context**: Operational deployment by U.S. Navy

- Phase I work successfully established feasibility of integrating MAD and passive acoustic sensors into S0 platform
- Phase II focuses on fabrication and validation of full-scale prototypes
