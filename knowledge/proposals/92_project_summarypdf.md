# The S0 UAS, an Air Deployed Solution for Boundary Layer Observations in Turbulent Environments

- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (CEO, Principal Investigator)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed to develop the S0, a low-cost, air-deployed unmanned aircraft system designed to sample boundary layer kinematics and thermodynamics in turbulent environments, particularly for tropical storm observation. The S0 aims to overcome cost, reliability, and observation time limitations of existing airborne-deployed UAS platforms while maintaining performance and measurement quality.

## Technical Approach
The S0 development approach builds on proven successes of the Aerosonde and Coyote platforms. The key innovation strategy is reducing complexity and weight compared to existing platforms to achieve an order of magnitude cost decrease. The design emphasizes simplicity and ease of use while maintaining endurance and measurement quality.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS
- **What it is:** A commercial, air-deployed unmanned aircraft system for boundary layer and sea surface observations
- **Intended use:** Deployment from manned aircraft to sample kinematics and thermodynamics of the lower boundary layer, particularly in tropical storms and turbulent environments
- **Specifications/Performance Goals:**
  - Target price: ~$5,000
  - Endurance: 1 hour minimum
  - Measurement capabilities: 3D wind vectors, PTH (pressure, temperature, humidity)
  - Sea surface characterization: laser altimeter and thermal IR sensor
  - Height measurement from water surface
  - AVAPS telemetry compatibility
  - Simple user interface for pre-programming

## Use Cases & Applications
- Tropical storm boundary layer observations
- Improved hurricane/tropical cyclone forecasting and modeling
- Sea surface temperature and characterization measurement
- Lower boundary layer kinematics and thermodynamics sampling in turbulent conditions
- Mobile, cost-effective alternative to land-based UAS operations for distant or rapidly evolving storm systems

## Key Challenges Addressed
- Regulatory obstacles limiting land-based UAS storm sampling
- Distance requirements to intercept evolving storms
- High platform costs of existing air-deployed systems
- Reliability issues with current platforms
- Limited observation time from existing systems
- Need for cost-effective, reliable platforms with adequate endurance

## Notable Details
- Builds on previous successful UAS tropical storm observation efforts that demonstrated value for forecast/model improvement
- Air deployment approach overcomes some limitations of land-based operations
- Proposes integration with existing AVAPS (Airborne Vertical Atmosphere Profiling System) telemetry infrastructure
- Emphasis on commercial viability and ease of use for operational deployment
- Targets order of magnitude cost reduction (to ~$5,000) compared to existing platforms while maintaining performance
