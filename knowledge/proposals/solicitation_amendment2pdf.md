## Document Metadata
- Type: SBIR/STTR Solicitation Amendment
- Client/Agency: Department of the Air Force (DAF), in partnership with AFWERX
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR AFX24.7 PHASE I (Commercial Solutions Opening)
- Date: October 7, 2024 (Amendment 2 issued)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None mentioned
- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor), Daniel J. Brewer (DAF SBIR/STTR Contracting Officer)

## Executive Summary
Amendment 2 to the DAF SBIR AFX24.7 Phase I solicitation clarifies requirements regarding NIST SP 800-171 cybersecurity assessments. The amendment removes disqualification language from Amendment 1 and clarifies that applicants must complete NIST assessments and upload scores to the SPRS module by January 6th, 2025, but do not need to submit assessment documentation as a supporting document.

## Document Content
This is an official solicitation amendment document, not a Black Swift Technologies proposal or report. It contains:

1. **Submission Timeline:**
   - Full proposals due: October 10, 2024 (12:00 PM ET)
   - Maximum SBIR funding: $75,000
   - Maximum performance period: 90 calendar days

2. **Key Solicitation Parameters:**
   - Topic: AFX247-PCSO1 - "Phase I Open Call for Innovative Defense Related Dual-Purpose Technologies/Solutions with a Clear Air Force Stakeholder Need"
   - Submission portal: DoD SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP)
   - Proposals must be 7 volumes including technical, cost, and commercialization components

3. **Evaluation Criteria (Equal Weight):**
   - Commercialization potential
   - Defense need fulfillment
   - Technical approach and merit

4. **Critical Eligibility Requirements:**
   - Small business (≤500 employees with affiliates)
   - >50% U.S. ownership and control
   - Minimum 2/3 of R&D work performed by applicant
   - Principal Investigator employed full-time by applicant
   - Work performed in United States (with rare exceptions)
   - NIST SP 800-171 assessment required by January 6, 2025

5. **NIST SP 800-171 Assessment (Amendment 2 Changes):**
   - Self-assessment must be completed via https://www.projectspectrum.io/#/cyberreadiness-Check
   - Score must be published in PIEE's SPRS module by January 6, 2025
   - No submission of assessment documentation required under Volume 5
   - Failure to complete assessment may result in disqualification

## Notable Details
- This is an "open topic" allowing broad technology submissions addressing Air Force operational imperatives
- Emphasis on dual-use commercial technologies adapted for defense
- DAF maintains right to award all, some, or none of proposals
- Multiple awards planned and anticipated
- National security due diligence review required for all proposals
- Restrictions on foreign ownership, affiliation, and talent recruitment program participation
- Export control compliance (ITAR/EAR) required for applicable technologies

**Note:** This document is solicitation guidance, not a BST proposal, capability statement, or project report. It provides the regulatory framework within which BST or other small businesses would submit proposals to the Air Force.
