## Executive Summary
This is a blank NASA CCRPP application template (30-page limit) designed for SBIR/STTR Phase II contractors seeking to mature and commercialize previously-developed technologies. The template requires applicants to demonstrate customer pull, investor commitment, clear maturation strategy, commercialization planning, and technology readiness level progression toward near-term deployment.

## Document Purpose
This is a **template document**, not a completed proposal. It provides the standardized form structure and detailed instructions that contractors (including BST) would use to apply for CCRPP funding.

## Key Required Sections

### 1.0 Customer Objectives
- Describe primary and target customers (NASA or non-NASA aligned with NASA interests)
- Justify customer acquisition plan and timing
- Identify investor and relationship to customer
- Demonstrate strong "customer pull" at project completion

### 1.2 Program/Project/Customer Alignment
- Show how CCRPP project matures the SBIR/STTR technology
- Describe expected impacts: mission enablement, new capabilities, performance/reliability improvements, cost savings
- Specify technology need dates (Quarter and FY)

### 2.0 Commercialization Information
Requires updated commercialization plan covering:
- **Market Feasibility**: Target markets, competitive advantages, potential customers, market size, time-to-market, 5-year market share projection, competitive threats
- **Commercialization Strategy**: Business plans for 5 years, commercialization experience, existing/projected commitments
- **Key Personnel**: Management/technical skills in commercialization, organizational structure, hiring plans
- **Financial Strategy**: Private resources committed, current investment/sales data, projected financial requirements, evidence of financial condition
- **Intellectual Property**: Plans to secure patents, copyrights, trade secrets

### 3.0 Implementation Approach

#### Maturation Strategy
- Summarize current technology status and prior Phase II results
- Justify accelerated maturation timeline
- List specific maturation tasks with descriptions
- Describe technology transition to operational use
- Identify integration, testing, certification requirements
- Reference supporting NASA programs/projects
- Address financial contingencies

#### 3.1.1 Schedule
- Detailed Gantt charts required
- Milestone table consistent with budget milestones

#### Procurement Strategy
- Describe all subcontracts and procurement relationships

#### Technical Strategy
- Implementation funding requests
- Technology/integration risks and mitigations
- Test and evaluation plan
- Environmental, safety, occupational health considerations

#### Logistics Strategy (as needed)
- Design interfaces, reliability requirements, technical data
- Maintenance planning, computer resources, manpower
- Supply support, training requirements
- Manufacturing readiness

#### Key Personnel
- Biographical information for essential personnel
- Relevant education and experience documentation

#### Readiness Levels
- Technology Readiness Level (TRL) progression required
- Manufacturing Readiness Level (MRL) if applicable
- Table 2 format specified

## Appendix Definitions

### Appendix A: Manufacturing Readiness Levels (MRL)
Defines manufacturability as the ease and cost-effectiveness of manufacturing a product with maximum reliability.

### Appendix B: Technology Readiness Level (TRL)
Detailed TRL framework (Levels 1-9) with definitions:
- **Proof of Concept**: Analytical/experimental demonstration of concepts
- **Breadboard**: Low-fidelity functional demonstration
- **Brassboard**: Medium-fidelity functional unit addressing scaling
- **Prototype Unit**: Full-scale representative demonstration
- **Engineering Unit**: High-fidelity unit resembling final product
- **Mission Configuration**: Final operational system architecture

#### Environment Definitions (TRL Support)
- **Laboratory Environment**: Demonstrates underlying principles only
- **Relevant Environment**: Subset of operational environment stressing technology advances
- **Operational Environment**: Actual deployment environment (space, airborne, ground-based, software platform)

## Required Entrance Criteria
- **Investor commitment letter required** - Agreement that project has demonstrated sufficient results to justify expansion and acceleration

## Notable Details
- All applications must be submitted through NASA SBIR/STTR Electronic Handbook (EHB)
- Information is treated as procurement-sensitive and protected by government
- Emphasis on "customer pull" and demonstrated market need at project completion
- Requires explicit connection between technology maturation and near-term customer acquisition plans
- 30-page limit enforced
