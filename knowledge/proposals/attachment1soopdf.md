## Executive Summary
This is the Statement of Objectives for AFRL's Multiple Award Contract (AMAC), an IDIQ vehicle designed to provide flexible, streamlined access to diverse contractors for rapid Science & Technology (S&T) development across all technology areas relevant to the Air Force and Space Force. The AMAC seeks to enable evolutionary, revolutionary, and disruptive capabilities through basic research, applied research, prototyping, and technology transition activities.

## Technical Approach
The AMAC employs a broad-based contracting vehicle with the following characteristics:
- **Contract Structure**: IDIQ with 5-year initial ordering period plus three 1-year options
- **Scope of Work**: Includes basic/applied research, data science, technology development, digital architecture (MBSE), modeling/simulation, manufacturing, experimentation, integration, and technology transition
- **Acquisition Model**: Streamlined and standardized across AFRL to enable rapid response to emerging needs
- **Re-competition**: Government reserves right to add additional contractors mid-term through on-ramp process; contractors can also be off-ramped for unsatisfactory performance with 30-day cure period

## Products & Capabilities Described
This is a framework solicitation, not specific to BST products. Rather, it outlines AFRL's desired contractor capabilities across multiple domains. The document does not describe Black Swift Technologies products, systems, or capabilities.

## Use Cases & Applications
The AMAC encompasses four major technical domain areas:

### 4.1 Air Domain Technologies (12 areas)
- Human Performance Optimization; Aerospace Medicine; Warfighter Survivability; Cognitive/Behavioral Sciences
- Advanced Air Vehicles; Air Propulsion Systems; Aerodynamics/Flight Sciences
- Hypersonics (Mach 5+ flight); Advanced Weapons; Energetics/Propellants
- Guidance Navigation and Control; Munitions Survivability and Countermeasures

### 4.2 Space Domain Technologies (8 areas)
- Space Access and Orbital Systems; Spacecraft/Satellite Technologies
- Space Power and Propulsion; On-Orbit Operations and Autonomy
- Space Situational Awareness; Resilient Space Architecture
- Space Environment and Effects; Position, Navigation, and Timing (PNT) from/for Space

### 4.3 Cyberspace/Electronic Warfare Domain Technologies (17 areas)
- Cyber Operations and Cybersecurity; Communications and Networks; Electronic Warfare
- Quantum Technologies; Electro-Optical and Infrared Sensors
- Radar and Radio Frequency Sensors; Multispectral/Hyperspectral Sensing
- Signal Processing and Sensor Fusion; Quantum Sensing
- Directed Energy (sensing, high-energy lasers, high-power microwave, beam control, effects/countermeasures)
- Photonics; Cybersecurity for Digital Environments; Navigation Warfare and Counter PNT

### 4.4 Cross-Cutting Domain Technologies (19 areas)
- Human Systems Integration; Human-Machine Teaming; Training/Simulation Technologies
- Rapid Multi-Domain Integration; Advanced Systems Engineering
- Integrated Vehicle Health Management; Power/Thermal Management
- Advanced Materials Development; Additive Manufacturing; Materials Processing/Fabrication/Characterization
- Environmentally Responsive Smart Materials; Microelectronics
- Model-Based Systems Engineering; Digital Engineering and Digital Thread
- Advanced Simulation/Virtual Prototyping; Data Analytics and AI
- Additive Manufacturing Digital Integration
- Low C-SWAP Alternative PNT Systems for Attritable Systems
- Modular Open Architecture approaches to Resilient PNT

## Key Results
This is a framework solicitation document, not a results report. It establishes objectives and requirements for future contract awards, not results from completed work.

## Notable Details

**Contract Administration:**
- All delivery orders/task orders will be unclassified; security requirements addressed at program level
- Data rights determined at task order level
- Post-award conference (max 60 minutes) via video/teleconference to establish relationship and educate contractors
- No security clearance required for award; contractors can be unclassified

**Performance Evaluation:**
- Contractors must demonstrate past performance in areas of interest through prior contracts and narrative justifications
- Off-ramp provisions: Contractors removed for unsatisfactory performance, failure to meet requirements, or unresponsiveness; 30-day cure period required except for direct DO/TO termination for default/convenience
- If DO/TO terminated for default/convenience, Government may remove contractor from entire awardee pool

**Areas of Particular Interest Relevant to Small Companies:**
- Low Cost, Size, Weight, and Performance (C-SWAP) Alternative PNT Systems for Attritable Systems (4.4.18)
- Modular Open Architecture approaches to Resilient PNT (4.4.19)
These suggest AFRL interest in affordable, compact navigation solutions for unmanned/expendable platforms—potentially relevant to organizations like BST focused on small aircraft systems.
