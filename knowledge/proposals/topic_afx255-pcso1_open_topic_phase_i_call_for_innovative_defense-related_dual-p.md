# AFX255-PCSO1: Open Topic Phase I Call for Innovative Defense-Related Dual-Purpose Technologies/Solutions with a Clear Air Force Stakeholder Need

## Document Metadata
- Type: Solicitation / RFP
- Client/Agency: Department of the Air Force (DAF) / United States Air Force (USAF)
- Program/Solicitation: AFX255-PCSO1 (SBIR/STTR), offered in partnership with AFWERX
- Date: 2025 (based on file metadata)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None
- Key Personnel: Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is an open-topic Air Force solicitation seeking innovative commercial technologies adapted for defense applications. The program targets non-defense commercial solutions with high probability of meeting Air Force stakeholder needs, focusing on feasibility studies and prototype-validated concepts under accelerated Phase I and Phase II schedules rather than basic research.

## Technical Approach
Not applicable—this is a solicitation document, not a proposal. However, the solicitation specifies that:
- Solutions must be tied to commercial technologies and commercial sectors
- Technical approaches should address OUSD(R&E) Critical Technology Areas or "BLUE SKY" initiatives
- Phase I requires feasibility study and product-mission fit validation
- Phase II requires prototype development, installation, integration, demonstration, and test/evaluation
- Applicants must define integration pathways with DAF operations, including regulatory navigation and system integration

## Technology Areas & Modernization Priorities Sought
**Technology Areas:** Air Platform, Battlespace, Bio Medical, Chem Bio Defense, Electronics, Ground Sea, Human Systems, Information Systems, Materials, Nuclear, Sensors, Space Platforms, Weapons

**Modernization Priorities:** Advanced Computing and Software, Advanced Materials, Biotechnology, Directed Energy (DE), FutureG, Human-Machine Interfaces, Hypersonics, Integrated Network Systems-of-Systems, Integrated Sensing and Cyber, Microelectronics, Quantum Science, Renewable Energy Generation and Storage, Space Technology, Trusted AI and Autonomy

## Use Cases & Applications
Not pre-specified. The solicitation is truly "open" and agnostic of industry, technology, and problem area. Proposers must identify:
- Specific Air Force stakeholder needs
- Product-mission fit for a non-defense commercial solution adapted for defense
- Pathway to DAF integration and operational use
- Potential for broader DoD/governmental customer use
- Dual-use civilian and commercial applications for Phase III

## Phase Structure & Key Requirements

**Phase I:**
- Determine scientific and technical merit and feasibility
- Validate product-mission fit with potential Air Force stakeholder
- Define clear, actionable plan with DAF customer, end-user, and TPOC
- Identify potential stakeholders
- Describe integration pathway with DAF operations (core tech development, regulatory navigation, system integration)
- Assess applicability to other DoD or governmental customers

**Phase II:**
- Develop, install, integrate, demonstrate, and test/evaluate prototype system
- Evaluate solution against proposed objectives and measurable key results
- Detail how adapted solution differs from commercial offering and scales for wide adoption
- Identify clear transition path with input from end users, engineering, sustainment, contracting, finance, legal, cyber security
- Specify integration with current and future solutions
- Describe sustainability and supportability
- Identify additional DoD/governmental customers

**Phase III (Dual-Use):**
- Some Phase I winners may fast-track to Phase III upon product-market fit verification
- Transition to expanded non-defense commercial solution for governmental and civilian users
- Support alternate mission applications

## Notable Details
- This is a **truly open solicitation**—no predetermined problem areas, industries, or technology focus
- Emphasis on **commercial-to-defense adaptation** rather than defense-originated innovation
- Applicants must demonstrate **high probability of product-mission fit** with named Air Force stakeholder
- Strong emphasis on **dual-use potential** (defense and non-defense markets)
- Program aims to **scale capability and grow the industrial base for defense**
- Follows SBA SBIR/STTR Policy Directive (3 May 2023)
- Government reserves right to award contracts or Other Transactions
- Program is intended for **applied R&D rather than basic research**
- No specific TPOC listed in this document
