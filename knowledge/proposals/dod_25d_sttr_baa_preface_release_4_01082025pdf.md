## Executive Summary

This is the administrative preface and instruction document for the DoD FY2025 STTR Program. It establishes the framework for small business concerns (SBCs) to submit Phase I proposals across DoD Services and Components on a rolling basis throughout fiscal year 2025. The document provides eligibility requirements, certification procedures, proposal preparation instructions, and program policies governing the three-phase STTR process (Phase I R&D feasibility, Phase II development, Phase III commercialization).

## Key Program Elements

### Program Structure & Objectives
- **Three-Phase Program:**
  - Phase I: 6-12 months, focused on proving scientific/technical feasibility and commercial merit
  - Phase II: ~24 months, continuation and development of Phase I work
  - Phase III: Commercialization using non-STTR funding (private sector, other federal sources)

- **Program Goals:**
  - Stimulate technological innovation
  - Strengthen small business role in meeting DoD R&D needs
  - Foster participation of minority and disadvantaged persons
  - Increase commercial application of DoD-supported research

### Release Schedule & Submission Deadlines
The BAA operates on a 12-release rolling schedule throughout FY2025:
- **Release 4** (current): Open Jan 29, 2025; Close Feb 26, 2025
- Releases 1-12 span from October 2024 through October 2025
- New topics pre-release first Wednesday of each month
- Each release has specific open and close dates

## Eligibility & Performance Requirements

### SBC Eligibility
- Must qualify as small business per 13 C.F.R. §§ 701-705 at time of award
- Must meet Small Business Ownership and Control requirements (13 CFR § 121.702)

### STTR-Specific Mandates
- **Minimum Work Distribution:**
  - Small business concern: minimum 40% of project (direct + indirect costs)
  - Research institution: minimum 30% of effort performed by single research institution
  - These percentages are statutorily mandated (15 USC 638(e)); no deviations allowed

- **Principal Investigator (PI) Requirements:**
  - PI primary employment must be with SBC or research institution at time of award and during performance
  - Primary employment defined as >50% time (40-hour work week basis)
  - At Phase I/II award, SBC must have at least one management employee whose primary employment is with SBC (not also employed by research institution)

- **Performance Location:** All R&D work must be performed in the United States

- **Written Agreement:** SBC and research institution must have written agreement allocating IP rights and follow-on research/commercialization rights (Model Agreement provided)

### Majority Ownership by VC/Private Equity Firms
- Generally, SBCs majority-owned by multiple venture capital operating companies (VCOCs), hedge funds, or private equity firms are **ineligible** (unless Service/Component specifies otherwise)
- If authorized by Service/Component, must disclose:
  - All foreign nationals/entities/governments holding >5% equity (direct or indirect)
  - Ultimate parent owners
  - Other entities/individuals owning >5% equity stake

### Performance Benchmarks for Experienced Firms
- Firms with multiple prior SBIR/STTR awards must meet Phase I-to-Phase II Transition Rate and Commercialization Benchmark standards per SBIR/STTR Extension Act of 2022 (Pub. L. 117-183)
- Failure to meet benchmarks results in cap on awards:
  - Army: 20 total Phase I and Direct-to-Phase II awards
  - Navy: 20 total Phase I and Direct-to-Phase II awards
  - Air Force: 20 total Phase I and Direct-to-Phase II awards
  - All other DoD Components combined: 20 total Phase I and Direct-to-Phase II awards

## Certifications & Registrations Required

### System for Award Management (SAM)
- SBC must register in SAM to provide business, capability, and financial information
- Generates Unique Entity ID (UEI) and Commercial and Government Entry (CAGE) code
- SBCs already registered must verify active status and current representations/certifications

### SBA Company Registry
- SBC registration required
- Must provide SBC Control ID and Proof of Registration/Certification during proposal submission

### Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP)
- All individuals from proposing SBCs must register to prepare and submit proposals
- Authenticated via Login.gov
- Designated DSIP Firm Admin responsible for creating Firm PIN and controlling access
- **IMPORTANT:** DSIP only accessible from within United States (50 states, US territories, Puerto Rico, Marshall Islands, Federated States of Micronesia, Palau, DC)
- Support: DoDSBIRSupport@reisystems.com (Monday-Friday, 9 a.m.-5 p.m. ET)

### Required Certifications at Proposal Submission
SBC must certify via proposal cover sheet:
- Meets size, ownership, and program requirements
- Certifications required multiple times across award lifecycle:
  - At Phase I award
  - At Phase II award
  - Prior to final payment on Phase I
  - Prior to receiving 50% of Phase II award amount
  - Prior to final payment on Phase II

## Due Diligence & Security Risk Assessment

### National Security Risk Review (15 U.S.C. §638(vv))
DoD, in coordination with SBA, conducts risk-based due diligence review assessing:
- **Cybersecurity practices**
- **Patent analysis**
- **Employee analysis and foreign ownership**, including:
  - SBC financial ties and obligations (surety, equity, debt)
  - Employee ties to foreign country/person/entity
- **Open-source verification** of provided information and undisclosed foreign affiliations per 15 U.S.C. 638(g)(13)

### Foreign Affiliations Disclosure
- SBC must complete "Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries" (Volume 7 of proposal)
- DoD may request true copies of contractual/financial obligations with foreign state-owned or foreign entities from prior 5-year period

### Consequences of Risk Assessment
- DoD **may not award** if SBC poses unacceptable national security risk that cannot be mitigated
- Non-selectable proposals rejected regardless of technical merit if national security risk identified

### FOCI Training
- DoD partnered with Project Spectrum to provide "Understanding Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence (FOCI)" online course
- Free course available at projectspectrum.io
- Teaches FOCI definition, impact on federal award eligibility

## Joint Ventures & Organizational Requirements

### Joint Ventures
- Must submit required representations for small business status, service-disabled veteran-owned SB, women-owned SB (WOSB), economically disadvantaged WOSB, or historically underutilized business zone small business
- Documents found at FAR citations 52.212-3(c) and 52.219-1(c), uploaded to Volume 5

### Conflicts of Interest
- SBCs owned by/employing current or former Federal Government employees may create conflicts of interest violating federal law

### Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI) - FAR 9.5
**Disclosure Requirements:**
- SBC must identify and disclose all facts relevant to potential OCIs involving proposing SBC and proposed team members (sub-awardees, consultants)
- Must include OCI mitigation plan describing actions taken/intended to prevent conflicting roles and unfair competitive advantage
- Mitigation plan must specifically address OCI limitations per FAR 9.505-1 through 9.505-4

**Agency Supplemental OCI Policy:**
- DoD may prohibit concurrent provision of SETA (Scientific Engineering Technical Assistance), A&AS (Advisory and Assistance Services), or similar support AND technical performance
- Must disclose if proposing SBC or proposed team member provides such support to any DoD office:
  - Under current award/sub-award, OR
  - Under past award/sub-award ended within one calendar year prior to proposal submission
- If applicable, must provide:
  - Name of DoD office receiving support
  - Prime contract number
  - Identification of team member providing support
  - OCI mitigation plan per FAR 9.5

**Government OCI Evaluation:**
- Government evaluates OCI mitigation plans per FAR 9.503, 9.504, 9.506
- May grant waiver if in government interest
- May request additional information supporting mitigation plan evaluation
- May reject proposal if SBC failed to fully disclose OCI, failed to provide government waiver, or failed to reasonably provide requested information

## Research Involving Human/Animal Subjects

### Human Subjects Research (RIHS/HSR)
- Must comply with applicable federal/state laws and agency policy/guidelines
- Institutions must provide documentation of current federal assurance (e.g., HHS Office for Human Research Protections federal-wide assurance)
- Personnel must complete appropriate human subject protection training
- **Exemptions:** Research meeting 32 CFR 219.101 exemption criteria may not require federal assurance
- **If Selected for Award:**
  - Must provide Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval documentation
  - IRB approval timeline: 1-3 months average
  - DoD review timeline: 3-6 months
  - No funding toward human subject research until ALL approvals granted
- **Recommendation:** Separate human subject research from other R&D in proposal to avoid delays

### Animal Subjects Research
- Must comply with applicable federal/agency rules on animal acquisition, transport, care, handling, and use
- Must describe plans for Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (IACUC) review and approval
- All recipients must receive IACUC approval plus secondary/HQ-level approval from DoD veterinarian
- No animal research conducted with DoD funding until all DoD office approvals granted
- **Recommendation:** Separate animal research from other R&D in proposal to avoid delays

### Recombinant DNA Research
- Must comply with applicable federal/state law and agency guidance
- Requires institutional biosafety committee approval

## Proposal Preparation Instructions

### Proposal Structure (DSIP Submission)
Proposals submitted through Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal in seven volumes:
1. **Volume 1:** Proposal Cover Sheet
2. **Volume 2:** Technical Volume
3. **Volume 3:** Cost Volume
4. **Volume 4:** Company Commercialization Report
5. **Volume 5:** Supporting Documents
6. **Volume 6:** Fraud, Waste and Abuse Training
7. **Volume 7:** Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries

**Note:** Service/Component guidance on allowable content may vary; completion in DSIP does not guarantee all volumes meet Service/Component-specific requirements
