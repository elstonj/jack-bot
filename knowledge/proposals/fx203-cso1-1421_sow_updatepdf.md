## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes a Phase I feasibility study to adapt its machine learning-based early warning/preventative maintenance monitoring system to address U.S. Air Force UAS maintenance and failure prevention needs. The 90-day study will engage stakeholders at the Air Force Academy's Center for Unmanned Aircraft Systems to understand mission requirements and develop a plan for technology adaptation suitable for Phase II prototype development.

## Technical Approach

**Core Technology:** ML-based early warning monitoring system applied to UAS maintenance and failure prediction

**Phase I Tasks:**
1. **Stakeholder Engagement** - Identify and engage potential customers including Dr. George York and Colonel Brian Neff at the Center for Unmanned Aircraft Systems (USAFA Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering)
2. **Research & Data Exploration** - Conduct guided research using UAS flight logs and mission failure records to build unsupervised learning datasets and understand mission requirements
3. **Technology Adaptation** - Determine required modifications to BST's commercial ML system to meet Air Force UAS maintenance mission needs
4. **Key Stakeholder Presentation** - Present adapted solution outline to customers and end-users with goal of obtaining Memorandum of Understanding (MOU)
5. **Phase II Preparation** - Develop timeline and milestone schedule for Phase II prototype delivery

**Key Advantage:** The system will analyze flight log details using unsupervised machine learning without requiring pre-structured/tagged data—a capability currently unavailable for Air Force UAS fleets.
