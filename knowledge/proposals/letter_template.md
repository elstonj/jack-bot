## Executive Summary
This document provides a template letter and detailed guidance for investor commitment letters required as matching funds for NASA SBIR Phase II CCRPP (Commercialization) proposals. It includes a sample commitment letter from University of Colorado for SuperSwift development ($23,900) and guidance on what qualifies as investment, plus documentation of multiple actual investor commitments totaling approximately $178K+ supporting soil moisture mapping and wildfire research applications.

## Technical Approach
The CCRPP investment structure funds continued development and commercialization beyond Phase II work, with committed funds (from government agencies, universities, and private sector) used for activities including:
- Further R&D
- Manufacturing and production
- Marketing and commercialization
- Field validation and testing
- Payload integration and swappable systems development
- Sensor validation against in situ measurements
- Personnel training for end-user operations

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift sUAS
- Modular aircraft platform developed during Phase II
- Designed as common platform for scientific data gathering missions
- Features modular, swappable nose cone and payload tray
- Applications: wildfire research (FIREX), soil moisture mapping
- Capability to quickly swap payloads in field for multi-mission use
- Target TRL/MRL at CCRPP completion: TRL 9, MRL 7

### SMM (Soil Moisture Mapping) sUAS
- Primary Phase II deliverable
- Combines radiometric, NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index), and thermal data
- Enables variable rate irrigation for agriculture
- Post-processing improvements targeting same-day soil moisture map generation
- Demonstrated >15% reduction in agricultural water usage while maintaining crop yield

### MALIBU Payload
- Multi AngLe Imaging Bidirectional Reflectance Distribution Function camera array
- Designed for integration into SuperSwift modular payload tray
- Complements soil moisture mapping (mutual benefit: soil moisture maps enhance MALIBU utility)

## Use Cases & Applications

### Wildfire Research (FIREX)
- University of Colorado investment: $22,390-$23,900
- Adaptation of SuperSwift modular nose cone for NOAA FIREX program
- SuperSwift to assess available fuel and model wildfire spread
- Application to major economic burden of Western States wildfires

### Agriculture & Water Conservation
- Target market for soil moisture mapping technology
- Agriculture represents ~70% of global freshwater use
- IRISS "Project Drought" investment: $50,000 for water usage reduction research
- UMES (University of Maryland Eastern Shore) investment: $15,000 for variable rate irrigation testing, including challenging corn crop canopy penetration studies
- Focus on accelerating soil moisture map generation for real-time variable rate irrigation

### Multispectral Remote Sensing
- MALIBU project investment: $91,000
- Field validation through 2 field campaigns
- Payload array integration and field testing

## Notable Details

### Investment Requirements (per NASA SBIR CCRPP guidelines)
- Must directly further Phase II work
- Commitment made prior to award; expenditure concurrent with CCRPP performance
- Non-federal investor arrangements provide funding in return for equity, royalties, technology rights, profit sharing, or combinations thereof
- Investor letters required on official letterhead with specific information elements (contact details, amount, timeline, TRL/MRL targets, use statement)
