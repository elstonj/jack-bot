Black Swift Technologies, in partnership with Colorado State University, submitted a proposal to the CO-WY Engine's inaugural grants program for wildfire monitoring work. ## Key Results (for reports)
**Proposal Status**: Not selected in first round.

**Strengths Noted by Reviewers**:
- Team qualifications
- Management plan
- Proposed concept of operations

**Recommended Refinements**:
1. **Stakeholder Partnerships**: Reviewers sought deeper and broader partnerships with public sector stakeholders to align with the "use-inspired" spirit of the solicitation; suggested named partners funded through subawards for robust collaboration.
2. **Competitive Differentiation**: Proposal lacked clear positioning relative to academic and private sector competitors in wildfire monitoring. Reviewers noted the field is highly competitive with multiple approaches underway and wanted clearer articulation of gaps addressed by the proposed activity.
3. **Geographic Scalability**: Unclear how the proposed activities would scale beyond the initial study site and what challenges/opportunities exist for broader geographic service areas.

## Notable Details
- The CO-WY Engine received "an overwhelming response" to its inaugural RFP, making the first round highly competitive.
- Evaluation criteria included: technical viability, project viability, translation potential, team readiness, engagement and partnerships, and potential impact.
- The process included conflict-of-interest disclosures and oversight from the Engine's R&D Advisory Board.
- A second RFP was announced for early 2025, and the CO-WY Engine invited BST to meet and discuss how to strengthen future applications.
- The CO-WY Engine is described as a "Climate Resilience Engine," indicating the program's focus on climate adaptation and resilience.
