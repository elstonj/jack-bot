## Executive Summary
The NightFOX project is developing a specialized UAS payload for nighttime fire observation and in situ sampling during wildfire events. As of September 2018, prototype remote sensing and in situ instruments were operated during initial test flights, with automated IR fire mapping algorithms developed and demonstrated. The project is progressing toward prescribed burn field testing scheduled for October 2018.

## Technical Approach
- Integration of multiple complementary sensing modalities on the S2 UAS platform for fire detection and smoke characterization
- **Remote sensing payload** includes scanning telescopes and cameras at multiple wavelengths (1.6 µm telescope, 1.6 µm camera, and 4 µm telescope) for IR-based fire mapping
- **In situ payload** for direct sampling of fire emissions, including:
  - CO2 sensors (long and short pathlength variants for different concentrations)
  - CO sensor (prototype stage, undergoing firmware modification)
  - POPS aerosol instrument (requiring flow dilution implementation for high-loading fire plume sampling)
- Automated algorithms for generating IR fire maps from scanning telescope and camera data
- Planned integration with fire weather modeling through data product assimilation protocols

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- Platform being used to carry NightFOX payloads
- Current TRL: 6 (systems/prototypes demonstrated in relevant environment)
- Underwent test flight operations in September 2018
- Experiencing flight stability issues during initial tests (resolved for remote sensing payload operations)

### Remote Sensing Payload
- **Status**: Prototype stage, mostly functional
- **Components**: 1.6 µm scanning telescope, 1.6 µm camera, 4 µm telescope
- **Capability**: Automated IR fire mapping to detect and characterize active fire areas during night operations
- **TRL**: 5 (component and/or breadboard validation in relevant environment)
- **Note**: 4 µm telescope does not detect reflected sunlight; still under evaluation/completion

### In Situ Sampling Payload
- **Status**: Prototype, mostly operational except CO2 sensor evaluation ongoing
- **Components**:
  - CO2 sensors (dual pathlength design to cover wide concentration range)
  - CO sensor (prototype, awaiting firmware modification)
  - POPS aerosol particle counter (requiring flow dilution modification for fire plume operation)
- **TRL**: 5 (component and/or breadboard validation in relevant environment)

### Automated IR Fire Mapping Algorithms
- Newly developed algorithms for generating fire maps from 1.6 µm telescope and camera data
- Demonstrated on September 6 test flight data
- Two example maps generated and documented

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Mission**: Nighttime wildfire observation and smoke characterization
- **Specific Application**: Detecting and quantifying active fires and emissions during nighttime conditions when other remote sensing platforms are limited
- **Field Testing**: Prescribed burn sampling at Security and Emergency Response Response Training Center (SERTC) near Pueblo, CO, planned for week of October 15, 2018
- **Integration Goal**: UAS fire observations for fire weather research and modeling; data assimilation for fire weather prediction models
