## Executive Summary
This report documents completion of Phase I Technical and Business Assistance tasks for the NASA SBIR project "Adaptive and Secure Autonomy for UAS: A Modular Approach to Flight Control, Machine Learning Integration, and Multi-Vehicle Coordination." BST conducted market research and developed strategic planning documents to prepare for Phase II, identifying key market segments, potential customers, and commercialization pathways for autonomous UAS technology.

## Technical Approach
The TABA effort focused on two primary deliverables:
1. **Phase II Needs Assessment** – Comprehensive market research on UAS and drone software markets
2. **Phase II Commercialization and Business Plan** – Strategic planning for commercialization

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Adaptive and Secure Autonomy for UAS Technology**
- Modular approach to flight control
- Machine learning integration
- Multi-vehicle coordination capabilities
- Status: Phase I (technology development in progress; commercialization planning underway)

## Use Cases & Applications
Market research identified priority application domains:
- Remote sensing
- Intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance (ISR)
- Environmental monitoring
- Emergency response & public safety
- Infrastructure inspection
- Precision agriculture
- Advanced air mobility

## Customer/Partner Segments Identified
Primary outreach focused on operators of large heterogeneous UAS fleets:
- **Military/Defense:** AFIT Autonomy and Navigation Technology Center, Navy Small Tactical UAS Program, US Army Corps of Engineers Aviation Program Office, US Air Force Academy Center for UAS Research
- **Federal Labs/Agencies:** DOE Argonne, Sandia, Idaho, and Pacific Northwest National Laboratories; MIT Lincoln Laboratory; Johns Hopkins APL
- **Academic:** Embry–Riddle Aeronautical University, University of Colorado Boulder, Colorado State University Drone Center, University of Alaska Fairbanks, Kansas State University, University of North Dakota Research Institute for Autonomous Systems

- Report identified these organizations as high-probability Phase II collaborators due to operation of large, heterogeneous UAS fleets
