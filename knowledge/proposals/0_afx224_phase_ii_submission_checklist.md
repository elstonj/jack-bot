## Executive Summary
This is an administrative checklist for Black Swift Technologies' Phase II SBIR submission to the Air Force under topic AFX224-OCSO1 (Soil Moisture Mapping). The checklist ensures compliance with all required submissions across six volumes: proposal coversheet, technical volume, cost volume, commercialization report, supporting documents, and fraud/waste/abuse training.

## Program Parameters
- **Maximum Period of Performance:** 21 months
- **Maximum Award Amount:** $1,250,000
- **Government Anticipated Awards:** ~110 awards this round
- **Submission Limit:** One proposal per small business per topic
- **Permissible Arrangements:** Joint ventures and limited partnerships allowed (13 U.S.C. § 121.701)

## Submission Requirements

### Volume 1 - Proposal Coversheet
- Certification questions
- Technical abstract (no proprietary information)
- Anticipated benefits (specific format required: "We solved [DAF problem] by providing [advantage]...")
- Contact information
- Keywords

### Volume 2 - Technical Volume (15-page limit)
- Coversheet and table of contents
- Glossary
- Identification and significance of problem/opportunity
- Air Force/Space Force technology strategic capabilities alignment
- Non-defense commercial solution description
- Proposed adaptation of non-defense solution
- Compliance and regulatory activities
- Phase II technical objectives and key results
- Phase II work plan
- Commercialization strategy
- Military applications and DoD customers
- Non-US citizens disclosure
- Key personnel
- Non-defense commercial customers
- Investors and partners
- Related work

### Volume 3 - Cost Volume
- Direct labor costs
- Direct material costs (with vendor quotes/justification in Vol 5)
- Other direct costs
- Second phase commitment/cost share (if applicable)
- **Key Requirement:** Minimum 50% of research/analytical work must be performed by proposing firm (measured by direct + indirect costs, excluding profit)

## Notable Details & Administrative Requirements

1. **Technical Abstract:** No proprietary information permitted
2. **Anticipated Benefits Format:** Must follow specific template addressing DAF problem, advantage provided, end-user organization, and non-defense commercial profit strategy
3. **Digital Signatures:** Must verify signatures are NOT stripped after upload (check Vol 5 PDFs)
4. **Document Consistency:** Proposal title and number must match across all documents and DSIP entry
5. **Page Limits:** Technical volume has strict 15-page limit; pages in excess will not be reviewed
6. **Cost Share/Commitment:** Second phase commitment information can be included if applicable
7. **Commercial Solution:** Must describe existing non-defense commercial solution and how it will be adapted for Air Force use
8. **NIST Cybersecurity:** NIST SP-800-171 assessment recommended early submission as it's one of first documents requested upon award selection
