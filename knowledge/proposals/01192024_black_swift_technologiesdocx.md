# NASA Phase II SBIR - Improved Wildfire Forecast Models (Letter of Support)

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support/Commitment
- Client/Agency: NASA (SBIR program)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA Phase II SBIR - Wildfire forecasting topic
- Date: January 19, 2024
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Uncrewed Aircraft System (UAS), meteorological sensor payload
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (CEO, BST); Ben Miller (Director, Colorado Center of Excellence for Advanced Technology Aerial Firefighting)

## Executive Summary
This is a letter of support from the Colorado Center of Excellence for Advanced Technology Aerial Firefighting confirming participation in BST's proposed NASA Phase II SBIR effort. The project aims to use meteorological data collected by a BST-developed UAS and sensor payload to refine weather models for wildfire forecasting, thereby improving operational planning for wildland fire suppression.

## Technical Approach
- **Core Technology**: Meteorological sensor payload designed for deployment on a BST-developed UAS
- **Data Application**: Collected meteorological data feeds into fire weather models to decrease forecast uncertainty
- **Partner**: National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR) developing/refining the fire weather model
- **Data Distribution**: Real-time data availability to deployed firefighters via systems like ATAK (common operating picture technology)

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **BST UAS**: Uncrewed aircraft system capable of carrying meteorological sensor payloads for wildfire operations
- **Meteorological Sensor Payload**: Custom instrument package for in-situ weather data collection (specific sensors/specs not detailed in this letter)

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary**: Wildland fire suppression planning and operations
- **Secondary Applications**: Real-time common operating picture for deployed firefighting teams
- **Supported Initiatives**: 
  - NASA FireSense Project
  - NASA Advanced Capabilities for Emergency Response Operations (ACERO)
  - US Forest Service operations
  - Department of Interior operations
  - State and local fire response teams

## Key Commitments
