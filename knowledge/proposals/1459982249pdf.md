## Executive Summary
The SuperSwift is a modular electric-powered small unmanned aircraft system (sUAS) with a 10-foot wingspan specifically designed to house an L-band radiometer payload in a removable nose cone for soil moisture mapping missions. The airframe combines hand-launchability with 1.5+ hour flight endurance and a modular design enabling rapid sensor integration and field-swappable payloads for multiple scientific applications.

## Technical Approach

### Aircraft Configuration
- **Airframe Type**: Pusher configuration with V-tail design
- **Wingspan**: 120 inches (10 feet)
- **Empty Weight**: 7.5 lbs
- **Gross Takeoff Weight**: 10.5 lbs
- **Payload Capacity**: 3 lbs nominal, 5 lbs maximum
- **Propulsion**: Electric motor with pusher propeller
- **Flight Endurance**: ≥1.5 hours
- **Operational Requirement**: Hand-launchable from unimproved areas

### Aerodynamic Design
- **Wing Airfoil**: MH 70 (11.08% thickness-to-chord ratio)
  - Maximum sectional lift coefficient: ~1.5
  - Pitching moment coefficient: ~-0.05
  - Provides low stall speed and smooth stall transition
- **Wing Planform**:
  - Area: 1,100 in² (7.64 ft²)
  - Root chord: 10 in
  - Tip chord: 7.5 in
  - Mean aerodynamic chord: 9.24 in
  - Taper ratio: 0.84
  - Aspect ratio: 13.09
  - No dihedral angle; forward sweep of 0.6°
- **Wing Loading**: 22 oz/ft²
- **Stall Speed**: 33.5 ft/s at 6,000 ft altitude
- **V-Tail Configuration**:
  - NACA 0012 airfoils
  - Root chord: 7.0 in; tip chord: 5.75 in
  - Located 40 in from wing leading edge
  - Horizontal tail volume: 0.48; vertical tail volume: 0.015
  - 1° positive incidence angle for near-zero lift operation
  - Predicted downwash angle: 1.3°

### Stability & Control
- **Neutral Point**: 4.25 in aft of wing leading edge
- **Center of Gravity**: 2.0 in from wing leading edge
- **Static Margin**: 21% (0.5 in forward of wing root quarter-chord)
- Forward CG placement balances moments in powered flight and maintains V-tail zero-lift condition

### Design Rationale

**Pusher Configuration**: 
- Keeps nose cone clear and easily removable for payload integration
- Separates motor/propeller from scientific payload area
- Enables safe hand launch

**V-Tail Design**:
- Reduces structural weight vs. conventional tail
- Simplifies mechanical/structural design
- Enables faster field setup and teardown
- Requires low-pitching-moment airfoil for efficiency

**Hand-Launchability**:
- Wing loading ~20 oz/ft² (rule of thumb for hand-launched aircraft)
- Low stall speed (33.5 ft/s) for safe operations
- Structural placement protects operator from propeller

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift Aircraft
**What It Is**: A modular electric unmanned aircraft system designed as a scientific platform for soil moisture mapping and other remote sensing missions.

**Primary Innovation – Removable Nose Cone**:
- Houses all soil moisture payload subsystems
- Field-swappable for different sensor packages
- Eliminates conducting materials that would interfere with L-band radiometer antenna
- Fiberglass construction of minimum thickness to avoid signal degradation

**Key Capability Features**:
1. **Tool-free field assembly** – can be assembled without tools for rapid deployment
2. **Long electric flight time** – 1.5+ hours enables adequate survey coverage
3. **Hand-launchable** – operates from unimproved areas with minimal support equipment
4. **Modular payload integration** – laser-cut payload tray for rapid prototyping of sensor mounts
5. **Modular flight avionics** – allows customization for different mission sensors
6. **Field-swappable payload nose cones** – enables multi-sensor campaigns at single site

**Payload Interface**:
- Well-defined mechanical and electrical interfaces
- Simplifies third-party sensor development
- Enables cost-effective airframe redundancy for field campaigns
