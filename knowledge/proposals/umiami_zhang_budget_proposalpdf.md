- Key Personnel: Dr. Jun Zhang (PI, University of Miami); Dr. Jack Elston (CEO, Black Swift Technologies)

## Executive Summary
This subcontract budget proposal outlines University of Miami's role in developing and deploying advanced air-deployed small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS) from C-130 aircraft for real-time hurricane atmospheric data collection. The project aims to address a boundary layer measurement gap in tropical cyclone forecasting by integrating high-frequency UAS data into operational forecasts at NOAA's National Hurricane Center and Environmental Prediction Center.

## Technical Approach
The project targets three primary activities:
1. **Advance technologies related to Air-Deployed UAS** for improved hurricane sampling
2. **Develop tools to enhance C-130 Operations** with real-time sUAS data collection
3. **Address data reduction and data impact** to move sUAS to fully operational status

**Primary deliverables:**
- Successful C-130 deployment of sUAS in both clear air and hurricane conditions
- Developed capability for data transfer from sUAS to C-130
- Real-time data transfer capability to operational centers (NOAA NHC, EMC)

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Air-Deployed UAS (sUAS)
- Small unmanned aircraft systems designed for deployment from C-130 aircraft
- Intended for continuous boundary layer sampling in tropical cyclone environments
- Capability for both clear air and hurricane condition operations
- Real-time data collection and transfer functionality

## Use Cases & Applications

### Hurricane Sampling & Tropical Cyclone Forecasting
- **Primary application:** Real-time atmospheric data collection during hurricane operations
- **Deployment platform:** C-130 aircraft
- **Target gap:** Boundary layer measurement gap in tropical cyclone (TC) forecasting
- **End users:** NOAA National Hurricane Center, Environmental Prediction Center, AOML Hurricane Research Division, Department of Defense
- **Operational benefit:** Improve tropical cyclone forecast accuracy to enhance emergency preparedness and response guidance

**Field Operations:**
- Integration with P3 aircraft data collection programs
- Data collection in hurricane conditions
- Support for forecaster decision-making and model initialization
