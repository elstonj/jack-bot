# Letter of Support for Black Swift Technologies Navy STTR Phase II Proposal

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy (NOAA/Hurricane Research Division writing in support)
- Program/Solicitation: Navy STTR Topic N25A-T025, "Expendable Air-sea Profiling Observations in Hazardous Weather Conditions"
- Date: December 31, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 platform
- Key Personnel: Dr. Joseph J. Cione (NOAA Lead Meteorologist, letter author); Jack Elston (last editor on BST side)

## Executive Summary
Dr. Joseph Cione from NOAA's Hurricane Research Division provides strong institutional support for BST's Navy STTR Phase II proposal to develop the S0 expendable profiling platform. The letter documents successful operational deployments of the S0 during the 2024-2025 hurricane seasons and endorses planned enhancements including icing protection, AI-based autonomous sampling, and integration with Navy forecasting models.

## Technical Approach
- S0 platform: expendable air-sea profiling system designed to operate in extreme weather conditions
- Collects 3D wind, pressure, temperature, and humidity data in the marine atmospheric boundary layer (MABL)
- Operates continuously for up to two hours per deployment
- Phase II objectives include:
  - Integration of S0 data into NOAA's HAFS operational forecasting model (by June 2026)
  - Extension to Navy's COAMPS-TC operational model
  - Addition of icing protection systems
  - Implementation of AI-based autonomous sampling capabilities

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 Platform
- **What it is**: Expendable atmospheric profiling system for deployment from aircraft into hazardous weather
- **Operational performance**: Successfully deployed 19 units in 2024 hurricane season and 23 units in 2025
- **Environmental specifications**: Tested in Category 5 hurricane conditions, including horizontal winds up to 209 knots and vertical velocities up to 30 m/s (Hurricane Milton)
- **Data collection**: Continuous 3D wind, pressure, temperature, and humidity measurements in lowest 2 km altitude (air-sea interface)
- **Cost effectiveness**: ~$15,000 per deployment for two hours of continuous data vs. traditional dropsondes
- **Transition status**: Evolved from developmental concept to proven operational asset during collaboration with NOAA

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Hurricane research and operations**: Primary use case in tropical cyclone intensity monitoring and track prediction
- **Marine atmospheric boundary layer profiling**: Addresses gap in observational capabilities at air-sea interface where storm intensification occurs
- **Dual-use applications**: Both civilian research (NOAA) and military defense requirements
- **Future expansion**: Polar operations and winter storm environments (with icing protection enhancements)
- **Forecasting support**: Data assimilation to improve track and intensity predictions in operational models

## Key Results (Operational Validation)
- **2024 Hurricane Season**: 19 successful S0 deployments from NOAA WP-3D aircraft
- **2025 Hurricane Season**: 23 successful deployments
- **Hurricane Milton Performance**: S0 survived and transmitted data in extreme conditions (209 knot horizontal winds, 30 m/s vertical velocities)
- **Data continuity**: Demonstrated ability to gather robust thermodynamic and dynamic measurements continuously rather than episodically
- **Operational readiness**: Technology matured enough for transition to military operations

## Notable Details
- **Institutional endorsement**: Strong support from NOAA Hurricane Research Division (Dr. Cione is Lead Meteorologist for Emerging Technologies)
- **Operational timeline**: NOAA commitment to S0 data assimilation in HAFS model by June 2026
- **Cost advantage**: Explicitly positioned as cost-effective alternative to AVAPS dropsondes, which are "gold standard" but expensive and episodic
- **Military relevance**: Navy transition described as "logical and necessary next step" given dual-use nature
- **Capability gap**: S0 fills specific observational need in lowest 2 km altitude range during extreme weather—area where traditional dropsondes have limitations
- **Proposed enhancements**: Icing protection and autonomous AI sampling will expand operational envelope and reduce operator cognitive load
