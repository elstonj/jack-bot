## Executive Summary
This is the official NIFA Grants Application Guide that provides comprehensive instructions for applicants seeking federal funding from the National Institute of Food and Agriculture (USDA). The document guides applicants through the entire pre-award process, from locating funding opportunities through submission of applications via Grants.gov Workspace. It covers eligibility requirements, registration procedures (SAM.gov, UEI, EIN), form completion, submission procedures, and validation processes.

## Technical Approach
The guide outlines a four-stage grant process: Pre-Award, Award, Post-Award, and Close Out. The document focuses exclusively on the Pre-Award Phase, which consists of nine steps:
1. Search for funding opportunity on Grants.gov
2. Review Request for Applications (RFA)
3. Determine eligibility
4. Register in SAM.gov and obtain UEI (Unique Entity Identifier) and EIN (Employer Identification Number)
5. Register in Grants.gov with appropriate user roles
6. Complete application forms in Workspace
7. Submit application package
8. Confirm and track submission
9. Receive application decision

Applications are submitted through Grants.gov using the Workspace portal, which allows completion of forms either through webforms or by downloading and uploading PDFs. All attachments must be in PDF format with specific formatting requirements (12-point minimum font, 1-inch margins, page-numbered, ≤50 character file names without special characters).

## Funding Opportunity Types Described
NIFA offers three grant types:
- **Competitive Grants**: Limited funds; rigorous peer review
- **Non-Competitive Grants**: Congressionally directed; designated institutions
- **Capacity Grants**: Formula-based funding for land-grant institutions, schools of forestry, and schools of veterinary medicine

## Notable Details

**Critical Registration Timeline**: SAM.gov registration requires 4-6 weeks, which is flagged as essential for meeting application deadlines.

**Key Registration Requirements**:
- UEI (Unique Entity Identifier) - automatically generated after SAM.gov registration
- EIN (Employer Identification Number) - obtained from IRS.gov
- Active SAM.gov registration must be current at submission (Grants.gov validates this)

**Grants.gov User Roles**:
- **E-Business Point of Contact (EBiz POC)**: Manages grant activities, assigns roles
- **Authorized Organizational Representative (AOR)**: Legally binds institution, submits applications
- **Custom Roles**: Organization-specific permissions

**Application Submission Requirements**:
- Deadline is based on date/time stamp when Submit button is clicked
- Applications submitted at 5:00:59 PM EST are on-time; 5:01 PM EST+ are late
- Late applications only considered with extenuating circumstances (natural disasters, confirmed Grants.gov outages) and NIFA program staff approval
- Two validation stages: Grants.gov validation (checks UEI and SAM.gov status), then NIFA validation (checks eligibility, formatting, page limits, budget limits)

**Form Categories Required**:
- Research and Related (R&R) Forms (SF-424, budget forms, senior/key person profiles)
- Cross-Agency Forms (AFRI Project Type, SBIR/STTR, Key Contacts)
- NIFA-Specific Forms (Supplemental Information, Attachments, Data Management Plan)

**RFA Instructions Override**: The document repeatedly emphasizes that RFA instructions supersede this guide's instructions and will always be controlling.
