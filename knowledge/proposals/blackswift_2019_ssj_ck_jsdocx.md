# USGS Volcano Monitoring - S2 UAS Integration Project

## Document Metadata
- Type: Sole Source Justification / Procurement Proposal
- Client/Agency: United States Geological Survey (USGS), Department of the Interior - Volcano Science Center
- Program/Solicitation: FY19/20 Volcano monitoring activities; sole source procurement under FAR 13.106-1(b)(1)
- Date: July 29, 2019
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 UAS platform
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (BST, document editor); Angie Diefenbach (USGS UAS Project Chief); Stewart Evans (Contracting Officer)

## Executive Summary
USGS sought a sole source contract award of up to $100,000 to Black Swift Technologies for integrating volcano monitoring sensors into the S2 UAS platform, developing telemetry and software for real-time data streaming, and conducting flight operations at a target stratovolcano. BST's S2 platform and sensor integration expertise were identified as the only commercially available solution meeting the government's specific operational and technical requirements.

## Technical Approach
- **Platform Integration**: Modification of S2 UAS airframe to accommodate interchangeable sensor nose cones without compromising operations
- **Sensor Development**: Integration of multiparametric monitoring sensors including:
  - Gas sensors: CO2, SO2, H2S
  - Upward-looking UV spectrometer for spectroscopic analysis
  - 3D wind sensor
  - Environmental sensors: temperature, relative humidity, pressure
  - GPS navigation
  - Nadir photogrammetry camera with RTK geotagging
  - Gimbaled/forward-looking video camera
- **Telemetry & Software**: Development of high-data-rate long-distance telemetry capable of real-time video streaming to base station (>25 km range) with integrated software for displaying video, sensor data in color-coded flight routes, and real-time spectroscopic UV analysis
- **Operations**: Flight planning, testing, logistics, and data acquisition at target volcano location

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS Platform
- **Specifications**:
  - Fixed-wing, intermediate-size platform
  - Flight ceiling: 15,000 ft
  - Endurance: 90 minutes with 5 lb payload
  - Payload capacity: 5+ lbs
  - Payload section size: 8" x 12" (large for intermediate UAS)
  - Operating temperature range: down to -20°C
  - Corrosive environment capable
  - Runway-independent takeoff/landing
  - Range: >25 km
  - Capable of low-altitude terrain-following flight (150 ft AGL)
  - Dynamically adaptable flight pattern creation in-flight
- **Proposed Use**: Multiparametric volcanic gas and atmospheric monitoring; safe platform for approaching volcanic regions of interest
- **Unique Capability**: Only commercially available intermediate-range UAS meeting all specified criteria

## Use Cases & Applications

### Volcanic Monitoring & Crisis Response
- **Mission Profile**: Safe monitoring of stratovolcano activity during volcanic crisis
- **Operational Scenario**: 
  - 15+ km approach flight to region of interest
  - 10,000 ft climb to altitude
  - Minimum 15 minutes on-station observation time
  - Real-time data telemetry to ground team for adaptive mission planning
  - Hazard forecasting support
- **Data Requirements**: Real-time video streaming, spectroscopic UV data analysis, multi-sensor atmospheric/gas measurements
- **Advantages**: Superior endurance and ceiling vs. small UAS previously used for volcano monitoring; ability to carry and interchange multiple sensor suites; remote operation capability enabling safer human positioning during volcanic activity
