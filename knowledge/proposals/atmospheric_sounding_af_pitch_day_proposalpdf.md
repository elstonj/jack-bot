# Atmospheric Sounding AF Pitch Day Proposal

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** SBIR Phase I Proposal
- **Client/Agency:** U.S. Air Force
- **Program/Solicitation:** AF192-005 (SBIR Topic); Proposal Number F192-006-1526
- **Date:** 2019 (submitted for FY2019 SBIR cycle)
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** S0 UAS, SwiftCore avionics/Flight Management System
- **Key Personnel:** Dr. Jack Elston (Principal Investigator, Founder and CEO)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to modify its existing S0 UAS platform—originally designed for hurricane deployment—into a purpose-built system for atmospheric sounding and thermodynamic measurements. The system would provide targeted, recoverable in situ atmospheric profiling at lower cost than existing alternatives (balloon radiosondes, dropsondes, or satellite remote sensing), with data formatted for direct integration into Air Force weather models and support for sensor-reactive flight control algorithms.

## Technical Approach

**Core Concept:**
- Adapt the S0 UAS from its hurricane-focused mission to specialized atmospheric profiling
- Leverage existing SwiftCore autopilot/avionics architecture with modular design for rapid radio/sensor integration
- Develop control algorithms for sensor-reactive missions that focus data collection on areas of greatest interest
- Standardize data output formats for assimilation into Air Force forecasting models

**Key Technical Elements:**
- Advanced wind velocity measurement capabilities (latest developments referenced)
- 5-hole probe for 3D wind measurement
- Industry-standard atmospheric sensors: pressure, temperature, humidity
- Compact form factor suitable for high-wind operations
- Two-hour cruise time capability
- Tablet-based control software with mission command and live data display capabilities
- Wireless command/control and telemetry (leveraging SwiftCore's modular radio architecture)

**Phase I Approach (3-month effort):**
1. Establish specific Air Force customer needs for high-resolution local-scale atmospheric data
2. Develop detailed modification plan for S0 subsystems (software, comms, instruments)
3. Create technical integration plan for Air Force weather operations
4. Document all changes and Phase II development roadmap

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0 UAS:**
- Small, tube-launched unmanned aircraft originally designed for hurricane deployment
- Existing prototype used as baseline for atmospheric sounding version
- Capable of operating in high-wind, hazardous environments
- Price point: $5,000 per aircraft (noted as significantly lower than competing solutions)
- 2-hour cruise time
- Can carry variety of atmospheric sensors
- All-U.S. designed and manufactured (autopilot, firmware, software by BST)

**SwiftCore Flight Management System:**
- BST-developed autopilot with highly capable control algorithms
- Modular avionics architecture allowing rapid integration of new radios
- Capable of supporting advanced mission profiles and sensor-reactive control
- Existing ground station and user interface compatible
- Supports data relay to external systems if required

**Instrument Package:**
- Starting point: existing "metpack" on S0 (details not specified)
- Flexible for sensor changes based on customer needs
- Integration expertise demonstrated with mass spectrometers, gas sensors, and basic atmospheric sensors in past projects

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Target:**
- Air Force weather operations and forecasting
- Integration into existing DoD weather modeling systems
- Low-altitude atmospheric boundary layer measurements

**Secondary Markets Identified:**
- NOAA and National Weather Service (future expansion)
- Meteorological community using weather balloon launches
- Global meteorological stations (800+ worldwide use balloon-borne radiosondes twice daily)
- Nomadic atmospheric measurement campaigns
- Severe convective storm research (existing S0 heritage with hurricane and severe storm applications)

**Specific Measurement Needs:**
- High-resolution local-scale atmospheric thermodynamic data
- Vertical atmospheric profiling
- Boundary layer characterization
- 3D wind field measurements
- Targeted sampling around features of interest (nowcasting/forecasting applications)

## Key Results
This is a proposal for Phase I work, not a completed study. No experimental results are presented. The proposal emphasizes existing S0 UAS capabilities proven in hurricane and severe storm applications, plus demonstrated sensor integration history.
