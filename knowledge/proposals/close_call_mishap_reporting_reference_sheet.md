## Executive Summary
On April 17, 2018, a Black Swift Technologies S2 UAS experienced an uncontrolled descent near the end of a planned flight at Fraser Experimental Forest, Colorado, resulting in ground impact away from the intended landing site. The incident was caused by pre-existing damage to the elevator control surface that, when combined with a flight parameter adjustment, rendered the aircraft unable to maintain level flight. No personnel injuries occurred; aircraft damage was moderate and repairable.

## Mishap Description

During final approach and preparation for landing, the flight team was adjusting flight parameters when the aircraft began descending from intended altitude. The manual pilot attempted control override but was unable to arrest the descent rate. The aircraft was returned to autonomous mode; however, recovery was not achievable and it impacted the ground away from the planned landing site.

**Root Cause:** Damage to the elevator control surface sustained during transit from a previous flight had compromised the surface state, making aircraft control difficult. The flight parameter adjustment—normally a benign operation—pushed the aircraft into a configuration where it could not maintain level flight.

## Root Cause & Lessons Learned

The investigation identified that damage to the elevator control surface from a previous flight transit had degraded the surface to a state where normal control inputs could result in loss of controllability. The parameter change that triggered the descent would not have caused issues with an undamaged elevator surface.

**Corrective Action:** BST modified aircraft construction to prevent this particular failure mode from recurring in the same manner.
