## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes the S/0, an air-deployed unmanned aircraft system designed for deployment from NOAA P-3 aircraft to gather 3D wind measurements and atmospheric data (pressure, temperature, humidity, sea surface temperature, vehicle height) in hurricane environments. The S/0 aims to achieve an order of magnitude cost reduction compared to existing platforms while maintaining endurance and improving measurement quality through advanced autonomy and integrated sensor systems.

## Technical Approach

**Primary Innovation:**
- Development of a small, lightweight UAS (<3 lbs) specifically engineered for tube launch from NOAA P-3 aircraft
- Integration of Multi-Hole Probe (MHP) for accurate 3D wind vector measurements
- Integration of RD-41 radiosonde for pressure, temperature, humidity, and sea surface temperature measurement
- AVAPS integration for data relay using existing dropsonde communication infrastructure
- Autonomous navigation to designated sampling points in storm environments
- Automated intelligent sampling utilizing science payload sensors to minimize operator workload

**Key Design Features:**
- Removable tube launch mechanism (alternative hand-launch variant under consideration)
- Autonomous flight control via SwiftCore FMS
- Reduced complexity and weight vs. existing platforms
- 2-hour cruise endurance capability
- Rugged airframe capable of autonomous launch, flight, and landing in difficult conditions

## Products & Capabilities Described

### 1. **S/0 UAS**
- **What it is:** Purpose-built, air-deployed UAS for boundary layer observations in turbulent environments
- **Proposed use:** Primary deployment from NOAA P-3 for tropical, extra-tropical, and polar storm sampling
- **Key specifications:**
  - Weight: <3 lbs (within Part 101 balloon sonde deployment rules)
  - Price target: $5,000
  - 2-hour cruise endurance
  - Autonomous launch and landing capabilities
  - Integrated MHP and thermodynamic sensor suite

### 2. **SwiftCore Flight Management System (FMS)**
- **What it is:** End-to-end avionics solution for fully autonomous UAS operation
- **Components:** SwiftPilot autopilot, SwiftTab tablet UI, SwiftStation ground station
- **Capabilities:**
  - Autonomous navigation to designated sampling points
  - Sensor-based autonomous flight path modification
  - Minimal operator workload through advanced autonomy
  - Support for multiple simultaneous deployments
- **Track record:** Approved/used by NASA, NOAA deployments, CU, UTSI, commercial users

### 3. **Multi-Hole Probe (MHP)**
- **What it is:** Integrated wind velocity measurement device for small UAS
- **Specifications:**
  - Differential pressure measurements + magnetometer + IMU
  - Fusion algorithms for full wind vector solution
  - Measures: airspeed, altitude, angle-of-attack, side-slip, ambient temperature, relative humidity
  - 3D printing manufacturing reduces cost by factor of 10 vs. older techniques
- **Commercial status:** 10 units already delivered; second generation in development from commercial sales

### 4. **S/1 UAS**
- **What it is:** Advanced autonomous survey and mapping platform
- **Capabilities:** Hand launch, autonomous flight, precision autonomous belly landings
- **Applications:** Surveying, land management, crop damage assessment, ecological studies
- **Included:** Aircraft, batteries, ground station, tablet, field toolbox, carrying case, handset

### 5. **S/2 UAS**
- **What it is:** Purpose-built for flying scientific payloads in demanding atmospheric environments
- **Advantages:** Larger payload capacity, longer endurance, higher ceiling, greater range than vector wing airframes
- **Capabilities:** Autonomous launch, flight, and landing in mountainous regions
- **Variant:** S/2 XT (ruggedized version for extreme environments)

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Market (S/0 Specific)
- **NOAA P-3 deployment:** Tropical, extra-tropical, and polar storm sampling (hurricanes, cyclones)
- **Lower cost enables:** Wider adoption; deployment of multiple S/0 units per storm (vs. fewer Coyote UAS currently used)

### Secondary Markets (S/0 with modifications)
1. **Atmospheric Boundary Layer Research (Hand-Launched Variant)**
   - University-level atmospheric science research
   - Scripps Institution and DOE boundary layer kinematic/thermodynamic sensing
   - Competitive advantage: Entire system cost below single Aeroprobe wind sensor; 2-hour endurance; operation in difficult atmospheric conditions
   - Target institutions already engaged with BST

2. **Weather Balloon/Radiosonde Replacement**
   - Deployment from weather balloons with glide-back capability
   - Market: 800+ weather stations worldwide deploy balloon radiosondes twice daily
   - Advantages: Reusable (vs. lost expendable sondes); provides cost savings; enables observations in remote ocean locations
   - Key barrier: regulatory hurdles (FAA line-of-sight and altitude restrictions)
   - Potential partners: National Weather Service for operational forecasting and monitoring
   - Historical collaboration: NOAA Earth System Research Laboratory (Ru-Shan Gao)

3. **Volcanic Sampling**
   - Deployable in harsh volcanic environments
   - Current NASA/government agency projects with BST personnel
   - Early validation and refinement path

4. **Commercial Remote Sensing & Environmental Monitoring**
   - Aerial photography and imaging
   - Data acquisition and analytics
   - Mapping and surveying
   - 3D modeling
   - Disaster risk management and mitigation
   - Inspection and environmental monitoring
   - Precision agriculture
   - Infrastructure monitoring/inspection

## Competitive Advantages

1. **Cost Leadership:** $5,000 target price vs. Coyote UAS (primary competitor)

2. **Integrated Superior Wind Measurement:** Multi-Hole Probe provides accurate 3D wind estimation vs. Coyote's autopilot-telemetry-based wind estimation

3. **Proven Sensor Integration:** RD-41 radiosonde is well-characterized with long operational history in hurricane environments

4. **Extended Communication Range:** AVAPS integration leverages existing dropsonde infrastructure vs. Coyote's limited two-way radio

5. **Operational Ease:** Automated intelligent sampling mirroring existing GPS dropsonde operations; reduces operator burden of continuous flight plan updates

6. **Vertical Integration:** Complete in-house ownership of hardware, firmware, software, airframe, and sensors enables:
   - Maximized accuracy and efficiency
   - Rapid evolution to emerging needs
   - Core subject matter expertise
   - 2-5 year development barrier to entry (>$1M investment)

7. **First-Mover Advantage:** First to integrate this capability into commercially available small UAS
