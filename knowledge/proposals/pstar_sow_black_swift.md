# PSTAR SOW Black Swift

## Document Metadata
- Type: Statement of Work (SOW)
- Client/Agency: NASA (via North Carolina A&T State University)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA PSTAR (Predict, Serve, Transform) - "Unmanned Aerial Systems based Active Hurricane Sensing"
- Date: September 23, 2021
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 Fixed wing drones
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston, Ph.D. (Founder and CEO)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies commits to building 16 S0 fixed-wing drones for hurricane and clear air turbulence sensing, developing flight planning and simulation software for integration with NC A&T's systems, conducting field testing in 2023-2024, and providing simulation support for a Jupiter atmospheric modeling application led by NC A&T and JPL.

## Technical Approach

**Task 1: Hardware Development**
- Build 16 S0 fixed-wing drones with one-way or two-way communications capability with P3 aircraft
- Design to support sequential or simultaneous deployment from P3 aircraft

**Task 2: Software Development**
- Integrate NC A&T flight planning software into BST simulation software suite
- Ensure theoretical model compatibility with real-world flight testing
- Support for both Clear Air Turbulence (CAT) and Tropical Cyclone (TC) testing scenarios
- Route re-optimization after data assimilation
- Leverage prior parameters and models from internal NOAA collaboration to establish starting point for CAT/TC parameters

**Task 3: Field Deployment**
- CAT and TC field testing in coordination with NOAA and NC A&T

**Task 4: Planetary Simulation**
- Provide accurate small vehicle simulation environment for planetary bodies
- Add Jupiter atmospheric model to existing simulation capabilities

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0 Fixed-Wing Drones**
- What it is: Small fixed-wing unmanned aerial system
- Proposed use: Deployment from P3 aircraft to sense conditions in hurricanes and clear air turbulence
- Capabilities: One-way/two-way communications with P3, sequential/simultaneous deployment capability

**BST Simulation Software Suite**
- Accurate small vehicle simulation environment
- Capable of modeling atmospheric conditions for Earth and planetary atmospheres
- Integration point for external flight planning software

## Use Cases & Applications

1. **Hurricane Sensing**: Active sensing and data collection in tropical cyclones
2. **Clear Air Turbulence (CAT) Detection**: Measurement and mapping of CAT for aviation safety
3. **Atmospheric Data Assimilation**: Route optimization based on collected data
4. **Planetary Atmosphere Modeling**: Jupiter atmospheric simulation support

## Notable Details
- Coordination with NOAA on prior CAT/TC parameters
- P3 aircraft as primary deployment platform
- Joint effort with NC A&T (flight planning) and JPL (Jupiter application)
- Testing timeline: 2023-2024 field campaigns
- Flexibility in deployment strategies (sequential vs. simultaneous drone release)
- Leverages internal NOAA collaboration experience for starting parameters
