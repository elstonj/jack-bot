## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes integrating its compact S0 uncrewed aircraft system into a stratospheric balloon gondola to create a transformative sensing platform for sustained atmospheric observation. The system would be deployed on-demand from the balloon to collect high-resolution measurements in hazardous or remote environments, with initial focus on wildfire detection and monitoring. NASA partnership would accelerate mechanical, electrical, and command-and-control integration with an Aerostar gondola, culminating in collaborative flight testing with NASA Ames.

## Technical Approach

**Core Concept:**
- S0 UAS deployed from a stratospheric balloon gondola via a vertical sonobuoy-style launch tube
- Balloon serves as persistent observing platform and mobile base station
- S0 recovers to controlled flight within seconds of tube launch and operates in boundary layer
- Real-time data feeds into operational models (e.g., WRF-SFIRE)

**Key Technical Objectives:**
1. Design and fabricate vertical balloon mount system with heated cradle
2. Integrate command and control (C2) systems for preflight avionics checks and mission uploads via balloon-mounted microcontrollers
3. Perform lab simulations and flight planning
4. Conduct live system demonstrations with NASA stakeholders

**Integration Challenges:**
- Establishing reliable power and data interface to support onboard warming in stratospheric conditions
- Executing minimal communication protocols to power on and verify S0 prior to launch
- FAA coordination for Part 107 waiver or Certificate of Authorization (COA) for simultaneous balloon flight and mid-air UAS release

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS
- **What it is:** Compact, lightweight uncrewed aircraft system
- **Specifications:**
  - Weight: 1.2 kg
  - Wingspan: 83 cm
  - Highly robust design proven in extreme environments
- **Payload Capabilities:** Measures 3D wind vectors, pressure, temperature, humidity, surface temperature, and terrain/wave height
- **Operational Record:**
  - Successfully executed multiple flights into hurricanes in 2024, including Category 5 Hurricane Milton
  - Proven ability to recover to controlled flight within seconds of tube launch and survive turbulent boundary layers
  - Extensive past performance with NOAA integrating complex sensor payloads (e.g., NightFOX)
- **Proposed Use:** On-demand deployment from stratospheric balloon gondola to collect targeted, high-resolution atmospheric measurements; initial 6-month demonstration focuses on single-aircraft deployment before scaling to multi-aircraft "swarming" capabilities
