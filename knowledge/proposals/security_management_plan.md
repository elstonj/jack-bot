## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies LLC submitted this IT Security Management Plan to NASA to describe processes and procedures for securing IT resources developed or used under SBIR/STTR contract 80NSSC22CA192. The plan outlines BST's compliance with NASA IT security requirements, including remote access protocols to the SBIR/STTR Awardee Firm Electronic Handbook (EHB), handling of Sensitive But Unclassified (SBU) information, and rules of behavior for personnel.

## Technical Approach
BST's security approach centers on:
- Remote access to NASA's SBIR/STTR Awardee Firm EHB (https://ehb8.gsfc.nasa.gov/contracts/public/firmHome.do) for submission of contract deliverables, invoices, and technical reports
- Access management through NASA Account Management System (NAMS) requiring NASA Agency User ID and IdMAX-issued profile passwords
- Registration limited to personnel involved in contract negotiation and administration
- Mandatory NASA Online Annual IT Security Training for all registered personnel
- Adherence to NIST and NASA IT Security requirements and guidelines

## Notable Details

**Information Handling Requirements:**
- Protection of confidentiality, integrity, and availability of NASA Electronic Information
- Specific handling of Sensitive But Unclassified (SBU) information as broadly defined unclassified information pertinent to U.S. national interest
- No additional NASA IT systems or Agency data access required beyond the EHB

**Sensitive Information Obligations (per 1852.237-72):**
- Use of sensitive information limited to contract performance purposes only
- Safeguarding from unauthorized use and disclosure
- Access restricted to employees with need-to-know
- Employee training and written affirmation of compliance required
- Monitoring and incident reporting procedures mandatory
- Understanding that violations may result in contract termination or contractor debarment

**General Rules of Behavior (15 specific rules):**
- Lawful, authorized use of NASA systems only
- Encryption of NASA data on mobile/transportable devices
- Screen-locking and logout protocols
- Incident reporting requirements
- Password protection standards
- Prohibition on anonymizer sites
- Separation of duties compliance

**Incident Response:**
- Immediate notification to NASA Security Operations Center for any information security incidents
- Notification to Contracting Officer (CO) and Contracting Officer's Technical Representative (COTR) required for data compromise, misuse, loss, or destruction

**Legal Framework Referenced:**
- NASA FAR Supplement clause 1852.204-76
- Computer Security Act of 1987 (PL 100-235)
- Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986
- National Information Infrastructure Protection Act of 1996
- Privacy Act (5 U.S.C. § 552a)
