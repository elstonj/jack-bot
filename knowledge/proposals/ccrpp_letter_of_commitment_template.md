# CCRPP Letter of Commitment Template

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Commitment Template (for NASA SBIR/STTR applications)
- Client/Agency: NASA (SBIR/STTR Program)
- Program/Solicitation: Civilian Commercialization Readiness Pilot Program (CCRPP)
- Date: Template created 2021-12-15; last modified 2021-12-30
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor)

## Document Purpose
This is a **template document** rather than a completed proposal or report. It provides the standardized format and language required for investor/partner commitment letters supporting NASA SBIR/STTR CCRPP applications.

## Key Components Required

### Letter Structure
- Official letterhead of investing firm/agency
- Date and addressee information
- Confirmation of commitment to fund a Phase II SBIR/STTR project through the CCRPP

### Required Financial Commitment Statement
- Dollar amount of unencumbered matching funds
- Timeline: funds must be available within 45 business days of CCRPP contract selection
- Direct payment to the small business concern (SBIR/STTR firm)

### Required Content Sections

**For Government Agency Investors:**
- Statement of how the resulting CCRPP technology will integrate into the agency's future activities
- Description of any planned future funding beyond the CCRPP effort
- Impact statement on the agency

**For Private Sector Investors:**
- Statement of how investment furthers the original Phase II technology or its commercialization
- Anticipated project impact
- Timeline of when investor/customer needs the resulting technology/product

### Signatory Information
- Investor name, title, phone, email
- Verification that contribution aligns with investor's programmatic and development priorities

## Notable Details
- Template includes conditional language ("is/is not") for customer relationship clarification
- Emphasizes unencumbered, unconstrained funds availability
- Requires specific linkage between Phase II work and CCRPP development objectives
- Document format accommodates both government and commercial co-investment models
