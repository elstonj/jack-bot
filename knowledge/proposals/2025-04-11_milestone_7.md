## Executive Summary
BST is developing a small UAS-based L-band radiometer system to measure soil moisture and derive runway soil integrity (cone index) for U.S. Air Force operations. Milestone #7 focuses on planning and execution of final field testing at Pond's Field, a private unimproved runway. The team has collected significant soil moisture data across wet and moderately wet conditions but requires additional dry soil data (<10% moisture) to complete coefficient derivation for soil integrity algorithms.

## Technical Approach
**System Design:**
- L-band radiometer as primary sensing instrument with modifications for enhanced accuracy
- Auxiliary sensors for surface temperature and NDVI to enable real-time soil moisture calculation
- Small UAS platform adapted to carry integrated sensor payload
- E2 sensor application architecture for command and telemetry relay to operator tablet

**Algorithm Development:**
- L-band-based soil moisture measurements converted to cone index (soil integrity metric)
- Algorithms account for soil type variations
- Coefficients derived from field-collected data paired with ground-truth cone penetrometer measurements

**Data Collection Strategy:**
- Three soil moisture regimes targeted: wet (>20%), moderately wet (10-20%), dry (<10%)
- Multiple sorties per test condition to build robust datasets
- In situ cone index measurements collected simultaneously for algorithm validation

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Small UAS with Integrated Sensor Payload
- **What it is:** Modified unmanned aircraft equipped with L-band radiometer, surface temperature sensors, NDVI sensor, and E2 telemetry architecture
- **Proposed use:** Autonomous or remote-piloted flights over airfields to measure soil moisture at runway and surrounding areas
- **Specifications:** 
  - Flight altitude: <50 ft AGL (worst-case glide distance 40-50 meters with 10kt airspeed, 10kt wind, 4-second reaction time)
  - Airspeed: <8 knots
  - Sortie duration: 0.2–0.5 hours typical
  - Part 107 compliant operations in uncontrolled airspace

### L-Band Radiometer
- **What it is:** Modified L-band sensing instrument refined from existing BST radiometer technology
- **Proposed use:** Primary soil moisture measurement from airborne platform
- **Specifications:** Detects soil moisture across range of 10–25%+ (data to date spans 15-25%)

### E2 Sensor Application Architecture
- **What it is:** Operator control and telemetry relay system for sensor payload
- **Proposed use:** Real-time command of sensor and display of telemetry on operator tablet during flight

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Use Case: Runway Integrity Assessment for Military Airlift**
- **Mission:** Validate whether small UAS-based soil moisture measurements can predict runway soil integrity (cone index) for C-130 aircraft landing operations
- **Operational Context:** Air Force need for rapid assessment of unimproved or damaged runways to determine load-bearing capacity
- **Specific Application:** Airfield readiness assessment, rapid repair prioritization, aircraft recovery operations

**Test Sites:**
1. **Pawnee National Grasslands (PNG):** Natural grassland with varied soil moisture; used for algorithm development and validation
2. **Sod Farm:** Agricultural setting for initial wet/moderately wet data collection
3. **Pond's Field:** Private unimproved runway (final validation site); 54m × 1500m strip; Part 107 operations; surrounded by collection and launch/recovery zones
