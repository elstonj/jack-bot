## Executive Summary
This is a blank NASA CCRPP Form B2 application template for SBIR/STTR contractors seeking to mature and commercialize previously-developed technologies with investor backing. The form requires applicants to detail customer objectives, commercialization strategy, implementation approach, and technology/manufacturing readiness levels across a 30-page maximum.

## Document Purpose
Form B2 is one component of a multi-part CCRPP Application submitted through NASA's SBIR/STTR Electronic Handbook. It requires:
- A **letter of commitment from an investor** as mandatory entrance criterion
- Demonstration that the SBIR/STTR project has achieved sufficient results to justify acceleration
- Clear evidence of "strong customer pull" at project completion

## Required Content Sections

### 1.0 Customer Objectives
- Overview of primary/target customer (NASA program or non-NASA with NASA-aligned applications)
- Customer's acquisition plan for the technology
- Identification of whether customer and investor are the same entity
- Description of program/project/customer alignment
- Expected impacts and benefits (mission-enabling, performance improvements, cost savings, new capabilities, reliability improvements, etc.)
- Technology need dates (Qtr. & FY specific)

### 2.0 Additional Commercialization Information
Applicants must provide (updated from Phase II or new):
1. **Market Feasibility & Competition Strategy**: target markets, competitive advantages, key customers/applications, projected market size, time-to-market, market share within 5 years, anticipated competition
2. **Commercialization Strategy**: innovation commercialization approach, company's 5-year business plans, prior commercialization experience, committed funding sources (Phase III, Industry Investment, etc.)
3. **Key Management/Technical Personnel**: skills/experience in commercialization, organizational structure, timelines for acquiring additional expertise
4. **Financial Strategy**: private financial resources committed, current investment/sales/licensing indicators, projected financial requirements, committed capital sources, current financial condition evidence, integration into broader commercialization planning
5. **Intellectual Property**: plans/status for securing patents, copyrights, trade secrets necessary for competitive advantage and investment

### 3.0 Implementation Approach

#### 3.1 Maturation Strategy
- Identify technology maturation tasks with descriptions
- Describe transition approach from maturation stage to initial capability achievement
- Required steps/activities to integrate, test, and certify SBIR/STTR technology in target program/application
- Identify supporting research efforts/programs required for successful maturation
- Describe how technology supports or is supported by other NASA programs
- Delineate application of funding sources and financial contingency management

#### 3.1.1 Schedule
- Tentative detailed schedule (Gantt chart format)
- Milestone table (consistent with budget Form C)
- Separate schedules if multiple subsystems/components involved

#### 3.2 Procurement Strategy
- All applicable procurements and subcontract relationships as separate line items

#### 3.3 Technical Strategy
Consider:
- Implementation funding requests
- Technology issues
- Integration issues
- Test and evaluation concept/implementation
- Environmental, safety, and occupational health (if required)

#### 3.4 Logistics Strategy (as needed)
Consider on as-needed basis:
- Design interface
- System reliability requirements
- Technical data
- Maintenance planning
- Computer resources support
- Manpower and personnel
- Procurements and subcontract relationships
- Supply support
- Support equipment requirements
- Facilities
- Asset management
- Environmental issues
- Training and training support

#### 3.5 Manufacturing Strategy (if applicable)
Address manufacturing readiness demonstration including:
- Demonstration of production readiness
- Sources of product production/supply
- Lean manufacturing and manufacturing development initiatives
- Industrial capabilities

#### 3.6 Readiness Levels
Provide Technology Readiness Levels (TRL) and Manufacturing Readiness Levels (MRL) as applicable, including:
- **Initial TRL/MRL** with explanation/justification and date
- **Projected Final TRL/MRL** with rationale supporting achievement based on customer needs
- **Minimum Acceptable Integration Readiness Levels** (TRL/MRL, provided by customer, matching investor letter)
- **Projected Final Integration Readiness Levels** with supporting rationale

## Appendices

### Appendix A: Manufacturing Readiness Levels (MRL 1-10)
Progressive scale from "Basic manufacturing implications identified" (MRL 1) through "Full Rate Production demonstrated and lean production practices in place" (MRL 10), organized by phase:
- Material Solutions Analysis (MRL 1-3)
- Technology Development (MRL 4-6)
- Engineering and Manufacturing Development (MRL 7-8)
- Production and Deployment (MRL 9-10)

Each level includes definition, description, and detailed capability requirements.

### Appendix B: Technology Readiness Level (TRL 1-9)
Describes technology maturity from observation of basic principles through flight-proven operations:
- **TRL 1**: Basic principles observed and reported
- **TRL 2**: Technology concept and/or application formulated
- **TRL 3**: Proof of concept (analytical and experimental)
- **TRL 4**: Component/breadboard validation in laboratory
- **TRL 5**: Component/breadboard validation in relevant environment
- **TRL 6**: System/prototype demonstration in relevant environment
- **TRL 7**: System prototype demonstration in operational environment
- **TRL 8**: Actual system completed and flight-qualified
- **TRL 9**: Actual system flight-proven through successful mission operations

Each TRL includes hardware description, software description, and exit criteria.

**Definitions Section** provides:
- Proof of Concept, Breadboard, Brassboard, Prototype Unit, Engineering Unit definitions
- Mission Configuration, Laboratory Environment, Relevant Environment, Operational Environment definitions
