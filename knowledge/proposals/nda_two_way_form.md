## Executive Summary
This is a standard two-way mutual nondisclosure agreement template used by Black Swift Technologies to protect confidential information exchanged during business discussions and evaluations of potential business relationships. The agreement establishes confidentiality obligations for both parties with a three-year term and includes standard protections for trade secrets, exceptions for publicly available information, and remedies for breach.

## Key Provisions

### Scope of Confidential Information
- Covers all technical and business information exchanged by either party, including oral and written materials, analyses, compilations, studies, and derivative documents
- Applies to information shared by representatives (employees, consultants, advisors, financing sources, lenders, etc.)

### Standard Exceptions
Information excluded from protection:
- Information already in public domain (not due to breach)
- Information received from third parties on nonconfidential basis with proper representation of authority to disclose
- Information known to receiving party prior to disclosure (confirmed by written records)
- Information disclosed with written approval

### Obligations & Use Restrictions
- Receiving party must keep information confidential without prior written consent from disclosing party
- Information may only be used for the stated "Evaluation" purpose
- Must limit disclosure to representatives with need-to-know
- Receiving party responsible for representatives' compliance
- No grant of intellectual property rights (patents, trademarks, trade secrets, copyrights)

### Required Disclosure Provision
- Party receiving legal/regulatory disclosure demand must notify other party promptly
- Other party may seek protective order or waive confidentiality obligation
- If legally compelled to disclose despite lack of protective order, disclosing party must provide advance written notice and use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment

### Liability & Warranties
- Neither party warrants accuracy or completeness of information
- No liability for reliance on provided information
- Representations only enforceable in definitive executed agreements
- Neither party liable to other's representatives for information-related claims

### Remedies & Enforcement
- Breach subject to injunctive relief and specific performance (not limited to money damages)
- Disclosing party entitled to recover costs and attorneys' fees in enforcement actions
- No bond requirement for equitable relief

### Term & Trade Secrets
- Confidentiality obligations: 3 years from execution
- Trade secret protections survive termination under applicable law

### Governing Law
- Colorado law, without conflict of law principles
- Venue: Boulder County state courts or Denver federal courts (Colorado)
- Recipient waives venue objections
