## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies completed a Phase II SBIR effort to develop a commercially viable, turn-key UAS capable of routine flights into harsh volcanic environments. The project successfully modified the S2 aircraft with a sub-2.5kg payload for measuring trace gases, particulates, meteorological parameters, and collecting remote sensing imagery, culminating in field operations at Hawaii's Fissure 8.

## Technical Approach
- Modified the S2 UAS platform for volcanic environment operations with dust/particulate protection measures
- Expanded operational altitude capability to 20,000 ft MSL
- Developed a sub-2.5kg integrated payload for simultaneous collection of multiple atmospheric parameters
- Designed and tested two complete S2 XT airframes
- Modified user interface to enable easy creation of sampling missions
- Conducted field testing and validation in relevant environment (volcanic plume)

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 XT (Modified S2 UAS)
- **What it is:** A ruggedized unmanned aircraft system designed for harsh environment operations
- **Modifications for volcano work:** Dust/particulate protection, expanded altitude capability (20,000 ft MSL), enhanced sampling mission interface
- **Sensors/payload:** 
  - Sub-2.5kg payload for atmospheric sampling
  - Trace gas measurement capability
  - Particulate sizing and density sensors
  - Pressure, temperature, humidity sensors
  - 3D wind measurement
  - EO (Electro-Optical) and thermal imagery
  - Forward video
  - Atmospheric sample collection cassettes for lab analysis

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary: Volcanic Plume Monitoring
- Detailed study of Fissure 8 (Hawaii) including:
  - Trace gas concentration mapping
  - Particulate size and distribution analysis
  - 3D wind field characterization
  - Atmospheric thermodynamic measurements
  - RGB orthomosaic and thermal mapping
  - 3D fissure and flow modeling
- Background trace gas level characterization

### Secondary/Planned Applications
- USGS Makushin volcano campaign (Aleutian Islands)
- Wildland fire observation (active use by NOAA)
- IAVCEI (International Association of Volcanology and Chemistry of the Earth's Interior) calibration campaign
- Potential first approved BVLOS (Beyond Visual Line of Sight) flights on US volcanoes
