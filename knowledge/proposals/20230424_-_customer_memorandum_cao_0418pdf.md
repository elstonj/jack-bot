# SBIR/STTR Phase II Customer Memorandum Template

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Template/Form (Customer Memorandum)
- **Client/Agency:** Department of the Air Force (DAF), AFWERX, AFRL/RGV
- **Program/Solicitation:** SBIR/STTR Phase II - Open Topic
- **Date:** April 24, 2023
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None (this is a blank template)
- **Key Personnel:** Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is a mandatory template document for Air Force SBIR/STTR Phase II proposals submitted through AFWERX. It provides the standardized form structure and instructions that companies must complete to document Air Force organizational interest, defense mission needs, stakeholder engagement, security/cybersecurity requirements, and Phase III transition strategy for their proposed Phase II efforts.

## Document Purpose & Use
This is a **form template and instruction guide**, not a proposal or report from Black Swift Technologies. The document serves as:
- A required form that SBIR/STTR Phase II applicants must complete and submit
- Official documentation of Department of the Air Force commitment to partner with a Phase II awardee
- A mechanism for identifying stakeholders, TPOCs (Technical Points of Contact), and government funding commitments
- Compliance documentation citing statutory authority (15 U.S.C. §638, SBIR/STTR Program Policy Directive Oct 2020)

## Key Sections & Requirements

### Section 2: Defense Mission Need
Companies must describe:
- How their technology development addresses a DAF/DoD mission need
- Impact to US Air Force and/or US Space Force operations
- Up to five measurable goals for Phase II completion

### Section 3: Project Timeline
Define start date and performance period for Phase II execution.

### Section 4: Phase II Funding Commitment
Documents non-SBIR/STTR Government funding supplements, if applicable. Requirements:
- Draft funding documents (Form 9s/MIPRs) due within 45 days of solicitation closing
- Certified funding documents due 5 business days after selection letters sent
- Contact: AFRL.SBIR.STTR@us.af.mil

### Section 5: Additional Agreement
Notes that DAF will formalize cooperation with appropriate legal, contracting, and program office personnel. May include:
- Government-Furnished Equipment/Information (GFE/I)
- Government facilities access for testing/demonstration
- Government-provided licenses
- Environmental considerations

### Section 6: Phase III Interest
Indicates DAF interest in transitioning successful Phase II innovations to Phase III contracts, subject to:
- Government acquisition planning
- Funds availability
- Security assessments
- Small Business/Legal/Contracting review
- **Important note:** This creates no obligation for Phase III funding

### Section 7: Milestones
References Attachment 1.1 (Schedule of Milestones) that must provide value to end-users/customers.

### Section 8: Stakeholders
**Required personnel identification:**
- **Primary End-User Organization POC:** The organization that will use/benefit from the solution
- **Primary Customer Organization POC:** The organization managing procurement, sustainment, funding, contracting, and logistics for Phase III
- **Primary Phase II Technical Point of Contact (TPOC):** Must be DAF civilian or military member (not support contractor/SETA contractor)
  - Name, Rank/Grade, Organization, Email, Duty Phone
- **Alternate Phase II TPOC:** Same format as primary
- **Strategic Stakeholders:** Identify MAJCOM/HQ function and PEO/Center most impacted

**Staff Function Concurrence Required:**
- Security Office
- Information Assurance/Cybersecurity
- Finance (if Government funding commitments)
- Contracting
- Strategy, Integration, and Requirements
- Small Business Office
- Commander
- Legal Counsel
- Engineering/SPO/Test
- Public Affairs

**Note:** Primary Customer and Primary End-User cannot be the same person.

### Section 9: Phase III Transition Strategy
Non-boilerplate narrative required describing:
- Phase III funding strategy
- Requirements personnel role (if applicable)
- Contract vehicle(s)
- Integration plan
- Resources available at afwerx.com

### Section 10: Security
Companies must specify:
- Whether military installation/facility access is required
- Whether Common Access Cards (CACs) are needed for DoD network access
- Whether classified accesses via DD254 are required
- TPOC will coordinate with visitor centers/security offices for sponsorship/CAC processing

### Section 11: Cybersecurity
For software/IT solutions connecting to DoD networks or utilizing Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI):
- Information Assurance/Cybersecurity office determines if Authorization to Operate (ATO) or Interim Authorization To Test (IATT) required
- Compliance with NIST SP 800-171 Rev 1 and DFARS PGI 239.76
- Platform One DevSecOps environments may be utilized
- AFVentures does not manage these processes; customer/TPOC responsible

### Section 12: Flight Testing or UAS Utilization
If company requires flight testing or unmanned aerial system (UAS) use:
- Must coordinate immediately after contract award with TPOC, AFRL/DO, and AFWERX Test Personnel
- Must prepare flight test planning documents per AFRL/DO instructions
- Blanket waiver exists for COTS UAS procurement for cyber analysis, signature, system checkout, or profile data in benign/controlled RF environment
- "Use waivers" may be required; early coordination recommended as may impact milestone schedules
- Contract execution not dependent on use waiver completion

**Flight testing definition:** Any set of related events where a vehicle moves through air using controlled/maneuvering aerial transport (includes balloon flight, attributed aircraft, manned/unmanned aircraft, air taxis, ground tests, cockpit evaluations). Does NOT include purely ballistic flight paths.

### Section 13: Signatories
**Critical Requirements:**
- Two CAC-enabled digital signatures MANDATORY—memoranda without both will NOT be accepted
- DAF Customer signatory and DAF End-User signatory cannot be the same person
- Signatories must be in positions to speak for organizations, understanding and exerting influence over operational needs, formal requirements, or funding
- Rank/grade requirements vary but individuals should be appropriately positioned
- Brief descriptions of suitability required for each signatory

## Mandatory Compliance Notes

The document emphasizes:
- **Template is MANDATORY; all elements MUST be present and complete**
- All fields must be filled in; if not applicable, indicate "N/A"
- Proposals without completed and signed memoranda per template will be determined **non-responsive** and disqualified
- Customer Memorandum without two CAC-enabled digital signatures will NOT be accepted
- No boilerplate language acceptable in transition strategy narrative—must be tailored to specific organization and solution

## Notable Details

### Process Timeline
- Solicitation close date is key milestone
- Draft funding documents: within 45 days of close
- Selection letters sent → Certified funding documents due 5 business days later
- If certified docs not provided by close +45 days, effort moves forward without Phase II funding commitment

### TPOC Responsibilities
TPOCs will:
- Receive and accept/reject deliverables
- Provide performance and progress feedback
- Overcome tactical execution barriers (base access, waivers, certifications)
- Drive effort toward Phase III transition if successful
- Coordinate security, CAC processing, classified accesses, flight testing approvals

### Important Limitations
- No obligation for Phase III funding despite expression of interest
- Subject to Government acquisition planning, funds availability, security assessments
- AFVentures does NOT manage security, cybersecurity, or ATO processes—customer/TPOC responsible
- Phase II Funding Commitments not required but may be offered to supplement program funds
