## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies completed Phase I development of a low-cost small unmanned aircraft system (sUAS) equipped with a passive L-band microwave radiometer for mapping volumetric soil moisture content. The system achieved tight integration of sensor and avionics to enable precise low-altitude flight (15-30m AGL) for high-resolution soil moisture mapping at ~5-15m resolution down to 5-20cm depth. This represents the first commercial system capable of providing localized, high-resolution soil moisture data at agricultural plot scales.

## Technical Approach
- **Sensor Integration**: L-band passive microwave radiometer integrated with sUAS avionics and airframe for coordinated low-altitude operations
- **Flight Control**: Precise flight control system designed for ultra-low altitude missions (15-30m AGL) required for sensor accuracy
- **Altitude Measurement**: Laser altimeter for measuring altitude above ground level (AGL)
- **Platform**: Tempest airframe modified to accommodate soil moisture sensor and auxiliary equipment
- **Data Management**: Custom circuit boards designed for NDVI/thermal imaging, data logging, and power management
- **Regulatory Path**: FAA Certificate of Authorization (COA) application preparation

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS
- **What it is**: A small unmanned aircraft system with integrated passive L-band microwave radiometer
- **Proposed use**: NASA soil moisture mapping for calibration and validation of satellite missions (SMAP, Aquarius); precision agriculture applications; flood planning and mitigation
- **Key specifications**:
  - Operating altitude: 15-30m AGL
  - Soil moisture measurement depth: 5-20cm
  - Spatial resolution: ~15m
  - Passive L-band microwave radiometer (no active transmission)
  - Low-cost, easy-to-operate design

### Tempest sUAS (Modified)
- **Platform**: Modified with sensor interfaces and payload accommodation
- **Modifications**: Airframe and powerplant modifications to integrate L-band radiometer, laser altimeter, and auxiliary electronics
- **User Interface**: Enhanced for soil moisture mapping mission planning and execution

## Use Cases & Applications

### NASA Applications
- Calibration and validation of SMAP (Soil Moisture Active-Passive) satellite mission with higher resolution, local-scale data
- Calibration and validation of Aquarius satellite mission (ocean surface salinity)
- Augmentation of Airborne Microwave Observatory of Subcanopy and Subsurface (AirMOSS) program follow-on work
- Earth observing missions requiring highly capable, robust sUAS

### Commercial/Non-NASA Applications
- **Precision Agriculture**: Crop productivity optimization through localized soil moisture data for farmers
- **Flood Planning & Mitigation**: Early warning and management through detailed moisture mapping
- **General Payload Applications**: Suitable for any commercial application with similar payload and mission requirements

## Technical Accomplishments (Phase I)
1. Defined comprehensive requirements and validation techniques for soil moisture mapping sUAS
2. Determined soil moisture payload configuration for sUAS operation, including:
   - Mission profile definition
   - Interface specifications
3. Completed sensor interface design for Tempest UAS with:
   - Airframe and powerplant modification tradeoff analysis
   - FAA COA application preparation
4. Designed, fabricated, and tested auxiliary circuit boards:
   - NDVI/thermal imaging boards
   - Data logger
   - Power management
   - Full integration of sensing payload into Tempest UAS
5. Created evaluation criteria documentation

## Notable Details
- **Unique Market Position**: Claimed to be "the only commercial system capable of mapping soil moisture content at critical depths in a low cost, easy to operate package"
- **Regulatory Readiness**: Proactively prepared FAA Certificate of Authorization application during Phase I
- **Dual-Use Potential**: Technology applicable to both government Earth science missions and commercial agricultural sector
- **Integration Approach**: Emphasis on tight coupling between sensor and platform (avionics, airframe, flight control) rather than bolt-on payload integration
- **Low Altitude Capability**: Notable technical achievement in precise flight control at 15-30m AGL, a challenging operational envelope for sUAS
- **Sensor Type**: Passive L-band radiometer eliminates spectrum licensing concerns and enables extended mission durations vs. active systems
