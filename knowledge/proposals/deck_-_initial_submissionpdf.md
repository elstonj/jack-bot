## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies seeks $250K in AIA grant funding to complement a $750K NASA commercialization commitment to advance the MLD (Methane Leak Detection) platform from prototype to first-unit production. The funding would enable deployment with anchor customer PDCE, catalyze an additional $1M in investment, and drive projected revenues to $40M within 4 years in the expanding UAS market.

## Technical Approach

**MLD Platform Architecture:**
- **Primary UAS**: S2 fixed-wing platform (best-in-class efficiency, reliability, long mission duration)
- **VTOL capability**: Fly from any location without infrastructure
- **Sensor integration**: Quanta3 < 2 ppb CH₄ laser absorption spectrometer (world's lightest methane sensor)
- **Navigation system**: AI-based, non-linear flight algorithms with real-time sensor-directed flight path optimization
- **"Follow the scent" capability**: Real-time flight plan modification based on methane plume detection
- **BVLOS expertise**: Proven beyond-visual-line-of-sight navigation and control
- **Obstacle avoidance**: Advanced vision and radar-based systems
- **Real-time wind measurement**: Onboard absolute wind-speed measurement (unique capability)

**Operational Method:**
1. Intelligent 3D flight path with nose-mounted sensor (PPB sensitivity methane detection)
2. Real-time flight plan adjustment to track CH₄ plumes
3. Multiple passes through detected plumes generate 3D rendering
4. Data analysis provides leak location and size to <10 SCFH resolution on geo-accurate image
5. Single S2 can survey 10+ square miles and 50+ facilities per day
