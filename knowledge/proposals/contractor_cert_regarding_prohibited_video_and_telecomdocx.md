# Contractor Certification Regarding Prohibited Video Surveillance and Telecommunications Services and Equipment

## Document Metadata
- Type: Compliance certification / contractor representation
- Client/Agency: Department of Defense (DoD)
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR/STTR Program (Phase II, 2020.3)
- Date: 31 May 2021
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None
- Key Personnel: Jack S. Elston (authorized signatory)

## Executive Summary
This is a mandatory DoD compliance certification in which Black Swift Technologies certifies regarding its use of prohibited telecommunications and video surveillance equipment/services. The document incorporates FAR clauses 52.204-24, 52.204-25, and 52.204-26, which implement Section 889 of the John S. McCain National Defense Authorization Act for Fiscal Year 2019 (Pub. L. 115-232), prohibiting use of covered telecommunications equipment and services from Chinese vendors (Huawei, ZTE, Hytera, Hikvision, Dahua).

## Compliance Framework
The certification requires contractors to represent whether they:
1. Will provide covered telecommunications equipment or services to the Government
2. Use covered telecommunications equipment or services in their operations

**Covered telecommunications equipment/services** are defined as:
- Equipment produced by Huawei Technologies Company or ZTE Corporation (and subsidiaries/affiliates)
- Video surveillance and telecommunications equipment produced by Hytera Communications Corporation, Hangzhou Hikvision Digital Technology Company, or Dahua Technology Company
- Services provided by or using such equipment
- Equipment/services owned or controlled by covered foreign governments per Secretary of Defense determination

## Key Prohibitions
- **Effective 13 August 2019**: Agencies prohibited from procuring equipment/systems/services using covered telecommunications equipment as substantial/essential component or critical technology
- **Effective 13 August 2020**: Agencies prohibited from contracting with entities that use covered telecommunications equipment/services, regardless of whether use is in performance of federal work

## Exceptions
The prohibition does not apply to:
1. Services connecting to third-party facilities (backhaul, roaming, interconnection arrangements)
2. Telecommunications equipment that cannot route/redirect user data traffic or permit visibility into user data/packets

## Reporting Requirements
If contractor identifies covered equipment/services during performance:
- **Within 1 business day**: Report contract number, supplier name/CAGE code, brand, model number, item description, mitigation actions
- **Within 10 business days**: Additional information on mitigation efforts and prevention measures
- **Reporting mechanism**: For DoD, submit to https://dibnet.dod.mil

## Notable Details
- Document is unsigned template (signature line blank despite date of 31 May 2021)
- Certification applies to contractor's own use of equipment/services AND subcontractor use at any tier
- "Reasonable inquiry" standard used—does not require internal or third-party audit to uncover covered equipment use
- Contractor must ensure compliance language flows down to all subcontracts and commercial item acquisitions
