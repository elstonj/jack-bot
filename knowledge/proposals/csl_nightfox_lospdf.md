## Executive Summary
NOAA Chemical Sciences Laboratory provides a letter of support for Dr. J. Elston's NASA ROSES A.47 proposal on improved fire weather forecasting. The letter endorses collaboration between NOAA CSL's NightFOX infrared remote sensing payload and Black Swift Technologies' S2 UAS platform to demonstrate continuous high-resolution wildfire observations and support operational incident response.

## Technical Approach
- **NightFOX Payload**: Multi-wavelength infrared remote sensing system with two IR scanners (shortwave ~1.6 µm and midwave ~4 µm bands) designed to operate at 1 km (3,300 feet) altitude above ground level
- **Performance**: Delivers ~20 m (60 feet) spatial resolution Fire Radiative Power (FRP) measurements over a 1 km wide swath—a fidelity previously achievable only with crewed aircraft using larger, more expensive instrumentation
- **Satellite Harmonization**: IR bands selected to align with fire detection bands of MODIS and VIIRS satellite instruments for improved interpretation of lower-resolution satellite FRP data
- **Additional Capability**: Additional imagers in payload provide higher spatial resolution fire location measurements in narrower swath
- **Data Processing**: NOAA CSL will provide data reduction and mapping software for rapid fire perimeter map generation post-flight
- **Real-time Enhancement**: Collaboration planned to implement payload-aircraft communications for real-time observation telemetry

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift Technologies S2 UAS
- Platform of choice for NightFOX payload deployment
- Proven capability operating in challenging environments
- Two existing NightFOX remote sensing payloads have been previously flown on S2 platforms
- Identified as ideal partner for operational wildfire deployment due to experience

### NightFOX Infrared Remote Sensing Payload
- Originally designed for deployment on S2 UAS
- Multi-wavelength IR capability (shortwave and midwave bands)
- Generates high-resolution fire extent, temperature, and radiative power measurements
- Demonstrated performance on NOAA Twin Otter aircraft during NOAA-NASA FIREX-AQ mission (2019)
- Limited sUAS testing conducted over grass fire; further wildfire and prescribed burn applications noted as strong priorities

## Use Cases & Applications
- Real-time wildland fire incident response and situational awareness
- Fire behavior and smoke forecast model improvement
- Fire perimeter and extent mapping
- Fire temperature measurement
- Operational decision support for fire response and management
- Satellite FRP data validation and interpretation
- Support for beneficial fire management practices and prescribed burns
