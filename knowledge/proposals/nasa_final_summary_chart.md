## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies completed a Phase I effort to develop machine perception, cognition, and decision-making capabilities enabling low-altitude autonomous flights by fixed-wing aircraft in rugged and forested terrain. The work investigated sensing technologies, selected collision avoidance strategies, developed an integrated payload suite for the S2 UAS, and prepared materials for NASA Flight Safety Review Board approval to enable Phase II flight testing.

## Technical Approach
- Investigation of sensing technologies from multi-rotor and automotive industries
- Review and selection of avoidance control strategies
- Development of integrated payload and sensing suite compatible with S2 UAS constraints
- Development of robust simulation environment demonstrating terrain following capability
- Demonstration of obstacle detection algorithms in both simulation and hardware
- Survey and selection of sensor fusion and collision avoidance algorithms
- Mechanical integration planning and flight test preparation

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S2 UAS:**
- Fixed-wing small UAS platform
- Design constraints limited payload and sensing integration options
- Previously used on several NASA missions
- Intended platform for terrain-following technology integration
- Planned for methane detection missions and volcano monitoring applications

**Terrain Following System:**
- Machine perception, cognition, and decision-making for autonomous low-altitude flight
- Obstacle detection and collision avoidance
- Sensor fusion capabilities
- Enables flights in rugged, forested, and mountainous terrain

## Use Cases & Applications

**Scientific Monitoring:**
- Low-altitude CO2 vent monitoring on volcanoes (potential JPL partnership)
- Methane release monitoring in Arctic regions (in discussion with University of Colorado and Discovery Acquisition Systems)
- Pipeline leak detection and monitoring

**Infrastructure & Commercial:**
- Infrastructure inspection
- Leak detection
- Mountain mapping and survey work
- Rock slide mitigation analysis
- Avalanche prediction support

## Key Achievements
- System requirements derived from actual BST mission experience
- Embedded sensor suite selected and integrated
- Simulation environment validated terrain-following functionality
- Obstacle detection algorithms demonstrated in simulation and hardware
- Mechanical integration and flight test plans developed
- All Phase I deliverables completed to enable Phase II flight testing

- Leverages existing BST operational experience with S2 platform
- Positions BST for expanded NASA mission opportunities requiring low-altitude terrain-hugging capabilities
- Early discussions with potential commercial and research partners identified
