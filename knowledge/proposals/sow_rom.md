## Executive Summary
DARPA's Albatross program seeks to develop autonomous aircraft soaring capabilities that harness wind energy to extend flight range and endurance. Rather than designing new aircraft, the program focuses on developing weather-informed mission planning, real-time wind sensing, and control solutions that can be integrated into existing small uncrewed aircraft to autonomously identify and exploit soaring conditions.

## Technical Approach
- **Weather forecast-informed mission planning**: Integration of weather forecasting into autonomous flight planning
- **Real-time onboard wind sensing**: Multiple environmental sensors to detect dynamic wind conditions
- **Energy harvesting from winds**: Active exploitation of atmospheric flow fields to reduce onboard power demand
- **Control solutions**: Autonomous response mechanisms to constantly evolving soaring conditions
- **Integration approach**: Applied to existing or quickly assembled s-UAS rather than dedicated new airframe design
- **Multi-environment operation**: Capability development across multiple operational domains

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Small Uncrewed Aircraft Systems (s-UAS)**
