# Advancement of "Science as a Service" (SaaS) for NASA and Commercial Partners – RFI

- Type: Request for Information (RFI) with Amendment #1
- Client/Agency: National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) – Science Mission Directorate
- Program/Solicitation: NNH26ZDA006L
- Date: Released March 24, 2026; Amendment #1 April 21, 2026; Response deadline May 7, 2026
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None mentioned
- Key Personnel: Michael Seablom (Earth Science Division), Shawn Domagal-Goldman (Astrophysics Division) listed as points of contact

## Executive Summary
NASA's Science Mission Directorate seeks industry input to inform development of a "Science as a Service" initiative designed to leverage commercial capabilities and identify where government technology investments will accelerate development and transition of next-generation technologies. The RFI addresses three NASA divisions: Earth Science (Venture Technology Accelerator program), Heliophysics Space Weather Program, and Astrophysics Division, seeking commercial perspectives on technology needs, demonstration pathways, partnerships, and commercialization opportunities.

## Technical Approach
This is a market research RFI seeking commercial input across multiple dimensions:

**For Earth Science:**
- The Venture Technology Accelerator (VTA) program aims to mature and transition advanced sensors, instruments, subsystems, components, and data processing systems through in-space demonstrations and on-orbit validation, moving beyond the legacy InVEST (In-Space Validation of Earth Science Technologies) program toward commercialization.

**For Heliophysics:**
- Shift from traditional monolithic missions toward resilient architectures of proliferated, hosted sensors for space weather monitoring and Earth orbit distributed monitoring stations through public-private partnerships.

**For Astrophysics:**
- Identify emerging commercial capabilities for spacecraft buses, telescopes, observatory hardware, and repurposing/improving capabilities to meet space-based astrophysics observation needs, with potential for VTA-like approaches.

## Products & Capabilities Described
This RFI does not describe BST products. Rather, it solicits industry information on:

**Earth Science technologies needed:**
- Advanced sensors and instruments
- Subsystems and components (calibration standards, thermal-vac compatible hardware, radiation-hardened components)
- On-board data processing systems
- Environmental testing and validation capabilities

**Space Weather capabilities:**
- Distributed monitoring sensors and platforms
- Hosted payload architectures
- Satellite constellation operators and instruments

**Astrophysics hardware:**
- Spacecraft buses with precision pointing capability
- Telescopes and telescope assemblies
- Observatory hardware and platforms

## Use Cases & Applications

**Climate & Environmental:**
- Climate analytics, carbon markets
- Agriculture and supply chain monitoring
- Water and resource management
- Infrastructure resilience

**Earth Observation & Operational:**
- Insurance/finance risk products
- Maritime/aviation situational awareness
- Environmental compliance monitoring
- Disaster response and resource management

**Space Weather:**
- Satellite drag modeling
- Radiation hazard assessment for orbital assets
- GNSS/PNT (Global Navigation Satellite Systems / Positioning, Navigation, and Timing) accuracy for critical infrastructure

**Data Services:**
- Data-as-a-service business models
- Analytics products
- Hosted payloads on commercial platforms

## Key Results
This is an RFI seeking information, not a report with results. No results are presented.

## Response Topics Sought
NASA solicits responses addressing one primary and up to three secondary topics:

- **Topic A:** Commercial Business Case Technology Needs (performance thresholds, supply chain gaps, development risk reduction)
- **Topic B:** Demonstration Pathways and On-Orbit Validation Needs (preferred platforms, validation requirements, joint campaigns)
- **Topic C:** Streamlined Access to Space and Integration Support (standardized interfaces, rapid contracting, ground segment support)
- **Topic D:** Transition from Demonstration to Commercial Operations (productization, manufacturability, business models)
- **Topic E:** Partnerships and Community Ecosystem (cofunded pilots, federal user collaboration)
- **Topic F:** Program Design, Funding Models, and Milestone Structures (pay-for-performance, agile management, data rights)
- **Topic G:** Market-Driven Application Needs (actionable data/products across multiple domains)
- **Topic H:** Commercialization Interest in VTA Earth Science and Space Weather Missions (commercial provision, hosting, product lines)
- **Topic I:** Other Relevant Topics
