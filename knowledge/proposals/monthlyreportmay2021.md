## Executive Summary
This monthly report documents May 2021 activities on the NASA AREN project, focusing on hardware updates and production activities for the SwiftFlow AeroPod and TerraROVER systems, including design improvements, parts manufacturing, equipment preparation for a summer institute, and shipments to partner researchers.

## Technical Approach
Work centered on iterative hardware refinement and manufacturing:
- **Design Updates**: Redesigned/edited mounts for GNSS (Global Navigation Satellite System) and battery holder on SwiftFlow AeroPod
- **Manufacturing**: 3D printing and assembly of AeroPod and TerraROVER components using Gizmo PLA filament
- **Quality Control**: Troubleshooting extruder clogging issues, printer maintenance, and testing new filament
- **Systems Integration**: Troubleshooting card reading errors on TerraROVER; researching component replacements (carabiners)

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SwiftFlow AeroPod**
- Modular atmospheric profiler platform with GNSS positioning and battery systems
- Design includes removable probe cover and customizable mounts
- Multiple configurations supported (e.g., PocketLab Weather variant)
- Can be equipped with monocam imaging capability

**TerraROVER**
- Ground-based rover system with data card recording capability
- Experiencing intermittent card reading errors (ongoing troubleshooting)
- Printed parts compatible with 3D manufacturing pipeline

**GitUp Cameras**
- 35 units charged and configured for summer institute deployment
- Appears to be imaging/documentation equipment for field operations
