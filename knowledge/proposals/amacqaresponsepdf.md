## Executive Summary
This document is a consolidated Q&A response from the USG to industry questions regarding the AMAC IDIQ solicitation. It clarifies eligibility requirements, evaluation criteria based on prime contractor experience in Areas of Interest (AOIs), submission procedures, and contract terms. The competition features separate pools for unrestricted and small business offerors, with a minimum self-score threshold of 1,000 points required for consideration.

## Document Type & Purpose
This is **not a Black Swift Technologies proposal or technical document**. It is a government-issued clarification document for a competitive solicitation. It contains no information about BST's products, capabilities, or technical approach.

## Key Solicitation Details

### Eligibility & Competition Structure
- **Open competition** with two separate pools: "Unrestricted" (all offerors) and "Small Business"
- Eligible participants: large businesses, small businesses, joint ventures, universities, nonprofits, FFRDCs (with compliance requirements)
- Companies may submit as prime and appear as subcontractor on other proposals
- FFRDCs not excluded but must comply with FAR/agreement restrictions
- Foreign-owned companies ineligible unless they have DCSA-approved FOCI (Foreign Ownership, Control, or Influence) mitigation plan

### Evaluation & Scoring
- **Minimum threshold**: 1,000 points required for all offerors (both pools use identical evaluation standards)
- Points awarded based on **prime contractor experience only** in defined Areas of Interest
- Subcontractor experience does not count toward scoring
- No time limit on relevant experience; any past or active U.S. Federal Government prime contract eligible
- No minimum dollar value required for contracts
- Automated scoring spreadsheet (formula-driven); offerors can claim points for each AOI only once
- Validation of self-scored points occurs prior to award

### Bonus Point Structure
- **Air Domain**: 1,200 points available; 600 points needed for 500-point bonus
- **Space Domain**: 800 points available; 500 points needed for 500-point bonus
- **Cyberspace Domain**: 1,700 points available; 700 points needed for 500-point bonus
- **Cross-Domain**: 1,900 points available; 800 points needed for 500-point bonus
- Evaluation stops once offeror achieves validated 1,000-point score

### Eligible Contract Experience
- FAR-based contracts
- SBIR/STTR (Phase I/II/III)
- Other Transaction Agreements (OTAs)
- Offeror must provide contract and supporting requirements documentation as proof

### Submission Requirements
- Single proposal submission per offeror for base IDIQ qualification
- **Small Business Participation Commitment Document (SBPCD)** mandatory for all offerors (both large and small); submitted as part of Volume II; distinct evaluation factor
- SAM.gov profile must be active and current at time of submission
- Representations and Certifications required per Section K
- Final proposal deadline to be posted on SAM.gov (January 9, 2026 was industry comment deadline only)

## Contract Vehicle Terms

### AMAC IDIQ Structure
- **No core funding** on base contract; all work funded through individual task orders
- Contract vehicle: IDIQ (Indefinite Delivery/Indefinite Quantity)
- Unclassified requirements only
- Base contract does not include FAR Part 12 (commercial items) clauses but includes provisions for commercial item acquisitions and "commercial item exception" requests at task order level

### Task Order Management
- Once awarded base contract, offeror eligible to compete for any task order regardless of experience cited for qualification
- Task order competition conducted with fair opportunity procedures among awardees
- Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI) managed at individual task order level post-award
- Government reserves right to use on-ramps (add contractors) and off-ramps (remove contractors) during IDIQ life
- Future on-ramped contractors receive truncated ordering period matching remaining original awardee period

### Intellectual Property & Data Rights
- Base contract uses standard DFARS clauses
- Specific IP/data frameworks defined at task order level
- Cybersecurity compliance mandatory; specific CUI (Controlled Unclassified Information) handling requirements defined at task order level

### Contract Flexibility
- Government can issue task orders with cost-based structures when Firm-Fixed-Price inappropriate
- "Cost-No-Fee" line items available for ancillary costs (e.g., travel); does not preclude fee-bearing cost contracts for primary effort
- Special clauses included to facilitate procurement from non-traditional defense contractors
