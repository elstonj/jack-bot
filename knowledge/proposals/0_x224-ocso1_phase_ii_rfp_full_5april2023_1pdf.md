# X224-OCSO1 Phase II RFP

## Document Metadata
- Type: Request for Proposal (RFP)
- Client/Agency: Department of the Air Force (DAF), AFWERX
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Phase II; Topic AFX224-OCSO1 (Phase I Open Call for Innovative Defense-Related Dual-Purpose Technologies/Solutions)
- Date: April 5, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None mentioned
- Key Personnel: Daniel J. Brewer (Air Force SBIR/STTR Contracting Officer, AFRL/RGK, AFWERX)

## Executive Summary
This RFP invites Phase II SBIR proposals from companies that successfully completed Phase I awards under topic AFX224-OCSO1. The solicitation seeks innovative dual-purpose technologies with clear Air Force or Space Force stakeholder needs. Phase II awards are capped at $1,250,000 over 21 months maximum, with proposals evaluated on commercialization potential, defense need appropriateness, and technical merit.

## Technical Approach
This document is an RFP framework rather than a technical proposal. It establishes:
- **Submission via DSIP system** (DoD SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal)
- **15-page technical volume** in white paper format
- **Minimum 50% of research/analytical effort** must be performed by the awardee
- **Three to five Phase II technical objectives** with measurable key results
- **Technical evaluation based on:**
  - Government/private sector commercialization potential and traction
  - Appropriateness and specificity of identified defense need (via Air Force Customer Memorandum)
  - Soundness, technical merit, and innovation of proposed approach

## Notable Details

### Submission Requirements
- **Deadline:** May 4, 2023 at 12:00 PM ET
- **Maximum Funding:** $1,250,000
- **Performance Period:** Maximum 21 months
- **Technical Volume:** 15 pages maximum (white paper format)

### Proposal Components
- **Volume 1:** Proposal Cover Sheet
- **Volume 2:** Technical Volume (15 pages max)
  - Includes non-proprietary work plan and commercialization strategy
- **Volume 3:** Cost Volume (itemized budget)
- **Volume 4:** Contractor Certification Records (CCR)
- **Volume 5:** Supporting Documents (customer memorandum, resumes, safety documentation, subcontractor agreements, etc.)
- **Volume 6:** Fraud, Waste, and Abuse Training (completed via DSIP)

### Evaluation Criteria (Equal Importance)
1. **Criteria A—Commercialization Potential:** Evidence of pre-sales, pilots, sales, revenue, active users, subscriptions, downloads, Phase II follow-on commitments, transition strategy
2. **Criteria B—Defense Need Appropriateness:** Signed Air Force Customer Memorandum from end-user/customer; relevance and specificity of identified need
3. **Criteria C—Technical Merit:** Soundness and innovation of proposed approach; differentiation from current alternatives; qualifications of PI and team

### Mandatory Documentation at Proposal Submission
- Signed Air Force Customer Memorandum (Attachment 1, required)
- DoD SBIR/STTR Lifecycle Certification (Attachment 2, required)
- ESOH Questionnaire (Attachment 3, required)
- Contractor Certification Regarding Prohibited Video Surveillance (Attachment 4, required)
- Foreign Ownership/Control Disclosure (Attachment 5, required)
- AF Security Program Questionnaire (Attachment 6, required)
- Research & Related Senior/Key Person Profile (Attachment 7, required)
- Coversheet Supplement (Attachment 10, required)
- Non-Proprietary Work Plan (required)
- SBIR/STTR Funding Agreement Certification (Attachment 9, encouraged at submission, required prior to award)

### Phase II Work Plan Requirements
- **Scope, Task Outline, Milestone Schedule, Deliverables**
- **Required Reports:**
  - Quarterly status reports (max 15 slides)
  - Draft final report due 30 days after technical effort completion
  - Phase II summary report (max 700 words) at technical effort completion
- **Safety Deliverables:**
  - Preliminary Hazard Analysis (if hardware developed)
  - Hazardous Material Reports (if applicable)

### Commercialization Plan Must Address
- Company information, core competencies, previous commercialization history
- Customer and competition analysis
- Market size, milestones, target market share
- Intellectual property strategy
- Financing plans
- Technical/business assistance plans
- First product incorporating technology
- Private sector or non-SBIR funding commitments
- Foreign investment disclosures
- Phase III follow-on commitments

### Applicant Eligibility
- Must be a Small Business Concern (SBC) per 13 CFR §§ 701-705 at award
- Principal Investigator must have primary employment with SBC (>50% time) at award and during performance
- Research/R&D typically performed in the United States
- Joint ventures/limited partnerships permitted if entity qualifies as small business
- Must disclose joint ventures with foreign entities
- Must meet SBA commercialization benchmarks:
  - Phase I awardees: Phase II-to-Phase I award ratio ≥ 0.25
  - Phase II awardees: ≥$100,000 average sales/investments per Phase II award OR ≥15% of Phase II awards as patents
