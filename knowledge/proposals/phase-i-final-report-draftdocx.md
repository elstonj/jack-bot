## Document Content Assessment
**Note:** This document does NOT contain Black Swift Technologies project information. It is a standardized NASA SBIR reporting template with the following structure:

### Required Report Sections:
1. **Project Summary** - Innovation significance, technical objectives, accomplishments, PI info
2. **Abstract**
3. **Technical Objectives**
4. **Work Plan** - Including technical approach, task descriptions, labor categories, schedules
5. **Potential Applications** - NASA and non-NASA uses
6. **Contacts** - Key participants and advisors
7. **Commercialization Activities** - Customer engagement, recent activities, future opportunities
8. **Additional Reports of Work** - Technical activities, milestones, issues, subcontractor changes, resource tracking
9. **References**
10. **NASA Standard Form SF298** (required final page)

### Key Compliance Requirements:
- **SBIR Rights Notice** (20-year protection period for contractor data)
- **Export Control Markings** (ITAR/EAR as applicable)
- **Proprietary Data Restrictions** (must be marked and approved by Contracting Officer)
- **Sensitive Information Footers** (required on each restricted page)
- **Monthly Communication** with Technical Monitor minimum
- **Contract Officer Approval** required for any deviations (personnel, subcontractors, facilities, budget changes)

### Notable Guidance:
- Reports cannot be entirely proprietary
- SBIR-funded work is NOT proprietary; only pre-existing company R&D can be marked proprietary
- Percentage completion tracking required (milestones, task status)
- Problem/issue reporting with corrective action plans required
- Phase II proposal submission is voluntary
