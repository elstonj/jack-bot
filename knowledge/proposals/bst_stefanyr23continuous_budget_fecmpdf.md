## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies submitted a three-year budget proposal for a DOE FECM project on orphaned wells detection and monitoring. The project involves conducting aerial surveys using BST's S2 unmanned aircraft system to detect and characterize abandoned oil/gas wells, with field operations in the San Luis Valley, Colorado (Years 1-2) and Osage Nation lands in Oklahoma (Year 3).

## Technical Approach
- Utilize BST S2 unmanned aircraft system for aerial survey and monitoring operations
- Partner with Atmofacts (Stefan Metzger) for data analysis and interpretation
- Conduct multiple flight missions over target areas to collect sensor data
- Project spans 3 years with increasing flight operations and geographic expansion

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 Aircraft System
- Primary platform for orphaned wells detection/monitoring missions
- Deployment across multiple geographic regions (Colorado, Oklahoma)
- Rental cost allocated across project: $64,194/year (Year 1-2), budgeted for 2x S2 systems
