# Using Small Uncrewed Aircraft Systems to Mitigate Post-Landfall Hurricane Disasters

## Document Metadata
- Type: Scientific Overview / Technical Proposal
- Client/Agency: NOAA (primary), with University of Florida, Lone Star UAS Center of Excellence and Innovation, Embry Riddle University
- Program/Solicitation: Post-landfall hurricane disaster mitigation initiative
- Date: 9 March 2022
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Black Swift S0
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (Black Swift Technologies), Joe Cione (NOAA), Forrest Masters (University of Florida), Mike Sanders (Lone Star UAS), Joe Henry (Lone Star UAS), Jim Crowell (Embry Riddle University)

## Executive Summary
This document proposes using Black Swift's small, electric S0 unmanned aircraft system to collect high-value post-landfall hurricane data in real-time for emergency response teams. A consortium of federal, academic, and private partners would deploy the S0 from shoreline locations immediately after hurricane landfall to gather atmospheric and imagery data for damage assessment and civil infrastructure evaluation.

## Technical Approach
- Deployment of Black Swift S0 aircraft from shoreline immediately after landfall when winds have subsided
- Operation under FAA Part 107 regulations (initially line-of-sight, under 400 ft AGL)
- Aircraft equipped with selectable sensor suites for atmospheric measurements or electro-optical/infrared imagery
- Automated flight processes for area-of-interest identification and coverage planning
- Video telemetry to ground station with geo-referencing for post-processing into orthomosaics and 3D models
- Real-time data relay through existing NOAA and emergency management channels
- Future advancement to Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) and higher altitude operations

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift S0
- **What it is:** Small electric, modular, ruggedized VTOL (vertical takeoff/landing) or tube-launched fixed-wing unmanned aircraft system compliant with FAA Part 107
- **Specifications:**
  - Maximum takeoff weight: 4 lbs
  - Wingspan: 54.6 inches
  - Payload capacity: 100 grams
  - Flight ceiling: 15,000 ft
  - Flight time: 60 minutes nominal
  - Cruise speed: 40 mph
  - Max speed: 100 mph
  - Max winds endured: 50 mph
  - Nose cone: 1.8 inch diameter
- **Proposed Use:** Real-time post-hurricane data collection for emergency response and damage assessment
- **Sensor Options:** 
  1. 3D wind, pressure, temperature, humidity (atmospheric thermodynamic package)
  2. Electro-optical/infrared (EO/IR) imagery
  3. Trace gas sensors (future capability)
- **Advantages:** Modern manufacturing techniques enable economical construction, operation, and maintenance relative to manned or larger UAS; designed for low-cost solutions including scenarios where aircraft may not be recovered

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Use Case:** Post-landfall hurricane damage assessment
- **Specific Applications:**
  - Real-time surface wind field data collection
  - Civil infrastructure damage assessment
  - Coastal and ecological system impact evaluation
  - Supporting first responders and emergency management teams
  - Atmospheric profiling in hurricane-affected areas
- **Geographic Context:** Landfalling hurricanes along U.S. coastline

## Operational Context
- University of Florida team has deployed sensors in nearly 40 landfalling tropical cyclones (including 10+ major hurricanes) since 1999
- UF team positioned in hurricane core at landfall for immediate post-storm deployment within hours of inland movement
- Established relationships with operational users (NOAA, FEMA) and research agencies (NSF, NIST) for rapid data dissemination

## Notable Details
- **Regulatory Strategy:** Document notes intention to initially operate under Part 107 (no formal FAA approval required) while later seeking BVLOS and above-400-ft authority
- **Consortium Model:** Partnership approach across federal (NOAA), academic (UF, Embry Riddle), and private sector (Black Swift, Lone Star UAS Center) organizations
- **Data Sharing Strategy:** Real-time processed imagery, video, and atmospheric data delivered through existing emergency response channels
- **FAA Engagement:** Planned proactive briefing with FAA to build goodwill for future beyond-Part-107 operations
