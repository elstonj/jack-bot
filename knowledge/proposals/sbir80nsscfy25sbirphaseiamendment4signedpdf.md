# NASA SBIR Phase I Solicitation - FY 2025 (Amendment 4)

## Document Metadata
- Type: Solicitation Amendment (Amendment 4 to NASA SBIR Phase I solicitation)
- Client/Agency: NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Program, Phase I, FY 2025 (80N SSCFY25SBIR)
- Date: Released January 7, 2025 (Amendment 4 signed February 12, 2026)
- Key Amendment Changes: Proposal deadline extended from March 10, 2025 to May 21, 2025; NAICS code requirement moved from proposal submission to contract award time; maximum proposals per firm reduced from 10 to 5; maximum awards per firm reduced from 5 to 3
- Key Personnel: Contracting Officer changed from Kenneth Albright to Charles Bridges

## Executive Summary
This is Amendment 4 to the NASA SBIR Phase I Fiscal Year 2025 solicitation, primarily extending the proposal submission deadline from March 10, 2025 to May 21, 2025 at 5:00 p.m. ET. The amendment also adjusts regulatory and administrative requirements, notably moving NAICS code small business registration verification from proposal submission time to contract award time, and reducing the maximum number of proposal submissions and awards per offeror.

## Key Changes in Amendment 4
1. **Proposal Deadline Extended**: From March 10, 2025, 5:00 p.m. ET to May 21, 2025, 5:00 p.m. ET
2. **NAICS Code Requirement Timeline Shifted**: Small businesses must now be registered under NAICS code 541713 or 541715 at time of contract award (not proposal submission)
3. **Help Desk Deadline Extended**: From March 3, 2025 to May 20, 2025 for guaranteed timely answers
4. **Maximum Submissions Reduced**: From 10 to 5 proposals per offeror for FY 2025
5. **Maximum Awards Reduced**: From 5 to 3 SBIR contracts per offeror
6. **Contracting Officer Change**: Kenneth Albright replaced by Charles Bridges for service of protests
7. **Technical Proposal Length Reduction**: From 19 pages to 15 pages
8. **Table of Contents Requirement Removed**: From technical proposal requirements

## Program Overview

### Purpose and Scope
The NASA SBIR Program supports small businesses to create innovative, disruptive technologies that benefit society and may be used in NASA programs/missions, other government agencies, and/or commercial markets. The program provides equity-free funding for early-stage research and development.

### Three-Phase Program Structure

**Phase I**
- Maximum Contract Value: $150,000
- Period of Performance: 6 months
- Purpose: Demonstrate technical feasibility of proposed innovation and potential for NASA program/mission use or commercial market application
- Eligibility: Any qualified small business concern

**Phase II**
- Maximum Contract Value: $850,000
- Maximum Period of Performance: 24 months
- Purpose: Continue R&D from Phase I toward use in NASA programs/missions or commercial markets
- Eligibility: Phase I awardees only

**Phase III**
- Purpose: Further development or use of technologies from Phase I or II
- Funding Source: Customers outside SBIR/STTR programs (NASA programs, other government agencies, private sector)
- Eligibility: Any Phase I or II awardee

### Post-Phase II Opportunities
NASA supports small businesses beyond Phase I and II with several Post Phase II initiatives; details available on NASA SBIR/STTR website.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Small Business Concern (SBC)
- Must be independently owned and operated
- Combined with affiliates: maximum 500 employees
- Must perform at least 67% of research effort; subcontractors/consultants may perform up to 33%
- Certification required at proposal submission, award, and at any other time per SBA regulations

### Principal Investigator (PI) Requirements
- Must be primarily employed with SBC at time of award and during project conduct
- Primary employment: more than 50% of employment time (19.9+ hours/week elsewhere constitutes conflict)
- Co-PIs not allowed for SBIR Phase I
- Substitution of PIs requires NASA approval after award
- Misrepresentation of qualifications will result in proposal decline or contract termination

### Restrictions
- Small businesses owned in majority by multiple venture capital operating companies, hedge funds, or private equity firms are **not eligible**
- Joint ventures and limited partnerships are permitted if resulting entity qualifies as SBC

### Registration and Certification Requirements

**SAM (System for Award Management) Registration**
- Must be active at time of proposal submission
- Must have Unique Entity Identifier (UEI) and Commercial and Government Entity (CAGE) code validated in SAM
- Registration must remain active from proposal submission through contract performance
- Purpose of registration must list "All Awards"

**SBA Company Registry**
- Must register and provide unique SBC Control ID
- Must update commercialization status
- Must upload PDF copy of registration with Firm Certification Form

**NAICS Code Requirement** (per Amendment 4)
- Small business registration under NAICS code 541713 or 541715 required at time of contract award (changed from proposal submission by Amendment 4)

### Performance Benchmark Requirement
- More experienced SBCs (21 or more Phase I awards) must meet performance benchmarks to continue participating
- Purpose: Ensure offerors with multiple prior awards are progressing toward commercialization
- SBA notifies non-compliant companies and relevant agency officials

## Security and Foreign Affiliation Requirements

### Due Diligence Program
- NASA reviews all proposals to assess security risks presented by offerors
- Assessment includes:
  - Cybersecurity practices
  - Patent analysis
  - Employee analysis
  - Foreign ownership
  - Financial ties and obligations (surety, equity, debt obligations) to foreign countries, persons, or entities

### Required Foreign Affiliation Disclosures
Offerors must complete "Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries" form disclosing:
- Identity of all owners and covered individuals in foreign talent recruitment programs
- Any joint venture or subsidiary based in, funded by, or affiliated with foreign countries of concern (including People's Republic of China)
- Current or pending contractual/financial obligations with foreign state-owned or foreign entities
- Whether SBC is wholly owned in People's Republic of China or other foreign country of concern
- Percentage of venture capital/institutional investment by entities with foreign affiliations
- Technology licensing or IP sales to foreign countries of concern (5-year lookback)
- Any foreign, offshore, or non-U.S. entities related to the small business concern

**Consequences of Non-Disclosure**: Failure to submit form results in proposal decline during administrative screening; foreign involvement does not independently disqualify but failing to disclose may result in denial of award.

## Place of Performance and American-Made Products
- Awardees must purchase American-made equipment and products when possible with SBIR funds
- Foreign vendors may be proposed only with justification of rare/unique circumstance on Foreign Vendor Form
- Contracts may be delayed or not awarded if foreign vendor proposed
- NASA does not approve purchases from countries on Designated Country list
- Deviation requests reviewed during contract negotiation

## Funding and Awards

### Availability of Funds
- NASA does not commit to fund any proposal or guarantee specific number of awards
- May elect to make several or no awards in any specific research subtopic
- Number of awards determined by level of appropriated funding in FY 2025
- **Maximum submissions per offeror: 5 proposals** (reduced from 10 by Amendment 4)
- **Maximum awards per offeror: 3 SBIR contracts** (reduced from 5 by Amendment 4)

### Contract Type
- Fixed-price contracts

### Award Conditions
- Subject to availability of funds and successful contract negotiations
- I-Corps participation: Phase I awardees may opt into NSF Innovation Corps program with up to $10,000 in additional funding
- Technical and Business Assistance (TABA): Awardees may request up to $6,500 supplement at Phase I (up to $50,000 at Phase II)

## Proposal Preparation Requirements

### Format and Length
- **Maximum length: 15 pages** (standard letter size 8.5" × 11", including all graphics and all 9 parts)
- Margins: 1.0 inch (2.5 cm)
- Type size: 10 point or larger for text/tables (except legends on reduced drawings)
- Header requirements: Include SBC name, proposal number, and project title on each page
- Footer requirements: Include page number and proprietary legend (if applicable)

### Technical Proposal Structure (9 Parts)
Part 1: Identification and Significance of Innovation (suggested 3.5 pages)
- Describe innovation relative to current state of the art
- Address scientific, technical, and commercial merit/feasibility
- Describe relevance and significance to NASA interests

Part 2: Technical Objectives (suggested 1 page)

Part 3: Work Plan (suggested 3 pages)

Part 4: Related R/R&D (suggested 1 page)

Part 5: Key Personnel and Bibliography of Directly Related Work (suggested 2.5 pages)

Part 6: The Market Opportunity (suggested 1 page)

Part 7: Facilities/Equipment (suggested 1 page)

Part 8: Subcontractors and Consultants (suggested 1 page)

Part 9: Related, Essentially Equivalent, and Duplicate Proposals and Award (suggested 1 page)

### Additional Forms Required (do not count against 15-page limit)
- Proposal Contact Information Form
- Proposal Certifications Form
- Proposal Summary Form
- Proposal Budget Form
- Briefing Chart (separate from technical proposal)
- NASA Evaluation License Application (if using NASA TAV)
- Request for Technical and Business Assistance (TABA) Supplement (if applicable)
- I-Corps Interest Form (if interested in I-Corps participation)
- SBC Level Forms

### Project Title Requirements
- Must be concise and descriptive
- Do not use NASA research subtopic title, acronyms, or phrases like "development of" or "study of"

### Content Requirements
A Technical Proposal must clearly and concisely:
1. Describe the proposed innovation relative to the current state of the art
2. Address the scientific, technical, and commercial merit and feasibility of the proposed innovation as well as its relevance and significance to NASA interests as described in solicitation Chapter 9
3. Provide preliminary strategy addressing key technical, market, and business factors pertinent to successful development, demonstration, and transition of innovation into products/services for NASA missions/programs, commercial markets, and other potential markets/customers

### Classified Information
- Any proposal package containing classified information will be declined

### Proprietary Information
- Offerors may mark proprietary information as "Proprietary" or "Company Confidential"
- Must clearly identify proprietary sections
- Non-NASA reviewers may have access to technical proposals; confidential business information will be protected

## Submission Process

### Submission Method
- All proposals submitted via secure Proposal Submissions and Award Management System (ProSAMS)
- URL: https://prosams.nasa.gov/
- Requires firm registration and login

### Deadline
- **May 21, 2025 at 5:00 p.m. Eastern Time** (per Amendment 4)
- Proposals must be received at ProSAMS before deadline
- Acknowledgment of submission email must be received before deadline

### Acknowledgment of Receipt
- Offerors must confirm receipt of submission acknowledgment email before 5:00 p.m. ET on May 21, 2025
- Failure to receive acknowledgment before deadline may result in offer rejection

### Withdrawal
- Proposals may be withdrawn via electronic communication
- Must reference solicitation number and include authorization signature

### Questions and Help Desk
- Help Desk contact: agency-sbir@mail.nasa.gov
- Help Desk will not guarantee timely answer to questions received after May 20, 2025, at 5:00 p.m. ET
- NASA will not answer questions about research subtopic intent/content during open solicitation period
