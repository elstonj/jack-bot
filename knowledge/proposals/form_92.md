## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes development and flight validation of the S0, a low-cost, air-deployable UAS designed to collect boundary layer and sea surface observations in tropical storm environments. The S0 aims to overcome limitations of existing platforms (high cost, limited observation time) by reducing complexity and weight while maintaining endurance and measurement quality.

## Technical Approach
The S0 will be developed as a commercial UAS platform for air deployment, building on successes of Aerosonde and Coyote platforms. Design emphasizes simplification and weight reduction to achieve an order of magnitude cost decrease compared to existing systems. The aircraft will incorporate:
- 3D wind measurement capability
- Pressure, temperature, humidity (PTH) measurements
- Sea surface temperature sensing
- Vehicle height measurement from water surface
- Focus on lower altitude boundary layer observations

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0 (Proposed Platform)**
- Purpose: Air-deployed UAS for boundary layer and sea surface observations
- Target cost: ~$5,000
- Endurance: 2 hours
- Sensor suite: 3D wind measurement, PTH, laser altimeter for sea surface characterization, thermal IR sensor
- Data transmission: AVAPS telemetry compatible
- User interface: Simple pre-programming capability
- Key advantage: Order of magnitude cost reduction versus Aerosonde/Coyote while maintaining performance and measurement quality

## Use Cases & Applications
- Tropical storm observation and sampling
- Boundary layer kinematics and thermodynamics measurement
- Sea surface characterization in storm environments
- Data collection for improving forecast models
- Alternative to land-based flights (addressing regulatory and interception distance obstacles)

## Notable Details
- Previous validation: UAS-collected data compared favorably with proven measurement methods
- Problem addressed: Previous obstacles included regulations and distance required to intercept evolving storms
- Platform advantage: Air deployment overcomes limitations of land-based operations
- Target market positioning: Low-cost alternative to high-cost platforms (Aerosonde, Coyote) while retaining capability
