# Black Swift Technologies Pitch Feedback

## Document Metadata
- Type: Pitch feedback / evaluation notes
- Client/Agency: Colorado Advanced Industries (CO Advanced Industries program)
- Program/Solicitation: Advanced Industries growth initiative
- Date: 2022-10-24
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2/S3 UAS platform, Methane Laser Detector (MLD), Quanta3 laser spectrometer sensor
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This document contains evaluation feedback from an Aerospace Subcommittee reviewing a Black Swift Technologies pitch to Colorado Advanced Industries. Evaluators noted strong technology and team but raised significant concerns about competitive positioning claims, inconsistent pricing models, incomplete patent/licensing status, financial projections, and insufficient technical specifications.

## Key Evaluation Themes

### Competitive Position Concerns
- BST claimed to be "way ahead of competition" in Product and Technology section, but elsewhere stated competitors could "displace BST as the new gold-standard for remote, airborne methane detection" without near-term funding
- This contradiction raised questions about actual competitive advantage and product lifecycle longevity
- Multiple reviewers skeptical of competitive analysis; believed market more competitive than BST represented
- One reviewer questioned whether competing development programs could prove superior

### Patent & Licensing Status
- **Critical gap**: No patents issued despite 11 years of operation and several million dollars in prior funding
- Quanta3 license agreement not yet in place (MLD solution dependent on this partnership)
- Evaluators questioned why neither patents nor licensing agreements had been completed
- Multiple reviewers requested clarification on licensing structure, pricing dependencies, and terms with Quanta3

### Financial & Pricing Inconsistencies
- **Major issue**: Pricing stated inconsistently across materials ($80 per well vs. $300)
- Revenue projections described as "questionably optimistic," though even 50% achievement seen as beneficial
- Data sales revenue stream mentioned but pricing model unclear
- One reviewer stated "Finances do not make sense, not well done"
- Another skeptical that requested finances would "get them over the finish line"
- MLD described as "fifth line of business" but economics unclear

### Technical Specification Gaps
- Claim of "most advanced UAS platform for methane detection that is significantly ahead of the competition" lacked supporting technical data
- Evaluators requested specific performance metrics: detection limits, accuracy, range, sensitivity specifications
- General comment: "needed more information"
- Application was "not thorough or complete"

### Positive Assessments
- "Excellent team with broad and deep skills and contacts"
- "Believable technology"
- "Strong contribution to CO"
- "Real players, very good for the state"
- Management team "fairly strong" with "no major concerns"
- "Business strategy and financials are very solid" (with caveats)

## Proposed Use Case
**Methane Detection for Oil & Gas Industry**: MLD system for detecting methane emissions at wellheads for drilling/extraction companies. Evaluators suggested potential channel through well-head equipment manufacturers.

## Business Model Details Described
- Five lines of business (MLD would be the fifth)
- Revenue streams: direct service ($80 or $300 per well) + data sales
- Growth projections: 3-year revenue and FTE growth (seen as overly optimistic)
- Job creation modest but tied to important CO industries

## Notable Concerns & Gaps

### Operational & Safety
- Questions about flight operational safety at intersection of UAS and manned aircraft operations

### Matching Funds Verification
- BST cited NASA as source of matching funds but no contract documentation was uploaded
- Evaluator unable to verify claimed funding

### Presentation Quality
- Executive summary contained typos
- Terminology: one reviewer questioned use of "Unmanned" vs. "Uncrewed"

### Other Strategic Questions
- Evaluators asked whether BST had considered marketing MLD through well-head equipment companies as integrators/resellers
- No discussion of manufacturing/maintenance outsourcing opportunities in Colorado

## Context Note
This pitch was submitted to Colorado Advanced Industries program (2022). The feedback suggests BST was seeking growth capital funding and the Methane Laser Detector represented a major new business line diversification from their core UAS business. The evaluation was moderately positive on team/technology but critical of financial clarity, competitive positioning substantiation, and incomplete intellectual property/licensing position.
