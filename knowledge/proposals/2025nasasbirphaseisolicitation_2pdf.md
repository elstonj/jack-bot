# 2025 NASA SBIR Phase I Solicitation

- Key Personnel: None BST personnel named

## Executive Summary
This is the official NASA SBIR Phase I Fiscal Year 2025 solicitation package describing requirements, eligibility criteria, and instructions for small businesses to submit innovation proposals. It outlines the three-phase program structure (Phase I: $150,000 max/6 months; Phase II: $850,000 max/24 months; Phase III: commercial/government funding), eligibility requirements for small business concerns, and the evaluation and selection process.

## Document Purpose & Key Information

This is NOT a Black Swift Technologies document. This is the official NASA solicitation that BST would use as the framework for submitting a Phase I proposal.

### Notable Changes in FY 2025:
- Maximum submissions per firm reduced from 10 to 5
- Maximum awards per firm reduced from 5 to 3
- Technical proposal length reduced from 19 to 15 pages
- Table of contents requirement removed
- Modified technical evaluation criteria and scoring

### Phase I Program Structure
- **Maximum Contract Value:** $150,000 (or $156,500 if requesting TABA supplement)
- **Period of Performance:** 6 months
- **Purpose:** Demonstrate technical feasibility and potential for use in NASA programs/missions or commercial market

### Key Eligibility Requirements
1. Small Business Concern (SBC) certification required
2. Maximum 500 employees (combined with affiliates)
3. Primary performer must conduct ≥67% of effort (subcontractors/consultants ≤33%)
4. Principal Investigator must be primarily employed with SBC (>50% of 40-hour workweek)
5. Registration required in:
   - SAM.gov (with UEI and CAGE code)
   - SBIR Firm Registry at sbir.gov
   - Must be registered under NAICS code 541713 or 541715 at time of submission
6. Must complete "Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries" form
7. No venture-capital-majority-owned businesses eligible
8. American-made equipment/products preferred (foreign vendor justification required)

### Technical Proposal Requirements
- **Maximum Length:** 15 pages (all 9 parts including graphics)
- **Format:** 8.5" x 11", 1.0" margins, 10-point minimum font
- **Required Components (9 parts):**
  1. Identification and Significance of Innovation (3.5 pp suggested)
  2. Technical Objectives (1 pp)
  3. Work Plan (3 pp)
  4. Related R/R&D (1 pp)
  5. Key Personnel and Bibliography (2.5 pp)
  6. Market Opportunity (1 pp)
  7. Facilities/Equipment (1 pp)
  8. Subcontractors and Consultants (1 pp)
  9. Related/Equivalent/Duplicate Proposals (1 pp)

### Submission Requirements
- Electronic submission via ProSAMS (https://prosams.nasa.gov)
- Proposal must include:
  - Proposal Contact Information Form
  - Proposal Certifications Form
  - Proposal Summary Form (public information)
  - Proposal Budget Form
  - Technical Proposal (15 pages max)
  - Briefing Chart (1 page, public information)
  - NASA Evaluation License Application (if using NASA Technology Available)
  - TABA request (optional, up to $6,500)
  - I-Corps Interest Form
  - SBC-Level Forms (Firm Information, Certifications, Audit Info, Foreign Affiliation Disclosures, Prior Awards, Commercial Metrics Report)

### Evaluation Criteria
- Technical Responsiveness
- Technical Merit
- Commercial Viability
- Price/Cost Reasonableness
- Scoring methodology detailed in Appendix A

### Notable Support Programs

**I-Corps Program:** Phase I awardees can participate in NSF Innovation Corps for customer discovery; up to $10,000 available funding, separate award modification.

**Technical and Business Assistance (TABA):** Optional supplement up to $6,500 for Phase I (up to $50,000 at Phase II) for:
- Technical problem-solving
- IP protection assistance
- Market research and validation
- Regulatory/manufacturing planning
- Can use approved vendors/consultants (not internal activities)

### NASA Technology Available (TAV)
- Over 1,400 NASA patents available for licensing
- Over 1,000 software applications/tools available
- Nonexclusive, royalty-free usage available
- No preference given in evaluation for using or not using NASA TAV

### Key Restrictions & Requirements

**Security & Foreign Relationships:**
- Full due diligence review on cybersecurity, patents, employees, foreign ownership
- Assessment of financial ties/obligations to foreign countries
- Detailed foreign affiliation disclosure mandatory (failure to disclose may result in denial)

**Place of Performance:**
- Research must be performed in United States
- American-made equipment/products required unless justified via Foreign Vendor Form

**Restrictions on SBIR Participation:**
- No joint ventures/partnerships with venture capital majority-owned businesses
- Must meet benchmark transition rates if ≥21 prior Phase I awards

**What NOT to Include:**
- Letters of interest/support
- Technical papers
- Product samples
- Videos
- PowerPoint decks
- Other ancillary items (will count against page limit if submitted)

### Registration & Certification Timeline
- SAM registration: Takes several weeks; must be active at submission and through contract performance
- SBIR Firm Registry: Must be completed before submission
- Certifications: Must complete FAR/NASA certifications in SAM
- Foreign Affiliation Form: Must be submitted; if not, proposal automatically declined

### Special Requirements

**Human/Animal Subjects:** Not permitted in Phase I (complexity of approval process)

**Flight Safety:** If proposing spacecraft delivery, must comply with NPR 8079.1

**Airworthiness:** Flight elements subject to Commercial Air Service (CAS) and NASA Airworthiness reviews; Phase I flight activities may be precluded due to short contract period

**Software Development:** May be required to comply with NASA NPR 7150.2D if proposing software development

**Security Access (HSPD-12):** If awarded contract requires 6+ consecutive months access to federally controlled facilities, must obtain PIV credentials

### Fraud & Compliance
- NASA reserves right to decline proposals with plagiarism or false claims
- False statements may be felony under 18 U.S.C. § 1001 (up to 5 years prison)
- Office of Inspector General has full access to all proposals
- Hotline: 1-800-424-9183 or cyber hotline at https://oigforms.nasa.gov/wp_cyberhotline.html

### Phase II & Phase III Opportunities
- **Phase II:** Only Phase I awardees eligible; up to $850,000 for 24 months; separate solicitation
- **Phase III:** Sole-source contracts available anytime after Phase I award from non-SBIR customers (NASA programs, other government agencies, private sector)
- **Post-Phase II:** Additional initiatives available for continued development

### Budget & Cost Information
- **Phase I Maximum:** $150,000 (or $156,500 with TABA)
- **Documentation Required:** Quotes, previous POs, published price lists for all costs
- **Rate Justification:** Must provide DCAA rate agreement or mathematical cost basis
- **No Cost Sharing Required** (but see section 5.6)
- **Profit/Fee:** Per section 5.5

### Commercial Metrics Report (CMR)
Required submission; evaluates commercialization progress and metrics for experienced firms (those with prior awards).

## Notable Details

1. **Reduced Competitiveness:** Notable change limiting firms to 3 awards max per year (down from 5) and 5 submissions max (down from 10) will increase competition intensity.

2. **Foreign Affiliation Due Diligence:** Emphasis on security risk assessment includes detailed disclosure requirements covering:
   - Foreign talent recruitment program participation
   - Joint ventures/subsidiaries in foreign countries
   - Foreign contractual obligations
   - Foreign ownership
   - Venture capital with foreign affiliations
   - Technology licensing to foreign countries (5-year lookback)
   - Any offshore entities related to company

3. **Tight Timeline:** Solicitation released January 7, 2025 with March 10, 2025 deadline (about 9 weeks); Help Desk support guaranteed only through March 3.

4. **Proposal Submission System:** ProSAMS is the only acceptable electronic submission system; all communication via email.

5. **Ownership & Flexibility:** SAM registration "Doing Business As" (DBA) name will appear on all contracts; companies must maintain active registrations throughout entire process.

6. **No NASA Commitment:** "NASA does not commit to fund any proposal or to make a specific number of awards. NASA may elect to make several or no awards in any specific research subtopic."

7. **Intellectual Property Rights:** Awardees retain rights in data; invention reporting and patent filing requirements apply.
