## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy
- Program/Solicitation: Navy Phase I SBIR – Sonobuoy-Launched Unmanned Aerial Vehicle for Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW)
- Date: January 30, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 UAS
- Partner/Vendor: QuSpin Inc. (providing magnetometer technology)
- Key Personnel: Dr. Elston (BST, recipient); Jeff Orton (Lead QTFM engineer, QuSpin)

## Executive Summary
QuSpin provides formal support for Black Swift Technologies' Navy Phase I SBIR proposal to develop a sonobuoy-launched unmanned aerial vehicle for anti-submarine warfare, integrating magnetic anomaly detection and passive acoustic sensing. QuSpin commits to providing their Gen-2 QTFM magnetometer and technical integration support for the project.

## Technical Approach
The project aims to develop an expendable sonobuoy-launched UAS platform capable of performing:
1. **Magnetic Anomaly Detection (MAD)** – using advanced magnetometer technology
2. **In-water passive acoustic sensing** – for submarine detection and localization

**Core sensor: QuSpin Gen-2 QTFM magnetometer**
- Optically pumped atomic scalar magnetometer
- Sensitivity: <2 pT/√Hz
- Dynamic range: 1–150 µT
- Bandwidth: 0–500 Hz
- Built-in 3-axis vector capabilities and IMU
- Purpose: Detect submarine magnetic signatures with minimal platform-induced interference

**Navy performance requirement:** 20 pT/√Hz sensitivity in the 0.01–100 Hz frequency band
