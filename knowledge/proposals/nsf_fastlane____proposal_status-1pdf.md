- Type: NSF proposal review feedback
- Client/Agency: National Science Foundation (NSF)
- Program/Solicitation: STTR Phase I
- Date: 2013-10-24 (created); 2013-12-19 (modified)
- Proposal Number: 1346510
- Organization: University of Colorado at Boulder
- Key Personnel: Jack S. Elston (Principal Investigator)

## Executive Summary
This NSF STTR Phase I proposal, led by Jack Elston at the University of Colorado at Boulder, proposed developing design documentation and strategies for a small unmanned aerial system (UAS) network to sample atmospheric boundary layer parameters. The proposal received a "Poor" rating, with reviewers acknowledging the potential for a low-cost research tool but criticizing the lack of technical detail, feasibility validation, and unclear development strategy.

## Technical Approach
- Proposed development of design documentation/strategies for an unmanned vehicle system network
- Focus on atmospheric boundary layer (ABL) sampling capabilities
- Six-month Phase I timeline focused on strategic planning rather than validation
- Intended outcome: low-cost, easy-to-operate system for weather forecasting applications

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Small UAS Network for ABL Sampling:**
- Proposed as a low-cost alternative to existing atmospheric measurement systems
- Claimed advantages: low cost, superior spatial resolution, easy operation
- Intended application: measurement of atmospheric boundary layer parameters

## Use Cases & Applications
- Atmospheric boundary layer sampling and parameter measurement
- Weather forecasting support
- Research tool for scientists without access to costly atmospheric measurement solutions

## Key Review Findings (Weaknesses Identified)
- Insufficient technical detail to assess feasibility
- Lack of specificity regarding which parameters would be measured and measurement methods
- No clear plan for validation given airspace restrictions (not opening until 2015)
- Unclear how claimed advantages (low cost, ease of operation) would be achieved
- Competitive advantages over existing technologies not articulated
- Proposal focused on planning rather than technical validation or proof-of-concept

## Notable Details
- BST had previous SBIR experience (noted as a strength)
- Regulatory constraint: U.S. airspace for UAS operations not opening until 2015, creating timeline validation challenges
- Reviewers acknowledged significant market need but found execution plan inadequate for Phase I funding

**Note:** This is a reviewer response document rather than a technical proposal or capability brief. It provides critical feedback but limited detail about BST's actual system design or specifications.
