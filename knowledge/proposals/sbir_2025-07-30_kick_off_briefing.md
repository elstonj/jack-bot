## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is developing a Sonobuoy-Launched Unmanned Aircraft System (SL UAS) that integrates magnetic anomaly detection (MAD) and passive acoustic sensing capabilities for Navy Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW) operations. The Phase I effort will demonstrate technical feasibility of sensor integration into the S0 UAS platform, validate performance in controlled environments, and develop data fusion algorithms for enhanced underwater target detection and classification.

## Technical Approach

### Phase I Objectives

**Objective 1: Demonstrate Sensor Integration Feasibility**
- Evaluate noise mitigation strategies to minimize platform-induced interference
- Ensure hydrophone meets Navy sensitivity, frequency range, and depth requirements

**Objective 2: Develop Platform Compatible with Sonobuoy Launch Constraints (SLC)**
- Modify existing S0 UAS platform to accommodate payload volume and weight constraints
- Design modifications to airframe and electronics to integrate sensors

**Objective 3: Validate Sensor Performance in Controlled Environments**
- Laboratory simulations and bench testing of magnetometer noise performance
- Characterization of hydrophone acoustic sensitivity

**Objective 4: Develop and Evaluate Data Fusion Algorithms**
- Design algorithms combining MAD and acoustic sensor outputs
- Enhance underwater target detection and classification capabilities

### Work Plan Summary

1. **Requirements Analysis and Preliminary Design:** Validate selected magnetometer and hydrophone sensors; plan compute module integration; optimize for size, weight, and power (SWAP) while maintaining capabilities

2. **S0-UAS Design Modifications:** Complete CAD design and updated electronics design; incorporate necessary modifications to S0 platform; represents large portion of Phase I base effort

3. **Sensor Integration and Testing:** Test magnetometer for noise spectral density compliance; continue validation using larger BST UAS currently flying QuSpin magnetometer

4. **UAS Prototype:** Construct prototype by modifying S0 UAS to accommodate sensors and Navy mission requirements

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS Platform
- **What it is:** BST's small tube-launched UAS system
- **Proposed use:** Base platform being modified to integrate MAD and acoustic sensing for Navy ASW operations
- **Specifications:** Designed for rapid prototyping and modification; airframe can be resized/reshaped; modular electronics; BST controls all design aspects enabling rapid component modifications
- **Modifications planned:** Trading battery capacity for payload; airframe resizing to accommodate sensors and compute modules; electronics integration for sensor fusion processing

### Magnetometer Integration
- **Sensor:** QuSpin QTFM Gen-2 magnetometer
- **Performance requirement:** Must meet Navy sensitivity, frequency range, and depth requirements
- **Challenge:** Maintaining noise performance within small UAS platform
- **Mitigation:** BST has conducted test flights with QuSpin sensor on similar UAS; design modifications to minimize platform-induced electromagnetic interference

### Hydrophone/Acoustic Sensing
- **Provider:** Cetacean Research Technology partnership
- **Requirements:** Navy sensitivity, frequency range, and depth specifications
- **Capabilities:** Passive acoustic detection of underwater targets
- **Support:** Adam Sniff (P.E.) provides SME guidance on sensor selection, sensitivity, frequency range, and deployment mechanisms

### Data Fusion Algorithms
- **Purpose:** Combine MAD and acoustic sensor outputs
- **Capability:** Enhanced underwater target detection and classification
- **Development approach:** Design, implement, and test during Phase I

## Challenges, Risks & Mitigation Strategies

| Challenge | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Maintaining magnetometer noise requirements in small UAS | BST has conducted test flights with QuSpin sensor on similar platform; BST designs all S0 aspects and can rapidly modify problematic components |
| Fitting all sensors and compute needs into tube-launched platform | S0 platform modularity allows airframe modifications, size/shape changes, battery-to-payload tradeoffs |
| Achieving requirements within $10K price point | Careful sensor/compute selection for cost-effectiveness; production efficiency improvements planned for Phase II |
