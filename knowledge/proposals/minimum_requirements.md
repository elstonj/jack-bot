## Executive Summary
USDA/APHIS Wildlife Services issued minimum specifications for procurement of four unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) to support animal damage management operations. The requirements define a tactical VTOL/STOL platform with extended flight endurance, thermal payload capacity, FPV capability, and autonomous flight features.

## Technical Requirements
The document specifies minimum performance and capability standards for the UAS procurement:

**Airframe & Propulsion:**
- VTOL or STOL capability
- Weight: <55 lbs
- Maximum wingspan: 14 ft
- Electric propulsion system
- Cruise speed: 20-40 mph (max 80 mph)
- Service ceiling: minimum 500 ft AGL
- Modular design for field assembly/disassembly

**Flight Performance:**
- Minimum 45 minutes flight time
- GPS navigation
- Autopilot with waypoint following, auto-return-to-launch, loiter, and circling capabilities
- Autonomous flight capability

**Payload & Sensors:**
- Minimum 2.5 lbs payload capacity
- Thermal camera with 360° pan, ≥90° tilt, and stabilization
- FPV (First Person View) video with minimum 5-mile range

**Control & Operations:**
- Dual controller setup (aircraft + camera control)
- Ground control station with telemetry and video
- Digital video recording
- Portable handheld wireless video monitors
- Turn-key system with customer support

## Use Cases & Applications
Animal damage management and wildlife monitoring operations by USDA/APHIS Wildlife Services.

## Notable Details
- Procurement quantity: 4 aircraft
- Emphasis on portability and rapid field deployment
- Requirement for integrated thermal imaging suggests surveillance/monitoring of wildlife movement or damage assessment
- FPV range specification (5 miles) indicates need for extended standoff distance operations
- Turn-key and customer support requirements suggest government preference for complete, supported solutions
