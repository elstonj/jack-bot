## Executive Summary
This RFI announces the Air Force's planned "Autonomy Prime" program, an initiative to accelerate development and adoption of autonomy technologies for USAF operations. The program will establish the "AFWERX Proving Ground"—a testing and development facility where industry partners can demonstrate and iterate autonomy technologies at pace, with an accelerated transition pipeline to field-ready capabilities. The RFI solicits information from industry on technology maturity, capabilities, commercial applications, testing needs, and interest in participation.

## Technical Approach
AFWERX intends to create a purpose-built autonomy testing facility (Proving Ground) with:
- Open architecture, modular UAS testbeds (Group 2 & 3 aircraft) configured with isolation layers to separate test articles from core flight functionality
- High-tempo autonomy testing and demonstration capability
- Simulation infrastructure (fully virtual, hardware-in-the-loop, virtual constructive/reality)
- Rapid iteration and fielding processes for medium-to-high TRL technologies
- Support infrastructure including air/ground range space, demonstration/test participation, warfighter input, and USAF data access

Technologies must meet minimum TRL requirements: TRL 4 for Proving Ground testing, TRL 6 for rapid autonomy acquisition/fielding process.

## Use Cases & Applications

### USAF Mission Areas Addressed
The RFI frames autonomy within USAF's six key capability sets:
- Air and Space Superiority
- Global Presence
- Rapid Global Mobility
- Precision Engagement
- Information Superiority
- Agile Combat Support

### Operational Tasks (OODA Loop Framework)
Technologies should automate or enhance:
- **Observe**: Data collection, perception, information gathering
- **Orient**: Processing, compilation, organization of information
- **Decide**: Situation evaluation, action selection
- **Act**: Execution of actions to create desired effects

## Categories of Technologies Sought

### Support Infrastructure Technologies
1. **Modular Open Architecture UAS Testbeds**
   - Group 2 & Group 3 UAS (VTOL and CTOL/fixed-wing)
   - Reliability, modularity, openness prioritized over pure performance
   - Must support rapid iteration and varied testing
   - Key specifications requested: range, speed, payload, certification status, production availability, cost

2. **Simulation Technologies**
   - Fully virtual, hardware-in-the-loop, virtual constructive/VR
   - Simulate test article behavior and operational environments
   - Enable rapid-paced testing

### Autonomy Technology Categories (Testing & Rapid Transition)
- **Detection & Recognition**: Logic+ML-based ATR algorithms
- **Sensor Processing**: Low SWaP-C, heterogeneous fusion, separate vehicle fusion, global operating picture creation
- **Data Storage**: Classification/localization libraries, real-time cloud storage
- **Sensor Array Optimization**: Area coverage, heterogeneous array organization, detection vs. classification trade-offs
- **Sensors**: RF, IR, optical, acoustic, Lidar, multispectral, active/passive
- **Communication**: Low-cost radios, LPI/LPD, mesh networks, BVLOS nodes/repeaters, jamming detection, resilience path planning
- **Localization**: GPS alternatives/supplements, swarming, relative localization
- **Human-Machine Interface**: Control interfaces, inceptors, command methodologies, translation algorithms
- **Command & Control**: Decentralized architectures, lost-comms mitigation, low-comms mission execution, GCS software, navigation algorithms
- **Compute Hardware**: Hardened/certifiable CPUs, GPUs, storage
- **Autonomous Software**: ML/RL-based and logic-based task-focused algorithms
- **Effectors**: Robotic actuators, EM spectrum effectors, delivery systems, payloads

## Notable Details

### Program Context
- **Agility Prime Legacy**: Autonomy Prime builds on the success of Agility Prime (launched 2020), which accelerated vertical lift/distributed electric propulsion development
- **Strategic Rationale**: AFWERX identified three key findings:
  1. Autonomy technological dominance is critical to USAF and U.S. competitiveness
  2. USAF acquisition/test enterprise lacks resources and expertise to meet autonomy demand at required pace
  3. Substantial autonomy technologies and expertise exist outside traditional DOD industrial base
