- Key Personnel: Eryan Dai (author), Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is a brief engineering test report characterizing the performance of thermoelectric cooler (TEC) modules operating in two separate channels. The report presents experimental data showing temperature-voltage relationships and power consumption across operating conditions for both channels.

## Technical Approach
Testing of TEC modules in a dual-channel configuration, measuring:
1. Temperature response to applied voltage (ranging approximately -2V to +2.5V)
2. Power consumption at various temperatures (-10°C to +60°C)
3. Comparative performance between Channel 1 and Channel 2

Each channel tested twice to establish repeatability (data set 1 and data set 2 for each channel).

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Thermoelectric Cooler (TEC) Modules**
- What it is: Active thermal management components integrated into RF PCB design
- Use case: Thermal control for soil moisture sensor electronics
- Performance characteristics: 
  - Operating voltage range: approximately -2V to +2.5V
  - Operating temperature range: -10°C to +60°C
  - Power consumption: 0 to 2.5W across operating range

## Key Results
- Temperature output shows linear relationship with applied voltage in both channels
- Temperature range achievable: approximately -10°C to +50°C
- Power consumption scales with temperature, reaching ~2.5W at maximum temperature
- Channel 1 and Channel 2 show consistent, parallel performance characteristics
- Dual datasets per channel demonstrate reproducible behavior
