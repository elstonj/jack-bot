# PY25.2 TACFI Program Matching Funds Guidance

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Program guidance document / eligibility rules
- **Client/Agency:** U.S. Air Force (DAF - Department of the Air Force) / AFWERX
- **Program/Solicitation:** TACFI (Tactical Funding Increase) Program; DAF SBIR/STTR Supplemental Funding Pilot Program
- **Date:** November 2024
- **Clearance:** AFRL-2024-6250
- **Related Systems:** None specific to Black Swift Technologies products

## Executive Summary
This document provides compliance guidance for companies applying to the DAF TACFI program, which offers supplemental SBIR/STTR funding increases. It establishes rules for eligible matching funds (government and third-party), defines affiliate restrictions, specifies funding caps and ratios, and clarifies what types of investments and government funding qualify for matching purposes.

## General Guidelines - Matching Fund Rules

**Fund Source Eligibility:**
- Prior SBIR/STTR Phase II matching funds cannot be reused
- SBIR/STTR funds from other Federal agencies do NOT count toward government matching
- Non-SBIR/STTR funds from Federal, State, or Local governments DO count toward government matching
- Foreign government funds may count toward third-party matching (subject to review/approval)
- Phase III subcontracts qualify if they logically extend/derive from Phase II work and meet specific criteria

**Fund Application & Constraints:**
- Matching funds applied directly to Phase IIB or Sequential Phase IIA must identify specific tasks in Spend Plan
- Both comptrollers (funding source and awardee organization) must agree on task allocation
- Spend Plan and Funding Letters must be completed and included with application
- SBIR/STTR contributions capped at: 100% of non-SBIR/STTR federal funds received OR 50% of third-party matching funds (unless written exemption from AFWERX Ventures Division Chief)

## TACFI-Specific Funding Parameters

**Funding Amounts:**
- **Minimum:** $375K per participant
- **Maximum:** $2.0M per participant

**Matching Ratios (applicant selects one option):**
1. **Dual-Use Matching Option:** 1:1 ratio (SBIR funds : eligible third-party funds)
   - Example minimum: $375K Air Force SBIR + $375K third-party
2. **Defense-Only Matching Option:** 1:1 ratio (SBIR funds : non-SBIR/STTR government funds)
   - Example minimum: $375K Air Force SBIR + $375K non-SBIR/STTR government funds

**Important Constraints:**
- Applicant selects one matching option; cannot combine approaches
- May receive MORE matching funds than ratios dictate
- Phase III awards (or government contracts/grants) executed within 6 months of Capability Package submission that logically extend Phase II work count as strategic matching
- Eligible third-party funding received after Phase II start and within 24 months of Capability Package submission qualifies as matching
- Non-SBIR/STTR government funds must be available at time of award
- Third-party matching funds must be confirmed received prior to TACFI award

## Third-Party Matching Fund Eligibility

**Eligible Third Parties:**
- Other businesses, academic institutions, nonprofits, venture capital firms, individual angel investors, or combinations thereof

**NOT Eligible:**
- Small business owners and family members
- "Affiliates" (as defined in 13 CFR §121.103), including:
  - Entities where one controls or has power to control another
  - Entities with identical or substantially identical business/economic interests
  - Family members and persons with common investments
  - **Exception:** Businesses owned in whole/substantial part by SBIC-licensed investment companies are NOT considered affiliates
- Joint ventures and limited partnerships must qualify as Small Business Concerns (SBCs)

**Qualified Investment Characteristics:**
- Must be cash provided by third party in return for equity, royalty share, technology rights, profit percentage, advance purchase orders, convertible debt, or combinations thereof
- Must reduce technical and commercialization risk (NOT sales of existing products; "pre-sales" of advanced products acceptable)
- Cannot be expected to be repaid in cash (NOT loans, unless convertible to equity automatically within 5 years)
- SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) notes and similar instruments qualify (not treated as loans)

**Ineligible Investment Types:**
- Company's own internal funds
- In-kind contributions (must be cash)
- Simple loans without conversion provisions
- Cross-company matching between two small companies (lacks outside validation)

## Net Investment Calculations

If third-party investor provides cash investment AND contracts with SBC:
- Only **net cash investment** counts toward matching
- Example: Investor provides $750K cash, then subcontracts for $250K → only $500K net counts
- **Exception:** If the separate contract predates TACFI/STRATFI submission, entire cash investment qualifies

## Eligible Investment Examples

**Qualifying Forms:**
- Debt convertible to equity (with automatic conversion within 5 years minimum, from public entity or private entity with conversion rights)
- Advance purchase orders for new products from SBIR project
- IPO funds designated for project-related activities (R&D, marketing, etc.)
- Other private funding sources (not limited to equity investment)

**Non-Qualifying Forms:**
- Straight loans without conversion
- In-kind contributions
- Affiliate contributions
- Investments in university partners or subcontractors (investment must be in small company itself)

## Performance & Work-Share Requirements

- Minimum of 50% of Phase II research/analytical effort must be performed by Phase II awardee (including TACFI/STRATFI work)
- Written approval from Contracting/Agreements Officer and Air Force SBIR Program Manager required for exceptions
- TACFI/STRATFI performance covered by SBIR life-cycle certifications from original Phase II award
- Third-party investors cannot require majority work to be performed by others
- Third-party investors cannot:
  - Condition awards on Federal lab subcontracting
  - Require subcontracting that violates SBIR work-share requirements
  - Require Federal lab pre-payment beyond 30 days of activities
  - Require subcontracting violating statutory/regulatory protections

## STRATFI (Sequential) Program Notes

- Third-party matching funds may be received during first month of STRATFI performance (differs from TACFI, which requires pre-award receipt)
- Same general matching eligibility rules apply otherwise

## Notable Administrative Requirements

- Private Funding Letters required for all third-party matching
- Specific third-party matching POCs must be identified
- Affiliation determinations use 13 CFR §121.103 standards
- AFWERX contact for questions: stratfi.tacfi@afwerx.af.mil
- No waivers for timing requirements (6-month Phase III date, 24-month third-party funding window)
- Document includes extensive FAQ section clarifying affiliate status, investment structure, and common scenarios

## Key Distinctions from Standard SBIR

- TACFI targets technology maturation and commercialization acceleration
- Requires external validation of commercial potential (hence third-party matching emphasis)
- Emphasizes risk reduction in both technical and commercialization dimensions
- Allows flexibility in matching sources (government, private, or combinations)
