# NASA SBIR Phase I Proposal: UAS-Based Low-Level Wind Modeling for Wildfire Operations

- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: 2023 NASA SBIR, Wildfire subtopic
- Date: 2023-06-07 (submission date)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: UAS-based sensor systems for meteorological observations
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor; likely PI or key team member)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed a Phase I pilot study to develop and validate algorithms for modeling 3D low-level winds and turbulence in active wildfire regions using multi-UAS platforms. The work aims to improve UAS navigation and fire retardant delivery accuracy by collecting targeted meteorological observations near or within wildfire smoke and fire zones, with potential commercialization for both NASA and broader markets.

## Technical Approach
- **Core objective**: Develop algorithms to model 3D low-level winds and turbulence in wildfire-affected regions using sensor data from UAS platforms
- **Data collection strategy**: Deploy multiple UAS to collect wind and turbulence measurements in/near active wildfires; leverage new sensor technologies already deployed on many UAS systems
- **Integration approach**: Assimilate UAS meteorological observations into wildfire forecasting and operational systems
- **Operational constraints**: Initial flight testing planned outside temporary flight restriction (TFR) boundaries or during nighttime operations (when crewed aircraft not operating); Phase II would seek permission for daytime flights within TFRs

## Products & Capabilities Described

**UAS Platforms with Meteorological Sensors**
- What it is: Multi-UAS systems equipped with sensors for wind and turbulence measurement
- Proposed use: Collect targeted observations in low-level regions affected by wildfires
- Performance goal: Enable accurate 3D wind modeling and forecasting to improve UAS navigation and fire retardant delivery

**Wind/Turbulence Modeling Algorithms** (to be developed)
- What it is: New algorithms for 3D low-level wind and turbulence forecasting specific to wildfire environments
- Proposed use: Generate real-time or near-real-time wind field forecasts for UAS suppression operations
- Technical risk noted: Algorithm development carries schedule risk

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary**: UAS-based wildfire suppression operations (navigation guidance, fire retardant delivery accuracy)
- **Secondary markets identified**: Any application requiring accurate 3D low-level wind modeling beyond wildfire operations
- **Operational context**: Integration with NASA wildfire monitoring and operational decision-support systems

## Phase I Work Plan (Seven Tasks)
1. UAS sampling strategies (design optimal observation patterns)
2. Multi-UAS integration into wildfire airspace
3. Gathering and assimilation of UAS measurements
4. Assessment of forecasting improvements for 3D wind and turbulence
5. Integration of UAS meteorological observations into wildfire operations
6. Commercial potential and product viability exploration
7. Reports and documentation

## Notable Details

**Partnership/Subcontractor Interest**
- Autonodyne expressed interest in providing software setup and Phase II subcontracting to develop a wildfire-centric UAS Traffic Management (UTM) and data dissemination platform

**Key Risk/Mitigation**
- **Critical risk**: Lack of pre-existing permission from Colorado's Center of Excellence (CoE) for Advanced Technology Aerial Firefighting to conduct UAS observations near active fires
- **Mitigation strategy**: Phase I work planned outside TFR boundaries or at night; Phase II would pursue formal permission to expand operations
- Reviewer noted mitigation approach may limit data quality needed to achieve stated goals
