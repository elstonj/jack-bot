## Executive Summary
This three-year research initiative proposes to develop high-resolution soil moisture and land surface characterization capabilities for Army boundary layer and Artillery Meteorology applications. The work integrates existing EMT+VS soil moisture downscaling systems with new data assimilation frameworks, UAS/satellite remote sensing, and field validation studies at Army-relevant scales (1-3 meter resolutions), with operational transition pathways into Army GIS frameworks.

## Technical Approach

**Overall Strategy:** Three integrated theme areas addressing Army NWP/Artillery Met needs:

**Theme A: Met Office Unified Model (UM) NWP Error Characterization and Error Modeling**
- Characterize biases and error distributions of the Met Office Unified Model as it relates to Artillery Meteorology and boundary layer applications
- Error modeling for Army-specific systems
- Exploration of error dependencies and distributions
- Balloon sounding collaborations with ARL

**Theme B: Boundary Layer Remote Sensing Data Assimilation at Army-Relevant Scales**
- Soil moisture data assimilation and surface energy balance studies to improve boundary layer representation
- Innovation vector analysis of soil moisture estimates using existing downscaled datasets
- Design and implement Army-scale data assimilation synthetic experiments with data distribution-dependent design
- Applications to trafficability and target recognition
- Extensions to Reynolds Creek, ID and White Sands, NM sites

**Theme C: Collaborative Calibration/Validation Field Studies Using UAS/Satellite Remote Sensing**
- UAS remote sensing of fine-scaled soil moisture for calibration/validation
- Integration of UAS/satellite observations within data assimilation frameworks
- Field experiments at Reynolds Creek, ID (summer 2018) and White Sands Missile Range, NM
- Characterization of microwave sensor errors and model initialization errors
- Joint preparations with ARL/BED Atmospheric Science Center staff

## Products & Capabilities Described

**EMT+VS Soil Moisture Downscaling System**
- CSU-developed system built on existing Army Phase 2 SBIR results
- Demonstrated capability at 10-30 meter resolutions
- Current work transitioning to 3 meter resolutions with anticipated achievement of 1 meter spatial resolution
- Enables very high-fidelity soil moisture data assimilation matching unprecedented downscaling capabilities
- Integrates into Army GIS spatial frameworks for operational transition

**Black Swift Technologies UAS Platform**
- Small UAS (sUAS) with soil moisture mapping capabilities
- Role: High-resolution remote sensing for field validation studies
- Three years of NASA soil moisture mapping sUAS development
- Over 500 flight experiments conducted by BST team
- Will conduct direct observations at Reynolds Creek and White Sands for cal/val integration

**Data Assimilation Frameworks**
- Army-scale innovation vector analysis capabilities
- Data assimilation systems designed for high-resolution (1-meter) soil moisture fields
- Advanced error handling addressing non-Gaussian error biases
- Quality control and sensor error modeling at fine spatial scales

## Use Cases & Applications

1. **Artillery Meteorology**: Improved accuracy for fire control and ballistics calculations
2. **Boundary Layer Applications**: Surface energy balance studies and atmospheric boundary layer characterization
3. **Trafficability and Mobility**: Soil moisture characterization for vehicle movement prediction (NATO NG-NRMM collaboration, terramechanics community)
4. **Target Recognition**: Enhanced through improved land surface characterization
5. **Arctic and Field Operations**: Addressing current inadequacy of satellite products (250-1000 meter resolution from USAF) for Army battlefield applications
6. **DoD Weather Satellite Follow-on (WSF) Program**: Direct relevance to future satellite system requirements
