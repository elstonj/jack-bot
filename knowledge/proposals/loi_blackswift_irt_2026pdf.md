# Letter of Intent: Black Swift Navy Phase II Icing Test

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Intent (LOI)
- Client/Agency: NASA Glenn Research Center (GRC), John H. Glenn Research Center, Cleveland, OH
- Program/Solicitation: Navy Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) Phase II; Hazardous Weather focus
- Date: February 19, 2026
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: BST uncrewed aircraft system (UAS)
- Key Personnel: Jack Elson, Ph.D. (CEO, BST); Dennis Eck (IRT Facility Manager, NASA GRC); Bryan K. Smith (Director, Facilities, Test, and Center Operations, NASA GRC)

## Executive Summary
NASA Glenn Research Center issued a non-binding letter of intent to support Black Swift Technologies' Navy STTR Phase II proposal by providing one day of testing access to the Icing Research Tunnel (IRT) facility. The testing will evaluate icing sensors and mitigation techniques developed for BST's uncrewed aircraft system, with estimated cost of $62,284 for facility use, staffing, and supporting services during 2029.

## Technical Approach
- Testing venue: NASA GRC's Icing Research Tunnel (IRT)
- Duration: One day of facility occupancy
- Scope: Evaluation of sensors and mitigation techniques for icing conditions
- Application: Validation of icing-related technologies developed under Navy STTR funding, deployed on BST's uncrewed aircraft system

## Products & Capabilities Described
**BST Uncrewed Aircraft System**
- Context: Developed through Navy Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) efforts
- Capability: Platform for deployment of icing sensors and mitigation techniques
- Testing need: Requires icing tunnel evaluation to validate sensor and mitigation performance

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Icing research and mitigation**: Development and validation of sensors and mitigation techniques for aircraft operating in icing conditions
- **Hazardous weather operations**: Support for Navy objectives related to hazardous weather operations (per STTR solicitation focus)

## Notable Details
- This is a non-binding, non-exclusive letter of intent only—not a legal agreement
- Commitment contingent on: selection of proposal, successful completion of NASA internal review (legal and export control), availability of appropriated funds
- Cost estimate: $62,284 (fully reimbursable)
- Planned testing window: January 1, 2029 – December 31, 2029
- Formal teaming arrangement to be documented in separate agreement if proposal accepted
