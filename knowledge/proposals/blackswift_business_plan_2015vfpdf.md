- Key Personnel: Not named individually

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is a Boulder, Colorado-based sUAS avionics startup founded in 2011 that provides customizable flight management systems and aircraft to research institutions, NASA, and government entities. BST sought Colorado state investment to expand from the restricted government/research market into the emerging commercial UAS market expected to open in 2016-2017, positioning the company with proven products and established customers ahead of FAA regulatory changes.

## Technical Approach
BST differentiated itself through proprietary control and estimation algorithms that enable high-level autonomy and optimization using affordable commercially available sensors (e.g., cell phone electronics). The SwiftCore Flight Management System was the core technology—modular software that could be scaled from simple operations to advanced features, deployed across multiple airframe manufacturers. The system architecture included:
- Lightweight autopilot (SwiftPilot: 50 grams)
- Mobile/real-time user interface (SwiftTab)
- Integrated ground station (SwiftStation)

This end-to-end integration allowed operators to theoretically control multiple aircraft simultaneously with minimal training, versus competing systems requiring crews of up to 12 personnel with expensive certification.

## Business Model & Revenue Streams
BST operated four primary business areas:

1. **Custom Development**: Tailored sUAS technologies and sensors for government and commercial customers (e.g., Soil Moisture Mapping system)
2. **Turn-Key Systems Sales**: End-to-end integrated sUAS; price range $20k-$100k per system
3. **SwiftCore Licensing**: Sale of flight management system to integrators and airframe manufacturers; ~$10k per unit
4. **Consulting Services**: FAA/NASA flight approval consulting

Business model leveraged distribution through existing channels and new airframe manufacturer partnerships. Strategy included discounts and free airframe compatibility customization to drive adoption of SwiftCore avionics.

## Competitive Advantages
- Unmatched technical team: three team members with Doctorates in Aerospace Engineering, all focused on sUAS automation, applications, and design
- Proprietary algorithms for control and estimation enabling superior autonomy
- Fully integrated end-to-end system architecture
- Reduced operational complexity: single operator can theoretically control multiple aircraft versus 12-person crews required by competitors
- Modular software allowing customization for different user experience levels
- Proven customer base and product validation through research institutions and NASA

## Market Context & Regulatory Environment
- Document written during pre-commercial UAS era (operations restricted to public entities with FAA Certificate of Authorization)
- FAA expected to complete commercial regulations by late 2016 or early 2017
- Teal Group market estimate: UAS market (excluding consumer/commercial) growing from $6.6B (2012) to $11.4B (2022)
- AUVSI estimate: Commercial UAS could deliver $13.6B economic impact in US 2015-2017 period
- 2017 projected as year commercial entities could legally operate UAS

## Marketing & Sales Strategy
- Direct sales through trade shows and industry events (effective in restricted market)
- Web-based lead management program
- Customer referral focus—prioritize product satisfaction to drive word-of-mouth
- Target customer profile: organizations with spending authority seeking to cut costs or gain competitive advantage; prefer usability over low cost; interested in data-driven operational optimization
- Plan to establish distribution networks through established channels once commercial regulations finalized
