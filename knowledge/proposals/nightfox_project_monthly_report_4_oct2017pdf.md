- Type: Monthly Progress Report
- Client/Agency: NOAA
- Program/Solicitation: NOAA UAS Project / FIREX (Fire Influence on Regional to Global Environments and Air Quality)

- Key Personnel: Ru-Shan Gao (Principal Investigator), Troy Thornberry, Schranz, Mandel, Kochanski

## Executive Summary
CU Boulder's NightFOX project is developing a UAS-based instrument suite for nighttime fire observations to support NOAA's FIREX mission. In October 2017, the project achieved approval of the S2 platform purchase order and accumulated 12 flight hours during soil moisture mapping operations conducted through a partnership with Black Swift Technologies. Instrument development for aerosol, CO, CO2, and infrared remote sensing capabilities is progressing toward sensor selection.

## Technical Approach
The project is integrating multiple sensor technologies onto the S2 UAS platform:
- **Aerosol measurement**: Development of coarse-mode aerosol instrument (POPS-design derivative); commercial instrument borrowed for testing
- **Optical remote sensing**: Preliminary optical design completed for near-IR and mid-IR cross-track-scanning telescopes; mechanical prototype design underway
- **Gas sensing**: CO sensor elements on order; CO2 NDIR source/detector testing initiated
- **Data modeling**: Access to NOAA supercomputer being established for fire weather modeling with UAS data products

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS (formerly SuperSwift)
- **Description**: Hand-built prototype UAS platform being transitioned to mass-production version
- **Status**: CU purchase order approved; delivery delayed from immediate to January 2018 to allow mass-production manufacturing (facilitates future parts procurement)
- **Operational use**: Flying through Black Swift Technologies partnership
- **Performance to date**: 12 accumulated flight hours on soil moisture mapping project
- **Planned enhancement**: Discussion with FAA underway regarding higher altitude operations and beyond-visual-line-of-sight (BVLOS) capability

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary mission**: Nighttime fire observations for FIREX campaign
- **Secondary validation**: Soil moisture mapping (current demonstration application)
- **Future application**: Prescribed burn sampling (planned for late FY18)
- **Integration planning**: Coordination with FASMEE field mission (2019)
