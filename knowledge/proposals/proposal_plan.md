## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes a three-year project to integrate S0 uncrewed aircraft wind data into National Hurricane Center forecasting operations. The primary focus is developing and validating a reduction factor to convert S0 measurements to standard 10-minute/10-meter wind speeds, demonstrating forecast improvements through testbed evaluation, and ultimately operationalizing real-time data delivery to NHC forecasters.

## Technical Approach

**Primary Research Effort:**
- Develop a reduction factor to standardize S0 wind measurements to 10m wind averaged to 1-minute
- Led by Jun Zhang and Josh
- De-emphasize data assimilation approach (shift from initial concept)

**Three-Year Phased Approach:**

**Year 1:** Reduction Factor Development
- Derive reduction factor from 2024 S0 flights combined with dropsonde and radar data
- Establish methodology for standardizing raw S0 measurements

**Year 2:** Validation & Testbed Operations
- Run reduced data on historical storms (with/without reduction factor applied)
- Demonstrate forecast improvement to NHC
- Requires NHC support (Letter of Support offered by Wallace)
- Testbed evaluation component

**Year 3:** Operational Implementation
- Implement reduction factor in S0 or ground station processing
- Integrate into HDOBS or other real-time data stream
- Deliver near-real-time data usable by NHC forecasters

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0 (Uncrewed Aircraft System)**
- Wind measurement platform for hurricane operations
- Current operational status: 19 flights completed as of document date
- Capability to operate at 10m altitude for data validation
- Target: Non-BST personnel operating S0 by end of 2025 season

**HDOBS (Data Stream)**
- Real-time data delivery system for NHC
- Proposed integration point for S0 reduction-factor-processed wind data

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Application:** Hurricane forecasting and analysis
- NHC forecaster familiarization with S0-derived wind data
- Improving hurricane track and intensity forecasts through enhanced observations
- Real-time operational support during hurricane season

**Operational Readiness & Expansion:**
- Increased S0 deployment (19 flights completed, planning 2025 expansion)
- Adding CAD (Civil Air Patrol or similar) launch capability to improve operational availability
- Air Force (AF) as potential user to significantly increase UAS data volume for NHC
- Multi-UAS operations capability
