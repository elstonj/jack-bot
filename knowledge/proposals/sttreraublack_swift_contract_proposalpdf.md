# STTR Phase II: Expandable Air-Sea Profiling Observations in Hazardous Weather Conditions via Small Uncrewed Aircraft System

## Document Metadata
- Type: Cost Proposal / Contract Proposal
- Client/Agency: Office of Naval Research (ONR), via STTR Phase II program
- Program/Solicitation: Navy STTR Phase II - Hazardous Weather
- Date: February 2026
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 (Small Uncrewed Aircraft System)
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (CEO, BST Technical Contact), Beck Cotter (Proposal Specialist, BST), Dr. Joshua Wadler (PI, ERAU)

## Executive Summary
This is a cost proposal for STTR Phase II funding where Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University (ERAU) will provide research support to Black Swift Technologies for developing expanded air-sea profiling capabilities using Black Swift's S0 small uncrewed aircraft system for operations in hazardous weather conditions. The project spans two phases: Base (2027-2028) and Option (2029-2030) periods.

## Technical Approach
- Implementation and analysis of turbulence algorithms developed in Phase I
- Analysis of field data and S0 UAS measurements for integration into atmospheric/oceanic models
- Development of wave height detection/measurement algorithms
- Field campaign planning, execution, and analysis for testing S0 upgrades
- Validation testing over water
- Expansion of S0 capabilities for extreme cold environments
- Development of operational concepts for S0 deployment

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S0 (Small Uncrewed Aircraft System)**
- Uncrewed platform for air-sea profiling observations
- Being upgraded with new turbulence detection algorithm capabilities
- Capable of operating in hazardous weather conditions
- Planned for deployment in extreme cold environments
- Can collect data for wave height and air-sea interaction measurements

## Use Cases & Applications
- Air-sea profiling in hazardous weather conditions
- Turbulence measurement and characterization
- Wave height observations
- Operation in extreme cold environments (Arctic/polar operations implied)
- Over-water data collection and validation
- Integration of UAS data into atmospheric/oceanographic models
