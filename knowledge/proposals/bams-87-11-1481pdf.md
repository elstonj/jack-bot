# The First Successful Typhoon Eyewall-Penetration Reconnaissance Flight Mission Conducted by the Unmanned Aerial Vehicle, Aerosonde

## Document Metadata
- Type: Academic journal article / observational report
- Publication: Bulletin of the American Meteorological Society (BAMS)
- Publication Date: November 2006
- Author: Po-Hsiung Lin, Department of Atmospheric Sciences, National Taiwan University
- DOI: 10.1175/BAMS-87-11-1482
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None (document is a reference publication about Aerosonde UAV operations, found in BST's NOAA SBIR Phase I proposal materials)
- Funding Organizations: National Science Council of Taiwan, Central Weather Bureau, National Science and Technology Center for Disaster Reduction

## Executive Summary
This peer-reviewed observational report documents the first successful penetration of a typhoon eyewall by a small unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV), the Aerosonde, flown by the Taiwan Aerosonde Team into Typhoon Longwang on October 1, 2005. The mission demonstrated the capability of low-cost UAVs to conduct typhoon reconnaissance and measured critical meteorological parameters including peak tangential winds of 54 m/s and an estimated sea surface pressure of 922 hPa.

## Technical Approach & Mission Details

**Vehicle & Sensors:**
- Platform: Aerosonde Mark III (small robotic UAV designed by Tad McGeer, manufactured by Australian Aerosonde Ltd.)
- Instrument: Vaisala RSS901 PTU sensor (pressure-temperature-humidity, same as used in GPS dropwindsondes)
- Altitude: 3,000 m flight level for eyewall penetration
- Communication: Iridium satellite link for real-time data transmission
- Data rate: 3-minute-averaged wind measurements

**Mission Profile (Typhoon Longwang, October 1, 2005):**
- Penetration time: 1620 UTC
- Flight duration: Nearly 11 hours
- Flight pattern: Counterclockwise approach from ~330 km outside storm center to eyewall
- Measurement period analyzed: 1300-1600 UTC
- Observation sequence: Eye sounding from 3,000 m down to 1,000 m altitude

## Key Meteorological Measurements

**Wind Data:**
- Peak tangential wind (relative to center): 54 m/s
- Radial wind speed (relative to radar): 51.3 m/s (from Hualien Doppler radar at 2.4° elevation, 3 km altitude)
- Sustained wind equivalent: 49.5 m/s [using R700 ratio of 0.9 × Vmax]
- Wind profile decay: Power law coefficient α ≈ −0.4 (smaller magnitude than typical typhoon structure with α values of −0.7 to −0.5)

**Thermodynamic Data:**
- Temperature at 3,000 m flight level: 2.5°C warm-core feature in the eye
- Estimated sea surface pressure: 922 hPa (from downward sounding profile)
- Japan Meteorological Agency concurrent analysis: 930 hPa, 49 m/s maximum sustained wind

## Historical Context & Prior Aerosonde Operations

**Previous Aerosonde Experience:**
- Hurricane Ophelia (September 16, 2005): Aerosonde circled outer band at ~2,500 ft, measured winds up to 85 mph (~38 m/s), but did NOT penetrate eyewall
- Multiple failed eyewall penetration attempts: 2000-2005 (before Typhoon Longwang success)
- Arctic operations: Aerosonde widely used for local circulation, thunderstorm environment, and sea surface temperature measurements
- Comparative validation: Intercomparison studies conducted between Aerosonde and radiosonde soundings

## Use Cases & Applications

1. **Typhoon Reconnaissance:** Low-cost alternative to manned aircraft for eyewall penetration and structural analysis
2. **Real-Time Forecast Support:** Data transmitted to Central Weather Bureau (CWB) in Taipei for operational analysis/forecasting
3. **Structural Characterization:** Measurement of tangential wind profiles, radial wind structure, and thermal characteristics of tropical cyclones
4. **Remote/Offshore Operations:** Capability to operate when eye is ~100 km offshore (Typhoon Longwang case)
5. **Arctic Operations:** High-latitude environmental observations
6. **Sea Surface Temperature Monitoring:** Demonstrated capability in Arctic regions

## Notable Results & Findings

- **Historic Achievement:** First-ever eyewall penetration by a small UAV into a typhoon/typhoon
- **Wind Profile Analysis:** Optimal power-law fit (V = Vmax(Rmax/R)^α) with α = −0.4 showed gentler decay than typical typhoon models, warranting further detailed analysis
- **Pressure Estimate Validation:** Aerosonde-derived 922 hPa compared well with JMA operational analysis of 930 hPa
- **Sustained Wind Conversion:** Demonstrated application of R700 ratio (0.9 × peak wind) for comparison with operational sustained wind definitions
- **Operational Coordination:** Real-time Web posting of route, temperature, and wind field to CWB for forecast use

## Competitive & Operational Advantages

- **Cost-Effectiveness:** Described as "relatively inexpensive" compared to manned reconnaissance aircraft
- **Extended Endurance:** 11-hour flight duration demonstrated capability for comprehensive structural sampling
- **Satellite Communication:** Iridium link enabled real-time data transmission and operational integration
- **Complementary to Manned Operations:** Proposed coordinated use with Astra jet aircraft (DOTSTAR project) for synoptic surveillance near Taiwan
- **Reliability:** Success after persistent development (5+ years of attempts)
