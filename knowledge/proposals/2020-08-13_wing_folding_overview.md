## Executive Summary
This presentation documents Black Swift Technologies' progress on a NASA SBIR Phase II project to develop a deployable, folding-wing UAS capable of surviving Venus's harsh upper atmosphere. The focus is on wing deployment mechanisms, materials selection, and prototyping of hinged airfoil sections using composite structures and high-temperature materials.

## Technical Approach
**Core Strategy:** Design a high-aspect-ratio aircraft with deployable (foldable) wings that can be stowed within an aeroshell for Venus entry, then deployed for persistent atmospheric observations via dynamic soaring.

**Key Technical Elements:**
- Deployable wing with hinged sections using HSC (High-Strength Composite) materials
- Full composite airfoil sections with integrated composite hinges
- Non-folding wing sections cut from foam via CNC milling
- Internal structural augmentation with bending sections of HSC (spar reinforcement)
- Composite tape and steel shim prototyping (noted as reaching its limit)
- Custom molds for curing airfoil sections (obtained from Scion Aviation)
- Investigation of 3D-printed airfoil section molds from high-temperature resin

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Deployable UAS for Venus:**
- High-aspect-ratio design to maximize dynamic soaring efficiency
- Foldable wing architecture for aeroshell constraints
- Required to carry 10 kg payload
- Must survive Venus atmosphere conditions (thermal/chemical environment)

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Mission:** Persistent observations of Venus upper atmosphere
- **Deployment Method:** Release from aeroshell during descent; deployment and flight via dynamic soaring
- **Payload Capability:** 10 kg minimum

## Notable Details
- **Phase I Achievement:** Completed first iteration of high-aspect-ratio deployable aircraft design meeting aeroshell volume constraints and 10 kg payload requirement
- **Material Innovation:** Transition from composite tape/steel shim to full composite airfoil sections with integrated composite hinges
- **Manufacturing Partnership:** Scion Aviation involved for custom mold production
- **Outstanding Questions for Prototyping:**
  - Control surface separation/shortening feasibility
  - Incomplete trailing edge design viability
  - Internal spar augmentation with HSC bending sections
  - Volume constraints for stowed vs. deployed configurations
- **Prototype Status:** Early concept prototype deployment confirmed hinge geometry model; composite hinges validated as proof of concept
- **Next Steps:** Finalize hinge dimensions/locations in CAD; conduct initial prototyping with hinged airfoil sections; conduct thermal testing; evaluate aeroshell integration; determine coating strategy (film vs. composite coatings)
