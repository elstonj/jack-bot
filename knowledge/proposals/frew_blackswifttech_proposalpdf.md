## Executive Summary
This is a CU Boulder subcontract to Black Swift Technologies for a NASA SBIR Phase I project focused on developing an extensible multi-UAS coordination architecture. The work involves reviewing cooperative flight algorithms, implementing them in BST's simulation environment, and planning for Phase II certification activities.

## Technical Approach
The subcontract outlines three primary tasks:
1. **Algorithm Selection & Review**: Evaluation and selection of 2-3 cooperative algorithms, with focus on sampling strategies previously developed by RECUV (Research and Engineering Center for Unmanned Vehicles), including horizontal and vertical stack formations
2. **Simulation Implementation**: Integration of selected algorithms into BST's simulation environment for testing
3. **Phase II Planning**: Preliminary work on certification requirements for cooperative flight experiments

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **BST Simulation Environment**: Multi-UAS coordination simulation capability used for algorithm testing and validation

## Use Cases & Applications
- Multi-UAS cooperative flight operations
- Coordinated sampling strategies using formation flight (horizontal and vertical stack formations)
