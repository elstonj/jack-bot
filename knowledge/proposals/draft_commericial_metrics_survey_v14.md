## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is responding to NASA SBIR commercialization metrics requirements, documenting its track record of successful S2 UAS commercialization across government (NASA, NOAA, USGS) and commercial sectors. The document highlights BST's progression from founding (2011) through multiple SBIR phases and current commercial traction with universities, engineering firms, and environmental monitoring organizations.

## Technical Approach
The referenced technology (from 2018 Phase I award 80NSSC18P1915) focuses on:
- Low-altitude fixed-wing UAS flight for close-in sensing over large areas
- Active navigation around obstacles and rugged terrain
- State-of-the-art sensors, control algorithms, and on-board processing
- Modular subsystem design deployable on S2 UAS and multi-rotor platforms
- Automated in situ sensing capabilities
- Enabling beyond visual line-of-sight (BVLOS) operations

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- Purpose-built scientific aerial platform
- Engineered for nomadic scientific field campaigns in harsh environments
- Equipped with SwiftCore system for flight tracking and accuracy in high winds
- Supports interchangeable payloads
- Approved for major scientific missions by NASA
- Deployed by NOAA and used by commercial users

### SwiftCore
- Flight tracking and navigation system
- Integrated with S2 UAS
- Designed for ease of use and accurate flight tracking in high wind conditions
