# Consultant Statement of Work - Tethersonde Development

## Document Metadata
- Type: Subcontract / Statement of Work (SOW)
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: NASA 2021 SBIR Phase I
- Date: January 6, 2021
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Advanced tethersonde system (development phase)
- Key Personnel: Dr. Gijs de Boer (consultant), Jack Elston (BST, document editor)

## Executive Summary
Dr. Gijs de Boer of the University of Colorado is contracted as a consultant to Black Swift Technologies for a NASA SBIR Phase I project developing an advanced tethersonde system for atmospheric observations. De Boer will provide expert guidance on state-of-the-art tethersonde wind sensing, contribute to instrument testing and data evaluation, and assist with potential Phase II proposal development over one year (June 1, 2021 onwards) at $165/hour.

## Technical Approach

**Consulting Structure:**
- Billing: $165/hour on as-needed basis plus travel costs
- Total estimated cost: $29.7k (180-hour equivalent) over one year
- Five primary work categories with allocated hours:

1. **Expert Guidance on State-of-the-Art & Community Needs (30 hours)**
   - Leverage previous experience with tethered balloon wind and thermodynamic measurements
   - Provide recommendations on required measurement accuracy
   - Define sampling environments and environmental condition considerations
   - Document best practices for lower-atmospheric wind structure and turbulence observation
   - Support safe operation of remotely-piloted and autonomous vehicles
   - Deliverable: Short report highlighting industry standard instrumentation performance and known limitations

2. **Instrument Testing Planning & Execution (40 hours)**
   - Guidance on efficient boundary layer wind structure sampling in different regimes
   - Selection of sampling locations and identification of verification data sources
   - Potential on-site travel to testing locations for experiment configuration assistance
   - Laboratory and surface-level measurement confirmation operations

3. **Data Evaluation & Quality Assessment (50 hours)**
   - Review laboratory and field test data
   - Initial evaluation of wind fields relative to other measurements
   - Assessment of measurement quality and utility for meteorological models
   - Analysis of data usefulness for atmospheric science

4. **Phase II Proposal Development Assistance (30 hours)**
   - Detailed recommendations on flight test configuration
   - Optimal sampling strategy development
   - Focus on meteorological understanding and forecasting applications

5. **Reporting & Communication (30 hours)**
   - Formalized documentation of results
   - Breakdown of work performed by task and time requirements
   - Analysis conclusions and overall instrument utility assessment
   - Interim reports as requested
   - Regular communication with BST team (email, telephone, in-person meetings)

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Advanced Tethersonde System**
- Purpose: Atmospheric observations, particularly wind and thermodynamic measurements
- Development stage: Phase I testing
- Testing phases: Laboratory environment and surface testing
- Intended capability: Lower-atmospheric wind structure and turbulence measurement
- Target application: Support for remotely-piloted and autonomous vehicle operations

## Use Cases & Applications

- **Lower atmospheric research**: Wind and thermodynamic profiling
- **Boundary layer studies**: Wind structure characterization in different regimes
- **Operational support**: Safe operation of remotely-piloted aircraft systems (RPAS)
- **Meteorological forecasting**: Data for guiding meteorological models
- **Phase II potential**: Flight testing in relevant atmospheric environments
