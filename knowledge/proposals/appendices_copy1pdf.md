## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to develop AI-enabled autonomous robotic measurement platforms (mobile autonomous laboratories) for real-time adaptive experimentation in dynamic environmental systems. The project is a collaboration between BST and Brookhaven National Laboratory over a nine-month period. The work focuses on environmental monitoring applications including wildfire detection, hurricane observation, and atmospheric profiling.

## Technical Approach
- **Primary Platform:** Black Swift S0-VTOL UAS (vertical takeoff and landing fixed-wing hybrid)
- **AI Framework Development:** Real-time navigation and decision-making algorithms (led by Dr. Nathan Urban at BNL)
- **Computing Infrastructure:** Leverage local and high-performance computing resources at Brookhaven National Laboratory for data processing, analysis, and storage
- **Integration Capability:** BST's dedicated UAS integration lab enables rapid iteration and adaptation of platforms to evolving project requirements
- **Data Management:** Compliant Data Management and Sharing Plan (DMSP) to be developed at time of award negotiation

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift S0-VTOL UAS
**What it is:** A vertical takeoff and landing fixed-wing hybrid unmanned aircraft system combining VTOL deployment flexibility with fixed-wing range and endurance

**Mission Capabilities:**
- Flight Ceiling: 15,000 ft
- Max Winds Endured: 50 mph
- Flight Time: 60 minutes nominal
- Cruise Speed: 47 mph
- Max Speed: 100 mph

**Vehicle Characteristics:**
- Max Takeoff Weight: 5.75 lbs
- Wingspan: 62 inches

**Payload Capabilities:**
- Sensor Suites: Air temperature, 3D wind speed and direction, dewpoint, atmospheric pressure, laser altimeter, thermal IR sensor, lightweight EO/IR cameras or laser target designators
- Maximum Payload Weight: 100 grams

**Proposed Use:** Testing and validation platform for AI-enabled autonomous environmental monitoring missions; capable of deployment from anywhere a multirotor could operate while providing the range, endurance, and speed of fixed-wing systems
