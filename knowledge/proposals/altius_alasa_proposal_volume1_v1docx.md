## Executive Summary
Altius Space Machines, in partnership with Able Space Corporation, Black Swift Technologies, and the University of Colorado's RECUV, proposes development and demonstration of the "Boomerang" partially-reusable air-launched microsatellite launch system. The proposal includes a high-level conceptual base option and two funded options: Option A for gamma maneuver flight demonstration and Option B for comprehensive preliminary design, targeting a system capable of delivering at least 100 lbs to LEO at approximately $1M per flight.

## Technical Approach

**System Concept:**
- Air-launched, partially-reusable microsatellite launch vehicle using a carrier aircraft to deploy a rocket vehicle
- Proposes use of "Gamma Maneuver" flight demonstration (specific details redacted in outline)
- Reusable launch vehicle (RLV) first stage design
- Glideforward recovery concept
- XCOR Lynx pumps and modified engines
- Ventions Upper Stage integration

**Phase Structure:**
- **Phase I Base Option:** High-level conceptual development (Aug-Sept 2012 start)
- **Phase I Option A:** Gamma maneuver flight demonstration with subscale rocket vehicle demonstrator
- **Phase I Option B:** Comprehensive preliminary design efforts

**Comparative Positioning:**
Proposal includes analysis comparing approach versus:
- All expendable systems
- Ground-launched partially reusable systems
- Other air-launch approaches (drop methods, other launcher types)
