## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes SwiftCore, a full-stack in-house designed avionics ecosystem, to address limitations of legacy airborne ISR systems that rely on inflexible third-party autopilots. The solution enables plug-and-play payload flexibility, real-time reconfiguration, and streamlined sensor fusion to deliver high-fidelity environmental intelligence in diverse and demanding environments.

## Technical Approach
SwiftCore represents a full-stack avionics architecture designed, engineered, and manufactured entirely in-house, comprising:
- Custom autopilot hardware boards
- Integrated flight control firmware
- SwiftTab mission interface/user interface
- Modular architecture supporting multiple aircraft types, payloads, and flight profiles with a single avionics core

Key approach elements:
- Plug-and-play payload flexibility and real-time mission reconfiguration
- Streamlined sensor fusion capabilities
- Support for rapid mission customization and easier payload integration
- Secure communication workflows meeting defense-grade ISR requirements

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SwiftCore (Avionics Core)**
- Full-stack avionics ecosystem: autopilot, firmware, and mission interface integrated together
- Modular design supporting multiple UAS platforms without platform-specific constraints
- Real-time reconfiguration and payload swapping capability
- Operates effectively in extreme and dynamic environments
- Enables data streaming and sensor fusion
- Defense-grade ISR security standards

**SwiftTab**
- Mission interface/user interface component
- Enables end-to-end system integration and mission adaptability

## Use Cases & Applications
- Airborne Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (ISR) operations
- Environmental intelligence collection in extreme and dynamic environments
- Rapid mission-adaptable operations
- Defense/military applications requiring reliable autonomous flight in diverse conditions

## Effort Goals (Proposed Work)
1. Develop and deliver modular, full-stack avionics prototype including autopilot hardware, control firmware, and mission interface (SwiftTab) for integration with multiple UAS platforms
2. Conduct field testing and performance validation in operationally relevant environments to assess:
   - Autonomy capabilities
   - Data quality
   - Real-time transmission capabilities under variable mission conditions
3. Demonstrate end-to-end system integration and mission adaptability including payload swapping, data streaming, and secure communication workflows to meet defense-grade ISR requirements

## Key Competitive Claims
- **Cost vs. Performance:** Chart positions SwiftCore as achieving "low cost & high performance" compared to legacy airborne systems and commodity dronecode solutions
- **Modularity:** Single avionics core supports multiple aircraft types, payloads, and flight profiles without being constrained by platform limitations
- **Integration Speed:** Tightly integrated in-house design enables faster adaptation to new mission needs compared to systems requiring third-party autopilot integration
- **System Resilience:** Full-stack ecosystem architecture provides unmatched modularity and operational resilience
