## Executive Summary
NOAA Federal (Joe Cione) requested a proposal from Black Swift Technologies for 75 drones to expand their existing fleet to 125 units total, with a $2.25 million budget covering hardware, labor, real-time streaming capability, training, and development of pressurized launch systems and enhanced storm-penetration software for tropical cyclone research and operations.

## Technical Approach

### Core Drone Procurement
- **Fleet expansion**: Order of 75 Black Swift drones to reach total of 125 units (assuming ~25-30 existing units)
- **Budget**: $2.25 million total
- **Includes**: Hardware costs, labor, expedited non-recurring engineering (NRE) for real-time streaming, and high-priority training

### Altus Integration Challenges
- **Current issue**: Power routing from main battery to propulsion is interfering with magnetic measurements used for heading calculations
- **Proposed solution**: Send at least one Altus unit to Jack Elston for testing with Patrick to resolve interference
- **Wind measurement**: Interfaces and data recording now functional, but significant work remains on accurate wind measurement; units are single-use, limiting flight testing options
- **Alternative consideration**: Generic pitot tube wind data available but NOAA prefers more precise measurements

### Pressurized Launch System Development
- **Concept**: Replace CAD (current launch module) with small pressure vessel capable of ejecting tubes from existing launchers
- **Capability**: Enable drone deployment from various aircraft (G550, P3) at altitude
- **Status**: Proposed to add to NRE budget; Jack Elston to revisit with Nick and AOC for support and interest assessment

### Software Development for Storm Operations
- **Storm penetration**: New software needed to enable drones to penetrate eye of storm from outside rather than from within
- **Extended flight duration**: Maintain communication for extended periphery flights
- **Gliding functionality**: Already available
- **Satellite communication**: Under investigation to enable longer duration flights without reliance on P3 support

### Pressure Sensor Procurement
- **Critical component**: Medical-grade pressure sensors for wind and turbulence probes
- **Lead time**: 8 months from manufacturer
- **Cost**: $52 per unit
- **Challenge**: Jack Elston exploring alternative sensors with shorter lead times without sacrificing quality

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift Drones
- **Use case**: Tropical cyclone research and data collection
- **Quantity requested**: 75 units (to bring fleet to 125 total)
- **Capabilities**: Real-time data streaming, storm penetration capability, wind/turbulence measurement
- **Sensor integration**: Wind probes, pressure sensors, magnetic heading sensors
- **Communication**: Currently RF-based; satellite communication enhancement under development
- **Launch method**: Standard launchers plus proposed pressurized launch system

### Altus Platform
- **Integration challenge**: MHTP (magnetic heading/turbulence probe) integration problematic due to power routing interference
- **Data capabilities**: Wind measurements, magnetic heading, data recording
- **Limitations**: Single-use aircraft, limiting flight test iterations
- **Status**: Requires additional engineering and testing

## Use Cases & Applications

### Tropical Cyclone Operations
- Eye penetration missions (approaching from outside the eye wall)
- Extended periphery flights for sustained data collection
- Real-time data streaming for operational support
- Wind and turbulence characterization in extreme weather

### Multi-Aircraft Deployment
- Launch from P3 (standard platform for NOAA hurricane operations)
- Launch from G550 (pressurized aircraft capability)
- Other aircraft compatibility with pressurized launch system
