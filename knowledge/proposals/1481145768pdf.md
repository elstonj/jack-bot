## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies developed an improved, compact multi-hole probe for measuring 3D winds and static pressure from small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS). The innovation leverages 3D printing technology to create a lightweight (62g), robust sensor package that is significantly smaller and more durable than commercial alternatives, enabling deployment on high-risk missions and atmospheric research.

## Technical Approach

**Core Design:**
- Small hemispherical sensor head with 5 pressure ports
- Center hole measures total pressure (airspeed)
- Four outer ports offset by 45° and equally spaced around hemisphere to measure angle of attack and sideslip

**Sensor Suite:**
- **SM9541 sensors:** 20cm H₂O version for airspeed measurement; 10cm H₂O versions for angle of attack/sideslip resolution
- **MS5607 sensor:** High-resolution static pressure, temperature, and humidity measurements
- **Custom sensor board:** Integrates all 5 dynamic pressure sensors with MS5607
- **Electronics:** Custom design with CAN bus output for autopilot or data logger integration

**Manufacturing & Materials:**
- Entire mechanical assembly 3D printed to high accuracy
- Custom designed electronics and firmware for data collection

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Multi-hole Probe (MHP):**
- **Purpose:** Measure 3D wind vectors and static pressure from sUAS
- **Key Specifications:**
  - Dimensions: 2" × 4"
  - Weight: 62g
  - Compact hemispherical design (does not protrude from airframe)
  - Measures: airspeed, angle of attack, sideslip angle, static pressure, temperature, humidity
  - Output: CAN bus interface
- **Advantages Over Prior Art:**
  - Lighter and more compact than commercial multi-hole probes
  - Robust blunt design eliminates fragility of protruding sensors
  - Low cost enables deployment on high-loss-probability missions
  - Rapid construction and design modification capability via 3D printing
  - Integrable into most existing sUAS platforms

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Applications:**
- Volcanic plume sampling and characterization
- Atmospheric validation of weather models (local and global scale)
- Mesoscale weather prediction improvement (6-24 hour convection forecasts)
- Coherent atmospheric structure measurement: smog, wildfire smoke, chemical fires, forest humidity

**Target Organizations:**
- NASA
- NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration)
- NSF (National Science Foundation)
- UCAR/NCAR (National Center for Atmospheric Research)
- NCAR Mesoscale Predictability Experiment (MPEX)
- Commercial partners: iMET (existing commercial interest noted)

**Mission Context:**
- Harsh environment operations requiring robustness
- High-risk missions where sUAS loss is probable
- Field operations in unimproved areas
