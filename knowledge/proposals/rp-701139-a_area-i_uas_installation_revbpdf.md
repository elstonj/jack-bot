# Area-I Altius UAS Installation - WP-3D Structural Substantiation

## Document Metadata
- Type: Structural Analysis/Engineering Report
- Client/Agency: NOAA (National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration) Aircraft Operations Center
- Program/Solicitation: Area-I UAS Installation on NOAA WP-3D Orion aircraft
- Date: 1/8/2021 (Revision B; Initial Release 12/17/2019)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Altius UAS (Area-I)
- Key Personnel: Akshar Patel (Author), J. Hartberger (Reviewer)

## Executive Summary
This document provides structural substantiation for installing and launching the Altius UAS (Area-I) from the free fall chute assembly on NOAA's WP-3D Orion aircraft (tail numbers N42 and N43). The analysis evaluates structural impact of the UAS installation on affected aircraft components under emergency landing and flight load conditions, demonstrating that all hardware maintains positive design margins and is acceptable for installation.

## Technical Approach
- **Analysis Method**: Applied emergency crash and flight load factors to UAS system per FAR 25.561 equivalency using original Lockheed Aircraft Corporation certification basis loads
- **Load Cases Evaluated**:
  - Ultimate Crash Load Factors: -7.1g down, +4.2g up, ±1.9g lateral, -10g forward (parallel to longitudinal axis), 450 lb axial along free fall tube
  - Most critical: 450 lb axial launch tube load during UAS ejection
- **Structural Components Analyzed**:
  - Free fall chute tube (AL 6061-T6, 0.125" thickness, ~64" length, attached via 32 MS 20246DD6 rivets)
  - Chute end collar to T-section to skin attachments
  - Clamp ring assembly
  - Antenna installations (AVDP1785 blade and AVDP430 rod, Hascall-Denke)
  - Radio tray at Station 7

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Altius UAS (Area-I)
- **What it is**: Unmanned Aircraft System designed for deployment from manned aircraft
- **Proposed Use**: Launched from NOAA WP-3D Orion's free fall chute to obtain research data during hurricane reconnaissance missions
- **Launch Method**: Free fall deployment from aircraft interior with 450 lb axial load generation
- **Communications**: Via two prototype antennas mounted on lower radiometer cover

## Use Cases & Applications
- Hurricane reconnaissance and research data collection
- Deployment from operational NOAA research aircraft (WP-3D Orion)
- In-service on two aircraft variants (N42 and N43)

## Key Results
- **Minimum Margin of Safety**: All analyzed structures maintain positive margins; critical component (chute clamp 6061-T6 aluminum plate) shows +0.02 margin of safety under bending at 450 lb load
- **Free Fall Chute Bending Stress**: 3.1 ksi (acceptable by inspection)
- **T-Section Attachment**: Maximum stress 4.3 ksi
- **Antenna Testing**: AVDP1785 blade antenna passed 50 lb lateral pull test with no deformation or damage
- **Radio Tray (8.9 lbs)**: Loading acceptable by inspection with four NAS bolts on rail
- **Installation Torque Requirement**: Clamp bolts limited to maximum 50 in-lb torque

## Notable Details

**Installation Configuration**:
- Free fall chute assembly includes: 2x clamp bars, 1x dog bone link, 2x 3/8" pins, bolts (AN10-33-2x), threaded rods (NAS1454-5-0802), 1x turn buckle (6061-T6), fasteners (MS 21042-4, MS 24665-446)
- Cargo strap restraint tied from Altius front face to outboard seat track to prevent component ejection during deployment
- Antenna mounting per Pagnotta Engineering drawings (1894-04-200) with Hi-Lok 21-6 fasteners

**Engineering Coordination**:
- Clamp ring analysis conducted by Pagnotta Engineering (Report P2073-18A-AR-01)
- Radiometer cover structural baseline per Pagnotta Engineering (P1894-04_Radiometer_Port_Cover_Summary_01)
- Antenna certification used previous structural analysis results

**Regulatory Basis**:
- Analysis follows FAR 25.561 emergency landing requirements
- Loads reference original Lockheed Aircraft Corporation certification basis
- Wind gust loading per FAR 25.415 (65-knot gust standard)

**Design Margins**: All hardware evaluated maintains positive design margins across all load factors, enabling approval by inspection without additional testing or modifications.
