## Executive Summary
The NightFOX project is developing a UAS-based remote sensing system to observe nighttime fire conditions and characterize fire-related atmospheric properties. During the first reporting month (July 2017), the team held planning meetings, initiated procurement of the SuperSwift UAS platform, began sensor evaluation, and advanced design of CO2 and other specialized instruments for fire weather data collection and model assimilation.

## Technical Approach
The project combines UAS platform deployment with specialized remote sensing and in-situ atmospheric measurements:
- **UAS Platform**: SuperSwift being procured by CU (University of Colorado)
- **Remote Sensing Sensors**: Evaluation underway for visible, near-infrared (SWIR), and long-wave infrared (LWIR) thermal imaging; mid-infrared (mid-IR) sensors under research
- **In-Situ Instruments**: 
  - CO2 sensor in development (tunable diode laser-based with key components ordered)
  - CO sensor for evaluation
  - Coarse mode aerosol measurement options being assessed
- **Data Integration**: Focus on assimilating UAS observations into WRF-SFIRE (fire weather model) with optimization of measurement and flight strategy

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SuperSwift UAS**
- Platform selected for NightFOX remote sensing payload
- Procurement in process as of July 2017
- Intended to carry multiple sensor suites for fire observations

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Nighttime Fire Observations**: Remote sensing of active fires during darkness
- **Fire Weather Modeling**: Data assimilation into WRF-SFIRE fire weather prediction model
- **Prescribed Burn Sampling**: Project pursuing opportunities to sample prescribed burns in late FY18
- **Fire-Related Atmospheric Characterization**: CO2, CO, and aerosol measurements in fire smoke plumes
