## Key Guidance for DARPA Submissions

### Heilmeier Catechism (Primary Evaluation Framework)
The Heilmeier Catechism is the mandatory framework PMs use to evaluate all proposals. BST should explicitly address each element in abstracts/introductions:
- **H1 (State of the Art)**: Rigorously define the current state of art. Avoid "dumbing down" ideas; be ambitious rather than conservative.
- **H2 (Novel Approach)**: Clearly articulate what is new/different in your technical approach with a viable technical path. Many proposals repeat similar ideas.
- **H3 (Why Now?)**: Justify timing and urgency.
- **H4 (Who Cares?)**: Define impact and beneficiaries.

### Strategic Positioning
- Focus on **REVOLUTIONARY** over **EVOLUTIONARY** advances
- Explicitly spell out H1-H4 correspondence in proposal language so PM recognizes framework alignment

## Common Missteps to Avoid
1. **Agency-specific language**: Don't repackage proposals from other agencies (Army, DOE, etc.) without complete re-branding to DARPA context
2. **Budget unrealism**: Costs must be reasonable, rates sufficient, and proposal must "hold together across the board"
3. **Redundant solicitation language**: Don't restate language from the solicitation itself (PMs wrote it and know its contents)
4. **Giving up**: Revise based on feedback and resubmit; PM turnover occurs every 2-4 years, creating new opportunities with different priorities

## Advanced Research Concepts (ARCs)
- Lower barrier to entry vehicle for rapid contracting
- Requires only 5-page abstract format
- "Swing for the fences" on these—ambition is valued
- PMs create and write ARC topic areas, so they are personally invested in outcomes

## Submission QA Process
Before final submission, search proposal text for mandatory keywords:
- "Shall"
- "Must"
- "Always"

These terms indicate mandatory and suggested items in solicitation guidance that enable compliance verification.

## Notable Details
- Persistence in resubmission is valued; DARPA uses metaphor of "fishing"—multiple casts may yield nothing, then suddenly yield consistent results
- PM perspectives on what constitutes fundable work are variable and not always predictable
- Heilmeier framework is described as "the language that is spoken at DARPA"
