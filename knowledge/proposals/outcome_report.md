## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies completed a NOAA SBIR Phase II to develop the S0-AD, a small air-deployed fixed-wing UAS for hurricane boundary layer research. Recognizing limited commercial potential for the hurricane-specific variant, BST is now commercializing a VTOL version of the S0 funded by the Air Force as a dual-use atmospheric sampling platform to replace weather balloons and augment weather stations. This report outlines BST's commercialization strategy with partner Larta, including market research, customer identification, regulatory pathway analysis, and performance optimization.

## Technical Approach

### S0-AD Development (NOAA SBIR)
- Fixed-wing sUAS designed for air deployment from NOAA P-3 Orion "Hurricane Hunting" aircraft
- Automatically guided along eyewall boundary layer using pressure isobar feedback from Multi-Hole Probe
- Intelligent flight control algorithms reactive to sensor data to optimize data collection with minimal human intervention
- Integrated with NOAA's existing telemetry protocol to match dropsonde data collection format

### S0 VTOL Variant (Air Force funded)
- Modified version with Vertical Take-Off and Landing capability
- Designed for ground-based operations replacing traditional weather balloons
- New wing design optimized for ground operations (vs. air deployment)
- New comms package using standard meteorological data formats
- Pre-programmed flight patterns for civilian data collection missions
- Lightweight avionics using SwiftCore FMS-L (lightweight version of SwiftCore FMS)

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0-AD / S0 VTOL
- **Form Factor:** 4 lbs maximum takeoff weight, 54.6 inch wingspan, 1.8 inch diameter nose cone
- **Performance:** 
  - Ceiling: 15,000 ft AGL
  - Flight time: 60 minutes nominal
  - Cruise speed: 40 mph
  - Max speed: 100 mph
  - Max winds endured: 50 mph
- **Payload:** 100 grams capacity
- **Sensor Options:** Temperature, 3D wind speed/direction, dewpoint, atmospheric pressure, laser altimeter, thermal IR, EO/IR cameras, laser target designators

### Multi-Hole Probe (MHP)
- 5-hole probe for central atmospheric sensing
- Measures wind speed, 3D wind profiling, wind direction, temperature, pressure, humidity
- Originally developed under NASA SBIR
- Improved and adapted for S0-AD
- Already has proven commercial sales track record
- Future capability development planned for trace gas sampling

### SwiftCore FMS-L
- Lightweight, low-cost flight management system version
- Developed specifically for S0
- Key avionics for future commercialization initiatives
- Potential application: glider versions, dropsonde variants, balloon-borne sondes

### SwiftCore FMS
- Full flight management system (referenced as existing product)
- Basis for FMS-L development

### Communications Package
- Uses NOAA's existing telemetry protocol
- Compatible with dropsonde data collection format
- Enables seamless integration into NOAA and other agency atmospheric sensing operations

## Notable Details

### Market Analysis & Economics
- **Cost of Goods Sold (COGS):** $4,800
- **Proposed Price Target:** $12,000 for full system with technical support
- **Competitive Advantage vs. Weather Balloons:**
  - Current balloon sampling: 2 launches/day × $395/balloon + radiosonde cost = $1,000+/day or $300,000+/year
  - S0 VTOL: Non-recurring cost + operational flights amortized over 1 year approaches cost of single weather station installation
  - S0 advantages: Reusable, horizontal sampling reach, programmable flight patterns, geographically dispersed sampling
  - Existing limitations of alternatives: Weather balloons limited to vertical columns with lost assets; weather stations single-point measurements; radar/LiDAR limited density; satellites low spatial resolution and frequency; radiosondes not recovered

### Regulatory Considerations
- FAA restrictions: Standard UAS limited to 400 ft AGL and visual line of sight
- BST has proven track record obtaining FAA Certificates of Authorization (COAs) for higher altitudes and beyond visual line of sight
- Potential for regulatory relief to access higher altitudes for scientific research
- International regulatory environment varies; identified as critical area for market research

### Technology Commercialization Strategy
- MHP improvements from S0-AD development available for future commercial sale
- Potential to develop new dropsonde glider variant enabling targeted observation
- Potential balloon-borne sonde variant allowing rapid re-use
- Sensor package development pipeline: trace gas sampling and other applications

### Risk & Market Uncertainty
- Document explicitly acknowledges market is "fairly new and unproven"
- Notes potential US market alone may be insufficient; global market necessary for viability
- Hurricane research UAS market competitive: two other companies also developing Coyote replacement
- No assured procurement contracts despite NOAA development work
- Identified need for additional work to assess commercial viability before proceeding with further flight system development
