## Executive Summary
This is a model agreement template for allocating intellectual property rights between a small business concern (SBC) and a research institution (RI) participating in a Navy Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) program. The document establishes ownership, licensing, and commercialization rights for inventions and intellectual property developed during STTR-funded research, with provisions protecting government interests and managing confidentiality and publication rights.

## Document Structure & Key Sections

### 1. Applicability
- Agreement covers only matters relating to the specific STTR project
- If funding is awarded, SBC must provide subaward to RI in accordance with funding agreement and this Agreement
- Parties will attempt in good faith to resolve inconsistencies between this Agreement and the actual funding agreement
- Applies to all consultants, subcontractors, and individuals employed by either party for the project

### 2. Background Intellectual Property
**Definition:** Property and legal rights developed before or independent of this Agreement, including inventions, patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, and proprietary technical data/software.

**Usage Rights:**
- Background IP of SBC may be used nonexclusively and without compensation by RI for R&D activities (parties specify which IP)
- Background IP of RI may be used nonexclusively and without compensation by SBC for R&D activities (parties specify which IP)
- RI's Background IP may be used by SBC nonexclusively for commercialization of project results, subject to:
  - Reasonable necessity for practical, efficient, and competitive commercialization
  - Government rights
  - Royalty payment to RI (percentage of net sales to be specified)

### 3. Project Intellectual Property
**Definition:** Legal rights relating to inventions (including Subject Inventions per 37 CFR 401), patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, and protectable information first made or generated during STTR performance.

**Ownership Structure:**
- Ownership vests in the party whose personnel conceived or first reduced the subject matter to practice
- Jointly conceived/generated Project IP is jointly owned unless otherwise agreed
- SBC has first option to perfect rights in jointly made/generated Project IP

**Nonexclusive Use:**
- Each party may use the other's Project IP nonexclusively without compensation for R&D activities and project reports/proposals for continued funding

**Government Rights:**
- Government receives irrevocable, royalty-free, nonexclusive license for any governmental purpose in Project IP (in addition to rights under 37 CFR 401.14)

### 4. SBC Commercialization Options
**For Patentable Inventions (RI's Project IP):**
- SBC has exclusive option to license for initial period of [blank] months after invention reported
- SBC may extend option for additional [blank] months by written notice
- During extended option, RI maintains patent protection at SBC's request
- SBC reimburses RI for patenting expenses within [blank] days of invoice
- SBC may terminate option at will
- If exercised, SBC enters negotiations for license including:
  - Reasonable royalties on sales
  - Reimbursement of RI's patent maintenance expenses (offset against up to [blank] of accrued royalties)
  - Reasonable commercialization milestones/minimum royalties (for exclusive license)

**For Non-Patentable Project IP (RI's Project IP):**
- SBC has exclusive option for license extending until [blank] months following completion of relevant STTR phase
- Exercise requires written notice and good faith negotiation
- License terms include:
  - Reasonable royalties on sales
  - Reasonable commercialization milestones/minimum royalties (for exclusive license)

**Royalty Stacking:**
- If multiple royalties would apply to a single product/service, parties negotiate in good faith to ameliorate commercial impact through reasonable royalty discount or cap

### 5. Follow-On Research/Development
- All follow-on work (licenses, contracts, subcontracts, sublicenses) must implement Project IP rights provisions
- Ensures parties and Government retain rights in all future research, development, or commercialization

### 6. Confidentiality & Publication
- Background IP and Project IP disclosed in confidence must be held in confidence by receiving party
- Confidentiality does not apply to information that becomes public without breach, comes from independent sources, or is independently developed

**Publication Rights:**
- Either party may publish results subject to:
  - Right of refusal for other party
  - [Blank] day review period for comments
  - Up to [blank] additional days deferral for patent application preparation (at reviewing party's request)

### 7. Liability
**Warranties:**
- Each party disclaims all warranties (express or implied) regarding information, results, designs, prototypes, products, or processes derived from it, including warranties of merchantability, fitness for particular purpose, and freedom from infringement

**Indemnification:**
- SBC indemnifies RI against claims arising from SBC's commercialization
- Both parties indemnify Government against claims arising from commercialization

### 8. Termination
- Either party may terminate with [blank] days written notice
- Either party may terminate for non-compliance
- Upon termination, each party responsible for its share of costs through effective termination date and related termination costs
- Confidentiality and non-disclosure obligations survive termination

## Notable Details
- This is a **template/model form** with multiple blank fields to be completed by parties
- Specific terms (option periods, royalty percentages, review periods, notification periods) are intentionally left blank for customization
- Agreement balances SBC commercialization interests with RI's IP ownership and Government's patent rights
- Strong emphasis on good faith negotiation and resolution of inconsistencies
- Designed for Navy STTR program requirements (references 37 CFR 401 patent regulations)
- Includes provisions for both patentable and non-patentable inventions
- Government retains royalty-free license for governmental purposes
- Reflects standard STTR program structure with small business as prime contractor and research institution as partner
