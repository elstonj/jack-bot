# GeoFaMoS Study Budget Narrative - Black Swift Technologies

## Document Metadata
- Type: Budget narrative (grant application support document)
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: ROSES A.25 (Research Opportunities in Space and Earth Sciences)
- Date: June 20, 2024
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 UAS Flight System
- Key Personnel: Dr. Maciej Stachura, Dr. Jack Elston, unnamed Engineer (Gas Sampler)

## Executive Summary
This budget narrative details Black Swift Technologies' labor, travel, and equipment costs for a three-year NASA ROSES A.25 project (GeoFaMoS Study) involving seismic geo-gas emissions estimation and UAS flight operations. The project requires deployment of the BST S2 UAS system to a California field site with multiple on-site flights across Years 1 and 2. Total requested budget: $126,020.00.

## Technical Approach
The project employs the BST S2 UAS Flight System for field operations at Pepperwood Reserve in Sonoma, California (used as budgeting reference location; actual site TBD). Two primary technical tasks are planned:
1. **Seismic geo-gas emissions estimation and flight control engineering** (Dr. Maciej Stachura)
2. **Electrical engineering, firmware design and programming** (Dr. Jack Elston)
3. **Gas sampler design/development** (dedicated engineer, Year 1 only)

Field deployments: 1 on-site flight in Year 1; 2 on-site flights in Year 2; no field work Year 3.

## Products & Capabilities Described

### BST S2 UAS Flight System
- Primary equipment platform for field operations
- Will be shipped to/from California site for deployments
- Includes shipping costs in travel budget
- Implies capability for gas sampling and emissions measurement

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Seismic geo-gas emissions monitoring**: Detection and measurement of gas emissions in geologically active regions
- **Field deployment capability**: Multi-year, multi-site operations with equipment mobilization and on-site flight operations
- **Application location**: Volcanic/geothermal regions (Pepperwood Reserve, Sonoma County reference suggests potential geothermal monitoring)
