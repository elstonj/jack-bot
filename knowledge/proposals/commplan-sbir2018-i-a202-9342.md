## Executive Summary
This commercialization plan outlines BST's strategy to bring autonomous terrain-following and obstacle-avoidance technology to market following Phase II SBIR completion. The system will enable safe low-altitude UAS operations for commercial and government customers, targeting precision agriculture, infrastructure inspection, and environmental monitoring markets with projected Year 1-3 revenues of $300K to $1.25M and 40%+ profit margins.

## Technical Approach
The SBIR develops onboard systems with selected algorithms for terrain and obstacle avoidance simulation and flight testing. The technology provides:
- Autonomous navigation in difficult terrain
- Safe low-altitude operation near vegetation, buildings, power lines, and other obstacles
- Active sensing from the UAS platform
- Beyond visual line of sight (BVLOS) autonomous mission execution
- Improved take-off and landing capabilities

Key capability: addresses limitations of standard Digital Terrain Models (100m accuracy insufficient for safe navigation in irregular terrain).

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Terrain Following & Obstacle Avoidance System
- **What it is**: Integrated autonomous flight system with terrain following and collision avoidance
- **Use case**: Integration with most UAS platforms to provide advanced onboard warnings and automatic navigation
- **Proposed adoption**: Early releases to existing commercial customers during Phase II; integration into BST platforms and third-party UAS
- **Benefit over competitors**: First-of-kind commercial availability; currently no competing small UAS systems with these capabilities integrated

### BST S2 Aircraft
- Fixed-wing platform with extended flight time advantage over multicopters
- Can cover 80 miles in single 2-hour flight
- Benefits from active sensing for identifying safe landing zones
- Planned as primary platform for technology integration

## Use Cases & Applications

### Commercial Markets (Primary Focus)
1. **Precision Agriculture** (Target: $4.42B [2017] → $9.53B [2023], CAGR 13.38%)
   - Crop health and soil moisture mapping
   - Lower altitude operation (30m AGL vs. current ~100m) improves sensor accuracy
   - Examples: radiometer-based soil moisture mapping (prior BST Phase II SBIR work)
   - Autonomous canopy-hugging capabilities for EO applications

2. **Infrastructure Inspection** (Target: $1.48B [2018] → $3.38B [2023], CAGR 17.93%)
   - Pipeline monitoring
   - Power transmission line inspection (visible light + infrared cameras)

   - Oil & gas methane leak detection (requires lower altitude for accuracy)
   - Benefits from autonomous BVLOS for cost efficiency

## Competitive Landscape

### Current Market Position
- **No direct competitors identified**: Fundamental capabilities (autonomous flight, terrain following, collision avoidance) not currently commercially available for small UAS
- Research exists (LiDAR, laser altimeter, vision systems, radar) but not integrated into any commercial small UAS offering
- **First-mover advantage** provides significant market opportunity

### Barriers to Entry
- **Development cost & timeline**: 2–5 years of development effort at >$1 million investment required
- **Technical complexity**: Integration of multiple sensor and algorithm systems
- **Market validation**: Requires extensive end-user collaboration during development and demonstration

### Competition Risk & Mitigation
- Risk: Larger UAS manufacturers could invest heavily and bring competing product to market
- Mitigation: BST first-mover advantage, established reputation for quality at fair price among technical early adopters, expected first-mover market share capture
- Impact if competition emerges: Potential earlier downward price pressure but technology remains profitable

## Intellectual Property Protection
- **Strategy**: Trade secrets protection rather than patents
- **Rationale**: Patents would disclose technology intricacies to market and require BST to defend patents
- **Implementation**: Non-disclosure agreements with potential customers for sensitive material discussions
