## Executive Summary
This is the official NOAA SBIR Phase I solicitation for FY2018, opening October 18, 2017 and closing January 31, 2018. The solicitation invites small businesses to submit research proposals across multiple topic areas aligned with NOAA's mission in oceanographic and atmospheric science. Phase I awards are up to $120,000 for up to 6 months of feasibility research. This document establishes eligibility requirements, evaluation criteria, technical topics, and submission procedures for Phase I proposals.

## Key Program Structure

**Three-Phase Program:**
- **Phase I:** Feasibility research; awards up to $120,000; up to 6 months performance
- **Phase II:** R&D and prototype development; awards up to $400,000; up to 24 months performance; followed by 1-year commercialization activity period
- **Phase III:** Commercialization using private/non-SBIR government funding

## Eligibility Requirements
- Offeror must qualify as a Small Business Concern (SBC) at time of award and throughout performance
- Phase I: Minimum 2/3 of research/analytical effort performed by awardee; Phase II: minimum 1/2
- Principal Investigator (PI)/Project Manager (PM) must have primary employment (>50% of time) with SBC at award and during project
- All work must be performed in the United States (50 states, US territories and possessions, Puerto Rico, DC, Marshall Islands, Micronesia, Palau)
- Waivers for foreign work require written justification submitted to NOAA CO at least 14 calendar days before solicitation closing
- Phase I to Phase II Transition Rate Benchmark: 0.25 minimum rate applies only to companies with >20 Phase I awards in past 5 years
- PI/PM cannot work full-time for another organization
- Multiple SBIR awards before must meet commercialization benchmarks per SBA registry

## Research Topics (Topic 8.0 Overview)

**8.1 Integrated Earth System Processes and Predictions**
- Radio occultation from recoverable air balloons for weather applications
- Determining timing and location of fish spawning
- Calibration of low-cost air quality sensors

**8.2 Environmental Observations** (largest topic area)
Includes 13 subtopics:
- Portable, fast, intelligent phytoplankton species-identifier and counter
- Bottom Feeder - benthic data provider
- Automating bearing/distance measurements in Big-Eye binoculars
- Improving cetacean tag attachment systems
- Low-cost mooring location beacons for coastal applications
- Next generation marine visibility (fog) sensors
- Autonomous mapping of hypoxic zones in Gulf of Mexico
- Under keel clearance management for precision navigation
- Open water surface ROV for bathymetry
- Next generation national water level observation stations
- Clean energy sources for long-term NOAA observation networks
- Inexpensive, novel weather observing systems
- **Cost-effective air-deployed unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) for turbulent environments** (subtopic 8.2.13)

**8.3 Decision Science, Risk Assessment and Risk Communication**
- Coral restoration outplanting
- Automated tools for detecting aquaculture entanglement risks
- "Permit Wizard" software for permit application assistance
- Location-specific threat tracking tools
- Low-cost, high-nutrition plant-based feeds for finfish aquaculture
- Disease monitoring tools for marine aquaculture

**8.4 SBIR - Technology Transfer (SBIR-TT)**
- Miniature open path CRDS (Cavity Ring-Down Spectroscopy) instrument

## Evaluation & Selection Process

**Phase I Screening Criteria:**
- Responsiveness to specific subtopic
- Technical and scientific merit
- Potential for commercial success
- Quality of performance capability

**Phase I Evaluation Criteria (Section 4.3):**
- Scientific and technical merit
- Commercial potential
- Company capabilities and qualifications

**Phase II Evaluation Criteria (Section 4.4):**
- Scientific and technical quality
- Commercial potential
- Available funds
- Consideration of possible research duplication
- Importance relative to NOAA mission needs

Final Phase II recommendations made by NOAA Technology Partnerships Committee (TPC) to Contracting Officer.

## Proposal Requirements

**Phase I Proposal Limitations:**
- Limited to 26 pages
- Must include: Cover Sheet, Data Collection Requirement, Project Summary, Technical Content
- Must respond directly to specific subtopic described in Section 8

**Phase I Budget:**
- Up to $120,000 total
- Budget proposal form required
- Budget instructions provided (Section 3.6, 9.4)

**Required Certifications:**
- Size, ownership, and SBIR program requirements
- Fraud, Waste and Abuse training completion with signed certification
- If human subjects involved: IRB approval requirements and FWA documentation
- If vertebrate animals involved: IACUC approval requirements
- Life cycle certification

**Data & Documentation:**
- Proposals must include data collection requirement (Section 3.3.2)
- Scientific and technical information sources listed in Section 7
- Contact for questions: Joan Clarkston (Contracting Officer) at joan.e.clarkston@noaa.gov
- Questions deadline: December 5, 2017, 2:00 p.m. Eastern

## Program Priorities

**Manufacturing-Related Priority (EO 13329):**
- NOAA gives high priority to manufacturing-related R&D where feasible
- Manufacturing-related defined as: manufacturing processes, equipment, systems, workforce skills and protection
- Does not interfere with core selection criteria

**Energy Efficiency & Renewable Energy Priority (EISA 2007):**
- NOAA gives high priority to energy efficiency or renewable energy system R&D
- Does not interfere with core selection criteria of scientific/technical merit and commercial success potential

## Intellectual Property & Data Rights

**Patents & Inventions:**
- Awardee retains rights to inventions developed under SBIR funding
- Awardees must report inventions per SBIR requirements
- Small business retains proprietary information rights

**Data Rights:**
- Government receives license to SBIR technical data (all data generated during award)
- Awardee retains substantial rights in most cases
- Detailed rights framework outlined in Section 5.5

**Copyrights:**
- Subject to applicable regulations and funding agreement terms
