## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is developing a small UAS equipped with L-band radiometry and auxiliary sensors to measure volumetric soil moisture for the purpose of calculating soil integrity (cone index) at airfields. Milestone 4 focused on soil integrity algorithm development, with volumetric soil moisture retrieval verified against in situ data. The team has completed Milestones 3 and 4, with local field testing (Milestone 5) preparation underway at two Colorado test sites.

## Technical Approach

**System Architecture:**
- E2 quadrotor UAS platform modified with integrated sensor suite
- L-band antenna array (upper element removed during design refinement)
- Multi-sensor integration: front-end processor, back-end processor, voltage regulators, SDI-to-USB converter
- Upward-facing NDVI sensor and downward-facing NDVI/thermal sensor
- Real-time surface temperature and NDVI calculation capability

**Algorithm Development:**
1. **Volumetric Soil Moisture Retrieval:** Algorithm converts antenna temperature measurements at sampling grids into volumetric soil moisture maps
2. **Gravimetric Conversion:** Remotely sensed volumetric soil moisture converted to gravimetric soil moisture using dry soil density (texture-dependent)
3. **Soil Classification:** Dual classification approach—Unified Soil Classification System (USCS) for soil integrity calculation; USDA soil texture classification for soil moisture retrieval; empirically derived lookup tables for conversion
4. **Soil Strength Metrics:** Rating Cone Index (RCI) and California Bearing Ratio (CBR) calculated by soil texture type and gravimetric soil moisture

**Bench Testing Results:**
- Custom HDL generation and integration for coherence calculation
- Testing with ambient temperature and liquid nitrogen (LN2) validated expected offset, phase imbalance, and sensitivity

- Integration of autopilot with back-end processor via defined data flows

**L-Band Radiometer System**
- Existing L-band sensing instrument modified for this application
- Measures antenna/brightness temperature for soil moisture retrieval
- Remotely sensed soil moisture shows good agreement with in situ data
- Refined radiometer design with auxiliary sensors

**Auxiliary Sensors**
- Surface temperature sensor
- NDVI (normalized difference vegetation index) sensors (upward and downward facing)
- Enables real-time soil moisture calculation during flight

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Application:** Airfield runway integrity assessment
- U.S. Air Force requirement for soil integrity data at airbases
- Cone index (soil bearing capacity) calculation to support aircraft operations planning

**Test Locations (Milestone 5):**
1. **Sunny Slope Sod Farm, Longmont, CO** — Private, subcontracted land; Part 107 operations
2. **McIntosh Lake, Longmont, CO** — Public open space; mitigations planned for public walking paths

**Operational Requirements:**
- Data resolution and accuracy specifications defined by DAF customer
- Testing across variety of soil types, soil moisture conditions, and vegetation cover types
- In situ cone index measurements collected for validation and algorithm refinement
