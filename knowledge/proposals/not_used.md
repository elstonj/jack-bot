## Executive Summary
BST proposed transitioning an L-band sUAS (small unmanned aerial system) sensor system to Army field operations, with plans to integrate additional sensing technologies including P-band radiometric and synthetic aperture radar (SAR) capabilities for enhanced soil moisture and surface characterization.

## Technical Approach
- **Primary System**: L-band sUAS sensor system being transitioned to DoD field operations
- **P-band Radiometric Sensor**: Under development; provides dual-band soil moisture sensing capability with penetration to ~20 cm depth
- **P-band SAR**: Compact synthetic aperture radar for surface soil roughness characterization and potentially surface topography mapping
- **Integration Strategy**: Additional sensing technologies to be incorporated in preparation for field demonstrations

## Products & Capabilities Described

### L-band sUAS Sensor System
- Primary sensor system for Army field operations transition
- Mounted on small unmanned aerial platform
- Existing capability being adapted for DoD use

### P-band Radiometric Sensor
- Dual-band soil moisture sensing (L-band + P-band)
- Soil penetration depth: ~20 cm
- Status: Under development

### P-band Synthetic Aperture Radar
- Characterizes surface soil roughness
- Potential topography mapping capability
- Form factor: Compact design for sUAS integration
- Status: Under development

## Use Cases & Applications

- Surface characterization (roughness, topography)
