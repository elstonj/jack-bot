## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to develop a health monitoring and fault detection system for unmanned aircraft systems (UAS) operating in hazardous environments. The system will integrate subsystem monitoring, machine learning algorithms, and an onboard network architecture to enable autonomous operation of scientific UAS platforms during demanding atmospheric science campaigns. This capability directly supports NASA's need for sustainable, integrated measurement systems on airborne platforms.

## Technical Approach
The proposal addresses the design of:
1. **Subsystem Monitoring** - Real-time monitoring of aircraft and payload systems
2. **Machine Learning Algorithms for Fault Detection** - Algorithms to detect anomalies and predict failures before they occur
3. **Monitoring Node** - Hardware/software integration point for sensor data collection and processing
4. **On-Board Network and Autopilot/Operator Interfaces** - Communication architecture enabling autonomous and remote-operated flight with health awareness

The work plan includes:
- Preliminary work on critical subsystem identification
- Machine learning algorithm development for fault detection
- Monitoring node design and integration
- Onboard network architecture design
- Full solution specification

## Context: NASA SBIR Topic S1.08 Requirements

The proposal responds to NASA's solicitation for in-situ sensors and sensor systems for Earth science, specifically addressing:

**Desired sensor and platform capabilities:**
- Aerosol and cloud particle measurements (0.01-200 micron range)
- Phase discrimination (liquid vs. ice) in supercooled and mixed-phase clouds
- Temperature, humidity, and wind measurements
- Water vapor and water content measurements
- Remote/unattended operation with minimum size, weight, power
- Sustainable measurement systems for NASA research programs

**Additional context from 2017 funded SBIRs cited:**
The document includes abstracts of four competing 2017-funded proposals in this topic area:
1. **Ammonia Reactor (NHxR)** by BRI - Gas-phase and particle-phase ammonia measurement via CEAS
2. **Luminescent pH Sensors for Ocean Water** by Intelligent Optical Systems - Ocean acidification monitoring
3. **Portable Atmospheric Scanning LIDAR (PASL)** by Physical Optics Corporation - Wind, temperature, humidity in 3D with 10 km range
4. **Compact CO2 Instrumentation** by Southwest Sciences - Diode laser CO2 measurement for small aerial platforms (<1L, <2W, ~1kg)

These examples demonstrate the scope of sensor innovation NASA was seeking alongside BST's health monitoring system proposal.

## Assessment Notes

This document represents BST's Phase I proposal strategy: leveraging their existing SwiftCore autopilot and UAS platform capabilities to address a NASA Earth science need (health monitoring for autonomous aircraft in hazardous conditions). The proposal emphasizes the team's proven track record in severe weather UAS operations, regulatory compliance, and integration of scientific payloads on unmanned platforms. The proposal appears focused on enabling safer, more autonomous operation of sensor platforms rather than developing specific atmospheric sensors themselves.
