## Executive Summary
This is a mandatory Air Force SBIR/STTR compliance certification form that all Phase I and Phase II awardees must complete at specified contract milestones. The form verifies contractor eligibility, work performance requirements, ownership status, and regulatory compliance. Submission is a condition of payment.

## Document Purpose
This is a boilerplate federal compliance document, not a technical proposal or capability brief. It serves as a template/checklist for SBIR/STTR contractors to certify they meet program requirements before receiving payment.

## Certification Requirements Covered

**Eligibility & Ownership:**
- Small business concern ownership and control (13 CFR 121.702)
- Venture capital/private equity ownership restrictions
- Principal investigator spending >50% time with prime contractor

**Work Performance:**
- SBIR Phase I: Prime contractor performs minimum 2/3 of work
- SBIR Phase II: Prime contractor performs minimum 1/2 of work
- STTR Phase I & II: Prime contractor minimum 40%, research partner minimum 30%
- Work performed in the United States (or approved deviation)
- Work performed at prime contractor facilities with prime contractor employees

**Duplicate Funding:**
- Verification that work has not been funded by another Federal grant, contract, or agreement
- Requirement to immediately notify contracting officer if duplicate funding exists

**Administrative Requirements:**
- Authorized officer signature required
- Certifier understands penalties for false information (criminal prosecution, False Claims Act liability, suspension/debarment, treble damages)

## Notable Details
- Form explicitly warns of severe penalties for misrepresentation, including potential criminal prosecution under 18 USC 1001 and civil liability under the False Claims Act
- Contractor certifies information "as of the date of proposal submission"
- Government retains right to pursue remedies regardless of prior disclosures
- Document emphasizes Air Force's reliance on accurate contractor representations for proper program administration
