- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None mentioned in this document
- **Key Personnel:** Not applicable (this is a solicitation document, not a BST proposal)

## Executive Summary
This is the NASA FY2019 SBIR/STTR General Solicitation document that opens application opportunities for small business concerns to submit Phase I proposals addressing NASA's research and technology needs. The solicitation emphasizes new lunar payload opportunities through Commercial Lunar Payload Services (CLPS) and provides updated submission procedures through the modernized Electronic Handbook (EHB). Phase I proposals were due March 29, 2019, with approximately 24% of SBIR and 35% of STTR Phase I submissions historically receiving awards.

## Key Program Parameters

### Funding Structure
- **Phase I:** Maximum $125,000 (SBIR/STTR); 6 months performance (SBIR), 13 months (STTR)
- **Phase II:** Maximum $750,000 (SBIR/STTR); 24 months maximum performance
- **Post-Phase II Options:** Phase II Extended (Phase II-E) and Civilian Commercialization Readiness Pilot Program (CCRPP) with $500K-$1M matching requirements

### Historical Success Rates
- SBIR Phase I award rate: ~24%
- STTR Phase I award rate: ~35%
- Phase I to Phase II conversion: ~41% of completed Phase I projects

### Award Limits Per Offeror
- Maximum 10 proposals per program (20 total) per firm per solicitation
- NASA does not plan to award more than 5 SBIR + 2 STTR contracts (7 total) to any single offeror

## Notable Initiatives

### Moon to Mars Campaign & Lunar Payloads
18 SBIR/STTR subtopics explicitly encourage development of lunar payloads for delivery via CLPS contracts at no cost to developers. Focus areas include:
- In situ resource utilization
- Thermal management
- Energy storage
- Rad-hard high performance computing
- Cryogenic fluid management
- Lunar sensors
- Solar arrays
- Space vehicle swarm coordination

CLPS companies selected by NASA in December 2018 are expected to provide payload user guides with size, power, interface, and constraint specifications.

### EHB Modernization
Second year of modernized Electronic Handbook submission system with continued refinements for user experience and efficiency.

### I-Corps Program
Partnership with National Science Foundation (NSF) to offer Innovation Corps training:
- SBIR: Shortened version (30 interviews); up to $10,000 funding
- STTR: Full version (100 interviews); up to $25,000 funding
- Approximately 42 total grants anticipated for SBIR/STTR Phase I contractors

### Civilian Commercialization Readiness Pilot Program (CCRPP)
Returns for FY2019 with focus on commercialization infusion (not just technology maturation). External investment matching of $500K-$1M available.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Small Business Concern (SBC) Definition
- Must qualify per SBA definitions at http://sbir.nasa.gov/content/nasa-sbirsttr-program-definitions
- Socially/economically disadvantaged and women-owned SBCs particularly encouraged

### Place of Performance
- R&D must be performed in United States
- Rare exceptions for unavailable supplies/materials require written Contracting Officer approval
- Cannot work with countries on NASA's Designated Countries List

### Principal Investigator (PI) Requirements
- **SBIR:** PI must be primarily employed with the SBC (>50% of total time including all concurrent employment)
- **STTR:** PI must be primarily employed with SBC or Research Institution (>50%)
- Full-time workweek = 40 hours nominally; 19.9+ hours elsewhere constitutes conflict
- Primary employment precludes full-time employment elsewhere
- Co-PIs not allowed
- PI substitution requires NASA approval

### Transition Rate Benchmark
- Applies to Phase I applicants with >20 Phase I awards in past 5 fiscal years
- Companies failing benchmark on June 1, 2018 ineligible for Phase I awards June 1, 2018 - May 31, 2019
- Check status at https://www.sbir.gov/faqs/performance-benchmarks

### Venture Capital Restrictions
- Small businesses owned in majority by multiple venture capital companies, hedge funds, or private equity firms are ineligible

### Joint Ventures & Partnerships
- Permitted if entity qualifies as SBC
- Must include workload distribution statement and copy/summary of agreement

### Registration Requirements
- SBA Company Registry registration required before submission with unique SBC Control ID
- System for Award Management (SAM) registration required before contract award
  - NAICS codes: 541713 or 541715
  - DUNS number and CAGE code required
  - Recommended: List "All Awards" as purpose

### Certifications Required
- FAR certifications via SAM (https://www.acquisition.gov/browsefar)
- VETS-4212 employment reporting compliance for contracts ≥$100,000
- Firm Certifications form (Yes/No attestations)
- NASA-specific clauses:
  - 1852.203-71: Whistleblower rights notification
  - 1852.225-72: Restriction on China participation representation

## NASA Technology Available (TAV)

### Patent Licensing
- NASA portfolio contains 1,400+ available patents
- Non-exclusive, royalty-free evaluation licenses available for SBIR/STTR use
- License application via NASA Automated Licensing System (ATLAS) after patent identification
- Successful awardees may negotiate non-exclusive or exclusive commercialization licenses per 37 CFR Part 404
- Inventor knowledge transfer available (non-exclusive basis) via Licensing Executives at NASA field centers; may require NDA and reimbursement

### Software Usage
- NASA Software Catalog contains 1,000+ available codes/tools
- Non-exclusive, royalty-free Software Usage Agreements (SUA) required
- Clause 48 C.F.R. 1852.227-88 applies
- SUA requested from appropriate NASA Center Software Release Authority after award

### Important Notes
- Use of TAV is optional and not a selection factor
- Technologies provided "as is" with no guarantee of infusion or commercial viability
- Additional innovation by private sector typically required for commercialization

## Technical and Business Assistance

**Available for Phase II Awards Only**
- Maximum $5,000 per year per Phase II project (does not count toward $750K award maximum)
- Services include: market research, IP protection, regulatory planning, manufacturing plans, technical problem-solving, access to non-NASA scientist/engineer networks
- Requires vendor name/contact, detailed service description, and price quote with DUNS number
- Contracting Officer approval required
- Description of services and benefits must be deliverable of Phase II contract

## Proposal Requirements

### Phase I Proposal Format
- **Maximum:** 19 pages (standard 8.5" x 11")
- **Margins:** 1.0 inch
- **Font:** Minimum 10-point
- **Numbering:** Consecutive page numbers at bottom
- **Header:** Firm name, proposal number, project title
- **Footer:** Page number and proprietary markings

### Prohibited Content
- No classified information accepted
- System studies, market research, routine engineering, or modifications of existing products without substantive innovation not funded

### Proposal Package Contents
1. Contact Information
2. Certifications (electronically endorsed)
3. Proposal Summary (non-proprietary)
4. Budget (including facility/subcontractor availability letters)
5. Technical Proposal (10-part structure, ≤19 pages with graphics/TOC)
6. Research Agreement (STTR only)
7. Briefing Chart (non-proprietary)
8. NASA Evaluation License Application (if TAV proposed)
9. I-Corps Opt-In Form

**Note:** General interest letters or funding support commitments not required/desired and not considered in review.

## Proprietary Information Protection
- Marked proprietary information treated confidentially per law
- Contact Information, Proposal Summary, and Briefing Chart publicly releasable
- Other proposal data protected from FOIA disclosure to extent permitted by law
- Unsuccessful proposals remain applicant property; government retains copies

## Additional Requirements

### Human/Animal Subjects
- Approved NASA Review Board protocol required
- Not allowed for Phase I contracts due to complexity of approval process
- Must disclose on Proposal Certifications form
- Reference 14 CFR 1230 and 1232

### HSPD-12 Compliance
- Required if contract requires 6+ consecutive months access to federally-controlled facilities or federal information systems
- Implements Personal Identity Verification (PIV) per FIPS PUB 201
- See FAR clause 52.204-9

### Software Development Standards
- Projects involving software development may require NASA Procedural Requirements (NPR) 7150.2A compliance
- Available at http://nodis3.gsfc.nasa.gov/displayDir.cfm?t=NPR&c=7150&s=2

### Mentor-Protégé Program
- Separate award fee opportunity for NASA contractors assisting Phase II SBIR/STTR contractors
- Evaluation based on technology transfer and business development raising protégé Technology Readiness Level (TRL)
- Info: http://www.osbp.nasa.gov/mpp/index.html

## Notable Policy Items

### Travel Restrictions
- Highly discouraged for Phase I due to limited budget and short performance period
- Videoconference/teleconference preferred
- If travel necessary, rationale required in budget with Contracting Officer approval

### False Statements Penalty
- Knowing false statements may constitute felony under 18 U.S.C. §1001
- Punishable by fine and up to 5 years imprisonment
- NASA OIG has full access to all proposals
- Multiple reporting channels available for fraud/waste/abuse

### Intellectual Property
- Patents: Invention reporting, title election, and patent application filing required per standard IP clauses
- Copyrights: Standard copyright provisions apply
- Data rights: Government retains certain data rights per standard SBIR clauses
