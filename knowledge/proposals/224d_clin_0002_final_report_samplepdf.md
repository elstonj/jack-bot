## Document Summary
This document is a **sample questionnaire template** for AFWERX SBIR Phase I final reports (CLIN 0002). It is **not a BST proposal, report, or capability document**. Rather, it is a template that BST (or any Phase I awardee) would use to structure responses when submitting their final report to AFWERX.

The document does not contain information about Black Swift Technologies' products, solutions, capabilities, or project outcomes. It is purely an administrative/procedural template distributed by the Air Force AFWERX program management team.

## What This Document Provides
A comprehensive set of reporting requirements organized into the following categories:

### Reporting Categories:
1. **Company and Team** – Founder background, personnel, subcontractors, funding history
2. **Your Solution and Overall Feasibility** – Solution summary, Phase I goals, Phase II/III plans
3. **Feasibility of Commercialization** – Customer identification, use cases, competitive landscape, market analysis
4. **Technical Feasibility** – Enabling technologies, technical approach, TRL assessment, integration requirements, risk mitigation
5. **Technical Feasibility – Test and Evaluation Strategy** – Test strategy, government resource utilization, milestones
6. **Regulatory Environment** – Legal/regulatory considerations, compliance requirements
7. **Feasibility of Follow-On Investment** – Government and commercial funding commitments
8. **DAF Engagement** – Customer discovery approach, stakeholder engagement, customer memorandums
9. **Phase 1 Lessons Learned** – Market understanding, DAF organizational insights, barriers encountered
10. **Feedback for AFWERX PM Team** – Program management rating and suggestions
11. **Next Steps** – Phase II intentions and success factors
12. **Support Documentation** – Required and optional supporting materials

## Notable Details
- Minimum character counts are specified for most narrative responses (ranging from 100 to 800 characters)
- Report must be submitted via "Deliverables Google Form" (not as email attachment)
- Document explicitly states: "DO NOT BEGIN DEVELOPING YOUR RESPONSES until the final questionnaire is distributed by the Phase I Team"
- Marked "SAMPLE ONLY" throughout – indicates this is an exemplar, not the final questionnaire
- Addresses dual-use technology commercialization (commercial vs. defense variants)
- Emphasizes TRL (Technology Readiness Level) assessment and IOC/FOC (Initial/Full Operational Capability) timelines
- Requires evidence of customer support via memorandum from DoD stakeholder
