## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed to develop capabilities enabling Beyond Line-of-Sight (BLOS) operations in the National Airspace System by providing accurate position information in GPS-denied environments. The solution combines an augmented sensor suite with machine learning to allow unmanned aircraft to maintain accurate localization and communicate position to UAS Service Suppliers without GPS, addressing FAA performance authorization requirements for BLOS flights.

## Technical Approach
- **Problem Focus**: BLOS operations authorization requires improved sensing capabilities, particularly position awareness, to meet FAA performance metrics in navigation, communications, intent sharing, collision avoidance, and near real-time FAA connectivity
- **Solution Architecture**: 
  - Augmented sensor suite (specific sensors not detailed in this summary)
  - Machine learning capabilities for position estimation
  - Multi-source localization without GPS dependence
  - GPS spoofing/jamming resilience
- **Regulatory Framework**: System designed to operate within UTM (Unmanned Traffic Management) rules and support FAA case-by-case authorization pathway that BST believes will eventually lead to streamlined approvals as standard capability packages emerge

## Products & Capabilities Described
**GPS-Denied Localization System** (unnamed in this summary):
- Primary capability: Accurate position determination without GPS
- Operational context: BLOS flights requiring real-time position reporting to UAS Service Suppliers
- Resilience: Functional during GPS jamming or spoofing events
- Integration: Maintains compliance with UTM/FAA requirements for BLOS authorization

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Government**: Extended-range UAS operations requiring position accuracy in denied-GPS environments
- **Commercial**:
  - Coastline monitoring
  - Powerline inspection
  - Pipeline inspection
  - Extended-distance mapping operations
- **Market Drivers**: Anticipated acceleration of commercial UAS adoption contingent on BLOS capability expansion
