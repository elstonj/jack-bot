## Executive Summary
This document outlines BST's plan to integrate a magnetometer and hydrophone into the SL UAS prototype during Phase 1, with laboratory testing at BST and CRT facilities to validate sensor performance, data fusion algorithms, and operational reliability for Navy applications.

## Technical Approach
- **Integration Location**: Laboratory environments at BST and CRT
- **Sensor Integration**: Magnetometer and hydrophone integrated into SL UAS prototype
- **Testing Validation**: 
  - Magnetometer: Compliance with noise spectral density requirements
  - Hydrophone: Calibration focusing on frequency response and deployment reliability
- **Data Fusion**: Algorithms developed and tested using simulated mission scenarios to evaluate combined magnetic and acoustic sensing capabilities
- **Risk Mitigation**: Advanced filtering techniques and sensor shielding to address sensor interference

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SL UAS
- Unmanned aerial system being adapted to carry magnetometer and hydrophone sensors
- Features simplified avionics interface allowing rapid adaptation/reconfiguration

### BST Avionics
- Successfully adapted in hours to collect QuSpin magnetometer data
- Demonstrated simplified interface enabling rapid sensor integration

### Magnetometer (QuSpin partnership)
- High-sensitivity magnetic sensor
- Noise spectral density requirements to be met
- QuSpin providing expertise in high-sensitivity magnetic sensor development

### Hydrophone
- Acoustic sensing capability
- CRT responsible for calibration, frequency response characterization, and deployment reliability
- To be integrated with magnetometer for dual-sensor capabilities

## Use Cases & Applications

  - Used to de-risk proposed Phase 1 work and provide preliminary validation

## Key Achievements

## Notable Details
