## Executive Summary
This document provides NASA contractors (including BST) with guidelines and templates for completing New Technology Summary Reports (NTSR) and New Technology Reports (NTR) through NASA's Electronic Handbook (EHB) and e-NTR website. It clarifies when and how to report new technologies developed under NASA contracts, with separate templates for cases with and without new technology to disclose.

## Document Purpose & Context
This is a procedural guidance document issued to NASA SBIR contractors outlining reporting requirements for technological developments. The document emphasizes that:
- NTSR submission is mandatory regardless of whether new technology is being reported
- NTR submission is only required when new technology has been developed
- Failure to properly complete these reports or failure to disclose new technology results in returned deliverables

## Key Guidelines

### NTSR (New Technology Summary Report)
- Required for all contractors, with or without new technology to report
- Must be completed online through EHB via e-NTR website
- Two separate templates provided depending on whether new technology is being reported
- Interim NTSR applies to Phase II and Post Phase II contracts
- Final NTSR also required

### NTR (New Technology Report)
- Only required if new technology has been developed
- One NTR should be submitted per new technology
- All fields must be completed
- Contact: Janelle Nguyen (Janelle.Nguyen@jpl.nasa.gov)

## New Technology Definition & Examples
The document provides NASA's broad definition: "any invention, discovery, improvement, or innovation—whether or not patentable—which includes, but is not limited to, new processes, machines, manufactures, and compositions of matter, and improvements to, or new applications of, existing processes, machines, manufactures, and compositions of matter."

**Reportable technologies include:**
- New technology, designs, or concepts
- New or improved hardware, devices, machines, or prototypes (including nano/molecular level)
- New or improved methods, assays, analyses, tests, processes, procedures, simulations, testbeds, integration, packaging, encapsulation, or miniaturization
- New or improved models (animals, tissue culture, cellular, molecular, biological)
- New materials and compositions of matter (synthetic, biological, genomic, proteomic, molecular)
- New or improved manufacturing/fabrication approaches
- New software applications, computer programs, or code
- New or novel uses/applications of existing technologies
- New processes, techniques, formulas
- New ways of assembling commercial components
- New, modified, or updated software, algorithms, concepts, simulations, apps
- Ideas for improvements not yet built or tested

**Key qualifier:** If something works faster, better, stronger, lighter, more robust, cheaper, or easier to make, it should be reported.
