- Date: Submitted 2020; document created/modified June 2021

- Key Personnel: Jack Elston PhD (CEO), Bill Nickerson

## Executive Summary
BST proposes development of a machine learning-based anomaly and fault detection system for UAS that provides early warning of system failures and faults requiring maintenance. The system continuously monitors for common flight hazards (icing, propeller damage, wireless communications failure) and enables diagnosis/resolution without requiring extensive operator experience, thereby improving USAF drone safety and reducing mission failure risk.

## Technical Approach
- Machine learning-based anomaly and fault detection algorithms
- On-board avionics subsystem that continuously monitors UAS health
- Automated detection of common flight hazards (icing, propeller damage, wireless communications failure)
- Early warning and predictive failure capabilities to prevent critical system failures
- Enables proper diagnosis and resolution of issues during flight operations

## Products & Capabilities Described

**On-Board Augmented Intelligence**
- Highly capable avionics subsystem with associated ML algorithms
- Provides intelligent fault detection predicting system failures before occurrence
- Continuous health monitoring for common failure modes
- Eliminates need for hundreds of hours of operator experience to detect equivalent-level faults

## Use Cases & Applications
- USAF drone operations and mission support
- Beyond-line-of-sight (BLOS) operations
- Flights over populated areas
- Fully autonomous operations without direct human oversight
- Integration into broader UAS reliability and robustness improvements

## Key Competitive/Strategic Advantages
- Addresses critical gap: UAS failure rates substantially exceed manned aircraft
- Supports broader USAF vision of ubiquitous UAS operations
- Enables safer operations in NAS (National Airspace System)
- Complements related technology needs (collision avoidance, GPS-denied navigation)
