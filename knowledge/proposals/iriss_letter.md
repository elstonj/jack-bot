## Executive Summary
University of Colorado's Project Drought commits $49,090 in funding to Black Swift Technologies to further develop the soil moisture mapping (SMM) sUAS system from NASA SBIR contract NNX14CG09C. The funding will support a second summer of flight campaigns and validation at the Irrigation Research Foundation test site in Eastern Colorado, with the goal of enabling variable rate irrigation to reduce agricultural water usage.

## Technical Approach
- Development of SMM sUAS for soil moisture mapping using radiometric, NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index), and thermal data
- Sensor fusion combining multiple data types to generate soil moisture maps
- Validation against in situ soil moisture sensors
- Algorithm optimization to reduce processing time for same-day soil moisture map generation
- Integration with variable rate irrigation systems

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SMM sUAS (Soil Moisture Mapping small Unmanned Aircraft System)**
- Small unmanned aircraft system developed during NASA SBIR Phase II and Phase II-E
- Uses radiometric, NDVI, and thermal data for soil moisture mapping
- Target TRL of 7 and MRL of 4 by end of CCRPP grant
- Planned for operational use by Project Drought in summer 2017

**SuperSwift**
- Referenced as related platform
- Target TRL of 9 and MRL of 7 by conclusion of CCRPP grant

## Use Cases & Applications

**Agricultural Water Management**
- Primary application: reduction of water usage in agriculture through precision irrigation
- Test site: Irrigation Research Foundation (IRF) in Eastern Colorado
- Demonstrated potential: >15% reduction in water usage while maintaining crop yield with accurate soil moisture knowledge
- Key capability: enabling variable rate irrigation

**Key Societal Need Addressed**
- Agriculture accounts for ~70% of freshwater use on Earth
- Soil moisture mapping enables significant water conservation
