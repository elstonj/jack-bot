## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is a Boulder, Colorado-based sUAS avionics startup (founded 2011) that provides cutting-edge flight management systems and customizable user interface software for small unmanned aircraft. The plan positions BST to capitalize on the emerging commercial UAS market expected to reach $11.4 billion by 2022, leveraging its technical expertise and early government/research customer base ahead of FAA commercialization regulations anticipated for 2016-2017.

## Technical Approach
BST's core technical approach centers on:
- Proprietary algorithms and integrated end-to-end systems combining avionics, flight management, and ground station software
- Modularized software features allowing customization from simple/safe operations for novice users to advanced data processing, storage, and payload control for skilled operators
- Tablet-based user interface design (patent pending)
- Partnership strategy with airframe manufacturers to integrate BST avionics as standard offerings

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SwiftCore Flight Management System
- Core avionics and autopilot product
- Enables single operator to theoretically control multiple sUAS simultaneously
- Described as "cheaper, easier to use, and in many ways more capable than military systems costing 10 times as much" (per RECUV quote)
- Reduces operational crew requirements from ~12 personnel to minimal training needed
- Accessible to operators with varying experience levels

### SuperSwift Aircraft
- sUAS airframe
- Compatible with SwiftCore avionics
- Designed for commercial and research applications

### Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS
- Specialized system for agricultural/hydrological applications
- Represents customized end-to-end solution approach

### Ground Station Software
- Fully customizable user interface
- Modular feature architecture
- Advanced features for data processing, storage, and sensor payload control

## Competitive Advantages
- Technical team of early innovators with three Ph.D.s in Aerospace Engineering (focus on sUAS automation, applications, design)
- Proprietary algorithms and fully integrated end-to-end systems
- Superior usability and reliability for high-value applications
- Established relationships with government/research customers enabling COA (Certificate of Authorization) experience
- Product simplicity reducing operational complexity and costs versus military-grade alternatives
- Modular software architecture serving both novice and advanced users

## Current Market Position & Customer Base
- Early customers: University researchers, NASA, local government entities
- Operating under FAA Certificates of Authorization (public entity restriction)
- Strong customer satisfaction driving referrals
- Direct sales success through trade shows and industry events
- Positive feedback loop: customer testing → product improvement → market reception

## Business Model
- End-to-end systems distribution through existing channels plus new channels as commercial market opens
- Airframe manufacturer partnerships (discounts and free compatibility customization offered)
- Pricing dependent on partnership agreements and competitive positioning
- Web-based lead management program for sales tracking
- Focus on product quality to drive customer satisfaction and word-of-mouth marketing

## Market Opportunity & Timing
- FAA expected to finalize commercial UAS regulations in late 2016/early 2017
- Current market restricted to public entities (universities, law enforcement, government)
- Teal Group estimate: UAS market (non-consumer/commercial) growing from $6.6B (2012) to $11.4B (2022)
- AUVSI estimate: 2015-2017 period could see $13.6B economic impact in US alone from commercial UAS
- 2017: Expected FAA approval for commercial UAS operations in agriculture, oil exploration, fishing, mapping, video production

## Problem Being Solved
Current UAS offerings require:
- Crews of up to 12 personnel
- Expensive operator training
- High operational costs and complexity
- Significant operational risk

BST solution:
- Enables single operator to run multiple aircraft
- Minimal training requirements
- Dramatically reduced operational costs
- Lower operational complexity and risk
