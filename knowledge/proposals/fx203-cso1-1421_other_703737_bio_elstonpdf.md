## Executive Summary
Dr. Elston is the CEO and co-founder of Black Swift Technologies with extensive expertise in conducting in-situ atmospheric measurements from unmanned aircraft systems, particularly in hazardous conditions. His background spans from PhD work on tornadic supercell sampling to developing BST's SwiftCore autopilot system, with hundreds of flight experiments and deployments across diverse environments including the Arctic.

## Background & Credentials

### Education & Early Research
- Ph.D. from University of Colorado Boulder focused on:
  - Complex meshed networks
  - Unmanned aircraft system design
  - Control algorithms for in-situ sampling of tornadic supercell thunderstorms
- NSF Atmospheric and Geospace Sciences Postdoctoral Research Fellowship titled "Improved In Situ Thermodynamic Sampling of Severe Storms with Unmanned Aircraft Systems Through Improved Wind Estimation and Energy Harvesting"

### Key Career Achievements
- Led design of **Tempest UAS** - first unmanned aircraft to intercept a tornadic supercell thunderstorm (VORTEX2 project, NSF and NOAA funded)
- Conducted simultaneous multi-vehicle operations profiling atmospheric boundary layer early morning transition
- Flew three vehicles in vertically stacked formation through a gust front
- Authored or co-authored over 100 Certificate of Authorization (COA) applications
- Conducted hundreds of flight experiments including Arctic deployments

## Technical Leadership at BST
