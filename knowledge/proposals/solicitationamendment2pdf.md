## Executive Summary
This is Amendment 2 to the DAF SBIR AFX24.7 Phase I solicitation, issued to clarify applicant responsibilities regarding NIST SP 800-171 cybersecurity assessments. The amendment rescinded previous requirements from Amendment 1 and removes the burden of submitting NIST assessment documentation with proposals, while establishing a deadline of January 6th, 2025 for completing assessments and uploading scores to the SPRS module.

## Document Scope
This document is a solicitation amendment and procedural guidance document—not a proposal or technical report. It contains no Black Swift Technologies-specific information, products, capabilities, or technical approaches. The document establishes the rules and requirements for responding to the DAF's open innovation solicitation for dual-purpose defense technologies.

## Key Procedural Changes (Amendment 2)

**Deleted Requirement:**
- Previous language threatening proposal disqualification for failure to publish NIST SP 800-171 scores was deleted.

**Clarification:**
- NIST Assessment Documentation is not required as a submission in Volume 5 (Supporting Documents).

**New Deadline:**
- NIST SP 800-171 Basic Assessments must be completed and uploaded to SPRS (Supplier Performance Risk System) module in PIEE (Procurement Integrated Enterprise Environment) no later than **January 6th, 2025**.

## Solicitation Overview (AFX24.7)

**Timeline:**
- Proposal submission deadline: October 10, 2024, 12:00 p.m. ET
- Maximum Phase I funding: $75,000
- Maximum period of performance: 90 calendar days

**Submission Portal:**
- All proposals must be submitted through the DoD SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP) at https://www.dodsbirsttr.mil/

**Topic Focus:**
- DAF Topic Number: AFX247-PCSO1
- Title: "Phase I Open Call for Innovative Defense Related Dual-Purpose Technologies/Solutions with a Clear Air Force Stakeholder Need"
- This is an open innovation topic seeking commercial technologies adaptable to Air Force needs, aligned with Air Force Operational Imperatives

## Evaluation Criteria (Equal Importance)
1. **Commercialization Potential** - Market size, business plan clarity, evidence of traction/adoption, phase II commitments
2. **Defense Need** - Appropriateness and specificity of identified need, adequacy of proposed effort, mission impact, breadth of applicability
3. **Technical Approach** - Problem clarity, soundness, innovation, differentiation from alternatives, team qualifications

## Eligibility Requirements

**Company Size:**
- Must have ≤500 employees (including affiliates)

**Ownership:**
- Must be >50% owned and controlled by U.S. citizens or permanent resident aliens
- OR >50% owned by multiple venture capital operating companies/hedge funds/private equity firms
- Small businesses with venture/private equity ownership are eligible but capped at 15% of DAF's SBIR budget

**Performance Requirements:**
- Minimum 2/3 of R&R&D work must be performed by proposing firm (measured in direct/indirect costs)
- Principal Investigator must have primary employment with applicant firm (>50% time)
- Work must be performed in the United States

**Security/Compliance:**
- Must register in SAM.gov with valid UEI (Unique Entity ID)
- Must register in DSIP with Login.gov account
- Must complete NIST SP 800-171 Basic Assessment by January 6, 2025
- No owners with affiliations to foreign countries of concern (People's Republic of China, etc.)

## Proposal Structure (7 Volumes Required)

1. **Volume 1:** Proposal Cover Sheet (technical abstract, benefits, commercial applications; max 200 words each section)
2. **Volume 2:** Technical Volume (PDF format, demonstrating innovative approach and scientific merit)
3. **Volume 3:** Cost Volume
4. **Volume 4:** Company Commercialization Report
5. **Volume 5:** Supporting Documents (NIST documentation NOT required as of Amendment 2)
6. **Volume 6:** Fraud, Waste, and Abuse Training
7. **Volume 7:** Disclosures of Foreign Affiliations or Relationships to Foreign Countries

## Work Definition Requirements
Proposed work must constitute Research/Research & Development (R&R&D):
- Systematic, intensive study directed toward greater knowledge or understanding
- Systematic study directed toward applying new knowledge to meet recognized need
- Systematic application of knowledge toward production of useful materials, devices, systems, or methods (design, development, improvement of prototypes, new processes)

## Notable Requirements & Restrictions

**Single Proposal Limit:**
- Each small business concern may submit **only one (1) proposal** to this solicitation
- Multiple submissions will result in disqualification of all prior versions; only the most recently submitted will be evaluated

**ITAR/Export Control:**
- If proposed technology is ITAR-restricted, applicant must provide DD Form 2345 (Military Critical Technology Agreement)
- Foreign nationals on ITAR projects must be lawfully admitted permanent residents or designated protected individuals
- Applicant must provide tentative determination of ITAR/Export Administration Regulations applicability

**Disqualifiers (Common Reasons for Rejection):**
- Improper SAM.gov registration
- Inadequate allocation of performance percentage of work
- Work not meeting R&R&D definition
- Submission after deadline
- Exceeding maximum funding ($75,000) or duration (90 days)
- Not meeting SBIR ownership/size requirements
- Prior award for essentially equivalent work
- NIST SP 800-171 assessment not published to SPRS by January 6, 2025

**Due Diligence & Security Review:**
- DoD will conduct risk-based due diligence on all proposals (per SBIR and STTR Extension Act of 2022)
- Assessment includes cybersecurity practices, patent analysis, employee analysis, foreign ownership, financial ties/obligations
- DAF may deny awards based on security risks and totality of circumstances review

## Phase I, II, and III Objectives

**Phase I:**
- Determine scientific and technical merit and feasibility
- Validate product-market fit between solution and Air Force stakeholder
- Define clear, actionable integration pathway
- Identify potential stakeholders
- Describe pathway to integrating with Air Force operations
- Assess applicability to other DoD/Government customers

**Phase II:**
- Development, installation, integration, demonstration, and/or test and evaluation
- Evaluate adapted solution against objectives and key results
- Detail how solution differs from commercial offering and scaling approach
- Identify clear transition path with input from stakeholders
- Specify integration with current/future solutions
- Describe sustainability and identify other potential DoD/Government customers

**Phase III:**
- Transition adapted non-Defense commercial solution to provide expanded mission capability
- Broader range of potential governmental and civilian users
- Alternate mission applications
