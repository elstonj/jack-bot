## Executive Summary
This is a template document showing the required format and structure for submitting NASA SBIR Phase I final reports through the Electronic Hand Book (EHB) system. It provides no substantive technical content but rather serves as a guidance document for contractors on how to structure their final project reports, including required sections, compliance notices, and submission procedures.

## Document Purpose
Instructional template showing NASA contractors how to format and submit Phase I SBIR final reports. Includes:
- Required report sections and subsection structure
- SBIR data rights notice requirements
- Export control compliance statements (ITAR/EAR)
- Proprietary information handling procedures
- Online submission instructions via EHB system

## Required Report Sections (As Outlined in Template)

**Part 1:** Table of Contents

**Part 2:** Identification and Significance of the Innovation (max 200 words/2,000 characters)

**Part 3:** Technical Objectives (max 200 words/2,000 characters)

**Part 4:** Work Plan
- 4.1 Technical Approach
- 4.2 Task Descriptions
- 4.3 Meeting the Technical Objectives
- 4.4 Task Labor Categories and Schedules

**Part 5:** Potential Applications
- 5.1 Potential NASA Applications (max 100 words/1,000 characters)
- 5.2 Potential Non-NASA Commercial Applications (max 200 words/2,000 characters)

**Part 6:** Contacts
- 6.1 Key Contractor Participants
- 6.2 Key NASA Participants
- 6.3 NASA and Non-NASA Advisors

**Part 7:** Technical Activities
- 7.1 Cumulative Technical Activities
- 7.2 Future Technical Activities

**Part 8:** Potential Customer and Commercialization Activities
- 8.1 Cumulative NASA Potential Customer Activities
- 8.2 Cumulative Non-NASA Potential Customer Activities
- 8.3 Other Cumulative Commercialization Activities
- 8.4 Future Potential Customer and Commercialization Activities

**Part 9:** Resources Status
- Physical completion percentage
- Amount expended
- Identification of any incomplete tasks/objectives with explanation

**Part 10:** Additional Reports of Work
- 10.1 Quantitative description with milestones, results, and feasibility assessment
- 10.2 Current problems and corrective actions
- 10.3 Next period work discussion
- 10.4 Subcontractor changes since award

**Part 11:** References

## Compliance Requirements

### SBIR Rights Notice
- Standard 4-year protection period for SBIR data after contract acceptance
- Government use restricted during protection period
- Full licensing available to government after protection period expires
- Mandatory inclusion on all report reproductions

### Export Control
- ITAR Notice required if item falls under U.S. Munitions List
- EAR Notice required for export-controlled technical information
- Requires approval from NASA export control officials for foreign national access
- Must include appropriate statement on front page

### Proprietary Information
- Highly discouraged unless required for report validity
- Requires Contracting Officer's Representative (COR) and Contracting Officer (CO) approval
- Cannot mark entire report as proprietary
- Specific legend required on cover page identifying proprietary pages
- Footer legend required on all content pages: "Use or disclosure of this data is subject to the restrictions on cover page of this report"
