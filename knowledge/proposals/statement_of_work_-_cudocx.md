# Statement of Work - CU (RECUV)

## Document Metadata
- Type: Statement of Work (SBIR Phase I Subcontract)
- Client/Agency: NASA (via Black Swift Technologies)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Phase I - "Advanced Technologies for Coordinated In Situ Atmospheric Sensing"
- Date: 24 January 2014
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Apollo (airframe)
- Key Personnel: Professor Brian Argrow, Professor Dale Lawrence, PRA James Mack (University of Colorado Boulder, RECUV)

## Executive Summary
The Research and Engineering Center for Unmanned Vehicles (RECUV) at the University of Colorado Boulder agreed to support Black Swift Technologies in Phase I of a NASA SBIR program focused on coordinated atmospheric sensing. RECUV's role involved assessing the Apollo airframe's capabilities, designing modifications for inclement weather operation, developing a removable payload bay, and selecting/validating sensors for atmospheric sampling missions.

## Technical Approach
RECUV conducted four primary technical tasks in support of BST's SBIR Phase I:

1. **Apollo Airframe Assessment & Verification**: Evaluated the Apollo airframe's ability to meet required performance characteristics. Identified necessary modifications and verified design performance using SWIL (software-in-the-loop) simulation to validate flight performance in target wind environments, tracking, and control.

2. **Inclement Weather Modification**: Modified Apollo to operate in adverse weather conditions, including heavy precipitation and cold weather environments.

3. **Payload Bay Design**: Redesigned the airframe with a removable nose cone including mechanical, electrical, and data interfaces to the avionics system.

4. **Sensor Selection & Validation**: Selected and validated atmospheric sensors for benchmark sampling missions.

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Apollo (Airframe)**
- BST's unmanned airframe platform for atmospheric sensing
- Base design required modifications for inclement weather operation and payload integration
- Capable of operating in wind environments suitable for atmospheric measurement
- Required integration of removable sensor payload bay with mechanical/electrical/data interfaces

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Coordinated in situ atmospheric sensing**: Primary application focus
- **Benchmark atmospheric sampling missions**: Specific near-term application for sensor validation
