# 2015 Virtual Site Visit: Black Swift Technologies

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Virtual Site Visit presentation (SBIR Phase II contract surveillance)
- **Client/Agency:** NASA (Goddard Space Flight Center)
- **Program/Solicitation:** SBIR Phase II, Soil Moisture Mapping
- **Contract Number:** NNX14CG09C
- **Date:** October 30, 2015 (presentation date); prepared September 21, 2015
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** SuperSwift UAS, Soil Moisture Mapping Tempest prototype, L-band antenna, LDCR (radiometer), FPGA-based circuit boards
- **Key Personnel:** Maciej Stachura (PI), Jack Elston (President and CEO), Al Gasiewski and Eryan Dai (University of Colorado subcontractors)
- **Contract Officer's Representative (COR):** Geoffrey Bland

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies presented progress on their NASA SBIR Phase II contract to develop an unmanned aircraft system (SuperSwift) equipped with a soil moisture mapping sensor payload. By October 2015, BST had successfully conducted prototype flight testing in Canton, Oklahoma, designed a next-generation aircraft, and developed advanced data collection systems, with plans to continue field demonstrations and pursue Phase II-E extension funding.

## Technical Approach

### System Architecture
- **SuperSwift UAS:** New aircraft design developed from lessons learned in initial prototype integration; first prototype completed and awaiting NASA permission to fly
- **Soil Moisture Mapping Tempest Payload:** Includes L-band radiometer for soil moisture measurement with custom antenna designed to fit within small UAS constraints
- **Data Collection System:** Next-generation FPGA-based circuit boards for high-rate data logging and on-board radiometer data processing

### Key Technical Work Completed
1. **L-band Antenna Design:** Custom antenna engineered to fit within small UAS airframe
2. **Radiometer Development:** Design, tuning, and refinement of radiometer unit with auxiliary sensors for NDVI and thermal measurements
3. **FPGA-Based Data Logging:** Custom circuit boards and FPGA code written for real-time data acquisition and processing
4. **Noise Mitigation:** Aircraft modifications to reduce motor and subsystem noise
5. **Radiometer Integration:** Full integration of LDCR radiometer system into SuperSwift airframe (25% of work performed by University of Colorado subcontractors)
