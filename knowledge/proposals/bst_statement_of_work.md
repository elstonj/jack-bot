## Executive Summary
BST proposed to develop an upper air sounding system capable of delivering radiosonde-quality meteorological data without the logistical overhead of balloon-based radiosondes. The work involved integrating iMet sensors with the SwiftTrainer platform and conducting trade studies on wind measurement approaches for atmospheric profiling using small unmanned aircraft systems (sUAS).

## Technical Approach
- **iMet Integration**: Design of hardware and software integration for iMet PTH (Pressure-Temperature-Humidity) system, including communications protocol modification to telemeter measurements to Ground Control Station (GCS) devices
- **Wind Measurement Trade Study**: Comparison between two approaches:
  - Aeroprobe Micro Air Data system (multi-hole probe embeddable in vehicle nose cone, previously developed by BST under NASA funding)
  - Advanced filter-based wind estimation derived from aircraft maneuvers
- **Platform Selection**: Utilization of SwiftTrainer sUAS as the baseline platform for prototype development

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SwiftTrainer
- Small unmanned aircraft system platform
- Selected as the basis for prototype atmospheric profiling system
- To be modified for lab testing with integrated sensor suite

### Aeroprobe Micro Air Data System
- Multi-hole air data probe solution
- Embeddable in vehicle nose cone
- Previously developed by BST under NASA funding
- Proposed as one option for wind measurement in trade study

### iMet PTH System
- Commercial meteorological sensor package
- Measures pressure, temperature, and humidity
- Requires integration and custom communications protocol for GCS telemetry

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Upper Air Sounding**: Atmospheric profiling without balloon-based radiosondes
- **Meteorological Measurements**: Collection of radiosonde-quality PTH data
- **Reduced Logistics**: Elimination of hardware recovery and replacement costs associated with expendable balloons
