## Executive Summary
This document outlines Black Swift Technologies' IT security procedures and compliance commitments for NASA contract 80NSSC25CA043 under the NASA SBIR/STTR program. BST commits to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of NASA electronic information and IT resources, including Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), in accordance with NASA FAR Supplement clause 1852.204-76 and NIST/NASA IT security requirements.

## Technical Approach
**NASA System Access:**
- Limited remote access to one NASA IT system: SBIR/STTR Awardee Firm ProSAMS (https://prosams.nasa.gov/)
- Purpose: Electronic submission of contract deliverables, invoices, and technical reports
- Access management: Through NASA Account Management System (NAMS) and Identity and Access Management System (IdMAX)
- Access limited to personnel involved in contract negotiation and administration
- All registered personnel required to complete NASA Online Annual IT Security Training

**No Additional NASA Systems:** Document explicitly states "None Applicable" for additional NASA IT systems or agency data access.

## Security Requirements & Commitments

**Information Protection Responsibilities:**
1. Protect confidentiality, integrity, and availability of NASA electronic information
2. Prevent unauthorized disclosure of NASA information
3. Comply with NIST and NASA IT security requirements, regulations, policies, and guidelines
4. Handle Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) with appropriate safeguards

**Sensitive Information Handling (Per 1852.237-72):**
BST commits to:
1. Use sensitive information only for contract performance, not competitive advantage
2. Safeguard sensitive information from unauthorized use and disclosure
3. Limit access to employees with need-to-know for contract services
4. Prevent access/disclosure to external persons and entities
5. Train employees on authorized uses and mandatory protections
6. Obtain written affirmations from employees confirming training compliance
7. Administer monitoring process to ensure compliance; report breaches to Contracting Officer

## General Rules of Behavior (15 Core Rules)
1. Use NASA systems for lawful, authorized purposes only
2. Protect all NASA information including PII and CUI
3. Report security incidents immediately to firm help desk/security manager/supervisor
4. Encrypt all NASA data on mobile/transportable devices when transported outside organization
5. Read and understand NASA privacy and security notices before accessing NASA network
6. Screen-lock or log off when leaving work area
7. Provide NASA information access only to properly cleared, authorized personnel with need-to-know
8. Minimize sensitive information on IT systems during travel; destroy copies when no longer required
9. Properly mask and label classified, sensitive, and proprietary documents per NASA IT Security Policies
10. Adhere to Separation of Duties principles; obtain management approval for conflicting roles
11. Do not use anonymizer sites that bypass Agency security mechanisms
12. Do not engage in behavior putting IT equipment/media at risk (destruction, damage, loss, theft, compromise)
13. Supervisors must adequately instruct, train, and supervise employees
14. Adhere to minimum password requirements for systems
15. Do not share account passwords; protect passwords at highest classification level

## Incident Response Procedures
**Immediate Notification Requirements:**
- In event of intentional or inadvertent information security incident affecting confidentiality, integrity, or availability:
  - Notify NASA Security Operations Center immediately
  - Notify appropriate NASA officials including Contracting Officer (CO) and Contracting Officer's Representative (COR)
- If any NASA IT system, data, or contract deliverables are compromised, misused, distorted, lost, or destroyed:
  - Immediately notify NASA Security Operations Center
  - Immediately notify CO and COR assigned to contract

## Notable Details

**Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) Definition:**
Document defines CUI as unclassified information not meeting National Security Classification thresholds but pertinent to U.S. national interest, requiring protection from disclosure with special handling safeguards and prescribed limits on exchange/dissemination.

**Legal Acknowledgments:**
- BST acknowledges that unauthorized attempts to upload or change information on NASA servers are strictly prohibited
- Violations may be prosecutable under Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986 and National Information Infrastructure Protection Act of 1996
- Compliance enforcement through sanctions commensurate with infraction level

**Potential Consequences:**
- Unauthorized use/disclosure of sensitive information may result in:
  - Contract termination for default
  - Debarment from government contracting for serious misconduct affecting present responsibility
