# SBIR N251-016 Letter of Support (Black Swift Technologies)

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy (SBIR Program)
- Program/Solicitation: SBIR Topic N251-016 (Phase II)
- Date: January 5, 2026
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: A-size sonobuoy-launched UAV
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (Black Swift Technologies); Travis Hamilton (Ultra Maritime USA - letter author)

## Executive Summary
Ultra Maritime USA (UM), a leading sonobuoy manufacturer with 70 years of experience and 7+ million units produced, provides formal support for Black Swift Technologies' Phase II SBIR proposal to integrate expendable acoustic sensors into an A-size sonobuoy-launched UAV. Ultra Maritime commits to participate in prototyping and sensor design, with potential for scale manufacturing if the project advances beyond Phase II.

## Technical Approach
- **Integration Goal**: Incorporate expendable acoustic sensors into an A-size sonobuoy-launched UAV
- **Payload Constraints**: Design must fit within A-size sonobuoy launch payload constraints
- **Sensor Types**: Low-cost acoustic and E-field sensors
- **Sensor Parameters** (to be finalized): frequency, range, sensitivity, and deployment requirements
- **Development Path**: Phase II prototyping with design concept services, prototype manufacturing, and required testing

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Black Swift Technologies UAV
- A-size sonobuoy-launched platform
- Proposed to carry expendable acoustic and E-field sensors
- Intended to support anti-submarine warfare (ASW) air missions

### Ultra Maritime Acoustic & E-Field Sensors
- Low-cost design
- Expendable architecture
- Custom specifications to be developed during Phase II (frequency, range, sensitivity)
- Designed to complement existing underwater acoustic products (sonobuoys)

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Mission**: Anti-submarine warfare (ASW) air operations
- **Platform Integration**: Deployment via A-size sonobuoy launch mechanism
- **Navy Application**: Support for undersea detection and acoustic sensing in naval operations
