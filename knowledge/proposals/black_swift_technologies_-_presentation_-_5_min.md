## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to deploy its S2 fixed-wing unmanned aircraft system equipped with a Quanta3-supplied methane detection sensor as a commercial data service for oil & gas companies, addressing regulatory compliance needs. The S2 Methane Leak Detection (MLD) platform can survey 50+ wellsites per day at $35 cost per site (vs. $750 for traditional on-site visits), targeting $35M in revenues within 3 years and creating 110 FTE jobs. The AIA grant ($250K) will complement a concurrent $750K NASA commercialization grant to accelerate product development, pilot deployment with anchor customer PDC Energy, and market launch.

## Technical Approach

**S2 Platform for Methane Detection:**
- Fixed-wing UAS with VTOL capability (vertical takeoff/landing variants available)
- Air-deployed or VTOL configurations
- Greater than 100 mph dash speed
- Only 3 lbs (for some variants)
- Modular, field-swappable payload system
- Extended endurance, operates at high altitudes

**Sensor Integration:**
- Laser Absorption Spectrometer with <2 ppb (parts per billion) CH₄ detection capability
- Partnered exclusively with Quanta3 (Colorado-based company) for sensor supply
- Described as world's lightest <2 ppb detection level methane sensor for fixed-wing UAS

**Proprietary Navigation & Control Technologies:**
- Real-time, on-board wind speed measurement capability (proprietary, patent-pending)
- Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) navigation with denied GPS capability
- "Follow the scent" sensor-based autonomous flight path adaptation
- Sensor-directed navigation using advanced vision and radar-based systems
- Class-leading wind detection enabling 3D plume imaging

**Operational Approach:**
1. Automated flight path generation over target areas
2. Real-time adaptive flight patterns triggered by methane detection
3. Multiple passes through detected plumes to generate 3D renderings
4. Geo-accurate leak location and size characterization (resolution to <10 SCFH - standard cubic feet per hour)

**SwiftCore/Avionics:**
- Custom-designed autopilot system built in-house
- One of few truly modular avionics solutions
- Multiply redundant systems
- Open interface for easy payload integration
- Used in demanding BVLOS missions approved by NASA and NOAA
