# Monthly Report 2024

## Document Metadata
- Type: Monthly activity report
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: AREN (Arctic Research and Education Network)
- Date: 2024 (covering January-December 2024)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Aeropod (minicam version), kites (paper and mini versions)
- Key Personnel: Not specified

## Executive Summary
This is an operational activity log documenting BST's involvement in the NASA AREN program throughout 2024, focusing on educational outreach, workshop coordination, equipment development and distribution, and curriculum materials creation. The work centers on preparing and delivering a Summer Institute workshop and associated follow-up activities to project participants.

## Technical Approach
Activities centered on:
- Iterative development and documentation of Aeropod (minicam version) including version control updates
- Creation of educational materials (printable templates, video tutorials, guides, slide decks, lesson plans)
- Equipment testing and validation (weather instruments comparison, PocketLab battery troubleshooting)
- Workshop coordination and logistics (supply procurement, packing/shipping, participant support)
- Data analysis tool development and training (CODAP practice analysis, QGIS learning activities)

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Aeropod (minicam version)**
- Educational atmospheric measurement platform
- Required version control documentation updates
- Associated training videos created with voice-over
- Printable trifold guide developed for users
- Tutorial content finalized into video format

**Kites (paper and mini versions)**
- Paper kite design templates created and updated throughout year
- 35 kites packed and shipped for workshop participants
- Shifted curriculum focus from minikite to paper kite design (October)

**Weather Instruments**
- Multiple instruments tested and compared for accuracy
- Data plotted to identify discrepancies
- Instrument mounts designed and distributed to participants

**Software/Data Tools**
- CODAP (Common Online Data Analysis Platform): sample data edited, practice analysis generated, new plugins and updates tested
- QGIS: learning activities developed; troubleshooting and feedback provided in December
- PocketLab: teaching resources investigated; battery/charging issues identified and resolved

## Use Cases & Applications
- Educational workshops for Arctic-focused remote sensing and atmospheric science
- Summer Institute workshop (4-week session with 35+ participants)
- Workshop follow-up sessions including data analysis training
- Remote sensing curriculum development and classroom resources
- Field-based atmospheric measurement training using kites and instruments

## Key Activities by Period
- **Winter (Jan-Feb)**: Planning, resource investigation, initial template development
- **Spring (Mar-May)**: Aeropod documentation and training content creation; supply logistics
- **Summer (June-Aug)**: Summer Institute workshop execution; material preparation; participant support
- **Fall (Sep-Dec)**: Workshop follow-up sessions; equipment upgrades; curriculum refinement; data tool training

## Notable Details
- Recurring attendance at bi-monthly production meetings, AREN group meetings, and Aerovator meetings throughout year
- AREN annual meeting attended in Detroit, MI (June)
- Transition from mini-kites to paper kites as primary educational tool
- Significant focus on post-workshop participant support and material distribution
- Emphasis on educational tool testing and validation (PocketLab batteries, weather instrument comparison)
- Curriculum materials designed for remote sensing and atmospheric data analysis education
- Close coordination with project partners and guest presenters
