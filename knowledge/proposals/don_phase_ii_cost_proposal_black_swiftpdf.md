## Notable Details & Special Considerations

### Key Operational Notes

1. **Subcontract Management Philosophy:** Black Swift states it does not typically engage in large subcontracted efforts as part of standard business model. Majority of technical, program management, and integration activities performed internally; subcontractors used selectively for specialized capabilities. This approach informs their G&A charging methodology (first $25K of each subcontract only).

2. **Sole-Source Justification:** All three universities selected on sole-source basis without competitive quotes obtained. Company provided cost/price analyses for each but did not pursue competitive bidding.

3. **Testing Emphasis:** Significant investment in testing infrastructure:
   - $40,000 in UAS equipment (4 units for testing/validation)
   - $62,284 budgeted for NASA Glenn Ice Research Tunnel facility access in Option I
   - This suggests validation testing is critical to project success

4. **Labor Cost Escalation:** 3% annual cost of living escalation applied to all three labor categories (Principals Elston and Stachura; Senior Engineer Fromm).

5. **Effort Distribution:**
   - Prime labor represents ~11% of total cost
   - Subcontractors represent ~55% of total cost
   - Remainder distributed across overhead, G&A, and direct costs

6. **Contract Type:** CPFF (Cost Plus Fixed Fee) with 7.0% fixed fee applied to total estimated costs. Fee calculated on approximately $1,867,055 estimated costs, yielding $130,694 in fixed fee.

7. **Period of Performance:** Base and Option I together span 48 months (2027-2030), with equal annual budgets in Base ($454,471 Year 1; $478,240 Year 2) and nearly balanced Option I ($470,834 Year 1; $463,510 Year 2).

8. **Geographic Footprint:** Field activities span multiple locations (Washington DC, Miami FL, Kill Devil Hills NC, Cleveland OH), suggesting distributed testing and collaboration model.
