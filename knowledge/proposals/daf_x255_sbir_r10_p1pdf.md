# Department of the Air Force X25.5 SBIR Release 10 Topic Release Instructions

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Solicitation Topic Release Instructions (TRI) / Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase I Call
- **Client/Agency:** Department of the Air Force (DAF), in partnership with AFWERX (Air Force) and SpaceWERX (Space Force)
- **Program/Solicitation:** DoD X25 Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO); DAF X25.5 Release 10
- **Date:** July 2, 2025 (topics open); August 5, 2025 (submission deadline at 12:00 p.m. ET)
- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None
- **Key Personnel:** Daniel Brewer (Air Force SBIR/STTR Contracting Officer)

## Executive Summary
This document provides comprehensive instructions for Department of the Air Force X25.5 Release 10 SBIR Phase I proposals. The solicitation contains two open topics seeking innovative, dual-purpose commercial technologies adaptable to Air Force (AFX255-PCSO1) and Space Force (AFX255-PCSO2) stakeholder needs. The program anticipates 260 awards with maximum funding of $75,000 per Phase I contract and 90 calendar days performance period.

## Solicitation Overview

### Two Open Topics:
1. **AFX255-PCSO1:** Phase I Call for Innovative Defense-Related Dual-Purpose Technologies/Solutions with Clear Air Force Stakeholder Need
2. **AFX255-PCSO2:** Phase I Call for Innovative Defense-Related Dual-Purpose Technologies/Solutions with Clear Space Force Stakeholder Need

### Key Timeline:
- **July 2, 2025:** Topics open; DoD begins accepting proposals in DSIP
- **July 15, 2025:** Deadline for submission of questions (4:00 p.m. ET)
- **August 5, 2025:** Proposal submission deadline (12:00 p.m. ET)
- **~September-October 2025:** Anticipated completion of reviews and notification

### Funding Parameters:
- **Maximum SBIR Funding:** $75,000 (does not include validated matching non-SBIR/STTR funding)
- **Maximum Period of Performance:** 90 calendar days total (no distinction between technical and reporting periods)
- **Number of Awards:** 260 anticipated awards
- **Award Type:** Contracts or Other Transactions (OT) for prototypes authorized

## Applicant Eligibility & Submission Rules

### Key Restrictions:
- Each Small Business Concern (SBC) may submit **only ONE proposal** under this topic release
- Applicant may apply to one, but **not both**, topics (AFX255-PCSO1 or AFX255-PCSO2)
- If multiple proposals received from same applicant for either/both topics, only most recent certified/submitted proposal prior to deadline will be evaluated; all others marked non-responsive
- No exceptions to submission deadlines; proposals submitted after 12:00 p.m. ET on August 5, 2025 will not be considered

### Venture Capital/Private Equity Restrictions:
- DAF may not award more than 15% of SBIR budget to SBCs majority-owned by multiple venture capital operating companies, hedge funds, or private equity firms (per 15 U.S.C. 638(dd))
- Investment by VC, private equity, or hedge funds shall **not** be used as award criterion
- Once selected proposals from such SBCs reach 15% of DAF SBIR budget, such SBCs no longer considered for award

### Proposal Approach Requirements:
- Non-defense **commercial** solutions adapted to meet Air Force or Space Force stakeholder needs
- Solutions must be closely tied to commercial technologies and solutions
- Applied research and development (not "front-end" or basic R/R&D)
- Feasibility studies and prototype-validated concepts under accelerated Phase I and Phase II schedules
- Proposers should identify which Critical Technology Area addressed or reference "BLUE SKY" with explanation of strategic capability

### What is NOT Acceptable:
- Commercial-Off-The-Shelf (COTS) resale of commercially-available solutions
- Non-RDT&E procurement activities
- Work that doesn't meet definition of Research/Research & Development (R/R&D)

## Evaluation Criteria (Non-Cost Factors, in Descending Order of Importance)

### 1. Defense Need (Primary)
Evaluates fulfillment of defense need and ability to impact that need:
- Appropriateness, relevance, and specificity of identified Defense Need
- Adequacy of proposed effort and relationship to fulfilling Defense Need
- Applicant's understanding of the proposed Defense Need and technology's logical fit
- Level of defense mission impact and urgency of Defense Need
- Breadth of applicability of solution

### 2. Technical Approach
- Clarity of problem statement, technical merit, soundness, and innovation
- Differentiation from current customer alternatives
- Qualifications of Principal Investigator/Project Manager, supporting staff, and consultants to execute approach

### 3. Commercialization (Important for Future Phases)
Evaluates potential for government or private sector commercialization:
- SBC's record of commercializing SBIR or other research (pre-sales, pilots, sales, revenue, active users, subscriptions, downloads, traction/adoption)
- Size of market and potential for revenue
- Clarity of SBC's business plan
- Existence of Phase II and/or Phase III follow-on commitments or private sector funding commitments
- Note: Broad adoption and commercial success are indicators but not mandated

### Cost/Price Consideration:
- All evaluation criteria other than cost/price, when combined, are **significantly more important** than cost/price
- Where technical evaluations are essentially equal in merit, cost/price will be considered in determining successful applicants
- Price reasonableness and realism will be considered as appropriate

### Volume 1 - Cover Sheet

### Volume 2 - Technical Volume (Mandatory Template Required)
**Critical Requirements:**
- Must use mandatory Phase I Proposal Template (Attachment 1) - "25.5 Release 10 - SBIR/STTR Phase I Proposal Template"
- Non-compliance renders submission non-responsive
- Single .pdf file (max 15 MB), virus-checked, not locked/encrypted
- No active graphics (videos, moving pictures, media)
- No hyperlinks or active links
- Font: minimum 10-pt with at least ½-inch margins (serif recommended)
- Each page must include: company name, topic number, DSIP-assigned proposal number
- Information only on designated pages in template will be evaluated
- Proprietary information must be marked with specific legends

**Required Technical Content (per template page designations):**
1. **Proposal Summary:** Company name and overview
2. **Technical Merit Summary with Key Personnel:**
   - Scientific merit of solution's central/enabling technological concepts
   - Current scientific/technical baseline (with references)
   - RDT&E advancement of baseline
   - Key personnel identification (including subcontractors/consultants)
   - Education, experience, citizenship information
   - Technical resume for PI with publications
   - Qualifications for both RDT&E and commercialization

3. **Concept and Significance of Problem/Opportunity:**
   - Specific technical problem/opportunity and importance
   - How proposed solution is better than current alternatives (commercial and defense)
   - Quantified benefits of proposed solution
   - Distinction between private sector and DoD variants if applicable
   - **Must address R/R&D requirement** - Non-RDT&E procurement of COTS not acceptable

4. **Defense Need:**
   - Knowledge of prospective end-users/customers and use-cases
   - Differentiation from current customer alternatives
   - Incremental progress toward identified defense need
   - Previous DoD funding for solution or underlying technology
   - Description of proposed solution vs. user's current alternative
   - Breadth of applicability

5. **Transition Plan:**
   - Plan to transition solution into DAF
   - Integration with existing DAF customer efforts

6. **Summary of Proposed Solution's Commercialization and Non-Defense Commercial Potential Business Plan:**
   - Demonstration of firm's ability to commercialize in both DoD and commercial markets
   - Description of non-defense commercial solution and its users
   - Qualitative and quantitative value to users
   - Supporting data (journal articles, literature, government publications)
   - Evidence of viable customer markets (pilots, revenue, users, investment)
   - Whether solution sold/leased/licensed with quantifiable data
   - Competitive landscape and scaling potential
   - Market segment and potential market share percentage
   - Whether entirely novel or modifies/builds on existing product
   - Comparison to competitor solutions
   - End-user use cases and fulfillment
   - Non-defense market opportunity (quantified/described)
   - Technical risks
   - Distinction between solution commercial success and company overall success
   - **Note:** This is RDT&E solicitation, not COTS resale

7. **Related Work (Dual-Use):**
   - Significant activities directly related to proposed effort
   - Conducted by PI, proposing firm, consultants, or others
   - Interface with proposed project and coordination with outside sources
   - State-of-the-art awareness
   - Similar previous and parallel work
   - Required for each: description, client POC name/phone, customer description/interviews, completion date
   - Scientific/technical literature search requirements per AFI 61-201

8. **Non-Proprietary Work Plan Outline (including subcontractor efforts) and Phase I Objectives:**
   - Clear description of approach
   - Planned tasks, how/where conducted, schedule of major events, final deliverables
   - Technical feasibility determination
   - Format requirements:
     - (a) Scope: major objectives and specifications
     - (b) Task Outline: brief work outline over effort span
     - (c) Deliverables (preliminary and final report)
     - (d) Deliverable Success Markers/Objectives: how success measured for prospective users
     - (e) Kickoff meeting within 30 calendar days of contract start
     - (f) Preliminary report
     - (g) Final report with SF 298 and DD Form 882
   - Human Subjects Research (HSR) disclosure if applicable

9. **Foreign Persons:**
   - Identification of all foreign persons, non-U.S. citizens, dual citizens proposed as direct employees, subcontractors, consultants
   - Countries of origin, visa/work permit types, involvement level
   - For export-controlled technologies: foreign persons limited to public domain work only; tasks must not enable understanding of project objectives
   - Foreign nationals prohibited from key positions (PI, Senior Engineer, etc.)
   - Additional verification may be requested
   - **ITAR Restrictions:** Non-U.S. citizens may only work if "Lawfully Admitted for Permanent Residence" (8 U.S.C. 1101(20)) or "Protected Individual" (8 U.S.C. 1324b(a)(3)); non-compliant proposals may be rejected

10. **Glossary:** Acronyms and abbreviations used
