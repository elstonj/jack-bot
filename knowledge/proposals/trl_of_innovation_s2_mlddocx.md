# TRL of Innovation S2 MLD

## Document Metadata
- Type: Technology Readiness Level (TRL) assessment application
- Client/Agency: U.S. Department of Energy (DOE), Federal Energy Compensation Management (FECM)
- Program/Solicitation: DOE FECM program (methane leak detection/emissions monitoring)
- Date: Submitted October 28, 2024 (modified November 1, 2024)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2 UAS, Quanta3 methane spectrometer, FluxMapper™, Eddy-Covariance (EC) towers with FluxMapper™
- Key Personnel: Not named in document

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies submitted a TRL assessment demonstrating that two integrated methane leak detection systems have achieved Technology Readiness Level 5: (1) the S2 UAS equipped with a Quanta3 methane spectrometer for autonomous, real-time methane plume detection and localization, and (2) Eddy-Covariance towers integrated with FluxMapper™ technology for continuous, high-sensitivity methane flux quantification. Both systems are validated for DOE's methane monitoring and Measurement, Reporting, and Verification (MRV) needs in oil and gas environments.

## Technical Approach

### S2 UAS with Methane Leak Detection (S2 MLD)
- **Platform**: 3-meter wingspan fixed-wing UAS with 100 km operational range
- **Primary Sensor**: Custom-designed Quanta3 methane spectrometer with detection capability down to 3 ppb (parts per billion)
- **Operational Capability**: Beyond Visual Line of Sight (BVLOS) autonomous flight with real-time adaptive path modification based on methane concentration gradients
- **Wind Measurement**: Simultaneous 3D wind field estimation at multiple altitudes for understanding vertical gas transport
- **On-Board Model**: Proprietary algorithm allowing autonomous flight optimization to minimize uncertainty in methane plume characterization
- **Flight Endurance**: Up to 90 minutes
- **Payload Capacity**: 2 kg
- **Operational Efficiency**: Can survey up to 50 sites in one day
- **3D Output**: Provides three-dimensional representation of methane plumes

### Eddy-Covariance Towers with FluxMapper™
- **Core Technology**: High-frequency methane concentration and wind speed measurements integrated with FluxMapper™ spatial analysis
- **Detection Sensitivity**: 0.2 kg h⁻¹ (unmatched sensitivity for discrete emission source detection)
- **Spatial Resolution**: Decameter resolution with FluxMaps™ generated from raw high-frequency data
- **Coverage Area**: Continuous measurements across 4 km² with sub-hourly temporal resolution
- **Instrumentation**: LI-COR gas analyzers, SmartFlux data acquisition systems
- **Measurement Scale**: Well pad-to-reservoir scale for MRV applications
- **Capture Rate**: 95% for emissions detection (vs. 60-80% for traditional eddy-covariance methods)
- **Quality Assurance**: Each FluxMap™ includes spatial uncertainty data

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- **What it is**: Fixed-wing unmanned aircraft system designed for efficient environmental monitoring
- **Proposed Use**: Autonomous methane leak detection and plume localization in oil and gas fields, orphaned/undocumented wells, mines, and other remote environments
- **Key Specifications**: 
  - 3-meter wingspan
  - 100 km range
  - 90-minute flight endurance
  - 2 kg payload capacity
  - BVLOS/FAA compliant
  - Real-time autonomous flight modification
- **Performance Claims**: Can operate over 50 sites daily; accurate 3D plume mapping; robust in harsh weather and rugged terrain

### Quanta3 Methane Spectrometer
- **What it is**: Custom-designed, highly sensitive methane detection sensor
- **Detection Threshold**: 3 ppb methane concentration
- **Integration**: Onboard S2 UAS for real-time detection and autonomous response
- **Capability**: Enables identification of leak magnitude and precise location

### FluxMapper™
- **What it is**: Advanced spatial analysis technology converting raw eddy-covariance high-frequency data into detailed emission maps
- **Proposed Use**: Enhance EC tower capabilities for methane MRV and emissions quantification
- **Key Specifications**:
  - Decameter spatial resolution
  - 0.2 kg h⁻¹ sensitivity
  - 95% capture rate for emissions in project area
  - Provides spatial uncertainty quantification
  - Adapts to varying wind directions and topographies
- **Integration**: Compatible with both tower-based and UAS-based measurements for comprehensive monitoring

### Eddy-Covariance Towers
- **What it is**: Fixed measurement infrastructure equipped with gas analyzers for continuous methane flux quantification
- **Proposed Use**: Baseline continuous monitoring for MRV at oil and gas sites
- **Coverage**: 4 km² continuous measurement area
- **Performance**: Sub-hourly temporal resolution; 95% capture rate with FluxMapper™ integration

## Use Cases & Applications

1. **Oil and Gas Emissions Monitoring**: Detection and quantification of methane leaks from active facilities
2. **Undocumented/Orphaned Wells (UOWs)**: Identifying emissions from unmapped or abandoned wells (noted as supporting greenhouse gas reduction)
3. **Remote and Hard-to-Reach Locations**: Mine emissions, permafrost regions, complex topographies
4. **Measurement, Reporting, and Verification (MRV)**: Supporting regulatory and compliance documentation for methane emissions
5. **Multi-Site Surveying**: Rapid assessment of multiple locations (up to 50 sites/day with S2 UAS)
6. **Environmental Monitoring**: Broader DOE environmental monitoring infrastructure applications
