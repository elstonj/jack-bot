## Executive Summary
This invention disclosure documents modifications to the Apollo small unmanned aircraft system (sUAS) airframe developed during Phase I of a NASA SBIR project. The work resulted in a highly modular, field-swappable airframe design that enables tool-less replacement of payloads and aircraft components, significantly improving operational flexibility and usability in harsh field conditions.

## Technical Approach
- Based on Rocketship Systems Inc.'s Apollo reference design as foundation
- Modified to support forward-mounted, modular payload bay (nose cone) with standardized mechanical and electrical connections to main fuselage
- Designed two payload bay configurations in 3D CAD to accommodate various sensor sizes and types
- Incorporated forward-swept wing design for improved spanwise efficiency and favorable center of gravity positioning for larger forward-mounted payloads
- Tool-less assembly/disassembly methodology enabling rapid field swaps
- Iterative test and rapid prototyping approach

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Apollo Airframe (CAPS Configuration)
**What it is:** Lightweight, hand-launchable sUAS with modular architecture based on Apollo reference design

**Key specifications/features:**
- Compact and rugged for hand launch and landing in unimproved terrain
- Forward-swept wing configuration
- Modular nose cone design (field-swappable without tools)
- Swappable major airframe components (also tool-less)
- Standardized payload bay mechanical and electrical interfaces
- Robust flight management system enabling low-altitude operations in hazardous conditions

**Proposed use:** Multi-platform cooperative missions with interchangeable sensor payloads

## Use Cases & Applications
- Plume monitoring
- Atmospheric remote sensing
- Radiation and particulate monitoring
- Radio frequency monitoring and localization
- Operations in rough terrain, inclement weather, and cold temperature environments
- Field campaigns requiring rapid sensor swapping between cooperative platforms

## Development Timeline
- **06/23/2014:** Initial discussion and conceptual design
- **07/2014:** First sketch/drawing created at Rocketship Systems Inc.
- **12/2014:** 3D CAD design completed
- **12/19/2014:** Construction of battery, nose cone, and autopilot module prototypes completed

## Notable Details

**Innovator Contributions:**
- Coby Leuschke: CAD creation and initial prototype construction
- Jack Elston: Electrical interface design and project management

**Unique/Novel Features:**
- Tool-less, field-swappable nose cone without requiring tools—particularly valuable in cold/inclement weather
- Tool-less swappable components throughout airframe for rapid field replacement of damaged parts
- Modular design enables deployment with multiple sensors that can be cycled during operations
- Streamlined operations for field scientists with focus on data gathering

**Technology Significance:** Classified as "Substantial Advancement in the Art"

**State of Development:** Prototype stage (initial prototypes of battery, nose cone, and autopilot modules completed)

**Competitive Advantage:** Modularity and tool-less operational design not typically considered in most sUAS designs; enables successful deployments in areas previously problematic due to logistics and component damage.
