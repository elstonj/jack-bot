## Executive Summary
This NOAA-WPO NOFO solicits proposals for the **Observations research competition** with approximately **$2.1M total funding available annually** (estimated 7 projects at $300K max per project per year, 2-year maximum award period). The competition seeks innovative sensor and observing technologies/strategies for improving weather observations from the surface through the troposphere, excluding satellite-based sensors. Projects should target Readiness Levels 4-7 and demonstrate clear transition potential to operations, commercialization, or adoption by the operational weather enterprise within 3-6 years.

## Technical Approach & Program Focus

**Core Objectives:**
- Develop, demonstrate, and/or analyze innovative sensor and observing technologies and strategies
- Improve accuracy, reliability, spatial coverage, cost-effectiveness, deployability, safety, and sustainability of weather observations
- Support eventual use by the operational weather enterprise (NOAA, National Mesonet Program, private sector, other government sectors)

**Scope:**
- Weather-related observations targeting the surface through planetary boundary layer/troposphere
- In-situ and ground-based remote sensing observations
- Satellite sensors included only as secondary objective (calibration, validation, integration with in-situ data)
- Encourages engagement with operational weather and commercial sector partners

## Competition Priorities (OBS-1 through OBS-4)

### **Priority OBS-1: Weather Buoy Mitigation Vehicle**
- **Challenge:** Weather buoy outages create observational data gaps impacting maritime weather monitoring and prediction
- **Sought Solution:** High-endurance, modular, portable Uncrewed Surface Vehicle (USV) with:
  - Artificial Intelligence (AI) User Interface (UI)
  - Easy payload integration capability
  - Automated station-keeping via AI
  - Purpose: Rapidly mitigate weather buoy outages until long-term moored buoy solutions deployed

### **Priority OBS-2: Rainfall Estimate Improvement**
- **Challenge:** Specific Differential Phase (KDP)—a critical dual-polarization weather radar parameter—needs improvement for:
  - Rainfall rate estimation (especially in hail presence)
  - Ice crystal growth identification in Dendritic Growth Layer
  - Thunderstorm downdraft location determination
- **Sought Solution:** Improved derived KDP product by:
  - Separating propagation from backscattered components of total differential phase
  - Filtering measurement variance while retaining resolution
  - Identifying return data corrupted by artifacts
  - Ultimately improving rainfall estimates, particularly heavy rainfall

### **Priority OBS-3: SFMR Improvement**
- **Challenge:** Stepped Frequency Microwave Radiometer (SFMR) on reconnaissance aircraft delivers surface wind speed and rain rate estimates along tropical cyclone tracks, but retrievals need improvement
- **Sought Solution:** Software development to:
  - Process SFMR brightness temperature measurements more effectively
  - Deliver more reliable rain rates and surface wind speeds from all SFMR instruments on reconnaissance aircraft
  - Leverage other in-situ and remotely sensed tropical cyclone observations for SFMR validation

### **Priority OBS-4: Tropical Cyclone Reconnaissance Data Advancements**
- **Challenge:** Aircraft reconnaissance missions collect critical tropical cyclone data for forecast improvement, requiring advanced use of NOAA's Unified Forecast System (UFS)
- **Sought Solutions** (using UFS, including Global Forecast System [GFS] and Hurricane Analysis and Forecast System [HAFS]):
  - Improved use of airborne reconnaissance observations already in operational data stream (e.g., super-obbingof high-resolution data)
  - Assimilation of existing experimental tropical cyclone reconnaissance data not yet in operational stream
  - Assimilation of other new/experimental in-situ and remotely sensed data from poorly sampled regions
  - Improved QA/QC procedures for tropical cyclone airborne reconnaissance data

## Key Award Information

**Funding Summary:**
- Total per-year funding: $2.1M
- Expected number of funded projects: 7
- Maximum funding per project per year: $300K
- Maximum award length: 2 years
- Recommended start date: August 1, 2025

**Eligibility:**
- Public/private corporations, partnerships, associations, entities, State/political subdivisions, Tribal governments/agencies
- No foreign applicants outside U.S.
- Federal employees/contractors not eligible for salary funding but may serve as unfunded co-PIs/collaborators
- NOAA federal collaborator costs (max 20% of project total) eligible only for: project-critical travel, equipment, indirect costs, infrastructure, testbed-related costs
- No cost-sharing required

**Award Type:** Grant or Cooperative Agreement (cooperative agreement required if NOAA employees substantially involved, including testbed projects)

## Evaluation Criteria (Total 100 points)

1. **Relevance to Competition Goals (30 points):**
   - Problem/opportunity clarity and alignment with priority(ies)
   - Appropriateness of proposed RL progression
   - Potential impact/benefit/outcomes
   - Quality of stakeholder collaboration and operational support

2. **Technical/Scientific Merit (30 points):**
   - Rigor of proposed methods and solutions
   - Innovation and advancement of field/knowledge
   - Well-defined milestones, outputs, timeline
   - Achievability given resources and timeline
   - Quality of Data/Software Management Plans

3. **Qualifications of Applicants (14 points):**
   - Research team experience, education, training, facilities, resources
   - Demonstrated ability in research, development, transition
   - Publication and dissemination track record
   - Effectiveness of collaborative partnerships

4. **Project Costs (10 points):**
   - Reasonableness, realism, allocability, necessity of requested funds
   - Alignment with project outputs, outcomes, timeline
   - Adequacy of staffing resources
   - Cost-efficiency and benefit-to-cost ratio

5. **Outreach and Education (8 points):**
   - Knowledge-sharing with scientific community/weather enterprise
   - Engagement with target groups, decision-makers, underserved populations
   - Student involvement and training opportunities

6. **Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, Accessibility (DEIA) (8 points):**
   - Potential to benefit underserved/vulnerable populations
   - Broadening participation of underrepresented groups
   - DEIA advancement through project outputs
   - Team commitment to DEIA principles
   - Accommodations for people with disabilities

## Selection Process

- Administrative review for compliance and responsiveness
- Independent peer review (≥3 subject-matter experts per proposal)
- Proposals ranked by average reviewer scores
- Final selections based on rank order and Selection Factors (geographic balance, institutional diversity, duplication check, program priorities, prior performance, partnerships, NEPA adequacy)
- WPO Director is Selecting Official
- Funding recommendations expected May 2025
- Award notifications typically 1 month before start date (Aug 1, 2025), but may be as late as September
