## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies presents the S0 UAS—a small (4 lbs MTOW), modular, electric VTOL or air-deployed fixed-wing UAS designed for 90-minute endurance missions up to 15,000 ft. The document showcases the S0's evolution from NOAA/USAF-selected atmospheric sensing platform to a proposed multi-mission system (including ISR capability), highlighting BST's end-to-end avionics architecture, extensive operational heritage in extreme weather, and field-validated performance exceeding USAF requirements for wind profiling.

## Technical Approach

### S0 Design Philosophy
- Small, simple to operate (<1 day training), low-cost (<$5K for full setup) platform
- Modular design enabling rapid payload swaps and multi-mission configurations
- Vertical takeoff/landing (VTOL) for minimal recovery space (10 ft) or tube-launched/air-deployed option
- Distributed avionics using SwiftCore FMS networked nodes for redundancy and maintainability
- Proprietary SwiftTab GUI focused on mission data gathering with safety-built-in monitoring

### S0 Technical Specifications
- **Weight:** 4 lbs MTOW
- **Wingspan:** 54.6 inches
- **Flight Time:** 90 minutes nominal (extrapolated 78-100 min; 62-80 min with VTOL landing)
- **Ceiling:** 15,000 ft AGL
- **Cruise Speed:** 40 mph; Dash: >100 mph
- **Climb Rate:** 2000-3000 ft/min (vertical transition)
- **Max Winds Endured:** 50 mph
- **Communications Range:** Up to 100 miles
- **Payload Capacity:** 100 grams
- **Launch Options:** VTOL or tube-launched (air-deployed from P-3 Orion)

### Advanced Systems & Capabilities
- **Wind Sensing:** SwiftFlow 3D wind probe calibrated via 9th-order polynomial fit using 3D-printed components; tunnel calibration with ~10⁶ total measurements
- **Avionics:** SwiftCore distributed architecture; onboard machine vision for GPS-denied navigation and automated emergency landing
- **Data Formats:** BUFR, GRIB, netCDF standards for meteorological compatibility
- **Autonomy:** Fully autonomous operation with minimal training; open command/control protocols (interoperable with Vigilant Spirit, TAK)
- **Sensors:** Atmospheric thermodynamics, EO/IR cameras, trace gas sampling, profiling sensors (100g payload)

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS (Three Variants Described)

**S0 Air-Deployed (Atmospheric Science)**
- Deployed from P-3 Orion "Hurricane Hunter" aircraft
- Designed for extreme atmospheric conditions, heavy precipitation operation
- NOAA and USAF competitively selected
- Adaptation to Common Launch Tube (CLT) possible

**S0 VTOL (Atmospheric Profiling)**
- Twin-engined VTOL variant designed under USAF contract
- User requirements: <1 day training, <$5K cost, profiles to 15,000 ft AGL
- Science requirements: ≥40 min flight duration for profiling, rapid and accurate results
- Temperature, relative humidity, static pressure, wind measurement
- Minimal recovery footprint (10 ft), vertical landing with "safe height"

**S0-ISR (Proposed)**
- Proposed variant for Blue sUAS 2.0 competition (ISR missions)
- Advantages over multirotors: superior duration, range, inclement weather ops, loiter capability
- 100+ mph dash speed for rapid ingress/egress
- VTOL simplicity with extended endurance vs. multirotor platforms

### S2 UAS (Multi-Mission Fixed Wing)
- **MTOW:** 20.5 lbs
- **Wingspan:** 10 ft
- **Range:** 63 miles with max payload
- **Flight Time:** 90 min with max payload
- **Ceiling:** 15,000 ft
- **Max Winds:** 30 mph
- **Payload:** 5 lbs in 8" diameter × 25" length nose cone; 50 W power available
- **Geotagging Accuracy:** <1 m typical
- **Certifications:** NASA, NOAA, DOE approved; BVLOS proven
- **Features:** S2 VTOL variant with solar panels (4-6 hr endurance), machine vision automated landing, terrain/wind-aware flight planning, rugged (-40 to 120°F), modular field-swappable payload system

### SwiftCore FMS (Flight Management System)
- Proprietary, end-to-end avionics system
- Distributed networked nodes (not Dronecode-based)
- Key capabilities: redundancy, field maintenance, automated part lifetime tracking, expandability, third-party integration
- Superior estimation and control schemes vs. Dronecode alternatives
- Advanced control algorithms for fixed-wing aircraft handling

### SwiftTab GUI (User Interface)
- Proprietary, intuitive design focused on mission data gathering
- Electronic centralized aircraft monitor (ECAM) system with fault trees
- Stand-alone simulator with fault injection capability for training

### E2 UAS (Autonomous Inspection)
- Multi-rotor system for infrastructure inspection
- **Endurance:** 35 minutes
- **Payload:** 6.5 lbs, field-swappable
- **Capabilities:** Combined imagery and LIDAR, wind turbine inspection in heavy rain/high winds
- Adaptable for DOD missions

### Supporting Systems
- **SwiftFlow 3D Wind Probe:** Heated pitot, rain mitigation for air data
- **Machine Vision:** Real-time semantic segmentation, GPS-denied navigation, automated landing plan generation
- **ML Predictive Maintenance:** Fault prediction/detection; web-based portal for flight analysis and maintenance recommendations

## Key Results (Field Testing Overview)

### S0 VTOL Flight Testing Summary
**Test Sites:** SGP (12 flights, 5.8 hrs) and OSU (4 flights, 1.7 hrs)

**Measured Performance:**
- Extrapolated max TOF: 78–100 minutes
- With VTOL landing: 62–80 minutes
- Flown in up to 38 mph winds with no control/stability issues
- Climbed to 15,000 ft in ~12.7 minutes (6 m/s rate) or ~19 minutes (4 m/s rate, optimal for wind measurement)

**Comparison to Standard Instruments (Radiosonde, Tower, Surface):**
- **Temperature Bias:** S0-1 and S0-2 showed biases of 1.03–1.70°C
- **Relative Humidity Bias:** S0-1 (-1.69 to ~0%), S0-2 (-11.34 to 2.5%) — ongoing calibration work near Boulder with NCAR
- **Static Pressure Bias:** S0-1 (-0.25 to -1.96 hPa), S0-2 (-2.13 to -1.94 hPa)
- **Wind Measurement:** Ongoing refinement; aircraft exceeding USAF performance needs

**Validation:**
- Concept of Operations (CONOPS) validated
- User interface validated
- Data file creation confirmed (BUFR, GRIB, netCDF formats)
- Aircraft performance exceeds USAF requirements for time-of-flight, max ceiling, and high-wind operation

### VTOL Work Status
- Vertical transition and VTOL landing tests planned
- All SGP flights conducted at VTOL weight with VTOL propulsion system
- Wind tunnel calibration of SwiftFlow probe completed
- Further calibration work ongoing with NCAR
