# NSF Small Business Innovation Research Program Phase I Solicitation FY-2014

## Document Metadata
- Type: Program Solicitation
- Client/Agency: National Science Foundation (NSF), Directorate for Engineering, Industrial Innovation and Partnerships
- Program/Solicitation: Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase I, FY-2014; Solicitation NSF 13-546
- Date: June 2013 (issued); Full Proposal Deadline: June 11, 2013
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is the NSF SBIR Phase I solicitation for FY-2014, inviting eligible small business concerns to submit proposals for high-risk, high-quality scientific, engineering, or science/engineering education research with significant commercial and public benefit potential. The program offers up to $150,000 per Phase I project with approximately 200 awards anticipated from $30 million total available funding.

## Key Program Information

### Eligible Topics
- Biological and Chemical Technologies (BC)
- Education Applications (EA)
- Electronics, Information and Communication Technologies (EI)
- Nanotechnology, Advanced Materials, and Manufacturing (NM)

### Eligibility Requirements
- Proposing organization must qualify as a small business concern per 13 CFR 121
- Minimum two-thirds of research (by budget) must be performed by the small business; one-third may be outsourced to consultants/subcontractors
- Principal Investigator must have primary employment (51%+) with the small business and commit minimum one calendar month to Phase I
- PI limit: One proposal per person
- Organization limit: Maximum two Phase I proposals per organization during this cycle (SBIR + STTR combined)
- Cannot be majority-owned by venture capital companies, hedge funds, or private equity firms
- Must register in: System for Award Management (SAM), SBA Company Registry, and have DUNS number

### Award Details
- Award Type: Fixed amount awards
- Phase I Duration: 6 months
- Maximum Budget: $150,000 per proposal
- Estimated Number of Awards: ~200
- Total Anticipated Funding: $30,000,000
- Award Effective Date: January 1, 2014
- Notification Timeline: 4-6 months from submission deadline

## Proposal Preparation Requirements

### Submission Details
- Submission Window: May 11 – June 11, 2013
- Deadline: 5 p.m. proposer's local time
- Method: NSF FastLane system (electronic submission required)
- Proposals outside submission window returned without review
- Electronic signature required from Authorized Organizational Representative (AOR)

### Required Proposal Components
1. **Cover Sheet and Certification** – Topic and subtopic must be completed; electronic signature required
2. **Project Summary** (1 page maximum)
   - Overview (≤200 words)
   - Intellectual Merit statement (≤200 words)
   - Broader/Commercial Impact statement (≤200 words)
   - Key words and subtopic name
3. **Project Description** (15-page maximum) with 7 required parts:
   - Part 1: Identification and Significance of Innovation
   - Part 2: Background and Phase I Technical Objectives
   - Part 3: Phase I Research Plan (technical discussion, approach, objectives, sequence of experiments)
   - Part 4: Commercial Potential (3-5 pages addressing market opportunity, company/team, product/competition, financing/revenue model)
   - Part 5: Consultants and Subawards/Subcontracts
   - Part 6: Equivalent or Overlapping Proposals/Awards to Other Federal Agencies
   - Part 7: Lineage of Innovation (connection to previous NSF-funded research)
4. **References Cited** (comprehensive list including patents)
5. **Biographical Sketches** (max 2 pages per person for PI and key personnel)
6. **Budget, Sub-budgets, and Budget Justification** (max $150,000 total)
7. **Current and Pending Support** (all ongoing and proposed projects for PI and senior personnel)
8. **Facilities, Equipment and Other Resources**
9. **Supplementary Documents** (if applicable):
   - Company Commercialization History (required if company certified previous Phase II awards)
   - Data Management Plan (required; must state "All data generated in this SBIR Phase I project is considered proprietary")
   - SBA Company Registry Documentation (required for all applicants)
   - Letters of Support for Technology (optional; max 3 letters; demonstrate market validation)
   - Post Doc Mentoring Plan (if postdoctoral scholars funded)
   - Human Subjects/Vertebrate Animals documentation (if applicable)

### Budget Requirements
- **Total Budget Limit:** $150,000 (Phase I)
- **Personnel:** PI must be budgeted minimum 1 month; list senior personnel with time commitments and dollar amounts
- **Consultant Rates:** Maximum $600/day (8-hour day); cannot exceed daily equivalent of Executive Level IV Federal employee
- **Grantee Workshop:** Mandatory 2-day attendance in DC area; budget ~$2,000 per person (up to 2 persons) for conference registration and travel
- **Indirect Costs + Fringe Benefits:** Limited to maximum 150% of direct salaries and wages
- **Prohibited Expenditures:**
  - Permanent equipment
  - Patent expenses
  - Foreign travel
  - Tuition costs
- **Reasonable Fee (Profit):** Maximum 7% of total indirect and direct project costs
- **Cost Sharing:** Voluntary committed cost sharing is PROHIBITED

### Consultant Requirements
- Services must be justified within proposal context
- Must provide expertise, organizational affiliation, and contribution details
- Must include signed statement confirming availability, time commitment, role, and agreed consulting rate
- Letters from consultants/subcontractors uploaded with Budget Justification, NOT as supplemental documents

### Non-Responsive Proposals Returned Without Review
Proposals missing any of the following will be returned without review:
- Submission after 5:00 p.m. deadline (proposer's local time)
- Missing required components in FastLane
- Budget exceeding $150,000
- Missing Company Commercialization History (if applicable)
- Documents in "Additional Single Copy Documents" module
- Collaborative proposals (simultaneous submissions from different organizations)
- Lack of technical/commercial substance
- Outside scope of designated topic/subtopic
- Not containing research in science, engineering, or education
- Unacceptable objectives (systems studies, market research, commercial development of existing products, straightforward engineering design, laboratory evaluations, incremental improvements, evolutionary optimization)

## Merit Review Criteria
- **Intellectual Merit:** Advance knowledge; research quality; innovation
- **Broader Impacts:** Commercial value, societal benefit, achievement of desired societal outcomes

## Phase I Objectives
- **Primary:** Investigate feasibility of proposed scientific/technical innovation; determine if innovation has sufficient technical and commercial merit for Phase II
- **Secondary:** Assess commercial feasibility
- **Deliverable:** Technical report summarizing experimental and theoretical accomplishments; serves as basis for Phase II proposal

## Notable Details

### Pre-Submission Communication
- Proposers encouraged to contact cognizant program officer via email before deadline to gauge responsiveness
- Should provide 1-2 page executive summary addressing: company/team experience with previous SBIR awards, market opportunity, technology/innovation, competition
- Communication becomes difficult as deadline nears

### Phase I vs. Phase II
- Phase I is feasibility study (6 months, $150,000 max)
- Successful Phase I projects eligible for Phase II funding (not specified in this document)
- Proposals declining or returned without review under same solicitation are NOT eligible for reconsideration; may be resubmitted under subsequent solicitation after revisions

### Proprietary Information Protection
- Proposers may mark proprietary/commercially sensitive information
- Check box on cover page and clearly mark proprietary sections (asterisks, underlining, different font)
- Entire proposal should not be marked proprietary
- Government will not release properly identified and marked proprietary data to extent permitted by law

### Fraud, Waste, and Abuse (FWA) Notification
- Requirement for all proposals
- SBIR/STTR Funding Agreement Certification required upon notification of award recommendation

### Related Requirements
- NSF requires compliance with revised NSF Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide (PAPPG) NSF 13-1
- Merit review criteria updated based on National Science Board report on review criteria revision
- Affects Project Summary and Project Description sections; also impacts Annual and Final Reports
