# BST CAI Letter of Support

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: Colorado Advanced Industries (CAI) Accelerator Program
- Program/Solicitation: Colorado Advanced Industries Accelerator Program
- Date: March 1, 2015
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Small unmanned aerial system (sUAS) for soil moisture mapping
- Key Personnel: Prof. Albin J. Gasiewski (CU Boulder), Drs. Maciej Stachura and Jack Elston (BST)

## Executive Summary
Prof. Albin J. Gasiewski of CU Boulder's Center for Environmental Technology provides strong endorsement of Black Swift Technologies' commercialization efforts for a small unmanned aerial system capable of soil moisture mapping. Gasiewski, who has been collaborating with BST's technical team since June 2013 on microwave sensor development, advocates for CAI program support to accelerate commercialization and market introduction of this technology.

## Technical Approach
- Development of a miniaturized microwave sensor package for small unmanned systems
- Sensor derived from larger NASA manned-aircraft systems, scaled down for sUAS deployment
- Collaboration between CU Boulder and BST on sensor design and integration
- Requires flight testing and product demonstration to generate repeatable case studies using real flight data

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Small UAS for Soil Moisture Mapping**
- Purpose: Remote soil moisture data collection from small unmanned platforms
- Technical basis: Miniaturized microwave sensor technology
- Development timeline: Collaboration began June 2013
- Current status (as of March 2015): Ready for flight testing and product demonstration phase

## Use Cases & Applications
- Precision agriculture
- Water management and hydrological management
- Flash flood prediction and forecasting
- Environmental monitoring and disaster management
- Weather forecasting support
- Scientific hydrology applications

## Notable Details
- Prof. Gasiewski's credentials: Former NOAA/ESRL Chief of Microwave Systems Development Branch (8 years), MIT-trained, Georgia Tech faculty experience, extensive NASA and USDA collaboration
- Prior precedent: 2007 NASA flight experiment using larger manned-aircraft sensor to collect pre-storm soil moisture data in northern Texas; data successfully predicted flash flooding confirmed by ground sensors
- Market readiness: Letter identifies "extensive interest" from early adopters in hydrological and soil moisture science communities, with NASA scientists expressing interest
- Key commercialization barrier identified: Need for repeatable case studies and user community communication
- BST team assessment: "Continually impressed with both the technical abilities and commercialization potential"
