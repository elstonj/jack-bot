## Executive Summary
This section describes Black Swift Technologies' facilities and resources for a USDA SBIR project focused on high-resolution soil moisture mapping using unmanned aircraft. BST details its Boulder, CO facility capabilities and partnerships with USDA's Limited Irrigation Research Farm near Greeley, Colorado, which provides extensive ground instrumentation and existing research infrastructure for the proposed work.

## Facilities Described

### Black Swift Technologies Facility
- **Location:** Boulder, CO
- **Size:** 1,600 sq. ft.
- **Capabilities:**
  - Prototyping, simulating, and testing electrical subsystems and avionics boards
  - Full suite of atmospheric and aircraft simulation tools for mission scenario analysis
  - Multiple full autopilot systems available for testing electrical and software interfaces
  - Manufacturing capability for integrating prototype and production unmanned aircraft
  - Sensor and avionics integration tools
  - Computing resources for simulation
  - Meeting space for collaborative work

### Limited Irrigation Research Farm (LIRF)
- **Location:** Near Greeley, Colorado
- **Size:** 16 hectares
- **Operator:** USDA Agricultural Research Service Water Management Unit (ARS-WMU)
- **Focus:** Crop water use research (e.g., corn) under limited and full irrigation conditions
- **Application:** Remote sensing techniques for water management and water savings improvement

## Existing Instrumentation at LIRF

The site includes comprehensive ground-based measurement systems:

1. **Soil Water Content Monitoring:** Volumetric sensors, bulk soil electrical conductivity sensors, and soil temperature sensors installed at 3-4 depths spanning the crop root zone
2. **Ground-Based Radiometry:** VIS, NIR, and TIR measurements
3. **Leaf Area Meter:** For vegetation analysis
4. **Onsite Weather Station:** Continuous atmospheric monitoring
5. **Infrared Thermometry:** Crop temperature measurements
6. **Gravimetric Soil Sampling:** Regular ground-truth sampling
7. **Neutron Probe:** Weekly interrogation to 1.5-2.0 m depth for volumetric water content; used to identify deep percolation and calibrate continuous soil water content sensors (hourly data collection)
8. **Plant Phenology Monitoring:** Weekly identification of crop growth stages and crop height measurements

## Key Partnership
- **Collaboration:** Dr. José Chávez (Co-PI) collaborates with USDA ARS LIRF scientists on existing crop water use research
- **Data Integration:** All existing LIRF data will be available and integrated with measurements from the proposed soil moisture mapping UAS

## Notable Details

- BST's on-site manufacturing and integration capabilities support rapid prototyping and iteration
- The partnership leverages established USDA research infrastructure and expertise in irrigation water management
