# AF X23.4 CSO Direct-to-Phase-II Solicitation (Amendment 2)

## Document Metadata
- Type: SBIR/STTR Solicitation - Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO)
- Client/Agency: Department of the Air Force (DAF)
- Program/Solicitation: AF Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Program; Direct-to-Phase-II (D2P2); Topic numbers AFX234-DCSO1 and AFX234-DCSO2
- Date: November 10, 2022 (Amendment 2); October 27, 2022 (Amendment 1)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None mentioned in this document
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This document is the official solicitation announcement for the Department of the Air Force's SBIR Commercial Solutions Opening (CSO) Direct-to-Phase-II program. It invites small business firms to submit proposals for innovative commercial technologies that can create new DAF solutions or fill capability gaps. The solicitation establishes approximately 104 anticipated awards and defines all administrative, technical, and contractual requirements for proposal submission and evaluation.

## Document Structure & Key Content

### Program Overview
- **Program Goal**: Stimulate technological innovation, strengthen small business role in meeting DoD R&D needs, foster minority and disadvantaged business participation, and increase commercial application of DoD-supported R&D results
- **Award Type**: Direct-to-Phase-II contracts (skipping Phase I)
- **Expected Awards**: Approximately 104 awards anticipated
- **Submission Portal**: Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal (DSIP) at https://www.dodsbirsttr.mil/
- **Submission Deadline**: November 22, 2022, 12:00 p.m. ET
- **Proposal Period**: October 25 - November 22, 2022

### Strategic Alignment
The solicitation targets alignment with six Air Force Strategic Capabilities:
1. Air Superiority
2. Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (ISR)
3. Rapid Global Strike
4. Command and Control
5. Responsibilities to organize, train, and equip Airmen
6. Blue Sky (unconsidered strategic areas)

And six Space Force Strategic Capabilities:
1. Space Security
2. Combat Power Projection
3. Space Mobility and Logistics
4. Information Mobility
5. Space Domain Awareness
6. Responsibilities to organize, train, and equip Guardians

### Eligibility Requirements

**Small Business Concern (SBC) Requirements:**
- Organized for profit with U.S. place of business
- Operates primarily within U.S. or makes significant contribution to U.S. economy
- 50%+ directly owned and controlled by U.S. citizens or permanent resident aliens
- No more than 500 employees (including affiliates)

**Principal Investigator Requirements:**
- Primary employment must be with the small business firm at time of award and during performance
- "Primary employment" = more than 50% of PI's time spent with the firm
- Deviations must be approved in writing by contracting officer prior to proposal submission

**Work Performance Requirements:**
- Minimum 50% of research/analytical work must be performed by proposing firm (measured by direct and indirect costs, excluding profit)
- All R&D work must be performed in the United States, except in rare circumstances where specific materials/supplies are unavailable domestically (requires DAF approval)

**Performance Benchmarks for Repeat Applicants:**
- Companies with >20 Phase I awards in past 5 FY must maintain Phase II-to-Phase I ratio of at least 0.25
- Companies with >15 Phase II awards in past 10 FY must have: (a) average $100,000+ sales/investments per Phase II award, OR (b) patents equal to 15%+ of Phase II awards received

### Proposal Content Requirements
The solicitation requires proposals to address:
- Scientific and technical merit
- Technical feasibility
- Commercialization potential
- Alignment with DAF strategic capabilities
- Performance metrics and deliverables
- Regulatory compliance plans (if applicable)

### Key Administrative Requirements

**Registrations Required:**
1. **DSIP (Defense SBIR/STTR Innovation Portal)**: Login.gov authentication required; accessible only within U.S.
2. **SAM.gov**: Active registration required before award; firms must use Unique Entity ID (UEI) rather than DUNS number (effective April 4, 2022)

**Proposal Certifications & Compliance:**
- No classified proposals accepted
- Fraud, Waste and Abuse (FWA) training required for Phase I and Direct-to-Phase-II proposals
- Must disclose prior, current, or pending support of similar proposals
- Proprietary information must be clearly marked; Work Plan must be non-proprietary
- Conflict of interest disclosures required
- Facility clearance required if Phase II work necessitates classified activities

**Special Research Compliance** (if applicable):
- **Human Subjects Research**: Must comply with 32 C.F.R. § 219 "Common Rule," 10 U.S.C. § 980, DoDI 3216.02; Federal Assurance of Compliance required; IRB approval required before funding
- **Animal Research**: Must comply with DoDI 3216.01, 9 C.F.R. parts 1-4, NAS Guide for Care & Use of Laboratory Animals; IACUC approval and secondary DoD veterinarian approval required
- **Recombinant DNA**: Must comply with NIH Guidelines for Research Involving Recombinant DNA Molecules (January 2011); Institutional Biosafety Committee approval required
- **Unmanned Aircraft Systems (UAS)**: Must comply with DoD memos (August 18, 2018 and November 16, 2018); exemption required for operational use; approval process can take up to 2 months

### Foreign Investment & National Security
- Firms must self-report and disclose foreign investment in ownership or influence over U.S. entities
- Foreign nationals subject to ITAR (22 CFR 120-130) and EAR (15 CFR 730-799) export control restrictions
- Participation in foreign talent recruitment programs from countries of concern (China, North Korea, Russia, Iran) or listed in NDAA Section 1286 is prohibited
- Foreign affiliations and foreign government relationships must be disclosed

### Data Rights & IP
**Significant Change from Prior Policy:**
- Government receives **unlimited rights** in technical data/software generated under SBIR/STTR contracts for:
  - Form, fit, and function data
  - Data necessary for installation, operation, maintenance, training
- **SBIR data rights** apply to all other technical data/software for 20 years from contract award
- (Prior policy provided only royalty-free license for Government purposes during 20-year period)

### Evaluation & Award Process
- Proposals evaluated on scientific merit, technical merit, and commercialization potential
- No FAR Part 15 debriefings provided (competitive procedures)
- Feedback provided only to "Not Selected" and "Selectable/Not Funded" proposals (limited to one feedback per proposal)
- Classified proposals not accepted
- Proposals exceeding maximum specified SBIR funding will not be considered

## Notable Details

1. **No BST Products Referenced**: This is a solicitation document, not a proposal or report, so no Black Swift Technologies systems are mentioned.

2. **Emphasis on Commercial Solutions**: The CSO specifically encourages conversion of commercial (non-Defense) technologies to military applications, with potential Phase III transition to civilian/commercial markets.

3. **Dual-Use Focus**: Phase III is explicitly designed for "Dual Use Applications" expanding adapted non-Defense commercial solutions for governmental and civilian users.

4. **Compressed Timeline**: The solicitation emphasizes rapid proposal-to-award lead times through streamlined contracting processes, requiring strict administrative compliance.

5. **Foreign Ownership Scrutiny**: Strong emphasis on identifying and managing foreign investment, talent recruitment program participation, and export controls to protect national security.

6. **Data Rights Enhancement**: Notable shift from 20-year royalty-free license to unlimited Government rights in specified technical data, expanding DoD access to innovations developed under SBIR funding.

7. **Accessibility**: DSIP limited to U.S.-based submissions; strong authentication requirements (Login.gov) implemented as security measure.
