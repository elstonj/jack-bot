# Statement of Work: LICOR 840A Integration into Black Swift S2 sUAS for ELEVATE Mission

## Document Metadata
- Type: Statement of Work (SOW)
- Client/Agency: NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL)
- Program/Solicitation: ELEVATE Earth Venture Suborbital Mission (EVS-4 proposal preparation); Mini ELEVATE FY21 campaign
- Date: July 22, 2020
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Black Swift S2 sUAS
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (editor), Ryan Pavlick (JPL contact)

## Executive Summary
JPL contracted BST to integrate a LI-COR 840A CO2 gas analyzer into the Black Swift S2 sUAS in preparation for a prototype sUAS airborne campaign in Costa Rica. The mission aims to map elevated CO2 concentration gradients around volcanic sites to support the ELEVATE Earth Venture Suborbital mission concept, which investigates tropical forest ecosystem responses to elevated CO2 using Costa Rican volcanoes as natural laboratories.

## Technical Approach
- **Payload Integration**: Custom engineering to integrate the LI-COR 840A gas analyzer within the Black Swift S2 sUAS nosecone
- **System Preparation**: Assembly of integrated airframe with modifications for autonomous flight
- **Calibration**: Manufacturer-standard calibration of LI-COR 840A including calibration gas refill
- **Documentation**: Preparation of Flight Test Plan and Flight Readiness Review presentation in collaboration with JPL
- **Regulatory**: Documentation for Costa Rican civil aviation authority permit requests (conducted by subcontractor NTCR in San Jose, Costa Rica)
- **Standards Compliance**: FAA Part 107 integration standards

## Products & Capabilities Described

**Black Swift S2 sUAS**
- Small unmanned aerial system capable of carrying scientific payload (LI-COR gas analyzer)
- Customizable nosecone for instrument integration
- Autonomous flight capable
- Previous deployment history in Costa Rica (January 2018) demonstrating system TRL and logistical maturity

## Use Cases & Applications

**ELEVATE Mission Context**
- **Science Objective**: Map distribution of elevated CO2 gradients in tropical forests to compare ecosystem responses across structure, composition, and function
- **Study Sites**: 8 volcanoes in Costa Rican rainforests identified for investigation; Rincon de la Vieja National Park selected as prototype site
- **Campaign Scope**: FY21 prototype deployment at one volcano focusing on CO2 characterization
- **Scientific Context**: Addresses the "beta-response"—the response of tropical forests to elevated CO2 over decadal scales; addresses fundamental uncertainty in terrestrial ecosystem science and Earth System climate projections

## Background & Context

**Prior NASA Review Feedback**
- ELEVATE proposed under EVS-3 solicitation but not selected despite strong reviews
- Primary reviewer concern: lack of data demonstrating elevated CO2 concentration exists in forests near target volcanoes
- NASA HQ debrief indicated successful EVS-4 proposal would require field-demonstrated map of elevated CO2 data at candidate sites

**Prior Fieldwork**
- March 2020: Ground campaign at Rincon de la Vieja measuring below-canopy CO2 flux and ecosystem heterogeneity (supported by Spontaneous RTD funding)
- January 2018: Previous sUAS/LI-COR deployment in Costa Rica that mapped limited area and demonstrated system TRL

**Scientific Rationale**
- Volcanoes continuously emit elevated CO2 into forests for hundreds of years (established volcanology principle)
- Eight Costa Rican volcanoes provide natural gradient studies of ecosystem response to elevated CO2
