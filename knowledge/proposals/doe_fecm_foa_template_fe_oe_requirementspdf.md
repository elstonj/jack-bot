## Executive Summary

This DOE FECM Funding Opportunity Announcement solicits applications for advancing cost-effective technologies toward commercialization to characterize undocumented orphaned wells (UOWs) and provide remediation solutions. The FOA addresses three complementary technical areas: advanced remediation techniques for wellbore plugging, non-invasive wellbore characterization, and long-term methane emissions monitoring. The program aligns with DOE's goal to eliminate methane emissions from carbon-based fuel supply chains by 2030.

## Background & Problem Statement

**Scope of the Problem:**
- Estimated 126,000+ documented orphaned wells and 310,000-800,000 undocumented orphaned wells across 32 U.S. oil and gas producing states
- Orphaned wells: unmonitored wells or those abandoned pre-1985 using obsolete techniques
- UOWs: wells unknown to regulatory agencies or requiring further investigation for precise location and characterization
- Contaminants of concern: methane, arsenic, hydrogen sulfide, benzene, volatile organic compounds (VOCs)
- UOWs can leak for years/decades undetected
- Traditional aerial survey methods (e.g., magnetometry) often ineffective on older, deteriorated wells
- Many older wells have had metal casings removed and were backfilled with whatever materials were available

**Current Challenges:**
- Highly skewed distribution of emissions from UOWs creates difficulty in prioritization
- Geographic factors, site access limitations, and environmental sensitivities constrain traditional technologies
- Limited adoption of existing commercial remediation technologies due to cost, material availability, and operational factors
- Lack of robust non-invasive characterization tools
- Scale of remediation needed is massive

## Areas of Interest & Technical Requirements

### AOI 1: Advanced Remediation Techniques for UOW Boreholes
**Funding**: ~$10,200,000 Federal (20% cost share required)

**Objectives:**
- Develop novel, cost-effective, environmentally beneficial, and robust plugging & abandonment (P&A) strategies
- Create low-cost materials for permanent plugging that work with variable geometries and damaged wellbores
- Focus on biofilm and biochar technologies, excluding common cementitious materials
- Achieve zero emissions profile at well sites

**Required Technical Elements:**
- Current state-of-the-art P&A practices for UOWs and differentiation of proposed technology
- Explanation of current technology readiness and specific field validation elements
- **Mandatory**: Defined well site location with letter of commitment from industry/State/Federal/regulatory partner at time of application
- Technology footprint discussion with overhead layout and equipment specifications
- Methane emissions monitoring plan (pre- and post-deployment per DOI Methane Measurement Guidelines)
- Preliminary economic assessment including:
  - Operating/maintenance costs
  - Utilities, supplies, materials, labor, waste handling costs
  - Impact of utility costs, natural gas prices, supply market costs, disposal costs
  - Direct comparison to competing technologies
- Statement of likely challenges and mitigation strategies
- Statement of Project Objectives (SOPO) with detailed task descriptions
- Project Management Plan (PMP) with technical scope, budget, schedule baselines, milestones, organizational structure, roles/responsibilities, communication approach, decision-making processes

**Technology Readiness:**
- Beginning TRL: 4-5
- Ending TRL: 6 (by project completion)

**Activities NOT of Interest (Non-Responsive):**
- Laboratory-scale development only
- No letter of commitment from relevant partner
- Below-requested starting TRL without lab validation
- Off-the-shelf or commercially available technologies
- Common/general cementitious materials for P&A
- Computer simulations/modeling without field deployment and prototype validation
- No field deployment validation

### AOI 2: UOW Wellbore Characterization
**Funding**: ~$2,400,000 Federal (20% cost share required)

**Objectives:**
- Develop non-invasive, surface-deployable technologies for wellbore characterization
- Determine wellbore conditions without entry into the wellbore
- Identify when novel P&A techniques are required
- Address cost, ease of installation, surface requirements, subsurface limitations, training needs

**Required Technical Elements:**
- Current state-of-the-art well characterization technologies and differentiation
- Explanation of current technology state and field validation aspects
- **Mandatory**: Defined well site location with letter of commitment at time of application
- Technology footprint with overhead, side-view, and/or subsurface layout
- Preliminary economic assessment:
  - Operating/maintenance costs
  - Utilities, supplies, materials, labor
  - Variable cost impacts (utilities, gas prices, supply costs)
  - Direct comparison to competing technologies
- Clearly defined challenges and mitigation strategies
- SOPO with detailed task descriptions
- PMP with baselines, milestones, organizational structure, communication, decision-making

**Technology Readiness:**
- Beginning TRL: 4-5
- Ending TRL: 6

**Activities NOT of Interest (Non-Responsive):**
- Laboratory-scale development only
- No letter of commitment from relevant partner
- Below-requested starting TRL without lab validation
- Invasive operations (wellbore entry for data collection)
- Computer simulations/modeling without field deployment
- Applications not addressing scalability and deployment strategies

**Funding**: ~$2,400,000 Federal (20% cost share required)

**Objectives:**

## Notable Details

**Strategic Alignment:**
- Part of DOE's Office of Resource Sustainability's Methane Mitigation Technologies Program
- Supports decarbonization goal of zero emissions from UOWs by 2030
- Consistent with DOI Methane Measurement Guidelines
- Leverages DOE CATALOG Consortium work on non-invasive well identification

**Emphasis on Field Validation:**
- All three AOIs require defined well sites with partner letters of commitment at proposal submission (non-negotiable)
- Laboratory-scale work alone is non-responsive
- TRL progression from 4-5 to 6 requires actual field prototype testing
- Pre- and post-deployment methane emissions measurement mandatory (per DOI guidelines)

**Economic Competitiveness:**
- All proposals must include detailed economic assessments
- Direct cost comparison to existing competing technologies required
- Scalability analysis across local, regional, and national scales mandatory
- Must demonstrate path to commercialization

**Technology Focus:**
- AOI 1: Biofilm, biochar, and other novel non-cementitious materials preferred
- AOI 2: Non-invasive, surface-based characterization (no wellbore entry)
- AOI 3: Autonomous, real-time, cost-effective monitoring solutions
