- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (document editor), Dr. James Pinto (research team – climate modeling/weather prediction), Ed Kase (EKase Consulting – business development), Ben Miller (Center of Excellence for Advanced Technology Aerial Firefighting – advisory role)

## Executive Summary

Black Swift Technologies proposes to commercialize a small UAS (S0) equipped with meteorological sensors for wildland fire support, operating in partnership with NCAR to improve weather prediction models. The system targets federal and state fire agencies, with projected cumulative revenue of $843,000 over three years post-Phase II, and plans to expand into adjacent markets including environmental monitoring and advanced air mobility applications.

## Market Feasibility and Competition Strategy

### Target Markets

**Primary Market: Wildland Fire Suppression**
- Federal coordination through National Interagency Fire Center (NIFC), National Wildfire Coordinating Group (NWCG)
- Participating agencies: USDA Forest Service, DOI (BLM, NPS, Bureau of Indian Affairs, USFWS), DOD, NOAA
- State-level customers: CalFire, Colorado agencies
- 2022 market data: 68,988 wildfires; 7.58M acres burned nationally

**Secondary/Adjacent Markets:**
- **UAS Industry Applications**: Cooperative control/teaming, beyond-visual-line-of-sight (BVLOS) operations, advanced air mobility (AAM) in mountainous and urban corridors
- **NASA Earth Science Programs**: 3D-Winds mission, Tropospheric Chemistry Program (TCP), Applied Sciences Air Quality Program, CALIPSO, Aura, CATS, OCO-2/3, Earth Ventures airborne campaigns
- **Other Government Agencies**: USGS, NOAA, DOE, NSF, and funded entities (UCAR, NCAR, Argonne National Laboratory ARM group, NSF-funded NEON)
- **Environmental Monitoring**: Volcano monitoring, weather modeling, soil moisture assessment, methane detection

### Competitive Advantages

1. **Partnership with NCAR**: UAS data collection augments satellite/aircraft observations in areas of model uncertainty; improves weather prediction accuracy through data assimilation
2. **Operations in High-Risk Environments**: Flies when crewed aircraft grounded; night operations provide updated forecasts for morning briefings
3. **Technology Platform Advantages**:
   - S0 UAS: low cost, rugged, VTOL capability
   - SwiftCore FMS + SwiftPilot autopilot: autonomous takeoff/landing for broader user base
   - SwiftTab user interface: simplified operations
   - Real-time data dissemination via Team Awareness Kit (TAK), COTAK, WFTAK

4. **Heritage & Experience**: 10+ years of UAS development; proven operations in extreme conditions (high-altitude, arctic, desert, corrosive particulates, turbulence); existing customer base with NCAR, USGS, NOAA, DOE
5. **Purpose-Built Scientific Platform**: Unlike general UAS, BST systems designed specifically for demanding atmospheric research in extreme conditions

**UAS Demand:**
- 2022 NICC received 131 UAS resource requests; 51 filled, 59 unable to fill – indicates unmet demand

**Broader Earth Observation UAS Market:**
- Global market valued at $9.62B in 2022
- Projected to reach $17.24B by 2031 (CAGR: 6.7%)

**BST Market Share Target:** <1% for five years post-Phase II (based on $6B market base)

### Time to Market and Market Share

- **Product Launch**: Sales begin at conclusion of Phase II following field trial validation
- **First Year Post-Phase II**: 1 customer agency, 3 UAS units
- **Year 2-3**: Scaling to 3-8 customer agencies, 10-50 UAS units
- **Market Entry Strategy**: Direct sales by BST initially; indirect sales through partners by Year 2

### Anticipated Competition

**Space-Based Weather Sensing:**
- Government-operated (NOAA, NASA); emerging commercial providers (Tomorrow.io)
- Barrier to entry: High capital investment, global coverage advantage
- BST positioning: S0 complements satellites by providing fine spatial resolution in high-variability areas, direct atmospheric measurements at lower altitudes than feasible from space

**Manned Aircraft:**
- Higher operational costs limit scope/applicability
- Limited to moderate flight altitudes; restricted from extreme atmospheric conditions
- Regional operators provide wildfire services
- BST advantage: Low-altitude capability (below safe manned-aircraft altitude), operation in severe weather/fire conditions

**Direct UAS Competitors:**
- Multiple UAS manufacturers exist, but BST differentiates through:
  - 10+ years invested in aircraft design, flight controller, sensor integration
  - Purpose-built for scientific payloads in demanding environments
  - Proven operations in wildfires, volcanoes, tornadoes, hurricanes globally
- Barrier to entry for competitors: Significant development time, specialized expertise in extreme-environment operations

**Other SBIR-Funded Competitors (same solicitation):**
- Anzen Unmanned: Air traffic management/airspace integration (complementary, not direct competitor)
- ATAC: Wildfire Prediction Alerting Service (complementary)

## Commercialization Strategy

### Business Model & Distribution Channels

**Primary Sales Channel:**
- Direct sales by BST to federal/state agencies initially
- Expansion to indirect sales through manufacturing partners and airframe resellers (existing distribution channels from prior products)

**Pricing Model:**
- Unit price: $10,000 per S0 UAS
- Non-recurring engineering (NRE) integration costs: $10,000 per customer (training, TAK/WFTAK integration, communications setup)
- Royalties from manufacturing partners: $500 per unit (Year 2 onward)

**Revenue Diversification:**
- Services revenue: BST personnel deployed to incidents providing field data collection and analysis
- Adjacent market applications: Environmental monitoring, weather, soil moisture/fire danger assessment
- Follow-on development: New aircraft variants and sensor payloads for expanded markets

### Commercialization Risks & Mitigation

**Key Risk: Temporary Flight Restrictions (TFRs) during active incidents**
- Mitigation strategies: Coordinate with incident managers for nighttime operations (no TFR conflicts); establish clear airspace deconfliction communications plans
- BST has extensive BVLOS operations experience
- Following UTM (Uncrewed Aircraft System Traffic Management) ecosystem development for long-term airspace integration solution

### Company Commercialization History

BST has successfully commercialized:
1. Two generations of autopilots and supporting technology for UAS
2. Fixed-wing UAS for soil moisture measurements and methane detection
3. E2 multirotor UAS – leading platform for wind turbine inspection with expansion to broader infrastructure markets
4. Products distributed through manufacturing partners, in-house sales, and airframe resellers
