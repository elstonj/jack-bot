# AF203-CSO1 Phase II Proposal Instructions

## Document Metadata
- Type: Solicitation Instructions / Proposal Preparation Guidance
- Client/Agency: Department of the Air Force (DAF) / AFWERX
- Program/Solicitation: AF203-CSO1 (Commercial Solutions Opening), Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase II
- Date: 2021 (instructions issued; Phase I completion deadline June 2, 2021; Phase II submission deadline June 3, 2021)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None
- Key Personnel: Captain Zoe Walters, AFRL/RGV (point of contact)

## Executive Summary
These are comprehensive instructions for small businesses preparing Phase II SBIR proposals in response to the Air Force's AF203-CSO1 Commercial Solutions Opening. The document outlines eligibility requirements, proposal structure, submission procedures, evaluation criteria, and regulatory compliance expectations. Maximum funding is $750,000 for a 15-month performance period (12 months technical, 3 months reporting), with approximately 60 awards anticipated.

## Key Sections & Requirements

### Eligibility & Compliance
- **Small Business Status**: Must qualify as small business per 13 CFR §§ 701-705 at time of award; minimum 50% of Phase II R/R&D work must be conducted by offeror
- **Principal Investigator**: Must have primary employment (>50% time) with small business throughout performance period
- **Performance Location**: All R/R&D work must be performed in the United States
- **Benchmarks**: Companies with >20 Phase I awards in past 5 years must achieve Phase II-to-Phase I ratio of ≥0.25; companies with >15 Phase II awards in past 10 years must demonstrate $100K+ in average sales/investments per Phase II award OR >15% patent rate from SBIR efforts
- **Ownership Restrictions**: Firms with majority ownership by venture capital operating companies, hedge funds, or private equity firms are ineligible
- **Joint Ventures/Partnerships**: Permitted if resulting entity qualifies as small business
- **Classified Work**: Classified proposals not accepted; if Phase II performance requires classified work, firm must obtain or be able to obtain facility clearance
- **Foreign Nationals**: Must be identified with countries of origin, visa/work permit types, and assigned tasks; participation may be restricted by ITAR/export control laws

### Proposal Structure & Content

**Volume 1: DoD Proposal Coversheet**
- Technical abstract (max 200 words): program objective, effort description, anticipated benefits, commercial applications
- "Anticipated Benefits" section must explicitly state: "We solved [DAF problem] by providing [advantage], to help [DAF end-user organization] accomplish [goal]. We will recognize profit by charging [non-defense commercial customers] to get [non-defense benefit]."
- Max 8 keywords/phrases for categorization
- Disclosure of prior/current/pending support of equivalent work to DAF or other federal agencies required
- Cannot contain proprietary or classified information

**Volume 2: Technical Volume (max 15 pages)**
- Table of Contents (not counted in page limit)
- Glossary of acronyms/abbreviations (not counted)
- Technical Summary: specific Phase I problem/opportunity and relationship to Phase II
- Non-Defense Commercial Solution description: scope, technical specifications, qualitative/quantitative value, previous commercial sales/licensing history, revenue, unit sales, user adoption, pre-sales, investments
- **Commercialization Potential**: Must address company information, customer/competition, market analysis (milestones, target dates, market size, estimated market share at year 1 and 5), intellectual property strategy (patents, trade secrets, competitive protection), financing plans (securing non-SBIR funding for Phase III), assistance/mentoring needs. Specific questions to address:
  - First product incorporating technology
  - Probable customers and estimated market size
  - Funding required to bring to market and sources
  - Marketing expertise availability
  - Probable competitors and anticipated price/quality advantage
  - Private sector/non-SBIR funding sources and commitments (venture capital, angel investors, state/local programs, affiliate investments)
  - Private/Government follow-on Phase III commitments
  - Past 12 months' total revenue from non-Defense commercial sales
  - Phase II anticipated end results and Phase III transfer plans
- Non-Defense Commercial Customer Discovery: past revenue from proposed solution customers (listed separately), potential revenue-generating projects with Defense and non-Defense customers directly related to solution
- **Phase I Feasibility Study Results**: Must document solution's ability to meet DAF end-user and customer needs through R&D
  - Scientific/technical R&D effort: research questions, methods, results, relevant literature
  - DAF end-user and customer exploration methods: detailed documentation including at least one in-depth interview with DAF end-user AND customer (both successful and unsuccessful methods)
  - **Empowered and Committed DAF End-Users**: specific operators willing to support Phase II R&D, test, evaluation, trials (signed Customer Memorandum required - using mandatory template, non-negotiable)
  - **Empowered and Committed AF Customers**: specific AF customer (e.g., Program Manager at system program office) willing to assist Phase II transition and Phase III (Customer Memorandum required)
  - **Empowered and Committed US Non-AF Government End-Users**: firm commitments via Customer Memoranda for additional US Government end-users
- **Phase II Technical Objectives and Key Results**: 3-5 clear R&D effort and trial objectives tied to specific Phase II tasks; objectives must be qualitative but specific to participating DAF end-users, tied to time periods, tangible, unambiguous about expected results. Each objective must have 3-5 measurable "key results" (likely quantitative). Must list anticipated stakeholder interactions (program managers, lawyers, test range officials, information assurance, system program office engineers) and Phase II completion requirements outside offeror's control
- **Compliance and Regulatory Activities**: Checklist indicating whether effort involves air flight, human subject research, animals, directed energy devices/RF radiation, explosives/propellants, hazardous materials, infectious agents/toxins/recombinant DNA
- **Phase II Work Plan**: Must emphasize work outcomes/results rather than methods; include measurable performance standards and assessment methods. Must accommodate regulatory/compliance processes, detailing submission of documentation, waivers, required materials
- **Task Outline**: Major work to be accomplished; provides scope indication
- **Milestone Progress and Funding Schedule**: Max 15 milestones/deliverables including refined Work Plan (CA+30 days), final technical report, intervening milestones. Each milestone must specify:
  - Expected delivery date (months after contract award)
  - Deliverable of value
  - Clear acceptance criteria
  - Associated payment amount reflecting justifiable costs
- **Deliverables**: Specific sample/prototype/hardware/software to be delivered; data deliverables, schedules, quantities. May require Unique Item Identification per DFARS 252.211-7003
- **Safety-Related Deliverables**: 
  - Hardware: preliminary hazard analysis required
  - Hazardous materials: identification, purpose, risk-minimizing alternatives/procedures/safeguards
- **Required Reports** (all deliverables):
  - **Final Report**: Draft due 30 days after Phase II technical completion; first page single-page summary (publishable, non-proprietary) of purpose, work accomplished, potential applications; detail objectives met, work completed, results, technical feasibility
  - **Status Reports**: Quarterly minimum; max 15 slides; describe progress toward DAF end-user needs, quantitative progress measure (0-1) toward each objective/key result
  - **Technical Interchange Meetings**: Quarterly minimum with Government technical personnel; conducted virtually (Zoom, Teams, etc.) to limit costs
  - **Phase II Summary Report**: Due at technical completion; email to Government Program Manager; max 700 words; technology description and anticipated applications/benefits for Government/private sector
  - **Additional Reporting**: As required by DAF end-user/customer (software documentation, engineering drawings, operation/maintenance docs, updated commercialization results, safety hazard analysis for partial/total hardware development)
- **Key Personnel**: Identify key project personnel including subcontractors/consultants with education, experience, citizenship information; technical resume for Principal Investigator including publications required
- **Investors and Partners**: Identify company investors, shareholders, lenders, technology licensors/licensees, strategic advisors with track record supporting Phase II
- **Related Work**: Significant activities directly related to proposed effort including previous programs by PI, proposing firm, consultants; application to proposed project; include reviewers' comments on state-of-the-art knowledge
- **Non-U.S. Citizens**: Identity all proposed non-U.S./dual citizens as direct employees, subcontractors, funding providers, technology licensees/licensors, consultants; provide countries of origin, visa/work permit types, anticipated involvement level. If ITAR subject, foreign nationals limited to public domain work; tasks must not enable understanding of overall project objectives (prevents foreign nationals in key positions like PI, Senior Engineer)
- **Supply Chain Integrity**: Address joint ventures/partnerships/joint tech development/teaming/collaborative agreements; main component/software/suppliers; entities utilizing firm's IP; significant non-US customer/investment base (>5% revenue) including countries; foreign interests with power to direct company management/IP/operations (Foreign Ownership, Control, Influence mitigation); classified information needs and approving agencies; verify owners/investors/PIs meet 13 CFR §121.701-705 requirements; compliance with DFARS 252.225-7048 (Export-Controlled Items); Committee on Foreign Investment in US (CFIUS) reviews if applicable for emerging/foundational technology; Bayh-Dole Act compliance (35 USC §204)

**Volume 3: Cost Volume (max $750,000 total)**
- Use DSIP online cost volume form
- Cover 15 months: 12 months technical + 3 months reporting
- Maximum $750,000 SBIR funding regardless of second phase funding commitments
- Fixed price payments tied to measurable milestones/deliverables
- Price analysis required including reasonableness; if comparison with prior efforts insufficient, cost analysis per FAR 15.404-1(c) required
- Direct cost items require substantiation as reasonable/realistic
- **Direct Labor**: Identify key personnel by name/labor category; include hours, labor category, overhead, fringe benefits, hourly rates
- **Direct Materials**: Itemized list with types, quantities, prices, purposes; vendor quotes/historical costs as justification
- **Other Direct Costs**: Specialized services (machining, testing, analysis), temporary specialized equipment leases (require lease vs. purchase justification)
- **Travel**: Break out by trip, destination, purpose, number of travelers, airfare, per diem, lodging, ground transportation; recommend at least one trip to DAF technical point of contact location
- **Subcontracts**: Include copies of agreements; minimum Statement of Work with detailed cost volume per planned subcontract
- **Consultants**: Separate agreement letter per consultant with tasks, hours, hourly rate
- **Constraint**: Total consultant fees, facility lease/usage fees, and other subcontract/purchase agreement costs may not exceed one-third of total contract price without written DAF Contracting Officer approval
- **Special Tooling/Test Equipment/Materials**: Must be advantageous to Government and relate directly to specific effort; title vests with DAF unless transfer more cost-effective
- Round all costs to nearest dollar
- Initial proposal should contain best technical and price terms; Government may award without negotiations

**Volume 4: Company Commercialization Report (CCR)**
- Required for firms with prior DoD or non-DoD SBIR/STTR Phase I or II awards (regardless of commercialization to date)
- Firms with no prior awards select "No"
- Complete via https://www.sbir.gov/ account (My Dashboard => My Documents)
- Create/update via My Commercialization tab
- May include explanatory material on Government/private-sector commercialization successes, mitigating factors for low commercialization, organizational/personnel changes, qualified third-party investments
- Download .pdf from Dashboard My Documents and upload to Volume 4 CCR area in Firm Information section of DSIP
- **WARNING**: Uploading new CCR to DSIP Firm Information or clicking "Save"/"Submit" in Volume 4 reopens ALL proposals under open BAAs/CSOs; previously certified Phase I or Direct to Phase II proposals must be recertified/resubmitted or will not be considered fully submitted/evaluated
