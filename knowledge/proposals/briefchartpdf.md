# A Ruggedized UAS for Scientific Data Gathering in Harsh Environments

- Client/Agency: NASA (Earth Science Division)
- Program/Solicitation: 2016 SBIR, Topic ID 2022-1-1118
- Date: January 11, 2022
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2-VTOL, SwiftCore (firmware), SwiftTab (app)
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (PI, Black Swift Technologies, Boulder CO)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposes to advance autonomous UAS operations in harsh environments by automating wind-aware flight planning, improving the manufacturability of the S2-VTOL vertical takeoff and landing system, and enhancing the SwiftTab user interface for flexible payload data management. The work builds on Phase II-E results demonstrating the only successful visual observations of a target volcano during challenging conditions, with goals of enabling routine, worldwide scientific missions in difficult weather and terrain.

## Technical Approach

**Key Innovation:** Automating mission management in harsh conditions to reduce dependency on highly trained operators and months of preparation.

**Four Primary Objectives:**

1. **Intelligent Wind-Aware Flight Planning:** Implement real-time environmental assessment combined with predictive modeling to adjust flight paths dynamically, minimizing energy usage during convective storms and mountainous terrain operations. Integration into SwiftCore firmware with flight demonstrations.

2. **User Interface Enhancements:** Update SwiftTab app to support flexible payload integration, displaying telemetry from interchangeable payloads and enabling payload control through a common operating picture.

3. **Advanced Mission Planning:** Automate terrain and communication limitation analysis in flight planning and flight control systems, similar to wind-aware planning approach.

4. **S2-VTOL Manufacturability Improvements:** Simplify construction and reduce cost of the S2-VTOL prototype while retaining its primary advantage: ability to launch and land in strong wind events nearly anywhere.

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S2-VTOL:**
- Vertical takeoff and landing small UAS capable of operations in strong wind conditions
- Can launch and land nearly anywhere
- Prototype demonstrated during Phase II-E volcano monitoring mission
- Proposed improvements: simplified construction, reduced manufacturing cost

**SwiftCore Firmware:**
- Flight control and navigation software
- Targeted for wind-aware flight planning implementation
- Real-time environmental assessment and predictive modeling capabilities

**SwiftTab App:**
- Mission planning and telemetry display interface
- Supports interchangeable payloads
- Planned enhancements for flexible payload data management and control

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Mission (Phase II-E Background):**
- Volcano monitoring with visual observations
- Achieved only successful visual observations of target volcano during calendar year despite difficult conditions
- Required months of flight planning preparation
- Frequent real-time wind observations necessary during flights to maintain safe battery margins for return and landing

**NASA Applications:**
- Tropospheric Chemistry Program (TCP)
- Applied Sciences Air Quality Program
- CALIPSO (Cloud-Aerosol Lidar and Infrared Pathfinder Satellite Observations) mission support
- Aura mission
- CATS (Cloud-Aerosol Transport System)
- OCO-2/3 (Orbital Carbon Observatory) programs
- Earth Ventures airborne field campaigns
- Volcanic and wildfire observations (JPL and NASA Ames partnerships)

**Non-NASA Applications:**
- USGS, NOAA, DOE, National Weather Service
- Wildfire monitoring and support (particulate sampling, severe turbulence environments)
- Commercial multi-hole probe sensor market

## Key Results (Phase II-E Context)
- Demonstrated first and only successful visual observations of target volcano during challenging conditions
- Validated small UAS utility for routine scientific data gathering
- Identified critical need for automation: current operations require exceptionally knowledgeable, well-trained teams and months of preparation
- Demonstrated necessity of real-time wind monitoring and custom software to ensure safe battery margins

- **Automation Focus:** Primary innovation emphasizes reducing operational complexity and team expertise requirements to enable "routine and worldwide" deployment
