# Letter from Creare LLC Regarding Cost Sharing on NASA Phase IIE Proposal

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of support/cost-sharing commitment
- Client/Agency: Black Swift Technologies LLC (from Creare LLC)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Phase IIE (Contract No. NNX17CP13C); NOAA Phase II effort
- Date: June 21, 2019
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Vertical Takeoff and Landing Aircraft (VTOL UAS), autopilot systems
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (BST CEO), Benjamin Cameron (Creare engineer)

## Executive Summary
Creare LLC confirms providing $104,571.00 in cost-sharing funds to Black Swift Technologies for development of autopilot systems for a novel VTOL aircraft. The letter supports BST's NASA SBIR Phase IIE proposal titled "A Ruggedized UAS for Scientific Data Gathering in Harsh Environments" (Contract No. NNX17CP13C), with technologies developed under a parallel NOAA Phase II effort to benefit both efforts.

## Technical Approach
BST is developing autopilot systems for a VTOL UAS platform. Work supported by Creare's matching funds will enhance flight characteristics during transition and vertical flight in strong wind conditions. Technologies from the ongoing NOAA Phase II effort will directly transfer to the NASA effort.

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **VTOL UAS Platform**: Novel vertical takeoff and landing aircraft under development, with improved autopilot capabilities for harsh environment operations
- **Autopilot Systems**: Focus on improved flight characteristics in transition and vertical flight modes, particularly in strong wind conditions

## Use Cases & Applications
- Scientific data gathering in harsh environments (per NASA contract title)
- Applications requiring VTOL capabilities in challenging wind conditions

## Notable Details
- Creare is providing direct funding to BST ($104,571.00) representing cost-share
- Synergy between two programs: NOAA Phase II and NASA SBIR Phase IIE
- Technologies benefit both Creare's UAS development and BST's system, indicating partnership with mutual advantage
- Focus on commercialization pathway and wider technology adoption
- This is a brief, formal letter rather than a detailed technical proposal
