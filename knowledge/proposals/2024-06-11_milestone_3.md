## Executive Summary
This milestone report documents BST's completion of Milestone 3 modifications to the UAS platform for soil integrity validation. The team successfully selected, procured, designed, and tested new thermal and NDVI sensors integrated into a self-contained bolt-on module. Preliminary field testing validates sensor performance against commercial reference instruments, with work progressing toward Milestone 4 algorithm development for soil integrity calculation from radiometer, NDVI, and thermal data.

## Technical Approach

**System Architecture:**
- Integration of L-band radiometer with new auxiliary sensors (thermal and NDVI) on BST's E2 UAS platform
- Single data logging point in radiometer digital back-end (eliminated Raspberry Pi and USB hub to reduce weight, complexity, and EMI)
- Modular, self-contained sensor payload that bolts onto the E2

**Key Design Decisions:**
- 2×2 patch antenna array redesigned using air-sandwich dielectric material to reduce cost while maintaining equivalent bandwidth
- Substrate integrated waveguide used for antenna excitation
- Complete ground plane on bottom layer reduces mutual coupling between up- and down-looking antennas
- Fixed (vs. adjustable) attenuators ensure stability of reference temperatures
- Sensor/autopilot communication via FTDI interface; payload power from 24VDC E2 power supply

**Data Interfaces:**
- NDVI sensor: SDI-12 protocol, new data every 2 seconds
- Thermal sensor: I2C protocol, near-instantaneous updates
- Sensor start/stop signals from autopilot through FTDI to back-end processor

## Products & Capabilities Described

**S2 and E2 UAS Platforms:**
- BST's existing small UAS systems being modified with new sensor suite
- E2 can carry integrated thermal, NDVI, and L-band radiometer payload
- 24VDC power supply available for payload

**NDVI Sensor (Two-Band Reflectance):**
- Wavebands: 650 nm, 810 nm
- Measures upward and downward-facing reflectance for NDVI calculation
- Range: 2× full sunlight
- Calibration uncertainty: ±5%
- Temperature response: <0.1%/°C
- Repeated measurement variance: <1%

**Thermal Sensor (Ambient-Compensated Infrared):**
- Range: −70°C to 380°C
- Resolution: 0.02°C
- Field of view: 35°
- Accuracy: ±0.3°C (22–40°C), ±0.5°C (0–60°C)

**L-Band Radiometer:**
- Previously developed by BST; refinements made in this phase
- Measures antenna temperature for soil moisture retrieval
- Now includes integrated digital back-end with single data logging point
- RF receiver board designed and out for fabrication at time of report

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Mission:**
Runway integrity validation at Air Force bases through soil moisture and soil strength (cone index) measurements, enabling rapid assessment of runway loadbearing capacity after environmental events (e.g., flooding, heavy rain).

**Field Testing Locations:**
- Sunny Slope Sod Farm (private property)
- Prairie Monarch Bison Ranch (private property)
- McIntosh Lake Open Space (public land)

These sites selected for soil type variation to support model development.

## Key Results

**Milestone 3 Achievements:**

*Hardware Development:*
- NDVI and thermal sensors procured, calibrated, and validated against known targets
- RF receiver board design complete; fabrication initiated (2-week lead time at time of report)
- Interface design and 3D CAD housing/mounts for sensors and radiometer completed
- Antenna design finalized with cost-optimized dielectric approach

*Sensor Validation Testing:*
- NDVI sensor compared against commercial Micasense Altum camera at 60 m altitude over sod farm
  - "Very good match, slight bias, but more than good enough to act as a replacement"
  - Raw NDVI map comparison shows strong agreement
  - Red channel performs well; NIR requires further calibration
  
- Thermal sensor compared against Fieldscout TDR 350
  - Measurements "in good agreement" with ground truth
  - Commercial camera bias noted as within ±5°C specification
  - Additional testing with ground truth still needed

- Ground calibration targets (85%, 33%, 3% reflectance) used for validation

**Milestone 4 Progress (In Progress):**
- Volumetric soil moisture retrieval algorithm completed and verified on previous flight data
- Remotely sensed soil moisture shows good agreement with in situ measurements
- Literature review and algorithm development for soil integrity (Rating Cone Index/RCI and California Bearing Ratio/CBR) completed
- Conversion methodology established: volumetric → gravimetric soil moisture via dry soil density lookup tables, then to soil strength metrics using Unified Soil Classification System (USCS)

## Notable Details

**Technical Innovations:**
- Elimination of Raspberry Pi and USB hub reduced system weight, complexity, and electromagnetic interference
- Centralized data logging in radiometer back-end simplifies architecture
- Cost-optimized antenna design (air-sandwich dielectric) maintains performance while reducing procurement cost
- Real-time payload capability: NDVI and thermal data can be processed in near-real-time for soil moisture calculation during flight

**Integration Challenges:**
- Altum NDVI camera interfered with L-band radiometer, requiring separate flights for validation
- Post-processing requirements of commercial NDVI cameras incompatible with Air Force mission timelines; BST's integrated two-band approach addresses this
- NIR channel calibration for NDVI sensor requires additional work

**Soil Classification Approach:**
- Bridges two standard classification systems: USDA (commonly used for soil moisture retrieval) and USCS (standard for soil integrity/cone index calculations)
- Uses empirically derived lookup tables for texture-based conversion
