- Program/Solicitation: AF192-005 (Air Force UAS Pitch Day)
- Proposal Number: F192-006-1526

- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 Hurricane Sampler, S2 UAS, 3D Wind Probe, Atmospheric Sensor Suite
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (PI, Lead Avionics Developer)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed to develop an Atmospheric Sounding UAS—a small, fixed-wing unmanned aircraft designed to provide real-time in situ atmospheric data in a recoverable, repeatable manner. The system would interface and modify BST's proven S0 Hurricane Sampler system for Air Force use, offering significantly lower cost than competitors with no Chinese components while enabling automated atmospheric profiling and data integration into Air Force systems.

## Technical Approach
- **Core Platform**: Modified S0 Hurricane Sampler system (air-launched UAS heritage)
- **Primary Sensor Suite**: 
  - 3D Wind Probe (100 Hz measurements, ±0.5 m/s accuracy, ±1° accuracy)
  - Co-located IMU, magnetometer, GPS
  - Pressure, temperature, humidity sensors
  - Small-scale turbulence measurement capability
- **Data Collection & Processing**: Automated sampling pipeline with UI-based scripting to create custom sampling plans; standardized data format for easy Air Force assimilation
- **Operational Profile**: 
  - Vertical profiling capability above 15,000 ft AGL
  - 1+ hour endurance targeted
  - Wider tolerable wind regime than equivalent-sized multirotors
  - Fully automated operation requiring less than half-day training
  - "Launch-and-forget" autonomous capability

## Use Cases & Applications

### Air Force Weather Operations
- Vertical atmospheric profiling above 15,000 ft AGL
- Integration into WRF (Weather Research and Forecasting) models
- Atmospheric boundary layer sampling
- Near real-time weather and plume monitoring
- Icing and fog prediction support
- Critical data for forecasting

### Balloon Sounding
- Future balloon-deployed variants (under 2 lbs weight)
- Compatibility with standard balloon sonde base stations
- High-altitude sampling with controlled descent profiling
- Improved over traditional balloon sondes through controlled sampling locations vs. wind drift
- Simplified recovery

### Civilian/Commercial Applications
- NOAA, NSSL, NCAR potential customers
- Replacement for 800+ weather stations worldwide using balloon-borne radiosondes twice daily
- Reusable instrument reducing long-term costs vs. expendable radiosondes
- Boundary layer sampling applications
