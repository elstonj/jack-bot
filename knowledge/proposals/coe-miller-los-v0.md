## Executive Summary
Ben Miller, Director of Colorado's Center of Excellence for Advanced Technology Aerial Firefighting, provides a letter of support for BST's Phase II SBIR proposal to use UAS-collected meteorological data to refine wildfire weather forecasting models developed by NCAR. Miller confirms his participation and expertise contribution to the project, endorsing its value for improved wildfire suppression planning.

## Technical Approach
- BST will collect meteorological data via a UAS equipped with a meteorological sensor payload
- Data will be fed into a fire weather model developed by the National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR)
- The goal is to decrease uncertainty in operational wildfire forecast models
- Real-time data dissemination to deployed firefighters is proposed via a system like ATAK (Android Tactical Assault Kit)

## Products & Capabilities Described
**BST UAS with Meteorological Sensor Payload:**
- Purpose: Collect atmospheric/weather data for fire forecasting refinement
- Deployment context: Operational wildfire environments
- Data application: Input to NCAR fire weather models to improve forecast accuracy

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Wildland fire suppression planning:** More accurate weather data to inform firefighting resource allocation and strategy
- **Real-time operational support:** Delivery of improved forecast data to active firefighting teams in the field
- **Multi-agency support:** US Forest Service, Department of Interior, state and local fire response teams

## Partnerships & Stakeholders
- **National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR):** Develops fire weather models
- **Colorado Center of Excellence for Advanced Technology Aerial Firefighting:** Subject matter expertise on wildland fire management and real-time common operating picture technology
- **NASA initiatives supported:** FireSense Project; Advanced Capabilities for Emergency Response Operations (ACERO)
- **Federal agencies:** US Forest Service, Department of Interior
