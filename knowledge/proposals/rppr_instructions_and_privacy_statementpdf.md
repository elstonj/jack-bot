## Executive Summary
This document provides standardized instructions for completing the Research Performance Progress Report (RPPR), a federal format developed by an interagency working group to standardize performance reporting across federal research agencies. The RPPR replaces other agency-specific reporting formats and applies to recipients of federal grants and cooperative agreements supporting research and research-related activities.

## Document Content Overview

**Note:** This is a procedural/instructional document rather than a BST proposal or technical report. It contains no information about Black Swift Technologies, its products, capabilities, or projects. The document is a reference guide for federal grant recipients on how to complete mandatory and optional reporting sections.

## Key Reporting Categories Defined

### Mandatory Component: Accomplishments
- Major goals and objectives
- Accomplishments under stated goals
- Training and professional development opportunities provided
- Results dissemination to communities of interest
- Plans for next reporting period

### Optional Components
- **Products:** Publications, websites, technologies/techniques, patents/licenses, data, software, models, educational materials, equipment, research materials, interventions, business creation
- **Participants & Collaborating Organizations:** Personnel involved, role changes, partner organizations, collaborators/contacts
- **Impact:** Effects on principal discipline, other disciplines, human resources development, teaching/education, infrastructure, technology transfer, society, foreign countries, budget allocation to foreign work
- **Changes/Problems:** Approach modifications, delays/problems and resolutions, expenditure impacts, changes in human subjects/animal use protocols, performance site changes
- **Special Reporting Requirements:** Agency-specific requirements
- **Budget Information:** Financial data collection
- **Project Outcomes:** Cumulative findings and outcomes summary

## Notable Administrative Details

- Submission is electronically managed through agency-specific systems
- Demographic information collection from significant contributors is voluntary
- Privacy Act protection applies to all personal/demographic data
- Significant contributors defined as those contributing ≥1 person-month of effort per year
- Format standardization intended to reduce administrative burden across federal agencies
- Agencies may add program-specific instructions but must minimize supplementation beyond standard categories

## Accessibility & Structure Notes

- Instructions use "Nothing to Report" option when no significant progress in specific areas
- Written for lay audiences in accessible language (referenced as "Scientific American" style)
- Budget forms align with SF-424 R&R family as implemented in Grants.gov
- Confidentiality of demographic data assured per Privacy Act (5 U.S.C. Section 552a)
