## Executive Summary
Internal strategic analysis for DARPA ERIS 2026 submission emphasizing need to reframe BST capabilities around "disruptive" paradigm shifts rather than incremental improvements. Document argues that current framing of BST's extreme-environment sensing, rapid deployment, and integrated autonomy reads as optimization of existing approaches rather than fundamental breaks with assumptions in how sensing systems are designed and deployed.

## Strategic Problem Identified
Current BST positioning conflates "many impressive capabilities" with disruptive innovation. DARPA ERIS requires demonstration of paradigm shifts, not better sensors/longer endurance/harsher environment tolerance. Core issue: submission reads additive ("we can do A, B, C in hard places") rather than subtractive ("we eliminate the need for X entirely").

## Three Proposed Disruptive Frames

### Frame 1: Platforms → Deployable Sensing Fields (Strongest)
**Broken Assumption:** Valuable sensing assets must survive, return, and be protected.

**Disruptive Claim:** Replace exquisite, survivable platforms with rapidly deployable, loss-tolerant sensing fields. Vehicles become consumable carriers of calibrated measurements; value is in coverage and adaptation, not platform survival.

**BST Assets Supporting:**
- S0-AD (magnetic detection, air-deployed, expendable)
- Tube-launched/air-deployed architectures
- Designed-for-loss platform philosophy
- Cost-per-measurement vs. cost-per-vehicle economics
- Sensor placement optimized for loss tolerance

**Reframed Applications:**
- ASW without capital ships
- ISR without airspace dominance
- Plume tracking without manned assets
- Wildfire detection without perimeter infrastructure

**Why DARPA Values:** Changes cost-risk equation fundamentally, not just performance metrics.

---

### Frame 2: Preplanned Autonomy → Information-Seeking Autonomy (Primary)
**Broken Assumption:** Vehicles navigate first; sensing happens along the way.

**Disruptive Claim:** Sensing objectives drive motion in real time based on uncertainty reduction. Vehicles move to reduce uncertainty; models command motion.

**BST Assets Supporting:**
- Sensor-directed navigation
- Pressure-based wind estimation + online calibration
- Coordinated multi-UAS sampling
- 3D wind field reconstruction

**Reframed Positioning:** Self-tasking measurement system, not UAV fleet.

**High-Value Applications:** Hurricanes, wildfire plumes, volcanic ash, unknown airborne threats where live information gain drives platform repositioning.

**Why DARPA Values:** Step toward adaptive, self-tasking systems beyond scripted autonomy.

---

### Frame 3: Data Delivery → Closed-Loop Model Control (Supporting)
**Broken Assumption:** Sensing systems stop at data delivery.

**Disruptive Claim:** System is incomplete until measurements actively reshape operational models in real time. Sensing + autonomy + modeling = one closed-loop system.

**BST Assets Supporting:**
- Measurement → model assimilation
- WRF-SFIRE integration
- Hurricane model feedback loops
- NWS operational inclusion

**Reframed Positioning:** Operational infrastructure provider, not platform vendor.

## Submission Strategy Analysis

### Option A (Flagship Umbrella)
**Risk:** "Extreme-Environment Distributed Sensing" framing reads as "very good multi-mission system" without explicit disruption focus. Only viable if umbrella explicitly positions as "Deployable, loss-tolerant sensing fields."

### Option B (Two Focused Submissions—Recommended)
**Submission 1:** Air-Deployed, Expendable Sensing Fields for Denied ISR
- Explicitly challenges survivability economics
- Anchors S0-AD, magnetic sensing, tube launch
- Native DARPA disruption narrative

**Submission 2:** Self-Directing Atmospheric Sensing for Persistent Environmental Awareness
- Breaks preplanned autonomy assumption
- Anchors hurricanes, wildfire, plume tracking
- Strong dual-use story

**Advantages:** More reviewer surface area, clearer disruptive hypotheses, reduced "too broad" rejection risk.

## TRL Framing Guidance
**Risk:** Stating "TRL 8–9" can reduce perceived disruption.

**Correct Framing:**
- "Operationally proven components enabling new system architecture"
- Emphasize architectural novelty over component maturity
- DARPA prefers: "new ways to assemble proven things" over "very mature versions of known systems"

---

## Critical Assessment of Current Draft
**What Falls Short:**
- Problem statement (expensive, fragile, SWaP-constrained) describes optimization pressure, not paradigm break
- "State-of-the-art advance" framing still sounds like "very good platform," not "different way of doing sensing"

**Path to Actual Disruption:**
1. Pick ONE assumption to kill
2. Explicitly state what becomes unnecessary
3. Reinforce assumption break in every section
4. Avoid structure where disruption is buried; embed it in problem/advance/impact sections

---

## Notable Strategic Insights
- Disruption ≠ "cheaper / smaller / tougher" or "more integrated" or "flown more"
- Disruption = "eliminate a class of assets" / "invert who adapts to whom" / "change sensing economics under risk"
- DARPA-familiar problem structure (problem → advance → team → impacts → TRL) won't carry disruption unless disruption lives *inside* each section
- Emphasis on architectural inversion (loss tolerance, information-driven motion, closed-loop model control) over capability breadth
