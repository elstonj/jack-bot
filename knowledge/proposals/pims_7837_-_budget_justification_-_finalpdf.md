## Executive Summary
This document justifies the budget allocation for a DOD SBIR 25.2 proposal titled "Coordinated Drone-Based Plume Tracking," a collaborative effort between Black Swift Technologies and Brookhaven National Laboratory. The budget covers BNL's direct labor costs, departmental costs, and indirect costs (G&A overhead, safeguards & securities, mission adder, and LDRD) for Year 1 of the project.

## Technical Approach
The project involves development of hardware and software for a volumetric plume capture system. Key technical activities include:
- System development and hardware/software integration
- Development of system testing protocols
- Testing outcomes reporting and system evaluation
- Coordination between BNL and Black Swift Technologies for planning, research execution, and reporting

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Volumetric Plume Capture System**
- A coordinated drone-based system for tracking and capturing plume data
- Requires integrated hardware and software development
- Will undergo formal testing protocol development and evaluation

## Cost Structure
**Direct Labor:** BNL FY25 Standard Labor Rates (escalated 4.5%) including fringe benefits

**Departmental Costs:** 41.78% burden rate covering:
- Departmental management
- Financial and business management support
- Environmental safety and health facility support
- Building space and equipment maintenance
- Waste disposal and electric power

**Indirect Costs:**
- G&A Overhead: G&A Recovery (7.20%) + Common Institutional G&A (46%)
- Safeguards & Securities: 6.30% (of VAB less electric power)
- Mission Adder: 6% (of total project cost)
- LDRD (Laboratory Directed Research and Development): 3.5% (of total project cost)

## Notable Details

- Significant indirect cost structure typical of federally funded research at national laboratories
