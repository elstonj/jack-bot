## Executive Summary
This document outlines Black Swift Technologies' IT security procedures and compliance framework for NASA SBIR Phase I contract NNX14CP21P (06/20/14 – 12/19/14). BST commits to protecting NASA's unclassified and Sensitive But Unclassified (SBU) information through adherence to NIST and NASA IT security requirements, with access limited to the SBIR/STTR Awardee Firm Electronic Handbook (EHB) for contract deliverable submission.

## Technical Approach
**IT System Access:**
- Single NASA IT system access required: SBIR/STTR Awardee Firm Electronic Handbook (EHB) at https://ehb8.gsfc.nasa.gov
- Access managed through NASA Account Management System (NAMS) and Identity and Access Management System (IdMAX)
- Registration limited to personnel involved in contract negotiation and administration
- All registered personnel required to complete NASA Online Annual IT Security Training

None—this is an administrative compliance document with no technical products or capabilities described.

## Use Cases & Applications
Contract deliverable management: electronic submission of invoices and technical reports related to the Cooperative Wind Sensing SBIR Phase I project.

## Notable Details

**Security Controls & Compliance Requirements:**
- Personnel must comply with 15 General Rules of Behavior covering data protection, incident reporting, encryption of mobile data, password management, and separation of duties
- Mandatory encryption of all NASA data on transportable/mobile computers and removable media
- Incident response protocol: immediate notification to NASA Security Operations Center if any compromise, misuse, distortion, loss, or destruction of NASA IT systems or data occurs
- Sensitive information safeguards: use limited to contract performance only, no competitive advantage exploitation, restricted employee access with need-to-know basis

**Existing Security Framework:**
- BST has an approved IT security plan associated with prior contract NNX14CG09C
- No formal risk assessment or FIPS 199 assessment was in place at time of document creation

**Legal Acknowledgment:**
Document signed acknowledging that unauthorized access to NASA servers is prohibited under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986 and the National Information Infrastructure Protection Act of 1996.
