## Executive Summary
This document provides instructions to NOAA TPO-funded research grant recipients (specifically grantee Principal Investigators) for completing Research Performance Progress Reports (RPPR) required by the Department of Commerce and Office of Management and Budget. It clarifies NOAA-specific reporting requirements, submission procedures, and formatting guidance for interim and final progress reports on TPO-managed projects including SBIR Phase I and multiyear grants.

## Document Purpose & Scope
This is not a BST proposal, report, or capability document. Rather, it is **administrative guidance from NOAA** to grant recipients on how to report progress on NOAA-funded research projects. The document establishes reporting templates, frequencies, content requirements, and submission procedures through NOAA's Grants Online system.

## Key Reporting Requirements

### Reporting Frequency
- **Multiyear grants**: Every 6 months
- **SBIR Phase I grants** (6-month projects): Every 3 months

### Required Report Sections
1. **Accomplishments** - Proposed vs. actual progress (tasks, milestones, deliverables)
2. **Products** - Publications, conference papers, presentations, technologies, inventions, patents, licenses, software, databases, etc.
3. **Participants & Collaborating Organizations** - Personnel and partner involvement
4. **Impact** - Effects on discipline, other disciplines, human resources, teaching, infrastructure, technology transfer, and society
5. **Changes/Problems** - Deviations from proposal with mitigation plans
6. **Special Reporting Requirements** - Award-specific requirements (none currently for TPO SBIR)
7. **Budgetary Information** - Budget status vs. original plan
8. **Project Outcomes** - Broad public/societal benefits (distinct from products/deliverables)
9. **Demographic Information** - Voluntary information on significant contributors

### Final Reports
Two reports due at end of grant period:
- Semi-annual (3-month for SBIR Phase I) interim progress report: Due 30 days after period end
- Comprehensive final progress report: Due per schedule in grant Specific Award Conditions (not standard 90 days for SBIR Phase I)

## Submission & Format Requirements
- **Submission method**: NOAA Grants Online system only (not email)
- **Format**: PDF file using the outline in Appendix A (not the DOC fillable PDF template due to limitations with graphics/tables)
- **Coordinator**: Institution's designated Grant Office Representative handles submission
- **Text content**: Also copy-pasted into Grants Online web form text boxes
- **Graphics/Tables**: Embedded within report file; can be attached separately in Grants Online
- **Page numbers**: Required throughout

## Acknowledgment/Attribution Requirements
All publications and presentations must include standard DOC language crediting NOAA/OAR/TPO and the SBIR Program, including the NOAA logo. Suggested language provided: "This material is based upon work supported by the SBIR Program within the NOAA Technology Partnerships Office under Grant No. XXXXXXX."

## NOAA Review Process
- Reports distributed to NOAA subject matter experts and program managers
- Review normally completed within 30 days
- Reviewer comments may be returned for revision/resubmission
- Revised reports must include "Responses to Reviewer Comments" section
- Resubmission required within 30 days of return

## Notable Details
- Document emphasizes that **final reports are "the single-most important deliverable"** and should be comprehensive, cumulative, stand-alone documents; expected to be significantly longer than interim reports
- Key distinction made between **products/deliverables** (concrete, NOAA-specific benefits) and **outcomes** (broad public/societal benefits)
- Instructions note deficiencies in DOC's new fillable PDF form and Grants Online web interface regarding inability to embed graphics/tables, requiring workaround procedures
- All major task or schedule changes require pre-approval from TPO through formal Grants Online request
- Budget and schedule status required in all reports
