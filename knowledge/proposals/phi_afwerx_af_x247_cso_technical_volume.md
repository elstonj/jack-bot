## Executive Summary

Black Swift Technologies proposes to modify and integrate its high-TRL S0 UAS (air-deployable, disposable platform) for USAF hurricane sampling missions aboard C-130 aircraft operated by the 53rd Weather Reconnaissance Squadron. The S0 has been validated through extensive operational deployments from NOAA's P3 aircraft into multiple high-wind hurricanes (2023-2024 season). The effort will enhance the system to meet DOD cybersecurity and network integration requirements while optimizing deployment mechanisms and flight profiles for military operations.

## Technical Approach

**Core Problem Addressed:** Current hurricane data collection relies on disposable dropsondes that provide only discrete data points during descent, with limited spatial and temporal coverage. The lower boundary layer and eyewall—critical regions for determining storm intensity—are historically under-sampled.

**BST's Solution:**
- Deploy the S0 UAS as a disposable, autonomous platform capable of remaining in storm environments for extended periods
- Collect continuous 3D wind vectors, pressure, temperature, humidity, and sea surface temperature data
- Autonomously navigate through hurricanes following pre-programmed or adaptive flight paths
- Integrate into existing C-130 deployment procedures and USAF command systems
- Implement DOD-grade cybersecurity and secure communications protocols

**Key Technical Advantages Over Baseline:**
- **Continuous Data Collection:** Unlike dropsondes (brief descent-based data), S0 provides extended autonomous sampling with significantly more data points
- **Autonomous Flight:** Actively navigates storm regions vs. passive drift; enables precise lower boundary layer and eyewall sampling
- **Customizable Architecture:** Proprietary SwiftCore flight management system enables real-time data streaming, secure communication, and mission-specific adaptation
- **Extreme Condition Design:** Purpose-built for high wind speeds and turbulence characteristic of tropical cyclones
