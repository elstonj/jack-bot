### Detailed Soil Descriptions

#### Dill Fine Sandy Loam Series (DfA, DfB, DfD)
**Parent Material**: Coarse-loamy residuum weathered from sandstone

**Typical Profile**:
- A horizon (0-10 inches): Fine sandy loam
- Bw horizon (10-39 inches): Fine sandy loam
- Cr horizon (39-50 inches): Bedrock (paralithic)

**Physical Properties**:
- Depth to restrictive feature (bedrock): 20-40 inches
- Natural drainage class: Well drained
- Runoff class: Negligible to Low (depending on slope)
- Ksat (water transmissivity): Moderately high (0.20-0.60 in/hr)
- Depth to water table: >80 inches
- Available water storage: Low (~5.1 inches)
- Hydrologic Soil Group: B
- No flooding or ponding frequency

**Land Capability Classifications (Nonirrigated)**:
- DfA (0-1% slopes): 3s
- DfB (1-5% slopes): 3e
- DfD (5-8% slopes): 4e

**Ecological Site**: Sandy Loam Prairie 23-31" PZ (R078CY110TX)

**Minor Components in Dill Units**:
- Grant (3-7% of map unit): Loamy Prairie
- Lucien (2-4% of map unit): Shallow Prairie
- Quinlan (1-2% of map unit): Shallow Upland
- Rock outcrop (1% of map unit)
- Lovedale and Teller (in DfD unit)

#### Grant Silt Loam (GrB) – 1-3% Slopes
**Parent Material**: Silty residuum weathered from sandstone and shale

**Typical Profile**:
- Ap horizon (0-12 inches): Silt loam
- BA horizon (12-16 inches): Silt loam
- Bt horizon (16-32 inches): Silty clay loam
- BC horizon (32-47 inches): Silt loam

**Physical Properties**:

- Depth to water table: >80 inches
- Available water storage: High (~11.7 inches) – much higher than Dill soils
- Hydrologic Soil Group: B
- No flooding or ponding

## Soil Moisture Relevance

### Key Properties for Moisture Sensing:
1. **Available Water Storage Capacity**:
   - Dill soils: ~5.1 inches (low)
   - Grant soils: ~11.7 inches (high)
   - This difference suggests varying soil moisture retention and availability across the field site

2. **Hydraulic Conductivity (Ksat)**:
   - Dill: Moderately high (0.20-0.60 in/hr) – faster drainage
   - Grant: Very low to moderately low (0.00-0.06 in/hr) – slower drainage
   - Important for understanding water movement and moisture persistence in different soil zones

3. **Drainage Class**: All soils are well-drained, indicating good surface water removal and minimal water-logging potential

4. **Water Table Depth**: All soils have water table >80 inches deep, indicating moisture dynamics controlled by precipitation infiltration rather than capillary rise from groundwater

5. **Hydrologic Soil Group**: All classified as Group B (moderate infiltration and runoff potential)

## Notable Details for Soil Moisture Research

- The field site represents a heterogeneous soil environment with significant spatial variability in water holding capacity (5.1 to 11.7 inches available water)
- Grant soils dominate 63.3% of the AOI, providing a large homogeneous area for baseline measurements
- Dill soils comprise 36.8% of AOI and are distributed across three slope classes, allowing study of slope-dependent moisture variation
- Both soil series are well-drained with adequate depth profiles (20-72 inches) for subsurface moisture monitoring
- Bedrock contact occurs at moderate depths (20-72 inches), defining the lower boundary for moisture migration
- The location in a semi-arid to sub-humid climate (20-48 inches annual precipitation) makes soil moisture dynamics critical for agricultural productivity
