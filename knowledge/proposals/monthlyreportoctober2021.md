# Monthly Progress Report – October 2021

## Document Metadata

- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: AREN (Arctic Research on Environmentally-vulnerable North) 2020-2021

- Key Personnel: Geoff Bland (Task Manager), Kay Rufty (Employee)

## Executive Summary
This October 2021 progress report documents production and assembly activities for the NASA AREN project, including 3D printing of AeroPod and TerraROVER components, camera mount modifications, and preparation of equipment for field deployment.

## Technical Approach
- **3D Printing Operations**: Ongoing fabrication and maintenance of AeroPod and TerraROVER parts using Monoprice printer; troubleshooting printer issues including extruder clogs; researching component disassembly and replacement parts.
- **Camera Mount Design**: Iterative design and modeling of camera mounts for multiple sensor types (Gitup1 camera, Kestrel 5500) with focus on proper orientation and mechanical fit (tab mechanism).
- **Equipment Assembly & Logistics**: Assembly of spare AeroPods for future shipments; collection of SwiftPod from BST office; packaging and shipment coordination.

## Products & Capabilities Described
- **AeroPod**: Unmanned aerial vehicle being printed, assembled, and prepared for deployment; multiple spare units manufactured for future shipments.
- **TerraROVER**: Ground-based rover platform with printed/manufactured components as part of AREN project.
- **SwiftPod**: Retrieved from BST office in October; operation manual documentation initiated.
- **Camera Integration**: Custom mounts designed for Gitup1 and Kestrel 5500 sensor integration to AeroPod platforms.

## Use Cases & Applications
- Arctic environmental monitoring (AREN project focus)
- Multi-sensor platform deployment requiring integrated camera systems
