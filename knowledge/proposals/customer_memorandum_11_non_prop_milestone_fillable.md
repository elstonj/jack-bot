## Executive Summary
This document outlines Black Swift Technologies' proposed milestone schedule for an Air Force SBIR Phase II project on soil moisture mapping and soil integrity determination. The project spans 21 months with eight major milestones, totaling $1.25M in SBIR funding, progressing from requirements analysis through field testing and final reporting.

## Technical Approach
BST's approach involves:
1. Requirements discovery and specification from DAF end-users
2. Modifications to an existing L-band sensing instrument (radiometer) with auxiliary sensor additions
3. UAS modifications incorporating new surface temperature and NDVI sensors for real-time soil moisture calculation
4. Algorithm development to compute soil integrity (cone index) from L-band soil moisture data and soil type
5. Local field testing with in situ data collection for validation
6. Model refinement based on test results
7. Flight campaign testing at Tyndall AFB or customer-defined locations
8. Final reporting and documentation

## Products & Capabilities Described

**L-Band Sensing Instrument/Radiometer**
- Existing instrument being modified for soil integrity application
- To be enhanced with auxiliary sensors
- Measures soil moisture data at L-band frequencies
- Will be integrated onto modified UAS platform

**UAS Platform**
- Modified to accommodate updated sensing payloads
- Enhanced with surface temperature and NDVI sensors
- Capable of real-time soil moisture calculation during flight
- Designated for testing at Tyndall AFB

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Soil Integrity Assessment for Military Operations**: Specifically targeting determination of cone index (a measure of soil bearing capacity/trafficability) for airfield and ground operations
- **Airfield Operations**: Flight testing at Tyndall AFB suggests focus on assessing runway/taxiway soil conditions
- **Real-time Assessment**: Real-time calculation capability suggests operational deployment for rapid soil integrity evaluation
