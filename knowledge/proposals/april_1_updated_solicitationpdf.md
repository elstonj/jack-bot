# DARPA ERIS Master Program Solicitation

## Document Metadata
- Type: Open solicitation / request for proposals
- Client/Agency: Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA)
- Program/Solicitation: Expedited Research Implementation Series (ERIS)
- Date: Originally December 1, 2024; Updated April 1, 2025
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None mentioned in this document
- Key Personnel: Not applicable (this is DARPA solicitation guidance, not a BST submission)

## Executive Summary
DARPA's ERIS is an open, continuous call for disruptive technology solutions from U.S. and international companies, universities, and individuals. The initiative operates as a digital marketplace where pre-vetted "awardable" solutions are made available to DoD activities for rapid acquisition through Other Transaction Authority. Companies submit 7-minute video pitches that are assessed monthly against four required elements and can receive awards for research, development, prototyping, and testing.

## Key Program Elements

### Eligibility & Participation
- **Who can submit:** U.S.-based (large/small business, university, non-profit, individual), plus foreign universities/research institutions (subject to export control compliance)
- **Topic areas:** Four focus areas defined by DARPA:
  - Detecting and tracking elusive objects of interest (air, land, space)
  - Overcoming sensing system limitations (SWaP-C, performance)
  - Advanced antenna technologies
  - Technologies for resilience/efficiency of strategic systems (critical infrastructure, command-and-control networks)

### Submission Timeline & Process
- **Open period:** December 9, 2024 – May 30, 2025
- **Collection cycles:** Monthly (deadline 12:00 Noon ET last day of month)
- **Assessment window:** Results within 30 days of collection period close
- **Continuous resubmission:** Non-awardable solutions can be resubmitted in subsequent months with feedback sessions available

### Technical Submission Requirements

**Video Format:**
- Maximum 7 minutes (exceeding this is primary rejection reason)
- HD 1280x720 resolution, .mp4 format under 1 GB
- Rec709 or sRGB color space
- No restrictive markings except copyright notice

**Required Video Content Elements (4):**
1. Define the problem and current state of the art
2. How the solution advances the state of the art
3. Team capability (key personnel vision, expertise, experience)
4. Defense and/or commercial market use case/impact

**Required Submission Form Elements:**
- Company/individual name (must match SAM.gov if applicable)
- Website, UEI (Unique Entity ID), business size classification
- Nontraditional vs. traditional contractor status
- Socioeconomic designations
- Principal place of business address
- Topic area selection
- Technology Readiness Level (TRL)
- At least 5 relevant keywords
- Video submission point of contact with email/phone
- Submission title (< 128 characters)
- Abstract description (1,500 characters max)
- Four supplemental slides in prescribed format (Appendix B)

**Supplemental Slides (4 mandatory):**
1. ERIS Submission Information (general submission data)
2. Company Introduction (succinct overview of company and motivation)
3. ERIS Submission Criteria Quad Chart (summary of four video content requirements)
4. Solution Overview & DARPA White Space Chart (what is being done and how; metrics demonstrating impact)

### Assessment & Evaluation

**Compliance Review (within 1 business day):**
- Video length, viewability, graphic legibility
- Prohibited markings check
- Format/resolution/file type compliance
- Submission form completeness
- SAM.gov accuracy verification
- Topic area fit and TRL consistency
- Supplemental slides format compliance

**Peer Assessment:**
- 3+ member panel (government, industry, academic subject matter experts)
- Non-government SMEs operate under NDAs
- Assessment against published scoring rubric (Appendix C)
- Final awardable/non-awardable decision by Government Selecting Official (GSO)
- Scoring and assessor comments provided to all submitters

**Organizational Conflicts of Interest (OCI) Mitigation:**
- SMEs prohibited from assessing their own solutions or competitors where they hold financial interest
- All assessors complete government ethics training
- Minimum 3-member peer panel format with government official final decision authority
- Government monitors assessor scoring consistency to identify bias

### Post-Award & Marketplace Terms

**"Awardable" Status Benefits:**
- Solutions deemed "post-competitive" under 10 USC 4021, 4022, 4023
- Eligible for research, development, prototyping, test/evaluation, and procurement contracts
- Fully negotiable award terms (payment, IP rights, data rights, schedule, milestones, pricing)
- Potential for multiple awards with single/multiple customers
- No price ceilings or duration limits on awards

**Marketplace Management:**
- "Awardable" solutions remain in ERIS marketplace for 12 months, then expire/removed
- DARPA may remove awardable submissions before 12-month expiration at its sole discretion
- Company names and solutions published on DARPAConnect.us (can opt out with written notice to ERIS@darpa.mil)
- Submitting entity retains ownership of video and submission data; may request removal anytime

### Data Rights & Licensing
By submitting, companies grant DARPA government-wide, nonexclusive, fully paid-up, non-assignable, non-royalty bearing licenses:
1. To view, review, research, comment, share video/data within government and covered contractors (assessments, market research, requirements development, procurement consideration, award)
2. To view, review, research, share for process improvement (assessment/selection/criteria/interface refinement) in perpetuity
3. To transcribe video for search, accessibility, marketing, reporting, market research, requirements development (perpetual license in transcription data)
4. To use video/data for government training (ERIS planning, implementation, processes, assessment, feedback, administration)

### Important Notes for Submitters
- All submission preparation costs borne by submitter; no government reimbursement
- "Awardable" rating does not guarantee any award
- Can submit one or multiple video solutions during open period
- Feedback sessions available via office hours to review scoring and plan resubmissions
- Questions directed to ERIS@darpa.mil
- More information: www.darpaconnect.us/ERIS

## Notable Details
- **Streamlined pathway:** ERIS designed as low-barrier entry point vs. traditional DARPA solicitations
- **Rapid timeline:** 30-day assessment turnaround; continuous monthly collection cycles
- **Multiple award potential:** Single submission can lead to awards from multiple DoD customers
- **Weekly office hours:** ERIS Manager (Applied Research Institute/ARI) provides feedback sessions to improve resubmissions
- **Non-proprietary submissions:** All videos/supplemental materials must be unclassified, non-proprietary, non-export controlled, non-CUI
- **SME pool:** Assessors from industry, academia, non-profit research institutions with programs aligned to DARPA mission
- **Dispute resolution:** Any scoring disputes addressed to GSO
