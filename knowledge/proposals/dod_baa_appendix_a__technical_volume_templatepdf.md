## Executive Summary
This is a DoD-issued template and instruction guide for preparing Phase I Technical Volumes (Volume 2) in response to DoD SBIR/STTR solicitations. It provides formatting requirements, disclosure statements, and structural guidance for small business contractors submitting proposals. This is not a BST proposal or technical document, but rather a government-provided template that BST would use when responding to Navy STTR solicitations.

## Document Purpose
This document serves as the official format model and instructions for contractors (including BST) preparing Phase I technical proposals for DoD SBIR/STTR programs. It establishes standardized requirements across DoD services/components.

## Key Sections Required in Technical Volume

1. **Identification and Significance of the Problem or Opportunity** - Define specific technical problem/opportunity and importance
2. **Phase I Technical Objectives** - Enumerate specific objectives and research questions to determine feasibility
3. **Phase I Statement of Work** - Detailed description of approach, tasks, schedule, deliverables, and methods
4. **Related Work** - Describe significant related activities by PI, proposing firm, consultants; demonstrate state-of-art awareness
5. **Relationship with Future Research** - Anticipated results, Phase II foundation, required clearances/approvals
6. **Commercialization Strategy** - Market need, market size, commercialization timeline (~1 page)
7. **Key Personnel** - Qualifications of PI and key staff; includes resumes, awards, patents, publications
8. **Foreign Citizens** - Disclosure of foreign nationals/dual citizens involved in work with visa/permit details
9. **Facilities/Equipment** - Available instrumentation, physical facilities, environmental compliance statement
10. **Subcontractors/Consultants** - Details on research institution involvement and cost allocation requirements
11. **Prior, Current or Pending Support** - Disclosure of similar proposals/awards at other agencies
12. **Restrictions on Government's Use of Technical Data** - Assertions on proprietary data restrictions

## Formatting Requirements
- Single column, single-spaced
- Standard 8.5" x 11" paper
- 1-inch margins all sides (header/footer allowable in margin)
- Minimum 10-point font (headers/footers/embedded graphics may be smaller)
- Header on each page with company name, topic number, DSIP-assigned proposal number
- No locked/encrypted files
- No embedded active graphics (videos, moving pictures, etc.)
- Page limits established by Service/Component-specific instructions (proposer responsible for verification post-upload)

## Proprietary Data Protection
- Offerors may mark proposal data as restricted from public disclosure
- Must mark first page of each volume with disclosure legend specifying restricted pages
- Must mark each restricted data sheet with secondary legend
- Restriction applies only to evaluation purposes unless contract awarded
- Government retains rights to use data obtained from other sources without restriction
- Government obtains royalty-free license for government-only use (20-year restriction period for SBIR data)

## SBIR vs. STTR Requirements

**SBIR:**
- Minimum 2/3 of Phase I research/analytical work (by direct+indirect costs) must be conducted by proposing small business
- May include university and other subcontractors/consultants
- Federal Laboratories and FFRDCs permitted (no waiver required); must certify
- Universities cannot publicly release Export Controlled/ITAR-restricted information

**STTR:**
- Minimum 40% of Phase I work by proposing small business
- Minimum 30% by single Research Institution (required)
- Federal Laboratories/FFRDCs permitted but do not qualify as research partner
- Universities cannot publicly release Export Controlled/ITAR-restricted information

## Notable Details
- Phase I option work should be described if allowed/required by Component
- Proposals addressing human/animal subjects or recombinant DNA research must identify applicable protocols
- Funds cannot be released for human/animal subject or recombinant DNA research until proper approvals obtained
- SBCs encouraged to separate human/animal subject tasks in technical and cost proposals to avoid award delays
- All resumes count toward Volume 2 page limit
- Supporting data for technical data restrictions may be provided in Volume 5 (Supporting Documents) outside page limitation
- Service/Component-specific topic instructions take precedence over general instructions
- Proposer responsible for ensuring Technical Volume does not exceed page limit after DSIP upload
