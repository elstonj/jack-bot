## Executive Summary
Progress update on an SBIR-funded project to develop a novel aerial drone platform for exploration of Titan. The team was actively conducting flight testing on a foam wing prototype and identified control and propulsion challenges that needed to be addressed.

## Technical Approach
- Foam wing aircraft design being tested for flight control
- Focus on altitude hold capability and pitch control
- Iterative testing to identify propulsion limitations and control dynamics

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Foam Wing Aerial Drone Platform
- **What it is:** An unmanned aerial vehicle with foam wing construction, designed for potential deployment on Titan
- **Testing status:** Flight testing underway
- **Identified issues:**
  - Altitude hold struggling even at high speed
  - Requires high angle of attack (alpha) to prevent descent
  - Speed limited to ~25 m/s with current motor/propeller configuration
  - Pitch control oscillations observed
  - Descent rate increases when pitch approaches 0 degrees

## Use Cases & Applications
Exploration of Titan (Saturn's moon) - presumed to be part of a future planetary science mission concept.

## Notable Details
- Flight video documentation created showing pitch/descent behavior
- Key technical constraint: Current motor/propeller combination inadequate for speeds >25 m/s; modification required
- Pitch control needs higher-pitch propellers to improve stability
- Cmdded pitch (~10°) produced oscillatory behavior rather than stable flight
