## Executive Summary
This document outlines Black Swift Technologies LLC's IT security procedures and compliance framework for NASA SBIR/STTR contract 80NSSC25CA052, covering the performance period February 20, 2025 - August 20, 2025. The plan establishes security protocols for accessing NASA systems, protecting Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), and managing IT security incidents in accordance with NASA FAR Supplement clause 1852.204-76 and NIST standards.

## Contract Scope & IT Access
- **Contract Number:** 80NSSC25CA052
- **Performance Period:** February 20, 2025 - August 20, 2025
- **NASA System Access:** Limited to one system—SBIR/STTR Awardee Firm ProSAMS (https://prosams.nasa.gov/) for electronic submission of contract deliverables, invoices, and technical reports
- **Access Management:** Through NASA Account Management System (NAMS) requiring NASA Agency User ID and profile password via Identity and Access Management System (IdMAX)
- **Additional NASA Systems:** None applicable
- **User Registration:** Limited to personnel involved in contract negotiation and administration; all registered personnel required to complete NASA Online Annual IT Security Training

## Security Requirements & Obligations

### Information Protection Standards
- Protection of confidentiality, integrity, and availability of NASA Electronic Information and IT resources
- Protection against unauthorized disclosure of NASA Electronic Information
- Compliance with NIST and NASA IT Security requirements, regulations, policies, and guidelines
- Handling of Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)—unclassified information pertinent to U.S. national interest requiring special handling safeguards and limited dissemination

### Sensitive Information Management (FAR Clause 1852.237-72)
As per Computer Security Act of 1987 (PL 100-235), Black Swift Technologies LLC agrees to:
1. Utilize sensitive information only for contract performance purposes
2. Safeguard sensitive information from unauthorized use and disclosure
3. Restrict access to employees with documented need-to-know
4. Preclude access and disclosure to external persons and entities
5. Train employees on authorized uses and mandatory protections
6. Obtain written affirmation from each employee confirming training compliance
7. Administer monitoring process to ensure compliance; report breaches to Contracting Officer and implement corrective actions

**Enforcement:** Unauthorized uses/disclosures may result in contract termination for default or contractor debarment.

## General Rules of Behavior
Personnel supporting the project must comply with 15 established security protocols:

1. Use NASA systems only for lawful, official, authorized purposes
2. Protect all NASA information including PII and CUI
3. Report all known/suspected security incidents immediately
4. Encrypt all NASA data on transportable/mobile devices and removable media
5. Review NASA Web Site Privacy and Security Notices before network access
6. Screen-lock/log off when away from workstation; log off at day's end
7. Restrict NASA information access to properly cleared, authorized personnel with need-to-know
8. During travel, minimize sensitive data on IT systems and destroy copies when no longer required
9. Properly mask/label classified, sensitive, and proprietary documents and electronic media
10. Adhere to Separation of Duties principles; obtain management approval for conflicting role deviations
11. Do not use anonymizer sites that bypass Agency security mechanisms
12. Protect IT equipment and media from destruction, damage, loss, theft, or data compromise
13. Supervisors must adequately instruct, train, and supervise employees
14. Comply with minimum password requirements
15. Never share account passwords; protect passwords at highest classification/sensitivity level applicable

## Incident Response Procedures
- **Immediate Notification Required:** If any intentional or inadvertent information security incident occurs affecting confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information
- **Escalation Path:** NASA Security Operations Center and appropriate NASA officials including Contracting Officer (CO) and Contracting Officer's Representative (COR)
- **Triggering Events:** Compromise, misuse, distortion, loss, or destruction of any NASA IT system, data, or contract deliverables

## Legal Compliance & Enforcement
- Unauthorized attempts to upload or change information on NASA servers are strictly prohibited
- Subject to penalties under Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986 and National Information Infrastructure Protection Act of 1996
- Compliance enforced through sanctions commensurate with infraction severity
