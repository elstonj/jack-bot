## Executive Summary
NightFOX is a NOAA-funded project to develop remote sensing and in situ sampling capabilities for nighttime fire observations using the S2 UAS platform. As of July 2018 (Month 13), the project had completed prototype payloads and successfully test-fit them to the S2, though test flights were delayed due to a UAS accident. The team planned August test flights and September controlled burn sampling to evaluate the integrated system.

## Technical Approach
- Integration of prototype remote sensing and in situ sampling payloads onto the S2 UAS platform
- Remote sensing payload includes IR telescopes (4 µm telescope still in development) and CO2 sensor evaluation
- In situ payload includes aerosol components and prototype CO sensors (sourced from external developer)
- Addition of strobe lighting to the S2 for nighttime flight operations
- Planned test flights at higher altitudes and beyond visual line of sight (BVLOS), requiring FAA Certificates of Waiver or Authorization (COA) and Temporary Flight Restrictions (TFR) coordination with incident command

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S2 UAS
- Platform for carrying remote sensing and in situ sampling payloads
- Current Platform TRL: 6 (demonstrated system)
- Successfully test-fit with both prototype payloads
- Being modified with strobe lighting for nighttime operations
- Capable of operating at higher altitudes and BVLOS with appropriate approvals

### Remote Sensing Payload
- Status: Prototype complete except for 4 µm telescope
- Components: IR telescopes (one incomplete), CO2 sensor (under evaluation/improvement)
- Current Payload TRL: 5 (component and breadboard validation)

### In Situ Sampling Payload
- Status: Aerosol component assembled and ready for flight
- Includes: Coarse mode aerosol instruments (commercial), CO sensors (prototype, external sourcing, delayed delivery expected December 2018)
- Ready for flight integration

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Nighttime fire observations** during active wildfires
- **Controlled burn sampling**: Tentatively scheduled sampling of Security and Emergency Response Training Center controlled burn near Pueblo, CO for week of September 24, 2018
- **Fire weather research** and data product assimilation for fire weather modeling
- **Wildfire incident support** via coordination with FAA, Department of Interior, USFS-NOAA Fire Weather Research MOU

- Aerosol component of in situ payload assembled and ready

## Notable Details
- **Setbacks**: UAS accident in July caused test flight cancellations and schedule delays; CO sensor delivery delayed from original timeline
- **Regulatory/Coordination Focus**: Project emphasizes FAA COA coordination, TFR access negotiation, state/federal agency engagement (especially Department of Interior), and identification of incident command officials (airboss) for wildfire operations
- **Data Integration**: Project planning includes assessment of UAS data product assimilation needs and synthetic data set development for fire weather modeling
- **Partnership**: Coordination with USFS-NOAA Fire Weather Research MOU Working Group (Observations Focus Area)
- **Fabrication Support**: BST fabricated custom payload mounting frames per Cooperative Institute for Research in Environmental Sciences (CSD) design specifications
