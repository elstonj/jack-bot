# Canton Article: Black Swift Technologies and CU-Boulder Complete First Test of Revolutionary Soil Moisture Mapping Unmanned Aircraft

## Document Metadata
- Type: Press release / project article
- Client/Agency: NASA (SBIR Program); CU-Boulder Center for Environmental Technology (CET)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Program
- Date: 2015 (article written); flight tests conducted in 2014/2015
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: SwiftCore Flight Management System, sUAS platform
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (BST CEO), Dr. Maciej Stachura (CU-Boulder, project PI), Prof. Al Gasiewski (CET Director), Eryan Dai (research assistant), Geoff Bland (NASA Technical Monitor, Goddard Space Flight Center/Wallops)
- Partners: CU-Boulder Center for Environmental Technology (CET), UASUSA (airframe manufacturer)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies and CU-Boulder's Center for Environmental Technology successfully completed the first flight test of a small unmanned aircraft system (sUAS) equipped with miniaturized microwave radiometry sensors for soil moisture measurement. This ~3-year SBIR-supported effort demonstrated the feasibility of on-demand, high-resolution soil moisture mapping from small aircraft, with applications in drought monitoring, flood prediction, and agricultural water management.

## Technical Approach

**Sensor Development:**
- CET designed a miniaturized L-band microwave radiometer based on their decade-long development history with NASA, NOAA, and DoE on manned aircraft platforms
- Reduced sensor from "mini-fridge sized" to sUAS-compatible form factor
- Key challenge: electromagnetic interference (EMI) management between broad-beam L-band radiometer and high-power electric motor—solved during development and validated in flight
- Research assistant Eryan Dai led miniaturization efforts under Prof. Gasiewski's direction

**Aircraft Platform:**
- Selected the Tempest airframe (CU heritage, originally developed for 2009 tornado chasing by RECUV)
- UASUSA constructed modified airframe to accommodate CET sensor payload requirements
- BST further modified system for sensor and avionics integration
- Final system weight: ~8 kg
- Transportable in standard passenger vehicle; operational from unimproved areas

**Flight Management & Operations:**
- SwiftCore Flight Management System (BST developed) served as core avionics
- Designed for terrain-skimming operations: 15 meters (49 ft) altitude, 18 m/s (40 mph) speed
- Incorporates advanced estimation algorithms, automatic processes, gesture-based user interface
- Modular design enabled custom interface to soil moisture sensor
- Real-time sensor monitoring in flight
- High-rate data integration: autopilot data synchronized with extremely high-frequency sensor data (critical for sensor performance analysis)

## Products & Capabilities Described

**SwiftCore Flight Management System:**
- Highly capable FMS developed by BST
- Features: advanced estimation algorithms, automated processes, intuitive gesture-based UI
- Designed for accessibility to operators with varying skill levels
- Modular architecture enables custom payload interfaces
- Proven capable of precision, low-altitude operations (15 m AGL at 18 m/s)
- Demonstrated real-time high-rate data integration capability

**Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS (SMM sUAS):**
- Complete system: ~8 kg platform with integrated L-band radiometer
- Portable and field-deployable
- Capable of high-resolution soil moisture mapping
- Version 1 successfully tested; Version 2 in development with design changes for commercial operations

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Applications (stated):**
- Drought monitoring
- Flood prediction
- Soil integrity evaluation
- Water savings in agriculture
- Hydrological studies

**Mission Context:**
- Validation/augmentation of NASA Soil Moisture Active Passive (SMAP) satellite data
- On-demand, high-resolution soil moisture measurements to supplement satellite footprints
- Support for CU Grand Challenge (soil moisture focus area)

**Historical Context:**
- Prior CET manned aircraft campaigns provided data for flood forecasting and relief efforts in Texas
- Demonstrated hydrological measurement capability

## Key Results

**Flight Test Campaign:**
- Three flights conducted in Canton, Oklahoma over instrumented test field
- Location: field designed to support SMAP satellite validation, equipped with in situ ground sensors
- Successfully demonstrated miniaturized radiometer operation on sUAS platform
- EMI problem solved: "we seem to have beaten it" (Gasiewski)
- Data collected comparable to both ground sensor array and SMAP satellite measurements
- Demonstrated feasibility of using higher-resolution airborne data to augment satellite observations

**Approvals & Partnerships:**
- FAA airspace permissions obtained
- NASA approvals obtained at Wallops Flight Facility
- Established operational relationship with NASA
- Built on existing collaboration history with FAA Unmanned Programs Office

## Notable Details

- **Project Duration:** Nearly 3 years from start to successful flight test
- **Heritage:** Tempest airframe from 2009 CU tornado chasing campaign; SwiftCore informed by "years of field work" per Elston
- **Accessibility Goal:** Technology designed to "significantly lower the barrier to entry, and enable on-demand soil moisture data to a wide variety of communities"
- **Regulatory Achievement:** Joint FAA/NASA approvals at Wallops represent quality assurance of both sensor and aircraft platform
- **Next Phase:** Version 2 planned for spring testing with design changes enabling commercial operations
- **Data Sharing:** Project website established at SoilMoistureMap.com
- **Academic Validation:** Work presented at USNC-URSI National Radio Science Meeting, January 2016
