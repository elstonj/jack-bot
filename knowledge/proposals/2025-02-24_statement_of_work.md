## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies will procure and deliver five (5) fully integrated S0 small unmanned aircraft systems equipped with meteorological/oceanographic (METOC) payloads for NOAA's tropical cyclone research program. These systems will undergo clear air testing prior to deployment from NOAA WP-3D aircraft to collect atmospheric data in the tropical cyclone boundary layer and improve cyclone intensity forecasting models.

---

## Technical Approach

**System Architecture:**
- Five (5) fully integrated, flight-ready S0 sUAS platforms
- Deployment via free-fall chute from NOAA WP-3D aircraft at 5,000–12,000 feet altitude
- Launch tube and air-pressure release mechanism
- Designed for airspeed of 250 KIAS; demonstrated reliability at 210 KIAS
- Minimum endurance: 1.5 hours with payload

**Command & Control:**
- Radio line-of-sight C2 link with 150 nautical mile range
- Telemetry loss tolerance: <1 minute
- Full autonomous flight capabilities with three pre-programmed flight profiles
- Payload data transmission via C2 link

**Operational Characteristics:**
- Designed to withstand severe turbulence
- Classified as expendable platform
- Emergency termination capability for safety compliance

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 sUAS
- **What it is:** A small unmanned aircraft system designed for atmospheric sampling in extreme environments, specifically tropical cyclones
- **Proposed Use:** Clear air testing prior to in-storm tropical cyclone deployments; atmospheric data collection from WP-3D aircraft
- **Key Specifications:**
  - Deployable from free-fall chute at launch altitudes 5,000–12,000 ft
  - Operational airspeed: 250 KIAS (design), 210 KIAS (demonstrated)
  - Minimum flight endurance: 1.5 hours with payload
  - C2 range: 150 nautical miles (radio line-of-sight)
  - Autonomous flight with pre-programmed profiles
  - Expendable design for harsh environment operations

### METOC Payload Suite
- **Pressure measurement** (barometer)
- **Temperature measurement** (thermometer)
- **Humidity measurement** (hygrometer)
- **Turbulence Probe:** Measures three-dimensional wind components at ≥5 Hz sampling rate
- **Data transmission:** Via C2 link to WP-3D

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Application: Tropical Cyclone Research**
- Collection of meteorological and oceanographic data in the tropical cyclone boundary layer—a region currently under-sampled by conventional platforms
- Deployment from NOAA WP-3D reconnaissance aircraft during active hurricane operations
- Enhancement of NOAA's situational awareness during tropical cyclone events
- Improvement of operational models for predicting tropical cyclone intensity changes

**Clear Air Testing (Pre-Deployment):**
- Calibration missions in clear air, low-wind conditions
- Validation of all operational parameters before in-storm deployment
- Performance evaluation against reference systems and government dropwindsondes

## Testing and Evaluation Plan

**Calibration Mission (CM) Objectives:**

| Test Criteria | Evaluation Method |
|---|---|
| **Range Validation** | Confirm sUAS-WP-3D communication over 150 nautical miles |
| **Endurance Test** | Validate minimum 1.5-hour flight duration with payload |
| **Payload Performance** | Measure pressure, temperature, humidity, wind speed, and wind direction against reference systems |
| **Safety Compliance** | Conduct emergency response tests to verify flight termination functionality |
| **Data Integrity** | Validate payload data transmission and cross-compare with government-launched dropwindsondes |

**Deliverables:**
- Performance report summarizing mission outcomes and compliance with NOAA requirements
