## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies developed dynamic soaring technology and simulation capabilities to enable persistent autonomous aircraft operations in Venus's upper atmosphere (45-60 km altitude). The Phase II effort produced a comprehensive simulation environment for planetary aircraft design, validated dynamic soaring algorithms through Earth-based testing, and delivered a Venus-optimized vehicle design capable of energy harvesting through autonomous wind interaction.

## Technical Approach

**Core Concept:**
- Dynamic soaring (energy extraction from atmospheric wind gradients) to achieve persistent flight without fuel consumption
- Integration of Venus atmospheric models into high-fidelity simulation environment
- Development of autonomous dynamic soaring controllers
- Materials and deployment engineering for Venus's corrosive atmosphere

**Key Technical Components:**
1. Web-based atmospheric data server with selectable planetary models
2. 6 DOF high-fidelity simulation environment modified for aircraft design development
3. Workflow scripts facilitating mission design and simulation execution
4. Dynamic soaring algorithms and control schemes
5. Analysis of hybrid solar + energy harvesting power systems for extended missions
