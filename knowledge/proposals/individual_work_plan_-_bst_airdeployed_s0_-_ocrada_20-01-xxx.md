## Executive Summary
This IWP establishes a cooperative research agreement between USSOCOM and Black Swift Technologies to demonstrate the feasibility of deploying the S0 unmanned aircraft system from the rear ramp of a C-130 aircraft and operating it in operationally relevant environments. The effort will validate air-deployment methods, autonomous flight transition, communications handoff procedures, and system recoverability to inform future development of air-deployed expendable UAS for USSOCOM operations.

## Technical Approach

**Demonstration Objectives:**
1. Deploy S0 from C-130 ramp and evaluate stable post-deployment flight operations, including sleeve integration and aircraft interface considerations (Summer or Fall 2026)
2. Demonstrate autonomous navigation to a handoff waypoint defined while aircraft is in holding pattern, with transfer of command authority to ground-based operator
3. (Optional) Integrate S0 Ground Station Link with 400–450 MHz antenna mounted on C-130 to support monitoring/limited command capability via SwiftTab interface in aircraft cabin
4. Assess system functionality, operator usability, and survivability during landing and post-mission recovery
5. Generate technical data relevant to airworthiness assessments, operational safety, and system recoverability

**Key Technical Tasks:**
- Configuration and fabrication of up to two (2) S0-AD systems with deployment sleeves and ground support equipment
- Development of concept of operations and mission planning procedures
- Provision of system descriptions and technical documentation to support USSOCOM airworthiness review and approval processes
- Support for demonstration execution including preflight activities, deployment operations, and post-flight analysis

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 UAS (S0-AD Air-Deployed Variant)
- **What it is:** Small unmanned aircraft system designed for air deployment from manned aircraft
- **Proposed use:** Demonstrate deployment from C-130 rear ramp with autonomous transition to stable flight, navigation to handoff waypoint, and ground-based operator takeover
- **Background:** BST has invested more than 5 years and approximately 15,000 hours in research, design, development, and testing of the S0-AD platform; more than 40 systems have been manufactured and delivered to NOAA for operational use since 2024
- **Specifications/Performance:** Capable of autonomous flight following deployment, command authority transfer while host aircraft maintains holding pattern, and post-mission recovery and landing

### SwiftStation (Datalink)
- **What it is:** Ground-based datalink system for S0 control and monitoring
- **Proposed use:** Loaned to USSOCOM for limited user assessment during demonstration

### SwiftTab (Tablet User Interface)
- **What it is:** Tablet-based user interface for S0 operation
- **Proposed use:** For monitoring/limited command capability in C-130 aircraft cabin via 400–450 MHz antenna integration (optional task)

### Deployment Sleeve/Canister
- **What it is:** Integration hardware for air deployment from C-130 ramp
- **Proposed use:** Enable safe storage and release of S0 during deployment operations

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Use Case: Special Operations Air-Deployed UAS**
- Rapid deployment of ISR or electronic warfare (EW) payloads from C-130 aircraft
- Handoff of aircraft control from pilot to ground-based operator
- Operations in USSOCOM Advanced UAS and ISR mission areas

**Validation of Existing Capability:**
- NOAA operational use of S0-AD for tropical storm and hurricane data collection during 2024 and 2025 hurricane seasons
- Deployment from NOAA "Hurricane Hunter" P-3 aircraft
- Evidence demonstrates proven air-deployment methodology applicable to military platforms

**Future Applications (Not yet developed):**
- Integration of ISR payloads for reconnaissance missions
- Integration of electronic warfare payloads for tactical applications
- Baseline platform for further air-deployed UAS development within USSOCOM
