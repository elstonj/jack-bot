## Summary
This document outlines the roles and compensation for two subcontractors (Lois Wardell and Jorge Diaz) supporting a NASA SBIR Phase II project focused on volcano monitoring using UAS technology. Total subcontractor costs: approximately $143,150 over 18 months.

## Subcontractor Details

### Lois Wardell, Ph.D.
**Consulting Costs:** $54,000
- Rate: $150/hr, 20 hrs/month, 18 months

**Main Contributions:**
- Results Analysis
- Comparison to Satellite Data
- Data Cataloging

**Secondary Contributions:**
- Sensor and Payload Design and Integration (including Calibration Techniques)
- Desired flight patterns
- UI design review

**Travel Budget:** $5,000
- 2 trips to Boulder (5 days each): Kick-off meeting and lab testing of full scientific payload with flights in Pawnee National Grasslands
- Deployment to Costa Rica (up to 15 days)

### Jorge Diaz, Ph.D.
**Consulting Costs:** $54,000
- Rate: $150/hr, 20 hrs/month, 18 months

**Main Contributions:**
- Sensor and Payload Design and Integration (including Calibration Techniques)
- Desired flight patterns
- UI design review
- Permissions for Operations in Costa Rica

**Secondary Contributions:**
- Results Analysis

**Flight Operations Budget:** $24,000
- Single deployment to Costa Rica (15 days)
- Ground data collection for verification and validation of UAS measurements

**Travel Budget:** $6,150
- 2 trips to Boulder (5 days each): Kick-off and Flight Testing in San Luis Valley

- Project locations include Boulder (BST likely location), Pawnee National Grasslands (Colorado), San Luis Valley (Colorado testing site), and Costa Rica (field deployment site for volcano monitoring)

- Jorge Diaz contributed local expertise for Costa Rica operations and regulatory permissions
- The 18-month timeline and deployment structure suggest active field testing and data collection phases
