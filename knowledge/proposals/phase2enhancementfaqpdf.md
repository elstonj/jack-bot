## Executive Summary
This is NASA's official guidance document clarifying what types of investor relationships and funding arrangements qualify as valid "outside investment" under the NASA SBIR/STTR Phase II Enhancement program, which provides additional funding to Phase II projects that attract matching cash from external investors. The document provides specific examples and rules for determining eligible investments.

## Key Findings / Guidance Provided

**Qualifying "Outside Investor" Criteria:**
- Must be external to the company (not owners, family members, or affiliates as defined in 13 C.F.R. § 121.103)
- Can include venture capital firms, angel investors, other companies, or non-SBIR/STTR government programs
- Cannot include company's own internal funds or affiliated entities
- Can involve multiple investor entities simultaneously

**Qualifying "Investment" Types:**
- Cash investments in exchange for equity, royalties, technology rights, profit share, or advance purchase orders
- Funds must further development/commercialization of SBIR technology (R&D, manufacturing, marketing, etc.)
- Advance purchase orders for products directly resulting from SBIR project qualify if non-refundable
- Convertible loans qualify only if: (1) from public entity, or (2) private entity loan actually converted to equity before Phase II Enhancement application
- IPO funds qualify if offering memo indicates portion will fund SBIR-related work

**What Does NOT Qualify:**
- Straight loans (non-convertible)
- In-kind contributions (must be cash)
- Company's own matching funds
- Investments from family members or affiliated entities
- Circular investments between two companies
- Investments in partner institutions (e.g., university in STTR) rather than the small company itself
- Contracts for goods/services (unless structured as advance purchase order meeting specific criteria)

**Timing & Logistics:**
- Investment can occur anytime after Phase I project start
- Investment in spin-off company can qualify parent company if A and B determined to be substantially the same entity (affiliates)
- Investor funds can pay for activities outside the Phase II contract Statement of Work
- Large company subcontracting must be netted against their investment contribution
- Investor must provide full cash payment within 45 days of application selection

**Special Cases:**
- Venture capital firm owning 22% of company can make additional investments (not affiliate if ownership <50%)
- Group investments qualify if all individual investors are outside investors; family member contributions don't count even in group
- Pre-existing contracts between investor and company can be excluded from netting calculation

## Notable Details
- Guidance is based on Small Business Administration regulations (13 C.F.R. § 121.103) but NASA reserves discretion in applying affiliate definitions
- The underlying rationale emphasizes outside validation of commercial potential and verifiable cash commitment
- NASA SBIR/STTR Help Desk available for specific determinations: (301) 937-0888 or sbir@reisys.com
- The Phase II Enhancement represents NASA's attempt to encourage commercial investment in SBIR-developed technologies
