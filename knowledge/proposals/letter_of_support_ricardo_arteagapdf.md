## Executive Summary
NASA System Engineer Ricardo Arteaga writes in support of Black Swift Technologies' Phase II SBIR proposal for a machine learning-based predictive maintenance system for small UAS. He validates the market need for this technology and emphasizes its importance for safety-critical applications including beyond-line-of-sight operations, flights over crowds, and Urban Air Mobility (UAM) operations.

## Technical Approach
- Unsupervised machine learning algorithms to provide early warning and diagnostics
- Focus on predicting and preventing critical system failures on small UAS
- Particular emphasis on battery failure prevention (identified as key challenge for eVTOL vehicles)

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Predictive Maintenance System (Software)**
- Uses unsupervised ML algorithms for early warning and diagnostics
- Targets small UAS critical system failures
- Intended to support safety-critical UAS operations
- Specific application: battery failure prediction for electric vertical takeoff and landing (eVTOL) vehicles

## Use Cases & Applications
- Beyond-line-of-sight (BLOS) UAS operations
- Flights over crowds/populated areas
- Urban Air Mobility (UAM) operations
- eVTOL vehicle operations and autonomy scaling
- General safety-critical UAS mission reliability
