## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies will provide fixed-wing drone support for a three-year University of Iowa USDA NIFA project investigating UAV-based precision agriculture practices, specifically focusing on nitrous oxide (N2O) emissions monitoring in corn fields during spring planting season (April-May). The project includes three flight campaigns (2025, 2026, 2027) with increasing technical complexity, progressing from commercial N2O sensors to integration of UI's proprietary N2O sensor.

## Technical Approach
- Provide fixed-wing UAV platform capable of carrying N2O sensors
- Support payload integration: commercial N2O sensor in Year 1, proprietary UI N2O sensor in Years 2-3
- Flight operations at 400-foot altitude covering 3-acre areas
- Onboard data acquisition (DAQ) system and storage device for sensor data logging during flight
- Post-flight data retrieval and support for post-processing
- No real-time in-flight data collection/telemetry required

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Fixed-Wing UAV (model not specified)**
- Capability: Carry N2O sensing payloads for agricultural monitoring
- Use: Supporting precision agriculture research on denitrification processes in corn fields
- Specifications/Requirements: 400-foot flight altitude, 3-acre coverage area, onboard data storage capability

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Precision agriculture**: Monitoring nitrous oxide emissions from agricultural soils
- **Denitrification monitoring**: Measuring N2O release from corn field soils during spring planting season (mid-April to mid-May)
- **Optimal timing**: Post-rain soil conditions when denitrification rates are highest
- **Geographic focus**: Eastern Iowa corn fields (Cedar Rapids area, Iowa City region)

## Project Timeline
- **Project period**: June 1, 2024 – May 30, 2027
- **Flight Campaign 1** (April-May 2025): Commercial N2O sensor (Venus Solo, LSE 4450, or Sensiron options); 3-day duration
- **Flight Campaign 2** (April-May 2026): UI proprietary N2O sensor with BST integration support; 3-day duration
- **Flight Campaign 3** (April-May 2027): UI proprietary N2O sensor; 3-day duration

## Notable Details

- Travel and lodging for BST team to Cedar Rapids airport and Iowa City included in scope
- UI considering three commercial N2O sensor options for Year 1 comparison
- Data architecture: Onboard logging to DAQ/storage device; post-flight processing (no real-time telemetry requirement simplifies flight operations)
- Funding contingent on April 2024 decision, with June 1, 2024 start date
- Project focuses on agricultural environmental impact monitoring (N2O is a potent greenhouse gas)
