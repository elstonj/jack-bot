- **Date:** August 30, 2023

## Executive Summary
This document outlines Black Swift Technologies LLC's IT security procedures and compliance framework for NASA SBIR/STTR contract 80NSSC23PB371. BST commits to protecting NASA electronic information and IT resources, adhering to NIST and NASA security requirements, and implementing appropriate safeguards for unclassified and Sensitive But Unclassified (SBU) information accessed through the contract.

## Scope of IT Access & Security Requirements
- **Single NASA IT System Required:** SBIR/STTR Awardee Firm Electronic Handbook (EHB) at https://ehb8.gsfc.nasa.gov/contracts/public/firmHome.do
  - Used for electronic submission of contract deliverables, invoices, and technical reports
  - Access managed through NASA Account Management System (NAMS) requiring NASA Agency User ID and IdMAX profile password
- **No Additional NASA IT Systems Required** for this contract
- **Data Classification:** Unclassified and Sensitive But Unclassified (SBU) information

## Key Compliance Obligations

### Personnel Requirements
- Registration in EHB limited to personnel involved in contract negotiation and administration
- All registered personnel must complete NASA Online Annual IT Security Training
- Written affirmation required from each employee confirming receipt of and compliance with training on authorized uses and mandatory protections of sensitive information

### Sensitive Information Protection (1852.237-72)
BST agrees to:
1. Use sensitive information solely for contracted services; not to gain competitive advantage in other procurements
2. Safeguard sensitive information from unauthorized use and disclosure
3. Limit access to employees with need-to-know
4. Preclude access/disclosure outside the contractor's organization
5. Train employees on authorized uses and mandatory protections
6. Monitor employee compliance and report breaches to Contracting Officer
7. Understand that unauthorized disclosure may result in contract termination or contractor debarment

## General Rules of Behavior (15 Key Requirements)
1. Use NASA systems for lawful, official, authorized purposes only
2. Protect all NASA information including PII and SBU data
3. Report known/suspected security incidents immediately to firm help desk, security manager, or supervisor
4. Encrypt all NASA data on mobile/transportable devices and removable media when transported outside organization
5. Read NASA website privacy, security, and accessibility notices before accessing NASA network
6. Screen-lock/log off when leaving work area; log off at end of day
7. Grant NASA information access only to authorized personnel with proper clearance and need-to-know
8. Minimize sensitive data on IT systems during travel; destroy copies of NASA deliverables when no longer required
9. Properly mask and label classified, sensitive, and proprietary documents per NASA IT Security Policies
10. Adhere to Separation of Duties principles; obtain management approval for conflicting roles
11. Do not use anonymizer sites that bypass Agency security mechanisms
12. Do not engage in behavior endangering IT equipment or media (destruction, damage, loss, theft, data compromise)
13. Supervisors must adequately instruct, train, and supervise employees
14. Adhere to minimum password requirements
15. Do not share account passwords; protect passwords at highest classification/sensitivity level

## Incident Response Procedures
- **Immediate Notification Required:** NASA Security Operations Center and appropriate NASA officials (CO/COTR) in event of:
  - Intentional or inadvertent information security incidents affecting confidentiality, integrity, or availability of information
  - Compromise, misuse, distortion, loss, or destruction of NASA IT systems, data, or contract deliverables

## Regulatory Framework
- Compliance required with:
  - NASA FAR Supplement clause 1852.204-76
  - NIST IT Security requirements
  - NASA IT Security requirements, policies, and guidelines
  - Computer Security Act of 1987 (PL 100-235)
  - Computer Fraud and Abuse Act of 1986
  - National Information Infrastructure Protection Act of 1996
- Enforcement through sanctions commensurate with infraction level
