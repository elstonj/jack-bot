# Support Letter - NTCR

## Document Metadata
- Type: Support letter (subcontractor commitment)
- Client/Agency: NASA
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Phase II
- Date: December 6, 2016
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Ruggedized UAS for Scientific Data Gathering in Harsh Environments
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (BST Founder and CEO)

## Executive Summary
Dr. Jorge Andres Diaz of NTCR Consulting expresses support and commitment to participate as a subcontractor on Black Swift Technologies' NASA SBIR Phase II proposal to develop a ruggedized UAS platform for scientific data collection in harsh environments, specifically volcanic plume monitoring.

## Technical Approach
The proposed system combines:
- Commercial off-the-shelf (COTS) gas sensors with advanced spectrometric capabilities
- Unmanned airborne platform technology
- Payload integration and design for harsh environment operation
- In situ gas monitoring capabilities

## Products & Capabilities Described
**Ruggedized UAS for Scientific Data Gathering in Harsh Environments**
- Designed for volcanic plume monitoring and research
- Capable of integration with advanced gas sensors and spectrometric instruments
- Intended for field testing in real volcanic environments
- Applicable to Earth science research, academic institutions, and commercial sector

## Use Cases & Applications
- Volcanic plume monitoring and research
- In situ gas monitoring in harsh environments
- Earth science and academic research applications
- Government and industry user applications

## Notable Details
- NTCR Consulting proposed as subcontractor for payload system design, sensor selection, integration, field testing, and data collection/interpretation
- Dr. Diaz has prior NASA experience at Kennedy Space Center and Jet Propulsion Lab on spectrometric studies for Space and Earth Science Directorate programs
- Emphasis on leveraging recent advances in COTS gas sensors and UAS technology to enable new system capabilities
