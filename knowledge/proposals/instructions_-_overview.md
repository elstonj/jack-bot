## Executive Summary
This document provides comprehensive instructions for Black Swift Technologies (or another small business) to apply for NASA's Phase II-E funding option, which matches external investor contributions dollar-for-dollar ($25,000-$125,000) to extend Phase II SBIR work by 6-12 months. The document outlines eligibility requirements, application components, funding mechanics, and clarifications from NASA program staff via email correspondence.

## Phase II-E Program Overview
**Objective:** Advance Phase II innovations through matched funding requiring external investor partnership.

**Funding Structure:**
- NASA match: $25,000–$125,000 (1:1 ratio)
- External investor funds must equal or exceed NASA contribution
- No maximum on external investor funds
- Total period of performance: 6–12 months

**Key Eligibility Rules:**
- Application window: Month 12–60 days before Phase II contract end
- Requesting Phase II extension may disqualify applicant from Phase II-E
- Funds must be NEW funds, not obligated/existing funds
- External investor proof of funds transfer required within 45 business days of approval notification
- If proof not received by deadline, Phase II-E matching funds expire
- No work can commence until Phase II-E contract modification is executed

## Application Requirements

### 1. Phase II-E Application Form
- Level of SBIR contribution
- External investment amount(s) and source(s)
- Start and end dates for Phase II-E work
- Justification required if performance period exceeds 12 months
- Final Technology Readiness Level (TRL) target at completion
- Additional comments

### 2. Letter of Commitment (From External Investor)
**Required content:**
- Investor contact information (address, title, email, phone) on official letterhead
- Confirmation of willingness to verify commitment
- Total investment amount
- Acknowledgment that investment responds to specific Phase II R&D effort
- Confirmation that entire matching amount will be available for transfer within 45 business days of approval, without constraints
- Statement of investment use:
  - **For government program investment:** How resulting technology will integrate into acquisition program activities and funding path
  - **For private sector investment:** How investment furthers original Phase II technology or commercialization of resulting product

### 3. Statement of Work (PDF)
- Specific objectives of Phase II-E effort
- Detailed work plan with tasks, methods, schedules, milestones, deliverables
- Demonstration of significant improvement over Phase II work
- **Critical:** Must separately show work funded by SBIR/STTR vs. external investor funds
- Must show work is NEW development beyond Phase II scope

### 4. Cost Proposal (PDF)
- Detailed budget for SBIR/STTR matching funds portion
- Detailed budget for external investor matching funds portion
- Estimated costs with detailed line items per cost accounting system

### 5. Briefing Chart
- Updated non-proprietary summary of Phase II-E work
- May be freely distributed by Government

### 6. Commercialization Plan (PDF)
**Minimum required sections:**
- **Market Feasibility and Competition:** Target markets, competitive advantages, potential customers (including NASA programs and prime contractors), projected market size, time to market, 5-year market share estimate, anticipated competition
- **Commercialization Strategy and Relevance:** Strategy presentation and alignment with company's 5-year business plans; NASA mission infusion as viable option
- **Key Management/Technical Personnel and Organization:** Skills/experience in commercialization, current structure, plans/timelines for obtaining necessary expertise
- **Production and Operations:** Current development status, milestones and plans for production readiness, physical resource acquisition plans
- **Financial Planning:** Private financial resources committed, projected requirements, expected/committed capital sources, current financial condition documentation (financial statements, cash flow)
- **Intellectual Property:** Plans and current status for securing IP rights (patents, copyrights, trade secrets) for investment attraction, competitive advantage, and commercialization

## Critical Funding Mechanics (From NASA Email Clarifications)

**Fund Timing:**
- Commitment from investor must be made PRIOR to award
- Expenditure must be CONCURRENT with Phase II-E performance
- Funds cannot be obligated before Phase II-E work begins
- Work cannot commence until Phase II-E contract modification is executed

**Proof of Funds:**
- Legal binding document required (e.g., contract) showing obligation/transfer between investor and SBIR
- Purchase Orders (POs) do NOT meet the requirement
- Full funding payment must occur within 45 business days of approval notification

**Eligible Matching Investments:**
- Existing contracts/grants can count IF directly related to Phase II-E and awarded AFTER Phase II-E proposal submission (e.g., Phase III contract for same technology or related contract modification)

**Timeline Clarifications:**
- No "typical" decision timeline—dependent on Phase II contract status and submission timing
- If submitted during Phase I review cycle, decision will be delayed
- Application window strictly enforced: Month 13 of Phase II through 60 days before contract end
- Late applications deemed ineligible
- Start/end dates must be proposed by applicant, coordinated with Phase II contract specialist
- No guarantee of Phase II-E exercise due to limited designated funds

## Notable Details & Constraints

1. **Phase II-E funds cannot cover incomplete/unsuccessful R&D from Phase II**—work must be genuinely new development
2. **Separate funding vehicle required:** NASA will not add Phase II-E investment funds to existing Phase II contract
3. **CRP (Commercialization Readiness Program)** can be proposed as early as Phase I end but generally considered Post-Phase II; noted as new program under modifications at document creation date
4. **Contract specialist coordination required** for start/end date feasibility discussion
5. **Government exercise is discretionary:** Limited funds designated for Phase II-E options; approval is not guaranteed even if all requirements met
