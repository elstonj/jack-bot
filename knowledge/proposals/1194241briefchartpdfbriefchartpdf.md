- Date: 2017 (submitted/active during this period)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: SuperSwift sUAS, L-band radiometer, MALIBU payload, modular nose cone
- Key Personnel: Maciej Stachura (PI), Jack Elston (firm contact)

## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies proposed to mature SuperSwift sUAS and L-band radiometer technologies for soil moisture mapping through improved manufacturability, new radiometer design, payload integration, and 10 validation flight campaigns. The work aims to transition mature SBIR technology (TRL 6→9) to NASA programs, government agencies, and the commercial precision agriculture market.

## Technical Approach
- Build three SuperSwift aircraft using new manufacturing methods (assessed one-at-a-time for process validation)
- Redesign L-band radiometer for scalable, lower-cost production (current design requires custom calibration and integration)
- Integrate new payloads with modular SuperSwift nose cone design
- Conduct 10 flight campaigns to validate and test all improvements
- Perform manufacturing optimization focused on ease of production and rapid deployment

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift sUAS
- Mature, robust small UAS platform
- Multi-payload capable (ability to swap payloads in field)
- Design focused on reliability and cost savings
- Modular nose cone for integrated payloads
- Proposed as mission-enabling technology for NASA with secondary market in commercial UAS

### L-Band Radiometer (Soil Moisture)
- New revision with significant performance improvements
- More stable operation enabling wider adoption
- Current version: custom setup, calibration, and integration required
- Proposed new design: focused on rapid, lower-cost production
- New circuit board design and electronics integration planned

### MALIBU Payload
- Compatible with SuperSwift airframe
- Used for NASA satellite calibration/validation missions
- Can be swapped with soil moisture radiometer in field using same aircraft

## Use Cases & Applications

### NASA Applications
- Product validation for satellite missions: JPSS-1, GOES-R, Sentinel 2a/b, 3a/b, Landsat-8, MSG-3
- Reference measurements for CEOS (Committee on Earth Observation Satellites) calibration and validation framework
- Land validation support as NASA leads CEOS validation activities

### Non-NASA Applications
- **Precision Agriculture**: Soil moisture mapping for irrigation optimization
  - Colorado test site demonstrated >15% water savings with accurate soil moisture data
- **NOAA Mission**: Wildland fire sampling (ongoing collaboration noted)

## Key Technical Objectives
1. Improve airframe manufacturability
2. Design radiometer version optimized for manufacturing
3. Integrate new payloads with modular architecture
4. Validate through 10 flight campaigns
