## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies developed the SuperSwift XT, a ruggedized modification of the SuperSwift airframe specifically designed for volcanic sampling and operation in harsh environments including high altitude, strong winds, ash, and difficult terrain. The innovation addresses the need for a 5 lb payload aircraft with 1.5+ hour flight time capable of safe operation near volcanoes through sealed airframe design, protected motor cooling, redundant airspeed sensing, and enhanced aerodynamic control surfaces.

## Technical Approach

### Core Design Philosophy
SuperSwift XT is an electric-powered pusher configuration with V-tail design, derived from the original SuperSwift soil moisture mapping platform but ruggedized specifically for harsh environmental operation.

### Key Ruggedization Modifications

**Motor Protection & Cooling:**
- Designed case around motor to prevent ash ingress while maintaining cooling efficiency for optimal motor operation
- Addresses simultaneous need for sealing and thermal management

**Airframe Sealing:**
- Permanent gasket solution for hatches (replacing field tape solutions which fail in cold, wet, dirty conditions)
- Control rod access points positioned away from airflow direction to minimize ash ingress
- All aircraft components sealed from ash except necessary openings

**Airspeed Measurement Redundancy:**
- Primary: New large-opening (1/4 inch diameter) pitot tube designed to prevent water droplet bridging in rain/ash conditions
- Backup 1: Multi-hole probe (acknowledges ash clogging risk)
- Backup 2: Nephelometer (laser-based particle sensor, eliminates reliance on pressure sensing)

**Aerodynamic Control Enhancement:**
- Increased aileron and flap span for high-altitude turbulence management
- Aerodynamic analysis confirmed necessity for operations up to 20,000 ft elevation
- Addresses volcano-specific environmental challenges

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SuperSwift XT Aircraft
- **What it is:** Electric-powered, modular pusher-configuration UAS with V-tail, derived from SuperSwift platform
- **Payload capacity:** 5 lbs
- **Flight endurance:** Minimum 1.5 hours
- **Operational envelope:** High altitude (up to 20,000 ft), strong winds, turbulence, ash-laden atmosphere, rough/slope terrain for launch/landing
- **Novel feature:** Modular, field-swappable nose cone for scientific payloads
- **Key specifications:**
  - Sealed fuselage design for particulate protection
  - Protected motor cooling case
  - Enhanced control surfaces (extended ailerons/flaps)
  - Redundant airspeed measurement system

## Notable Details

### Innovation Novelty
Document identifies three novel features:
1. Sealing aircraft from ash with minimal mass increase
2. Control surface geometry modification for turbulence and altitude
3. Redundant airspeed measurement system ensuring continuity in particle-heavy atmospheres

### Competitive Advantages
- Modular, field-swappable nose cone is foundational differentiator
- Only harsh-environment rated fixed-wing sUAS platform able to operate in these conditions
- Redundant systems and backup sensors reduce mission failure risk

### Environmental Resilience
- Specific design for ash (corrosive particles)
- Rain/moisture resistance (large pitot tube opening)
- Cold/wet operational conditions
- Rocky, sloped terrain for field operations
