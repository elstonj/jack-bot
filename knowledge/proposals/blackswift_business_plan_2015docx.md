## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies is a Boulder-based sUAS avionics startup founded in 2011 that provides cutting-edge small unmanned aircraft systems and customizable flight management software. The company seeks to position itself as the leading avionics supplier for commercial and research UAS markets ahead of anticipated FAA regulatory approval for commercial drone operations in 2017, targeting agriculture, mapping, and scientific research applications.

## Technical Approach
BST's strategy centers on developing modular, user-friendly avionics and flight management systems that reduce operational complexity and personnel requirements. Key technical differentiators include:
- Proprietary algorithms integrated into end-to-end systems
- Tablet-based user interface (patents pending)
- Scalable software with basic operation modes for novice users and advanced features for experienced operators
- Electronics designs and software protected primarily through trade secrets
- Single operator capable of managing multiple aircraft simultaneously

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SwiftCore Flight Management System
- Core autopilot/avionics product
- Fully customizable user interface and control systems
- Quoted by RECUV: "cheaper, easier to use, and in many ways more capable than military systems costing 10 times as much"
- Reduces operational crew requirements from ~12 personnel to minimal staffing
- Leverages proprietary algorithms

### SuperSwift Aircraft
- BST's custom airframe offering
- Compatible with SwiftCore avionics

### Soil Moisture Mapping sUAS
- Specialized system for agricultural/environmental monitoring applications

## Competitive Advantages
- Technical team includes three Ph.D.s in Aerospace Engineering with focus on sUAS automation, applications, and design
- End-to-end integrated systems delivering superior usability and reliability
- Proprietary algorithms and electronics designs
- Early market positioning with government and research customers
- Proven customer satisfaction and referral-driven sales
- Modular architecture meeting diverse customer skill levels and needs

## Business Model & Market Strategy
- **Current Market (2015)**: Limited to COA (Certificate of Authorization) holders—universities, law enforcement, government entities
- **Direct sales** through trade shows and industry events to existing customer base
- **Partnership strategy** with airframe manufacturers—offering discounts and free compatibility customization to increase adoption
- **Lead management** via web-based system with ongoing customer communication and referral generation
- **Anticipated expansion**: When FAA approves commercial operations (late 2016/early 2017), BST plans to establish distribution networks through established channels and expand to commercial markets

## Market Opportunity
- Teal Group estimate: UAS market growing from $6.6 billion (2012) to $11.4 billion (2022)
- AUVSI estimate: $13.6 billion economic impact in U.S. during 2015-2017
- Expected 2017 FAA approval for commercial agricultural, oil, fishing, mapping, and video production applications
