# Letter of Support: Black Swift Technologies Fire Weather Forecasting Project

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: USDA Forest Service (United States Forest Service)
- Program/Solicitation: NASA ROSES 2024 (A.47)
- Date: 2024-05-24
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Small uncrewed aircraft systems (UAS)
- Key Personnel: Erik C. Rodin (USFS Aviation Management Specialist); Erik Rodin contact provided

## Executive Summary
USDA Forest Service expresses support for Black Swift Technologies' NASA ROSES proposal to demonstrate persistent fire monitoring using small UAS systems for improved fire weather forecasting. USFS will provide operational expertise feedback and guidance on integrating UAS operations into active firefighting missions.

## Proposed Project Details
**Project Title:** "Improved Fire Weather Forecasting with Continuous and Efficient Monitoring"

**Partners:** Black Swift Technologies, NASA Ames, NOAA

## Technical Approach
- Deploy small UAS for continuous observation of fire hotspots and fire perimeters
- Facilitate more accurate fire weather forecasting using persistent monitoring data
- Phased operational approach:
  - Year 1-2: Flights over small controlled burns with no other aircraft present (building on past demonstration experience)
  - Year 3: Either larger controlled burns or active fire operations at night (when other aircraft not in airspace)

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary Application:** Real-time fire perimeter monitoring and hotspot detection
- **Operational Integration:** Active firefighting operations (both controlled and wild fires)
- **Data Products:** Continuous fire perimeter observations and updated operational forecasts
- **Gap Filling:** Addressing data gaps in current firefighting efforts through persistent aerial monitoring

## Key Support & Commitments
USFS will provide:
- Expert feedback on utility of data products and forecasts for operational firefighting
- Guidance on airspace integration procedures for UAS operations with other active airborne operations
- Evaluation of results over controlled burns
- Operational perspective on practical deployment feasibility

## Notable Details
- Letter emphasizes methodical, phased approach to operational integration
- USFS recognizes BST's past demonstrated capability in controlled burn operations
- Multi-agency partnership demonstrates interagency coordination (USDA Forest Service, NASA, NOAA)
- Constraint awareness: addresses airspace management concerns by proposing night operations for active fire scenarios
- Potential future operational deployment if successful
