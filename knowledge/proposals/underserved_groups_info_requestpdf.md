## Executive Summary
This is a guidance document from NOAA's Weather and Water Prediction program that expands on application requirements and procedures for the FY25 funding opportunity. It provides clarification on eligibility, submission processes, readiness levels, and special considerations (DEIA, MSIs, human subjects research, etc.) for federal research funding competitions.

## Relevant Sections (Administrative Guidance Only)

### Key Administrative Guidance
- **Federal Institution Eligibility:** Federal collaborator costs limited to travel (critical cases only), equipment, overhead (max 20% of project), infrastructure, and testbed-related costs
- **Cooperative Institutes:** CI employees treated as university employees; funding awarded to universities; indirect costs transferred from NOAA to host laboratory
- **NOAA Testbeds:** Researchers must coordinate with testbed managers; Testbed Collaboration Form required if leveraging testbed resources
- **Submission Systems:** Applicants must register in SAM.gov, Grants.gov, and eRA Commons; two-stage validation (Grants.gov then eRA)
- **Common eRA Errors:** Missing/invalid eRA Commons username, PDF formatting issues, file size/name problems
- **Readiness Levels (RL):** Projects range from RL1 (basic research) to RL9 (deployment); NOAA does not expect all RLs to be achieved in 2-3 year projects

### Special Considerations
- **DEIA (Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, Accessibility):** Formal DEIA statement required; proposals evaluated on diversity initiatives, partnerships with Minority Serving Institutions, accommodations for disabilities, and team composition
- **Minority Serving Institutions (MSIs):** WPO strongly encourages proposals involving MSIs (HBCUs, HSIs, TCCUs, AANAPISIs, ANNHSIs); definitions and categories provided
- **Student Opportunities:** Proposals encouraged to include undergraduate/graduate student involvement; references to Ernest F. Hollings Scholarship, William M. Lapenta Internship, Jose E. Serrano EPP/MSI program
- **Human Subjects Research:** IRB approval required before research proceeds; OMB/PRA clearance may be needed for public information collection
- **Unified Forecast System (UFS):** Projects improving forecast models should focus on UFS compatibility and forecaster priorities; collaboration with Earth Prediction Innovation Center (EPIC) encouraged
- **High-Performance Computing:** Modest cloud computing expenses permitted for EPIC-engaged projects; federal institution direct costs may not exceed 20% of total project cost
- **Research Transitions:** Comprehensive Transition Plan required if project advances to RL4+ or intends post-award application transition; follows NOAA Administrative Order 216-105B
- **Invention Disclosure:** Any inventions must be reported to NOAA Technology Partnerships Office before public disclosure

## Notable Details
- LOI submission deadline: October 2, 2024 (5:00 PM ET)
- Proposal title page requirements include PI information, total requested funds, readiness levels (starting and ending)
- Abstract page: Must address project goal, problem/opportunity, methodology, outputs, expected results/benefits, and intended beneficiaries (max 50 words each)
- Multiple validation checks required; applications must pass both Grants.gov and eRA validation to be considered successful
- Organization registration can take weeks; early compliance with UEI, SAM, and eRA requirements recommended
- No funding can be made to applicants until all UEI, SAM, and eRA requirements are met
