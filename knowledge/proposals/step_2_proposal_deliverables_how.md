## Executive Summary
This document is an internal Black Swift Technologies working guide for preparing NASA ROSES 2024 A.59 FireTech proposal Step 2 submission. It outlines the required proposal components, formatting specifications, content requirements, and page limits for a NASA grant application focused on wildland fire management technology.

## Document Content & Structure

This is a **proposal preparation checklist/guidance document**, not a BST proposal itself. It extracts and organizes NASA's submission requirements for ROSES A.59.

### Required Proposal Components (in order):

1. **Table of Contents** (1 page max, optional)

2. **Scientific/Technical/Management (S/T/M) Section** (main proposal, typically 15 pages)
   - Objectives and significance
   - Perceived impact
   - Program relevance
   - Technical approach and methodology
   - Uncertainty sources and mitigation strategies
   - Team member roles and responsibilities
   - Work plan with milestones and management structure
   - Data sharing/archiving plan (if required)

3. **References** (no page limit, only peer-reviewed/publicly available sources)

4. **Open Science and Data Management Plan (OSDMP)** (2 pages)

5. **Biographical Sketches/CVs**
   - PI: up to 2 pages
   - Co-Is: 1 page each
   - Collaborators: optional

6. **Personnel and Work Effort Table** (outside budget section)
   - All personnel names/titles
   - NASA-funded time commitment
   - Non-funded time commitment (if any)

7. **Current and Pending Support** (not page-limited)
   - Required for PI and funded team members >10% effort
   - List commitments >10% effort per year

8. **Statements of Commitment and Letters of Support**
   - **Priority requirement**: Letter of affirmation from wildland fire management agency attesting to project significance/positive impact
   - Include in "Expertise and Resources Not Anonymized" document
   - Letters of endorsement required only in special cases (Foreign Co-Is, SLV vendors)
   - Letter of feasibility required for ISS proposals

9. **Budget** (no page limit)
   - Budget Narrative (justification of all costs, basis of estimate)
   - Detailed Budget (itemized expenses, separate budgets per subaward)
   - **Note**: Salary, fringe, overhead values excluded from peer-reviewed sections

10. **Facilities and Equipment** (no page limit)
    - Description only; no scientific/technical content

11. **PDF Appendices** (separate files)
    - Total Budget document (required separate PDF)
    - HEC Appendix (if NASA HEC resources used)

### Formatting Requirements (S/T/M Section):

- **Page size**: 8.5" × 11.0"
- **Layout**: Single-spaced, single column
- **Margins**: 1 inch all sides, no reviewable content in margins
- **Density**: No more than 5.5 lines per vertical inch
- **Text**: No more than 15 characters per horizontal inch (typically 12pt font)
- **Figures/Tables/Captions**: All text legible without magnification, 12pt font
- **Content requirement**: Text cannot be solely in figures/tables/captions

## Key Requirements for A.59 FireTech

- **Stakeholder letter priority**: "Priority will be given to proposals that provide a letter of affirmation from an agency involved in wildland fire management operations attesting to the significance or positive impact of the proposed project."
- **Reviewer experts**: Proposers encouraged to provide names and contact information of qualified proposal reviewers
