## Executive Summary
This is a biographical sketch of José L. Chávez, an Associate Professor at Colorado State University with expertise in irrigation engineering, remote sensing, and evapotranspiration (ET) estimation. The document covers his educational background, academic appointments, research publications, and extension/outreach activities related to irrigation water management and precision agriculture.

## Professional Background
- **Current Position:** Associate Professor, Colorado State University (2015-present)
- **Education:**
  - B.Sc. Agricultural Engineering, Paraiba Federal University, Brazil (1992)
  - M.Sc. Irrigation Engineering, Utah State University (1999)
  - Ph.D. Agricultural Engineering, Utah State University (2005)
  - Postdoctoral training in Precision Irrigation at Washington State University (2006) and Remote Sensing of ET at USDA ARS CPRL, Bushland, TX (2008)

## Research Expertise & Key Publications
Primary research focus on:
- **Remote sensing for evapotranspiration (ET) estimation** - multiple publications on thermal remote sensing indices and ET algorithms (SEBAL-A)
- **Crop water stress assessment** - using spectral reflectance and canopy temperature measurements
- **Deficit irrigation management** - particularly for maize above the High Plains Aquifer
- **Integration of airborne imagery and sensor data** into soil water balance models
- **Energy balance methods** - Bowen ratio, eddy covariance, Large Aperture Scintillometry (LAS)

Representative publications (2014-2018) include work on corn water stress assessment, thermal remote sensing indices, crop ET estimation models, and soil water deficit improvement methodologies.

## Teaching & Extension Activities
- Developed/upgraded graduate courses: "Irrigation Systems Design," "Irrigation Water Management"
- Created MS Excel-based decision support tool for Central Colorado Water Conservancy District
- Active extension specialist role providing technical assistance to Colorado farmers and county extension agents on:
  - Drought and irrigation management
  - Soil moisture sensor selection/installation
  - Drainage issues
  - Water conservation
- Presents at local, state, and national conferences; publishes fact sheets and technical materials
- Seeks funding from farmer associations, water conservation districts, state/federal agencies (USDA)

## Notable Details
- Bilingual expertise (Brazilian agricultural engineering background)
- Strong collaborative network across multiple institutions and agencies
- Focus on practical, applied research with direct farmer/stakeholder engagement
- Emphasis on "real world" field data in student training
- Experience with diverse measurement technologies: soil sensors, infra-red thermometry, multispectral radiometry, spaceborne remote sensing, etc.
