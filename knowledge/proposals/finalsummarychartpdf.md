## Executive Summary
Black Swift Technologies conducted a Phase I effort to demonstrate how UAS observations can improve wind and turbulence forecasts around wildland fires to enhance firefighting safety. The project focused on optimizing targeted UAS measurements, quantifying their impact on meteorological predictions compared to non-UAS simulations, and determining integration pathways with operational fire agencies and the Team Awareness Kit (TAK).

## Technical Approach
- Conducted experiments with UAS measurements for data assimilation into meteorological forecasts
- Developed software tools to convert raw UAS meteorological data into WMO standard and PREPBUFR formats used by modeling centers
- Utilized NCAR's Ensemble Sensitivity Analysis (ESA) methods to identify optimal locations for gap-filling observations
- Tested continuous-flying and multi-UAS flight operations for atmospheric data collection and verification
- Analyzed existing wildland fire data dissemination methods for TAK integration
- Coordinated with fire agencies to establish risk-mitigation techniques for concurrent UAS/manned aircraft operations

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 VTOL UAS
- **What it is:** Vertical takeoff and landing uncrewed aircraft system
- **Proposed use:** Targeted meteorological observations around wildland fires to improve wind/turbulence forecasts
- **Development:** Prototype updated through significant hardware revisions optimized for mountainous terrain and wildland fire conditions
- **Capabilities:** Capable of continuous flying and multi-UAS coordinated operations

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Wildland Firefighting Operations:** Providing enhanced meteorological data to improve forecasting accuracy for operational fire agencies
- **Gap-filling Observations:** Using ESA methods to identify priority locations for UAS deployment around active fires
- **Data Assimilation:** Incorporating real-time UAS observations into numerical weather prediction models
- **Safety Enhancement:** Improving situational awareness for manned firefighting aircraft and ground operations

## Key Results

### Accomplishments
1. **Meteorological Improvements:** Significant improvement demonstrated in meteorological predictions when utilizing UAS data assimilation (vs. non-UAS simulations)
2. **NCAR Partnership:** Established that NCAR's ESA methods can utilize current forecasts to determine optimal sites for gap-filling observations
3. **Operational Integration:** Networking with fire agencies enabled pathway for conducting S0 UAS flights alongside future wildfire operations
4. **Data Formatting:** Continuous-flying and multi-UAS flights successfully collected atmospheric data and verified data formatting processes
5. **TRL Status:** Maintained Technology Readiness Level 4 (both begin and end)
