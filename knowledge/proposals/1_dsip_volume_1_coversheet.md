## Executive Summary
This is an instructional coversheet template for SBIR Phase II proposals submitted to AFWERX (Air Force). It provides guidance on completing the Technical Abstract, Anticipated Benefits/Potential Commercial Applications, and Keywords sections for submission to the Defense Startup Information Portal (DSIP). The document emphasizes format requirements and the distinction between proprietary/classified content and publicly releasable information.

## Technical Approach
**Document Purpose:** This is a template/instruction document, not a technical proposal. It provides:
- Format and content requirements for Technical Abstracts (brief program objective/effort descriptions)
- Structure for Anticipated Benefits sections
- Guidelines for Keywords selection

**Key Structural Requirement:** The Anticipated Benefits section must follow a specific template:
"We solved [DAF problem] by providing [advantage], to help [DAF end-user organization] accomplish [DAF end-user's] goal. We will recognize profit by charging [non-Defense commercial customer organizations] to get [non-Defense commercial benefit]."

## Use Cases & Applications
This template applies to proposals addressing Department of Air Force (DAF) problems with commercial applications. Examples provided (but not related to this specific project):
- Smart Parachute Suspension Lines (SPASL) for strain monitoring
- Compton scattering imaging for aircraft structure inspection (IMHOS)

## Notable Details
- Document explicitly states: "DO NOT UPLOAD TO DSIP" – text should be copy/pasted into DSIP Volume 1
- Technical Abstracts are submitted to the Office of the Secretary of Defense (OSD) for public release; must not contain proprietary or classified information
- Maximum 8 keywords required; guidance suggests considering SEO-style keywords and Phase III transition partner identification
- Distinguishes between DAF end-user and commercial customer (often different entities)
- Emphasizes explicit identification of potential non-SBIR funding sources post-Phase II

**Note:** This document is instructional/template material. It does not contain specific technical details about a Black Swift Technologies proposal. The actual Phase II proposal content would be found in separate proposal documents referenced in the file path structure.
