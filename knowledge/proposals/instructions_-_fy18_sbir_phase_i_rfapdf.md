# Instructions - FY18 SBIR Phase I RFA

## Document Metadata
- **Type:** Request for Applications (RFA) / Program Instructions
- **Client/Agency:** National Institute of Food and Agriculture (NIFA), U.S. Department of Agriculture (USDA)
- **Program/Solicitation:** Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Program Phase I, FY 2018
- **Date:** September 27, 2017 (created); October 4, 2017 (modified)
- **Application Deadline:** October 5, 2017
- **Key Personnel:** Jack Elston (last editor)

## Executive Summary
This is the official USDA NIFA SBIR Phase I RFA for FY 2018, providing eligibility requirements, funding information, topic areas, and application instructions for small businesses seeking Phase I SBIR grants. The program seeks to strengthen small business innovation in federally-funded R&D across 13 topic areas related to agriculture, forestry, food science, rural development, and related fields.

## Document Structure & Content

### Key Program Information
- **Funding Source:** NIFA/USDA Small Business Innovation Research Program
- **Authorization:** Extended through 2022 (most recent authorization)
- **Registration Requirement:** SBA registration required
- **Application Deadline:** October 5, 2017, 5 p.m. Eastern Time
- **Program Goals:** Stimulate technological innovation, strengthen small business role in federal R&D, increase commercialization, foster participation by women-owned and disadvantaged small businesses

### SBIR Phase I Topic Areas (13 total)

**8.1 Forests and Related Resources**
- Focus: Forest health, productivity, sustainability, value-added materials from woody resources
- Key priorities: Sustainable bioenergy, climate change impacts, nanotechnology applications, wildfire management
- Contact: Dr. Charles Cleland

**8.2 Plant Production and Protection - Biology**
- Focus: Crop improvement, plant breeding, pest/disease resistance
- Key priorities: Floriculture, new crop development, plant diagnostics, bio-based pest control
- Contact: Dr. Robert Nowierski

**8.3 Animal Production and Protection**
- Focus: Livestock/poultry productivity, disease detection, alternatives to antibiotics, animal welfare
- Key priorities: Feed efficiency, biosecurity, product safety, environmental mitigation
- Contact: Dr. Robert Smith

**8.4 Air, Water and Soils**
- Focus: Conservation, monitoring, and protection of natural resources
- Key priorities: Water quality/conservation, irrigation technology, soil erosion/health, air quality
- Contact: Dr. Karelyn Cruz

**8.5 Food Science and Nutrition**
- Focus: Food safety, processing, packaging, nutrition, food quality
- Key priorities: Rapid pathogen detection, processing technologies, childhood obesity reduction, food-borne illness reduction
- Contact: Dr. Jodi Williams

**8.6 Rural and Community Development**
- Focus: Quality of life improvements in rural America
- Key priorities: Government efficiency systems, environmental protection, hazard resilience, youth/elderly/disabled services, employment generation
- Contact: Mr. Brent Elrod

**8.7 Aquaculture**
- Focus: Farmed seafood production expansion, species health and productivity
- Key priorities: Reproductive efficiency, genetic improvement, disease management, production systems, algal production
- Contact: Dr. Gene Kim

**8.8 Biofuels and Biobased Products**
- Focus: Non-food biobased products and biofuels from agricultural materials
- Key priorities: New industrial crops, competitive manufacturing processes, cellulosic biofuels, sustainable conversion
- Contact: Dr. Toby Ahrens

**8.12 Small and Mid-Size Farms**
- Focus: Sustainability and profitability of small/mid-size farms
- Key priorities: New agricultural enterprises, marketing strategies, farm management tools, renewable energy, educational outreach, urban farming
- Contact: Dr. Denis Ebodaghe

**8.13 Plant Production and Protection - Engineering**
- Focus: Engineering technologies for crop production and protection
- Key priorities: Crop production methods/equipment, pest/pathogen management, energy conservation, precision agriculture, pollinator support
- Contact: Dr. Rachel Melnick

## Key Requirements & Specifications

### Eligibility
- Small business concerns only
- Must be registered with SBA
- Specific size and other criteria detailed in Part III, Section A

### Application Components
- Electronic submission required
- Formatting requirements: Page limits, budget limitations specified
- Content requirements detailed in Part IV, Section C

### Evaluation Criteria
- Technical approach and feasibility
- Commercialization potential
- Qualifications of project personnel
- Phase I applications should include vision for Phase II outcomes and Phase III commercialization

### Special Considerations
- Applicants encouraged to contact National Program Leaders (NPLs) for topic suitability
- Applications exceeding budget/page limits or not meeting formatting requirements excluded
- Emphasis on proof-of-concept and commercialization pathway
- Strategic alignment with NIFA/USDA strategic goals

## Notable Details
- Phase I awards support feasibility/proof-of-concept
- Three-phase program structure (Phase I, II, III)
- Program subject to 7 CFR Part 3403
- Recipients must comply with responsible/ethical conduct of research
- Intellectual property rights provisions included
- Grantee commitments required post-award
- Program aligns with USDA Strategic Plan Goals 1-4 and NIFA Strategic Goal 1
