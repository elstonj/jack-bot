# NASA Letter of Support for Black Swift Technologies SBIR Phase II - Wildfire Project

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support
- Client/Agency: NASA Headquarters, Langley Research Center
- Program/Solicitation: NASA SBIR Phase II (2023)
- Date: November 14, 2023
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: Uncrewed Aircraft System (UAS) with meteorological sensor payload
- Key Personnel: Melissa Yang Martin (NASA Airborne Science Program); Jack Elston (BST, last editor)

## Executive Summary
NASA Langley Research Center provides formal support for Black Swift Technologies' SBIR Phase II proposal to deploy meteorological data collection via UAS to refine weather models for wildland fire forecasting. The project addresses critical needs in fire suppression planning by reducing uncertainty in fire weather models developed by the National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR), with potential applications for real-time data delivery to firefighters and alignment with NASA's FireSense and ACERO initiatives.

## Technical Approach
- BST proposes to develop and operate a UAS platform equipped with a meteorological sensor payload
- Collected meteorological data will be assimilated into fire weather models (NCAR partnership)
- Data reduction focuses on decreasing uncertainty in operational fire weather forecasting models
- Real-time data delivery capability being explored via systems such as ATAK (Android Tactical Assault Kit)

## Products & Capabilities Described
**UAS with Meteorological Sensor Payload:**
- Purpose: Collect atmospheric data for fire weather modeling
- Integration: Developed by BST
- Application: Data assimilation into NCAR fire weather models to improve operational forecasts
- Use case: Support wildland fire suppression planning and operations

## Use Cases & Applications
- Wildland fire weather forecasting and operational planning
- Real-time data delivery to deployed firefighters in field operations
- Reduction of fire weather model uncertainty for improved prediction accuracy
- Support to US Forest Service, Department of Interior, and state/local fire response teams

## Notable Details
- **Research Support Context:** Aligns with NASA FireSense Project and Advanced Capabilities for Emergency Response Operations (ACERO)
- **Stakeholder Support:** Addresses needs of multiple federal agencies (USFS, DOI) plus state and local fire response teams
- **Partnership:** NCAR as research partner for fire weather model development
- **NASA Commitment:** Beyond funding, NASA commits to:
  - Advisory support on data delivery to meet NASA data needs
  - Use case guidance
  - Deployment opportunities during NASA field campaigns for operational validation
- **Characterization:** Melissa Yang Martin describes BST as having "vision, nimbleness, and commitment to exceptional performance"
- **Strategic Value:** Letter emphasizes SBIR as critical enabler for small firms developing innovative operational tools
