## Executive Summary
NOAA's Weather Program Office is soliciting proposals for the Testbeds grant competition with approximately $4.2 million in annual funding available for 13-15 projects. Projects should focus on applied research, development, and demonstration/testing in NOAA's quasi-operational forecasting environment through engagement with one of five testbeds (Climate, Fire Weather, Hazardous Weather, Hurricane and Ocean, or Hydrometeorogy). Maximum funding per project is $300K/year for a maximum 3-year award period beginning August 1, 2025.

## Technical Approach & Program Objectives
The Testbeds Program supports technology transition from applied research to NOAA operations and services. Key characteristics:

- **Maturity Level Target**: Projects should fall in the "demonstration" level of technical maturity (Readiness Levels 5-8), representing mature projects nearly ready for or ready for testing in quasi-operational forecasting environments
- **Timeline**: Expected to speed transition of new research/technology into forecasting operations within 3-5 years to improve NOAA weather and water services
- **Focus**: Improvements to NOAA's existing or near-term future technologies rather than totally new/revolutionary approaches
- **Testbed Integration**: Close collaboration with at least one NOAA testbed is critical and required

## NOAA Testbeds & Program Priorities

### Five NOAA Testbeds:

**1. Climate Testbed (CTB)** - Managed by NWS Climate Prediction Center and Environmental Modeling Center (College Park, MD)
- Focus: Sub-seasonal-to-seasonal (2 weeks to 2 years) prediction
- Priority CTB-1: Data assimilation system improvements, including AI/ML methods for ocean, sea ice, and land data assimilation using JEDI and coupled GDAS/GFS/GEFS/SFS
- Priority CTB-2: Developmental activities to accelerate Seasonal Forecast System (SFS) - new methods, physics parameterizations, coupled system improvements
- Priority CTB-3: Improvements to S2S outlook methods including post-processing, statistical techniques, AI/ML, probabilistic forecasts, and diagnostic/verification tools

**2. Fire Weather Testbed (FWT)** - Managed by OAR Global Systems Laboratory (Boulder, CO) in collaboration with NWS and NESDIS
- Priority FWT-1: Fire weather verification datasets, success metrics, tools for fire conditions/evolution, environmental parameters, research on microscale-to-mesoscale conditions responsible for rapid fire spread
- Priority FWT-2: Social science research and decision support services, co-production approaches to improve end-user comprehension and forecaster use of long-lead fire weather data

**3. Hazardous Weather Testbed (HWT)** - Jointly managed by OAR National Severe Storms Laboratory, NWS Storm Prediction Center, NWS Norman Weather Forecast Office (Norman, OK)
- Focus: Severe convective weather (tornadoes, large hail, damaging wind, lightning)
- Priority HWT-1: Concepts/techniques to improve convection-allowing/resolving ensemble forecast systems from next-few-hours through next-few-days
- Priority HWT-2: Innovative post-processing and verification techniques for deterministic models and ensembles
- Priority HWT-3: New high temporal/spatial resolution in-situ and remotely-sensed observation datasets with dynamic 3D objective data analysis
- Priority HWT-4: Social/behavioral science frameworks to support improved forecaster and end-user decision-making

**4. Hurricane and Ocean Testbed (HOT)** - Managed by NWS National Hurricane Center and OAR Atlantic Oceanographic and Meteorological Laboratory (Miami, FL)
- Focus: Tropical weather and marine phenomena
- Priority HOT-1: Improved near-real-time 2D analysis of surface wind/wave fields in tropical cyclones
- Priority HOT-2: Improved guidance on supplemental observing system strategies
- Priority HOT-3: Tools/techniques to improve TC guidance (0-7 days) and probabilistic forecasts
- Priority HOT-4: Tools to help forecasters interpret disparate real-time observational data (remote sensing, aircraft reconnaissance)
- Priority HOT-5: Tools to diagnose NWP model characteristics compared to available observations

**5. Hydrometeorology Testbed (HMT)** - Located at NWS Weather Prediction Center (College Park, MD), partnered with OAR Physical Sciences Laboratory (Boulder, CO)
- Focus: Extreme precipitation (convection) leading to flash flooding; precipitation leading to winter weather
- Priority HMT-1: Improve probabilistic flash flood and winter precipitation forecasting using convection-allowing ensemble systems
- Priority HMT-2: Improve post-processing and verification for flash flood and winter precipitation forecasts
- Priority HMT-3: Enhance forecaster use of probabilistic information
- Priority HMT-4: Improve atmospheric forcings for hydrologic models

### Full Application Package
- **Due**: December 2, 2024, 5:00 PM ET
- **Format**: PDF, single-spaced, 12-point font, 1-inch margins, 8.5x11 paper
- **Submission**: Online at Grants.gov only; must pass 2-step validation (Grants.gov + eRA Commons)

### Project Narrative (14-page limit, excluding attachments)
Components (a-i count toward page limit):
- a) **Title Page** (1 page): Full PI/co-PI/co-I names, titles, institutions, contact info; total requested funds by year and institution; NOFO number; competition priorities; starting/ending RLs
- b) **Abstract** (1 page, separate): Concise responses to 6 prompts (max 50 words each): Project Goal, Problem/Opportunity, Methodology, Primary Products, Expected Results/Outcomes, Intended Beneficiaries
- c) **Problem/Opportunity Statement**: Relevance to competition science priorities, citations of prior research, NOAA project history if applicable, mention of existing NOAA Transition Plans
- d) **Methods and Activities**: Detailed description of methods, key activities, model frameworks, simulations, HPC availability, data collection/analysis, collaborations, necessary travel. If applicable: IRB/human subjects requirements, HPC access, testbed use
- e) **Project Products/Outputs**: Planned deliverables (recommendations, methodologies, tools, software, algorithms, training materials, datasets, code, outreach). Include current/starting and target/project-completion RLs with explanations
- f) **Project Impacts, Benefits, Outcomes, & Recipients**: Description of planned impacts, ultimate recipients (local weather offices, national centers, repositories, commercial entities), relevant metrics/success indicators
- g) **Schedule with Key Milestones**: Table/chart with completion timeline, products/outputs progression, RL iteration
- h) **Outreach and Education**: Plans for sharing results (publications, presentations, websites, workshops, training, code repositories); educational activities for students (K-12, undergraduate, graduate); scholarships/internships
- i) **Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, Accessibility (DEIA)**: How project broadens participation of underrepresented groups (gender, ethnicity, disability, geography). Specific activities addressing DEIA in research environment, including partnerships with HBCUs/TCUs/MSIs, collaborative diversity programs, accessibility accommodations, training initiatives

- j) **Data and/or Software Management Plans**: How data/software will be made publicly available; compliance with institutional policies

- l) **Current and Pending Support**: For each PI/co-PI/co-I requesting funds: project title, supporting agency, funding period (with months), investigator months, total dollar value. Include amounts requested in current proposal as pending support
