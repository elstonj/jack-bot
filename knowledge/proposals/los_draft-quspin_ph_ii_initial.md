# Letter of Support: Navy Phase II SBIR - Sonobuoy-Launched UAS with Magnetometer

## Document Metadata
- Type: Letter of Support (from technology partner QuSpin)
- Client/Agency: U.S. Navy
- Program/Solicitation: Navy SBIR Phase II - Sonobuoy-Launched Unmanned Aerial Vehicle for ASW (Anti-Submarine Warfare)
- Date: Draft dated January 28, 2025 (Phase II proposal due January 12)
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S0 platform, SL UAV (Sonobuoy-Launched UAS)
- Key Personnel: Dr. Jack Elston (BST)
- Partner: QuSpin (technology partner providing magnetometer)

## Executive Summary
QuSpin provides formal support for Black Swift Technologies' Navy Phase II SBIR proposal to develop an expendable Sonobuoy-Launched Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (SL UAS) equipped with Magnetic Anomaly Detection (MAD) and passive acoustic sensors. Phase I successfully established feasibility; Phase II will focus on full-scale prototype fabrication and validation.

## Technical Approach
- Integration of QuSpin's Gen-2 QTFM (Quantum Tunable Filtering Magnetometer) into the SL UAV platform
- Combination of MAD sensor and passive acoustic sensors for anti-submarine warfare
- Platform derived from BST's S0 platform (previously demonstrated in Phase I)
- Designed as expendable/single-use system launched from sonobuoys

## Products & Capabilities Described

### SL UAV (Sonobuoy-Launched UAS)
- **What it is**: Expendable unmanned aerial vehicle designed to launch from sonobuoys for maritime surveillance
- **Intended use**: Anti-submarine warfare (ASW) maritime surveillance
- **Sensors**: Magnetic Anomaly Detection (MAD) + passive acoustic sensors
- **Platform basis**: Derived from BST's S0 platform

### QuSpin Gen-2 QTFM Magnetometer
- **What it is**: State-of-the-art quantum tunable filtering magnetometer
- **Performance**: Noise spectral density <1 pT/√Hz
- **Key advantage**: Minimizes platform-induced interference
- **Design**: Robust, validated in laboratory and field settings for mobile platforms
- **Navy requirement target**: 20 pT/√Hz in 0.01–100 Hz frequency band

## Use Cases & Applications
- **Primary mission**: Anti-Submarine Warfare (ASW) maritime surveillance
- **Deployment model**: Cost-effective persistent maritime surveillance using expendable platforms
- **Launch method**: Sonobuoy-launched (rapid deployment capability implied)

## QuSpin's Phase II Support Commitments
1. Provide Gen-2 QTFM magnetometer for integration
2. Technical expertise for sensor integration into SL UAV
3. Laboratory testing to validate sensor performance against Navy specs
4. Guidance on data processing techniques for dynamic platform conditions

## Notable Details
- Phase I successfully completed and proved concept feasibility
- QuSpin explicitly committed to supporting full-scale prototype fabrication and validation
- Document emphasizes cost-effectiveness and operational readiness as key benefits
- Technology maturation goal stated as "operational deployment"
- Letter signed by QuSpin leadership supporting Navy funding
