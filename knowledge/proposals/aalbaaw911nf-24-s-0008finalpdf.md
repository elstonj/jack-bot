# Army Applications Lab (AAL) Broad Agency Announcement for Disruptive Applications

- Program/Solicitation: W911NF-24-S-0008
- Date: Issued 5 April 2024 – Valid through 4 April 2029
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: None mentioned in this document
- Key Personnel: Director, Army Applications Laboratory (signature only, no name given)

## Executive Summary
This is the foundational solicitation document for the Army Applications Lab's Broad Agency Announcement seeking disruptive technology innovations across 16 technology areas. The BAA invites proposals from academia, nonprofit organizations, government, and for-profit companies to develop breakthrough technologies that provide 2-4x improvements over current Army capabilities and support the Army's modernization priorities. Awards will be made as contracts, grants, cooperative agreements, or Other Transactions, with no specific funding reserved and continuous submission windows available.

## Key Program Focus Areas
The AAL seeks innovations in 16 technology domains:

1. **Autonomous Platforms** – Ground and air vehicles operating in cluttered, urban, underground terrains and contested environments; low electronic/physical profiles; rapid operator training; dynamic reprogramming
2. **AI/ML** – Autonomous vehicle behaviors, threat prediction, robust networking, intelligence analysis, multi-source data fusion
3. **Data Visualization & Synthetic Environments** – Sensor data visualization, large datasets, complex multi-source data, training and virtual-live validation
4. **Assured PNT** – GPS-denied operations, commercial tech enhancement, robust security, guidance/control, multi-source fusion
5. **Power Generation & Management** – Power algorithms, low-power electronics, compact sources for small UAVs/robots/munitions, storage/transmission
6. **Sensing** – Detection of people, equipment, weapons; obscured target detection; signature-based detection
7. **Communications & Networks** – Secure/reliable comms, protocols, multi-national interoperability, high-efficiency components
8. **Computation** – Throughput, power efficiency, edge computing for AI/ML algorithms
9. **Space** – Persistent high-resolution sensing, low-cost space access
10. **IoT** – Automated management concepts, ML-accelerated decision-making, secure/resilient networks in mixed cooperative/adversarial environments
11. **Quantum Technologies** – Quantum computing for complex problems, quantum sensing, quantum communications
12. **Signature Reduction** – RF, optical, thermal, acoustic, magnetic signature reduction for Soldiers and platforms
13. **Protection** – CBRN threat sensors, lightweight armor, cyber protection, human performance optimization, counter-UAS/missile/overhead surveillance
14. **Human Performance** – Human-machine interaction, training effectiveness, cognitive/perceptual/physical augmentation
15. **Contested Logistics** – Predictive analytics, autonomous technology for reach/endurance, logistics tail reduction, alternative fuels, point-of-need manufacturing
16. **Kinetic Payload** – COTS aerial system augmentation, additive manufacturing, interchangeable delivery systems, autonomous/semi-autonomous effects delivery

## Application & Award Structure

**Three-Stage Process:**
1. **Whitepaper/Demonstration Submission** – Required initial stage; applicants submit via AAL website tool; AAL provides feedback within 30 days if requested
2. **SAM Registration (Optional)** – Applicants can register early for efficiency
3. **Full Proposal Submission** – Invited applicants submit complete proposals via email (contracts) or Grants.gov (assistance instruments)

**Funding Mechanisms:**
- Procurement Contracts
- Grants
- Cooperative Agreements
- Other Transactions for Research (OT Research) – typically 50/50 cost-share; flexible IP protections
- Other Transactions for Prototype (OTA)

**Eligibility:**
- Institutions of higher education
- Nonprofit organizations
- State/local governments
- Foreign organizations/public entities (with security restrictions possible)
- For-profit organizations (large and small businesses)
- Federally Funded Research and Development Centers (FFRDCs) as allowed

**Cost Sharing:** Not required for general proposals; may be required/negotiable for OT instruments

## Technical Approach Requirements

Proposals must demonstrate:
- Significant scientific innovation
- Advancement of scientific state-of-the-art
- Clear Army relevance and operational impact
- Basic and applied research focus (not specific system/hardware procurement)
- Specific approaches for addressing scientific uncertainties
- Intermediate milestones for progress assessment

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Concept's scientific merit
- Potential contribution to Army mission
- State of technology/readiness level
- Impact potential

## Notable Details

**Key Program Characteristics:**
- **Continuous Solicitation:** Proposals accepted throughout the 5-year period
- **No Reserved Funding:** Government reserves right to select all, some, or none of proposals
- **Two Submission Pathways:** Ongoing Topics (continuous) and Special Topics (posted with specific deadlines)
- **Government Support Available:** Applicants may request use of Government-furnished facilities, Army expertise, or other Government assets
- **Experimentation Opportunities:** Selected proposals may be invited to participate in Army experimentation events (Edge, Project Convergence, Army Warfighting Experiment) with travel potentially covered

**Strategic Alignment:**
- Directly supports Army Futures Command's seven modernization priorities:
  - Long-Range Precision Fires
  - Next Generation Combat Vehicles
  - Future Vertical Lift Platforms
  - Army Network (Command, Control, Communications, Intelligence; Assured PNT)
  - Air and Missile Defense
  - Soldier Lethality
  - Contested Logistics

**Commercialization Focus:** AAL emphasizes dual-use solutions supporting both Army needs and scalable commercial markets as a key area of interest.

**Submission Platforms:**
- Whitepapers: https://aal.army (automated tool)
- Proposals for assistance instruments: Grants.gov
- Proposals for contracts: Email to aal-baa@army.mil
- Preliminary inquiries: AAL website contact form
