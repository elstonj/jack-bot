## Executive Summary
Keven Gambold of Unmanned Experts confirms his participation as a subcontractor on Black Swift Technologies' NASA Phase II SBIR proposal to develop an advanced UAS system for wildfire response. The project aims to use meteorological data collected by BST's UAS to refine weather models for operational wildland fire forecasting, with integration into the Unmanned Aircraft System Traffic Management (UTM) as a key requirement.

## Technical Approach
- BST will develop and deploy a meteorological sensor payload on a BST-designed UAS platform
- Collected atmospheric data will be used to decrease uncertainty in fire weather models developed by the National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR)
- Real-time data dissemination to firefighters via systems such as ATAK (Android Tactical Assault Kit)
- Integration into UTM infrastructure for operational deployment in wildfire response scenarios
