- **BST Products/Systems Referenced:** None identified in this document
- **Key Personnel:** None identified (this is a NASA solicitation document, not a BST proposal)

## Executive Summary
This is the official NASA Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase I solicitation for fiscal years 2026-2027. The announcement solicits proposals for 37 SBIR subtopics across 14 technical themes, ranging from aeronautics and propulsion to space exploration and planetary science. Phase I awards are limited to $225,000 with a maximum 6-month project duration. Proposals must be submitted electronically via ProSAMS by May 21, 2026.

## Key Program Parameters
- **Maximum Phase I Funding:** $225,000
- **Maximum Duration:** 6 months
- **Optional Technical Assistance (TABA):** Up to $6,500
- **Submission Limit:** Maximum 2 proposals per company
- **Technical Proposal Length:** 15 pages maximum (8.5" x 11", 1" margins)
- **Evaluation:** Rubric-based (see Attachment 26B.1)

## Research Topics Overview
The solicitation covers 37 SBIR subtopics organized into 14 technical areas:

### Aeronautics Research (AERO) - 8 Subtopics
1. **AERO.1.S26B:** Health Management and Sensing Technologies for Sustainable Aviation Fuel Systems
   - Focus: Fault detection, diagnostics, prognosis for electric/hybrid propulsion and alternate fuel systems
   - Areas: Electric machines, converters, power cables, thermal management, hydrogen systems
   - TRL Target: 3-6

2. **AERO.2.S26B:** Quiet Performance-Aircraft Propulsion Noise
   - Focus: Tools and technologies for predicting, diagnosing, and reducing aircraft propulsion noise
   - TRL Target: 2-5

3. **AERO.3.S26B:** Advanced Thermal Management for High-Efficiency Engine Cycles
   - Focus: Thermal management for hybrid-electric, all-electric, and advanced non-electric cycles
   - Need: Compact heat exchangers, cooling systems for high-power motors, battery thermal management
   - TRL Target: 2-6

4. **AERO.4.S26B:** Flight Test Measurement Technologies
   - Focus: Sensors, data acquisition, UAV architectures, digital telemetry, optical sensing
   - Emphasis areas: UAV navigation, sensor suites, low SWaP-C systems, high-data-rate telemetry
   - TRL Target: 3-6

5. **AERO.5.S26B:** Improved Material Discovery through Novel Modeling and Experimental Approaches

6. **AERO.6.S26B:** Wind Tunnel Test Measurement Technologies

7. **AERO.7.S26B:** Airspace Operations and Safety

8. **AERO.8.S26B:** Fuel to Electric Conversion
