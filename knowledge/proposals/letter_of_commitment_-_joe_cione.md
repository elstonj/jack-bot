## Executive Summary
NOAA commits $508,987.14 in matching funds to support BST's NASA SBIR Phase II development of a ruggedized small unmanned aircraft system (S0) for scientific data collection in harsh environments, specifically for hurricane boundary layer observations. The work encompasses payload integration, flight performance enhancement, mission automation, and development of both fixed-wing and VTOL variants for operational deployment from NOAA aircraft and ground stations.

## Technical Approach
BST's technical development focuses on:
- **S0 sUAS platform** optimization for tropical cyclone (TC) operations
- **Payload integration**: Laser altimeter and Multi-Hole Turbulence Probe (MHTP)
- **Communication system**: Extended range command and control (150 nm distance capability) between deployment aircraft (WP-3D) and sUAS
- **Automation**: Full mission automation based on scientific measurement goals
- **Data management**: Integration with NOAA's KARMA (real-time situational awareness) system and WMO HDOBS data delivery requirements
- **Design validation**: Preliminary Design Review (PDR), Critical Design Review (CDR), calibration missions, and dummy missions
- **VTOL variants**: Two VTOL S0 configurations for ground-based meteorological observations

## Products & Capabilities Described

### S0 sUAS
- Multi-hour duration small unmanned aircraft system
- Designed to be air-deployed from NOAA WP-3D hurricane reconnaissance aircraft
- Capable of safe operation in severely turbulent tropical cyclone environments
- Intended as expendable/unrecoverable technology
- Operates with continuous command and control up to 150 nm range
- Payload capacity: laser altimeter + MHTP
- Delivery milestones include 2 field-ready units (April 2022), 6 units (July 2022), and additional units (April/July 2023) with video capture capability

### S0 VTOL Variants
- Two VTOL configurations of S0 platform
- Purpose: Observations from surfaced-based (ground station) locations
- Delivery: September 26, 2022

### Multi-Hole Turbulence Probe (MHTP)
- To be integrated on Area-I Altius 600 platform (not a BST product, but BST contributes to development)
- Enhanced design to handle heavy precipitation
- Deliverables: 2 units (March 2023), 8 units (June 2023)
- Requires calibration missions and potential CDR

## Use Cases & Applications

**Primary Application**: Tropical cyclone (hurricane) boundary layer observations
- Data collection within the TC inner core and boundary layer
- Supports NOAA operational meteorological and oceanographic data requirements
- Enables safer, more persistent observations than manned aircraft alone
- Targets operational deployment August-November 2022 and August-November 2023 during hurricane season

**Secondary Application**: Ground-based meteorological observations using VTOL variants
