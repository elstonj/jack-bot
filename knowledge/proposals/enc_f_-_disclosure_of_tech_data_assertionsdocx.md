## Executive Summary
This is a DFARS-compliant regulatory guidance document instructing proposers on how to properly disclose and assert restrictions on technical data, computer software, and intellectual property when responding to a DARPA BAA solicitation. It outlines requirements for identifying noncommercial items, commercial items, patents, and other IP that will be used or generated under a proposed contract or Other Transaction award.

## Technical Approach
Not applicable—this is a compliance/procedural document, not a technical proposal.

## Key Requirements & Assertions Categories

### Noncommercial Items (Technical Data and Computer Software)
- Proposers must identify all noncommercial technical data and software planned for generation, development, or delivery
- Must assert specific restrictions on deliverables where Government will acquire less than unlimited rights
- Must follow DFARS 252.227-7017 format
- **Default assumption:** If no list submitted, Government assumes "unlimited rights"
- **Government Purpose Rights (GPR):** Mixed-funded data/software subject to GPR with automatic 5-year limitation (DFARS 252.227-7013, 252.227-7014)
- Failure to provide full information may result in proposal non-compliance and rejection

### Commercial Items (Technical Data and Computer Software)
- Must identify all commercial technical data and software that may be embedded in noncommercial deliverables
- Must list any applicable restrictions on Government use
- **Default assumption:** If no list submitted, Government assumes no restrictions
- Government may evaluate impact during source selection

### Patents
- Must document ownership or appropriate licensing rights to all patented inventions used in the proposal
- For pending patent applications containing proprietary information: may provide only patent number, inventor/assignee names, filing dates, patent title summary, plus representation of ownership or proof of licensing rights

### Other IP
- Proposers must provide good faith representation of ownership or appropriate licensing rights to all other IP
- Must provide short summary for each item asserted with less than unlimited rights, describing:
  - Nature of the restriction
  - Intended use in proposed research
