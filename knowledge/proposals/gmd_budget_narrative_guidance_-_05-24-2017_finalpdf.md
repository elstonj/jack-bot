## Executive Summary
This is a procedural guidance document from NOAA's Grants Management Division providing standardized instructions for applicants preparing budget narratives for NOAA grant applications. It specifies requirements for justifying and organizing Federal and non-Federal expenditures across standard budget object classes (personnel, fringe, travel, equipment, supplies, contractual, other, and indirect costs).

## Document Purpose & Scope
This is not a proposal or report from Black Swift Technologies, but rather a template and instructional guidance document that NOAA uses for evaluating grant applications. It provides:
- Standard budget categories and how to present them
- Required justification levels for each category
- Regulatory references (2 CFR §200)
- Sample budget formats with example justifications
- Requirements for multi-year budgets
- Cost share/matching fund documentation standards

## Key Budget Categories & Requirements

**Personnel**: Must include position title, name (if known), annual salary, percent time allocation, number of months, and total amount. Must justify each position's relevance to program objectives. Personnel cannot exceed 100% time across all projects. Administrative/clerical costs normally treated as indirect costs unless specific conditions are met.

**Fringe Benefits**: Must state rate used (typically 30-35%), provide annual and total amounts. If >35%, must provide detailed breakdown unless NICRA (Negotiated Indirect Cost Rate Agreement) provided. Must be proportional across Federal/non-Federal shares.

**Travel**: Domestic and foreign travel require separate justification. Must include origin/destination, number of trips, travelers, purpose, dates, airfare costs, per diem, lodging, ground transportation. Foreign travel must certify Fly America Act compliance. Travel details must be based on estimates if unknown.

**Equipment**: Defined as tangible property with >1 year useful life and per-unit cost ≥$5,000 (or organization's lower threshold). Must justify use and relation to objectives. General-purpose equipment typically ineligible. Lease vs. purchase analysis recommended.

**Supplies**: List by item with unit cost, quantity needed, total amount. Explanation required if >$5,000 or >5% of award. May estimate general office supplies as monthly amount.

**Contractual**: Separate budgets encouraged for each contract, required for substantial contracts. Must describe products/services and necessity. Subject to procurement standards (2 CFR §200.317-326) requiring full and open competition.

**Other**: Includes subawards and miscellaneous expenses. Subawards require separate budgets for substantial amounts. Training-related costs (materials, rental, speakers, substitute teachers) placed here. Conference costs allowable if reasonable and necessary. Excludes alcohol, fundraising, meals/coffee breaks.

**Indirect Costs**: Computed using most recent negotiated indirect cost rate agreement (NICRA). If no NICRA exists, may elect de minimis rate of 10% of modified total direct costs (MTDC). Rate types: fixed, provisional, or pre-determined. Foreign grantees without negotiated rate may use 10% de minimis rate.

**Cost Share/Match**: Must demonstrate sources and provide same level of detail as Federal share, broken down by object class. Must meet matching requirements stated in solicitation.

## Regulatory Framework
- Primary reference: 2 CFR §200 (Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards)
- Required forms: SF-424 (application), SF-424A (budget for non-construction), SF-424C (budget for construction)
- Key principles: Costs must be reasonable, allowable, allocable, and necessary

## Notable Requirements
- Budget narrative must match dollar amounts on all required forms
- Multi-year awards require separate budgets and justifications for each year
- No Federal or non-Federal grant funds for Federal employee transportation, travel, or expenses
- Cost of living increases must be built into multi-year budgets and justified
- Historical data acceptable basis for cost estimates if actual costs unknown
- Applicants should provide internal equipment policies to avoid closeout delays
- Training materials kept on hand as supplies; training materials ordered for specific activities placed in "other"
