# ByLight M2 Design Statement of Work

## Document Metadata
- Type: Statement of Work (SOW)
- Client/Agency: ByLight (integration center in Fayetteville, NC)
- Program/Solicitation: ByLight M2 Mustang Follow-On (Halo) project
- Date: April 7, 2026
- BST Products/Systems Referenced: S2/M2 airframe, BST Avionics, SwiftCore, EchoPilot, LW20/C Laser Altimeter, ADS-B (Ping200x)
- Key Personnel: Jack Elston (BST), Bryan Sparling (ByLight), Beck Cotter (last editor)

## Executive Summary
BST will design, test, and build out ByLight's M2 airframe platform for ISR applications, including design optimization for a higher-capacity battery, integration of BST avionics and sensors, and construction of two flight-ready M2 aircraft capable of 400km missions with car launch/skid landing capability. Work spans approximately 8 weeks (April 3 - May 30, 2026 for design phase, plus one month for builds upon fuselage receipt) at a fixed price of $126,629.

## Technical Approach

**Scope 1: M2 Airframe Design & Prototype (4/3/26 - 5/30/26)**
- Design and construct pouch cell battery with higher capacity to fit within fuselage without Hawkeye Munitions Payload
- Integrate BST Avionics (SwiftCore or EchoPilot options) with hot-swap capability
- Mount LW20/C Laser Altimeter
- Attach boom, tail, and tail surfaces to By Light design
- Design interim attachment points for car launch system
- Validate center of gravity and aerodynamic properties
- Conduct full 400km mission test flight
- Validate airframe durability: multiple skid landings, car launches, 6G dive maneuvers
- Iterate CAD designs based on flight test analysis
- Edit CAD for retractable car launch hard mounting points (for skid landing consistency)
- Design Trillium/alternative gimbal deployment mechanism
- Deliver final CAD files with manufacturing recommendations

**Scope 2: Build Two Production M2 Aircraft (within 1 month of fuselage receipt)**
- Construct 2x M2 airframes with carbon fiber wing and tail sets
- Integrate ADS-B (Ping200x)
- Test gimbal implementation, avionics hot swap, retractable launch mounts, laser altimeter
- Build smaller battery variant for Hawkeye munitions payload configuration
- Flight test both aircraft for autopilot tuning and efficiency
- Validate 400km ISR range in non-munitions configuration
- Verify consistency and durability across both platforms
- Ship completed M2 aircraft to ByLight Integration Center (Fayetteville, NC)

## Products & Capabilities Described

**M2 Airframe Platform**
- Larger version of BST's existing S2 system
- Designed for 400km long-endurance ISR missions
- Supports multiple payload configurations (ISR sensors vs. Hawkeye munitions)
- Capable of car launch system recovery and repeated skid landings
- Can execute 6G dive maneuvers
- Two variants: higher-capacity battery (ISR config) and smaller battery (Hawkeye config)

**BST Avionics Suite**
- SwiftCore or EchoPilot autopilot options with hot-swap capability
- Integrated with ADS-B (Ping200x) for airspace compliance

**BST Sensors**
- LW20/C Laser Altimeter for terrain-relative navigation

**Gimbal/Sensor Deployment**
- Trillium or alternative gimbal mechanism for payloads

## Use Cases & Applications
- Long-endurance ISR (Intelligence, Surveillance, Reconnaissance) operations with 400km range
- Non-munitions ISR configuration primary focus
- Munitions delivery (Hawkeye) secondary configuration with acknowledged range/performance trade-offs
- Car launch and skid landing operations (tactical/austere environment capability)
