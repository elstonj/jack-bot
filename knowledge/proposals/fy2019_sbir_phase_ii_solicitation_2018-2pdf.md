## Executive Summary
This is the official NOAA SBIR Phase II solicitation document for FY2019, announcing research topics and submission requirements for Phase II proposals. The solicitation invites Phase I awardees from NOAA2018-1 to submit comprehensive Phase II R&D proposals with awards up to $400,000 and 24-month performance periods. It covers program rules, eligibility requirements, evaluation criteria, and 27 specific research subtopics across four main technical areas.

## Document Structure & Content

This is a regulatory/administrative solicitation document rather than a proposal or technical report. It does not contain BST-specific information. Key sections include:

### Program Framework
- **Phase I**: Up to $120,000, 6-month feasibility research (already completed for Phase II applicants)
- **Phase II**: Up to $400,000, up to 24 months R&D/prototype development + 1-year commercialization activity period
- **Phase III**: Commercialization using non-SBIR funding sources
- Target award date: June 2019

### Eligibility Requirements
- Small business concern (SBC) status required (max 500 employees)
- Principal Investigator must spend >50% time with SBC
- Minimum 50% of research performed by awardee
- All work performed in United States (rare waivers possible with justification)
- No venture capital operating companies, hedge funds, or private equity firm participation
- Only Phase I awardees from NOAA2018-1 may submit Phase II proposals
- Proposers may not participate in topic selection or proposal review

### Submission Requirements for Phase II
- Comprehensive technical proposals (unlimited pages, more detailed than Phase I)
- Commercialization plan required
- Cover sheet (Form 9.1)
- Project summary (Form 9.2)
- Budget (Form 9.3)
- Certification of company size and SBIR requirements (Forms 9.5, 9.6)
- Fraud, Waste, and Abuse training completion required

### Evaluation Criteria
- Scientific and technical quality
- Commercial potential
- Available funds
- Possible duplication of ongoing research
- Importance to NOAA mission needs

### Human Subjects & Animal Research
- IRB approval required if human subjects involved
- IACUC approval required if live vertebrate animals involved
- Detailed compliance documentation specified

## Research Topics & Subtopics

The solicitation lists 27 specific research subtopics organized under 4 main topics:

### Topic 8.1: Integrated Earth System Processes and Predictions
- Radio Occultation from Recoverable Air Balloons for Weather Applications
- Determining the Timing and Location of Fish Spawning
- Calibration of Low-cost Air Quality Sensors

### Topic 8.2: Environmental Observations (12 subtopics)
- Portable phytoplankton species-identifier and counter
- Benthic data provider systems
- Bearing/distance measurement automation for binoculars
- Cetacean tag attachment systems
- Low-cost mooring location beacons
- Marine visibility/fog sensors
- Autonomous hypoxic zone mapping (Gulf of Mexico)
- Under keel clearance management for precision navigation
- Open water surface ROV for bathymetry
- Next generation water level observation stations
- Clean energy sources for NOAA long-term observation networks
- Cost-effective air-deployed UAS for turbulent environments

### Topic 8.3: Decision Science, Risk Assessment & Risk Communication (6 subtopics)
- Coral restoration outplanting
- Automated entanglement risk detection for aquaculture
- "Permit Wizard" software for permit applications
- Location-specific threat tracking tool
- Low-cost, high-nutrition plant-based finfish feed
- Disease monitoring tools for marine aquaculture

### Topic 8.4: SBIR Technology Transfer (1 subtopic)
- Miniature open path CRDS (cavity ring-down spectroscopy) instrument

## Notable Details

1. **Manufacturing Priority**: Executive Order 13329 requires high priority consideration for manufacturing-related R&D projects
2. **Energy Efficiency Priority**: Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007 requires consideration for energy efficiency/renewable energy projects
3. **NEPA Requirement**: All Phase I and II awards must undergo National Environmental Policy Act assessment before award finalization
4. **Data Rights**: Government receives license to SBIR technical data generated during award performance
5. **Patent Rights**: Detailed provisions for invention reporting and patent rights in Section 5.5
6. **Fraud/Waste/Abuse**: Emphasis on compliance; reporting mechanisms provided through DOC Office of Inspector General
7. **HUBZone & WOSB**: Special attention to historically underutilized business zones and women-owned small business participation
