## Executive Summary
This publication documents the development and testing of the Coyote sonobuoy-launched unmanned air vehicle (SLUAV) for atmospheric and ocean boundary layer profiling. Over ~6 years and nearly 50 test flights, the team integrated meteorological sensors into the Coyote to collect data compatible with AMDAR (Aircraft Meteorological Data Relay) formats for use in COAMPS® weather prediction models operated by NOAA and the US Navy. The platform demonstrated capability to profile atmospheric conditions from launch altitude to near sea level while operating from conventional P-3 aircraft sonobuoy launch tubes.

## Technical Approach
- **Platform Design**: Coyote SLUAV designed to fit in standard A-sized sonobuoy launch tubes (4.875" diameter, 36" length, max 39 lbs); deployable from P-3 aircraft at 170-220 knots at altitudes up to 30,000 feet
- **Flight Characteristics**: ~60-minute endurance; nominal cruise speed 45 knots (sustainable ~90 min) with dash capability to 75 knots; minimum 20 nautical mile range
- **Control System**: UTC Aerospace Systems Piccolo autopilot provides real-time command/control and data links to ground control station (GCS); autopilot gathers time, position, barometric pressure, wind speed, turbulence
- **Launch/Recovery**: Canister-based deployment with parachute descent; launch-to-flight sequence <0.5 seconds; aircraft remains on parachute in steady descent for 10 seconds during GPS lock; parachute released after 15 seconds when platform commences climb
- **Data Format**: Sensor data converted to FM-42 AMDAR message format for direct integration with COAMPS® weather models; WMO standard format compliance
- **Development Blocks**: Five major design iterations (prototypes, Block A1, A2, A2-1, B1, B1-1, B2); 41 total aircraft fabricated

## Products & Capabilities Described

### Coyote SLUAV
- **Purpose**: Provide on-demand atmospheric profiling without requiring manned aircraft to change altitude; extend sensor range and aircraft on-station time; improve survivability
- **Key Features**: 
  - Stores in standard sonobuoy launch tube
  - Real-time telemetry to GCS
  - Can operate <100m above sea surface
  - ~60-minute flight duration
  - Compatible with existing P-3 launch protocols

### Integrated Sensor Suite
**Operational (Green - tested on Coyote):**
- Piccolo autopilot (time, position, pressure, wind, turbulence)
- Pressure-Temperature-Humidity (PTHU) sensor - reconfigured from commercial aerosondes; approximately 3.5" × 2.5" × 1.5" with extended flexible sensors (~8" total length); produces continuous altitude profiles
- EO/IR imagery (640×480 or 320×240 cameras; dual gimbaled systems available from vendors like Trillium)
- Micro-Tesla magnetometer

**Tested on Surrogate (Yellow - Silver Fox UAV):**
- Ocean temperature profiling (capability to measure vertical temperature profiles)
- Sea surface temperature
- Sea wave height and periodicity (inertial measurement unit + GPS-based approach)
- Ocean surface currents

**Deferred/Future (Orange/Red):**
- Hyperspectral imaging (HSI)
- Radar
- Altimeter
- Aerosol sensors

## Use Cases & Applications

### Primary Mission: Meteorological Data Collection
- **Tropical Cyclone Monitoring**: Planned Caribbean field testing summer 2014 to collect data directly within tropical cyclones; offers extended data duration (>1 hour vs. dropwindsondes' minutes) with flexible positioning capability
- **Boundary Layer Profiling**: Detailed atmospheric profiles from launch altitude to sea surface to better assess boundary layer tropical cyclone structure
- **COAMPS® Model Augmentation**: Provide "actionable data" (AMDAR-compatible) to complement AMDAR streams from commercial aircraft and improve Navy/NOAA weather prediction models

### Secondary Missions
- Anti-submarine warfare (ASW)
- Intelligence, Surveillance, Reconnaissance (ISR)
- Magnetic field mapping

### Operational Integration
- Complement Aircraft Meteorological Data Relay (AMDAR) system which generates 300,000+ high-quality observations per day
- Augment Coupled Ocean Atmospheric Mesoscale Prediction System (COAMPS®) - Navy's two-way coupled atmospheric/ocean prediction model
- Support COAMPS-OS® visualization and scheduling interface

## Key Results

### Flight Test History
- **2007-2009**: Nine flights with Coyote A2-1 block; ~3.4 hours total flight time; testing focused on launch protocols and sonobuoy procedures
- **2013**: Seven flights with Coyote B2 block; ~4.7 hours flight time without incident
- **2014 (through publication date)**: Three flights with Coyote B1-1 block; ~2.63 hours flight time without incident
- **Total to Date**: ~49 flight tests and sorties; approximately 10+ hours cumulative flight time

### PTHU Sensor Data
- Successfully tested on Silver Fox surrogate UAV in 55-minute flights
- Demonstrated continuous atmospheric profiling with altitude increases in 500'-1000' increments to 4,000'
- Captured air temperature oscillations and humidity variations
- Confirmed ability to gather METOC data in WMO standard format

### Magnetometer Testing
- Commercial micro-Tesla magnetometer integration verified as feasible
- Test flights at 50 feet AGL altitude over known magnetic targets (parked vehicles)
- Initial testing showed insufficient sensitivity; identified need for 3-axis pico-Tesla magnetometer for future flights
- More sensitive magnetometer identified for future evaluation

### Wave Height & Current Measurement
- Successfully demonstrated use of Piccolo autopilot IMU and GPS to measure ocean surface conditions after platform ditching
- December 2011 test off Monterey Bay captured:
  - 14-second wave periodicity corresponding to 4.9-foot wave height
  - 11-second periodicity corresponding to 3.1-foot wave height
  - Platform drift tracking over 17.4-minute duration
- Confirmed feasibility of collecting ocean state data with autopilot sensors suite

### Data Format Conversion
- Successfully converted sensor data to FM-42 AMDAR message format
- AMDAR decoder tested at NRL Monterey; conversion deemed successful
- Custom .log file at GCS generates AMDAR records directly for COAMPS® ingestion
- AMDAR records include: OBS Type, VEH Code, Latitude, Longitude, Clock D/H/M, Altitude, Air Temp, Rel. Humidity, Wind Direction, Turbulence, Icing, Additional Data, Pitch, Roll, Pressure, E-Lat, E-Long

## Notable Details

### Development Philosophy
- Focused on mission-specific sensor integration without significantly escalating platform cost or schedule
- Emphasized data compatibility with existing operational systems (AMDAR, COAMPS®) for immediate operational utility
- Miniaturized commercial sensors where possible (e.g., PTHU probe from aerosondes)
- Leveraged existing aircraft infrastructure (P-3 sonobuoy launch tubes) for deployment

### Partnership & Support
- Multi-agency collaboration: US Navy (primary funder across multiple contracts from 2004-2014), NOAA (Hurricane Sandy funding), US Air Force Research Laboratory (2013 testing)
- University and private sector partnerships: Sensintel Inc., Itri Corporation, Hydronalix

### Competitive Advantages
- First sonobuoy-launchable UAV with integrated METOC sensors
- Compatibility with existing aircraft platforms and launch procedures (no modification required)
- Extended observation duration compared to dropwindsondes while maintaining deployment flexibility
- Real-time data link and near real-time 3D atmospheric profiling capability
- Direct integration with Navy/NOAA operational weather models
