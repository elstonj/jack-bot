## Executive Summary
Dr. Lois J. Wardell is a geochemist and environmental engineer with 15+ years of scientific research experience and expertise in UAS technology, volcanic emissions monitoring, and polar research. She founded Arapahoe SciTech to commercialize leading-edge technology and has collaborated extensively on UAS development for atmospheric research and remote sensing applications.

## Professional Background

**Current Role:**
- Founder, Arapahoe SciTech (focused on technology development and commercialization for commercial and research sectors)

**Previous Employment:**
- Advanced Ceramics Research: Chief Scientist for UAS sensor development and integration; managed remote field projects
- MicroBioSystems: Led development of handheld sensors for microbial detection (medical and environmental applications)
- University of Mississippi & National Center of Pharmaceutical Research: Research in chemical isolation, structure elucidation, anti-cancer treatments

**Recognition:**
- Featured as one of Top 13 Women to Watch in 2013 (Arizona Business Journal) for technology innovation
- Published 60+ professional papers and presentations

## Key Technical Expertise & Experience

### UAS Development & Operations
- Managed UAS development and flight operations for Scripps Maldives Campaign (2006) using Manta UAS, demonstrating utility of UAS stack flight for atmospheric research
- Collaborator with Latitude Engineering on range of UAS projects
- Team member for NASA Navsonde development (rapid high-altitude response, long-range Arctic UAS)
- Published on transitioning unmanned technologies for Earth science applications and autonomous platforms for polar research

### Volcanic Emissions Monitoring
- 15+ years studying emissions from active volcanoes
- Developed remote sensing systems and techniques (one resulted in commercially available miniaturized spectrometer)
- Research on volcanic metal and halogen emission rates (White Island, New Zealand; Mt. Erebus, Antarctica)
- Monitoring carbon dioxide emission rates from Mt. Erebus
- Satellite measurements of SO2 flux from volcanic gas plumes
- Simultaneous scanning LIDAR and DOAS measurements of smelter and volcanic emissions

### Polar & Antarctic Research
- Led collaborative effort developing down-hole cable disinfection system for WISSARD Antarctic ice drill
- Successfully retrieved first viable samples from bottom of ice sheet (2012/2013 field season)
- Expertise in subglacial microbial population monitoring instrumentation
- Environmental remediation and recycling program implementation for US Antarctic bases
- Antarctic stromatolite research for NASA exobiology program (biomarker discovery)
- Field work across all seven continents, including remote polar regions

### Sensor Development
- Microbial detection sensors (handheld, medical and environmental applications)
- UV-absorption and fluorescence spectroscopy for mercury detection
- Remote sensing systems for volcanic monitoring
