### Key Financial Observations

1. **Strong Budget Control**: Despite labor and equipment category overruns, the project was managed to stay within the total $148,729 contract value, with only $17.37 remaining unspent.

2. **Equipment Rental Premium**: The 11.4% overage in Materials/Equipment Rental suggests either:
   - Additional deployment days beyond the 20 days originally planned (4 deployments × 5 days/year = 20 days Year One; 3 deployments × 5 days/year = 15 days Year Two)
   - Higher aircraft rental rates than the $1,000/day assumption
   - Post-deployment mobilization or equipment repositioning costs

3. **Travel Efficiency**: Strong underspend of $6,189 in travel costs indicates cost-conscious crew management and possibly smaller crew sizes or more economical accommodations than budgeted.

4. **No Cost Overruns Relative to Contract**: The project did not exceed the $148,729 contract ceiling. All overspends in labor and equipment were offset by travel savings.

5. **No Documented Payment Issues**: The Drive Budget Documents do not flag any payment delays, rejected invoices, or disputed amounts. Invoice 1595 (final, 11/29/2023) was submitted; **payment receipt status unknown (QuickBooks data not provided)**.

### Concerns & Red Flags

- ⚠️ **Labor Overrun**: +2.5% across both years suggests either scope creep or underestimated labor rates. However, the overrun was modest and managed within overall budget.
- ⚠️ **Equipment Rental Overrun**: +11.4% is the largest category variance. The 20% Year Two overrun may indicate scope expansion or unforeseen equipment costs.
- ❓ **Invoice Documentation Gaps**: Invoices 1535, 1566, 1580, 1586 lack detailed supporting documents (period covered, itemization). Only amounts inferred from Budget Tracking spreadsheet.
- ❓ **Payment Confirmation**: No QuickBooks data provided to confirm whether invoices 1514–1595 were actually paid by CU Boulder. Invoice status listed as "Signed" or "Submitted" but not "Paid."

**Project 450-1 demonstrated strong financial management.** Despite modest overruns in labor (+$2,172) and equipment rental (+$4,000), the project finished essentially on budget (–$17.37 or 0.01% variance) through disciplined spending in travel costs (–$6,189).
