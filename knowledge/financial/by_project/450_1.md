## Financial Health Assessment

### Overall Budget Performance
✅ **Project is on budget overall**: Total invoiced ($148,711.63) vs. contracted ($148,729.00) = **$17.37 underspend** (0.01%). Project **CLOSED** as of November 29, 2023.

### Performance by Cost Category

#### ✅ Materials/Equipment Rental: **$4,000 OVERSPENT** (–$4,000 / 111% of budget)
- **Year One**: +$1,000 over (budgeted $20K, spent $21K)
- **Year Two**: +$3,000 over (budgeted $15K, spent $18K)
- **Root Cause**: Likely additional aircraft rental days or higher daily rates ($1,000/day may have been insufficient estimate). Year Two overage is more significant (20% over budget).
- **Assessment**: Recurring overspend pattern suggests initial rate assumptions were underestimated. However, total project underspend in Travel category (below) offset this variance.

#### ⚠️ Labor: **$2,172 OVERSPENT** (–$2,172 / 102% of budget)
- **Year One**: +$1,508 over (budgeted $50,352, spent $51,860)
- **Year Two**: +$664 over (budgeted $37,764, spent $38,428)
- **Root Cause**: Additional crew operations hours, project management, or data processing beyond planned scope. Smaller overage in Year Two (1.8%) suggests better scope management or reduced deployment intensity.
- **Assessment**: Labor cost control deteriorated in Year One but improved in Year Two. Overages are modest relative to total labor budget (~2% overall).

#### ✅ Travel: **$6,189 UNDERSPENT** (+$6,189 / 76% of budget)
- **Year One**: –$3,167 under (budgeted $14,636, spent $11,468.64)
- **Year Two**: –$3,022 under (budgeted $10,977, spent $7,954.99)
- **Root Cause**: Travel savings consistent across both years (22–27% underspend). Likely reflects:
  - Shorter actual field deployment durations than budgeted
  - Lower per-diem utilization or meal cost savings
  - Vehicle rental optimization or consolidation
  - Fewer lodging nights than the 12 nights/deployment baseline
- **Assessment**: Significant and consistent savings in travel suggest either efficient execution or reduced field scope. This underspend effectively absorbed the Materials and Labor overruns, keeping overall project within 0.01% of budget.
