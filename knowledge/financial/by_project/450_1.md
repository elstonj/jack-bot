### **Category-Level Performance**

#### **✓ Materials/Equipment Rental: 111.4% of Budget (SLIGHTLY OVER)**
- **Budgeted**: $35,000.00
- **Actual**: $39,000.00
- **Overage**: –$4,000.00
- **Analysis**: Year One overspent by $1,000 (105% of $20,000 budget); Year Two overspent by $3,000 (120% of $15,000 budget). This likely reflects actual S2 aircraft rental costs exceeding the $1,000/day rate assumption, possibly due to additional flight hours, extended deployments, or higher-than-budgeted daily rates. **Concern Level: Low** — overages are modest relative to total contract value (2.7% of total) and were absorbed within the overall budget ceiling.

#### **✓ Direct Labor: 102.5% of Budget (MINOR OVERAGE)**
- **Budgeted**: $88,116.00
- **Actual**: $90,288.00
- **Overage**: –$2,172.00
- **Analysis**: Year One overspent by $1,508 (102.9%); Year Two overspent by $664 (101.8%). Modest overages suggest additional hours were billed for project management, flight crew operations, or data processing beyond the planned hourly allocations. **Concern Level: Low** — overages total only 2.5% of budgeted labor and indicate scope creep or underestimation of effort, but both are within acceptable variance.

#### **✓ Travel: 75.8% of Budget (UNDER BUDGET)**
- **Budgeted**: $25,613.00
- **Actual**: $19,423.63
- **Under-spend**: $6,189.37 (24.2% savings)
- **Analysis**: Year One saved $3,167.36 (78.4% of budget); Year Two saved $3,022.01 (72.5% of budget). Significant and consistent under-spending suggests actual meal, lodging, and vehicle rental costs were lower than estimated, or that fewer field days/personnel were deployed than originally planned. This offset the overages in materials and labor. **Assessment: Positive** — disciplined cost control in travel category.
