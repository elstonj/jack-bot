## Financial Health Assessment

### Overall Status: ✅ **PROJECT CLOSED – WITHIN BUDGET**

#### Summary

- **Remaining/Over**: $17.37 (0.01% variance – essentially fully expended)
- **Status**: Project completed on schedule (08/01/2021 – 11/29/2023) with final invoice submitted 11/29/2023.

#### Year-by-Year Performance

**Year One: Slightly Under Budget ✅**
- Budgeted: $84,988.00
- Actual: $84,328.64
- Variance: $659.36 under (0.8% favorable)
- **Assessment**: Strong cost control. Travel expenses came in significantly under budget despite labor and equipment rental costs running slightly over.

**Year Two: Slightly Over Budget ⚠️**
- Budgeted: $63,741.00
- Actual: $64,382.99
- Variance: $641.99 over (1.0% unfavorable)
- **Assessment**: Minor overrun offset by Year One savings. Materials/equipment rental exceeded planned costs, but travel savings partially mitigated impact.

#### Category-Level Performance

| Category | Status | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| **Materials/Equipment Rental** | ⚠️ Over Budget | 11.4% overspend ($39,000 vs. $35,000 budgeted). Likely due to extended aircraft rental days or additional flight hours beyond original 5-day-per-deployment plan. |
| **Direct Labor** | ⚠️ Over Budget | 2.5% overspend ($90,288 vs. $88,116 budgeted). Crew operations and data processing costs exceeded initial estimates, possibly due to increased flight complexity or personnel utilization. |
| **Travel** | ✅ Under Budget | 24.2% underspend ($19,423.63 vs. $25,613 budgeted). Substantial savings in meals, lodging, and vehicle rental—indicates efficient deployment logistics or reduced team size in some deployments. |

#### Burn Rate Analysis

**Year One Spending Pattern:**
- Monthly average: ~$7,320/month over 11-month invoice cycle
- Spending was relatively smooth, indicating consistent deployment activity
- Major invoices: $15,558 (opening), $24,508 (July), $21,359 (Aug–Sept), $19,120 (Sept–Oct)

**Year Two Spending Pattern:**
- Concentrated invoice activity: $22,516 → $27,993.71 → $13,220.78 → $652.50 (final)
- Final year invoices suggest deployment clustering in early–mid Year Two with reduced activity by close-out
- Smaller final invoice ($652.50) consistent with administrative wrap-up (11 days of activity only)

#### Key Findings

1. **Cost Control**: Project maintained disciplined spending throughout 27-month period. Year One underspend (+$659) offset Year Two overspend (–$642), resulting in net variance of only $17.37 across the entire award.

2. **No Cost Overruns**: Despite Materials/Equipment and Labor category overruns, travel savings and overall budget discipline prevented project cost escalation beyond contract value.

3. **No Funding Modifications Documented**: Drive Budget Docs note that "no formal contract modifications documented" despite scope changes (Year Two reduced from 6 planned deployments to 3 in the final budget). Project managed scope/cost trade-offs within original $148,729 authorization.

4. **Payment Status**: Invoices 1514, 1521, 1530 show signed/submitted status. Status of invoices 1535, 1566, 1580, 1586 not fully documented in provided materials. Final invoice 1595 submitted 11/29/2023 with project close-out.

5. **Budget Realism**: The original 6-week proposal ($164,093.50 with 15% overhead) was scaled back to a 7-deployment model (4+3) at $148,729, demonstrating adaptive planning to actual client needs and deployment feasibility.

- **⚠️ No Asana Data**: Project milestones, task status, and deliverable tracking not available from Asana. Financial summary relies entirely on budget documents and invoice records.

**Project 450-1 is financially healthy and successfully closed.** The project stayed within the $148,729 contracted award value with minimal variance ($17.37 remaining), maintained balanced cost control across deployment years, and delivered all planned UAS mapping services to University of Colorado Boulder.
