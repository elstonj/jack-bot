### Performance by Cost Category

#### ✅ **Aircraft Rental (S2 UAS) — OVERSPENT**
- **Budgeted**: $35,000.00 ($20K Y1 + $15K Y2)
- **Actual**: $39,000.00
- **Variance**: **–$4,000.00 (11.4% over budget)**
- **Assessment**: Rental costs exceeded budget by approximately $1,000 per deployment across both years. Possible causes: extended flight days, additional rental days, equipment add-ons, or market rate increases. **This is the largest single budget overrun on the project.**

#### ✅ **Labor (All Functions) — OVERSPENT**
- **Budgeted**: $88,116.00 (Project Mgmt + Flight Ops + Data Processing)
- **Actual**: $90,288.00
- **Variance**: **–$2,172.00 (2.5% over budget)**
- **Breakdown by Year**:
  - **Year One Labor**: Budgeted $50,352 → Actual $51,860 (**–$1,508 over**)
  - **Year Two Labor**: Budgeted $37,764 → Actual $38,428 (**–$664 over**)
- **Assessment**: Labor costs slightly overran due to:
  - Extended flight crew operations (7-day operations per deployment budgeted; actual deployments may have required additional crew days or overtime)
  - Data processing hours exceeding estimates (NRE and per-flight-day processing)
  - Wage/rate increases during the 27-month contract period
- **Note**: This overrun was partially offset by travel savings.

#### ✅ **Travel — UNDERSPENT**
- **Budgeted**: $25,613.00 ($14.636K Y1 + $10.977K Y2)
- **Actual**: $19,423.63
- **Variance**: **$6,189.37 (24.2% under budget)**
- **Breakdown by Year**:
  - **Year One Travel**: Budgeted $14,636 → Actual $11,468.64 (**$3,167.36 under**)
  - **Year Two Travel**: Budgeted $10,977 → Actual $7,954.99 (**$3,022.01 under**)
- **Assessment**: Substantial travel savings across both years, likely due to:
  - Reduced meal per diem expenditures (possibly shorter deployments or group meals lower than $76/day budgeted rate)
  - Fewer lodging nights than budgeted (budget assumed 12 nights per deployment; actual likely lower)
  - Vehicle rental optimization or use of owned/leased vehicles instead of daily rentals
- **Outcome**: The $6,189.37 travel savings nearly offset the $4,000 aircraft rental overrun and $2,172 labor overrun, keeping the project nearly on budget.

### Key Financial Observations

#### 1. **Aircraft Rental — Primary Cost Driver**
- S2 UAS rental at $1,000/day × 5 days per deployment = $35K (budgeted), but actuals came to $39K
- This $4K overrun (11.4%) represents the single largest variance on the project
- **Implication**: For future similar projects, aircraft daily rental rates should be validated or indexed for escalation

#### 2. **Labor Costs Stable but Slightly Elevated**
- Hourly labor rates of $174/hr (engineers) and $1,500/day (flight crew) held constant across the contract
- Overruns suggest hours/days exceeded budget, not rate changes
- **Implication**: Deployment timelines or crew scheduling could be tightened in future estimates

#### 3. **Travel Efficiency Achieved**
- $6,189 in travel savings (24% under budget) demonstrates strong cost management
- Lodging, meals, and vehicle costs likely optimized through experience/route learning across deployments
- **Implication**: Year Two travel estimates were more conservative/realistic than Year One

#### 4. **Project Margin Essentially Zero**
- Final position: $17.37 remaining on a $148.7K contract = **0.01% margin**
- Indicates tight budget management and little room for contingencies (but project completed successfully)
- **Risk**: Any additional scope or extended operations would have required budget modification
