## Financial Health Assessment

### Overall Project Status: **CLOSED – FULLY EXPENDED**

**1. On-Budget Status**
- ✅ **Project Overall**: Essentially on budget with only $17.37 remaining (0.01% variance against $148,729 approved contract).
- **Year One**: Underspent by $659.36 (0.78% favorable variance) – Total actual $84,328.64 vs. budget $84,988.00
- **Year Two**: Overspent by $641.99 (1.01% unfavorable variance) – Total actual $64,382.99 vs. budget $63,741.00
- The two-year variances nearly offset, demonstrating strong overall cost control.

### Budget by Cost Category Assessment

**2. Materials/Equipment Rental — 111.4% of Budget (OVERSPENT)**
- **Budgeted**: $35,000.00
- **Actual**: $39,000.00
- **Variance**: –$4,000.00 (4.0% overrun)
- **Analysis**: Aircraft rental costs exceeded budget by approximately $4,000 over the 27-month project period. This may reflect:
  - Additional or extended flight days beyond the original 4-deployment (Y1) + 3-deployment (Y2) plan
  - Higher-than-anticipated daily rates ($1,000/day) for S2 aircraft access
  - Extended rental periods for operations or maintenance
  - **Concern Level**: Moderate – equipment is a variable cost tied directly to deployment scope; overage suggests scope creep or underestimation of flight requirements.

**3. Direct Labor — 102.5% of Budget (OVERSPENT)**
- **Budgeted**: $88,116.00
- **Actual**: $90,288.00
- **Variance**: –$2,172.00 (2.5% overrun)
- **Analysis**: Labor costs exceeded budget by $2,172 across project management (Jack Elston), flight crew operations (Maciej Stachura, Joshua Fromm, Daniel Troke), and data processing (Ben Busby). Possible causes:
  - Extended field operations or additional personnel days
  - Higher labor rates or unforeseen staffing needs
  - Additional data processing work post-deployment
  - **Concern Level**: Low – 2.5% overage is typical for research projects; likely absorbed by efficiency in travel category.

**4. Travel — 75.8% of Budget (UNDERSPENT)**
- **Budgeted**: $25,613.00
- **Actual**: $19,423.63
- **Variance**: +$6,189.37 (24.2% favorable variance)
- **Analysis**: Travel costs came in significantly under budget, representing a $6,189 savings across meals, lodging, vehicle rental, and logistics overhead. Favorable drivers:
  - More efficient meal per diems or team members sharing accommodations
  - Shorter or fewer travel days than budgeted
  - Vehicle rental or logistics optimization
  - **Positive Note**: This $6,189 underspend effectively offset the $4,000 equipment overrun and $2,172 labor overrun, resulting in the near-zero net project variance.
