# Project 024-03 — Financial Summary

## Project Overview
- **Full Project Name**: "On-demand wildfire susceptibility from fuel moisture and vegetative index maps"
- **Client/Agency**: NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration)
- **Funding Opportunity**: NASA ROSES 2024 (A.59)
- **Contract Type**: NASA ROSES research grant (Step 2 submission)
- **Period of Performance**: April 1, 2025 – April 1, 2028 (3 years)
- **Project Partners**: Black Swift Technologies (prime), Pepperwood Reserve, Weather Stream
- **Brief Description of Scope**: Development and deployment of small UAS platforms for high-resolution soil moisture and live fuel moisture mapping in wildfire-prone regions. Integration with NISAR satellite and UAVSAR data. Planned 192 UAS flights annually generating 2m-resolution soil moisture maps; field validation campaigns; data processing and open science data archival.

### Areas of Potential Risk / Concern

1. **Black Swift Prime Contractor Budget Not Itemized**: The Drive Budget Documents provide detailed breakdowns for Pepperwood and Weather Stream subcontractors, but no separate budget justification for Black Swift Technologies' prime contractor role, management overhead, or direct contributions. This represents ~$176K (34% of total) with no cost detail provided.

2. **UAS Flight Operations Scaling**: 192 planned flights annually at 2m resolution soil moisture mapping is ambitious. The budget shows support for "9 field validation campaigns" but no detailed cost model for the 192 routine flights (aircraft maintenance, pilot time, fuel, data processing for 10 GB/year output). This cost may be underestimated or subsumed in Weather Stream data processing hours.

3. **NISAR Satellite Dependency**: Project assumes NISAR begins operations October 2025. Any delay could impact data integration timeline and require budget reallocation.

4. **Indirect Cost Cap**: Both Pepperwood and Weather Stream apply a 20% indirect cost rate, stated as "maximum allowed." Verify this aligns with NASA ROSES allowable indirect rates for the funding opportunity.

5. **Personnel Effort Concentration**: Ryan Ferrell (Research Scientist) is allocated 1,000 hours/year (8.25 weeks), representing 53.5% of Pepperwood direct labor hours. Ensure this is realistic given his concurrent commitments listed in "Current and Pending Support" document (10% effort on other NASA projects, 15% on DOI volcanic data project, etc.).

6. **No Payment Schedule Defined**: Drive Budget Documents do not specify invoice schedule, payment milestones, or cash flow timing over the 3-year period.
