# Project 024-03 — Financial Summary

## Project Overview
- **Project Title**: "On-demand wildfire susceptibility from fuel moisture and vegetative index maps"
- **Client/Agency**: NASA (ROSES 2024, Opportunity A.59)
- **Contract Type**: NASA ROSES research grant (Step 2 submission)
- **Period of Performance**: April 1, 2025 – April 1, 2028 (3 years)
- **Scope**: Multi-partner research project combining field validation campaigns (Pepperwood partner) with aerial data collection and processing (Weather Stream partner) to map soil moisture and live fuel moisture for wildfire susceptibility assessment. Uses small UAS platforms, NISAR satellite data, and UAVSAR flights for comprehensive soil moisture mapping.
- **Project Partners**: Pepperwood Reserve (field operations), Weather Stream (aerial platform & data processing), Black Swift Technologies (systems/coordination)

**Personnel (Direct Labor)**
- **Pepperwood**: $158,900 budgeted for 6 staff roles across 3 years

**Operating & Field Expenses**
- **Pepperwood**: $6,455 total
  - Lodging: $3,500 (supports 9 field campaigns over 3 years; ~70 nights total)
  - Fuel: $1,000 (vehicle access to remote preserve)
  - Supplies: $1,955 (scales, poles, containers, marking supplies)
- **Weather Stream**: $2,974 (annual travel for programmatic meetings)
- **Total Operating**: $9,429 (1.8% of total budget)
- **Assessment**: Reasonable and modest. Field supplies are consumables (water bottles, wax, nails, tags) and durable equipment (scale, pruners) for 3-year campaign. Lodging uses Bechtel House (in-house Pepperwood facility rental) at $50/night, well below commercial rates.

**Indirect Costs**

- **Allocation**: Common costs, utilities, technology, general support services
- **Assessment**: 20% is the maximum allowed under NASA ROSES guidelines and represents standard R&D indirect rate for nonprofit and academic partners.

- **Period of Performance**: 3 years (April 2025 – April 2028)

- **Assessment**: Project is frontloaded with personnel and field campaign costs. No budget overages anticipated provided staffing levels remain stable and field operations proceed on schedule.

### Key Risks & Concerns

1. **Key Personnel Over-Allocation**
   - Jack Elston & Maciej Stachura each committed to 45% effort across multiple active projects (AREN, WPO Hurricane, Ruggedized UAS, Volcanic Data Collection) plus one pending proposal (AFWERX Runway Integrity, $1.25M pending)
   - **Risk**: Schedule pressure; potential for delays if other projects slip. Mitigation: verify effort commitments are realistic and consider delegation to Weather Stream Project Lead.

2. **NISAR Satellite Dependency**
   - NISAR operations begin October 2025 (6 months into project start)
   - No cost risk, but data integration timeline is critical for project success
   - **Risk**: Satellite launch/commissioning delays could impact data fusion deliverables

3. **Partner Execution Readiness**
   - Pepperwood and Weather Stream budgets are separate line items. Interdependencies (e.g., Weather Stream providing aerial data for Pepperwood field validation) require coordination
   - **Risk**: Cost or schedule impact if either partner cannot perform on schedule. Mitigation: define clear integration points and success metrics in contract/statement of work.

4. **Indirect Cost Rate Stability**
   - Both partners use 20% indirect rate (maximum allowed)
   - **Risk**: Low risk, as rate is fixed and pre-approved by NASA. Monitor for any material changes in overhead allocation or facility costs.

5. **Open Science Data Management**
   - Project commits to releasing all data and code publicly upon validation completion
   - **Risk**: No incremental cost noted for data archive/repository infrastructure. Verify that indirect costs or partner contributions cover long-term archival obligations.
