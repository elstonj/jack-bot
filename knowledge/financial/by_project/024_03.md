# Project 024-03 — Financial Summary

## Project Overview
- **Full Project Name**: "On-demand wildfire susceptibility from fuel moisture and vegetative index maps"
- **Client/Agency**: NASA (ROSES 2024, A.59 funding opportunity)
- **Contract Type**: NASA ROSES research grant (Step 2 submission)
- **Period of Performance**: April 1, 2025 – April 1, 2028 (3 years)
- **Description**: Research project to develop soil moisture and live fuel moisture mapping capabilities using small UAS, NISAR satellite, and UAVSAR data to support wildfire susceptibility assessment. Project involves two primary partners: Pepperwood Preserve (field validation) and Weather Stream (UAS/aerial data collection support).

### Overall Status: **Cannot Be Assessed — Insufficient Actual Data**

#### What We Know (Proposed Budget):
✅ **Budget is internally consistent.**
- Pepperwood budget ($198,426) and Weather Stream budget ($239,900.40) total the stated aggregate award of $518,293.20
- All budget categories include approved amounts with clear line-item detail
- Indirect cost rates are capped at 20% (reasonable for NASA grants)

#### What We Cannot Assess (Missing QuickBooks Data):

❌ **Project burn rate**: No data on actual monthly/quarterly spending vs. budget
❌ **Cost category overruns or savings**: No comparison of actual expenses to proposed amounts
❌ **Invoice status & payment timing**: No data on whether Pepperwood/Weather Stream have invoiced NASA or when payments were received
❌ **Labor vs. actual hours**: No timesheet or actuals to validate whether personnel are tracking to budgeted effort levels
❌ **Operating expense utilization**: No confirmation that lodging, fuel, and field supply budgets are being consumed as projected

#### Key Areas of Potential Risk (Based on Proposal & Budget Design):

1. **High reliance on field campaigns**: $55K scientist + $25.5K technician labor (43% of Pepperwood budget) depends on successful execution of 9 field validation campaigns across 3 years. Any campaign delays or cancellations could create schedule/budget pressure.

2. **Concurrent commitments**: Per "Current and Pending Support" disclosure, PI (Jack Elston) and key team member (Maciej Stachura) have 45% combined effort commitment on active NASA/NOAA/DOI/DoD projects totaling $1.73M. This PROJECT 024-03 effort is in addition to those commitments. Risk of attention/resource conflicts exists.

3. **Satellite data dependencies**: Project relies on NISAR (operational beginning Oct 2025) and UAVSAR flights (9 planned Year 1). Delays in satellite availability or airborne campaign scheduling could affect deliverable timelines, though these are not direct cost drivers in the budget.

4. **Multi-partner coordination**: Two separate partner budgets (Pepperwood & Weather Stream) require coordination and invoicing synchronization. No documented CLIN structure or payment schedule provided to manage this.

5. **Indirect cost recovery**: 20% indirect rate is at the maximum allowed for many NASA grant types. If actual overhead costs exceed this rate, the project could face cost overruns with no contingency budget identified.

#### Recommendations for Monitoring:

To assess true financial health, obtain and review:
1. **QuickBooks export** for PROJECT 024-03 (invoices, expenses, payments, GL entries)
2. **Timesheet/labor tracking** for all assigned personnel to compare actual hours to budget
3. **Expense reports** from field campaigns to validate operating expense budgets
4. **NASA disbursement records** to confirm payment schedule and timing
5. **Asana project schedule** to track milestone completion and identify any delays affecting labor burn
