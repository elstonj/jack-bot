# Project 024-03 — Financial Summary

## Project Overview

- **Full Project Name**: "On-demand wildfire susceptibility from fuel moisture and vegetative index maps"
- **Client/Agency**: NASA (ROSES 2024, A.59 funding opportunity)
- **Contract Type**: NASA ROSES research grant (Step 2 submission)
- **Period of Performance**: April 1, 2025 – April 1, 2028 (3 years)
- **Scope**: Research project integrating small UAS platforms, satellite data (NISAR, UAVSAR), and ground validation campaigns to map soil moisture and live fuel moisture for wildfire susceptibility assessment. Project involves field validation at Pepperwood Preserve (9 campaigns/year) and Weather Stream remote sensing data integration (3 flights/year).

## Financial Health Assessment

### Overall Project Status
- **Stage**: Pre-award / Early project phase

- **Actuals vs. Budget**: No actual expenditures have been recorded (QuickBooks data unavailable)

### Key Observations

#### ✅ **Strengths**
1. **Clearly structured multi-partner budget**: Pepperwood and Weather Stream budgets are well-defined with line-item detail
2. **Conservative indirect rate**: 20% rate applied consistently (at or below NASA ROSES thresholds)
3. **Defined field work scope**: 9 annual validation campaigns with clear staffing and supply costs
4. **Reasonable fringe benefits**: 40% rate is standard for academic/research institutions
5. **Three-year award provides stability**: Weather Stream portion is clearly scaled across 3-year extension period

#### ⚠️ **Potential Concerns & Gaps**

1. **No actual spending baseline established**
   - Cannot assess whether budget categories are realistic
   - No variance analysis available (all % used = N/A)
   - Risk: Field campaign costs, equipment durability, or travel may exceed estimates once work begins

2. **Field operations cost uncertainty**
   - Bechtel House lodging ($50/night × 70 nights = $3,500/year) is modest; may not cover all 9 campaigns
   - Vehicle fuel estimate ($1,000/year for Pepperwood preserve access via 9 campaigns) assumes ~22 gallons/campaign
   - Field supply budget ($1,955 for 3 years = $652/year) is thin; no contingency for equipment replacement or consumables

3. **Personnel effort may be optimistic**
   - Ryan Ferrell allocated 1,000 hours over 3 years (333 hr/year ≈ 8.25 weeks/year) for field validation, data synthesis, and 9 campaigns
   - Makayla Freed allocated 850 hours (283 hr/year ≈ 7 weeks/year) for field sampling and post-processing
   - Risk: Scope creep or underestimated field time could lead to cost overruns or requirement for labor reallocation

4. **Weather Stream effort tied to specific deliverables**
   - Flight Campaign & Data Processing Support (185 hr/year) contingent on 3 flights/year and data delivery
   - If flights are delayed or rescheduled, timeline and labor utilization may shift

5. **No documented payment schedule or milestones**
   - Drive budget documents do not specify when draws/invoices are due (e.g., monthly, quarterly, milestone-based)
   - NASA ROSES typically allow quarterly or semi-annual invoicing; unclear if budget assumes that cadence
   - No data on NASA's award decision date, funding availability, or pre-award conditions

6. **Satellite data cost allocation unclear**
   - NISAR and UAVSAR are provided by NASA/other agencies (no direct cost to project)
   - However, data processing labor is embedded in Weather Stream budget
   - Risk: If satellite data availability is delayed (e.g., NISAR launch slip), labor costs may shift or be unrecoverable

7. **Missing cost categories**
   - No equipment depreciation or capital cost for small UAS platforms (proposal mentions "192 flights/year" but does not budget for aircraft purchase/maintenance in summary budgets provided)
   - No software licenses for IDL or other development tools
   - No contingency fund for unexpected expenses
