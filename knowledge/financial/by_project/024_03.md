# Project 024-03 — Financial Summary

## Project Overview
- **Full Project Name**: "On-demand wildfire susceptibility from fuel moisture and vegetative index maps"
- **Client/Funding Agency**: NASA (ROSES 2024, A.59 funding opportunity)
- **Contract Type**: NASA ROSES research grant (Step 2 submission)
- **Period of Performance**: April 1, 2025 – April 1, 2028 (3 years)
- **Brief Description**: Multi-institutional research project integrating small UAS platforms, satellite data (NISAR, UAVSAR), and field validation campaigns to produce high-resolution soil moisture and live fuel moisture maps for wildfire susceptibility assessment. Key partners include Pepperwood Reserve (field operations, data synthesis) and Weather Stream (airborne data collection and processing support).

### Budget Concerns & Observations

1. **Unaccounted Budget Gap**: Documents reference aggregate contract/award of **$518,293.20**, but itemized budgets total only **$438,326.40**. A difference of ~**$79,967** remains unaccounted for. **Potential explanations:**
   - Additional partner contributions not documented in provided sources
   - Contingency reserve or cost-share from non-federal sources
   - Overhead/administrative burden not itemized in line-item budgets
   - **Action Required**: Confirm source and allocation of missing ~$80K to ensure complete budget reconciliation.

2. **Thin Administrative/Management Overhead**: Executive-level personnel (Margaret Boeger, Zach Winter, Hana Nguyen) allocated only 120 hours each over 3 years (~40 hrs/year or 1 week/year). For a multi-partner NASA grant involving field operations, flight campaigns, and data integration, this may be insufficient if:
   - Complex coordination with satellite data providers (NISAR, UAVSAR) requires more PM effort
   - Data management plan (OSDMP) compliance and public data release impose regulatory/administrative burden
   - Unexpected technical issues (e.g., sensor failures, schedule delays) require escalation
   - **Recommendation**: Monitor actual PM effort post-project-start and request budget amendment if needed.

3. **Field Campaign Execution Risk**: Pepperwood personnel labor ($80,500 for Research Scientist + Field Technician) is contingent on:
   - Successful execution of 9 field validation campaigns over 3 years (3 campaigns/year)
   - Favorable weather and site access conditions at Pepperwood Reserve
   - No staffing delays or attrition (Ryan Ferrell and Makayla Freed are linchpins)
   - **Recommendation**: Develop contingency staffing plan and site access agreements prior to project kickoff.

4. **Data Processing & Satellite Integration Dependencies**: Weather Stream budget assumes:
   - Timely operationalization of NISAR satellite (stated as October 2025, but space missions frequently slip)
   - Reliable UAVSAR flight availability (9 flights scheduled in Year 1)
   - Processing workflow maturity for 192 UAS flights/year + satellite data fusion
   - **Recommendation**: Establish satellite/flight schedule dependency tracking and develop data processing pipeline testing plan.

5. **Budget Realism Check**: 
   - **3-year budget: $438–$518K** for a NASA ROSES research grant is moderate-to-small (typical ROSES A.59 awards range $500K–$2M+).
   - **Per-year burn rate: $146–$173K/year** is manageable given 2–3 partner organizations and field-intensive scope.
   - **No apparent budget overallocation** within individual cost categories; rates (labor, equipment, travel) are reasonable and NASA-compliant.

6. **Missing Actuals Data**: **Critical Gap**: No QuickBooks transaction data, invoice records, or expense reports provided. Cannot assess:
   - Whether spending to date aligns with proposals
   - Which budget categories have overruns or underspends
   - Cash flow and payment status from NASA
   - Earned value vs. planned value
   - **Action Required**: Obtain QuickBooks cost reports, invoice aging, and payment history once project becomes active (April 1, 2025 onward).
