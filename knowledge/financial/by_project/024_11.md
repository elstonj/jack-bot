## Key Projects Identified from Proposals

### 1. **DOE FECM Methane Leak Detection Program**
- **Source:** Proposal document dated 2024-11-06  
- **Scope:** S2 UAS equipped with Quanta3 methane spectrometer to detect and quantify methane emissions from orphaned oil and gas wells  
- **Target Cost Reduction:** <$100 per well (vs. current $750/well with ground technicians)  
- **System Capabilities:**
  - 3-meter wingspan fixed-wing UAS
  - 100 km operational range
  - BVLOS autonomous operations
  - Methane detection sensitivity: 3 ppb
  - Can survey up to 50 well sites per day
  - Real-time autonomous flight path modification
  - 3D wind field measurement
- **Technology Readiness:** TRL 5 (demonstrated in controlled releases at BST Colorado test site)
- **Validation Plan:** Field validation using flux towers for comparison
- **QuickBooks Allocation:** $3,087 recorded in 2025-01 (labeled "Methane Emission")

### 2. **NOAA Testbed – S0 Hurricane Integration**
- **Source:** Proposal documents dated 2024-11-04 and 2024-11-22  
- **Client:** NOAA / National Hurricane Center (via Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University)
- **Scope:** Integrate S0 uncrewed aircraft wind data into NHC forecasting operations over three years  
- **Three-Year Phased Approach:**
  - **Year 1:** Develop reduction factor to standardize S0 wind measurements to 10m/10-minute equivalent
  - **Year 2:** Validation & testbed operations (historical storm reanalysis, forecast improvement demonstration)
  - **Year 3:** Operational implementation (integrate reduction factor into HDOBS or real-time data stream)
- **Project Leadership:** Jun Zhang, Josh (reduction factor research), Wallace (NHC contact)
- **QuickBooks Allocation:** $1,101,673 total allocated to "S0 Hurricane" projects (2024-11 to 2026-01) — **largest single allocation in the project portfolio**
