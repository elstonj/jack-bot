### DOE FECM Methane Leak Detection Program
- **Proposed Deliverables (from 2024-11-06 proposal):**
  - Demonstrate S2 UAS with Quanta3 methane spectrometer at <$100 per well detection cost (vs. $750 baseline)
  - Autonomous BVLOS flight capability for methane gradient detection
  - Real-time 3D wind field measurement simultaneous with methane detection
  - Field validation using flux towers for accuracy confirmation
  - Technology advancement from TRL 5 (demonstrated) toward operational deployment
  - Target capability: survey up to 50 well sites in one day

- **Financial Implication:** Proposal indicates cost-competitive positioning ($100/well target); no specific contract budget provided in documents.

- **QuickBooks Spending (inferred):** ~$3,087 (2025-01, "Methane Emission" entry) + potentially other allocations under SMM Phase II or S2 refurbishment ($57,814).

### NOAA Testbed — Hurricane Forecasting Integration
- **Proposed Deliverables (from proposals dated 2024-11-04 through 2024-11-22):**
  - **Year 1:** Develop reduction factor for S0 wind measurements (convert to 10m/1-minute standard)
  - **Year 2:** Validate reduced data against historical storms; demonstrate forecast improvement to NHC
  - **Year 3:** Implement reduction factor in operational processing; deliver near-real-time HDOBS data stream to NHC forecasters
  - Testbed evaluation component with NHC support (Letter of Support from Wallace, NHC)
  - Based on 19 completed S0 flights (as of proposal date)

- **Financial Implication:** Multi-year project; primary partner is Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University (Dr. Joshua Wadler). BST acts as UAS service provider.

- **QuickBooks Spending:** S0 Hurricane family = $770,073 total (2025-02 through 2025-12), representing primary cost allocation for this project.
