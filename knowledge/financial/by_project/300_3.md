#### Risk Factors & Concerns

**1. Critical Path Hardware Development Compressed into May–June 2026**
- **Status:** 6 open firmware, QC, and assembly tasks; all due May 1 – June 26, 2026
- **Risk:** Staggered firmware releases (May 1, May 29, June 26) are tightly sequenced with Sam Hild's QC/assembly tasks. Any delay cascades to final delivery.
- **Mitigation:** Jack Elston and Sam Hild tasks are clearly tracked in Asana; no cost overruns documented, but schedule adherence is critical.
- **Financial Implication:** If firmware development slips beyond June 26, platform delivery could exceed the July 31, 2026 deadline, potentially triggering:
  - Late payment penalties or contractual adjustments from NOAA
  - Rescheduled hurricane season deployment (affects CLIN 1002/1003/1006 timing and invoicing)

**2. Operational Support CLINs (1002, 1003, 1006) Largely Uninvoiced**
- **Status:** Only $18k of $122,750 (15%) invoiced for operational phases
- **Risk:** Heavy invoicing expected Q2–Q3 2026 as hurricane season approaches. Timing delays in operational phase startup could compress invoicing into Q3 2026 and challenge cash flow.
- **Financial Implication:** 
  - CLIN 1002 (Mission Days, $42,750) and CLIN 1006 (Travel, $14,250) not yet billed; likely contingent on NOAA's readiness and mission scheduling.
  - CLIN 1003 remaining ($48k) depends on post-deployment support completion dates.
