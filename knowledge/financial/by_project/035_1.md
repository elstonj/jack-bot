**Positive Indicators:**
1. **Payment Track Record:** $76,500 of $105,500 invoiced has been paid (72.5% collection rate). Remaining $29,000 is documented and under final invoice follow-up.
2. **Labor Spending Under Control:** Despite 11% hour overrun, labor costs stayed within approved budget due to fixed hourly rate ($250/hr) discipline.
3. **Project Completion:** All major milestones delivered on schedule or ahead; aircraft delivered Jan 2026; only final reporting remains.
4. **Burn Rate Sustainable:** Average ~$5,156/month (labor-based) across 19-month period; no evidence of runaway costs.

**Concerns & Red Flags:**

1. **Labor Hour Overrun:** 
   - Budgeted 372 hours; actual 413:26 hours = **11% overrun** (+41:26 hours)
   - Root cause not documented, but overrun absorbed within $93K labor budget due to fixed rate
   - **Risk:** Additional final report work could push hours further over

2. **Invoiced Amount vs. Internal Spend Mismatch:**
   - Drive Budget Doc shows: $105,500 invoiced vs. $27,708.02 internal spend (as of Mar 18, 2026)
   - This 3.8x gap suggests either: (a) accrual vs. cash accounting difference, (b) invoicing includes indirect costs/markups not captured in "spend" line, or (c) data inconsistency
   - **Impact:** Unclear if remaining $54,500 budget is truly available or already committed

3. **Uncertain VTOL Funding:**
   - Original verbal agreement: $90,000 for 6 S0 VTOLs
   - Formalized: Only $15,000 (for 1–2 units, 1 already invoiced)
   - **Unresolved:** $75,000 of original allocation lacks documentation
   - **Risk:** If all 6 VTOLs were promised, project is $75K underfunded; if promise was scaled back, excess budget may exist

4. **Outstanding Payment Aging:**
   - Invoice 1713 submitted Oct 9, 2025; partial payment received Nov 2025; **remaining $29,000 still outstanding as of Mar 18, 2026** (5+ months unpaid)
   - Final invoice due Apr 10, 2026 per Asana; no sign of payment for $29K yet
