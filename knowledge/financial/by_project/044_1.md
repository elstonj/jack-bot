**Summary**:
- **61.1% of budget invoiced ($55,000 of $90,000)** with $35,000 remaining.

### Detailed Findings:

#### 1. **Schedule Risk — Compressed Final Phase with Zero Buffer**

   - **Validation Flight #1 completed Apr 25 (2 days early)**, indicating some schedule recovery, but flights #2–#3 have **no margin for delay**.

   - **Dan Prendergast is the only formal assignee in Asana for Flights #2 & #3**, despite team feedback (Maciej, Apr 19–20) identifying Jack Elston (controller completion) and Nate Straus (flight coordination) as active contributors.

#### 3. **Budget Utilization — 61% Invoiced, Timing of Closeout Uncertain**

   - **Remaining $35,000 likely allocated to final validation flights, data analysis, and Final Report** (Apr 25–30 window).
   - **Risk**: If final validation work extends beyond Apr 30 project end date due to Flight #2–#3 delays or report rework, BST may need to absorb cost overruns or negotiate schedule extension with EMASS.

#### 4. **Invoice & Payment Flow**
   - **Both Mar 2026 invoices paid promptly** (same-month payment completion suggests healthy customer relationship and no payment delays).
   - **No final invoice yet issued as of Apr 26, 2026** — suggests closeout billing expected post-project completion or combined with final deliverables.
   - **Risk**: Timing of final invoice and payment collection depends on EMASS acceptance of Final Report. If report is rejected or requires rework, payment and project closure could slip.

#### 5. **Budget Headroom Assessment**
   - **$35,000 remaining of $90,000 total** (38.9% of budget available for final work).
