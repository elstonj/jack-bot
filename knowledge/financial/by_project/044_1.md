## Financial Health Assessment

### Overall Budget Status
- ✅ **Not overspent** at total project level: $55,000 invoiced of $90,000 contracted (61.1% utilized), leaving $35,000 remaining.
- ⚠️ **Category-level visibility limited**: QB actuals do not break down expenses by cost category (labor, equipment, travel, etc.), so variances within individual categories cannot be assessed.

### Burn Rate & Schedule Risk
- **Timeline Pressure**: Original period of performance was Nov 10, 2025 – Jan 31, 2026 (2.5 months). Project was extended to Apr 20, 2026, indicating schedule delays or scope expansion.
- **Compressed Final Phase**: All 7 remaining tasks are due Apr 15–20 (6-day window), all assigned to single team member (Dan Prendergast).
- **Execution Risk**: Bench tests, flight profile creation, three validation flights, and final report concentrated into single week. Weather delays, technical failures, or data analysis bottlenecks (Maciej Stachura's work lacks due dates) could cause cascading delays into final report.

### Key Concerns

1. **Execution Risk (Critical)**
   - **All remaining work on single person**: Dan Prendergast assigned all 7 open tasks in final validation phase with no backup resources visible.
   - **Compressed timeline**: 6-day window for sequential flight tests (Apr 15–17) plus bench test and report creation creates high risk of delay.
   - **Data analysis dependency**: Maciej Stachura's data analysis work (implicit prerequisite for final report) lacks due dates; no buffer if analysis is incomplete by Apr 20.

2. **Invoicing vs. Completion**
   - $55,000 (61%) has already been invoiced as of Mar 31, 2026, despite final deliverable (report) not due until Apr 20. This suggests either:
     - Work-in-progress invoicing (appropriate for milestone-based contracts), *or*
     - Front-loaded invoicing with remaining $35,000 held pending final report acceptance.
   - **Recommendation**: Verify with EMASS whether final invoice for remaining $35,000 is contingent on report acceptance or will be invoiced upon completion date.

3. **Budget Utilization**
   - Remaining budget ($35,000) represents 39% of contract value. If project is 80% task-complete, this suggests favorable cost posture, but final test flights and report writing may consume remaining budget quickly.
   - **No overruns visible**, but lack of category-level QB data prevents detailed burn-rate analysis.

4. **Schedule Extension Impact**
   - 2.5-month extension (Nov–Jan → Nov–Apr) suggests either:
     - Technical challenges requiring additional testing, *or*
     - Scope additions, *or*
     - Resource constraints.
   - Asana notes "Priority field updated to **High** (previously Low)" — consistent with elevated schedule/execution risk.

### Recommendations

1. **Monitor Dan Prendergast capacity** through final week (Apr 15–20). Identify backup personnel or contingency plan if weather or technical issues delay flights.
2. **Confirm data analysis schedule** with Maciej Stachura: ensure analysis can be completed in time for Apr 20 final report deadline.
3. **Clarify final invoice trigger**: Confirm with EMASS whether the remaining $35,000 is due upon Apr 20 completion date or contingent on report acceptance/approval.
