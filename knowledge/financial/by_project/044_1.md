### Key Findings

**✅ Strengths**:
1. **Invoicing on pace**: $55K invoiced by end of March for May 4 project end suggests healthy cash flow tracking.
2. **Early milestones completed**: Validation Flight #1 completed 2 days early (Apr 25); earlier Phase 1–4 deliverables completed on or ahead of schedule.
3. **Budget headroom**: $35K remaining (38.9%) provides buffer for scope adjustments or cost overruns.

**⚠️ Risks & Concerns**:
1. **Final deliverable compression**: Validation Flights #2–#3 and Final Report compressed to May 4, with 4–5 day slips from original Apr 29–30 targets. Schedule recovery is **behind** per Asana risk signals.
2. **Single point of failure on execution**: Dan Prendergast is assigned to remaining validation tasks in Asana; Jack Elston (controller completion) and Nate Straus (flight coordination) active in execution but not formally assigned in Asana task list.
3. **Controller completion blocking**: Per Maciej (Apr 24, 2026), "remaining work just waiting on those guys [Dan and Jack] to finish the controller and another round of test flying." Controller status unclear as of latest data; no confirmation that hardware is complete and ready for final flights.
4. **Final invoice timing unknown**: $35K remaining may not be fully invoiced until after May 4 Final Report delivery. If payment is delayed, cash flow risk for May and beyond.
5. **Cost category visibility gap**: QuickBooks invoices do not break down labor, materials, subcontractors, or other cost categories. Unable to validate proposed budget allocation against actuals or flag category-level overruns.

### Recommendations

1. **Confirm controller completion**: Verify Jack Elston's hardware/controller status and whether final test flying can commence on schedule (target Validation Flight #2: Apr 30).
2. **Formal assignment of Validation Flights #2–#3**: Ensure Dan Prendergast, Jack Elston, and Nate Straus are formally assigned to open Asana tasks (Validation Flight #2, Validation Flight #3, Final Report) with clear ownership.
3. **Final invoice planning**: Confirm invoice trigger (upon Final Report delivery, May 4) and expected payment date to manage cash flow.
4. **Cost tracking by category**: Request QuickBooks expense detail (by cost type: labor, materials, travel, etc.) to validate proposed budget allocation and identify any category-level overruns before project close.
5. **May 4 closeout readiness**: Given 4–5 day slips and compressed schedule, confirm team availability and blockers (controller, test flying) by Apr 28 to ensure May 4 Final Report delivery is achievable.
