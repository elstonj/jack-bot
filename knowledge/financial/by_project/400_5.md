# Project 400-5 — Financial Summary

## Project Overview
- **Full Project Name:** X22.4 AFWERX SBIR Phase II - Soil Moisture Mapping (Runway Integrity Validation through Soil Moisture Measurements from a Small UAS)
- **Client/Agency:** U.S. Air Force (DoD SBIR Phase II)
- **Contract Type:** SBIR Phase II (Small Business Innovation Research)
- **Period of Performance:** November 2023 – July 2025 (21 months)
- **Project Code:** 400-5
- **Status:** ARCHIVED — Successfully completed with all deliverables accepted

**Description:** Development and validation of a UAS-based L-band radiometer for mapping soil integrity. The system measures soil moisture as a key variable to understand soil integrity—as soil moisture increases, soil becomes more slippery and sticky while decreasing in strength. The team (BST, Orbital Micro Systems, and CET) adapted NASA soil moisture mapping technology for Air Force use case: mapping landing area suitability for C-130 aircraft operations. Soil integrity is quantified as cone index and compared against volumetric water content (VWC) measurements.
