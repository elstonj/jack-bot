# Project 400-5 — Financial Summary

## Project Overview
- **Full Project Name:** Runway Integrity Validation through Soil Moisture Measurements from a Small UAS (AFWERX SBIR Phase II - SMM DoD 22.4D)
- **Client/Agency:** U.S. Air Force (AFWERX), SBIR Program
- **Contract Type:** SBIR Phase II (Small Business Innovation Research)
- **Prime Contractor:** Black Swift Technologies LLC
- **Subcontractor:** Orbital Micro Systems, Inc. (OMS)
- **Period of Performance:** November 2023 – July 2025 (21 months)
- **Project Status:** ARCHIVED – Successfully completed with all deliverables accepted
- **Key Personnel:** Dan Prendergast (Technical Lead), Maciej Stachura, Meredith O'hara Needham, Nate Straus, Beck Cotter, Jack Elston
- **Primary TPOC:** Jason Foley (jason.foley.1@us.af.mil); Contracting Officer: Thomas Shea

**Project Description:**
Development and validation of a UAS-based L-band radiometer for mapping soil integrity. The system measures soil moisture as a key variable to understanding runway structural integrity—as soil moisture increases, soil becomes slippier and stickier while decreasing in strength. The team (BST, OMS, and CET) adapted NASA soil moisture mapping technology for Air Force use case: mapping landing area suitability for C-130 operations. Soil integrity is quantified as cone index and compared against volumetric water content (VWC) measurements. Final report delivered 2025-07-21; approved by TPOC 2025-09-16.
