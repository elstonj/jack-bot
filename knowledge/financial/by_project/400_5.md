# Project 400-5 — Financial Summary

## Project Overview
- **Full Project Name:** X22.4 AFWERX SBIR Phase II - Soil Moisture Mapping (Runway Integrity Validation through Soil Moisture Measurements from a Small UAS)
- **Client/Agency:** U.S. Air Force (USAF), AFWERX
- **Contract Type:** SBIR Phase II (Small Business Innovation Research)
- **Prime Contractor:** Black Swift Technologies LLC
- **Period of Performance:** November 2023 – July 2025 (21 months)
- **Contract Due Date:** 2025-07-11
- **Status:** **ARCHIVED – Successfully Completed** (All deliverables accepted; final report submitted 2025-07-21)

**Brief Description of Scope:**
Development and validation of a UAS-based L-band radiometer for mapping soil integrity and runway suitability. The system measures soil moisture as a key variable for understanding soil integrity—as soil moisture increases, soil becomes more slippery and sticky while decreasing in strength. BST, in partnership with subcontractor Orbital Micro Systems (OMS) and CET, adapted NASA soil moisture mapping technology for Air Force use case: mapping landing area suitability for C-130 Hercules aircraft operations. Soil integrity is quantified as cone index and compared against volumetric water content (VWC) measurements.
