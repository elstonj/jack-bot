## Accrued Expenses vs. Invoiced Amounts (Key Discrepancy)

**Critical Finding:** QuickBooks shows **$213,000.00** total accrued expenses for project [550-1], but only **$99,459.00** has been invoiced to the Navy as of Sep 28, 2026.

**Possible Explanations:**
1. **Capitalized Equipment:** The difference may include equipment purchases (S0 UAS units at $18,000 ea, magnetometer sensors) that are capitalized on the balance sheet rather than immediately expensed or invoiced.
2. **Labor Accrual:** Phase I Option invoicing is deliverable-based ($50K kick-off, $35K progress, $14.5K final), but internal labor costs (salaries, burden) are accrued against the project and may not be invoiced line-by-line.
3. **Timing Difference:** Expenses may have been incurred and accrued in Q1–Q3 2026, with invoicing following on the CLIN/deliverable schedule.
4. **Unresolved Expenditures:** Some costs may be pending final approval, final invoice, or adjustment.

**Recommendation:** Reconcile the $213,000 QBO figure against:
- Detailed labor timesheets/cost allocations by project
- Equipment purchase orders and delivery dates
- Subcontractor invoices from Ultra Maritime
- Travel expenses for Camp Pendleton/demonstrations
