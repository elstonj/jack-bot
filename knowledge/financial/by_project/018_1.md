# Project 018-1 — Financial Summary

## Project Overview
- **Full Project Name:** Murphy's Pond CH4 Monitoring
- **Client/Agency:** Murray State University (Dr. Bassil El Masri, Department of Environmental Sciences)
- **Contract Type:** Commercial service contract for drone-based greenhouse gas measurement
- **Period of Performance:** Original completion date 2025-01-31; field campaigns extended through 2026-06-01 (S2 rental due date)
- **Brief Description:** Collection of CH4, CO2, and H2O measurements over a 5 km × 5 km area above Murphy's Pond, Hickman County, Kentucky. Research focuses on methane emissions from wetlands and bottomland hardwood forests, using remote sensing to upscale site measurements to larger geographic areas. Project involves 3 planned flights using Black Swift S2 VTOL aircraft (2 completed as of December 2025, Flight #3 planned for Spring 2026).

## Financial Health Assessment

### Overall Budget Status
**Summary:** Project is **on budget overall** but in early stages of spend. Total invoiced ($62,283.66) represents **70.1%** of the contract value ($88,862.94), but only **8.2%** of total proposed costs have been expended in non-labor direct costs to date. The project is financially healthy with no cost overruns identified.

### Budget Performance by Category

**Strengths:**
1. **Payment Collection:** 60.5% of invoiced amount received to date ($37,704.37 of $62,283.66). Invoice #1698 was paid on time (30 days); Invoice #1728 is due 01/10/2026 and on track.
2. **Travel Spending:** Tracking at 48% of proposed budget ($3,956.24 of $8,213). Conservative spend for first two field trips suggests good cost control.
3. **No Labor Overruns:** Labor is embedded in invoiced service charges; no labor cost variance is visible, suggesting Jack Elston and Maciej Stachura are working within budgeted hour allocations.
4. **Unbudgeted Freight Absorbed:** $2,966.24 in freight costs (Nolan Transportation + Aeronet) were not in original budget estimate, but represent < 3.4% of contract value and are reasonable for equipment/aircraft logistics.

**Concerns:**
1. **Outstanding Invoice #1728:** $24,579.29 due 01/10/2026 (as of report date, likely unpaid). If not received by due date, cash flow impact should be monitored.
2. **Outstanding Freight Invoice:** Aeronet invoice #103029915 ($1,269.24) due 12/19/2025. Status unclear; if overdue, may indicate payment processing delays.
3. **Deferred Costs:** Atmofacts consulting ($2,220) and CO2 sensor ($2,000) have not been invoiced yet. These will be triggered during/after Flight #3.
4. **Scope Creep Risk:** S2 aircraft rental (2 units) is noted as "approaching order window" with "training/travel required"—potential for additional unbudgeted expenses if scope expands.
5. **Technical Delays:** FLIR triggering issues and methane payload data rate investigation are unresolved. If these require troubleshooting flights or extended engineering labor, they could impact budget and schedule.
