- **Risk:** If actual labor, travel, and material costs exceed the original $22,337, BST may be absorbing overages. No cost tracking by project is visible in QuickBooks to confirm whether actuals are being charged back.

#### 2. **Active Technical Blocker (Crash Bug)**
- **Issue:** S0 VTOL has an active crash bug under investigation as of week of 2026-04-20 (Maciej Stachura, 2026-04-24).
- **Status:** Flight test restart in progress; bench test work overdue.
- **Threat:** May 1, 2026 handoff delivery date is **at imminent risk** if bug repair extends beyond late April 2026.
- **Cost Implication:** Extended troubleshooting and flight testing consume labor and potentially equipment resources not separately budgeted under the original $22,337 contract.

#### 3. **Overdue Deliverables on Critical Path**
- **Visual Observation Bench Test:** Overdue as of 2026-04-24 (per Maciej Stachura, 2026-04-24). Must complete before flight test restart and S0 VTOL delivery.
- **Instrumented Bench Test:** Due mid-week after 2026-04-24; dependent on Visual Observation completion.
- **No documented labor contingency or schedule buffer** in the contract to absorb these delays.

#### 4. **Unbudgeted/Unassigned Training Deliverable**
- **Operator Training Materials & Supplies:** Due 2026-07-01 but unassigned with no curriculum finalized (as of 2026-04-24).
- **Travel & Training Delivery:** Jack Elston (handoff/training lead) scheduled to deliver training 2026-07-01–04 in Barbados. Travel, per diem, and training materials costs are **not separately invoiced** in the current $22,337 contract or visible in QuickBooks.
- **Risk:** If BST is providing training and materials at no additional cost, actual expenses may exceed contracted value.

#### 5. **Hardware Scarcity Risk Flagged**
- **Ground Station Assets:** Jack Elston flagged (2026-04-20) potential need to "shift some older ground stations from the S0-VTOL kits" depending on timeline. This suggests hardware originally allocated to the Barbados S0 VTOL may be reprioritized to higher-priority projects (e.g., S3 IRAD, ERAU S0 VTOL delivery, both ranked above this project in company priorities).
- **Cost Impact:** If substitution with "older" ground stations occurs, refurbishment, testing, or re-inventory costs may apply.

#### 6. **Regulatory/Compliance Uncertainty**
- **NDAA Compliance:** S0 VTOL is "can be" compliant but not "fully" compliant yet (Alex Lomis, 2026-04-17). Finalization status and cost impact unknown.
- **ISM Band Interference Risk:** Barbados Prime Minister's Office flagged 902–928 MHz ISM band interference risk. S0 VTOL uses Microhard P900 radio (FCC ID: NS913P900). Frequency-hopping may mitigate but **requires verification before operational deployment**. Cost of verification testing, potential radio swap, or regulatory remediation unknown.
