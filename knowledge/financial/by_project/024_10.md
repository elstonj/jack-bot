## Financial Health Assessment

### Overall Budget Status: **ON INVOICE, BUT DELIVERY AT CRITICAL RISK**

**Positives:**
- ✅ Contract fully funded and invoiced ($22,337 received in Feb. 2024).
- ✅ No payment delays or AR issues.
- ✅ Project has been allocated and paid in full; no outstanding contract value disputes.

**Critical Concerns:**

1. **Delivery Blocker — May 1, 2026 Handoff at Imminent Risk**
   - **S0 VTOL crash bug under active repair** (week of 2026-04-20); flight test restart is on critical path.
   - **Bench test work overdue as of 2026-04-24:** "Visual Observation Bench Test" incomplete; "Instrumented Bench Test" due mid-week after 2026-04-24 (Maciej Stachura).
   - If repair extends beyond late April 2026, May 1 delivery date will slip.
   - **Financial Impact:** Contract is fully invoiced, but performance failure (non-delivery or late delivery) creates reputational and potential refund/rework liability.

2. **Hardware Scarcity Flagged — Ground Station Asset Risk**
   - Jack Elston flagged (2026-04-20): "depending on timeline we might be able to shift some older ground stations from the S0-VTOL kits. (I hate to do that, but might be best)."
   - **Signal:** If timeline slips further, BST may need to reprioritize ground station components away from Barbados kit to support higher-priority S3 IRAD (ranked #1 among 5 company priorities per Maciej Stachura).
   - **Financial Impact:** Incomplete kit delivery could trigger client dispute or require expedited procurement/rework—potential cost overrun or margin erosion.

3. **Training Delivery Unassigned and Unbudgeted**
   - Operator training delivery scheduled July 1–4, 2026, but training materials and curriculum **unassigned.**
   - Travel costs for 2-person team (Jack Elston + support) to Barbados not yet isolated or confirmed in expense budget.
   - **Financial Impact:** If travel/training costs exceed the lump-sum contract value, BST absorbs overrun (no cost-plus mechanism indicated).

4. **ISM Band Interference Risk — Potential Rework Cost**
   - Barbados Prime Minister's Office flagged 902–928 MHz interference risk.
   - Microhard P900 radio (FCC/IC approved) operates in unregulated ISM band; frequency-hopping (200 kHz channels) may mitigate, but **verification required before operational deployment.**
   - **Financial Impact:** If verification fails, radio replacement or firmware redesign required—potential $2–5K+ rework cost absorbed by BST (not budgeted in fixed $22,337 contract).

5. **NDAA Compliance Not Finalized**
   - S0 VTOL, S0 AD, E2 platforms "can be" compliant but not "fully" compliant yet (Alex Lomis, 2026-04-17).
   - If DoD audit or client requirement (Barbados is CARICOM nation; likely indirect DoD/State Dept. scrutiny) forces remediation, rework cost risk.
   - **Financial Impact:** Unbudgeted compliance remediation could erode project margin.

6. **Project Ranking Risk — Low Priority vs. S3 IRAD**
   - Maciej Stachura ranked S0-VTOL as #2 among 5 company priorities (behind S3 IRAD with end-of-May delivery target).
   - If S3 IRAD slips, resources may be reallocated from Barbados S0-VTOL, compounding delivery delay.
   - **Financial Impact:** Delayed delivery = potential client penalty or goodwill cost.
